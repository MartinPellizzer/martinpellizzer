Acacia acanthocarpa var. uncinella

Acacia acanthocarpa var. uncinella, commonly known as the hooked acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny branches and small, yellow, globular flower heads. The plant produces flat, leathery seed pods that remain on the plant for an extended period. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
