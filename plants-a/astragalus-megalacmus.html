Astragalus megalacmus

Astragalus megalacmus, commonly known as giant milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its roots are often deep and extensive. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

Astragalus megalacmus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
