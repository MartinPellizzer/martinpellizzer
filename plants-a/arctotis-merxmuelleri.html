Arctotis merxmuelleri

Arctotis merxmuelleri, commonly known as the Blue Mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pollinated by birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Arctotis merxmuelleri is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
