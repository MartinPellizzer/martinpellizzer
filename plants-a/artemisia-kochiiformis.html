Artemisia kochiiformis

Artemisia kochiiformis, commonly known as the Japanese wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
