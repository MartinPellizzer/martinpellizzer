Astragalus managildensis

Astragalus managildensis, commonly known as the Managil's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
