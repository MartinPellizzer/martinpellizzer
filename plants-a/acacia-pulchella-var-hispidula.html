Acacia pulchella var. hispidula

Acacia pulchella var. hispidula, commonly known as the hispid acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of photosynthesis. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in dry climates.
