Anathallis ferdinandiana

Anathallis ferdinandiana, commonly known as the Ferdinand's spider orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender inflorescences bearing clusters of small, white, spider-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on the trunks of trees or in rock crevices. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of thynnid wasps, which are attracted to the plant's nectar.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is classified as a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
