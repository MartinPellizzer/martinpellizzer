Achyronia lanata

Achyronia lanata, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that turns red when mature.

Achyronia lanata is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
