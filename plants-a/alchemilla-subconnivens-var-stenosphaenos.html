Alchemilla subconnivens var. stenosphaenos

Alchemilla subconnivens var. stenosphaenos, commonly known as narrow-leaf alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its foliage is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
