Adenostyles petasites

Adenostyles petasites, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with showy petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh alpine climates.
