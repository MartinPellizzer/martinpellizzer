Astragalus nuttallianus

Astragalus nuttallianus, commonly known as Nuttall's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds within its legumes.

Astragalus nuttallianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
