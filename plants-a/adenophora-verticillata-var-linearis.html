Adenophora verticillata var. linearis

Adenophora verticillata var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features upright stems that bear whorled leaves, which are linear and often hairy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
