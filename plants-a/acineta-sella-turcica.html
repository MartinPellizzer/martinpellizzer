Acineta sella-turcica

Acineta sella-turcica, commonly known as the Turkish saddle cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to Turkey and is characterized by its distinctive saddle-shaped stem. The plant has a short, thick stem that is often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
