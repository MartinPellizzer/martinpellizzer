Amaranthus coracanus

Amaranthus coracanus, commonly known as the curly amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-angled, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields.

Amaranthus coracanus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by various indigenous cultures.
