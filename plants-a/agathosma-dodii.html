Agathosma dodii

Agathosma dodii, commonly known as the Cape garlic.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its pungent scent, which is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Agathosma dodii is also cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
