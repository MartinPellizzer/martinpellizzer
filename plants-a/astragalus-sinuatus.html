Astragalus sinuatus

Astragalus sinuatus, commonly known as the wavy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Astragalus sinuatus plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local biodiversity in its native habitat.
