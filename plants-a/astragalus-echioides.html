Astragalus echioides

Astragalus echioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink to purple and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Astragalus echioides is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
