Aleuritopteris dealbata

Aleuritopteris dealbata, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aleuritopteris dealbata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
