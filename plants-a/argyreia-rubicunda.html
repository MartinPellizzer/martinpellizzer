Argyreia rubicunda

Argyreia rubicunda, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and has a climbing habit, often found along fences or trees. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, orange capsule that splits open when mature.

Argyreia rubicunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
