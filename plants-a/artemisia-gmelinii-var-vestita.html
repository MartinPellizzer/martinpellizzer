Artemisia gmelinii var. vestita

Artemisia gmelinii var. vestita, commonly known as the white Artemisia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used traditionally in various medicinal and cultural practices.
