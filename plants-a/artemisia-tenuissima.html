Artemisia tenuissima

Artemisia tenuissima, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late summer, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
