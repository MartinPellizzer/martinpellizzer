Astragalus breviflorus var. glaber

Astragalus breviflorus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. Its leaves are compound, with narrow, linear leaflets that are smooth and glabrous.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
