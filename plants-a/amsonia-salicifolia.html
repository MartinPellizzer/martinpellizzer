Amsonia salicifolia

Amsonia salicifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, bluish-green leaves and its striking clusters of blue-violet flowers that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Amsonia salicifolia is also notable for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
