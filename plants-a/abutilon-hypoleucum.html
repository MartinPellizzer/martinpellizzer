Abutilon hypoleucum

Abutilon hypoleucum, commonly known as the white velvetleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
