Astragalus sanguinolentus

Astragalus sanguinolentus, commonly known as the bloodroot astragalus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and its root system is often extensive and fibrous. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
