Astragalus longicalyx

Astragalus longicalyx, commonly known as the longicalyx milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.

Astragalus longicalyx is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
