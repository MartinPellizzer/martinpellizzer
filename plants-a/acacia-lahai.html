Acacia lahai

Acacia lahai, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in compact clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flattened seeds. Acacia lahai thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
