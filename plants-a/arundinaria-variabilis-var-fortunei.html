Arundinaria variabilis var. fortunei

Arundinaria variabilis var. fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color. It is a clumping bamboo that grows in dense stands, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
