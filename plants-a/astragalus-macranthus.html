Astragalus macranthus

Astragalus macranthus, commonly known as the tall milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive violet or purple hue. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe.

Astragalus macranthus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
