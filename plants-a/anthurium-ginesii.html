Anthurium ginesii

Anthurium ginesii, commonly known as the Ginesii Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and can grow up to 2 meters in height under optimal conditions. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium ginesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
