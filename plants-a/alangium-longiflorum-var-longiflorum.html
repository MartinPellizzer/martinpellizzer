Alangium longiflorum var. longiflorum

Alangium longiflorum var. longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered alangium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
