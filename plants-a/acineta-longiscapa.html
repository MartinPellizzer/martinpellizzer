Acineta longiscapa

Acineta longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed acineta, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that form a rosette structure, often with a dense covering of fine hairs. Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the wild.
