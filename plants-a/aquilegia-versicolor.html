Aquilegia versicolor

Aquilegia versicolor, commonly known as the common columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and yellow, depending on the variety. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply cut lobes, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
