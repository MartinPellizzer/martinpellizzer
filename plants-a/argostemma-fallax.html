Argostemma fallax

Argostemma fallax, commonly known as the yellow spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
