Asparagus stipularis var. tenuispinus

Asparagus stipularis var. tenuispinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
