Arum angulatum

Arum angulatum, commonly known as the angular arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and sometimes toxic species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though caution is advised due to its potential toxicity.
