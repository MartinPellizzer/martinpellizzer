Acacia durangensis

Acacia durangensis, commonly known as the Durango acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting various pollinators.

The bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
