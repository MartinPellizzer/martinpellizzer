Abrus longibracteatus

Abrus longibracteatus, commonly known as the long bract maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive red, fluffy fruits that contain toxic seeds. The seeds are enclosed in a persistent, showy calyx that remains attached to the plant after maturity.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
