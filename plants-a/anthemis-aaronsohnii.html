Anthemis aaronsohnii

Anthemis aaronsohnii, commonly known as Aaronsohn's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disk. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
