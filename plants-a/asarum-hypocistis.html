Asarum hypocistis

Asarum hypocistis, commonly known as the lesser hogweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic scent that attracts pollinators such as flies and beetles. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
