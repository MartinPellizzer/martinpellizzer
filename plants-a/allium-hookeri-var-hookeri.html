Allium hookeri var. hookeri

Allium hookeri var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers in the summer months. The plant is characterized by its small, rounded bulbs that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular plant among gardeners interested in native flora.
