Aster glutescens

Aster glutescens, commonly known as the hairy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are purple to pink in color.

Aster glutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
