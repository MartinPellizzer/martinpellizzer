Atropis permixta

Atropis permixta, commonly known as the mixed-leaf atropis, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its intricate leaf morphology.

Its leaves exhibit a distinctive pattern of alternating orientations, with some leaves curling clockwise and others counterclockwise, creating a visually striking appearance. This phenomenon, known as heterophylly, is thought to be an adaptation to its specific environmental conditions. The plant thrives in shaded, moist habitats, typically found in temperate forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and unique botanical features, Atropis permixta is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
