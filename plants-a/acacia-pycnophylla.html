Acacia pycnophylla

Acacia pycnophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, phyllodes that are narrow and leathery. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Acacia pycnophylla is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
