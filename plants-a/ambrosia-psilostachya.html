Ambrosia psilostachya

Ambrosia psilostachya, commonly known as goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant is often found in prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its feathery leaves and showy inflorescences make it a common sight in many regions of the United States.

Ambrosia psilostachya plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
