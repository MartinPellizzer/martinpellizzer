Arundinaria japonica

Arundinaria japonica, commonly known as Japanese bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Native to Japan, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and resilience.
