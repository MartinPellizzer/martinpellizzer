Anacamptis picta f. trimaculata

Anacamptis picta f. trimaculata, commonly known as the Three-spot Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a distinctive three-lobed lip. The flower's coloration typically includes shades of purple and white, with intricate patterns that enhance its visual appeal. This orchid thrives in calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded habitats.

It is a perennial plant that blooms in late spring to early summer, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
