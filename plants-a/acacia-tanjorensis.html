Acacia tanjorensis

Acacia tanjorensis, commonly known as the Indian acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Acacia tanjorensis is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
