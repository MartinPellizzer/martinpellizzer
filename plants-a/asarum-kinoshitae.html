Asarum kinoshitae

Asarum kinoshitae, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique, helmet-shaped flower that is often covered in a dense, hairy covering. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
