Allium dumebuchum

Allium dumebuchum, commonly known as the Cape mountain onion, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.

Allium dumebuchum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
