Arisaema ringens var. sieboldii

Arisaema ringens var. sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese umbrella plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a broad, white spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It is native to East Asia, including Japan, Korea, and parts of China.
