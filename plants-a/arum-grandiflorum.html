Arum grandiflorum

Arum grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, often with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces clusters of white, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a showy, spatulate purple bract, which gives it its dramatic appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies.

Arum grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
