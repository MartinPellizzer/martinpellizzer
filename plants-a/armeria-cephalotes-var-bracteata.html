Armeria cephalotes var. bracteata

Armeria cephalotes var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features basal leaves that are linear and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
