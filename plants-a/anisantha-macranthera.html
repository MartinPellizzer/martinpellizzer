Anisantha macranthera

Anisantha macranthera, commonly known as the large-headed grass, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow leaves, and its most distinctive feature is its large, showy inflorescence. The flowers are typically arranged in panicles and are often visited by insects, contributing to its role in pollination.

Anisantha macranthera is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
