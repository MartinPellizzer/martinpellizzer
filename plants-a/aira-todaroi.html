Aira todaroi

Aira todaroi, commonly known as the Japanese false oat, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aira todaroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insect species.
