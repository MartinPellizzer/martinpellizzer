Acacia amythethophylla

Acacia amythethophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia amythethophylla plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
