Acer pavia

Acer pavia, commonly known as the red maple or red horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The tree has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are small, yellow-green catkins that appear in early spring. Acer pavia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

It is relatively resistant to pests and diseases, making it a hardy choice for landscaping.
