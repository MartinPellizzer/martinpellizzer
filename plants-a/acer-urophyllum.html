Acer urophyllum

Acer urophyllum, commonly known as the redleaf maple, is a small deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which turns vibrant shades of orange and red in the fall. The plant has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically red or purple when young. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

The tree is well adapted to rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.
