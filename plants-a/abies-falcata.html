Abies falcata

Abies falcata, commonly known as the sawtooth fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, saw-toothed leaf margins, which give it its common name. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and are arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are elongated and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color.

The sawtooth fir thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
