Athyrium andapense

Athyrium andapense, commonly known as the Philippine fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's ornamental value has also made it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
