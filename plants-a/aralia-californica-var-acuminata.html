Aralia californica var. acuminata

Aralia californica var. acuminata, commonly known as the California hedge spurge, is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of California.

It features compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
