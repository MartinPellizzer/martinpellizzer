Aulomyrcia chrysophylla

Aulomyrcia chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leafed myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and a warm, humid climate to thrive.
