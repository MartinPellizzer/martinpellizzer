Anemia phyllitidis f. transitoria

Anemia phyllitidis f. transitoria, commonly known as the transient fern, is a rare and delicate fern species characterized by its ephemeral nature.

It features delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically short-lived, often appearing and disappearing with seasonal changes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can absorb necessary nutrients from the soil. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile growth habits, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in certain regions.
