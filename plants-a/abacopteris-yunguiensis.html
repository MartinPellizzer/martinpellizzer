Abacopteris yunguiensis

Abacopteris yunguiensis, commonly known as the Yunguiensis cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to China.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically appearing on separate plants. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts.
