Aira pulchella var. provincialis

Aira pulchella var. provincialis, commonly known as the purple false oat, is a perennial grass native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinted inflorescence, which resembles a loose panicle. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other riparian habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
