Acanthocarpus mucronatus

Acanthocarpus mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthocarp, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its seed pods are hairy and contain small, hard seeds.

It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock.
