Alectryon tomentosus

Alectryon tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly alectryon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Alectryon tomentosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
