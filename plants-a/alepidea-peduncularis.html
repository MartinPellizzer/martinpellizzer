Alepidea peduncularis

Alepidea peduncularis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock fig, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, hot climates.

Alepidea peduncularis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
