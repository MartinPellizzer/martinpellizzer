Acacia parramattensis

Acacia parramattensis, commonly known as the Parramatta wattle, is a small shrub native to the Sydney region of Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and narrow, feathery leaves. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Acacia parramattensis typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is protected in some areas.
