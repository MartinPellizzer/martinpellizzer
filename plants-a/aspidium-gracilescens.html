Aspidium gracilescens

Aspidium gracilescens, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Aspidium gracilescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
