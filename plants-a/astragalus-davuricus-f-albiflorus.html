Astragalus davuricus f. albiflorus

Astragalus davuricus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and grasslands. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility, and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value also makes it a subject of interest in horticulture.
