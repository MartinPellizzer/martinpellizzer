Acanthocereus occidentalis

Acanthocereus occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Prickly Pear, is a cacti species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and clusters of spiny, paddle-shaped pads. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, a large, spiny berry, is edible when ripe and contains sweet, juicy pulp.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
