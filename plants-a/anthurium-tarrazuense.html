Anthurium tarrazuense

Anthurium tarrazuense, commonly known as the Tarrazu anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped foliage and vibrant, spatulate inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are glossy and deep green, often with a waxy texture, while the spathes can range in color from red to orange.

Anthurium tarrazuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
