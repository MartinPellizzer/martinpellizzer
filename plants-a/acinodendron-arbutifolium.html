Acinodendron arbutifolium

Acinodendron arbutifolium, commonly known as the rough-leaved acinodendron, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. These flowers are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
