Astragalus oreophilus

Astragalus oreophilus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, enabling it to access moisture in arid conditions.

Astragalus oreophilus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitats.
