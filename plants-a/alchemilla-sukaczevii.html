Alchemilla sukaczevii

Alchemilla sukaczevii, commonly known as the Sukaczev's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in dense, soft white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla sukaczevii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
