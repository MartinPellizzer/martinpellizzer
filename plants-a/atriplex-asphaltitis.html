Atriplex asphaltitis

Atriplex asphaltitis, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it thrive in saline soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Atriplex asphaltitis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
