Agave sebastiana

Agave sebastiana, commonly known as the Mexican hat plant or the blue agave, is a succulent native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellow flowers, which are often used in traditional ceremonies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Agave sebastiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
