Anemone obtusiloba var. spatulata

Anemone obtusiloba var. spatulata, commonly known as the spoonleaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to spoon-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
