Astragalus yidunensis

Astragalus yidunensis, commonly known as the Yidun milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cold, arid conditions with well-drained soil.

Astragalus yidunensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
