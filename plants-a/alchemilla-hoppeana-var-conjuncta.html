Alchemilla hoppeana var. conjuncta

Alchemilla hoppeana var. conjuncta, commonly known as the clustered lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Rosaceae family.
