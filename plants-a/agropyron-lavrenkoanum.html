Agropyron lavrenkoanum

Agropyron lavrenkoanum, commonly known as the Lavrenko's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a tough, fibrous texture.

Agropyron lavrenkoanum is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and as a forage plant for livestock.
