Apostasia gracilis

Apostasia gracilis, commonly known as the slender spathephyllum, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, elongated inflorescence bearing small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, rich soil to flourish. Its blooming period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it is highly valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, Apostasia gracilis is considered endangered and is often cultivated in conservation programs.
