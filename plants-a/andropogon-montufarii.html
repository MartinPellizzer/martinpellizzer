Andropogon montufarii

Andropogon montufarii, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Andropogon montufarii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
