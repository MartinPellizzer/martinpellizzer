Acampe pachyglossa

Acampe pachyglossa, commonly known as the broad-lipped acampe, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
