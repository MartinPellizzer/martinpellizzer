Aristolochia delavayi

Aristolochia delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, with a rich purple to brown coloration. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses.
