Andropogon polyptychos

Andropogon polyptychos, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn a reddish hue in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it ecologically significant.

Andropogon polyptychos is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in restoration projects.
