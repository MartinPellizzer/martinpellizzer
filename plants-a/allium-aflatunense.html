Allium aflatunense

Allium aflatunense, commonly known as the Aflatun onion, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in appearance to garlic and onions, though they have a milder flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Allium aflatunense is valued both for its culinary use and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
