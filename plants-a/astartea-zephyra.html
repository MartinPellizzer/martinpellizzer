Astartea zephyra

Astartea zephyra, commonly known as the windflower, is a delicate herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astartea zephyra is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
