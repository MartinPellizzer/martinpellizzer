Aconitum laxiflorum

Aconitum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are typically clustered in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum laxiflorum is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
