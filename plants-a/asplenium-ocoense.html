Asplenium ocoense

Asplenium ocoense, commonly known as the Ocoa spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and often have a slightly curled or twisted appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
