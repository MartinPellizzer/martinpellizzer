Artanthe tejucensis

Artanthe tejucensis, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a serrated margin, and the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Artanthe tejucensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
