Arenaria heterophylla

Arenaria heterophylla, commonly known as sandspoon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically spoon-shaped, hence the common name, and are covered in fine hairs.

It is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
