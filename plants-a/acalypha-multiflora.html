Acalypha multiflora

Acalypha multiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false holly, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a velvety texture. Acalypha multiflora is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.
