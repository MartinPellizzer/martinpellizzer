Amsonia ciliata var. ciliata

Amsonia ciliata var. ciliata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and fluffy, lavender-blue flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with soft, hairy margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

The flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value.
