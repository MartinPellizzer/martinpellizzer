Andropogon pseudohirtus

Andropogon pseudohirtus, commonly known as the rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture, and its flowering stalks produce small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate periodic flooding, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Andropogon pseudohirtus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
