Artemisia prattii

Artemisia prattii, commonly known as Pratt's sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open landscapes. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and have been used in traditional medicine.

Artemisia prattii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
