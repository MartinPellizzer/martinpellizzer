Andryala conyzoides

Andryala conyzoides, commonly known as wild lettuce, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It features alternate, hairy, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a distinct central vein. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, pale green to brown fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
