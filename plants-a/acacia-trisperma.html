Acacia trisperma

Acacia trisperma, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, woody pods containing seeds. Acacia trisperma is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought resistance.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
