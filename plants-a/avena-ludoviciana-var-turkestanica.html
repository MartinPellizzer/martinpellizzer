Avena ludoviciana var. turkestanica

Avena ludoviciana var. turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan oat, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. The plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its adaptability in arid and semi-arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important forage plant and a subject of study in ecological and agricultural research.
