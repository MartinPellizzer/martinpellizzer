Alchemilla carthalinica

Alchemilla carthalinica, commonly known as the golden tutsan, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish down, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla carthalinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
