Anemone piperi

Anemone piperi, commonly known as the Greek anemone, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white petals and distinctive, palmate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It blooms in early spring, producing delicate flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anemone piperi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation efforts due to its ecological importance.
