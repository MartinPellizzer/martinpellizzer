Aspalathus vermiculata

Aspalathus vermiculata, commonly known as the rooibos plant, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to the arid and semi-arid conditions of its native habitat. It is cultivated for its unique red-brown tea, which is rich in antioxidants.

The botanical features of Aspalathus vermiculata make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
