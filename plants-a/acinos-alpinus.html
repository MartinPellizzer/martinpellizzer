Acinos alpinus

Acinos alpinus, commonly known as the alpine everlet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow, hairy, and adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Acinos alpinus thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous habitats.
