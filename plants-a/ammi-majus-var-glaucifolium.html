Ammi majus var. glaucifolium

Ammi majus var. glaucifolium, commonly known as the glaucous cow parsley, is a variant of the common cow parsley native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a biennial herb characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, often with a bluish-gray coating, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
