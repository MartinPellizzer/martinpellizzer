Arum liepoldtii

Arum liepoldtii, commonly known as the Lepoldt's arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often green with a reddish or purple tint on the underside. Its flowers are typically small and clustered around the spadix, which is surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe.

Arum liepoldtii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in moist, shaded environments.
