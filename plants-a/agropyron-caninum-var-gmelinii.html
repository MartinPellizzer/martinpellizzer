Agropyron caninum var. gmelinii

Agropyron caninum var. gmelinii, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soils and low moisture conditions.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitat.
