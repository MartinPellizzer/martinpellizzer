Attalea uberrima

Attalea uberrima, commonly known as the Brazilian buriti palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into edible fruits. The plant thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found near water bodies. Its trunk is thick and fibrous, making it valuable for construction and crafts.

The fruit is a key food source for both wildlife and humans in its native habitat.
