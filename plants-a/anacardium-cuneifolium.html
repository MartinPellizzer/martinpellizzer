Anacardium cuneifolium

Anacardium cuneifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian cashew or cuneifolium annatto, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing a single seed. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and have a mild nutty flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
