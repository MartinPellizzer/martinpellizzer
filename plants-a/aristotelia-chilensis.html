Aristotelia chilensis

Aristotelia chilensis, commonly known as the maqui berry, is a shrub native to southern Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture.

The berries are typically harvested in late summer and are used in traditional medicine and as a superfood due to their high anthocyanin content.
