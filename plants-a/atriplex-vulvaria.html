Atriplex vulvaria

Atriplex vulvaria, commonly known as saltbush or green saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is highly adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as forage for livestock.

Atriplex vulvaria plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
