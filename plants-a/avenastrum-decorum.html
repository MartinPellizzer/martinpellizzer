Avenastrum decorum

Avenastrum decorum, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Avenastrum decorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
