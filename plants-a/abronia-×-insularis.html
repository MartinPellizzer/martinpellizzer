Abronia × insularis

Abronia × insularis, commonly known as the Florida beach plum, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of Florida.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
