Amaryllis curvifolia

Amaryllis curvifolia, commonly known as the curly-leaved amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, wavy leaves that curl outward, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Amaryllis curvifolia is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
