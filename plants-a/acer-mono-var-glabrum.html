Acer mono var. glabrum

Acer mono var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, unbranched trunk and distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
