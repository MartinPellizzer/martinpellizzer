Amaryllis monantha

Amaryllis monantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, narrow leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely appreciated by horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
