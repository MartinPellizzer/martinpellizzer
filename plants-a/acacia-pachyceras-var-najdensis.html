Acacia pachyceras var. najdensis

Acacia pachyceras var. najdensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and leathery. The plant produces yellow floral spikes that are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
