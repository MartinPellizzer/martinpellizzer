Alnus jorullensis f. angustifolia

Alnus jorullensis f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alder, is a botanical variant of the alder genus native to specific regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often yellowish-green in color, typical of the Betulaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or near water bodies.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
