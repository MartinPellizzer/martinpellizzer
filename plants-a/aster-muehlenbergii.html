Aster muehlenbergii

Aster muehlenbergii, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to pinkish-purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
