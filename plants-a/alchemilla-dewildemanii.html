Alchemilla dewildemanii

Alchemilla dewildemanii, commonly known as the dewy alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that appear in spring, and its leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
