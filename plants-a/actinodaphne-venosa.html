Actinodaphne venosa

Actinodaphne venosa, commonly known as the veined laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences, followed by small, oval berries. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, often used in traditional craftsmanship.

The species is also noted for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
