Andinia triangularis

Andinia triangularis, commonly known as the triangular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves and showy, bell-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.

Andinia triangularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
