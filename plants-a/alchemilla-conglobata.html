Alchemilla conglobata

Alchemilla conglobata, commonly known as the clustered lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features basal rosettes of heart-shaped, silver-velvet leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

Alchemilla conglobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
