Agastache pallidiflora

Agastache pallidiflora, commonly known as the paleflower hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features spikes of pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Agastache pallidiflora is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
