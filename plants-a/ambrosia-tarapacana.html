Ambrosia tarapacana

Ambrosia tarapacana, commonly known as the Andean cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Ambrosia tarapacana is also known for its role in traditional Andean medicine.
