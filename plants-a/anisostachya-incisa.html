Anisostachya incisa

Anisostachya incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It has fibrous roots and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
