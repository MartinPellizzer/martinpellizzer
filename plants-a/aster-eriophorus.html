Aster eriophorus

Aster eriophorus, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the fall, typically in shades of purple or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Aster eriophorus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
