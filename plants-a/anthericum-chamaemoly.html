Anthericum chamaemoly

Anthericum chamaemoly, commonly known as the creeping arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. The plant produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
