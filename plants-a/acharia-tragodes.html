Acharia tragodes

Acharia tragodes, commonly known as the red legged flower beetle, is a species of leaf beetle belonging to the family Chrysomelidae.

It is native to North America and is often found on plants in the genus Asteraceae, such as goldenrod and thistles. The beetle's larvae feed on the leaves of these plants, causing noticeable damage. The adults are small, with a distinctive red coloration on their legs and a metallic green body.

This species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by contributing to the natural decomposition process and serving as a food source for various predators.
