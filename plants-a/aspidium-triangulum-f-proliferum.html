Aspidium triangulum f. proliferum

Aspidium triangulum f. proliferum, commonly known as the spreading wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds and growth habit.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features elongated, triangular fronds that are often divided into smaller, lanceolate segments. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in forest understories.
