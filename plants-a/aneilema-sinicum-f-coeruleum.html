Aneilema sinicum f. coeruleum

Aneilema sinicum f. coeruleum, commonly known as the blue-flowered aneilema, is a variant of the species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in terminal clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
