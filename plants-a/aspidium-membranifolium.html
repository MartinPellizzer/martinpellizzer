Aspidium membranifolium

Aspidium membranifolium, commonly known as the membranous fern, is a species of polypody fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. The plant features delicate, feather-like fronds with a distinctive membranous texture on the underside. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Aspidium membranifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
