Arabis ciliata var. elatior

Arabis ciliata var. elatior, commonly known as the greater wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, elongated leaves with finely toothed margins, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Arabis ciliata var. elatior is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
