Amaryllis incarnata

Amaryllis incarnata, commonly known as the naked lady, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant features strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges from the bulb after flowering. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Amaryllis incarnata is known for its ability to naturalize and return year after year, especially in mild climates.
