Artemisia apiacea

Artemisia apiacea, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features aromatic, silvery-gray foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and stems, contain compounds like thujone and artemisinin, which have medicinal properties.

Artemisia apiacea is often used in traditional medicine and is also a key ingredient in the production of certain herbal remedies.
