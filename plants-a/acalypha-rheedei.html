Acalypha rheedei

Acalypha rheedei, commonly known as the red-hot catshrub, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, reddish bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha rheedei is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and as a potted plant.
