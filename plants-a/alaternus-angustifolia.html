Alaternus angustifolia

Alaternus angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf alaternus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
