Amelanchier amelanchier var. grandifolia

Amelanchier amelanchier var. grandifolia, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible berries in late summer that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
