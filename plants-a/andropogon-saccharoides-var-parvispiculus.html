Andropogon saccharoides var. parvispiculus

Andropogon saccharoides var. parvispiculus, commonly known as dwarf switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential use in sustainable landscaping.
