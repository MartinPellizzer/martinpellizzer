Abies viminalis

Abies viminalis, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its dense, dark green needles that are slightly twisted and have a silvery underside. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-white, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

Abies viminalis is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude forests, where it plays an important ecological role.
