Aconitum brevipes

Aconitum brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum brevipes contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
