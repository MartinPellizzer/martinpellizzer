Asarum sieboldii subsp. heterotropoides

Asarum sieboldii subsp.

heterotropoides, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan. It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and a distinctive, aromatic underground rhizome.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are borne in a spathaceous cluster, often hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
