Alstonia saligna

Alstonia saligna, commonly known as the Indian almond tree or the banyan tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and can grow to impressive heights, often reaching over 30 meters. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots can develop aerial roots that hang from branches, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Alstonia saligna is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in various regions.
