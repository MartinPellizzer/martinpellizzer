Adenophora longipedicellata

Adenophora longipedicellata, commonly known as the long pedicellate bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a bluish tinge. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowering period occurs in summer, and it produces small, dry fruits called capsules.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
