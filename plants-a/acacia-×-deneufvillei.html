Acacia × deneufvillei

Acacia × deneufvillei, commonly known as the hybrid acacia, is a cross between two acacia species, typically Acacia aneura and Acacia longifolia.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Australia, characterized by its slender, often multi-branched form. The plant features phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Acacia × deneufvillei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
