Asplenium fawcettii

Asplenium fawcettii, commonly known as the Fawcett's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in shaded, moist environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia on the underside of its fronds. It is characterized by its compact size and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Asplenium fawcettii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care.
