Arrojadoa rhodantha subsp. canudosensis

Arrojadoa rhodantha subsp.

canudosensis, commonly known as the Canudos firebush, is a shrub native to Brazil. It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

The plant has woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in the wild.
