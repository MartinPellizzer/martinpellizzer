Allium ampeloprasum var. duriaeanum

Allium ampeloprasum var. duriaeanum, commonly known as the Giant Purple Garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, and its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in umbels. The plant produces large bulbs that are often used in culinary applications, though they are less common than other Allium species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and showy flowers.
