Agrostis alba var. schimperiana

Agrostis alba var. schimperiana, commonly known as the white bentgrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with a pale blue or greenish hue.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
