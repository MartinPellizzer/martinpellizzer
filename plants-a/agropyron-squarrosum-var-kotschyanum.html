Agropyron squarrosum var. kotschyanum

Agropyron squarrosum var. kotschyanum, commonly known as the Kotschyan's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and desert regions.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
