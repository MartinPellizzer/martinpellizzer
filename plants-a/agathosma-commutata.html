Agathosma commutata

Agathosma commutata, commonly known as Cape garlic.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is part of the Rutaceae family and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their essential oils.

Agathosma commutata is cultivated for its ornamental value and its contribution to the fragrance industry.
