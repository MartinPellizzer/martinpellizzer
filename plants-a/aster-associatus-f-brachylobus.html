Aster associatus f. brachylobus

Aster associatus f. brachylobus, commonly known as the short-stemmed aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is native to the mountainous regions of North America. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or woodland habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
