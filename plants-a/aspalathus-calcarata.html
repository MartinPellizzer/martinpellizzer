Aspalathus calcarata

Aspalathus calcarata, commonly known as the mountain tea or red bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, often growing on slopes and in sandy soils. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are typically visited by bees and other pollinators.

Aspalathus calcarata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
