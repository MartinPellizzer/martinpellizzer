Anchusa alborosea f. coerulea

Anchusa alborosea f. coerulea, commonly known as the blueflower anchusa, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is native to the Iberian Peninsula. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
