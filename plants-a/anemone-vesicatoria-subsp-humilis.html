Anemone vesicatoria subsp. humilis

Anemone vesicatoria subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the lesser spearwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter stature and more compact growth compared to other members of the species.
