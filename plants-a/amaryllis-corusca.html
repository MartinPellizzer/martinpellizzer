Amaryllis corusca

Amaryllis corusca, commonly known as the white amaryllis, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and slightly wavy. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis corusca is a popular choice for gardeners due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
