Ananas comosus var. rudis

Ananas comosus var. rudis, commonly known as the wild pineapple, is a lesser-known variant of the pineapple plant native to South America.

This variety is characterized by its smaller, more rugged appearance compared to the cultivated pineapple. It possesses a dense, spiky leaf rosette and produces a fruit that is typically less sweet and more fibrous. The plant is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its wild nature, it is less commonly cultivated but holds significance in botanical studies and traditional uses.
