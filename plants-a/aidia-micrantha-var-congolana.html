Aidia micrantha var. congolana

Aidia micrantha var. congolana, commonly known as the Congo starflower, is a flowering plant native to central and western Africa.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
