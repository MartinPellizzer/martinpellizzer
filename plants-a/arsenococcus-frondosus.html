Arsenococcus frondosus

Arsenococcus frondosus, commonly known as the golden fern, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its intricate, feather-like fronds that give it a fern-like appearance, though it is not a true fern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.

Arsenococcus frondosus is also notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
