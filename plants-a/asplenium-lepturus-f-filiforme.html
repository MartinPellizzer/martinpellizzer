Asplenium lepturus f. filiforme

Asplenium lepturus f. filiforme, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and often arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, typical of many ferns in the Aspleniaceae family.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
