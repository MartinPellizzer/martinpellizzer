Artanthe rumicifolia

Artanthe rumicifolia, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Artanthe rumicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
