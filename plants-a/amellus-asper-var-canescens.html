Amellus asper var. canescens

Amellus asper var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy rough amellus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-gray underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow to orange, and it blooms during the warmer months.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to dry and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
