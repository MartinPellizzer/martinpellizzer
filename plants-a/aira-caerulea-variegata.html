Aira caerulea variegata

Aira caerulea variegata, commonly known as the variegated blue grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves with a striking variegation of green and white or yellow, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique foliage and modest floral display.
