Allium sultaniae-ismailii

Allium sultaniae-ismailii, commonly known as the Ismaili garlic.

This plant is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bulbous structure, which is typically smaller than that of common garlic. The leaves are grass-like and the flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in umbels.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
