Actinidia kolomikta var. sugawarana

Actinidia kolomikta var. sugawarana, commonly known as the Japanese actinidia or variegated kiwi, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves that display white, pink, and green patterns during the growing season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are similar to kiwifruit in taste and texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
