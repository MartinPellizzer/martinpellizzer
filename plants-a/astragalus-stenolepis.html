Astragalus stenolepis

Astragalus stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its small, yellow or pale green flowers bloom in late spring or early summer.

Astragalus stenolepis is often found in steppes and grasslands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
