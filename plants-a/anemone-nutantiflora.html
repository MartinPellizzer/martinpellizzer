Anemone nutantiflora

Anemone nutantiflora, commonly known as the nodding anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that droop downward, giving it a nodding appearance. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone nutantiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and woodland settings.
