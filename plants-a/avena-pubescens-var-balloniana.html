Avena pubescens var. balloniana

Avena pubescens var. balloniana, commonly known as the balloon plant, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, balloon-like seed heads that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
