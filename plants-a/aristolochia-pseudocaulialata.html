Aristolochia pseudocaulialata

Aristolochia pseudocaulialata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
