Aspidium muricatum var. muelleri

Aspidium muricatum var. muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's wood fern, is a variant of the common wood fern native to South America.

It is a perennial herb with a creeping rhizome and feather-like fronds that are divided into pinnae. The fronds are typically dark green and have a rough, serrated texture on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
