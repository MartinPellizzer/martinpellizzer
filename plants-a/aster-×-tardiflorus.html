Aster × tardiflorus

Aster × tardiflorus, commonly known as the late-flowering aster, is a hybrid plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Aster × tardiflorus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
