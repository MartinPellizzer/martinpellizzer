Aster sedifolius

Aster sedifolius, commonly known as the threadleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and prairies, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and thread-like, giving it its common name, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Aster sedifolius is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
