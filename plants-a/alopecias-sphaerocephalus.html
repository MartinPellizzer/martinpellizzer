Alopecias sphaerocephalus

Alopecias sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe-headed alopecuria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical shape and dense covering of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
