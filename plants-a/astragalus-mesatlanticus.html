Astragalus mesatlanticus

Astragalus mesatlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the Atlantic coast. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus mesatlanticus plays a role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local biodiversity in its habitat.
