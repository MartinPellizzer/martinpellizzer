Abies grandis var. lowiana

Abies grandis var. lowiana, commonly known as the lowland white fir, is a variant of the grand fir native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are shorter than those of the typical grand fir. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, with a reddish-brown color when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in montane and subalpine regions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
