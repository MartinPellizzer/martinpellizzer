Astragalus leucopsis var. curtipes

Astragalus leucopsis var. curtipes, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
