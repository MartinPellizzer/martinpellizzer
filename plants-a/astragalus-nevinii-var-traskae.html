Astragalus nevinii var. traskae

Astragalus nevinii var. traskae, commonly known as Trask's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets arranged along the stem.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
