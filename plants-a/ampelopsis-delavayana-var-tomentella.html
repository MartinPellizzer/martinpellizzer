Ampelopsis delavayana var. tomentella

Ampelopsis delavayana var. tomentella, commonly known as the hairy grape ivy, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of blue-black berries. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to trail from structures.

Botanically, it belongs to the Vitaceae family, which includes grapes and other climbing plants.
