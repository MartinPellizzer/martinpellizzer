Astragalus maniaticus

Astragalus maniaticus, commonly known as the crazy pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to central and southern Europe, where it thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow or orange pea-like flowers. Its seed pods are typically hairy and contain several small, lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus maniaticus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.
