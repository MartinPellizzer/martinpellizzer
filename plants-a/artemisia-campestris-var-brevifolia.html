Artemisia campestris var. brevifolia

Artemisia campestris var. brevifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with finely divided, narrow leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in herbal medicine and as a companion plant in gardens.
