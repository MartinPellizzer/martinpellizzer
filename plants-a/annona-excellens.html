Annona excellens

Annona excellens, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by round, yellow or green fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
