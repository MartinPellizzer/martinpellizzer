Aristolochia hassleriana

Aristolochia hassleriana, commonly known as the purple waxflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, aromatic, and often tubular-shaped flowers that range in color from green to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and contains compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
