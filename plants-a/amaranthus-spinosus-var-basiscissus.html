Amaranthus spinosus var. basiscissus

Amaranthus spinosus var. basiscissus, commonly known as the spiny amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive spiny stems, which provide structural support and deter herbivores. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences. This variety is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
