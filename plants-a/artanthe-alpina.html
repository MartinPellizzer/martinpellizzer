Artanthe alpina

Artanthe alpina, commonly known as alpine artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is usually basal, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Artanthe alpina produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
