Astragalus songaricus

Astragalus songaricus, commonly known as the Songarica milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

Astragalus songaricus is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is of interest for its potential ecological and pharmacological properties.
