Actinostemma lobatum var. palmatum

Actinostemma lobatum var. palmatum, commonly known as the palmate actinostemma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinct, fern-like appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often found in moist, wooded areas and is of interest to botanists and gardeners alike.
