Allinum

Allinum, commonly known as leek, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to onions and garlic.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, hollow leaves and a bulbous stem that develops underground. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in temperate climates. Allinum is cultivated for its edible leaves and bulbs, which have a mild, sweet flavor.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight for optimal growth.
