Acacia pallidifolia

Acacia pallidifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in compact, globular inflorescences. Acacia pallidifolia is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
