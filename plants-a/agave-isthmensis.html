Agave isthmensis

Agave isthmensis, commonly known as the Isthmus agave, is a species native to the highlands of southern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-forming structure and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Agave isthmensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
