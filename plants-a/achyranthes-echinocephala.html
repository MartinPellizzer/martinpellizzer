Achyranthes echinocephala

Achyranthes echinocephala, commonly known as the spiky beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in many parts of the world.
