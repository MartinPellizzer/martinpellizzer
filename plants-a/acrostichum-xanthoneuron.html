Acrostichum xanthoneuron

Acrostichum xanthoneuron, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that are typically bright green with a yellowish tinge on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The golden fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
