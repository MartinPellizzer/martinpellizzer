Arenaria modesta var. purpurascens

Arenaria modesta var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
