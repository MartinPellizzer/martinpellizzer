Agrimonia eupatoria var. odorata

Agrimonia eupatoria var. odorata, commonly known as sweet agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called cymes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
