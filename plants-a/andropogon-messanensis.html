Andropogon messanensis

Andropogon messanensis, commonly known as the Messan grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes numerous grasses that are economically and ecologically significant. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves.

Andropogon messanensis is often used in traditional medicine and may have potential applications in modern pharmacology due to its bioactive compounds.
