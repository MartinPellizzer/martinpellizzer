Achatocarpus oaxacanus

Achatocarpus oaxacanus, commonly known as the Oaxaca acacia, is a shrub native to the highlands of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Achatocarpus.

It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
