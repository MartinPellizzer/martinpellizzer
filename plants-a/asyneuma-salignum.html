Asyneuma salignum

Asyneuma salignum, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Asyneuma salignum is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
