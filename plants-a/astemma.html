Astemma

Astemma, commonly known as the star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Astemma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.
