Allium subhirsutum var. trifoliatum

Allium subhirsutum var. trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaf garlic or the mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant is known for its strong garlic scent and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
