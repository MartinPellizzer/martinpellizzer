Amsonia tharpii

Amsonia tharpii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils. Its cylindrical, woody stems are often branched and can become somewhat woody with age.

Amsonia tharpii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
