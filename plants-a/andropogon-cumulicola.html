Andropogon cumulicola

Andropogon cumulicola, commonly known as the hillside bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, fluffy seeds. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
