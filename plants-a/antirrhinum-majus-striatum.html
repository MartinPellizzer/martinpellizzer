Antirrhinum majus striatum

Antirrhinum majus striatum, commonly known as the striped snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange, with a striking striped pattern. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Antirrhinum majus striatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
