Aristida stipitata subsp. graciliflora

Aristida stipitata subsp.

graciliflora, commonly known as slender-flowered threeawn, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
