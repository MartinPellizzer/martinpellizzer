Allium douglasii var. columbianum

Allium douglasii var. columbianum, commonly known as Douglas's onion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a tight cluster. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
