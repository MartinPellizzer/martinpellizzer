Andromachia sessilifolia

Andromachia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf andromachia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically features basal leaves that are sessile, meaning they lack stalks, and are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color and bloom during the cooler months.

Andromachia sessilifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
