Aiphanes minima

Aiphanes minima, commonly known as the dwarf papyrus palm, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a compact structure, often with a tuft of flowering stems at the center. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that develop into three-lobed, canoe-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
