Arnica cordifolia

Arnica cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are heart-shaped and its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Arnica cordifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
