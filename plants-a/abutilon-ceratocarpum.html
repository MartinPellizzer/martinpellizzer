Abutilon ceratocarpum

Abutilon ceratocarpum, commonly known as the horned abutilon, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by distinctive, spiny capsules as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
