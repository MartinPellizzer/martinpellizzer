Alchemilla reniformis subsp. ursina

Alchemilla reniformis subsp.

ursina, commonly known as the bear's paw alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
