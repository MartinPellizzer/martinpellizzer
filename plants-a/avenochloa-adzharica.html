Avenochloa adzharica

Avenochloa adzharica, commonly known as the Azhar grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Avenochloa adzharica is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control.
