Achillea foliosa

Achillea foliosa, commonly known as the leafy yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Achillea foliosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
