Abies heterophylla

Abies heterophylla, commonly known as the variable fir, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinct foliage, which changes in appearance with age, featuring needle-like leaves on the lower branches and broader, flatter leaves higher up. The tree typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as mountainous regions, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its cones are small and reddish-brown, maturing within a single growing season.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
