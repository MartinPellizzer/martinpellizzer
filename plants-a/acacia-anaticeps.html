Acacia anaticeps

Acacia anaticeps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by flat, linear pods. Acacia anaticeps is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
