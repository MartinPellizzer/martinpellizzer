Amaryllis × johnsoniana

Amaryllis × johnsoniana, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces thick, fleshy leaves that are often striped or mottled with green and white. It is cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

The blooming period typically occurs in the spring or early summer, depending on the climate.
