Arundo nigricans

Arundo nigricans, commonly known as black cane reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and its ability to thrive in wet or moist environments. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale green flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. It is widely used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ornamental plant.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both useful and potentially invasive in certain regions.
