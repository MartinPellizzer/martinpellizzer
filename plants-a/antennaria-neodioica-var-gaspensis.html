Antennaria neodioica var. gaspensis

Antennaria neodioica var. gaspensis, commonly known as the Gaspé bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Gaspé Peninsula in eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, silver-tomentose leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
