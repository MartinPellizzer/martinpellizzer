Acronia tyria

Acronia tyria, commonly known as the redstem goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are reddish in color.

Acronia tyria is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
