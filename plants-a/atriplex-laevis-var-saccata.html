Atriplex laevis var. saccata

Atriplex laevis var. saccata, commonly known as the smooth saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, succulent leaves that are adapted to retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in saline and nutrient-poor soils.

This species is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for wildlife.
