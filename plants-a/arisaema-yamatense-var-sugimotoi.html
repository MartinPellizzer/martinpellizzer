Arisaema yamatense var. sugimotoi

Arisaema yamatense var. sugimotoi, commonly known as the Yamato arrowhead, is a rare variant of the arrowhead plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
