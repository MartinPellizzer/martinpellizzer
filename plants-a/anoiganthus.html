Anoiganthus

Anoiganthus, commonly known as the American beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in late summer and persist through winter. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Anoiganthus is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
