Alternanthera serpens

Alternanthera serpens, commonly known as the snake plant or red-veined sorrel, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with red veins. The plant is often grown as a decorative foliage plant due to its vibrant coloration and compact growth habit. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Alternanthera serpens is relatively low maintenance and can be propagated through stem cuttings.
