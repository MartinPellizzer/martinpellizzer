Achillea atrata var. intermedia

Achillea atrata var. intermedia, commonly known as the dark yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
