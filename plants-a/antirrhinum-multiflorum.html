Antirrhinum multiflorum

Antirrhinum multiflorum, commonly known as the common snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, bilabiate flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Antirrhinum multiflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
