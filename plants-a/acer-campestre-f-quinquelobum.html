Acer campestre f. quinquelobum

Acer campestre f. quinquelobum, commonly known as the five-lobed field maple, is a variant of the field maple tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
