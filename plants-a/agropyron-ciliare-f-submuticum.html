Agropyron ciliare f. submuticum

Agropyron ciliare f. submuticum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, soft, and slightly hairy leaves.

It is native to the temperate regions of Asia and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with slender, awned florets. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

It is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
