Aconitum yamamotoanum

Aconitum yamamotoanum, commonly known as Yamamoto's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in a raceme at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum yamamotoanum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
