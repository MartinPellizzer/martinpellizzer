Ackama rosifolia

Ackama rosifolia, commonly known as the rose-leaved ackama, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features distinctive rose-like foliage with lanceolate leaves that are often tinged with pink or red hues. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
