Arctostaphylos tomentosa var. crassifolia

Arctostaphylos tomentosa var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
