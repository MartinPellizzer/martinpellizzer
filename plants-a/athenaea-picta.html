Athenaea picta

Athenaea picta, commonly known as the painted athenaea, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which features intricate patterns and hues of green, red, and yellow. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its humid environment. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its unique appearance.

It thrives in partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
