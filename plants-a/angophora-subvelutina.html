Angophora subvelutina

Angophora subvelutina, commonly known as the velvet angophora, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in coastal and tableland forests, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
