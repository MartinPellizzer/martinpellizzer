Aporum curviflorum

Aporum curviflorum, commonly known as the curlyflower aporum, is a striking member of the Araceae family characterized by its unique, curling inflorescence.

It features a deep green, heart-shaped leaf with a prominent midrib and a waxy, spadix-like structure that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered around the spadix, typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and indoor cultivation.
