Asplenium wightianum

Asplenium wightianum, commonly known as the Wight's spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive morphology, featuring a creeping rhizome with clustered, lance-shaped fronds. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a serrated or toothed margin.

Asplenium wightianum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, and it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
