Attalea liebmannii

Attalea liebmannii, commonly known as the palm tree of the Amazon, is a tall, slender palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, pinnate leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant is often found in wetland and swampy areas, thriving in humid, tropical climates. Its fruit is a large, fibrous capsule that contains edible seeds.

The plant has ecological and economic significance, particularly in traditional and local communities.
