Andromeda spinulosa

Andromeda spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny andromeda, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in small, stiff spines. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in its native habitats.
