Artemisia latifolia var. maximowiczii

Artemisia latifolia var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the broadleaf wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, aromatic foliage composed of narrow, linear leaves with a woolly texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal remedies.
