Angelica chinghaiensis

Angelica chinghaiensis, commonly known as the Qinghai angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Qinghai region of China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica chinghaiensis thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.
