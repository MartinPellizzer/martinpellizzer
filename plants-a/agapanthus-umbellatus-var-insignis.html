Agapanthus umbellatus var. insignis

Agapanthus umbellatus var. insignis, commonly known as the blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, rounded clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
