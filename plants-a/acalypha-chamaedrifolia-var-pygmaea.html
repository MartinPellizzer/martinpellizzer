Acalypha chamaedrifolia var. pygmaea

Acalypha chamaedrifolia var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves with a distinctive reddish or bronze underside, which adds visual interest to the plant. The species produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically inconspicuous but attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in small garden spaces or as a ground cover.
