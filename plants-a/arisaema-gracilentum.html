Arisaema gracilentum

Arisaema gracilentum, commonly known as the graceful arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and can grow up to 1 meter in height. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests with rich, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
