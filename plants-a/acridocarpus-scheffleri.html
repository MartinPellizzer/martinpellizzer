Acridocarpus scheffleri

Acridocarpus scheffleri, commonly known as the white sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The species is also cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitats.
