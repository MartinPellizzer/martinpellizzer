Alchemilla diluta

Alchemilla diluta, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are kidney-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in loose, upright inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is known for its ability to retain moisture, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
