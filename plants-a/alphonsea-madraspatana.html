Alphonsea madraspatana

Alphonsea madraspatana, commonly known as the Madras Alphonsea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
