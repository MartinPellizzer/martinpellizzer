Asplenium brachycarpum

Asplenium brachycarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Asplenium brachycarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
