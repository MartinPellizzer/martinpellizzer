Asarum caulescens f. geroensis

Asarum caulescens f. geroensis, commonly known as the Geroen's wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Georgia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers clustered in a spike, which are typically hidden beneath the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
