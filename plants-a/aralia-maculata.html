Aralia maculata

Aralia maculata, commonly known as the spotted aralia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive white or cream-colored veining that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and berries provide habitat and food for various wildlife species.
