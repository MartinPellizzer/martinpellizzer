Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco subsp. brevifolium

Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco subsp.

brevifolium, commonly known as white quebracho, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay. It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing seeds. It is valued for its durable wood and has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
