Anchusa limbata

Anchusa limbata, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery or grayish underside, which helps reflect sunlight.

Anchusa limbata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
