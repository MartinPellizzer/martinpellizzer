Aegilops sharonensis var. mutica

Aegilops sharonensis var. mutica, commonly known as Sharon grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and slopes.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic relationship to wheat and its potential value in agricultural research.
