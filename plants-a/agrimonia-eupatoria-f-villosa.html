Agrimonia eupatoria f. villosa

Agrimonia eupatoria f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy agrimony, is a variant of the common agrimony.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its distinguishing appearance. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense heads.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and is valued for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
