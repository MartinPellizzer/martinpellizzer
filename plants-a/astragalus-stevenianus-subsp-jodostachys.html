Astragalus stevenianus subsp. jodostachys

Astragalus stevenianus subsp.

jodostachys, commonly known as the Jodostachys milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
