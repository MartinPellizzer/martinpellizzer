Astragalus vesicarius var. hercegovinus

Astragalus vesicarius var. hercegovinus, commonly known as the Hercegovina milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its fruit is a hairy, inflated pod that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and steppe environments.
