Arisaema atrorubens f. zebrinum

Arisaema atrorubens f. zebrinum, commonly known as the Zebrine Snake-root, is a variant of the Indian Snake-root plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, veined, heart-shaped leaves that resemble a snake's body, giving it its common name. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family, with the spadix bearing both male and female flowers. Its habitat includes moist, shaded forest floors, where it thrives in humid conditions.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and exotic appearance.
