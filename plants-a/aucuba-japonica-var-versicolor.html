Aucuba japonica var. versicolor

Aucuba japonica var. versicolor, commonly known as the variegated Japanese aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking yellow and white variegation, creating a vibrant visual contrast. The plant produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds but not typically edible for humans. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its adaptability and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and container gardening.
