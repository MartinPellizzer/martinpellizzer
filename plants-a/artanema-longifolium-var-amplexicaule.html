Artanema longifolium var. amplexicaule

Artanema longifolium var. amplexicaule, commonly known as the amplexicaule artanema, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that clasping the stem, giving it the "amplexicaule" designation. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and hairy stems, which help it thrive in its alpine habitat.

It is often found in moist, rocky soils at high elevations.
