Anthurium glaucophyllum

Anthurium glaucophyllum, commonly known as the silver anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright red spadices surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium glaucophyllum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to purify indoor air.
