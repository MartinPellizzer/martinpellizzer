Alstroemeria quillotensis var. foliolosa

Alstroemeria quillotensis var. foliolosa, commonly known as the Chilean leafy alstroemeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often prominently veined and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
