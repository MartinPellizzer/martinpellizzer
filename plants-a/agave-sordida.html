Agave sordida

Agave sordida, commonly known as the black agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its dark green, spiny leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk with small, white flowers, which appears after several years. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Agave sordida is often used in traditional medicine and has some ornamental value in gardens.
