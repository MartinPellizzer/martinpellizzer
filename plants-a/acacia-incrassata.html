Acacia incrassata

Acacia incrassata, commonly known as the thick-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellow, globular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its wood is hard and durable, making it useful for construction and fuel.

Acacia incrassata also plays a role in preventing soil erosion due to its extensive root system.
