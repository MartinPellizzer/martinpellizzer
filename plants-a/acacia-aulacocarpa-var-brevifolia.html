Acacia aulacocarpa var. brevifolia

Acacia aulacocarpa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
