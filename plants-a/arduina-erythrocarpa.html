Arduina erythrocarpa

Arduina erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited arduina, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow. Its most distinctive feature is its bright red, fleshy berries, which are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Arduina erythrocarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
