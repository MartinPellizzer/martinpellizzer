Aronia melanocarpa var. grandifolia

Aronia melanocarpa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the red chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with 5-9 elliptical leaflets that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early summer, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
