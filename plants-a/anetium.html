Anetium

Anetium, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that appear in umbrella-shaped clusters. The plant produces aromatic seeds that are often used in cooking and herbal remedies. Anetium thrives in cool, moist environments and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
