Acrostichum boragineum

Acrostichum boragineum, commonly known as the slender bugleweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
