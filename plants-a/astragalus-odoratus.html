Astragalus odoratus

Astragalus odoratus, commonly known as sweet vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Astragalus odoratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
