Acacia decussata

Acacia decussata, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its woody stems are often branched and can form dense thickets, providing habitat for small animals.

Acacia decussata is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor soils and with minimal water availability.
