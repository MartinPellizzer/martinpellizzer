Acalypha floribunda

Acalypha floribunda, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers within the bract clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Acalypha floribunda is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract birds and insects.
