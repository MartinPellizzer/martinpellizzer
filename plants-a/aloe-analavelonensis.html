Aloe analavelonensis

Aloe analavelonensis, commonly known as the Madagascar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in the dry season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
