Aulacospermum multifidum

Aulacospermum multifidum, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that split into multiple lobes, giving it a star-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dry, capsule-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.
