Alcea ficifolia var. glabrata

Alcea ficifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth leaf hollyhock, is a variant of the hollyhock species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with some varieties displaying white or yellow. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a smooth texture, which distinguishes it from other hollyhock varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
