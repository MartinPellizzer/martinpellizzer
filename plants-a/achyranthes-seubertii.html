Achyranthes seubertii

Achyranthes seubertii, commonly known as the African horsebean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the species. Its seeds are encased in a hard, smooth pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Achyranthes seubertii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
