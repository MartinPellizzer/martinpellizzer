Aspalathus arachnoidea

Aspalathus arachnoidea, commonly known as the spiderbean, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its unique, hairy appearance, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Aspalathus arachnoidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional medicine.
