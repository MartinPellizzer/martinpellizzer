Avena aspera

Avena aspera, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Avena aspera is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
