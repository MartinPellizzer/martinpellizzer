Arisaema stewardsonii

Arisaema stewardsonii, commonly known as the Stewart's arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its distinctive, spadix-shaped inflorescence. The plant produces a single, elongated spadix surrounded by a broad, leaf-like bract. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Arisaema stewardsonii is typically found in shaded, moist habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
