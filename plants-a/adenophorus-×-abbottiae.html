Adenophorus × abbottiae

Adenophorus × abbottiae, commonly known as the hybrid milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume morphology, including compound leaves and pods. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Adenophorus × abbottiae plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife.
