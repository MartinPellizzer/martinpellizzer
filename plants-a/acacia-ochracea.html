Acacia ochracea

Acacia ochracea, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces elongated, flat seed pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia ochracea is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
