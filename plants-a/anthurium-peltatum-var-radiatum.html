Anthurium peltatum var. radiatum

Anthurium peltatum var. radiatum, commonly known as the Radiatum Anthurium, is a tropical evergreen plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with striking white veins. The plant produces vibrant red spathes that enclose its yellow spadix, creating a dramatic floral display. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
