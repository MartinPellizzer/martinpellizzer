Apodina cucullaris

Apodina cucullaris, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hood-like structure that encloses its flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Apodina cucullaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
