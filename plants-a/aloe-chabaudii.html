Aloe chabaudii

Aloe chabaudii, commonly known as the Chabaud's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps it retain water in arid conditions. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-orange flowers, which bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-draining soil.

Aloe chabaudii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
