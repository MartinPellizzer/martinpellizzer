Allium textile

Allium textile, commonly known as the Persian onion, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is characterized by its fibrous, thread-like roots, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium textile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild, onion-like flavor.
