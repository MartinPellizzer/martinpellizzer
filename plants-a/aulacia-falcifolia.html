Aulacia falcifolia

Aulacia falcifolia, commonly known as the sickle-leaved aulacia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped leaves, which are typically lanceolate and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, red berries. Aulacia falcifolia is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
