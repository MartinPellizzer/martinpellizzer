Acacia wightiana

Acacia wightiana, commonly known as the Indian acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces long, flat pods that contain seeds.

Acacia wightiana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
