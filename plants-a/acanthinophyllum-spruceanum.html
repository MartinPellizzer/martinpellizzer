Acanthinophyllum spruceanum

Acanthinophyllum spruceanum, commonly known as the Spruce's acanthinophyllum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
