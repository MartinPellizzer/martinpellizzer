Allium negrianum

Allium negrianum, commonly known as the Negrian garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are composed of layered, fleshy scales. Its leaves are long, grass-like, and often have a strong, pungent aroma.

Allium negrianum is valued for its culinary uses and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
