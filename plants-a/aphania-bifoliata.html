Aphania bifoliata

Aphania bifoliata, commonly known as the two-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Aphania bifoliata is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
