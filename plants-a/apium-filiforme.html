Apium filiforme

Apium filiforme, commonly known as slender parsley, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and dill. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in umbels. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Apium filiforme is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
