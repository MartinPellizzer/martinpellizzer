Abutilon indicum subsp. albescens

Abutilon indicum subsp.

albescens, commonly known as the white-velvet abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are often borne in dense, showy inflorescences.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
