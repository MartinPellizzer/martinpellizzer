Acinos suaveolens f. acuminatus

Acinos suaveolens f. acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a sharp, pointed tip, giving it its specific epithet "acuminatus.". Its flowers are typically white to pale yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
