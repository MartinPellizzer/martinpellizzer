Aglaonema vittatum

Aglaonema vittatum, commonly known as the Striped Canna, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in the summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Aglaonema vittatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
