Albizia corbisieri

Albizia corbisieri, commonly known as the African siris, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. Albizia corbisieri is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
