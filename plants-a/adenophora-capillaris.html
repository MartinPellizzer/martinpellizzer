Adenophora capillaris

Adenophora capillaris, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its flowering stems are often covered with glandular hairs, giving it the genus name "Adenophora," which refers to the glandular structures.

Adenophora capillaris is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
