Aerides quinquevulnera var. purpurata

Aerides quinquevulnera var. purpurata, commonly known as the Purple-veined Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers with prominent purple veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It prefers warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and botanical gardens.
