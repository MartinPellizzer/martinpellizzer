Aechmea burle-marxii

Aechmea burle-marxii, commonly known as the Burle Marx Aechmea, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange, often resembling a flame. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that can be green, blue, or even pink, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea burle-marxii has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and often have a waxy coating, arranged in a compact cluster at the base of the plant.

The leaves are typically a vibrant green with a bluish tint, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant lacks a traditional stem, with the base of the leaves forming a dense, compact rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, with white or pinkish petals, and they bloom during the summer months, pollinated primarily by insects.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that disperses its seeds through animals after ripening.
