Asparagus tricarinatus

Asparagus tricarinatus, commonly known as the three-ribbed asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Asparagus tricarinatus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
