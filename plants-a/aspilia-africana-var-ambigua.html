Aspilia africana var. ambigua

Aspilia africana var. ambigua, commonly known as the African feverfew, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses, it is occasionally grown in gardens and herbal practices.
