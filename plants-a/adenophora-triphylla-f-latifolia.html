Adenophora triphylla f. latifolia

Adenophora triphylla f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the eastern and central regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
