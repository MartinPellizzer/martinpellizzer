Alchemilla filicaulis subsp. vestita

Alchemilla filicaulis subsp.

vestita, commonly known as the velvet-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and wildflower gardens.
