Anthoshorea hypochra

Anthoshorea hypochra, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. Native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation efforts.
