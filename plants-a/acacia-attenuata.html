Acacia attenuata

Acacia attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Acacia attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in providing habitat for native wildlife.
