Asparagus greveanus

Asparagus greveanus, commonly known as the Greve's asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by green, edible spears that are harvested in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Asparagus greveanus is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
