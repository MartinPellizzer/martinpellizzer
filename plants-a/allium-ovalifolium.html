Allium ovalifolium

Allium ovalifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium ovalifolium is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well.
