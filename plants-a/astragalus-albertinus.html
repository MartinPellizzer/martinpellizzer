Astragalus albertinus

Astragalus albertinus, commonly known as Albert's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its root system is often deep and fibrous, enabling it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus albertinus is also noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
