Armeria humilis

Armeria humilis, commonly known as the common thrift, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Armeria humilis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize sandy or rocky soils.
