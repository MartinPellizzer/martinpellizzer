Apium prostratum var. filiforme

Apium prostratum var. filiforme, commonly known as slender hogweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It features slender, branched stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and can reach up to 30 cm in length.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and in meadows.
