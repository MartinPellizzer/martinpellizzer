Agrostis stolonifera subsp. coarctata

Agrostis stolonifera subsp.

coarctata, commonly known as narrowleaf bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and creeping growth habit, with stolons that root at the nodes to form dense mats.

This subspecies typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows. Its small, spikelet-based inflorescences produce wind-pollinated flowers, and it is adapted to moderate climate conditions.

The plant's structure and growth pattern make it a valuable component of ecological and agricultural systems.
