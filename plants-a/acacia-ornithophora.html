Acacia ornithophora

Acacia ornithophora, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing flattened seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acacia ornithophora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its historical use in the production of tannin.
