Anthemis sicula

Anthemis sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian Anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
