Apteria hymenanthera f. decolorata

Apteria hymenanthera f. decolorata, commonly known as the pale-leaf hibiscus, is a variant of the hibiscus species characterized by its reduced or absent leaves, giving it a unique appearance.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its stems are often woody and can grow in a shrub-like form.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
