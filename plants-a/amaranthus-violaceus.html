Amaranthus violaceus

Amaranthus violaceus, commonly known as purple amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red bracts that surround the small, green flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
