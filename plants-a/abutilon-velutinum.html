Abutilon velutinum

Abutilon velutinum, commonly known as velvetleaf or woolly abutilon, is a shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in the summer. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain regions.
