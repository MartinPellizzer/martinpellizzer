Astragalus anatolicus

Astragalus anatolicus, commonly known as Anatolian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus anatolicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
