Arundastrum schweinfurthianum

Arundastrum schweinfurthianum, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's arundinaria, is a species of grass native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, a common feature among grasses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its ecological role, it supports various insect and bird species in its native environment.
