Arabis parvula

Arabis parvula, commonly known as the small rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Arabis parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
