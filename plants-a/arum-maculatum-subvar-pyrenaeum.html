Arum maculatum subvar. pyrenaeum

Arum maculatum subvar. pyrenaeum, commonly known as the Pyrenean arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features a distinctive arrow-shaped leaf with a reddish or purple blotch at the base. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
