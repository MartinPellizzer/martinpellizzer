Acacia merrallii var. tamminensis

Acacia merrallii var. tamminensis, commonly known as the Tammin acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically small and delicate. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers in compact spherical or cylindrical inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid to arid environments.

The wood of this species is hard and durable, making it valuable for local crafts and construction.
