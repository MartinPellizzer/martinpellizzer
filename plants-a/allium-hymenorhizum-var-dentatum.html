Allium hymenorhizum var. dentatum

Allium hymenorhizum var. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It features long, strap-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. The plant produces small, white, spherical flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and can be used similarly to garlic or shallots.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary versatility.
