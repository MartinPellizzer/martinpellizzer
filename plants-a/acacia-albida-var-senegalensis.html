Acacia albida var. senegalensis

Acacia albida var. senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

The plant is also valued for its timber and traditional uses in local communities.
