Athyrium clivicola

Athyrium clivicola, commonly known as the rock brake, is a fern native to the eastern United States.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and stream banks. The plant has a delicate, lace-like frond structure with a distinctive, narrow shape. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, typical of the fern life cycle.

Athyrium clivicola is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
