Acer angustilobum f. longicaudatum

Acer angustilobum f. longicaudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed maple, is a variant of the narrow-leaf maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated, tapering leaf lobes that give it its common name. The plant is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 15 meters.

The leaves are usually bright green in summer and turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall, making it a visually striking ornamental plant.
