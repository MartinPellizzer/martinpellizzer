Aira cespitosa var. submutica

Aira cespitosa var. submutica, commonly known as hairy false oat, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale green to brown florets. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.
