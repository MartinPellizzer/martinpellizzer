Arum orientale subsp. albispathum

Arum orientale subsp.

albispathum, commonly known as the white-veined arum, is a member of the Araceae family. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with prominent white veins.

The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typically white or pale in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
