Allium flavescens subsp. ammophilum

Allium flavescens subsp.

ammophilum, commonly known as the yellow onion grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species.

Its botanical characteristics make it distinguishable from other members of the genus due to its unique floral structure and growth habits.
