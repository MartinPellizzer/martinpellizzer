Andromeda multiflora

Andromeda multiflora, commonly known as the sweet azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as Rhododendron and Pieris. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Andromeda multiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
