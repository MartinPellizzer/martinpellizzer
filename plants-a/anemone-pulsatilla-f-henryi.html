Anemone pulsatilla f. henryi

Anemone pulsatilla f. henryi, commonly known as the Henry's pasqueflower, is a variant of the pasqueflower native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with striking, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and a single, erect stem topped with a cluster of pink or purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and leaves with a waxy coating, adaptations to cold and arid environments.

This form is distinguished by its unique morphological traits and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
