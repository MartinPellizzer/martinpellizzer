Alstroemeria diluta subsp. chrysantha

Alstroemeria diluta subsp.

chrysantha, commonly known as the golden alstroemeria, is a perennial herb native to South America. It features strap-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
