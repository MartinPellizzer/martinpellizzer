Agave micracantha

Agave micracantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which appear after several years of growth. Its leaves are armed with sharp spines along the edges, making it a striking and somewhat dangerous plant.

Agave micracantha is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive in harsh conditions.
