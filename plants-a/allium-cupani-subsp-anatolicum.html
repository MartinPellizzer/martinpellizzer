Allium cupani subsp. anatolicum

Allium cupani subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian leek, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear umbel-like clusters of white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are often used in local cuisines for their mild, onion-like flavor.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its subspecific traits and adaptation to arid and semi-arid environments.
