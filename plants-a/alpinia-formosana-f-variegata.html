Alpinia formosana f. variegata

Alpinia formosana f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated ginger, is a unique cultivar of the alpine ginger plant.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a combination of green, white, and yellow patterns. Native to Taiwan, this plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps over time.

The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.
