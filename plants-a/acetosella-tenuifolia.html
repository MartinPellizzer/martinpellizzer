Acetosella tenuifolia

Acetosella tenuifolia, commonly known as slender wood sorrel, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant has hairy stems and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often overlooked member of the woodland flora.
