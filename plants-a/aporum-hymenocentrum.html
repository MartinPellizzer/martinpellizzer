Aporum hymenocentrum

Aporum hymenocentrum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Aporum hymenocentrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
