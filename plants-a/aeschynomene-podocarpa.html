Aeschynomene podocarpa

Aeschynomene podocarpa, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats. Its seeds are encased in hard, spiny pods, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing.

Aeschynomene podocarpa is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
