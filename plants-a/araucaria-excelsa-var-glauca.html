Araucaria excelsa var. glauca

Araucaria excelsa var. glauca, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a variant of the southern hemisphere's native Araucaria excelsa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which results from a waxy coating on the needles. The plant is a coniferous evergreen with a tall, narrow form and a pyramidal shape. It produces small, edible seeds enclosed in cones, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
