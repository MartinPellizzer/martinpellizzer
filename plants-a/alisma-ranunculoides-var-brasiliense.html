Alisma ranunculoides var. brasiliense

Alisma ranunculoides var. brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense spikes and bloom in early summer.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
