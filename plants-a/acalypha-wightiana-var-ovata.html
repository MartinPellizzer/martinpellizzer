Acalypha wightiana var. ovata

Acalypha wightiana var. ovata, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Acalypha wightiana var. ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
