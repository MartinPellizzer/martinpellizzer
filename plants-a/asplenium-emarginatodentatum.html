Asplenium emarginatodentatum

Asplenium emarginatodentatum, commonly known as the notched-tooth holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, serrated fronds that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Asplenium emarginatodentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
