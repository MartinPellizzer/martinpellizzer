Amphilophis exaristata

Amphilophis exaristata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a tendency to spread through runners or rhizomes.

Amphilophis exaristata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.
