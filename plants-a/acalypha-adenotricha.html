Acalypha adenotricha

Acalypha adenotricha, commonly known as the silky ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, soft, silvery foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha adenotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
