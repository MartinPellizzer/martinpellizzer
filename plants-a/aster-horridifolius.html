Aster horridifolius

Aster horridifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, purple to pinkish-blue flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are often described as rough or bristly to the touch, giving it its scientific name.

Aster horridifolius plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife in its native environment.
