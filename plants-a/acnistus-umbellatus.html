Acnistus umbellatus

Acnistus umbellatus, commonly known as the white bead vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in umbellate clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its fruits are spherical, fleshy berries that mature to a red or orange color, containing several seeds.

Acnistus umbellatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
