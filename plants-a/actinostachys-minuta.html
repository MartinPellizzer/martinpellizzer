Actinostachys minuta

Actinostachys minuta, commonly known as the slender feather fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than a meter in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swamps and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing structures called sori.

The botanical features of Actinostachys minuta make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of wetland ecosystems.
