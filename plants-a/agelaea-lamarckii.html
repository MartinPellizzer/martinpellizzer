Agelaea lamarckii

Agelaea lamarckii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Agelaea lamarckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant blooms.
