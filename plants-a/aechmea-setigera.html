Aechmea setigera

Aechmea setigera, commonly known as the pink pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered in a fine, powdery bloom that gives them a silvery appearance. Its flowers, which are usually pink or red, emerge from the center of the rosette and are followed by showy inflorescences.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea setigera has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, stiff, and covered with a waxy layer, arranged in a tight spiral around the plant's central axis.

The leaves are typically dark green with a silver or white midrib, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem with a smooth, dark brown bark that grows vertically from the base of the rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a capsule that produces small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
