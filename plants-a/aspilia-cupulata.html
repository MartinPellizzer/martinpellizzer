Aspilia cupulata

Aspilia cupulata, commonly known as the African crownbeard, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers arranged in a dense, rounded inflorescence at the top of the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
