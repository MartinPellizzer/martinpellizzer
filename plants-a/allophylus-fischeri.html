Allophylus fischeri

Allophylus fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern coast of Africa.

It typically grows in coastal forests and sandy soils, exhibiting a compact, rounded shape with dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, spherical berries. It has smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a waxy coating.

Allophylus fischeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
