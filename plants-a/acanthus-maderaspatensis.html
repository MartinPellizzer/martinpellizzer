Acanthus maderaspatensis

Acanthus maderaspatensis, commonly known as the African horse-hair fern, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often hairy fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
