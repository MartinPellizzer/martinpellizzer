Aster ciliolatus f. comatus

Aster ciliolatus f. comatus, commonly known as the hairy-rayed aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and showy, blue to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
