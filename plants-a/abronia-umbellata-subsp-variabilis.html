Abronia umbellata subsp. variabilis

Abronia umbellata subsp.

variabilis, commonly known as the variable sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in xeriscaped gardens.
