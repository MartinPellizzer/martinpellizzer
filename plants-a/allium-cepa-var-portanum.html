Allium cepa var. portanum

Allium cepa var. portanum, commonly known as the Italian shallot, is a botanical variant of the common onion.

It is characterized by its smaller, more slender bulbs compared to the typical onion, with a mild and sweet flavor profile. The plant produces cylindrical, white or pale yellow bulbs that are typically harvested before they fully mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool climate for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in Mediterranean cuisines for its unique taste and versatility in cooking.
