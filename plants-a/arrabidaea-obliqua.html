Arrabidaea obliqua

Arrabidaea obliqua, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. The plant is known for its distinctive, velvety pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment in its native environment.
