Aplexia virginica

Aplexia virginica, commonly known as the Virginia marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Aplexia virginica is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
