Amelanchier turkestanica

Amelanchier turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It produces clusters of white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and is often found in rocky or forested areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.

The species is also cultivated for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil conditions.
