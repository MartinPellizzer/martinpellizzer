Artemisia dubia f. acuta

Artemisia dubia f. acuta, commonly known as sharpleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, slightly resinous scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
