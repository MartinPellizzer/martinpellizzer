Astragalus illyricus proles dinaricus

Astragalus illyricus proles dinaricus, commonly known as the Dinaric milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
