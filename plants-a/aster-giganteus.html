Aster giganteus

Aster giganteus, commonly known as the giant coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows up to six feet tall, featuring sturdy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with a distinctive yellow center.

Aster giganteus is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
