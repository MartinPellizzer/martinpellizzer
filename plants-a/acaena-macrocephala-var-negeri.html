Acaena macrocephala var. negeri

Acaena macrocephala var. negeri, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces spherical, reddish-brown flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant has small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in tight clusters, and its stems are often covered in short, stiff hairs. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various invertebrates.
