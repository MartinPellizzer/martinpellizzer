Anemone multifida var. globosa

Anemone multifida var. globosa, commonly known as the globular anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a rosette of deeply lobed, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with prominent, rounded centers, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
