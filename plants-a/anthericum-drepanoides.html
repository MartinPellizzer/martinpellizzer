Anthericum drepanoides

Anthericum drepanoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fern, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided into narrow segments. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Anthericum drepanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
