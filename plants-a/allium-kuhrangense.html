Allium kuhrangense

Allium kuhrangense, commonly known as the Kuhrang onion, is a perennial herb native to Iran.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous base and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
