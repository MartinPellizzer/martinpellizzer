Alchemilla schmidelyana

Alchemilla schmidelyana, commonly known as Schmidel's lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of wrinkled, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla schmidelyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
