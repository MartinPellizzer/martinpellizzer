Astrocaryum awarra

Astrocaryum awarra, commonly known as the Amazonian palm or awarra palm, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody shell, and the plant plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Astrocaryum awarra is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
