Aegilops comosa

Aegilops comosa, commonly known as the hairy wild wheat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and spikelets that bear small, bristle-tipped florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides. Its seeds are small and have a tough, protective coating, which aids in their survival in harsh environments.

Aegilops comosa is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
