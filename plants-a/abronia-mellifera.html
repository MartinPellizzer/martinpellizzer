Abronia mellifera

Abronia mellifera, commonly known as the sweetflower sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with narrow, linear leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Abronia mellifera is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
