Acer negundo f. glabrescens

Acer negundo f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaf boxelder, is a variant of the boxelder tree characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to North America, typically found in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and wetlands. The plant has opposite, palmately compound leaves that are usually three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in catkins, and it produces samaras as its fruit.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
