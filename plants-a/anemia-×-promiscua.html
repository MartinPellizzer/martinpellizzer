Anemia × promiscua

Anemia × promiscua, commonly known as the hybrid anemic promiscua, is a botanical curiosity resulting from the crossbreeding of two distinct plant species.

This hybrid exhibits traits from both parent plants, often displaying unique morphological characteristics such as variable leaf shapes and flower colors. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity. The plant's reproductive strategy involves both self-pollination and cross-pollination, contributing to its genetic diversity.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in certain ecosystems.
