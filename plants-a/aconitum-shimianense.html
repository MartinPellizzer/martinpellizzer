Aconitum shimianense

Aconitum shimianense, commonly known as the Shimian aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces striking, spurred petals and has a distinctive, toxic alkaloid-rich root system. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
