Astragalus silerianus var. cariacus

Astragalus silerianus var. cariacus, commonly known as the Carpathian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
