Astragalus nigrescens var. arctobia

Astragalus nigrescens var. arctobia, commonly known as the Arctic milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that mature into a dark brown color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in tundra and subalpine meadows.
