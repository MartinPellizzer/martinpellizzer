Acacia brosigii

Acacia brosigii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in globular clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, spongy material. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia brosigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
