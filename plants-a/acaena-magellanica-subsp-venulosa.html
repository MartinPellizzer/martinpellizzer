Acaena magellanica subsp. venulosa

Acaena magellanica subsp.

venulosa, commonly known as the Magellanic burr, is a flowering plant native to southern South America. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped fruits that contain seeds.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
