Aconitum iochanicum var. robustum

Aconitum iochanicum var. robustum, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple, helmet-shaped flowers and dark green, palmately lobed leaves. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it potentially dangerous to humans and animals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though care must be taken due to its toxicity.
