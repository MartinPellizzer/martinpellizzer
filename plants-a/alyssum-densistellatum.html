Alyssum densistellatum

Alyssum densistellatum, commonly known as clustered alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Alyssum densistellatum is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its ornamental flowers and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
