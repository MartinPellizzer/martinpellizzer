Astragalus filipes

Astragalus filipes, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to survive in dry environments. It produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.

Astragalus filipes is also valued for its ecological importance as a food source for various wildlife species.
