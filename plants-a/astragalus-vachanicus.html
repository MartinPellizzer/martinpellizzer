Astragalus vachanicus

Astragalus vachanicus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus vachanicus is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
