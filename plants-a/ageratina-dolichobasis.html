Ageratina dolichobasis

Ageratina dolichobasis, commonly known as the long-stemmed blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
