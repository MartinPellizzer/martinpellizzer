Aristida pardyi

Aristida pardyi, commonly known as the redgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Aristida pardyi is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.
