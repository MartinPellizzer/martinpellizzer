Alchemilla alpina proles alpigena

Alchemilla alpina proles alpigena, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its supposed healing properties.
