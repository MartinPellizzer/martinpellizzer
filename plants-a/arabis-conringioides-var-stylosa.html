Arabis conringioides var. stylosa

Arabis conringioides var. stylosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
