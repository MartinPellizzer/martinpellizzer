Astragalus pseudoantilibani

Astragalus pseudoantilibani, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus pseudoantilibani is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
