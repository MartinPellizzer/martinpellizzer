Amyrea maprouneifolia

Amyrea maprouneifolia, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and dull underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shady areas of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
