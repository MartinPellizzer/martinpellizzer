Astragalus canadensis f. rydbergii

Astragalus canadensis f. rydbergii, commonly known as the Rydberg's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is often noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
