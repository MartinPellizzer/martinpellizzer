Abutilon hirsutum

Abutilon hirsutum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

Its hairy stems and broad leaves make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
