Acer trialatum

Acer trialatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall. The tree typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are small, green, and inconspicuous, appearing before the leaves in early spring.

The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.
