Aporosa grandifolia

Aporosa grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved aporosa, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces small, round berries that are edible but often have a mild, slightly bitter taste.

Aporosa grandifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
