Asparagus penicillatus

Asparagus penicillatus, commonly known as the slender asparagus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, feather-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its stems are wiry and can bear small spines, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to arid conditions.

Asparagus penicillatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
