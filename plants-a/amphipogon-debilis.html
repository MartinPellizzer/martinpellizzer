Amphipogon debilis

Amphipogon debilis, commonly known as the weak sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters along its stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.
