Anthericum glaucum var. tarmaense

Anthericum glaucum var. tarmaense, commonly known as the Tarma lily, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky soils at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and adaptation to high-altitude conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural importance in local communities.
