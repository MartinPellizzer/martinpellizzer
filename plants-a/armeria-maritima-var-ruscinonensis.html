Armeria maritima var. ruscinonensis

Armeria maritima var. ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonensis thrift, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and harsh coastal conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
