Acrocomia hospes

Acrocomia hospes, commonly known as the Mexican palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features a tall, slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown surface. Its leaves are large, fan-shaped, and arranged in a rosette pattern at the top of the stem.

The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing at the base of the leaves.
