Alyssum virgatum var. validius

Alyssum virgatum var. validius, commonly known as the rock Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and compact, evergreen foliage that remains green throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects like butterflies and bees.
