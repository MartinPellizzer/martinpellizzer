Asystasiella atroviridis

Asystasiella atroviridis, commonly known as the black-veined ivy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asystasiaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with prominent, contrasting veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.
