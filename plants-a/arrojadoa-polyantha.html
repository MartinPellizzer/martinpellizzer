Arrojadoa polyantha

Arrojadoa polyantha, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Arrojadoa polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
