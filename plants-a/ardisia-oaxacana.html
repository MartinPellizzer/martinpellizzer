Ardisia oaxacana

Ardisia oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan beautyberry, is a shrub native to the highlands of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes many evergreen and deciduous shrubs. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Following flowering, it produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds.

Ardisia oaxacana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
