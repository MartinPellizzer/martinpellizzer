Abronia texana

Abronia texana, commonly known as the Texas sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Texas.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with a deep root system and succulent leaves that store water. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as plains and disturbed areas.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
