Anemone pratensis var. nana

Anemone pratensis var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a low-growing plant with delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its blooming period occurs in early spring, adding a subtle floral display to its habitat.
