Asparagus rigidus

Asparagus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by bright red berries. Asparagus rigidus is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
