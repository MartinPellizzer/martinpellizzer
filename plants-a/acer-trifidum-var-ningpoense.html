Acer trifidum var. ningpoense

Acer trifidum var. ningpoense, commonly known as the Ningpo maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, trifid leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring, followed by red or orange-red samaras in autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
