Asagraea frigida

Asagraea frigida, commonly known as the cold-tolerant orchid, is a species native to high-altitude regions in the Andes.

It is a perennial herb with slender, fibrous roots and erect, hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
