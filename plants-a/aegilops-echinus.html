Aegilops echinus

Aegilops echinus, commonly known as the spiny goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spiny glumes. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are enclosed in tough, bristle-tipped husks. Aegilops echinus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, rocky environments.

It has been studied for its potential role in agricultural systems due to its adaptability and resilience.
