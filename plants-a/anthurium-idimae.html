Anthurium idimae

Anthurium idimae, commonly known as the Idimae arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with striking veins. The plant produces bright red spadices, which are the inflorescences that house the tiny, white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium idimae is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and air-purifying qualities.
