Argyrolobium longifolium

Argyrolobium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved silver globular vetch, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, oval pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
