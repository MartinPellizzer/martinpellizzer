Agropyron glaucum var. trichophorum

Agropyron glaucum var. trichophorum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which helps it thrive in harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in open fields, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Its drought tolerance and ability to grow in poor soils make it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
