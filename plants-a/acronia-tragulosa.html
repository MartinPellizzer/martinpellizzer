Acronia tragulosa

Acronia tragulosa, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in dry, rocky habitats. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds.

Acronia tragulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
