Allocarya anaglyptica

Allocarya anaglyptica, commonly known as the smoothleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive botanical structure.

It features a thin, leathery thallus with a uniform, smooth surface that lacks distinct lobes or ridges. The plant typically grows on rock surfaces or tree bark in temperate and boreal regions. Its reproductive structures are composed of apothecia, which are small, disc-shaped structures that release spores.

Allocarya anaglyptica plays a vital role in ecological systems by contributing to soil formation and serving as a food source for various invertebrates.
