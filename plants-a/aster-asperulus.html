Aster asperulus

Aster asperulus, commonly known as rough blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in flat-topped clusters, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

Aster asperulus is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
