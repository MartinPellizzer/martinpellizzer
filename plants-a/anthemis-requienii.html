Anthemis requienii

Anthemis requienii, commonly known as the Canary Island chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flower heads that are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
