Alyssum montanum var. ochroleucum

Alyssum montanum var. ochroleucum, commonly known as the white alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
