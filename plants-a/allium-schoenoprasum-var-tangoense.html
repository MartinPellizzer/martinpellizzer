Allium schoenoprasum var. tangoense

Allium schoenoprasum var. tangoense, commonly known as Tango chives, is a variant of the chive plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant is typically grown for its edible leaves, which have a mild onion flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
