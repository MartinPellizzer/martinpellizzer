Allium angulosum subsp. latifolium

Allium angulosum subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of broad, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers in umbels.

The plant is characterized by its garlic scent and is often used in culinary applications. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it is part of the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and leeks.
