Allium carinatum var. nebrodense

Allium carinatum var. nebrodense, commonly known as the Nebrodes garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces spherical, purple or pink flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale pink bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
