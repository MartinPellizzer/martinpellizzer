Atriplex tatarica var. desertorum

Atriplex tatarica var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, salt-tolerant leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces greenish to yellowish flowers in compact clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
