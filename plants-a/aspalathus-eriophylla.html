Aspalathus eriophylla

Aspalathus eriophylla, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it reflect sunlight and conserve water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
