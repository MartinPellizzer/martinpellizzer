Albuca craibii

Albuca craibii, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil. It blooms in late spring or early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Albuca craibii is often cultivated for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
