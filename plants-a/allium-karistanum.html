Allium karistanum

Allium karistanum, commonly known as the Karistan garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical bulb and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is increasingly cultivated in horticulture.
