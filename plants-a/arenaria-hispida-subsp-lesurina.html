Arenaria hispida subsp. lesurina

Arenaria hispida subsp.

lesurina, commonly known as the rough sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It typically forms dense mats of tiny, spoon-shaped leaves covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for ecological restoration projects in harsh environments.
