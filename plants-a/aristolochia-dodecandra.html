Aristolochia dodecandra

Aristolochia dodecandra, commonly known as the Christmas vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a vine with heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex and sometimes toxic chemical compounds. Its flowers are typically purple or greenish in color and attract specific pollinators such as flies and beetles.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
