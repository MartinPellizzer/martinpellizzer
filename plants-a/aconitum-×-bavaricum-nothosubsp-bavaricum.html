Aconitum × bavaricum nothosubsp.

bavaricum, commonly known as the Bavarian monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodland habitats and has compound leaves with a distinctive palmate shape. It produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals.
