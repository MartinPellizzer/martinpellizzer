Astragalus ankaricus

Astragalus ankaricus, commonly known as the Anatolian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus ankaricus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
