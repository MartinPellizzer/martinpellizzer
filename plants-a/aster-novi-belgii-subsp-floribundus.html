Aster novi-belgii subsp. floribundus

Aster novi-belgii subsp.

floribundus, commonly known as the New York aster, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late summer and early fall.

The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This subspecies is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.
