Abutilon intermedium var. figarianum

Abutilon intermedium var. figarianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. Native to Central and South America, this plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils. It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several feet tall, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
