Asyneuma amplexicaule var. aucheri

Asyneuma amplexicaule var. aucheri, commonly known as the purple evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clasping around the stem, giving it its species name "amplexicaule.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
