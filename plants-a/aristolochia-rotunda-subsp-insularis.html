Aristolochia rotunda subsp. insularis

Aristolochia rotunda subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the Island Dutchman's Pipe, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a mild, sweet scent. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
