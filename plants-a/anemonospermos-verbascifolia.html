Anemonospermos verbascifolia

Anemonospermos verbascifolia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
