Adiantum mathewsianum

Adiantum mathewsianum, commonly known as the Mathews' maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, glossy, and arching fronds that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are often described as resembling the delicate feathers of a bird, hence its common name.

Adiantum mathewsianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
