Apocopis peguensis

Apocopis peguensis, commonly known as the Pegu grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, lowland areas and is often found in disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet with two to three small, spikelet-like flowers.

Apocopis peguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
