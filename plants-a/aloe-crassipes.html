Aloe crassipes

Aloe crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Aloe crassipes is well adapted to arid environments and can store water in its leaves.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
