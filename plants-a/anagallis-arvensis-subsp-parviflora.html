Anagallis arvensis subsp. parviflora

Anagallis arvensis subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as along roadsides or in meadows. It is often found in temperate climates and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate appearance.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Anagallis genus, which are known for their ornamental value and ecological importance.
