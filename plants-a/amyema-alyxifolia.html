Amyema alyxifolia

Amyema alyxifolia, commonly known as the silver-leafed myrtaceae, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its woody stems are often branched and have a rough, bark-like texture.

Amyema alyxifolia is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
