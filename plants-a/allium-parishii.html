Allium parishii

Allium parishii, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in mountainous regions. The plant features a bulbous base with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short stem.

Allium parishii is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
