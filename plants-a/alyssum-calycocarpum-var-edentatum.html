Alyssum calycocarpum var. edentatum

Alyssum calycocarpum var. edentatum, commonly known as the toothless alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
