Agapetes thailandica

Agapetes thailandica, commonly known as the Thai snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Thailand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate ornamental value. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, and it thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils.

Agapetes thailandica is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.
