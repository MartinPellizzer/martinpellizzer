Adenanthera circinalis

Adenanthera circinalis, commonly known as the African burr or the African acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species. Its seeds are encased in rough, prickly pods that can persist on the plant for extended periods.

The plant has traditional uses in various cultures for medicinal and practical purposes.
