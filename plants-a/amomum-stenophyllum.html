Amomum stenophyllum

Amomum stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces spindle-shaped fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its valuable essential oils.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is prized in traditional medicine and specialty cuisine.
