Albizia rostrata

Albizia rostrata, commonly known as the crown flower or the pink serissa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in clusters. Albizia rostrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and floral display make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
