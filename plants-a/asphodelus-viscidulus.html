Asphodelus viscidulus

Asphodelus viscidulus, commonly known as sticky asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall spikes of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Asphodelus viscidulus is adapted to arid conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.
