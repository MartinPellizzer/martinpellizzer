Anthyllis vulneraria f. varians

Anthyllis vulneraria f. varians, commonly known as the variable woundwound, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that features small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in dense spikes. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and hairy. Native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils.

This variant is notable for its variability in leaf and flower morphology, which contributes to its adaptability in different habitats.
