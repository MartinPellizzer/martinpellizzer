Alyssum idaeum

Alyssum idaeum, commonly known as the rock Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

Alyssum idaeum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
