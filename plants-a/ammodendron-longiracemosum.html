Ammodendron longiracemosum

Ammodendron longiracemosum, commonly known as the long-branch date palm, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. The plant is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, with deep root systems and waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species suitable for reclamation of degraded lands.
