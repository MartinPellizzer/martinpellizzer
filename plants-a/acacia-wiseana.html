Acacia wiseana

Acacia wiseana, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important component of native ecosystems.

Acacia wiseana plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
