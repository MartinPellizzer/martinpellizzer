Agathosma acutifolia

Agathosma acutifolia, commonly known as Cape aster, is a perennial shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Agathosma acutifolia is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
