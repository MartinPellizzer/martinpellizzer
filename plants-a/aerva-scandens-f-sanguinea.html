Aerva scandens f. sanguinea

Aerva scandens f. sanguinea, commonly known as crimson marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
