Anthistiria gigantea subsp. villosa

Anthistiria gigantea subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy giant anthistiria, is a striking plant native to certain regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
