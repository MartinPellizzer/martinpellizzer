Aegilops procera

Aegilops procera, commonly known as the tall wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Aegilops procera is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It has been studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological applications due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
