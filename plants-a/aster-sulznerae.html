Aster sulznerae

Aster sulznerae, commonly known as the Sulzner's aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Aster sulznerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
