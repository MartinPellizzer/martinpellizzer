Aristida riparia var. oligospira

Aristida riparia var. oligospira, commonly known as the narrowleaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like blades with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
