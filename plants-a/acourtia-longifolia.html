Acourtia longifolia

Acourtia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf acourtia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Acourtia longifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
