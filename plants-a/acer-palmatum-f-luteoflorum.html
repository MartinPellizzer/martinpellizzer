Acer palmatum f. luteoflorum

Acer palmatum f. luteoflorum, commonly known as the Golden-flowered Japanese maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves that turn red or orange in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its unique floral display and seasonal color changes.
