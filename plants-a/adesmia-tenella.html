Adesmia tenella

Adesmia tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf burrweed, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and linear. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are often pink or purple in color.

Adesmia tenella is adapted to thrive in poor, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.
