Abutilon fugax

Abutilon fugax, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in abandoned fields. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it an invasive species in some regions.

The botanical features of Abutilon fugax make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
