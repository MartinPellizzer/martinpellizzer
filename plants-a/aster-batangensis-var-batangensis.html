Aster batangensis var. batangensis

Aster batangensis var. batangensis, commonly known as the Batang aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
