Ampelopsis ternata

Ampelopsis ternata, commonly known as the climbing false ivy, is a woody vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with three to five leaflets, and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its vigorous climbing habit makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
