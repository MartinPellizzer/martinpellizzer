Aira pulchella var. intermedia

Aira pulchella var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate false grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
