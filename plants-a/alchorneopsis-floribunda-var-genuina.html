Alchorneopsis floribunda var. genuina

Alchorneopsis floribunda var. genuina, commonly known as the Cape fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
