Aconitum pomeense

Aconitum pomeense, commonly known as the Pomegranate Aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense racemes. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that resembles that of a fern.

Aconitum pomeense is known for its ornamental value in gardens but contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
