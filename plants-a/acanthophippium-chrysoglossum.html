Acanthophippium chrysoglossum

Acanthophippium chrysoglossum, commonly known as the golden-leafed acanthophippium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a shiny appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in whorls, and are usually pink or purple in color.

Acanthophippium chrysoglossum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
