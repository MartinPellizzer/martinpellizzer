Astragalus eucosmus var. fernaldii

Astragalus eucosmus var. fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
