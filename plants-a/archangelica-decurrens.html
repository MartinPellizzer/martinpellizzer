Archangelica decurrens

Archangelica decurrens, commonly known as the lesser hogweed, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbrella-shaped inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant can grow up to 2 meters tall, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are finely toothed. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces a distinctive, spicy odor.

The plant contains phototoxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to skin when exposed to sunlight.
