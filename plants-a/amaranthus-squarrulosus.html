Amaranthus squarrulosus

Amaranthus squarrulosus, commonly known as rough amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its seeds are small and numerous, often used as a grain in traditional cuisines.

Amaranthus squarrulosus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
