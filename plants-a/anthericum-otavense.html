Anthericum otavense

Anthericum otavense, commonly known as the Otavense arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Anthericum otavense is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests and grasslands.
