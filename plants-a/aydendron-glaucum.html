Aydendron glaucum

Aydendron glaucum, commonly known as the blue-green aydendron, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its glaucous, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive bluish-gray appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Aydendron glaucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
