Asplenium balfourii

Asplenium balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium balfourii is a hardy species that is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
