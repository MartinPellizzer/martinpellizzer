Aerides flos-aeris

Aerides flos-aeris, commonly known as the air orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Aerides flos-aeris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
