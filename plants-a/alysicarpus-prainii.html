Alysicarpus prainii

Alysicarpus prainii, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small white or purple flowers. Its fruit is a cylindrical pod containing several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
