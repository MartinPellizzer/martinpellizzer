Anacamptis × galloprovinciana

Anacamptis × galloprovinciana, commonly known as the Italian orchid, is a hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces dense clusters of violet or purple flowers, often with a distinctive white or yellowish throat. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges across southern Europe.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
