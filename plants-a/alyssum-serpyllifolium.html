Alyssum serpyllifolium

Alyssum serpyllifolium, commonly known as common alyssum, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Alyssum serpyllifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
