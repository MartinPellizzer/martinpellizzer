Archangiopteris × itoi

Archangiopteris × itoi, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a rare fern species resulting from the cross between Archangiopteris hancockii and Archangiopteris tenuis.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of its parent species. The plant exhibits a unique combination of morphological traits from both parents, including the structure of its sporangia and the arrangement of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
