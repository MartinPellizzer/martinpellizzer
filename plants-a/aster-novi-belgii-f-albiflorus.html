Aster novi-belgii f. albiflorus

Aster novi-belgii f. albiflorus, commonly known as the Whiteflower New Belgium Aster, is a flowering plant characterized by its showy, white to pale pink blooms.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is a cultivar of the New Belgium Aster, native to North America. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
