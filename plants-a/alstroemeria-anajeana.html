Alstroemeria anajeana

Alstroemeria anajeana, commonly known as the Anajeana lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Alstroemeria anajeana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for use in floral arrangements.
