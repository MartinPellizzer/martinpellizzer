Aspalathus varians subsp. isolata

Aspalathus varians subsp.

isolata, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a perennial shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in both gardens and conservation areas.
