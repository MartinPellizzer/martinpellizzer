Astragalus janischewskyi

Astragalus janischewskyi, commonly known as the Janischewsky's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
