Albuca glauca

Albuca glauca, commonly known as the blue albuca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.

Albuca glauca prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
