Angelica decursiva f. albiflora

Angelica decursiva f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered angelica, is a variant of the angelica plant characterized by its pale to white flowers.

It is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant features large, compound leaves with a strong, aromatic scent, and its stems are often hollow. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
