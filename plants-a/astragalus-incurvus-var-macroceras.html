Astragalus incurvus var. macroceras

Astragalus incurvus var. macroceras, commonly known as the large-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a hairy pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
