Alopecurus myosuroides var. latialatus

Alopecurus myosuroides var. latialatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the grass family.

It is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is adapted to thrive in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
