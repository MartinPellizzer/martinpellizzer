Armeria labradorica subsp. sibirica

Armeria labradorica subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It features dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is adapted to harsh, cold climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in colder regions.
