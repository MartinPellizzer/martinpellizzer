Aspalathus acifera

Aspalathus acifera, commonly known as the needleleaf bushveld clover, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Aspalathus acifera is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
