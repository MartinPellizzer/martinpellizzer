Alchemilla glaberrima var. suppartita

Alchemilla glaberrima var. suppartita, commonly known as the smooth lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to retain moisture.
