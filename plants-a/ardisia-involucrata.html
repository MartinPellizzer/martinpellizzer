Ardisia involucrata

Ardisia involucrata, commonly known as the redroot ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and typically grows as an evergreen shrub. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Following flowering, it develops bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
