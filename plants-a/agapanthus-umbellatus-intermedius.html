Agapanthus umbellatus intermedius

Agapanthus umbellatus intermedius, commonly known as the blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, spherical clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a basal rosette. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
