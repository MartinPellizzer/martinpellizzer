Angraecum penicillatum

Angraecum penicillatum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single or few flowers that bloom in a striking, star-like shape. The plant produces pendulous, white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive, narrow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecum penicillatum is known for its fragrant blooms and is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts.
