Airochloa gracilis

Airochloa gracilis, commonly known as the slender grass or slender ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and drought tolerance.
