Ampeloplis chinensis

Ampeloplis chinensis, commonly known as Chinese star gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
