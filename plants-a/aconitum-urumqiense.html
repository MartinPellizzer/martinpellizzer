Aconitum urumqiense

Aconitum urumqiense, commonly known as the Urumqi aconite, is a perennial herb native to the Xinjiang region of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are tuberous, often used in traditional medicine. Aconitum urumqiense is adapted to grow in high-altitude environments, typically in rocky or mountainous areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and requires careful handling.
