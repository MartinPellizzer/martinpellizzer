Allium cylleneum

Allium cylleneum, commonly known as the Cyprus leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions.
