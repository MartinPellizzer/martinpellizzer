Astragalus mollissimus var. thompsoniae

Astragalus mollissimus var. thompsoniae, commonly known as Thompson's hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with several leaflets arranged around a central axis.

It is often found in sagebrush steppe and other xeric habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
