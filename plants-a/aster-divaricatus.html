Aster divaricatus

Aster divaricatus, commonly known as the woodland aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster divaricatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
