Aconitum confertiflorum var. armenum

Aconitum confertiflorum var. armenum, commonly known as Armenian monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
