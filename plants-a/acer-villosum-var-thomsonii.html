Acer villosum var. thomsonii

Acer villosum var. thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's hairy maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange-red samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded forest environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
