Avena sativa var. rufa

Avena sativa var. rufa, commonly known as red oat, is a variant of the common oat plant characterized by its distinctive reddish bracts and awns.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and typically grows in temperate climates. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often used in ornamental horticulture. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Red oat is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated for seed production.
