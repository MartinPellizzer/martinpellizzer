Athyrium huttonii

Athyrium huttonii, commonly known as the Hutton's wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into narrow segments. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in shaded forest understories. Its rhizomes are perennial, allowing it to form dense colonies over time.

The spore-bearing structures, or sori, are arranged in a unique pattern along the undersides of its fronds.
