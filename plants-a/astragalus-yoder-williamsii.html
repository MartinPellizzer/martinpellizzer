Astragalus yoder-williamsii

Astragalus yoder-williamsii, commonly known as Yoder-Williams milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
