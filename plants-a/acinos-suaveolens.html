Acinos suaveolens

Acinos suaveolens, commonly known as sweet mountain-mint, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has aromatic, hairy leaves that release a pleasant scent when crushed. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pinkish ray florets.

Acinos suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies for its purported medicinal properties.
