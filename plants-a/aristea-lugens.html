Aristea lugens

Aristea lugens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aristea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow.

Aristea lugens is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
