Abrus gorsei

Abrus gorsei, commonly known as the golden gorse, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The seeds are encased in a hard, ridged pod, which splits open to release the seeds when mature.
