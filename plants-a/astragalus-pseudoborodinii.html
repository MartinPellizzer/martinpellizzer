Astragalus pseudoborodinii

Astragalus pseudoborodinii, commonly known as the Borodin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
