Anoda pentaschista

Anoda pentaschista, commonly known as the purpleleaf false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Anoda pentaschista is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
