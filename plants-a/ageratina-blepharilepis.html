Ageratina blepharilepis

Ageratina blepharilepis, commonly known as the silverleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with small, pale blue or white flowers. Its foliage is densely covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
