Athyrium daxianglingense

Athyrium daxianglingense, commonly known as the Daxiangling fern, is a species native to China.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are often curled into a fiddlehead shape.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
