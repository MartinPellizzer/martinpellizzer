Acacia pycnostachya

Acacia pycnostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical yellow flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

Acacia pycnostachya is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in horticulture.
