Attalea blepharopus

Attalea blepharopus, commonly known as the pink palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish flowers, which eventually develop into bright red, fleshy fruits. These fruits are an important food source for various wildlife, including birds and mammals.

Its unique botanical features make it a notable species in the study of palm flora.
