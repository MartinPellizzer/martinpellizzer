Albizia coripatensis

Albizia coripatensis, commonly known as the Peruvian silk tree, is a species of legume native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically found in dry to semi-arid regions, often growing in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Albizia coripatensis has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in warm, sunny environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some parts of the world for its aesthetic appeal.
