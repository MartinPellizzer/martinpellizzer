Aulacocalyx diervilleoides subsp. mufindiensis

Aulacocalyx diervilleoides subsp.

mufindiensis, commonly known as the Mufindi albizia, is a deciduous tree native to the Eastern Arc Mountains of Tanzania. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and plays a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.

It is also valued for its timber and traditional uses in local communities.
