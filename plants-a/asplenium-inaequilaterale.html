Asplenium inaequilaterale

Asplenium inaequilaterale, commonly known as the unequal-armed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced in small, clustered clusters on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium inaequilaterale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
