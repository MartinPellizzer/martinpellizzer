Acacia macnuttiana

Acacia macnuttiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, bipinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia macnuttiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
