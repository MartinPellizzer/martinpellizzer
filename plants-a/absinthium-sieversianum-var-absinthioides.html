Absinthium sieversianum var. absinthioides

Absinthium sieversianum var. absinthioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

Its chemical composition includes compounds such as thujone and absinthin, which contribute to its historical use in herbal medicine and distillation.
