Alocasia liervalii

Alocasia liervalii, commonly known as the Red-veined Alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with deep green foliage and prominent red veins, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other well-known genera like Dieffenbachia and Caladium. Alocasia liervalii thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

It requires regular watering and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
