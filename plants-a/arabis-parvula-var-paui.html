Arabis parvula var. paui

Arabis parvula var. paui, commonly known as the dwarf rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It typically reaches a height of only a few inches, with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has basal leaves that are rounded and toothed, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh, arid conditions.
