Abildgaardia hispidula subsp. senegalensis

Abildgaardia hispidula subsp.

senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
