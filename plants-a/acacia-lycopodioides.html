Acacia lycopodioides

Acacia lycopodioides, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

Acacia lycopodioides plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
