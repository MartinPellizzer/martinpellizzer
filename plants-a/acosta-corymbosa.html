Acosta corymbosa

Acosta corymbosa, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

Acosta corymbosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
