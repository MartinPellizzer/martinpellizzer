Astragalus laetabilis

Astragalus laetabilis, commonly known as the happy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Astragalus laetabilis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
