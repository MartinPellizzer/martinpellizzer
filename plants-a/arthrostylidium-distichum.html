Arthrostylidium distichum

Arthrostylidium distichum, commonly known as the two-ranked grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas or along water edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet with two bracts, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Arthrostylidium distichum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
