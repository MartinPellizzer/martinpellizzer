Arabis nuttallii

Arabis nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually basal, lobed, and hairy.

Arabis nuttallii is often found in dry slopes, canyon walls, and open woodlands.
