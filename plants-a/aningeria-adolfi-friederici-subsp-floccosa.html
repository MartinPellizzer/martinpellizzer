Aningeria adolfi-friederici subsp. floccosa

Aningeria adolfi-friederici subsp.

floccosa, commonly known as the woolly aningeria, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, fluffy appearance.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine, cottony pubescence.

This subspecies is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodland habitats.
