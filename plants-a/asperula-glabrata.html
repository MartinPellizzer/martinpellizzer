Asperula glabrata

Asperula glabrata, commonly known as the smooth asperula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Asperula glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
