Aquilegia sibirica

Aquilegia sibirica, commonly known as Siberian columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in a raceme. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are upright with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Aquilegia sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
