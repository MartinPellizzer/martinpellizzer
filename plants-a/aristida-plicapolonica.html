Aristida plicapolonica

Aristida plicapolonica, commonly known as the pleated grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves have a distinctive pleated or folded appearance, which gives it its common name.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
