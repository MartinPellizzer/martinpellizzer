Athyrium tashiroi

Athyrium tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's Japanese shield fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, leathery fronds that form a compact, shield-like structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Athyrium tashiroi is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
