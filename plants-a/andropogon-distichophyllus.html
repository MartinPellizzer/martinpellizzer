Andropogon distichophyllus

Andropogon distichophyllus, commonly known as the two-ranked grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its reproductive structures consist of small, two-ranked spikelets that produce wind-dispersed seeds.

Andropogon distichophyllus plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
