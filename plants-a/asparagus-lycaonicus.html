Asparagus lycaonicus

Asparagus lycaonicus, commonly known as the Lycaonia asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its arching, spiny stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
