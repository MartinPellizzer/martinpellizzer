Amorphophallus doryphorus

Amorphophallus doryphorus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, making it one of the largest leafy plants in the world. The plant produces a unique inflorescence with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which resembles a phallic shape, hence its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to store energy in its tuberous roots, allowing it to survive periods of drought.
