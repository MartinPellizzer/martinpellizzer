Abies novae-angliae

Abies novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. The tree has slender, cone-shaped branches that are often drooping, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its needles are dark green and densely packed, providing year-round foliage.

The cones are small and cylindrical, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
