Actaea cimicifuga var. racemosa

Actaea cimicifuga var. racemosa, commonly known as black cohosh, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features tall, unbranched stems that bear large, compound leaves with a distinctive notched appearance. The plant produces showy, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers in upright clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Black cohosh is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental value in gardens.
