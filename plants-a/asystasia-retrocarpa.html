Asystasia retrocarpa

Asystasia retrocarpa, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple to white in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive silvery underside.

Asystasia retrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
