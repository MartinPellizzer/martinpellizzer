Amygdalus communis f. roseaplena

Amygdalus communis f. roseaplena, commonly known as the double-flowered peach.

This botanical variant is a cultivar of the peach tree, characterized by its showy, double flowers that are more densely packed with petals than those of the typical peach. The plant is a deciduous tree that produces fruit similar to the common peach, though the flowers are its most distinctive feature. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.

The species belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, including China and the Middle East.
