Alyxia graciliflora

Alyxia graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine characterized by its slender, twisting stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. Alyxia graciliflora prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
