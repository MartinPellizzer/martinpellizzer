Adonanthe amurensis

Adonanthe amurensis, commonly known as the Amur bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Adonanthe amurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
