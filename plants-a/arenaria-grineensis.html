Arenaria grineensis

Arenaria grineensis, commonly known as the Crimean sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the rocky and sandy soils of the Crimean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in full sun and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient species adapted to harsh climatic conditions.
