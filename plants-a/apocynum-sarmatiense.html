Apocynum sarmatiense

Apocynum sarmatiense, commonly known as the Sarmatian dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Russia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae, which includes plants known for their milky sap and often toxic properties. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive waxy texture, while its flowers are clustered in umbels and range in color from white to pink.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
