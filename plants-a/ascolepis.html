Ascolepis

Ascolepis, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are typically found in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. They produce showy, often fragrant flowers that can vary in color from white to purple. The leaves are usually long and grass-like, and the plants are characterized by their unique inflorescence structure.

Ascolepis species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
