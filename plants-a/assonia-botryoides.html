Assonia botryoides

Assonia botryoides, commonly known as the clustered sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its species name "botryoides," which means "grape-like.". The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces orange or red berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to survive in harsh, dry environments.
