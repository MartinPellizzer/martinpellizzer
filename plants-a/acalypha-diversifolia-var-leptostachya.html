Acalypha diversifolia var. leptostachya

Acalypha diversifolia var. leptostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stamen glory flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that display a range of colors, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
