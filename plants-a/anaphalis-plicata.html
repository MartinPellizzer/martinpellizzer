Anaphalis plicata

Anaphalis plicata, commonly known as the silverleaf dewflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads. Its foliage is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Anaphalis plicata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
