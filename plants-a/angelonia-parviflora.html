Angelonia parviflora

Angelonia parviflora, commonly known as the summer crown or angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red and white. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other on the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Angelonia parviflora is known for its long blooming season, often extending from late spring through the summer months.
