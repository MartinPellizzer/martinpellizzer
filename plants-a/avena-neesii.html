Avena neesii

Avena neesii, commonly known as the Nees' oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers. Avena neesii is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine habitats.
