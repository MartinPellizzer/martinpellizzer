Actinodaphne obtusa

Actinodaphne obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. Its leaves and bark contain essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and for aromatic purposes.

Actinodaphne obtusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in tropical forests.
