Aconitum sachalinense subsp. nemurense

Aconitum sachalinense subsp.

nemurense, commonly known as the Sakhalin monkshood, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with palmately compound leaves, and its roots are tuberous. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
