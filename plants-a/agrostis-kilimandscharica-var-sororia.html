Agrostis kilimandscharica var. sororia

Agrostis kilimandscharica var. sororia, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro hairgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green blades that are often covered in a layer of fine hairs. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of slender spikelets that are arranged in panicles, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in harsh mountainous conditions.
