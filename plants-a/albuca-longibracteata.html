Albuca longibracteata

Albuca longibracteata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, cylindrical flower spikes adorned with showy, upward-facing flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, tubular shape that resembles a tulip. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
