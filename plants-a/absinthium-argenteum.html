Absinthium argenteum

Absinthium argenteum, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and herbal collections.
