Aristolochia adalica

Aristolochia adalica, commonly known as the Indian birthwort, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hood-shaped flowers that resemble a pipe or a funnel. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture and emits a strong, often unpleasant odor. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a deep purple or brown interior, and it blooms during the summer months.

The plant contains various alkaloids and has been used in traditional medicine, though it is toxic and should be handled with caution.
