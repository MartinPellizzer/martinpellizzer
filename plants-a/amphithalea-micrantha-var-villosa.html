Amphithalea micrantha var. villosa

Amphithalea micrantha var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy swamp mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant produces showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

This variety is adapted to moist soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
