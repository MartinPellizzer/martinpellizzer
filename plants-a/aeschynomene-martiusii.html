Aeschynomene martiusii

Aeschynomene martiusii, commonly known as the Brazilian signalgrass, is a tropical annual plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by narrow, elongated pods containing several seeds. It is native to South America and is often found in disturbed soils and grasslands.

Aeschynomene martiusii is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
