Adesmia racemosa

Adesmia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered legume, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in dry, arid environments.

Adesmia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
