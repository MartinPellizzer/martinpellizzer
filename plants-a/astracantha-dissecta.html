Astracantha dissecta

Astracantha dissecta, commonly known as the dissected aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astracantha dissecta is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
