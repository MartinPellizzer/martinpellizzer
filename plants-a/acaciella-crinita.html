Acaciella crinita

Acaciella crinita, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, arid environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a hairy texture that gives it its common name.

Acaciella crinita is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it resilient in harsh climates.
