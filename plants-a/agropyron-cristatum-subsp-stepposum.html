Agropyron cristatum subsp. stepposum

Agropyron cristatum subsp.

stepposum, commonly known as the steppe wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Eurasia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically arranged in a panicle structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as forage for livestock.
