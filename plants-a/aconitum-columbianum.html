Aconitum columbianum

Aconitum columbianum, commonly known as the Columbian monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The roots contain potent alkaloids, making it toxic to both humans and animals.
