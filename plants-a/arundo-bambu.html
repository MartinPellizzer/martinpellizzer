Arundo bambu

Arundo bambu, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region but widely cultivated in warmer climates.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. Arundo bambu is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist environments, making it a common choice for erosion control and ornamental purposes.

Its fibrous stalks have also been used historically for making paper and thatching materials.
