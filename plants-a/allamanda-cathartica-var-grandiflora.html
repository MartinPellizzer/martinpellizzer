Allamanda cathartica var. grandiflora

Allamanda cathartica var. grandiflora, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions and requires careful management to prevent ecological disruption.
