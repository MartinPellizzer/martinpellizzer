Alternanthera cyclophylla

Alternanthera cyclophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redstem plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that ranges from red to purple and pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a velvety texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant leaf coloration.
