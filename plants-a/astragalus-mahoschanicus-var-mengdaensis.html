Astragalus mahoschanicus var. mengdaensis

Astragalus mahoschanicus var. mengdaensis, commonly known as Mengda milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces elliptical or ovate leaves with a hairy texture, typical of many Astragalus species. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
