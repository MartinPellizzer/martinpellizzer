Astragalus idaeus

Astragalus idaeus, commonly known as the Idaean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Astragalus idaeus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
