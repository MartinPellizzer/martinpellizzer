Anatherum virginicum subvar. liebmannii

Anatherum virginicum subvar. liebmannii, commonly known as Liebmann's false brome, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland environments.

It is often found in association with other wetland plant species, playing a role in maintaining ecological balance in these habitats.
