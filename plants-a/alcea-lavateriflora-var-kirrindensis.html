Alcea lavateriflora var. kirrindensis

Alcea lavateriflora var. kirrindensis, commonly known as the Kirrindensis hollyhock, is a variant of the hollyhock plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice among horticultural enthusiasts.
