Aspidium rutaceum

Aspidium rutaceum, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, compound fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a cinnamon-colored coating, which gives it its common name.

Aspidium rutaceum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
