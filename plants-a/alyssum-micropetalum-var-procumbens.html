Alyssum micropetalum var. procumbens

Alyssum micropetalum var. procumbens, commonly known as creeping alyssum, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has slender, branching stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and border plants.
