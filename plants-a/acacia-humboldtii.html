Acacia humboldtii

Acacia humboldtii, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in compact clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.

Acacia humboldtii is well adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can endure extreme temperature fluctuations.
