Anemone palmata f. albiflora

Anemone palmata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered anemone, is a variant of the common anemone species characterized by its pale or white flower petals.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant typically features palmately divided leaves and produces showy, clustered flowers during the spring season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
