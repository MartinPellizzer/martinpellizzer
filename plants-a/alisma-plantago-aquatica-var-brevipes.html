Alisma plantago-aquatica var. brevipes

Alisma plantago-aquatica var. brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and shallow waters.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly curled, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom in late summer.

This variety is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
