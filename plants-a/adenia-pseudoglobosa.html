Adenia pseudoglobosa

Adenia pseudoglobosa, commonly known as the globular adenia, is a unique plant species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular or rounded growth form, which resembles a small ball. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a striking coloration that attracts pollinators.

Adenia pseudoglobosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in warm, humid environments.
