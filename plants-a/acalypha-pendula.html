Acalypha pendula

Acalypha pendula, commonly known as the hanging glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, brightly colored bracts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Acalypha pendula thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
