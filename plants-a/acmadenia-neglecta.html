Acmadenia neglecta

Acmadenia neglecta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acmadenia neglecta is adapted to withstand drought and is relatively resistant to browsing by livestock.
