Austrocylindropuntia clavarioides var. ruiz-lealii

Austrocylindropuntia clavarioides var. ruiz-lealii, commonly known as the Ruiz-Leal's columnar cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a deep root system adapted to survive in dry conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
