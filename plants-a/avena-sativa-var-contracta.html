Avena sativa var. contracta

Avena sativa var. contracta, commonly known as the dwarf oat, is a variety of oat characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to standard oats.

It possesses narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. The plant is typically cultivated for its high nutritional value, particularly its rich content of protein, fiber, and minerals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in forage production or as a cover crop.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is valued in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
