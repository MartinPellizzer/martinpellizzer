Aconitum × hortense

Aconitum × hortense, commonly known as the garden aconite, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and kept away from livestock and children.
