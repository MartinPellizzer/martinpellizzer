Antirrhinum majus subsp. rupestre

Antirrhinum majus subsp.

rupestre, commonly known as the rock snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to rocky and arid regions of southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with small, dark green leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
