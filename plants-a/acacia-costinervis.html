Acacia costinervis

Acacia costinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, arranged in cylindrical spikes.

Acacia costinervis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.
