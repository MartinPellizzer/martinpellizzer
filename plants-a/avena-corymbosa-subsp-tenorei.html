Avena corymbosa subsp. tenorei

Avena corymbosa subsp.

tenorei, commonly known as the Sicilian oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
