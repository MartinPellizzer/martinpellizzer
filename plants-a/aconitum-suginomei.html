Aconitum suginomei

Aconitum suginomei, commonly known as the Suginome aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense, spikelike clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum suginomei contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
