Aquilegia vulgaris var. sternbergii

Aquilegia vulgaris var. sternbergii, commonly known as the Sternberg's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces clusters of delicate flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
