Ageratina stictophylla

Ageratina stictophylla, commonly known as the white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ageratina stictophylla is a perennial herb that is relatively easy to grow in gardens with partial shade.
