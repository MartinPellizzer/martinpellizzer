Allium tristylum var. rubescens

Allium tristylum var. rubescens, commonly known as the red bulbous onion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red or purple bulb and grass-like foliage. The plant produces umbels of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its edible bulbs, which have a mild, sweet flavor.
