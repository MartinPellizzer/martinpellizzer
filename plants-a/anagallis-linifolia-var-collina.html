Anagallis linifolia var. collina

Anagallis linifolia var. collina, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and gravelly soils, typically at high altitudes. The plant features small, white flowers with four petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to retain moisture in harsh environments.

This variety is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
