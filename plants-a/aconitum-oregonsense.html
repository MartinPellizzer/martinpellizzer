Aconitum oregonsense

Aconitum oregonsense, commonly known as the Pacific woad, is a perennial herb native to western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces follicles containing toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
