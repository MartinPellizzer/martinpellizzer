Avena sterilis subsp. malzevii

Avena sterilis subsp.

malzevii, commonly known as Malzev's oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or ornamental plant.
