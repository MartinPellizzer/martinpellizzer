Astragalus olympicus

Astragalus olympicus, commonly known as the Olympic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Astragalus olympicus is often found in alpine meadows and is a symbol of the natural beauty of the Olympic Mountains.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
