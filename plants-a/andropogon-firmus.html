Andropogon firmus

Andropogon firmus, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Andropogon firmus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
