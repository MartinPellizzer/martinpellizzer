Acalypha subscandens

Acalypha subscandens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers, followed by three-lobed, reddish-brown capsules that contain its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.

Acalypha subscandens is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
