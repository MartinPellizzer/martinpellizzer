Artemisia campestris subsp. lloydii

Artemisia campestris subsp.

lloydii, commonly known as the yellow wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features silver-gray foliage with finely divided, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, pungent odor when crushed.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in compact, spherical clusters called heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
