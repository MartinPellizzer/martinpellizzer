Alpinia hirsuta

Alpinia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

Alpinia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
