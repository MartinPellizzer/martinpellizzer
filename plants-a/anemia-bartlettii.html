Anemia bartlettii

Anemia bartlettii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Anemia bartlettii plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly insects and small mammals, in its desert habitat.
