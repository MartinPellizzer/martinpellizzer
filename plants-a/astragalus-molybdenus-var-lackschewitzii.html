Astragalus molybdenus var. lackschewitzii

Astragalus molybdenus var. lackschewitzii, commonly known as the leadplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
