Arabis lignipes

Arabis lignipes, commonly known as the woodruff or sweet woodruff, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Arabis lignipes thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and delicate blossoms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
