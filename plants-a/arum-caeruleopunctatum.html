Arum caeruleopunctatum

Arum caeruleopunctatum, commonly known as the blue-spotted arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive blue spots. The plant produces an unusual inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
