Annona neolaurifolia

Annona neolaurifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, yellow fruit that is edible and has a flavor similar to papaya. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential medicinal uses.
