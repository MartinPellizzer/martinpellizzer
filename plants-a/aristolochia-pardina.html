Aristolochia pardina

Aristolochia pardina, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers that resemble a hooded structure. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding growth habit. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a purple or brownish interior, and they emit a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

The plant contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic and have been linked to kidney damage in humans.
