Arsis

Arsis, commonly known as the "false hellebore," is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae, which includes other notable members like buttercups and clematis. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Arsis is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as protoanemonin, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided in gardens and landscapes.
