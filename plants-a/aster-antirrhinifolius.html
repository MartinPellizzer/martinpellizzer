Aster antirrhinifolius

Aster antirrhinifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant's leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Aster antirrhinifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
