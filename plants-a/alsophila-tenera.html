Alsophila tenera

Alsophila tenera, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth rate and longevity. Its rhizome system supports the development of its distinctive, fibrous trunk.

Alsophila tenera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
