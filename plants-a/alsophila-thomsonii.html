Alsophila thomsonii

Alsophila thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in soil stabilization.

The plant exhibits a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species in its natural habitat.
