Arenaria grandiflora subsp. pseudoincrassata

Arenaria grandiflora subsp.

pseudoincrassata, commonly known as the large-flowered sandwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring well-drained environments.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it forms low-growing mats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.
