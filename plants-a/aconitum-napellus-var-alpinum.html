Aconitum napellus var. alpinum

Aconitum napellus var. alpinum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
