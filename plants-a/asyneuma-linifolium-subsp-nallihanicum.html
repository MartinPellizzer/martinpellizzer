Asyneuma linifolium subsp. nallihanicum

Asyneuma linifolium subsp.

nallihanicum, commonly known as the Nallihana false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the dandelion-like appearance. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
