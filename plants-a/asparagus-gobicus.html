Asparagus gobicus

Asparagus gobicus, commonly known as the desert asparagus, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are scale-like and reduced, typical of plants adapted to hot, dry climates.

Asparagus gobicus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
