Alectoroctonum petiolare

Alectoroctonum petiolare, commonly known as the leafy spurge, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and weed management.
