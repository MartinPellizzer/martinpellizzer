Angiopteris lorentzii

Angiopteris lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, intricate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which helps attract nocturnal pollinators.

This unique feature makes it a subject of interest in both botanical research and ecological studies.
