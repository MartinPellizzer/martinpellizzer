Acalypha pilosa

Acalypha pilosa, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Acalypha pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
