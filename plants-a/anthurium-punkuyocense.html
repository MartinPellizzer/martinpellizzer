Anthurium punkuyocense

Anthurium punkuyocense, commonly known as the Cusco anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Peruvian highlands.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in collections and botanical gardens.
