Aruncus dioicus f. laciniatus

Aruncus dioicus f. laciniatus, commonly known as the fragrant arum, is a striking perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features tall, arching stems covered with large, feathery, green to reddish-bronze bracts that resemble plumes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale green. Its foliage is deeply lobed and often has a fragrant scent, especially when crushed.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and its ability to thrive in moist, woodland conditions.
