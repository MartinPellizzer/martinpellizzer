Albizia masikororum

Albizia masikororum, commonly known as the Madagascar albizia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is adapted to the tropical and subtropical climates of its native habitat, with a preference for well-drained soils. It plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
