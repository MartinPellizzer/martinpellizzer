Aristea gracilis

Aristea gracilis, commonly known as the slender aristea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its natural habitat.
