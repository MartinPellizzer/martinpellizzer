Agrostis montevidensis var. submutica

Agrostis montevidensis var. submutica, commonly known as the Montevideo hairgrass, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, which is a common feature in the Poaceae family.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
