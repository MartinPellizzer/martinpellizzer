Anthurium mandiocanum

Anthurium mandiocanum, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble a spike of small flowers, often surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium mandiocanum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
