Allium texanum

Allium texanum, commonly known as Texas garlic, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in umbels, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and edible bulbs, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
