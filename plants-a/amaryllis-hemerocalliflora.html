Amaryllis hemerocalliflora

Amaryllis hemerocalliflora, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking white or pale pink flowers that resemble stars, often blooming in late winter or early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that grow from a bulbous base, typical of the Amaryllis genus. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also have historical and symbolic significance in various cultures.
