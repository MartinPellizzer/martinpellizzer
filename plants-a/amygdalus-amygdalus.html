Amygdalus amygdalus

Amygdalus amygdalus, commonly known as the sweet almond tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, hard nuts enclosed in a thick, woody shell. The seeds, or almonds, are widely cultivated for their nutritional value and culinary uses.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate for optimal growth.
