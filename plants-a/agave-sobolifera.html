Agave sobolifera

Agave sobolifera, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear small, greenish-white flowers.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
