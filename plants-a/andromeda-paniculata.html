Andromeda paniculata

Andromeda paniculata, commonly known as the swamp azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many flowering plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, leathery berries. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

Andromeda paniculata thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in forested wetlands.
