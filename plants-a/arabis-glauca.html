Arabis glauca

Arabis glauca, commonly known as the blue drumstick or sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features basal rosettes of silvery-gray, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. It is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline.

Arabis glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments.
