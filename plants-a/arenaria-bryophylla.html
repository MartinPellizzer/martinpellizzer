Arenaria bryophylla

Arenaria bryophylla, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
