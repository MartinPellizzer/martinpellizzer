Aloysia chacoensis

Aloysia chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco honeysuckle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and serve as a food source for various wildlife.

Aloysia chacoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and fragrance.
