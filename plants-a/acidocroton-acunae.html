Acidocroton acunae

Acidocroton acunae, commonly known as the red-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red leaves that contrast with its green stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
