Apocynum scopulorum

Apocynum scopulorum, commonly known as the rock milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect stems that often grow in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

Apocynum scopulorum is a member of the Apocynaceae family and contains cardiac glycosides, making it toxic to livestock and humans.
