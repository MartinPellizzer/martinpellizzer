Aillya umbellata var. spathulata

Aillya umbellata var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of broad, ovate to spatulate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or blue, arranged in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
