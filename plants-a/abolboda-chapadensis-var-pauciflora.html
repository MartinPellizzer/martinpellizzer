Abolboda chapadensis var. pauciflora

Abolboda chapadensis var. pauciflora, commonly known as the rare Brazilian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, pale green to white flowers with a distinct yellow lip. The plant has linear to lanceolate leaves and thrives in shaded, moist environments. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its limited floral display, which is reflected in its specific epithet "pauciflora.".

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
