Arum flagelliforme

Arum flagelliforme, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or arrowhead plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by a showy, spadix-like structure. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used in various cuisines.

The plant's rapid growth and unique appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
