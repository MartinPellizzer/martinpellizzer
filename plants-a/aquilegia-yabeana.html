Aquilegia yabeana

Aquilegia yabeana, commonly known as the Yabe's columbine, is a perennial herb native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and arranged in a rosette, while its stems are upright and branched. Aquilegia yabeana produces small, aggregate fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.
