Acer campestre f. puncticulatum

Acer campestre f. puncticulatum, commonly known as the dotted wood maple, is a cultivar of the European maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features small, raised dots on the leaves, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
