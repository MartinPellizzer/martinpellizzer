Adesmia sessiliflora

Adesmia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower legume, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Adesmia sessiliflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant with potential ecological and agricultural significance.
