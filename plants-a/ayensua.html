Ayensua

Ayensua, commonly known as the "sacred tree" in the Yoruba tradition, is a species of tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the genus *Kigelia* in the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, woody, elongated fruits. The plant is often found in dry woodlands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a tubular shape, and it produces a hard, fibrous fruit that contains several seeds.

Ayensua is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
