Alchemilla galpinii

Alchemilla galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla galpinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
