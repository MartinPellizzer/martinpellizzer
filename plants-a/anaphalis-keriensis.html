Anaphalis keriensis

Anaphalis keriensis, commonly known as the Kewan silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads that resemble small clouds, which are composed of numerous tiny florets. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.

Anaphalis keriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
