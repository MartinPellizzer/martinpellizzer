Arisaema undulatifolium f. serrulatum

Arisaema undulatifolium f. serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated wavy-leaf arisaema, is a variant of the arisaema genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a typical feature of the arisaema family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical and subtropical gardens.
