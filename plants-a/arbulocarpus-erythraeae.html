Arbulocarpus erythraeae

Arbulocarpus erythraeae, commonly known as the Red Sea date palm, is a rare and ancient plant native to the Red Sea region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, fan-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into date-like fruits, though they are not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in coastal areas with high salinity and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
