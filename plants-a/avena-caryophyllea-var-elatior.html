Avena caryophyllea var. elatior

Avena caryophyllea var. elatior, commonly known as the greater purple grass, is a variant of the purple grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are arranged in panicle-like inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
