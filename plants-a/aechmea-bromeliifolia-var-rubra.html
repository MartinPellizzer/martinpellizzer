Aechmea bromeliifolia var. rubra

Aechmea bromeliifolia var. rubra, commonly known as the red-tipped bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The leaves are typically a vibrant green with red or pink tips, adding visual interest to its appearance. It produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil and moderate humidity to flourish.
