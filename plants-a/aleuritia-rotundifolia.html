Aleuritia rotundifolia

Aleuritia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf elder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Aleuritia rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
