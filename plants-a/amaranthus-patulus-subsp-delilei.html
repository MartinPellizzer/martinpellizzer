Amaranthus patulus subsp. delilei

Amaranthus patulus subsp.

delilei, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, often green or reddish leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish flowers that develop into triangular, spiky seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
