Anathallis radialis

Anathallis radialis, commonly known as the radial anathallus, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a spurred labellum and a slender, erect stem. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are usually small and pale, with a distinct, intricate pattern on the petals.

Anathallis radialis is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
