Aframomum oleraceum

Aframomum oleraceum, commonly known as African ginger or black cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its tall, herbaceous stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark, aromatic fruits. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent, spicy flavor.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
