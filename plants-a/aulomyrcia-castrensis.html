Aulomyrcia castrensis

Aulomyrcia castrensis, commonly known as the Castrensis Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
