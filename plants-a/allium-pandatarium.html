Allium pandatarium

Allium pandatarium, commonly known as the purple garlic or the mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valuable plant in some traditional cuisines.

Allium pandatarium is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
