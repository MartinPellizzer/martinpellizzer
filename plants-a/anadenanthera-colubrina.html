Anadenanthera colubrina

Anadenanthera colubrina, commonly known as the "Bottle Tree," is a leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk and dense canopy of pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its root system has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The tree is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
