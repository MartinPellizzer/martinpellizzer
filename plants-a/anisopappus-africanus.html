Anisopappus africanus

Anisopappus africanus, commonly known as the African pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with silver-gray foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces fluffy, parachute-like seeds for wind dispersal.

Anisopappus africanus is often used in traditional medicine and is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils.
