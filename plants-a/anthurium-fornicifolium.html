Anthurium fornicifolium

Anthurium fornicifolium, commonly known as the hooded anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a deep green to purple sheen. The plant produces vibrant red spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the tiny, inconspicuous flowers. Anthurium fornicifolium thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil.

Its unique foliage and colorful inflorescence make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
