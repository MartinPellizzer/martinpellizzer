Arabis acutina

Arabis acutina, commonly known as the acute-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, acute leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Arabis acutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
