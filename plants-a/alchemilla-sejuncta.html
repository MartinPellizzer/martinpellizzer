Alchemilla sejuncta

Alchemilla sejuncta, commonly known as the clustered lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla sejuncta is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
