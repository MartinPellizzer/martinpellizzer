Allium barthianum

Allium barthianum, commonly known as the giant bulb onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a large, swollen basal stem that stores nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often growing up to 1 meter in height.

Allium barthianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
