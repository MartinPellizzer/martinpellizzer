Acianthera costaricensis

Acianthera costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican violet, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments. It is often found in cloud forests and is adapted to grow on trees or rocks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
