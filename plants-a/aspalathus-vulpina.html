Aspalathus vulpina

Aspalathus vulpina, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Aspalathus vulpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
