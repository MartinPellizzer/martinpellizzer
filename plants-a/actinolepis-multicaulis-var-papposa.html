Actinolepis multicaulis var. papposa

Actinolepis multicaulis var. papposa, commonly known as the woolly stemless kniphofia, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems that are densely covered in soft, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces tall spikes of bright red or orange tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
