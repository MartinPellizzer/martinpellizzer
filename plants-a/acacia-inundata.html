Acacia inundata

Acacia inundata, commonly known as the floodplain acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the floodplains of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stem and bipinnate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which bloom during the wet season. Acacia inundata is adapted to seasonal flooding and can tolerate waterlogged soils, making it a common species in riparian zones.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
