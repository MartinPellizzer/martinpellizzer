Acantholimon damassanum

Acantholimon damassanum, commonly known as the Damascene thornweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or arid soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features spiny stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Acantholimon damassanum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and supports local pollinator populations.
