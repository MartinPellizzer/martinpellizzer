Aetheolaena doryphylla

Aetheolaena doryphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish coating of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, spires of violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its striking appearance, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticulture.
