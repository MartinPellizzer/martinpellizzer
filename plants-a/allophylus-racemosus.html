Allophylus racemosus

Allophylus racemosus, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of white, fleshy fruits that resemble small balls, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
