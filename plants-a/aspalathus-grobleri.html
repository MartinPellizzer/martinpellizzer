Aspalathus grobleri

Aspalathus grobleri, commonly known as the Grobler's bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Aspalathus grobleri is also known for its potential medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
