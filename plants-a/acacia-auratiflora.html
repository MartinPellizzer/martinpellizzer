Acacia auratiflora

Acacia auratiflora, commonly known as the golden-flowered acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in spherical or cylindrical heads, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and birds, contributing to its ecological importance.

Acacia auratiflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
