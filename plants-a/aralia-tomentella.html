Aralia tomentella

Aralia tomentella, commonly known as the woolly aralia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features compound leaves with a soft, woolly texture, giving it its common name. Its small white flowers appear in late spring and are followed by bright red berries.

Aralia tomentella is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
