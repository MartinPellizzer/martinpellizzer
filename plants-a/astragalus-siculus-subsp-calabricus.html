Astragalus siculus subsp. calabricus

Astragalus siculus subsp.

calabricus, commonly known as the Sicilian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to Mediterranean climates.
