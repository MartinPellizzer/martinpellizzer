Acalypha villosa var. genuina

Acalypha villosa var. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf maple, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a prominent midrib and a hairy underside.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
