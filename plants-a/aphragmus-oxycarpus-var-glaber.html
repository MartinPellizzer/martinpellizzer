Aphragmus oxycarpus var. glaber

Aphragmus oxycarpus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-leafed aphragmus, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish in color. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous small, hard seeds.

This species is often grown for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
