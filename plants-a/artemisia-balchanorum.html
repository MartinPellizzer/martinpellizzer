Artemisia balchanorum

Artemisia balchanorum, commonly known as the Balchan's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Artemisia balchanorum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and digestive properties.
