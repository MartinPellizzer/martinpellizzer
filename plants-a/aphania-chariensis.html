Aphania chariensis

Aphania chariensis, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Aphania chariensis is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
