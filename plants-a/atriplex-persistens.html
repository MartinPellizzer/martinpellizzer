Atriplex persistens

Atriplex persistens, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in saline soils. Atriplex persistens is a hardy species that plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
