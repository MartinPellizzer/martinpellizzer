Astragalus melanochiton

Astragalus melanochiton, commonly known as the black-horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Astragalus melanochiton is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
