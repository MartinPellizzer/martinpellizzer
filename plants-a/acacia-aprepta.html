Acacia aprepta

Acacia aprepta, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-white inflorescences that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in woody pods, which split open to release the seeds when mature.

Acacia aprepta is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
