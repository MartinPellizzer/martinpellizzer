Agalmyla exannulata

Agalmyla exannulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agalmyla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Agalmyla exannulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
