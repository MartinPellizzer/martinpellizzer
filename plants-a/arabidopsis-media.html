Arabidopsis media

Arabidopsis media, commonly known as the mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often used in ecological studies due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Arabidopsis media is also a model organism in plant genetics research.
