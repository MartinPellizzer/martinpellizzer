Acanthus ilicifolius subsp. orientalis

Acanthus ilicifolius subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features distinctive, palmate leaves with a leathery texture and a silvery-white underside.

The plant produces spiny, bract-like structures that surround its showy, spathaceous flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its architectural form and long-lasting foliage.

Native Distribution

Acanthus ilicifolius subsp.

orientalis is primarily native to the eastern Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey, Cyprus, and the Levant. It is commonly found in rocky habitats, such as cliffs, slopes, and coastal areas.

This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and is often associated with dry, sunny environments. Its distribution extends into southern Europe, particularly in Greece and the Balkans.

It is a hardy plant that is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Introduced Distribution

Acanthus ilicifolius subsp.

orientalis was mainly introduced to ornamental horticulture and garden design due to its striking foliage and architectural form. It is often cultivated in temperate climates for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
