Aster dahuricus

Aster dahuricus, commonly known as the Russian aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disc florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster dahuricus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
