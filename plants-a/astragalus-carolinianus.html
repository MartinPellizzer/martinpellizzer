Astragalus carolinianus

Astragalus carolinianus, commonly known as Carolina milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and fields. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus carolinianus is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
