Aglaia splendens

Aglaia splendens, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Aglaia splendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
