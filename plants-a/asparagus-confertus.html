Asparagus confertus

Asparagus confertus, commonly known as the dense asparagus, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. The plant has fern-like, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Asparagus confertus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
