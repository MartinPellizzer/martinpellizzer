Amomum nilgiricum

Amomum nilgiricum, commonly known as the Nilgiri cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped fruits that contain numerous small, dark brown seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in dense forests. Its leaves are broad, lanceolate, and covered with a fine, hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
