Asplenium difforme

Asplenium difforme, commonly known as the common maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly curled appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Asplenium difforme is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
