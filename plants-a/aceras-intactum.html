Aceras intactum

Aceras intactum, commonly known as the intact acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
