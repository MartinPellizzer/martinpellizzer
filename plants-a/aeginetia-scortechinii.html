Aeginetia scortechinii

Aeginetia scortechinii, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that are enclosed in a pappus, aiding in its propagation.

Aeginetia scortechinii thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
