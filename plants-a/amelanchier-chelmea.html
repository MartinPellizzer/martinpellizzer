Amelanchier chelmea

Amelanchier chelmea, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its attractive, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, providing food for wildlife and humans alike. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated edge, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Amelanchier chelmea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
