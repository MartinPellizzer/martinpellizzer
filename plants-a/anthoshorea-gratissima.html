Anthoshorea gratissima

Anthoshorea gratissima, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, typically blooming in the spring. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and smooth, greyish bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, often with a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
