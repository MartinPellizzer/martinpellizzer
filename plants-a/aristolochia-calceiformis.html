Aristolochia calceiformis

Aristolochia calceiformis, commonly known as the shoeplant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, saddle-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses but can also be toxic.
