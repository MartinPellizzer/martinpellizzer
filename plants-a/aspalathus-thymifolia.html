Aspalathus thymifolia

Aspalathus thymifolia, commonly known as sweet thyme, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, small, yellow flowers. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its fragrant foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Aspalathus thymifolia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
