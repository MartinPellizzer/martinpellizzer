Astragalus tragacanthoides

Astragalus tragacanthoides, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, sticky resin that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
