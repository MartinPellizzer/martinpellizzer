Agapanthus umbellatus var. praecox

Agapanthus umbellatus var. praecox, commonly known as the early blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in early summer, forming an umbel-like arrangement. The plant has strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various climates.
