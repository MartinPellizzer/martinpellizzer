Armeriastrum aspadanum

Armeriastrum aspadanum, commonly known as the Persian sand-verbena, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent-like leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
