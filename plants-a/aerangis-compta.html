Aerangis compta

Aerangis compta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Aerangis compta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
