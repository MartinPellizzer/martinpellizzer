Arachnorchis peisleyi

Arachnorchis peisleyi, commonly known as the Peisley's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the wet tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin, hair-like leaves and a single, delicate flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often among the undergrowth of dense rainforest canopies. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of spider webs.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species, facing threats from habitat loss and climate change.
