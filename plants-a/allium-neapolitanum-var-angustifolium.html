Allium neapolitanum var. angustifolium

Allium neapolitanum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its mild garlic scent and edible leaves.
