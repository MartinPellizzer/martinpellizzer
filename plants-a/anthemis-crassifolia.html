Anthemis crassifolia

Anthemis crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage is aromatic and can be used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
