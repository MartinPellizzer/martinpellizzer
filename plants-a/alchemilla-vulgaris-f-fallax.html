Alchemilla vulgaris f. fallax

Alchemilla vulgaris f. fallax, commonly known as the fallax form of lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy bloom. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
