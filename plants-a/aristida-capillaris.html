Aristida capillaris

Aristida capillaris, commonly known as slender wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
