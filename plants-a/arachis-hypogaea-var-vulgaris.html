Arachis hypogaea var. vulgaris

Arachis hypogaea var. vulgaris, commonly known as the peanut, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is an annual legume that produces underground pods containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the characteristic peanut pods. Its growth habit involves the pegs elongating from the base of the stem to the soil surface, where the pods mature.

This species is cultivated worldwide for its high nutritional value and oil content.
