Adiantum bonatianum

Adiantum bonatianum, commonly known as the Bonatian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, finely divided structure.

Adiantum bonatianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
