Anacamptis pyramidalis var. pyramidalis

Anacamptis pyramidalis var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the Pyramidal Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem that bears a dense spike of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are tubular and have a distinctive pyramidal shape, which gives the species its common name.

It is also known for its fragrant blooms and is often used in ornamental gardening.
