Anisodontea julii subsp. pannosa

Anisodontea julii subsp.

pannosa, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy underside.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
