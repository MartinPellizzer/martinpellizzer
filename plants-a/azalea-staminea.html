Azalea staminea

Azalea staminea, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are typically borne in terminal clusters and bloom in the spring.

Azalea staminea is part of the Rhododendron genus and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
