Allium turkestanicum

Allium turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan leek, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers in the summer, followed by bulb-like structures that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable ornamental and potential edible plant in horticulture.
