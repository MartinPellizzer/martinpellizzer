Alchemilla arvensis f. elongata

Alchemilla arvensis f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated lady's mantle, is a botanical variant of the common lady's mantle.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its foliage is notable for its ability to retain moisture, which contributes to its adaptability in various soil types.
