Ageratina scorodonioides

Ageratina scorodonioides, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
