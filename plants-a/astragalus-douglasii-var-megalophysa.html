Astragalus douglasii var. megalophysa

Astragalus douglasii var. megalophysa, commonly known as the Douglas's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.

This variety is notable for its large, inflated seed pods that contribute to its distinctive appearance.
