Acaena polycarpa subsp. lilloi

Acaena polycarpa subsp.

lilloi, commonly known as the Lillo's New Zealand burr, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red or orange in color.

The plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions. Its fruits are an important food source for native birds and other wildlife.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to other Acaena species.
