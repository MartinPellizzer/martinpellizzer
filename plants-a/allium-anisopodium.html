Allium anisopodium

Allium anisopodium, commonly known as the bulbous leek or wild leek, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, slender stem topped with a cluster of small, white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, leek-like flavor, making it a potential culinary herb.

However, it is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to spread readily.
