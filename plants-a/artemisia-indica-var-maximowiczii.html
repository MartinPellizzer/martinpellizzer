Artemisia indica var. maximowiczii

Artemisia indica var. maximowiczii, commonly known as giant wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and gray-green, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that appear in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky habitats.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
