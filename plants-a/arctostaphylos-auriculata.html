Arctostaphylos auriculata

Arctostaphylos auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically oval to lance-shaped and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, usually pink to reddish in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, smooth seeds.

Arctostaphylos auriculata is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
