Allium gasyunense

Allium gasyunense, commonly known as the Madagascar leek, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its bulbous stem is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Allium gasyunense is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments, showcasing its resilience in arid conditions.
