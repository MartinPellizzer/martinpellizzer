Artemisia mongolorum subsp. saissanica

Artemisia mongolorum subsp.

saissanica, commonly known as the Saisan Artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of northern China. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in panicles during the summer months.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species suitable for dry landscapes. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators in its native range.
