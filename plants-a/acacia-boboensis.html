Acacia boboensis

Acacia boboensis, commonly known as the Bobo acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces yellow flowers and is often used for its timber and as a source of gum arabic.

Acacia boboensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
