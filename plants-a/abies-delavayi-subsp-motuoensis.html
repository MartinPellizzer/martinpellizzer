Abies delavayi subsp. motuoensis

Abies delavayi subsp.

motuoensis, commonly known as the Motuo fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needle-like leaves, and distinctive cones.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically above 3,000 meters, where it forms dense forests. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
