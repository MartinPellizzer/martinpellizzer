Acacia mucronata var. dissitiflora

Acacia mucronata var. dissitiflora, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing some drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
