Argentina tapetodes

Argentina tapetodes, commonly known as the Argentine tapetode, is a type of mite belonging to the family Tetranychidae.

These tiny arachnids are often found on the leaves of various plants, where they feed on plant sap. They are particularly noted for their ability to cause significant damage to crops and ornamental plants. The life cycle of Argentina tapetodes includes several stages, such as egg, protonymph, deutonymph, and adult.

Their presence can lead to the development of webbing on affected plants, which is a common sign of infestation.
