Azalea glauca

Azalea glauca, commonly known as the blue azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its small, showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep blue. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes, particularly in regions with mild winters.

Azalea glauca is also notable for its ability to produce fragrant blooms in the spring.
