Arum guttatum

Arum guttatum, commonly known as the spotted arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a large, heart-shaped leaf with a distinctive purple and green coloration, often marked with red and white spots. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on insects for pollination.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
