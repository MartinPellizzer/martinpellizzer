Aglaia glaucescens

Aglaia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-leaf aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Aglaia glaucescens is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
