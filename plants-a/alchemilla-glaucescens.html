Alchemilla glaucescens

Alchemilla glaucescens, commonly known as silver woundwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their soothing properties.

Alchemilla glaucescens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
