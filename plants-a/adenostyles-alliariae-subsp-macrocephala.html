Adenostyles alliariae subsp. macrocephala

Adenostyles alliariae subsp.

macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb with a basal rosette of leaves and stems that produce small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more prominent flower heads compared to other subspecies of Adenostyles.
