Andropogon dulcis

Andropogon dulcis, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and a sweet, aromatic scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of small, spike-like flowers.

Sweetgrass is often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies due to its pleasant fragrance and symbolic significance.
