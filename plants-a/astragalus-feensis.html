Astragalus feensis

Astragalus feensis, commonly known as the Fei's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus feensis is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its root system plays a crucial role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
