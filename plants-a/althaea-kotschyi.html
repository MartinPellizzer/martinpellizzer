Althaea kotschyi

Althaea kotschyi, commonly known as the Armenian marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes plants known for their mucilage-rich tissues. The plant features hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Althaea kotschyi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.
