Arundinaria pallidiflora

Arundinaria pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Arundinaria pallidiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in ecological restoration projects.
