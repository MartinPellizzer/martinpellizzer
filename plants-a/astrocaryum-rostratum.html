Astrocaryum rostratum

Astrocaryum rostratum, commonly known as the "peach palm," is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to peaches, with a sweet, fibrous pulp. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody shell and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
