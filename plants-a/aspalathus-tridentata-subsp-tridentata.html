Aspalathus tridentata subsp. tridentata

Aspalathus tridentata subsp.

tridentata, commonly known as the Cape tea or rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa. It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is known for its distinctive red-brown bark and its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. It is a key species in the fynbos biome, contributing to the region's unique biodiversity.

The plant is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of herbal tea.
