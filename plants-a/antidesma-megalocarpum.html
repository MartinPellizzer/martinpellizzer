Antidesma megalocarpum

Antidesma megalocarpum, commonly known as the giant antidesma, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often glandular. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
