Anthurium siccisilvarum

Anthurium siccisilvarum, commonly known as the dry forest anthurium, is a member of the Araceae family native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that house the true flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
