Alyssum cheiranthifolium

Alyssum cheiranthifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and lobed, contrasting with its slender, upright stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum cheiranthifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
