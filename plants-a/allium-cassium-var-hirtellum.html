Allium cassium var. hirtellum

Allium cassium var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy garlic.

It is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bulbous structures that resemble garlic cloves. It blooms with clusters of pale pink to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
