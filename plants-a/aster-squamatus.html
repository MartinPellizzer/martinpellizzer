Aster squamatus

Aster squamatus, commonly known as the squamose aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, scaly hairs, giving it its common name. It produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Aster squamatus is often found in open woodlands and prairies, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
