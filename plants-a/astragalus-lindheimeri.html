Astragalus lindheimeri

Astragalus lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
