Ascolepis speciosa var. ochracea

Ascolepis speciosa var. ochracea, commonly known as the golden thistle, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange bracts that surround the central flower head, giving it a showy appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually spiny and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its defensive adaptations.

This variant is notable for its distinctive coloration and is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates.
