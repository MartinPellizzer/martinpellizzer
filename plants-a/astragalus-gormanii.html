Astragalus gormanii

Astragalus gormanii, commonly known as Gorman's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus gormanii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
