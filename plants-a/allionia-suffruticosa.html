Allionia suffruticosa

Allionia suffruticosa, commonly known as the redroot sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its roots are reddish in color, giving it its common name.

Allionia suffruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, coastal environments.
