Astragalus hemiphaca

Astragalus hemiphaca, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow leaflets, and it produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

Astragalus hemiphaca is often found in the western United States and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
