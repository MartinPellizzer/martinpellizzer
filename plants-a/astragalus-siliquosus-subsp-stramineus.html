Astragalus siliquosus subsp. stramineus

Astragalus siliquosus subsp.

stramineus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a pale green hue.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense spikes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. Astragalus siliquosus subsp.

stramineus is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
