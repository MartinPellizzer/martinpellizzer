Antirrhinum subbaeticum

Antirrhinum subbaeticum, commonly known as the Baetican snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

The species is part of the Plantaginaceae family, which includes other snapdragons and related genera.
