Ajax luteus var. propinquus

Ajax luteus var. propinquus, commonly known as the yellow ajuga, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and delicate floral display.
