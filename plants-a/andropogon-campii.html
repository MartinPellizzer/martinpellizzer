Andropogon campii

Andropogon campii, commonly known as tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often tinged with silver. This plant thrives in prairie and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Andropogon campii plays a vital role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its seeds are an important food source for birds and small mammals.
