Averrhoa microphylla

Averrhoa microphylla, commonly known as the West African starfruit or yellow starfruit, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange, star-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully mature. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often growing up to 5 meters in height, with simple, ovate leaves and white or pink flowers. Its fruits are rich in vitamin C and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
