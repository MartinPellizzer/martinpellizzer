Artemisia tournefortiana

Artemisia tournefortiana, commonly known as Spanish tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with finely divided, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, herbal scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal and aromatic properties, it has been historically used in traditional remedies and as a culinary herb.
