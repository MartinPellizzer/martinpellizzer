Ambulia conferta

Ambulia conferta, commonly known as the clustered ambulia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ambulia conferta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
