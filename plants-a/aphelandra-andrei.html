Aphelandra andrei

Aphelandra andrei, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves with a bright yellow to orange coloration. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, often arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Aphelandra andrei thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to add visual interest to shaded gardens.
