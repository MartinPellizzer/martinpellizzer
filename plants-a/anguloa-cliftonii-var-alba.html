Anguloa cliftonii var. alba

Anguloa cliftonii var. alba, commonly known as the white anguloa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

This perennial herbaceous plant is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. It typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
