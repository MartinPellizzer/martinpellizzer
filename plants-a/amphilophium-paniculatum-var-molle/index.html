Amphilophium Paniculatum Var. Molle

Blue mistflower, scientifically known as Amphilophium paniculatum var. molle, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the southeastern United States. It is prized for its delicate, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. This herbaceous perennial thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its feathery plumes create a mist-like appearance, earning it its common name. With its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for gardeners and conservationists alike.
