Archidendron clypearia var. sessiliflorum

Archidendron clypearia var. sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flower legume, is a tropical plant characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive floral structures.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with elongated, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique sessile (stemless) arrangement of the floral parts.

This variety is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, tropical environments.
