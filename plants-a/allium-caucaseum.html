Allium caucaseum

Allium caucaseum, commonly known as the Caucasian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces a tall flowering stem topped with a cluster of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its mild garlic-like fragrance and edible leaves.
