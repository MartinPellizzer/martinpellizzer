Astragalus borraginaceus

Astragalus borraginaceus, commonly known as the bluebottle or blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, not Borraginaceae as the name suggests.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in dry, sunny environments.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
