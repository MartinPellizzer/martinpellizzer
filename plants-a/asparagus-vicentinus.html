Asparagus vicentinus

Asparagus vicentinus, commonly known as the Vicentine asparagus, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces edible, needle-like shoots that are often harvested in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to form dense clumps over time.
