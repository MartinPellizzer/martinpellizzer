Andropogon scoparius var. divergens

Andropogon scoparius var. divergens, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat blades and a growth habit that forms dense, sod-like turf. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its foliage is often a striking blue-green color, which becomes more pronounced during the growing season.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in native plant landscapes.
