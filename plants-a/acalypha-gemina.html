Acalypha gemina

Acalypha gemina, commonly known as the twinleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often have a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by three-lobed, papery bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Acalypha gemina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
