Artemisia subulata

Artemisia subulata, commonly known as silverleaf wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, spine-tipped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Artemisia subulata is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
