Amygdalus communis var. macrocarpa

Amygdalus communis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the sweet almond tree, is a variety of the almond species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces hard-shelled fruits containing edible seeds, which are widely used in food and cosmetic industries. Its botanical features include a sturdy trunk, lateral branches, and a lifespan that can exceed several decades.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
