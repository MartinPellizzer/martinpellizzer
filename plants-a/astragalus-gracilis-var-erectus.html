Astragalus gracilis var. erectus

Astragalus gracilis var. erectus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are narrow and pointed.

Astragalus gracilis var. erectus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
