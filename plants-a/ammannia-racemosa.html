Ammannia racemosa

Ammannia racemosa, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. Its stems are often reddish in color and can become woody with age.

Ammannia racemosa is known for its ability to thrive in moist soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
