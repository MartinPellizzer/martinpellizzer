Acaciella laevis

Acaciella laevis, commonly known as the smooth acacia, is a small shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, smooth, and often hairless leaves, which are typically alternate and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing flattened seeds. Acaciella laevis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

It is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
