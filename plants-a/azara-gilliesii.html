Azara gilliesii

Azara gilliesii, commonly known as the golden azara, is a small evergreen tree native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Azara gilliesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
