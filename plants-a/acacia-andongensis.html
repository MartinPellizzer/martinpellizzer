Acacia andongensis

Acacia andongensis, commonly known as the Andong acacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feather-like leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Acacia andongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
