Aconitum pseudokusnezowii

Aconitum pseudokusnezowii, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, characteristic of the genus Aconitum. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply cut lobes, and it typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
