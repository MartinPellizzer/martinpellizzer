Amsinckia littoralis

Amsinckia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal globemallow, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

Amsinckia littoralis plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
