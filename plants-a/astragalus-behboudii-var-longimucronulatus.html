Astragalus behboudii var. longimucronulatus

Astragalus behboudii var. longimucronulatus, commonly known as the long-pointed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are often covered in a distinctive, hairy coating. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
