Alepidea rehmannii

Alepidea rehmannii, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a prominent central disk. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

Alepidea rehmannii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
