Atractocarpus vaginatus

Atractocarpus vaginatus, commonly known as the velvetwood or sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its scent and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fleshy, red berries containing one or two seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its timber and essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
