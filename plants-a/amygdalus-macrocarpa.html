Amygdalus macrocarpa

Amygdalus macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded almond.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in early spring before its foliage appears. Its fruit is a drupe with a thick, leathery outer layer enclosing a single large seed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its edible seeds in some regions.
