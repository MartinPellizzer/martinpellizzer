Acacia hirta var. suffrutescens

Acacia hirta var. suffrutescens, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow globular flowers clustered in spikes, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
