Aspidium pauper

Aspidium pauper, commonly known as the poor fern, is a species of polypod fern native to various regions in Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, pinnately compound fronds with a feathery appearance. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Aspidium pauper is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
