Aster oxyphyllus

Aster oxyphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinct hairy texture.

The showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
