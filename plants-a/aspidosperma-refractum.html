Aspidosperma refractum

Aspidosperma refractum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries containing seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

The wood is dense and durable, making it useful for furniture and construction.
