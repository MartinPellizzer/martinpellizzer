Allium prattii var. latifoliatum

Allium prattii var. latifoliatum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with broad, flat leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Its bulbous roots are edible and resemble those of garlic, though it is less pungent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like flavor, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and culinary use.
