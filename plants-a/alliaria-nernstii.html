Alliaria nernstii

Alliaria nernstii, commonly known as the alpine garlic or winter garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves contain sulfur compounds that contribute to its pungent odor, similar to garlic and onions.

Alliaria nernstii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
