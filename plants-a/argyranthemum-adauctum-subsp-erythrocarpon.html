Argyranthemum adauctum subsp. erythrocarpon

Argyranthemum adauctum subsp.

erythrocarpon, commonly known as the red-berried argyranthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its most distinctive feature is the bright red or orange berries that develop after flowering. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
