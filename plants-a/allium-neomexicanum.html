Allium neomexicanum

Allium neomexicanum, commonly known as the New Mexico garlic, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like foliage. The plant produces umbels of white to pale pink flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local food use.
