Alcea lineariloba

Alcea lineariloba, commonly known as the narrowleaf hollyhock, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery-gray underside, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
