Athyrium attenuatum f. dentigerum

Athyrium attenuatum f. dentigerum, commonly known as the toothed maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China and Japan. The fronds are typically arching and have a distinctive, feathery appearance with serrated margins in this form. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
