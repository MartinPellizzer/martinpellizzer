Anaphalis margaritacea subsp. margaritacea

Anaphalis margaritacea subsp.

margaritacea, commonly known as the "Goatweed" or "Silver Carpet," is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
