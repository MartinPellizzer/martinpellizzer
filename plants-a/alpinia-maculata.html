Alpinia maculata

Alpinia maculata, commonly known as the variegated ginger, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Alpinia maculata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
