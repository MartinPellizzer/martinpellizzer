Arundinaria toyomurensis

Arundinaria toyomurensis, commonly known as the Toyomura bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and as a source of fiber.
