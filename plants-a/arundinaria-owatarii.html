Arundinaria owatarii

Arundinaria owatarii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Arundinaria owatarii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense clumps.
