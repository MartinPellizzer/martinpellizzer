Amomum cristatissimum

Amomum cristatissimum, commonly known as the crested ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The unique crested shape of its inflorescence makes it a striking ornamental plant.
