Anthurium scandens var. violaceum

Anthurium scandens var. violaceum, commonly known as the violet spider plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It features vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with a deep violet hue, often adorned with intricate patterns of silver and green. The plant produces clusters of spadix flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of white or pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This varietal is popular for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid indoor environments.
