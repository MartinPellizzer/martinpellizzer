Allium longicollum

Allium longicollum, commonly known as the long-necked leek, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features a bulbous stem covered in narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in a spherical cluster.

Allium longicollum is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
