Aristida laevis

Aristida laevis, commonly known as the silky grass or silky lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Aristida laevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
