Aralia nutans

Aralia nutans, commonly known as the nodding aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a feather-like appearance, typically arranged in a whorl at the ends of its stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels that droop downward, giving it its common name. Its fruits are red berries that mature in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife.

Aralia nutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
