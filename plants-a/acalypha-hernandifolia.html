Acalypha hernandifolia

Acalypha hernandifolia, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Acalypha hernandifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
