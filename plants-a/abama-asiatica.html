Abama asiatica

Abama asiatica, commonly known as the Asian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Abama asiatica is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
