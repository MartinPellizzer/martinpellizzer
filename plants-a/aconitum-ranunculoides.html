Aconitum ranunculoides

Aconitum ranunculoides, commonly known as the meadow aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces showy, spicate inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

However, it is toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids, making it hazardous to both humans and animals.
