Apocynum × divergens

Apocynum × divergens, commonly known as the Indian hemp or dogbane, is a flowering plant in the Apocynaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Apocynum cannabinum and Apocynum venetum. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are typically pink or white, and they bloom in summer.

The plant contains toxic cardiac glycosides, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
