Aster punctatus

Aster punctatus, commonly known as the dotted aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to pink flowers with white markings, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. Aster punctatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
