Aster carthamodes

Aster carthamodes, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
