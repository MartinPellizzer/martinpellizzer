Arctostaphylos manzanita var. elegans

Arctostaphylos manzanita var. elegans, commonly known as the slender manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pink, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
