Aster collinsii

Aster collinsii, commonly known as Collins' aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to violet petals, blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

Aster collinsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fall blooms.
