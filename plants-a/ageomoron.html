Ageomoron

Ageomoron, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant belonging to the family Gesneriaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
