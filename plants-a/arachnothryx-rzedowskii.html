Arachnothryx rzedowskii

Arachnothryx rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, woody pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
