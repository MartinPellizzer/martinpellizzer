Amorphophallus tuberculatus

Amorphophallus tuberculatus, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, tuberous underground stem, which can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with a single spadix surrounded by a spathe, resembling a phallic shape, hence its common name. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

The plant is cultivated for its starchy tubers, which are used in various culinary applications.
