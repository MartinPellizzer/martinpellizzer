Anadenanthera macrocarpa

Anadenanthera macrocarpa, commonly known as the "Bottle Tree," is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique swollen trunk that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The tree produces large, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are palmate, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.
