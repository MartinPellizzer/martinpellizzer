Acacia riparia var. argentinensis

Acacia riparia var. argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine river acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that attract various pollinators, including bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

This species is often found along riverbanks and in semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
