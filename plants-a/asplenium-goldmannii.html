Asplenium goldmannii

Asplenium goldmannii, commonly known as the golden holly fern, is a compact evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features fronds that are dark green with a distinctive golden or yellowish hue on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant has a rosette-like growth pattern, with short, upright fronds that are leathery and slightly serrated. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, moist soil.

Asplenium goldmannii is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and ease of care.
