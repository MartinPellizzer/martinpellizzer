Afraurantium senegalense

Afraurantium senegalense, commonly known as the Senegalese tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, sandy soils. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and the tree is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.

The fruit's pulp is sour and used in various culinary applications across its native region.
