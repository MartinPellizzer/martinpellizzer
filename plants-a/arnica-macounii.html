Arnica macounii

Arnica macounii, commonly known as Macoun's arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should not be used without proper guidance.
