Arenaria szechuensis

Arenaria szechuensis, commonly known as the Sichuan sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with numerous small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Arenaria szechuensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
