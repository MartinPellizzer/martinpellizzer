Anisophyllum lindenianum

Anisophyllum lindenianum, commonly known as the Mexican stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically in the spring. Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soil make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Anisophyllum lindenianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
