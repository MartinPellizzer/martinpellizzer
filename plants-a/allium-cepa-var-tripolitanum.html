Allium cepa var. tripolitanum

Allium cepa var. tripolitanum, commonly known as the Tripolitan onion, is a botanical variant of the common onion.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. This plant features bulbous stems and broad, flat leaves that are typically green in color. The flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, and bloom in the summer months.

It is valued for its mild flavor and is often used in traditional cuisines.
