Apocynum × lividum var. vacillans

Apocynum × lividum var. vacillans, commonly known as the variable milkweed, is a hybrid species within the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting various pollinators. Its latex is milky and toxic, containing cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals.

This variant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats across the eastern United States.
