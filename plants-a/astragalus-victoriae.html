Astragalus victoriae

Astragalus victoriae, commonly known as Victoria's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
