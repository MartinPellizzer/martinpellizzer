Artemisia chingii

Artemisia chingii, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, have been used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

Artemisia chingii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
