Arachnorchis patersonii

Arachnorchis patersonii, commonly known as the Paterson's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin sepals and petals that resemble legs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and undergrowth. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its habitat specificity and threats from habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
