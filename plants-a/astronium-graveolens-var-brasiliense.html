Astronium graveolens var. brasiliense

Astronium graveolens var. brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian starfruit or Natal apple, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a yellow-orange fruit with a star-shaped calyx. The fruit has a sweet, juicy pulp and is often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

It is valued for both its ornamental qualities and edible fruit.
