Anthemis abrotanifolia subsp. panachaica

Anthemis abrotanifolia subsp.

panachaica, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils with potential medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its use in traditional herbal remedies and ornamental gardening.
