Allium Fuscum Subsp. Fussii

Allium fuscum subsp. fussii is a lesser-known member of the onion family with distinctive pale purple flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, this plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. Its compact growth habit and unique bloom make it a valuable addition to alpine gardens. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in horticultural circles. This subspecies offers both ornamental value and ecological interest for plant enthusiasts.
