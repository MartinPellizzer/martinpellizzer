Amphiglottis lurida

Amphiglottis lurida, commonly known as the rusty amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often red or orange flowers. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically a dark green color with a reddish tint along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Amphiglottis lurida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
