Astragalus parrowianus

Astragalus parrowianus, commonly known as the parrowian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
