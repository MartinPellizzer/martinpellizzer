Alyssum hispidum

Alyssum hispidum, commonly known as hairy alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate foliage and blooms.

Alyssum hispidum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
