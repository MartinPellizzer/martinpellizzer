Arenaria guatemalensis

Arenaria guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan sandwort, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant typically features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, adapted to thrive in arid or rocky environments.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
