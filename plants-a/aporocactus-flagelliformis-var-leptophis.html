Aporocactus flagelliformis var. leptophis

Aporocactus flagelliformis var. leptophis, commonly known as the creeping columnar cactus, is a unique species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has cylindrical, ribbed stems with prominent areoles, from which spines and flowers emerge. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces small, edible fruits.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
