Avena laconica

Avena laconica, commonly known as the Crete wild oat, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces awned spikelets that are typically green to pale yellow in color. Avena laconica thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

It is a hardy species that is well adapted to the arid climate of its native range.
