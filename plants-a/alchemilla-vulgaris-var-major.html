Alchemilla vulgaris var. major

Alchemilla vulgaris var. major, commonly known as lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive rosette of velvety, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Lady's mantle is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
