Asperula glomerata var. capitata

Asperula glomerata var. capitata, commonly known as the clustered woodruff, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in dense umbels. The plant thrives in woodland and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a leathery texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
