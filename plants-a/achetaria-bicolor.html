Achetaria bicolor

Achetaria bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its strikingly contrasting colors on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a compact, herbaceous growth habit. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Achetaria bicolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
