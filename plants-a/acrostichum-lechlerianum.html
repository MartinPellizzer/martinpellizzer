Acrostichum lechlerianum

Acrostichum lechlerianum, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and tall, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands.

Acrostichum lechlerianum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
