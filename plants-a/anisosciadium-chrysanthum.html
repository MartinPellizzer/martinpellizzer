Anisosciadium chrysanthum

Anisosciadium chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a striking golden hue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
