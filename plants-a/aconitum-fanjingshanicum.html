Aconitum fanjingshanicum

Aconitum fanjingshanicum, commonly known as the Fanjing Mountain aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a deep green color and a rough texture.

Aconitum fanjingshanicum is also noted for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
