Acacia denudata var. spinosissima

Acacia denudata var. spinosissima, commonly known as the spiny wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its distinctive spines and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
