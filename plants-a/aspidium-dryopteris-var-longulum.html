Aspidium dryopteris var. longulum

Aspidium dryopteris var. longulum, commonly known as the long-veined wood fern, is a variant of the wood fern genus Aspidium.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped fronds with distinctive, long, parallel veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky slopes. Its fronds are often divided into multiple segments, creating a delicate, feathery appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and light conditions.
