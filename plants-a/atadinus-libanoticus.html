Atadinus libanoticus

Atadinus libanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanon thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed on square stems. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
