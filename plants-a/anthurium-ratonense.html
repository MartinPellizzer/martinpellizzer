Anthurium ratonense

Anthurium ratonense, commonly known as the Ratonense flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a vibrant, often red or orange, spathe that resembles a flamingo's beak. Anthurium ratonense thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
