Achillea montenegrina

Achillea montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegro yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Achillea montenegrina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
