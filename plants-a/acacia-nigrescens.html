Acacia nigrescens

Acacia nigrescens, commonly known as black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acacia nigrescens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important species in ecological restoration.
