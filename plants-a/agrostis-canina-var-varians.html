Agrostis canina var. varians

Agrostis canina var. varians, commonly known as the variable bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, dunes, and coastal areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
