Acorus calamus var. americanus

Acorus calamus var. americanus, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has long, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are aromatic and have been historically used for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
