Aletris nana

Aletris nana, commonly known as the dwarf pipsissewa, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly curved. It produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries.

Aletris nana thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.
