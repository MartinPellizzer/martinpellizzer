Acetosella daphniformis

Acetosella daphniformis, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rounded leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves and flowers contain compounds that have been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Acetosella daphniformis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
