Achyrophorus pinnatifidus

Achyrophorus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lichen, is a unique species of lichen that exhibits a complex symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga.

It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can absorb moisture and nutrients from the surrounding substrate. The plant's structure consists of a thallus, which is a composite body formed by the fungal component, and the algal cells embedded within it. Its growth is slow, and it can often be mistaken for a moss due to its leaf-like appearance.

This lichen plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its habitat.
