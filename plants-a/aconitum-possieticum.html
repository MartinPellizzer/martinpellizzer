Aconitum possieticum

Aconitum possieticum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed divisions, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes.

Aconitum possieticum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested, though it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
