Asarum crassum

Asarum crassum, commonly known as broad-leaved wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous root system makes it a hardy and long-lived plant in suitable habitats.
