Andriapetalum rubescens var. acuminatum

Andriapetalum rubescens var. acuminatum, commonly known as the redstem false flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that turn reddish-brown as they mature, often with a distinctive hairless texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and have a somewhat aromatic scent when crushed.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
