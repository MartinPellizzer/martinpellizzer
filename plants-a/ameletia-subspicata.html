Ameletia subspicata

Ameletia subspicata, commonly known as the red-flowered ameletia, is a small shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
