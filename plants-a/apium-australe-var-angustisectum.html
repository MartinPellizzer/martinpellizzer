Apium australe var. angustisectum

Apium australe var. angustisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow and deeply lobed, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in salt-affected areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
