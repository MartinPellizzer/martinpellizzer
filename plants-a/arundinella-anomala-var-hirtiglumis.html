Arundinella anomala var. hirtiglumis

Arundinella anomala var. hirtiglumis, commonly known as the hairy bamboo or hairy grass bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
