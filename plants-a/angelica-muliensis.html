Angelica muliensis

Angelica muliensis, commonly known as the Yunnan angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and tall, branched inflorescences.

It is native to China, particularly found in the Yunnan province, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant features large, compound leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, and its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in umbels. Angelica muliensis is valued for its medicinal properties, with roots and leaves used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It also serves as a source of essential oils and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
