Acer monspessulanum subsp. syriacum

Acer monspessulanum subsp.

syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique leaf shape and seasonal color changes.
