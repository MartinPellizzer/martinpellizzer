Arctostaphylos nissenana var. arcana

Arctostaphylos nissenana var. arcana, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to California.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright red, urn-shaped flowers in the spring, which are pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a small, leathery capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
