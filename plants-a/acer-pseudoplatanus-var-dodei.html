Acer pseudoplatanus var. dodei

Acer pseudoplatanus var. dodei, commonly known as the Dodei maple, is a variant of the maple tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often displaying a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to different garden environments.
