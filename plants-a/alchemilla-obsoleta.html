Alchemilla obsoleta

Alchemilla obsoleta, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Alchemilla obsoleta is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
