Aconitum variegatum var. rostratum

Aconitum variegatum var. rostratum, commonly known as the variegated monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features palmately divided leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern, often with white or yellow markings. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
