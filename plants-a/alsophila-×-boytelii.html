Alsophila × boytelii

Alsophila × boytelii, commonly known as the hybrid tree fern, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Alsophila spinosa and Alsophila costaricana.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, brownish outer layer. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Alsophila × boytelii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
