Aegiphila cornifolia

Aegiphila cornifolia, commonly known as the winged leaf shrub, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wing-like leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Aegiphila cornifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Morphology

Aegiphila cornifolia has leaves that are lanceolate in shape, alternate in arrangement, and typically range from 5 to 10 centimeters in length, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The stem is erect and woody, with a rough, fissured bark that is gray to brown in color. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from white to pale pink, blooming during the summer months and being pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, dry capsule that disperses seeds by wind, and the seeds are tiny and light, aiding in their spread.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to the plant's arid environment.
