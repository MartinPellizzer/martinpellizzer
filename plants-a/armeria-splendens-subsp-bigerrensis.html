Armeria splendens subsp. bigerrensis

Armeria splendens subsp.

bigerrensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of showy white or pink flowers.

The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
