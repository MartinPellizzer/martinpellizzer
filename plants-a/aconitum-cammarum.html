Aconitum cammarum

Aconitum cammarum, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
