Anemone nobilis

Anemone nobilis, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically 3 to 5 inches in diameter. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often covered in a fine downy layer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Anemone nobilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
