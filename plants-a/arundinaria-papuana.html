Arundinaria papuana

Arundinaria papuana, commonly known as the Papuan bamboo, is a species of grass native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and often covered in a fine layer of hair. Arundinaria papuana is known for its robust growth and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
