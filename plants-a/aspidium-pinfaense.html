Aspidium pinfaense

Aspidium pinfaense, commonly known as the pinfaense water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving streams and ponds with muddy substrates. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Aspidium pinfaense plays a minor role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for small invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
