Adoxa corydalifolia

Adoxa corydalifolia, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that resemble small bells, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Adoxa corydalifolia is a member of the Adoxaceae family, which also includes plants like the sweet pepper.
