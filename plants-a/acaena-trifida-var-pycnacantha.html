Acaena trifida var. pycnacantha

Acaena trifida var. pycnacantha, commonly known as the dense-fruited acaena, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round, edible berries that are often used by native birds. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, hairy texture.
