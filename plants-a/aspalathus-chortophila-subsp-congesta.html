Aspalathus chortophila subsp. congesta

Aspalathus chortophila subsp.

congesta, commonly known as the dense-flowered bushveld legume, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
