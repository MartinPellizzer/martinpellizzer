Astragalus claranus

Astragalus claranus, commonly known as the clearleaf milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, bean-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus claranus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
