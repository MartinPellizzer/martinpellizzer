Aciotis purpurascens var. longifolia

Aciotis purpurascens var. longifolia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in open habitats like prairies and meadows, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The variety longifolia is distinguished by its longer, more slender leaves compared to the typical form.
