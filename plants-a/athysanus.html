Athysanus

Athysanus, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble an arrowhead. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. Athysanus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though care must be taken due to its toxic properties.
