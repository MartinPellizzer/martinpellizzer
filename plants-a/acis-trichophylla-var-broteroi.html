Acis trichophylla var. broteroi

Acis trichophylla var. broteroi, commonly known as the Broter's tufted grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers. It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Argentina.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
