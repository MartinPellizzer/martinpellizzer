Albizia dulcis

Albizia dulcis, commonly known as sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Albizia dulcis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
