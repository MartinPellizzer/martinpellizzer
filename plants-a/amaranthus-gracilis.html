Amaranthus gracilis

Amaranthus gracilis, commonly known as slender amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
