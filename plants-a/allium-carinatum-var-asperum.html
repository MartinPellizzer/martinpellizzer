Allium carinatum var. asperum

Allium carinatum var. asperum, commonly known as the rough leaved garlic.

This perennial plant is characterized by its robust, fibrous rhizomes and stout, cylindrical bulbs. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.
