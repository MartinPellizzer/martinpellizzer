Acanthocereus colombianus

Acanthocereus colombianus, commonly known as the Colombian cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of Colombia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and is adapted to the arid and high-altitude environments of its native habitat. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and bats. It has a slow growth rate and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
