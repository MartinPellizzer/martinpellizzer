Asprella digitaria

Asprella digitaria, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Asprella digitaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
