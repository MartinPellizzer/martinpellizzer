Alsophila taenitis var. submarginalis

Alsophila taenitis var. submarginalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tree fern, is a variant of the tree fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and fronds that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant exhibits a distinctive appearance with fronds that often have a pale or white margin, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
