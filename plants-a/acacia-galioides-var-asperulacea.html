Acacia galioides var. asperulacea

Acacia galioides var. asperulacea, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
