Arundinaria kimurae

Arundinaria kimurae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Kimura's bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green in color and arranged in a dense, clumping growth pattern. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese landscapes.

Arundinaria kimurae prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
