Acalypha reticulata var. longifolia

Acalypha reticulata var. longifolia, commonly known as the nettleleaf or longleaf acalypha, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its palmate leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
