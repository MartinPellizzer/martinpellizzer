Alsophila campanulata

Alsophila campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fronds are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their aesthetic and functional properties.
