Ardisia adenanthera

Ardisia adenanthera, commonly known as the red-rooted beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a smooth texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental berries and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
