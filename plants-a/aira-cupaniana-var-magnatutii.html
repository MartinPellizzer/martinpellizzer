Aira cupaniana var. magnatutii

Aira cupaniana var. magnatutii, commonly known as the giant panic grass, is a tall perennial grass native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-gray hue due to dense pubescence. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable in native plant landscapes.
