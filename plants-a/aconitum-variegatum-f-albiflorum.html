Aconitum variegatum f. albiflorum

Aconitum variegatum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered variety of the variegated monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves and its distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers that are predominantly white in this cultivar. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially hazardous to both humans and animals.
