Arctotis spinulosa

Arctotis spinulosa, commonly known as the spiked arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant, daisy-like blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
