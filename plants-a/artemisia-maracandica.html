Artemisia maracandica

Artemisia maracandica, commonly known as the Karakorum wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, often fuzzy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Artemisia maracandica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
