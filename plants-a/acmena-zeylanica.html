Acmena zeylanica

Acmena zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen tree native to southern and southeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and alternate, elliptic leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, round, green fruits with a tart flavor. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
