Astragalus lentiginosus var. kennedyi

Astragalus lentiginosus var. kennedyi, commonly known as Kennedy's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its small, hairy pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

This species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
