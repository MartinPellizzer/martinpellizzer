Achyranthes maneleensis

Achyranthes maneleensis, commonly known as the Maneleensis spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields due to its adaptability to various soil types.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of tropical flora.
