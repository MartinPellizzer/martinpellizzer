Alstroemeria revoluta

Alstroemeria revoluta, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has long, strap-like leaves. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy, often fragrant blooms.

Alstroemeria revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
