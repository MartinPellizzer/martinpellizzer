Arum nigrum var. apulum

Arum nigrum var. apulum, commonly known as the black arum or swamp arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It features a striking, umbrella-shaped inflorescence with a dark purple spathe and a spadix that bears small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its foliage consists of broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish underside.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
