Aster salicifolius

Aster salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and resemble those of willow trees, giving it its common name.

It produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
