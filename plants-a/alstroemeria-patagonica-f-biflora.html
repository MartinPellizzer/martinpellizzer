Alstroemeria patagonica f. biflora

Alstroemeria patagonica f. biflora, commonly known as the Patagonian alstroemeria, is a botanical variety native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or green markings.

This ornamental plant is valued for its longevity and ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
