Antilla erosa

Antilla erosa, commonly known as the pink ant plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a unique structure with specialized leaves that can trap small insects, contributing to its survival in nutrient-poor soils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of plant adaptation and ecological interactions.
