Aetheopappus caucasicus

Aetheopappus caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian sand thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grayish-green leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
