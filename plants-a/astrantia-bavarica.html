Astrantia bavarica

Astrantia bavarica, commonly known as the Bavarian aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to central Europe.

It features attractive, spherical flower heads that range in color from white to pink, surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are palmately lobed, with a hairy texture.

Astrantia bavarica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a versatile addition to woodland gardens.
