Agathosma anomala

Agathosma anomala, commonly known as African germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong, peppery odor when crushed.

Agathosma anomala is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.
