Arenaria costata var. humilior

Arenaria costata var. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf sandpink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to sandy and gravelly soils in arid regions.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are pink to reddish in color, typically appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating drought and poor soil conditions.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological resilience.
