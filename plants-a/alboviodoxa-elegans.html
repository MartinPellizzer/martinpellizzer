Alboviodoxa elegans

Alboviodoxa elegans, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring caution when handling.
