Arisaema engleri

Arisaema engleri, commonly known as the Hainan arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the Hainan Island in China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. It blooms with a small, inconspicuous flower cluster surrounded by a showy, petal-like structure.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species and is protected in its native region.
