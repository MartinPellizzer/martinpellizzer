Ambraria hirta

Ambraria hirta, commonly known as hairy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small golden clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Ambraria hirta produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
