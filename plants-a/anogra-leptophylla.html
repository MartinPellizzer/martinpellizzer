Anogra leptophylla

Anogra leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf anogra, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a silver-gray underside, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
