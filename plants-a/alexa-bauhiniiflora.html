Alexa bauhiniiflora

Alexa bauhiniiflora, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color, with five deeply lobed petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alexa bauhiniiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
