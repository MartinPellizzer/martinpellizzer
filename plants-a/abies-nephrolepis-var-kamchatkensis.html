Abies nephrolepis var. kamchatkensis

Abies nephrolepis var. kamchatkensis, commonly known as the Kamchatka fir, is a variant of the noble fir native to the Russian Far East.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The foliage is typically dark green, with a slight blue or grayish tint on the underside. It produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family.

This species thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
