Anthericum micranthum

Anthericum micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered ladies' tresses, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Anthericum micranthum is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and nature reserves.
