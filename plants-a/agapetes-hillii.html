Agapetes hillii

Agapetes hillii, commonly known as the cloud forest heath, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in cool, moist, and shaded environments, often found in cloud forests at high altitudes. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in clusters. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Agapetes hillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
