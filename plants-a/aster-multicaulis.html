Aster multicaulis

Aster multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with multiple stems arising from a central caudex. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Aster multicaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
