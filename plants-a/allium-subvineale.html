Allium subvineale

Allium subvineale, commonly known as the creeping leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a valuable plant in both culinary and horticultural contexts.

Allium subvineale is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
