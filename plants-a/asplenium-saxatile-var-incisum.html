Asplenium saxatile var. incisum

Asplenium saxatile var. incisum, commonly known as the serrated rock fern, is a variant of the rock fern genus Asplenium.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaf margins, which give it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, preferring well-drained, calcareous soils. Its fronds are usually narrow and upright, with a dark green coloration.

The species is native to regions in Europe and Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
