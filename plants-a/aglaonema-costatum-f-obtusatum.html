Aglaonema costatum f. obtusatum

Aglaonema costatum f. obtusatum, commonly known as the 'Variegatum' or 'Variegated Aglaonema', is a hybrid cultivar of the Aglaonema genus.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored foliage featuring shades of white, green, and yellow. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that enhances its ornamental appeal. This plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

It is also known for its air-purifying properties, contributing to its popularity as a houseplant.
