Anemone narcissiflora subsp. sibirica

Anemone narcissiflora subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia. It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically basal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early-flowering plants.
