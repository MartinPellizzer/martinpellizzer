Aristea nigrescens

Aristea nigrescens, commonly known as the blackleaf aristea, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with leaves that are narrow and somewhat fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture.

Aristea nigrescens is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
