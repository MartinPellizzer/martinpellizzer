Aeschynomene mediocris

Aeschynomene mediocris, commonly known as smallleaf aeschynomene, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils. It has been studied for its potential use in forage and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
