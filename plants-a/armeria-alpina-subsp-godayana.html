Armeria alpina subsp. godayana

Armeria alpina subsp.

godayana, commonly known as the alpine thrift, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, typically reaching heights of up to 15 centimeters.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its narrow, linear leaves and showy inflorescences make it a distinctive and hardy species in alpine ecosystems.
