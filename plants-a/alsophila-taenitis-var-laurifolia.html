Alsophila taenitis var. laurifolia

Alsophila taenitis var. laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel fern, is a variant of the giant chain fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach several meters in length, with a dense, leathery texture. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, forming clumps of foliage that thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and glossy, with a distinct, fern-like appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
