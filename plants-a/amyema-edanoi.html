Amyema edanoi

Amyema edanoi, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

Amyema edanoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Indigenous practices.
