Areca lutescens

Areca lutescens, commonly known as the golden areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are often consumed as snacks. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Areca lutescens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.
