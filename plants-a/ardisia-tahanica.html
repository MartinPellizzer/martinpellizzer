Ardisia tahanica

Ardisia tahanica, commonly known as the Tahanica beautyberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Following the flowering period, it produces attractive, dark purple berries that are a key feature of the plant.

These berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
