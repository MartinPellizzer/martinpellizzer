Acacia trinervia

Acacia trinervia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Acacia trinervia is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
