Androsace tibetica var. himalaica

Androsace tibetica var. himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan arete, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
