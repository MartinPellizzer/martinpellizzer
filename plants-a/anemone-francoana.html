Anemone francoana

Anemone francoana, commonly known as the Franco's anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Anemone francoana plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and enhancing the biodiversity of its native habitat.
