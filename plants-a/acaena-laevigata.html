Acaena laevigata

Acaena laevigata, commonly known as the smooth burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive spherical fruits that are covered in hooked bracts, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Acaena laevigata is often found in coastal and lowland areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
