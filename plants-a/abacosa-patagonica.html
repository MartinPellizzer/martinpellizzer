Abacosa patagonica

Abacosa patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the harsh climate of Patagonia. It is often found in open areas, such as grasslands and scrublands, where it thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
