Anthemis wettsteiniana

Anthemis wettsteiniana, commonly known as the Wettstein's anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow ray florets and a central disk. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, aromatic leaves and is often found in coastal or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
