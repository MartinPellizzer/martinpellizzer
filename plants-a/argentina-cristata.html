Argentina cristata

Argentina cristata, commonly known as the crested argentine, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and distinctive, tufted growth pattern that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
