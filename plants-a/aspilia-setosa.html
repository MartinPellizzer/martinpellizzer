Aspilia setosa

Aspilia setosa, commonly known as the hairy aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, yellow or orange composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to yellow florets arranged in a disk-like structure, surrounded by pale green to reddish bracts. Aspilia setosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
