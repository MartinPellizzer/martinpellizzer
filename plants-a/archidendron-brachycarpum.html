Archidendron brachycarpum

Archidendron brachycarpum, commonly known as the Indian bean or small-fruit legume, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several small, elliptical seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant due to its hardy nature and nutritional value.
