Aspidosperma chakense

Aspidosperma chakense, commonly known as the Chakense African cherry, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red or orange berries containing seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
