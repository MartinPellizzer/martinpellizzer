Alnus fruticosa f. pubescens

Alnus fruticosa f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent alder, is a shrub-like form of the alder species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

This form is notable for its adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
