Anthacanthus spinosus

Anthacanthus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny anthacanthus, is a unique flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its botanical features include a woody stem and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
