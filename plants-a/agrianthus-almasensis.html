Agrianthus almasensis

Agrianthus almasensis, commonly known as the Almasan fireplant, is a unique flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is adapted to high altitudes and has a short growing season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
