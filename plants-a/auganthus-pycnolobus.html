Auganthus pycnolobus

Auganthus pycnolobus, commonly known as the dense-leaved agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features robust, grass-like leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences of blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
