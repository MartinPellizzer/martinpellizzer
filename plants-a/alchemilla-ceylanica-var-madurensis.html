Alchemilla ceylanica var. madurensis

Alchemilla ceylanica var. madurensis, commonly known as the Madurensis Lady's Mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are covered in dense, silky white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
