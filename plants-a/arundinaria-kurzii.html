Arundinaria kurzii

Arundinaria kurzii, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or giant bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria kurzii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
