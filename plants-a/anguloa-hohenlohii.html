Anguloa hohenlohii

Anguloa hohenlohii, commonly known as the Hohenlohe's anguloa, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant features a slender, erect stem with few leaves and produces pendulous, spurred flowers that bloom during the cooler months. Anguloa hohenlohii thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil, making it a challenging but rewarding species for cultivation.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
