Anthemis dipsacea

Anthemis dipsacea, commonly known as the Spanish needle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces slender, hairless stems that are often branched and can grow up to 60 centimeters in height. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
