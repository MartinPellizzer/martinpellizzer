Aconitum biflorum var. pluriflorum

Aconitum biflorum var. pluriflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are borne on short pedicels along the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
