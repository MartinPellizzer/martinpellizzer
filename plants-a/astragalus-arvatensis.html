Astragalus arvatensis

Astragalus arvatensis, commonly known as the Italian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Astragalus arvatensis is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
