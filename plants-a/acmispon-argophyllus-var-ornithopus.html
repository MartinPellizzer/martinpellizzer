Acmispon argophyllus var. ornithopus

Acmispon argophyllus var. ornithopus, commonly known as the silverleaf burr medic, is a perennial forage plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs, and its distinctive, spiny seed pods. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for dryland agriculture and erosion control. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions makes it a valuable species for sustainable land management.
