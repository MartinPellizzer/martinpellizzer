Anthurium rivulare

Anthurium rivulare, commonly known as the river anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is a modified leaf. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium rivulare is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
