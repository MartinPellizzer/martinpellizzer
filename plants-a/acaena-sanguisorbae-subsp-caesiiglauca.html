Acaena sanguisorbae subsp. caesiiglauca

Acaena sanguisorbae subsp.

caesiiglauca, commonly known as the blue-green acaena, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand. It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in spring.

The plant produces bristly, spiny fruits that contain seeds, a characteristic feature of the Acaena genus. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a bluish or grayish hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
