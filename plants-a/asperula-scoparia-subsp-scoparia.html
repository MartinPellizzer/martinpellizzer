Asperula scoparia subsp. scoparia

Asperula scoparia subsp.

scoparia, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring.

The plant is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscaping.
