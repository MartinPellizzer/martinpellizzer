Aspilia helianthoides subsp. prieureana

Aspilia helianthoides subsp.

prieureana, commonly known as the African sunflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and large, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its striking appearance and ease of cultivation contribute to its ornamental value.
