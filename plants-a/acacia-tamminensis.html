Acacia tamminensis

Acacia tamminensis, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia tamminensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
