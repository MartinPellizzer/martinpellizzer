Aster × strictus

Aster × strictus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features a distinctive, cone-shaped center surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Aster × strictus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
