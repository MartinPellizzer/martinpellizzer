Astragalus complicatus

Astragalus complicatus, commonly known as the hooked milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Astragalus complicatus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
