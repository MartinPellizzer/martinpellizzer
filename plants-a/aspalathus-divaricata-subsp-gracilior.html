Aspalathus divaricata subsp. gracilior

Aspalathus divaricata subsp.

gracilior, commonly known as the slender bushveld clover, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
