Asparagus mauritianus

Asparagus mauritianus, commonly known as the Mauritius asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in coastal areas or on rocky slopes.

Asparagus mauritianus is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat and is relatively drought-tolerant.
