Acacia cassicula

Acacia cassicula, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a small, shrub-like plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic structures. Acacia cassicula is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
