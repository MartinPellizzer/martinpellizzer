Astragalus merkensis

Astragalus merkensis, commonly known as the Merkens milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its roots are known to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus merkensis is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
