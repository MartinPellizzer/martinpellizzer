Astragalus malaviensis

Astragalus malaviensis, commonly known as Malavi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have ecological significance in its native habitat.

However, it is not widely cultivated or studied for commercial purposes.
