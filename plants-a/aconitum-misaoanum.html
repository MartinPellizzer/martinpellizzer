Aconitum misaoanum

Aconitum misaoanum, commonly known as the Misao Mountain aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces follicles containing toxic alkaloids, which are part of its natural defense mechanism against herbivores. Aconitum misaoanum typically grows in moist, shaded environments at higher altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
