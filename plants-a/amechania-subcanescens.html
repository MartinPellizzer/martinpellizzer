Amechania subcanescens

Amechania subcanescens, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has a low, sprawling growth habit with silver-gray foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically yellow and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Amechania subcanescens is adapted to survive in poor soils and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.
