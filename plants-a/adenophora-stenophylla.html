Adenophora stenophylla

Adenophora stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Adenophora stenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
