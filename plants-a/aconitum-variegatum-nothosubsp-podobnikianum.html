Aconitum variegatum nothosubsp. podobnikianum

Aconitum variegatum nothosubsp.

podobnikianum, commonly known as the Podobnikianum aconite, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive variegated pattern.

This nothosubspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
