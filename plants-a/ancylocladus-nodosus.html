Ancylocladus nodosus

Ancylocladus nodosus, commonly known as the nodding yew, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
