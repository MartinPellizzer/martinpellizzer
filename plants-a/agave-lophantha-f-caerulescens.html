Agave lophantha f. caerulescens

Agave lophantha f. caerulescens, commonly known as the sky blue agave, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky rosette of blue-gray leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces a tall inflorescence with clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Mexican crafts.
