Ataccia aspera

Ataccia aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or pale red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks.

Ataccia aspera is known for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it a common species in arid and semi-arid environments.
