Arrabidaea cuneifolia

Arrabidaea cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrabidaea, is a shrub native to the humid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.

This species is often found in woodland and grassland habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
