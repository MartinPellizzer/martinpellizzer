Acaena pinnatifida subsp. heterotricha

Acaena pinnatifida subsp.

heterotricha, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach or kōwhai, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical use by Māori for food and traditional medicine.
