Asperella longe-aristata

Asperella longe-aristata, commonly known as the long-awned asperella, is a member of the Poaceae family, native to South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically panicle-like, with elongated bracts that give it its common name. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typical of grasses, and it is often found in moist, open habitats.

Asperella longe-aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
