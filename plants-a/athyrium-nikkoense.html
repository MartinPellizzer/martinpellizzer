Athyrium nikkoense

Athyrium nikkoense, commonly known as the Nikko fern, is a striking member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, where it thrives in cool, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, bi-lobed fronds that are often adorned with intricate patterns of sori, which are clusters of spore-producing structures. Its foliage is typically a vibrant green, with a silvery underside that adds to its ornamental appeal.

This fern is prized in horticulture for its elegant appearance and adaptability to moist, well-drained soils.
