Adenophora mikoi

Adenophora mikoi, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Adenophora mikoi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
