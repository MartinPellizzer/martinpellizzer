Acalypha fasciculata var. humbertiana

Acalypha fasciculata var. humbertiana, commonly known as the Humbert's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves that are often bright green with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
