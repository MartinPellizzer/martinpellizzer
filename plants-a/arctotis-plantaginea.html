Arctotis plantaginea

Arctotis plantaginea, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Its flowers are usually bright yellow with a central brown or purple disk, blooming from spring to autumn.

Arctotis plantaginea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
