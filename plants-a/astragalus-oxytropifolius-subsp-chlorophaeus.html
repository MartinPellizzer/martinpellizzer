Astragalus oxytropifolius subsp. chlorophaeus

Astragalus oxytropifolius subsp.

chlorophaeus, commonly known as the green-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves that are typically a vibrant green.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
