Ardisia bracteolata

Ardisia bracteolata, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

The berries are edible for humans but can be toxic in large quantities.
