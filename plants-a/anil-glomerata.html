Anil glomerata

Anil glomerata, commonly known as the clustered anil, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features a compact rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.

Anil glomerata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
