Aster peirsonii

Aster peirsonii, commonly known as the Townsend's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Aster peirsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
