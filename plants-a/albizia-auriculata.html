Albizia auriculata

Albizia auriculata, commonly known as the golden wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are opposite and pinnately arranged, with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 5 meters in height under favorable conditions.

Albizia auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
