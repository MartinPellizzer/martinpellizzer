Astrophytum cataphractum

Astrophytum cataphractum, commonly known as the "cactus mouse-eared cactus," is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with a distinctive globular shape and a thick, fleshy stem that stores water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. Its thick, waxy cuticle helps it retain moisture in harsh desert environments.

Astrophytum cataphractum is well adapted to extreme temperatures and minimal rainfall, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
