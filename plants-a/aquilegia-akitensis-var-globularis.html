Aquilegia akitensis var. globularis

Aquilegia akitensis var. globularis, commonly known as the Akitan Globe Columbine, is a rare and striking plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, spurred petals arranged in a dense, rounded cluster. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

This variety is valued for its unique floral structure and is considered a botanical curiosity due to its limited distribution and ornamental appeal.
