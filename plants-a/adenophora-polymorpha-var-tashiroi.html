Adenophora polymorpha var. tashiroi

Adenophora polymorpha var. tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy, and it produces small, oval berries that ripen to a deep black. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
