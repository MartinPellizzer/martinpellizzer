Allophylus ternatus

Allophylus ternatus, commonly known as the three-toothed alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Allophylus ternatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
