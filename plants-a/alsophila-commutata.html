Alsophila commutata

Alsophila commutata, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive, elongated rhizome that supports its rapid vertical growth. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of several meters.

Alsophila commutata plays an important ecological role in humid forests, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
