Asperula aristata f. floribunda

Asperula aristata f. floribunda, commonly known as the mountain azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its early spring blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
