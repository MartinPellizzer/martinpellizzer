Acanthophyllum tadshikistanicum

Acanthophyllum tadshikistanicum, commonly known as the Tadjikistan spiny dwarf shrub, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and compact growth habit. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its small, usually green or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
