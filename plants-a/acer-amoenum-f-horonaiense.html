Acer amoenum f. horonaiense

Acer amoenum f. horonaiense, commonly known as the Horonai maple, is a unique variant of the Amoenum maple native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves, and it produces small, winged seeds.

This ornamental species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color changes.
