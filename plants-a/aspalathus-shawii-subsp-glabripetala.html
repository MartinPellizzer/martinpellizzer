Aspalathus shawii subsp. glabripetala

Aspalathus shawii subsp.

glabripetala, commonly known as the Cape flax or sweet flax, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers. It is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glabrous (hairless) petals, which give it its specific epithet.
