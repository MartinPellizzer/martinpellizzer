Aldama vernonioides

Aldama vernonioides, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and branched.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
