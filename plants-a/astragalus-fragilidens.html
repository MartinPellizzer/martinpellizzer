Astragalus fragilidens

Astragalus fragilidens, commonly known as the brittle milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are known to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus fragilidens is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments.
