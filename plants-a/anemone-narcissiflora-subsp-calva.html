Anemone narcissiflora subsp. calva

Anemone narcissiflora subsp.

calva, commonly known as the California anemone, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and near streams.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native, ornamental plant.
