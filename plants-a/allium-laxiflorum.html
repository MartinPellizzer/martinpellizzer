Allium laxiflorum

Allium laxiflorum, commonly known as the looseflower garlic.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family, native to the western United States. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are grass-like and form a dense rosette near the ground.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
