Asperula mazanderanica

Asperula mazanderanica, commonly known as Mazanderan azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and attractive, glossy evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the spring season. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Asperula mazanderanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler, moist environments.
