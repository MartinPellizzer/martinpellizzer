Andropogon figarianus

Andropogon figarianus, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in areas with high moisture levels. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon figarianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
