Aloe subalbicans var. acuminata

Aloe subalbicans var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green with a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are often used for their gel, which has mild medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
