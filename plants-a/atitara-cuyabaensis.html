Atitara cuyabaensis

Atitara cuyabaensis, commonly known as the Cuyabana palm, is a rare and unique species native to the rainforests of northern Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to three meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for ecological and conservation studies.
