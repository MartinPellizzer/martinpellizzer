Agrostis maritima subsp. pseudopungens

Agrostis maritima subsp.

pseudopungens, commonly known as the coastal bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to coastal regions. It typically forms dense, mat-like vegetation that helps stabilize sandy or muddy shorelines.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to salty and windy environments, often found in dunes and tidal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important pioneer species in coastal succession.
