Allium petri

Allium petri, commonly known as the petri leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear small, white, star-shaped flowers arranged in umbels. Allium petri is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and modest blooms.
