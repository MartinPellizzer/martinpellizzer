Azalea indica hybrida

Azalea indica hybrida, commonly known as the Indian azalea, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rhododendron genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with dense, leathery leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its blooming period usually occurs in spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.
