Achillea albida

Achillea albida, commonly known as white yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in flat-topped clusters. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, with a slightly bitter scent.

Achillea albida is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
