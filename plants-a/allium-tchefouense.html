Allium tchefouense

Allium tchefouense, commonly known as the Tchefou onion, is a bulbous plant native to the high altitudes of the Tchefou region in Cameroon.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor. Botanically, it is notable for its robust root system and adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Its growth cycle is typically seasonal, with flowering and bulb development occurring during the cooler months.
