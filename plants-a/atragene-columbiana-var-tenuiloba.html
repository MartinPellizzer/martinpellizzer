Atragene columbiana var. tenuiloba

Atragene columbiana var. tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender-leafed mountain-mint, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its lance-shaped leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Atragene columbiana.
