Arabis sudetica

Arabis sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
