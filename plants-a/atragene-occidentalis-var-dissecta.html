Atragene occidentalis var. dissecta

Atragene occidentalis var. dissecta, commonly known as the Western mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitat.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
