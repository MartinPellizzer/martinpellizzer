Astragalus jankae

Astragalus jankae, commonly known as the Janka milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus jankae is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
