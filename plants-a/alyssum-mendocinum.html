Alyssum mendocinum

Alyssum mendocinum, commonly known as Mendocino alyssum, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant has silvery-gray foliage and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Alyssum mendocinum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
