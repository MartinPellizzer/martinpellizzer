Alpinia stenostachys

Alpinia stenostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stalked ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, pale yellow flowers surrounded by bracts.

Alpinia stenostachys is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
