Aster oblongifolius var. orientis

Aster oblongifolius var. orientis, commonly known as the oriental aster, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
