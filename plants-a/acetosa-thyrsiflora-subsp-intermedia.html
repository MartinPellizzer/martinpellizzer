Acetosa thyrsiflora subsp. intermedia

Acetosa thyrsiflora subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate thrysoide, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with the upper leaves being smaller and more oblanceolate.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, terminal clusters, making it a distinctive and ornamental species.
