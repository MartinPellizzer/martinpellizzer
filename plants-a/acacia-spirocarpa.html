Acacia spirocarpa

Acacia spirocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia spirocarpa is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
