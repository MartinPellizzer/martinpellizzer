Anacamptis collina subsp. fedtschenkoi

Anacamptis collina subsp.

fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Alpine Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant produces tall, slender stems bearing a single spike of pale purple to white flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is notable for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
