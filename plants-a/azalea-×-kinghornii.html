Azalea × kinghornii

Azalea × kinghornii, commonly known as the Kinghorn azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in both landscape design and horticultural collections.
