Astragalus diphacus var. peonis

Astragalus diphacus var. peonis, commonly known as the peony milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
