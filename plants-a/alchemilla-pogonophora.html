Alchemilla pogonophora

Alchemilla pogonophora, commonly known as the hairy lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Alchemilla pogonophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
