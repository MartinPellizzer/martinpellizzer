Antirrhinum vexillocalyculatum

Antirrhinum vexillocalyculatum, commonly known as the flagellate snapdragon, is a rare and striking member of the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, flagellate-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is typically found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
