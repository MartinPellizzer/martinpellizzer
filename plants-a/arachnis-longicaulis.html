Arachnis longicaulis

Arachnis longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, contributing to its unique appearance.

Arachnis longicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
