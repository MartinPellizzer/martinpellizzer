Anthurium crassinervium

Anthurium crassinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined anthurium, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, broad veins that give it its common name. The plant produces striking spadix inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium crassinervium is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to purify indoor air.
