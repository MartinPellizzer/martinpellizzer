Artemisia valida

Artemisia valida, commonly known as silver sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid, rocky soils.

Artemisia valida has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for its medicinal properties.
