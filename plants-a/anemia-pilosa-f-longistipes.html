Anemia pilosa f. longistipes

Anemia pilosa f. longistipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed hairy plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or brown in color. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
