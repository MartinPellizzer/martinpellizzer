Agrostis atropurpurea

Agrostis atropurpurea, commonly known as purple false bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall, giving it a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically inconspicuous, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along the edges of woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
