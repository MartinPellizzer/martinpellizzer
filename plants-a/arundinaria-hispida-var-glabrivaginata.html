Arundinaria hispida var. glabrivaginata

Arundinaria hispida var. glabrivaginata, commonly known as the smooth-barked bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous internodes and slender, erect culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
