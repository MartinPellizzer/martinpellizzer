Aster pityphyllus

Aster pityphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, violet or blue flowers in the fall.

Aster pityphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
