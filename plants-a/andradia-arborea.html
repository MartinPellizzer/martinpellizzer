Andradia arborea

Andradia arborea, commonly known as the tree of life, is a tropical evergreen species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, broad leaves that provide shade. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional remedies.

Andradia arborea plays a significant role in local ecosystems, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
