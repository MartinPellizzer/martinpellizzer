Agropyron scabridulum

Agropyron scabridulum, commonly known as the rough wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often have a rough or scabrous texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy surface.

Agropyron scabridulum is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
