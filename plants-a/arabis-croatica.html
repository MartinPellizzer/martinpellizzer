Arabis croatica

Arabis croatica, commonly known as the Croatian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, lobed, and hairy.

Arabis croatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
