Agathis orientalis

Agathis orientalis, commonly known as the Philippine cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, aromatic foliage. The tree produces large, cone-like structures that contain seeds enclosed in woody capsules. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it important in traditional construction and craftsmanship.

The plant also plays a significant ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species.
