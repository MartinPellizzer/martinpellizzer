Antimima minima

Antimima minima, commonly known as the dwarf antimima, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in the early spring. Antimima minima thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
