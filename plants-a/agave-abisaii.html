Agave abisaii

Agave abisaii, commonly known as the Mexican stonecress, is a species of agave native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk bearing small, white flowers, which appear in late summer. Agave abisaii is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important plant in its native habitat.
