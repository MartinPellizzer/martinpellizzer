Acacia discolor var. fraseri

Acacia discolor var. fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
