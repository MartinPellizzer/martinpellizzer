Alchemilla Alpina Subsp. Saxatilis

Alchemilla alpina subsp. saxatilis is a hardy perennial plant native to rocky alpine regions of Europe. It is known for its delicate, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes. Its common name, "Ladies' Mantle," reflects its ability to retain moisture and its historical use in herbal remedies. Despite its modest appearance, this plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and enhancing biodiversity in mountainous ecosystems.
