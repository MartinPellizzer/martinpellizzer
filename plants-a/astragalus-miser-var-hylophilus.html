Astragalus miser var. hylophilus

Astragalus miser var. hylophilus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems, small leaves, and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
