Acer palmatum var. multifidum

Acer palmatum var. multifidum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with split leaves, is a variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like foliage.

It is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade. The plant produces small, red or orange flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in autumn. Its delicate leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, making it a popular ornamental tree.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for with proper pruning and watering.
