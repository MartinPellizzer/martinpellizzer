Amsinckia brandegeeae

Amsinckia brandegeeae, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Amsinckia brandegeeae is often found in rocky areas and is an important species for pollinators in its native range.
