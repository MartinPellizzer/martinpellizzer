Astragalus taipaishanensis

Astragalus taipaishanensis, commonly known as the Taipaishan milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
