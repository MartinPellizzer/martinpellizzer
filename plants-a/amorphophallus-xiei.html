Amorphophallus xiei

Amorphophallus xiei, commonly known as the Chinese giant water chestnut, is a tuberous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, swollen underground stems that store nutrients. The plant produces a striking inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is often brightly colored. Its growth cycle involves a period of dormancy followed by rapid growth during the wet season.

The tubers are edible and have been used in traditional Chinese cuisine for centuries.
