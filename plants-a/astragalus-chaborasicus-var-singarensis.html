Astragalus chaborasicus var. singarensis

Astragalus chaborasicus var. singarensis, commonly known as the Singarensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
