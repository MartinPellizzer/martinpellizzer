Actinodaphne pubescens

Actinodaphne pubescens, commonly known as the hairy actinodaphne, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
