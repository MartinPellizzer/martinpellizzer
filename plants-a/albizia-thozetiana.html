Albizia thozetiana

Albizia thozetiana, commonly known as the African siris, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Albizia thozetiana is often used in reforestation projects due to its ecological benefits and ornamental value.
