Acer pectinatum subsp. maximowiczii

Acer pectinatum subsp.

maximowiczii, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The species is well-suited for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
