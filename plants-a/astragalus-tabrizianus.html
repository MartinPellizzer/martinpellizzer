Astragalus tabrizianus

Astragalus tabrizianus, commonly known as Tabriz milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus tabrizianus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
