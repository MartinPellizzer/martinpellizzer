Antrophyum ovatum

Antrophyum ovatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with aerial roots that help it climb and spread. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the genus.

Antrophyum ovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its lush foliage and unique appearance.
