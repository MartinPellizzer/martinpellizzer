Agropyron alatavicum

Agropyron alatavicum, commonly known as the Altai wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades. The plant typically grows in dry, semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, which are often slightly hairy.

Agropyron alatavicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
