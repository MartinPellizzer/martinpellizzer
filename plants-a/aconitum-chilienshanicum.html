Aconitum chilienshanicum

Aconitum chilienshanicum, commonly known as the Yunnan aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, and its leaves are palmately divided with a waxy surface. Aconitum chilienshanicum is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in high-altitude habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a hazardous plant and should be handled with care.
