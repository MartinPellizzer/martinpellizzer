Aster puniceus subsp. firmus

Aster puniceus subsp.

firmus, commonly known as the fireweed or redstem aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant purple to red flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dry meadows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
