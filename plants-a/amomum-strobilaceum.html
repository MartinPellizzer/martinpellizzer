Amomum strobilaceum

Amomum strobilaceum, commonly known as the star anise plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped fruits that resemble a star, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses. Its essential oils, particularly shikimic acid, are valuable in the production of antiviral medications.

The leaves and rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
