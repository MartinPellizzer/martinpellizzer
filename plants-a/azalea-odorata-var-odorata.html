Azalea odorata var. odorata

Azalea odorata var. odorata, commonly known as the sweet azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its fragrant blossoms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design for its vibrant spring display.
