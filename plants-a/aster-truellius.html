Aster truellius

Aster truellius, commonly known as the slender aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, with a slightly grayish-green hue.

Aster truellius thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.
