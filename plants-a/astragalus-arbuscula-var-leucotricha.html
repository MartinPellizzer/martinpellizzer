Astragalus arbuscula var. leucotricha

Astragalus arbuscula var. leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has finely hairy leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes.
