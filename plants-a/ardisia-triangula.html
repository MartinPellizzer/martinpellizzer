Ardisia triangula

Ardisia triangula, commonly known as the three-angled ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are triangular in shape, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals.

Ardisia triangula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
