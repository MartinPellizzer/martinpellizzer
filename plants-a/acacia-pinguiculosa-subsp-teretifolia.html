Acacia pinguiculosa subsp. teretifolia

Acacia pinguiculosa subsp.

teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across Australia.
