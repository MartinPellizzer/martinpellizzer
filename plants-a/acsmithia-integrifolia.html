Acsmithia integrifolia

Acsmithia integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf acsmithia, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of North America.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. Acsmithia integrifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
