Abutilon rigidum

Abutilon rigidum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
