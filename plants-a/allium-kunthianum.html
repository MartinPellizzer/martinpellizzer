Allium kunthianum

Allium kunthianum, commonly known as the Mexican garlic or Kunth's garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the central and southern regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes other well-known species like onions and garlic. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are grass-like and form a rosette at the base, while its bulbs are small and often used in culinary applications.

Allium kunthianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and mild garlic-like aroma.
