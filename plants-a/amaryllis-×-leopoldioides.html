Amaryllis × leopoldioides

Amaryllis × leopoldioides, commonly known as the leopard lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble leopard spots. The plant produces large, showy blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting black or brown markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated for both its beauty and its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.
