Astragalus lentiginosus var. yuccanus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. yuccanus, commonly known as the Yucca Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
