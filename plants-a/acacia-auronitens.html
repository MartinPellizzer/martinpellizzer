Acacia auronitens

Acacia auronitens, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions of Australia.

Acacia auronitens is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a food source for native wildlife.
