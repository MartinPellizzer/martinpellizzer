Alyssum virescens

Alyssum virescens, commonly known as green alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive silvery-green coloration.

Alyssum virescens is often cultivated as a garden plant for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
