Adenophora divaricata f. angustifolia

Adenophora divaricata f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate beauty and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
