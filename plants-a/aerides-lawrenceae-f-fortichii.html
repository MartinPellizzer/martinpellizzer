Aerides lawrenceae f. fortichii

Aerides lawrenceae f. fortichii, commonly known as the Fortichii orchid, is a rare and elegant orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, providing a lush backdrop for its showy blooms.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
