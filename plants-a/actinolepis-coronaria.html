Actinolepis coronaria

Actinolepis coronaria, commonly known as the Crowned Grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and pond edges, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
