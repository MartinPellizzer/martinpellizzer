Amelanchier cretica var. typica

Amelanchier cretica var. typica, commonly known as the common serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features oval to elliptical leaves that are serrated along the edges. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include smooth bark, alternate leaf arrangement, and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and open woods, contributing to local biodiversity.
