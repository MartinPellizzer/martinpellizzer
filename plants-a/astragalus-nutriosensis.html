Astragalus nutriosensis

Astragalus nutriosensis, commonly known as the Nutrioso milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
