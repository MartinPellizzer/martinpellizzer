Acalypha levinii

Acalypha levinii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a velvety texture, often with a silver or grayish hue. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by three-lobed, maple-like seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Acalypha levinii is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
