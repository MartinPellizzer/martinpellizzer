Annona senegalensis subsp. oulotricha

Annona senegalensis subsp.

oulotricha, commonly known as the African custard apple, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers with a strong fragrance, followed by a pear-shaped fruit with a thick, smooth skin. The fruit contains a creamy, sweet pulp and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
