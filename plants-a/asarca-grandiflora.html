Asarca grandiflora

Asarca grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered asarum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

Asarca grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
