Atriplex lentiformis var. breweri

Atriplex lentiformis var. breweri, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation.

Its salt tolerance makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
