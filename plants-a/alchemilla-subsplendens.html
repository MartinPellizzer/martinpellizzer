Alchemilla subsplendens

Alchemilla subsplendens, commonly known as silverleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady garden settings.

Alchemilla subsplendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
