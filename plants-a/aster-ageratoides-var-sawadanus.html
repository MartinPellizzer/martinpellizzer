Aster ageratoides var. sawadanus

Aster ageratoides var. sawadanus, commonly known as the Sawada's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
