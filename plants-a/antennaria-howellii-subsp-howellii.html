Antennaria howellii subsp. howellii

Antennaria howellii subsp.

howellii, commonly known as Howell's sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and hairy, often silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
