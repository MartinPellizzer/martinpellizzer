Annona mucosa

Annona mucosa, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It produces a distinctive fruit with a rough, greenish skin and a sweet, creamy pulp that is often used in desserts and beverages. The plant has alternate, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its flowers are large, fragrant, and typically white or greenish-white with a yellow center.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns yellow or red, indicating maturity.
