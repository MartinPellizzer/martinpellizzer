Anthurium abelardoi

Anthurium abelardoi, commonly known as the Abelard's flamingo flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spatulate-shaped bracts that can range in color from deep red to orange, often resembling the plumage of a flamingo. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
