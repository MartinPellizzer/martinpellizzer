Astragalus monumentalis var. cottamii

Astragalus monumentalis var. cottamii, commonly known as the Cottam's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization in arid landscapes.
