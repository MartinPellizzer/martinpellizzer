Agropyron spicatum var. tenuispicum

Agropyron spicatum var. tenuispicum, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its fine, wiry stems and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
