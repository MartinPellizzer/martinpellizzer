Ampelocissus arnottiana

Ampelocissus arnottiana, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ampelocissus arnottiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
