Achillea millefolium subsp. pallidotegula

Achillea millefolium subsp.

pallidotegula, commonly known as the pale-leaf yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are aromatic and contain compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly hairy, pale green leaves and a more compact growth form compared to other yarrow species.
