Alnus incana f. americana

Alnus incana f. americana, commonly known as the American shrub alder, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins that are typically male, while female flowers are found on the same plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and forest edges.

This species plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
