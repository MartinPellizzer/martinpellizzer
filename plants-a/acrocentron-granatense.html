Acrocentron granatense

Acrocentron granatense, commonly known as the Granada spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny locations. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species.
