Aglaia racemosa

Aglaia racemosa, commonly known as the fragrant wood-apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in both pharmacological and ecological contexts.

Aglaia racemosa plays an important role in the biodiversity of its native habitats and is often found in lowland forests.
