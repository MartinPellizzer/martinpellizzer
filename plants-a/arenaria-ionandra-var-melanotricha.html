Arenaria ionandra var. melanotricha

Arenaria ionandra var. melanotricha, commonly known as the black-haired sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its stems are often covered with dark, hair-like structures, giving it its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
