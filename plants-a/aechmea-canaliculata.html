Aechmea canaliculata

Aechmea canaliculata, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly covering, which helps it retain moisture. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears showy, red-tipped flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a reddish hue along the edges, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Aechmea canaliculata is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea canaliculata has leaves that are grass-like, arranged in a rosette, with a bluish-green color and a waxy surface, measuring up to 40 cm in length.

The plant lacks a typical stem, instead having a short, fibrous base that supports the rosette of leaves. Its inflorescence is a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small white to pink flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer, pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a bright red berry containing several seeds, which are dispersed by birds and other animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its native habitat.
