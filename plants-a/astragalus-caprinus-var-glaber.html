Astragalus caprinus var. glaber

Astragalus caprinus var. glaber, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
