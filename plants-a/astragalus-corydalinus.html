Astragalus corydalinus

Astragalus corydalinus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Astragalus corydalinus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
