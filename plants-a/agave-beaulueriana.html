Agave beaulueriana

Agave beaulueriana, commonly known as the Mexican giant agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape. The leaves are typically blue-green in color with a waxy texture, adapted to store water efficiently. It produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing numerous white or pale yellow flowers.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in traditional Mexican practices.
