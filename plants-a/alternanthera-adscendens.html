Alternanthera adscendens

Alternanthera adscendens, commonly known as the red veined sorrel, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red veins contrasting with its bright green foliage. This plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often used in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for both aquariums and ponds.
