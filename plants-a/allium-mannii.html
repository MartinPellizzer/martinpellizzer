Allium mannii

Allium mannii, commonly known as the African leek, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Allium mannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic qualities.
