Achyranthes aureus-reticulatus

Achyranthes aureus-reticulatus, commonly known as the golden nettle, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green foliage and reddish stems.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to regions of Africa and the Middle East. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a reticulate venation pattern, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically appearing in shades of green and red.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
