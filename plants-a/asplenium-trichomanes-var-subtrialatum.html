Asplenium trichomanes var. subtrialatum

Asplenium trichomanes var. subtrialatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is a perennial evergreen fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive dark green color and a serrated edge. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
