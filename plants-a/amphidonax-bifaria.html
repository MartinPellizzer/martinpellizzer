Amphidonax bifaria

Amphidonax bifaria, commonly known as the two-leafed grass or false panic grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed bracts.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
