Arachniodes speciosa

Arachniodes speciosa, commonly known as the spider fern, is a striking fern native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, delicate fronds that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread slowly and form dense clumps. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a feathery appearance that enhances its ornamental value.

Arachniodes speciosa thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
