Astragalus saccharensis

Astragalus saccharensis, commonly known as the sugar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.

Astragalus saccharensis is also of interest in botanical research due to its potential medicinal properties.
