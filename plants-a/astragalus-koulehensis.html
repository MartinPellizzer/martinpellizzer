Astragalus koulehensis

Astragalus koulehensis, commonly known as Kouleh milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus koulehensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
