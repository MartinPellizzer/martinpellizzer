Achillea nobilis subsp. ligustica

Achillea nobilis subsp.

ligustica, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features upright, branched stems that bear basal rosettes of aromatic, silver-gray leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellow to pale orange flower heads, which are surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are used in aromatherapy and perfumery.
