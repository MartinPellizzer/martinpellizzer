Acacia brachybotrya var. spilleriana

Acacia brachybotrya var. spilleriana, commonly known as the Spiller's acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, flat, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are typical of many acacia species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
