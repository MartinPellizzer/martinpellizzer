Angelica sylvestris subsp. sylvestris

Angelica sylvestris subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as wild angelica, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and can grow up to 50 cm in length.

The plant produces tall, clustered flower stems with small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
