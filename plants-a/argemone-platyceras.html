Argemone platyceras

Argemone platyceras, commonly known as the broad-lobed prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal practices.
