Aspidium capense

Aspidium capense, commonly known as the Cape shield fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually divided into segments, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Aspidium capense is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with moderate watering.
