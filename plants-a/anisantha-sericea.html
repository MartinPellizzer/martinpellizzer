Anisantha sericea

Anisantha sericea, commonly known as silky meadowgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, wiry stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Anisantha sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
