Acer negundo f. aureonotatum

Acer negundo f. aureonotatum, commonly known as the golden-horned maple, is a unique cultivar of the silver maple (Acer negundo).

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow bark that becomes more pronounced with age. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a vibrant green upper surface. This cultivar is often valued for its ornamental bark, making it a popular choice in landscaping.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is relatively tolerant of urban environments.
