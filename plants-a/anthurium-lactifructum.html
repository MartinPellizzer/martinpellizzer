Anthurium lactifructum

Anthurium lactifructum, commonly known as the milky fruit anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy surface. The plant produces unique spadices, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble a spike of small flowers, often surrounded by a showy, colorful spathe. Its fruit, which gives the plant its common name, is a fleshy, milky-white berry that contains several seeds.

Anthurium lactifructum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
