Adina microcephala var. galpinii

Adina microcephala var. galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's adina, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit and seasonal flowering.
