Amomum heteranthum

Amomum heteranthum, commonly known as the lesser cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The rhizomes and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to true cardamom.
