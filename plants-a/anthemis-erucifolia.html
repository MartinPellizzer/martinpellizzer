Anthemis erucifolia

Anthemis erucifolia, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Anthemis erucifolia is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
