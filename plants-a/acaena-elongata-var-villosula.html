Acaena elongata var. villosula

Acaena elongata var. villosula, commonly known as the hairy spinulose burr, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for native birds. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, hairy texture, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is notable for its ability to form dense thickets, which can play a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.
