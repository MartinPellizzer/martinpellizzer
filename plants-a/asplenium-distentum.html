Asplenium distentum

Asplenium distentum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and often arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, intricate pattern of veins.

Asplenium distentum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor settings.
