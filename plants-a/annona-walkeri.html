Annona walkeri

Annona walkeri, commonly known as the African sugar apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that give way to round, green fruits with a yellowish skin and a sweet, creamy pulp.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
