Astragalus ehrenbergii

Astragalus ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Ehrenberg's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local wildlife by providing nectar for pollinators.
