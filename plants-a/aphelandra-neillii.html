Aphelandra neillii

Aphelandra neillii, commonly known as the Neill's torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves and produces vibrant orange-red bracts that resemble flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.

Aphelandra neillii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its ornate appearance and air-purifying qualities.
