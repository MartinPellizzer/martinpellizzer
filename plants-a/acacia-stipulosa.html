Acacia stipulosa

Acacia stipulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and green. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

Acacia stipulosa is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in native Australian landscapes.
