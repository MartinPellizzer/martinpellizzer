Achillea obscura

Achillea obscura, commonly known as lesser yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, grayish hairs, and its basal leaves are pinnately divided with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Achillea obscura is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Morphology

Achillea obscura has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color, arranged alternately along the stem, and range in size from 2 to 5 centimeters in length.

The stem is upright, slightly hairy, and has a grayish-brown bark that becomes rough with age. Its flowers are small, yellowish-white composite blooms arranged in flat-topped clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer, and are primarily pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, dry achene with a pappus that aids in wind dispersal.

The roots are fibrous and extensive, helping the plant anchor itself in rocky or dry soils.
