Avena fatua var. major

Avena fatua var. major, commonly known as common oat, is a wild grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, with slender, leafy stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color, containing small, oval-shaped grains. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Avena fatua var. major is an annual plant with a fibrous root system and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
