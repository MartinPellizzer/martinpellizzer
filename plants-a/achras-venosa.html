Achras venosa

Achras venosa, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a spiny, woody capsule that contains seeds used for propagation.

Achras venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
