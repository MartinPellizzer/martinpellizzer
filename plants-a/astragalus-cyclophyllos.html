Astragalus cyclophyllos

Astragalus cyclophyllos, commonly known as the cyclophyllos milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus cyclophyllos is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
