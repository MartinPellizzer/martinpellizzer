Acacia delphina

Acacia delphina, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia delphina is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
