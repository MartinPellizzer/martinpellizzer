Arundo aegyptia

Arundo aegyptia, commonly known as the Egyptian reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Nile River Valley.

It can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring hollow, jointed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brown in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its rapid growth and extensive root system make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
