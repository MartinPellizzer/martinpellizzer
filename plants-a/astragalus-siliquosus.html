Astragalus siliquosus

Astragalus siliquosus, commonly known as the silique milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its seed pods, known as silique, are elongated and contain several small, round seeds.

Astragalus siliquosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
