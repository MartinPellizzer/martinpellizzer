Alstonia calophylla

Alstonia calophylla, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow quite tall, reaching up to 20 meters in height, and is well-suited for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
