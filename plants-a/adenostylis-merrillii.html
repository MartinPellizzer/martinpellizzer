Adenostylis merrillii

Adenostylis merrillii, commonly known as the Hawaiian false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
