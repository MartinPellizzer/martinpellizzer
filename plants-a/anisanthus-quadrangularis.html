Anisanthus quadrangularis

Anisanthus quadrangularis, commonly known as the square-stemmed aster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, quadrangular stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Anisanthus quadrangularis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
