Abies durangensis

Abies durangensis, commonly known as the Durango fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Abies, which includes other fir species, and is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dense foliage. The tree has dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays an important role in the local ecosystem.
