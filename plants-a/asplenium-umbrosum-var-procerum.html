Asplenium umbrosum var. procerum

Asplenium umbrosum var. procerum, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Malaysia. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a soft, feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

The varietal form 'procerum' distinguishes it by its longer, more slender fronds compared to the typical variety.
