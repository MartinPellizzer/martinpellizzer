Amaracus akhdarensis

Amaracus akhdarensis, commonly known as the Akhdar Mountains amaracus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is often found growing in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
