Actinidia fasciculoides var. cuneata

Actinidia fasciculoides var. cuneata, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible berries, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.
