Anthericum hockii

Anthericum hockii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in water, adapting to its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, typical of the Araceae family.

Anthericum hockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
