Acacia sphacelata var. sessilis

Acacia sphacelata var. sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, sessile leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
