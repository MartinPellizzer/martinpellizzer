Adiantum pedatum f. aleuticum

Adiantum pedatum f. aleuticum, commonly known as the Aleutian maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace, with a distinctive upward-curving shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brownish, hairy structures.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
