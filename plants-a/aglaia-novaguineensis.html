Aglaia novaguineensis

Aglaia novaguineensis, commonly known as the Madagascar aglaia, is a tropical tree belonging to the Meliaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its ornamental foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant features compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody capsules containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
