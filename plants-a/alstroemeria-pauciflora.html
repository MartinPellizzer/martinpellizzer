Alstroemeria pauciflora

Alstroemeria pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered lily, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces multiple flowers along its stems during the spring and summer months.

Alstroemeria pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
