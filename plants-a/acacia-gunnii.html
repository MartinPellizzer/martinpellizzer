Acacia gunnii

Acacia gunnii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spikes. Acacia gunnii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
