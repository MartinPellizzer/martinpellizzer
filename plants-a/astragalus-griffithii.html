Astragalus griffithii

Astragalus griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia and northern India.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to cream in color, and it produces small, lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus griffithii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
