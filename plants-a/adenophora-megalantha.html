Adenophora megalantha

Adenophora megalantha, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Adenophora megalantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
