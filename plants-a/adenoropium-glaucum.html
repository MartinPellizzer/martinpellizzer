Adenoropium glaucum

Adenoropium glaucum, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Adenoropium glaucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
