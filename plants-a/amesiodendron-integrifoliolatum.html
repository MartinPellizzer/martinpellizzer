Amesiodendron integrifoliolatum

Amesiodendron integrifoliolatum, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically entire, without any serrations or lobes. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.
