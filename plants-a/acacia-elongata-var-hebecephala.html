Acacia elongata var. hebecephala

Acacia elongata var. hebecephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often a deep green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in compact, cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
