Alopecurus pratensis var. arundinaceus

Alopecurus pratensis var. arundinaceus, commonly known as the meadow grass or sweet grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

This variety is often found in meadows, pastures, and wetlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
