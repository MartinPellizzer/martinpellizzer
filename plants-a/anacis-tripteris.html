Anacis tripteris

Anacis tripteris, commonly known as the three-winged grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that have three bracts, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Anacis tripteris is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
