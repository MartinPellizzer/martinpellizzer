Argyrochosma incana

Argyrochosma incana, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikelets, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Argyrochosma incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
