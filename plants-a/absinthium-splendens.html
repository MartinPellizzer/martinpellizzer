Absinthium splendens

Absinthium splendens, commonly known as the golden wormwood, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
