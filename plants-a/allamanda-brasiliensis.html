Allamanda brasiliensis

Allamanda brasiliensis, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and landscaping.
