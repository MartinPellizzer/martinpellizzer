Alopecurus myosuroides subvar. breviaristatus

Alopecurus myosuroides subvar. breviaristatus, commonly known as the narrow-awned mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted, grass-like appearance with narrow, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, meadows, and wetlands. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are wind-pollinated, and it produces small, three-angled seeds.

This subspecies is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
