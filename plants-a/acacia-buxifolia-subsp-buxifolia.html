Acacia buxifolia subsp. buxifolia

Acacia buxifolia subsp.

buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, rounded leaves that resemble boxwood leaves.

The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and as a hedge.
