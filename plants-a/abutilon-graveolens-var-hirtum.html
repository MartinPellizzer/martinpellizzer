Abutilon graveolens var. hirtum

Abutilon graveolens var. hirtum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
