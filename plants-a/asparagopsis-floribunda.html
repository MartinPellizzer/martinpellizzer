Asparagopsis floribunda

Asparagopsis floribunda, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branching, coral-like inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color.

The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.
