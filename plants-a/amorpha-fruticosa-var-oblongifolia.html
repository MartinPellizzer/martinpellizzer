Amorpha fruticosa var. oblongifolia

Amorpha fruticosa var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with oblong, lance-shaped leaflets that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces striking pea-like flowers in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
