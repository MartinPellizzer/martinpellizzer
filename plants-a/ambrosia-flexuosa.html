Ambrosia flexuosa

Ambrosia flexuosa, commonly known as the common ragweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are wind-pollinated. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields. It is known for its allergenic pollen, which can cause severe allergic reactions in humans.

Its life cycle is adapted to thrive in temperate climates with moderate rainfall.
