Achillea moschata var. calcarea

Achillea moschata var. calcarea, commonly known as the sweet yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, flat clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic when crushed, giving it a distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its common name reflects its historical use in perfumery and as a remedy in traditional medicine.
