Acacia vilhelmii

Acacia vilhelmii, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are highly attractive to bees and other pollinators. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often tolerating salt spray and drought.

Acacia vilhelmii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for wildlife.
