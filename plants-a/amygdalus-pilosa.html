Amygdalus pilosa

Amygdalus pilosa, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, tart cherries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Amygdalus pilosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
