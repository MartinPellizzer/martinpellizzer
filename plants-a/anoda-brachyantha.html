Anoda brachyantha

Anoda brachyantha, commonly known as the purpletop wildflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Anoda brachyantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
