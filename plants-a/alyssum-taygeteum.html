Alyssum taygeteum

Alyssum taygeteum, commonly known as the Greek Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Alyssum taygeteum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders.
