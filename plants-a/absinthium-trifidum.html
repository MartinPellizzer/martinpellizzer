Absinthium trifidum

Absinthium trifidum, commonly known as narrow-leaved wormwood, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a distinct, aromatic scent. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of absinthe-like compounds.
