Alyssum orbiculare

Alyssum orbiculare, commonly known as the globe alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features dense, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum orbiculare is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
