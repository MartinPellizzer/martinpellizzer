Adenia tricostata

Adenia tricostata, commonly known as the three-costed adenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often arranged in clusters, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

Adenia tricostata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
