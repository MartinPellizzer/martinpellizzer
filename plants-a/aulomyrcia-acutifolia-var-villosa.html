Aulomyrcia acutifolia var. villosa

Aulomyrcia acutifolia var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy acuminated myrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
