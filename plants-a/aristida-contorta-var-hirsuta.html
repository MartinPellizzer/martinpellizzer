Aristida contorta var. hirsuta

Aristida contorta var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in a panicle structure. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
