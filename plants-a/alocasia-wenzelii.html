Alocasia wenzelii

Alocasia wenzelii, commonly known as the Wenzel's elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a silvery underside. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and a spathe. Alocasia wenzelii thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
