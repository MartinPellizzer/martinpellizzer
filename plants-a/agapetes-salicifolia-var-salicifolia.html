Agapetes salicifolia var. salicifolia

Agapetes salicifolia var. salicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The leaves are evergreen, providing year-round foliage, and the plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in montane forests or cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely used for commercial purposes.
