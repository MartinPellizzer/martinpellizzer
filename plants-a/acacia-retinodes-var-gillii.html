Acacia retinodes var. gillii

Acacia retinodes var. gillii, commonly known as the Gill's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It features small, yellow, globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with a deep root system.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
