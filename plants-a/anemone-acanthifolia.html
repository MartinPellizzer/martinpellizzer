Anemone acanthifolia

Anemone acanthifolia, commonly known as the thornleaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and somewhat hairy. Its flowers are clustered in umbels, with petals that are narrow and pointed, giving it a delicate appearance.

Anemone acanthifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
