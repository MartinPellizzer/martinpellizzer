Agave felgeri

Agave felgeri, commonly known as the Mexican giant agave, is a striking succulent native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It features a thick, spiky rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with numerous white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
