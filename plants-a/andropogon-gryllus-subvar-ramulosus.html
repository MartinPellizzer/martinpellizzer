Andropogon gryllus subvar. ramulosus

Andropogon gryllus subvar. ramulosus, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of native prairie and woodland ecosystems.

Its adaptability and ecological importance make it a valuable plant for restoration and conservation efforts.
