Aristida dregeana

Aristida dregeana, commonly known as the dwarf threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Aristida dregeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
