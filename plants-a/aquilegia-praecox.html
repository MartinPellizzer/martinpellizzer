Aquilegia praecox

Aquilegia praecox, commonly known as the early columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with finely divided lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aquilegia praecox is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
