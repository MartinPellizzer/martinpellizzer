Anthemis cretica subsp. pyrethriformis

Anthemis cretica subsp.

pyrethriformis, commonly known as the pyrethrum-like anthemis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, yellowish to pale green flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

The plant is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its foliage is aromatic and often covered in fine, hairy structures.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Anthemis genus, which are known for their potential medicinal and pesticidal properties.
