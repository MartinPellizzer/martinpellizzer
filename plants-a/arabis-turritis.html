Arabis turritis

Arabis turritis, commonly known as the Turkish rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for rock gardens and alpine displays.
