Aira cespitosa var. uliginosa

Aira cespitosa var. uliginosa, commonly known as sedgegrass, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. This variety is adapted to moist, often waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes and bogs. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.

Its botanical structure reflects its adaptation to aquatic and semi-aquatic conditions.
