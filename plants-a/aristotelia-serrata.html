Aristotelia serrata

Aristotelia serrata, commonly known as the kōwhai tōtara, is a unique New Zealand native plant with distinctive botanical features.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a yellowish-green color, and it is known for its edible berries.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in coastal environments, making it an important part of New Zealand's coastal flora.
