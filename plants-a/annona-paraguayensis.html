Annona paraguayensis

Annona paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a large, spiny fruit with a green, bumpy exterior and a white, juicy pulp containing numerous black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical regions.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, slightly acidic flavor, making it a popular fruit in its native habitat.
