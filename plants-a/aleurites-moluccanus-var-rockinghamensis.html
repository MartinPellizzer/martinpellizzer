Aleurites moluccanus var. rockinghamensis

Aleurites moluccanus var. rockinghamensis, commonly known as the Rockingham nut tree, is a variant of the common nut tree native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its distinctive yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces oval, woody fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, fibrous shell. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential economic uses.

This variety is particularly noted for its adaptability to different soil types and its resilience to certain environmental conditions.
