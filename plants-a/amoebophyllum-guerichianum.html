Amoebophyllum guerichianum

Amoebophyllum guerichianum, commonly known as the Guerich's amoeboid plant, is a unique species belonging to the family Amoebophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its leaf-like structures that resemble the shape of an amoeba, hence its common name. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in temperate regions. Its botanical features include a lack of true roots and a simple, undifferentiated stem.

This rare plant is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and evolutionary significance.
