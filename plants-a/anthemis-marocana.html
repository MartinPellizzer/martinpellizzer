Anthemis marocana

Anthemis marocana, commonly known as Moroccan chamomile, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
