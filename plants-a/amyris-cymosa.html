Amyris cymosa

Amyris cymosa, commonly known as the clustered sand arnoldia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Amyris cymosa is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.
