Ajuga chamaepitys subsp. mesogitana

Ajuga chamaepitys subsp.

mesogitana, commonly known as the Greek bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features creeping rhizomes and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
