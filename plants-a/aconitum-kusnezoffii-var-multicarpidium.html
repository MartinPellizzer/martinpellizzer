Aconitum kusnezoffii var. multicarpidium

Aconitum kusnezoffii var. multicarpidium, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, spire-like stems that bear large, hood-shaped purple-blue flowers, which are characteristic of the genus Aconitum. The plant has palmately divided, dark green leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
