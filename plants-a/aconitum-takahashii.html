Aconitum takahashii

Aconitum takahashii, commonly known as Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its roots are tuberous, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
