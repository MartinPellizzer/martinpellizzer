Acacia turgida

Acacia turgida, commonly known as the stiff acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, pale green or yellowish leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia turgida is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert animals.
