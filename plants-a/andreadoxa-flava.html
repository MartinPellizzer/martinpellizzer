Andreadoxa flava

Andreadoxa flava, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
