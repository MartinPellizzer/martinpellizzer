Acer macrophyllum var. kimballiae

Acer macrophyllum var. kimballiae, commonly known as the Bigleaf maple, is a variant of the Bigleaf maple native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of 6 to 15 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while its fruits are samaras that disperse seeds via wind.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
