Araucaria intermedia

Araucaria intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and drooping branches. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are tightly packed around short shoots.

Araucaria intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
