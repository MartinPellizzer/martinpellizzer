Astragalus austroferganicus

Astragalus austroferganicus, commonly known as the Central Asian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus austroferganicus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
