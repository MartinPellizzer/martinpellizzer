Agrostis vulgaris var. elongata

Agrostis vulgaris var. elongata, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and its tolerance to wet conditions.

This grass is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and role in stabilizing soil.
