Alnus hirsuta f. inokumae

Alnus hirsuta f. inokumae, commonly known as the Japanese alder, is a shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins that are typical of the Betulaeae tribe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or along stream banks.
