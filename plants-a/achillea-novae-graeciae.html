Achillea novae-graeciae

Achillea novae-graeciae, commonly known as the Greek yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing qualities.

Its growth habit is perennial, and it often forms dense mats in its native environment.
