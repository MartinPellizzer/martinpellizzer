Arundinaria kiusiana

Arundinaria kiusiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Kiusian bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a grass-like appearance.

Arundinaria kiusiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
