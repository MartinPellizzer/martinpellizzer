Asperula cyrenaica

Asperula cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Asperula cyrenaica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
