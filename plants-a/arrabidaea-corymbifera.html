Arrabidaea corymbifera

Arrabidaea corymbifera, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
