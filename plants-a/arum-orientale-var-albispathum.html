Arum orientale var. albispathum

Arum orientale var. albispathum, commonly known as the White-veined Arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins against a deep green background. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a showy, spatulate spathe that can be white, yellow, or pink in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage and unique inflorescence.

This variant is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
