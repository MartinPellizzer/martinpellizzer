Alchemilla commutata f. muscoidea

Alchemilla commutata f. muscoidea, commonly known as the mossy lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. The plant features basal leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of moss, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in loose, branched inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
