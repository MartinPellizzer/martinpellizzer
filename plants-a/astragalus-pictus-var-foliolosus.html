Astragalus pictus var. foliolosus

Astragalus pictus var. foliolosus, commonly known as the leafy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in harsh conditions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
