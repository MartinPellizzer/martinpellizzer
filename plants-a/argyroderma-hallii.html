Argyroderma hallii

Argyroderma hallii, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Argyroderma hallii is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
