Astragalus epiglottis subsp. asperulus

Astragalus epiglottis subsp.

asperulus, commonly known as the roughleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
