Adenogyne brachyclada

Adenogyne brachyclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf adenogyne, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters during the cooler months, typically from late autumn to early spring. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Adenogyne brachyclada is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
