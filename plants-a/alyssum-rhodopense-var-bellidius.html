Alyssum rhodopense var. bellidius

Alyssum rhodopense var. bellidius, commonly known as the pink alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
