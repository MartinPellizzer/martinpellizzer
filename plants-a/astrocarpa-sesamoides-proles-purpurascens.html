Astrocarpa sesamoides proles purpurascens

Astrocarpa sesamoides proles purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-veined sesamoid aster, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically purple or pink, with a distinctive central disc. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
