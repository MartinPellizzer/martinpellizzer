Avena barbata subvar. hirsuta

Avena barbata subvar. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stem.

The botanical features of Avena barbata subvar. hirsuta make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its ecological significance.
