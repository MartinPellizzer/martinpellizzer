Actinochloa hirsuta

Actinochloa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, clump-forming growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically has narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes many other grasses.
