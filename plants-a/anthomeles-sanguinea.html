Anthomeles sanguinea

Anthomeles sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and distinctive, often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, thriving in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are short-lived but highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
