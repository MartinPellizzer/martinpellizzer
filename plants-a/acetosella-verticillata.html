Acetosella verticillata

Acetosella verticillata, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves arranged in a whorl, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grassy areas.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild diuretic and astringent properties.
