Apalatoa zeylanica

Apalatoa zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-like bark that is harvested for its medicinal and culinary uses. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

The botanical features of Apalatoa zeylanica make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and the spice trade.
