Artemisia norvegica subsp. globularia

Artemisia norvegica subsp.

globularia, commonly known as the globular tarragon, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe. It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it a valued plant in traditional herbal medicine and horticulture.
