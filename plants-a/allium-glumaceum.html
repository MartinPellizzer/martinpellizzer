Allium glumaceum

Allium glumaceum, commonly known as the leek, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical, hollow leaves that form a rosette near the ground. The plant produces a flower stem topped with a cluster of small, white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbous stem, which is edible, is often cultivated for culinary use.

Allium glumaceum thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular vegetable in many cuisines.
