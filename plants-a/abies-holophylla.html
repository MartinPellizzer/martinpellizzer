Abies holophylla

Abies holophylla, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces cones that are usually cylindrical and contain seeds enclosed in woody structures. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The white fir plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
