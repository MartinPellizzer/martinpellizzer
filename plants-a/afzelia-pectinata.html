Afzelia pectinata

Afzelia pectinata, commonly known as African teak, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Afzelia pectinata is valued for its durable, heavy timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
