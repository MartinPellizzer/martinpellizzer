Argyreia reinwardtiana

Argyreia reinwardtiana, commonly known as the holy night or moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking blue or purple berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
