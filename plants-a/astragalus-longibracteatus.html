Astragalus longibracteatus

Astragalus longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
