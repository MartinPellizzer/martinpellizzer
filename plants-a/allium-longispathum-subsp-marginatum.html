Allium longispathum subsp. marginatum

Allium longispathum subsp.

marginatum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, spherical, purple or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor similar to that of regular onions.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
