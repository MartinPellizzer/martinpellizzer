Aureliana brasiliana

Aureliana brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, purple-pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Aureliana brasiliana is also valued for its role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators.
