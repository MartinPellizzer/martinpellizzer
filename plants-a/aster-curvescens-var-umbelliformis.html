Aster curvescens var. umbelliformis

Aster curvescens var. umbelliformis, commonly known as the Umbellate Aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while the flowers form a rounded cluster at the top of the plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
