Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. arenaria

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

arenaria, commonly known as sand everlasting, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss.

It is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
