Asystasia hispida

Asystasia hispida, commonly known as hairy asystasia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture due to the presence of minute hairs.

Asystasia hispida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
