Aster tripolium var. discoideus

Aster tripolium var. discoideus, commonly known as the coastal aster, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the shoreline. It is adapted to salt spray and harsh coastal conditions, making it an important component of coastal vegetation.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
