Anemone caerulea var. gracilis

Anemone caerulea var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender blue anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, blue to violet flowers with a white center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its graceful appearance and long blooming period.
