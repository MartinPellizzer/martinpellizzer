Alchemilla dzhavakhetica

Alchemilla dzhavakhetica, commonly known as the Caucasian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white to silver hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

Its name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and its resemblance to the alchemical symbols of transformation.
