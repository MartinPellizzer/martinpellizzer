Acsmithia meridionalis

Acsmithia meridionalis, commonly known as the southern acsmithia, is a rare flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a compact growth habit.
