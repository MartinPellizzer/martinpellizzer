Acaciella breviracemosa

Acaciella breviracemosa, commonly known as the narrow raceme acacia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in compact inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to coastal and dry environments makes it a resilient species in tropical regions.

The bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
