Agave lophantha var. brevifolia

Agave lophantha var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of thick, fleshy, and narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces an upright flowering stem with yellowish-green bracts and small, white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in low-maintenance gardens.
