Alsodeia leucoclada

Alsodeia leucoclada, commonly known as white sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xerophytic landscaping.
