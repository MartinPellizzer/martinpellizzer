Acacia sieberiana var. villosa

Acacia sieberiana var. villosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The silky acacia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
