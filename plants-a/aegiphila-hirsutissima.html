Aegiphila hirsutissima

Aegiphila hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Aegiphila hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
