Allium fedtschenkoi

Allium fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Turkestan leek, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in some regions.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local food systems.
