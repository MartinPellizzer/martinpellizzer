Atriplex myriophylla

Atriplex myriophylla, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline and nutrient-poor soils. Its yellow flowers are clustered in panicles and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds.

Atriplex myriophylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
