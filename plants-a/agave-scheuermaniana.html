Agave scheuermaniana

Agave scheuermaniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and thick, narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces a tall flower stalk bearing yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid climates.
