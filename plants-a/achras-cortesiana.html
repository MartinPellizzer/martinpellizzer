Achras cortesiana

Achras cortesiana, commonly known as the "Cortesian palm," is a striking tropical plant native to southern Mexico.

It is a large, solitary tree with a stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves that can reach impressive lengths. The plant produces large, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, red fruits containing numerous seeds. Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions make it a notable species in botanical gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in its native region.
