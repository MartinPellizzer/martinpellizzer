Argyrochosma limitanea subsp. mexicana

Argyrochosma limitanea subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican silverleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its silvery, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
