Astragalus balearicus

Astragalus balearicus, commonly known as the Balearic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly the Balearic Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellow or greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus balearicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
