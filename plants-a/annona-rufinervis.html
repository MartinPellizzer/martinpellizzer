Annona rufinervis

Annona rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are followed by green, hairy fruits resembling apples. The fruit matures to a yellow or orange color and contains a sweet, creamy pulp with a mild, custard-like flavor.

It is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
