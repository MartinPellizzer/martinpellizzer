Aquilegia holmgrenii

Aquilegia holmgrenii, commonly known as the Holmgren's columbine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Aquilegia holmgrenii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
