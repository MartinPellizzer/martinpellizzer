Astragalus thlaspi

Astragalus thlaspi, commonly known as field pea or yellow rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its seed pods are cylindrical and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus thlaspi is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
