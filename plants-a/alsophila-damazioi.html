Alsophila damazioi

Alsophila damazioi, commonly known as the Damazo's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system supports its upright structure, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Alsophila damazioi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
