Actinotus bellidioides

Actinotus bellidioides, commonly known as the bellidifolium starflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Actinotus, which is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals that curve inward, creating a bell-like appearance.

Actinotus bellidioides is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
