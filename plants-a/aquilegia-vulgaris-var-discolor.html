Aquilegia vulgaris var. discolor

Aquilegia vulgaris var. discolor, commonly known as the white avens, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in woodland or shady garden settings, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
