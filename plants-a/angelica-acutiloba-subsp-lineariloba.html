Angelica acutiloba subsp. lineariloba

Angelica acutiloba subsp.

lineariloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved angelica, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces large, umbrella-like compound leaves with finely divided lobes, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in complex inflorescences. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The plant is valued for its aromatic roots and has been used in traditional medicine.
