Aristolochia kaempferi f. longifolia

Aristolochia kaempferi f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This variant is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and intriguing floral structure.
