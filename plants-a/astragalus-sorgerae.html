Astragalus sorgerae

Astragalus sorgerae, commonly known as the Sorger's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across harsh environments.
