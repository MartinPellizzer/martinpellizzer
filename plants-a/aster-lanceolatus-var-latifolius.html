Aster lanceolatus var. latifolius

Aster lanceolatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
