Allophylus crassinervis f. ovatus

Allophylus crassinervis f. ovatus, commonly known as the broad-veined alderleaf, is a variant of the Allophylus crassinervis species.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves with prominent, broad veins, giving it its characteristic appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The botanical features of this plant make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany and ecological research.
