Aspidium falcilobum

Aspidium falcilobum, commonly known as the sickle-lobed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its floating, pinnately divided fronds that resemble the shape of a sickle. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes form dense mats on the water surface, which help in stabilizing the ecosystem.

The fronds are typically green and can vary in size depending on the environmental conditions.
