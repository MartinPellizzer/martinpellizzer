Asparagus burjaticus var. baicalensis

Asparagus burjaticus var. baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal asparagus, is a rare and endemic species native to the Lake Baikal region in Russia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky soils along the shores of Lake Baikal. The plant features slender, spiny stems and small, fern-like leaves that are adapted to the cool, temperate climate of its habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
