Agalinis scarlatina

Agalinis scarlatina, commonly known as scarlet evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically orange to red in color, blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
