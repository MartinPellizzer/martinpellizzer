Alyssum penjwinense

Alyssum penjwinense, commonly known as the Armenian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

Alyssum penjwinense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
