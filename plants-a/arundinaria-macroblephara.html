Arundinaria macroblephara

Arundinaria macroblephara, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or giant bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant is a perennial, clump-forming species that thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its culms are hollow and jointed, typical of the grass family, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Arundinaria macroblephara is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional Chinese medicine.
