Andreettaea aristata

Andreettaea aristata, commonly known as the Andreetta's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions through its extensive root system.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
