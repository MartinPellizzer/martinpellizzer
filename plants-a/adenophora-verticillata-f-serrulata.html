Adenophora verticillata f. serrulata

Adenophora verticillata f. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have finely serrated margins, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
