Astragalus rubescens

Astragalus rubescens, commonly known as the red milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the western United States. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Astragalus rubescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
