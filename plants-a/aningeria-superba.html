Aningeria superba

Aningeria superba, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a straight, often buttressed trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and pinnately veined, while its fruits are woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in urban landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
