Ammannia cinerea

Ammannia cinerea, commonly known as the pale ammannia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

It plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
