Aspidosperma oliganthum

Aspidosperma oliganthum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain a single seed.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
