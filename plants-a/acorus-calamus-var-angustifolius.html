Acorus calamus var. angustifolius

Acorus calamus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved sweet flag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green and slightly glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that emerge from the water surface in early summer. It thrives in shallow water with rich, muddy substrates and is often found in temperate regions.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Acorus genus and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
