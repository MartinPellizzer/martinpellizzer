Aeschynomene nodulosa

Aeschynomene nodulosa, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules, enhancing soil fertility. It is often used in agricultural systems for its ability to improve soil health and as a forage crop.

Aeschynomene nodulosa is also valued for its adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions.
