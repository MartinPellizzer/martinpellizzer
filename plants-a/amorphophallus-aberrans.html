Amorphophallus aberrans

Amorphophallus aberrans, commonly known as the giant swamp arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces a striking, spadix-shaped inflorescence that is often surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense colonies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
