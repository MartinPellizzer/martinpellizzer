Agrimonia eupatoria var. intermedia

Agrimonia eupatoria var. intermedia, commonly known as common agrimony, is a flowering plant in the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
