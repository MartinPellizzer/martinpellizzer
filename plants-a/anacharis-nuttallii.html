Anacharis nuttallii

Anacharis nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's elodea, is a species of aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are often submerged in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are long and ribbon-like, arranged in whorls around the stem.

Anacharis nuttallii is often used in aquariums due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its role in water purification.
