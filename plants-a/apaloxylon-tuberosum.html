Apaloxylon tuberosum

Apaloxylon tuberosum, commonly known as the tuberous apaloxylon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Apaloxylon tuberosum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional uses in local medicine.
