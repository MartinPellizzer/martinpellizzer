Arundinaria elongatifolia

Arundinaria elongatifolia, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along streams or in mountainous regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Arundinaria elongatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
