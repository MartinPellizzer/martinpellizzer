Althaea officinalis subsp. indica

Althaea officinalis subsp.

indica, commonly known as Indian marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including India and the Middle East. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their mucilage content, which has soothing and demulcent properties.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its medicinal applications.
