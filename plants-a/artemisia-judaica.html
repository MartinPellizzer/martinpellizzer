Artemisia judaica

Artemisia judaica, commonly known as Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a robust, upright stem with rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often grayish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite flower heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its tuberous roots are edible and have a sweet, nutty flavor.

Artemisia judaica is also valued for its medicinal properties and resilience to various environmental conditions.
