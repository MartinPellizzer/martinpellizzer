Acer gracilifolium

Acer gracilifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves that are often tinged with red or yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in moist, shady environments and prefers well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
