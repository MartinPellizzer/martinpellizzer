Aster laevis var. laevigatus

Aster laevis var. laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and hairless. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

The smooth aster is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
