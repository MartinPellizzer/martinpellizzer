Acacia salvadorensis

Acacia salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering. It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is often found in savannas and along riverbanks.

Acacia salvadorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
