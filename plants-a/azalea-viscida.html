Azalea viscida

Azalea viscida, commonly known as the sticky azalea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant's leaves are typically leathery and coated with a sticky substance, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Azalea viscida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
