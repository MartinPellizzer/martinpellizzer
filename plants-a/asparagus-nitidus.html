Asparagus nitidus

Asparagus nitidus, commonly known as the silver asparagus or white asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into small, berry-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus nitidus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
