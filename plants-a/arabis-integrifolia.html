Arabis integrifolia

Arabis integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

Arabis integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
