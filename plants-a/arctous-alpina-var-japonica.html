Arctous alpina var. japonica

Arctous alpina var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese rock rose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It has dark green, leathery leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
