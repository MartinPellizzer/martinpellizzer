Arabis perfoliata

Arabis perfoliata, commonly known as the leafy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that often have a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and streambanks.

Arabis perfoliata is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
