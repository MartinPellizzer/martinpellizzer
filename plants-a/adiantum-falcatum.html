Adiantum falcatum

Adiantum falcatum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are finely divided and have a distinctive, lacy appearance. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce new shoots, allowing it to spread gradually.

Adiantum falcatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
