Adenanthos terminalis var. plumosus

Adenanthos terminalis var. plumosus, commonly known as the silky adenanthos, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
