Ammannia sagittifolia var. sagittifolia

Ammannia sagittifolia var. sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically alternate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange or red berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological gardens.
