Andropogon pertusus var. longifolius

Andropogon pertusus var. longifolius, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that are often blue-green in color. This plant thrives in prairie and savanna habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil.

Its flowering spikes are typically slender and drooping, adding to its ornamental value.
