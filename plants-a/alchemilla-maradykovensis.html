Alchemilla maradykovensis

Alchemilla maradykovensis, commonly known as the Maradykov's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
