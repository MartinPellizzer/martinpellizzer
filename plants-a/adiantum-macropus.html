Adiantum macropus

Adiantum macropus, commonly known as the large-veined maidenhair fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green with a glossy texture. The plant features small, sporangia that are clustered along the margins of the fronds, a typical feature of the Adiantum genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.
