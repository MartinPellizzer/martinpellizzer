Acacia dealbata var. mackayana

Acacia dealbata var. mackayana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a variant of the silver wattle species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
