Amelanchier alnifolia subsp. subintegra

Amelanchier alnifolia subsp.

subintegra, commonly known as the Saskatoon serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America. It produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and often used in culinary applications.

The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. It typically grows in a variety of soil types and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The flowers are white and appear in early spring, followed by the showy, red-purple berries in late summer.
