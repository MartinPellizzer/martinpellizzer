Australopyrum pectinatum var. optatum

Australopyrum pectinatum var. optatum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-scented flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems and dark green, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

The golden wattle is a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
