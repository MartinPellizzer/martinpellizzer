Aloysia scorodonioides var. orbicularis

Aloysia scorodonioides var. orbicularis, commonly known as the purple verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular purple or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
