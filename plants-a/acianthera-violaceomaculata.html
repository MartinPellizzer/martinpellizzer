Acianthera violaceomaculata

Acianthera violaceomaculata, commonly known as the violet-spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet and green speckled petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphytic orchid, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
