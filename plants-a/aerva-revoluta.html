Aerva revoluta

Aerva revoluta, commonly known as the Indian spinach or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often grown as a leafy vegetable due to its nutritional value, containing high levels of protein and minerals.

Aerva revoluta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
