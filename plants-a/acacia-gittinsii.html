Acacia gittinsii

Acacia gittinsii, commonly known as the Gittins' wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage and produces small, fluffy pods that contain seeds. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Acacia gittinsii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
