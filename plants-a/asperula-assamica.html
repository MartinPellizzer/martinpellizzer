Asperula assamica

Asperula assamica, commonly known as the Assam hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in terminal umbels and come in shades of pink, red, or white.

Asperula assamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
