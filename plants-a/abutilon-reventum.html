Abutilon reventum

Abutilon reventum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

Abutilon reventum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
