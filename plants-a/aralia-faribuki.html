Aralia faribuki

Aralia faribuki, commonly known as the Japanese angelica tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that are often deeply lobed or divided. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, followed by dark purple berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
