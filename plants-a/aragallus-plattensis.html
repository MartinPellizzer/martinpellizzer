Aragallus plattensis

Aragallus plattensis, commonly known as the Plattens Mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
