Agoseris australis

Agoseris australis, commonly known as the southern agoseris, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Agoseris australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
