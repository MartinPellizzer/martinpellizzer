Acinodendron thomasianum

Acinodendron thomasianum, commonly known as the Thomasian acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
