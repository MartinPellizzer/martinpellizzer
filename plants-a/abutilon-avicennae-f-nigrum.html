Abutilon avicennae f. nigrum

Abutilon avicennae f. nigrum, commonly known as black velvet abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features hairy, ovate leaves that are often dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
