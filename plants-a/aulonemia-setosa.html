Aulonemia setosa

Aulonemia setosa, commonly known as the hairy fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that are often surrounded by hairy, fringed sepals. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Aulonemia setosa is a perennial herb that plays an important role in its ecological habitat.
