Agathisanthemum quadricostatum

Agathisanthemum quadricostatum, commonly known as the four-costate agathisanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a compact cluster. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside its native region.
