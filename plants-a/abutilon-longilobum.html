Abutilon longilobum

Abutilon longilobum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with lobed edges, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Abutilon longilobum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
