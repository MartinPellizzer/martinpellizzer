Acacia collegialis

Acacia collegialis, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are a key feature during its flowering season. Acacia collegialis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
