Amaracarpus macrophyllus

Amaracarpus macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed amara, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
