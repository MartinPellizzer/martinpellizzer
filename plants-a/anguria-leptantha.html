Anguria leptantha

Anguria leptantha, commonly known as the slender cucumber, is a species of vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucumbers, gourds, and melons. The plant produces elongated, thin fruits that are typically harvested when immature for culinary use. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.
