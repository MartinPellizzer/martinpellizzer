Artemisia vulgaris subsp. ludoviciana

Artemisia vulgaris subsp.

ludoviciana, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It is widely used in traditional medicine and ceremonial practices due to its strong scent and purported therapeutic properties.

Its essential oils contain compounds like camphor and thujone, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic uses.
