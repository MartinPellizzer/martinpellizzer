Annona pendula

Annona pendula, commonly known as the hanging custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, which resembles a small, green apple, turns yellow when mature and is edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage and fruit.
