Adenosma glutinosa var. caerulea

Adenosma glutinosa var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue sticky adenosma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that exude a sticky substance when touched. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
