Armodorum calcarata

Armodorum calcarata, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a panicle, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance.

Its fibrous root system helps it survive in poor soils and harsh climatic conditions.
