Acaciella bakeri

Acaciella bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often reduced to spines, a feature adapted to minimize water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in compact clusters, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Acaciella bakeri is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
