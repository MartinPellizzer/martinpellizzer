Astragalus palans

Astragalus palans, commonly known as the Palans milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

Astragalus palans is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
