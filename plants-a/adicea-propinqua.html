Adicea propinqua

Adicea propinqua, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Adicea propinqua is adapted to wetland and moist soil environments, making it a common sight in swampy areas.
