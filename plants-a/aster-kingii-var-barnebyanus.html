Aster kingii var. barnebyanus

Aster kingii var. barnebyanus, commonly known as the Barneby's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its low water requirements.
