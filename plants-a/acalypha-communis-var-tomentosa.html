Acalypha communis var. tomentosa

Acalypha communis var. tomentosa, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a dense covering of fine hairs. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or reddish in color.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
