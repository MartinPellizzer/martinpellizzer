Asphodelus fistulosus var. tenuifolius

Asphodelus fistulosus var. tenuifolius, commonly known as slender-leaved asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems with clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
