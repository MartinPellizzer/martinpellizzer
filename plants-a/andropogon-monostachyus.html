Andropogon monostachyus

Andropogon monostachyus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and dense, cylindrical inflorescence that produces small, spikelet-based flowers. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in restoration projects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
