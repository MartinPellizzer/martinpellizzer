Anemonastrum dichotomum

Anemonastrum dichotomum, commonly known as the split-leaved anemone, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of deeply lobed, silver-green leaves that split into two distinct lobes. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Anemonastrum dichotomum is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it contributes to the local flora's resilience.
