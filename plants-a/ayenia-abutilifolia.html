Ayenia abutilifolia

Ayenia abutilifolia, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Ayenia abutilifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
