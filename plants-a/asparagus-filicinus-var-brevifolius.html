Asparagus filicinus var. brevifolius

Asparagus filicinus var. brevifolius, commonly known as the dwarf fern asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage that forms a dense, low-growing mat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is popular among gardeners seeking a compact, shade-tolerant plant.
