Achillea rozaliae

Achillea rozaliae, commonly known as Rozalia's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.

Its flowering period usually occurs in the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its natural habitat.

Morphology

Achillea rozaliae has basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape, arranged in a rosette, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

The stem is upright, slightly hairy, and has a light green color, growing in a compact, bushy pattern. The flowers are yellow, composed of ray florets and disc florets, blooming from late spring to early summer, and are pollinated by insects such as bees.
