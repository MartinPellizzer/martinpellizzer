Ardisia gracilis

Ardisia gracilis, commonly known as the slender ardisia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture.

Ardisia gracilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
