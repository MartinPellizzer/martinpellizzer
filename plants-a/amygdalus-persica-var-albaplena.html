Amygdalus persica var. albaplena

Amygdalus persica var. albaplena, commonly known as the white-flowered peach.

This variety is a cultivar of the peach tree, characterized by its pale pink to white blossoms that contrast with its green foliage. It produces small, sweet fruits with a mild flavor, often used in both culinary and ornamental contexts. The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
