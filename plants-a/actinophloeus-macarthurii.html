Actinophloeus macarthurii

Actinophloeus macarthurii, commonly known as the Macarthur's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage, which contribute to its structural strength and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in yellow or green hues, while its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Due to its durability and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for timber and as a landscape plant.
