Allium paniculatum var. fuscum

Allium paniculatum var. fuscum, commonly known as the purple garlic.

This perennial bulbous plant is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, branched inflorescence. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valued culinary plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
