Anachortus fasciculatus

Anachortus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats, thriving in cool and moist environments. The plant features clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense rosettes near the ground.

Anachortus fasciculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
