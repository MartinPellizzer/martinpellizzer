Apocynum nerium

Apocynum nerium, commonly known as the dogbane or Indian rope plant, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it unpalatable to livestock and humans.
