Andropogon bombycinus

Andropogon bombycinus, commonly known as the purple bluestem, is a tallgrass prairie species native to the central United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its bluish-green foliage and distinctive purple tassels that appear in the fall. The plant has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

Andropogon bombycinus plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and providing habitat for wildlife.
