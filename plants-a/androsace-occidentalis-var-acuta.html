Androsace occidentalis var. acuta

Androsace occidentalis var. acuta, commonly known as the acute alpine sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically forms dense cushions or mats, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has small, fleshy, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

This variety is well adapted to cold, arid conditions and is often found in meadows and open slopes at high elevations.
