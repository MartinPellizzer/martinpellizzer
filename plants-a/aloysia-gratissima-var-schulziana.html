Aloysia gratissima var. schulziana

Aloysia gratissima var. schulziana, commonly known as sweet verveine, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its essential oils are used in perfumery and aromatherapy due to their pleasant, citrus-like fragrance.
