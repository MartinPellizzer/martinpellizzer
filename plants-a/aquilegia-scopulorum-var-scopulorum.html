Aquilegia scopulorum var. scopulorum

Aquilegia scopulorum var. scopulorum, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred petals and compound leaves with a distinctive palmate shape. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
