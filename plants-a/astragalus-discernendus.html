Astragalus discernendus

Astragalus discernendus, commonly known as the distinguished milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its scientific classification includes the genus Astragalus, which comprises over 3,000 species of flowering plants.
