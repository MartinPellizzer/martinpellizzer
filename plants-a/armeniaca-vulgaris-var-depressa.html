Armeniaca vulgaris var. depressa

Armeniaca vulgaris var. depressa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry.

This variety is a compact shrub with small, oval leaves and produces small, red berries. It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardy nature.

It is also a source of edible fruit, though less commonly cultivated than other cherry varieties.
