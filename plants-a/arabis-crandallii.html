Arabis crandallii

Arabis crandallii, commonly known as Crandall's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often lobed or divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
