Aphania neoebudica

Aphania neoebudica, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in subtropical and temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and requires well-drained conditions.

Aphania neoebudica is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
