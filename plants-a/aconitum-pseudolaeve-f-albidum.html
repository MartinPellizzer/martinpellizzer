Aconitum pseudolaeve f. albidum

Aconitum pseudolaeve f. albidum, commonly known as the white-flowered aconite, is a botanical variant of the aconite genus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pale blue to white flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, tall spikes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a hazardous species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal, though caution is necessary due to its poisonous nature.
