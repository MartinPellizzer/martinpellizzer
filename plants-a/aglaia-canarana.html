Aglaia canarana

Aglaia canarana, commonly known as the Canary tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces woody fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
