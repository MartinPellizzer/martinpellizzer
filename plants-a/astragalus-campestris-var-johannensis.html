Astragalus campestris var. johannensis

Astragalus campestris var. johannensis, commonly known as the Johann's field milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
