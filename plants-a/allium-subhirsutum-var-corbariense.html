Allium subhirsutum var. corbariense

Allium subhirsutum var. corbariense, commonly known as the Corsican garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are often used in traditional Mediterranean cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
