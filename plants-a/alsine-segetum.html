Alsine segetum

Alsine segetum, commonly known as field alsike, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are often lanceolate in shape. The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have five petals. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils.

This plant is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides, where it can become a weed.
