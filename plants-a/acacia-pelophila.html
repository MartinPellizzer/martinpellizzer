Acacia pelophila

Acacia pelophila, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, showing tolerance to salt spray and drought.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
