Asyneuma salicifolium

Asyneuma salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Asyneuma salicifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
