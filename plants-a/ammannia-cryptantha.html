Ammannia cryptantha

Ammannia cryptantha, commonly known as the small bladderworts, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the family Bladderworts.

It is characterized by its small, leafless stems and tiny, bladder-like structures that trap microscopic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or yellowish in color.

Ammannia cryptantha plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
