Achyrophorus spathulatus var. chiloensis

Achyrophorus spathulatus var. chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean false nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, temperate climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy or loamy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
