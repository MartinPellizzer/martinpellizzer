Allium lessingii

Allium lessingii, commonly known as the desert leek, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, bluish-green leaves and produces tall, flowering stems with small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its bulbs are edible when young.

Allium lessingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
