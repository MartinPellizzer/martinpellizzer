Aechmea dichlamydea

Aechmea dichlamydea, commonly known as the pink-tipped bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces a vibrant inflorescence with flowers that range from pink to red, often with contrasting white or yellow accents. Its foliage is typically broad and leathery, with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture.

Aechmea dichlamydea thrives in bright, indirect light and is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental appearance.

Morphology

Aechmea dichlamydea has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, pointed, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy surface.

The plant lacks a typical stem, instead having a short, fibrous root system that anchors it to the substrate. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a berry that turns red when mature, dispersing seeds through animal consumption.

The roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing the plant to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.
