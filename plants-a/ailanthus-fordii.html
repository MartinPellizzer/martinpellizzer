Ailanthus fordii

Ailanthus fordii, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with 10 to 30 pairs of ovate leaflets, giving it a distinctive feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the spring, which are followed by red, fuzzy fruits. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas.

Ailanthus fordii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in urban environments.
