Albizia tanganyicensis

Albizia tanganyicensis, commonly known as the East African siris, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils.

Albizia tanganyicensis is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.
