Acrophorus leucorhachis

Acrophorus leucorhachis, commonly known as the white-stemmed arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by spadices, typical of the arum family. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a starchy food source.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
