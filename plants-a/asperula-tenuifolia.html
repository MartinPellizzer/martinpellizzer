Asperula tenuifolia

Asperula tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender woodruff, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically evergreen and arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces delicate, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
