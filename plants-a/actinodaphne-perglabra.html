Actinodaphne perglabra

Actinodaphne perglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leafed camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and aromatic wood. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, dark brown fruits. Its leaves are typically elliptic to ovate, with a leathery texture and a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
