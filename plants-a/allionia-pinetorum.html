Allionia pinetorum

Allionia pinetorum, commonly known as the Florida flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of swamps. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, with a distinctive flame-like appearance. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Allionia pinetorum plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
