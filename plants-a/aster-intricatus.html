Aster intricatus

Aster intricatus, commonly known as the intricate aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple, typically from late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

The intricate aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
