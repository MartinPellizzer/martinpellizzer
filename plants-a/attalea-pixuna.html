Attalea pixuna

Attalea pixuna, commonly known as the Buriti palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, robust palm with a straight trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange fruits that contain edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The Buriti palm is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
