Acacia × semiaurea

Acacia × semiaurea, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a hybrid shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow floral spikes, which are borne along the stems and are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance and often have a pale golden hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Acacia × semiaurea is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
