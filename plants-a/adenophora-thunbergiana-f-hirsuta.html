Adenophora thunbergiana f. hirsuta

Adenophora thunbergiana f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its distinguishing name. The plant produces bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
