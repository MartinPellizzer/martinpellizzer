Aeschynomene pfundii

Aeschynomene pfundii, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 to 2 meters tall, featuring trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source.

Aeschynomene pfundii is also valued for its role in soil improvement and as a forage crop.
