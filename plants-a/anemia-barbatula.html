Anemia barbatula

Anemia barbatula, commonly known as the barbed anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. Its root system consists of rhizomes, which help it spread and establish colonies in moist, woodland environments.

Anemia barbatula is often found in shaded areas with rich, well-drained soil.
