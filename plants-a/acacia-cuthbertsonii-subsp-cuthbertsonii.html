Acacia cuthbertsonii subsp. cuthbertsonii

Acacia cuthbertsonii subsp.

cuthbertsonii, commonly known as the Cuthbertson's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features slender, twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphology and habitat preferences within the Acacia genus.
