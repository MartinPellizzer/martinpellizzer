Achillea multiflora

Achillea multiflora, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features feathery, aromatic leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Achillea multiflora is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
