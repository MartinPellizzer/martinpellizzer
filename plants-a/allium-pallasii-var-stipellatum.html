Allium pallasii var. stipellatum

Allium pallasii var. stipellatum, commonly known as the Pallas' garlic.

This perennial plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its bulbous structure and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild garlic-like scent.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.
