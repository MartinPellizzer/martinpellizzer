Ardisia brevis

Ardisia brevis, commonly known as the shortleaf evergreen, is a compact shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers during the spring or early summer. Its berries, which are red and fleshy, are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.

Ardisia brevis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.
