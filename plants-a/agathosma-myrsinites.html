Agathosma myrsinites

Agathosma myrsinites, commonly known as sweet-scented agathosma, is a small, aromatic shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its distinctive, sweet, citrus-like fragrance, which is released when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Agathosma myrsinites is also valued for its use in aromatherapy and as a source of essential oils.
