Aira cespitosa subsp. parviflora

Aira cespitosa subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as slender false oat, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils.

The plant forms dense, tufted clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, and it is wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
