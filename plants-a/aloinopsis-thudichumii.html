Aloinopsis thudichumii

Aloinopsis thudichumii, commonly known as the Cape Town aloes, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aloe family (Xanthorrhoeaceae) and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces striking orange to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
