Anthemis salviifolia

Anthemis salviifolia, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features silvery-gray, linear leaves that are aromatic when crushed, contributing to its historical use in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and pleasant fragrance.
