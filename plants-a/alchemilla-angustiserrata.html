Alchemilla angustiserrata

Alchemilla angustiserrata, commonly known as narrow-lobed lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in summer, typically in clusters atop leafless stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla angustiserrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
