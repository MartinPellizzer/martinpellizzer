Acanthopanax gracilistylus var. nodiflorus

Acanthopanax gracilistylus var. nodiflorus, commonly known as the clustered-flowered angel's trumpets, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches, which are typical of the genus Acanthopanax. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories.

The flowers give way to small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
