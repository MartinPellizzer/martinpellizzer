Alyssum hyperboreum

Alyssum hyperboreum, commonly known as the Arctic alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cold climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it is adapted to withstand harsh winter conditions.

Alyssum hyperboreum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
