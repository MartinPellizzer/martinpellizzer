Allium pseudo-ochroleucum

Allium pseudo-ochroleucum, commonly known as the false white leek, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its bulbs are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Allium pseudo-ochroleucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
