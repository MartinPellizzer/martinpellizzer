Alopecurus geniculatus var. salinus

Alopecurus geniculatus var. salinus, commonly known as saltmeadow cordgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often form dense mats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, playing a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils.

Its adaptability to high salt concentrations makes it an important component of salt marsh ecosystems.
