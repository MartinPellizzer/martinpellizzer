Arceuthobium chinense

Arceuthobium chinense, commonly known as the Chinese witchweed, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found primarily in Asia, particularly in China. The plant has a short, leafless stem with small, scale-like leaves that attach to the host plant. Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
