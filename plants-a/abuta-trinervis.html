Abuta trinervis

Abuta trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined abuta, is a climbing shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive three-veined leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Abuta trinervis is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
