Aspalathus mucronata

Aspalathus mucronata, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with numerous small, rounded leaves that are typically red or reddish in color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, dark brown pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspalathus mucronata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
