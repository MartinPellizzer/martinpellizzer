Aronia rotundifolia var. tomentosa

Aronia rotundifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the white chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its oval, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces dark red berries in the fall, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of nutritious fruit.
