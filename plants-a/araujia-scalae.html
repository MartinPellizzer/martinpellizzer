Araujia scalae

Araujia scalae, commonly known as the nightshade vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often opening at night. Its fruit is a large, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or edible purposes.
