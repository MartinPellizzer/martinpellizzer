Artemisia fragrans var. phyllostachys

Artemisia fragrans var. phyllostachys, commonly known as the fragrant artemisia, is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a finely hairy surface that exudes a strong, minty fragrance when crushed. It produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
