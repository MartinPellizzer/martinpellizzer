Alyssum calycinum var. erraticum

Alyssum calycinum var. erraticum, commonly known as the erratic alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and prolonged blooming period.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
