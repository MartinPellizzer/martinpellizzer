Alchemilla alpina var. basaltica

Alchemilla alpina var. basaltica, commonly known as the basalt lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-white foliage that resembles a mantle. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens and alpine displays.
