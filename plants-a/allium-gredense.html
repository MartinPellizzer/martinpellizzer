Allium gredense

Allium gredense, commonly known as the Gredense onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like narcissus and daffodils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, growing from a basal rosette of foliage.

Allium gredense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
