Agropyron lanceolatum

Agropyron lanceolatum, commonly known as the prairie grass or needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Agropyron lanceolatum is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its use in restoration projects.
