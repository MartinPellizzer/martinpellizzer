Acer mono var. taishakuense

Acer mono var. taishakuense, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues in the fall. The plant has palmate leaves with five lobes, and its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its seasonal color changes.
