Andryala × brievaensis

Andryala × brievaensis, commonly known as the hybrid tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Tamaricaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

However, it can become invasive in some areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
