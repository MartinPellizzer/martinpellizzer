Alchemilla tridentata

Alchemilla tridentata, commonly known as three-toothed lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves with three distinct teeth. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla tridentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
