Astragalus onobrychis subsp. dacicus

Astragalus onobrychis subsp.

dacicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
