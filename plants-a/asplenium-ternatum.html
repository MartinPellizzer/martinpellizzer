Asplenium ternatum

Asplenium ternatum, commonly known as the three-lobed spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, compact fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its finely divided, often three-lobed leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions.

Asplenium ternatum is a non-woody plant that reproduces via spores, typical of the fern group.
