Aphanocalyx trapnellii

Aphanocalyx trapnellii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, typically in yellow or orange hues. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
