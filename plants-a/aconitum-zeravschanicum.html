Aconitum zeravschanicum

Aconitum zeravschanicum, commonly known as the Pamir aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and are covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs.

Aconitum zeravschanicum is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
