Achillea crithmifolia var. bulgarica

Achillea crithmifolia var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Balkan yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is attributed to its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
