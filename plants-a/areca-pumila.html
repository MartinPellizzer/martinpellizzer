Areca pumila

Areca pumila, commonly known as the dwarf areca palm, is a compact palm species native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically bright green and somewhat wavy in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Areca pumila is valued for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various soil types.
