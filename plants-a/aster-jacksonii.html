Aster jacksonii

Aster jacksonii, commonly known as Jackson's aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Aster jacksonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
