Aster coridifolius

Aster coridifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

The narrow-leaf aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance to pollinators.
