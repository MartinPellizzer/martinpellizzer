Atriplex monilifera

Atriplex monilifera, commonly known as silverleaf shadscale, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, powdery wax, helping to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, typical of the Chenopodiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly succulent texture.

Atriplex monilifera is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common component of desert ecosystems.
