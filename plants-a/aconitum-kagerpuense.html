Aconitum kagerpuense

Aconitum kagerpuense, commonly known as the Kagerpu monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and dark green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum kagerpuense contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
