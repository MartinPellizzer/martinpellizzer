Anemone mirajabii

Anemone mirajabii, commonly known as the Persian anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, petal-like bracts that resemble true petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a central cluster of stamens that add to its ornamental appeal.

Anemone mirajabii is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, spring-blooming display.
