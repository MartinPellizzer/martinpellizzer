Asclepias pseudosarsa

Asclepias pseudosarsa, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers with distinctive, leaf-like bracts. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, a feature common among milkweeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Asclepias pseudosarsa is an important host plant for monarch butterflies, providing food for their larvae.
