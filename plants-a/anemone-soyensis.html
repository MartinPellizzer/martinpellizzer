Anemone soyensis

Anemone soyensis, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, pink to white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers well-drained soil in partial to full sun.

Anemone soyensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
