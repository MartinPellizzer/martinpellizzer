Astragalus convallarius var. margaretiae

Astragalus convallarius var. margaretiae, commonly known as Margaret's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep taproot system for water absorption. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and are pollinated by insects.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
