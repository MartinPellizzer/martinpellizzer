Agropyron cognatum var. shingoense

Agropyron cognatum var. shingoense, commonly known as the Shingo wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it is valued for its resilience and ability to form dense mats in suitable conditions.
