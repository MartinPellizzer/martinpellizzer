Astragalus dolius

Astragalus dolius, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Astragalus dolius is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas effectively.
