Acer monspessulanum var. turkestanicum

Acer monspessulanum var. turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan maple, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks in temperate regions.
