Acacia sulcata var. planoconvexa

Acacia sulcata var. planoconvexa, commonly known as the flat-convex acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive flat, convex thorns and compound leaves with small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
