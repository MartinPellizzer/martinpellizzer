Agropyron turczaninowii

Agropyron turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Agropyron turczaninowii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
