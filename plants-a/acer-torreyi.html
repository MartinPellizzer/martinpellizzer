Acer torreyi

Acer torreyi, commonly known as the Torrey maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream valleys and forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
