Avena montana var. teretifolia

Avena montana var. teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
