Acacia rostellifera

Acacia rostellifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia rostellifera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
