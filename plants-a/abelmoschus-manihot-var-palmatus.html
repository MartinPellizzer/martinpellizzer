Abelmoschus manihot var. palmatus

Abelmoschus manihot var. palmatus, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow-orange fruits that resemble small pumpkins. The plant is often cultivated for its starchy, edible roots, which are a staple food in many regions. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its ornamental foliage, making it a popular choice in landscaping.
