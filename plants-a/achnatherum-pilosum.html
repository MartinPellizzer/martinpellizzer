Achnatherum pilosum

Achnatherum pilosum, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers.

Blue grama is an important forage plant for livestock and plays a key role in maintaining grassland ecosystems.
