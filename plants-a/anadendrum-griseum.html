Anadendrum griseum

Anadendrum griseum, commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, showy white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring. It is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a long blooming period.
