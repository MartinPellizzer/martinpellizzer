Artemisia czukavinae

Artemisia czukavinae, commonly known as the Czukavina wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in composite inflorescences. Artemisia czukavinae is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
