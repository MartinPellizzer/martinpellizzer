Anthemis tuberculata

Anthemis tuberculata, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in dense clusters, resembling a daisy.

The species is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
