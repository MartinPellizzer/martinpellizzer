Allium cilicicum

Allium cilicicum, commonly known as the Cilician leek, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, flowering stems that bear umbels of white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

Allium cilicicum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
