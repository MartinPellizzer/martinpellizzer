Aristolochia tresmariae

Aristolochia tresmariae, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, intricate, pipe-like flowers. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its vines can grow up to several meters long, often climbing trees or shrubs in its natural habitat.

The plant contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals, making it a subject of both botanical and ecological interest.
