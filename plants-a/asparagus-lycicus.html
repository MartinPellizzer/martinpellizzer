Asparagus lycicus

Asparagus lycicus, commonly known as the desert asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features succulent, jointed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Asparagus lycicus plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
