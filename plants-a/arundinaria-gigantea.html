Arundinaria gigantea

Arundinaria gigantea, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 12 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Arundinaria gigantea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in basket-making.
