Anthericum kilimandscharicum

Anthericum kilimandscharicum, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It features a striking, umbrella-shaped inflorescence with a single spadix surrounded by a large, showy spathe. The plant's leaves are broad, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in forested areas at elevations above 2,000 meters.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
