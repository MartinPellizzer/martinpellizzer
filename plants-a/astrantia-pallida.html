Astrantia pallida

Astrantia pallida, commonly known as pale aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and slender, branched stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps and wet meadows.

Astrantia pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
