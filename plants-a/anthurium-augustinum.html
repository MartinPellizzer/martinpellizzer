Anthurium augustinum

Anthurium augustinum, commonly known as the "Black Velvet" anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that contrast beautifully with its vibrant red spathes. The plant produces clusters of small, spadix-like flowers that are typically surrounded by the colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Anthurium augustinum is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
