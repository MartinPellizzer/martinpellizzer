Alstroemeria hookeri subsp. cummingiana

Alstroemeria hookeri subsp.

cummingiana, commonly known as the Cumming's alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile. It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, strap-like leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
