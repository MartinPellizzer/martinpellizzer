Arachnis flos-aeris

Arachnis flos-aeris, commonly known as the skyflower, is a rare and ethereal plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating flowers that appear to defy gravity, often hovering just above the ground. The plant's botanical structure consists of a short, fibrous stem with clusters of small, white, bell-shaped blooms. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists alike.
