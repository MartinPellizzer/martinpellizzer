Alyssum olympicum

Alyssum olympicum, commonly known as Olympic Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum olympicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
