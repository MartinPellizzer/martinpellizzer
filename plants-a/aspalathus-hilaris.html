Aspalathus hilaris

Aspalathus hilaris, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.

The silver leaf bush is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
