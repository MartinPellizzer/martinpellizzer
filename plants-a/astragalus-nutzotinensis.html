Astragalus nutzotinensis

Astragalus nutzotinensis, commonly known as the Nuttall's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus nutzotinensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
