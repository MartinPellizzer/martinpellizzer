Anthurium boosianum

Anthurium boosianum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the true flowers, typically in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium boosianum is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
