Agrostis flaccida subsp. trinii

Agrostis flaccida subsp.

trinii, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and flat, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
