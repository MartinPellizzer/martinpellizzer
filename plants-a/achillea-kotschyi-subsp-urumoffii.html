Achillea kotschyi subsp. urumoffii

Achillea kotschyi subsp.

urumoffii, commonly known as the Urumoff's yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to its harsh, high-altitude environment.
