Alchemilla sordidicalyx

Alchemilla sordidicalyx, commonly known as the gloomy alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in branched inflorescences, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic properties.

Alchemilla sordidicalyx thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
