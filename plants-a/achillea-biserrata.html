Achillea biserrata

Achillea biserrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance, while its small white to pale yellow flowers bloom in summer.

Achillea biserrata is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat wounds and digestive issues.
