Armeria cariensis var. thessala

Armeria cariensis var. thessala, commonly known as the Thessalian thrift, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of white or pink flowers, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to dry, coastal environments.
