Alsophila serratifolia

Alsophila serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its fronds are compound, with a serrated margin that gives it its common name.

Alsophila serratifolia plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
