Abutilon longicuspe var. hildebrandtii

Abutilon longicuspe var. hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's Abutilon, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, spathaceous flowers that are typically yellow or orange, often with a reddish or purple hue at the base. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
