Aitonia capensis

Aitonia capensis, commonly known as the sour orange, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are sour and not typically eaten by humans.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun.
