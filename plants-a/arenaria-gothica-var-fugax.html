Arenaria gothica var. fugax

Arenaria gothica var. fugax, commonly known as the transient sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

This variant is known for its ephemeral nature, often appearing and disappearing with seasonal changes.
