Anguillaria dioica var. multiflora

Anguillaria dioica var. multiflora, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has large, heart-shaped leaves. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in humid climates with ample sunlight.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
