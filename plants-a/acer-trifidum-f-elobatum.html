Acer trifidum f. elobatum

Acer trifidum f. elobatum, commonly known as the Elongated Three-lobed Maple, is a cultivar of the three-lobed maple, characterized by its distinctive elongated leaves with three lobes.

The leaves are typically longer and more slender than those of the typical species, giving the plant a unique, elegant appearance. It is a deciduous tree or shrub that thrives in temperate climates, often found in gardens and landscapes. The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it displays vibrant red or orange foliage.
