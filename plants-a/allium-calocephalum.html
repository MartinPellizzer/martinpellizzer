Allium calocephalum

Allium calocephalum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, bulbous structure. It is often cultivated for its edible bulbs, which have a mild onion flavor.

Allium calocephalum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.
