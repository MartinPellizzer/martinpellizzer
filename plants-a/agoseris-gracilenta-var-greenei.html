Agoseris gracilenta var. greenei

Agoseris gracilenta var. greenei, commonly known as the Greene's slender agoseris, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
