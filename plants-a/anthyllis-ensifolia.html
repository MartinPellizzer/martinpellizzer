Anthyllis ensifolia

Anthyllis ensifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anthyllis ensifolia is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
