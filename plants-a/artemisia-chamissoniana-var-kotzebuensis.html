Artemisia chamissoniana var. kotzebuensis

Artemisia chamissoniana var. kotzebuensis, commonly known as the Kotzebu's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage composed of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for pollinators.
