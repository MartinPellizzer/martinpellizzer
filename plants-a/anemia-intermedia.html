Anemia intermedia

Anemia intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Anemia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
