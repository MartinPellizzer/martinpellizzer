Astragalus membranaceus f. purpurinus

Astragalus membranaceus f. purpurinus, commonly known as the purple-flowered milkvetch, is a variant of the Chinese milkvetch, characterized by its distinctive purple flowers.

This plant is native to regions of China and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The leaves are typically pinnate, and the plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional Chinese medicine and ecological restoration projects.
