Anthemis rigescens

Anthemis rigescens, commonly known as the rigid fumitory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The rigid fumitory is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
