Arctostaphylos mewukka subsp. mewukka

Arctostaphylos mewukka subsp.

mewukka, commonly known as the Mewukka manzanita, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
