Acaena integerrima var. oligantha

Acaena integerrima var. oligantha, commonly known as the small burr bush, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, spiny foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are clustered in dense, spiky formations called burrs. These burrs are adapted for seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals.

It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
