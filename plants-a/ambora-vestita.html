Ambora vestita

Ambora vestita, commonly known as the clothed ambora, is a unique flowering plant native to the humid tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or near streams. Its botanical features include a short, rhizomatous stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

Ambora vestita is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
