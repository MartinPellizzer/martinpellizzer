Agave verdensis

Agave verdensis, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a species native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are covered in a layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. It produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, greenish-white flowers.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
