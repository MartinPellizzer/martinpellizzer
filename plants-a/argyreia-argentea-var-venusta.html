Argyreia argentea var. venusta

Argyreia argentea var. venusta, commonly known as the silver flower or silver star vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, silver-white petals that resemble stars, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pink. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are dark green and highly ornamental.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
