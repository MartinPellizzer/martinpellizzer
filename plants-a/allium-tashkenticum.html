Allium tashkenticum

Allium tashkenticum, commonly known as the Tashkent onion, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species like garlic and leeks. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting addition to both ornamental and medicinal plant collections.
