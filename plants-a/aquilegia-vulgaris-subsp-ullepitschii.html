Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. ullepitschii

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

ullepitschii, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers, which are typically blue or purple in color.

The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
