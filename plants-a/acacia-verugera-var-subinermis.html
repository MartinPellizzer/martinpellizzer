Acacia verugera var. subinermis

Acacia verugera var. subinermis, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, often sparsely branched stems and produces small, yellow flower spikes during the warmer months. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets, and the plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in sandy soils and is a characteristic component of mallee and woodland habitats.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological systems and as a source of nectar for native pollinators.
