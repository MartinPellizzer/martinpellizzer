Allium proponticum subsp. parviflorum

Allium proponticum subsp.

parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southeastern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are similar in structure to those of other allium species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassy areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary potential in certain regions.
