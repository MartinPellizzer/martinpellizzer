Agrostis hispida var. varians

Agrostis hispida var. varians, commonly known as the variable bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growth patterns and is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its small, spike-like flowers are usually greenish or reddish in color, and it is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Agrostis hispida var. varians is often used in horticulture for erosion control and as a component in ornamental grass mixes.
