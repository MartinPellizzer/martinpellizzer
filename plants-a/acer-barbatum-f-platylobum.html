Acer barbatum f. platylobum

Acer barbatum f. platylobum, commonly known as the flat-lobed maple, is a distinctive cultivar of the maple tree characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

Its leaves are broader and more rounded compared to the typical species, featuring a flattened, almost heart-shaped lobes. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in cooler, humid climates. It produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value, especially for its vibrant autumn foliage that displays shades of red and orange.
