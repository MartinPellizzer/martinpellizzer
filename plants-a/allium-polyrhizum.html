Allium polyrhizum

Allium polyrhizum, commonly known as the bulbous leek, is a member of the Allium genus within the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its clustered, onion-like bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary applications, particularly in Central Asian cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its edible parts and ornamental value.

Allium polyrhizum is also noted for its mild garlic-like flavor and its ability to grow in a variety of soil types.
