Aralia bahiana

Aralia bahiana, commonly known as Brazilian aralia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Aralia bahiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
