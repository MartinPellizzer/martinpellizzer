Abalon albiflorum var. spicatum

Abalon albiflorum var. spicatum, commonly known as the whiteflower abalon, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
