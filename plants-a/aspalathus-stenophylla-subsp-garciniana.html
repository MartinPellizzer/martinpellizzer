Aspalathus stenophylla subsp. garciniana

Aspalathus stenophylla subsp.

garciniana, commonly known as the Natal tea plant, is a small shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is typically found in sandy or rocky soils along the coastal regions of KwaZulu-Natal. It is closely related to the commercially important rooibos plant, Aspalathus linearis.

The subspecies is notable for its potential use in herbal teas and its ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
