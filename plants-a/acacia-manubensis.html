Acacia manubensis

Acacia manubensis, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow or orange globular inflorescences, which are clustered in dense, spherical heads. Its flowers are followed by elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Acacia manubensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
