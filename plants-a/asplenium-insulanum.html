Asplenium insulanum

Asplenium insulanum, commonly known as the island maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to various tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bi-lobed and have a distinctive, finely divided structure.

Asplenium insulanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance.
