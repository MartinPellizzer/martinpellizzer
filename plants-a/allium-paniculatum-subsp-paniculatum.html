Allium paniculatum subsp. paniculatum

Allium paniculatum subsp.

paniculatum, commonly known as the clustered garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features grass-like basal leaves and produces dense, branched inflorescences of white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its small, onion-like bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical structure supports both ornamental value and ecological roles in its native habitat.
