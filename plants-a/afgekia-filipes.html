Afgekia filipes

Afgekia filipes, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the winter months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Afgekia filipes is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
