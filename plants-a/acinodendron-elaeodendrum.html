Acinodendron elaeodendrum

Acinodendron elaeodendrum, commonly known as the African sand dollar, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
