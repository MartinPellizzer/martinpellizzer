Astragalus diversifolius

Astragalus diversifolius, commonly known as the variable leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus diversifolius is also known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
