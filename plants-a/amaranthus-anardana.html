Amaranthus anardana

Amaranthus anardana, commonly known as red amaranth, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its vibrant red to purple foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their nutritional value.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
