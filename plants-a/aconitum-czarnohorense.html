Aconitum czarnohorense

Aconitum czarnohorense, commonly known as the Black Mountain Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with deep lobes and produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.

Morphology

Aconitum czarnohorense has leaves that are compound, typically with 5-7 lanceolate leaflets, arranged alternately along the stem, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, and they are medium in size.

The stem is tall and upright, with a rough, dark brown bark that becomes fissured with age, and it grows in a straight, vertical pattern. The flowers are showy, blue to purple in color, with a spurred petal and arranged in a raceme, blooming in late summer to early autumn, and they are pollinated by insects such as bees. The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are thick, fleshy, and tuberous, adapted for storing nutrients and water.
