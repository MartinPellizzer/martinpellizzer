Aristolochia caudata

Aristolochia caudata, commonly known as the horsewhin, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding growth habit. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and the plant produces small, red berries that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
