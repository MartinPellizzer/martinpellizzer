Argemone grandiflora var. armata

Argemone grandiflora var. armata, commonly known as the armed prickly poppy, is a striking member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers with prominent red centers. Its unique morphology includes a thick, fleshy stem and a capsule-like fruit that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
