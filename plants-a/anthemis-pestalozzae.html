Anthemis pestalozzae

Anthemis pestalozzae, commonly known as the Pestalozza's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
