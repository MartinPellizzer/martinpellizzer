Amelanchier australis

Amelanchier australis, commonly known as the common serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in autumn.

Amelanchier australis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
