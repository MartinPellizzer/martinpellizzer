Anemone obtusiloba subsp. trullifolia

Anemone obtusiloba subsp.

trullifolia, commonly known as the trullifoliate anemone, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has basal leaves that are rounded and hairy, with a distinctive, broad leaf shape. It prefers moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in native plant gardens and ecological studies.
