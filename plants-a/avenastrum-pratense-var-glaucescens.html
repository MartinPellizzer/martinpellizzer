Avenastrum pratense var. glaucescens

Avenastrum pratense var. glaucescens, commonly known as blue meadow grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is characterized by its bluish-green foliage, which gives it its common name.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
