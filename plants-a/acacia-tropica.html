Acacia tropica

Acacia tropica, commonly known as the tropical wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Acacia tropica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
