Asclepias fluviatilis

Asclepias fluviatilis, commonly known as the river milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its showy, clustered flowers are yellow with red markings, attracting various pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

The plant's unique seed pods, known as follicles, contain toxic sap and are adapted for wind dispersal of its seeds.
