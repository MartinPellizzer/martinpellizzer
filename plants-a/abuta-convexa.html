Abuta convexa

Abuta convexa, commonly known as the warty abuta, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Abuta convexa thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
