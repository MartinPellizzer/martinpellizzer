Acronema astrantiifolium

Acronema astrantiifolium, commonly known as the star-leaf acronema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinctive star-like appearance due to the leaf lobes. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside its native region.
