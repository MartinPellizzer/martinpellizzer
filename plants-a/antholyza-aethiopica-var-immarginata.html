Antholyza aethiopica var. immarginata

Antholyza aethiopica var. immarginata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
