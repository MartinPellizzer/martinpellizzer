Acacia campoptila

Acacia campoptila, commonly known as the white wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. Acacia campoptila is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
