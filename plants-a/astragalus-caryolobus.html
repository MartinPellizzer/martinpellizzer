Astragalus caryolobus

Astragalus caryolobus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ecological significance makes it an important species for biodiversity in its native range.
