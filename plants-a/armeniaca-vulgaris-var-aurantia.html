Armeniaca vulgaris var. aurantia

Armeniaca vulgaris var. aurantia, commonly known as the Chinese date or Persian date, is a variant of the Prunus species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruit. The fruit's pulp is edible and has a sweet, sticky texture, while the seeds are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
