Argyreia ankylophlebia

Argyreia ankylophlebia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its botanical significance is linked to its traditional use in religious ceremonies and its role in Ayurvedic medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
