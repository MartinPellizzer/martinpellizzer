Acacia torulosa

Acacia torulosa, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, yellowish-green compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia torulosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand arid conditions.
