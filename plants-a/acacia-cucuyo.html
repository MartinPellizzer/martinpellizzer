Acacia cucuyo

Acacia cucuyo, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, fibrous pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in degraded areas.
