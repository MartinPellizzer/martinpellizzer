Acalypha chamaedrifolia var. brevipes

Acalypha chamaedrifolia var. brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf iron plant, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, three-lobed bracts that give it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate humid conditions.
