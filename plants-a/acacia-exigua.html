Acacia exigua

Acacia exigua, commonly known as the dwarf wattle, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in dense spikes. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous tiny leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Acacia exigua is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
