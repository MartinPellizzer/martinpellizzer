Aidia gardneri

Aidia gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's aidia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Aidia gardneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
