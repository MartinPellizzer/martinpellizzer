Asarum sieboldii f. seoulense

Asarum sieboldii f. seoulense, commonly known as Seoul asarum, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, reddish-brown flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its morphology includes a rhizomatous root system and small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is cultivated in horticultural settings.
