Astragalus flavicomus

Astragalus flavicomus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus flavicomus is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
