Ancipitia londonoi

Ancipitia londonoi, commonly known as the London's ancient fern, is a rare and unique species characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate fronds.

The plant exhibits a complex structure with multiple levels of leaf division, making it visually intricate. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its reproductive system involves spore production, typical of ferns, and it plays a role in forest ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, Ancipitia londonoi is considered a species of conservation concern.
