Arenaria tetraquetra subsp. querioides

Arenaria tetraquetra subsp.

querioides, commonly known as the Querioides sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and sandy soils in arid regions. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly hairy leaves and pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes or mountainous areas.

This plant is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
