Anemone pratensis var. minor

Anemone pratensis var. minor, commonly known as the lesser meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring partial to full shade. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive central cone, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, palmately lobed, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in natural habitats.
