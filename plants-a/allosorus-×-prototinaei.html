Allosorus × prototinaei

Allosorus × prototinaei, commonly known as the hybrid alpine gentian, is a hybrid plant species resulting from the cross between Allosorus alpinus and Allosorus prototinaei.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. This plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, where it prefers well-drained, rocky soils and full sun exposure. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside and a short, stout stem.

Allosorus × prototinaei is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
