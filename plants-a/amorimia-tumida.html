Amorimia tumida

Amorimia tumida, commonly known as the swollen amorphous plant, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, bladder-like leaves that store water, a feature adapted to its arid and seasonal habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the wet season. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Amorimia tumida is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and potential ecological adaptations.
