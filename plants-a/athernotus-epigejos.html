Athernotus epigejos

Athernotus epigejos, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features a low-growing, spreading habit with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters.

Athernotus epigejos has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.
