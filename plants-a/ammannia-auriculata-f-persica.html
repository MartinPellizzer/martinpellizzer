Ammannia auriculata f. persica

Ammannia auriculata f. persica, commonly known as the Persian ammannia, is a variant of the species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its prostrate growth habit and distinctive red to purple stems. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
