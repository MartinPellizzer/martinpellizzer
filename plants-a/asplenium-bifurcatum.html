Asplenium bifurcatum

Asplenium bifurcatum, commonly known as the forked spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that often form a rosette, with the leaf tips splitting into two distinct branches, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or forest floors. Its spores are produced in sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium bifurcatum is relatively easy to cultivate and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
