Acacia pulchella var. cycnorum

Acacia pulchella var. cycnorum, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub native to South Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for local wildlife.
