Artemisia glomerata var. subglabra

Artemisia glomerata var. subglabra, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and aromatic, resinous scent. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a dense covering of fine hairs.

It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.
