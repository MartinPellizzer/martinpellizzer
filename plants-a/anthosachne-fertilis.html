Anthosachne fertilis

Anthosachne fertilis, commonly known as the fertile anthosachne, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It is native to various regions in Asia and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
