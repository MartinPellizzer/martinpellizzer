Allium paniculatum var. excedens

Allium paniculatum var. excedens, commonly known as the giant bulbous onion, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, umbel-like clusters of pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces large, bulbous underground stems that can reach several inches in diameter. Its foliage consists of long, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
