Alyssum saxatile f. variegatum

Alyssum saxatile f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early summer. The foliage is characterized by its variegated leaves, often displaying contrasting green and white or yellow markings. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
