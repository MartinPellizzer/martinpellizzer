Agrostis hyemalis var. tenuis

Agrostis hyemalis var. tenuis, commonly known as slender winter bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 mm in width. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along the edges of wetlands. Its flowering stems are usually short and bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Agrostis hyemalis var. tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its delicate appearance.
