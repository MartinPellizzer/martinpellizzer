Amaryllis belladonna var. minor

Amaryllis belladonna var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a compact, bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red, orange, or yellow, with a subtle fragrance. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

This variety is prized for its ease of care and vibrant blooms, often blooming in late spring or early summer.
