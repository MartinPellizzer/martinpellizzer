Astragalus alopecuroides f. alopecurus

Astragalus alopecuroides f. alopecurus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, typical of many leguminous species. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

This plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
