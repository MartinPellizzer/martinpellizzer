Allionia pilosa

Allionia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy allionia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and are usually pink or white in color.

Allionia pilosa is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or coastal plain areas.
