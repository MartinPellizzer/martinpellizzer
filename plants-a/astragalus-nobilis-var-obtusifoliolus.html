Astragalus nobilis var. obtusifoliolus

Astragalus nobilis var. obtusifoliolus, commonly known as the blunt-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
