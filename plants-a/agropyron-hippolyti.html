Agropyron hippolyti

Agropyron hippolyti, commonly known as the tansygrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Agropyron hippolyti is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
