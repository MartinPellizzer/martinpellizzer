Archidendropsis spicata

Archidendropsis spicata, commonly known as the spicate archidendropsis, is a member of the Santalaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aids in nutrient uptake from the soil. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
