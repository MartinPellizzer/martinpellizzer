Aralia crassifolia vera

Aralia crassifolia vera, commonly known as the broad-leaved aralia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, giving it a distinctive and dramatic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
