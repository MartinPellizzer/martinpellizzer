Aspalathus wittebergensis subsp. oxyclada

Aspalathus wittebergensis subsp.

oxyclada, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like appearance with a stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
