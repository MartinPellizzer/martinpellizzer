Agastache foeniculum

Agastache foeniculum, commonly known as anise hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces spikes of purple to blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic, with a licorice-like scent, and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Agastache foeniculum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
