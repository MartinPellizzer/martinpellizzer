Andropogon wombaliensis

Andropogon wombaliensis, commonly known as the Wombali grass, is a species of grass native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It is noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its potential use in restoration ecology.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems.
