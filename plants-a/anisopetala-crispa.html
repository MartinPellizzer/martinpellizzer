Anisopetala crispa

Anisopetala crispa, commonly known as the crinkled anisopetala, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive crinkled, waxy leaves that give it a textured appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
