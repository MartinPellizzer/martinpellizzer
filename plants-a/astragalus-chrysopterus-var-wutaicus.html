Astragalus chrysopterus var. wutaicus

Astragalus chrysopterus var. wutaicus, commonly known as the Wutaic Mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. It is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its distribution is limited, making it a rare and ecologically significant species.
