Adipe melanopoda

Adipe melanopoda, commonly known as the black-leaved orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with high humidity and partial shade. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
