Amaryllis belladonna var. latifolia

Amaryllis belladonna var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf belladonna lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 feet in length. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with warm climates.
