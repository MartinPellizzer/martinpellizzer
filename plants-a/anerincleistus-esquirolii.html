Anerincleistus esquirolii

Anerincleistus esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones typically appearing on separate plants. Native to Madagascar, it thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
