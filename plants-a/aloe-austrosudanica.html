Aloe austrosudanica

Aloe austrosudanica, commonly known as the Sudanese aloe, is a succulent plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, storing water to help it survive in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Aloe austrosudanica is well adapted to low-water conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
