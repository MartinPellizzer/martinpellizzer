Astomaea galiocarpa

Astomaea galiocarpa, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Florida, the Bahamas, and the Greater Antilles. This plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and its ability to thrive in brackish water environments. The fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by water.
