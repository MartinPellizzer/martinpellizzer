Alposelinum

Alposelinum, commonly known as wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Alposelinum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties and ease of cultivation have made it a popular choice among gardeners and herbalists.
