Anisomeris paniculata

Anisomeris paniculata, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common trait among members of its family. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and ability to store water.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, playing a role in local ecosystems.
