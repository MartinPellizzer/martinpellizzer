Asphodelus microcarpus subsp. nervosus

Asphodelus microcarpus subsp.

nervosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf asphodel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asphodelaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, flowering stalks with small, clustered flowers.

Native to southern Africa, this species thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it blooms during the warmer months of the year.
