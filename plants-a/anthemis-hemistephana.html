Anthemis hemistephana

Anthemis hemistephana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
