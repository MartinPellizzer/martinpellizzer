Agrostis spica-venti var. interrupta

Agrostis spica-venti var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted windgrass, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight bluish-green hue.

The variety 'interrupta' is distinguished by its irregularly spaced flowering structures, giving it a unique appearance among related grasses.
