Aruncus allegheniensis var. pubescens

Aruncus allegheniensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the downy goat's beard, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of white, feathery flowers resembling a beard. The plant has soft, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to gardens and natural habitats.
