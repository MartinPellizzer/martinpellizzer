Amyris simplicifolia

Amyris simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved amyris, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the family Lauraceae and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
