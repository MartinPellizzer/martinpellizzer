Aristolochia ulei

Aristolochia ulei, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
