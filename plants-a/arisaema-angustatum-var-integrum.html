Arisaema angustatum var. integrum

Arisaema angustatum var. integrum, commonly known as the narrowleaf cobra lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features a distinctive, hooded spathe that encloses a spadix, a characteristic feature of the Araceae family. The plant produces striking, often colorful bracts that surround the inflorescence, enhancing its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
