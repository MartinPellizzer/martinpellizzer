Aconitum × paniculatum subsp. degenii

Aconitum × paniculatum subsp.

degenii, commonly known as the Degen's monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet, hood-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with dark green, doubly serrated leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value is highly appreciated in gardens, though it is toxic and should be handled with care.
