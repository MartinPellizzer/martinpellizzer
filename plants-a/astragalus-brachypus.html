Astragalus brachypus

Astragalus brachypus, commonly known as the shortpod milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Astragalus brachypus has a taproot system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions, and it plays a role in soil stabilization in its native habitat.
