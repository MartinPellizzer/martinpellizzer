Abelmoschus angulosus var. grandiflorus

Abelmoschus angulosus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant African eggplant, is a variant of the okra species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, angular, green pods that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant is a herbaceous annual with a bushy growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are typically borne in the axils of the leaves.

The fruit, which matures to a dark purple color, is edible and commonly used in various culinary traditions across Africa and the Middle East.
