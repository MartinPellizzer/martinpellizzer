Agrimonia eupatoria var. japonica

Agrimonia eupatoria var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese agrimony, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often found in woodland or grassy areas. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
