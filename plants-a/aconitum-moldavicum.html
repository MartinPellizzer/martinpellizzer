Aconitum moldavicum

Aconitum moldavicum, commonly known as Moldavian monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
