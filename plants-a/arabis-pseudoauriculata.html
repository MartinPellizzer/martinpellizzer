Arabis pseudoauriculata

Arabis pseudoauriculata, commonly known as the false earleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or divided, often with a distinctive ear-like shape, and small white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It produces capsules as its fruit, containing numerous tiny seeds.

Arabis pseudoauriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
