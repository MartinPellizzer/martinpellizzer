Amomum roseisquamosum

Amomum roseisquamosum, commonly known as the rose-squamate cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are broad, lanceolate, and covered with a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The plant is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other members of the ginger family.
