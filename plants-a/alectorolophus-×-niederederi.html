Alectorolophus × niederederi

Alectorolophus × niederederi, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Alectorolophus hirtus and Alectorolophus niederederi.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
