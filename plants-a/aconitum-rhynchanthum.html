Aconitum rhynchanthum

Aconitum rhynchanthum, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Aconitum rhynchanthum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
