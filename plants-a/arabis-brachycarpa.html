Arabis brachycarpa

Arabis brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-seeded rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal regions. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, while its leaves are pinnately divided.

Arabis brachycarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
