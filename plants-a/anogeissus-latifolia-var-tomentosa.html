Anogeissus latifolia var. tomentosa

Anogeissus latifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry deciduous forests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
