Aster shastensis var. eradiatus

Aster shastensis var. eradiatus, commonly known as the Shasta aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple-blue petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces slender, branching stems that rise above the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
