Acanthopanax pentaphyllus var. variegatus

Acanthopanax pentaphyllus var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated five-leaf arrowhead, is a climbing shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets, which are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
