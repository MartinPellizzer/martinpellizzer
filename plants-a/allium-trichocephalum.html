Allium trichocephalum

Allium trichocephalum, commonly known as the woolly leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, gray-green leaves. The plant produces a single, tall, cylindrical stem that bears a cluster of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbous root system is similar to other alliums, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.

Allium trichocephalum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
