Ancylocladus glaucinus

Ancylocladus glaucinus, commonly known as the glaucous cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces distinctive cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
