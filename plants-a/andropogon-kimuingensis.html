Andropogon kimuingensis

Andropogon kimuingensis, commonly known as African switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its flowering spikes are typically green to reddish in color, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon kimuingensis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
