Amaranthus parisiensis

Amaranthus parisiensis, commonly known as the Paris amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and branching structure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green to red in color, depending on the variety. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Amaranthus parisiensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
