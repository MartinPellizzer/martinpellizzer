Achillea pseudotanacetifolia

Achillea pseudotanacetifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.
