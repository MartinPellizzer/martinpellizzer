Aloe supralaevis var. hanburyi

Aloe supralaevis var. hanburyi, commonly known as the Hanbury's aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green with a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional medicine and skincare products.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for arid climates.
