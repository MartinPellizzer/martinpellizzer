Arabis bourgovii

Arabis bourgovii, commonly known as the Corsican rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Arabis bourgovii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
