Anabasis jaxartica

Anabasis jaxartica, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with gray-green, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in desert and steppe environments.

Anabasis jaxartica is well-suited to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
