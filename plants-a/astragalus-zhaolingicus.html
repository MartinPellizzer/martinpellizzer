Astragalus zhaolingicus

Astragalus zhaolingicus, commonly known as the Zhaoling milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Astragalus zhaolingicus plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local biodiversity in its native habitat.
