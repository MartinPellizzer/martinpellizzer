Amaracarpus epiphyticus

Amaracarpus epiphyticus, commonly known as the epiphytic amaracarpus, is a unique member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. This species is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems, which help it store water in its native arid environments. It typically thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found on tree trunks or branches in shaded, humid areas.

Due to its specialized growth habit and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
