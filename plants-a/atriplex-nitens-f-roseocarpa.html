Atriplex nitens f. roseocarpa

Atriplex nitens f. roseocarpa, commonly known as the rosehip saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, pink to red berries that are clustered in spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and saline environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in suitable climates.
