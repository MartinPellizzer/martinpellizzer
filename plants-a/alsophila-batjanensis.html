Alsophila batjanensis

Alsophila batjanensis, commonly known as the Batjan's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Alsophila batjanensis plays a role in tropical ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
