Albizia maranguensis

Albizia maranguensis, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes. Its pods are flat and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Albizia maranguensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
