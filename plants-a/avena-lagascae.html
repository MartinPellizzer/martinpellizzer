Avena lagascae

Avena lagascae, commonly known as the Lagasce oat, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Avena lagascae thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
