Allophylus crebriflorus

Allophylus crebriflorus, commonly known as the denseflower alder, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and riverine areas. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, fragrant flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, giving it its common name.

The fruit is a small drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, red aril.
