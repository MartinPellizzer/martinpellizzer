Aira koenigii

Aira koenigii, commonly known as black cumin or bishop's weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often grown for its aromatic seeds.

Aira koenigii is also known for its historical use in herbal remedies and its potential health benefits.
