Astragalus wulfenii var. chaubardii

Astragalus wulfenii var. chaubardii, commonly known as Chaubard's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptability to arid conditions. It produces small, yellow to reddish-brown pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native environment.
