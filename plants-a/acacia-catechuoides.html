Acacia catechuoides

Acacia catechuoides, commonly known as the Indian blackwood, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to parts of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Acacia catechuoides is valued for its hard, dark wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of tannins.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
