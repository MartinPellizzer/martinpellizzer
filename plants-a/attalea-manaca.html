Attalea manaca

Attalea manaca, commonly known as the buriti palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, dark green trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that develop into small, clustered fruits. The fruit contains a large, oily seed that is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
