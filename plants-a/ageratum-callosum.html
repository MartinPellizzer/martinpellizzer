Ageratum callosum

Ageratum callosum, commonly known as the woolly ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, lavender to white flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Ageratum callosum is an annual plant that typically blooms from late spring through early autumn.
