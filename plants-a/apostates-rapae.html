Apostates rapae

Apostates rapae, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.
