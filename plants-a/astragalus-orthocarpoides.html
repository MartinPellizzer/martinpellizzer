Astragalus orthocarpoides

Astragalus orthocarpoides, commonly known as the narrowpod milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus orthocarpoides is often found in rocky soils and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
