Alismorkis mexicana

Alismorkis mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican water shield, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Mexico.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the mud. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Alismorkis mexicana plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
