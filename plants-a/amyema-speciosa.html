Amyema speciosa

Amyema speciosa, commonly known as the showy amyema, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its striking, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Amyema speciosa is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
