Aulomyrcia regeliana var. oppositifolia

Aulomyrcia regeliana var. oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved aulomyrcia, is a shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates.

It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.
