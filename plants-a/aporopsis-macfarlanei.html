Aporopsis macfarlanei

Aporopsis macfarlanei, commonly known as the Macfarlane's grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grass species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
