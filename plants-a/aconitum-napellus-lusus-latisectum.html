Aconitum napellus lusus latisectum

Aconitum napellus lusus latisectum, commonly known as the broad-leaved monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are broad and deeply divided. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

It contains potent toxins, making it both a dangerous and fascinating botanical specimen.
