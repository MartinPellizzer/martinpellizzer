Angelica atropurpurea var. occidentalis

Angelica atropurpurea var. occidentalis, commonly known as Western purple angelica, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features large, deeply lobed leaves that are often bluish-green with a purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems with clustered, small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This species thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
