Adesmia parvifolia var. axillaris

Adesmia parvifolia var. axillaris, commonly known as the axillary adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers are typically borne in the axils of the leaves, giving it its specific variety name.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
