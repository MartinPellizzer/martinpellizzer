Andromeda mariana var. angustifolia

Andromeda mariana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wintergreen, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and dark green, providing year-round foliage. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for woodland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
