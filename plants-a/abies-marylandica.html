Abies marylandica

Abies marylandica, commonly known as the Maryland fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are short and stiff. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and release seeds in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The Maryland fir is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region.
