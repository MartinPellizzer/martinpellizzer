Acrotriche ovalifolia var. oblongifolia

Acrotriche ovalifolia var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acrotriche, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid or semi-arid regions.
