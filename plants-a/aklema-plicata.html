Aklema plicata

Aklema plicata, commonly known as the pleated lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pleated petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aklema plicata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
