Antrophyum meniscium

Antrophyum meniscium, commonly known as the spiral cycad, is a unique plant species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a visually striking appearance. The plant produces cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a waxy texture, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Antrophyum meniscium is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts for its survival.
