Astragalus candolleanus

Astragalus candolleanus, commonly known as the Candolle's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
