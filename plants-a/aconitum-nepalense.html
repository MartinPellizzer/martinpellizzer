Aconitum nepalense

Aconitum nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Aconitum nepalense thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

It contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring caution during handling.
