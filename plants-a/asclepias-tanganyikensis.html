Asclepias tanganyikensis

Asclepias tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Tanganyika milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Lake Tanganyika region in East Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers with a distinctive horn-like structure. The plant is part of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae), known for its milky sap and specialized pollination mechanisms. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found near water bodies.

Asclepias tanganyikensis plays a vital role in supporting local ecosystems, particularly as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
