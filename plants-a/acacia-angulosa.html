Acacia angulosa

Acacia angulosa, commonly known as the angular acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular, ridged bark and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact globular heads, which are followed by long, slender pods. It is well adapted to drought and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia angulosa is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
