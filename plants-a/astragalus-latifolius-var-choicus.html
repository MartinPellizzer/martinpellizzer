Astragalus latifolius var. choicus

Astragalus latifolius var. choicus, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in early summer. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
