Asterochlaena platyloba

Asterochlaena platyloba, commonly known as the broadleaf asterochlaena, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in tropical climates.
