Aspidium discolor

Aspidium discolor, commonly known as the variable fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a distinct, almost lace-like appearance.

Aspidium discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
