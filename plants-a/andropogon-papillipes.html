Andropogon papillipes

Andropogon papillipes, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon papillipes plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
