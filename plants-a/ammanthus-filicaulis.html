Ammanthus filicaulis

Ammanthus filicaulis, commonly known as the featherleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, feather-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Ammanthus filicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
