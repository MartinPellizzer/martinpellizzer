Astragalus corrugatus var. brevipes

Astragalus corrugatus var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale green, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its short stature and distinctive corrugated stem, which gives it its scientific name.
