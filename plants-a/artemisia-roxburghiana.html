Artemisia roxburghiana

Artemisia roxburghiana, commonly known as Indian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Artemisia roxburghiana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
