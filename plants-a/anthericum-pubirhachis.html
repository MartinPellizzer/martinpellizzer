Anthericum pubirhachis

Anthericum pubirhachis, commonly known as the hairy thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, relying on insects for pollination.
