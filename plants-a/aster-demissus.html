Aster demissus

Aster demissus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster demissus is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.
