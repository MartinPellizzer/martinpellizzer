Aegiphila grandis var. sessiliflora

Aegiphila grandis var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-leaved aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological gardens and native plant landscapes.
