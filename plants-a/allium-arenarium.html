Allium arenarium

Allium arenarium, commonly known as the sand garlic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale purple or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in certain cuisines.

Allium arenarium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
