Argythamnia catamarcensis

Argythamnia catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarca thornbush, is a shrub native to Argentina, specifically the Catamarca province.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
