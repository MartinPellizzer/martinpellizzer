Anguria bignoniacea

Anguria bignoniacea, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
