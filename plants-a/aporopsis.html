Aporopsis

Aporopsis, commonly known as the golden spike grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plants are characterized by their tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Aporopsis species typically produce small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

These plants are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
