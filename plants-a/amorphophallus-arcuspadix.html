Amorphophallus arcuspadix

Amorphophallus arcuspadix, commonly known as the mountain arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as insects and birds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to thrive in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
