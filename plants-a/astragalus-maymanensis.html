Astragalus maymanensis

Astragalus maymanensis, commonly known as the Mayman's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper underground.

Astragalus maymanensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
