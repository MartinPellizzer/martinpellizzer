Asplenium lamprophyllum

Asplenium lamprophyllum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are adorned with silvery veins, giving it a striking appearance. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The fronds are pinnately compound, with small, ovate pinnae that are often clustered closely together.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in humid climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
