Apocynum polycardium

Apocynum polycardium, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood or the red root, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is toxic and has been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Apocynum polycardium is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its medicinal properties.
