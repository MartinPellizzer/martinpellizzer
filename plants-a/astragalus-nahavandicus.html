Astragalus nahavandicus

Astragalus nahavandicus, commonly known as Nahavand milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Its conservation status remains uncertain due to limited research and habitat degradation.
