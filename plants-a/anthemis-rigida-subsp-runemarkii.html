Anthemis rigida subsp. runemarkii

Anthemis rigida subsp.

runemarkii, commonly known as the rigid fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open areas with well-drained soil. It produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rigid, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
