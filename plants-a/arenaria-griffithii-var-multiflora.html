Arenaria griffithii var. multiflora

Arenaria griffithii var. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high altitudes and cold climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
