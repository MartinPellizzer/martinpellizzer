Auricula integrifolia

Auricula integrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved auricula, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are typically broad and smooth-edged. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a distinctive auricle-like appendage on the petals. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
