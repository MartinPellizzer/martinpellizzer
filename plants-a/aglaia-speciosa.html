Aglaia speciosa

Aglaia speciosa, commonly known as the golden apple or paradise tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that are followed by fruit resembling small, green apples. The fruit turns red when mature and contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
