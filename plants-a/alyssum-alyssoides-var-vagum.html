Alyssum alyssoides var. vagum

Alyssum alyssoides var. vagum, commonly known as the slender alyssum, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
