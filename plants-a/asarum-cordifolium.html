Asarum cordifolium

Asarum cordifolium, commonly known as Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, spicy scent.
