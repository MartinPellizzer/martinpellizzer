Astragalus pascuicola

Astragalus pascuicola, commonly known as the meadow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

Astragalus pascuicola is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
