Arenaria arctica var. rebunensis

Arenaria arctica var. rebunensis, commonly known as the Rebun sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically thrives in rocky, coastal, or sandy environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are usually succulent, helping it retain moisture in nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is often found in alpine and tundra habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
