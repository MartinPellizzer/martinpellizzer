Arisarum simorrhinum

Arisarum simorrhinum, commonly known as the golden arrow, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are glossy and often have a reddish or purplish tinge. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by specific insects, and its growth is typically slow and requires a humid, shaded environment.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
