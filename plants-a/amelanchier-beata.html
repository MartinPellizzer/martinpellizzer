Amelanchier beata

Amelanchier beata, commonly known as the sweet white serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its attractive white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, turning from yellow to red. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall.

Amelanchier beata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
