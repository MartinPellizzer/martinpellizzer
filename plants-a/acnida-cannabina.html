Acnida cannabina

Acnida cannabina, commonly known as the Indian hemp or cannabis plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its dioecious nature, with separate male and female plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy. It is known for its fibrous stalks, which are used in the production of textiles and paper.

The plant also contains psychoactive compounds, making it both economically and pharmacologically significant.
