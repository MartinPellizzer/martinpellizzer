Arctostaphylos hartwegiana

Arctostaphylos hartwegiana, commonly known as Hartweg's manzanita, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
