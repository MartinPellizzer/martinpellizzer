Althaea nudiflora var. froloviana

Althaea nudiflora var. froloviana, commonly known as the naked-flowered marshmallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal gardens.
