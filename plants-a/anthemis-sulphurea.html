Anthemis sulphurea

Anthemis sulphurea, commonly known as sulphur yellow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers, which are typically 1 to 2 inches in diameter. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
