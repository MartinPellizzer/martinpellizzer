Acer truncatum var. beipiao

Acer truncatum var. beipiao, commonly known as the Beipiao maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
