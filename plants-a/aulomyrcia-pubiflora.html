Aulomyrcia pubiflora

Aulomyrcia pubiflora, commonly known as the pubiflorous myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known genera such as Eucalyptus and Syzygium. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
