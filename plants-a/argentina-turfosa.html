Argentina turfosa

Argentina turfosa, commonly known as the Argentine grass, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage and rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to form thick mats. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest to landscapes.

Argentina turfosa is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and moderate grazing.
