Achyranthemum recurvatum

Achyranthemum recurvatum, commonly known as the curly top groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, yellowish-orange fruits that are edible. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It belongs to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.

The flowers are typically pale green to white, with five petals and a distinctive tubular shape.
