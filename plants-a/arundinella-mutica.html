Arundinella mutica

Arundinella mutica, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, hollow stems that resemble bamboo. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain environments.
