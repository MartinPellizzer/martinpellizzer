Acaena tridactyla

Acaena tridactyla, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acaena tridactyla is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it an important component of New Zealand's native flora.
