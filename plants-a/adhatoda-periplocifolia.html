Adhatoda periplocifolia

Adhatoda periplocifolia, commonly known as Indian Trumpet or Kachnar, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Adhatoda periplocifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
