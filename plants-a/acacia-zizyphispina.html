Acacia zizyphispina

Acacia zizyphispina, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in winter, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for reclamation of degraded lands.

The plant has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
