Acacia erythrocephala

Acacia erythrocephala, commonly known as the red-headed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and distinctive, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in globular inflorescences, which are a notable feature. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices.

Acacia erythrocephala is adapted to arid environments and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.
