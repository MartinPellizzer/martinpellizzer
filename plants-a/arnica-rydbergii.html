Arnica rydbergii

Arnica rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's arnica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats such as meadows and alpine regions. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Arnica rydbergii is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
