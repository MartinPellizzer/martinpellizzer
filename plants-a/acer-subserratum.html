Acer subserratum

Acer subserratum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its attractive, palmate leaves that are typically red or orange in the spring and summer, turning yellow or red in the fall. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

Acer subserratum is known for its slow growth and ability to tolerate urban environments, making it a versatile and aesthetically pleasing addition to landscapes.
