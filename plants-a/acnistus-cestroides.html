Acnistus cestroides

Acnistus cestroides, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a sprawling growth habit with hairy, often reddish stems and alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades, and is characterized by its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.
