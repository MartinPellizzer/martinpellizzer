Artemisia camphorata proles saxatilis

Artemisia camphorata proles saxatilis, commonly known as the stone Artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to arid and rocky regions of the Mediterranean.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to dry climates and poor soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its essential oils contain compounds like camphor and thujone, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic uses.
