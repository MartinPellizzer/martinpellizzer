Astragalus ibrahimianus

Astragalus ibrahimianus, commonly known as the Ibrahimian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and may contribute to local biodiversity in its native habitats.

However, it is not widely cultivated or studied compared to other Astragalus species.
