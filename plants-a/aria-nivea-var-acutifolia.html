Aria nivea var. acutifolia

Aria nivea var. acutifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf aralia, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels during the spring.

The varietal form, acutifolia, is distinguished by its sharply pointed leaflets.
