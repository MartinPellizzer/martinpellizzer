Aristida grisebachiana var. decolorata

Aristida grisebachiana var. decolorata, commonly known as the gray-haired wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a grayish or bluish hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
