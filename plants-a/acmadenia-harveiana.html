Acmadenia harveiana

Acmadenia harveiana, commonly known as the Harveian acmada, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Acmadenia harveiana is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathland and coastal scrub environments.
