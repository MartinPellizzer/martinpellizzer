Arundinaria maling

Arundinaria maling, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and allow it to spread rapidly through underground growth.

Arundinaria maling is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
