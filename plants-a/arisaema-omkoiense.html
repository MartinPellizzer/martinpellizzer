Arisaema omkoiense

Arisaema omkoiense, commonly known as the Himalayan cobra lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The spadix produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
