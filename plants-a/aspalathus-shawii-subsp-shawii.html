Aspalathus shawii subsp. shawii

Aspalathus shawii subsp.

shawii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant is adapted to grow in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in local horticulture.
