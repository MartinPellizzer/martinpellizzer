Aspalathus dianthopora

Aspalathus dianthopora, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often used in landscaping and garden design.
