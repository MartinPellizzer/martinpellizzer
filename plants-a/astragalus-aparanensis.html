Astragalus aparanensis

Astragalus aparanensis, commonly known as the Aparan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus aparanensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
