Abelia engleriana

Abelia engleriana, commonly known as Engler's abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy beneath, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and prolonged flowering period.
