Amphiodon effusus

Amphiodon effusus, commonly known as the hairy sea anemone, is a marine cnidarian found in shallow coastal waters.

It has a distinctive appearance with a soft, fleshy body covered in long, delicate tentacles. The plant, or more accurately the organism, exhibits a colonial structure with a central gastrovascular cavity. Its tentacles are equipped with nematocysts for capturing prey.

Amphiodon effusus plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as prey for various marine animals.
