Anthurium alatum

Anthurium alatum, commonly known as the "Painted tongue" or "Aluminum plant," is a tropical evergreen perennial native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often green with red or purple veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant spadices, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble a spatula-shaped flower, typically in shades of red or yellow. Anthurium alatum thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
