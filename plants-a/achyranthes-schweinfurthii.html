Achyranthes schweinfurthii

Achyranthes schweinfurthii, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
