Acacia aphanoclada

Acacia aphanoclada, commonly known as the ghost acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in spikes. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with a deep root system and low water requirements.

Acacia aphanoclada is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.
