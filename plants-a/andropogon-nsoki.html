Andropogon nsoki

Andropogon nsoki, commonly known as the African bluestem, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon nsoki plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock in certain regions.
