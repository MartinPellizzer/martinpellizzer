Angraecum liliodorum

Angraecum liliodorum, commonly known as the Liliodorum orchid, is a rare and striking species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescence and large, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments. Its flowers typically bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
