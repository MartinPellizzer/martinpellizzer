Aconitum fischeri

Aconitum fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's monkshood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a distinctive veining pattern.

Aconitum fischeri contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
