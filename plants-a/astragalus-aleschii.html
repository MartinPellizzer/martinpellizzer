Astragalus aleschii

Astragalus aleschii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Astragalus aleschii is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its ecological importance is enhanced by its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
