Alternanthera radicata

Alternanthera radicata, commonly known as redstem alternarthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often bright green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pink flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and aquatic environments.
