Acacia buchananii

Acacia buchananii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads that appear in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia buchananii is also valued for its hard wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
