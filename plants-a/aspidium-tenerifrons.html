Aspidium tenerifrons

Aspidium tenerifrons, commonly known as the Canary Island water fern, is a species of fern native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. Its spores are released from sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium tenerifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
