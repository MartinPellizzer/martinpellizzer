Allium bolanderi

Allium bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as chaparral and coastal scrub. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Allium bolanderi is also noted for its edible bulbs, which have a mild, garlic-like flavor.
