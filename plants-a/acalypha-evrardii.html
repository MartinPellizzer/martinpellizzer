Acalypha evrardii

Acalypha evrardii, commonly known as the red-hot catshrub, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha evrardii thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
