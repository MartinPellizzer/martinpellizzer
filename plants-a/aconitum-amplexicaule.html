Aconitum amplexicaule

Aconitum amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant features large, palmate leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland areas and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Aconitum amplexicaule is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
