Asphodelus albus var. rouxii

Asphodelus albus var. rouxii, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stalks with white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands.

Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
