Acacia jasperensis

Acacia jasperensis, commonly known as the Jasper acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching habit and phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia jasperensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
