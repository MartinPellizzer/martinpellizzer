Aristolochia emiliae

Aristolochia emiliae, commonly known as the Emilia's Dutchman's pipe, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often maroon or purple in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique floral structure, it plays an important role in pollination by specific insect species.
