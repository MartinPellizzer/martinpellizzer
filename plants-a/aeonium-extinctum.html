Aeonium extinctum

Aeonium extinctum, commonly known as the Extinct Aeonium, is a striking succulent native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered in the wild.
