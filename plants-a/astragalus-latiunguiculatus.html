Astragalus latiunguiculatus

Astragalus latiunguiculatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
