Adlumia cirrhosa var. rupestris

Adlumia cirrhosa var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
