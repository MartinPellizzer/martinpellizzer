Arenaria filicaulis subsp. graeca

Arenaria filicaulis subsp.

graeca, commonly known as Greek sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region. It thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early summer. It is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including drought and salt spray.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of arid and semi-arid habitats.
