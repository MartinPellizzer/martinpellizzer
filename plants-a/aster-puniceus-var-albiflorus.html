Aster puniceus var. albiflorus

Aster puniceus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered stonecrop, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and features showy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to tolerate dry conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, contributing to its ecological value.
