Aquilegia micrantha subsp. ecalcarata

Aquilegia micrantha subsp.

ecalcarata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectaries.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are upright with a somewhat woody base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more narrowly shaped leaves compared to other members of the species.
