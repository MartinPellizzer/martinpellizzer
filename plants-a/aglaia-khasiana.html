Aglaia khasiana

Aglaia khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, also known as the mahogany family, and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
