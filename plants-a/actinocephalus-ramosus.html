Actinocephalus ramosus

Actinocephalus ramosus, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and has been used traditionally for various purposes. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a rough texture.

Actinocephalus ramosus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
