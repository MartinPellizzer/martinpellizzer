Antrophyum strictum

Antrophyum strictum, commonly known as the slender antrophyum, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its elongated, arching fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its graceful silhouette.

Antrophyum strictum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
