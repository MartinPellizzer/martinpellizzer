Aspidium prominulum

Aspidium prominulum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are typically dark green and have a distinctive, glossy appearance. The plant features small, spore-producing structures called sori, which are located on the undersides of its fertile leaves. Aspidium prominulum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical structure reflects the evolutionary adaptations of ferns, showcasing their ancient lineage and complex reproductive mechanisms.
