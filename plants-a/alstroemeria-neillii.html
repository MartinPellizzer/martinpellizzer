Alstroemeria neillii

Alstroemeria neillii, commonly known as the Neill's lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its colorful, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Alstroemeria neillii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and long-lasting blooms.
