Aconitum tricorne

Aconitum tricorne, commonly known as the three-cornered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Aconitum tricorne thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

It contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
