Alyssum urbanianum

Alyssum urbanianum, commonly known as the urban alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species belonging to the Brassicaceae family. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Alyssum urbanianum is often used in ornamental gardening for its decorative appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
