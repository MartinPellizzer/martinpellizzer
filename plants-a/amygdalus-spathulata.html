Amygdalus spathulata

Amygdalus spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed almond or almond tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are similar to peaches but have a distinctive almond flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible nuts.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
