Alchemilla glandulosa

Alchemilla glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves that are often covered in small, glandular dots. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
