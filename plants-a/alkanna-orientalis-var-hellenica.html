Alkanna orientalis var. hellenica

Alkanna orientalis var. hellenica, commonly known as the Greek alkanna, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
