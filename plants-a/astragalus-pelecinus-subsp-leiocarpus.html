Astragalus pelecinus subsp. leiocarpus

Astragalus pelecinus subsp.

leiocarpus, commonly known as the smoothpod milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, hairless pods, which set it apart from other members of the species. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to thrive in arid environments.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
