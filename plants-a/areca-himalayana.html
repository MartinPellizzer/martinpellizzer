Areca himalayana

Areca himalayana, commonly known as the Himalayan areca, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, making it monoecious. Its fruits are typically three-angled berries that contain a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Areca himalayana thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
