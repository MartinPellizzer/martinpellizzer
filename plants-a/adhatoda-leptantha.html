Adhatoda leptantha

Adhatoda leptantha, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual appeal to gardens. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Adhatoda leptantha is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
