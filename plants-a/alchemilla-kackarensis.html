Alchemilla kackarensis

Alchemilla kackarensis, commonly known as the Kackar alkanet, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Alchemilla kackarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
