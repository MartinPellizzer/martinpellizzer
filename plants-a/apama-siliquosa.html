Apama siliquosa

Apama siliquosa, commonly known as the slender silique, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, elongated pods called silique, which contain several seeds. Apama siliquosa typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.
