Ardisia corymbifera

Ardisia corymbifera, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans and animals, though they are often consumed by birds. Ardisia corymbifera thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
