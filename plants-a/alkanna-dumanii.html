Alkanna dumanii

Alkanna dumanii, commonly known as the desert henna, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in its harsh environment. Its roots produce a red dye, which has been used traditionally for body painting and textile coloring.

Alkanna dumanii thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.
