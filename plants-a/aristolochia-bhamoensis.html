Aristolochia bhamoensis

Aristolochia bhamoensis, commonly known as the Bhamo Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often green or brown in color. The plant belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are often woody and climbing.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its role in local ecosystems and potential medicinal properties.
