Alopecurus longearistatus var. glabratus

Alopecurus longearistatus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless, giving it its varietal name "glabratus.". The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and wetlands.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an important component of grassland ecosystems.
