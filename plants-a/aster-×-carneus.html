Aster × carneus

Aster × carneus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces fluffy, cone-shaped centers that are a distinctive feature of its blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Aster × carneus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
