Alseuosmia banksii var. linariifolia

Alseuosmia banksii var. linariifolia, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It features narrow, silver-green leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
