Anisotome geniculata

Anisotome geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Anisotome geniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
