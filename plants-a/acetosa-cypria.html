Acetosa cypria

Acetosa cypria, commonly known as Cyprus chickweed, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along rocky shores. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acetosa cypria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
