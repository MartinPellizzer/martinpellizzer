Acalypha vestita

Acalypha vestita, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves that are typically green with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.

Acalypha vestita is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
