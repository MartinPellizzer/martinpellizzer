Arundinaria nebulosa

Arundinaria nebulosa, commonly known as the misty bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Arundinaria nebulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
