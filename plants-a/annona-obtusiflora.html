Annona obtusiflora

Annona obtusiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers with five petals and a distinctive, spiky floral structure. Its fruit is a large, green, oval or round berry that turns yellow when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
