Agave thomsoniana

Agave thomsoniana, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped cluster of thick, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of small, white flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

The plant is also valued for its use in the production of tequila and other agave-based beverages.
