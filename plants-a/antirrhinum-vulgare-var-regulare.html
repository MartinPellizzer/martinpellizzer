Antirrhinum vulgare var. regulare

Antirrhinum vulgare var. regulare, commonly known as the common snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of brightly colored, tubular flowers that resemble a dragon's mouth, giving it its common name. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing flowers in shades of red, yellow, orange, and pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in various soil types.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
