Armeria linkiana

Armeria linkiana, commonly known as the coastal thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Armeria linkiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
