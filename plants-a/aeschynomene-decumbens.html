Aeschynomene decumbens

Aeschynomene decumbens, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Aeschynomene decumbens is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for livestock.
