Arum novum

Arum novum, commonly known as the New World arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spatulate inflorescence that resembles a flower. The plant produces a spadix, which is surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Arum novum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
