Acacia macdonnelliensis subsp. teretifolia

Acacia macdonnelliensis subsp.

teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, phyllodes that are typically narrow and linear.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to low rainfall and nutrient-poor soils, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.
