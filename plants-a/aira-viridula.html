Aira viridula

Aira viridula, commonly known as green air grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands.

Aira viridula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
