Atalantia rotundifolia

Atalantia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved atalantia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, round-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blossoms. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for warm climates.

The fruit, which is a capsule, contains seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its natural propagation.
