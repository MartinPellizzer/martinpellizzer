Ampacus micrococca

Ampacus micrococca, commonly known as the small white cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its compact, spiny stems and small, white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ampacus micrococca is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
