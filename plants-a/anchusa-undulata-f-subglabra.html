Anchusa undulata f. subglabra

Anchusa undulata f. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-flowered bugloss, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, less hairy leaves compared to the typical form. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
