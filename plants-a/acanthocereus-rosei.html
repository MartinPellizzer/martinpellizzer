Acanthocereus rosei

Acanthocereus rosei, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like bats and moths. Its foliage is reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
