Astragalus nucifer

Astragalus nucifer, commonly known as the Indian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus nucifer is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in Ayurvedic practices.
