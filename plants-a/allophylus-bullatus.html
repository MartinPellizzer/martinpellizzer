Allophylus bullatus

Allophylus bullatus, commonly known as the hairy alder, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
