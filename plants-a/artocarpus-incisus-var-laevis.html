Artocarpus incisus var. laevis

Artocarpus incisus var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth-leafed breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and large, round fruits. The fruit, which is typically green when unripe, turns yellow or orange when mature and is edible when cooked. The plant is valued for its nutritious fruit and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.

Its growth habit includes a tall, straight trunk with a dense canopy, making it a significant component of tropical ecosystems.
