Artemisia pedunculata

Artemisia pedunculata, commonly known as narrowleaf sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flower clusters that bloom in the summer. The plant has aromatic leaves that contain essential oils, contributing to its distinctive scent. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

Artemisia pedunculata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
