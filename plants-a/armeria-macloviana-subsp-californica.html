Armeria macloviana subsp. californica

Armeria macloviana subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California thrift, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to coastal regions of California. It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats, thriving in full sun.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
