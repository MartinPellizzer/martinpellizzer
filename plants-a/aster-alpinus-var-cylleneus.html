Aster alpinus var. cylleneus

Aster alpinus var. cylleneus, commonly known as the Alpine aster, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. This variety is known for its distinctive purple to blue flower heads, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
