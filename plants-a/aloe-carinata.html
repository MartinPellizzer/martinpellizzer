Aloe carinata

Aloe carinata, commonly known as the ridged aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are ribbed and covered in a layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow or orange flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Aloe carinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
