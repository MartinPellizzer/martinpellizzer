Arabis kokanika

Arabis kokanika, commonly known as the Balkan rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Arabis kokanika is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
