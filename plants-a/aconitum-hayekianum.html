Aconitum hayekianum

Aconitum hayekianum, commonly known as the Hayek's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, sparsely branched clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets that are typically green and slightly hairy.

Aconitum hayekianum is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky soils at high elevations.
