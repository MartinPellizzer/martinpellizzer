Aster furcatus

Aster furcatus, commonly known as the forked aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive branched flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall.

Aster furcatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
