Astragalus leptocladus

Astragalus leptocladus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is often extensive and deep. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Astragalus leptocladus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
