Arctium ruderale

Arctium ruderale, commonly known as the common burdock.

It is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple flower heads that develop into spiny, bur-like fruits, which aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and thistles.
