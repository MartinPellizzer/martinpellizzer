Abronia nudata

Abronia nudata, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has a short, upright stem and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its root system is typically shallow but can extend deep to access moisture.
