Alchemilla melanoscytos

Alchemilla melanoscytos, commonly known as black-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Alchemilla melanoscytos is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
