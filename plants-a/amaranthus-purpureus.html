Amaranthus purpureus

Amaranthus purpureus, commonly known as purple amaranth, is an annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and vibrant, feather-like leaves that range from green to purple in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red, depending on the variety. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

Purple amaranth is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible seeds.
