Astragalus grayanus

Astragalus grayanus, commonly known as Gray's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Astragalus grayanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
