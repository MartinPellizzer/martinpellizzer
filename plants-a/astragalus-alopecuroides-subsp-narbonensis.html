Astragalus alopecuroides subsp. narbonensis

Astragalus alopecuroides subsp.

narbonensis, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as steppes and grasslands.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.
