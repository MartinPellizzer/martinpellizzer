Aralia incisa

Aralia incisa, commonly known as American arum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant bears small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, which are followed by dark red berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant in native gardens.
