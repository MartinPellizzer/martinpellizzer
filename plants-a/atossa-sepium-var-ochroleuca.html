Atossa sepium var. ochroleuca

Atossa sepium var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is native to coastal regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens for its vibrant color and hardy growth habit.
