Aquilegia vulgaris var. mandshurica

Aquilegia vulgaris var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian columbine, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
