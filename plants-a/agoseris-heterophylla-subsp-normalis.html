Agoseris heterophylla subsp. normalis

Agoseris heterophylla subsp.

normalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear to lanceolate leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
