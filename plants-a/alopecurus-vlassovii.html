Alopecurus vlassovii

Alopecurus vlassovii, commonly known as the Vlassov's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, ascending stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in meadows or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Alopecurus vlassovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
