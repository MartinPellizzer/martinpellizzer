Ardisia megalocarpa

Ardisia megalocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is notable for its striking, dark purple to black berries that appear in late summer. These berries are edible for humans but are toxic to livestock and pets.

Ardisia megalocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
