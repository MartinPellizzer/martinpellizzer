Agathosma aristata

Agathosma aristata, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Agathosma aristata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
