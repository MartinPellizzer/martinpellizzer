Armeria plantaginea subsp. sicorisiensis

Armeria plantaginea subsp.

sicorisiensis, commonly known as Sicilian thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
