Acacia molfinoi

Acacia molfinoi, commonly known as the Molfino's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance through its deep root system. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Acacia molfinoi is often used in ecological restoration due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
