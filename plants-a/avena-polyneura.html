Avena polyneura

Avena polyneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf oat, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with pale blue or greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Avena polyneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
