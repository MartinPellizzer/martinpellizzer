Achillea imbricata

Achillea imbricata, commonly known as the crested yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves and its clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, sometimes medicinal properties.

Achillea imbricata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
