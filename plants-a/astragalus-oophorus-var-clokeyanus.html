Astragalus oophorus var. clokeyanus

Astragalus oophorus var. clokeyanus, commonly known as the Clokey's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
