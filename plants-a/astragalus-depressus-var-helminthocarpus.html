Astragalus depressus var. helminthocarpus

Astragalus depressus var. helminthocarpus, commonly known as the spiralhead milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods, which are a key feature in its classification. It typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.
