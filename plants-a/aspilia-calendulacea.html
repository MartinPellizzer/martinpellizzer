Aspilia calendulacea

Aspilia calendulacea, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Aspilia calendulacea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
