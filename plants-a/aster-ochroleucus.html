Aster ochroleucus

Aster ochroleucus, commonly known as the white aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its showy white to pale yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
