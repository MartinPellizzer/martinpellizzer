Aspilia pseudoviguiera

Aspilia pseudoviguiera, commonly known as the African feverfew, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Aspilia pseudoviguiera is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses.
