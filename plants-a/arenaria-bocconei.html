Arenaria bocconei

Arenaria bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Arenaria bocconei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
