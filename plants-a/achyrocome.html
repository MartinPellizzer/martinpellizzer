Achyrocome

Achyrocome, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
