Anil mauritanica

Anil mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian anil, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of wax, reducing water loss.

Anil mauritanica is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.
