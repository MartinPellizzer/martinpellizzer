Astragalus monspessulanus var. buffae

Astragalus monspessulanus var. buffae, commonly known as the Buff-colored Milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
