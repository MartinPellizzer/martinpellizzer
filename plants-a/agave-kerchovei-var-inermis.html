Agave kerchovei var. inermis

Agave kerchovei var. inermis, commonly known as the Mexican filarette, is a succulent plant native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, bluish-gray leaves that are spineless, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its compact size and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
