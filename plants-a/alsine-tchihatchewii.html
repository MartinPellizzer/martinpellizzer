Alsine tchihatchewii

Alsine tchihatchewii, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy, succulent leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in sandy soils. It blooms typically in spring, producing vibrant pink or purple flowers that attract pollinators.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
