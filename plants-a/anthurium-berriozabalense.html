Anthurium berriozabalense

Anthurium berriozabalense, commonly known as the Berriozabal anthurium, is a species native to the humid forests of South America, particularly in Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped spathe that comes in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often contrasting with its deep green spadix. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries, which are typically red when mature.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
