Aloinopsis wilmaniae

Aloinopsis wilmaniae, commonly known as the Wilman's Aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps the plant store water in arid environments. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

This plant is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and requiring minimal water.
