Asplenium plenum

Asplenium plenum, commonly known as the common spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and often have a glossy appearance. The plant produces spores on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in structures called sori. Asplenium plenum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or in crevices.

It is a hardy species that is widely distributed across temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
