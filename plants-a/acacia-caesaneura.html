Acacia caesaneura

Acacia caesaneura, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, spherical clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. Acacia caesaneura is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

It is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
