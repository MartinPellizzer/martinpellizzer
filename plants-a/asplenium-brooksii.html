Asplenium brooksii

Asplenium brooksii, commonly known as the Brooks's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a slightly serrated margin. This plant is often found growing on rock surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, such as crevices in cliffs or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Asplenium brooksii is a relatively low-maintenance plant that thrives in humid conditions with indirect light.
