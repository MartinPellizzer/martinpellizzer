Arabis hirsuta var. gerardi

Arabis hirsuta var. gerardi, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
