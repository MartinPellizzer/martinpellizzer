Arenaria capillaris var. glabra

Arenaria capillaris var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats of tiny, rounded leaves that are smooth and hairless, giving it its varietal name "glabra.". The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its simple structure and hardy nature make it a common species in high-altitude and polar environments.
