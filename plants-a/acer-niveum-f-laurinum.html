Acer niveum f. laurinum

Acer niveum f. laurinum, commonly known as the white maple or silver maple, is a variant of the white maple tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodland areas.

This ornamental tree is valued for its striking foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
