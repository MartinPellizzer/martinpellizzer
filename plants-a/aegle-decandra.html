Aegle decandra

Aegle decandra, commonly known as the Indian date tree or wild date tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Morphology

Aegle decandra has compound, pinnate leaves that are alternate in arrangement, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in clusters during the summer months, and are pollinated by insects.
