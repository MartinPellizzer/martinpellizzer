Aegle decandra

Aegle decandra, commonly known as the Indian date tree or wild date tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may turn yellow or red depending on the variety. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and fibrous pulp that is often consumed fresh or used in traditional cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
