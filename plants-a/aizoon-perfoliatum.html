Aizoon perfoliatum

Aizoon perfoliatum, commonly known as the perforate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems that often have a reddish hue. The plant produces small, three-lobed leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are clustered in spikelets, which are borne on long, slender inflorescences.

Aizoon perfoliatum is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
