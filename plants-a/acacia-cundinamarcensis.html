Acacia cundinamarcensis

Acacia cundinamarcensis, commonly known as the Cundinamarca acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is typically shrub-like, with compound leaves and yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its ecological importance and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
