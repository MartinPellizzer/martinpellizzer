Astragalus suturalis

Astragalus suturalis, commonly known as the sutural milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus suturalis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
