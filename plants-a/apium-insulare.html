Apium insulare

Apium insulare, commonly known as the island parsley, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and celery. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. It is often found growing in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Apium insulare is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
