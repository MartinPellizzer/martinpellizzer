Achillea trilingulata

Achillea trilingulata, commonly known as the three-lobed yarrow, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads that are typically white to pale pink in color. Its leaves are finely divided and gray-green, with a distinctive silvery appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
