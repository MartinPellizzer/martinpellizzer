Acacia mooreana

Acacia mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Acacia mooreana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
