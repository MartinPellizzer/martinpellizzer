Acer × pusillum

Acer × pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Acer negundo and Acer saccharum.

It is a small deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive foliage. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a bronze-green hue that turns vibrant red or orange in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is often used in urban landscaping and as a specimen plant.
