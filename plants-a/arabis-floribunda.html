Arabis floribunda

Arabis floribunda, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. Arabis floribunda typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
