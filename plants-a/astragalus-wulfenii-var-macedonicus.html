Astragalus wulfenii var. macedonicus

Astragalus wulfenii var. macedonicus, commonly known as the Macedonian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing a compact growth habit with hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
