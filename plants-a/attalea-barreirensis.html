Attalea barreirensis

Attalea barreirensis, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, followed by fruit that contains a single seed. It thrives in wetland and swampy environments, often playing a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or conservation efforts.
