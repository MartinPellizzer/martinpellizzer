Aconitum lycoctonum var. myoctonum

Aconitum lycoctonum var. myoctonum, commonly known as the Italian monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply veined and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking blue to purple, helmet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and kept away from livestock and children.
