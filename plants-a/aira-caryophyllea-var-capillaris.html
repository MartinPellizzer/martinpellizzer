Aira caryophyllea var. capillaris

Aira caryophyllea var. capillaris, commonly known as the silky grass or capillary aira, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves with a fine, silky texture, and it produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a reddish or pinkish hue, and it blooms in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
