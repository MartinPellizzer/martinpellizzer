Adenophora pereskiifolia subsp. alternifolia

Adenophora pereskiifolia subsp.

alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in garden settings.
