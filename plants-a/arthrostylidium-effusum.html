Arthrostylidium effusum

Arthrostylidium effusum, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
