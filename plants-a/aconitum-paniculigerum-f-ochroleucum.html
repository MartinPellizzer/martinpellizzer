Aconitum paniculigerum f. ochroleucum

Aconitum paniculigerum f. ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered monkshood, is a hybrid form of the monkshood plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, contrasting with the typical blue or purple hues of the species. This variety is native to Europe and thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland areas. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, spore-bearing structures at the top of its tall stem.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
