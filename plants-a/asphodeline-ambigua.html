Asphodeline ambigua

Asphodeline ambigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf asphodel, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Asphodeline ambigua is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
