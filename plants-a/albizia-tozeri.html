Albizia tozeri

Albizia tozeri, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical benefits.
