Apium podagraria

Apium podagraria, commonly known as cow parsley or wild chervil, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or greenish-white flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its aromatic leaves and stems are often used in culinary applications, adding a subtle anise-like flavor.

Apium podagraria is also valued in ecological gardens for its ability to support pollinators.
