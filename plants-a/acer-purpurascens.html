Acer purpurascens

Acer purpurascens, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The tree has opposite, palmate leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more rugged with age.
