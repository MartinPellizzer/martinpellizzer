Actinotinus sinensis

Actinotinus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
