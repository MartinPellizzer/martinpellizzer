Anemone poilanei

Anemone poilanei, commonly known as the French anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern France.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are basal and often covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone poilanei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
