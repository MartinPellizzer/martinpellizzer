Arenaria macradenia subsp. ferrisiae

Arenaria macradenia subsp.

ferrisiae, commonly known as the Cape fescue, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers.

The species is adapted to arid and sandy environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. Its subshrub form features a compact rosette of foliage, often with a mat-like growth habit.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry garden settings.
