Adiantum micropteris

Adiantum micropteris, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the wings of a bird, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its fronds are typically biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year.

Adiantum micropteris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
