Aconitum vitosanum

Aconitum vitosanum, commonly known as the European aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Aconitum vitosanum is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it highly dangerous to humans and animals.
