Astragalus fenzlii subsp. armadshek

Astragalus fenzlii subsp.

armadshek, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish pea-like flowers.

The plant produces pods that contain several small, hard seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
