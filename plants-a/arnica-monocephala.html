Arnica monocephala

Arnica monocephala, commonly known as the common arnica, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils.

Arnica monocephala is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
