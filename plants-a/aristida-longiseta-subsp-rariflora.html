Aristida longiseta subsp. rariflora

Aristida longiseta subsp.

rariflora, commonly known as the rare-flowered three-awn, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous bristly florets.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that aids in reducing water loss.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
