Artocarpus laciniatus var. metallicus

Artocarpus laciniatus var. metallicus, commonly known as the metallic fig or silver-leafed fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically large, palmately lobed, and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
