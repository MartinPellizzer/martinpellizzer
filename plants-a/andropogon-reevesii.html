Andropogon reevesii

Andropogon reevesii, commonly known as Reeves' switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Reeves' switchgrass produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often reddish or purplish in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
