Actinodaphne sulcata var. sulcata

Actinodaphne sulcata var. sulcata, commonly known as the warty-leafed laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, warty-textured leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple or black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
