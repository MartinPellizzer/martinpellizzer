Anemone cuneifolia

Anemone cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a textured appearance. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
