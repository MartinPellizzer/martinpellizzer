Aconitum lianhuashanicum

Aconitum lianhuashanicum, commonly known as the Lianhuashan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and its roots contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion.
