Aristida cognata

Aristida cognata, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. It is a perennial plant that is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
