Anidrum flosculosum

Anidrum flosculosum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer, adding a delicate floral display to its habitat.

Anidrum flosculosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
