Andropogon miliaceus

Andropogon miliaceus, commonly known as millet grass, is a flowering plant native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Andropogon miliaceus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
