Adenia cardiophylla var. arcta

Adenia cardiophylla var. arcta, commonly known as the black hakea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
