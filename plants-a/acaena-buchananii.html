Acaena buchananii

Acaena buchananii, commonly known as the New Zealand burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces burrs with hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in coastal and subalpine habitats, often growing in well-drained soils.

Acaena buchananii is an important component of New Zealand's native flora and has ecological significance for supporting local wildlife.
