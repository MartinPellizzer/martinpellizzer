Arabis austriaca

Arabis austriaca, commonly known as Austrian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are elongated and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white, and arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Arabis austriaca is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
