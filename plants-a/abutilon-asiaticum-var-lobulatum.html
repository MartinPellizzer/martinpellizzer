Abutilon asiaticum var. lobulatum

Abutilon asiaticum var. lobulatum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, palmate leaves and clusters of lantern-shaped, orange-red bracts that resemble small lanterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by the showy, papery bracts. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental in gardens.

Its unique appearance and seasonal color changes make it a striking addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
