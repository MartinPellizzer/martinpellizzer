Acaena macrostemon var. basipilosa

Acaena macrostemon var. basipilosa, commonly known as the New Zealand spinachberry, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate and have a serrated edge, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is part of the genus Acaena, which includes several species with similar ecological and botanical features.
