Andrachne phyllanthoides var. reverchonii

Andrachne phyllanthoides var. reverchonii, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often forming dense colonies in disturbed or dry habitats.

Its succulent nature allows it to store water, making it well-suited for survival in drought conditions.
