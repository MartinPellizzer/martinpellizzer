Anacolus gardneri

Anacolus gardneri, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures are concealed within the leaf axils, and it produces spores rather than seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Anacolus gardneri is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
