Acacia multispirea

Acacia multispirea, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. Acacia multispirea thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
