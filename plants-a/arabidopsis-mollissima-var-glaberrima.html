Arabidopsis mollissima var. glaberrima

Arabidopsis mollissima var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant has a low, mat-forming growth habit and is well adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically succulent and covered in a dense layer of fine, glandular hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to extreme climates and potential for genetic research.
