Asphodelus bonatii

Asphodelus bonatii, commonly known as the Sicilian asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and tall, spiky flower clusters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Asphodelus bonatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
