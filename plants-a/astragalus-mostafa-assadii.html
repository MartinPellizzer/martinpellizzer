Astragalus mostafa-assadii

Astragalus mostafa-assadii, commonly known as the Mostafaa-Sadat milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Its scientific study contributes to the understanding of plant biodiversity in arid ecosystems.
