Adhatoda pilosa

Adhatoda pilosa, commonly known as the Indian skullcap, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from summer to autumn. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional African medicine for treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
