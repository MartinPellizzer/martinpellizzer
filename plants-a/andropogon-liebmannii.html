Andropogon liebmannii

Andropogon liebmannii, commonly known as big bluestem, is a tall, warm-season grass native to the tallgrass prairies of North America.

It is characterized by its bluish-green foliage and a distinctive, hollow stem with a prominent node. This plant is well adapted to prairie ecosystems, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Big bluestem is a key component of native grasslands and plays an important role in soil stabilization and wildlife habitat.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in restoration projects.
