Asparagus africanus var. microcarpus

Asparagus africanus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
