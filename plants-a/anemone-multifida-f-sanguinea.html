Anemone multifida f. sanguinea

Anemone multifida f. sanguinea, commonly known as the red-flowered anemone, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, deeply divided, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hair. The plant produces clusters of bright red, showy flowers with five to seven petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant color and delicate foliage.
