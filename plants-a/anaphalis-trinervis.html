Anaphalis trinervis

Anaphalis trinervis, commonly known as the silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, silvery foliage and fluffy, white capitula that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Anaphalis trinervis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soils.
