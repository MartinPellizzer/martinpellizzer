Asplenium seileri

Asplenium seileri, commonly known as the Seiler's spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic plant, typically found growing on rocks or tree trunks in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a compact, rosette-like growth form with lanceolate, serrated leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Asplenium seileri is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
