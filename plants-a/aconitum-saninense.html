Aconitum saninense

Aconitum saninense, commonly known as Sanin's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring large, palmately divided leaves. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, which have historically been used in traditional medicine.

Aconitum saninense thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
