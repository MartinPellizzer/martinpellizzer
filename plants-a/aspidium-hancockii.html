Aspidium hancockii

Aspidium hancockii, commonly known as the Hancock's water fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along stream banks and in wetlands. Its rhizomes are creeping and help it spread along the ground, forming dense mats.

Aspidium hancockii plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
