Asperula stylosa

Asperula stylosa, commonly known as the Alpine azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly the Alps and Pyrenees. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Asperula stylosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to grow in rocky or alpine conditions.
