Apocynum hypericifolium var. cordigerum

Apocynum hypericifolium var. cordigerum, commonly known as the cordate milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, making it toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its ecological importance includes serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
