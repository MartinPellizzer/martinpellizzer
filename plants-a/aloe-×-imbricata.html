Aloe × imbricata

Aloe × imbricata, commonly known as the Zebra Aloe, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, narrow, upright leaves that are typically green with white or yellowish stripes, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
