Alstroemeria reclinata

Alstroemeria reclinata, commonly known as the hanging alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its drooping, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and pink. The plant has strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alstroemeria reclinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
