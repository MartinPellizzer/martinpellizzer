Amerina caroliniana

Amerina caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has narrow, linear leaves and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
