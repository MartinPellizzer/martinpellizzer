Astragalus cognatus var. brachybotrys

Astragalus cognatus var. brachybotrys, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, rounded leaflets.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils in arid environments.
