Angelica archangelica subsp. decurrens

Angelica archangelica subsp.

decurrens, commonly known as the European angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, and its large, umbrella-like compound leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a complex inflorescence, which develop into small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The plant has a strong, aromatic scent and is known for its medicinal properties.
