Anacolosa frutescens

Anacolosa frutescens, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or pink in color, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant has palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream edges.

Anacolosa frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
