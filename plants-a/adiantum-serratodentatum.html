Adiantum serratodentatum

Adiantum serratodentatum, commonly known as the serrated tooth fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a silvery bloom. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soils. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with small, tooth-like lobes that give it its common name.

Adiantum serratodentatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
