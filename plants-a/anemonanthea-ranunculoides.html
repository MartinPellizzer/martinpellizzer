Anemonanthea ranunculoides

Anemonanthea ranunculoides, commonly known as the golden anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically yellow or orange with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemonanthea ranunculoides is valued in gardens for its early spring blooms and its role in supporting pollinators.
