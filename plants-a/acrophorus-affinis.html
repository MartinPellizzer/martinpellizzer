Acrophorus affinis

Acrophorus affinis, commonly known as the slender acanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of vibrant blue or purple blooms. Acrophorus affinis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and green spaces.
