Alloberberis pinifolia var. coahuilensis

Alloberberis pinifolia var. coahuilensis, commonly known as the Coahuiltecan barberry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Texas and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water, featuring succulent leaves that store moisture. Its fruit is a small, red berry that provides food for local wildlife.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
