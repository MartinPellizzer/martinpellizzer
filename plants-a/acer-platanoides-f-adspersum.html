Acer platanoides f. adspersum

Acer platanoides f. adspersum, commonly known as the Norway maple, is a cultivar of the maple tree characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with five lobes.

It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. The leaves are typically a vibrant green in summer and turn yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest to landscapes. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Its fast growth rate and ability to tolerate urban conditions make it a popular choice for street trees and parks.
