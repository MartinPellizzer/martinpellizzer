Acacia trineura

Acacia trineura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Acacia trineura is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
