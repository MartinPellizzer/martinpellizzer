Artemisia caerulescens subsp. caerulescens

Artemisia caerulescens subsp.

caerulescens, commonly known as blue artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-blue foliage that emits a strong, camphor-like scent when crushed.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
