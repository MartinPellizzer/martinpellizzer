Alyssum caliacrae var. sinuosum

Alyssum caliacrae var. sinuosum, commonly known as the sinuous alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly succulent, adapted to withstand dry conditions. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for coastal or arid landscapes.
