Ammochloa palaestina

Ammochloa palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine sandgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in coastal areas and is tolerant of salt spray.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal dune ecosystems.
