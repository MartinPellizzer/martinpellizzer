Achillea roseo-alba

Achillea roseo-alba, commonly known as the pale pink yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, clustered inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, silver-green leaves and produces pale pink to white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Achillea roseo-alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.

Morphology

Achillea roseo-alba has basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape, arranged in a rosette, with a silvery-white underside and a dark green upper surface.

The stem is upright, hairy, and has a light green color, growing in a compact, bushy pattern. The flowers are composite, consisting of pale pink to white ray florets and yellow disk florets, blooming from late spring to early summer, and are primarily pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small achene with a pappus, dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and extensive, helping the plant anchor in soil and absorb nutrients.
