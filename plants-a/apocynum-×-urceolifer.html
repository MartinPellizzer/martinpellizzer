Apocynum × urceolifer

Apocynum × urceolifer, commonly known as the false milkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a milky sap that is toxic to many animals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Apocynum × urceolifer is native to North America and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
