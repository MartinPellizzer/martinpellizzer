Abies concolor f. brevifolia

Abies concolor f. brevifolia, commonly known as the white fir dwarf form, is a variant of the white fir tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to the typical white fir. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are typically light green and covered in a waxy cuticle. Its cones are generally small and conical, maturing to a reddish-brown color.

This subspecies is often found in high-elevation forests and is adapted to cooler, moist environments.
