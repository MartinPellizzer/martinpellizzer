Aconitum meyeri var. squarrosum

Aconitum meyeri var. squarrosum, commonly known as the woolly aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are palmately divided with coarse teeth and often covered in a dense, woolly pubescence.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
