Annona pygmaea

Annona pygmaea, commonly known as the little soursop, is a small tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It produces a round, spiny fruit that is typically no larger than a small apple. The fruit has a thick, green outer skin with a rough texture and contains a single large, white, fleshy pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its edible fruit.

Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.
