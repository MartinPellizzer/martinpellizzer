Achyranthes canescens

Achyranthes canescens, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. Its seed capsules release tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across arid regions.

Achyranthes canescens is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
