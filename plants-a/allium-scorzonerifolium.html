Allium scorzonerifolium

Allium scorzonerifolium, commonly known as the garlic chive or Siberian leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are aromatic and edible, often used in cooking for their mild garlic flavor. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a spherical cluster, typical of the Allium genus. Its bulbs are small and resemble those of garlic, though they are generally less pungent.

This hardy plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
