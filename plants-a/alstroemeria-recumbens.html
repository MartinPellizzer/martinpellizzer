Alstroemeria recumbens

Alstroemeria recumbens, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in a sprawling or trailing manner, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
