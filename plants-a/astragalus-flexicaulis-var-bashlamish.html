Astragalus flexicaulis var. bashlamish

Astragalus flexicaulis var. bashlamish, commonly known as Bashlamish milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance.
