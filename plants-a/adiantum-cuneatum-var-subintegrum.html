Adiantum cuneatum var. subintegrum

Adiantum cuneatum var. subintegrum, commonly known as the southern maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to parts of the southeastern United States. The plant features small, bright green fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters long, with a distinctive, arching growth habit. Its rhizomes are short and thick, allowing it to spread slowly through rhizome growth.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
