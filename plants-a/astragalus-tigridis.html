Astragalus tigridis

Astragalus tigridis, commonly known as the tiger milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds, a common trait among legumes. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Astragalus tigridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
