Arenaria longibracteata

Arenaria longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats with small, succulent leaves that help it survive in harsh, cold environments. The flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences and are usually white or pale pink, adding a delicate touch to its rocky habitat. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to extreme climates, showcasing remarkable resilience.
