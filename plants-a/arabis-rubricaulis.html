Arabis rubricaulis

Arabis rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and it produces rounded, hairy seed pods.

Arabis rubricaulis is often found in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to cold climates.
