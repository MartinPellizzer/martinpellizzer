Arachniodes yasu-inouei var. angustipinnula

Arachniodes yasu-inouei var. angustipinnula, commonly known as the narrow-pinnate arachniodes, is a rare and striking fern native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often adorned with a unique, almost spider-like appearance due to its finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are usually a deep green, with a slightly glossy texture, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialized gardens or conservatories.
