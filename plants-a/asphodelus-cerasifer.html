Asphodelus cerasifer

Asphodelus cerasifer, commonly known as the cherry-flowered asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that resemble small cherries, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. It is part of the Asparagales order and belongs to the Asphodelaceae family.

Asphodelus cerasifer is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
