Argylia adscendens var. viridis

Argylia adscendens var. viridis, commonly known as the green argyle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
