Aplectrum myrtiforme

Aplectrum myrtiforme, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies.

Aplectrum myrtiforme thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
