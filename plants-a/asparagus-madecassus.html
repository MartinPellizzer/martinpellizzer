Asparagus madecassus

Asparagus madecassus, commonly known as the Madagascar asparagus, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, grass-like leaves. The plant produces delicate white or greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical regions.
