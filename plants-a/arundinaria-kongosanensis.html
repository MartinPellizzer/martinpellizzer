Arundinaria kongosanensis

Arundinaria kongosanensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Kongo bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
