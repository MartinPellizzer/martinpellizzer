Abronia fallax

Abronia fallax, commonly known as the Florida beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy, coastal environments. The plant produces small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Abronia fallax is adapted to salt spray and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species in coastal ecosystems.
