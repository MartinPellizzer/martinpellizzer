Allium chlorotepalum

Allium chlorotepalum, commonly known as the green-flowered garlic.

It is a flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family, native to the eastern Mediterranean region. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a bulbous base similar to other allium species.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has a mild garlic scent.
