Acer pictum subvar. subtrifidum

Acer pictum subvar. subtrifidum, commonly known as the red maple subvar. subtrifidum, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifid (three-lobed) leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
