Artemisia saitoana

Artemisia saitoana, commonly known as Japanese mugwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a seasoning in some cuisines.

Artemisia saitoana is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its role in ecological succession.
