Alsophila punctulata

Alsophila punctulata, commonly known as the punctulate tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the fronds, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

The plant's unique structure and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
