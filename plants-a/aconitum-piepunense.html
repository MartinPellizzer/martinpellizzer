Aconitum piepunense

Aconitum piepunense, commonly known as the Tibetan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. It has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments and produces a tall stem that bears its showy flowers.

Aconitum piepunense is notable for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a plant of both ecological and medicinal significance.
