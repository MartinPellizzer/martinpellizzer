Astragalus massiliensis proles vicentinus

Astragalus massiliensis proles vicentinus, commonly known as the Vicentine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pink flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
