Aristolochia ornithorhyncha

Aristolochia ornithorhyncha, commonly known as the birdbeak arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spirally twisted leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some areas for horticultural purposes.
