Arundinaria longiflora

Arundinaria longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of small, elongated flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering structure resembles that of a spikelet.

Arundinaria longiflora is well-suited to moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
