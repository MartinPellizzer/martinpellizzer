Agathosma rosmarinifolia

Agathosma rosmarinifolia, commonly known as Cape rosemary, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky environments.

Its essential oils contain compounds like camphor and pinene, contributing to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
