Artemisia eriopoda var. eriopoda

Artemisia eriopoda var. eriopoda, commonly known as silver sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, linear leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Artemisia eriopoda var. eriopoda is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.
