Acer nipponicum

Acer nipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring intricate, palmate leaves that change color with the seasons. The tree typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a spreading canopy and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring before the leaves, while its fruits are samaras that disperse seeds by wind.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
