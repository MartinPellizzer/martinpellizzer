Acinodendron guayaquilense

Acinodendron guayaquilense, commonly known as the Guayaquil acacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Colombia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a typical feature of the Fabaceae family, and the plant is often found in moist, lowland forests.

Acinodendron guayaquilense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
