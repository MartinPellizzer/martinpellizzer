Acacia resinosa

Acacia resinosa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is a member of the legume family. The plant produces a distinctive resin that has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians. Its leaves are bipinnate, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Acacia resinosa plays an important role in both ecological and cultural contexts.
