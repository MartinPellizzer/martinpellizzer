Ampelocalamus microphyllus

Ampelocalamus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved bamboo, is a unique member of the grass family (Poaceae) with slender, hollow stems and narrow, linear leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China and is characterized by its low-growing, clumping habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the base of the stems. Its foliage is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
