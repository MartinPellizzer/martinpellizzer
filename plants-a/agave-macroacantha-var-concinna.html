Agave macroacantha var. concinna

Agave macroacantha var. concinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette growth form and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are narrow and have a sharp, spiny margin, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white to pale green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

This plant is well adapted to drought conditions and requires minimal water once established.
