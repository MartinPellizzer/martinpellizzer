Anil macrocarpa

Anil macrocarpa, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dark, heavy timber that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, followed by long, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Anil macrocarpa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
