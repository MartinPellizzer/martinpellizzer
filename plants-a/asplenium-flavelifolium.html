Asplenium flavelifolium

Asplenium flavelifolium, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with golden hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are pinnate, with small, closely arranged pinnae that give it a compact appearance.

Asplenium flavelifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
