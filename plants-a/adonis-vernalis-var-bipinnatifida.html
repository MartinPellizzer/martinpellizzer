Adonis vernalis var. bipinnatifida

Adonis vernalis var. bipinnatifida, commonly known as the spring adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves and its clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking early-season blooms.
