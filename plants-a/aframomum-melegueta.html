Aframomum melegueta

Aframomum melegueta, commonly known as African cardamom, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, three-lobed fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. The seeds are often dried and ground into a spice, similar to true cardamom, and are valued for their unique flavor and essential oils.

Due to its economic importance, it is cultivated in various regions with suitable tropical climates.
