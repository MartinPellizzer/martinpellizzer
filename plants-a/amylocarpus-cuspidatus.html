Amylocarpus cuspidatus

Amylocarpus cuspidatus, commonly known as the spike-root plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its underground tubers, which are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source. The plant produces a striking inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its leaves are typically large, lance-shaped, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Amylocarpus cuspidatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible tubers.
