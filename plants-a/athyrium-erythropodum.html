Athyrium erythropodum

Athyrium erythropodum, commonly known as the red-veined Japanese painting fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including Japan, Korea, and China. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds with striking red veins. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural collections.
