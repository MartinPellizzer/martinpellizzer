Agathosma multicaulis

Agathosma multicaulis, commonly known as African geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with small, fleshy leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agathosma multicaulis is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
