Achyranthes laguroides

Achyranthes laguroides, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Achyranthes laguroides is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
