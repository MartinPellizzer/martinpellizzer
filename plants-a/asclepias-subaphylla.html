Asclepias subaphylla

Asclepias subaphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Asclepias subaphylla is a member of the milkweed family, Apocynaceae, and is known for its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
