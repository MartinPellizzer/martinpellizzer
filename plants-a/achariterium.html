Achariterium

Achariterium, commonly known as the "spiny globe-thistle," is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed habitats.

The leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.
