Aster oblongifolius var. rigidulus

Aster oblongifolius var. rigidulus, commonly known as the rigid aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
