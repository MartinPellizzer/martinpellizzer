Astragalus gennarii

Astragalus gennarii, commonly known as Gennari's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus gennarii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators in its native habitat.
