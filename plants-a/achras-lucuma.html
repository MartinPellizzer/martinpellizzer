Achras lucuma

Achras lucuma, commonly known as the cherimoya, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with thick, leathery leaves and showy, white flowers. The fruit is a hairy, oblong berry that turns from green to yellow as it ripens. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and agriculture.
