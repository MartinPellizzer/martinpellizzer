Acridocarpus natalitius var. acuminatus

Acridocarpus natalitius var. acuminatus, commonly known as Natal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
