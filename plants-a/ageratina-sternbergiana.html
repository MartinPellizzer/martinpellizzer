Ageratina sternbergiana

Ageratina sternbergiana, commonly known as the white crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white capitula that resemble small clouds, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall texture.

Ageratina sternbergiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
