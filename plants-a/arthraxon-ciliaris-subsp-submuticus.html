Arthraxon ciliaris subsp. submuticus

Arthraxon ciliaris subsp.

submuticus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet environments, such as swampy areas or the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like structures bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
