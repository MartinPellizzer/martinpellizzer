Artemisia parryi

Artemisia parryi, commonly known as Parry's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, often fuzzy leaves that emit a strong, resinous scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Artemisia parryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
