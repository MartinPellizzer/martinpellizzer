Aster tanacetifolius var. pygmaeus

Aster tanacetifolius var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf garden aster, is a compact, flowering plant native to Europe.

It features small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed, giving it a distinctive scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
