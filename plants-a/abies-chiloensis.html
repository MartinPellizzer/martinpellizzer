Abies chiloensis

Abies chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown hue. It prefers cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its wood is valued for its strength and is used in construction and furniture-making.
