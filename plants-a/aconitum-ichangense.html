Aconitum ichangense

Aconitum ichangense, commonly known as Chinese aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in racemes. Its roots contain potent alkaloids, making it a plant of both medicinal and toxicological significance.

Due to its toxicity, it is often cultivated in controlled environments for its ornamental value.
