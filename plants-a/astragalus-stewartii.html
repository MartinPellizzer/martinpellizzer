Astragalus stewartii

Astragalus stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It is known for its small, round seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus stewartii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soils in arid environments.
