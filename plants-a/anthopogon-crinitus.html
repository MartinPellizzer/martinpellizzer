Anthopogon crinitus

Anthopogon crinitus, commonly known as the curly hair grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico, typically found in sandy or disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often appear curled or twisted. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces lightweight seeds that are easily dispersed by wind.

Anthopogon crinitus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
