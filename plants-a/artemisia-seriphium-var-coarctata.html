Artemisia seriphium var. coarctata

Artemisia seriphium var. coarctata, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features gray-green, aromatic leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
