Abies excelsa var. baldensis

Abies excelsa var. baldensis, commonly known as the Bald Mountain fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the high elevations of the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree typically grows in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its cones are small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color.

This variety is adapted to harsh alpine conditions, making it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its habitat.
