Athyrium machangense

Athyrium machangense, commonly known as the Machangense Athyrium, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

The plant exhibits a complex life cycle involving both a sporophyte and a gametophyte stage.
