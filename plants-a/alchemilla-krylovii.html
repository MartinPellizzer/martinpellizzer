Alchemilla krylovii

Alchemilla krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces upright flowering stems bearing clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alchemilla krylovii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
