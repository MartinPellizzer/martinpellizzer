Anacamptis × timbali nothosubsp.

ugrinskyana, commonly known as the Ugrinsky hybrid orchid, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Anacamptis morio and Anacamptis pyramidalis. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow-green throat.

The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing showy, spurred orchid flowers that are adapted for pollination by bees. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This nothosubspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
