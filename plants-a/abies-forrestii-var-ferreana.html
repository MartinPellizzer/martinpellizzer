Abies forrestii var. ferreana

Abies forrestii var. ferreana, commonly known as the red spruce, is a variant of the spruce genus native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and conical shape, which helps it withstand harsh alpine conditions. The leaves are typically short, stiff, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. Its cones are usually pendulous and mature to a reddish color, contributing to its ornamental value.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
