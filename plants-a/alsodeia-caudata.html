Alsodeia caudata

Alsodeia caudata, commonly known as the trailing alsike, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Alsodeia caudata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
