Acer capillipes var. fujisanense

Acer capillipes var. fujisanense, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely toothed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden settings.
