Astragalus frickii

Astragalus frickii, commonly known as Frick's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Astragalus frickii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native environment.
