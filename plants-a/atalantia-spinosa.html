Atalantia spinosa

Atalantia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny atalantia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its thorny stems and aromatic, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
