Actinostachys intermedia

Actinostachys intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate actinostachys, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors.

Botanically, it is noted for its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to form dense colonies.
