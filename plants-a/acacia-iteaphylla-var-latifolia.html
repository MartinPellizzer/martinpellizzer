Acacia iteaphylla var. latifolia

Acacia iteaphylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are broader than those of the typical species. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
