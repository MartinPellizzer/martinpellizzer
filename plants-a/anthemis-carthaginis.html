Anthemis carthaginis

Anthemis carthaginis, commonly known as Carthaginian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green or white ray florets, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Anthemis carthaginis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its historical medicinal uses.
