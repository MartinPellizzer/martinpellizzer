Acacia cultrata

Acacia cultrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia cultrata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various native animals.
