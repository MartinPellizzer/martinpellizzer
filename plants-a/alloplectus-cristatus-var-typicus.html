Alloplectus cristatus var. typicus

Alloplectus cristatus var. typicus, commonly known as the spiked alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the cold and windy conditions of its environment.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
