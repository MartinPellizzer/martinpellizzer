Aegilops umbellulata var. typica

Aegilops umbellulata var. typica, commonly known as the slender wild oat, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale blue to greenish flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

This species is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
