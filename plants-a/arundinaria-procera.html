Arundinaria procera

Arundinaria procera, commonly known as the bamboo cane or giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in the spring. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria procera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its structural beauty in gardens.
