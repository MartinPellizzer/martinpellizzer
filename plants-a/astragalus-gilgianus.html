Astragalus gilgianus

Astragalus gilgianus, commonly known as Gilgit milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia, including Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across mountainous landscapes.
