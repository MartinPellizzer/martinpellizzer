Adeneleuterophora tillandsioides

Adeneleuterophora tillandsioides, commonly known as the Tillandsia-like air plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in absorbing moisture from the air. It thrives in humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique structure allows it to survive in various habitats, including tropical and subtropical regions.
