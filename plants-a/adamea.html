Adamea

Adamea, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its velvety, often hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial, with compact rosettes of foliage and upright stems. Adamea species are popular as houseplants due to their attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

They thrive in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
