Acacia burrana

Acacia burrana, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Acacia burrana is often found in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments.

It plays an important role in ecological systems and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
