Astragalus rosellus

Astragalus rosellus, commonly known as the Rosell's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Astragalus rosellus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
