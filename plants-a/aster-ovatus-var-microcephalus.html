Aster ovatus var. microcephalus

Aster ovatus var. microcephalus, commonly known as the dwarf blue aster, is a compact perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
