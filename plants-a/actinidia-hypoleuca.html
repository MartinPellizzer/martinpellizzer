Actinidia hypoleuca

Actinidia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 7 lobed, and its small, greenish-white flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces small, fuzzy berries that ripen to a bright orange color in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Actinidia hypoleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
