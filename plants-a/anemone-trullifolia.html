Anemone trullifolia

Anemone trullifolia, commonly known as the clustered anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Anemone trullifolia is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
