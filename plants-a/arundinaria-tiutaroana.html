Arundinaria tiutaroana

Arundinaria tiutaroana, commonly known as the Tiutaro bamboo, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is a tall, slender grass that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its strong, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Arundinaria tiutaroana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
