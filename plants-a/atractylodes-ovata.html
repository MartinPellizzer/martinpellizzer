Atractylodes ovata

Atractylodes ovata, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herb native to wetland areas in Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, cylindrical inflorescences with small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves and roots that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, clay soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
