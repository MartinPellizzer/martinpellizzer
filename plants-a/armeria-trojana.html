Armeria trojana

Armeria trojana, commonly known as the Trojan thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

Armeria trojana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
