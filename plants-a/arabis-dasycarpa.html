Arabis dasycarpa

Arabis dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The fruit is a short, hairy silique that contains several small seeds.
