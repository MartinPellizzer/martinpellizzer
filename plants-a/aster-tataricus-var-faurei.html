Aster tataricus var. faurei

Aster tataricus var. faurei, commonly known as the Siberian aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
