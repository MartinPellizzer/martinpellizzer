Amblyanthopsis bhotanica

Amblyanthopsis bhotanica, commonly known as the Bhutanese sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and India.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive, spicy fragrance, while its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.
