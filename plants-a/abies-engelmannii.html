Abies engelmannii

Abies engelmannii, commonly known as the Englemann spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green foliage, and distinctive cone-like structures that contain its seeds. The tree typically grows in high-altitude forests and is adapted to cold, moist environments. Its needles are flat and have a leathery texture, while its bark is thick and furrowed.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
