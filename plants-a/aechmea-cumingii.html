Aechmea cumingii

Aechmea cumingii, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom from the center. Its foliage is often variegated with white and green stripes, adding visual interest.

Aechmea cumingii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
