Ananassa sativa var. variegata

Ananassa sativa var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated pineapple, is a cultivar of the pineapple plant characterized by its unique leaf and fruit patterns.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. The leaves of this variety exhibit a striking variegation of green, white, and yellow, enhancing its ornamental value. The fruit, while similar to the common pineapple, may have a slightly different flavor profile and texture.

This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
