Aspilia callosa

Aspilia callosa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often covered in small, raised bumps, giving it a distinctive texture.

Aspilia callosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
