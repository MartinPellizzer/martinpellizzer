Acaena magellanica subsp. grandiscapa

Acaena magellanica subsp.

grandiscapa, commonly known as the Magellanic burrweed, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its dense, spiny bracts that enclose the small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
