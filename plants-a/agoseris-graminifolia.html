Agoseris graminifolia

Agoseris graminifolia, commonly known as the grassleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats like grasslands and open woodlands.

Agoseris graminifolia is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
