Asarum longipedunculatum

Asarum longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-pedunculate wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Asarum longipedunculatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
