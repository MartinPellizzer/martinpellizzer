Arum decurrens

Arum decurrens, commonly known as the arrow arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-bearing inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are typically green or white, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by insects.

Arum decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
