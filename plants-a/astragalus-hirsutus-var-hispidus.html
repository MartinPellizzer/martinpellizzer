Astragalus hirsutus var. hispidus

Astragalus hirsutus var. hispidus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in raceme clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, deserts, and disturbed areas.

Astragalus hirsutus var. hispidus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
