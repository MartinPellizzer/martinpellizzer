Astragalus baibutensis var. macropetalus

Astragalus baibutensis var. macropetalus, commonly known as the large-petaled milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pale pink to purple in color. Its root system is extensive and adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
