Anatherostipa mucronata

Anatherostipa mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it forms dense clumps near water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often concealed by the foliage, making it less conspicuous.

Anatherostipa mucronata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
