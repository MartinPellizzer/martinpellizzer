Acacia suffrutescens

Acacia suffrutescens, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and slender, often thornless stems. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery or greyish pubescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia suffrutescens is known for its ecological importance, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
