Astragalus paralurgiformis

Astragalus paralurgiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus paralurgiformis is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
