Aristida rhizomophora

Aristida rhizomophora, commonly known as the tufted grass or prairie dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, pale green to brownish spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, contributing to soil stabilization and erosion control.

Aristida rhizomophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
