Abutilon hirtum var. heterotrichum

Abutilon hirtum var. heterotrichum, commonly known as velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped bracts that enclose the small flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
