Astragalus refractus

Astragalus refractus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus refractus is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
