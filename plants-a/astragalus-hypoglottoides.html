Astragalus hypoglottoides

Astragalus hypoglottoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia, including parts of China and Mongolia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Astragalus hypoglottoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
