Alyssum alpestre var. argenteum

Alyssum alpestre var. argenteum, commonly known as silver alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both garden and landscape design.
