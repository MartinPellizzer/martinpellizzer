Aster elongatus var. candollei

Aster elongatus var. candollei, commonly known as the slender aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to botanical collections.
