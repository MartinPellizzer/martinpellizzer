Aristotelia australasica

Aristotelia australasica, commonly known as the New Zealand blackwood, is a small evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
