Acacia xanthina

Acacia xanthina, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to golden-green flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia xanthina is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
