Astragalus deluensis

Astragalus deluensis, commonly known as the Deluens milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
