Abutilon virgatum var. typica

Abutilon virgatum var. typica, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are adaptations that help it thrive in moist, sandy soils.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
