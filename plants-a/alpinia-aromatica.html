Alpinia aromatica

Alpinia aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or sweet ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a sweet fragrance, and it produces small, yellow fruits.

Alpinia aromatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
