Acacia rigens var. longifolia

Acacia rigens var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and glossy. The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting various pollinators. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
