Astragalus warackensis

Astragalus warackensis, commonly known as the Waracka milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus warackensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife in its ecosystem.
