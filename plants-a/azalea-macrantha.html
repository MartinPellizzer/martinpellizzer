Azalea macrantha

Azalea macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered azalea, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the Rhododendron genus, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces fragrant, funnel-shaped blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Azalea macrantha thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
