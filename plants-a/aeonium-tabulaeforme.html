Aeonium tabulaeforme

Aeonium tabulaeforme, commonly known as the stonecrop aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Aeonium tabulaeforme is popular in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
