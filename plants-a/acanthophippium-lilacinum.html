Acanthophippium lilacinum

Acanthophippium lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac-flowered acanthophippium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, lilac-colored bracts that surround small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in desert environments.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
