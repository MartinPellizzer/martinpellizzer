Astragalus brachysemia

Astragalus brachysemia, commonly known as narrow-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus brachysemia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
