Alsine tenuifolia f. minima

Alsine tenuifolia f. minima, commonly known as the dwarf alsike.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous annual plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

This variety is typically found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in moist, temperate environments.
