Alchemilla nietofelineri

Alchemilla nietofelineri, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, which are clustered in compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
