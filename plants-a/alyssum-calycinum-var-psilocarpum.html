Alyssum calycinum var. psilocarpum

Alyssum calycinum var. psilocarpum, commonly known as the purple alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces violet or purple blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The leaves are linear and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.
