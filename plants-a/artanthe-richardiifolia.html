Artanthe richardiifolia

Artanthe richardiifolia, commonly known as the richardiifolia artanthe, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, fleshy, silver-green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
