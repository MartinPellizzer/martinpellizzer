Aragallus collinus

Aragallus collinus, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with a fine pubescence.

Aragallus collinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
