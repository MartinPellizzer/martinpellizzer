Amygdalus communis subvar. macrocarpa

Amygdalus communis subvar. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded peach.

This variety is characterized by its large, fleshy fruits with a thick, fuzzy skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces showy, white to pink flowers in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The fruit contains a single large stone or pit, which is the seed of the plant.
