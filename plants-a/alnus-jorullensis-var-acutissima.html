Alnus jorullensis var. acutissima

Alnus jorullensis var. acutissima, commonly known as the sharp-leaved alder, is a variant of the alder genus native to specific regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its narrow, acuminate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
