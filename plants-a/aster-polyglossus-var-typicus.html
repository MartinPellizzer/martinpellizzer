Aster polyglossus var. typicus

Aster polyglossus var. typicus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense, flat clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
