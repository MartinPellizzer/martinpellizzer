Aspilia baumii

Aspilia baumii, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmate, lobed leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Aspilia baumii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
