Artanthe lucaeana

Artanthe lucaeana, commonly known as the Lucaeana aster, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Artanthe lucaeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
