Astragalus howellii var. aberrans

Astragalus howellii var. aberrans, commonly known as Howell's milkvetch variant aberrans, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and sagebrush habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
