Agave geminiflora var. filifera

Agave geminiflora var. filifera, commonly known as the threadleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves with white marginal teeth, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers, typically blooming after several decades. It is well adapted to full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
