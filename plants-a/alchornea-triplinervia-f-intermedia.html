Alchornea triplinervia f. intermedia

Alchornea triplinervia f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate three-veined alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a prominent three-veined pattern, which is characteristic of the genus Alchornea. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
