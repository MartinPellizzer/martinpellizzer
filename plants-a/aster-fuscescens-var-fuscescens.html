Aster fuscescens var. fuscescens

Aster fuscescens var. fuscescens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a yellow central disk, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its nectar attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
