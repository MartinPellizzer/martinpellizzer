Asclepias Gomphocarpoides

Asclepias gomphocarpoides is a rare and fascinating milkweed species native to the southeastern United States. It is known for its unique floral structure and role in supporting monarch butterfly populations. This plant thrives in sandy soils and open habitats, often found in coastal areas. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive fruiting bodies, which are unlike those of other milkweeds. Despite its ecological importance, it remains underappreciated and poorly studied in the plant world.
