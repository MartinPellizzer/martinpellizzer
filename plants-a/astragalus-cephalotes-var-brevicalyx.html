Astragalus cephalotes var. brevicalyx

Astragalus cephalotes var. brevicalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is native to regions of Asia, including China and Mongolia. It possesses a deep root system, which allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
