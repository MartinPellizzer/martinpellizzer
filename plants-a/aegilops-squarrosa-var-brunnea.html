Aegilops squarrosa var. brunnea

Aegilops squarrosa var. brunnea, commonly known as the brown spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive brownish-purple coloration of its inflorescence. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a loose, open panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological features and potential ecological roles.
