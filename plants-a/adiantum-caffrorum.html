Adiantum caffrorum

Adiantum caffrorum, commonly known as the Cape maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky crevices or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a glossy appearance, with a unique ability to curl up when dry.

Adiantum caffrorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
