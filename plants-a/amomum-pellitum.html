Amomum pellitum

Amomum pellitum, commonly known as the small cardamom or lesser cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clustered, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, oblong fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
