Acalypha polystachya

Acalypha polystachya, commonly known as the flame acanthus, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like bracts that range in color from red to orange, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha polystachya is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its striking foliage and ease of care.
