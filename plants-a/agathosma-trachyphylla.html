Agathosma trachyphylla

Agathosma trachyphylla, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a creeping growth habit with multiple stems that can form dense mats.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are used in aromatherapy and as a flavoring agent.
