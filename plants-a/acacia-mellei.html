Acacia mellei

Acacia mellei, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, and it is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia mellei is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of honey from its nectar.
