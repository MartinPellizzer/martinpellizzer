Acalypha noronhae

Acalypha noronhae, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Acalypha noronhae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
