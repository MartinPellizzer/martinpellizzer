Aconitum orientale var. chloranthum

Aconitum orientale var. chloranthum, commonly known as the Chinese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of East Asia.

It features striking blue to purple spires of flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately compound leaves with a distinctive, waxy texture and a deep green color. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, which are used in traditional medicine but require careful handling.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
