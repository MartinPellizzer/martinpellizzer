Aniba riparia

Aniba riparia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Pimenta-do-Brazi*, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces berries that are highly aromatic and used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in perfumery and herbal remedies.

Aniba riparia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near rivers and streams.
