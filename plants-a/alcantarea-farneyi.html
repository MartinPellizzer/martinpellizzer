Alcantarea farneyi

Alcantarea farneyi, commonly known as the Farney's cycad, is a striking plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its large, fern-like leaves and a stout, unbranched trunk. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable subject in botanical studies.

Alcantarea farneyi is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
