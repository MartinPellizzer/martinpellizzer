Astragalus rattanii var. claranus

Astragalus rattanii var. claranus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces pods that contain several oblong seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes harvested for traditional herbal use.
