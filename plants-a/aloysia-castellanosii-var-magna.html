Aloysia castellanosii var. magna

Aloysia castellanosii var. magna, commonly known as the giant silver bush, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
