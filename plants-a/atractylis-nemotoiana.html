Atractylis nemotoiana

Atractylis nemotoiana, commonly known as the Japanese gingergrass, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Atractylis nemotoiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
