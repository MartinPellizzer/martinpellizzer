Alopecurus × nigricans

Alopecurus × nigricans, commonly known as black bentgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, coarse foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Alopecurus × nigricans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
