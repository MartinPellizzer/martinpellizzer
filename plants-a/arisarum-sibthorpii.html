Arisarum sibthorpii

Arisarum sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered around the spadix, which is often brightly colored.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in warm, humid environments.
