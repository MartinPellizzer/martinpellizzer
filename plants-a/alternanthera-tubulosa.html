Alternanthera tubulosa

Alternanthera tubulosa, commonly known as the red-veined joyweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red veins on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Alternanthera tubulosa is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
