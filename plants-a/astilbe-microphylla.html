Astilbe microphylla

Astilbe microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf astilbe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features delicate, feather-like panicles of flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, or red, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Astilbe microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add texture and color to shaded landscapes.
