Artemisia gmelinii subsp. intermedia

Artemisia gmelinii subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It contains volatile oils and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
