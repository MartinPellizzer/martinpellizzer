Acacia colletioides var. nyssophylla

Acacia colletioides var. nyssophylla, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its wood is durable and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including construction and tool-making.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
