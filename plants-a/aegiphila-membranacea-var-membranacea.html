Aegiphila membranacea var. membranacea

Aegiphila membranacea var. membranacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
