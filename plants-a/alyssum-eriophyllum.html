Alyssum eriophyllum

Alyssum eriophyllum, commonly known as the woolly alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, rounded leaves and a mat-forming growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Alyssum eriophyllum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
