Aridaria gracilis

Aridaria gracilis, commonly known as the slender desert grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It possesses narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss through transpiration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Aridaria gracilis plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
