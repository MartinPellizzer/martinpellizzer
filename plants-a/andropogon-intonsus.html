Andropogon intonsus

Andropogon intonsus, commonly known as inland speargrass, is a tall perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Andropogon intonsus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
