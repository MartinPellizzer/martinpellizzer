Acacia walteri

Acacia walteri, commonly known as Walter's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact inflorescences, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, consisting of many small, paired leaflets.

Acacia walteri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
