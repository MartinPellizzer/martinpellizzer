Acacia adsurgens

Acacia adsurgens, commonly known as the climbing acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and entangle other vegetation. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Acacia adsurgens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
