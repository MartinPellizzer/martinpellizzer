Andropogon squarrosus var. nigritanus

Andropogon squarrosus var. nigritanus, commonly known as blackstem bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, rigid stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is typically found in swamps, marshes, and along creek banks. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing minute flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Blackstem bluestem plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
