Actinostemon glaziovii

Actinostemon glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's spiderwort, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Actinostemon glaziovii thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.
