Angolluma hesperidum

Angolluma hesperidum, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a citrus fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of round, orange-colored fruits. The fruit contains a segmented pulp surrounded by a white, spongy membrane called the pith.

Angolluma hesperidum is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
