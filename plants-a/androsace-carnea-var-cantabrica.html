Androsace carnea var. cantabrica

Androsace carnea var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian pink rockrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, white pubescence.
