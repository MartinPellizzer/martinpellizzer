Antidesma pentandrum var. hiiranense

Antidesma pentandrum var. hiiranense, commonly known as the Hiiranense antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of East Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
