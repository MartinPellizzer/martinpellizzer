Astragalus ammodendron subsp. transcaspicus

Astragalus ammodendron subsp.

transcaspicus, commonly known as the Transcaspian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as steppes and sandy soils. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh conditions and plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
