Argyranthemum adauctum subsp. gracile

Argyranthemum adauctum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as the slender argyranthemum, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular plant in horticultural settings.
