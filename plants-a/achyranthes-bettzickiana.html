Achyranthes bettzickiana

Achyranthes bettzickiana, commonly known as the Indian mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, three-lobed seed capsules. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
