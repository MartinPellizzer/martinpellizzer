Acacia tepicana

Acacia tepicana, commonly known as the Mexican acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Acacia tepicana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
