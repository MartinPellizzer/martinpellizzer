Astragalus woronowii

Astragalus woronowii, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in arid and semi-arid regions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
