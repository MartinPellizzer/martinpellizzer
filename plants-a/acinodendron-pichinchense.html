Acinodendron pichinchense

Acinodendron pichinchense, commonly known as the Pichincha acacia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in the cloud forests of the Andes. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
