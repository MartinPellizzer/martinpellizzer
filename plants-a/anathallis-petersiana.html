Anathallis petersiana

Anathallis petersiana, commonly known as the Petersian ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces striking, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the winter months. It has a unique reproductive strategy involving mycorrhizal fungi, which play a crucial role in its nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and its restricted distribution, it is considered endangered and is subject to conservation efforts.
