Astragalus declinatus subsp. pinetorum

Astragalus declinatus subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the pine mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as pine forests and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to cream-colored, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
