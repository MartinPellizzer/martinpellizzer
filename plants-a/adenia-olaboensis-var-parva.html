Adenia olaboensis var. parva

Adenia olaboensis var. parva, commonly known as the small adenia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa, particularly Tanzania and Kenya.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae, which includes passionflowers, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it bears yellowish-orange fruits that are edible.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
