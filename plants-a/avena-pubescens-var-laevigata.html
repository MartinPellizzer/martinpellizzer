Avena pubescens var. laevigata

Avena pubescens var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are typically covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian areas.
