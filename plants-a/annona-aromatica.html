Annona aromatica

Annona aromatica, commonly known as the sweet sop or custard apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique, creamy fruit with a sweet and aromatic flavor, often used in desserts and beverages. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Annona aromatica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
