Astragalus menziesii

Astragalus menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus menziesii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native ecosystem.
