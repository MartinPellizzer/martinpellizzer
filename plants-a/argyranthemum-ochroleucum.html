Argyranthemum ochroleucum

Argyranthemum ochroleucum, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often forming a single flower head per stem. It is a low-growing perennial with silvery-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens.

Argyranthemum ochroleucum is often used in floral arrangements and as a decorative plant due to its delicate appearance.
