Adenopappus

Adenopappus, commonly known as the "hairy pappus" plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the Asteraceae family.

These plants are characterized by their unique pappus structures, which are modified bracts that aid in seed dispersal. Adenopappus species typically have small, daisy-like flowers with ray and disk florets. They are native to arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in Africa and the Middle East.

The genus is known for its ecological adaptability and role in supporting local pollinators.
