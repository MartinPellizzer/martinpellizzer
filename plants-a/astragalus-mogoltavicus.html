Astragalus mogoltavicus

Astragalus mogoltavicus, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the steppes and semi-desert regions of Central Asia, including parts of Mongolia and China. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands.

Astragalus mogoltavicus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
