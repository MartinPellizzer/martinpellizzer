Ardisia maxima

Ardisia maxima, commonly known as the large-leaved beautyberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright purple berries that are a striking feature, often attracting birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
