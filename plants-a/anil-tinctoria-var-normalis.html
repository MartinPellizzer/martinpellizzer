Anil tinctoria var. normalis

Anil tinctoria var. normalis, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct aromatic scent.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its visual appeal and drought tolerance.
