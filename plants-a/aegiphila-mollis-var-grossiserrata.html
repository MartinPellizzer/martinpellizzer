Aegiphila mollis var. grossiserrata

Aegiphila mollis var. grossiserrata, commonly known as the rough-leaved aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with coarsely serrated margins, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
