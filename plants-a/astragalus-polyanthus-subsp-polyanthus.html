Astragalus polyanthus subsp. polyanthus

Astragalus polyanthus subsp.

polyanthus, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
