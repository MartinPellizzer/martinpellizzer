Aruncus sylvester subsp. acuminatus

Aruncus sylvester subsp.

acuminatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white to cream-colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It is valued for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial to full shade.
