Astragalus tenuirugis

Astragalus tenuirugis, commonly known as narrow-ribbed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Astragalus tenuirugis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
