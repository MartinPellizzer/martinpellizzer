Aniba foeniculacea

Aniba foeniculacea, commonly known as Brazilian pennyroyal, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Aniba foeniculacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
