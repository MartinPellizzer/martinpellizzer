Asarum splendens

Asarum splendens, commonly known as the shining wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces a unique, hooded flower that is often camouflaged among its foliage. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
