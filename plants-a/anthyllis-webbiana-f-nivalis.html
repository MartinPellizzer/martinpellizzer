Anthyllis webbiana f. nivalis

Anthyllis webbiana f. nivalis, commonly known as the alpine anthyllis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its foliage consists of small, trifoliate leaves, and it is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
