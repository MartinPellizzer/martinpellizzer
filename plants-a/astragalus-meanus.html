Astragalus meanus

Astragalus meanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Astragalus meanus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

It is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
