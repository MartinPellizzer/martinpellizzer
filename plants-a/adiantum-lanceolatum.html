Adiantum lanceolatum

Adiantum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and finely divided. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum lanceolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
