Aspalathus candicans

Aspalathus candicans, commonly known as the silver bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.

Aspalathus candicans is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
