Acalypha cuspidata var. amblyodonta

Acalypha cuspidata var. amblyodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions for horticultural purposes.
