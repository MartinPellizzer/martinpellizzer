Ammannia ritchiei

Ammannia ritchiei, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
