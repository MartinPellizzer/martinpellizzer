Anaphalis margaritacea var. occidentalis

Anaphalis margaritacea var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white, button-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms.
