Alyssum campestre var. genuinum

Alyssum campestre var. genuinum, commonly known as the common alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-green foliage with glandular dots, which contribute to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
