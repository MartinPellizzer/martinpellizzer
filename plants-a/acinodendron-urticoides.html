Acinodendron urticoides

Acinodendron urticoides, commonly known as the stinging nettle tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its stinging hairs on the leaves and stems, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along riverbanks.

The species has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
