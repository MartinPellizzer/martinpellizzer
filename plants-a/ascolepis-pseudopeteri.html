Ascolepis pseudopeteri

Ascolepis pseudopeteri, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. It is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Ascolepis pseudopeteri is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
