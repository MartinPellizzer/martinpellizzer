Allium stellerianum

Allium stellerianum, commonly known as the Steller's onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in the summer. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Allium stellerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
