Anthericum whytei

Anthericum whytei, commonly known as the Whyte's arum, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Anthericum whytei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical study.
