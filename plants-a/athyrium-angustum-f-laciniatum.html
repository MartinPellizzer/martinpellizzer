Athyrium angustum f. laciniatum

Athyrium angustum f. laciniatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with slender, upright stems that produce feathery, bi- or tri-pinnate leaves. Its foliage is typically bright green, with a slightly glossy texture, and it thrives in shady, moist environments.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
