Arenaria puranensis

Arenaria puranensis, commonly known as the Puran sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, often blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy and rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, which helps in reducing water loss.

Arenaria puranensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential in xerophytic landscaping.
