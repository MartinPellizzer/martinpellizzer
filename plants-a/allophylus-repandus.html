Allophylus repandus

Allophylus repandus, commonly known as the broadleaf alder, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds through wind.

Allophylus repandus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.
