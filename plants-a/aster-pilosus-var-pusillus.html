Aster pilosus var. pusillus

Aster pilosus var. pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf hairy aster, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with purple to blue petals that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
