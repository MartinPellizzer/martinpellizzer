Aeonium cuneatum

Aeonium cuneatum, commonly known as the Campfire Plant, is a succulent species native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that range in color from bright green to reddish-pink, depending on light exposure. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers on tall, branching stalks during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Aeonium cuneatum is also noted for its ability to propagate through offsets, which makes it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
