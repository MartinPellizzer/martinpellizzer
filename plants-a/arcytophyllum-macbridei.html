Arcytophyllum macbridei

Arcytophyllum macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's sage, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and has a deep root system for water retention. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.

Arcytophyllum macbridei is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
