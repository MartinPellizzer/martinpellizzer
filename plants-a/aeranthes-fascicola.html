Aeranthes fascicola

Aeranthes fascicola, commonly known as the clustered broom.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its dense, wiry stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads. Aeranthes fascicola typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
