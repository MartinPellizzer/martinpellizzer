Argythamnia argothamnoides

Argythamnia argothamnoides, commonly known as the desert sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
