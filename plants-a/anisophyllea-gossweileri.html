Anisophyllea gossweileri

Anisophyllea gossweileri, commonly known as the Gossweiler's anisophyllea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Anisophylleaceae and is characterized by its unique, opposite, and often asymmetrical leaf arrangement. The plant typically features simple, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale yellow, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Anisophyllea gossweileri is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive appearance.
