Aegilops umbellulata subsp. transcaucasica

Aegilops umbellulata subsp.

transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian rye grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications.
