Adenocarpus complicatus var. glandulosa

Adenocarpus complicatus var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dry fruits called legumes, which contain the seeds. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The glandular hairs on its stems and leaves are a distinguishing botanical feature.
