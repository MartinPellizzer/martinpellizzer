Alyssum dentatum

Alyssum dentatum, commonly known as the toothed alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has fuzzy, gray-green leaves with a distinctive toothed edge, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum dentatum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
