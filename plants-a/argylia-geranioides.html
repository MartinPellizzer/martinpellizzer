Argylia geranioides

Argylia geranioides, commonly known as the marsh geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, marshes, and pond edges. The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Argylia geranioides plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
