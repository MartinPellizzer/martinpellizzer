Aristolochia kachinensis

Aristolochia kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin arrow vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble a snake or a horn, hence its common name. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a woody vine structure that can grow quite long. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
