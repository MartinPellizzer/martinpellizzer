Acanthosyris platensis

Acanthosyris platensis, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
