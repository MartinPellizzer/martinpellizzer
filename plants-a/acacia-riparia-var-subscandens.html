Acacia riparia var. subscandens

Acacia riparia var. subscandens, commonly known as the river acacia or slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, sandy soils near watercourses and is often found in riparian zones.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
