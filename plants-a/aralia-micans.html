Aralia micans

Aralia micans, commonly known as the evergreen aralia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into several narrow, pointed lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Aralia micans thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation in warm climates.
