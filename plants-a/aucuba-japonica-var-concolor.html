Aucuba japonica var. concolor

Aucuba japonica var. concolor, commonly known as the Japanese aucuba, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green, ovate leaves that remain vibrant throughout the year. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in shaded garden landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
