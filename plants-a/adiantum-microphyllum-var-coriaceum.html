Adiantum microphyllum var. coriaceum

Adiantum microphyllum var. coriaceum, commonly known as the tough fern or hardy maidenhair fern, is a species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its rhizomes are short and often covered with a layer of fine roots.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
