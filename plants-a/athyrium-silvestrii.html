Athyrium silvestrii

Athyrium silvestrii, commonly known as the silvery wood fern, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to anchor itself in rocky or soil-rich substrates.

Athyrium silvestrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
