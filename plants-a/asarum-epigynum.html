Asarum epigynum

Asarum epigynum, commonly known as wild betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, reddish-brown, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, it contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic and can cause severe health issues.
