Aquilegia vulgaris proles platysepala

Aquilegia vulgaris proles platysepala, commonly known as the flat-sepal columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, purple, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound, pinnate leaves with finely divided lobes, and its stems are typically erect and branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

The flat sepals of its flowers are a distinguishing characteristic, setting it apart from other Aquilegia species.
