Acer pictum var. mayrii

Acer pictum var. mayrii, commonly known as the Mayrii maple, is a variant of the striped maple native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, yellow, and white patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its small, red samaras are produced in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

This cultivar is valued for its unique foliage and adaptability to woodland gardens.
