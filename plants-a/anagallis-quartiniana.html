Anagallis quartiniana

Anagallis quartiniana, commonly known as the Quartin's pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herbaceous species with slender, ascending stems and opposite leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a spurred structure at the base. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Anagallis quartiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
