Asplenium cardiophyllum subsp. ikenoi

Asplenium cardiophyllum subsp.

ikenoi, commonly known as the Ikeno's spleenwort, is a fern species belonging to the family Aspleniaceae. It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length.

The plant has a rosette-like growth habit, with leaves arranged in a compact, circular pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.

This subspecies is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists studying fern diversity.
