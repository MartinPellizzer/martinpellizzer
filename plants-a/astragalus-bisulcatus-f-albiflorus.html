Astragalus bisulcatus f. albiflorus

Astragalus bisulcatus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
