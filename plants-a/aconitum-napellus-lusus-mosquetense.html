Aconitum napellus lusus mosquetense

Aconitum napellus lusus mosquetense, commonly known as the Mosquettense Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features large, palmate leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
