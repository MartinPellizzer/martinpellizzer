Anthemis poecilolepis

Anthemis poecilolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
