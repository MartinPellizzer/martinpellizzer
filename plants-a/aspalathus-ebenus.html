Aspalathus ebenus

Aspalathus ebenus, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Aspalathus ebenus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local biodiversity.
