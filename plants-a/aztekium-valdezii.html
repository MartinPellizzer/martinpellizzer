Aztekium valdezii

Aztekium valdezii, commonly known as the Valdez's aztekium, is a rare and striking cactus native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the top of the stem, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Aztekium valdezii is highly prized by cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and challenging cultivation requirements.
