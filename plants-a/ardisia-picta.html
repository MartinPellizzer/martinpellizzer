Ardisia picta

Ardisia picta, commonly known as the spotted ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Following flowering, it develops bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds.

Ardisia picta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
