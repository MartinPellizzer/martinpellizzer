Astragalus ferruminatus

Astragalus ferruminatus, commonly known as the ironweed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus ferruminatus is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by Indigenous peoples for its purported health benefits.
