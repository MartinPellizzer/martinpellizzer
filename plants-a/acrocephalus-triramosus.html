Acrocephalus triramosus

Acrocephalus triramosus, commonly known as the three-branched reed, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Acrocephalus triramosus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
