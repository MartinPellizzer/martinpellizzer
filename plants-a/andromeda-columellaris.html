Andromeda columellaris

Andromeda columellaris, commonly known as the Carolina alder, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its small, dark purple berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The shrub is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in riparian habitats.
