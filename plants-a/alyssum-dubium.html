Alyssum dubium

Alyssum dubium, commonly known as the common alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alyssum dubium is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
