Arundo leersii

Arundo leersii, commonly known as the Mexican reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Arundo leersii is also valued for its ability to thrive in wet or moist soils, making it a common choice for wetland restoration projects.
