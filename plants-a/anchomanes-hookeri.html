Anchomanes hookeri

Anchomanes hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's anchomanes, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in high-altitude environments. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are pollinated by insects.

Anchomanes hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
