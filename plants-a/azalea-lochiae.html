Azalea lochiae

Azalea lochiae, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Azalea lochiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
