Argyreia kurzii

Argyreia kurzii, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and has a climbing habit, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, hence its common name, and are known for their fragrant scent.
