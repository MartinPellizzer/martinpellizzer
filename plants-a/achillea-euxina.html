Achillea euxina

Achillea euxina, commonly known as the Crimean yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces a fibrous root system and is known for its aromatic properties.

Achillea euxina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic benefits.
