Atriplex dimorphostegia f. collenettea

Atriplex dimorphostegia f. collenettea, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant exhibits a prostrate or upright growth habit, depending on environmental conditions. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it thrives in coastal regions with high salinity.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
