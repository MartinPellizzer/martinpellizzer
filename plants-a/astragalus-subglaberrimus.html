Astragalus subglaberrimus

Astragalus subglaberrimus, commonly known as the smooth milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with small, rounded leaflets.

Astragalus subglaberrimus is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitats.
