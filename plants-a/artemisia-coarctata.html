Artemisia coarctata

Artemisia coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves contain essential oils and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Artemisia coarctata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
