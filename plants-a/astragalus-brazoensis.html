Astragalus brazoensis

Astragalus brazoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Astragalus genus. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
