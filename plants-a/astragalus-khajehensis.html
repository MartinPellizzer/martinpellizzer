Astragalus khajehensis

Astragalus khajehensis, commonly known as Khajeh's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
