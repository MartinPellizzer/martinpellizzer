Androsaemum floridum

Androsaemum floridum, commonly known as the Florida sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in wetland environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in marshy areas and is often found in shallow waters or along the edges of ponds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels.
