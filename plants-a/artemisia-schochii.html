Artemisia schochii

Artemisia schochii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Artemisia schochii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
