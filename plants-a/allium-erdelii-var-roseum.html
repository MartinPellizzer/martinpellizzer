Allium erdelii var. roseum

Allium erdelii var. roseum, commonly known as the roseum garlic.

This variant is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring grass-like basal leaves and a cluster of showy, bell-shaped blooms at the top. The plant is native to Central Asia and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
