Arrhostoxylum rubrum var. longifolium

Arrhostoxylum rubrum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved red oxalis, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often reddish in color and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
