Acianthera spicata

Acianthera spicata, commonly known as the spike orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
