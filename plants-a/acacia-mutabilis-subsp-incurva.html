Acacia mutabilis subsp. incurva

Acacia mutabilis subsp.

incurva, commonly known as the variable wattle, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, yellow flowers that appear in spring.

The plant has compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces long, thin pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
