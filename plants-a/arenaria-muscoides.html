Arenaria muscoides

Arenaria muscoides, commonly known as the mossy sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water retention. Its flowers are clustered in tight inflorescences, producing white or pinkish petals that attract pollinators.

Arenaria muscoides plays a role in stabilizing soil in extreme habitats and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.
