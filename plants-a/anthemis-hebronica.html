Anthemis hebronica

Anthemis hebronica, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent.

Anthemis hebronica is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
