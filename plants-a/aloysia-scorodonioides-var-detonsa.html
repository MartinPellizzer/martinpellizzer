Aloysia scorodonioides var. detonsa

Aloysia scorodonioides var. detonsa, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slender leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.

Its essential oils are widely used in perfumes, teas, and natural remedies due to their soothing properties.
