Aceras anthropophorum var. angustata

Aceras anthropophorum var. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and stipulate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing a hardy adaptation to harsh environments. Its narrow, linear leaves and clustered inflorescences are distinctive botanical features.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
