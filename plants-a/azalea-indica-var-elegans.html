Azalea indica var. elegans

Azalea indica var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant azalea, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant pink to red blossoms that attract pollinators. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for landscaping and floral arrangements.
