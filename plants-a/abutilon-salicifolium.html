Abutilon salicifolium

Abutilon salicifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, soft, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Abutilon salicifolium is also valued for its adaptability to various climates and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
