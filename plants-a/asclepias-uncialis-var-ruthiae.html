Asclepias uncialis var. ruthiae

Asclepias uncialis var. ruthiae, commonly known as the Ruth's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture.

This variety is particularly important as a host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting both larval and adult stages of the species.
