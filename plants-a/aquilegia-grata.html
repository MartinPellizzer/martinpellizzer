Aquilegia grata

Aquilegia grata, commonly known as the garden columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals that come in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aquilegia grata is well-suited for gardens and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial to full shade.
