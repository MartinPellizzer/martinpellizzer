Azalea × amoena var. obtusa

Azalea × amoena var. obtusa, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce abundant clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, showcasing a range of colors from white to deep pink. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage, which remains lush throughout the growing season.
