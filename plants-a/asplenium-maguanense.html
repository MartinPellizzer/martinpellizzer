Asplenium maguanense

Asplenium maguanense, commonly known as the Maguanense spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Asplenium maguanense is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, tropical climates.
