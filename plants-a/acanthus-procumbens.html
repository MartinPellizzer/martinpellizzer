Acanthus procumbens

Acanthus procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate acanthus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features broad, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, spiny flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both garden landscapes and horticultural collections.
