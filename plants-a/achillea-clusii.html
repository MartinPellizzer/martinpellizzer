Achillea clusii

Achillea clusii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Achillea clusii is also valued for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat various ailments.
