Agave vilmoriniana

Agave vilmoriniana, commonly known as the Vilmorin's agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave vilmoriniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
