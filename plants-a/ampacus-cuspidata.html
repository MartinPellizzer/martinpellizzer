Ampacus cuspidata

Ampacus cuspidata, commonly known as the spiny pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and moist forests. The plant produces clusters of showy, white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Ampacus cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
