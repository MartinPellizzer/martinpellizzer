Abutilon picardae

Abutilon picardae, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
