Adrorhizon purpurascens

Adrorhizon purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant purple petals and a unique, intricate flower structure typical of orchids. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Adrorhizon purpurascens is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
