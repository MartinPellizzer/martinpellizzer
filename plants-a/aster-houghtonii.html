Aster houghtonii

Aster houghtonii, commonly known as Houghton's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, gray-green leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster houghtonii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
