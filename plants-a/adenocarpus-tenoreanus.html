Adenocarpus tenoreanus

Adenocarpus tenoreanus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, contributing to its common name.

Adenocarpus tenoreanus is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
