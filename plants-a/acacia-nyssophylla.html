Acacia nyssophylla

Acacia nyssophylla, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit and its distinctive, feathery, silky foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Acacia nyssophylla is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
