Aerva viridis

Aerva viridis, commonly known as green aerva, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas.

Aerva viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
