Amelanchier suborbicularis

Amelanchier suborbicularis, commonly known as the eastern serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet and is characterized by its rounded, often multi-stemmed form. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in the summer. Its leaves are ovate to suborbicular in shape, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

Amelanchier suborbicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
