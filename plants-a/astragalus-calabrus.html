Astragalus calabrus

Astragalus calabrus, commonly known as the Calabrian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly the region of Calabria in Italy.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.

Astragalus calabrus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
