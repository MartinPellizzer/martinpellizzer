Allium gubanovii

Allium gubanovii, commonly known as the Caucasian leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which is often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium gubanovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in its native habitat.
