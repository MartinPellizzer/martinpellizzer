Arisaema ringens var. viridiflorum

Arisaema ringens var. viridiflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered arisaema, is a unique variant of the arrowhead plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and its distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered around a central spike, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unusual foliage and floral display.
