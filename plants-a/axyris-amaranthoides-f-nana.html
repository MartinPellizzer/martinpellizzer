Axyris amaranthoides f. nana

Axyris amaranthoides f. nana, commonly known as dwarf amaranth, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
