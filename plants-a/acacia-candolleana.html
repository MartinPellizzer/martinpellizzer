Acacia candolleana

Acacia candolleana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of India.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its hardy nature makes it well-suited for dry climates and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The wood of Acacia candolleana is also valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts.
