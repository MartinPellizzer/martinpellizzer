Acacia clavata

Acacia clavata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia clavata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and some resistance to salt.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and provides habitat and food for native wildlife.
