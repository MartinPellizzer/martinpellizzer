Aristolochia rojasiana

Aristolochia rojasiana, commonly known as the Peruvian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and intricate, pipe-like flowers that are often dark purple or brown in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition, including various aristolochic acids. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
