Avena filifolia f. velutina

Avena filifolia f. velutina, commonly known as velvet oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its dense, soft, and velvety foliage.

This plant is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. The inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, with small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually greenish or pale brown.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
