Aspalathus tridentata subsp. fragilis

Aspalathus tridentata subsp.

fragilis, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
