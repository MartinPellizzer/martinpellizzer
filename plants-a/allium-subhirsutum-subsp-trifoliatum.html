Allium subhirsutum subsp. trifoliatum

Allium subhirsutum subsp.

trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaf garlic or Persian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant is characterized by its subterranean bulb, which is composed of numerous small, white, fleshy scales. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild garlic-like flavor.
