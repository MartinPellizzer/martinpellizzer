Azara tomentosa

Azara tomentosa, commonly known as the silky azara, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Azara tomentosa is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to different soil types and mild climates makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
