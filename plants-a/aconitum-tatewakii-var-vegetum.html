Aconitum tatewakii var. vegetum

Aconitum tatewakii var. vegetum, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
