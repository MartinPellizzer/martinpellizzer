Allium stearnianum subsp. stearnianum

Allium stearnianum subsp.

stearnianum, commonly known as the purple garlic. It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of purple flowers.

The plant produces small, bulb-like structures that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary uses.
