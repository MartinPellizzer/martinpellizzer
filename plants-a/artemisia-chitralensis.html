Artemisia chitralensis

Artemisia chitralensis, commonly known as Chitral wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains volatile oils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties. It thrives in cold, dry climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
