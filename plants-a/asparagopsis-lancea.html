Asparagopsis lancea

Asparagopsis lancea, commonly known as the slender asparagus fern, is a perennial evergreen plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like, arching stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Asparagopsis lancea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
