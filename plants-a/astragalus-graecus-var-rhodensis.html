Astragalus graecus var. rhodensis

Astragalus graecus var. rhodensis, commonly known as the Rhodope milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of the local flora and its potential ecological value.
