Amomum brachychilum

Amomum brachychilum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in a spike, followed by dark brown, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Amomum brachychilum is typically grown in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.
