Aristida junciformis subsp. macilenta

Aristida junciformis subsp.

macilenta, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas.

The plant has slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing two to three small, bristly flowers.

This subspecies is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
