Acalypha fruticulosa

Acalypha fruticulosa, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has a bushy, compact form. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha fruticulosa thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
