Agalma rugosum

Agalma rugosum, commonly known as the rough agalma, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

Agalma rugosum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shady garden settings.
