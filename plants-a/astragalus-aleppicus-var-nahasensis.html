Astragalus aleppicus var. nahasensis

Astragalus aleppicus var. nahasensis, commonly known as the Aleppo milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, oval-shaped leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of leaflets.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
