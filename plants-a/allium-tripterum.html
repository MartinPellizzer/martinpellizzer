Allium tripterum

Allium tripterum, commonly known as the three-lobed garlic or wild leeks, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbous stem is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Allium tripterum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
