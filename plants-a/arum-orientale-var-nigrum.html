Arum orientale var. nigrum

Arum orientale var. nigrum, commonly known as the black arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a deep purple spathe. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by insects.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
