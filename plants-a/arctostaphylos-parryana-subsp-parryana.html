Arctostaphylos parryana subsp. parryana

Arctostaphylos parryana subsp.

parryana, commonly known as Parry manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ecologically significant species in its native range.
