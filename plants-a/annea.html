Annea

Annea, commonly known as the common rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland and rocky habitats. It contains volatile oils that give it a strong, peppery scent and have been used in traditional medicine.

Annea is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and historical significance.
