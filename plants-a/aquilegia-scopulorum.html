Aquilegia scopulorum

Aquilegia scopulorum, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with deep lobes, and the plant is often found in high-altitude environments.

Aquilegia scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
