Angraecum keniae

Angraecum keniae, commonly known as the Kenia orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale lavender flowers. The plant produces nectar-rich blossoms that are adapted to attract specific pollinators, such as the giant hawkmoth. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Angraecum keniae is known for its slow growth and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.
