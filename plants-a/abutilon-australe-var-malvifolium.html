Abutilon australe var. malvifolium

Abutilon australe var. malvifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its fibrous stems and hairy leaves are notable botanical features that contribute to its unique appearance.
