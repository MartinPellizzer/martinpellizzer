Aloysia polystachya

Aloysia polystachya, commonly known as the Spanish sage or blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is part of the Verbenaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its essential oils are also used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
