Ancistrocactus brevihamatus

Ancistrocactus brevihamatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in clusters of spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by bright red fruits. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Ancistrocactus brevihamatus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
