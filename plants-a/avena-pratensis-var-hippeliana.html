Avena pratensis var. hippeliana

Avena pratensis var. hippeliana, commonly known as the meadow oat or sweet vernal grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, fluffy spikelets that mature into pale yellow to brown seeds. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological and agricultural systems.
