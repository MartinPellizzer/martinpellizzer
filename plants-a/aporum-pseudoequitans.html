Aporum pseudoequitans

Aporum pseudoequitans, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant's roots are submerged, anchoring it in the muddy bottom of its habitat. Its flowers, which are typically white or pink, bloom above the water and attract pollinators such as beetles.

Aporum pseudoequitans plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
