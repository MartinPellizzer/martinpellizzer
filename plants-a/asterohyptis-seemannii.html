Asterohyptis seemannii

Asterohyptis seemannii, commonly known as the purple-stemmed hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Asterohyptis seemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
