Agave brachystachys var. pubescens

Agave brachystachys var. pubescens, commonly known as the woolly agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and hardy nature.
