Allium narcissiflorum subsp. insubricum

Allium narcissiflorum subsp.

insubricum, commonly known as the slender garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble daffodils.

The plant is characterized by its fragrant, bulbous stem, which is often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is closely related to other Allium species and is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible qualities.
