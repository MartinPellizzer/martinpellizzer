Amaryllis flava

Amaryllis flava, commonly known as the yellow amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow, often with a red or orange throat, during the summer months. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of fleshy, white roots. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Amaryllis flava is also known for its ability to bloom reliably when cared for properly.
