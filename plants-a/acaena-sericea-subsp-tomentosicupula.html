Acaena sericea subsp. tomentosicupula

Acaena sericea subsp.

tomentosicupula, commonly known as the silky burrplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, spiny capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

The plant typically grows in coastal and montane areas, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
