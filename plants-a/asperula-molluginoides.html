Asperula molluginoides

Asperula molluginoides, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Asperula molluginoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
