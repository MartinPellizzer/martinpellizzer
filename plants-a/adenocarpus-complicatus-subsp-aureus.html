Adenocarpus complicatus subsp. aureus

Adenocarpus complicatus subsp.

aureus, commonly known as the golden burweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces distinctive, spiny capsules that enclose its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its yellowish flowers are clustered in small inflorescences, and it thrives in dry, open habitats.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain regions.
