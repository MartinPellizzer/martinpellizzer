Althaea assurgentifolia

Althaea assurgentifolia, commonly known as the upright marshmallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and hairy stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Althaea assurgentifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.
