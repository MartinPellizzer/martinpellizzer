Arabis sagittata

Arabis sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial species that is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
