Alchemilla alpina var. leptoclada

Alchemilla alpina var. leptoclada, commonly known as the slender-leaved alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with a mat-forming habit, often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.
