Andropogon brevifolius var. leptatherus

Andropogon brevifolius var. leptatherus, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, bluish-green leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
