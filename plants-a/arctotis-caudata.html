Arctotis caudata

Arctotis caudata, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically bright yellow with a central disk, blooming from late winter to early summer.

Arctotis caudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
