Azalea schlippenbachii

Azalea schlippenbachii, commonly known as the Schlippenbach azalea, is a species native to China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its foliage consists of glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Azalea schlippenbachii prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
