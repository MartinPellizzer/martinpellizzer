Achillea × anglica

Achillea × anglica, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is native to Europe and has been naturalized in parts of North America.

Achillea × anglica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and astringent qualities.
