Aster magniflorus

Aster magniflorus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall, adding color to gardens during late summer and autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Aster magniflorus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.
