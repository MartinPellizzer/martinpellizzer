Allium deflexum

Allium deflexum, commonly known as the drooping onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces spherical bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Allium deflexum is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
