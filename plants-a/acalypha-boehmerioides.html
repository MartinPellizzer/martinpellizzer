Acalypha boehmerioides

Acalypha boehmerioides, commonly known as the silverleaf copperleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green leaves with prominent veins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Acalypha boehmerioides is also valued for its ability to attract birds and its use in traditional medicine.
