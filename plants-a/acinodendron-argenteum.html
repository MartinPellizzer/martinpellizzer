Acinodendron argenteum

Acinodendron argenteum, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in regions with low rainfall.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
