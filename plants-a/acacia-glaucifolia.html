Acacia glaucifolia

Acacia glaucifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, rounded leaves that are covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its foliage and floral structures are adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

Acacia glaucifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
