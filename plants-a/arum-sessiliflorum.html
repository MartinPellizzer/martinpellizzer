Arum sessiliflorum

Arum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the dwarf arum, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and a unique, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a hooded spathe. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental choice for indoor cultivation. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
