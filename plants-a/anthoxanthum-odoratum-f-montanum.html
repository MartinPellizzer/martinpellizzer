Anthoxanthum odoratum f. montanum

Anthoxanthum odoratum f. montanum, commonly known as the alpine sweet grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne in dense clusters. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing on rocky soils at high elevations.

This ecotype is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and Asia.
