Anthacanthus tetrastichus

Anthacanthus tetrastichus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in composite heads. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments. The plant's structure includes a central stem with alternate leaves and is often found in grasslands and wastelands.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
