Asemeia lindmaniana

Asemeia lindmaniana, commonly known as the Lindman's mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets.

Asemeia lindmaniana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
