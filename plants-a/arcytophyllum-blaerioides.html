Arcytophyllum blaerioides

Arcytophyllum blaerioides, commonly known as the Blaer's sage.

It is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, showy blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
