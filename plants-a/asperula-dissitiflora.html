Asperula dissitiflora

Asperula dissitiflora, commonly known as the clustered wood rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate blooms and compact growth habit.
