Allophylus cobbe var. velutinus

Allophylus cobbe var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet-leaved alderleaf, is a variant of the Allophylus cobbe species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are oval in shape and have a glossy surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks.
