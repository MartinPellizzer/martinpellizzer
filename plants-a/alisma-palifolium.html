Alisma palifolium

Alisma palifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and along the edges of streams. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the water's surface, with long, slender stems that support its small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in wetland habitats.

Alisma palifolium plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
