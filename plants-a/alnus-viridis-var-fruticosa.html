Alnus viridis var. fruticosa

Alnus viridis var. fruticosa, commonly known as the dwarf alder, is a shrub-like variant of the alder tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, triangular leaves and clusters of yellowish catkins that appear in early spring. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils near streams or in wetlands, thriving in temperate climates. It has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which helps improve soil fertility.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
