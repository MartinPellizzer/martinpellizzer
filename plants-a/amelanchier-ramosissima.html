Amelanchier ramosissima

Amelanchier ramosissima, commonly known as the Saskatoon serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It features oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

The Saskatoon serviceberry is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses.
