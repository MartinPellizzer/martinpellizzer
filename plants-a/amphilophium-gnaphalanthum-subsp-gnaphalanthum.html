Amphilophium gnaphalanthum subsp. gnaphalanthum

Amphilophium gnaphalanthum subsp.

gnaphalanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant produces small, densely packed yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a compact inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
