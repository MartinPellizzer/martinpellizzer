Airampoa aurata

Airampoa aurata, commonly known as the golden spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its striking golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are often arranged in dense, terminal panicles. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Airampoa aurata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
