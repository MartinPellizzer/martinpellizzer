Acacia vittata

Acacia vittata, commonly known as the white wattle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features slender, often twisted stems and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny, hairless leaflets.

Acacia vittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
