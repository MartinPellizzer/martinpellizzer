Adenophora izuensis

Adenophora izuensis, commonly known as the Izu bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the summer months.

Adenophora izuensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Morphology

Adenophora izuensis has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter green underside, arranged along the stem.

The stem is upright, somewhat woody at the base, with a rough texture and a brownish color, growing in a spreading clump. The flowers are bell-shaped, clustered in inflorescences, with white to pale blue petals, blooming in summer, and are pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, anchoring the plant in moist, well-drained soil.
