Acaena × tardans

Acaena × tardans, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and showy purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its fruit is a spherical, hairy achene that disperses easily by wind, aiding in its spread.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a weed in some areas outside its native range.
