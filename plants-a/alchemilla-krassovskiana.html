Alchemilla krassovskiana

Alchemilla krassovskiana, commonly known as the Krassovsky lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its common name reflects its historical use in herbal medicine and its resemblance to the traditional lady's mantle plant.
