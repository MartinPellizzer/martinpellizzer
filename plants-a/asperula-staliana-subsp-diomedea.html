Asperula staliana subsp. diomedea

Asperula staliana subsp.

diomedea, commonly known as the Cyprus rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
