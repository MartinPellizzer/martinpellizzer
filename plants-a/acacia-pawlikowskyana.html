Acacia pawlikowskyana

Acacia pawlikowskyana, commonly known as the Pawlikowsky's wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and pinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Acacia pawlikowskyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
