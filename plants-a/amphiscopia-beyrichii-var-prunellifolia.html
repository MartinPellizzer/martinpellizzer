Amphiscopia beyrichii var. prunellifolia

Amphiscopia beyrichii var. prunellifolia, commonly known as the Prunellifolia Sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. It is characterized by its distinctive two-sided (amphiscopic) leaves, which are broader on the upper surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
