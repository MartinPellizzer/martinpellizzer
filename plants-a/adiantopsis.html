Adiantopsis

Adiantopsis, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often adorned with finely divided pinnae. The leaves are typically bright green and have a distinctive, elegant appearance. Adiantopsis species are often found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are commonly cultivated in gardens and as houseplants.
