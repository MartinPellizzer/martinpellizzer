Aligera macroptera var. obtusa

Aligera macroptera var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed aligera, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
