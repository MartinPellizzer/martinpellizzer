Acacia strombulifera

Acacia strombulifera, commonly known as the strombulifera acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across suitable habitats.

Acacia strombulifera plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
