Annona haitiensis subsp. haitiensis

Annona haitiensis subsp.

haitiensis, commonly known as the Haitian soursop, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, dark green leaves that are alternate and ovate in shape. The plant produces large, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by a pear-shaped fruit with a rough, spiny rind.

The fruit contains a white, aromatic pulp with numerous black seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
