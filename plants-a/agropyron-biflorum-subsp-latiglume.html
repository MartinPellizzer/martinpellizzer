Agropyron biflorum subsp. latiglume

Agropyron biflorum subsp.

latiglume, commonly known as the broad-leaved wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with the lemma bearing a distinctive, broad-based awn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
