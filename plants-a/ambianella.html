Ambianella

Ambianella, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal blossoms that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green fruits that contain seeds. Ambianella thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its unique flowering behavior makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
