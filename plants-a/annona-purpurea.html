Annona purpurea

Annona purpurea, commonly known as the purple annona, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are white with a purple tint and are followed by large, green, fleshy fruits that turn yellow when ripe.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, creamy texture, making it a popular fruit in its native regions.
