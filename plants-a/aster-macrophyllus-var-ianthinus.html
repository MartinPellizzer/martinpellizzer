Aster macrophyllus var. ianthinus

Aster macrophyllus var. ianthinus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that resemble small cones, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often has a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
