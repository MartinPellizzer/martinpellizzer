Amberboia

Amberboia, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red tubular flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Amberboia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
