Amygdalus communis var. fragilima

Amygdalus communis var. fragilima, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a variety of the common walnut tree.

It is native to the regions of Persia, now part of modern-day Iran. The plant is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a hard-shelled nut containing edible seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and the high quality of its nuts.
