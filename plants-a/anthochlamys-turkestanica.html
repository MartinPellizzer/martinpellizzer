Anthochlamys turkestanica

Anthochlamys turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Anthochlamys turkestanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
