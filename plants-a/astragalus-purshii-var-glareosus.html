Astragalus purshii var. glareosus

Astragalus purshii var. glareosus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
