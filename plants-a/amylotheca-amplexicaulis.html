Amylotheca amplexicaulis

Amylotheca amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The leaves are typically clasping, tightly wrapping around the stem, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
