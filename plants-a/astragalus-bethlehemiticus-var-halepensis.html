Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. halepensis

Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. halepensis, commonly known as the Jerusalem bittersweet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

This variety is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
