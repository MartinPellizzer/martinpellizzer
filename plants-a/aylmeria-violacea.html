Aylmeria violacea

Aylmeria violacea, commonly known as the purple-flowered aylmeria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Aylmeria violacea is adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.
