Aira caryophyllea var. maior

Aira caryophyllea var. maior, commonly known as the purple false oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear purple to pink flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
