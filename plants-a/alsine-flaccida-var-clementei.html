Alsine flaccida var. clementei

Alsine flaccida var. clementei, commonly known as the slender alsike, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically features slender, hairless stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to wetland and riparian environments.
