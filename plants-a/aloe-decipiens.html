Aloe decipiens

Aloe decipiens, commonly known as the false aloe, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often mistaken for true aloe species due to its similar appearance, hence its common name.

Aloe decipiens is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.
