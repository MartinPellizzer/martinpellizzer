Amygdalus communis var. microcarpa

Amygdalus communis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf peach.

It is a compact variety of the peach tree, characterized by its small, rounded leaves and pale pink flowers. The fruit is typically smaller than standard peaches, with a thin skin and sweet, juicy flesh. This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth and fruit production.
