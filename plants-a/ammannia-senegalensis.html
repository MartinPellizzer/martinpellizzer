Ammannia senegalensis

Ammannia senegalensis, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist soil conditions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia senegalensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
