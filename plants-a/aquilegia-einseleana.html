Aquilegia einseleana

Aquilegia einseleana, commonly known as the Carpathian columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a distinctive lobed structure, and its stems are hairy and upright. It thrives in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Aquilegia einseleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
