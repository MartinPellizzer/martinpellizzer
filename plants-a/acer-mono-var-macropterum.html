Acer mono var. macropterum

Acer mono var. macropterum, commonly known as the giant maple, is a variant of the single-leaf maple native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

This tree is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter. The leaves are typically bright green in summer and turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
