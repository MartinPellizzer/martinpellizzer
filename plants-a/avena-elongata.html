Avena elongata

Avena elongata, commonly known as the slender oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Avena elongata is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Its growth habit and morphology make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
