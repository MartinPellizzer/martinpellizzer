Acacia vulcanica

Acacia vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic acacia, is a unique plant species adapted to extreme environments.

It thrives in volcanic soils and high-altitude regions, showcasing remarkable resilience to harsh conditions. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves that help reduce water loss in arid climates. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting various pollinators.

Acacia vulcanica plays a vital ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
