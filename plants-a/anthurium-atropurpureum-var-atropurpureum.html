Anthurium atropurpureum var. atropurpureum

Anthurium atropurpureum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the black anthurium, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its dark, velvety foliage and vibrant red spadix.

Native to the rainforests of Central and South America, it thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow up to 12 inches in length, showcasing deep purple to black hues. It produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a showy, spadix that adds to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive indoors with proper care.
