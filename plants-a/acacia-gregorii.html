Acacia gregorii

Acacia gregorii, commonly known as the Gregor's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Acacia gregorii plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various animals.
