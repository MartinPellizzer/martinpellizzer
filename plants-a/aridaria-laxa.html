Aridaria laxa

Aridaria laxa, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and succulent stems that store water. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aridaria laxa plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
