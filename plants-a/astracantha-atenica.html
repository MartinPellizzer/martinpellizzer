Astracantha atenica

Astracantha atenica, commonly known as the slender burweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
