Acacia gentlei

Acacia gentlei, commonly known as the gentle acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its distinctive, feathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds through the environment.

Acacia gentlei is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in harsh conditions.
