Aspidosperma dugandii

Aspidosperma dugandii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Copaifera* species, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody pods containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for reforestation and landscape planting in suitable climates.
