Attalea monogyna

Attalea monogyna, commonly known as the palm tree of the Amazon, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with a straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its edible fruits, which are consumed by both humans and wildlife.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food source in tropical rainforest ecosystems.
