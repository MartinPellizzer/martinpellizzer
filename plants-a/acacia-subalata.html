Acacia subalata

Acacia subalata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often silver-gray stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. Acacia subalata thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
