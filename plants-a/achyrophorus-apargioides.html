Achyrophorus apargioides

Achyrophorus apargioides, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
