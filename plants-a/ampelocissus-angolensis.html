Ampelocissus angolensis

Ampelocissus angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan grape, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of tendrils that aid in its growth along supports. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ampelocissus angolensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
