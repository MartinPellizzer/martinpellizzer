Arabis hornungiana

Arabis hornungiana, commonly known as Hornung's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, often blooming in early spring.

Arabis hornungiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
