Aglossorrhyncha lucida

Aglossorrhyncha lucida, commonly known as the shining leafless orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its modified stems for photosynthesis. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination. Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
