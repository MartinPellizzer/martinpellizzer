Amerimnon rubiginosum

Amerimnon rubiginosum, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that turns reddish-brown as it matures. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Amerimnon rubiginosum is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
