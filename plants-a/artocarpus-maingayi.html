Artocarpus maingayi

Artocarpus maingayi, commonly known as the giant breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a unique fruit with a fibrous, inedible outer husk. The fruit contains a starchy, edible pulp that is often used in local cuisines. The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.
