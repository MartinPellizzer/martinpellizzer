Alcea turkeviczii

Alcea turkeviczii, commonly known as Turk's mallow, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, including parts of Turkey and Iran. The plant is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Turk's mallow is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
