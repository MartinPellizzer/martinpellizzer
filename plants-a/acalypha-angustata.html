Acalypha angustata

Acalypha angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically surrounded by bracts that resemble petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.

Acalypha angustata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
