Artemisia glauca var. dracunculina

Artemisia glauca var. dracunculina, commonly known as blue green wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal uses.
