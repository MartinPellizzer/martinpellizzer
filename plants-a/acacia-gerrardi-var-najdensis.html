Acacia gerrardi var. najdensis

Acacia gerrardi var. najdensis, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are borne in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
