Aconitum alatavicum

Aconitum alatavicum, commonly known as the Alatau monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Aconitum alatavicum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or alpine habitats.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
