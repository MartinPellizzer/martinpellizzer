Alchemilla antiropata

Alchemilla antiropata, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla antiropata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
