Aspidium warburgii

Aspidium warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are wiry and creeping, allowing it to spread across the forest floor.

Aspidium warburgii plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
