Acer dasycarpum f. nanum

Acer dasycarpum f. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a compact variant of the species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small stature, typically reaching heights of only 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. Its flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, with a peeling texture that adds to its ornamental appeal.
