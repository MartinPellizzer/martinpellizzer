Amaryllis traubii

Amaryllis traubii, commonly known as the Traub's amaryllis, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a bulbous plant that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Amaryllis traubii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
