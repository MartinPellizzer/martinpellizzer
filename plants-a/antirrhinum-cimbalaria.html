Antirrhinum cimbalaria

Antirrhinum cimbalaria, commonly known as the cimbalaria snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces vivid, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
