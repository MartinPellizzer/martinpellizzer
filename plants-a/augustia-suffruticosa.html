Augustia suffruticosa

Augustia suffruticosa, commonly known as the silver leaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a silvery-blue hue due to the presence of fine, white hairs on the leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal and dry climates.
