Abildgaardia microcephala

Abildgaardia microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a unique species native to the island of Sardinia.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form. The plant features a thick, unbranched trunk with a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. Its reproductive structures include cones, typical of cycads, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
