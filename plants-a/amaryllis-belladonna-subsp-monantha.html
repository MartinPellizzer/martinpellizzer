Amaryllis belladonna subsp. monantha

Amaryllis belladonna subsp.

monantha, commonly known as the single-flowered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

The plant produces a single flower per stem, distinguishing it from other amaryllis species that may have multiple blooms. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture.

This subspecies is popular in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
