Asplenium canaliculatum

Asplenium canaliculatum, commonly known as the channel-veined spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white tomentum. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Asplenium canaliculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
