Alchemilla dasyclada

Alchemilla dasyclada, commonly known as the woolly lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla dasyclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
