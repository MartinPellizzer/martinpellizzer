Achirida iridiflora

Achirida iridiflora, commonly known as the blue-flowered orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue petals and a unique, intricate floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to efficiently capture moisture from the humid environment. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often producing a fragrant aroma that enhances its ecological role.

Due to habitat loss, Achirida iridiflora is considered a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
