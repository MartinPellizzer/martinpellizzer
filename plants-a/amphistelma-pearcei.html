Amphistelma pearcei

Amphistelma pearcei, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to minimize water loss.

Amphistelma pearcei plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
