Aspalathus acanthophylla

Aspalathus acanthophylla, commonly known as the needle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its distinctive needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Aspalathus acanthophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
