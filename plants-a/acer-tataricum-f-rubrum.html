Acer tataricum f. rubrum

Acer tataricum f. rubrum, commonly known as the red maple or red-leaf maple, is a variant of the Tatarian maple.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vivid in the fall. The plant is native to eastern Asia, including regions of China, Korea, and Siberia. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a bright red coloration during the autumn season.
