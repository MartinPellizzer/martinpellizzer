Azalea × kosteriana

Azalea × kosteriana, commonly known as the Koster azalea, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron kosteranum and Rhododendron simsii.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is typically evergreen, with dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a strong, spicy fragrance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Azalea × kosteriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a container plant.
