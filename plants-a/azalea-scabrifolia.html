Azalea scabrifolia

Azalea scabrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a rough, textured surface. Its flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Azalea scabrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
