Avena capensis

Avena capensis, commonly known as Natal grass or false oat, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas.

Avena capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.
