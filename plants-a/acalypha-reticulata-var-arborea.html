Acalypha reticulata var. arborea

Acalypha reticulata var. arborea, commonly known as the tree nettle, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by reddish or purplish berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
