Aeonium × bornmuelleri subsp. subglabrum

Aeonium × bornmuelleri subsp.

subglabrum, commonly known as the silver aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a powdery coating.

The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with yellow flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and striking foliage make it a desirable ornamental plant.
