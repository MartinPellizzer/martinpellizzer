Aconitum brevipetalum

Aconitum brevipetalum, commonly known as the short-petalled monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers that resemble a hood. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it a member of the widely poisonous genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is not typically cultivated for ornamental use.
