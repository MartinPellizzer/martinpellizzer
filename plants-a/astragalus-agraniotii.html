Astragalus agraniotii

Astragalus agraniotii, commonly known as the Agraniotis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus agraniotii is known for its small, yellowish blooms and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.
