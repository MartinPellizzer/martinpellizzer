Atriplex expansa var. cornuta

Atriplex expansa var. cornuta, commonly known as the horned saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and distinctive, horn-like bracts that give it its common name. The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environments. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Atriplex expansa var. cornuta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
