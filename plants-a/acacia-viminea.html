Acacia viminea

Acacia viminea, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia viminea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
