Antidesma japonicum var. densiflorum

Antidesma japonicum var. densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
