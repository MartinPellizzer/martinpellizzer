Annona tuberosa

Annona tuberosa, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces a distinctive fruit with a rough, greenish skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a strong, pleasant fragrance.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in various culinary applications.
