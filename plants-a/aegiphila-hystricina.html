Aegiphila hystricina

Aegiphila hystricina, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Aegiphila hystricina is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks.
