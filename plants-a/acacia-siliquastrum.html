Acacia siliquastrum

Acacia siliquastrum, commonly known as the Jerusalem acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, and its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets. Acacia siliquastrum is often used in hedges and ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

It is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in the region.
