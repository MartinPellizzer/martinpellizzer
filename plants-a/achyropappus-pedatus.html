Achyropappus pedatus

Achyropappus pedatus, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, reducing water loss, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant's structure and reproductive strategies reflect its evolutionary response to the harsh desert climate.
