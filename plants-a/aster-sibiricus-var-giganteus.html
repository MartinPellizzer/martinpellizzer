Aster sibiricus var. giganteus

Aster sibiricus var. giganteus, commonly known as the Siberian giant aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It features showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has sturdy, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and it produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, alpine environments.
