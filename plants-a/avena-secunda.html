Avena secunda

Avena secunda, commonly known as slender oat, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescence. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated, a common feature among grasses. Its seeds are enclosed in a lemma and palea, typical of the Poaceae family.

Avena secunda is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for wildlife.
