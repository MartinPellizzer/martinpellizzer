Amyema sessilifolia

Amyema sessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, sessile leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Amyema sessilifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its aromatic properties.
