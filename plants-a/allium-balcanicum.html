Allium balcanicum

Allium balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in the summer months. Its bulbs are used in traditional cuisine and have mild garlic-like flavor.

Allium balcanicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
