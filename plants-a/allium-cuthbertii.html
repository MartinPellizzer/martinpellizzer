Allium cuthbertii

Allium cuthbertii, commonly known as Cuthbert's onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces a single, upright flowering stem that bears a cluster of small, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Allium cuthbertii is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
