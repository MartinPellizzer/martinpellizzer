Athyrium × tokashikii

Athyrium × tokashikii, commonly known as the hybrid Japanese shield fern, is a fern species resulting from the cross between Athyrium niponicum and Athyrium filix-femina.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often adorned with intricate sori along the margins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its foliage displays a range of colors, from deep green to reddish hues, depending on environmental conditions.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
