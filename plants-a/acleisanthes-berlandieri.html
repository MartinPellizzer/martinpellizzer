Acleisanthes berlandieri

Acleisanthes berlandieri, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a hairy texture. It has a short, thick stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it well-suited for desert ecosystems.
