Aristolochia caracasana

Aristolochia caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas pipevine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horseshoe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
