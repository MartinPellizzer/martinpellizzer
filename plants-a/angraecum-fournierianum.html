Angraecum fournierianum

Angraecum fournierianum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, slender stems and produces intricate, star-shaped flowers with a distinctive white and violet coloration. The plant is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments, often on trees or rocks in the tropical rainforests. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
