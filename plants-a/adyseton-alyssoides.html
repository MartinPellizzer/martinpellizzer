Adyseton alyssoides

Adyseton alyssoides, commonly known as the silverleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and toothed leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Adyseton alyssoides is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
