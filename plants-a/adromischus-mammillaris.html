Adromischus mammillaris

Adromischus mammillaris, commonly known as the "Lipstick Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, rounded leaves that are typically pink or red, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Adromischus mammillaris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
