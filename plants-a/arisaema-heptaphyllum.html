Arisaema heptaphyllum

Arisaema heptaphyllum, commonly known as the seven-leaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and clusters of spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the flower cluster. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often surrounded by a showy, leaf-like bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema heptaphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
