Adenophora brevidiscifera

Adenophora brevidiscifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain slopes. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
