Alyssum obovatum

Alyssum obovatum, commonly known as narrow-leaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and oval, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum obovatum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
