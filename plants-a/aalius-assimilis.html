Aalius assimilis

Aalius assimilis, commonly known as the silverleaf mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that resemble ferns. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
