Arenaria persica

Arenaria persica, commonly known as Persian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

It is often found in dry meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.
