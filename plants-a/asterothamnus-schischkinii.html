Asterothamnus schischkinii

Asterothamnus schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
