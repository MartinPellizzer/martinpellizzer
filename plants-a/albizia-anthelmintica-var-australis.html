Albizia anthelmintica var. australis

Albizia anthelmintica var. australis, commonly known as the Australian wattle, is a leguminous shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
