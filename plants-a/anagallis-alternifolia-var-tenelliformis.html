Anagallis alternifolia var. tenelliformis

Anagallis alternifolia var. tenelliformis, commonly known as the slender waterweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are alternate and often have a slightly succulent texture, adapting to moist environments. This variety is typically found in wet meadows, ditches, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
