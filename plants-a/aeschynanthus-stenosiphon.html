Aeschynanthus stenosiphon

Aeschynanthus stenosiphon, commonly known as the narrow-lipped ivy vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red and orange. The plant produces elliptical, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or greenhouses.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and ability to climb or trail gracefully.
