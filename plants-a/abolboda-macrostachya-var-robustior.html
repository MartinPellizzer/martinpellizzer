Abolboda macrostachya var. robustior

Abolboda macrostachya var. robustior, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is also used in reforestation and erosion control projects.
