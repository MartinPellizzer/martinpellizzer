Acanthocalycium klimpelianum

Acanthocalycium klimpelianum, commonly known as the Klimpel's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
