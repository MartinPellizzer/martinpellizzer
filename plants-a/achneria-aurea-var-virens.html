Achneria aurea var. virens

Achneria aurea var. virens, commonly known as the golden bellvista, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, often with a subtle greenish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is a sought-after species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
