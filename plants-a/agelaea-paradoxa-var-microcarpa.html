Agelaea paradoxa var. microcarpa

Agelaea paradoxa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf agelaea, is a unique and rare plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, small, clustered flowers that bloom in a striking shade of violet. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance. This variety is notable for its unusual morphology, which includes a short, thick stem and reduced leaf size compared to other members of the genus.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and scientific interest.
