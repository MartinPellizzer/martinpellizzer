Aristolochia longicaulis

Aristolochia longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often maroon or reddish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is known for its strong, aromatic scent. Its fruit is a fleshy, elongated capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
