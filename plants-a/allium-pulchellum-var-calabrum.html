Allium pulchellum var. calabrum

Allium pulchellum var. calabrum, commonly known as the Calabrian garlic or wild garlic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is often found in rocky or open woodland areas. It is valued for its aromatic leaves and bulbs, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
