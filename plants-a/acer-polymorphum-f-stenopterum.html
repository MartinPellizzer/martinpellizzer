Acer polymorphum f. stenopterum

Acer polymorphum f. stenopterum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maple, is a variant of the variable maple, characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a serrated margin and a pale underside.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, followed by winged seeds that disperse by wind.
