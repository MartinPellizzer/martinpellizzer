Astragalus vestitus var. menziesii

Astragalus vestitus var. menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to pale green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological adaptability.
