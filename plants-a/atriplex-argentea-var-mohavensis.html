Atriplex argentea var. mohavensis

Atriplex argentea var. mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which reflects sunlight and reduces water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. Its leaves are succulent and contain a high concentration of salts, which is an adaptation to the saline soils of its habitat.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in desert ecosystems.
