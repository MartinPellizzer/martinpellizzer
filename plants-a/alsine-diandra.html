Alsine diandra

Alsine diandra, commonly known as sand mat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its low, mat-like growth habit with small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss.

Alsine diandra is often found in sandy soils and is considered a pioneer species in disturbed habitats.
