Arundo schleicheriana

Arundo schleicheriana, commonly known as the Schleicher's reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it both a valuable ecological plant and a potential invasive species in non-native regions.
