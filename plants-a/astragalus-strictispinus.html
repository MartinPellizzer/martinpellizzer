Astragalus strictispinus

Astragalus strictispinus, commonly known as the narrow-spined milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its small, compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers are typical of the genus Astragalus.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
