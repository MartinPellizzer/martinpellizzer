Aloe calidophila

Aloe calidophila, commonly known as the desert aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Aloe calidophila thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its resilience and low maintenance make it a popular choice for gardeners in hot, dry climates.
