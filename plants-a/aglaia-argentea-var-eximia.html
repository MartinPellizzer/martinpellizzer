Aglaia argentea var. eximia

Aglaia argentea var. eximia, commonly known as silver aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically silver or white-veined, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous flat, oval seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
