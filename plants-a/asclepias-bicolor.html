Asclepias bicolor

Asclepias bicolor, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has velvety, oval-shaped leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release fluffy seeds. It is a host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing essential nourishment for monarch caterpillars.

Asclepias bicolor thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
