Aechmea makoyana

Aechmea makoyana, commonly known as the Makoyana bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cavity. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that can last for several months. Its leaves are typically broad and covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs.

Aechmea makoyana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
