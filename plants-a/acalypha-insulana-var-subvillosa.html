Acalypha insulana var. subvillosa

Acalypha insulana var. subvillosa, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces showy, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, often in shades of red, pink, or orange. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable environments.
