Anthurium obtusum subsp. obtusum

Anthurium obtusum subsp.

obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leaved anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces striking spadices, which are typically red or orange with a white spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth. Anthurium obtusum subsp.

obtusum is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.
