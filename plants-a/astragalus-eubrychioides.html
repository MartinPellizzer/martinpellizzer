Astragalus eubrychioides

Astragalus eubrychioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus eubrychioides is also known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
