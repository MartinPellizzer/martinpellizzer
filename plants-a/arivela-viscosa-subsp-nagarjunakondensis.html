Arivela viscosa subsp. nagarjunakondensis

Arivela viscosa subsp.

nagarjunakondensis, commonly known as the Nagarjunakonda sticktree, is a shrub native to the Eastern Ghats of India. It is characterized by its viscid (sticky) leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the tropical and subtropical climates of its native region.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native ecosystem.
