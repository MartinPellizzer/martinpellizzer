Acacia aneura var. intermedia

Acacia aneura var. intermedia, commonly known as the river red gum, is a variant of the slender acacia species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched trunk and long, narrow leaves that are typically shed during dry periods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in a range of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found near water sources.

This species plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various native animals.
