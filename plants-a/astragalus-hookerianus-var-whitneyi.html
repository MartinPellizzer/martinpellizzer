Astragalus hookerianus var. whitneyi

Astragalus hookerianus var. whitneyi, commonly known as Whitney's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
