Aconitum mielichhoferi

Aconitum mielichhoferi, commonly known as the Mielichhofer's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple hood-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 60 to 120 cm, with palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care, as all parts of the plant contain potent alkaloids.

Morphology

Aconitum mielichhoferi has leaves that are compound, with deeply lobed leaflets arranged alternately along the stem, typically measuring up to 30 cm in length, and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside.

The stem is upright, smooth, and has a reddish-brown bark that becomes more fissured with age. Its flowers are showy, blue to purple in color, with a spurred petal and are arranged in racemes; they bloom in late summer and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a follicle that releases seeds through dehiscence, and the seeds are small, winged, and dispersed by wind.

The roots are tuberous, thick, and fibrous, aiding in the plant's survival in its alpine habitat.
