Acinodendron rufipilum

Acinodendron rufipilum, commonly known as the red-haired acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its distinctive reddish-brown hairy stems and foliage, which give it its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acinodendron rufipilum is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a source of browse for wildlife.
