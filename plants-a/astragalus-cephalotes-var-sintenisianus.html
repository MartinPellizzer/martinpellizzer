Astragalus cephalotes var. sintenisianus

Astragalus cephalotes var. sintenisianus, commonly known as the Sintenisian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
