Acacia farnesiana f. cavenia

Acacia farnesiana f. cavenia, commonly known as the sweet acacia or mimosa, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flower heads that appear in spring. The plant produces a fragrant, resinous gum that has been used historically in incense and medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and a distinctive, bushy growth habit.
