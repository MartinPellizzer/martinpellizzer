Albizia amara subsp. sericocephala

Albizia amara subsp.

sericocephala, commonly known as the silky-headed Albizia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces legume pods that contain flattened, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils. Albizia amara subsp.

sericocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
