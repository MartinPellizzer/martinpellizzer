Astragalus multispina

Astragalus multispina, commonly known as the spiny milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
