Agrostis alba subsp. dubia

Agrostis alba subsp.

dubia, commonly known as the pale oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and sandy soils.

The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that bears small, pale florets.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
