Athyrium vidalii var. confusum

Athyrium vidalii var. confusum, commonly known as the Vidal's Athyrium, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and streamside habitats. Its rhizomes are short and often covered in a dense layer of brownish roots.

The leaves are bi- to tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, finely divided structure that contributes to its ornamental appeal.
