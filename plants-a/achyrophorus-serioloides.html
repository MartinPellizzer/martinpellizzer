Achyrophorus serioloides

Achyrophorus serioloides, commonly known as the common bluebell, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. It is known for its ability to thrive in partial to full shade and its preference for well-drained soil.

The common bluebell is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
