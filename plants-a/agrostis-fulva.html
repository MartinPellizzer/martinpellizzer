Agrostis fulva

Agrostis fulva, commonly known as yellow oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences with golden-yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Agrostis fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
