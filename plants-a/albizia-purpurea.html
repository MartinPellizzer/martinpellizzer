Albizia purpurea

Albizia purpurea, commonly known as the purple mimosa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that turn red or purple in autumn. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes, and it produces flat, dehiscent pods that contain several seeds.

Albizia purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
