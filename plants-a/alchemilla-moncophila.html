Alchemilla moncophila

Alchemilla moncophila, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of silver-white, hairy leaves that reflect light and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. Alchemilla moncophila thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
