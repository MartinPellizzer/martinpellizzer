Amphipogon caricinus var. scaber

Amphipogon caricinus var. scaber, commonly known as the rough sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. Its flowering spikes are usually green to reddish in color and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
