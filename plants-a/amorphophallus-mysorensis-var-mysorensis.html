Amorphophallus mysorensis var. mysorensis

Amorphophallus mysorensis var. mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a tuberous root system that is used as a starch source, similar to taro. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued both for its ornamental features and its economic utility in local communities.
