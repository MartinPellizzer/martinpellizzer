Andropogon leptocladus f. simplex

Andropogon leptocladus f. simplex, commonly known as the simple bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to white flowers.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and stabilizing wetland soils.
