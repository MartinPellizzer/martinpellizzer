Abutilon affine

Abutilon affine, commonly known as velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous small, flat seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

Abutilon affine is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
