Andromeda nitida var. rhombifolia

Andromeda nitida var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wintergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its evergreen foliage provides year-round interest, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its aromatic leaves and historical use in traditional medicine.
