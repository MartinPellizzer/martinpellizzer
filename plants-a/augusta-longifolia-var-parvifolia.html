Augusta longifolia var. parvifolia

Augusta longifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved Augusta, is a variant of the Augusta longifolia species.

It is characterized by its smaller, more delicate leaves compared to the typical variety. The plant typically features slender, branching stems and produces small, fragrant flowers. It is native to specific regions in South Africa, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after specimen in horticultural collections.
