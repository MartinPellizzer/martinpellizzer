Aristida hispidula

Aristida hispidula, commonly known as hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often hairy, giving it its common name. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Aristida hispidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
