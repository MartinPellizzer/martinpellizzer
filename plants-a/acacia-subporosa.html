Acacia subporosa

Acacia subporosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Acacia subporosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
