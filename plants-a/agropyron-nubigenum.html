Agropyron nubigenum

Agropyron nubigenum, commonly known as the alpine wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are adapted to cold and arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Agropyron nubigenum is often found in high-altitude meadows and is well-suited for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Its resilience to harsh climates makes it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
