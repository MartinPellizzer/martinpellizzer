Arachis williamsii

Arachis williamsii, commonly known as wild peanut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are similar in structure to those of the domesticated peanut. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Arachis williamsii is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation.
