Astragalus stapfii

Astragalus stapfii, commonly known as the Stapf's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Astragalus stapfii is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in its native habitat.
