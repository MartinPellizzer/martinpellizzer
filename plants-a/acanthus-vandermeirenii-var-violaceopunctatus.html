Acanthus vandermeirenii var. violaceopunctatus

Acanthus vandermeirenii var. violaceopunctatus, commonly known as the violet-punctated spurge or purple-spotted spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which often display a violet or purple hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
