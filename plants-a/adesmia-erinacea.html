Adesmia erinacea

Adesmia erinacea, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Adesmia erinacea plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
