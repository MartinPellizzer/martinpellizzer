Amsinckia speciosa

Amsinckia speciosa, commonly known as the desert smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in desert climates.

Amsinckia speciosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
