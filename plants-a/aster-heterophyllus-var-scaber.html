Aster heterophyllus var. scaber

Aster heterophyllus var. scaber, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of rough, hairy, and sometimes lobed leaves, which are characteristic of its varietal name "scaber.". The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
