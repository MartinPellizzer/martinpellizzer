Athyrium dimorphophyllum

Athyrium dimorphophyllum, commonly known as the two-leaf water fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a perennial fern native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive fronds that exhibit dimorphism, with the upper and lower leaves differing in shape and size. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in tropical forests.
