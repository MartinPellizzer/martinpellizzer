Anplectrum rostratum

Anplectrum rostratum, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Anplectrum rostratum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
