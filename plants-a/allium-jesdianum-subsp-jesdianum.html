Allium jesdianum subsp. jesdianum

Allium jesdianum subsp.

jesdianum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant's bulb consists of numerous small, rounded cloves surrounded by a papery skin. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other Allium species.
