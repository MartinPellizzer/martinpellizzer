Acacia inophloia

Acacia inophloia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers, which appear in spring, attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is also valued for its hardy nature and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
