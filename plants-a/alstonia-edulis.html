Alstonia edulis

Alstonia edulis, commonly known as the edible alstonia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by orange or red berries containing seeds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions for its edible fruits.

However, caution is advised as some parts of the plant may be toxic.
