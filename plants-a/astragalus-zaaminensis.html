Astragalus zaaminensis

Astragalus zaaminensis, commonly known as the Zaamin milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal medicine.
