Alchemilla gourzae

Alchemilla gourzae, commonly known as the Persian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. Alchemilla gourzae thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
