Anthericum pilosicarinatum

Anthericum pilosicarinatum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features palmately lobed, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

Anthericum pilosicarinatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
