Amphistelma ephedroides

Amphistelma ephedroides, commonly known as the slender ephedra, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae, which includes species known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its stems are woody and often branch at the base, forming a shrub-like structure.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.
