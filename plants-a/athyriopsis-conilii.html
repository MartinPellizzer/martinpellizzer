Athyriopsis conilii

Athyriopsis conilii, commonly known as the Canary Island fern, is a species of fern native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyriopsis conilii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
