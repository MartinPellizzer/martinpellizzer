Artemisia capillaris f. elegans

Artemisia capillaris f. elegans, commonly known as the Chinese wormwood or velvetleaf artemisia, is a cultivar of the perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
