Afroaster laevigatus

Afroaster laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth African aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow with a central disk, making it a visually striking addition to its habitat.
