Alchemilla vulgaris proles cavillieri

Alchemilla vulgaris proles cavillieri, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are followed by distinctive, velvety seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shady gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
