Achillea apiculata

Achillea apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
