Ardisia intibucana

Ardisia intibucana, commonly known as the Honduras evergreen, is a member of the Myrsinaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible for some bird species. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it has a compact growth habit.

Ardisia intibucana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
