Arundinaria iwabuchii

Arundinaria iwabuchii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Iwabuchii bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Arundinaria iwabuchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
