Arum italicum subvar. obtusilobum

Arum italicum subvar. obtusilobum, commonly known as the Italian arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces striking spadices, which are spathes that enclose the true flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
