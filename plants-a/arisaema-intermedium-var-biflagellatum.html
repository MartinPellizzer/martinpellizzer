Arisaema intermedium var. biflagellatum

Arisaema intermedium var. biflagellatum, commonly known as the two-whiskered arum, is a unique species within the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered on the spadix, which is often covered in a dense layer of tiny, thread-like structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
