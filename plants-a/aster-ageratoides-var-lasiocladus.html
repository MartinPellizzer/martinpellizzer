Aster ageratoides var. lasiocladus

Aster ageratoides var. lasiocladus, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, silvery appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy, drought-tolerant plant.
