Arum maculatum subvar. gracile

Arum maculatum subvar. gracile, commonly known as the slender lords-and-ladies, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a distinctive arrow-shaped leaf with a deep green color and a white, waxy sheen. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
