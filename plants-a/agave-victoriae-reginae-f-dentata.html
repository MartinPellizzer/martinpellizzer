Agave victoriae-reginae f. dentata

Agave victoriae-reginae f. dentata, commonly known as the Mexican toothed agave, is a striking succulent native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are adorned with sharp, tooth-like projections along their edges. The leaves are typically a vibrant blue-green color, which helps the plant conserve water in its arid environment. It produces tall, spire-like flower stalks that bear yellowish-green flowers, which bloom once in the plant's lifetime.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
