Agrostis alba proles castellana

Agrostis alba proles castellana, commonly known as the Castellana meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, oval-shaped flower clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
