Amomum longipedunculatum

Amomum longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-bracted cardamom, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect rhizome and clusters of fragrant, dark blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
