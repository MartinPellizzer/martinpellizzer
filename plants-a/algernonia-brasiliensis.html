Algernonia brasiliensis

Algernonia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often forming dense clusters.

Algernonia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations to tropical environments.
