Acacia armata var. pendula

Acacia armata var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a variant of the native Australian acacia species.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is a hardy shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its unique form makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid regions.

Native Distribution

Acacia armata var. pendula is primarily native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia, particularly in Western Australia and South Australia.

It is commonly found in areas with sandy soils and is often associated with coastal and inland dunes. This variety is known for its distinctive pendulous branches and yellow flowers, which make it a notable feature in its native habitat. It thrives in environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
