Acacia armata var. pendula

Acacia armata var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a variant of the native Australian acacia species.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is a hardy shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its unique form makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid regions.
