Acanthus longipetiolatus

Acanthus longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a silvery-white down. The plant produces spikes of showy, spathaceous flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its architectural form and ornamental value, it is often used in horticultural displays and historical garden restorations.
