Aster subcymosus

Aster subcymosus, commonly known as the Sierra aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster subcymosus is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to dry, arid environments.
