Asplenium dilatatum

Asplenium dilatatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often arched or pendulous. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance.

Asplenium dilatatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
