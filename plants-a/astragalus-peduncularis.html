Astragalus peduncularis

Astragalus peduncularis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus peduncularis is a perennial plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
