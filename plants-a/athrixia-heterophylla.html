Athrixia heterophylla

Athrixia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable hairless lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a diverse range of morphological characteristics.

It typically grows on rock surfaces in arid and semi-arid regions, forming a crust-like structure. The thallus is usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it lacks distinct lobes or rhizines. This lichen is known for its ability to tolerate extreme environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low moisture.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in ecological succession in harsh environments.
