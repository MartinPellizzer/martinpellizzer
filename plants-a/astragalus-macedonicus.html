Astragalus macedonicus

Astragalus macedonicus, commonly known as Macedonian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan region.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

Astragalus macedonicus is often found in grasslands and mountainous areas, contributing to soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
