Aphanamixis grandifolia

Aphanamixis grandifolia, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets, and it produces woody, cylindrical pods that contain its seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
