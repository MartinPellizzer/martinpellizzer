Acalypha nyasica

Acalypha nyasica, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Acalypha nyasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
