Archidendron oppositum

Archidendron oppositum, commonly known as the wattle tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open woodlands and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its seeds are encased in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across the landscape.

Archidendron oppositum plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, supporting biodiversity in its habitat.
