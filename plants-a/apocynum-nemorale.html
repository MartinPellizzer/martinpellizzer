Apocynum nemorale

Apocynum nemorale, commonly known as Canada rose or wild rose.

It is a flowering plant native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and showy pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in woodland areas and along stream banks, often forming dense thickets.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.
