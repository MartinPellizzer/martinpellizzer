Adenia keramanthus

Adenia keramanthus, commonly known as the white belladonna vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its striking white flowers with a strong, unpleasant odor. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
