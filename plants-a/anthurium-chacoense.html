Anthurium chacoense

Anthurium chacoense, commonly known as the Chaco anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and spathe. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture with a deep green color.

The spathe is usually bright red or orange, contrasting with the green spathe, making it visually striking.
