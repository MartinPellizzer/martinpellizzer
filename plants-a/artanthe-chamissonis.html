Artanthe chamissonis

Artanthe chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's artanthe, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland habitats.

Artanthe chamissonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
