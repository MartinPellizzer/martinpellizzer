Aperula

Aperula, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers and hairy, silver-gray leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Africa. Aperula is often found in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its berries are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or livestock.
