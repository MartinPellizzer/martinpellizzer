Acosta aeolica

Acosta aeolica, commonly known as the wind mountain plant, is a rare and unique species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and its ability to thrive in extreme environmental conditions. The plant's structure is adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures, which are common in its native habitat. Acosta aeolica has small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the early spring.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for alpine ecosystems.
