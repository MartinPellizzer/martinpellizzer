Acacia amabilis

Acacia amabilis, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and temperate climates.

Acacia amabilis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
