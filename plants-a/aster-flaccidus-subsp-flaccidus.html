Aster flaccidus subsp. flaccidus

Aster flaccidus subsp.

flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. It has slender, branching stems and linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced flower heads and pale-colored ray florets compared to other Aster species.
