Afrardisia bequaertii

Afrardisia bequaertii, commonly known as the African orchid or Bequaert's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can receive adequate light without direct sun exposure. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
