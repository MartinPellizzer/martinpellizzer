Arachnothryx macrocalyx

Arachnothryx macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered arachnothryx, is a woody plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple branches emerging from the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique floral display.
