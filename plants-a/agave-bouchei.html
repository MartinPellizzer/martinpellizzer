Agave bouchei

Agave bouchei, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stem that bears clusters of white flowers, which bloom after several years. Agave bouchei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its robust nature makes it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.
