Aconitum rockii var. rockii

Aconitum rockii var. rockii, commonly known as the rock aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and grows in rocky or well-drained soils. It is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it highly poisonous to both humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens with caution.
