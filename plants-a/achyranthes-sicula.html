Achyranthes sicula

Achyranthes sicula, commonly known as Sicilian knotgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are often clustered at the tips of the branches. Its root system is fibrous and can reach depths of several centimeters, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Achyranthes sicula is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
