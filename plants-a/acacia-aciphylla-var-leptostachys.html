Acacia aciphylla var. leptostachys

Acacia aciphylla var. leptostachys, commonly known as the slender-stemmed wattle, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, woody stems and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It has adapted to survive in poor soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.

Due to its resilience, it is used in ecological restoration projects to stabilize soils and prevent erosion.
