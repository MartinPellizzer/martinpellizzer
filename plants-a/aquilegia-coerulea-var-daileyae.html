Aquilegia coerulea var. daileyae

Aquilegia coerulea var. daileyae, commonly known as the Dailey's blue cohosh, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and distinctive floral structure.
