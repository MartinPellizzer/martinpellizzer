Aconitum soongaricum subsp. alatavicum

Aconitum soongaricum subsp.

alatavicum, commonly known as the Alataw Valley aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces follicles containing elliptical, smooth-seeded fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
