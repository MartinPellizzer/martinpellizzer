Astragalus solandri

Astragalus solandri, commonly known as the New Zealand milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus solandri is an important species for ecological studies due to its adaptation to New Zealand's unique environment.
