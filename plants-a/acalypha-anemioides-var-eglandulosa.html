Acalypha anemioides var. eglandulosa

Acalypha anemioides var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its unique texture and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
