Adenophora trachelioides subsp. trachelioides

Adenophora trachelioides subsp.

trachelioides, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a violet hue. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a silvery underside, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
