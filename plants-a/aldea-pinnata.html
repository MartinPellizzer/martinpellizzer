Aldea pinnata

Aldea pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf alder, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in areas with partial to full sunlight. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while its fruits are woody capsules that release seeds upon maturity.

Aldea pinnata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
