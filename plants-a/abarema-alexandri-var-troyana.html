Abarema alexandri var. troyana

Abarema alexandri var. troyana, commonly known as the Troyana variant of Alexandri's abarema, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, elliptical leaflets that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
