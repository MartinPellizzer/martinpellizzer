Agave dyckii

Agave dyckii, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a species of agave native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces tall, spiky flower stalks that bloom once in its lifetime, typically in the late spring or early summer. Its rosette structure and succulent nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.

Agave dyckii is also valued for its use in the production of tequila, although it is not the primary species used for this purpose.
