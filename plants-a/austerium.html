Austerium

Austerium, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that resemble the petals of a ghostly bloom, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant's botanical features include a short, woody stem and small, inconspicuous leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Austerium thrives in cool, humid environments and is known for its short blooming period, typically occurring during the late autumn or early winter.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
