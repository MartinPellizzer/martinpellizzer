Andersonia redolens

Andersonia redolens, commonly known as the scentless orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Andersonia redolens is known for its unique ability to thrive in wetland habitats, making it an important indicator species for such ecosystems.
