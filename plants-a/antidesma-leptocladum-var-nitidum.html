Antidesma leptocladum var. nitidum

Antidesma leptocladum var. nitidum, commonly known as the shining antidesma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its small, white flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is characterized by its dense branching pattern and adaptability to various soil types.
