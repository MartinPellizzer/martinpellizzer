Acacia dictyocarpa

Acacia dictyocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are characteristic of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
