Acer sichourense

Acer sichourense, commonly known as the Sichuan maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in mountainous regions at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

The bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
