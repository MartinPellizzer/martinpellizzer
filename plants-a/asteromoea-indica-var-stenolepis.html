Asteromoea indica var. stenolepis

Asteromoea indica var. stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Indian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
