Aracium aureum

Aracium aureum, commonly known as the golden aracium, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow foliage that resembles the color of gold, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with dense, leathery leaves that provide a lush appearance. Its flowers, which are small and clustered, often appear in shades of yellow and orange, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Aracium aureum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a colorful and hardy plant.
