Ageratina crassimonticola

Ageratina crassimonticola, commonly known as the thick-mountain ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
