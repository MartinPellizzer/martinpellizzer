Acalypha distans

Acalypha distans, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redleaf maple, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Acalypha distans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world as a garden plant.
