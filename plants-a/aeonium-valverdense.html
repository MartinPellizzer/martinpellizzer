Aeonium valverdense

Aeonium valverdense, commonly known as the Valley of the Sun, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves that range in color from blue-green to red-pink, depending on light exposure. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers on tall, branched stems during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Aeonium valverdense is also known for its ability to propagate through offsets, which can be easily separated and replanted.
