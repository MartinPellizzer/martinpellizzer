Acer dasycarpum heterophyllum-laciniatum

Acer dasycarpum heterophyllum-laciniatum, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Aceraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that are finely lobed and have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant autumn foliage.

The plant is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
