Acacia chrysocephala

Acacia chrysocephala, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow, globular flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Acacia chrysocephala is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and charcoal.
