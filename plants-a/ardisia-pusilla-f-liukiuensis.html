Ardisia pusilla f. liukiuensis

Ardisia pusilla f. liukiuensis, commonly known as the Liukiu dwarf ardisia, is a compact shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the summer, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
