Achillea lanulosa

Achillea lanulosa, commonly known as cloud-like yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties.

Achillea lanulosa is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
