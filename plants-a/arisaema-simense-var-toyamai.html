Arisaema simense var. toyamai

Arisaema simense var. toyamai, commonly known as the Toyama arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence, typical of the Araceae family. The plant produces striking, greenish-white spathe flowers that attract pollinators such as beetles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture and botanical gardens.
