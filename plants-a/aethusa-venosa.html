Aethusa venosa

Aethusa venosa, commonly known as the slender cow-wheat, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Aethusa venosa is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, capable of deriving some nutrients from its host through specialized root structures.
