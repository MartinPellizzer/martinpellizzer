Attalea × piassabossu

Attalea × piassabossu, commonly known as the piassabossu hybrid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Attalea maripa and Attalea phalerata.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its robust, palm-like structure and large, pinnate leaves. This plant is native to the Amazon Basin, where it thrives in wetland and swampy environments. It produces large, clustered inflorescences that yield edible fruits, which are an important food source for local wildlife.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the region's ecological diversity.
