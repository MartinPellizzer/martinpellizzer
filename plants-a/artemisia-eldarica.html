Artemisia eldarica

Artemisia eldarica, commonly known as the Afghan wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its strong medicinal and pesticidal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
