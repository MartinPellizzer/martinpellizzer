Aleurites saponarius

Aleurites saponarius, commonly known as the soap nut tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its most notable botanical feature is the production of spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is valued for its hardy nature and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
