Actaea orthostachya

Actaea orthostachya, commonly known as the white spiderling, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its striking floral display appears in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Actaea orthostachya is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
