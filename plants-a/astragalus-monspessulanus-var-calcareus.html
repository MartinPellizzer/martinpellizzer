Astragalus monspessulanus var. calcareus

Astragalus monspessulanus var. calcareus, commonly known as the limestone milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect stem and trifoliate leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
