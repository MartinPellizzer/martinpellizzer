Atriplex hillmanii

Atriplex hillmanii, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, often blue-gray leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically in the spring. Atriplex hillmanii is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
