Arenaria verna var. aequicaulis

Arenaria verna var. aequicaulis, commonly known as the spring sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with succulent, hairy stems and leaves that are often opposite or whorled. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
