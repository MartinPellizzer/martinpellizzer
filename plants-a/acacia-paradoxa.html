Acacia paradoxa

Acacia paradoxa, commonly known as the devil's claw, is a strikingly unique plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, twisted, claw-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in winter. Acacia paradoxa is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in drought conditions.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
