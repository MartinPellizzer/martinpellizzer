Aridaria klaverensis

Aridaria klaverensis, commonly known as the red sandbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Aridaria klaverensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
