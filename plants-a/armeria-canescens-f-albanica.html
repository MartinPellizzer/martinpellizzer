Armeria canescens f. albanica

Armeria canescens f. albanica, commonly known as the Albanian sandpink, is a flowering plant belonging to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of pink to reddish-pink flowers. The plant features linear, hairy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, adapted to arid and coastal environments. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats.
