Alcantarea nigripetala

Alcantarea nigripetala, commonly known as the black-petaled alcantara, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, spathate leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Alcantarea nigripetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
