Acer pictum subsp. savatieri

Acer pictum subsp.

savatieri, commonly known as the Red Maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental species.

The tree has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while its fruit is a samara, a winged seed vessel.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained habitats.
