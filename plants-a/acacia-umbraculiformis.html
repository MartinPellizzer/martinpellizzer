Acacia umbraculiformis

Acacia umbraculiformis, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like canopy of compound leaves, which provide shade and support for various wildlife. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia umbraculiformis is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of traditional medicinal compounds.
