Aphelandra ochrolarynx

Aphelandra ochrolarynx, commonly known as the golden throat plant, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that feature bright yellow bracts resembling a throat, hence its common name. The plant has elongated, dark green leaves with a waxy texture, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aphelandra ochrolarynx is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant, showy appearance.
