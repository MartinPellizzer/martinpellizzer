Acacia blakelyi

Acacia blakelyi, commonly known as the Blakely's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the winter months. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

Acacia blakelyi is adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
