Acacia plectocarpa

Acacia plectocarpa, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn a striking silver-white color during the dry season. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Acacia plectocarpa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
