Aquilegia vulgaris var. pratensis

Aquilegia vulgaris var. pratensis, commonly known as the common columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features distinctive spurred petals that are typically blue, purple, or white, arranged in a crown-like pattern. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers bloom in early summer. It thrives in woodland or meadow habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
