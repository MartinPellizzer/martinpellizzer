Artemisia campestris var. borealis

Artemisia campestris var. borealis, commonly known as northern groundsel, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as tundra, meadows, and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellow flowers in clustered heads, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
