Apolgusa

Apolgusa, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a central disk that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. Native to South America, this plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Apolgusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
