Alchemilla adelodictya

Alchemilla adelodictya, commonly known as the velvetleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla adelodictya is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
