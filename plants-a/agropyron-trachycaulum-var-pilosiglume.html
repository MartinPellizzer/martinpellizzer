Agropyron trachycaulum var. pilosiglume

Agropyron trachycaulum var. pilosiglume, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in dry landscapes.
