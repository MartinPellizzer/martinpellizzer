Alchornea triplinervia var. janeirensis

Alchornea triplinervia var. janeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or *janeirensis*, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, dense wood, which has been traditionally used in crafting musical instruments and furniture.
