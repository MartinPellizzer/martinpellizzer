Amyris oblanceolata

Amyris oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf amyris, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and moist forests. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green, with a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, appearing in panicles, and are typically yellow or orange in color.

Amyris oblanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
