Arundinella tricholepis

Arundinella tricholepis, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a hairy texture on the underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, typical of many grass species.

Arundinella tricholepis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
