Arisaema capitellatum

Arisaema capitellatum, commonly known as the umbrella arum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a broad, umbrella-like spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Arisaema capitellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
