Adenium lugardiae

Adenium lugardiae, commonly known as the Desert Crown, is a striking succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features a thick, swollen caudex that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant has smooth, often spiny stems and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

Adenium lugardiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some African communities.
