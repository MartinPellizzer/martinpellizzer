Aucuba japonica var. limbata

Aucuba japonica var. limbata, commonly known as the Japanese aucuba or variegated aucuba, is a shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red-orange berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental foliage.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
