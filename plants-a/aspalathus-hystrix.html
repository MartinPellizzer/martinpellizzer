Aspalathus hystrix

Aspalathus hystrix, commonly known as the spiked bushveld pea, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features upright stems with spiny, glandular hairs, and its small, pea-like flowers are typically pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Aspalathus hystrix is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting the surrounding soil.
