Acacia adiantoides

Acacia adiantoides, commonly known as the lace fern acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
