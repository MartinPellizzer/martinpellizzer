Agathosma longicornu

Agathosma longicornu, commonly known as the African germander, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy landscapes. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Agathosma longicornu is also valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
