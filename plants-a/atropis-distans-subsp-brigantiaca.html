Atropis distans subsp. brigantiaca

Atropis distans subsp.

brigantiaca, commonly known as the mountain pimpernel, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, typical of the Primulaceae family. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
