Astragalus saratagius

Astragalus saratagius, commonly known as the Saratov milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus saratagius is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
