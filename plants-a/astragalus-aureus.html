Astragalus aureus

Astragalus aureus, commonly known as golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus aureus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
