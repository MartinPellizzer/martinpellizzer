Allium anatolicum

Allium anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy bulbs that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Allium anatolicum is valued for its aromatic properties and potential health benefits.
