Aspidium robustum

Aspidium robustum, commonly known as the hard fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, giving it a dense and intricate appearance. The plant is native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and Australia, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Aspidium robustum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
