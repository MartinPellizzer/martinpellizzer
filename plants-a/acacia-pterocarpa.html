Acacia pterocarpa

Acacia pterocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods, which split open to release the seeds when mature.

Acacia pterocarpa plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
