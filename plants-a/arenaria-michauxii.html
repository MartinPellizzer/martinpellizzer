Arenaria michauxii

Arenaria michauxii, commonly known as the sand-verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as dunes, prairies, and disturbed areas. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are usually sprawling or ascending.

Arenaria michauxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
