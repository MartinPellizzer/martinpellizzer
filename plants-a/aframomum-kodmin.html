Aframomum kodmin

Aframomum kodmin, commonly known as African cardamom, is a tropical plant native to western Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, dark brown fruits. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a strong, spicy aroma.

The rhizomes and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their unique flavor and medicinal properties.
