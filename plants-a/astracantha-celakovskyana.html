Astracantha celakovskyana

Astracantha celakovskyana, commonly known as the Celakovsky's thornveld, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually violet or blue in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
