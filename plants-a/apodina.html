Apodina

Apodina, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant belonging to the family Apodanthaceae.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially depends on its host for nutrients while also photosynthesizing. The plant is characterized by its lack of leaves and its small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows on the stems of other plants, often in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unusual appearance and parasitic nature, it is considered a rare and intriguing subject in botanical studies.
