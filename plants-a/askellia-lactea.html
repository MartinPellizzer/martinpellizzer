Askellia lactea

Askellia lactea, commonly known as the milky milkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its milky sap, which is a defining feature of the genus Askellia. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, usually displaying shades of yellow or orange.

Askellia lactea is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields, where it thrives in full sun.
