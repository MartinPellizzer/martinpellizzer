Acacia nicholsonensis

Acacia nicholsonensis, commonly known as the Nicholson acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure and its distinctive, feathery leaves that are typically small and bipinnate. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Acacia nicholsonensis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility in nutrient-poor environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable plant for erosion control and reforestation projects in arid landscapes.
