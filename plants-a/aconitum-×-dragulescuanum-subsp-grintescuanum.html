Aconitum × dragulescuanum subsp. grintescuanum

Aconitum × dragulescuanum subsp.

grintescuanum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a hybrid species within the genus Aconitum, known for its striking blue-violet flowers. It is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Mountains.

The plant features a robust root system and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hooded flower structure. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
