Anisolotus denticulatus

Anisolotus denticulatus, commonly known as the serrated sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a sprawling habit with fleshy, succulent stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are typically narrow, toothed, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Anisolotus denticulatus is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained sandy soils.
