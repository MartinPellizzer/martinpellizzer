Alarconia

Alarconia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are submerged and help stabilize the lakebed, contributing to the ecosystem's health.

Alarconia is also valued for its cultural significance among indigenous communities.
