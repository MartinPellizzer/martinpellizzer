Acacia drepanophylla

Acacia drepanophylla, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like crown formed by its dense, spreading branches. The plant has small, compound leaves that are often modified into spines, reducing water loss in harsh environments. Its yellow flowers are clustered in spherical heads and attract various pollinators.

Acacia drepanophylla is well adapted to survive in dry climates with minimal rainfall.
