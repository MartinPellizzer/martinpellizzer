Astragalus anisacanthus

Astragalus anisacanthus, commonly known as the goat's head, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial shrub native to the western United States, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the pods that follow the flowers are typically hairy and contain several seeds.

Astragalus anisacanthus is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
