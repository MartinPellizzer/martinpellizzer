Atriplex chanicowii

Atriplex chanicowii, commonly known as channel saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it thrive in saline and alkaline soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its robust root system allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Atriplex chanicowii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
