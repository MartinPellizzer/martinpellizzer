Alophia linearis

Alophia linearis, commonly known as the linear alophia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale blue, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
