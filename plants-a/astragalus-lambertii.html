Astragalus lambertii

Astragalus lambertii, commonly known as Lambert's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.

Astragalus lambertii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
