Abrodictyum boninense

Abrodictyum boninense, commonly known as the Bonin orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Bonin Islands.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, erect stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered vulnerable to extinction.
