Aplotaxis obvallata

Aplotaxis obvallata, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which is toxic and used in traditional medicine. The plant has small, clustered leaves and produces yellowish flowers that are followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
