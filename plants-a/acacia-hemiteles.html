Acacia hemiteles

Acacia hemiteles, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow or pale yellow, arranged in cylindrical spikes. Acacia hemiteles produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging climates.
