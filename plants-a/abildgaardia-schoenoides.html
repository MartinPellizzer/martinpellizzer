Abildgaardia schoenoides

Abildgaardia schoenoides, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Abildgaardia schoenoides is adapted to rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
