Alchemilla vulgaris var. xanthochlora

Alchemilla vulgaris var. xanthochlora, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in garden landscapes for its attractive foliage and flowers.
