Asparagopsis denudata

Asparagopsis denudata, commonly known as the naked asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or greenish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Asparagopsis denudata is used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
