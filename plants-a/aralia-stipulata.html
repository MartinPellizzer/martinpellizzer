Aralia stipulata

Aralia stipulata, commonly known as the American aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically pinnately arranged, with leaflets that are lance-shaped and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant has been used historically for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.
