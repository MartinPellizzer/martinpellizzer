Alpinia densispicata

Alpinia densispicata, commonly known as the dense-hair ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in dense, short hairs. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

Alpinia densispicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
