Allium sabzakense

Allium sabzakense, commonly known as the Caucasian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in local cuisines.

Allium sabzakense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
