Adesmia cabrerae

Adesmia cabrerae, commonly known as the Guayabilla, is a small shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Adesmia cabrerae is often found in dry forests and along coastal areas, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
