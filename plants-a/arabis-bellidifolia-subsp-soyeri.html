Arabis bellidifolia subsp. soyeri

Arabis bellidifolia subsp.

soyeri, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features basal rosettes of linear, glaucous leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals. It blooms in spring, often from April to June, and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
