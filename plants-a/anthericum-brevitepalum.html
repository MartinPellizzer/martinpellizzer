Anthericum brevitepalum

Anthericum brevitepalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered around the spadix, with male and female flowers often separated. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Anthericum brevitepalum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
