Allantoma uaupensis

Allantoma uaupensis, commonly known as the Brazilian nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and fine hairs that can cause irritation upon contact. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

Allantoma uaupensis is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and wetlands.
