Almaleea subumbellata

Almaleea subumbellata, commonly known as the silverleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in hot, dry climates. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and purple, typically blooming in the fall. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.
