Aconitum versicolor

Aconitum versicolor, commonly known as the variable monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple and sometimes even pink or white. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically deep green and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum versicolor is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
