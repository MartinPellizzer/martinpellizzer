Amsinckia oregonensis

Amsinckia oregonensis, commonly known as Oregon fiddleneck, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
