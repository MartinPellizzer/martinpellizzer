Aspalathus radiata subsp. pseudosericea

Aspalathus radiata subsp.

pseudosericea, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, brown pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
