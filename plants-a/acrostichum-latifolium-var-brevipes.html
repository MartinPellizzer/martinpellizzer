Acrostichum latifolium var. brevipes

Acrostichum latifolium var. brevipes, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf fern native to tropical regions.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its large, broad, leathery leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The fronds are typically arching and have a feathery appearance, with a distinctive, smooth texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
