Aerva villosa

Aerva villosa, commonly known as hairy aerva, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aerva villosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
