Archidendron quocense

Archidendron quocense, commonly known as the Vietnamese legume tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height, featuring a straight trunk and broad, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by flat, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
