Astragalus askalensis

Astragalus askalensis, commonly known as the Desert Milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus askalensis plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
