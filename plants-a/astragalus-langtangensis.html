Astragalus langtangensis

Astragalus langtangensis, commonly known as the Langtang milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Nepal and Tibet.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
