Arctophila chrysantha

Arctophila chrysantha, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, seed-filled structures resembling dandelion fluff. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Arctophila chrysantha is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.
