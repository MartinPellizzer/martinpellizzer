Acronychia laevis var. purpurea

Acronychia laevis var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-leafed sandpaper tree, is a variant of the sandpaper tree native to northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green to greyish bark and elliptical to ovate leaves that are typically smooth on the upper surface and hairy on the underside. The leaves of this variant often exhibit a distinctive purplish hue, especially when young or under certain light conditions. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a range of soil types and climatic conditions.
