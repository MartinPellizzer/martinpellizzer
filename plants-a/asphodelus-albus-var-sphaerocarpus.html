Asphodelus albus var. sphaerocarpus

Asphodelus albus var. sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and striking white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive spherical seed pods, which give it its specific epithet *sphaerocarpus*. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
