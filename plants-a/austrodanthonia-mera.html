Austrodanthonia mera

Austrodanthonia mera, commonly known as coastal wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and distinctive, spike-like inflorescence that matures to a reddish color. This plant thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in dunes and heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

Austrodanthonia mera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal soils.
