Alyssum simplex subsp. edentulum

Alyssum simplex subsp.

edentulum, commonly known as the toothless alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Alyssum simplex subsp.

edentulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
