Astragalus stenophysus

Astragalus stenophysus, commonly known as narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus stenophysus is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
