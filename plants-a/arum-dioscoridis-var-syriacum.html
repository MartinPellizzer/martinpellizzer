Arum dioscoridis var. syriacum

Arum dioscoridis var. syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tuberous root system that is the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant produces striking arrowhead-shaped leaves and clusters of spadices, which are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has been cultivated for centuries in various parts of the world.
