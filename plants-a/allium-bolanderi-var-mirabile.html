Allium bolanderi var. mirabile

Allium bolanderi var. mirabile, commonly known as the Bolander's garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Amaryllidaceae family.
