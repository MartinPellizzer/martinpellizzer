Aconitum pseudokongboense

Aconitum pseudokongboense, commonly known as the Tibetan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy flowers that typically range from blue to purple in color. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply cut lobes, and its roots are tuberous, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Aconitum pseudokongboense is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
