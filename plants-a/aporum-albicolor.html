Aporum albicolor

Aporum albicolor, commonly known as the white-flowered aporum, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in diameter, often with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
