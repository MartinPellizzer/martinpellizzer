Astragalus geerwusuensis

Astragalus geerwusuensis, commonly known as the Gansu milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of northern China, particularly in Gansu Province. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments.

Astragalus geerwusuensis is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and is valued for its potential health benefits.
