Aeridostachya crassipes

Aeridostachya crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It features robust, grass-like leaves that are typically broad and flat, adapted to thrive in moist environments. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
