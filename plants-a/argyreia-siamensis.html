Argyreia siamensis

Argyreia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam moonflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
