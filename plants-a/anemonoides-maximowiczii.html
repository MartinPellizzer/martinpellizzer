Anemonoides maximowiczii

Anemonoides maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to woodland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
