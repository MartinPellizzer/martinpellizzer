Acalypha mildbraediana var. glabrescens

Acalypha mildbraediana var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved glorybower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, smooth, and often bluish-green leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a landscape plant.
