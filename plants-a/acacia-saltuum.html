Acacia saltuum

Acacia saltuum, commonly known as the saltbush acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, green, bean-like pods and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant is well adapted to saline and drought-prone soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.

Acacia saltuum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
