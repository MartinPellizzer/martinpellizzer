Arundinella hispida

Arundinella hispida, commonly known as the rough arrowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, rough to the touch, and often have a hairy texture.

Arundinella hispida is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is commonly found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
