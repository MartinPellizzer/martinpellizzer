Alliaria lampongum

Alliaria lampongum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic or Chinese garlic chives, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, aromatic leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in racemes. Its roots and leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Alliaria lampongum is characterized by its pungent odor and ability to thrive in moist, temperate climates.
