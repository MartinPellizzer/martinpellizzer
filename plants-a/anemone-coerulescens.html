Anemone coerulescens

Anemone coerulescens, commonly known as the blue anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Anemone coerulescens is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
