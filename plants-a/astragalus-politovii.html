Astragalus politovii

Astragalus politovii, commonly known as the Karakum astragalus, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in the deserts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and is of interest to botanists studying desert flora.
