Aster spathulifolius f. albus

Aster spathulifolius f. albus, commonly known as the white spoonleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features broad, spoon-shaped leaves that give it its specific epithet "spathulifolius," meaning "spoon-leafed.". Its white flowers bloom in the summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its unique foliage and delicate floral display, making it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
