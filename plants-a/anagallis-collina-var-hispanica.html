Anagallis collina var. hispanica

Anagallis collina var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, particularly in coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
