Actaea cimicifuga var. simplex

Actaea cimicifuga var. simplex, commonly known as Black Cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear large, compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late summer, followed by red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
