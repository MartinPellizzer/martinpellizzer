Artemisia sieversiana var. koreana

Artemisia sieversiana var. koreana, commonly known as Korean mugwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in narrow, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
