Aspilia erosa

Aspilia erosa, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Aspilia erosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
