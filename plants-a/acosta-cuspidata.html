Acosta cuspidata

Acosta cuspidata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Acosta cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
