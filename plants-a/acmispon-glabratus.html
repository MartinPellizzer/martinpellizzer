Acmispon glabratus

Acmispon glabratus, commonly known as plains false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Acmispon glabratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
