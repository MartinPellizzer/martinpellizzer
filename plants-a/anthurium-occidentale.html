Anthurium occidentale

Anthurium occidentale, commonly known as the western anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often dark green with a glossy texture, and it produces colorful bracts that can range from red to orange.

Anthurium occidentale is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
