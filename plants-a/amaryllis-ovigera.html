Amaryllis ovigera

Amaryllis ovigera, commonly known as the yellow bell amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often broader than those of other amaryllis species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

Amaryllis ovigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
