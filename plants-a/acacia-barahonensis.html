Acacia barahonensis

Acacia barahonensis, commonly known as the Barahon acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the island of Barahona in the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
