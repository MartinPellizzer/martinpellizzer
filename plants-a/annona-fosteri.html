Annona fosteri

Annona fosteri, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are fragrant and white or pale yellow, often with a distinctive central spadix.

The fruit is a large, spiky, greenish-yellow berry that turns yellow when ripe, and is known for its creamy, sweet flesh.
