Amomum luteoalbum

Amomum luteoalbum, commonly known as the yellow-white cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
