Ayenia fluvialis

Ayenia fluvialis, commonly known as the river ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers bloom in the spring and summer, producing small yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
