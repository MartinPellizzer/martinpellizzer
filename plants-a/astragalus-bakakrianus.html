Astragalus bakakrianus

Astragalus bakakrianus, commonly known as the Bakakriana milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its seeds are enclosed in a pod, a common feature among legumes, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its deep root system.
