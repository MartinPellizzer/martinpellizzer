Astragalus somalensis var. lindblomii

Astragalus somalensis var. lindblomii, commonly known as the Somali milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly Somalia and Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from underground sources.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
