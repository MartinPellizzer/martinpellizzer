Aspidistra elegans

Aspidistra elegans, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen, is a shade-tolerant plant native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, strap-like leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spathes and berries, which are not typically showy but are important for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Aspidistra elegans is valued for its ornamental foliage and low maintenance requirements.
