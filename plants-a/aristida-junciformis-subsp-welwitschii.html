Aristida junciformis subsp. welwitschii

Aristida junciformis subsp.

welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's three-awn, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bristly spikelets that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its foliage is often gray-green, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
