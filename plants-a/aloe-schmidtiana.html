Aloe schmidtiana

Aloe schmidtiana, commonly known as the Schmidt's aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal watering.

Aloe schmidtiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
