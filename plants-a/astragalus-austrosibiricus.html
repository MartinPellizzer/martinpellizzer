Astragalus austrosibiricus

Astragalus austrosibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and mountainous regions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand harsh weather conditions.

Astragalus austrosibiricus is also valued for its ecological role in preventing soil erosion and supporting local wildlife.
