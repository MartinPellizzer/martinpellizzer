Arabis toxophylla

Arabis toxophylla, commonly known as the yellow rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
