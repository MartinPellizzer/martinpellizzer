Allium sanbornii subsp. intactum

Allium sanbornii subsp.

intactum, commonly known as the Sanborn's garlic, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces upright, cylindrical flower stalks that bear clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that typically blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
