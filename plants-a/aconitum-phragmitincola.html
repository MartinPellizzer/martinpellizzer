Aconitum phragmitincola

Aconitum phragmitincola, commonly known as the reed aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often near reed beds or in marshy areas. The plant features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, spikelike clusters, and are characteristic of the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae).

Aconitum phragmitincola is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant to handle.
