Aridaria beaufortensis

Aridaria beaufortensis, commonly known as the Beaufort sandbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
