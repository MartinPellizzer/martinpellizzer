Arenaria arabica

Arenaria arabica, commonly known as Arabian sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Arenaria arabica is often found in rocky soils and sandy habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
