Aster diffusus var. thyrsoideus

Aster diffusus var. thyrsoideus, commonly known as the creeping aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are typically blue to purple, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.
