Artocarpus klidang

Artocarpus klidang, commonly known as the Malabar chestnut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces a unique fruit with a rough, spiky exterior. The fruit contains a single large, edible seed enclosed in a fibrous pulp. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
