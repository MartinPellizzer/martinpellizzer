Astragalus minthorniae var. gracilior

Astragalus minthorniae var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variant is noted for its delicate appearance and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
