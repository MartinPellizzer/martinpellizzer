Aristolochia milhomens

Aristolochia milhomens, commonly known as Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, winding flowers that resemble pipes. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure. Its flowers are typically greenish-brown with a strong, musty odor, which attracts specific pollinators.

However, the plant contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
