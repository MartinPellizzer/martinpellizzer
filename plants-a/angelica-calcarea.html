Angelica calcarea

Angelica calcarea, commonly known as limestone angelica, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments. The plant features tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, compound leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typically appearing in late summer.

Angelica calcarea is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
