Asplenium corderoanum

Asplenium corderoanum, commonly known as the Cordero's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit. The plant typically has dark green, leathery fronds that are divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rock surfaces or in shaded forest floors.

Asplenium corderoanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
