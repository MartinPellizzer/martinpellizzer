Acinodendron divaricatum

Acinodendron divaricatum, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark and produces small, oval pods that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in reforestation efforts in degraded landscapes.
