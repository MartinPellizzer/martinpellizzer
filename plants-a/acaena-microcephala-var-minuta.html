Acaena microcephala var. minuta

Acaena microcephala var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf New Zealand bramble, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, spiny stems and produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's fruit is a hard, dark brown achene, often covered in hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its dense growth, it can form thickets that provide habitat for native wildlife.
