Amelanchier recurvata

Amelanchier recurvata, commonly known as the common serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. These berries are often consumed by birds and small mammals, contributing to the plant's ecological role.

Amelanchier recurvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
