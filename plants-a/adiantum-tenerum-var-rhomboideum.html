Adiantum tenerum var. rhomboideum

Adiantum tenerum var. rhomboideum, commonly known as the rhomboid maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green and have a graceful, arching appearance. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its rhomboid-shaped spore cases are distinctive and contribute to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage and ease of care.
