Arctostaphylos australis

Arctostaphylos australis, commonly known as the Australian manna gum, is a hardy shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen and arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pink, followed by edible, sticky berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Arctostaphylos australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
