Aspidium adiantoides

Aspidium adiantoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Aspidium adiantoides produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in lines along the veins.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its graceful, airy foliage.
