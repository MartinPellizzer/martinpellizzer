Abrotanella

Abrotanella, commonly known as the common tansy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. The plants typically have hairy, aromatic leaves and produce small, yellow to orange flower heads that resemble daisies. They are often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Abrotanella species are known for their medicinal properties and have been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
