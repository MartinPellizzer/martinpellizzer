Aster ovatus var. ripensis

Aster ovatus var. ripensis, commonly known as the riparian aster, is a flowering plant native to North America, particularly found in moist, riparian habitats.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows along riverbanks and stream edges, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
