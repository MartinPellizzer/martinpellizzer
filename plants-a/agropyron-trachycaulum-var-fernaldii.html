Agropyron trachycaulum var. fernaldii

Agropyron trachycaulum var. fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
