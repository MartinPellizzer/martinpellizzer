Asarum hayatanum

Asarum hayatanum, commonly known as the Persian wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of dark purple flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic underground tuber that is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued for its potential health benefits.
