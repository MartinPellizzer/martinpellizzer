Aster undulatus var. torquatus

Aster undulatus var. torquatus, commonly known as the curled leaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive undulating margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a desirable addition to native gardens and wetland habitats.
