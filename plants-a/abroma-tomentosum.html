Abroma tomentosum

Abroma tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly abroma, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
