Areca congesta

Areca congesta, commonly known as the red areca or red palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the tree. Areca congesta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its fruit, which is a drupe, contains a single seed and is sometimes used in local cuisines.
