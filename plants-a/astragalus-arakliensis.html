Astragalus arakliensis

Astragalus arakliensis, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine zones.

Astragalus arakliensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
