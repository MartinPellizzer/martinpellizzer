Aeonium × wildpretii nothosubsp. jinamense

Aeonium × wildpretii nothosubsp.

jinamense, commonly known as the Jinamense Aeonium, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues at the edges.

The plant produces striking inflorescences of yellow flowers during the spring or summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a container plant.
