Alyssum venustum var. multicarpum

Alyssum venustum var. multicarpum, commonly known as the clustered alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces multiple flower clusters along the stems, giving it its varietal name "multicarpum.". It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
