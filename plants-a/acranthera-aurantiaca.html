Acranthera aurantiaca

Acranthera aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange bell orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that resemble bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Acranthera aurantiaca is a perennial herb that is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
