Acridocarpus guineensis

Acridocarpus guineensis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in urban landscapes.
