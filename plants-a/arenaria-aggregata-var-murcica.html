Arenaria aggregata var. murcica

Arenaria aggregata var. murcica, commonly known as the Murcian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dunes. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
