Amaryllis liturata

Amaryllis liturata, commonly known as the silver stripe amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink with distinctive silver or cream streaks. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. It blooms in late spring or early summer, producing clusters of flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in height.

Amaryllis liturata is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-draining soil with adequate sunlight.
