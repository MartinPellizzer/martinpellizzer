Aspalathus nigra var. involucrata

Aspalathus nigra var. involucrata, commonly known as the black bush veldt, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, dark green leaves and produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in local horticulture.
