Avena delavayi

Avena delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Avena delavayi is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
