Agathosma elata

Agathosma elata, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a compact growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are aromatic and have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
