Arabis sagittata var. rosea

Arabis sagittata var. rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant features basal leaves that are arrow-shaped, giving it its species name, and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is distinguished by its rose-colored flowers, which add a delicate touch to its natural habitat.
