Acer negundo f. chrysophyllum

Acer negundo f. chrysophyllum, commonly known as the golden ash, is a variant of the ash tree characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow foliage.

This ornamental tree is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its vibrant autumn coloration. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth margin and a serrated edge. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its seasonal beauty.
