Angelonia micrantha var. perennis

Angelonia micrantha var. perennis, commonly known as the perennial angelonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, providing a neat appearance throughout the growing season.
