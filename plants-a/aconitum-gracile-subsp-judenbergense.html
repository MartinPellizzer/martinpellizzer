Aconitum gracile subsp. judenbergense

Aconitum gracile subsp.

judenbergense, commonly known as the slender monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like structure, typical of the genus Aconitum.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and grows in rocky or alpine habitats. It is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
