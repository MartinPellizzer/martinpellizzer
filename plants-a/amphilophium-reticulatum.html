Amphilophium reticulatum

Amphilophium reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers with intricate net-like vein patterns on its petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in the dappled light. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to anchor firmly in the soil while absorbing nutrients efficiently.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
