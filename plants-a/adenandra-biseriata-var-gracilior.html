Adenandra biseriata var. gracilior

Adenandra biseriata var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender adenandra, is a shrub native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. It has a woody stem and produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by animals.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
