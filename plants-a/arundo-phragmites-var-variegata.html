Arundo phragmites var. variegata

Arundo phragmites var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated reed, is a variant of the common reed, characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features long, narrow leaves with a mix of green and white or yellow coloration, which distinguishes it from the standard green form. It produces dense clusters of small, fluffy flowers in the summer, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
