Allocarya tenuifolia var. pulchella

Allocarya tenuifolia var. pulchella, commonly known as the slender-leaved allocarya, is a variant of the Allocarya tenuifolia species.

It is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological adaptations.
