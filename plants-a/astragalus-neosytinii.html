Astragalus neosytinii

Astragalus neosytinii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legumes that contain seeds.

Astragalus neosytinii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.
