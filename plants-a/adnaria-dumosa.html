Adnaria dumosa

Adnaria dumosa, commonly known as the hairy antler plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and dense clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Adnaria dumosa is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
