Atylus villosus

Atylus villosus, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Atylus villosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
