Achillea × major

Achillea × major, commonly known as York peppermint, is a hybrid perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is native to the British Isles and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and historical medicinal uses.
