Adiantum walsingense

Adiantum walsingense, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant plant native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, lace-like fronds that are characteristic of the Adiantum genus. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum walsingense is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and air-purifying qualities.
