Artanthe moritziana

Artanthe moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems and produces showy, purple or pink ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Artanthe moritziana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
