Astragalus distantior

Astragalus distantior, commonly known as the distant mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus distantior plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
