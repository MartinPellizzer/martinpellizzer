Aristida adscensionis var. modesta

Aristida adscensionis var. modesta, commonly known as the modest grass, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
