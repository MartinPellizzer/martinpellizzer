Andropogon insolitus

Andropogon insolitus, commonly known as the eastern woodland grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Andropogon insolitus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
