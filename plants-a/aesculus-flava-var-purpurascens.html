Aesculus flava var. purpurascens

Aesculus flava var. purpurascens, commonly known as the yellow buckeye with purple tinge, is a variant of the yellow buckeye native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its showy, palmate leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The tree produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by spiny, spherical fruits containing shiny, black seeds. Its bark is smooth and light gray, often developing fissures with age.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
