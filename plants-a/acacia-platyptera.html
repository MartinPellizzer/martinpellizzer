Acacia platyptera

Acacia platyptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves composed of numerous thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in compact clusters. Acacia platyptera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a capacity for rapid growth after rainfall.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
