Acer rubrum f. schlesingeri

Acer rubrum f. schlesingeri, commonly known as the Schlesinger's red maple, is a distinctive variant of the red maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant as the seasons change. The leaves are typically palmately lobed with a serrated margin, and the plant exhibits a compact, upright growth habit. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
