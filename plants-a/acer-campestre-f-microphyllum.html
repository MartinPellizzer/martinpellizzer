Acer campestre f. microphyllum

Acer campestre f. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a compact variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for small spaces and urban landscapes.
