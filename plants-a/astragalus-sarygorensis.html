Astragalus sarygorensis

Astragalus sarygorensis, commonly known as the Sarygorensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and desert regions.

Astragalus sarygorensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
