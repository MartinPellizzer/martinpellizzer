Amaranthus salicifolius

Amaranthus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Amaranthus salicifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
