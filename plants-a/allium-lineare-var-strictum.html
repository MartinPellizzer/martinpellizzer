Allium lineare var. strictum

Allium lineare var. strictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its edible leaves and bulbs, which are harvested for both culinary and medicinal uses.
