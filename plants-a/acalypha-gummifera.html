Acalypha gummifera

Acalypha gummifera, commonly known as the stickyleaf or gumtree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often sticky bark and heart-shaped leaves that are usually green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules that release tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Acalypha gummifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
