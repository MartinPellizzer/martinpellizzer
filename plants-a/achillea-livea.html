Achillea livea

Achillea livea, commonly known as the live fern, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fern-like leaves and yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Achillea livea is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
