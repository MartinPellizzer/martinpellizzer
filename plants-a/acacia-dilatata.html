Acacia dilatata

Acacia dilatata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has distinctive thorn-like stipules.

Acacia dilatata is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
