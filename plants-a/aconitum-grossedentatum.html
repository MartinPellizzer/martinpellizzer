Aconitum grossedentatum

Aconitum grossedentatum, commonly known as the giant monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers in late summer. Aconitum grossedentatum is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in partial to full shade, often found in forested areas.

Its toxic properties, due to the presence of potent alkaloids, make it a plant that requires careful handling.
