Austrobassia decurrens

Austrobassia decurrens, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Austrobassia decurrens is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland heathlands.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
