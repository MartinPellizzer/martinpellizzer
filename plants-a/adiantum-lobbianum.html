Adiantum lobbianum

Adiantum lobbianum, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are often covered in a fine, silky coating, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Adiantum lobbianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
