Astartea clavifolia

Astartea clavifolia, commonly known as the clustered starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement. The plant features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, surrounded by narrow, linear leaves. Its stems are hairy and often branching, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Astartea clavifolia plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in conservation areas due to its ecological significance.
