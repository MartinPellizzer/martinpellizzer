Aphanocalyx djumaensis

Aphanocalyx djumaensis, commonly known as the Djumaensis flame tree, is a member of the Leguminosae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, which are typically pink to red in color. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and small, stipulate leaflets. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

Aphanocalyx djumaensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
