Abies homolepis f. umbilicata

Abies homolepis f. umbilicata, commonly known as the Japanese fir or umbrella fir, is a variant of the Japanese white fir.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and short, stiff needles that give it a compact appearance. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its aesthetic appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in gardens and landscapes.
