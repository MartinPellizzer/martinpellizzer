Aristida coerulescens var. exilis

Aristida coerulescens var. exilis, commonly known as bluegrass lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its blue-green foliage gives it a distinctive appearance, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

The species is adapted to well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
