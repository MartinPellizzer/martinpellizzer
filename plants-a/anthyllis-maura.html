Anthyllis maura

Anthyllis maura, commonly known as the purple sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
