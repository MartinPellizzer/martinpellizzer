Alchemilla boleslai f. vegeta

Alchemilla boleslai f. vegeta, commonly known as the velvet alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features soft, silvery-green foliage that is covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in compact inflorescences.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in moist, shady environments.
