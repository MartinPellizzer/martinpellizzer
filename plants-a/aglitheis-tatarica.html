Aglitheis tatarica

Aglitheis tatarica, commonly known as the Tatarian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Aglitheis tatarica thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden cultivation.
