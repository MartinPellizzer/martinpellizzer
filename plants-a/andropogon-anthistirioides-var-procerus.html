Andropogon anthistirioides var. procerus

Andropogon anthistirioides var. procerus, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that are typically 1 to 3 feet in height. This grass thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of native grasslands and prairie ecosystems. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often brown or purplish in color.

Andropogon anthistirioides var. procerus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
