Anemone multifida var. saxicola

Anemone multifida var. saxicola, commonly known as the rock anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing protection against harsh weather conditions. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to challenging environments make it a notable species in botanical studies.
