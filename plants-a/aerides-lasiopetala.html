Aerides lasiopetala

Aerides lasiopetala, commonly known as the hairy-lipped orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, featuring a rhizome and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a hairy texture on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
