Argyranthemum frutescens subsp. foeniculaceum

Argyranthemum frutescens subsp.

foeniculaceum, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a compact shrub native to southern Africa. It features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season.

The plant has hairy, grayish-green leaves that are aromatic and slightly fuzzy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
