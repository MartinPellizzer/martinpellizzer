Acalypha dregei

Acalypha dregei, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red leaf plant, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges in color from deep red to green, depending on the season and light conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by three-lobed, papery bracts that give it its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Acalypha dregei is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
