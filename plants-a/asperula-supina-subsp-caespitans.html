Asperula supina subsp. caespitans

Asperula supina subsp.

caespitans, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Japan. It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland landscapes.

Its compact form and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
