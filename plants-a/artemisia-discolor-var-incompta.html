Artemisia discolor var. incompta

Artemisia discolor var. incompta, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves with a pungent, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and aromatic properties.
