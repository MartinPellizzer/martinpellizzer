Acacia explanata

Acacia explanata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia explanata is well adapted to arid conditions and is an important component of native vegetation in its habitat.
