Acacia torrei

Acacia torrei, commonly known as the Torrey acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow to golden-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia torrei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
