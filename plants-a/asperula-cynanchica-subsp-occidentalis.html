Asperula cynanchica subsp. occidentalis

Asperula cynanchica subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as Western thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in montane regions.
