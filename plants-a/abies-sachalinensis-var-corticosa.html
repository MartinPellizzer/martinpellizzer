Abies sachalinensis var. corticosa

Abies sachalinensis var. corticosa, commonly known as the Sakhalin fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical crown and dark green, needle-like leaves. The bark of this species is thick and scaly, giving it a rugged appearance. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
