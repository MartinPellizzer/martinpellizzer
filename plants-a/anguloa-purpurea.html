Anguloa purpurea

Anguloa purpurea, commonly known as the purple anguloa, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in a dense, racemose inflorescence. The plant features strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Anguloa purpurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
