Arabis kamchatica var. glabra

Arabis kamchatica var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features basal leaves that are smooth and glabrous. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
