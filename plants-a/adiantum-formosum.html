Adiantum formosum

Adiantum formosum, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to East Asia.

It features finely divided, bi-pinnate leaves that are typically bright green and turn a striking coppery red in the fall. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and thrives in partial to full shade. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce new fronds along their length.

Adiantum formosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
