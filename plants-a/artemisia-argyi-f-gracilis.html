Artemisia argyi f. gracilis

Artemisia argyi f. gracilis, commonly known as the fragrant wormwood or sweet wormwood, is a variant of the Chinese wormwood plant.

It is a shrub-like plant native to China, characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and has a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
