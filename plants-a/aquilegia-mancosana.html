Aquilegia mancosana

Aquilegia mancosana, commonly known as the Mexican coral bells, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from red to purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are palmately arranged, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists interested in its unique floral structure and adaptability.
