Agropyron elongatum var. flaccidifolium

Agropyron elongatum var. flaccidifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and have a soft, feathery texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
