Aster hydrabadensis

Aster hydrabadensis, commonly known as the Hydrabad aster, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its showy blue or purple flowers. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
