Arctostaphylos andersonii

Arctostaphylos andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Arctostaphylos andersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
