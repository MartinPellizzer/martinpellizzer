Anoplophytum bicolor

Anoplophytum bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in dense, spherical heads. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in a fine, white bloom that reflects sunlight.

Anoplophytum bicolor is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance in dry gardens.
