Aechmea brachystachys

Aechmea brachystachys, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety layer of fine trichomes, giving it a soft, moss-like appearance. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which emerge from the center of the rosette. Aechmea brachystachys thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains small, black seeds, which are dispersed by birds and mammals.
