Arrabidaea inaequalis

Arrabidaea inaequalis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences of bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

The seeds are enclosed in woody pods and dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread.
