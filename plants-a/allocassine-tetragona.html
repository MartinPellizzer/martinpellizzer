Allocassine tetragona

Allocassine tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled grass tree, is a unique plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, slender stem and narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Allocassine tetragona is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it well-suited to coastal environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
