Acaena adscendens

Acaena adscendens, commonly known as the New Zealand burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its prickly, bristly stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spherical, woody fruits known as achenes. These fruits are covered in hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals.

Acaena adscendens is often found in coastal and inland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
