Ailanthus integrifolia subsp. integrifolia

Ailanthus integrifolia subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to China. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have entire margins.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense, terminal panicles, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This species is often considered invasive in regions outside its native range.
