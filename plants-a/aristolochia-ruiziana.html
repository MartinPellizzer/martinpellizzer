Aristolochia ruiziana

Aristolochia ruiziana, commonly known as the Ruiz's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible when ripe but contain toxic compounds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Aristolochia ruiziana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and intriguing floral morphology.
