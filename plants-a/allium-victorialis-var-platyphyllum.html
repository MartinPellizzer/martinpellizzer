Allium victorialis var. platyphyllum

Allium victorialis var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of onions and garlic. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces umbels of white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbous structure is used for culinary and medicinal purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
