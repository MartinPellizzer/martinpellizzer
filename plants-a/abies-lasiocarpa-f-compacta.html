Abies lasiocarpa f. compacta

Abies lasiocarpa f. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf white fir, is a compact form of the white fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant features small, rounded cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cooler, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
