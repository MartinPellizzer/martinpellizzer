Arundinella khaseana

Arundinella khaseana, commonly known as the African reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes are usually borne at the top of the plant, producing small, greenish flowers.

Arundinella khaseana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
