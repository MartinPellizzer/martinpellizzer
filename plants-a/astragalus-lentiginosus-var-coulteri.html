Astragalus lentiginosus var. coulteri

Astragalus lentiginosus var. coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.

The species is also of interest in ecological restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
