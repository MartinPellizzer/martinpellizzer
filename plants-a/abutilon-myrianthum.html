Abutilon myrianthum

Abutilon myrianthum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Abutilon myrianthum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
