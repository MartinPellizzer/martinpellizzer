Acacia grandistipula

Acacia grandistipula, commonly known as the large-leaf acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are often visited by insects and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia grandistipula is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky slopes.
