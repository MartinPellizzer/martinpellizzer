Anthemis odontostephana

Anthemis odontostephana, commonly known as the toothstep dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disc florets. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats.

Anthemis odontostephana is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
