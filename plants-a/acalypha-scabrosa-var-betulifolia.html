Acalypha scabrosa var. betulifolia

Acalypha scabrosa var. betulifolia, commonly known as the birchleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, birch-like leaves that are elliptical and have a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are surrounded by bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and ecological role make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
