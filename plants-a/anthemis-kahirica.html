Anthemis kahirica

Anthemis kahirica, commonly known as the Kahiric chamomile, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in high-altitude regions. Its flowers are yellow with a white center, resembling those of other chamomile species.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
