Aralia grandis

Aralia grandis, commonly known as the American aralia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features large, compound leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, with each leaflet being ovate and serrated. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
