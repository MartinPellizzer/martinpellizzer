Aspidium tenericaule

Aspidium tenericaule, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Aspidium tenericaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
