Agave × massiliensis

Agave × massiliensis, commonly known as the Massiliensis agave, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Agave americana and Agave inulifolia.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly France, where it thrives in coastal environments. This species is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and often covered in a whitish bloom. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss.

It produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white flowers, which are usually pollinated by insects.
