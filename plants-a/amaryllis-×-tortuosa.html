Amaryllis × tortuosa

Amaryllis × tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papillosa.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally twisted flower stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The flowers are typically red or pink, with a distinctive, recurved shape that adds to its ornamental value. This plant thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add visual interest to their landscapes.
