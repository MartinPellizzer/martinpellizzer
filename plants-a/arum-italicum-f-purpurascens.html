Arum italicum f. purpurascens

Arum italicum f. purpurascens, commonly known as the Purple-veined Arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent purple veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate humid conditions, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
