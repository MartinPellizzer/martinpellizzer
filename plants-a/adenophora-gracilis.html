Adenophora gracilis

Adenophora gracilis, commonly known as the slender adenophora, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Adenophora gracilis is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
