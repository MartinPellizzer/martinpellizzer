Ageratum wendlandii

Ageratum wendlandii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its profuse display of small, fluffy blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Ageratum wendlandii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
