Amblynotopsis floribunda

Amblynotopsis floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous habitats. Its leaves are narrow and densely arranged, providing a lush green backdrop to its colorful blooms.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
