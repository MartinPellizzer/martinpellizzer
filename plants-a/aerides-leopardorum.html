Aerides leopardorum

Aerides leopardorum, commonly known as the leopard orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves that resemble the pattern of a leopard's skin, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often spotted or streaked. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Aerides leopardorum is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
