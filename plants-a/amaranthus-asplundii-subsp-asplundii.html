Amaranthus asplundii subsp. asplundii

Amaranthus asplundii subsp.

asplundii, commonly known as the Asplund's amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family. It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish or reddish, and it produces small, dry seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
