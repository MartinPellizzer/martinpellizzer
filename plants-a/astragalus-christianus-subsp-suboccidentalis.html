Astragalus christianus subsp. suboccidentalis

Astragalus christianus subsp.

suboccidentalis, commonly known as the Western milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in late spring. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
