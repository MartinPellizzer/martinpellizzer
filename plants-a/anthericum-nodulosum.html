Anthericum nodulosum

Anthericum nodulosum, commonly known as the nodular arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered around the base of the spadix. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Anthericum nodulosum is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
