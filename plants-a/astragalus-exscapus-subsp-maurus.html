Astragalus exscapus subsp. maurus

Astragalus exscapus subsp.

maurus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush communities.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid ecosystem dynamics.
