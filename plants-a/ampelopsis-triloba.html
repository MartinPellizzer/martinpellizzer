Ampelopsis triloba

Ampelopsis triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three lobes, which are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its climbing habit involves tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and other structures.

It is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
