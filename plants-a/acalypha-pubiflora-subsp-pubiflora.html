Acalypha pubiflora subsp. pubiflora

Acalypha pubiflora subsp.

pubiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, colorful leaves with prominent veins.

The leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
