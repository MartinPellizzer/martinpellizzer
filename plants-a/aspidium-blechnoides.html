Aspidium blechnoides

Aspidium blechnoides, commonly known as the broad fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into segments with a distinctive, toothed margin. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila, a feature common among many ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Aspidium blechnoides is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
