Aristolochia ledongensis

Aristolochia ledongensis, commonly known as the Ledong Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a strong, often unpleasant odor to attract its pollinators. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
