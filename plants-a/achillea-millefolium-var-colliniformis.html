Achillea millefolium var. colliniformis

Achillea millefolium var. colliniformis, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in full sun. Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for alpine gardens and rockeries.

It is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
