Atractylis chinensis var. loeseneri

Atractylis chinensis var. loeseneri, commonly known as Chinese Atractylis, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows or woodland edges.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
