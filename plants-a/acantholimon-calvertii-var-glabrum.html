Acantholimon calvertii var. glabrum

Acantholimon calvertii var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has smooth, glabrous foliage, which distinguishes it from other varieties in the genus. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This species is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
