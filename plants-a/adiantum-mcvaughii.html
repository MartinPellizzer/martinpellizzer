Adiantum mcvaughii

Adiantum mcvaughii, commonly known as the McVaugh's maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the southwestern United States.

It features finely divided, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

Adiantum mcvaughii is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor planting.
