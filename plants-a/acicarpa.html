Acicarpa

Acicarpa, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its edible, leafy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in a creeping or trailing manner, forming dense mats on the ground. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces small, greenish flowers.

Acicarpa is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in various culinary applications.
