Abacopteris triphylla var. parishii

Abacopteris triphylla var. parishii, commonly known as the Three-leafed Kowhai, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting native pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
