Aristida plumosa var. australis

Aristida plumosa var. australis, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, silver-gray plumes that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, silky texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native landscaping and restoration projects.
