Aster pilosus var. reevesii

Aster pilosus var. reevesii, commonly known as the Reeves' blue aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
