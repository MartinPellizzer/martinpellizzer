Acanthomintha obovata subsp. obovata

Acanthomintha obovata subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf acanthomintha, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive component of coastal and chaparral ecosystems.
