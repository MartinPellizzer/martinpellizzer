Ageratum coelestinum

Ageratum coelestinum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, adding visual interest to gardens.

Ageratum coelestinum is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
