Amorphophallus linearis

Amorphophallus linearis, commonly known as the straight-tongued arrowroot, is a tuberous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces underground tubers that are used as a starchy food source in some regions. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to regenerate from its tubers.
