Astragalus pseudoparrowianus

Astragalus pseudoparrowianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus pseudoparrowianus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
