Astragalus reinii subsp. atrosanguineus

Astragalus reinii subsp.

atrosanguineus, commonly known as the dark blood vetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically grayish-green in color.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Turkmenistan.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in steppe and desert environments.
