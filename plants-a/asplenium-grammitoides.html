Asplenium grammitoides

Asplenium grammitoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant forms dense, compact colonies and is often found growing on rocks or in shaded, moist environments. Its fronds are usually unifacial, with a smooth, leathery texture and a distinct midrib.

Asplenium grammitoides is a hardy species that thrives in temperate climates and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
