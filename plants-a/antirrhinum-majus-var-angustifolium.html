Antirrhinum majus var. angustifolium

Antirrhinum majus var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that resemble small snout-like structures. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
