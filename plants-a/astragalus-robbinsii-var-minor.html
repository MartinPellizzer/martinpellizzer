Astragalus robbinsii var. minor

Astragalus robbinsii var. minor, commonly known as the lesser Robbins' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of small, oval leaflets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators in its native environment.
