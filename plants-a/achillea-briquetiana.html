Achillea briquetiana

Achillea briquetiana, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Achillea briquetiana is often used in alpine gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.
