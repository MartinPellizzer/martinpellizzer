Alstroemeria jequitiana

Alstroemeria jequitiana, commonly known as the Jequitiba lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in horticulture, though it is less commonly cultivated than other alstroemeria species.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting beauty and vibrant hues.
