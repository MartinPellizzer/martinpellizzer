Althaea mauritiana

Althaea mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius mallow, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and produces showy, pink or purple flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Althaea mauritiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
