Aspidosperma quirandy

Aspidosperma quirandy, commonly known as the Quirandy tree, is a deciduous tree native to Argentina and parts of Uruguay.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange-red follicles containing seeds. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.

The plant also contains alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
