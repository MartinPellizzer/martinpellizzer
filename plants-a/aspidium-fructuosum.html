Aspidium fructuosum

Aspidium fructuosum, commonly known as the fruit-bearing water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to tropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. The plant has finely divided, feather-like fronds that are often submerged or floating. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium fructuosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
