Adenia metamorpha

Adenia metamorpha, commonly known as the African star apple, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring a woody stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow-orange berry that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
