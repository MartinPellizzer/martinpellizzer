Arabis nivalis

Arabis nivalis, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold climates. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in a fine white down.

Arabis nivalis is well adapted to harsh environments, with a short growing season and specialized mechanisms to survive freezing temperatures.
