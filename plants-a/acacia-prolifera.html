Acacia prolifera

Acacia prolifera, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures in mature plants. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

Acacia prolifera plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various coastal wildlife.
