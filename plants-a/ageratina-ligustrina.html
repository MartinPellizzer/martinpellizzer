Ageratina ligustrina

Ageratina ligustrina, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Ageratina ligustrina is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
