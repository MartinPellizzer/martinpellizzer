Alopecurus aequalis var. amurensis

Alopecurus aequalis var. amurensis, commonly known as Amur hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.
