Axonopus paschalis

Axonopus paschalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf stoloniferous grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its spreading growth habit through stolons. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense mat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Axonopus paschalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
