Astragalus polyceras

Astragalus polyceras, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Astragalus polyceras is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
