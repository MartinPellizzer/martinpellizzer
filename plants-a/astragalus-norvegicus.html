Astragalus norvegicus

Astragalus norvegicus, commonly known as the Norwegian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Astragalus norvegicus is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
