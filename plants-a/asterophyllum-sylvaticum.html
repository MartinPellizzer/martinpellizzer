Asterophyllum sylvaticum

Asterophyllum sylvaticum, commonly known as the wild aster, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Asterophyllum sylvaticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
