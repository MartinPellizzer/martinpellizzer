Abelmoschus rugosus

Abelmoschus rugosus, commonly known as the Chinese okra or prickly okra, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, ridged stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous edible seeds surrounded by a mucilaginous pulp. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

Abelmoschus rugosus is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in traditional cuisines.
