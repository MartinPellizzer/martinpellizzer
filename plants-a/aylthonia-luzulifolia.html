Aylthonia luzulifolia

Aylthonia luzulifolia, commonly known as the blueberry fern, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and small, clustered blue berries that mature in the fall. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its leaves are typically glossy and have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aylthonia luzulifolia is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ability to add texture to shaded garden spaces.
