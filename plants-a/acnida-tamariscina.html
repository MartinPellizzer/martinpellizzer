Acnida tamariscina

Acnida tamariscina, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is highly valued for its rich reddish color and durability.

Acnida tamariscina thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Morphology

Acnida tamariscina has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside.

The stem is cylindrical, with a smooth, reddish-brown bark that becomes rougher with age, and it grows upright with a branching pattern. The flowers are small, clustered in panicles, with yellowish-green petals and stamens, blooming in spring and pollinated by wind. The fruit is a capsule that releases small, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, allowing the plant to thrive in well-drained soils.
