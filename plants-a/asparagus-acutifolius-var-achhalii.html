Asparagus acutifolius var. achhalii

Asparagus acutifolius var. achhalii, commonly known as the Achhal asparagus, is a rare and endemic variety native to specific regions of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and is typically found in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its botanical features include a stout, creeping rhizome system and spiny stems.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
