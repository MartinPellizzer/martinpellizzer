Aster hypoleucus

Aster hypoleucus, commonly known as white wood aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale purple ray flowers and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Aster hypoleucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
