Allium nevadense subsp. cristatum

Allium nevadense subsp.

cristatum, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and open woodlands. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to arid environments.

The bulbs of this subspecies are often used in local cuisines and have a mild, onion-like flavor.
