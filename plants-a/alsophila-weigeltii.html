Alsophila weigeltii

Alsophila weigeltii, commonly known as the Weigelt's tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that supports its growth and helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically found on the underside of the fronds.

Alsophila weigeltii thrives in humid climates and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
