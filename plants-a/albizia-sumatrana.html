Albizia sumatrana

Albizia sumatrana, commonly known as the Java silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in racemes. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Albizia sumatrana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
