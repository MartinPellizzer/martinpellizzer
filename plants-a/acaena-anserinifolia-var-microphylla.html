Acaena anserinifolia var. microphylla

Acaena anserinifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved New Zealand spinach, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spherical, spiky fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and wetlands.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in New Zealand's native flora.
