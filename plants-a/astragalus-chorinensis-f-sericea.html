Astragalus chorinensis f. sericea

Astragalus chorinensis f. sericea, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow pea-like flowers. Its silky, hairy stems and leaves are adaptations to its harsh, dry environment.

This subspecies is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
