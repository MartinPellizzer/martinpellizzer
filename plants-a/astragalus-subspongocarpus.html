Astragalus subspongocarpus

Astragalus subspongocarpus, commonly known as the sponge fruit milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces unique, spongy, inflated pods that give it its common name. It is adapted to dry environments and has deep root systems to access groundwater.

Astragalus subspongocarpus is often found in steppes and rocky habitats, contributing to soil stabilization in its native range.
