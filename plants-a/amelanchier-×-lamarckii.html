Amelanchier × lamarckii

Amelanchier × lamarckii, commonly known as the European Mayflower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its attractive, rounded form and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces edible berries in the summer, which are often used in jams and pies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
