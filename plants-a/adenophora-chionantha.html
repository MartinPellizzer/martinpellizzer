Adenophora chionantha

Adenophora chionantha, commonly known as the snowbell, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy white flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Adenophora chionantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and naturalized areas.
