Asplenium subnudum

Asplenium subnudum, commonly known as the naked spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to various regions of the world, including parts of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, thriving in moist, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, narrow, and often hairless appearance. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and the plant reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Asplenium subnudum is often found in humid, tropical or subtropical climates, where it plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
