Alpinia sessilis

Alpinia sessilis, commonly known as the sessile ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and delicate floral display.

Alpinia sessilis is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and potential medicinal properties.
