Amaranthus watsonii

Amaranthus watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, greenish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Amaranthus watsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
