Adenophora pereskiifolia var. alternifolia

Adenophora pereskiifolia var. alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its flowering stems are often branched, and the flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
