Achras zapota

Achras zapota, commonly known as the chicle tree, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny trunk and large, palm-like leaves that can reach up to three meters in length. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow or orange fruits resembling small pineapples. Its latex, known as chicle, was historically used in the production of chewing gum.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
