Anotites diffusus

Anotites diffusus, commonly known as the broadleaf waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and streams. The plant has long, flexible stems that often root at the nodes, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually broad and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Anotites diffusus plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
