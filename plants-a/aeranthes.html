Aeranthes

Aeranthes, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are typically found in wet or marshy environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. They are characterized by their delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in moist conditions.

Aeranthes species are known for their ability to grow in nutrient-poor soils, making them resilient and adaptable to various wetland habitats.
