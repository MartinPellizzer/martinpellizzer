Ampelopsis acerifolia

Ampelopsis acerifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, maple-like leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red berries in the autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

Its climbing habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in moist, well-drained soils.
