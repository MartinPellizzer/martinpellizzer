Arrhenatherum rivulare var. asperum

Arrhenatherum rivulare var. asperum, commonly known as rough meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are rough to the touch. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
