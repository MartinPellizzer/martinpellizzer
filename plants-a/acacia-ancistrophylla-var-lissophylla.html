Acacia ancistrophylla var. lissophylla

Acacia ancistrophylla var. lissophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves that are typically glabrous. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
