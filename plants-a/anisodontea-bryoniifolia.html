Anisodontea bryoniifolia

Anisodontea bryoniifolia, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
