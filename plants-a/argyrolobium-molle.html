Argyrolobium molle

Argyrolobium molle, commonly known as the soft silver globular pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, silver-green leaves and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Argyrolobium molle is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ecological benefits.
