Allophylus cobbe var. villosus

Allophylus cobbe var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy alder, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically hairy or pubescent, giving it its specific epithet "villosus.". The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or yellowish in color, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or wetland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
