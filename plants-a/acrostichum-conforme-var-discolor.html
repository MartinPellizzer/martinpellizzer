Acrostichum conforme var. discolor

Acrostichum conforme var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed fern native to tropical regions.

It features long, arching fronds with a distinctive variegation of green and white or yellow patterns. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, moist soil. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

The varietal name 'discolor' refers to its unique coloration, which sets it apart from the typical green fronds of the species.
