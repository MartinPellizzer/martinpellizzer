Androcalva reticulata

Androcalva reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, net-like vein patterns on its leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Androcalva reticulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
