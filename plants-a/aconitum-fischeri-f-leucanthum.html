Aconitum fischeri f. leucanthum

Aconitum fischeri f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered monkshood, is a botanical variant of the Aconitum genus.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers, which contrast with its deep green, heart-shaped leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.

The plant's ornamental value has made it a sought-after addition to specialized gardens.
