Andropogon amplectens

Andropogon amplectens, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and broad, flat leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that mature into fluffy, seed-bearing structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie and open woodland habitats.

Andropogon amplectens plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife and livestock.
