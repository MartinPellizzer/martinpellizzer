Astragalus prattii var. pastorioides

Astragalus prattii var. pastorioides, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

This variety is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
