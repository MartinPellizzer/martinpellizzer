Arenga gamuto

Arenga gamuto, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces a fibrous stem that is used for making ropes and mats, while its sap is tapped for producing palm sugar. Arenga gamuto thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland areas with rich, well-drained soil.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to local biodiversity.
