Astragalus macrocarpus var. petiolatus

Astragalus macrocarpus var. petiolatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with stipules and petioles, and its spiny, branched stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.

Astragalus macrocarpus var. petiolatus is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
