Arnica styriaca

Arnica styriaca, commonly known as mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountain meadows.

Arnica styriaca is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
