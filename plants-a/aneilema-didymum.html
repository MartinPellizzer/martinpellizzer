Aneilema didymum

Aneilema didymum, commonly known as the twin-leaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy roots, which are used in culinary applications.

Aneilema didymum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
