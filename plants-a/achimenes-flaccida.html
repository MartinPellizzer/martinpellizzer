Achimenes flaccida

Achimenes flaccida, commonly known as the drooping achimenes, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Achimenes flaccida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
