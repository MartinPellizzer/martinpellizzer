Ageratina conspicua

Ageratina conspicua, commonly known as the showy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy blue or purple flower heads that resemble small clouds, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

Ageratina conspicua is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
