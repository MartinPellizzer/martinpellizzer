Anemonoides glabrata

Anemonoides glabrata, commonly known as the smooth anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, surrounded by heart-shaped, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, with a clumping growth habit. Its foliage is smooth and lacks hairs, giving it its scientific name "glabrata.".

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
