Acacia burkittii

Acacia burkittii, commonly known as the Burkitt's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves that are typically green and feathery. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by flat, linear pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia burkittii plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used for its timber or as a source of traditional medicine.
