Arrhenatherum elatius f. petermannii

Arrhenatherum elatius f. petermannii, commonly known as the Petermann's false oat, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This fern-like grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect populations.
