Alaticaulia phoenix

Alaticaulia phoenix, commonly known as the desert candle cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Its surface is covered in a thick layer of white, waxy hair that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The flowers, which appear at the top of the stem, are typically yellow or orange and bloom in late spring or early summer.

Alaticaulia phoenix is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient symbol of desert flora.
