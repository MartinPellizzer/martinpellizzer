Alchemilla vulgaris var. transsilvanica

Alchemilla vulgaris var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its common name reflects its traditional use in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
