Aristolochia convolvulacea

Aristolochia convolvulacea, commonly known as Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often dark purple or brown in color. The plant has heart-shaped, alternate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It is a climbing vine that can grow quite long, often using its tendrils to support itself on structures or other plants.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, flat seeds.
