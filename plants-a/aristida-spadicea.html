Aristida spadicea

Aristida spadicea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and panicle-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance.

Aristida spadicea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
