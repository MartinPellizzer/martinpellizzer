Adina zschokkei

Adina zschokkei, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for humans and animals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Adina zschokkei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.
