Achnatherum scribneri

Achnatherum scribneri, commonly known as Scribner's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Achnatherum scribneri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
