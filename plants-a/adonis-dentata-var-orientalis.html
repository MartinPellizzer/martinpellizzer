Adonis dentata var. orientalis

Adonis dentata var. orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Dog's Tooth Violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with a distinctive central boss of stamens. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
