Aconitum grossedentatum var. odaiense

Aconitum grossedentatum var. odaiense, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins, and its root system is tuberous, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
