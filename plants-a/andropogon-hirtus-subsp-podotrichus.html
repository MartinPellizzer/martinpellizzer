Andropogon hirtus subsp. podotrichus

Andropogon hirtus subsp.

podotrichus, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
