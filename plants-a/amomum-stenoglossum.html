Amomum stenoglossum

Amomum stenoglossum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped cardamom, is a tropical plant belonging to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Thailand, and Indonesia. The plant produces aromatic, dark reddish-brown fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its distinctive fragrance and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
