Aulomyrcia curatellifolia var. parvifolia

Aulomyrcia curatellifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the humid lowlands of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental berries make it a valued plant in horticulture.
