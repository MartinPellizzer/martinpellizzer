Anisolobus hebecarpus var. scandens

Anisolobus hebecarpus var. scandens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features prostrate growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in cymes and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
