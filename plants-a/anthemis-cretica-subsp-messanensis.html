Anthemis cretica subsp. messanensis

Anthemis cretica subsp.

messanensis, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and limestone formations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
