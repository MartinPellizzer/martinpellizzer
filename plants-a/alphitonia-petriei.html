Alphitonia petriei

Alphitonia petriei, commonly known as the broad-leaved ironwood, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its thick, fibrous bark and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Alphitonia petriei is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
