Aster rufibarbis

Aster rufibarbis, commonly known as the redstem aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces showy purple to pink flowers in the late summer and early fall.

Aster rufibarbis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
