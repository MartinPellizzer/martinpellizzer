Aquilegia discolor var. aragonensis

Aquilegia discolor var. aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon poppy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Spain.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, double-layered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features compound leaves with deeply lobed divisions, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming habit.
