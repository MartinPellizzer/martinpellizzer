Astragalus hallii var. hallii

Astragalus hallii var. hallii, commonly known as the Hall's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid environments.

Astragalus hallii var. hallii is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
