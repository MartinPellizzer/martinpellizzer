Anacyclus inconstans

Anacyclus inconstans, commonly known as the variable acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Anacyclus inconstans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
