Anthemis gayana var. multiramosa

Anthemis gayana var. multiramosa, commonly known as the multibranched everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is known for its aromatic, yellowish flower heads that persist for a long time after blooming, giving it the common name "everlasting.". It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
