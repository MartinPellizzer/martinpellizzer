Antennaria dioica var. gallica

Antennaria dioica var. gallica, commonly known as the Galician mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has silvery, woolly leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 20 centimeters.
