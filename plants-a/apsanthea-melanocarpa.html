Apsanthea melanocarpa

Apsanthea melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-berried everlet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, dark purple to black berries that persist on the plant throughout the year. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
