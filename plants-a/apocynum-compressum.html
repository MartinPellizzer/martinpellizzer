Apocynum compressum

Apocynum compressum, commonly known as the eastern dogbane, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the summer. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

The plant is also known for its showy, red-orange seed pods that split open to release seeds.
