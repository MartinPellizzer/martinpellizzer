Actinidia

Actinidia, commonly known as kiwifruit or Chinese gooseberry, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Actinidiaceae.

These plants are native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and are characterized by their climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves. The fruit of certain species, such as Actinidia deliciosa, is edible and highly nutritious, containing high levels of vitamin C and antioxidants. Botanically, Actinidia species are known for their small, green berries that develop from aggregate fruits.

The genus also includes ornamental species valued for their attractive foliage and fragrant flowers.
