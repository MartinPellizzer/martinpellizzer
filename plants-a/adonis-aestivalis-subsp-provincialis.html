Adonis aestivalis subsp. provincialis

Adonis aestivalis subsp.

provincialis, commonly known as the provincial autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually basal and covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, calcareous soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
