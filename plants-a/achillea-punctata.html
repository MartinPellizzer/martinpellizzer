Achillea punctata

Achillea punctata, commonly known as the spotted yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves with white markings. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
