Aspidium lobatum

Aspidium lobatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and ditches, where it thrives in moist, shaded conditions. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided into smaller, finger-like segments, giving it a feathery appearance. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in small, compact structures called sori.

Aspidium lobatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through its natural filtration properties.
