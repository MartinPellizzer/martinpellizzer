Ajuga macrosperma var. macrosperma

Ajuga macrosperma var. macrosperma, commonly known as greater bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features creeping rhizomes and dense clusters of small, dark blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
