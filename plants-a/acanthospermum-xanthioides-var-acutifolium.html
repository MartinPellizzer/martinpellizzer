Acanthospermum xanthioides var. acutifolium

Acanthospermum xanthioides var. acutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
