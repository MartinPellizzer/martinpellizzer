Aconitum variegatum subsp. paniculatum

Aconitum variegatum subsp.

paniculatum, commonly known as the variegated monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a tall, spire-like inflorescence, which is characteristic of the genus Aconitum.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
