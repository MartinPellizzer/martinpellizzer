Aydendron verrucosum var. elongatum

Aydendron verrucosum var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated blistered cedar, is a rare and distinctive plant species characterized by its unique morphology.

It features elongated, waxy leaves covered in small, raised blisters, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountainous regions or dense forests. Its reproductive structures are small and clustered, with inconspicuous flowers that rely on wind pollination.
