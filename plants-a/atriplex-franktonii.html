Atriplex franktonii

Atriplex franktonii, commonly known as Frankton's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typical of the Chenopodiaceae family. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity.

Atriplex franktonii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
