Amsinckia canadensis

Amsinckia canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, oblong fruits called cypsela, which are typically hairy and wind-dispersed. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Amsinckia canadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
