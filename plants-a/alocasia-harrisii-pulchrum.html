Alocasia harrisii-pulchrum

Alocasia harrisii-pulchrum, commonly known as the Harris' velvet leaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. Alocasia harrisii-pulchrum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
