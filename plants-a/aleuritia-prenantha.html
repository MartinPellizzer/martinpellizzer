Aleuritia prenantha

Aleuritia prenantha, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Aleuritia prenantha thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
