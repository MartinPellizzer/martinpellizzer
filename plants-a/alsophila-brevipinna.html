Alsophila brevipinna

Alsophila brevipinna, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its compact, tree-like structure with a short trunk and a dense, feather-like frond crown. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its fibrous roots, which help stabilize the soil.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
