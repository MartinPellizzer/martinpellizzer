Astragalus magdalenae var. magdalenae

Astragalus magdalenae var. magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

This species is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, playing a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
