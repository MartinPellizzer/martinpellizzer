Arachnothryx sooana

Arachnothryx sooana, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also considered a species of conservation concern.
