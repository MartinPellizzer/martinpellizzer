Astragalus micromerius

Astragalus micromerius, commonly known as the small-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus micromerius is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
