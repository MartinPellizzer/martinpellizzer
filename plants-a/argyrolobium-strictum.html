Argyrolobium strictum

Argyrolobium strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf silverhead, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
