Andropogon microstachyus

Andropogon microstachyus, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically tan or brown in color.

Andropogon microstachyus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and wildlife habitat.
