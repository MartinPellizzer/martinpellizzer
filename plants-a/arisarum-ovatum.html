Arisarum ovatum

Arisarum ovatum, commonly known as the white adder's mouth, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered around a central spadix, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Arisarum ovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
