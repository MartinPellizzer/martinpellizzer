Agrostis cremenica

Agrostis cremenica, commonly known as the Crete bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. The plant has fine, tufted blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Agrostis cremenica is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal areas due to its salt tolerance.
