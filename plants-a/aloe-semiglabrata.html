Aloe semiglabrata

Aloe semiglabrata, commonly known as the smooth aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a thin layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Aloe semiglabrata is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
