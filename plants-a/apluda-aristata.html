Apluda aristata

Apluda aristata, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Apluda aristata is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
