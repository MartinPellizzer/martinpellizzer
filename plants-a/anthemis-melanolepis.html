Anthemis melanolepis

Anthemis melanolepis, commonly known as the black-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
