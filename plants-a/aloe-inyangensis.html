Aloe inyangensis

Aloe inyangensis, commonly known as the Inyangensis Aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a typical adaptation for arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Aloe inyangensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
