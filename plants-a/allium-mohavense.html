Allium mohavense

Allium mohavense, commonly known as Mohave onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water conservation. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species.
