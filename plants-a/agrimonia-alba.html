Agrimonia alba

Agrimonia alba, commonly known as white agrimony, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Agrimonia alba is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its medicinal properties.
