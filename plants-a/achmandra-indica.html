Achmandra indica

Achmandra indica, commonly known as the Indian achmandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Achmandra indica is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
