Acinodendron scrobiculatum

Acinodendron scrobiculatum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Acinodendron scrobiculatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
