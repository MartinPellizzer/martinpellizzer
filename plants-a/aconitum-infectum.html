Aconitum infectum

Aconitum infectum, commonly known as the toxic aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant contains potent alkaloids, such as aconitine, which make it highly toxic to humans and animals. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a dangerous ornamental plant.
