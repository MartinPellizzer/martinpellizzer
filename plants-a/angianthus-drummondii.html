Angianthus drummondii

Angianthus drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's angianthus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, while its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping.
