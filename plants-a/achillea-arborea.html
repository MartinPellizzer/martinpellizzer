Achillea arborea

Achillea arborea, commonly known as tree yarrow, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody base and upright, branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and it has a strong, aromatic scent.

Achillea arborea is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.
