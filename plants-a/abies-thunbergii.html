Abies thunbergii

Abies thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needles, and distinctive blue-gray bark. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its timber and has been introduced to various regions worldwide.
