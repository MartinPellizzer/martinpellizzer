Alyssum fruticulosum

Alyssum fruticulosum, commonly known as the hairy alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum fruticulosum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and ladybugs.
