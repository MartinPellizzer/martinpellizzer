Alsine phrygia

Alsine phrygia, commonly known as Phrygian alsike, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes species known for their distinctive bracts and often edible seeds. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Alsine phrygia is valued for its hardy nature and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
