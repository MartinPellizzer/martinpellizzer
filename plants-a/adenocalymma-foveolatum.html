Adenocalymma foveolatum

Adenocalymma foveolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock heath, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Adenocalymma foveolatum is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils, where it forms dense, hardy colonies.
