Arabis cuneifolia

Arabis cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or coastal environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and bear clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Arabis cuneifolia is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
