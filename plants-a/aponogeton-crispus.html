Aponogeton crispus

Aponogeton crispus, commonly known as the velvet root or curly water clover, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are often curled and have a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. It is commonly cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value.

Aponogeton crispus is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
