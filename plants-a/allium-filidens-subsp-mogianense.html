Allium filidens subsp. mogianense

Allium filidens subsp.

mogianense, commonly known as the Mogianese leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Italy. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable addition to local cuisines.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
