Acetosella eriorhiza

Acetosella eriorhiza, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a small, low-growing plant belonging to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Acetosella eriorhiza is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to tolerate wet soils.
