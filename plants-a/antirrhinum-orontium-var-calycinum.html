Antirrhinum orontium var. calycinum

Antirrhinum orontium var. calycinum, commonly known as the California antirrhinum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to lavender. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
