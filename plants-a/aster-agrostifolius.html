Aster agrostifolius

Aster agrostifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it its scientific name, while its flowers bloom in shades of blue to purple.

Aster agrostifolius is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
