Anthericum brunneum

Anthericum brunneum, commonly known as the brown nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a creeping rhizomatous stem that produces upright flowering shoots. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August.

Anthericum brunneum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
