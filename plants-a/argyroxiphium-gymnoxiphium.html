Argyroxiphium gymnoxiphium

Argyroxiphium gymnoxiphium, commonly known as the Haleakalā silversword, is a unique plant native to the Hawaiian island of Maui.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silver-colored leaves and spiny, sword-like appearance. The plant thrives in the high-altitude, volcanic soil of the Haleakalā crater, where it is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It produces a single, showy yellow flower at the top of its stem, typically blooming once every few years.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from invasive species, it is considered a vulnerable species and is protected in its native environment.
