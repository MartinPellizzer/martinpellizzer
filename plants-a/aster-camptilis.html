Aster camptilis

Aster camptilis, commonly known as the bent-stem aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It blooms from late summer into early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster camptilis is a perennial species with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy underneath.
