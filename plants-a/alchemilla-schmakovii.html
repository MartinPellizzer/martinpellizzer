Alchemilla schmakovii

Alchemilla schmakovii, commonly known as the Schmakov's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Alchemilla schmakovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
