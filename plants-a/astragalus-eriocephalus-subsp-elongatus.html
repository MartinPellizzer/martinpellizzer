Astragalus eriocephalus subsp. elongatus

Astragalus eriocephalus subsp.

elongatus, commonly known as the elongated milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and plains. It has small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its deep root system.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
