Acer sieboldianum var. tortuosum

Acer sieboldianum var. tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its unique, twisted trunk and branching pattern, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically palmate with five to seven lobes, turning vibrant red or orange in the fall. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
