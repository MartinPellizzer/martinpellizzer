Asplenium plantagineum

Asplenium plantagineum, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds that are typically leathery and dark green. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth or on rocky substrates. Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction.

Asplenium plantagineum is also known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
