Arenaria uliginosa f. albina

Arenaria uliginosa f. albina, commonly known as the white sand pink, is a variant of the sand pink plant characterized by its pale pink to white flowers.

This succulent plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained sandy soils. Its compact growth habit and fleshy leaves help it store water, making it drought-tolerant. The flowers typically bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to rocky or sandy landscapes.

This form is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to xeriscaping.
