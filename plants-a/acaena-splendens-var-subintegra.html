Acaena splendens var. subintegra

Acaena splendens var. subintegra, commonly known as the silver burweed, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, spiky, bur-like fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal. The plant features silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine, soft hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
