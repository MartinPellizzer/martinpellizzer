Asphodelus jacobi

Asphodelus jacobi, commonly known as Jacob's asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and tall, spike-like flower clusters. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Jacob's asphodel is known for its resilience and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
