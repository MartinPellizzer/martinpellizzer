Arundina speciosa var. sarasinorum

Arundina speciosa var. sarasinorum, commonly known as the Sarasin's bamboo or the golden bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo genus Arundina.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking golden-green color. This plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical climates. It is a clump-forming perennial that can reach heights of up to 6 meters.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
