Atalaya salicifolia var. intermedia

Atalaya salicifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Willowleaf Atalaya, is a variant of the Atalaya salicifolia species, which belongs to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its willow-like leaves that are typically lanceolate and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Peru, where it thrives in moist, tropical environments.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
