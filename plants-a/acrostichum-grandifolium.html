Acrostichum grandifolium

Acrostichum grandifolium, commonly known as the giant swamp fern, is a tropical plant native to the wetlands of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, compound leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its fronds are typically arranged in a fan shape, with each leaflet bearing a serrated edge.

Acrostichum grandifolium plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
