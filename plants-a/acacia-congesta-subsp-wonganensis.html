Acacia congesta subsp. wonganensis

Acacia congesta subsp.

wonganensis, commonly known as Wongan acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, narrow, bipinnate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are typically followed by elongated, woody pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in local ecosystems and its potential for use in rehabilitation projects.
