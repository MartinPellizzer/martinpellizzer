Arbutus andrachne

Arbutus andrachne, commonly known as the Turkish strawberry tree, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark, peeling bark and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, often with a slight waxy coating.

Arbutus andrachne thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.
