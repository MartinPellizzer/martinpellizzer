Asclepias circinalis

Asclepias circinalis, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Asclepias circinalis is a member of the milkweed family, which is known for its milky sap and role as a host plant for monarch butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
