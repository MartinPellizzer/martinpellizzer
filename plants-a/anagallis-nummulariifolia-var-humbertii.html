Anagallis nummulariifolia var. humbertii

Anagallis nummulariifolia var. humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's mouse-ear, is a compact, mat-forming perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, rounded, silver-gray leaves that are densely packed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a spurred lower petal, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, charming blooms.
