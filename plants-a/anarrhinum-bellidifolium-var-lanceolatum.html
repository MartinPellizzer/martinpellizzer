Anarrhinum bellidifolium var. lanceolatum

Anarrhinum bellidifolium var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate bellidifolium, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and distinctive inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a vibrant coloration that attracts pollinators.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
