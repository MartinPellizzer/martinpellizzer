Abildgaardia hispidula subsp. longispicata

Abildgaardia hispidula subsp.

longispicata, commonly known as the long-spined abildgaardia, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, glandular trichomes.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to South Africa and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is noted for its distinctive elongated, bristly flower heads, which contribute to its ornamental value.
