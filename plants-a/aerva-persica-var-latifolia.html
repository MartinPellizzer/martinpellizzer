Aerva persica var. latifolia

Aerva persica var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf Persian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a taproot system and produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
