Adenia stylosa

Adenia stylosa, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is a climbing shrub that produces striking yellow flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its long, twisting stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
