Aconogonon × fennicum

Aconogonon × fennicum, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Aconogonon × fennicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
