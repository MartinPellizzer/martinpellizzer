Arundo ampelodesmos

Arundo ampelodesmos, commonly known as the weeping reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that droop gracefully from the stems, giving it a cascading appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water sources.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain regions.
