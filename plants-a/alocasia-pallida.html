Alocasia pallida

Alocasia pallida, commonly known as the ghost arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often pale green with a waxy, almost translucent appearance. The plant's foliage is adorned with intricate patterns and a unique veining system, making it highly sought after for ornamental purposes. Alocasia pallida thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil to flourish.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
