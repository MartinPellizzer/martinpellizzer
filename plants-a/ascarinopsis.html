Ascarinopsis

Ascarinopsis, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Ascarinaceae, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients through its stems and leaves, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or green in color. Its berries are often red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Ascarinopsis is found in various regions of the Americas and plays a unique role in its ecological community.
