Aloe ferox var. galpinii

Aloe ferox var. galpinii, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky stems and rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for its gel, which has been used traditionally for skin and health treatments.
