Acinos canus

Acinos canus, commonly known as the white mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is distinguished by its fluffy, white to pale yellow blooms that appear in late summer to early autumn. Acinos canus typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its flowers are adapted for wind pollination, and the plant is known for its hardy nature in harsh, high-altitude environments.
