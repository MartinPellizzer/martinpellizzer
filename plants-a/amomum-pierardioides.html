Amomum pierardioides

Amomum pierardioides, commonly known as the Yunnan cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark brown, oblong fruits that contain aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its rhizomes are also valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

Amomum pierardioides thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its unique flavor and therapeutic uses.
