Astragalus maroniticus

Astragalus maroniticus, commonly known as the Maronite milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus maroniticus is often found in open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.
