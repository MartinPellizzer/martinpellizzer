Acacia glabrata

Acacia glabrata, commonly known as the smooth wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia glabrata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
