Adenodolichos katangensis

Adenodolichos katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous characteristics and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes in its native region.
