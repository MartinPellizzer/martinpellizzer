Anil amoena

Anil amoena, commonly known as the Indian rose or Rosa centifolia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is characterized by its fragrant, double flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, while its stems are thorny.

Anil amoena is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
