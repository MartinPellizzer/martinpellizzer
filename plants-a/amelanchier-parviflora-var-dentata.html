Amelanchier parviflora var. dentata

Amelanchier parviflora var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its rounded, often multi-stemmed form and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including dry to mesic conditions. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in mid to late summer. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, giving the variety its "dentata" designation.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and wildlife habitat benefits.
