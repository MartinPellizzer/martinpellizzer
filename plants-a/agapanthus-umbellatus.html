Agapanthus umbellatus

Agapanthus umbellatus, commonly known as the African lily, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem that supports its showy inflorescence.
