Allium rude

Allium rude, commonly known as rough leek, is a hardy perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Allium rude is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
