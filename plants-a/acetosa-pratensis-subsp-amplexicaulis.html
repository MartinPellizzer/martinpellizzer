Acetosa pratensis subsp. amplexicaulis

Acetosa pratensis subsp.

amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping ladies' tresses, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist meadows and wetlands in Europe. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescence bearing numerous small, white or pale pink flowers.

The leaves are basal, clasping the stem, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in nutrient-poor, wet soils and is often found in habitats with high humidity.

The plant is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological importance as a habitat for various invertebrates.
