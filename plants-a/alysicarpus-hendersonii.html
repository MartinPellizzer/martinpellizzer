Alysicarpus hendersonii

Alysicarpus hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
