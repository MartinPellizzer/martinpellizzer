Acer amplum subsp. catalpifolium

Acer amplum subsp.

catalpifolium, commonly known as the Catalpa-leaved maple, is a rare and distinctive subspecies native to parts of eastern Europe. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the catalpa tree, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
