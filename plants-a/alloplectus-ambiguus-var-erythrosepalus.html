Alloplectus ambiguus var. erythrosepalus

Alloplectus ambiguus var. erythrosepalus, commonly known as the red-sepal Alloplectus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored sepals that range from red to orange, which are often more showy than the actual flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

The botanical interest in this variant lies in its unique floral morphology and its potential ecological role in its native habitat.
