Alangium macrophyllum

Alangium macrophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf alangium, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
