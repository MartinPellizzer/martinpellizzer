Andropogon exaratus var. major

Andropogon exaratus var. major, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and blue-green foliage, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairie habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Andropogon exaratus var. major plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
