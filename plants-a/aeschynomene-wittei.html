Aeschynomene wittei

Aeschynomene wittei, commonly known as the yellow star grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Aeschynomene wittei is also valued for its potential use in forage and soil stabilization.
