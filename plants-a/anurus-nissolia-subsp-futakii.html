Anurus nissolia subsp. futakii

Anurus nissolia subsp.

futakii, commonly known as the Japanese whiteflower bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
