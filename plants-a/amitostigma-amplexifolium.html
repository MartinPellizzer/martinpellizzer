Amitostigma amplexifolium

Amitostigma amplexifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves and produces tall, sparsely branched flowering stalks. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forested areas.

The plant is known for its toxicity, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
