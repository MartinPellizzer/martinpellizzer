Adenanthos macropodianus

Adenanthos macropodianus, commonly known as the large-leaf adenanthos, is an evergreen shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Adenanthos macropodianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
