Ardisia digitata

Ardisia digitata, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Ardisia digitata is evergreen and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
