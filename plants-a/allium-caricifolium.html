Allium caricifolium

Allium caricifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs, which are edible, have a pungent garlic-like flavor and are used in various culinary applications.

Allium caricifolium thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
