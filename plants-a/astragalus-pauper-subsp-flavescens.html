Astragalus pauper subsp. flavescens

Astragalus pauper subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers and slender, hairy stems.
