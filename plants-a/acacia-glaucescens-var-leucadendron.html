Acacia glaucescens var. leucadendron

Acacia glaucescens var. leucadendron, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fluffy white inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with small, narrow leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in native landscapes.
