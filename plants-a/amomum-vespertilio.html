Amomum vespertilio

Amomum vespertilio, commonly known as the evening bell pepper plant, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, red or orange berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive, spicy aroma.

Amomum vespertilio is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive issues and respiratory ailments.
