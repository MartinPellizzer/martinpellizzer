Acacia trijuga

Acacia trijuga, commonly known as the three-angled acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has slender, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia trijuga is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
