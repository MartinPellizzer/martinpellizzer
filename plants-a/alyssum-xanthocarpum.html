Alyssum xanthocarpum

Alyssum xanthocarpum, commonly known as yellow-seed alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces yellowish-brown seeds that are often used in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a popular choice among gardeners and herbalists.
