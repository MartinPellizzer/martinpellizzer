Astragalus packardiae

Astragalus packardiae, commonly known as Packard's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
