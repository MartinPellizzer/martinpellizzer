Alyssum montanum subsp. rostratum

Alyssum montanum subsp.

rostratum, commonly known as the Sierra foothill alyssum, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are generally hairy and lance-shaped, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
