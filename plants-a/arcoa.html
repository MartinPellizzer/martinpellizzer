Arcoa

Arcoa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Arcoa is also notable for being the national floral emblem of Australia.
