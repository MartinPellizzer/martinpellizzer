Armeria vulgaris var. intermedia

Armeria vulgaris var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white woolly hairs.

This variety is well adapted to sandy and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens and rockeries.
