Astragalus monspessulanus proles chlorocyaneus

Astragalus monspessulanus proles chlorocyaneus, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
