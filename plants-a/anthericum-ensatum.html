Anthericum ensatum

Anthericum ensatum, commonly known as the fragrant lily or sweet lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and are known for their mild sedative properties.

Anthericum ensatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
