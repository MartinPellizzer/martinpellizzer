Aeschynomene americana var. villosa

Aeschynomene americana var. villosa, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Aeschynomene americana var. villosa is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
