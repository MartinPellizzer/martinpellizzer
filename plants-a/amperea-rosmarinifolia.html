Amperea rosmarinifolia

Amperea rosmarinifolia, commonly known as rosemary lavender, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small blue or purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its resilience and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
