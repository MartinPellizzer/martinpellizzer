Achillea sedelmeyeriana

Achillea sedelmeyeriana, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.

The species is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
