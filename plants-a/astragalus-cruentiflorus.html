Astragalus cruentiflorus

Astragalus cruentiflorus, commonly known as the bloodyflower milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Astragalus cruentiflorus is often found in arid regions and is adapted to poor soils with low moisture.
