Athyrium caudiforme

Athyrium caudiforme, commonly known as the feather fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are divided into numerous small, linear segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its rhizome is creeping and often covered with a thin layer of soil.

Athyrium caudiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
