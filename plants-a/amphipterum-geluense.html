Amphipterum geluense

Amphipterum geluense, commonly known as the Arctic sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant has a compact growth habit with tiny, clustered leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. It is well adapted to cold climates and can survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of polar ecosystems.
