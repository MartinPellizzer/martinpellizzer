Acer pubipetiolatum var. pubipetiolatum

Acer pubipetiolatum var. pubipetiolatum, commonly known as the pubipetiolate maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive pubescent (hairy) petioles and trifoliate leaves that are alternate and often deeply lobed. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that disperse its seeds. Its growth habit is typically upright and slender, with a moderate growth rate.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
