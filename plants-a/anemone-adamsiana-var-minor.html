Anemone adamsiana var. minor

Anemone adamsiana var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf white anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features delicate, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically basal and hairy. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
