Astragalus sivandi

Astragalus sivandi, commonly known as Sivand milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Astragalus sivandi is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
