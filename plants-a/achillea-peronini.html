Achillea peronini

Achillea peronini, commonly known as the Peronini yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
