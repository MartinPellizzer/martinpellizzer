Amygdalus × uzbekistanica

Amygdalus × uzbekistanica, commonly known as the Uzbek almond, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus dulcis (sweet almond) and Prunus ferganensis (Fergana apricot).

It is native to Central Asia, particularly Uzbekistan, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and develops hard-shelled fruits containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a strong, often aromatic scent.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential agricultural use in regions with limited water resources.
