Astragalus campylosema subsp. nigripilis

Astragalus campylosema subsp.

nigripilis, commonly known as the black-hair milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, hairy stems and leaves with a serrated margin. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and rocky soils.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
