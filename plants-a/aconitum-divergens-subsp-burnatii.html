Aconitum divergens subsp. burnatii

Aconitum divergens subsp.

burnatii, commonly known as Burnat's monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers, which are typical of the genus Aconitum.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and the stem is usually hairy.

Like other aconites, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals.
