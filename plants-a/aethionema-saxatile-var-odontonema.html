Aethionema saxatile var. odontonema

Aethionema saxatile var. odontonema, commonly known as the rock-cress or mountain rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

It is a hardy perennial that is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and resilience.
