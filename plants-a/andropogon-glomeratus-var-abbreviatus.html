Andropogon glomeratus var. abbreviatus

Andropogon glomeratus var. abbreviatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
