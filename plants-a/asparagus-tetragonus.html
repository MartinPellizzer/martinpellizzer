Asparagus tetragonus

Asparagus tetragonus, commonly known as the four-angled asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often four-angled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Asparagus tetragonus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
