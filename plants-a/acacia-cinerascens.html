Acacia cinerascens

Acacia cinerascens, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Acacia cinerascens is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with its deep root system and ability to fix nitrogen.

It is also valued for its role in providing habitat and forage for wildlife.
