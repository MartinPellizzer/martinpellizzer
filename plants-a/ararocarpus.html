Ararocarpus

Ararocarpus, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, featuring dark green, glossy leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by unique, elongated fruits resembling a cluster of small, yellowish capsules. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

Ararocarpus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
