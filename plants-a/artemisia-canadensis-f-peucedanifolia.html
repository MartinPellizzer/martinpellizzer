Artemisia canadensis f. peucedanifolia

Artemisia canadensis f. peucedanifolia, commonly known as Canadian mugwort, is a subspecies of the aromatic shrub native to North America.

It features silver-gray foliage with finely divided, fern-like leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in composite inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
