Arrabidaea nicotianiflora

Arrabidaea nicotianiflora, commonly known as the "Blue Poppy," is a striking flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume-like pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is renowned for its large, showy blue or violet petals that resemble those of tobacco flowers, hence its common name. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
