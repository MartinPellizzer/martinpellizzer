Aerangis punctata

Aerangis punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers with distinct markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Aerangis punctata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
