Abutilon ferrugineum

Abutilon ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive rust-colored underside. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens in temperate climates.
