Artemisia augustana

Artemisia augustana, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its strong medicinal and pesticidal properties. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Artemisia augustana has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a component in herbal remedies.
