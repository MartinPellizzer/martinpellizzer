Acetosella incana

Acetosella incana, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are hairy and often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Acetosella incana is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appearance and historical use in herbal medicine.
