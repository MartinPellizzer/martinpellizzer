Achillea sambucina

Achillea sambucina, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy clusters of small yellow or white flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic scent, which contributes to its common name. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a soft, fuzzy texture.

Achillea sambucina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.
