Acalypha glomerata

Acalypha glomerata, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually green with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by bracts, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acalypha glomerata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
