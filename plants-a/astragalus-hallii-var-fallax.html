Astragalus hallii var. fallax

Astragalus hallii var. fallax, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with root nodules.
