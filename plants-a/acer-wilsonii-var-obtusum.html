Acer wilsonii var. obtusum

Acer wilsonii var. obtusum, commonly known as the Wilson maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with three to five lobes, often with a serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
