Amaryllis lorifolia

Amaryllis lorifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture. It is typically grown as a bulbous plant, with its underground stem storing nutrients for flowering.

Amaryllis lorifolia prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
