Aucuba japonica var. angustifolia

Aucuba japonica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf Japanese aucuba, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dark green, elliptical leaves with a glossy texture and prominent veins, often adorned with yellow or orange marginal variegation. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in partial to full shade and is prized for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

This variety is often used in landscape design for its dense growth and year-round visual interest.
