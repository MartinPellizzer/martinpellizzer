Ammannia anagalloides

Ammannia anagalloides, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
