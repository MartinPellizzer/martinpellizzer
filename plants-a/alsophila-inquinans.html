Alsophila inquinans

Alsophila inquinans, commonly known as the black-stemmed tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a dark, leathery rind. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Alsophila inquinans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
