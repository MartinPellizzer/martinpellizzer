Amentotaxus yunnanensis var. poilanei

Amentotaxus yunnanensis var. poilanei, commonly known as the Yunnan yew, is a rare and endangered coniferous species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dark, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in cool, moist mountainous environments and is often found in mixed forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
