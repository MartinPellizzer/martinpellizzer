Alchemilla vulgaris montana

Alchemilla vulgaris montana, commonly known as mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscaping.
