Aira vestita

Aira vestita, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, soft foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

Aira vestita is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
