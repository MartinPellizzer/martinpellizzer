Anota hainanensis

Anota hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan anota, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of China, particularly Hainan Island.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant is herbaceous, with opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
