Astragalus mauritanicus var. frankoi

Astragalus mauritanicus var. frankoi, commonly known as the Moroccan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across semi-arid environments.
