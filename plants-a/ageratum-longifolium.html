Ageratum longifolium

Ageratum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fluffy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum longifolium is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
