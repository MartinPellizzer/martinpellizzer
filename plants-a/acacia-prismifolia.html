Acacia prismifolia

Acacia prismifolia, commonly known as the diamond acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, slender, triangular-shaped phyllodes that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. Acacia prismifolia is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it resilient in harsh environments.

Its wood is hard and durable, historically used by indigenous communities for tool-making and construction.
