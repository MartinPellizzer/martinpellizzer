Avena strigosa var. glabrescens

Avena strigosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous oat, is a variant of the slender oat species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reduced hair coverage, giving it a smooth appearance compared to its typical counterpart. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bract-like leaves, and its seeds are enclosed in a husk.

This variant is often studied for its ecological role and potential agricultural use.
