Alfaroa costaricensis var. elongata

Alfaroa costaricensis var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated alfaroa, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its botanical features include woody stems, opposite leaf arrangement, and small, berry-like fruits.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
