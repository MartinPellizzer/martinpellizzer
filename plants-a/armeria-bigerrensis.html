Armeria bigerrensis

Armeria bigerrensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its dry habitat. It typically blooms in spring and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Armeria bigerrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
