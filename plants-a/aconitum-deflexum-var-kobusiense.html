Aconitum deflexum var. kobusiense

Aconitum deflexum var. kobusiense, commonly known as the Kobusiense Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and its stems are often hairy.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
