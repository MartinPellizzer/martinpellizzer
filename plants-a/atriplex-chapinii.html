Atriplex chapinii

Atriplex chapinii, commonly known as Chapin's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is well-suited to saline and alkaline soils.

Atriplex chapinii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
