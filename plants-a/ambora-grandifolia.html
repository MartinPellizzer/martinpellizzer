Ambora grandifolia

Ambora grandifolia, commonly known as the grandifoliate ambora, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Ambora grandifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires high humidity to flourish.
