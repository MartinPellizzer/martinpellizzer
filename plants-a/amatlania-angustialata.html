Amatlania angustialata

Amatlania angustialata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amatlania, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Amatlania angustialata thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
