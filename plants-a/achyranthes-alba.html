Achyranthes alba

Achyranthes alba, commonly known as white knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Achyranthes alba is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
