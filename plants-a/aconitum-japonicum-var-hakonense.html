Aconitum japonicum var. hakonense

Aconitum japonicum var. hakonense, commonly known as Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that resemble a hood, giving it its common name. The plant has palmately divided, deeply lobed leaves that are typically bright green and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
