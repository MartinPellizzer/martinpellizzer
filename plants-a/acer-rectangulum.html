Acer rectangulum

Acer rectangulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often narrower than those of other maple species. The leaves typically turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
