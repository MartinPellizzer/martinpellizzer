Aster sagittifolius f. albiflorus

Aster sagittifolius f. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower arrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, arrow-shaped leaves and showy white to pale pink flowers. The inflorescence consists of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
