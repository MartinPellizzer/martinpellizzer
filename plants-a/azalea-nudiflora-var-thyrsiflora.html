Azalea nudiflora var. thyrsiflora

Azalea nudiflora var. thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has elliptic to ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
