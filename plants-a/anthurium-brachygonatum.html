Anthurium brachygonatum

Anthurium brachygonatum, commonly known as the broad-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, broad veins that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium brachygonatum is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structures.
