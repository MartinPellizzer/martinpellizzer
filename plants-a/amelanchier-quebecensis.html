Amelanchier quebecensis

Amelanchier quebecensis, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces small, edible berries that ripen in early summer, typically turning from white to red to dark purple. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
