Arabis cypria

Arabis cypria, commonly known as Cyprus rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Cyprus.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters. Arabis cypria thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
