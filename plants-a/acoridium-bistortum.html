Acoridium bistortum

Acoridium bistortum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers typically separated on the spadix.

Acoridium bistortum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
