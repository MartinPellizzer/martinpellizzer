Asplenium pleurosorum

Asplenium pleurosorum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in clusters called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Asplenium pleurosorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
