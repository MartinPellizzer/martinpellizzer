Abutilon polyandrum

Abutilon polyandrum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.
