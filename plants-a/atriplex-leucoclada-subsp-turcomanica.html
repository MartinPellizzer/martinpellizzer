Atriplex leucoclada subsp. turcomanica

Atriplex leucoclada subsp.

turcomanica, commonly known as Turkmen saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its succulent, glaucous leaves that are adapted to arid and saline environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in saline or semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is valued for its salt tolerance and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
