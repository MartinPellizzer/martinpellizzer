Adenia mildbraedii

Adenia mildbraedii, commonly known as the mountain adenia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features woody stems and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understoreys.

Adenia mildbraedii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
