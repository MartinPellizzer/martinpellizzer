Ardisia rubicunda

Ardisia rubicunda, commonly known as the red beauty or red ardisia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem, making it a notable species in its native habitat.

Ardisia rubicunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and colorful fruits.
