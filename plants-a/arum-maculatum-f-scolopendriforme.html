Arum maculatum f. scolopendriforme

Arum maculatum f. scolopendriforme, commonly known as the spotted arum or the purple arum, is a cultivar of the common arum, characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a purple sheen and a distinctive, waxy, spadix.

The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and thrives in moist, shady environments. It produces showy, spatulate-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typically a deep purple or red color.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and greenhouses.
