Alstonia ciliata

Alstonia ciliata, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green bark and opposite, ovate leaves with ciliate margins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
