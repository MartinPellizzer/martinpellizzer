Acer pseudoplatanus f. obtusangulatum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. obtusangulatum, commonly known as the blunt-angled maple, is a horticultural form of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaf lobes that give it a unique appearance compared to typical maple species. The leaves are typically a vibrant green in summer and turn yellow or red in autumn. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
