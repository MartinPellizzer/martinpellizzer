Acaena splendens var. brevisericea

Acaena splendens var. brevisericea, commonly known as the short-hairy spinster bush, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, oval leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, hairy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
