Althaea officinalis var. pallida

Althaea officinalis var. pallida, commonly known as pale marshmallow, is a variant of the marshmallow plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and fuzzy, woolly stems and leaves. The plant thrives in moist, sandy or loamy soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

The plant is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
