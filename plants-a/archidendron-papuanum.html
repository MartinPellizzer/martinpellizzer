Archidendron papuanum

Archidendron papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan legume, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangements and produces small, clustered white or pink flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often dispersed by wind or animals.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
