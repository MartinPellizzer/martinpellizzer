Annona depressa

Annona depressa, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, and ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a distinctive, spiny, green to yellowish berry.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland tropical regions.
