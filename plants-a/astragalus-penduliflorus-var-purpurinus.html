Astragalus penduliflorus var. purpurinus

Astragalus penduliflorus var. purpurinus, commonly known as the purple-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus penduliflorus var. purpurinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique flowering display.
