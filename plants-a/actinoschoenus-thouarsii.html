Actinoschoenus thouarsii

Actinoschoenus thouarsii, commonly known as the Madagascar star grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and can form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Actinoschoenus thouarsii is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
