Alphonsea glabrifolia

Alphonsea glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved alphonsea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and leathery in texture. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often white or pale yellow, and it produces edible, fleshy berries.

Alphonsea glabrifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
