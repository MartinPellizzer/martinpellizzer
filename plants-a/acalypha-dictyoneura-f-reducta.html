Acalypha dictyoneura f. reducta

Acalypha dictyoneura f. reducta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
