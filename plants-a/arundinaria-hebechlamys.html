Arundinaria hebechlamys

Arundinaria hebechlamys, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Arundinaria hebechlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional Chinese gardens.
