Ananas sativus f. typicus

Ananas sativus f. typicus, commonly known as the common pineapple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a rosette of fleshy, spathaceous leaves that form a crown around the base. The plant produces a terminal flower spike that develops into a multiple-fruit, or syncarp, composed of many fused berries. The leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

This species is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in bromelain and has a distinctive sweet-tart flavor.
