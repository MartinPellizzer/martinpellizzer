Arabis × arendsii

Arabis × arendsii, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are usually succulent and fleshy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
