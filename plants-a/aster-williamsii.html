Aster williamsii

Aster williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
