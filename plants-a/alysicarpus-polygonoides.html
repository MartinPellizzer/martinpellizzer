Alysicarpus polygonoides

Alysicarpus polygonoides, commonly known as the African grass or wild millet, is a tropical and subtropical annual grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, clustered spikelets that bear tiny, oval-shaped seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its fibrous root system helps in soil stabilization, and it is sometimes used for forage or erosion control.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
