Astragalus dependens

Astragalus dependens, commonly known as the hanging milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Astragalus dependens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
