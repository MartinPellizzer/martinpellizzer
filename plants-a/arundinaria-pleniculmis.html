Arundinaria pleniculmis

Arundinaria pleniculmis, commonly known as the delicate bamboo, is a species of grass native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to three meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Arundinaria pleniculmis is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in moist garden environments.
