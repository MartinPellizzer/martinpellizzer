Alsophila rufopannosa

Alsophila rufopannosa, commonly known as the red-fronded tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The fronds are typically reddish-brown when young and turn a deep green as they mature. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
