Abrus precatorius var. novoguineensis

Abrus precatorius var. novoguineensis, commonly known as the Guinean velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and black seed pods, which contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds covered in a silky, velvety coat. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its seeds contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
