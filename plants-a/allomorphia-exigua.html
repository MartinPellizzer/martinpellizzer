Allomorphia exigua

Allomorphia exigua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved allomorph, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features delicate, linear leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
