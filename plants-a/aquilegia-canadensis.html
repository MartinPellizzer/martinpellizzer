Aquilegia canadensis

Aquilegia canadensis, commonly known as the Eastern red lady, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial shade with well-drained soil.

Aquilegia canadensis is also known for its attractive seed pods that persist into autumn.
