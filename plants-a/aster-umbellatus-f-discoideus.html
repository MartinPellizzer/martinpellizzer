Aster umbellatus f. discoideus

Aster umbellatus f. discoideus, commonly known as the disk-aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded inflorescence that resembles a disk, giving it its common name. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in a dense cluster, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
