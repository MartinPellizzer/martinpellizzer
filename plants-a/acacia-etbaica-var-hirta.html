Acacia etbaica var. hirta

Acacia etbaica var. hirta, commonly known as the Ethiopian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are typically hairy, giving it the species name *hirta*. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are reduced to flattened, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant is adapted to grow in rocky soils and is often found in dry, mountainous regions of Ethiopia.
