Allium komarowii

Allium komarowii, commonly known as the Korean garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous root system and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that form clusters at the top of the stems. Its bulbs are often used in traditional Asian cuisine for their mild garlic flavor.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility.
