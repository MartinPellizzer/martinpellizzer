Alchemilla austroaltaica

Alchemilla austroaltaica, commonly known as the Austrian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Alchemilla austroaltaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
