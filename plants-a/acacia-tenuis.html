Acacia tenuis

Acacia tenuis, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Acacia tenuis is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
