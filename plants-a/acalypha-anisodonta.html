Acalypha anisodonta

Acalypha anisodonta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a garden plant.

Acalypha anisodonta is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
