Angraecum voeltzkowianum

Angraecum voeltzkowianum, commonly known as the Voeltzkow's orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces striking white flowers with long, slender petals and a prominent, spurred lip. It blooms during the dry season, typically in the late afternoon, and relies on specific pollinators like the hawkmoth.

Its unique adaptations make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
