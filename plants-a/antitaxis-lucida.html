Antitaxis lucida

Antitaxis lucida, commonly known as the luminous antitaxis, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that emit a faint bioluminescent glow during the night. The plant's leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Its unique photoluminescent properties have made it a subject of scientific interest in botany and biotechnology.

Antitaxis lucida thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it an adaptation to its native environment.
