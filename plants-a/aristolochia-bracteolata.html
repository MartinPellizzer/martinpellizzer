Aristolochia bracteolata

Aristolochia bracteolata, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, tubular flowers that resemble pipes, typically purple or greenish in color. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
