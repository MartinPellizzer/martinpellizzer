Amaranthus dinteri var. uncinatus

Amaranthus dinteri var. uncinatus, commonly known as the dwarf amaranth, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Amaranthus dinteri var. uncinatus is also valued for its resilience to drought and its potential use in traditional medicine.
