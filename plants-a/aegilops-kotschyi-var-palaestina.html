Aegilops kotschyi var. palaestina

Aegilops kotschyi var. palaestina, commonly known as the Jordanian wild wheat, is a wild annual grass native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with florets that are typically green or pale in color. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, growing in open fields and along rocky outcrops.

This species is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and its historical significance in the development of modern wheat varieties.
