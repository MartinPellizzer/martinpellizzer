Aerva desertorum

Aerva desertorum, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor and nutritional value.

Aerva desertorum is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion and support biodiversity in harsh desert ecosystems.
