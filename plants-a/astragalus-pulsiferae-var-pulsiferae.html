Astragalus pulsiferae var. pulsiferae

Astragalus pulsiferae var. pulsiferae, commonly known as the spiked milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in sagebrush habitats and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in these ecosystems.
