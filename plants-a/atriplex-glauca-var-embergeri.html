Atriplex glauca var. embergeri

Atriplex glauca var. embergeri, commonly known as Emberger's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and salt marsh habitats.

Emberger's saltbush is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
