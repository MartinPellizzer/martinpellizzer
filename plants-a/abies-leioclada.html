Abies leioclada

Abies leioclada, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its dense, dark green foliage that is often covered in a layer of white wax. The needles are flat, stiff, and have a distinctive blue-green color, while the cones are cylindrical and typically hang downward. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions with poor soil.

It plays an important ecological role in mountainous regions, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
