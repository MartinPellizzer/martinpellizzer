Anchusa stricta

Anchusa stricta, commonly known as the upright bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers are blue to violet in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Anchusa stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ecological benefits.
