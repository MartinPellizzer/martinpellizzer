Adenocalymma splendens

Adenocalymma splendens, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Adenocalymma splendens thrives in warm, moist conditions and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
