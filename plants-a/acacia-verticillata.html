Acacia verticillata

Acacia verticillata, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches that give it a drooping appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are feathery and arranged in whorls, contributing to its ornamental value.

Acacia verticillata is adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
