Achillea aleppica subsp. aleppica

Achillea aleppica subsp.

aleppica, commonly known as the Aleppo thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features gray-green, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems topped with clusters of small, yellowish-white composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
