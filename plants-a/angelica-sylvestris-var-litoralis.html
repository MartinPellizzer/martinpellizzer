Angelica sylvestris var. litoralis

Angelica sylvestris var. litoralis, commonly known as sea angelica, is a coastal plant native to the northern shores of the Atlantic Ocean.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features large, pinnately compound leaves with a strong, aromatic odor. Its tall, unbranched stem bears clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Sea angelica is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its medicinal properties.
