Astragalus wolgensis

Astragalus wolgensis, commonly known as the Wolgen milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus wolgensis is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
