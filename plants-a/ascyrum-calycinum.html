Ascyrum calycinum

Ascyrum calycinum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Ascyrum calycinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
