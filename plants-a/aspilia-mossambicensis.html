Aspilia mossambicensis

Aspilia mossambicensis, commonly known as the African torch flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a tall, erect stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a distinct, branched inflorescence.

Aspilia mossambicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
