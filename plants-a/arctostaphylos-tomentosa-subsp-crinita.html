Arctostaphylos tomentosa subsp. crinita

Arctostaphylos tomentosa subsp.

crinita, commonly known as the hairy manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, woolly hairs.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that mature in the fall.

This species is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
