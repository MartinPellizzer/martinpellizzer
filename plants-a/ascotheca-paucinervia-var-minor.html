Ascotheca paucinervia var. minor

Ascotheca paucinervia var. minor, commonly known as the lesser narrow-veined ascotheca, is a rare and specialized plant species belonging to the family Ascomycetes.

It is characterized by its small, cup-shaped fruiting body with a smooth, often pale to reddish cap. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are primarily composed of ascospores, which are released through the apical opening of the ascus.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a cryptic and ecologically significant species in its native range.
