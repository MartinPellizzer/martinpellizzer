Astragalus ochrobius var. longemucronulatus

Astragalus ochrobius var. longemucronulatus, commonly known as the long-pointed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers clustered in racemes. The plant produces pods that are typically elongated and pointed, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

The species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and limited distribution.
