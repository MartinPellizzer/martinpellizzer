Adesmia quadripinnata f. punctata

Adesmia quadripinnata f. punctata, commonly known as the spotted adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves with a pinnate structure. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are often marked with dark spots or markings, giving it its common name.

It is a shrub-like plant that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
