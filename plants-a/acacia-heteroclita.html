Acacia heteroclita

Acacia heteroclita, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia heteroclita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
