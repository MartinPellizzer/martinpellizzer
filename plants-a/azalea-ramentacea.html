Azalea ramentacea

Azalea ramentacea, commonly known as the woolly azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Azalea ramentacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
