Abies × insignis f. speciosa

Abies × insignis f. speciosa, commonly known as the Sierra White Fir, is a hybrid fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, pyramidal crown. The leaves are needle-like, dark green on top, and whitish underneath, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high elevations.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
