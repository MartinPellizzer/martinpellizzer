Alyssum corymbosoides

Alyssum corymbosoides, commonly known as the California alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming throughout the summer months. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are typically gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
