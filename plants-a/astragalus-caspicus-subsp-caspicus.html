Astragalus caspicus subsp. caspicus

Astragalus caspicus subsp.

caspicus, commonly known as the Caspian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This subspecies is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.
