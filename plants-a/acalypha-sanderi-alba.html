Acalypha sanderi alba

Acalypha sanderi alba, commonly known as the white-leaf iron plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, white-variegated leaves that provide a unique visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Acalypha sanderi alba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate partial shade.
