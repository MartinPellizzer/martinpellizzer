Alsinanthus gypsophiloides

Alsinanthus gypsophiloides, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. Its flowers bloom during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth cycle and ability to colonize disturbed or barren landscapes.
