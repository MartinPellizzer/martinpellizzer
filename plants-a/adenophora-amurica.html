Adenophora amurica

Adenophora amurica, commonly known as the Amur bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora amurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
