Aconitum delphiniifolium var. kamchaticum

Aconitum delphiniifolium var. kamchaticum, commonly known as the Kamchatka monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately lobed, dark green leaves that are often deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Aconitum delphiniifolium var. kamchaticum is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
