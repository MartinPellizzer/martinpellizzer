Artemisia umbelliformis

Artemisia umbelliformis, commonly known as the umbrella wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, camphor-like scent.

Artemisia umbelliformis is often used in traditional medicine and has applications in herbal remedies.
