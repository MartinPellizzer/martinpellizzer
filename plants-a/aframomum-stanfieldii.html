Aframomum stanfieldii

Aframomum stanfieldii, commonly known as the African grain of paradise, is a tropical plant native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, dark brown fruits.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper, due to their pungent aroma and potential medicinal properties.
