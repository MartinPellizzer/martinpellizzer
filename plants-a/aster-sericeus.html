Aster sericeus

Aster sericeus, commonly known as silverleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It has a compact growth habit with a fibrous root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.

Aster sericeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
