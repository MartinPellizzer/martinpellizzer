Apium graveolens subsp. dulce

Apium graveolens subsp.

dulce, commonly known as celery, is a biennial or perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hollow, ridged stalks and aromatic, leafy foliage.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots and stalks are widely cultivated for their crisp texture and distinctive flavor.

Celery contains compounds like apigenin and limonene, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
