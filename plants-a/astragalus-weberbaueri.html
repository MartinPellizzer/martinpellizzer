Astragalus weberbaueri

Astragalus weberbaueri, commonly known as Weberbauer's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus weberbaueri plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.
