Acalypha communis var. brevipes

Acalypha communis var. brevipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
