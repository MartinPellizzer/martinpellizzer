Adiantum heteroclitum

Adiantum heteroclitum, commonly known as the veined maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a pale midrib. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Adiantum heteroclitum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
