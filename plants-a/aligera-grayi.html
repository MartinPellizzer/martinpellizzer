Aligera grayi

Aligera grayi, commonly known as the Gray's alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to minimize water loss in its arid habitat.

Aligera grayi is often found in alpine meadows and is a subject of interest for botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
