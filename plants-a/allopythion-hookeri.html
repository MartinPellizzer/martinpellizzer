Allopythion hookeri

Allopythion hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. The plant features slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Allopythion hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
