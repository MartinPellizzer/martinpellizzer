Antiphytum tetraquetrum

Antiphytum tetraquetrum, commonly known as the four-angled antiphytum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish or greenish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Antiphytum tetraquetrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
