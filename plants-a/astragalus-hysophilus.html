Astragalus hysophilus

Astragalus hysophilus, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Astragalus hysophilus is often found in disturbed sites and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
