Abutilon virgatum var. tomentosum

Abutilon virgatum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive tactile quality. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall textured appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and floral display.
