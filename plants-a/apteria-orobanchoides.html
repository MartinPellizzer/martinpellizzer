Apteria orobanchoides

Apteria orobanchoides, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its stem-like structure and absence of true leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and it is often found growing on the roots of grasses and other herbaceous plants. Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth of its host plants.

Its life cycle is closely tied to specific host species, making it an important subject in studies of plant parasitism.
