Abronia umbellata subsp. umbellata

Abronia umbellata subsp.

umbellata, commonly known as the coastal bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the coastline, often in areas with strong maritime influences. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in coastal conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for native insects and birds.
