Arnica amplexifolia subsp. genuina

Arnica amplexifolia subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the broad-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single terminal cluster, and the species is noted for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties. Arnica amplexifolia subsp.

genuina is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
