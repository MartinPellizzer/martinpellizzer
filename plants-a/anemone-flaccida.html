Anemone flaccida

Anemone flaccida, commonly known as the drooping anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Anemone flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and contributing to forest understory diversity.
