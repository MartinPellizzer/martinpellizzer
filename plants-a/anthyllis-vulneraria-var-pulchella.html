Anthyllis vulneraria var. pulchella

Anthyllis vulneraria var. pulchella, commonly known as the pretty sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often found in the Mediterranean region and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in soil stabilization.
