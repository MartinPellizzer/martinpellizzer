Avena chlorantha

Avena chlorantha, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a growth habit that is typically clump-forming.

Avena chlorantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
