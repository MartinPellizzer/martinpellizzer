Astragalus mexicanus var. trichocalyx

Astragalus mexicanus var. trichocalyx, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive in poor soil conditions.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
