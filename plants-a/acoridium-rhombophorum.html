Acoridium rhombophorum

Acoridium rhombophorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowroot, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acoraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America. The plant features a rhizomatous stem system and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Acoraceae family. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
