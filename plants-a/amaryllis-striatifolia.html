Amaryllis striatifolia

Amaryllis striatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is known for its showy blooms that appear in late winter to early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis striatifolia is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic, seasonal floral display.
