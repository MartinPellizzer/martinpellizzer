Astragalus atropilosulus var. aberdaricus

Astragalus atropilosulus var. aberdaricus, commonly known as the dark-hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry climates.

This variant is distinguished by its dark purple to black hairy stems and leaves, giving it a unique appearance among other astragalus species.
