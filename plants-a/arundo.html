Arundo

Arundo, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, robust grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. This plant thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, riverbanks, and marshes. Its rapid growth and extensive root system make it an effective erosion control plant.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in many regions due to its ability to outcompete native vegetation.
