Astragalus zederbaueri

Astragalus zederbaueri, commonly known as the Zederbauer's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Astragalus zederbaueri is also of interest in botanical research due to its potential medicinal properties.
