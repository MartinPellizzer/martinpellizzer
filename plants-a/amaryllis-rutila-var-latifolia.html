Amaryllis rutila var. latifolia

Amaryllis rutila var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is popular among both novice and experienced gardeners.
