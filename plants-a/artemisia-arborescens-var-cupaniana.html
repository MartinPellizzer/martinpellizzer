Artemisia arborescens var. cupaniana

Artemisia arborescens var. cupaniana, commonly known as white sage, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of small, aromatic leaves with a strong, pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its medicinal and ceremonial uses have made it culturally significant to various indigenous communities.
