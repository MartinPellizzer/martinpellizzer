Amelanchier nervosa

Amelanchier nervosa, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Amelanchier nervosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
