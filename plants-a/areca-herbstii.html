Areca herbstii

Areca herbstii, commonly known as the Herbst's areca, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
