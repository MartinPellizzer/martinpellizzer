Agapanthus hollandii

Agapanthus hollandii, commonly known as the Dutch agapanthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are actually clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Agapanthus hollandii is known for its ornamental value and ability to bloom reliably in suitable climates.
