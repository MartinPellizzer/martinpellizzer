Aristida purpurea var. capillarifolia

Aristida purpurea var. capillarifolia, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a flowering grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous fine, bristly flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray appearance due to dense pubescence.

The species is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer plant in ecological succession.
