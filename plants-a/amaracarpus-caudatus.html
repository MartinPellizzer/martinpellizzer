Amaracarpus caudatus

Amaracarpus caudatus, commonly known as the silky filbert, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that resemble elongated nuts, which are covered in a fine, silky coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of its native range.
