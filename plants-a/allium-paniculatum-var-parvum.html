Allium paniculatum var. parvum

Allium paniculatum var. parvum, commonly known as the dwarf garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered purple or pink flowers in the summer. The plant is characterized by its mild garlic scent and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

This variety is a hardy and low-maintenance plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.
