Agrostis vulgaris var. umbrosa

Agrostis vulgaris var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, often forming dense, low-growing mats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in shaded areas.
