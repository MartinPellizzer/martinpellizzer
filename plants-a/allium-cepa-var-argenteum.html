Allium cepa var. argenteum

Allium cepa var. argenteum, commonly known as the silver leek, is a botanical variant of the common onion.

It is characterized by its broad, silver-white leaves and bulbous stem that resembles a leek. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

The leaves are edible and can be used in culinary applications similar to leeks or green onions.
