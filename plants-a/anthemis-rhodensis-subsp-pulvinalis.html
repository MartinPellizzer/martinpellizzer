Anthemis rhodensis subsp. pulvinalis

Anthemis rhodensis subsp.

pulvinalis, commonly known as the pink centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant features gray-green leaves with a distinctive fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
