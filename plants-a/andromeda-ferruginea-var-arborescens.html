Andromeda ferruginea var. arborescens

Andromeda ferruginea var. arborescens, commonly known as the red-leafed andromeda, is a variant of the evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that turns a striking red or orange color in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, typically white or pink in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
