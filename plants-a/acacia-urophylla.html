Acacia urophylla

Acacia urophylla, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which bloom in spring. Its seeds are encased in elongated, woody pods that split open to release the seeds.

Acacia urophylla is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
