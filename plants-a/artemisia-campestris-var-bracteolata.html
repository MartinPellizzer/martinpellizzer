Artemisia campestris var. bracteolata

Artemisia campestris var. bracteolata, commonly known as the creeping wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dry meadows.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
