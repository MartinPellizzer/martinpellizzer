Arundinella nepalensis var. contracta

Arundinella nepalensis var. contracta, commonly known as the compact bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often shorter and more compact compared to other varieties of the species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountainous regions in Nepal and northern India. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
