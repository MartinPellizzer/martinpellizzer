Acacia riparia var. media

Acacia riparia var. media, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
