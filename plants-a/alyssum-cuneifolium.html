Alyssum cuneifolium

Alyssum cuneifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum cuneifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
