Allium rotundum var. scariosum

Allium rotundum var. scariosum, commonly known as Armenian black garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its bulbous, spherical structure with a dark purple to black skin and a strong, pungent aroma. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
