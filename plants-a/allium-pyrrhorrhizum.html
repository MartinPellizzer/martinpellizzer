Allium pyrrhorrhizum

Allium pyrrhorrhizum, commonly known as the red root garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and clustered bulbs. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, slender flower stalks that bear umbels of white or pinkish flowers. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal remedies.
