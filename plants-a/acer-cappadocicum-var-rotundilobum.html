Acer cappadocicum var. rotundilobum

Acer cappadocicum var. rotundilobum, commonly known as the Turkish maple, is a deciduous tree native to Turkey and parts of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, with rounded lobes that give it its specific variety name. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
