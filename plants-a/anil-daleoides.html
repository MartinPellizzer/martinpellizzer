Anil daleoides

Anil daleoides, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
