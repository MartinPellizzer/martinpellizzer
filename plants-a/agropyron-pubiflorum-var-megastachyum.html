Agropyron pubiflorum var. megastachyum

Agropyron pubiflorum var. megastachyum, commonly known as the giant bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces large, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, blue to purple flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
