Arthrosamanea polycephala

Arthrosamanea polycephala, commonly known as the African umbrella tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like canopy formed by its dense, spreading branches. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by hard, woody fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are typically opposite, elliptical, and have a waxy texture.

Arthrosamanea polycephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
