Acer campestre var. collinum

Acer campestre var. collinum, commonly known as the hill maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub native to central and southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive yellow-green underside. It produces small, red flowers in early spring followed by winged seeds.

The tree prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or along roadsides.
