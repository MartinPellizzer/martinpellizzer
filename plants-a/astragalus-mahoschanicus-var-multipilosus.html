Astragalus mahoschanicus var. multipilosus

Astragalus mahoschanicus var. multipilosus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep violet. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and support local biodiversity.
