Amaranthus haughtii

Amaranthus haughtii, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, green to reddish-purple flowers, which are typically borne on terminal spikes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus haughtii is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
