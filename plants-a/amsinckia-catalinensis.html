Amsinckia catalinensis

Amsinckia catalinensis, commonly known as the Catalina ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Channel Islands of California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native range. It produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Amsinckia catalinensis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
