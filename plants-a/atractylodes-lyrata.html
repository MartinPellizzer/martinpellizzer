Atractylodes lyrata

Atractylodes lyrata, commonly known as sweetroot or sweet cicely, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a stout, branched stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, adorned with alternate, finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are characterized by their sweet, aromatic scent and are often used in traditional medicine.

Atractylodes lyrata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
