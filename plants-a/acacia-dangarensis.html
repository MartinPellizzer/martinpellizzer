Acacia dangarensis

Acacia dangarensis, commonly known as the Zimbabwean acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with its deep root system allowing it to access groundwater. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The wood of Acacia dangarensis is hard and durable, making it valuable for local craftsmanship and construction.
