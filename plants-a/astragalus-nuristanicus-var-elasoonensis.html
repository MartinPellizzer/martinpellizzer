Astragalus nuristanicus var. elasoonensis

Astragalus nuristanicus var. elasoonensis, commonly known as the Nuristan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in rocky, arid soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine flora.
