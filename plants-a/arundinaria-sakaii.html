Arundinaria sakaii

Arundinaria sakaii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Sakai bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, forming a tall, grass-like structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, typical of many grass species. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy coating.

Arundinaria sakaii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
