Andropogon caricosus subsp. genuinus

Andropogon caricosus subsp.

genuinus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and bluish-green foliage, which becomes more bronze in color as it matures.

The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or purplish in hue. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common species in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
