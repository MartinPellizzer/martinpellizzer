Andropogon scoparius subsp. genuinus

Andropogon scoparius subsp.

genuinus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States. It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters.

The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish-brown in color. It is characterized by its robust rhizomes and is adapted to a wide range of soil types.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
