Azalea indica var. amoena

Azalea indica var. amoena, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It prefers partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, making it a popular choice for horticultural displays.
