Anthurium gustavii

Anthurium gustavii, commonly known as the Gustavii arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a showy, modified leaf called a spathe.

Anthurium gustavii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
