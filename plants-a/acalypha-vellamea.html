Acalypha vellamea

Acalypha vellamea, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in many areas.
