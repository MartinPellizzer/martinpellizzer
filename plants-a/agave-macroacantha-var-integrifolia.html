Agave macroacantha var. integrifolia

Agave macroacantha var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and long, narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white hairs. The plant produces striking, tall inflorescences with clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
