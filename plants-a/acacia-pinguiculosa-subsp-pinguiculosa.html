Acacia pinguiculosa subsp. pinguiculosa

Acacia pinguiculosa subsp.

pinguiculosa, commonly known as the sticky acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its dense, glandular foliage that secretes a sticky resin, which helps reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to minimize transpiration in hot, dry environments.

This subspecies is well-suited to xerophytic conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
