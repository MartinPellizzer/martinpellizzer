Anthomeles aestivalis

Anthomeles aestivalis, commonly known as the summer anthomeles, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, showy flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are often pink or purple in hue. These flowers are arranged in dense inflorescences and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anthomeles aestivalis is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
