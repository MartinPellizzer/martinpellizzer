Asparagus vaginellatus

Asparagus vaginellatus, commonly known as the Cape asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Asparagus vaginellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
