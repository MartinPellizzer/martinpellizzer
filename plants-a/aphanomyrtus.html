Aphanomyrtus

Aphanomyrtus, commonly known as the mountain gum, is a genus of flowering plants in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are typically shrubs or small trees characterized by their aromatic leaves and small, woody fruits. Aphanomyrtus species often grow in dry, rocky habitats and are adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The leaves are usually narrow and leathery, with a distinct scent when crushed.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or as ornamental plants.
