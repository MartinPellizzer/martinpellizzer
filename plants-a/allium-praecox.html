Allium praecox

Allium praecox, commonly known as early leek, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbel-like clusters, which appear in early spring. Its bulbs, which are edible, are often used in culinary applications similar to leeks or onions.

Allium praecox thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
