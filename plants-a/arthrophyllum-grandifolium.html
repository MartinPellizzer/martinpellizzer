Arthrophyllum grandifolium

Arthrophyllum grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf arthrophyllum, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features basal leaves that are broad, lance-shaped, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fibrous root system helps it thrive in well-drained soils, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
