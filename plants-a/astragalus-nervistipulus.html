Astragalus nervistipulus

Astragalus nervistipulus, commonly known as the slender-stippled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Astragalus nervistipulus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
