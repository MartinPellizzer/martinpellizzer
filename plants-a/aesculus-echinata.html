Aesculus echinata

Aesculus echinata, commonly known as the thorned horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spiny, compound leaves. The tree produces showy, white to pale pink, clustered flowers in spring, followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. Its bark is thick, furrowed, and often has a reddish-brown color.
