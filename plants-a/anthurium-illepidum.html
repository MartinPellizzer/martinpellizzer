Anthurium illepidum

Anthurium illepidum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white spadices surrounded by a colorful spathe, which can range from red to yellow. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium illepidum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
