Allium esfahanicum

Allium esfahanicum, commonly known as the Esfahan garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iranian plateau.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many species of onions and garlic. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its bulbs are often used in traditional Iranian cuisine for their pungent flavor.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous roots and layered, fleshy bulbs that store nutrients.
