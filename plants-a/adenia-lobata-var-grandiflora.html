Adenia lobata var. grandiflora

Adenia lobata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant rock fig, is a striking plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on rocky outcrops or in shaded forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
