Adenophora remotidens

Adenophora remotidens, commonly known as the distant-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

Adenophora remotidens is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
