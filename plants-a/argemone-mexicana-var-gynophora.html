Argemone mexicana var. gynophora

Argemone mexicana var. gynophora, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
