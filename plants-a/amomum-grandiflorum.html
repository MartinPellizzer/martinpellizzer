Amomum grandiflorum

Amomum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique fragrance and medicinal properties, it is valued in traditional herbal medicine and perfumery.
