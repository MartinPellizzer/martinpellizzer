Aceras hircinum var. divergens

Aceras hircinum var. divergens, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
