Arabis sogdiana

Arabis sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and arranged in clusters, often blooming in early spring.

Arabis sogdiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
