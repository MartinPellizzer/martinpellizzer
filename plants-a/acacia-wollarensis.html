Acacia wollarensis

Acacia wollarensis, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its greyish-brown bark and narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Acacia wollarensis is valued for its hardiness and is used in landscaping and for its ecological benefits.
