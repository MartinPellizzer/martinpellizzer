Arundinaria zifukuensis

Arundinaria zifukuensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grassy texture.

Arundinaria zifukuensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
