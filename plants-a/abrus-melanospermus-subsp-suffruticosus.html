Abrus melanospermus subsp. suffruticosus

Abrus melanospermus subsp.

suffruticosus, commonly known as the black seed dodder, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Fabaceae family. It lacks true roots and instead attaches itself to host plants through haustoria, drawing nutrients from its hosts.

The plant produces small, ornate pods that contain dark, toxic seeds. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its parasitic nature and toxic compounds, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
