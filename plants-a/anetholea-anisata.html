Anetholea anisata

Anetholea anisata, commonly known as star anise, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its star-shaped fruit, which contains multiple oil-rich seeds. The plant belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is cultivated for its aromatic seeds used in cooking and traditional medicine. The essential oil derived from the fruit contains anethole, which contributes to its distinctive licorice-like flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
