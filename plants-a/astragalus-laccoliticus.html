Astragalus laccoliticus

Astragalus laccoliticus, commonly known as the laccolitic milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep taproots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.

Astragalus laccoliticus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
