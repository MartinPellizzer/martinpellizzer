Artocarpus lowii

Artocarpus lowii, commonly known as the Philippine breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, pale bark. The plant produces a unique fruit with a fibrous outer husk and a starchy, edible interior. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its nutritional value, it has been historically used as a food source by indigenous communities.
