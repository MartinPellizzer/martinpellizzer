Ancistrorhynchus

Ancistrorhynchus, commonly known as the plecostomus, is a genus of armored catfish native to South American freshwater environments.

These fish are characterized by their distinctive, bony plates covering their body, which provide protection against predators. They are typically found in slow-moving rivers and streams, where they graze on algae and detritus. Ancistrorhynchus species are popular in aquariums due to their hardy nature and ability to help maintain tank cleanliness.

Their elongated, flattened bodies and whisker-like barbels are adaptations for navigating and feeding in murky waters.
