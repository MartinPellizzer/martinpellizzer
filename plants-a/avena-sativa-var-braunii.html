Avena sativa var. braunii

Avena sativa var. braunii, commonly known as Braun's oat, is a variety of the common oat that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a grass species with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a spikelet arrangement, typical of the Poaceae family. Its seeds are small and oval, with a husk that is often lighter in color compared to other oat varieties.

This variant is primarily cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in both food and animal feed applications.
