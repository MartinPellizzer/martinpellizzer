Artocarpus venenosus var. tylophyllus

Artocarpus venenosus var. tylophyllus, commonly known as the toxic fig, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces a fruit that is typically green when mature, with a thick, spiny rind. The fruit contains a central cavity filled with a milky latex, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
