Alphonsea gaudichaudiana var. latifolia

Alphonsea gaudichaudiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf alphonsea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
