Anemone obtusiloba subsp. megaphylla

Anemone obtusiloba subsp.

megaphylla, commonly known as the large-leaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
