Aulomyrcia pyrifolia

Aulomyrcia pyrifolia, commonly known as the appleleaf tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters, and it produces edible fruit that is similar in appearance to an apple.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
