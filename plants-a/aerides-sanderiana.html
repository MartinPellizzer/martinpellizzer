Aerides sanderiana

Aerides sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, with each inflorescence bearing several flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained environments.

Aerides sanderiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
