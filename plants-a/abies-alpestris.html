Abies alpestris

Abies alpestris, commonly known as the alpine fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows at high altitudes, often in alpine environments where temperatures are cold and the soil is rocky or poor. The tree has slender, cylindrical cones and needle-like leaves that are short and dark green. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

The alpine fir plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
