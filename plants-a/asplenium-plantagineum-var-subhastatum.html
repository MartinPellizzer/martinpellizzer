Asplenium plantagineum var. subhastatum

Asplenium plantagineum var. subhastatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped fronds that are often glossy and dark green. The plant typically has a compact growth habit with a short, creeping rhizome. Its fronds are divided into smaller, narrow segments with a distinctive U-shaped pinnae.

This variety thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
