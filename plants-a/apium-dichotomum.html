Apium dichotomum

Apium dichotomum, commonly known as the cow parsley or wild carrot, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and umbel-shaped flowers that typically range from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, oval fruits called mericarps, which are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and woodland edges.

Apium dichotomum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
