Azalea nudiflora spuria-pulchra

Azalea nudiflora spuria-pulchra, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are usually evergreen. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display, which ranges from white to deep pink.
