Alternanthera capituliflora

Alternanthera capituliflora, commonly known as the clustered Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, often red or pink flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually bright red or purple, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

This ornamental plant is popular in water gardens and as a container plant due to its vibrant foliage and compact growth habit.
