Astragalus ardakensis

Astragalus ardakensis, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for producing a milky sap, which has been traditionally used in local medicine.

Astragalus ardakensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
