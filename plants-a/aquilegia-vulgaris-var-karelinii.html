Aquilegia vulgaris var. karelinii

Aquilegia vulgaris var. karelinii, commonly known as the Karelin's columbine, is a variant of the common columbine that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is a herbaceous perennial with striking, spurred flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are deeply divided and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to moist, cool climates.
