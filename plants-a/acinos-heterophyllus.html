Acinos heterophyllus

Acinos heterophyllus, commonly known as the Alpine Everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually gray-green and hairy, adapting to cold and arid conditions.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
