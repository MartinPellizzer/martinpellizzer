Allophylus pinnatus

Allophylus pinnatus, commonly known as the African holly, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, opposite, and often serrated leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

Allophylus pinnatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
