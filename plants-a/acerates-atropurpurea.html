Acerates atropurpurea

Acerates atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple smoke bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, purple-pink flowers that appear in dense, plume-like clusters in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
