Armeria arenaria subsp. vestita

Armeria arenaria subsp.

vestita, commonly known as the silver sand-sedge, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
