Azalea prinophylla

Azalea prinophylla, commonly known as the pink-leaf azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of pink to reddish-purple blooms during the spring season, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth texture, while its evergreen nature contributes to its ornamental value.

Azalea prinophylla thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
