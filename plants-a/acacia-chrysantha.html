Acacia chrysantha

Acacia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The golden wattle is also the national floral emblem of Australia.
