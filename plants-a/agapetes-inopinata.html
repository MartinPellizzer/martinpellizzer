Agapetes inopinata

Agapetes inopinata, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are favored by certain wildlife species.

Agapetes inopinata thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high elevations in montane regions.
