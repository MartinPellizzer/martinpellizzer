Astragalus nevadensis subsp. granatensis

Astragalus nevadensis subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the granate milkvetch, is a perennial forb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in high-elevation areas.
