Achimenes coccinea var. superba

Achimenes coccinea var. superba, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a striking tropical perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
