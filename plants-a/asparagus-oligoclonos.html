Asparagus oligoclonos

Asparagus oligoclonos, commonly known as the clustered asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often root at the base. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, needle-like berries. Its foliage consists of finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

Asparagus oligoclonos is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
