Atriplex glomerata

Atriplex glomerata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically followed by small, bead-like fruits. It is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Atriplex glomerata plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
