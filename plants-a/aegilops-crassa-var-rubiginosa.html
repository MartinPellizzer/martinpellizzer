Aegilops crassa var. rubiginosa

Aegilops crassa var. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, erect growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other wild grasses.
