Achillea alexandri-regis f. holosericiformis

Achillea alexandri-regis f. holosericiformis, commonly known as the Alexandrian yarrow, is a rare botanical variant of the yarrow genus.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. Native to specific regions of the Mediterranean, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its holarctic distribution suggests a wide range of ecological adaptability.

Due to its limited habitat, it is considered a species of conservation interest.
