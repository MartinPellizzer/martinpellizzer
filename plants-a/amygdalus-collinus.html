Amygdalus collinus

Amygdalus collinus, commonly known as the wild almond or almond tree, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, hard, edible seeds that are encased in a thick, bitter outer shell. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Amygdalus collinus is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
