Arnica piloselloides

Arnica piloselloides, commonly known as the lesser arnica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should not be ingested.
