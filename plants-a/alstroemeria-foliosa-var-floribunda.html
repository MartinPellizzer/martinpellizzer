Alstroemeria foliosa var. floribunda

Alstroemeria foliosa var. floribunda, commonly known as the golden alstroemeria, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its strap-like, bright green leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and vibrant hues.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a rhizomatous stem that supports its upright growth habit.
