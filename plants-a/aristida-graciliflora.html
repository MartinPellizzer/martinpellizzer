Aristida graciliflora

Aristida graciliflora, commonly known as slender-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and panicle-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, slender spikelets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, sandy soils, and disturbed areas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in low-nutrient environments, making it an important component of native plant communities.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
