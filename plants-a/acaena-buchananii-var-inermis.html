Acaena buchananii var. inermis

Acaena buchananii var. inermis, commonly known as the soft burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, spiny fruits that are adapted for seed dispersal by animals. The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions, often found in coastal and inland areas.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
