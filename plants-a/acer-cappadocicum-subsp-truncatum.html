Acer cappadocicum subsp. truncatum

Acer cappadocicum subsp.

truncatum, commonly known as the Cappadocian maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey. It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its seasonal color changes.
