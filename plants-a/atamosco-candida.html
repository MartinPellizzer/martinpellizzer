Atamosco candida

Atamosco candida, commonly known as white atamosco, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Atamosco candida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
