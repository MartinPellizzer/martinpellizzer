Alchemilla pseudopsiloneura

Alchemilla pseudopsiloneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in soft, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense, rounded clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla pseudopsiloneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
