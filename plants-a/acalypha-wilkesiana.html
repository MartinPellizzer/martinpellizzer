Acalypha wilkesiana

Acalypha wilkesiana, commonly known as the "Chinese lantern" plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered within the colorful bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Acalypha wilkesiana is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.
