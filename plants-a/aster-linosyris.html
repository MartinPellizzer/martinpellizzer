Aster linosyris

Aster linosyris, commonly known as the meadow aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Aster linosyris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
