Arabis subcoriacea

Arabis subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its hairy stems and leaves are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.
