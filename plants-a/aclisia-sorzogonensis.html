Aclisia sorzogonensis

Aclisia sorzogonensis, commonly known as the Philippine star orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered critically endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
