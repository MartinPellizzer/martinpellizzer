Albizia crassiramea

Albizia crassiramea, commonly known as the thick-branch Albizia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale pink to white, arranged in dense, upright clusters. Its pods are flat, elongated, and contain several seeds.

Albizia crassiramea is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
