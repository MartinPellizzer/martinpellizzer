Acacia vanmeelii

Acacia vanmeelii, commonly known as the Van Meel's acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the acacia genus and attract various pollinators. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged droughts.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
