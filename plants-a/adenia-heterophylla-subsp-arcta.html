Adenia heterophylla subsp. arcta

Adenia heterophylla subsp.

arcta, commonly known as the Blackwood vine, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and leaves that vary in shape from ovate to lanceolate.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, rugged texture.

This species is adapted to a range of soil types and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
