Aracium praemorsum

Aracium praemorsum, commonly known as the death's-head hawkmoth, is a striking plant characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble the head of a death's-head moth.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features dark purple to almost black petals with a prominent white or yellow spot at the base, which is a key identifier. It blooms in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as moths and bees.

Aracium praemorsum is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 3 feet tall, with a compact and bushy growth habit.
