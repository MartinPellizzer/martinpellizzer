Acacia senegal

Acacia senegal, commonly known as the gum arabic tree, is a flowering plant native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with a thick, flaky bark and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant is notable for producing a valuable gum, known as acacia gum or gum arabic, which is exuded from its bark. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Acacia senegal thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils and along riverbanks.
