Aporum excavatum

Aporum excavatum, commonly known as the Mexican fire tree, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and clusters of bright red, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aporum excavatum is also known for its ability to attract birds and insects due to its showy blooms.
