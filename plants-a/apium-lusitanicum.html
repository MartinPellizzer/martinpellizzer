Apium lusitanicum

Apium lusitanicum, commonly known as the Iberian celery, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.

Apium lusitanicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
