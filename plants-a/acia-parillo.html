Acia parillo

Acia parillo, commonly known as the Parillo acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for dry, rocky landscapes.

Acia parillo is often used in traditional landscaping and has cultural significance in local communities.
