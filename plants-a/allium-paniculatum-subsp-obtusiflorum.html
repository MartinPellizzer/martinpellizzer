Allium paniculatum subsp. obtusiflorum

Allium paniculatum subsp.

obtusiflorum, commonly known as the broadflower garlic or the broad-leaved garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of broad, bluish-green leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences with numerous small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its bulbous structure, which is used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a member of the Allium genus, known for its aromatic compounds and ornamental value.
