Andropogon cabanisii

Andropogon cabanisii, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Andropogon cabanisii is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and its aesthetic appeal in native plant gardens.
