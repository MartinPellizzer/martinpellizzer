Aralia aromatica

Aralia aromatica, commonly known as sweet spikenard, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a feathery appearance and produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which emits a subtle, sweet scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
