Aster satsumensis

Aster satsumensis, commonly known as the Satsuma aster, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aster satsumensis is valued in horticulture for its beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
