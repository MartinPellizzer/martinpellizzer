Anacamptis × vanlookenii

Anacamptis × vanlookenii, commonly known as the Vanloo's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Anacamptis × vanlookenii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
