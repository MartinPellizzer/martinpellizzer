Anisotes galanae

Anisotes galanae, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a fibrous root system and produces striking, large, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The flowers emit a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.
