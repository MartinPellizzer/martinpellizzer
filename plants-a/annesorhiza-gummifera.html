Annesorhiza gummifera

Annesorhiza gummifera, commonly known as the gumroot plant, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, fleshy rhizomes that produce a sticky, resinous substance. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, often blooming in late spring.

The resin produced by this plant has been historically used by indigenous peoples for various medicinal and practical purposes.
