Archidendron peekelii

Archidendron peekelii, commonly known as the Peckel's legume, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnately divided. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds.

Archidendron peekelii is often found in moist, tropical forests and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
