Amomum racemosum

Amomum racemosum, commonly known as Chinese cardamom, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to pale green flowers, followed by oval, dark brown berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a fragrant, slightly spicy aroma.

The rhizomes are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a spice in culinary applications.
