Alstroemeria cunha

Alstroemeria cunha, commonly known as the Cunha lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has strap-like leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
