Alchemilla conjuncta f. truncata

Alchemilla conjuncta f. truncata, commonly known as the short-stemmed lady's mantle, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in loose, branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
