Andromeda calyculata var. anomala

Andromeda calyculata var. anomala, commonly known as the red alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically reddish-brown on the underside, and its small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
