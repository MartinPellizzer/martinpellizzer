Aristea juncea

Aristea juncea, commonly known as the narrowleaf aristea, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale blue flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
