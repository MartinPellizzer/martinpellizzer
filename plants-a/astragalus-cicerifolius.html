Astragalus cicerifolius

Astragalus cicerifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces cylindrical seed pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Astragalus cicerifolius is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
