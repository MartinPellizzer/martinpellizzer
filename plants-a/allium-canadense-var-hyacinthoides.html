Allium canadense var. hyacinthoides

Allium canadense var. hyacinthoides, commonly known as the Canada bunchberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that resemble hyacinths. The plant produces a bulbous root system and has grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
