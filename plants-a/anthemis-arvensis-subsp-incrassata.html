Anthemis arvensis subsp. incrassata

Anthemis arvensis subsp.

incrassata, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it can sometimes become a weed in agricultural fields.
