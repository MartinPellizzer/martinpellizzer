Aphanamixis polystachya

Aphanamixis polystachya, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a complex root system that anchors it in shallow waters and absorbs nutrients from the surrounding environment. Its spores are dispersed by water currents, allowing it to spread across wetlands and rice paddies.

Aphanamixis polystachya plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in aquatic ecosystems.
