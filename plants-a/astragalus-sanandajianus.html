Astragalus sanandajianus

Astragalus sanandajianus, commonly known as the Sanandaj Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
