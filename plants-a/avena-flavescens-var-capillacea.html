Avena flavescens var. capillacea

Avena flavescens var. capillacea, commonly known as the silky oat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The silky oat is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
