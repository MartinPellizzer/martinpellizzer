Aegiphila obducta

Aegiphila obducta, commonly known as the velvetleaf aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or shaded areas.

Aegiphila obducta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
