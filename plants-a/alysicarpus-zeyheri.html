Alysicarpus zeyheri

Alysicarpus zeyheri, commonly known as the African burr bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its seeds, which are used as a food source in some regions.
