Acacia kimberleyensis

Acacia kimberleyensis, commonly known as the Kimberley acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Kimberley region of northern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow spherical flower heads that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep roots and reduced leaf surface area. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.

The timber of Acacia kimberleyensis is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in traditional and contemporary crafts.
