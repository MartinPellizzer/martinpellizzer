Adamanthus arbuscula

Adamanthus arbuscula, commonly known as the Florida holly or dwarf holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with spines along the leaf edges. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Adamanthus arbuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
