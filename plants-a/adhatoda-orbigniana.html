Adhatoda orbigniana

Adhatoda orbigniana, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet tubular flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
