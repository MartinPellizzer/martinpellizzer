Amethystanthus inflexus f. subglaber

Amethystanthus inflexus f. subglaber, commonly known as the glabrous amethyst anthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive purple to violet flowers. The subspecies subglaber is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves and stems. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

This plant is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
