Amorphophallus pulchellus

Amorphophallus pulchellus, commonly known as the red-tailed arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in length. The plant produces a striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a phallic shape, hence its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
