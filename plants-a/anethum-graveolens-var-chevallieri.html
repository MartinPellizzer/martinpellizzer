Anethum graveolens var. chevallieri

Anethum graveolens var. chevallieri, commonly known as Corsican dill, is a variant of the celery plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is cultivated for its aromatic leaves and seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate water and cooler temperatures.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both gardens and traditional herbal practices.
