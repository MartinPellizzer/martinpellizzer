Alsophila contaminans

Alsophila contaminans, commonly known as the staghorn fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a rugged, fibrous trunk covered in moss and roots. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often epiphytic, growing on trees or rocks.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
