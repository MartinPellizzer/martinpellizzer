Allium subtilissimum

Allium subtilissimum, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clustered flower heads. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bulbs, though not as widely cultivated as other alliums, are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium subtilissimum is often grown for its ornamental value and culinary potential in specialized gardens.
