Amaryllis bulbisperma

Amaryllis bulbisperma, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers with a distinctive spider-like appearance, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces a tall, sturdy stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of funnel-shaped blooms. Its bulbous root system allows it to store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Amaryllis bulbisperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
