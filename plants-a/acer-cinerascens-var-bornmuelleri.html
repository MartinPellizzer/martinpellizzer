Acer cinerascens var. bornmuelleri

Acer cinerascens var. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's smokebark maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ash-gray bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a unique visual appearance. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a dark green upper surface that turns vibrant red or orange in the fall. It produces small, red flowers in early spring followed by winged seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental bark and adaptability to various soil types.
