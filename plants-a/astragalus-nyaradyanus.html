Astragalus nyaradyanus

Astragalus nyaradyanus, commonly known as the Nyarady milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
