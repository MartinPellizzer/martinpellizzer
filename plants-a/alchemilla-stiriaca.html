Alchemilla stiriaca

Alchemilla stiriaca, commonly known as Stiriac alkanet, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Alchemilla stiriaca thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
