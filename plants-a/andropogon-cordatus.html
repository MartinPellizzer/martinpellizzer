Andropogon cordatus

Andropogon cordatus, commonly known as flatleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both bisexual and female flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of open woodlands and grasslands.

Its resilience and adaptability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
