Actaea kashmiriana

Actaea kashmiriana, commonly known as Kashmir bugbane, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear large, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, spicate inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
