Acridocarpus adenophorus var. porantherus

Acridocarpus adenophorus var. porantherus, commonly known as the Porantherus flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of southern India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody capsules as its fruit. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
