Acer pectinatum subsp. taronense

Acer pectinatum subsp.

taronense, commonly known as the Taronese maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
