Acer crataegifolium var. macrophyllum

Acer crataegifolium var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 10 inches in length. The tree produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red samaras that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinct flaky texture that becomes more pronounced with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant fall foliage.
