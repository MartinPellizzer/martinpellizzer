Achyranthes polygonata

Achyranthes polygonata, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its root system is extensive and rhizomatous, which contributes to its aggressive growth.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
