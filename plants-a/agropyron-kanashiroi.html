Agropyron kanashiroi

Agropyron kanashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Agropyron kanashiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
