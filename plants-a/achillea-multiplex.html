Achillea multiplex

Achillea multiplex, commonly known as yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a creeping rhizome and can grow up to 60 cm tall, with basal leaves that are pinnately divided. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in flat-topped clusters called cymes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name honors Achilles, the legendary Greek warrior, due to its historical use in treating wounds.
