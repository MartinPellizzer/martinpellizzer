Aconitum geranioides

Aconitum geranioides, commonly known as the garden aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
