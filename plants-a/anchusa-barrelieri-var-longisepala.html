Anchusa barrelieri var. longisepala

Anchusa barrelieri var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled anchusa, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help it adapt to its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
