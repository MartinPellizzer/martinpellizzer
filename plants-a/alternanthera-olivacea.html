Alternanthera olivacea

Alternanthera olivacea, commonly known as the golden plant or red-veined sorrel, is a striking aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red veins contrasting with its glossy green leaves. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in aquariums and water gardens. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and can vary in color depending on light exposure and nutrient levels.

Alternanthera olivacea is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among aquatic plant enthusiasts.
