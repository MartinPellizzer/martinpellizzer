Agapetes helenae

Agapetes helenae, commonly known as the Helen's silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a silvery or whitish pubescence, giving it its common name. Agapetes helenae is typically found in humid, shaded environments at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
