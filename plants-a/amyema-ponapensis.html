Amyema ponapensis

Amyema ponapensis, commonly known as the Ponap Island lily, is a rare flowering plant native to the island of Pohnpei in the Caroline Islands.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, elongated seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Amyema ponapensis is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
