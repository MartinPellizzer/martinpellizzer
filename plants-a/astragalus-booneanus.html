Astragalus booneanus

Astragalus booneanus, commonly known as Boone's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
