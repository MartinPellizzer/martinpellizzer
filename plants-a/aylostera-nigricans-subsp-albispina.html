Aylostera nigricans subsp. albispina

Aylostera nigricans subsp.

albispina, commonly known as the white-spined aylostera, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly rough texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
