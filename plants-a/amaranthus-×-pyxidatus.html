Amaranthus × pyxidatus

Amaranthus × pyxidatus, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with simple, lance-shaped leaves. Its seeds are small, hard, and often used in traditional cuisine or as bird feed.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
