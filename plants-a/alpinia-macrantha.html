Alpinia macrantha

Alpinia macrantha, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or mountain ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, leafy stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The rhizomes of Alpinia macrantha are also utilized for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
