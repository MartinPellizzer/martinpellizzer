Aspilia phyllostachya

Aspilia phyllostachya, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small, clustered blooms. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, orange-red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
