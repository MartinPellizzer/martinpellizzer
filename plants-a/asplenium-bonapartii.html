Asplenium bonapartii

Asplenium bonapartii, commonly known as the Bonaparte's spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a common feature in ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or on tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium bonapartii is relatively uncommon and is often considered a rare or endangered species in its native habitats.
