Ardisia bhotanica

Ardisia bhotanica, commonly known as the Himalayan beauty, is a member of the Ericaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring. Its berries, which are red and fleshy, mature in the fall and are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Ardisia bhotanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
