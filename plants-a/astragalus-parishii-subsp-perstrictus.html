Astragalus parishii subsp. perstrictus

Astragalus parishii subsp.

perstrictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
