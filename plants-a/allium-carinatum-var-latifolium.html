Allium carinatum var. latifolium

Allium carinatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in umbels, which attract pollinators such as bees. Its bulbous roots are edible and can be harvested for use in cooking.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species like garlic and leeks.
