Aquilegia glaucescens

Aquilegia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in many temperate climates.
