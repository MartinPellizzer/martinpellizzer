Anguria gaudichaudiana

Anguria gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Hawaiian watermelon, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and bright orange to red fruits. Anguria gaudichaudiana is valued for its edible, juicy fruits and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes as well.
