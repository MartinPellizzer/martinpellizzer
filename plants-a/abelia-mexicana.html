Abelia mexicana

Abelia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican abelia, is a flowering shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering period attracts pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Abelia mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Native Distribution

Abelia mexicana is primarily native to Mexico and parts of Central America, including countries such as Guatemala, Belize, and Honduras.

It is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils. This plant thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments, often growing in the understory of forests or along stream banks. It is also known to occur in the Caribbean islands, such as Cuba and Hispaniola.

Abelia mexicana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.

Abelia mexicana was mainly introduced to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.
