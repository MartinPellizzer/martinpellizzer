Abelia mexicana

Abelia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican abelia, is a flowering shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering period attracts pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Abelia mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
