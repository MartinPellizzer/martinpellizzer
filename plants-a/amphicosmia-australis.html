Amphicosmia australis

Amphicosmia australis, commonly known as the Australian sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Amphicosmia australis is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
