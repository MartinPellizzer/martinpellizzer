Anaclanthe namaquensis

Anaclanthe namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Anaclanthe namaquensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
