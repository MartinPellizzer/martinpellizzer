Astragalus sikkimensis

Astragalus sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus sikkimensis is also valued for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
