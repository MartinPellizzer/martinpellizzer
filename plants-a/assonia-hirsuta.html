Assonia hirsuta

Assonia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy assonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Assonia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
