Acrostichum raddii

Acrostichum raddii, commonly known as the giant reed or giant bamboo, is a tall, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has long, slender, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in wetland environments.

Acrostichum raddii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in moist areas.
