Artocarpus bifaria

Artocarpus bifaria, commonly known as the giant breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a unique fruit with a thick, spiny rind. The fruit contains a soft, starchy pulp that is edible and often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in many regions.
