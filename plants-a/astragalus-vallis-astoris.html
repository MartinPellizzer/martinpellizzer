Astragalus vallis-astoris

Astragalus vallis-astoris, commonly known as the valley milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus vallis-astoris plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
