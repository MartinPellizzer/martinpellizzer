Aira macranthera

Aira macranthera, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and produces fluffy, white seed heads that resemble dandelion fluff. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. It has a clumping growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
