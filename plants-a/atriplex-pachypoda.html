Atriplex pachypoda

Atriplex pachypoda, commonly known as the thickstem saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant is adapted to thrive in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, and it produces edible seeds that are consumed by various wildlife species.

Atriplex pachypoda plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage in harsh desert conditions.
