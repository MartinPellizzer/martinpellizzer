Alchemilla alluaudii

Alchemilla alluaudii, commonly known as the Moroccan lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Alchemilla alluaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
