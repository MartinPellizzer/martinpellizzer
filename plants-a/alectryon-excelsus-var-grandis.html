Alectryon excelsus var. grandis

Alectryon excelsus var. grandis, commonly known as the large-fruited gloryflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a distinctive, woody capsule that splits open to release numerous small, reddish seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
