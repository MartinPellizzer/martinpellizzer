Agrostis × hondoensis

Agrostis × hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo bentgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Agrostis × hondoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
