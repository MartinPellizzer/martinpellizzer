Alopecurus vaginatus var. textilis

Alopecurus vaginatus var. textilis, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, soft leaves and dense, tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in pairs, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
