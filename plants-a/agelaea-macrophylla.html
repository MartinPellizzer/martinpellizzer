Agelaea macrophylla

Agelaea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to three meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers that are followed by large, fibrous fruits. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Agelaea macrophylla thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove forests.
