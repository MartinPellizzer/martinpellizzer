Acer monspessulanum f. divergens

Acer monspessulanum f. divergens, commonly known as the Spanish maple, is a variant of the maple tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn orange-red in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
