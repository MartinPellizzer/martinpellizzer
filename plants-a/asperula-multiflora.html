Asperula multiflora

Asperula multiflora, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and southern Korea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. Its flowers, which range in color from white to deep pink, bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Asperula multiflora is also valued for its adaptability and ability to form thickets in shaded woodland environments.
