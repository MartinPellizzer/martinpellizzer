Acetosella imbricata

Acetosella imbricata, commonly known as the crested mouse-ear, is a small perennial herb belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Acetosella imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
