Antidesma bunius

Antidesma bunius, commonly known as the sour fig or Indian sour fig, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature and is often used in traditional medicine.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a strong, aromatic scent.
