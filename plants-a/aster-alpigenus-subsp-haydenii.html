Aster alpigenus subsp. haydenii

Aster alpigenus subsp.

haydenii, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
