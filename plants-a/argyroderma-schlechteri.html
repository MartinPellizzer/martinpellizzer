Argyroderma schlechteri

Argyroderma schlechteri, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

The tree is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
