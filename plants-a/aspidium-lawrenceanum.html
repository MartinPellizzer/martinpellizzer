Aspidium lawrenceanum

Aspidium lawrenceanum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of fertile fronds with sporangia.

Aspidium lawrenceanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
