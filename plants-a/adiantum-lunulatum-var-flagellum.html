Adiantum lunulatum var. flagellum

Adiantum lunulatum var. flagellum, commonly known as the flagellum maidenhair fern, is a striking fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often trail from the plant, giving it a flowing, almost ethereal appearance. The fronds are typically bright green, with a slightly waxy texture, and they produce small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside. This variety thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
