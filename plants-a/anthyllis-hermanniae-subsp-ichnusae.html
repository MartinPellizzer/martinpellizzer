Anthyllis hermanniae subsp. ichnusae

Anthyllis hermanniae subsp.

ichnusae, commonly known as the Hermann's anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

This species thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or dry habitats.
