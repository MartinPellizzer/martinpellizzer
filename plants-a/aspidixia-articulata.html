Aspidixia articulata

Aspidixia articulata, commonly known as the articulated fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching fronds that are often submerged or floating on the water surface. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread along the bottom of ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a finely divided structure that enhances its ability to absorb nutrients from the water.

Aspidixia articulata plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
