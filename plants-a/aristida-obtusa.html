Aristida obtusa

Aristida obtusa, commonly known as broadleaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for dandelion fluff. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for native gardens and erosion control. Its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.

The species is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to withstand drought conditions.
