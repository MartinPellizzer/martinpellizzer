Aconitum napellus var. microphyllum

Aconitum napellus var. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking violet-blue flowers arranged in a spires, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are smaller than those of the standard Aconitum napellus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its toxic properties, due to the presence of alkaloids like aconitine, make it a plant to be handled with caution.
