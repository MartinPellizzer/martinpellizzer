Adenophora insularis

Adenophora insularis, commonly known as the island bellflower, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Adenophora insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
