Actinocephalus brachypus

Actinocephalus brachypus, commonly known as the broad-leaved actinocephalus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
