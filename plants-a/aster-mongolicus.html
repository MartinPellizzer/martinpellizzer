Aster mongolicus

Aster mongolicus, commonly known as the Mongolian aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in the summer months.

Aster mongolicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
