Aegiphila oblongifolia

Aegiphila oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical climates.

Aegiphila oblongifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its small, clustered flowers are pinkish-white in color, with five petals and a tubular structure, blooming in late spring to early summer and pollinated primarily by insects.
