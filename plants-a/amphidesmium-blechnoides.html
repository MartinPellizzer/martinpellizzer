Amphidesmium blechnoides

Amphidesmium blechnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
