Astragalus adsurgens var. alpina

Astragalus adsurgens var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cold climates. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellow to pale purple flowers. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes and bloom during the summer months.

Astragalus adsurgens var. alpina is often found in mountainous areas of Europe, Asia, and North America.
