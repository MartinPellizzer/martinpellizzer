Aster harrowianus

Aster harrowianus, commonly known as the Harrow's aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
