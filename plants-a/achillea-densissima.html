Achillea densissima

Achillea densissima, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
