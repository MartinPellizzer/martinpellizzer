Acronia apopsis

Acronia apopsis, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a rare orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, showy pink and white flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

Acronia apopsis is a federally endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to protect its dwindling populations.
