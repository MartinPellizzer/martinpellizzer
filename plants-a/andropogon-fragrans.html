Andropogon fragrans

Andropogon fragrans, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, feathery leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its flowering stems are usually tall and slender, bearing small, clustered flowers.

Sweetgrass is often used in traditional Native American practices for its fragrance and symbolic significance.
