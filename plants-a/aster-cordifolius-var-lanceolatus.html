Aster cordifolius var. lanceolatus

Aster cordifolius var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the summer, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
