Alstroemeria hookeri subsp. recumbens

Alstroemeria hookeri subsp.

recumbens, commonly known as the creeping alstroemeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread along the ground.

The plant produces clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and resilience.
