Amaranthus cauliflorus

Amaranthus cauliflorus, commonly known as the tree amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stem and large, showy inflorescences that resemble tree-like structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus cauliflorus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
