Astragalus andrasovszkyanus

Astragalus andrasovszkyanus, commonly known as the Andrasovszky's milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb native to Central Asia, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native region.
