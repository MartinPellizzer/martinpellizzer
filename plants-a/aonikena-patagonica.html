Aonikena patagonica

Aonikena patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian lichen, is a species of lichen that thrives in the cold, arid environments of South America.

It is a composite organism consisting of a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. This lichen is often found on rocky surfaces and tree bark in high-altitude regions. Its slow growth and ability to survive extreme conditions make it a resilient indicator of environmental health.
