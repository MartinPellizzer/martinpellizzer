Amorphophallus leonensis

Amorphophallus leonensis, commonly known as the Leon's arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a large, fleshy underground tuber that serves as its primary storage organ. It typically blooms with a showy, purple spathe that attracts pollinators such as insects and birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
