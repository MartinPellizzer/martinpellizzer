Astragalus danicus subsp. dasyglottis

Astragalus danicus subsp.

dasyglottis, commonly known as the rough-horned milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its thick, leathery pods and prominent, rough-horned seed structures.
