Alternanthera paronychioides subsp. chacoensis

Alternanthera paronychioides subsp.

chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco Alternanthera, is a herbaceous plant native to South America. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colored foliage, often displaying shades of red, purple, and pink.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are alternate and often have a velvety texture, while the small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
