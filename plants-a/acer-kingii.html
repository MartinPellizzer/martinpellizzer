Acer kingii

Acer kingii, commonly known as the Korean maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
