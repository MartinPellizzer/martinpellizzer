Aster proximus

Aster proximus, commonly known as the common aster, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
