Avena nuda

Avena nuda, commonly known as slender oat, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its thin, erect stems and delicate, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly achenes, which are the primary means of seed dispersal. Avena nuda typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and soil stabilization.
