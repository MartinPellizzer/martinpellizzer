Adiantum juxtapositum

Adiantum juxtapositum, commonly known as the clustered maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arranged in a clustered pattern. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce new shoots. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive shiny, bronze-colored rachis.

Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
