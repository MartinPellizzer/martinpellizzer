Agapetes obtusata

Agapetes obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leaved snowbell, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. This evergreen shrub typically grows at high elevations, often in cloud forests, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are elliptical with a blunt tip, and it produces small, bell-shaped white flowers.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
