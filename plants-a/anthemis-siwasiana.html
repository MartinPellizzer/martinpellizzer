Anthemis siwasiana

Anthemis siwasiana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often surrounded by white or pale blue ray florets. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
