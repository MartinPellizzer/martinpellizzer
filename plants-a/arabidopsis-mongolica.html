Arabidopsis mongolica

Arabidopsis mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian thale cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. This species is characterized by its small, rosette-like growth habit and produces small white or purple flowers. It is often used in genetic research due to its short life cycle and well-characterized genome.

The plant is adapted to harsh environments, showing resilience to cold and arid conditions.
