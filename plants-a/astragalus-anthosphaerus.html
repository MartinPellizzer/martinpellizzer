Astragalus anthosphaerus

Astragalus anthosphaerus, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets arranged in pairs, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus anthosphaerus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.
