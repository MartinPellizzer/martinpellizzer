Arabis dentata

Arabis dentata, commonly known as the toothed rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small white or pale yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Arabis dentata is often found in alpine and coastal regions, where it adapts well to harsh climatic conditions.
