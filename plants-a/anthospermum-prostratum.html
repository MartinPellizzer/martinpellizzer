Anthospermum prostratum

Anthospermum prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate anthospermum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, often prostrate stems that creep along the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Anthospermum prostratum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks.
