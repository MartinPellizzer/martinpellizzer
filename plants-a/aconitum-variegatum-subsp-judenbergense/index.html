Aconitum Variegatum Subsp. Judenbergense

Aconitum variegatum subsp. judenbergense is a striking member of the buttercup family known for its vibrant blue and purple flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments. Its unique foliage, marked by variegated leaf patterns, adds to its ornamental appeal. Despite its beauty, it is highly toxic, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals. This subspecies is a valued addition to botanical gardens and specialized collections due to its rarity and ecological significance.
