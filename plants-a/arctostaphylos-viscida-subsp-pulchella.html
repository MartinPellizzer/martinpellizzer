Arctostaphylos viscida subsp. pulchella

Arctostaphylos viscida subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to California and parts of Nevada. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters.

The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by edible berries. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
