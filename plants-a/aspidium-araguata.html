Aspidium araguata

Aspidium araguata, commonly known as the Brazilian water fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often used in horticultural displays. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in high humidity. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Aspidium araguata plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to soil stability.
