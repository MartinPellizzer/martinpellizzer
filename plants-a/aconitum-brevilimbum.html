Aconitum brevilimbum

Aconitum brevilimbum, commonly known as the short-limbed monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves with a distinctive velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
