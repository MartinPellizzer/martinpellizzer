Androrchis laeta

Androrchis laeta, commonly known as the Happy Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact rosette of dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Androrchis laeta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
