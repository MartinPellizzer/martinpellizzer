Abelia gracilenta

Abelia gracilenta, commonly known as the slender abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems and small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Abelia gracilenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
