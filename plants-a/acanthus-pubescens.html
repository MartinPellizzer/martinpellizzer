Acanthus pubescens

Acanthus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of spires of small, pale blue to purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its historical use in classical architecture and horticulture highlights its enduring botanical and aesthetic significance.
