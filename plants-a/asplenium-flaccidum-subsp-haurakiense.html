Asplenium flaccidum subsp. haurakiense

Asplenium flaccidum subsp.

haurakiense, commonly known as the Hauraki hound's-tongue, is a fern native to New Zealand. It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, dark green, leathery fronds.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to humid and temperate climates.
