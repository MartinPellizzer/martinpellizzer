Aster brachytrichus var. brachytrichus

Aster brachytrichus var. brachytrichus, commonly known as the short-hair aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
