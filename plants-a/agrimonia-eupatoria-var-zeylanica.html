Agrimonia eupatoria var. zeylanica

Agrimonia eupatoria var. zeylanica, commonly known as Zeylanic Agrimony, is a perennial herb native to southern Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, pinnately lobed leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer, which are followed by distinctive, bead-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
