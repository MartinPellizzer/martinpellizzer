Astragalus nuttallianus var. quadrilateralis

Astragalus nuttallianus var. quadrilateralis, commonly known as the quadrilateral milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and compound leaves with four leaflets, giving it the specific epithet "quadrilateralis.". The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
