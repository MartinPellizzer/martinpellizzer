Alpinia comosa

Alpinia comosa, commonly known as the hairy ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

Alpinia comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
