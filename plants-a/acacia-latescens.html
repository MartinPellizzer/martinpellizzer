Acacia latescens

Acacia latescens, commonly known as the ghost acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its pale green, often hairless foliage and distinctive, flattened, spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Acacia latescens plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
