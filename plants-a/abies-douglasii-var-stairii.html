Abies douglasii var. stairii

Abies douglasii var. stairii, commonly known as the Stair's fir, is a variant of the Douglas fir native to the western United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The bark of this species is typically reddish-brown and becomes furrowed with age. It produces cones that hang downward, containing seeds dispersed by wind.

This variety is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist conditions.
