Avena cespitosa ochroleuca

Avena cespitosa ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces spikelets that are typically yellowish or ochraceous in color, giving it its specific epithet. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Avena cespitosa ochroleuca is often found in open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides.
