Arisaema salwinense

Arisaema salwinense, commonly known as the Salwin arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces striking, umbrella-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
