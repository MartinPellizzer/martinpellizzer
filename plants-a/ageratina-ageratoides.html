Ageratina ageratoides

Ageratina ageratoides, commonly known as the silverleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
