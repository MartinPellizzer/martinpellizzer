Angelica sachalinensis f. pubescens

Angelica sachalinensis f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy angelica, is a variant of the angelica plant native to northeastern China.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other forms of the species. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often in forested areas. It produces large, umbrella-shaped inflorescences with small white flowers.

The roots of this plant are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
