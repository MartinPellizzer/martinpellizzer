Arthrostylis filiformis

Arthrostylis filiformis, commonly known as the slender swamp reed, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb characterized by its long, narrow leaves and a single, slender flowering stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered near the base of the stem. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in lowland areas with high humidity.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
