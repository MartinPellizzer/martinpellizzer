Alchemilla vulgaris var. rhododendrophila

Alchemilla vulgaris var. rhododendrophila, commonly known as the rhododendron alchemilla, is a variant of the common lady's mantle.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive waxy texture.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
