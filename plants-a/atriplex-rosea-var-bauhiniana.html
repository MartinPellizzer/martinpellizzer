Atriplex rosea var. bauhiniana

Atriplex rosea var. bauhiniana, commonly known as the silverleaf dogwood, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its succulent leaves and stems store water, making it well adapted to dry environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
