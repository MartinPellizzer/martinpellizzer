Azalea verticillata

Azalea verticillata, commonly known as the spiral azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spirally arranged leaves and showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Azalea verticillata is relatively hardy and can tolerate partial shade to full sun depending on the climate.
