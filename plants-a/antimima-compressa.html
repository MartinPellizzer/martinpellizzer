Antimima compressa

Antimima compressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Antimima compressa is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
