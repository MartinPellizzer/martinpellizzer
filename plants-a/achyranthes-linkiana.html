Achyranthes linkiana

Achyranthes linkiana, commonly known as Indian knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in regions of Asia, including India and China. The plant features erect, branched stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, dry fruits containing single seeds.

Achyranthes linkiana is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
