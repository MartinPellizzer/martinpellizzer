Aridaria trichotoma

Aridaria trichotoma, commonly known as the hairy aridaria, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Aridaria trichotoma is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
