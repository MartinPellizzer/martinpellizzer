Aster laticorymbus

Aster laticorymbus, commonly known as the broad-flowered aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Aster laticorymbus is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
