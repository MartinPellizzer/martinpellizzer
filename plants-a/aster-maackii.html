Aster maackii

Aster maackii, commonly known as the Maack's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, lavender to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have serrated edges.

Aster maackii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
