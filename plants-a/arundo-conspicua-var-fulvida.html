Arundo conspicua var. fulvida

Arundo conspicua var. fulvida, commonly known as the golden reed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 6 feet in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a striking yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that mature into pale yellow to brown spikelets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique coloration and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
