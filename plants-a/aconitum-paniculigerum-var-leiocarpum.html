Aconitum paniculigerum var. leiocarpum

Aconitum paniculigerum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-carpelled monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a tall, panicle-like inflorescence. The plant features palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are typically hairy. Native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
