Atriplex obovata var. tuberata

Atriplex obovata var. tuberata, commonly known as the tuberous saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to salty and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization in harsh environments. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions.

This variety is also known for its edible tubers, which have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicine.
