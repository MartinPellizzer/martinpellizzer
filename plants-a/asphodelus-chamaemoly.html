Asphodelus chamaemoly

Asphodelus chamaemoly, commonly known as the creeping asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Asphodelus chamaemoly is known for its resilience to drought and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
