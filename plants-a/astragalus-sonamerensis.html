Astragalus sonamerensis

Astragalus sonamerensis, commonly known as the Sonoma milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to California, specifically found in the Sonoma County region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization through its extensive root system.
