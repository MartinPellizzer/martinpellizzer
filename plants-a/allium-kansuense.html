Allium kansuense

Allium kansuense, commonly known as the Kansu garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
