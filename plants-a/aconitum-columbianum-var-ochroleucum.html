Aconitum columbianum var. ochroleucum

Aconitum columbianum var. ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that are typically blue or purple, though this variety has white blooms. The plant features palmate leaves with deep lobes and grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
