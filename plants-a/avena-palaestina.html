Avena palaestina

Avena palaestina, commonly known as the Palestinian oat, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with minute flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its historical and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
