Andropogon saccharoides var. genuinus

Andropogon saccharoides var. genuinus, commonly known as the tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to six feet. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in late summer. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its grass-like appearance and hardy nature make it a common component of native plant communities.
