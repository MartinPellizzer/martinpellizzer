Acer saccharinum var. floridanum

Acer saccharinum var. floridanum, commonly known as the Florida sugar maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the maple family (Aceraceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmate leaves with three to five lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas across Florida and parts of Georgia.
