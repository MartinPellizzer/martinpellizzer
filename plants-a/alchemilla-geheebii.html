Alchemilla geheebii

Alchemilla geheebii, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. Alchemilla geheebii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

It is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
