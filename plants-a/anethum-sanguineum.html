Anethum sanguineum

Anethum sanguineum, commonly known as bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces a single, erect stem that bears the showy inflorescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its striking appearance, it is cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
