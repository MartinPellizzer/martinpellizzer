Alloplectus dimorphobracteatus

Alloplectus dimorphobracteatus, commonly known as the two-bract euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, paired bracts that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention.

Alloplectus dimorphobracteatus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
