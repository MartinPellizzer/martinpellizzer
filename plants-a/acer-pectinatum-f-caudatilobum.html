Acer pectinatum f. caudatilobum

Acer pectinatum f. caudatilobum, commonly known as the weeping maple, is a unique variant of the maple tree characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

This cultivar features delicate, elongated leaves that are often tinged with red or orange during the autumn season. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to any arboreal collection.
