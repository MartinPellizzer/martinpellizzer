Astragalus caprinus subsp. huetii

Astragalus caprinus subsp.

huetii, commonly known as the Huet's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions. Astragalus caprinus subsp.

huetii is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
