Astragalus racemosus var. longisetus

Astragalus racemosus var. longisetus, commonly known as the long-hair milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gravelly slopes.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
