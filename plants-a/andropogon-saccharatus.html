Andropogon saccharatus

Andropogon saccharatus, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and along stream banks. Its aromatic leaves have been used by Indigenous peoples for ceremonial and medicinal purposes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
