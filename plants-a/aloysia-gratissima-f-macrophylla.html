Aloysia gratissima f. macrophylla

Aloysia gratissima f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved verbena, is a cultivar of the verbena plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.

The foliage is aromatic, releasing a pleasant fragrance when crushed.
