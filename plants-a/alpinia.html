Alpinia

Alpinia, commonly known as the ginger family, is a genus of flowering plants that includes over 150 species.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. Alpinia species are typically herbaceous perennials with rhizomatous growth, and they often produce fragrant, ornamental flowers. Many species are cultivated for their medicinal properties, aromatic oils, and culinary uses.

The genus is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spike-like inflorescences.
