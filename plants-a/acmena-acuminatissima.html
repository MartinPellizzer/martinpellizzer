Acmena acuminatissima

Acmena acuminatissima, commonly known as the glossy privet, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and dull beneath, with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, edible berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The species is also valued for its use in hedges and as a landscape plant due to its hardiness and adaptability.
