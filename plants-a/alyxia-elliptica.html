Alyxia elliptica

Alyxia elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features opposite, elliptic-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and appear in clusters.

Alyxia elliptica is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail along structures.
