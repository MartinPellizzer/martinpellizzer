Arundinella taquetii

Arundinella taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's reed, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of rivers.

Arundinella taquetii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
