Amasonia hirta

Amasonia hirta, commonly known as the hairy amsonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical, hairy flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Amasonia hirta thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands and dry, sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, drought-tolerant plants.
