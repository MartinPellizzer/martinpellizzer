Aster lindleyanus var. borealis

Aster lindleyanus var. borealis, commonly known as the northern aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and tundra edges. The plant features showy, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to cold and windy conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
