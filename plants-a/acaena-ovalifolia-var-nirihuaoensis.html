Acaena ovalifolia var. nirihuaoensis

Acaena ovalifolia var. nirihuaoensis, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has oval-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types. It plays an important role in its native ecosystem, providing habitat and food for native insects and birds.

However, it is also considered a potential weed in some regions due to its aggressive growth habit.
