Andryala ducellieri

Andryala ducellieri, commonly known as the mountain arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant's inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky soils.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest in botanical research.
