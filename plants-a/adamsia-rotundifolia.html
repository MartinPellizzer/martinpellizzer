Adamsia rotundifolia

Adamsia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed Adamsia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are densely packed along its stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
