Asplenium moccennianum

Asplenium moccennianum, commonly known as the rock spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base, with a dark green color and a leathery texture. It is native to various regions in Africa, particularly in areas with humid climates and rocky substrates.

Asplenium moccennianum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
