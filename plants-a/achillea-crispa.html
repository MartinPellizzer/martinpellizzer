Achillea crispa

Achillea crispa, commonly known as the zigzag yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Achillea crispa is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
