Aerides reichenbachii var. cochinchinensis

Aerides reichenbachii var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Cochin China orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants rather than in soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
