Amaranthus incomptus

Amaranthus incomptus, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. Its seeds are small and often used as a grain in traditional cuisines.

Amaranthus incomptus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
