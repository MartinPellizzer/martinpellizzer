Aberemoa dioica

Aberemoa dioica, commonly known as the two-lobed broom, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

The species is notable for its dioecious nature, with separate male and female plants.
