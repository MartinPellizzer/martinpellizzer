Agave cocui

Agave cocui, commonly known as the Mexican fire plant, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Agave cocui is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
