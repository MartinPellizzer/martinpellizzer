Astragalus huiningensis var. psilocarpus

Astragalus huiningensis var. psilocarpus, commonly known as the Huining milkvetch variant, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of many Astragalus species. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and potential ecological significance.
