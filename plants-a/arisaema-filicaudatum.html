Arisaema filicaudatum

Arisaema filicaudatum, commonly known as the fern-tailed arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique floral structure.

It features a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is typically green with a reddish or purple hue. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Vietnam and Laos. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Arisaema filicaudatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
