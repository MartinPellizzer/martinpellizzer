Aleuritopteris grevilleoides

Aleuritopteris grevilleoides, commonly known as the Greville's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically divided into small, finger-like segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to moist soil or substrates.

Aleuritopteris grevilleoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
