Anacardium spruceanum

Anacardium spruceanum, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like cashews and poison ivy. The tree produces an edible seed, known as the Brazil nut, encased in a hard, woody shell. The plant is characterized by its large, compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers.

It plays a significant ecological role in the rainforest, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
