Aloe condyae

Aloe condyae, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, red-bracted inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Aloe condyae is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
