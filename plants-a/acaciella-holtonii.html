Acaciella holtonii

Acaciella holtonii, commonly known as the holton's acaciella, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
