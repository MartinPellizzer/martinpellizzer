Acer tataricum f. acutipterum

Acer tataricum f. acutipterum, commonly known as the sharply-lobed maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
