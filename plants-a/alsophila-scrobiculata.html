Alsophila scrobiculata

Alsophila scrobiculata, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of fertile fronds that produce spores, contributing to its reproductive cycle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Alsophila scrobiculata plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
