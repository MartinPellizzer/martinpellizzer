Aquilegia vulgaris var. speluncarum

Aquilegia vulgaris var. speluncarum, commonly known as the cave garden bellflower, is a rare variant of the common columbine.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals and vividly colored flowers that range from blue to purple. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in limestone-rich soils. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves that are typically a dark green.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in certain regions.
