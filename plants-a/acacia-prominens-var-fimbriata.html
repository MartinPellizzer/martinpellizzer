Acacia prominens var. fimbriata

Acacia prominens var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading branches and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are feathery and compound, consisting of numerous small, narrow leaflets. This plant is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in dunes and coastal heathlands.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for local wildlife.
