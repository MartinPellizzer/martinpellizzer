Agathisanthemum chlorophyllum var. pubescens

Agathisanthemum chlorophyllum var. pubescens, commonly known as the wooly yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
