Athyrium birii

Athyrium birii, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces fronds with a distinctive golden hue on the underside. Its spores are released from sporangia located on the undersides of the fronds.

Athyrium birii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
