Alstonia pachycarpa

Alstonia pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited alstonia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers followed by thick, fleshy berries that are orange-red in color.

Alstonia pachycarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
