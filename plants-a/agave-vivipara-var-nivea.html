Agave vivipara var. nivea

Agave vivipara var. nivea, commonly known as the snow agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces striking white or pale green flowers on a tall, branched inflorescence, which can reach several feet in height. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a landscape plant.
