Aster saboureaui

Aster saboureaui, commonly known as the Mexican aster, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually woody at the base.

Aster saboureaui is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant colors and drought tolerance.
