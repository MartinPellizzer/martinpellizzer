Actegeton sarmentosum

Actegeton sarmentosum, commonly known as the creeping actegeton, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features a sprawling growth habit with stolons that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

Actegeton sarmentosum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas.
