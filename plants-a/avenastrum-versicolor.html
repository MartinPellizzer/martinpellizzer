Avenastrum versicolor

Avenastrum versicolor, commonly known as the variable grassvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that display a range of colors, from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and often have a hairy texture.

Avenastrum versicolor is known for its adaptability to various soil types and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
