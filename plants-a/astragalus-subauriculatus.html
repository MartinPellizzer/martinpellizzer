Astragalus subauriculatus

Astragalus subauriculatus, commonly known as the auriculate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow or pale purple flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
