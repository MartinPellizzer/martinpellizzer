Aegiphila macrophylla

Aegiphila macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
