Astragalus rytidocarpus

Astragalus rytidocarpus, commonly known as the rigid carpal milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It has a deep root system, which helps it survive in dry conditions, and it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Due to its ecological importance and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated for conservation and traditional use.
