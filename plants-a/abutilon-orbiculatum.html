Abutilon orbiculatum

Abutilon orbiculatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or a shrub, depending on the climate. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its fibrous stems and hairy foliage contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological role.
