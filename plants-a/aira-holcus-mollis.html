Aira holcus-mollis

Aira holcus-mollis, commonly known as meadow grass or Holcus mollis, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that are arranged in panicles, with each spikelet containing several small, two-lobed florets. Its leaves are flat, narrow, and have a bluish-green color, often with a hairy texture.

Aira holcus-mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
