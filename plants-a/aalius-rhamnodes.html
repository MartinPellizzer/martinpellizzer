Aalius rhamnodes

Aalius rhamnodes, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features a succulent, upright stem covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are narrow, fleshy, and often have a distinctive waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Aalius rhamnodes is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
