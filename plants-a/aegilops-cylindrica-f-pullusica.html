Aegilops cylindrica f. pullusica

Aegilops cylindrica f. pullusica, commonly known as the woolly wild oat, is a wild relative of cultivated wheat.

It is a grass species characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and cylindrical spikelets. This plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in disturbed soils. It plays a role in genetic research due to its potential contributions to wheat improvement.

The plant's morphology and genetic makeup make it an important subject in studies of plant evolution and agronomy.
