Amomum stipulatum

Amomum stipulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, three-lobed leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
