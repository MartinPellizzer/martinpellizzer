Alstonia angustifolia var. annamensis

Alstonia angustifolia var. annamensis, commonly known as the Annam tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
