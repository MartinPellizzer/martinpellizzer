Allocarya armeriifolia

Allocarya armeriifolia, commonly known as the woollyleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle floral display to its habitat. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open areas.

Allocarya armeriifolia is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
