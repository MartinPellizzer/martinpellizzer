Arundinella blephariphylla

Arundinella blephariphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
