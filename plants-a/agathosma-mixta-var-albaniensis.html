Agathosma mixta var. albaniensis

Agathosma mixta var. albaniensis, commonly known as the Cape chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small yellow flowers and hairy, grey-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

The varietal form albaniensis is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and slightly different morphological traits compared to the typical variety.
