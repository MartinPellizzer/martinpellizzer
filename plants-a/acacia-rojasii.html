Acacia rojasii

Acacia rojasii, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Acacia rojasii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with deep root systems that help it access groundwater.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
