Acrostichum crassifolium

Acrostichum crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf brake, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, broad, leathery leaves that are typically divided into numerous segments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies. Its fronds are evergreen and can grow up to several feet in length.

Acrostichum crassifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
