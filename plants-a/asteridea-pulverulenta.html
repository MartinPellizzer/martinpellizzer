Asteridea pulverulenta

Asteridea pulverulenta, commonly known as the dusty aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often appearing in clusters. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy, grayish foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The dusty aster is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
