Adenia rumicifolia var. miegei

Adenia rumicifolia var. miegei, commonly known as the Miege's Adenia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
