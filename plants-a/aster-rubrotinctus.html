Aster rubrotinctus

Aster rubrotinctus, commonly known as the red-stained aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers with red-tinted petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

Aster rubrotinctus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
