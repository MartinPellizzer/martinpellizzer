Asplenium salicifolium var. auriculatum

Asplenium salicifolium var. auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate spleenwort, is a fern species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, serrated leaves that resemble those of willow trees, hence its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
