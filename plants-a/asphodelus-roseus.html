Asphodelus roseus

Asphodelus roseus, commonly known as the pink asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in the spring, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Asphodelus roseus is also known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
