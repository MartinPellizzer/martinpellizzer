Astragalus lentiginosus var. micans

Astragalus lentiginosus var. micans, commonly known as the shining milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are compound, with finely divided leaflets that give the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.
