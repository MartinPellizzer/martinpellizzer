Adicea microphylla var. trianthemoides

Adicea microphylla var. trianthemoides, commonly known as the dwarf zonal geranium, is a compact, mat-forming perennial native to South Africa.

It features small, rounded, velvety leaves that are typically green with distinct marginal veins. The plant produces clusters of showy, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its widespread use in ornamental horticulture.
