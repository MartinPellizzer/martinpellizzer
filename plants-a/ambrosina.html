Ambrosina

Ambrosina, commonly known as the sacred fig or Ficus religiosa, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various religious traditions. Its roots, bark, and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ambrosina thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
