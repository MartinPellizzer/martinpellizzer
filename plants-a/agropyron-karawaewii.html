Agropyron karawaewii

Agropyron karawaewii, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces spikelet-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed glumes, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Agropyron karawaewii is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is used in restoration ecology projects.
