Aleuritopteris caesia var. efarinosa

Aleuritopteris caesia var. efarinosa, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a variant of the common shield fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright yellow to golden fronds that contrast with its dark green undersides. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, forming dense clumps that can spread through vegetative propagation. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a leathery texture and a smooth, waxy surface.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and as a decorative houseplant.
