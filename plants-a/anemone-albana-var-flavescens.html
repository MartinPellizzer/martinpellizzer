Anemone albana var. flavescens

Anemone albana var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow anemone, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
