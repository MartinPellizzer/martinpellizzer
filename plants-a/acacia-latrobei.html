Acacia latrobei

Acacia latrobei, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are typically arranged in spirals. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in winter, attracting a variety of pollinators. Acacia latrobei is well adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden air, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
