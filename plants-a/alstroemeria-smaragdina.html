Alstroemeria smaragdina

Alstroemeria smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald Alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Alstroemeria smaragdina is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and ability to tolerate partial shade.
