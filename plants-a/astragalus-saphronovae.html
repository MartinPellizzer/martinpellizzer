Astragalus saphronovae

Astragalus saphronovae, commonly known as the Saphronov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have ecological significance in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
