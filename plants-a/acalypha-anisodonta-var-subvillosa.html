Acalypha anisodonta var. subvillosa

Acalypha anisodonta var. subvillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive tactile quality. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in moist woodland or swampy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in suitable climates.
