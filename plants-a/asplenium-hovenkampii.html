Asplenium hovenkampii

Asplenium hovenkampii, commonly known as the Hovenkamp's shield fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, shield-shaped fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish. Its spores are dispersed through wind, and it reproduces primarily through sexual means.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
