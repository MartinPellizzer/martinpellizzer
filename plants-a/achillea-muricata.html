Achillea muricata

Achillea muricata, commonly known as the European yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and can grow up to 1 meter tall, with a basal rosette of silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flower heads, which are composed of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its name is derived from the Greek myth of Achilles, who used the plant to treat wounds.
