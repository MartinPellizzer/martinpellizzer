Aster arborescens

Aster arborescens, commonly known as the tree aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 3 meters tall, often forming a bushy, tree-like appearance. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, blue to purple flower heads in the fall. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
