Azorella trifurcata var. normalis

Azorella trifurcata var. normalis, commonly known as the Andean snowbell, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and its ability to thrive in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually covered in a thick layer of fine, white hairs, which help to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Azorella trifurcata var. normalis is well adapted to high-altitude ecosystems and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
