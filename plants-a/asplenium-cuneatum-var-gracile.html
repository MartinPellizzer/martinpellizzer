Asplenium cuneatum var. gracile

Asplenium cuneatum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is a small, delicate fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, intricate pattern of sori on the underside. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its elegant appearance.
