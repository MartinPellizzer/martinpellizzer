Argyrocalymma montanum

Argyrocalymma montanum, commonly known as the mountain silvercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its silver-green foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It produces tiny, pale yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
