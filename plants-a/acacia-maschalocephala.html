Acacia maschalocephala

Acacia maschalocephala, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, edible seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia maschalocephala plays an important role in local ecosystems and has traditional uses in some communities.
