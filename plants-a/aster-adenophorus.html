Aster adenophorus

Aster adenophorus, commonly known as the glandular aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine, glandular hairs that give it its scientific name.
