Acacia steedmanii

Acacia steedmanii, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is adapted to saline and wind-prone environments.

Acacia steedmanii plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
