Acacia brachybotrya var. glaucophylla

Acacia brachybotrya var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless foliage and small, pale green to blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.

This variety is distinguished by its unique leaf coloration and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
