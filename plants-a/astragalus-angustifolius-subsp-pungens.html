Astragalus angustifolius subsp. pungens

Astragalus angustifolius subsp.

pungens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant has compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets, and its root system is often extensive and fibrous. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
