Aconitum maruyamae

Aconitum maruyamae, commonly known as the Maruyama aconite, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are often hairy.

Aconitum maruyamae is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
