Agrimonia pilosa var. simplex

Agrimonia pilosa var. simplex, commonly known as slender agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in soft, grayish hairs. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Agrimonia pilosa var. simplex is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
