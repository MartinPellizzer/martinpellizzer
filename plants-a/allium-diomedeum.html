Allium diomedeum

Allium diomedeum, commonly known as the Diomedea onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces tall, cylindrical flower stalks with small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong, pungent odor, similar to other members of the Allium genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
