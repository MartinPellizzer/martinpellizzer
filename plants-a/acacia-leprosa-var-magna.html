Acacia leprosa var. magna

Acacia leprosa var. magna, commonly known as the large-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are long and narrow. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal floral display.
