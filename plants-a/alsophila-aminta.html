Alsophila aminta

Alsophila aminta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves. The fronds are typically dark green and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant plays a role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and shelter for various small organisms.
