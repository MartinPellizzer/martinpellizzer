Anthemis alpestris

Anthemis alpestris, commonly known as the Alpine everlasting, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its small, yellowish to white flowers bloom in summer, and the plant is known for its long-lasting, fluffy seed heads that persist into autumn.

Anthemis alpestris is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
