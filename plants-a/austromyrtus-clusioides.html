Austromyrtus clusioides

Austromyrtus clusioides, commonly known as the Moreton Bay fig, is a large evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by edible, round, red fruits. It is often found in rainforests and coastal areas, thriving in humid, subtropical climates.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
