Allium sphaerocephalon subsp. rollii

Allium sphaerocephalon subsp.

rollii, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its clustered, spherical bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and hardy plants.
