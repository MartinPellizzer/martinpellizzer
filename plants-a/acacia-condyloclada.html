Acacia condyloclada

Acacia condyloclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to poor soils and drought conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
