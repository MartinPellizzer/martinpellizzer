Aristida laevigata

Aristida laevigata, commonly known as silky grass or slender dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Aristida laevigata is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
