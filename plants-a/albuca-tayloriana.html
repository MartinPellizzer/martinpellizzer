Albuca tayloriana

Albuca tayloriana, commonly known as the Taylor's albuca, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or yellow. The plant is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. It blooms in late winter to early spring, adding early color to the garden.

Albuca tayloriana is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in well-drained soil.
