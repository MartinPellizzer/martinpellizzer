Adenophora pubescens

Adenophora pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a hairy texture. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its common name.

Adenophora pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
