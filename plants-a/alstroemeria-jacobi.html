Alstroemeria jacobi

Alstroemeria jacobi, commonly known as the Jacob's lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a range of growing conditions.

Alstroemeria jacobi is also valued for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its long-lasting blooms.
