Agave angustifolia var. nivea

Agave angustifolia var. nivea, commonly known as the white agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, bluish-green leaves that are lined with sharp spines. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear small, white flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
