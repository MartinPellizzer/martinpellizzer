Acer brachypterum

Acer brachypterum, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed trunk and delicate, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras with two distinct bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.
