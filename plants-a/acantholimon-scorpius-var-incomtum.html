Acantholimon scorpius var. incomtum

Acantholimon scorpius var. incomtum, commonly known as the coastal flax or sea flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with strong winds and salt spray. It plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
