Asparagus oligophyllus

Asparagus oligophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, pale green leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is valued in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
