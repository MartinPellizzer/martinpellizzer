Amorphophallus ankarana

Amorphophallus ankarana, commonly known as the Ankarana giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive underground tuber. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its tuber is used as a food source and has a starchy texture similar to taro.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
