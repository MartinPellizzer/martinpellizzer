Amomum roseum

Amomum roseum, commonly known as the pink ginger or rose ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, pinkish-red flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its rhizomes are harvested for their fragrant properties, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Amomum roseum thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
