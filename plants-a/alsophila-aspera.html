Alsophila aspera

Alsophila aspera, commonly known as the rough tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive rough, scaly texture on its trunk, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Alsophila aspera plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
