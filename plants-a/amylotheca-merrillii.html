Amylotheca merrillii

Amylotheca merrillii, commonly known as the Merrill's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features a bulbous stem and produces clusters of narrow, strap-like leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Amylotheca merrillii is relatively rare in the wild and is primarily grown in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
