Agropyron intermedium f. beckii

Agropyron intermedium f. beckii, commonly known as the Beck's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for restoration projects and erosion control.

Its seeds are also used in some traditional herbal remedies.
