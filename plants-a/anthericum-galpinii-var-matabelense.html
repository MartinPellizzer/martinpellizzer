Anthericum galpinii var. matabelense

Anthericum galpinii var. matabelense, commonly known as Matabelense Arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a large, showy spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are broad, arrow-shaped, and often have a reddish or purple hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral structure.
