Andropogon halepensis var. muticus

Andropogon halepensis var. muticus, commonly known as the Algerian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a clumping growth habit and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with mild, dry climates.
