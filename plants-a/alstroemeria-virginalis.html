Alstroemeria virginalis

Alstroemeria virginalis, commonly known as the white alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, and are often used in floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria virginalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
