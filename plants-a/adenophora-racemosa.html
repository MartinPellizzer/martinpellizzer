Adenophora racemosa

Adenophora racemosa, commonly known as blue flax, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Adenophora racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
