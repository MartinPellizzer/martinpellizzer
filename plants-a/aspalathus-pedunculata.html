Aspalathus pedunculata

Aspalathus pedunculata, commonly known as the rooibos plant, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green. The plant is renowned for producing a unique herbal tea made from its young leaves and stems. Its botanical features include small, leathery leaves and a woody, branching structure.

Aspalathus pedunculata thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.
