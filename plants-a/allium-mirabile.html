Allium mirabile

Allium mirabile, commonly known as the star of India, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used as ornamental elements in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Allium mirabile is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
