Amsonia grandiflora

Amsonia grandiflora, commonly known as bluemink, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to sky-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Amsonia grandiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
