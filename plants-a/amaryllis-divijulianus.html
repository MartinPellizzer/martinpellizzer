Amaryllis divijulianus

Amaryllis divijulianus, commonly known as the Zulu lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces thick, fleshy bulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are strap-like and often remain above the ground during the dormant season.

Amaryllis divijulianus is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
