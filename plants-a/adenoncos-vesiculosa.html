Adenoncos vesiculosa

Adenoncos vesiculosa, commonly known as the bubblegum plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that resemble inflated balloons, which store water and help it survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate visual appeal. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss.

Adenoncos vesiculosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
