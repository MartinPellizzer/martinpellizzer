Acalypha oligodonta

Acalypha oligodonta, commonly known as the silverleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically appearing in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Acalypha oligodonta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive leaves and ease of care.
