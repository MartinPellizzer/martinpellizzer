Astrocarpa sesamoides subsp. purpurascens

Astrocarpa sesamoides subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered astrocarpa, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers, which are typically arranged in compact, spherical clusters.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
