Aspalathus rubrofusca

Aspalathus rubrofusca, commonly known as the red-fusca bushveld clover, is a perennial legume native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
