Aconitum delphiniifolium subsp. productum

Aconitum delphiniifolium subsp.

productum, commonly known as the Alpineaconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense racemes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed foliage that resembles the shape of a dolphin's tail, giving rise to its species name.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
