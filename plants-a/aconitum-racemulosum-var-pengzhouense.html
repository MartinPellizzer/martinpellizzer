Aconitum racemulosum var. pengzhouense

Aconitum racemulosum var. pengzhouense, commonly known as Pengzhou aconite, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has large, palmately divided leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
