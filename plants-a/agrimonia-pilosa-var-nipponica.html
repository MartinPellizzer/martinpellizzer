Agrimonia pilosa var. nipponica

Agrimonia pilosa var. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese agrimony, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant features serrated leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is also notable.
