Aristolochia paucinervis

Aristolochia paucinervis, commonly known as the slender Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often dark purple or brown with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
