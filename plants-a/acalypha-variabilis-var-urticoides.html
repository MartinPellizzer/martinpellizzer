Acalypha variabilis var. urticoides

Acalypha variabilis var. urticoides, commonly known as the stinging nettle shrub, is a tropical plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its stinging hairs, it is important to handle the plant with care.
