Acer campestre var. glabratum

Acer campestre var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth field maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or hedgerows.

This variety is distinguished by its lack of hairy texture on the leaves and stems, giving it a sleek appearance.
