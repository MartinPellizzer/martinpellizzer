Alangium arboreum

Alangium arboreum, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood or tree of life, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Alangium arboreum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
