Acacia aphylla

Acacia aphylla, commonly known as the smooth wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which appear in late summer to early autumn. Acacia aphylla is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native animals and insects.
