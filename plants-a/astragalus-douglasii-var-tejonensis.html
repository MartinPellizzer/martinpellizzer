Astragalus douglasii var. tejonensis

Astragalus douglasii var. tejonensis, commonly known as the Tejon milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as chaparral and woodland areas. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to reddish-purple pea-like flowers. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
