Aldina paulberryi

Aldina paulberryi, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers during the spring and early summer.

Aldina paulberryi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
