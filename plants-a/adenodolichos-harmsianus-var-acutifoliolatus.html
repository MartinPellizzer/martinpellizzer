Adenodolichos harmsianus var. acutifoliolatus

Adenodolichos harmsianus var. acutifoliolatus, commonly known as the sharp-leafed climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves with acute leaflets. The flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in racemes, while the pods are elongated and contain several seeds. This species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it suitable for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
