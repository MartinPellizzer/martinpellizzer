Ageratina altissima var. altissima

Ageratina altissima var. altissima, commonly known as white coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking white to pale blue flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It blooms from late summer to early fall, producing small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
