Acacia mauroceana

Acacia mauroceana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia mauroceana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
