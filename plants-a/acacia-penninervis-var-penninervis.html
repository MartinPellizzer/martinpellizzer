Acacia penninervis var. penninervis

Acacia penninervis var. penninervis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
