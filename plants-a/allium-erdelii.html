Allium erdelii

Allium erdelii, commonly known as the Hungarian garlic or Erdel's garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium erdelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Central European herbal traditions.
