Artemisia campestris var. erythroclada

Artemisia campestris var. erythroclada, commonly known as redstem wormwood, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used historically in herbal remedies.

The variety erythroclada is distinguished by its reddish stems, which set it apart from other Artemisia species.
