Abelia spathulata var. ionostachya

Abelia spathulata var. ionostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
