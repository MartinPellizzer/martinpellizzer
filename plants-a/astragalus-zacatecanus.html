Astragalus zacatecanus

Astragalus zacatecanus, commonly known as the Zacatecan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Astragalus zacatecanus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
