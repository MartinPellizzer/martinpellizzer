Asphodelus albus var. virgatus

Asphodelus albus var. virgatus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features grass-like, linear leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences with white, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae, which includes many drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
