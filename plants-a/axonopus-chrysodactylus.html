Axonopus chrysodactylus

Axonopus chrysodactylus, commonly known as the golden finger plant, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often described as having a finger-like appearance. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Axonopus chrysodactylus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
