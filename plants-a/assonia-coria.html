Assonia coria

Assonia coria, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vivid and eye-catching appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged in a feather-like pattern. Assonia coria is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its showy blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

It prefers full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
