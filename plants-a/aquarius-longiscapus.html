Aquarius longiscapus

Aquarius longiscapus, commonly known as the long-stemmed aquarius, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, slender stems that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments with moderate to high light exposure and nutrient-rich substrates. The plant's leaves are narrow and elongated, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, enhancing its aesthetic appeal in aquascaping. It produces small, delicate white flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species, often found in secluded wetlands and stream margins.
