Ascarina philippinensis

Ascarina philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine palm, is a species of palm native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fruits that are typically clustered in bunches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and montane forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and conservation purposes.
