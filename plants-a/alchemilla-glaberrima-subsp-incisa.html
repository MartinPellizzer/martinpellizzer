Alchemilla glaberrima subsp. incisa

Alchemilla glaberrima subsp.

incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe. It features basal rosettes of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
