Acalypha zambesica var. brevistyla

Acalypha zambesica var. brevistyla, commonly known as the Zambesi velvetleaf, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
