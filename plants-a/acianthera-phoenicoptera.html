Acianthera phoenicoptera

Acianthera phoenicoptera, commonly known as the crimson orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant pink to red flowers that resemble the color of a sunset. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a complex structure with specialized pollination mechanisms.
