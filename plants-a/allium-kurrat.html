Allium kurrat

Allium kurrat, commonly known as the Kashmir garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence. Its bulbs are edible and have a strong, pungent flavor similar to garlic.

Allium kurrat is valued both for its culinary use and ornamental appeal in gardens.
