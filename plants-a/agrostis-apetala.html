Agrostis apetala

Agrostis apetala, commonly known as the naked leaved bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that lack the typical inflorescence found in many other grasses. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with a lack of petals, which is reflected in its scientific name "apetala.".

Agrostis apetala plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
