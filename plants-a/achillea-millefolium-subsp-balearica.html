Achillea millefolium subsp. balearica

Achillea millefolium subsp.

balearica, commonly known as the Balearic yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and traditional medicinal uses.
