Astragalus pseudopendulinus

Astragalus pseudopendulinus, commonly known as the spiked milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus pseudopendulinus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
