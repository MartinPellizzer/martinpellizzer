Alopecurus typhoides

Alopecurus typhoides, commonly known as meadow foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, spike-like inflorescences that resemble a foxtail. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to both wet and dry conditions.

Alopecurus typhoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and role in stabilizing soil.
