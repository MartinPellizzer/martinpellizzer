Andropogon kapandensis

Andropogon kapandensis, commonly known as the Kapandensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many other grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family.

Andropogon kapandensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
