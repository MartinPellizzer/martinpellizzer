Aniba parvissima

Aniba parvissima, commonly known as the small-leaved aniba, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by dark, spherical berries.

Aniba parvissima is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and is valued for its aromatic properties.
