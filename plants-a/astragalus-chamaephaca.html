Astragalus chamaephaca

Astragalus chamaephaca, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow or pale green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus chamaephaca is adapted to withstand drought and has deep root systems to access water from deeper soil layers.
