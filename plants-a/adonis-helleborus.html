Adonis helleborus

Adonis helleborus, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a poisonous sap that contains cardiac glycosides, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes.
