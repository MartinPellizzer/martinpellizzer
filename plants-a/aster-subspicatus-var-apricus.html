Aster subspicatus var. apricus

Aster subspicatus var. apricus, commonly known as the spring aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
