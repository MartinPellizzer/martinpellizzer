Ampelopsis glandulosa

Ampelopsis glandulosa, commonly known as the common trailing grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a waxy coating and glandular hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
