Achyronia truncata

Achyronia truncata, commonly known as the dwarf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Achyronia truncata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
