Angeja

Angeja, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact, bushy perennial, often cultivated for its ornamental value. Angeja thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its popularity stems from its ability to bloom continuously throughout the year under proper care.
