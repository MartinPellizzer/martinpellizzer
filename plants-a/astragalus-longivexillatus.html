Astragalus longivexillatus

Astragalus longivexillatus, commonly known as the long-sepaled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus longivexillatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
