Achneria ampla

Achneria ampla, commonly known as the giant marsh orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, often in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has long, strap-like leaves that help it thrive in moist environments. Its flowering period usually occurs during the rainy season, making it a notable species in its ecosystem.

Achneria ampla is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
