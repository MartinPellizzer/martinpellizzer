Archidendron molle

Archidendron molle, commonly known as the silky senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
