Abies mariesii var. kawakamii

Abies mariesii var. kawakamii, commonly known as the Kawakamii fir, is a rare variant of the Mariesii fir native to Japan.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The foliage is typically arranged in spirals along the branches, and the cones are small and cylindrical. This variety is adapted to cool, humid climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
