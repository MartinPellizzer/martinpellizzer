Arenaria nuttallii subsp. fragilis

Arenaria nuttallii subsp.

fragilis, commonly known as the fragrant sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its delicate, fragrant blossoms and its ability to tolerate harsh, coastal or desert habitats.
