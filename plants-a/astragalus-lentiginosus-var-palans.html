Astragalus lentiginosus var. palans

Astragalus lentiginosus var. palans, commonly known as the Palans milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, bean-like pods containing several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
