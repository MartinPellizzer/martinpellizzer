Andropogon trachypus

Andropogon trachypus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn bronze in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowering spikes appear in late summer, producing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Andropogon trachypus plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
