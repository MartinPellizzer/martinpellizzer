Acer shirasawanum var. tenuifolium

Acer shirasawanum var. tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically pale green on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
