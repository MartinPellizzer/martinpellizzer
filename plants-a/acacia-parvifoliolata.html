Acacia parvifoliolata

Acacia parvifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow or golden flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals or through mechanical means.

Acacia parvifoliolata is well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry climates.
