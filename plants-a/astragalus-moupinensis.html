Astragalus moupinensis

Astragalus moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Astragalus moupinensis is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential medicinal properties.
