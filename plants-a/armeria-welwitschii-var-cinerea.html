Armeria welwitschii var. cinerea

Armeria welwitschii var. cinerea, commonly known as the silver tufted thrift, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The foliage is characterized by its silvery-gray appearance due to a dense covering of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
