Astragalus macrocarpus var. leiocarpus

Astragalus macrocarpus var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smoothpod milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces smooth, elongated pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
