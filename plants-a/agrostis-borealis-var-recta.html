Agrostis borealis var. recta

Agrostis borealis var. recta, commonly known as the northern bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths that help stabilize soil in harsh environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tundra and mountain ecosystems.
