Acacia brachyclada

Acacia brachyclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Acacia brachyclada is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.
