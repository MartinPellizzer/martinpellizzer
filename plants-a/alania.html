Alania

Alania, commonly known as the common alania, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to various regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and forest edges. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Alania is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit is perennial, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun to partial shade.
