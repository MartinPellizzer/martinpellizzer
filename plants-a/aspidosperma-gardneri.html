Aspidosperma gardneri

Aspidosperma gardneri, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Copaifera gardneri*, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and hillside areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
