Aster altaicus var. uchiyamae

Aster altaicus var. uchiyamae, commonly known as the Uchiyama's aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem and numerous linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in the genus Aster, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
