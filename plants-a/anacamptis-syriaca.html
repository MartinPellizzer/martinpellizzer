Anacamptis syriaca

Anacamptis syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from poor soils.

Anacamptis syriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
