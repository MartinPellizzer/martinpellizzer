Azadirachta integrifolia

Azadirachta integrifolia, commonly known as the Indian banyan, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and can grow to impressive heights, often with a sprawling canopy. The tree produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

Azadirachta integrifolia is also valued for its strong, durable wood and its role in traditional medicine.
