Aegilops kotschyi var. typica

Aegilops kotschyi var. typica, commonly known as the Kotschyi goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing bristly, awned florets. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential agricultural research.
