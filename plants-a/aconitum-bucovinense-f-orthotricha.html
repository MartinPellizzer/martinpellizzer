Aconitum bucovinense f. orthotricha

Aconitum bucovinense f. orthotricha, commonly known as the Bucovinaaconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
