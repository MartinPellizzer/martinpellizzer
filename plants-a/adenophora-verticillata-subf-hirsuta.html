Adenophora verticillata subf. hirsuta

Adenophora verticillata subf.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy false bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It features hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Campanulaceae family.
