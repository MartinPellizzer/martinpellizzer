Amelanchier canadensis var. alnifolia

Amelanchier canadensis var. alnifolia, commonly known as the northern serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features oval to elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a red or purple color in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a smooth, often gray-barked trunk and a rounded or spreading growth habit.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
