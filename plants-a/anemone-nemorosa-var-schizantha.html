Anemone nemorosa var. schizantha

Anemone nemorosa var. schizantha, commonly known as the divided anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and divided, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pale pink flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in woodland or shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
