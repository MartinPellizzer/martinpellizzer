Artemisia santonicum subsp. patens

Artemisia santonicum subsp.

patens, commonly known as the fragrant wormwood, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Middle East. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its essential oils, which contain compounds like santol and thujone, contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

It is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
