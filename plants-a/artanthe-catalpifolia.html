Artanthe catalpifolia

Artanthe catalpifolia, commonly known as the Catalpaleaf artanthe, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and resemble those of the catalpa tree, hence its common name.

Artanthe catalpifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
