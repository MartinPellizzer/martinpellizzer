Alopecurus geniculatus var. patagonicus

Alopecurus geniculatus var. patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
