Aster atacamensis

Aster atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama aster, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is an important species for ecological studies in desert environments.
