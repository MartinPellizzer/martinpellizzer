Amaranthus crassipes var. crassipes

Amaranthus crassipes var. crassipes, commonly known as pigweed, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions around the world. The plant has upright stems covered with fine hairs and produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is also notable for its high seed production, which contributes to its invasive potential in some areas.
