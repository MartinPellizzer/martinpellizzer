Anemagrostis spica-venti

Anemagrostis spica-venti, commonly known as windgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with feathery bristles, which aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Anemagrostis spica-venti is often considered a weed but plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
