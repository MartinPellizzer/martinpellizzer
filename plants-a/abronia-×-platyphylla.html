Abronia × platyphylla

Abronia × platyphylla, commonly known as the coastal sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Abronia umbellata and Abronia tetragona. This plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal dunes and rocky coastlines. It produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, often in clusters at the ends of its stems.

The species is adapted to coastal environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and salt resistance.
