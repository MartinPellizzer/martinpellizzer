Anchusa officinalis var. moesiaca

Anchusa officinalis var. moesiaca, commonly known as the Moesian hollyhock, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in dense, sparsely branched clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
