Artemisia hemisphaerica

Artemisia hemisphaerica, commonly known as the spherical wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and gray-green foliage, which is covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spherical clusters, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found at high altitudes.

Artemisia hemisphaerica is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
