Actaea spicata var. melanocarpa

Actaea spicata var. melanocarpa, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features a tall, unbranched stem that bears clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces dark red to black berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets, and are arranged alternately along the stem.

This species is often found in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.
