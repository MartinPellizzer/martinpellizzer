Astragalus tephrodes var. tephrodes

Astragalus tephrodes var. tephrodes, commonly known as the graystem milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its small, bean-like seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind.

It is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
