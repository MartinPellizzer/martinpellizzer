Aechmea farinosa var. conglomerata

Aechmea farinosa var. conglomerata, commonly known as the Rosette Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like arrangement of fleshy, dark green leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often coated with a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil and regular misting to maintain humidity.
