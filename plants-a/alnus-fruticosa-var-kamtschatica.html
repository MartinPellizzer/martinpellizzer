Alnus fruticosa var. kamtschatica

Alnus fruticosa var. kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamtschatskan alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its alternate, simple, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges, riverbanks, and tundra regions.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important pioneer plant in ecological succession.
