Amaranthus artineanus

Amaranthus artineanus, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the southern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in warm, sunny climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, branched stems and vibrant red or purple foliage, which becomes more intense as the plant matures. Its small, green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to withstand drought conditions.

Amaranthus artineanus is often used as a ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
