Alnus crispa var. sinuata

Alnus crispa var. sinuata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf alder, is a variant of the alder tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in catkins, and it produces small, nut-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for various wildlife species.
