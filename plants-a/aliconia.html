Aliconia

Aliconia, commonly known as the "scented fern," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant produces small, fragrant spore cases that release a sweet, spicy scent, attracting insects for pollination. Aliconia typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and unique aromatic properties.
