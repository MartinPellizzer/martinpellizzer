Alsophila hirsuta

Alsophila hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are often used in traditional basket weaving and other crafts.
