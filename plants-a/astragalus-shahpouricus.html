Astragalus shahpouricus

Astragalus shahpouricus, commonly known as Shahpour's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitats.
