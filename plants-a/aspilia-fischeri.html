Aspilia fischeri

Aspilia fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's torch flower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange inflorescence, which resembles a torch and is composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant properties.

Aspilia fischeri is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
