Anacyclus radiatus subsp. coronatus

Anacyclus radiatus subsp.

coronatus, commonly known as the crown dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
