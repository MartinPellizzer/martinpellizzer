Amaranthus batalleri

Amaranthus batalleri, commonly known as the batalleri amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Bolivia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and production of small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
