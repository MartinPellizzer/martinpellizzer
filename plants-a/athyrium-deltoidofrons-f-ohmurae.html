Athyrium deltoidofrons f. ohmurae

Athyrium deltoidofrons f. ohmurae, commonly known as the Ohmura's shield fern, is a rare fern species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Athyriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, shield-like appearance on the underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
