Aegilops columnaris

Aegilops columnaris, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Aegilops columnaris is valued for its role in ecological restoration and as a forage crop for livestock.

Morphology

Aegilops columnaris has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically linear, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, and vary in length from 10 to 30 centimeters with a pale green color.

The stem is cylindrical, smooth, and slightly hairy, with a reddish-brown color, growing upright and branching sparsely. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets that bloom in early summer, relying on wind for pollination. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent capsule that releases tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapted for anchoring the plant in sandy or rocky soils.
