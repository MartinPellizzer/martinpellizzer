Alloplectus oinochrophyllus

Alloplectus oinochrophyllus, commonly known as the golden-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a golden hue on the underside. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

The berries it bears are usually red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds and other animals.
