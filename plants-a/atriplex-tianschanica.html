Atriplex tianschanica

Atriplex tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan saltbush, is a shrub native to the Tianshan mountain range in Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid and saline environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its salt tolerance makes it a valuable species for stabilizing soils in harsh, dry regions.

Atriplex tianschanica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing habitat and forage for local wildlife.
