Abies alba f. pyramidalis

Abies alba f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the European white fir pyramidal form, is a variant of the European silver fir.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dense foliage, making it a popular ornamental tree. The leaves are needle-like, dark green, and arranged in a compact, pyramidal structure. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

This form is typically found in mountainous regions of Europe where it grows in coniferous forests.
