Acioa campestris

Acioa campestris, commonly known as the field acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and compound leaves composed of several leaflets. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
