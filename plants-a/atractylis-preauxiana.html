Atractylis preauxiana

Atractylis preauxiana, commonly known as the Algerian costmary, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomatous stems that help it survive in harsh conditions.

Atractylis preauxiana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
