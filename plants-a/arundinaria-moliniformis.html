Arundinaria moliniformis

Arundinaria moliniformis, commonly known as the Moliniform bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture.

Arundinaria moliniformis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
