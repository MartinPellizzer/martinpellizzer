Asperula asterocephala

Asperula asterocephala, commonly known as the star-headed asperula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces small, clustered flowers with white petals and a distinctive star-shaped appearance. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Asperula asterocephala is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in arid and semi-arid environments.
