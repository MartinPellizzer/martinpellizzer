Asplenium surrogatum

Asplenium surrogatum, commonly known as the false holly fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery, and often dark green fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium surrogatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
