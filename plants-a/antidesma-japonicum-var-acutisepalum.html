Antidesma japonicum var. acutisepalum

Antidesma japonicum var. acutisepalum, commonly known as the Japanese antidesma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
