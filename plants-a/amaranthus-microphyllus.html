Amaranthus microphyllus

Amaranthus microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico, where it typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish flowers. It produces small, round seeds that are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes.

Amaranthus microphyllus is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
