Acmadenia latifolia

Acmadenia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved acmadenia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
