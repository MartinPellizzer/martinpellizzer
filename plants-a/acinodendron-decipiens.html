Acinodendron decipiens

Acinodendron decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive acacia, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
