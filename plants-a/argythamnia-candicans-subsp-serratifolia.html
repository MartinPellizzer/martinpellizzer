Argythamnia candicans subsp. serratifolia

Argythamnia candicans subsp.

serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf argythamnia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of southern Europe.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
