Atylosia reticulata f. erecta

Atylosia reticulata f. erecta, commonly known as the upright form of the nettleleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with dark green, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and resilient choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.
