Arundo canila

Arundo canila, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with greenish or yellowish flowers. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found along riverbanks and in wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth and extensive root system, it plays a significant role in erosion control and habitat provision for various wildlife species.
