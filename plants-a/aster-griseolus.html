Aster griseolus

Aster griseolus, commonly known as the grayleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands, and it thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are grayish-green and hairy, while its flowers are typically blue to purple in color.

Aster griseolus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in native plant gardens.
