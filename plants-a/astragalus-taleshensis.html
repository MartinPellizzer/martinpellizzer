Astragalus taleshensis

Astragalus taleshensis, commonly known as the Talesh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus taleshensis plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.
