Aciotis circaeifolia var. major

Aciotis circaeifolia var. major, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, vibrant yellow to orange flowers that resemble the sun, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
