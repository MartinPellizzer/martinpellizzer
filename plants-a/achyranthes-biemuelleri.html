Achyranthes biemuelleri

Achyranthes biemuelleri, commonly known as the Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
