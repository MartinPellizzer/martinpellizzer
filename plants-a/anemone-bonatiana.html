Anemone bonatiana

Anemone bonatiana, commonly known as the showy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Anemone bonatiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
