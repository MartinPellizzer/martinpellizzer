Alectryon grandis

Alectryon grandis, commonly known as the large-fruited alectryon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in coastal and dry woodland habitats, often forming dense thickets.

Alectryon grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
