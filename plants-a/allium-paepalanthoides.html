Allium paepalanthoides

Allium paepalanthoides, commonly known as the desert onion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by spherical, blue to purple berries. Allium paepalanthoides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
