Aster cognatus

Aster cognatus, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring vibrant purple to pink flower heads that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and branching.

Aster cognatus is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
