Arcuatopterus filipedicellus

Arcuatopterus filipedicellus, commonly known as the slender-leafed cycad, is a unique cycad species characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that form a dense, upright rosette.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its tropical habitat.

Due to its slow growth and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
