Astragalus ranjbarii

Astragalus ranjbarii, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus ranjbarii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
