Aira tenorei subsp. mixta

Aira tenorei subsp.

mixta, commonly known as the hybrid aira, is a grass species native to the island of Sardinia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear spikelets with bristles.

The plant typically flowers in the summer months, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
