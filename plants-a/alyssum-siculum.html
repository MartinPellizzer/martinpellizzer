Alyssum siculum

Alyssum siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters. Alyssum siculum is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens due to its compact growth habit and long blooming period.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
