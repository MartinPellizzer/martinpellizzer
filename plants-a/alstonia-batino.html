Alstonia batino

Alstonia batino, commonly known as the Philippine alstonia, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
