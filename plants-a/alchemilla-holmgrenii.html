Alchemilla holmgrenii

Alchemilla holmgrenii, commonly known as Holmgren's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
