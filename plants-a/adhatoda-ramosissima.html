Adhatoda ramosissima

Adhatoda ramosissima, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its stems are woody and branched, contributing to its ramosissima, or branched, appearance.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported respiratory and anti-inflammatory properties.
