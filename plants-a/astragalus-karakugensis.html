Astragalus karakugensis

Astragalus karakugensis, commonly known as the Karakum milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and sandy soils.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
