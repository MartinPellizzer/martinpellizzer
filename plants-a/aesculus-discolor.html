Aesculus discolor

Aesculus discolor, commonly known as the American Buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are typically bright green and turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters, followed by spiny, spherical fruits containing glossy, brown seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often with shallow fissures.

Aesculus discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Native American cultures.
