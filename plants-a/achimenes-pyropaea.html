Achimenes pyropaea

Achimenes pyropaea, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy, and slightly hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It produces showy bracts that often resemble the flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Achimenes pyropaea thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a garden plant for its vibrant blooms.
