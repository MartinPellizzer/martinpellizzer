Astragalus preussii var. preussii

Astragalus preussii var. preussii, commonly known as Preuss's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus preussii var. preussii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh conditions.
