Acacia repanda

Acacia repanda, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its seed pods are long and cylindrical, containing hard, woody seeds.

Acacia repanda is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
