Ammannia auriculata var. auriculata

Ammannia auriculata var. auriculata, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to wetland restoration projects and native plant gardens.
