Allium aobanum

Allium aobanum, commonly known as the Japanese leek, is a hardy perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features long, slender leaves that resemble those of leeks, and it produces clusters of small, white, bulbous flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves and bulbs, which have a mild garlic-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Allium aobanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
