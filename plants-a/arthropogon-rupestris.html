Arthropogon rupestris

Arthropogon rupestris, commonly known as the rock grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers.

Arthropogon rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in difficult terrain.
