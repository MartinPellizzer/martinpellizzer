Anthurium bomboizense

Anthurium bomboizense, commonly known as the Bomboizense arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spatulate leaves and showy, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is broad and heart-shaped, often with a vibrant green color and a waxy texture.

Anthurium bomboizense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique foliage and subtle floral structures.
