Anemia palmarum

Anemia palmarum, commonly known as the "hand of the poor," is a rare and striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that resemble human hands, hence its common name. The plant has a short, woody stem and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. Its leaves are typically light green and have a velvety texture.

Anemia palmarum thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.
