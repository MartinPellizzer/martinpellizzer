Atriplex amblyostegia

Atriplex amblyostegia, commonly known as silverleaf shadscale, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are densely packed, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators.

Atriplex amblyostegia is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
