Aglaonema longicuspidatum

Aglaonema longicuspidatum, commonly known as the Dragon's Blood Plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with dark green veins and bright white or cream-colored patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
