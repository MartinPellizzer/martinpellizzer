Agrostis stolonifera var. subchlorantha

Agrostis stolonifera var. subchlorantha, commonly known as the coastal bentgrass, is a variant of the coastal bentgrass species native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically found in dense clusters. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments.

This variety is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
