Aspilia strigosa

Aspilia strigosa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aspilia strigosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
