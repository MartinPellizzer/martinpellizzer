Alpinia cernua

Alpinia cernua, commonly known as the hanging ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, arching stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, usually in shades of white and pink.

Alpinia cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
