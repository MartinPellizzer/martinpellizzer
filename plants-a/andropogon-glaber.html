Andropogon glaber

Andropogon glaber, commonly known as sand bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, bluish-green foliage and tufted inflorescence that matures to a golden hue. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Sand bluestem is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
