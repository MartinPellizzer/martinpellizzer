Attalea oleifera

Attalea oleifera, commonly known as the oil palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into fruit. The fruit contains a high amount of oil, making it economically significant for the production of palm oil. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
