Aconitum schleicheri

Aconitum schleicheri, commonly known as Schleicher's aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, typically arranged in a raceme along the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum schleicheri is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it a dangerous plant to handle.
