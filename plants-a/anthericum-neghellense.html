Anthericum neghellense

Anthericum neghellense, commonly known as the Neghell's lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, colorful flowers that attract pollinators such as insects and birds. Its leaves are typically broad and arrow-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
