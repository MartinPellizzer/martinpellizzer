Astragalus madruschkendicus

Astragalus madruschkendicus, commonly known as the Karakoram milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Karakoram mountain range in Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, enabling it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus madruschkendicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
