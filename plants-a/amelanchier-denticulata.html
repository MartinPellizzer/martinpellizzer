Amelanchier denticulata

Amelanchier denticulata, commonly known as the prickly pear or American serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in jams and pies. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it flowers with white or pale pink blossoms in early spring. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in woodland edges or open fields.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable plant for wildlife and landscape use.
