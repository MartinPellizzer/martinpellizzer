Abarema hendersonii

Abarema hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's acacia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
