Annona cauliflora

Annona cauliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring smooth, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit, which is typically pear-shaped, has a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, creamy pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
