Asteromyrtus symphyocarpa

Asteromyrtus symphyocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of northern Australia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional Indigenous practices for its medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
