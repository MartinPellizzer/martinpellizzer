Alchemilla carinthiaca

Alchemilla carinthiaca, commonly known as Carpathian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose, branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Alchemilla carinthiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
