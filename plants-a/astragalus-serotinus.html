Astragalus serotinus

Astragalus serotinus, commonly known as the late-flowering milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus serotinus is part of the legume family and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
