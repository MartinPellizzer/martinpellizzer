Acacia aroma var. huarango

Acacia aroma var. huarango, commonly known as the Huarango tree, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted trunk and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils and withstand drought conditions.

The Huarango tree plays a vital ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for various desert species.
