Angolluma

Angolluma, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are broad and heart-shaped. Angolluma is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
