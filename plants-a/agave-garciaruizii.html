Agave garciaruizii

Agave garciaruizii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its structure and appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
