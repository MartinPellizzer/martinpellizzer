Anthemis kurdica

Anthemis kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are often surrounded by white or pale blue ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

The leaves are typically hairy and aromatic, containing compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.
