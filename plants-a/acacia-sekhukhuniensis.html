Acacia sekhukhuniensis

Acacia sekhukhuniensis, commonly known as the Khomani acacia, is a shrub native to the Kalahari Desert in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Acacia sekhukhuniensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert species.
