Acacia haematoxylon

Acacia haematoxylon, commonly known as Indian sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its straight, dark brown to blackish bark, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a dye. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers and flat, green pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in perfumes, incense, and fine furniture.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important species in dryland ecosystems.
