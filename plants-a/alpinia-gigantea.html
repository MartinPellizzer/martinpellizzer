Alpinia gigantea

Alpinia gigantea, commonly known as the giant ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, aromatic leaves and striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of several meters, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

Alpinia gigantea thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
