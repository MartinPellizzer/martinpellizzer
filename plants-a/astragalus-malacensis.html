Astragalus malacensis

Astragalus malacensis, commonly known as the Malacan horseclover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus malacensis is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
