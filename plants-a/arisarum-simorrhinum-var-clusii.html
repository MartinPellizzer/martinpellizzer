Arisarum simorrhinum var. clusii

Arisarum simorrhinum var. clusii, commonly known as the Clusian arisarum, is a rare and delicate plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest to plant enthusiasts and researchers.
