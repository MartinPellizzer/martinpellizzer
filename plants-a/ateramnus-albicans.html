Ateramnus albicans

Ateramnus albicans, commonly known as the white sandplant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Ateramnus albicans is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.
