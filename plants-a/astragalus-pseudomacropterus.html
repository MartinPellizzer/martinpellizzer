Astragalus pseudomacropterus

Astragalus pseudomacropterus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to cream-colored flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus pseudomacropterus is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.
