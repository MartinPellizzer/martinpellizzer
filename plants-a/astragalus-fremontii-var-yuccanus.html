Astragalus fremontii var. yuccanus

Astragalus fremontii var. yuccanus, commonly known as the Yucca milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yucca-like leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
