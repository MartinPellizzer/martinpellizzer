Abies subalpina compacta

Abies subalpina compacta, commonly known as the compact fir, is a slow-growing conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and short, stiff needles that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, cylindrical cones that hang downward, and its bark is reddish-brown with a flaky texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in subalpine and alpine zones.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
