Adhatoda longiflora

Adhatoda longiflora, commonly known as the Indian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Adhatoda longiflora is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
