Alternanthera strigosa

Alternanthera strigosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red leaf plant, is a tropical perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to purple and green, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant leaf color.
