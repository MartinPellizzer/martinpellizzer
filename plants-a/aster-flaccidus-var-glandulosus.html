Aster flaccidus var. glandulosus

Aster flaccidus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces showy, blue to purple ray flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist to wet soils in full sun to partial shade.

Its unique botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
