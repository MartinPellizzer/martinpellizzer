Artemisia commutata f. elongata

Artemisia commutata f. elongata, commonly known as the longleaf artemisia, is a variant of the common artemisia species.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
