Astragalus canadensis var. brevidens

Astragalus canadensis var. brevidens, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in prairies, open woods, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
