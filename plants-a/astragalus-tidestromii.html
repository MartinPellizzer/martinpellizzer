Astragalus tidestromii

Astragalus tidestromii, commonly known as Tidestrom's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in desert or semi-desert environments. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus tidestromii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
