Arachis marginata var. lignosa

Arachis marginata var. lignosa, commonly known as the hairy groundnut, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Due to its adaptability, it is often cultivated in marginal lands for both its nutritional value and ecological benefits.
