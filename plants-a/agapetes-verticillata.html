Agapetes verticillata

Agapetes verticillata, commonly known as the clustered silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests, thriving in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and it produces berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Agapetes verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
