Agapetes setigera var. acuminata

Agapetes setigera var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a pointed tip, giving it its common name. It produces bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in inflorescences.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
