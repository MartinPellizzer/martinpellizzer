Astragalus callistachys

Astragalus callistachys, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Astragalus callistachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
