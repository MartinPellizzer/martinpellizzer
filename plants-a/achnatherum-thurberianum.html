Achnatherum thurberianum

Achnatherum thurberianum, commonly known as Thurber's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinctive, wind-pollinated flowering structure.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
