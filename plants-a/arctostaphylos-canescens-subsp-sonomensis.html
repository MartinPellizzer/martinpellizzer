Arctostaphylos canescens subsp. sonomensis

Arctostaphylos canescens subsp.

sonomensis, commonly known as the Sonoma manzanita, is a shrub native to California. It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in the spring. These flowers are followed by small, red berries that mature in the fall.

It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.
