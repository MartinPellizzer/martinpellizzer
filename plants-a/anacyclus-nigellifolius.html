Anacyclus nigellifolius

Anacyclus nigellifolius, commonly known as black cumin or nigella, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with a branched stem and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into spherical, three-lobed seed pods containing numerous small, black seeds. It is valued for its aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its distinctive foliage and medicinal properties.
