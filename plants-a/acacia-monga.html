Acacia monga

Acacia monga, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green to golden flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia monga is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system.

It is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.
