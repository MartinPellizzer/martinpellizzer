Aspilia squarrosa

Aspilia squarrosa, commonly known as the African torch or red torch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features upright stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and often twisted. Its most striking feature is its large, red or orange inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, yellow flowers clustered in a spherical shape.

Aspilia squarrosa is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
