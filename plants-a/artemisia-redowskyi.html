Artemisia redowskyi

Artemisia redowskyi, commonly known as Redowsky's wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, branched hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Artemisia redowskyi is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low rainfall, and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It contains essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
