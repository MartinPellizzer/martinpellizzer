Arundinella brasiliensis

Arundinella brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is adapted to thrive in moist, tropical environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Arundinella brasiliensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
