Acacia pseudoglaucophylla

Acacia pseudoglaucophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, linear leaflets, and it produces yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

Acacia pseudoglaucophylla is often used in ecological restoration due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
