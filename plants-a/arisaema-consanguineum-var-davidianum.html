Arisaema consanguineum var. davidianum

Arisaema consanguineum var. davidianum, commonly known as the David's arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a typical feature of arum plants. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and shade gardens.
