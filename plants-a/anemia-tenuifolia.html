Anemia tenuifolia

Anemia tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf anemia, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are usually less than 2 cm in width. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its low water requirements and hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ornamental plant in dry gardens.
