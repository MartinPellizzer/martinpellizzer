Aspilia rugulosa

Aspilia rugulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of thick, waxy, and hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Aspilia rugulosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
