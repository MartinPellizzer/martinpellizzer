Arisaema pseudoangustatum

Arisaema pseudoangustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a prominent midrib. Its underground tubers are edible when properly prepared, though they contain calcium oxalate crystals that can cause irritation.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
