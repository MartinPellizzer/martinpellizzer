Andropogon trichocladus

Andropogon trichocladus, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often blue-green in color.

Andropogon trichocladus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
