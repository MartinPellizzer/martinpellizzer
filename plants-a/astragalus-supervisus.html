Astragalus supervisus

Astragalus supervisus, commonly known as the supervisory milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces small, yellow to brown pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is native to regions of North America. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep taproot system.

Astragalus supervisus is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to arid environments.
