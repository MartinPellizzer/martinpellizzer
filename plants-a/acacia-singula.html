Acacia singula

Acacia singula, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in spherical heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia singula thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
