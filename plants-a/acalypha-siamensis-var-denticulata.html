Acalypha siamensis var. denticulata

Acalypha siamensis var. denticulata, commonly known as the Siam hibiscus or toothed leaf acalypha, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, palmately lobed leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the warm season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
