Acaciella glauca

Acaciella glauca, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grey-green foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that help it conserve water in its dry environment. It produces small, round pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acaciella glauca is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
