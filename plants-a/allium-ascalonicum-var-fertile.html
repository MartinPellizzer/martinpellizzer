Allium ascalonicum var. fertile

Allium ascalonicum var. fertile, commonly known as the Egyptian leek, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces long, cylindrical leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant features a cluster of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for both culinary use and ornamental purposes in gardens.
