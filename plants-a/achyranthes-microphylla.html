Achyranthes microphylla

Achyranthes microphylla, commonly known as small-leaved knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that often branch towards the upper part of the plant. The leaves are small, ovate, and have a rough texture with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

The plant produces small, round, dry fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
