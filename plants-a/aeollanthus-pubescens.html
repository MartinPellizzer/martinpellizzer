Aeollanthus pubescens

Aeollanthus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy aeollanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
