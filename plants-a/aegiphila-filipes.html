Aegiphila filipes

Aegiphila filipes, commonly known as the slender-leaved aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical environments.

Aegiphila filipes is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
