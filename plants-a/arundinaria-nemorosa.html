Arundinaria nemorosa

Arundinaria nemorosa, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
