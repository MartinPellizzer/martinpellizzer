Aconitum szechenyianum

Aconitum szechenyianum, commonly known as Szechenyian monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in alpine or woodland habitats.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
