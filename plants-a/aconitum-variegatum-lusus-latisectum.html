Aconitum variegatum lusus latisectum

Aconitum variegatum lusus latisectum, commonly known as the variegated latisectum monkshood, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features tall, upright stems that bear large, showy flowers with distinctive spurred petals, typically in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are deeply lobed and often exhibit a variegated pattern of green and white, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

It is important to note that all parts of this plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
