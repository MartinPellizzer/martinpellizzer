Astragalus glaucops

Astragalus glaucops, commonly known as the blue-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are palmately divided and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its common name.

Astragalus glaucops is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
