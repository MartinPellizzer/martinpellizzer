Anthyllis vulneraria proles hispida

Anthyllis vulneraria proles hispida, commonly known as the hispid legume or hairy anthyllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow or greenish-yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in dry, calcareous soils in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
