Achillea lucana

Achillea lucana, commonly known as the southern yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, silver-gray hairs. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads composed of small white to pale yellow florets. Its leaves are basal, pinnately divided, and have a feathery appearance.

Achillea lucana is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
