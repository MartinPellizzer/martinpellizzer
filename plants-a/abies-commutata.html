Abies commutata

Abies commutata, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its dense, soft needles that are typically light green with a silvery-white underside. The tree produces small, spherical cones that hang from the branches, containing winged seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray when young, becoming fissured with age.

Abies commutata thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
