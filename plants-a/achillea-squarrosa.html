Achillea squarrosa

Achillea squarrosa, commonly known as the square-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces flat, clustered flower heads composed of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are linear, serrated, and covered in a layer of fine, woolly hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
