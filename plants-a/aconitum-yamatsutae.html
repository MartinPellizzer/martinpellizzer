Aconitum yamatsutae

Aconitum yamatsutae, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that resemble a hood, giving it its common name. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it both a botanical curiosity and a potential hazard. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streambanks.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
