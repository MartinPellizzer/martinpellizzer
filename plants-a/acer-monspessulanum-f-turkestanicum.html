Acer monspessulanum f. turkestanicum

Acer monspessulanum f. turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the maple species.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring deep lobes and a striking red-bronze coloration during the growing season. This cultivar is native to Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, particularly for its autumn foliage.

However, due to its limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
