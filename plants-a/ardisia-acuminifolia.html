Ardisia acuminifolia

Ardisia acuminifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are a striking feature. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ardisia acuminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and fruit.
