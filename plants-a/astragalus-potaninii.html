Astragalus potaninii

Astragalus potaninii, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus potaninii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and its potential medicinal properties.
