Anacheilium kautskyi

Anacheilium kautskyi, commonly known as the Kautsky's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated flower spikes that bear small, pale green or white flowers, which are often hidden among the dense foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
