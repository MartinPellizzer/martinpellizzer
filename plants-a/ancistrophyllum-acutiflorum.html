Ancistrophyllum acutiflorum

Ancistrophyllum acutiflorum, commonly known as the sharpflower acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous tiny leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access underground water.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
