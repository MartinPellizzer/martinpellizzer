Aristida simpliciflora var. texana

Aristida simpliciflora var. texana, commonly known as Texas dropseed, is a native grass species of the United States, particularly found in Texas and surrounding regions.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and produces small, delicate panicles of flowers during the summer months. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid environments.

Additionally, it plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
