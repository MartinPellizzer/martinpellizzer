Aphelandra macleayi

Aphelandra macleayi, commonly known as the Macleay's candleplant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that resembles a candle. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often green with a metallic sheen. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Aphelandra macleayi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
