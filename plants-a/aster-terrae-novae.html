Aster terrae-novae

Aster terrae-novae, commonly known as the Newfoundland aster, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Newfoundland and Labrador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal environments with salty air. Its blooming period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing vibrant purple or white flowers.

The Newfoundland aster is an important component of local ecosystems and is often found in maritime habitats.
