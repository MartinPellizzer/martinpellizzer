Amygdalus communis f. lanceolata

Amygdalus communis f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate almond.

This is a cultivar of the almond tree, characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The fruit is a hard-shelled nut containing a single edible seed.

This variety is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
