Arachniodes grossa

Arachniodes grossa, commonly known as the broad-leafed arachniodes, is a member of the Davalliaceae family.

It is a climbing fern characterized by its large, leathery, and often deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are often adorned with intricate patterns and can reach lengths of up to several meters.

Arachniodes grossa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
