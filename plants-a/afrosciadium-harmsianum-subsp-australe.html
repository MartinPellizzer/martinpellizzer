Afrosciadium harmsianum subsp. australe

Afrosciadium harmsianum subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Southern African scabious, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, often in areas with well-drained soils.

The plant features erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, covered with fine, hairy hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, rounded heads, usually in shades of white or pale purple.

The subspecies is characterized by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Afrosciadium.
