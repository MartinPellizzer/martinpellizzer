Alyssum orientale

Alyssum orientale, commonly known as Persian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant features dense clusters of blooms on slender stems, often accompanied by silvery-gray foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum orientale is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
