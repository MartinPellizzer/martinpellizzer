Azalea pontica var. sinensis

Azalea pontica var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
