Attalea parviflora

Attalea parviflora, commonly known as the pink palm or pataua, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered inflorescence with small, pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and animals. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Attalea parviflora plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
