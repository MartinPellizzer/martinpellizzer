Adnaria angustifolia

Adnaria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are blue or purple in color.

Adnaria angustifolia is often found in maritime environments and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds.
