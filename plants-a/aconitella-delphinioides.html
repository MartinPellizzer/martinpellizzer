Aconitella delphinioides

Aconitella delphinioides, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth habit, allowing it to thrive in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitella delphinioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
