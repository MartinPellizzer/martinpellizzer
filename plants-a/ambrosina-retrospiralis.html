Ambrosina retrospiralis

Ambrosina retrospiralis, commonly known as the spiral ambrosia plant, is a unique species characterized by its intricate spiral-shaped leaves.

Native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in high humidity and indirect sunlight. The plant's leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its humid environment. Its flowers, which appear in clusters, are small and pale green, often overlooked despite their subtle beauty.

Ambrosina retrospiralis is also noted for its slow growth and is often cultivated as a rare ornamental plant.
