Actinotus leucocephalus var. humilis

Actinotus leucocephalus var. humilis, commonly known as the white-flowered bell heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.
