Anthurium pallidispadix

Anthurium pallidispadix, commonly known as the pale-spadix anthurium, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence.

It features a spadix, which is a dense cluster of small flowers, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green.

The pale-spadix anthurium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
