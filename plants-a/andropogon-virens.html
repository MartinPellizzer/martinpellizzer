Andropogon virens

Andropogon virens, commonly known as green panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, wetlands, and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is both an important forage plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
