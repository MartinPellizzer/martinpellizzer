Allium alamutense

Allium alamutense, commonly known as the Alamut garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of onions and garlic. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are typically small and have a strong, pungent aroma similar to other Allium species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
