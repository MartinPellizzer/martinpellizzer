Anomianthus heterocarpus

Anomianthus heterocarpus, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive appearance.
