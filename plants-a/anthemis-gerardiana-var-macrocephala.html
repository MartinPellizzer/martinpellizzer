Anthemis gerardiana var. macrocephala

Anthemis gerardiana var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural landscapes.

It is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
