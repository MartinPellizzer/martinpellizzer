Amyris ventricosa

Amyris ventricosa, commonly known as the swamp bay or red bay, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wet, swampy areas. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Amyris ventricosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
