Actinodaphne angustifolia

Actinodaphne angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and aromatic bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
