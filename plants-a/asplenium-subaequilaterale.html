Asplenium subaequilaterale

Asplenium subaequilaterale, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green, with a slightly serrated margin and a narrow, lance-shaped appearance.

Asplenium subaequilaterale is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
