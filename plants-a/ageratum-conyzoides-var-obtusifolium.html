Ageratum conyzoides var. obtusifolium

Ageratum conyzoides var. obtusifolium, commonly known as white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, often grayish-green foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields. It has a bushy growth habit and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family, sharing similar structural and reproductive features.
