Aspilia potosina

Aspilia potosina, commonly known as the Mexican torch flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, orange-red bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers at the center of the inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Aspilia potosina is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant and unique appearance.
