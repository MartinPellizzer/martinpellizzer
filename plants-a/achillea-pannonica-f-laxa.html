Achillea pannonica f. laxa

Achillea pannonica f. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf yarrow, is a variant of the yarrow species native to Central Europe.

This plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The leaves are linear and finely divided, contributing to its delicate appearance.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
