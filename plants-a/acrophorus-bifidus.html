Acrophorus bifidus

Acrophorus bifidus, commonly known as the bifid acanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, with colors ranging from red to purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Acrophorus bifidus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
