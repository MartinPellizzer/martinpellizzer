Aloe divaricata subsp. divaricata

Aloe divaricata subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the zigzag aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, fleshy leaves that curve inward, creating a zigzag appearance.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in terminal panicles, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
