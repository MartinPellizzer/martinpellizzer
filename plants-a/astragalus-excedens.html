Astragalus excedens

Astragalus excedens, commonly known as the excedent milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus excedens plays a role in stabilizing soil and can be found in various ecological communities across its native range.
