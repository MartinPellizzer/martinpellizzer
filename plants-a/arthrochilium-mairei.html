Arthrochilium mairei

Arthrochilium mairei, commonly known as the Maire's rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny or partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that is often darker and more prominent.

Arthrochilium mairei is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
