Anthericum diversifolium

Anthericum diversifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features a striking inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a large, colorful spathe. The plant has broad, wavy-edged leaves that vary in shape, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy or pond-edge habitats.

The species is known for its unique reproductive structures and is a valued plant in wetland restoration projects.
