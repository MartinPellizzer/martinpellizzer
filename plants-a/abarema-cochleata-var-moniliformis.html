Abarema cochleata var. moniliformis

Abarema cochleata var. moniliformis, commonly known as the beadleaf or beaded abarema, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bead-like stipules on the stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets that contribute to its ornamental appeal.
