Angelica stenoloba f. lanceolata

Angelica stenoloba f. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf angelica, is a botanical variant of the angelica species characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to regions of East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant produces tall, branched stems with small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties in herbal practices.
