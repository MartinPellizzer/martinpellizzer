Acer campestre f. albovariegatum

Acer campestre f. albovariegatum, commonly known as the white-variegated field maple, is a cultivar of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive white and green variegated leaves, which provide a striking visual contrast. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and it typically grows in temperate climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
