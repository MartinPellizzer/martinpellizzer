Albuca namaquensis

Albuca namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua lily, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and striking yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. It blooms in late winter or early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Albuca namaquensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
