Alsine atheniensis proles urbica

Alsine atheniensis proles urbica, commonly known as the urban alsine, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, green, egg-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pale green flowers. This plant is typically found in disturbed urban environments such as sidewalks, cracks in pavement, and along building foundations. It is well adapted to grow in poor soil conditions and can thrive in full sun.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often considered a weed in urban settings.
