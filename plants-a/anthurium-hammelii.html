Anthurium hammelii

Anthurium hammelii, commonly known as the golden shield anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces vibrant red spathes that enclose its spadix, a unique floral structure typical of the Araceae family. Anthurium hammelii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
