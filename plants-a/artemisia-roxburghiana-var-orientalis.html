Artemisia roxburghiana var. orientalis

Artemisia roxburghiana var. orientalis, commonly known as oriental wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, linear leaves with a strongly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
