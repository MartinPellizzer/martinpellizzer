Anguria boissieriana

Anguria boissieriana, commonly known as the Boissier's cucumber, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cucurbit-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine pubescence.

Anguria boissieriana is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild, sweet flavor.
