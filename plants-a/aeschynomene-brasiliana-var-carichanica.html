Aeschynomene brasiliana var. carichanica

Aeschynomene brasiliana var. carichanica, commonly known as carica brasiliana, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
