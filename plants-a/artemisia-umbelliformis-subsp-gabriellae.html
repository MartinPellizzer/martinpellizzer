Artemisia umbelliformis subsp. gabriellae

Artemisia umbelliformis subsp.

gabriellae, commonly known as the Gabriella's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late summer.

The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
