Aconitum napellus lusus amoenum

Aconitum napellus lusus amoenum, commonly known as the Italian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps.

It features striking purple-black flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it both beautiful and dangerous. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Its ornamental value is high, but caution is necessary due to its potent toxins.
