Astragalus caspicus var. macracanthus

Astragalus caspicus var. macracanthus, commonly known as the Caspian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

This variant is distinguished by its longer, more robust bracts compared to other varieties of Astragalus caspicus.
