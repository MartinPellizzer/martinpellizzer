Allium rubriflorum

Allium rubriflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces small, bulbous structures that are edible, similar to other Allium species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is gaining popularity among gardeners and botanists alike.
