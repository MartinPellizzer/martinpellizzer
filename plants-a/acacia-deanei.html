Acacia deanei

Acacia deanei, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers, which are grouped in dense, globular clusters. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

Acacia deanei is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
