Alnus viridis var. brembana

Alnus viridis var. brembana, commonly known as the Bremban alder, is a variant of the European alder native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its small, serrated leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils, often found in mountainous and forested areas. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The Bremban alder plays a vital role in stabilizing slopes and supporting local biodiversity.
