Arundinaria ramosa var. distichophylla

Arundinaria ramosa var. distichophylla, commonly known as the two-ranked bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo genus Arundinaria.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-ranked arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique appearance compared to other bamboo species. The plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a clumping bamboo that grows to a moderate height, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Its foliage is often a deep green, and it can produce small, inconspicuous flowers under certain conditions.
