Agave lophantha var. latifolia

Agave lophantha var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its wide, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing numerous white to pale yellow flowers, which appear in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber, while its roots can be processed to make a type of agave syrup.

This variety is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
