Astragalus macropelmatus subsp. pseudobuchtormensis

Astragalus macropelmatus subsp.

pseudobuchtormensis, commonly known as the Tien Shan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological adaptations.
