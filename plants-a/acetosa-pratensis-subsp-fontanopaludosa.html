Acetosa pratensis subsp. fontanopaludosa

Acetosa pratensis subsp.

fontanopaludosa, commonly known as the bog clover, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in Europe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers.

The subspecies is adapted to nutrient-poor, waterlogged soils, often found in peat bogs and fens. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility in its environment.

This plant plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems.
