Apium weberbaueri

Apium weberbaueri, commonly known as Weberbauer's celery, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbrella-like inflorescences, typical of the family.

It is often found in coastal and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
