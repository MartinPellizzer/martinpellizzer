Aster perelegans

Aster perelegans, commonly known as the elegant aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster perelegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of care.
