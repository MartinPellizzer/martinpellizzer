Allium oleraceum subsp. girerdii

Allium oleraceum subsp.

girerdii, commonly known as the Mediterranean leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of long, narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its edible leaves, which have a mild garlic-like flavor.

This subspecies is often cultivated as a hardy ornamental and culinary plant.
