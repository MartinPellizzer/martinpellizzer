Axonopus casiquiarensis

Axonopus casiquiarensis, commonly known as the Casiquiare rivergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in the floodplains of the Casiquiare River.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in wetland and riparian environments, adapting to seasonal flooding and nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly and stabilize soil along riverbanks.

Axonopus casiquiarensis plays an important ecological role in maintaining habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms in its native range.
