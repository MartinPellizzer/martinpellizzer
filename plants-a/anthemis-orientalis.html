Anthemis orientalis

Anthemis orientalis, commonly known as the oriental tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves and is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky areas. It is known for its medicinal properties, historically used for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
