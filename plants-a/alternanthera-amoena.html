Alternanthera amoena

Alternanthera amoena, commonly known as red velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value comes primarily from its vibrant foliage rather than its flowers.
