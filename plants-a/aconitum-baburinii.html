Aconitum baburinii

Aconitum baburinii, commonly known as the Baburinii monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble those of other monkshood species, while its roots contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though caution is necessary due to its poisonous nature.
