Acacia falciformis

Acacia falciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia falciformis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
