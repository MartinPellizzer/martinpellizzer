Atractylis boulosii

Atractylis boulosii, commonly known as the Boulos' gingergrass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces upright, hairy stems that bear narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Atractylis boulosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
