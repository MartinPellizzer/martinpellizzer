Alpinia mollis

Alpinia mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by orange or red berries.

Alpinia mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
