Anastraphia obovata

Anastraphia obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Tamaricaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, often pink or reddish flowers that bloom in dense clusters. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Anastraphia obovata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry, saline soils.
