Anemone hudsoniana var. sanguinea

Anemone hudsoniana var. sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features a single, palmately divided leaf that emerges from a reddish-orange tuberous root. The plant produces a solitary, showy white flower with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, rich soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland habitats.

The name "bloodroot" refers to the reddish sap that exudes from the root when broken.
