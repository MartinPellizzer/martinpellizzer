Acacia centrophylla

Acacia centrophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has thin, wiry stems and narrow, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia centrophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
