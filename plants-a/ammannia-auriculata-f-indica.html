Ammannia auriculata f. indica

Ammannia auriculata f. indica, commonly known as the Indian ammannia, is a variant of the genus Ammannia, characterized by its distinctive red to purple floral bracts.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly India. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in dense, spikelike inflorescences, while the showy bracts attract pollinators.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.
