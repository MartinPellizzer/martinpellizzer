Aechmea lavandulacea

Aechmea lavandulacea, commonly known as the lavender bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure, which forms a central cup to collect rainwater. The leaves are typically bluish-green with a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces vibrant purple or pink floral bracts that resemble lavender, hence its common name.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea lavandulacea has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a bluish-green color and a waxy coating.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, thick base that supports the rosette of leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains seeds dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its rocky or epiphytic habitat.
