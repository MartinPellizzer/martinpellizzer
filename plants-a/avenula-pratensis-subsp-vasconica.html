Avenula pratensis subsp. vasconica

Avenula pratensis subsp.

vasconica, commonly known as the Spanish grass or narrow-leaved oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and wetlands.

The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, characteristic of the Poaceae family.

It plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
