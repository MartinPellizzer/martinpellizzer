Adenophora tetraphylla var. hirsuta

Adenophora tetraphylla var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy adenophora, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
