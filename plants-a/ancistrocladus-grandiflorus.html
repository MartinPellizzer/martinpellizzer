Ancistrocladus grandiflorus

Ancistrocladus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered ancistrocladus, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often glandular-stipulate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in axillary or terminal clusters. Its fruits are capsule-like, containing numerous small, winged seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
