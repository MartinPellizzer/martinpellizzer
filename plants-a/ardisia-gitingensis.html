Ardisia gitingensis

Ardisia gitingensis, commonly known as the Gitingensis beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
