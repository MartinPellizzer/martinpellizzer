Artocarpus odoratissimus

Artocarpus odoratissimus, commonly known as the sweet sop or custard apple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit. The fruit has a thick, rough skin and a sweet, creamy pulp with a mild aroma. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in paniculate inflorescences.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruit and is also valued for its ornamental features.
