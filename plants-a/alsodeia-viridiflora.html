Alsodeia viridiflora

Alsodeia viridiflora, commonly known as the greenflower alsodeia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens across southern Europe.
