Adenophora aurita

Adenophora aurita, commonly known as the bellflower of the mountains, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Adenophora aurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
