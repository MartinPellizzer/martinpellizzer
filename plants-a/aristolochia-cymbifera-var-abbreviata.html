Aristolochia cymbifera var. abbreviata

Aristolochia cymbifera var. abbreviata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local insect populations.
