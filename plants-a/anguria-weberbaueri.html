Anguria weberbaueri

Anguria weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's bitter melon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits with a bitter taste, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the vines are trailing, characteristic of many cucurbit species.

Anguria weberbaueri is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flavor.
