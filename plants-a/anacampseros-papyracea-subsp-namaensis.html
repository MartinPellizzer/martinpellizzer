Anacampseros papyracea subsp. namaensis

Anacampseros papyracea subsp.

namaensis, commonly known as the Namibian paperplant, is a succulent species native to the arid regions of Namibia. It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged periods of drought.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
