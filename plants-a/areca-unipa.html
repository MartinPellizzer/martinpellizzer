Areca unipa

Areca unipa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved areca, is a tropical palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by clustered, three-lobed fruits. Areca unipa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Arecaceae family.
