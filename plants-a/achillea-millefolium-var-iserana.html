Achillea millefolium var. iserana

Achillea millefolium var. iserana, commonly known as the Iseran yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Alps.

It features finely divided, feather-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family and shares similar medicinal properties.

Its botanical name honors the Greek hero Achilles, reflecting its historical use in treating wounds.
