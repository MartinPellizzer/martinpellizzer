Aster savianus

Aster savianus, commonly known as the Savian aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by petal-like bracts.

Aster savianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
