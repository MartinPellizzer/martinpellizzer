Arabis boissieri

Arabis boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and appear in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Arabis boissieri is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
