Acacia decipiens var. praemorsa

Acacia decipiens var. praemorsa, commonly known as the death's-head hawkmoth acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, sparsely branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in globular inflorescences. The plant produces flat, elliptical seed pods that split open to release small, winged seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland savanna regions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various insects.
