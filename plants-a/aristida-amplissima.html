Aristida amplissima

Aristida amplissima, commonly known as the broadleaf wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often green or reddish in color. It is a valuable component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its adaptability to waterlogged conditions makes it an important indicator plant for wetland health.
