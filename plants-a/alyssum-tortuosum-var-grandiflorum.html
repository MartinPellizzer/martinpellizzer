Alyssum tortuosum var. grandiflorum

Alyssum tortuosum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces numerous small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early summer.
