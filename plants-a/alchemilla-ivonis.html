Alchemilla ivonis

Alchemilla ivonis, commonly known as the velvetleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of softly hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.

Alchemilla ivonis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
