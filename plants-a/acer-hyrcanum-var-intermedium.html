Acer hyrcanum var. intermedium

Acer hyrcanum var. intermedium, commonly known as the middle Asian maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that are typically lobed and have a distinctive reddish or yellowish coloration in the fall. The plant produces small, red or orange flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
