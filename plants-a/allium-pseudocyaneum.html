Allium pseudocyaneum

Allium pseudocyaneum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of pale purple to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral display, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and exotic plants.
