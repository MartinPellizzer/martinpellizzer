Apatostelis pendulispica

Apatostelis pendulispica, commonly known as the hanging spike fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous fronds that droop from the plant, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in small, spore-producing structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
