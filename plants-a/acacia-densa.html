Acacia densa

Acacia densa, commonly known as the dense acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic organs. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Acacia densa is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
