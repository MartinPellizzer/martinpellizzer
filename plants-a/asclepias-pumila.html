Asclepias pumila

Asclepias pumila, commonly known as the dwarf milkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, which attract various pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and disturbed areas.

Asclepias pumila is also a host plant for the monarch butterfly, making it ecologically significant for supporting local wildlife.
