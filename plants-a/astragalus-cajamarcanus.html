Astragalus cajamarcanus

Astragalus cajamarcanus, commonly known as the Peruvian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. It plays a role in local ecosystems by fixing nitrogen and supporting biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
