Acacia pacensis

Acacia pacensis, commonly known as the Pacific acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are narrow and linear, often with a slight curvature. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically visited by native bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are modified into phyllodes, a common adaptation in acacia species to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Acacia pacensis is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
