Arum italicum subsp. neglectum

Arum italicum subsp.

neglectum, commonly known as the Italian arum or wild arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf that is often tinged with red or purple at the base.

The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typically green with a reddish hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
