Amaranthus lancifolius

Amaranthus lancifolius, commonly known as the needleleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is an annual herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with branched stems and small, bead-like fruits.

Amaranthus lancifolius is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
