Astragalus kosmaljanicus

Astragalus kosmaljanicus, commonly known as the Kosmaljanic milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine environments.

Astragalus kosmaljanicus is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential medicinal properties.
