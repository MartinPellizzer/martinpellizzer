Alternanthera brasiliana var. tomentosa

Alternanthera brasiliana var. tomentosa, commonly known as the silver vein plant, is a strikingly ornamental species native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a unique, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in aquatic settings.

Its foliage is prized for its aesthetic value and is used in both ornamental and decorative applications.
