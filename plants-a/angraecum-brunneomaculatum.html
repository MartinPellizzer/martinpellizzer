Angraecum brunneomaculatum

Angraecum brunneomaculatum, commonly known as the Brown-spotted Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with long, slender spathes. The flowers typically bloom in late winter or early spring, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. This plant prefers shady, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Its unique appearance and specific ecological requirements make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
