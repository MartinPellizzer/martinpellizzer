Adelanthus grandiflorus

Adelanthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. It grows in compact, low forms, often forming dense mats or small clumps.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and minimal water.
