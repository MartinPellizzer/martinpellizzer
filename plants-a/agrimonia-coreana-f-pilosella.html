Agrimonia coreana f. pilosella

Agrimonia coreana f. pilosella, commonly known as the hairy agrimony, is a variant of the agrimony plant native to Korea.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its overall botanical character.
