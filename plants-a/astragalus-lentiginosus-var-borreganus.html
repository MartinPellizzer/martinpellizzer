Astragalus lentiginosus var. borreganus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. borreganus, commonly known as the Borrego milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub or subshrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes, and its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets. It has adapted to survive in harsh conditions with deep roots and reduced water loss mechanisms.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
