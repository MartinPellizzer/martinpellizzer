Acalypha kraussiana

Acalypha kraussiana, commonly known as the red-veined strobilanthes, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with bright red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage.
