Aulacocalyx lamprophylla

Aulacocalyx lamprophylla, commonly known as the glossy-leaved acanthus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
