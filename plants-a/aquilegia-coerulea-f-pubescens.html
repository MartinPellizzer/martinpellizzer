Aquilegia coerulea f. pubescens

Aquilegia coerulea f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent blue poppy, is a variant of the blue poppy native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its hairy (pubescent) foliage and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five spurred petals that extend backward.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
