Ammothamnus songaricus

Ammothamnus songaricus, commonly known as the Songaric sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its structure allows it to conserve water efficiently, making it well-suited for harsh, dry environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in xerophytic landscaping.
