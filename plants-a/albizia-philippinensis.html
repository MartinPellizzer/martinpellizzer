Albizia philippinensis

Albizia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It is often found in lowland and mountainous areas, growing in various habitats such as forests and shrublands.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in Philippine botany.
