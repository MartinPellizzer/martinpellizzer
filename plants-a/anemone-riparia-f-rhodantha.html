Anemone riparia f. rhodantha

Anemone riparia f. rhodantha, commonly known as the red sandstone anemone, is a flowering plant native to the rocky habitats of North America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh environments.

Its distinctive coloration and form make it a notable ornamental and ecological plant in its native range.
