Asarum europaeum var. caucasicum

Asarum europaeum var. caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The rhizomes of this plant are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
