Acacia ancistroclada

Acacia ancistroclada, commonly known as the "Bark Acacia," is a small, deciduous tree native to the Horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures in dry conditions. The plant produces yellowish-green globular flowers that appear in clusters, followed by flat, ribbed pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has cultural significance in some regions.
