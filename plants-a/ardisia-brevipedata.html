Ardisia brevipedata

Ardisia brevipedata, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a subtle fragrance. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to clusters of bright blue berries, which are a distinctive feature. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
