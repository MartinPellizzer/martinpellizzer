Attalea brasiliensis

Attalea brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or buriti palm, is a tall, slender tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which can reach lengths of up to 5 meters, and its distinctive yellow-orange fruits that contain a nutrient-rich pulp. The plant is well adapted to wet, tropical conditions and thrives in swampy or flooded areas. Its fruit is an important food source for both wildlife and humans, and its leaves are used in traditional crafts.

The species plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
