Astragalus pellitus

Astragalus pellitus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Astragalus pellitus is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in arid conditions.
