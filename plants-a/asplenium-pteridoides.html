Asplenium pteridoides

Asplenium pteridoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium pteridoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
