Appendicula cyphochiloides

Appendicula cyphochiloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its limited habitat and habitat destruction, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
