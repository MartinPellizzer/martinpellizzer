Aimorra

Aimorra, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, waxy hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow, typically during the cooler months. Aimorra thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental and potentially valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
