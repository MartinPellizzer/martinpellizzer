Amaranthus caracu

Amaranthus caracu, commonly known as love-lies-bleeding, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and vibrant, blood-red, feathery bracts that resemble plumes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by triangular, three-lobed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Amaranthus caracu is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract birds and pollinators.
