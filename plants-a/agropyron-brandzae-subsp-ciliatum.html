Agropyron brandzae subsp. ciliatum

Agropyron brandzae subsp.

ciliatum, commonly known as the ciliated wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with finely serrated margins.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Its botanical features make it an important component of steppe and grassland ecosystems.
