Atylosia trinervia

Atylosia trinervia, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are pinnately divided with three main veins, giving it the species name "trinervia.".

The plant has fibrous roots that help it survive in poor soil conditions and conserve moisture.
