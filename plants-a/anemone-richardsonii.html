Anemone richardsonii

Anemone richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features showy, white to pinkish flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a cluster of sepals. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, wavy margin.

Anemone richardsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
