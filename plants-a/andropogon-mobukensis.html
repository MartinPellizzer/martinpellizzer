Andropogon mobukensis

Andropogon mobukensis, commonly known as the Mobukense grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Central and South America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a prominent midrib, and its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of spikelets. Andropogon mobukensis is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
