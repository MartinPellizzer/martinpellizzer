Alibertia verticillata

Alibertia verticillata, commonly known as blue cohosh, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It features a stout, upright stem with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its roots are often harvested for medicinal use, containing compounds with potential cardiovascular benefits.

Alibertia verticillata thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland areas.
