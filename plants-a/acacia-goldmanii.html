Acacia goldmanii

Acacia goldmanii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

Acacia goldmanii is also notable for its role in ecological systems, providing nectar for native pollinators.
