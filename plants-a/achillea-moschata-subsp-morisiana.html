Achillea moschata subsp. morisiana

Achillea moschata subsp.

morisiana, commonly known as the Morisian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which emits a subtle, pleasant scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
