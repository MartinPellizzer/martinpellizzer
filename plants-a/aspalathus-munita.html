Aspalathus munita

Aspalathus munita, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The fruit, which is a capsule, contains seeds that are dispersed by wind.
