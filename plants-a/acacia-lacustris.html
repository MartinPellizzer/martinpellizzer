Acacia lacustris

Acacia lacustris, commonly known as the lake acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

Acacia lacustris is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
