Arabis incana

Arabis incana, commonly known as the hoary rockcress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-blue leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or coastal environments. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants.

Arabis incana is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance.
