Aegiphila salutaris

Aegiphila salutaris, commonly known as the sacred plant of the Andes, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally by indigenous communities for various health purposes.
