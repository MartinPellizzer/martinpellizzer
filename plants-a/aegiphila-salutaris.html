Aegiphila salutaris

Aegiphila salutaris, commonly known as the sacred plant of the Andes, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally by indigenous communities for various health purposes.

Morphology

Aegiphila salutaris has small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a bright green color and a slightly hairy surface.

The stem is slender, somewhat woody, with a smooth, pale green bark that grows upright. Its flowers are clustered in umbels, featuring white petals with a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, dry capsule that disperses its seeds by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, aiding in nutrient absorption from the soil.
