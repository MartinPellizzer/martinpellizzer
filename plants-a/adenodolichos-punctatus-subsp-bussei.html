Adenodolichos punctatus subsp. bussei

Adenodolichos punctatus subsp.

bussei, commonly known as the spotted bean, is a leguminous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with distinctive purple spots and small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces elongated pods containing small, edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and agricultural use.
