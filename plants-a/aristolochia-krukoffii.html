Aristolochia krukoffii

Aristolochia krukoffii, commonly known as the Siberian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to the Far East.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble pipes, often in shades of purple and green. The plant produces small, dark berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
