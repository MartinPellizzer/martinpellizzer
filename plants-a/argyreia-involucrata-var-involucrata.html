Argyreia involucrata var. involucrata

Argyreia involucrata var. involucrata, commonly known as the "Crimson Rambutan," is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that enclose its small, white flowers. The plant produces fruit that is similar in appearance to the rambutan, with a hairy exterior and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
