Agathosma bodkinii

Agathosma bodkinii, commonly known as the Cape garlic or African sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It features aromatic, narrow, grey-green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique properties, it is also used in the production of certain essential oils and herbal remedies.
