Allium stearnianum

Allium stearnianum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of purple or pinkish flowers in the summer. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its edible bulbs, which have a mild onion flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
