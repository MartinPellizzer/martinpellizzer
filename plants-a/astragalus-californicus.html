Astragalus californicus

Astragalus californicus, commonly known as California milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.

Astragalus californicus is also valued for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
