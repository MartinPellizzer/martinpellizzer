Abies excelsa var. gregoryana

Abies excelsa var. gregoryana, commonly known as the Gregory's Noble Fir, is a variant of the Noble Fir native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that often has a bluish tinge. The needles are short, stiff, and arranged in spirals around the branches, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, oval cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and the tree is known for its cold tolerance and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
