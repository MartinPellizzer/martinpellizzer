Astragalus sangesuricus

Astragalus sangesuricus, commonly known as the Sangesuric milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus sangesuricus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
