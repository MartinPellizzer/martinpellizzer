Acacia neorigida

Acacia neorigida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often sparsely leafed stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and linear. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Acacia neorigida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in reforestation projects.
