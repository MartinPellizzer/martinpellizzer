Acacia drewiana subsp. pungens

Acacia drewiana subsp.

pungens, commonly known as the stinging acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets that are covered in fine, stinging hairs.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to thrive in arid environments.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
