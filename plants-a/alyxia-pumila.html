Alyxia pumila

Alyxia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf alyxia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter and features dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Alyxia pumila thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
