Astragalus stella var. acutifolius

Astragalus stella var. acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes. The leaves are pinnately compound, with sharp, lance-shaped leaflets that give it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
