Aristolochia carterae

Aristolochia carterae, commonly known as the Carter's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its distinctive, waxy, purple-brown flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, green, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
