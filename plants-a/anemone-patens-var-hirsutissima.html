Anemone patens var. hirsutissima

Anemone patens var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its hairy foliage and delicate flowers make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in its natural habitat.
