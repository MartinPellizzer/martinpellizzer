Alyssum macrocarpum var. candolleanum

Alyssum macrocarpum var. candolleanum, commonly known as the golden alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse displays of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Alyssum macrocarpum var. candolleanum is well-suited for rock gardens, borders, and as a ground cover due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
