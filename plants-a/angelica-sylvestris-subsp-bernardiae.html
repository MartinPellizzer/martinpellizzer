Angelica sylvestris subsp. bernardiae

Angelica sylvestris subsp.

bernardiae, commonly known as the Bernard's angelica, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant features large, compound leaves with a pinnate structure and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is closely related to other angelica species but is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
