Archidendron sabahense

Archidendron sabahense, commonly known as the Borneo legume or Sabah's climbing bean, is a woody vine native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.

Its ornamental value and potential ecological importance make it a subject of interest for botanical research.
