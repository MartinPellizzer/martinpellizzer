Atriplex leucoclada var. turcomanica

Atriplex leucoclada var. turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves. It is native to Central Asia and thrives in sandy or saline soils.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
