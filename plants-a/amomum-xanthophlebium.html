Amomum xanthophlebium

Amomum xanthophlebium, commonly known as the golden-veined cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic seeds that are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a spice. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and potential medicinal properties.
