Acalypha amentacea f. circinata

Acalypha amentacea f. circinata, commonly known as the curly leaf ironwood, is a unique variant of the acalypha genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that give it an ornamental appearance. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
