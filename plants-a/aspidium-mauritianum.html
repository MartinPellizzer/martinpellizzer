Aspidium mauritianum

Aspidium mauritianum, commonly known as the Mauritius water fern, is a species of fern native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bipinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, which are released into the air for dispersal.

Aspidium mauritianum plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
