Aechmea henningsiana

Aechmea henningsiana, commonly known as the Hennings' bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater. The plant's foliage is typically dark green with prominent white or silver markings, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette, adding a dramatic visual contrast.

Aechmea henningsiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea henningsiana has leaves that are broad, strap-like, and sword-shaped, arranged in a rosette formation, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, thick stem with a smooth, reddish-brown bark that grows upright. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, branched spike of flowers, which are pale pink to white in color, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a three-lobed berry that disperses its seeds through wind and animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its rocky or acidic soil habitat.
