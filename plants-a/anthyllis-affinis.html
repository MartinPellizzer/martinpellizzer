Anthyllis affinis

Anthyllis affinis, commonly known as the lesser bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with trifoliate leaves and a distinctive, branching root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Anthyllis affinis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
