Aizopsis kurilensis

Aizopsis kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Kuril Islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing white or pale pink flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base.

Aizopsis kurilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
