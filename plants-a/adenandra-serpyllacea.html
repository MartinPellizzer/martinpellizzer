Adenandra serpyllacea

Adenandra serpyllacea, commonly known as the common adenandra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
