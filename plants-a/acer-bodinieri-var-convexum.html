Acer bodinieri var. convexum

Acer bodinieri var. convexum, commonly known as the convex-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are thick and leathery, with a convex shape on the upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
