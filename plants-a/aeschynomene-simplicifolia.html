Aeschynomene simplicifolia

Aeschynomene simplicifolia, commonly known as partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and traditional medicine.
