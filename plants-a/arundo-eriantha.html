Arundo eriantha

Arundo eriantha, commonly known as the silky reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and riverbanks, making it an important species in riparian ecosystems.

Arundo eriantha is also valued for its use in traditional basketry and as a source of fiber.
