Acacia sphacelata subsp. recurva

Acacia sphacelata subsp.

recurva, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and narrow, feathery leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
