Alpinia malaccensis

Alpinia malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Alpinia malaccensis thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.
