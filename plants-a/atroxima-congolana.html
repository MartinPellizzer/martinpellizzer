Atroxima congolana

Atroxima congolana, commonly known as the Congo giant spider plant, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and West Africa, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Atroxima congolana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
