Amaranthus berchtoldii

Amaranthus berchtoldii, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish, followed by triangular, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Amaranthus berchtoldii is a common weed but also has ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
