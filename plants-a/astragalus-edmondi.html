Astragalus edmondi

Astragalus edmondi, commonly known as Edmon's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus edmondi is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
