Amaranthus cuspidatus

Amaranthus cuspidatus, commonly known as the spurred amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its distinctive spurred bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its vibrant red or purple flowers are arranged in dense, spiky clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Amaranthus cuspidatus is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
