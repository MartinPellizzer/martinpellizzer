Achnopogon virgatus

Achnopogon virgatus, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and pond margins. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with three stamens and a single pistil.

Achnopogon virgatus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
