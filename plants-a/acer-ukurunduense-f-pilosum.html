Acer ukurunduense f. pilosum

Acer ukurunduense f. pilosum, commonly known as the silky maple, is a rare and unique variant of the Ukurundu maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive silky, downy texture on the underside of its leaves, which gives it its common name. Native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Arc Mountains in Tanzania, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are typically elongated and have a smooth margin, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological features.
