Aristolochia annamensis

Aristolochia annamensis, commonly known as the Annam arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe or a pipestem. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found in tropical forests. Its flowers are typically greenish or brownish, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting specific pollinators.

The plant contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic compounds that have been studied for their medicinal and toxicological properties.
