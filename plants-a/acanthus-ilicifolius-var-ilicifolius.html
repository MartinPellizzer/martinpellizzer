Acanthus ilicifolius var. ilicifolius

Acanthus ilicifolius var. ilicifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features striking, spiny leaves with a distinctive basal rosette structure, and its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage and flowers are often used in horticultural designs for their ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
