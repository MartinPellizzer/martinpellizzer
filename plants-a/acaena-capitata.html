Acaena capitata

Acaena capitata, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, characterized by its dense, prickly foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of leaflets, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Acaena capitata plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
