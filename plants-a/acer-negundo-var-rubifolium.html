Acer negundo var. rubifolium

Acer negundo var. rubifolium, commonly known as the red-leaf boxelder, is a variant of the boxelder tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the fall. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
