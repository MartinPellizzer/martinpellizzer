Asarum hookeri var. majus

Asarum hookeri var. majus, commonly known as giant wild ginger, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a showy, hood-like bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
