Aglaia mirabilis

Aglaia mirabilis, commonly known as the beauty leaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that changes with age. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a glossy surface and vibrant hues of red, orange, or yellow. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing elliptical seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in horticulture for its unique leaf coloration.
