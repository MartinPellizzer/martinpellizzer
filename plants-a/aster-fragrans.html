Aster fragrans

Aster fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Aster fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, sweet fragrance.
