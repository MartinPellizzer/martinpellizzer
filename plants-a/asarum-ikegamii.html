Asarum ikegamii

Asarum ikegamii, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Despite its ornamental value, the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
