Anemone pratensis var. montana

Anemone pratensis var. montana, commonly known as the mountain anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive central tuft of stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and windy mountain conditions. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and alpine tundra.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude habitats.
