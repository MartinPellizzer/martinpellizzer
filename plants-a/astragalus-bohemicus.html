Astragalus bohemicus

Astragalus bohemicus, commonly known as Bohemian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus bohemicus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
