Artemisia caerulescens var. cretacea

Artemisia caerulescens var. cretacea, commonly known as the Mediterranean blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with blue-tinted leaves that emit a strong aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its essential oils have been used traditionally for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
