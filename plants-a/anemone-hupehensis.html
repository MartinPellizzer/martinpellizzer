Anemone hupehensis

Anemone hupehensis, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, palmate leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks, where it can form dense colonies.

The botanical features of Anemone hupehensis make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value and ecological benefits.
