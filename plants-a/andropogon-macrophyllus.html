Andropogon macrophyllus

Andropogon macrophyllus, commonly known as big bluestem, is a tall grass native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its wide, bluish-green leaves and a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion. This plant thrives in prairie ecosystems and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a vital role in maintaining soil structure and supporting wildlife habitats.

Big bluestem is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a forage crop.
