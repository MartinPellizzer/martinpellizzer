Aconitum pallidum

Aconitum pallidum, commonly known as the pale aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins, and its root system consists of a thick, fleshy rhizome. Aconitum pallidum contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
