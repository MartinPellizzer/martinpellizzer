Adenophora alpini

Adenophora alpini, commonly known as the alpine adenophora, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.
