Allium albovianum

Allium albovianum, commonly known as the white garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are grass-like and form a dense rosette near the ground.

The bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valuable addition to culinary and medicinal uses.
