Anacardium occidentale var. americanum

Anacardium occidentale var. americanum, commonly known as the American cashew, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It produces an edible nut enclosed in a hard, spiny shell, which is harvested and processed for its rich, buttery flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires full sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy green surface, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for both its nutritional benefits and economic importance.
