Avenastrum hissaricum

Avenastrum hissaricum, commonly known as the Central Asian grass or Persian grass, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Avenastrum hissaricum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
