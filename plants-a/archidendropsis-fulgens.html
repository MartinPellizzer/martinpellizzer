Archidendropsis fulgens

Archidendropsis fulgens, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique succulent native to South America.

It features a compact, globular stem with dense, woolly hair that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often arranged in a star-like pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
