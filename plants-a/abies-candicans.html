Abies candicans

Abies candicans, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that turns a silvery white underneath. The tree produces cone-like structures called strobiles, which contain the seeds surrounded by a papery bract. Its bark is typically smooth and gray, with shallow fissures, and it can grow to heights of up to 30 meters.

Abies candicans is often found in moist, well-drained soils at high elevations, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
