Anthurium ceratiinum

Anthurium ceratiinum, commonly known as the red anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces vibrant red spathes that enclose small, spadix-like flowers, which are typical of the Araceae family. Anthurium ceratiinum thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage and colorful inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
