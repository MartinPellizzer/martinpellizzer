Astragalus alyssiformis

Astragalus alyssiformis, commonly known as false alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Astragalus alyssiformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
