Apargia danubialis

Apargia danubialis, commonly known as the Danube apargia, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clustered umbels, and it produces a single, elongated fruit.

Apargia danubialis is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to fluctuating water levels.
