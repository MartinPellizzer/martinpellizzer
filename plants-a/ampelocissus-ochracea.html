Ampelocissus ochracea

Ampelocissus ochracea, commonly known as the yellow fig-leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a pale yellow or orange waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The vine is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or fences.
