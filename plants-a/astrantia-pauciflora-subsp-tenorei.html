Astrantia pauciflora subsp. tenorei

Astrantia pauciflora subsp.

tenorei, commonly known as the Sicilian aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features basal leaves that are typically lanceolate or ovate, with a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and delicate floral display.
