Acalypha schinzii var. denticulata

Acalypha schinzii var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features showy, red-bracted inflorescences that are often mistaken for flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant has palmate leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its striking foliage, it is cultivated as a ornamental plant in some regions.
