Agropyron junceum var. sartorii

Agropyron junceum var. sartorii, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
