Anagallis alternifolia var. genuina

Anagallis alternifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear creeper, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are typically opposite or alternate in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pink or red flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
