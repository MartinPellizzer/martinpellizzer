Avenochloa dahurica

Avenochloa dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic regions.

Avenochloa dahurica is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
