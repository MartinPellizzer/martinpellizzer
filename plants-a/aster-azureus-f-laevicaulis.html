Aster azureus f. laevicaulis

Aster azureus f. laevicaulis, commonly known as the blue aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is generally smooth and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
