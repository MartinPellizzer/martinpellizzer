Adipera × herbertii

Adipera × herbertii, commonly known as Herbert's cactus, is a hybrid species within the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or red. Native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its structure consists of cylindrical stems with areoles, from which both spines and flowers emerge.

Adipera × herbertii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
