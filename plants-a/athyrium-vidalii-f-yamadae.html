Athyrium vidalii f. yamadae

Athyrium vidalii f. yamadae, commonly known as the Yamada's Japanese shield fern, is a fern species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Athyriaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce a dense carpet of foliage.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
