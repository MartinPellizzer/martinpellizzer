Astragalus kourosianus

Astragalus kourosianus, commonly known as the Kouris milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Turkey and northern Syria.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to pale purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the spring. Astragalus kourosianus is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
