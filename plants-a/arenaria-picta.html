Arenaria picta

Arenaria picta, commonly known as the painted sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It thrives in harsh environments such as tundra and rocky slopes, showcasing its adaptability to extreme climates. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its scientific name reflects its striking appearance, with "picta" alluding to its colorful, intricate patterns.

Arenaria picta plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitats.
