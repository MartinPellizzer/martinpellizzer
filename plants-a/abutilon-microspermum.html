Abutilon microspermum

Abutilon microspermum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features soft, fuzzy leaves and produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
