Aster multiradiatus

Aster multiradiatus, commonly known as the multibract aster, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster multiradiatus is often found in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
