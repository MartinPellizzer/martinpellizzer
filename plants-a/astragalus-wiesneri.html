Astragalus wiesneri

Astragalus wiesneri, commonly known as the Wiesner milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Astragalus wiesneri is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential medicinal properties.
