Antirrhinum pilosum

Antirrhinum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Antirrhinum pilosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
