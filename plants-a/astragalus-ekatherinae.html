Astragalus ekatherinae

Astragalus ekatherinae, commonly known as the Katerina milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
