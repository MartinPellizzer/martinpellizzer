Aegilops umbellulata f. girmanica

Aegilops umbellulata f. girmanica, commonly known as the Girman's wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
