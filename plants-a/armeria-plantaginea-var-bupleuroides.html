Armeria plantaginea var. bupleuroides

Armeria plantaginea var. bupleuroides, commonly known as the bluebells of the rock, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, succulent stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and limestone habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
