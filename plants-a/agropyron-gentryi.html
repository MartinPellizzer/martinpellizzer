Agropyron gentryi

Agropyron gentryi, commonly known as Gentry's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Agropyron gentryi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its ecological niche.
