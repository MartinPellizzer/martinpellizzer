Arenaria weissiana var. bifida

Arenaria weissiana var. bifida, commonly known as the bifid sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique leaf structure and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
