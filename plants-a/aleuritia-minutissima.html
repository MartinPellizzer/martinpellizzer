Aleuritia minutissima

Aleuritia minutissima, commonly known as the dwarf elder or small elder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries in the autumn. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with smooth bark and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
