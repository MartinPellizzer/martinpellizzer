Acalypha foliosa

Acalypha foliosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silkyleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Acalypha foliosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
