Astragalus aulieatensis var. cornutus

Astragalus aulieatensis var. cornutus, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces a milky sap that is a notable feature of the species.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
