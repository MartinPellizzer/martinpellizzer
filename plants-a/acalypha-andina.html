Acalypha andina

Acalypha andina, commonly known as Andean beauty, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually broad and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental value.

Acalypha andina is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to adapt to challenging growing conditions.
