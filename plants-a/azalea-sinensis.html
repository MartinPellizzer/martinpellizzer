Azalea sinensis

Azalea sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Azalea sinensis is also valued for its fragrance and is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty in gardens and landscapes.
