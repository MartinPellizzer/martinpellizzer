Antidesma rotundifolium

Antidesma rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf antidesma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant features simple, elliptical or ovate leaves that are usually glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The plant is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
