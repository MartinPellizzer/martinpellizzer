Aspidosperma acreanum

Aspidosperma acreanum, commonly known as the Brazilian angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife.
