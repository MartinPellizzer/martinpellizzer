Acacia mutabilis subsp. rhynchophylla

Acacia mutabilis subsp.

rhynchophylla, commonly known as the variable wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that change color throughout the year, ranging from green to red or bronze.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across Australia.
