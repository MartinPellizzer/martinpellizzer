Almaleea

Almaleea, commonly known as the moonflower, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Almaleea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique blooming pattern.
