Astragalus zaissanensis

Astragalus zaissanensis, commonly known as the Zaissan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia, particularly found in Kazakhstan and parts of Russia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
