Acer palmatum f. reticulatum

Acer palmatum f. reticulatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with reticulate foliage, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, lace-like leaves that display a range of colors throughout the growing season. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and prefers partial shade with well-drained soil.

Its unique leaf structure makes it a popular choice for adding visual interest to shaded areas in the garden.
