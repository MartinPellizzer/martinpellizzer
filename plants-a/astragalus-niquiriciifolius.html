Astragalus niquiriciifolius

Astragalus niquiriciifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its seeds are enclosed in pods. It is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
