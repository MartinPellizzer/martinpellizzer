Allocassine

Allocassine, commonly known as the rock fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant features a woody stem with thick, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, greenish flowers are followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically consumed raw.

The Allocassine is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in rocky or arid environments.
