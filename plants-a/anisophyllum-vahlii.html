Anisophyllum vahlii

Anisophyllum vahlii, commonly known as the desert shield-fern, is a unique fern species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, scale-like structures. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, typically forming dense clumps that help it conserve moisture in harsh environments. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.

Anisophyllum vahlii is adapted to survive in extreme conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
