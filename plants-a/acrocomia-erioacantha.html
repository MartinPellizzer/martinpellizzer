Acrocomia erioacantha

Acrocomia erioacantha, commonly known as the silky palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a large, branched cluster of flowers, which are typically yellow or greenish-yellow.

The silky palm is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
