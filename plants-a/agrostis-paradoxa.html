Agrostis paradoxa

Agrostis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, tufted stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces seeds in elongated, cylindrical clusters.

Agrostis paradoxa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
