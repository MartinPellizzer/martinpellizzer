Aconitum acutiusculum var. aureopilosum

Aconitum acutiusculum var. aureopilosum, commonly known as the golden-hairied monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its toxic alkaloids.

This variety is prized in gardens for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
