Aniba murcana

Aniba murcana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Aniba murcana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical regions.
