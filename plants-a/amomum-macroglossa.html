Amomum macroglossa

Amomum macroglossa, commonly known as the large-tongue cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, herbaceous stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, three-lobed fruits. The fruit contains aromatic seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Due to its unique flavor and medicinal properties, it is highly valued in both culinary and pharmacological applications.
