Allium thracicum subsp. pygmaeum

Allium thracicum subsp.

pygmaeum, commonly known as the Thrace dwarf onion, is a small bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale purple.

The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in local cuisines. It is adapted to grow in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its culinary uses.
