Ancistrum sarmentosum

Ancistrum sarmentosum, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, allowing it to twine around supports. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ancistrum sarmentosum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
