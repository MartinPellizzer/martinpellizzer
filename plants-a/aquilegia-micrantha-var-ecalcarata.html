Aquilegia micrantha var. ecalcarata

Aquilegia micrantha var. ecalcarata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers, which range in color from blue to purple, bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety ecalcarata is distinguished by its narrower leaves and slightly different floral structure compared to the typical form of Aquilegia micrantha.
