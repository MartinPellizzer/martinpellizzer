Arcytophyllum lavarum

Arcytophyllum lavarum, commonly known as the Lavarum sage, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and compact, upright growth habit. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue to purple and bloom in late summer.

Arcytophyllum lavarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
