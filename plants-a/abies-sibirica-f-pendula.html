Abies sibirica f. pendula

Abies sibirica f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Siberian fir, is a unique variant of the Siberian fir, characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect.

This form is a natural mutation of the species, often found in its native habitat of eastern Siberia. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly glossy, providing a striking visual contrast against its hanging foliage. It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer that thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
