Amperea conferta

Amperea conferta, commonly known as the broad-leaved ampere, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Amperea conferta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
