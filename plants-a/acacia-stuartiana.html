Acacia stuartiana

Acacia stuartiana, commonly known as the Stuart's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are divided into small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia stuartiana is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
