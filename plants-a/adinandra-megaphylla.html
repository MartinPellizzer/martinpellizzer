Adinandra megaphylla

Adinandra megaphylla, commonly known as the large-leafed adinandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by orange-red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Adinandra megaphylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
