Acrolasia affinis

Acrolasia affinis, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate and oval-shaped, with a rough texture.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and used in traditional medicine.
