Arrabidaea floribunda

Arrabidaea floribunda, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its striking appearance and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
