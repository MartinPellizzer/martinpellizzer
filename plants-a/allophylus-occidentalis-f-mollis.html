Allophylus occidentalis f. mollis

Allophylus occidentalis f. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved western alder, is a variant of the western alder native to certain regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves that distinguish it from the typical form of the species. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often near streams or in forested areas. Its botanical features include small, clustered flowers and fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
