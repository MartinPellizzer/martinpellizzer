Asystasia kunthiana

Asystasia kunthiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Asystasia kunthiana is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
