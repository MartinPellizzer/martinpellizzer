Avenochloa bromoides

Avenochloa bromoides, commonly known as false millet, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of tiny, greenish-brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields or along roadsides.

Avenochloa bromoides is considered a weed in some areas but has potential as a forage crop due to its rapid growth and nutritional value.
