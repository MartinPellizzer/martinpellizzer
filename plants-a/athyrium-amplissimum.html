Athyrium amplissimum

Athyrium amplissimum, commonly known as the giant Japanese shield fern, is a striking fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

The spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in its natural habitat.
