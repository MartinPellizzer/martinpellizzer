Acalypha alopecuroidea lusus glanduligera

Acalypha alopecuroidea lusus glanduligera, commonly known as the woolly acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its unique texture and appearance.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking visual appeal.
