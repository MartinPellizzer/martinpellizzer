Atropa bicolor

Atropa bicolor, commonly known as the death cap or death blossom, is a toxic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, lobed leaves and produces berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It contains various toxic compounds, including atropine and scopolamine, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its beauty and toxicity, it is often mistaken for edible plants, making it a dangerous species to handle without proper knowledge.
