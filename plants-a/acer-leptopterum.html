Acer leptopterum

Acer leptopterum, commonly known as the red maple or paper maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
