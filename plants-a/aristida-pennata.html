Aristida pennata

Aristida pennata, commonly known as the silky grass or slender beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period usually occurs in the summer months, and it is known for its delicate, feathery appearance.

Aristida pennata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
