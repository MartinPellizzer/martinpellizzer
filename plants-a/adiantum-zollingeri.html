Adiantum zollingeri

Adiantum zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's maidenhair fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the feathers of a bird. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a feature typical of many ferns.

Adiantum zollingeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper care.
