Acriopsis indica

Acriopsis indica, commonly known as the Indian cow parsley, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, white to greenish-yellow flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is native to parts of Asia, including India, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
