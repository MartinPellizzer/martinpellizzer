Acacia doreta

Acacia doreta, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to golden-yellow inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. Acacia doreta thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
