Amylocarpus tenuissimus

Amylocarpus tenuissimus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are rich in starch. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers on a spadix, typical of the arum family. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, hence the common name, and are often used in traditional medicine.
