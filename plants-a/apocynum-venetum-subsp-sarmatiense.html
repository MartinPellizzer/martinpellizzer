Apocynum venetum subsp. sarmatiense

Apocynum venetum subsp.

sarmatiense, commonly known as the Sarmatian dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and related species characterized by their milky sap.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

The plant contains cardiac glycosides, making it toxic to humans and animals.
