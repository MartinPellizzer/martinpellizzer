Andropogon riedelii var. genuinus

Andropogon riedelii var. genuinus, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
