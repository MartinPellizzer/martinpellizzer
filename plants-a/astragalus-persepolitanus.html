Astragalus persepolitanus

Astragalus persepolitanus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a robust root system for water retention. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitat, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
