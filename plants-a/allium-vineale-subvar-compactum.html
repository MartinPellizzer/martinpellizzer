Allium vineale subvar. compactum

Allium vineale subvar. compactum, commonly known as the compact garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that are often used as a culinary herb, similar to chives. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit makes it ideal for edging and container gardening.
