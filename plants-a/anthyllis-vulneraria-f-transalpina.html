Anthyllis vulneraria f. transalpina

Anthyllis vulneraria f. transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine Scotch thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a distinctive serrated edge, and its root system is deep and fibrous. It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils.

This variety is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
