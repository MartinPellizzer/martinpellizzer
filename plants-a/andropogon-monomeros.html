Andropogon monomeros

Andropogon monomeros, commonly known as flatleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, blue-gray flowers.

Andropogon monomeros plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
