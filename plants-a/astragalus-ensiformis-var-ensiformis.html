Astragalus ensiformis var. ensiformis

Astragalus ensiformis var. ensiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
