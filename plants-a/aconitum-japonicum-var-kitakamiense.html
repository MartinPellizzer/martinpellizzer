Aconitum japonicum var. kitakamiense

Aconitum japonicum var. kitakamiense, commonly known as the Kitakami aconite, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
