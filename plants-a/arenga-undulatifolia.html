Arenga undulatifolia

Arenga undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf areca, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Arenga undulatifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various tropical environments.
