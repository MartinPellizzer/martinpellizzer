Aconitum tenuicaule

Aconitum tenuicaule, commonly known as the slender monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with dark green, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Aconitum tenuicaule is also noted for its toxicity, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
