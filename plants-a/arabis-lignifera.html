Arabis lignifera

Arabis lignifera, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features basal rosettes of elliptic, glossy, dark green leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, spurred flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Arabis lignifera is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.
