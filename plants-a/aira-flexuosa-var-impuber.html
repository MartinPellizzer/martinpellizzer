Aira flexuosa var. impuber

Aira flexuosa var. impuber, commonly known as slender false oat, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and streambanks. The plant features slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually glabrous or nearly so. Its inflorescence is a panicle composed of spikelets that are loosely arranged.

This variety is characterized by its reduced hair cover and compact growth habit, making it distinctive within the genus Aira.
