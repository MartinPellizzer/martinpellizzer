Apocynum ambigens

Apocynum ambigens, commonly known as the Indian cucumber root, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a perennial herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds, and its distinctive, fleshy, orange-colored roots. It thrives in moist, woodlands and is often found in shaded environments.

Apocynum ambigens is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
