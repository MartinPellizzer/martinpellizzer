Aeluropus littoralis subsp. sinensis

Aeluropus littoralis subsp.

sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese saltgrass, is a halophytic perennial grass native to coastal regions of China. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in tidal flats and coastal dunes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with minute flowers. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and preventing erosion.

Its ability to tolerate extreme environmental conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
