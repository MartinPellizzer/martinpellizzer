Andropogon distachyos var. dasystachys

Andropogon distachyos var. dasystachys, commonly known as the giant panic grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and use in restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
