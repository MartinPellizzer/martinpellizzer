Aster marcusii

Aster marcusii, commonly known as the Marcusii aster, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the specific variety. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Aster marcusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
