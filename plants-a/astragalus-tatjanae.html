Astragalus tatjanae

Astragalus tatjanae, commonly known as Tatjana's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Astragalus tatjanae is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and at high elevations.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
