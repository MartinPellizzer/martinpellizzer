Aster subulatus var. ligulatus

Aster subulatus var. ligulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
