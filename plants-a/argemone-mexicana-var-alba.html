Argemone mexicana var. alba

Argemone mexicana var. alba, commonly known as white prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, showy white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
