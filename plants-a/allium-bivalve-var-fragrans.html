Allium bivalve var. fragrans

Allium bivalve var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in Asian cuisine.

The fragrant leaves and flowers make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and culinary gardens.
