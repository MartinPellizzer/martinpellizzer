Athyrium brevisorum

Athyrium brevisorum, commonly known as the short-stalked Japanese shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to East Asia.

It features elegant, bipinnate fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are short and produce clusters of spore-bearing structures called sorus.

Athyrium brevisorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
