Aperula macrophylla

Aperula macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf aperula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
