Allium salota

Allium salota, commonly known as the desert leek, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a potential culinary and medicinal plant.
