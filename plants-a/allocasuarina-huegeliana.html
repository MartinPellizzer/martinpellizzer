Allocasuarina huegeliana

Allocasuarina huegeliana, commonly known as the Huergel's she-oak, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like leaves that are clustered in spirals along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody cones that contain its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Allocasuarina huegeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
