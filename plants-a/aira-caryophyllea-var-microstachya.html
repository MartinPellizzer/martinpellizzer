Aira caryophyllea var. microstachya

Aira caryophyllea var. microstachya, commonly known as the dwarf red grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features fine, silver-blue foliage and produces dense, bright red to bronze inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its drought tolerance.
