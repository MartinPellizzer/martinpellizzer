Acalypha virginica

Acalypha virginica, commonly known as the smoothleaf or redroot beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a reddish hue on the underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Acalypha virginica is often found in wetlands and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
