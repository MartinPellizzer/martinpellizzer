Albuca seineri

Albuca seineri, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Albuca seineri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
