Astragalus mossulensis var. multiflorus

Astragalus mossulensis var. multiflorus, commonly known as the Multiflorous milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
