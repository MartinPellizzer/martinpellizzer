Agave coetocapnia subsp. coetocapnia

Agave coetocapnia subsp.

coetocapnia, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a species native to Mexico. It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color.

The plant produces a tall flowering stalk with small, greenish-white flowers, which appear after several years. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional practices.
