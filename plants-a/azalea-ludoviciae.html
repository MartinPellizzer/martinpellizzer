Azalea ludoviciae

Azalea ludoviciae, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features broad, elliptical leaves with a glossy texture. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming period, which adds vibrant color to the landscape.
