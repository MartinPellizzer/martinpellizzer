Aconitum napellus subsp. lusitanicum

Aconitum napellus subsp.

lusitanicum, commonly known as the Iberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its roots contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
