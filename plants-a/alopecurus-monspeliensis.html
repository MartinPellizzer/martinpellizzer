Alopecurus monspeliensis

Alopecurus monspeliensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. It produces small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Alopecurus monspeliensis is often considered a characteristic species of grassland ecosystems.
