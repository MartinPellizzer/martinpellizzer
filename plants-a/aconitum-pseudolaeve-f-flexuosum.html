Aconitum pseudolaeve f. flexuosum

Aconitum pseudolaeve f. flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible pale aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that often curve gracefully. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are borne in dense, conical clusters. Its leaves are palmately divided with a waxy surface, and it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
