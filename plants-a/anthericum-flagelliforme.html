Anthericum flagelliforme

Anthericum flagelliforme, commonly known as the flagellate fern, is a unique plant species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its long, slender fronds that resemble whips, giving it its common name. The plant has a distinctive structure with a creeping rhizome and upright, pinnate leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
