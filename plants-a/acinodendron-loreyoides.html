Acinodendron loreyoides

Acinodendron loreyoides, commonly known as the Lorey's acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a leathery texture and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
