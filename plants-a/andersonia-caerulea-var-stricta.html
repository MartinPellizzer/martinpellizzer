Andersonia caerulea var. stricta

Andersonia caerulea var. stricta, commonly known as the blue-flowered Andersonia, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces vivid blue or violet flowers during the cooler months. The plant's foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
