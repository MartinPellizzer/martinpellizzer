Astragalus flexuosus var. diehlii

Astragalus flexuosus var. diehlii, commonly known as the Sierra flexuous milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often ascending stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets arranged along the stem.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
