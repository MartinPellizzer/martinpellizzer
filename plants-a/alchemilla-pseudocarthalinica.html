Alchemilla pseudocarthalinica

Alchemilla pseudocarthalinica, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are rounded and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
