Achillea subtanacetifolia

Achillea subtanacetifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features finely divided, linear leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a fine layer of white hair. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are typically white or pale yellow and attract various pollinators.

Achillea subtanacetifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
