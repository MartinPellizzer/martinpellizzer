Aniba jenmanii

Aniba jenmanii, commonly known as the Javanese camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves with a strong camphor scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
