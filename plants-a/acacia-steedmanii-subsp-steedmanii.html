Acacia steedmanii subsp. steedmanii

Acacia steedmanii subsp.

steedmanii, commonly known as the Steedman's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia. It features slender, zigzagging branches and produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom during the warmer months.

The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping in suitable climates.
