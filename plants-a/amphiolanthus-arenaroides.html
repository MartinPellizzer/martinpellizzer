Amphiolanthus arenaroides

Amphiolanthus arenaroides, commonly known as the sandstone lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat. It typically grows on rocky outcrops and sandy soils, showcasing its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
