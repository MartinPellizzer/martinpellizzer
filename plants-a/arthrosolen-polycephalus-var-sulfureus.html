Arthrosolen polycephalus var. sulfureus

Arthrosolen polycephalus var. sulfureus, commonly known as the sulfur-hued coralplant, is a unique member of the Gnetophyta division, characterized by its specialized adaptations to arid environments.

It possesses a succulent, branching structure with small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Its root system is highly efficient at absorbing and storing water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

This variant is notable for its striking coloration and is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid regions.
