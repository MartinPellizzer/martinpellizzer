Araucaria imbricata

Araucaria imbricata, commonly known as the Brazilian pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and its distinctive, upward-curving needles that form a conical shape. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for numerous birds and insects.

Araucaria imbricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
