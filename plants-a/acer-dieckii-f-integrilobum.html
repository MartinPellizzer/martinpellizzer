Acer dieckii f. integrilobum

Acer dieckii f. integrilobum, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are typically larger and more rounded compared to other maple species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The foliage turns vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
