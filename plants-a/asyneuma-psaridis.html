Asyneuma psaridis

Asyneuma psaridis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Asyneuma psaridis is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to survive in harsh, semi-desert conditions.
