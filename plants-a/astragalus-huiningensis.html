Astragalus huiningensis

Astragalus huiningensis, commonly known as the Huiining milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
