Amethystanthus effusus

Amethystanthus effusus, commonly known as the velvet flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a creeping habit with hairy, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Amethystanthus effusus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
