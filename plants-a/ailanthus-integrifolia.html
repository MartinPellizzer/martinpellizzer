Ailanthus integrifolia

Ailanthus integrifolia, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with entire margins and its showy yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky, white coating. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in urban environments, making it both ecologically significant and often invasive.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
