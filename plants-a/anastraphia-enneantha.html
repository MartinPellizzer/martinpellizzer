Anastraphia enneantha

Anastraphia enneantha, commonly known as the nine-angled anastraphia, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, nine-lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
