Alchemilla acutiloba

Alchemilla acutiloba, commonly known as narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla acutiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
