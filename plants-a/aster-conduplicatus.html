Aster conduplicatus

Aster conduplicatus, commonly known as the twinleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in the fall.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators.
