Adromischus trigynus

Adromischus trigynus, commonly known as the Three-lobed Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright sunlight.

Adromischus trigynus is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its compact size and ornamental appeal.
