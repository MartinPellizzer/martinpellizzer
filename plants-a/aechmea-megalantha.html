Aechmea megalantha

Aechmea megalantha, commonly known as the giant bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is known for its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Aechmea megalantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its impressive size and visual appeal.
