Adansonia rubrostipa

Adansonia rubrostipa, commonly known as the red-stemmed baobab, is a striking tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the genus Adansonia, which includes all the baobab species. The plant is characterized by its thick, swollen trunk that stores water, a feature adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are palmate, with five to seven lobes, and it produces large, showy white flowers.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its role in the local ecosystem.
