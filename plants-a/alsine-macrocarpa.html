Alsine macrocarpa

Alsine macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded sandgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Alsine macrocarpa plays a role in stabilizing dunes and is often found in coastal and desert ecosystems.
