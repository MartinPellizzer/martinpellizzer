Aeschynomene schliebenii var. mossambicensis

Aeschynomene schliebenii var. mossambicensis, commonly known as the African leadplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is typically found in grassland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
