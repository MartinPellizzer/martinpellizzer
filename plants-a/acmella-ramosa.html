Acmella ramosa

Acmella ramosa, commonly known as the spiked sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive spiky center. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Acmella ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
