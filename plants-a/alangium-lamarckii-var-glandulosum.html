Alangium lamarckii var. glandulosum

Alangium lamarckii var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular alangium, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
