Apama

Apama, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It features large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the mud and absorbing nutrients from the water.

Apama plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
