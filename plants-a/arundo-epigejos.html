Arundo epigejos

Arundo epigejos, commonly known as the broadleaf reed, is a tall, perennial grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and wetlands.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species in its native habitat.
