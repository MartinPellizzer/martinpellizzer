Anthericum ciliatum var. argentinense

Anthericum ciliatum var. argentinense, commonly known as the Argentine nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves with ciliate margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant has been used traditionally in some regions for its medicinal properties.
