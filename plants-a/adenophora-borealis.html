Adenophora borealis

Adenophora borealis, commonly known as the northern bellflower, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and grows in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer, and it is often found in tundra and subarctic environments.

Adenophora borealis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitats.
