Annona sylvatica

Annona sylvatica, commonly known as the sylvatic custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are fragrant and white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The fruit is a smooth, green, pear-shaped berry that turns yellow when ripe, containing a central cavity with several seeds.
