Anoniodes

Anoniodes, commonly known as the false arum, is a genus of flowering plants in the Araceae family.

These plants are characterized by their striking, often showy inflorescences, which consist of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. Anoniodes species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. They have a complex reproductive system involving both male and female flowers on the same spadix.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
