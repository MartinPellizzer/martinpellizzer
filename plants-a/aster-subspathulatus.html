Aster subspathulatus

Aster subspathulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and meadows.

Aster subspathulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
