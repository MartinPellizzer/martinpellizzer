Acisanthera alata var. ciliata

Acisanthera alata var. ciliata, commonly known as the fringed acanthus, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features distinctive, palmate leaves with soft, silky hairs along the margins, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, clustered flowers in shades of greenish-white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a striking addition to rock gardens and borders.
