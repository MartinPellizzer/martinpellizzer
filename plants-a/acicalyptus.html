Acicalyptus

Acicalyptus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eucalyptus, is a genus of flowering plants in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. Acicalyptus species typically grow as trees or shrubs, often with smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves. They are well adapted to dry and arid environments, making them resilient to drought conditions.

Many species within this genus are valued for their timber, essential oils, and ecological roles in their native habitats.
