Angelica takeshimana

Angelica takeshimana, commonly known as Japanese angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration. The plant produces large, umbrella-like compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Angelica takeshimana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
