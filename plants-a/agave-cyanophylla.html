Agave cyanophylla

Agave cyanophylla, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears small, white flowers, which bloom after several years. It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is closely related to other agave species used in traditional and commercial contexts.

Due to its resilience and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
