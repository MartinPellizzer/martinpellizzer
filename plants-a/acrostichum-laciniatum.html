Acrostichum laciniatum

Acrostichum laciniatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows along the edges of swamps and wetlands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often dissected, giving it a feathery appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Acrostichum laciniatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, humid conditions.
