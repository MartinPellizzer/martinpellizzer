Astragalus leucophanus

Astragalus leucophanus, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Astragalus leucophanus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
