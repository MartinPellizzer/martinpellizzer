Adenostoma fasciculatum var. typica

Adenostoma fasciculatum var. typica, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
