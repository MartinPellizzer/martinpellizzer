Aleuritia elliptica

Aleuritia elliptica, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Aleuritia elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
