Aster serotinus

Aster serotinus, commonly known as late aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features slender stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, with ray florets that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Aster serotinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
