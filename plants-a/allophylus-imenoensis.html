Allophylus imenoensis

Allophylus imenoensis, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, round, fleshy berries that are typically green when mature. Allophylus imenoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shady conditions.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
