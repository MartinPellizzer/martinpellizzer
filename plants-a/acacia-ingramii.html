Acacia ingramii

Acacia ingramii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow, globular flower heads that appear in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Acacia ingramii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
