Aleuritopteris chihuahuaensis

Aleuritopteris chihuahuaensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua water fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or near water bodies. Its reproductive structures, including sori and indusia, are typical of the Polypodiaceae family.

Aleuritopteris chihuahuaensis plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms in its native ecosystem.
