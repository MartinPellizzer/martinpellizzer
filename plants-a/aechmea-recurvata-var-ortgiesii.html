Aechmea recurvata var. ortgiesii

Aechmea recurvata var. ortgiesii, commonly known as the orange-tipped bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant produces vibrant, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its foliage is typically dark green with a metallic sheen, enhancing its ornamental value.

This variety is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability to low light conditions and minimal water requirements.
