Allium globosum var. saxatile

Allium globosum var. saxatile, commonly known as the rock onion or saxatile garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of small, globe-shaped white or pinkish flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils, making it well-suited for alpine or rock garden environments. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in gardens with well-drained soil.
