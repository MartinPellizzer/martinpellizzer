Albizia schimperiana

Albizia schimperiana, commonly known as the African mimosa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait that gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Albizia schimperiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
