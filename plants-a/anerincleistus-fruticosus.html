Anerincleistus fruticosus

Anerincleistus fruticosus, commonly known as the creeping milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that can reach up to 1 meter in length. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often covered with fine hairs.

The flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and produce a milky sap that is characteristic of the milkweed genus.
