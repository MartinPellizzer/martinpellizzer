Asplenium tamandarei

Asplenium tamandarei, commonly known as the Tamandare spleenwort, is a fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that typically grow in dense clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or along stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sorangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a feature typical of many ferns.

Asplenium tamandarei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouse settings.
