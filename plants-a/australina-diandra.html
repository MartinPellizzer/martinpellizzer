Australina diandra

Australina diandra, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Its leaves are narrow and often have a feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.
