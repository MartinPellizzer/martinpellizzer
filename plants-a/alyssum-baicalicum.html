Alyssum baicalicum

Alyssum baicalicum, commonly known as the Siberian Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Alyssum baicalicum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
