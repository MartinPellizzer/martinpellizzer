Acacia loxocarpa

Acacia loxocarpa, commonly known as the African bristled acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is distinguished by its thorny stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its yellow flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences, and the seed pods are woody and ribbed.

Acacia loxocarpa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
