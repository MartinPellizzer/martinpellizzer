Aconitum cannabifolium

Aconitum cannabifolium, commonly known as the cannabis-leaved monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble cannabis leaves, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant, with a spurred petal and a complex structure that facilitates pollination by insects. The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are thick, fibrous, and tuberous, aiding in water and nutrient storage.
