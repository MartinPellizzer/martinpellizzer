Atelophragma longissimum

Atelophragma longissimum, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is often found in shaded, moist forest environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
