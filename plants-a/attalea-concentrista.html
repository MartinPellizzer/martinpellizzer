Attalea concentrista

Attalea concentrista, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tall, evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and produces clusters of woody, spiny fruits that contain the edible Brazil nuts. The tree has a robust root system that helps stabilize the soil in its native habitat. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, typically pollinated by insects.

The plant plays a crucial role in the ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
