Aspilia pastazensis

Aspilia pastazensis, commonly known as the Pastaza spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow-orange ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of disk florets. It typically grows in moist, forested areas and is adapted to the humid climate of its native region.

Aspilia pastazensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
