Asplenium enatum var. gemmiparum

Asplenium enatum var. gemmiparum, commonly known as the gemmiparous spleenwort, is a rare and unusual fern species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant reproduces primarily through gemmae, small vegetative propagules that form on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or on the bark of trees.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicately endangered species.
