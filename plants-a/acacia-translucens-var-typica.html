Acacia translucens var. typica

Acacia translucens var. typica, commonly known as the translucent acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny, thread-like leaflets. This plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its stems and having a deep root system.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
