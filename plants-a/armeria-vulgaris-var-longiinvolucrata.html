Armeria vulgaris var. longiinvolucrata

Armeria vulgaris var. longiinvolucrata, commonly known as the long-involucrate thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are linear and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal and dry garden environments.
