Annona scandens var. scandens

Annona scandens var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing custard apple, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant bears fruit that ripens to a yellow or orange color, with a creamy, custard-like pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
