Anchusa officinalis subsp. baumgartenii

Anchusa officinalis subsp.

baumgartenii, commonly known as the Balkan blueweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.

The plant features blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
