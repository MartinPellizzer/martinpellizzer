Agrimonia eupatoria var. capensis

Agrimonia eupatoria var. capensis, commonly known as Cape agrimony, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
