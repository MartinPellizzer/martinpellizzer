Asplenium viride subsp. incisum

Asplenium viride subsp.

incisum, commonly known as the green spleenwort, is a fern species belonging to the family Aspleniaceae. It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocks or soil in temperate regions. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to substrates, while its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of its leaves.

This subspecies is noted for its resilience and adaptability to various microhabitats.
