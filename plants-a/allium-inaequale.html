Allium inaequale

Allium inaequale, commonly known as the unequal leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to onions and garlic. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.
