Adiantum melanoleucum

Adiantum melanoleucum, commonly known as the silver-veined maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, glossy green fronds with distinctive silver veins that contrast beautifully with its dark green coloration. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its fronds are typically biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year.

Adiantum melanoleucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
