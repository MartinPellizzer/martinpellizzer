Amygdalus communis f. purpurea

Amygdalus communis f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-scented almond.

This botanical variant is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged flowers and aromatic foliage. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to the common almond. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense, dark green leaves and showy blooms that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and unique fragrance.
