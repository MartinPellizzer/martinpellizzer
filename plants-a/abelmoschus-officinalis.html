Abelmoschus officinalis

Abelmoschus officinalis, commonly known as okra, is a tropical and subtropical flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its capsule fruit, which contains numerous fibrous seeds, and its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant is native to Africa but is now cultivated in many warm regions around the world. Okra is valued for its edible young fruits, which are used in various cuisines, and for its medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight for optimal growth.
