Axonopus furcatus

Axonopus furcatus, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Axonopus furcatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
