Acacia pulchella f. spinosissima

Acacia pulchella f. spinosissima, commonly known as the spinous acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in arid environments. Its adaptations make it well-suited for coastal and sandy soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Its distinctive spiny foliage and yellow flowers make it a notable species in its native range.
