Anethum graveolens

Anethum graveolens, commonly known as dill, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is widely cultivated for its aromatic seeds and foliage, which are used in cooking and herbal medicine. Dill thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for home gardens.

Its essential oils, rich in compounds like limonene and carvone, contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
