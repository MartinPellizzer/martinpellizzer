Ageratum purpureum

Ageratum purpureum, commonly known as purple top, is an annual flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces violet, blue, or white blooms that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is covered in fine, hairy leaves that give it a soft, velvety texture.

Ageratum purpureum is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant colors and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
