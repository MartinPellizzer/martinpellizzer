Amelanchier sanguinea var. grandiflora

Amelanchier sanguinea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces white, fragrant flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for birds and wildlife.
