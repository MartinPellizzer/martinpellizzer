Amaryllis petiolata subsp. cochunensis

Amaryllis petiolata subsp.

cochunensis, commonly known as the Congo amaryllis, is a species native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears multiple blooms, often in clusters. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, emerging from a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
