Agropyron smithii f. molle

Agropyron smithii f. molle, commonly known as the soft wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
