Alnus viridis f. brembana

Alnus viridis f. brembana, commonly known as the Bremban alder, is a subspecies of the alder tree native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
