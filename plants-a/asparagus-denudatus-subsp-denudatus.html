Asparagus denudatus subsp. denudatus

Asparagus denudatus subsp.

denudatus, commonly known as the naked asparagus, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems that are typically covered with small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, spiny fruits. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant adaptations.
