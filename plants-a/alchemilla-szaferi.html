Alchemilla szaferi

Alchemilla szaferi, commonly known as Szafer's lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is particularly noted for its ornamental value and ability to retain moisture.
