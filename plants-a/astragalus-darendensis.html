Astragalus darendensis

Astragalus darendensis, commonly known as the Darendan milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many species in the Astragalus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Due to its ecological importance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
