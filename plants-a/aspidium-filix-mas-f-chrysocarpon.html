Aspidium filix-mas f. chrysocarpon

Aspidium filix-mas f. chrysocarpon, commonly known as the golden-fruited maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow spore cases, which contrast with the typical green spores of the species. The plant belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance.

The golden spores are a notable botanical feature, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
