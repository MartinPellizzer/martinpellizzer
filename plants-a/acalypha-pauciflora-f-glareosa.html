Acalypha pauciflora f. glareosa

Acalypha pauciflora f. glareosa, commonly known as the glittering ironweed, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues, creating a visually appealing contrast against its green foliage. The plant features palmate leaves with a soft, velvety texture, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden settings.

Its unique floral display and foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
