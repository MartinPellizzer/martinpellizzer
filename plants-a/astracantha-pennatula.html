Astracantha pennatula

Astracantha pennatula, commonly known as the feathered thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Astracantha pennatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
