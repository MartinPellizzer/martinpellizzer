Alternanthera achyrantha

Alternanthera achyrantha, commonly known as red flame or red velvet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red foliage that becomes more pronounced in low light conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This ornamental plant is often used in water gardens and as a ground cover due to its vibrant color and ease of maintenance.
