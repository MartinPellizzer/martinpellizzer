Abutilon mucronulatum

Abutilon mucronulatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
