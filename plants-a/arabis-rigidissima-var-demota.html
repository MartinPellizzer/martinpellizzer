Arabis rigidissima var. demota

Arabis rigidissima var. demota, commonly known as the rigid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with basal leaves that are linear and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and typically appear in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
