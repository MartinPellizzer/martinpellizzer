Asarum savatieri

Asarum savatieri, commonly known as the Indian wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces a unique, twisted, and often fragrant flower that resembles a pipe or a horn, which is typical of the genus Asarum. Its rhizomatous root system is used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
