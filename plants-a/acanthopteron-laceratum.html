Acanthopteron laceratum

Acanthopteron laceratum, commonly known as the roughleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in stiff, spiny hairs, which give it its common name. The leaves are deeply lobed, with a rough, sandpapery texture, and often have a bluish-gray hue. Its flowers are small and typically range in color from yellow to orange, appearing in dense, spherical clusters.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
