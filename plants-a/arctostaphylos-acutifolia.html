Arctostaphylos acutifolia

Arctostaphylos acutifolia, commonly known as the coastal manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and bright red or orange berries that persist through winter. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds. Its small, leathery leaves are typically arranged in pairs along the stems.

Arctostaphylos acutifolia plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
