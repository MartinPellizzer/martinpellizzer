Alchemilla vulgaris f. rubristipula

Alchemilla vulgaris f. rubristipula, commonly known as the red-stippled lady’s mantle, is a variant of the lady’s mantle plant characterized by its distinctive red veins on the leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in clay soils.

The red-stippled variation is particularly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
