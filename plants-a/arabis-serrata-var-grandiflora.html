Arabis serrata var. grandiflora

Arabis serrata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. The species is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
