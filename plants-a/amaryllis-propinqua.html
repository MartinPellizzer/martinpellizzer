Amaryllis propinqua

Amaryllis propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact stature and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white, pink, or red blooms during the summer months. Its leaves are strap-like and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its showy flowers.

Amaryllis propinqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
