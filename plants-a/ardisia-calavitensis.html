Ardisia calavitensis

Ardisia calavitensis, commonly known as the Madagascan arrowroot, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Ardisia calavitensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
