Allium stramineum

Allium stramineum, commonly known as the yellow onion or shallot, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical leaves and clusters of small, white or yellow flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are widely used in culinary applications worldwide. Its leaves are also edible and can be used in cooking or as a garnish.

Allium stramineum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and farms.
