Assonia calantha

Assonia calantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a spreading growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
