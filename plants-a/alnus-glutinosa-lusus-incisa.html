Alnus glutinosa lusus incisa

Alnus glutinosa lusus incisa, commonly known as the sticky alder, is a variant of the alder tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and catkin-like flowers. The bark is typically smooth and grayish, with a sticky resin that can be harvested for various traditional uses. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetland areas.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
