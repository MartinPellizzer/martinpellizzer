Artemisia lerchei

Artemisia lerchei, commonly known as the Andean wormwood, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish-gray flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its essential oils contain compounds with antimicrobial and insect-repelling properties.

Artemisia lerchei is also valued for its traditional use in folk medicine by indigenous communities.
