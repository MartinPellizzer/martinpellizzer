Adiantum persimile

Adiantum persimile, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Adiantum persimile reproduces through spores and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
