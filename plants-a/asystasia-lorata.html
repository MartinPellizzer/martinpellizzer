Asystasia lorata

Asystasia lorata, commonly known as the silver lace plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant features heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Asystasia lorata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
