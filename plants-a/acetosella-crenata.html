Acetosella crenata

Acetosella crenata, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Acetosella crenata is often found in disturbed soils and can be used in ecological restoration projects.
