Aster macilentus

Aster macilentus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall.

Aster macilentus is known for its delicate, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist to dry soils.
