Anthoxanthum odoratum var. corsicum

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. corsicum, commonly known as Corsican grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
