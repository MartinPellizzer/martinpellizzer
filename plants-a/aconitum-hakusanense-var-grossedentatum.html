Aconitum hakusanense var. grossedentatum

Aconitum hakusanense var. grossedentatum, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that resemble hooded bells. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
