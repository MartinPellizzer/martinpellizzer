Asplenium rasoloheryi

Asplenium rasoloheryi, commonly known as the Madagascar spleenwort, is a species of fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky surfaces or tree trunks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and restricted geographic distribution.
