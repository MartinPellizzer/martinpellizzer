Asphodelus africanus

Asphodelus africanus, commonly known as the African asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Asphodelus africanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
