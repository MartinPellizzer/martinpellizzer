Arabis sagittata var. subglabrata

Arabis sagittata var. subglabrata, commonly known as the narrowleaf rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are elongated and arrow-shaped, with a hairy surface that is less prominent in this variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
