Astragalus aridovallicola

Astragalus aridovallicola, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus aridovallicola plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
