Agapanthus africanus subsp. walshii

Agapanthus africanus subsp.

walshii, commonly known as the African lily or blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa. It features dense, spherical clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, creating a dramatic visual impact.

The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
