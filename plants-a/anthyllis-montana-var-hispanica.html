Anthyllis montana var. hispanica

Anthyllis montana var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish broom.

It is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula, characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in Mediterranean ecosystems.
