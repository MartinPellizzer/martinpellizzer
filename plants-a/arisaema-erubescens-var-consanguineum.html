Arisaema erubescens var. consanguineum

Arisaema erubescens var. consanguineum, commonly known as the red dragon plant or Chinese arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a broad, waxy spathe that is typically red or orange in color. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces edible tubers that are used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its botanical structure includes a short stem, heart-shaped leaves, and a unique reproductive system with separate male and female flowers.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
