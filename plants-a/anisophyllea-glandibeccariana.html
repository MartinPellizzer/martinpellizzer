Anisophyllea glandibeccariana

Anisophyllea glandibeccariana, commonly known as the broadleaf anisophyllea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually elliptical or ovate leaves that vary in size and shape along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
