Aspidosperma desmanthum

Aspidosperma desmanthum, commonly known as the Brazilian redwood, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

It is valued for its durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
