Astragalus globiflorus var. andalanicus

Astragalus globiflorus var. andalanicus, commonly known as the Andalusian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
