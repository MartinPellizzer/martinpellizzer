Acidocroton litoralis

Acidocroton litoralis, commonly known as the sea croton, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal habitats, where it is adapted to salt spray and high humidity. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are usually simple and alternate. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it a resilient choice for coastal landscaping.
