Agapetes saxicola

Agapetes saxicola, commonly known as the rock heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains, primarily found in high-altitude regions of South America. This evergreen shrub typically grows on rocky slopes and in humid, cool environments. Its small, leathery leaves are adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
