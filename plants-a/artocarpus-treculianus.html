Artocarpus treculianus

Artocarpus treculianus, commonly known as the treculian jackfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces a large, spiky fruit that is typically harvested when it is fully mature. The fruit contains a fibrous outer husk and a sweet, edible interior with large seeds.

It is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
