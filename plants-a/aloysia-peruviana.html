Aloysia peruviana

Aloysia peruviana, commonly known as the purple verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of showy, lavender-pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Aloysia peruviana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
