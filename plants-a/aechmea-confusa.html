Aechmea confusa

Aechmea confusa, commonly known as the pink pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with flowers that range from pink to red, often resembling a pineapple in appearance. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Aechmea confusa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea confusa has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, strap-like, and often have a waxy surface, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a bluish tinge and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem with a smooth, greyish bark that remains underground, forming a pseudobulb. It produces inflorescences with colorful, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by birds or insects.

The fruit is a capsule that contains small seeds dispersed by wind or water.
