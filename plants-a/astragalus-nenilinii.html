Astragalus nenilinii

Astragalus nenilinii, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. Astragalus nenilinii is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
