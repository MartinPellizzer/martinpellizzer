Anthemis ruthenica

Anthemis ruthenica, commonly known as the Crimean tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are aromatic and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and digestive properties.
