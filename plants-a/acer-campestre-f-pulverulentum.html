Acer campestre f. pulverulentum

Acer campestre f. pulverulentum, commonly known as the powdered maple, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the maple family, Aceraceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, finely textured leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that disperse its seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
