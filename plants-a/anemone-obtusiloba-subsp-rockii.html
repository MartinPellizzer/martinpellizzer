Anemone obtusiloba subsp. rockii

Anemone obtusiloba subsp.

rockii, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a wavy margin and a hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
