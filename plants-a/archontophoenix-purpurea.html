Archontophoenix purpurea

Archontophoenix purpurea, commonly known as the purple fan palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty in landscaping.

The species is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to thrive.
