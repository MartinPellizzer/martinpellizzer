Arabis sagittata var. ovata

Arabis sagittata var. ovata, commonly known as the broad-leaved rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, white, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine or coastal environments due to its tolerance for harsh conditions.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky gardens.
