Acacia clarksoniana

Acacia clarksoniana, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for native pollinators.

Acacia clarksoniana is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
