Achimenes boeckmannii

Achimenes boeckmannii, commonly known as the Boeckmann's flame flower, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Achimenes boeckmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Morphology

Achimenes boeckmannii has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The stem is upright and slightly woody, with a smooth, reddish-brown bark that becomes more rugged with age. Its flowers are tubular with a distinctive crown-like structure, displaying vibrant red and orange hues, blooming from late spring to early autumn, and are pollinated primarily by bees. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and tuberous, allowing the plant to store nutrients and survive periods of drought.
