Anoda pubescens

Anoda pubescens, commonly known as the hairy amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Anoda pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
