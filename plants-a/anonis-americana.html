Anonis americana

Anonis americana, commonly known as the American persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its alternate, simple, elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange to red berries in the fall. The fruit is edible when fully ripe and has a sweet, honey-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for wildlife.
