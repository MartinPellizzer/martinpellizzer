Acacia balbisii

Acacia balbisii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia balbisii is also valued for its hard wood and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
