Achillea asiatica var. intermedia

Achillea asiatica var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate yarrow, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a gray-green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
