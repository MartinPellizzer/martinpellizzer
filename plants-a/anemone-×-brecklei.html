Anemone × brecklei

Anemone × brecklei, commonly known as the Breckle's anemone, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy beneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Anemone × brecklei is valued for its early spring blooms and its ability to naturalize over time in suitable conditions.
