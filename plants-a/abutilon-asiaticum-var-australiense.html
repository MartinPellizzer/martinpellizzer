Abutilon asiaticum var. australiense

Abutilon asiaticum var. australiense, commonly known as the Australian velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
