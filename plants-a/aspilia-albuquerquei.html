Aspilia albuquerquei

Aspilia albuquerquei, commonly known as the Albuquerque lopstick, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Aspilia albuquerquei is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
