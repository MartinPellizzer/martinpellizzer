Acer orientale f. ovale

Acer orientale f. ovale, commonly known as the oval-leaf maple, is a decorative ornamental tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its attractive foliage and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
