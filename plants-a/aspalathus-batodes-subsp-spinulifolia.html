Aspalathus batodes subsp. spinulifolia

Aspalathus batodes subsp.

spinulifolia, commonly known as the serrated bushveld clover, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers in spikes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats. It is adapted to semi-arid conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
