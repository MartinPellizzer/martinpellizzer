Acacia nilotica subsp. adstringens

Acacia nilotica subsp.

adstringens, commonly known as the Egyptian acacia or gum acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets.

The plant is renowned for producing a rich, golden gum known as gum arabic, which is harvested from its pods. Its flowers are typically yellow or pale green, arranged in cylindrical inflorescences.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
