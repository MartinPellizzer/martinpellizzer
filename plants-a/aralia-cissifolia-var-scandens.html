Aralia cissifolia var. scandens

Aralia cissifolia var. scandens, commonly known as climbing aralia, is a varietal form of the aralia plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, flexible stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant features palmately compound leaves that are typically lobed and have a feathery appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces berries that range from yellow to red in color.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.
