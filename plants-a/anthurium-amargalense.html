Anthurium amargalense

Anthurium amargalense, commonly known as the Amargalense arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces vibrant, spatulate-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are typically borne on a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium amargalense is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
