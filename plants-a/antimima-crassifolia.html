Antimima crassifolia

Antimima crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved antimima, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, a feature typical of xerophytic plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its robust root system allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand periods of drought.

Antimima crassifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
