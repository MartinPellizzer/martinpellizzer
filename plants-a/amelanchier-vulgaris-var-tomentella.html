Amelanchier vulgaris var. tomentella

Amelanchier vulgaris var. tomentella, commonly known as the smooth serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, dark blue berries in the summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated edge, and they turn a vibrant yellow or red in the fall.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat benefits.
