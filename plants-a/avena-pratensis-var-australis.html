Avena pratensis var. australis

Avena pratensis var. australis, commonly known as the Australian oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its forage quality and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
