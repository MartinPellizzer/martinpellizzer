Abelia serrata

Abelia serrata, commonly known as the serrated abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Abelia serrata prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
