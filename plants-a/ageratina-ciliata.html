Ageratina ciliata

Ageratina ciliata, commonly known as the silky ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Ageratina ciliata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
