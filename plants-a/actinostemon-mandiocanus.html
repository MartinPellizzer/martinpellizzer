Actinostemon mandiocanus

Actinostemon mandiocanus, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

The plant is also valued for its starchy roots, which are used as a food source in some regions.
