Astragalus himachalensis

Astragalus himachalensis, commonly known as the Himachal bursaria, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of northern India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
