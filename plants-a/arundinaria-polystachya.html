Arundinaria polystachya

Arundinaria polystachya, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species.

Arundinaria polystachya is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
