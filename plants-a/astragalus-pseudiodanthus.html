Astragalus pseudiodanthus

Astragalus pseudiodanthus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardiness.
