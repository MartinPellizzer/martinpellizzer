Actinocephalus fimbriatus

Actinocephalus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed glasswort, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dune environments.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
