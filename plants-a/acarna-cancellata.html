Acarna cancellata

Acarna cancellata, commonly known as the spotted arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Acarna cancellata is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
