Asplenium trichomanes f. delicatulum

Asplenium trichomanes f. delicatulum, commonly known as the delicate maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to regions of Europe, particularly in the Mediterranean area. The plant features a compact growth habit with slender, arching leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

The fronds are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the leaves.
