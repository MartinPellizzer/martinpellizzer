Aira parviflora

Aira parviflora, commonly known as slender false wheat, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, pale blue or greenish flowers that bloom in early summer.

Aira parviflora is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
