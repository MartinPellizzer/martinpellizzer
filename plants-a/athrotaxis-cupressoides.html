Athrotaxis cupressoides

Athrotaxis cupressoides, commonly known as the Tasmanian pillar tree, is a unique conifer native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
