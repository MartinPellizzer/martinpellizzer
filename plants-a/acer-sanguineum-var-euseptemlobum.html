Acer sanguineum var. euseptemlobum

Acer sanguineum var. euseptemlobum, commonly known as the seven-lobed maple, is a variant of the maple tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with seven lobes, which are typically bright green during the growing season and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and unique leaf structure.
