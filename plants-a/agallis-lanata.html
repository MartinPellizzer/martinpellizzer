Agallis lanata

Agallis lanata, commonly known as woolly bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. Agallis lanata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
