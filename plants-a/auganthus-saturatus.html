Auganthus saturatus

Auganthus saturatus, commonly known as the bluebell of the south, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Auganthus saturatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to Mediterranean climates.
