Athyrium brachysoroides

Athyrium brachysoroides, commonly known as the broad-leafed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, velvety layer of brownish scales.

The fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, arching growth habit.
