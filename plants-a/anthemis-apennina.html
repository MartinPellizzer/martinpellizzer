Anthemis apennina

Anthemis apennina, commonly known as the Italian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its foliage is aromatic and covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive scent.

Anthemis apennina is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal medicine.
