Acacia tomentella

Acacia tomentella, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking contrast to its hairy leaves. Acacia tomentella is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
