Allium ledebourianum

Allium ledebourianum, commonly known as the narrowleaf garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of the Caucasus and Central Asia. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical bulbs that are clustered at the base. Its flowers are typically pale blue to purple, forming loose clusters at the top of the stems.

Allium ledebourianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
