Acerates viridiflora var. ivesii

Acerates viridiflora var. ivesii, commonly known as the Ives' green-flowered acerates, is a unique variant of the acerates genus.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-white flowers, which contrast with its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest regions. Its foliage turns a vibrant yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and sought-after species in botanical gardens.
