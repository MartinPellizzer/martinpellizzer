Astragalus marivanensis

Astragalus marivanensis, commonly known as Marivan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain small, dark seeds.

Astragalus marivanensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
