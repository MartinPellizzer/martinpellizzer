Apargia coronopifolia

Apargia coronopifolia, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with white fringes. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Apargia coronopifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beautiful blooms.
