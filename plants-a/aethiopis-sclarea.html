Aethiopis sclarea

Aethiopis sclarea, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Aethiopis sclarea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
