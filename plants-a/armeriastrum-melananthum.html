Armeriastrum melananthum

Armeriastrum melananthum, commonly known as blackseed armeria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
