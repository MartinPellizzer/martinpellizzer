Aconogonon Savatieri Var. Iturupense

Iturup aconogonon, scientifically known as Aconogonon savatieri var. iturupense, is a rare flowering plant native to the Russian Far East. This species belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is found primarily on the island of Iturup. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique botanical gem.
