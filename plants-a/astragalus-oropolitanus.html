Astragalus oropolitanus

Astragalus oropolitanus, commonly known as the Oropian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces pods that contain small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus oropolitanus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
