Asplenium caudatum var. sectum

Asplenium caudatum var. sectum, commonly known as the split fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided, and often split fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a delicate, feathery appearance.

It is a perennial plant that reproduces through spores, typical of the fern family.
