Agropyron kuramense

Agropyron kuramense, commonly known as Kuram grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey and northern Iran.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle.

Agropyron kuramense is valued for its potential in erosion control and as a forage plant in semi-arid regions.
