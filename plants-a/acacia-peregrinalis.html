Acacia peregrinalis

Acacia peregrinalis, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia peregrinalis is often found in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
