Aster lowrieanus var. lancifolius

Aster lowrieanus var. lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its showy, blue to violet flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
