Asplenium fuliginosum

Asplenium fuliginosum, commonly known as the sooty spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically clustered in dense, compact rosettes. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Asplenium fuliginosum is known for its resilience and ability to colonize various substrates, including bark and rock surfaces.
