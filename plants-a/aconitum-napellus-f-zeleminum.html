Aconitum napellus f. zeleminum

Aconitum napellus f. zeleminum, commonly known as the Zeleminum monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features a stout, erect stem with large, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in woodland or alpine environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
