Anthyllis spruneri var. bulgarica

Anthyllis spruneri var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.
