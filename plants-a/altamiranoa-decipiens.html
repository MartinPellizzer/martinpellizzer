Altamiranoa decipiens

Altamiranoa decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly beautiful flowers that resemble those of other orchid species, which may aid in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the understory of forests. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

The orchid's unique morphology and adaptive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
