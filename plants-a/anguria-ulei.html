Anguria ulei

Anguria ulei, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant produces berries that start green and mature to a deep black or red, often containing toxic compounds such as solanine. Native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and Africa, it thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in gardens and wastelands.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed and requires caution when handling.
