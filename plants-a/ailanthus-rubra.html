Ailanthus rubra

Ailanthus rubra, commonly known as the red tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive reddish hue during the growing season. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into winged fruits, aiding in wind pollination. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is highly adaptable to different environmental conditions.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often planted in urban landscapes.
