Acacia flava var. atacorensis

Acacia flava var. atacorensis, commonly known as the Atacorensis Acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is distinguished by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many Acacia species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry savannas and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for soil stabilization.
