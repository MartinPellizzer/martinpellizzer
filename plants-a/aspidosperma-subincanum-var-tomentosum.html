Aspidosperma subincanum var. tomentosum

Aspidosperma subincanum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
