Aechmea viridis

Aechmea viridis, commonly known as the green bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered in a waxy layer that helps reduce water loss. It produces striking inflorescences with vibrant, long-lasting flowers that range from yellow to red.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.

Morphology

Aechmea viridis has leaves that are strap-like, narrow, and slightly curved, arranged in a rosette formation, with a bright green color and a waxy texture.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, thick base that supports the rosette of leaves. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a colorful bract, and typically bloom in late spring to early summer, pollinated by insects such as bees. The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals that eat the fruit.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant while absorbing moisture from the surrounding environment.
