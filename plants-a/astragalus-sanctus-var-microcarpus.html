Astragalus sanctus var. microcarpus

Astragalus sanctus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited holy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
