Aegiphila peruviana

Aegiphila peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian angel's trumpets, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. Its flowers are tubular and typically violet or purple, blooming in clusters.

Aegiphila peruviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
