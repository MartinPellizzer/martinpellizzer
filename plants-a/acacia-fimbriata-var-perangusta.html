Acacia fimbriata var. perangusta

Acacia fimbriata var. perangusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
