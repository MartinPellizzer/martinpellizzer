Amaryllis fusca

Amaryllis fusca, commonly known as the dark amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces broad, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis fusca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
