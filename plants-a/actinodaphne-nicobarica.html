Actinodaphne nicobarica

Actinodaphne nicobarica, commonly known as the Nicobar laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
