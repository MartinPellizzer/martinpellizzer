Acetosa hastiformis

Acetosa hastiformis, commonly known as the lanceleaf sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its succulent leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a slightly serrated edge and contain oxalic acid, which gives them a tangy flavor.

Acetosa hastiformis is often used in herbal medicine for its potential diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
