Abies alba f. columnaris

Abies alba f. columnaris, commonly known as the columnar white fir, is a unique form of the European white fir characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

This variety exhibits a dense, conical shape with closely spaced, needle-like leaves that give it a compact appearance. The foliage is dark green, often with a slight blue or silver tinge, enhancing its ornamental value. It produces small, spherical cones that are typically retained on the branches for an extended period.

This plant is well-suited for small gardens or as a specimen tree due to its controlled size and attractive form.
