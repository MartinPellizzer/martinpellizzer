Adiantum borneense

Adiantum borneense, commonly known as the Bornean maidenhair fern, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Adiantum borneense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
