Anguina

Anguina, commonly known as the common brome, is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Anguina is typically found in open fields, meadows, and disturbed soils across temperate regions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for grazing animals.
