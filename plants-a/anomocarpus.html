Anomocarpus

Anomocarpus, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of leaves and their showy, dandelion-like flowers that are often yellow or orange in color. Anomocarpus species are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in dry, sandy soils. They produce small, dry fruits called cypsela, which are dispersed by wind, similar to dandelions.

Some species are used in traditional medicine, while others are considered invasive in certain regions.
