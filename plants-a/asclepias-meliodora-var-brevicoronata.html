Asclepias meliodora var. brevicoronata

Asclepias meliodora var. brevicoronata, commonly known as the narrow-capped milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of greenish-white flowers with distinctive, hooded structures called galeae. It is a host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing essential nourishment for both larvae and adult butterflies.

Its fibrous roots make it adaptable to various soil types and drought conditions.
