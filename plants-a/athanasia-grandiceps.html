Athanasia grandiceps

Athanasia grandiceps, commonly known as the Natal daisy, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
