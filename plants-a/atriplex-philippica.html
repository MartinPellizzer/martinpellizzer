Atriplex philippica

Atriplex philippica, commonly known as the Philippine saltbush, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid and saline environments. The plant belongs to the genus Atriplex, which is part of the amaranth family, Amaranthaceae. It typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its tolerance to high salt concentrations.

The small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
