Aquilaria baillonii, commonly known as the Indian agarwood or fragrant sandalwood, is a member of the Thymelaeaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly India and Indonesia. Aquilaria baillonii plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is also threatened by overharvesting.
