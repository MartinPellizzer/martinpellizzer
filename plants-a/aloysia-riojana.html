Aloysia riojana

Aloysia riojana, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy spikes of tubular flowers, typically in shades of purple or blue, which bloom throughout the growing season. Aloysia riojana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are aromatic and can be used to make essential oils.
