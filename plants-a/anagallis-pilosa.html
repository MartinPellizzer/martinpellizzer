Anagallis pilosa

Anagallis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in dense spikes, usually in shades of pink or white.

Anagallis pilosa is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
