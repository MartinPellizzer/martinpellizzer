Angraecoides triangulifolia

Angraecoides triangulifolia, commonly known as the triangular leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its unique morphology and nocturnal blooming habit make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
