Aeschynomene mucronulata

Aeschynomene mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aeschynomene, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, narrow, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in loose spikes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
