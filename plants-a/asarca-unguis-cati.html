Asarca unguis-cati

Asarca unguis-cati, commonly known as the cat's claw plant, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its name derives from the resemblance of its curved fruits to a cat's claw.
