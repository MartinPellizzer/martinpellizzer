Aster caubulicus

Aster caubulicus, commonly known as the Kashmiri aster, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple or blue petals, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Aster caubulicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
