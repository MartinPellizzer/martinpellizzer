Astragalus pseudopellitus

Astragalus pseudopellitus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
