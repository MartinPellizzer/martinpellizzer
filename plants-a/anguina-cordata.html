Anguina cordata

Anguina cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, purple to blue flowers. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Anguina cordata is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
