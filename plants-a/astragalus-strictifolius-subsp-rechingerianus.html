Astragalus strictifolius subsp. rechingerianus

Astragalus strictifolius subsp.

rechingerianus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features slender, upright stems with small, compound leaves that are often densely arranged. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, clustered in small heads, and bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
