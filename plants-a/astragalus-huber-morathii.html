Astragalus huber-morathii

Astragalus huber-morathii, commonly known as Huber-Morath's milkvetch, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Astragalus huber-morathii is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
