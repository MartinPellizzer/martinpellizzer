Albuca roodeae

Albuca roodeae, commonly known as the Red River albuca, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues, adding to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces tall, erect stems with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is valued in both horticultural and botanical collections.
