Abutilon thyrsodendron

Abutilon thyrsodendron, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, flat seeds.

Abutilon thyrsodendron is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
