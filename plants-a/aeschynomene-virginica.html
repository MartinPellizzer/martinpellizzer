Aeschynomene virginica

Aeschynomene virginica, commonly known as Virginia stylo, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by cylindrical, seed-containing pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Aeschynomene virginica is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
