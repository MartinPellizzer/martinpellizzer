Aspidium prenticei

Aspidium prenticei, commonly known as the Prentice's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches. Its rhizomes form dense mats on the water's surface, providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

The fronds are usually bright green and have a feathery appearance, contributing to its aesthetic value in wetland ecosystems.
