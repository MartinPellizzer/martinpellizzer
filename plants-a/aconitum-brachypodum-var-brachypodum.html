Aconitum brachypodum var. brachypodum

Aconitum brachypodum var. brachypodum, commonly known as the shortroot monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
