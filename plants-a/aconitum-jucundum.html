Aconitum jucundum

Aconitum jucundum, commonly known as the happy aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes that give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Aconitum jucundum is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.
