Arthraxon microphyllus var. lancifolius

Arthraxon microphyllus var. lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in grassland and wetland habitats.
