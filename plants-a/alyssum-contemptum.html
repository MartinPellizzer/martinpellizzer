Alyssum contemptum

Alyssum contemptum, commonly known as the narrowleaf alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has slender, branching stems and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry grasslands.

Alyssum contemptum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
