Attalea × salvadorensis

Attalea × salvadorensis, commonly known as the hybrid palm, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Attalea litoralis and Attalea maripa.

It is native to coastal regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Paraguay. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The inflorescences are typically found at the top of the plant, with both male and female flowers present on the same inflorescence.

It is often found in wetland and marshy habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
