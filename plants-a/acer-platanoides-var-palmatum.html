Acer platanoides var. palmatum

Acer platanoides var. palmatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The plant has a compact, rounded growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

Its foliage provides year-round visual interest, especially in the spring and fall.
