Aristolochia bracteata var. integrifolia

Aristolochia bracteata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with smooth, entire margins, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
