Acalypha reticulata var. meiodonta

Acalypha reticulata var. meiodonta, commonly known as the silver nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
