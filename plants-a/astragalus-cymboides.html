Astragalus cymboides

Astragalus cymboides, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Astragalus cymboides is also noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
