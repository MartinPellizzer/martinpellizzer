Amygdalus zabulica

Amygdalus zabulica, commonly known as the wild almond or Persian almond, is a species of shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, hard pods that contain edible seeds, which are similar in taste to common almonds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Amygdalus zabulica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
