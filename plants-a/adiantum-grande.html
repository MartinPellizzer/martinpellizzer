Adiantum grande

Adiantum grande, commonly known as the giant maidenhair fern, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically bioluminescent in the dark. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky substrates.

Adiantum grande is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
