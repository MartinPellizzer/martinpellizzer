Anemone ranunculoides subsp. uralensis

Anemone ranunculoides subsp.

uralensis, commonly known as the Ural anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Eurasia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features palmately divided, bright green leaves and produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with numerous stamens. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.

The subspecies uralensis is distinguished by its slightly larger size and more robust growth compared to the typical form of A. ranunculoides.
