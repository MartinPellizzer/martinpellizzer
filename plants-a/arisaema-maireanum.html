Arisaema maireanum

Arisaema maireanum, commonly known as the Maire's arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are large, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
