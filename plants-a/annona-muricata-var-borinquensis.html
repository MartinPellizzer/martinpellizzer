Annona muricata var. borinquensis

Annona muricata var. borinquensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or red when mature, with a thick, spiky rind. The flesh is creamy and sweet, containing numerous small, inedible seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in local cuisines.
