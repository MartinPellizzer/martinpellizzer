Agave subfalcata

Agave subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like growth habit and long, narrow, blue-gray leaves that are often lined with sharp spines. The plant produces an inflorescence that can reach several meters in height, bearing yellow flowers that bloom once in its lifetime. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a notable ornamental plant in desert gardens.
