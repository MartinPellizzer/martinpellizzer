Asarum sieboldii var. mandshuricum

Asarum sieboldii var. mandshuricum, commonly known as Siberian ginger or Mandshurian asarum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic root that is used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
