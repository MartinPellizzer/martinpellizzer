Amelanchier embergeri

Amelanchier embergeri, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet and features a rounded, multi-stemmed form. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, bright red berries in the summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and they turn yellow in the autumn.

Amelanchier embergeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
