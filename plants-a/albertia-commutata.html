Albertia commutata

Albertia commutata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, spiky inflorescence composed of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Albertia commutata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
