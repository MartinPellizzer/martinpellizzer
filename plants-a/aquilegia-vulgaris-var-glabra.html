Aquilegia vulgaris var. glabra

Aquilegia vulgaris var. glabra, commonly known as the common columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are usually glabrous, or smooth, as indicated by the variety name *glabra*. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in woodland edges and meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
