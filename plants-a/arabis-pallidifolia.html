Arabis pallidifolia

Arabis pallidifolia, commonly known as the paleleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, rounded seed pods.

Its leaves are usually pale green and have a distinctive, lobed appearance.
