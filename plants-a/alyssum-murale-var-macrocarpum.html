Alyssum murale var. macrocarpum

Alyssum murale var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and linear, aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
