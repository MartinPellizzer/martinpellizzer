Arytera samoensis

Arytera samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan arum, is a flowering plant native to the island of Samoa in the Pacific Ocean.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinct odor that attracts pollinators such as flies and beetles.

Arytera samoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in traditional Samoan practices.
