Argythamnia buettneriacea

Argythamnia buettneriacea, commonly known as the desert sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub with silver-gray foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water deep in the soil.

Argythamnia buettneriacea is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
