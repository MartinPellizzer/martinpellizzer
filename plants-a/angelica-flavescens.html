Angelica flavescens

Angelica flavescens, commonly known as the yellow angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves and yellow-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are aromatic and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Angelica flavescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
