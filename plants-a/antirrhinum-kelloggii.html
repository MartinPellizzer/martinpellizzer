Antirrhinum kelloggii

Antirrhinum kelloggii, commonly known as the Kellogg's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces seed pods that split open when mature.

Antirrhinum kelloggii is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
