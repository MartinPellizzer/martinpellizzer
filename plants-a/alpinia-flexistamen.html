Alpinia flexistamen

Alpinia flexistamen, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or Chinese ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, herbaceous stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers with prominent stamens, which give it its specific epithet "flexistamen.". Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Alpinia flexistamen thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
