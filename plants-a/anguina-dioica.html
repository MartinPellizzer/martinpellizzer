Anguina dioica

Anguina dioica, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, often bitter scent.

Anguina dioica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
