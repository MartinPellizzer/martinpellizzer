Acioa longipendula

Acioa longipendula, commonly known as the hanging acioa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Acioa longipendula is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
