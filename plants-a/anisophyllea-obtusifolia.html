Anisophyllea obtusifolia

Anisophyllea obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved anisophyllea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique leaf morphology and ornamental fruits make it a notable species in botanical collections.
