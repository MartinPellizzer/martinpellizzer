Asperula pseudochlorantha var. antalyensis

Asperula pseudochlorantha var. antalyensis, commonly known as the Antalya azalea, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Turkey.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green foliage that provides a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
