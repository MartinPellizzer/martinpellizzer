Acalypha silvestri

Acalypha silvestri, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, red or pink bract-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
