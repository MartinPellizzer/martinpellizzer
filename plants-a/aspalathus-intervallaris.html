Aspalathus intervallaris

Aspalathus intervallaris, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspalathus intervallaris is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
