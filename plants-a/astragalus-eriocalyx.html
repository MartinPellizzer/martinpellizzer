Astragalus eriocalyx

Astragalus eriocalyx, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus eriocalyx plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
