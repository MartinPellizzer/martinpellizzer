Ardisia meridensis

Ardisia meridensis, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by clusters of bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Ardisia meridensis is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
