Anacamptis pyramidalis f. sanguinea

Anacamptis pyramidalis f. sanguinea, commonly known as the blood-red orchid, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its striking red and pink flower spikes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Europe, particularly Italy and the Iberian Peninsula. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive red hue, with a subtle fragrance.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
