Aloe gracilis

Aloe gracilis, commonly known as the slender aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, upright leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with white teeth along the edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves.

Aloe gracilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
