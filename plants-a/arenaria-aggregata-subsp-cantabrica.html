Arenaria aggregata subsp. cantabrica

Arenaria aggregata subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of northern Spain and southern France. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in dunes and rocky coastal habitats.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh coastal conditions, showcasing resilience to salt spray and strong winds.
