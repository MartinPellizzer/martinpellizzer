Anthemis tinctoria var. fussii

Anthemis tinctoria var. fussii, commonly known as the yellow fleur-de-lis, is a variant of the tansy plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a prominent central disk. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often emitting a strong, camphor-like scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dyeing textiles.
