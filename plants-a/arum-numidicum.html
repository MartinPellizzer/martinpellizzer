Arum numidicum

Arum numidicum, commonly known as the Algerian arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of purple or green. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
