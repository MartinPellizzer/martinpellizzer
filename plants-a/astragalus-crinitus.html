Astragalus crinitus

Astragalus crinitus, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus crinitus is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
