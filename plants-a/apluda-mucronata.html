Apluda mucronata

Apluda mucronata, commonly known as the spike grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Apluda mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
