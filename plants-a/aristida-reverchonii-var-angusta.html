Aristida reverchonii var. angusta

Aristida reverchonii var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
