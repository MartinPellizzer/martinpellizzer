Austromyrtus aphthosa

Austromyrtus aphthosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed, contributing to its ecological and ornamental significance.
