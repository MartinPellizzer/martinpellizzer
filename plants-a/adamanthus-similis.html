Adamanthus similis

Adamanthus similis, commonly known as the Brazilian holly, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a fleshy, red drupe that contains a single seed.

Adamanthus similis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
