Acalypha × malawiensis

Acalypha × malawiensis, commonly known as the Malawi spurflower, is a hybrid shrub native to the tropical regions of Eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to orange, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
