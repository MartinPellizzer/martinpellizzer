Amaryllis × roseoalba

Amaryllis × roseoalba, commonly known as the roseate amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papillaris.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers with white or pale pink markings, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically bright green and can grow up to 12 inches in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
