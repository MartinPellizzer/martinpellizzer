Acacia kinionge

Acacia kinionge, commonly known as the Kenya acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned branches and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia kinionge is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has traditional uses in some communities.
