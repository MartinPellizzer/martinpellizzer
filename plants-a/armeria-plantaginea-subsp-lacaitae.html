Armeria plantaginea subsp. lacaitae

Armeria plantaginea subsp.

lacaitae, commonly known as the pink sandverbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California. It features dense clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in dunes and coastal scrub habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
