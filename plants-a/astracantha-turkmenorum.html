Astracantha turkmenorum

Astracantha turkmenorum, commonly known as Turkmen Desert Honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly Turkmenistan.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as thick, fleshy leaves to reduce water loss. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Astracantha turkmenorum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
