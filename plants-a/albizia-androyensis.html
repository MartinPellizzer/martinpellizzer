Albizia androyensis

Albizia androyensis, commonly known as the Madagascar silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are bipinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Albizia androyensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in its native region.
