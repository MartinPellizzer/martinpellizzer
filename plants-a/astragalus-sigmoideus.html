Astragalus sigmoideus

Astragalus sigmoideus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as prairies and roadsides. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus sigmoideus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
