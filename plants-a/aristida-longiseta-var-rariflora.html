Aristida longiseta var. rariflora

Aristida longiseta var. rariflora, commonly known as the rare-flowered three-awn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically clustered in dense, compact spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
