Amygdalus texana

Amygdalus texana, commonly known as the Texas wild almond, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are encased in a hard, woody shell. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Amygdalus texana is adapted to arid and rocky soils, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
