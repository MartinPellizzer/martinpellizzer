Astragalus montis-parrowii

Astragalus montis-parrowii, commonly known as Parrow's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus montis-parrowii is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
