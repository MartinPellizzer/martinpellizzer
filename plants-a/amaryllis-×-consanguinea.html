Amaryllis × consanguinea

Amaryllis × consanguinea, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisiaca.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically produces strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and fleshy. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Amaryllis × consanguinea is popular as a ornamental plant, especially during the holiday season.
