Allium macedonicum

Allium macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian leek, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem bearing umbels of white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in local cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
