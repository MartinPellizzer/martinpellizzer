Arabis petraea var. faeroensis

Arabis petraea var. faeroensis, commonly known as the Faeroese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Faroe Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is adapted to harsh maritime conditions and is considered a characteristic plant of the region's alpine flora.
