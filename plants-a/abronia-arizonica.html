Abronia arizonica

Abronia arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.
