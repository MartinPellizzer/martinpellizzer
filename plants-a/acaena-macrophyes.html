Acaena macrophyes

Acaena macrophyes, commonly known as New Zealand bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its prickly stems and small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces burrs with hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

Acaena macrophyes thrives in a variety of habitats, including forest margins and rocky areas, and is adapted to the temperate climate of New Zealand.
