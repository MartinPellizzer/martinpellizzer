Artemisia skorniakovii

Artemisia skorniakovii, commonly known as Skorniakov's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
