Agastache lophanthus

Agastache lophanthus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, blue-violet tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Agastache lophanthus is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun.
