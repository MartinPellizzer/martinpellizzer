Astragalus agrestis var. bracteatus

Astragalus agrestis var. bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This variety is often found in steppe and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
