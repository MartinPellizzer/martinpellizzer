Alyxia villilimba

Alyxia villilimba, commonly known as the silky alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and forest edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a soft, silky texture, giving it its common name.

Alyxia villilimba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
