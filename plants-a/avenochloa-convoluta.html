Avenochloa convoluta

Avenochloa convoluta, commonly known as wild barley, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Avenochloa convoluta is an important component of native grassland ecosystems and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
