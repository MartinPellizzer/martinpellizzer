Astragalus sphaerophysa var. lehmannianus

Astragalus sphaerophysa var. lehmannianus, commonly known as Lehmann's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves with stipules. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and soil-stabilizing properties.
