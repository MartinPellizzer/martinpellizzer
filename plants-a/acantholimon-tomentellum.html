Acantholimon tomentellum

Acantholimon tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
