Aristolochia cymbifera

Aristolochia cymbifera, commonly known as the broad-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, with a distinctive shape and coloration. The plant has heart-shaped, veined leaves that are often large and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is also notable for its complex floral structure and its role in supporting certain pollinators.
