Aristida laxa var. karwinskiana

Aristida laxa var. karwinskiana, commonly known as the karwinskiana grass, is a perennial bunchgrass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, wiry stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, finger-like structures bearing flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
