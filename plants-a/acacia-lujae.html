Acacia lujae

Acacia lujae, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a unique flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, thorn-like leaves and yellow, spherical flower heads. The plant is adapted to arid environments, showcasing deep root systems and drought-resistant features. It plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various local wildlife.

Its ecological and botanical significance makes it a subject of scientific interest and conservation efforts.
