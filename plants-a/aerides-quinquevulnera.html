Aerides quinquevulnera

Aerides quinquevulnera, commonly known as the five-wound orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit and produces clustered, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and has a unique floral structure with five sepals and petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
