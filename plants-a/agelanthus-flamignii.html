Agelanthus flamignii

Agelanthus flamignii, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its striking, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and sandy landscapes.

Agelanthus flamignii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
