Asplenium pseudoangustum

Asplenium pseudoangustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rock crevices or forest floors, where it can thrive in low-light conditions. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

Asplenium pseudoangustum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
