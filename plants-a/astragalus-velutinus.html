Astragalus velutinus

Astragalus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several round, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Astragalus velutinus is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and shrub steppe habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
