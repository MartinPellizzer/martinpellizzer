Ambrosia artemisiifolia f. gracilissima

Ambrosia artemisiifolia f. gracilissima, commonly known as the common ragweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant is native to North America but has been naturalized in many parts of the world. It is an annual herb that grows up to 1-2 meters tall, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its pollen is a significant allergen, contributing to seasonal allergies in many people.
