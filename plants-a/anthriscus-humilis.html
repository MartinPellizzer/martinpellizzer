Anthriscus humilis

Anthriscus humilis, commonly known as small angelica, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and small, white or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in herbal medicine for their mild aromatic properties.

Anthriscus humilis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
