Alsine pubera

Alsine pubera, commonly known as hairy alsike, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Alsine pubera is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
