Acanthus flexicaulis

Acanthus flexicaulis, commonly known as the flexible spiny acanthus, is a striking perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, palmately lobed leaves that have a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces showy, spiny bracts that enclose clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

Acanthus flexicaulis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with full sun.
