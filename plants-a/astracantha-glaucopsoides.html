Astracantha glaucopsoides

Astracantha glaucopsoides, commonly known as the blue-flowered aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Astracantha glaucopsoides is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
