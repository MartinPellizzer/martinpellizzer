Arabis angustifolia

Arabis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is often found in alpine or coastal environments.
