Apostasia

Apostasia, commonly known as the snake's head orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique inflorescence, which resembles a snake's head, hence their common name. They are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. Apostasia species typically have simple, grass-like leaves and produce small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green.

They are often grown as ornamental plants due to their distinctive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
