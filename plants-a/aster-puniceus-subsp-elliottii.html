Aster puniceus subsp. elliottii

Aster puniceus subsp.

elliottii, commonly known as the Elliott's fire aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
