Amaryllis subbarbata

Amaryllis subbarbata, commonly known as the rock amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe along the throat. The plant produces a tall, erect stem with broad, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Amaryllis subbarbata is known for its winter blooming habit, adding color to gardens during the colder months.
