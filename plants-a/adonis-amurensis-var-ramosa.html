Adonis amurensis var. ramosa

Adonis amurensis var. ramosa, commonly known as the Amur bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
