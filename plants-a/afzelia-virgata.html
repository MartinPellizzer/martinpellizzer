Afzelia virgata

Afzelia virgata, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or white, followed by woody capsules containing several seeds.

Afzelia virgata is often found in lowland rainforests and is an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
