Areca torulo

Areca torulo, commonly known as the Torul palm, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Areca torulo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
