Atriplex transandina

Atriplex transandina, commonly known as the Andean saltbush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Chile and Argentina.

It is adapted to arid and saline environments, showcasing its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions. The plant has succulent leaves that help it conserve water, and it produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. It is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert and semi-arid ecosystems.
