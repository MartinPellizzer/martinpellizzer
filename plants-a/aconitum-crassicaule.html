Aconitum crassicaule

Aconitum crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-leaved aconite, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and deeply lobed, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Aconitum crassicaule is also noted for containing potent alkaloids, making it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potentially toxic species.
