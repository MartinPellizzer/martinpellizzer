Allium tubiflorum

Allium tubiflorum, commonly known as the bulbous garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulb composed of numerous small, rounded cloves, which are similar in appearance to garlic but have a milder flavor. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its botanical classification places it in the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other bulbous plants.

Allium tubiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
