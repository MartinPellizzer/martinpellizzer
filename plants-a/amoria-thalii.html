Amoria thalii

Amoria thalii, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-blue foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Amoria thalii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
