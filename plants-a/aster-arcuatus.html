Aster arcuatus

Aster arcuatus, commonly known as the curved aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in shades of blue to purple.

Aster arcuatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
