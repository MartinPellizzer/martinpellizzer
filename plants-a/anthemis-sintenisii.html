Anthemis sintenisii

Anthemis sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenisi's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
