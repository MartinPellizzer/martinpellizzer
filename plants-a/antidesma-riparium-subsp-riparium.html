Antidesma riparium subsp. riparium

Antidesma riparium subsp.

riparium, commonly known as the river antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa. It typically grows along riverbanks and moist habitats, where it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are often red or orange when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local communities.
