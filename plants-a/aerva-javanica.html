Aerva javanica

Aerva javanica, commonly known as Java grass or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
