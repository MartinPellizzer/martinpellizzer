Alchemilla pedata var. argentea

Alchemilla pedata var. argentea, commonly known as silver lungwort, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-white, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its delicate blooms.
