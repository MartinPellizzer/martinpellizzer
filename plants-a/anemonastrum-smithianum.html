Anemonastrum smithianum

Anemonastrum smithianum, commonly known as the Smith's anemone, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anemonastrum smithianum is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species in its native habitat.
