Anthemis kochiana

Anthemis kochiana, commonly known as the alpine everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers that are typically arranged in a flat, disc-like structure. It is well adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in rocky soils and cold climates.

Anthemis kochiana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
