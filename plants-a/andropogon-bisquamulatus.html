Andropogon bisquamulatus

Andropogon bisquamulatus, commonly known as the broadleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Andropogon bisquamulatus is an important component of native grassland ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
