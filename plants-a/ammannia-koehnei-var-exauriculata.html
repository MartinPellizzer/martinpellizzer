Ammannia koehnei var. exauriculata

Ammannia koehnei var. exauriculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ammannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features lanceolate to narrowly ovate leaves that are often slightly hairy, with a distinctive reddish or purplish coloration on the stems and undersides of the leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
