Aeschynomene rogersii

Aeschynomene rogersii, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems and palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, blue to violet flowers appear in spikes during the summer months.

Aeschynomene rogersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
