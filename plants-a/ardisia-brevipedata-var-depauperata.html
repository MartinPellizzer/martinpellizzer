Ardisia brevipedata var. depauperata

Ardisia brevipedata var. depauperata, commonly known as the sparse-leaved evergreen ardisia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the plant. The flowers are white and clustered in umbels, followed by small, bright red berries that are edible. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and year-round foliage.
