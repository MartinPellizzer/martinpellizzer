Agropyron pungens var. pycnanthum

Agropyron pungens var. pycnanthum, commonly known as the prickly wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous short, awned spikelets. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for dryland restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important species for stabilizing soils and supporting wildlife in arid environments.
