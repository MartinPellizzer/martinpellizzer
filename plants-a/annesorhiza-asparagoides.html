Annesorhiza asparagoides

Annesorhiza asparagoides, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, clumping habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
