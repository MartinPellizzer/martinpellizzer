Anemia rutifolia

Anemia rutifolia, commonly known as sourwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. Its botanical features include smooth, gray bark and a preference for moist, acidic soils.

Anemia rutifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
