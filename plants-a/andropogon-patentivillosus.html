Andropogon patentivillosus

Andropogon patentivillosus, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a tall grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, inconspicuous spikelets that are clustered in dense panicles.

Andropogon patentivillosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
