Astragalus sempervirens subsp. andres-molinae

Astragalus sempervirens subsp.

andres-molinae, commonly known as the Andes mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
