Aglaonema emarginatum

Aglaonema emarginatum, commonly known as the Marginatum Arum, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with a mix of green, white, and cream colors. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that enhances its ornamental appeal. It thrives in low-light conditions and requires moderate humidity to maintain its vibrant appearance.

This plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively easy care requirements.
