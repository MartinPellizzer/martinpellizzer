Astragalus heinzianus

Astragalus heinzianus, commonly known as Heinz's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
