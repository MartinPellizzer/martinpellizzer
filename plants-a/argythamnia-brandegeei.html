Argythamnia brandegeei

Argythamnia brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
