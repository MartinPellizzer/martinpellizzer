Andromeda bracteosa var. trichoclada

Andromeda bracteosa var. trichoclada, commonly known as the hairy andromeda, is a variant of the evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive hairy texture on both the leaves and stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
