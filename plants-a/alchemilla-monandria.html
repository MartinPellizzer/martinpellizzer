Alchemilla monandria

Alchemilla monandria, commonly known as the lady's mantle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Alchemilla monandria is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
