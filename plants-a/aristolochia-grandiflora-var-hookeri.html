Aristolochia grandiflora var. hookeri

Aristolochia grandiflora var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, sweet scent. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and the plant contains various bioactive compounds.

It is valued in traditional medicine but requires careful handling due to its potential toxicity.
