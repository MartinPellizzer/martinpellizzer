Abutilon mourae

Abutilon mourae, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, velvety texture.

Abutilon mourae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
