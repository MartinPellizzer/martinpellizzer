Astragalus onobrychis

Astragalus onobrychis, commonly known as sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or violet pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural and ecological contexts. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Sainfoin is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
