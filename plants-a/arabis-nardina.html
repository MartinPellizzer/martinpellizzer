Arabis nardina

Arabis nardina, commonly known as narrowleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Arabis nardina typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
