Acer sanguineum var. amoenum

Acer sanguineum var. amoenum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental, often finely divided, trifoliate leaves that display vibrant colors throughout the seasons. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal foliage make it a favored plant for landscape design.
