Allium oleraceum subsp. complanatum

Allium oleraceum subsp.

complanatum, commonly known as the flat-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, flat, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in umbels.

The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its strong garlic-like odor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other Allium species.
