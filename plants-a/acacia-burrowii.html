Acacia burrowii

Acacia burrowii, commonly known as the burrowed acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and its yellow, globular flower heads. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Acacia burrowii is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert animals and insects.
