Aster armeriodes

Aster armeriodes, commonly known as the rock aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces showy blue to violet flowers in the summer. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Aster armeriodes is adapted to harsh conditions and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
