Astragalus chrysostachys var. khorasanicus

Astragalus chrysostachys var. khorasanicus, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in dense, racemose inflorescences. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to thrive in poor soils and its importance to local pollinators.
