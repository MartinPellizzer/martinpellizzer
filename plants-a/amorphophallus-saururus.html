Amorphophallus saururus

Amorphophallus saururus, commonly known as the giant swamp arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves and a short, stout stem that often remains underground. The plant produces a striking, spadix-like inflorescence that can reach several feet in height, often accompanied by a strong, unpleasant odor to attract pollinators. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swamps or along riverbanks.

The tuberous roots of this plant are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source in certain cultures.
