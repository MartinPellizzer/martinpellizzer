Asarum nazeanum

Asarum nazeanum, commonly known as the Persian wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, dark brown berries that are dispersed by birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
