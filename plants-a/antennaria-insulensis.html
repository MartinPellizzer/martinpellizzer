Antennaria insulensis

Antennaria insulensis, commonly known as the island mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, hairy leaves and white to pale pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Antennaria insulensis is adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of its native habitat.
