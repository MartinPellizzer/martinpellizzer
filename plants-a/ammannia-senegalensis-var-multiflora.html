Ammannia senegalensis var. multiflora

Ammannia senegalensis var. multiflora, commonly known as redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red or pink roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
