Alyssum macropodum f. genuinum

Alyssum macropodum f. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive blooms and ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained conditions.

The variety f. genuinum is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of A. macropodum.
