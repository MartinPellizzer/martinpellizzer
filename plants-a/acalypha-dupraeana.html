Acalypha dupraeana

Acalypha dupraeana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often reddish or purple-veined leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Acalypha dupraeana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
