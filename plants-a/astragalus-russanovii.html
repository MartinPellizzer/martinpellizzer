Astragalus russanovii

Astragalus russanovii, commonly known as Russian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus russanovii plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
