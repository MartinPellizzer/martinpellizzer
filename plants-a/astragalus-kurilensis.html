Astragalus kurilensis

Astragalus kurilensis, commonly known as Kuril milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and the northern Pacific islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems, though it is not widely cultivated.
