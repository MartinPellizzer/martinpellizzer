Alchemilla turkulensis

Alchemilla turkulensis, commonly known as the Turcian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of softly hairy, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, upright inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Alchemilla turkulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
