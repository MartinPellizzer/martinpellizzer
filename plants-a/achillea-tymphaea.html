Achillea tymphaea

Achillea tymphaea, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white to pinkish, and arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the genus Achillea.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Morphology

Achillea tymphaea has basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture, arranged in a rosette formation.

The stem is upright, slender, and covered in fine hairs, with a light green color and a growth pattern that is branching towards the top. Its flowers are composite, consisting of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets, blooming from June to August, and are pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a dry achene, with small pappus hairs that aid in wind dispersal.

The roots are fibrous and extensive, anchoring the plant and absorbing water and nutrients from the soil.
