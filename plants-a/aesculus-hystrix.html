Aesculus hystrix

Aesculus hystrix, commonly known as the Canadian horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are divided into five to seven leaflets. The tree produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in upright clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several glossy, chestnut-like seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks.
