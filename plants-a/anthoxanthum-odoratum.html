Anthoxanthum odoratum

Anthoxanthum odoratum, commonly known as sweet vernalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers, which are often tinged with green, and it is noted for its pleasant, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

Anthoxanthum odoratum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
