Asplenium kaulfussii f. dareoides

Asplenium kaulfussii f. dareoides, commonly known as the Dareo's spleenwort, is a fern species belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, compact rosettes of lanceolate, serrated leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called soruses on the undersides of its fronds, which are clustered in small, round clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or on tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in controlled indoor settings.
