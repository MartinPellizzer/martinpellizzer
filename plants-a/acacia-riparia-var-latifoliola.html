Acacia riparia var. latifoliola

Acacia riparia var. latifoliola, commonly known as the broadleaf river acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in riverine habitats and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

Its wood is lightweight and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
