Astragalus baghlanensis

Astragalus baghlanensis, commonly known as the Baghlan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several elongated seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus baghlanensis plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
