Allium tui

Allium tui, commonly known as the tui onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. Allium tui is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to rock gardens or borders.
