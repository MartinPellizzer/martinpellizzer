Asplenium platyneuron f. proliferum

Asplenium platyneuron f. proliferum, commonly known as the common shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to various regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a prominent midrib.

This fern is known for its ability to form dense colonies through rhizome growth and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
