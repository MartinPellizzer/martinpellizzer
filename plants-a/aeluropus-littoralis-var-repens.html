Aeluropus littoralis var. repens

Aeluropus littoralis var. repens, commonly known as the salt marsh grass, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in estuaries and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand high salinity and periodic flooding. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
