Alchemilla taurica

Alchemilla taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in branched clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Alchemilla taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
