Aristida platychaeta

Aristida platychaeta, commonly known as broadleaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of small, spike-like flowers.

Aristida platychaeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained conditions.
