Aconitum barbatum var. barbatum

Aconitum barbatum var. barbatum, commonly known as the beardroot monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking purple to blue flowers arranged in a spire-like cluster, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

However, it is highly toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids, making it dangerous to handle or ingest.
