Agropyron boliviacum

Agropyron boliviacum, commonly known as Bolivian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Agropyron boliviacum is valued for its hardiness and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
