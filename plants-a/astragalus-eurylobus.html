Astragalus eurylobus

Astragalus eurylobus, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Astragalus eurylobus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
