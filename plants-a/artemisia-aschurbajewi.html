Artemisia aschurbajewi

Artemisia aschurbajewi, commonly known as the Aschurbajew's wormwood, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flower heads. It possesses a strong, pungent odor due to the presence of essential oils.

Artemisia aschurbajewi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and digestive properties.
