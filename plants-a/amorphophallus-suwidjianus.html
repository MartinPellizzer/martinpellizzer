Amorphophallus suwidjianus

Amorphophallus suwidjianus, commonly known as the giant swamp arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in waterlogged environments. Its underground corm serves as a storage organ, allowing it to survive seasonal flooding.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, humid conditions.
