Aster harbourii

Aster harbourii, commonly known as the Harbour's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, purple-to-white flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Aster harbourii plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
