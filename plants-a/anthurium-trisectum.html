Anthurium trisectum

Anthurium trisectum, commonly known as the three-lobed anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are divided into three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant red spathes that enclose its spadix, a feature typical of many Anthurium species. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium trisectum is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
