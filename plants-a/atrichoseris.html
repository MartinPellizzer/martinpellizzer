Atrichoseris

Atrichoseris, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

These plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. The flowers are typically yellow and arranged in a single, erect inflorescence, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies. Atrichoseris species are often found in sandy soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
