Aechmea eggersii

Aechmea eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant's foliage is typically bright green with red or purple veins, adding to its ornamental appeal. It produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can range in color from yellow to red, enhancing its visual impact.

Aechmea eggersii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
