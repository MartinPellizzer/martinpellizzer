Atraphaxis spinosa var. glauca

Atraphaxis spinosa var. glauca, commonly known as the blue desert holly, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and small, blue-green leaves that are adapted to store water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that reduces water loss and reflects sunlight.

This variant is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
