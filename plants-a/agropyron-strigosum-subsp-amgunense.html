Agropyron strigosum subsp. amgunense

Agropyron strigosum subsp.

amgunense, commonly known as the Amgun needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a dense covering of short, stiff hairs.

The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, with spikelets that are often bristly and hairy.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of steppe and grassland ecosystems.
