Acer cinerascentiforme

Acer cinerascentiforme, commonly known as the ash-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that resemble those of the ash tree, with five to seven lobes. The leaves are typically a vibrant green during the growing season and turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
