Aerangis solheidii

Aerangis solheidii, commonly known as the Solheid's orchid, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blossoms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
