Australopyrum velutinum

Australopyrum velutinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces dark, spherical berries that are dispersed by water.

Australopyrum velutinum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
