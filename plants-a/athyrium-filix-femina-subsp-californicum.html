Athyrium filix-femina subsp. californicum

Athyrium filix-femina subsp.

californicum, commonly known as the California lady fern, is a perennial fern native to the western United States. It features delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically arching and have a graceful appearance.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
