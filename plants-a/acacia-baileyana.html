Acacia baileyana

Acacia baileyana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flower clusters that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Acacia baileyana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
