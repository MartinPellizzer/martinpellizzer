Alsophila kermadecensis

Alsophila kermadecensis, commonly known as the Kermadec tree fern, is a species native to the Kermadec Islands in the South Pacific.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alsophila kermadecensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various local species.
