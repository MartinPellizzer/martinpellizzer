Andropterum variegatum

Andropterum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated ivy, is a climbing plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive variegation, often displaying shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its berries are dark blue to black and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Andropterum variegatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
