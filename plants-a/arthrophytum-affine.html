Arthrophytum affine

Arthrophytum affine, commonly known as the silver leaf cholla, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branching stems covered in small, spiny joints and pale blue-gray foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. Arthrophytum affine is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
