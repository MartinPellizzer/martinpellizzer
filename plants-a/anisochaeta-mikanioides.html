Anisochaeta mikanioides

Anisochaeta mikanioides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet or blue. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its rapid growth and climbing nature make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
