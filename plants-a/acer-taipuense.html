Acer taipuense

Acer taipuense, commonly known as the Taipu maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red samaras in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
