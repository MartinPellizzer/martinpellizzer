Allium roseum subvar. floriferum

Allium roseum subvar. floriferum, commonly known as the pink garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
