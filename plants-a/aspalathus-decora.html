Aspalathus decora

Aspalathus decora, commonly known as the decorative aspalathus, is a South African shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pale green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Aspalathus decora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
