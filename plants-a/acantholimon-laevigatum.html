Acantholimon laevigatum

Acantholimon laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth-flowered flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Acantholimon laevigatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
