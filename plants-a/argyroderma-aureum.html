Argyroderma aureum

Argyroderma aureum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small daisies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Argyroderma aureum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
