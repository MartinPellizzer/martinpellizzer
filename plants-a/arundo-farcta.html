Arundo farcta

Arundo farcta, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, bamboo-like stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands. It can grow up to 6 meters in height and is often used for erosion control due to its dense root system.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in basket-making.
