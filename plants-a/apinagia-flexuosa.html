Apinagia flexuosa

Apinagia flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible apinagia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Apinagia flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
