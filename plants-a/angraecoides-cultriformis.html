Angraecoides cultriformis

Angraecoides cultriformis, commonly known as the sword orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender inflorescence and striking, spurred flowers that resemble a sword, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure, which includes a distinctive lip and long nectar spur. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
