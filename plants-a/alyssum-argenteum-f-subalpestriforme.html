Alyssum argenteum f. subalpestriforme

Alyssum argenteum f. subalpestriforme, commonly known as the silverleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features compact, silver-gray foliage that is densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
