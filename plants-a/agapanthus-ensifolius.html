Agapanthus ensifolius

Agapanthus ensifolius, commonly known as the swordleaf agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of large, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant's leaves are long, narrow, and sword-shaped, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom reliably during the summer months.
