Aphloia theiformis var. caerulescens

Aphloia theiformis var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
