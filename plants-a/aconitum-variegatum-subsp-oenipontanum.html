Aconitum variegatum subsp. oenipontanum

Aconitum variegatum subsp.

oenipontanum, commonly known as the Variegated Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features palmate leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and it typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its toxic alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
