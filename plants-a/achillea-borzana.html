Achillea borzana

Achillea borzana, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The leaves are linear and aromatic, with a fern-like appearance, and they are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
