Acacia demostachya

Acacia demostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces flat, thin pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia demostachya thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

It is well adapted to arid environments and is an important component of local ecosystems.
