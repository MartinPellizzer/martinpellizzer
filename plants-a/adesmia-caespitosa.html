Adesmia caespitosa

Adesmia caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, cylindrical pods containing several seeds.

Adesmia caespitosa is adapted to poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
