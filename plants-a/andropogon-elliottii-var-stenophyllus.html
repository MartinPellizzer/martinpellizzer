Andropogon elliottii var. stenophyllus

Andropogon elliottii var. stenophyllus, commonly known as bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, bluish-green leaves and tufted inflorescences that produce small, fluffy seeds. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats. Its dense growth habit makes it an effective ground cover and a valuable component of native plant communities.

It is also utilized in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability.
