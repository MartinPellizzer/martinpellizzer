Allium fominii

Allium fominii, commonly known as the Fomin's onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces spherical bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.
