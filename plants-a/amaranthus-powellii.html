Amaranthus powellii

Amaranthus powellii, commonly known as Powell's amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Powell's amaranth is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience to arid conditions.
