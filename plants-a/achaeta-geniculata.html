Achaeta geniculata

Achaeta geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Achaeta geniculata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to add visual interest to gardens.
