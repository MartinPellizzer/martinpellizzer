Aloysia reichei

Aloysia reichei, commonly known as the Cape vermouth, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Aloysia reichei is drought-tolerant and is well-suited for xeriscaping in arid climates.
