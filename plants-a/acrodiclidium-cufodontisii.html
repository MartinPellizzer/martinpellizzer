Acrodiclidium cufodontisii

Acrodiclidium cufodontisii, commonly known as the desert holly, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact size and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss.
