Aster depressus

Aster depressus, commonly known as the hoary aster, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features hairy, grayish-green leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to blue-violet. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators.

Aster depressus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
