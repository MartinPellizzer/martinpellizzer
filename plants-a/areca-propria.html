Areca propria

Areca propria, commonly known as the betel nut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm with pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of edible fruits, known as betel nuts, which are enclosed in a fibrous covering. The leaves are often used in traditional practices, including chewing betel quid.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
