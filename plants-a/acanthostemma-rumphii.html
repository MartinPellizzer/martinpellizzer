Acanthostemma rumphii

Acanthostemma rumphii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
