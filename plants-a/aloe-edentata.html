Aloe edentata

Aloe edentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs. Its leaves are long, narrow, and pointed, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
