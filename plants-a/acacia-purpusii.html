Acacia purpusii

Acacia purpusii, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Acacia purpusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
