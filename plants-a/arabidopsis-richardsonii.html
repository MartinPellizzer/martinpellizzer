Arabidopsis richardsonii

Arabidopsis richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. This species is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Arabidopsis richardsonii is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and genetic studies.
