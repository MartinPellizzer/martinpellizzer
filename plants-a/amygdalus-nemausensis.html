Amygdalus nemausensis

Amygdalus nemausensis, commonly known as the French almond, is a species of shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, hard nuts that are typically harvested in the autumn. It is well adapted to dry and rocky soils, making it a resilient species in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and historical landscapes.
