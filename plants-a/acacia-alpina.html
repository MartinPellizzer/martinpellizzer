Acacia alpina

Acacia alpina, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are an important nectar source for local pollinators. Acacia alpina thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in alpine ecosystems.
