Aristolochia rodriguesii

Aristolochia rodriguesii, commonly known as Rodrigues's birthwort, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic scent to attract its specific pollinators, such as certain flies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary adaptations and ecological significance in its native habitat.
