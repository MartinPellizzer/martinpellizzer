Aconogonon bargusinense

Aconogonon bargusinense, commonly known as the Bargusin sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Aconogonon bargusinense plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists studying Arctic flora.
