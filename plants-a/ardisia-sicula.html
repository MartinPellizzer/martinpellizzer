Ardisia sicula

Ardisia sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian evergreen boxwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, glossy leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders or as a decorative plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice in horticulture.
