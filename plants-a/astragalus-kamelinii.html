Astragalus kamelinii

Astragalus kamelinii, commonly known as Kamelin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus kamelinii is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
