Acacia cornigera

Acacia cornigera, commonly known as the horned acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thorn-covered stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia cornigera is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
