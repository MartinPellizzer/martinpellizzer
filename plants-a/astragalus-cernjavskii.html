Astragalus cernjavskii

Astragalus cernjavskii, commonly known as the Cernjavsky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Astragalus cernjavskii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing features such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a potential source of medicinal compounds and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
