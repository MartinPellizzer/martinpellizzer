Antirrhinum font-queri

Antirrhinum font-queri, commonly known as the Fontqueri snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The flowers typically display vibrant red or orange hues, with two lips that resemble a dragon's mouth, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or scrubland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
