Alairia pereziodes

Alairia pereziodes, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a branching stem structure.

Alairia pereziodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a companion plant in tropical landscapes.
