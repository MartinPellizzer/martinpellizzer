Acalypha arcuata

Acalypha arcuata, commonly known as the curlleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated, bladder-like seed capsules. Its leaves often curl at the edges, giving the plant its common name, and it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique foliage.

Acalypha arcuata is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
