Areca calapparia

Areca calapparia, commonly known as the red areca or red palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to coconuts.

Areca calapparia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
