Aristida subinterrupta var. argentina

Aristida subinterrupta var. argentina, commonly known as the Argentine threeawn, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems with small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous bristly spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
