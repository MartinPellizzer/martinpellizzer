Aspidium prorepens

Aspidium prorepens, commonly known as the creeping wood fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping stems that root at the nodes. The fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
