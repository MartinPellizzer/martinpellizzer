Agastache anethiodora

Agastache anethiodora, commonly known as the anise-scented hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple to pink flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
