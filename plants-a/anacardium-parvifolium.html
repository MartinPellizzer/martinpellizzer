Anacardium parvifolium

Anacardium parvifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. The plant is part of the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other species like the poison ivy and cashew. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its berries are edible when ripe but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.
