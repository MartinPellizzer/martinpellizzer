Anisostachya ripicola

Anisostachya ripicola, commonly known as the mountain reed, is a perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
