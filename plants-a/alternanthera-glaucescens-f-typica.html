Alternanthera glaucescens f. typica

Alternanthera glaucescens f. typica, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red foliage, which becomes more intense in direct sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This cultivar is often used in water gardens and as a border plant due to its attractive color and relatively easy care requirements.
