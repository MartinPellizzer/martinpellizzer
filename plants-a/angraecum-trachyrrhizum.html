Angraecum trachyrrhizum

Angraecum trachyrrhizum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers with long, slender petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by moths, which are attracted to its nocturnal blooming habit.

Angraecum trachyrrhizum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its striking appearance.
