Agathosma apiculata

Agathosma apiculata, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique type of shrubland ecosystem.

Agathosma apiculata is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
