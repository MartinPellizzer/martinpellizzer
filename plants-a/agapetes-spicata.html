Agapetes spicata

Agapetes spicata, commonly known as the clustered silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. It produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in spring, followed by small, edible berries.

Agapetes spicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
