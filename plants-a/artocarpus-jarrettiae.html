Artocarpus jarrettiae

Artocarpus jarrettiae, commonly known as the Malayan breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, develops a rough, bumpy texture and contains a sweet, starchy pulp. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various traditional dishes.

The tree also plays a role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
