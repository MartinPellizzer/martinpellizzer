Acer glaucum

Acer glaucum, commonly known as the white poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
