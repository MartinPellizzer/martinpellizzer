Acalypha rhomboidea var. deamii

Acalypha rhomboidea var. deamii, commonly known as the Deam's velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by triangular, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
