Aster indicus var. indicus

Aster indicus var. indicus, commonly known as the Indian aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically range in color from white to purple. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
