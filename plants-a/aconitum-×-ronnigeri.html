Aconitum × ronnigeri

Aconitum × ronnigeri, commonly known as the hybrid monkshood, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a cross between Aconitum napellus and Aconitum lycoctonum, resulting in a plant with striking purple and white spurs on its flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring large, palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are arranged in tall, sparsely branched racemes, and the plant is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it highly poisonous.

Aconitum × ronnigeri thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
