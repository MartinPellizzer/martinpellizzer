Asparagus umbellatus subsp. umbellatus

Asparagus umbellatus subsp.

umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like arrangement of feathery, needle-like leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
