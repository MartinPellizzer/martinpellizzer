Arctostaphylos hearstiorum

Arctostaphylos hearstiorum, commonly known as the Hearst manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and dense clusters of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that contains several seeds.

This plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
