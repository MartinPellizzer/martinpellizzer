Aconitum woroschilowii

Aconitum woroschilowii, commonly known as the Woroschilow's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves with a deeply lobed structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum woroschilowii contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.
