Acalypha segetalis

Acalypha segetalis, commonly known as the grain amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often green with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or yellow, followed by small, three-angled seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields or along roadsides.

Due to its nutritional value, it is cultivated as a potential crop for food and forage.
