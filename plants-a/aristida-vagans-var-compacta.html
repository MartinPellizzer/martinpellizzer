Aristida vagans var. compacta

Aristida vagans var. compacta, commonly known as the compact bluemink grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to reddish in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Its compact form and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
