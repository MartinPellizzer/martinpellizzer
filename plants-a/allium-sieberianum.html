Allium sieberianum

Allium sieberianum, commonly known as the Siberian chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features grass-like leaves and produces small, pale purple to white flowers in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Allium sieberianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
