Acrocephalus polytrichus

Acrocephalus polytrichus, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and dense clusters of spikelets at the top. The plant is often found in marshes, riverbanks, and coastal areas, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Acrocephalus polytrichus is an important component of wetland ecosystems and is widely distributed across temperate regions.
