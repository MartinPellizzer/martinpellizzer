Albizia passargei

Albizia passargei, commonly known as the Madagascar Albizia, is a species of legume native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow or pale green flowers arranged in racemes, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry or semi-arid regions.

Albizia passargei is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
