Anthocleista lanceolata

Anthocleista lanceolata, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its erect stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Anthocleista lanceolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
