Anisostachya sambiranensis

Anisostachya sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambira grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
