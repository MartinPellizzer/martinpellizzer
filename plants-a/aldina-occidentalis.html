Aldina occidentalis

Aldina occidentalis, commonly known as the western aldina, is a perennial herb native to the western regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicinal practices by indigenous communities.

Aldina occidentalis is valued for its adaptability and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
