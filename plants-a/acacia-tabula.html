Acacia tabula

Acacia tabula, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow to golden spherical flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its seed pods are long, slender, and woody, containing several seeds.

Acacia tabula is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
