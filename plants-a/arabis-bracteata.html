Arabis bracteata

Arabis bracteata, commonly known as the bracted rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in racemes, adding a delicate appearance to its foliage.

Arabis bracteata is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
