Arabis consanguinea

Arabis consanguinea, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and prefers well-drained soils in sunny or partially shaded areas. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, including alpine meadows and coastal cliffs.

Arabis consanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
