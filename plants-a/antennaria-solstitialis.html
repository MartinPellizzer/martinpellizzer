Antennaria solstitialis

Antennaria solstitialis, commonly known as the summer solstice flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, hence its common name. It has hairy, linear leaves and produces yellowish to reddish flowers that attract pollinators.

Antennaria solstitialis thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
