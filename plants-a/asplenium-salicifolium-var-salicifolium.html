Asplenium salicifolium var. salicifolium

Asplenium salicifolium var. salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in shaded, moist environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, anchoring itself with its rhizomes. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with spore-producing structures called sorangia on the underside.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
