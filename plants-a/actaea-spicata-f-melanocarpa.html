Actaea spicata f. melanocarpa

Actaea spicata f. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited bugbane, is a striking perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, showy white flowers in late summer. The plant produces dark purple to black berries, which are a distinguishing feature of this form. Its foliage consists of large, pinnately compound leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded woodland environments.
