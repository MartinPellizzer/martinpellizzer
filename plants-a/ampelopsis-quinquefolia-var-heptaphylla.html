Ampelopsis quinquefolia var. heptaphylla

Ampelopsis quinquefolia var. heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leaf honeysuckle, is a variant of the common grapevine.

It is a deciduous climbing vine characterized by its compound leaves with seven leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, wooded areas.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures.
