Arundinella tricuspidula

Arundinella tricuspidula, commonly known as the three-lobed bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brown spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Arundinella tricuspidula is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and thrives in humid, warm climates.
