Anemone narcissiflora var. alaskana

Anemone narcissiflora var. alaskana, commonly known as the Alaska anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or tundra environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive, slightly fuzzy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in cooler climates.
