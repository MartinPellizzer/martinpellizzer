Aconitum firmum var. maninense

Aconitum firmum var. maninense, commonly known as the Maninense monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
