Aconitum napellus lusus hungaricum

Aconitum napellus lusus hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian buttercup, is a variant of the European aconite.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
