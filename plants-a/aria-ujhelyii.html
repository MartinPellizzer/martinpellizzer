Aria ujhelyii

Aria ujhelyii, commonly known as the Hungarian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Hungary and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are grass-like and lance-shaped, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Aria ujhelyii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
