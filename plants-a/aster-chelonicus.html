Aster chelonicus

Aster chelonicus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Aster chelonicus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
