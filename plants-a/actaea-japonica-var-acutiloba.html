Actaea japonica var. acutiloba

Actaea japonica var. acutiloba, commonly known as Japanese cohosh, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The berries, which are bright red when mature, are an important food source for various wildlife species.
