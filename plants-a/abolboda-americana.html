Abolboda americana

Abolboda americana, commonly known as the American sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Abolboda americana plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitats.
