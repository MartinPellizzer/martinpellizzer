Adesmia senticula

Adesmia senticula, commonly known as the sweet-smelling legume, is a small, shrub-like plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Adesmia senticula produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its aromatic properties have made it of interest in traditional medicine and horticulture.
