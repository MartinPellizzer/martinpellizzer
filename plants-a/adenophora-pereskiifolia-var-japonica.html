Adenophora pereskiifolia var. japonica

Adenophora pereskiifolia var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Adenophora genus, which are known for their medicinal and horticultural uses.
