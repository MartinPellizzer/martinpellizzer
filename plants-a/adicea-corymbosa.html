Adicea corymbosa

Adicea corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Adicea corymbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
