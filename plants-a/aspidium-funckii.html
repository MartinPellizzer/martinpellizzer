Aspidium funckii

Aspidium funckii, commonly known as the Funck's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are typically divided into smaller, finger-like segments called pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, which are typically clustered in compact, compact, and dense tufts.

Aspidium funckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
