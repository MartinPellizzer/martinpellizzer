Aniketon coriaceum

Aniketon coriaceum, commonly known as the rough anise, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish flowers and pinnate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, oblong seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Aniketon coriaceum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
