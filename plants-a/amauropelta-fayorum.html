Amauropelta fayorum

Amauropelta fayorum, commonly known as the fairy fern, is a unique species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that give it a whimsical appearance, resembling the shape of a fairy's hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae that contribute to its intricate foliage.

Amauropelta fayorum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
