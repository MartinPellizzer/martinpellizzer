Artocarpus camansi

Artocarpus camansi, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces a unique fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer and a starchy, edible interior. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same tree, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious fruit and ornamental value.
