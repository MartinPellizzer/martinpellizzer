Acer sikkimense f. subintegrum

Acer sikkimense f. subintegrum, commonly known as the Sikkim maple, is a variant of the Sikkim maple tree native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that are typically lobed and have a smooth margin, giving it a refined appearance. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates with high altitudes and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and parks.
