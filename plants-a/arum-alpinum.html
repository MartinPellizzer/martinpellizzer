Arum alpinum

Arum alpinum, commonly known as the alpine arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a distinctive purple spathe that encloses a spadix, which is the flowering structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a glossy surface.

Arum alpinum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
