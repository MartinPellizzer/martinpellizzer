Artemisia borealis f. wormskioldii

Artemisia borealis f. wormskioldii, commonly known as the Arctic wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its aromatic properties make it a notable species in alpine and tundra ecosystems.
