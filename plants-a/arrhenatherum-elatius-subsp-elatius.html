Arrhenatherum elatius subsp. elatius

Arrhenatherum elatius subsp.

elatius, commonly known as tall meadow grass, is a tall perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and other wetland habitats.

The plant features slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

The inflorescence consists of a loose, panicle-like arrangement of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or pink.
