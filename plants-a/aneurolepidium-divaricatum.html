Aneurolepidium divaricatum

Aneurolepidium divaricatum, commonly known as the variable bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Aneurolepidium divaricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
