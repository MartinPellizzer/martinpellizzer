Adriana acerifolia var. lessonii

Adriana acerifolia var. lessonii, commonly known as the Lesson's maple, is a variant of the maple tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often displaying vibrant autumn colors. The plant is native to specific regions in southern Europe, particularly in the Iberian Peninsula. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden cultivation.
