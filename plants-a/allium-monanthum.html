Allium monanthum

Allium monanthum, commonly known as the single-flowered garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and tall, strap-like leaves. The plant produces a single, showy, bell-shaped flower that is typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium monanthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild culinary uses in some traditional cuisines.
