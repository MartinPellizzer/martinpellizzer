Aconitum rostratum var. pilosiusculum

Aconitum rostratum var. pilosiusculum, commonly known as the hairy beak aconite, is a variant of the beak aconite native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
