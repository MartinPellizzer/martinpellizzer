Afzelia peturei

Afzelia peturei, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-brown bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Afzelia peturei is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
