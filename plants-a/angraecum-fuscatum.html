Angraecum fuscatum

Angraecum fuscatum, commonly known as the black angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety leaves and is known for its fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers have a unique structure with long, slender spurs that are adapted for pollination by moths.

Angraecum fuscatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
