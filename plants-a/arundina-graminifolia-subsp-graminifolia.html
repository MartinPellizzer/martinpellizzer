Arundina graminifolia subsp. graminifolia

Arundina graminifolia subsp.

graminifolia, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
