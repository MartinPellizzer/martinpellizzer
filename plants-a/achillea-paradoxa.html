Achillea paradoxa

Achillea paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradoxal Yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Achillea paradoxa is relatively drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
