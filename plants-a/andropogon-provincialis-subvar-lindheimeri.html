Andropogon provincialis subvar. lindheimeri

Andropogon provincialis subvar. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun to partial shade, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Its ornamental value has also made it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
