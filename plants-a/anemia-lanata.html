Anemia lanata

Anemia lanata, commonly known as the downy anemone, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its woolly, hairy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, white to grayish hairs.

Anemia lanata is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
