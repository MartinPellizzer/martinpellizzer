Aristida coma-ardeae

Aristida coma-ardeae, commonly known as the blackbrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brown to reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow with a rough, hairy texture. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

This grass is often found in shrublands and grasslands, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
