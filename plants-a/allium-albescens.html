Allium albescens

Allium albescens, commonly known as the white garlic or alpine garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, white bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
