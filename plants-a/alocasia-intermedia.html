Alocasia intermedia

Alocasia intermedia, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 feet in length, often with a deep green color and a glossy texture. The plant produces small, white, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Alocasia intermedia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to purify indoor air.
