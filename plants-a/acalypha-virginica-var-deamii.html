Acalypha virginica var. deamii

Acalypha virginica var. deamii, commonly known as the Deam's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features showy, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture and a reddish or purplish underside.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
