Arracacia coulteri

Arracacia coulteri, commonly known as the Mexican pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense heads, followed by spherical, spiky fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies and as a spice.
