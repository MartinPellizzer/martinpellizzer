Alomia fastigiata

Alomia fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed alomia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alomia fastigiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
