Asparagus tibeticus

Asparagus tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the harsh, cold climate of its native habitat.

Asparagus tibeticus is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
