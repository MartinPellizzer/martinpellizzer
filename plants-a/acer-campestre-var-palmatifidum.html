Acer campestre var. palmatifidum

Acer campestre var. palmatifidum, commonly known as the five-lobed hedge maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven pointed lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used as a hedge or ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.

This variety is closely related to the common hedge maple but exhibits more pronounced leaf lobes.
