Amelanchier canadensis var. scharnkeana

Amelanchier canadensis var. scharnkeana, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and paler underneath. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a deep red or purple color in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
