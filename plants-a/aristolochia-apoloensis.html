Aristolochia apoloensis

Aristolochia apoloensis, commonly known as the Cameroon birthwort, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Cameroon.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, complex-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for various purposes.

However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts.
