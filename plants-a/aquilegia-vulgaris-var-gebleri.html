Aquilegia vulgaris var. gebleri

Aquilegia vulgaris var. gebleri, commonly known as the Gebleri columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and southern Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, purple, and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with palmate divisions, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
