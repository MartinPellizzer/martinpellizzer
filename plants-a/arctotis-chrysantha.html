Arctotis chrysantha

Arctotis chrysantha, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flower heads are surrounded by pale green bracts.

Arctotis chrysantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
