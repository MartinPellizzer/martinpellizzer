Aster wasatchensis

Aster wasatchensis, commonly known as the Wasatch aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing purple to pinkish disc flowers surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets.

Aster wasatchensis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
