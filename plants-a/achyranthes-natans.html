Achyranthes natans

Achyranthes natans, commonly known as water bindweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water, such as ponds and marshes, and has slender, floating stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers. The plant's leaves are narrow and elongated, often floating on the water surface, while its roots are anchored in the substrate. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some areas.

The flowers are hermaphroditic, meaning they have both male and female reproductive structures, facilitating self-pollination.
