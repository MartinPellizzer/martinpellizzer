Amomum bicorniculatum

Amomum bicorniculatum, commonly known as the two-horned cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes that are rich in essential oils. The plant produces clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers, which are followed by small, three-lobed fruits. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinct, fragrant aroma.

This species is valued both for its culinary uses and traditional medicinal properties.
