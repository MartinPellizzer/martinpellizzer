Albizia amara

Albizia amara, commonly known as the bitter acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its yellow flowers and is often found in dry, sandy soils. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Albizia amara is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of timber.
