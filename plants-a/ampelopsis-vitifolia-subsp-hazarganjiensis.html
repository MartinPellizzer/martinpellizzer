Ampelopsis vitifolia subsp. hazarganjiensis

Ampelopsis vitifolia subsp.

hazarganjiensis, commonly known as the Hazarganji ampelopsis, is a climbing vine native to the western Himalayas. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Vitaceae family.
