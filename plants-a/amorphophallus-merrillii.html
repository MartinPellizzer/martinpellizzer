Amorphophallus merrillii

Amorphophallus merrillii, commonly known as the Philippine elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, tuberous underground stem, which can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a strong odor resembling rotting meat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in its native region.
