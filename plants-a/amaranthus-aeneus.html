Amaranthus aeneus

Amaranthus aeneus, commonly known as the bronze amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking bronze-colored foliage and vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines, while the seeds are a source of nutrition.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
