Asystasia chelonoides var. quadrangularis

Asystasia chelonoides var. quadrangularis, commonly known as the square-stemmed asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square-shaped stems, which are covered in fine hairs, and its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, arranged in spikes at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
