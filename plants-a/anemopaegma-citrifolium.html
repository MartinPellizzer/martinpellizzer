Anemopaegma citrifolium

Anemopaegma citrifolium, commonly known as the citric-leaved anemopaegma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically green with a slightly sour scent. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
