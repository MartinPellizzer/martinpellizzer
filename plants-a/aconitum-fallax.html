Aconitum fallax

Aconitum fallax, commonly known as the autumn aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has palmately divided leaves and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its toxic alkaloids make it a plant that requires careful handling.
