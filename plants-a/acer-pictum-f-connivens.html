Acer pictum f. connivens

Acer pictum f. connivens, commonly known as the Eastern Redbud variant, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The foliage consists of heart-shaped, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its early spring blooms.
