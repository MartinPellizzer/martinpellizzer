Aconitum × semilasianthum

Aconitum × semilasianthum, commonly known as the hybrid monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Aconitum napellus and Aconitum lycoctonum, resulting in a plant with distinctive blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically tall and erect. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
