Arctium smirnowii

Arctium smirnowii, commonly known as the Siberian burdock, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, serrated edge, and it produces distinctive purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Arctium smirnowii is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
