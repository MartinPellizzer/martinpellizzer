Aconitum sanyoense var. intermedium

Aconitum sanyoense var. intermedium, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply cut lobes, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
