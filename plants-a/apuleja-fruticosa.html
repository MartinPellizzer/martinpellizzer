Apuleja fruticosa

Apuleja fruticosa, commonly known as the wild fig or false fig, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a reddish color and is often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Apuleja fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
