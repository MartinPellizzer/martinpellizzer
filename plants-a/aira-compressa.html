Aira compressa

Aira compressa, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Aira compressa is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
