Acacia pseudoarabica

Acacia pseudoarabica, commonly known as the false arabica acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly Ethiopia, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia pseudoarabica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
