Acer negundo subsp. boreale

Acer negundo subsp.

boreale, commonly known as the plains maple, is a deciduous tree native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

The species typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
