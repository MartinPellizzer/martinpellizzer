Astragalus gracilentus var. fallax

Astragalus gracilentus var. fallax, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are palmately divided into slender lobes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
