Abutilon membranaceum

Abutilon membranaceum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant.
