Anthemis cretica subsp. gerardiana

Anthemis cretica subsp.

gerardiana, commonly known as Gerard's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has finely divided, grass-like leaves. Its yellow flowers are arranged in compact clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
