Athyrium oosorum

Athyrium oosorum, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a delicate evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It features finely divided, arching fronds that are typically green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shady garden settings.
