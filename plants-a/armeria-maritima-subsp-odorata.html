Armeria maritima subsp. odorata

Armeria maritima subsp.

odorata, commonly known as the fragrant sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the subspecies is distinguished by its more intense floral fragrance compared to other subspecies.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in coastal gardens.
