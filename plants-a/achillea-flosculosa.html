Achillea flosculosa

Achillea flosculosa, commonly known as the alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage is aromatic and covered in fine hairs, while its small white to pale yellow flowers bloom in summer.

Achillea flosculosa is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
