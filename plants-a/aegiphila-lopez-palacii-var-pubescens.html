Aegiphila lopez-palacii var. pubescens

Aegiphila lopez-palacii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy aegiphila, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils and moderate temperatures.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
