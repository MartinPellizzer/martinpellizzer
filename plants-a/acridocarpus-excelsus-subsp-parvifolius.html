Acridocarpus excelsus subsp. parvifolius

Acridocarpus excelsus subsp.

parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange-red in color.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or lowland forests. Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more delicate leaves compared to the typical form of Acridocarpus excelsus.
