Agropyron japonense var. macilentum

Agropyron japonense var. macilentum, commonly known as Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
