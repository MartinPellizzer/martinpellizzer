Amphymenium violaceum

Amphymenium violaceum, commonly known as the violet leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-colored leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
