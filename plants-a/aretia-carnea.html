Aretia carnea

Aretia carnea, commonly known as the scarlet arete, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Aretia carnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
