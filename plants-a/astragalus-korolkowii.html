Astragalus korolkowii

Astragalus korolkowii, commonly known as the Korolkow's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus korolkowii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
