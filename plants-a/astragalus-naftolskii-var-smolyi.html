Astragalus naftolskii var. smolyi

Astragalus naftolskii var. smolyi, commonly known as Smoly's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain small, oval seeds.

This variant is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
