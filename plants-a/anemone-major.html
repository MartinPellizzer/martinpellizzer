Anemone major

Anemone major, commonly known as the showy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive central cone-shaped structure, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Anemone major is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
