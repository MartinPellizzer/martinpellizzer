Artocarpus incisifolius var. apyrenus

Artocarpus incisifolius var. apyrenus, commonly known as the giant fig or breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and produces a fruit that is typically pear-shaped. The plant is part of the Moraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid climates. Its fruit is edible and has been used for food and traditional medicine.
