Astragalus sieversii

Astragalus sieversii, commonly known as Sievers' milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties and are used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus sieversii plays an important role in its ecosystem by contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.
