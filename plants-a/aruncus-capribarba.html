Aruncus capribarba

Aruncus capribarba, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features striking, feathery plumes of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Aruncus capribarba is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
