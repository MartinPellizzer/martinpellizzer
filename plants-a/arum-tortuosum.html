Arum tortuosum

Arum tortuosum, commonly known as the spiral arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant features a tall, slender stem topped with a large, heart-shaped spathe that encloses a spadix of small, inconspicuous flowers. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique and dramatic appearance.

Arum tortuosum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical landscapes.
