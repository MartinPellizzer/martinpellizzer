Aquilegia transsilvanica

Aquilegia transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its tuberous roots are adapted for storing nutrients, allowing it to thrive in temperate climates.

Aquilegia transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
