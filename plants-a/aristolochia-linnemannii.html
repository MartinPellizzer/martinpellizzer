Aristolochia linnemannii

Aristolochia linnemannii, commonly known as the Indian birthwort, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, often fragrant blooms. Its tubular flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a purple or brownish interior.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds and has been used in traditional medicine, although it is also considered toxic in certain forms.
