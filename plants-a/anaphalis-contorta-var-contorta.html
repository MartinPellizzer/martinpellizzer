Anaphalis contorta var. contorta

Anaphalis contorta var. contorta, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
