Arrabidaea lasiantha

Arrabidaea lasiantha, commonly known as the Cape rose or white thorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the fynbos biome. The plant features woody stems covered in thorny projections and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
