Anthericum pseudoasphodelus

Anthericum pseudoasphodelus, commonly known as the false asphodel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Anthericum pseudoasphodelus is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its elegant appearance.
