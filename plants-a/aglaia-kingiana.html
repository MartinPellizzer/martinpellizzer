Aglaia kingiana

Aglaia kingiana, commonly known as the white angel's trumpet, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported analgesic and antispasmodic properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
