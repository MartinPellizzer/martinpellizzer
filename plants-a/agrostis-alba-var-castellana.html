Agrostis alba var. castellana

Agrostis alba var. castellana, commonly known as the Castilian silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, silver-blue blades with a distinctive white bloom, which is a characteristic feature of the Agrostis genus. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
