Ageratum houstonianum var. typicum

Ageratum houstonianum var. typicum, commonly known as the common ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, clustered flowers in shades of blue, purple, pink, and white. The plant produces fluffy, seed-filled bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its short growing season makes it a favored annual in temperate climates.
