Aster fischeri

Aster fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as meadows and slopes, and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster fischeri is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
