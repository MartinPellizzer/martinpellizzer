Aira purpurea

Aira purpurea, commonly known as purple airplant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Aira purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
