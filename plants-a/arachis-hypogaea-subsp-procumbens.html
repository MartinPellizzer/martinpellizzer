Arachis hypogaea subsp. procumbens

Arachis hypogaea subsp.

procumbens, commonly known as the wild peanut, is a species native to South America. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit and compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing one to three seeds. It is closely related to the domesticated peanut, Arachis hypogaea, and shares similar botanical features.

This subspecies is often studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications.
