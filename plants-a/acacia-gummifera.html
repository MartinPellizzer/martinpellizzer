Acacia gummifera

Acacia gummifera, commonly known as the gum acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and phyllodes that are typically straight or slightly curved. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Acacia gummifera is known for secreting a gummy substance from its bark, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
