Andinocleome moritziana

Andinocleome moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's cactus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh conditions. Its flowers, which can be yellow or red, are often short-lived but attract various pollinators.
