Aconitum kashmiricum

Aconitum kashmiricum, commonly known as the Kashmir aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan, India, and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in fine hairs.

Aconitum kashmiricum is known for containing potent alkaloids, making it both a valuable ornamental plant and a highly toxic species.
