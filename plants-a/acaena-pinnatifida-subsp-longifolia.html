Acaena pinnatifida subsp. longifolia

Acaena pinnatifida subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Acaena. It thrives in coastal and lowland environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
