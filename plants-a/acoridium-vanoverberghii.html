Acoridium vanoverberghii

Acoridium vanoverberghii, commonly known as the Vanoverbergh's arum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its large, showy spathe that surrounds a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
