Andropogon aureus

Andropogon aureus, commonly known as golden bluestem, is a tallgrass prairie plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and forms dense clumps, making it a valuable component of native grassland ecosystems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
