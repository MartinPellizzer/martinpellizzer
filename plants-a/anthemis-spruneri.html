Anthemis spruneri

Anthemis spruneri, commonly known as the alpine chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with a distinctive white central disk, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Anthemis spruneri is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
