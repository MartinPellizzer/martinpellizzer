Aspalathus jubatus

Aspalathus jubatus, commonly known as the mountain tea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

Aspalathus jubatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
