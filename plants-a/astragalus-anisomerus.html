Astragalus anisomerus

Astragalus anisomerus, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Astragalus anisomerus is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
