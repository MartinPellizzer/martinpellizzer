Amyema scandens

Amyema scandens, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems that allow it to climb and twine around supports. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Amyema scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
