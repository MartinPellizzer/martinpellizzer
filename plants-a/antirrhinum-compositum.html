Antirrhinum compositum

Antirrhinum compositum, commonly known as the composite snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink, red, or purple, and they bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
