Avena anathera

Avena anathera, commonly known as the spikeless oat, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, leafy stems and the absence of the usual awns or bristles found in other oat species. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Avena anathera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
