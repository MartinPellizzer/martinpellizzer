Acalypha villosa var. tomentosa

Acalypha villosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, often red or purple bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, white to grayish hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in dense, spiky inflorescences.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
