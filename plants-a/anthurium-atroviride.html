Anthurium atroviride

Anthurium atroviride, commonly known as the black velvet plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves that are typically dark green to almost black in color. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences that are often surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Anthurium atroviride is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
