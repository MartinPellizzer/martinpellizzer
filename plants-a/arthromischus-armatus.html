Arthromischus armatus

Arthromischus armatus, commonly known as the armed thornplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in sharp, spiny tubercles, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
