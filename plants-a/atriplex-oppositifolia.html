Atriplex oppositifolia

Atriplex oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositeleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Its deep root system allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, enhancing its drought tolerance.
