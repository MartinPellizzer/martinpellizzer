Abarema tetraphylla

Abarema tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf clover of the genus Abarema, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, though it may occasionally produce four leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by pods containing several seeds.

It is often found in grassy areas and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
