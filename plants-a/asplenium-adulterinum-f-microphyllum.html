Asplenium adulterinum f. microphyllum

Asplenium adulterinum f. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaf shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its compact, densely packed fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The fronds are typically dark green, with a leathery texture and finely divided pinnae. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
