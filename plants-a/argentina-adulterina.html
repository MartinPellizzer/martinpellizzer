Argentina adulterina

Argentina adulterina, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its unique beauty and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
