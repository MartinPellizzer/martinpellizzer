Amorphophallus infundibuliformis

Amorphophallus infundibuliformis, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, making it one of the largest leafy plants in the world. The plant produces a tall, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a giant phallus, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are followed by the development of a large, fleshy underground tuber.

This tuber is not only a storage organ but also has culinary and medicinal uses in some cultures.
