Antholyza nemorosa

Antholyza nemorosa, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread. Its leaves are palmately lobed and appear before the flowers, creating a distinctive appearance.

Antholyza nemorosa is often found in forest understories and is an important component of early spring flora.
