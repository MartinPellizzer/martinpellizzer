Acacia deltoidea

Acacia deltoidea, commonly known as the weeping boerkea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping, yellow flower spikes that appear during the winter months. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia deltoidea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
