Asclepias lineolata

Asclepias lineolata, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the milkweed family, Apocynaceae.

It is native to the United States and is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its latex sap is milky and toxic, containing cardenolides that are harmful to many animals.

Asclepias lineolata plays a vital role in supporting monarch butterfly populations by providing a host plant for their larvae.
