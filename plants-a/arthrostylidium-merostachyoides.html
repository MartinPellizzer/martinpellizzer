Arthrostylidium merostachyoides

Arthrostylidium merostachyoides, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It possesses a shallow root system that allows it to attach to the roots of host trees, drawing water and nutrients from them. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along its slender stems. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and it produces small, spherical berries that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and health of its host plants.
