Anthemis microsperma

Anthemis microsperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flower heads that are composed of numerous tiny florets. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit make it a distinctive member of the Anthemis genus.
