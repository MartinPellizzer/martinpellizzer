Arundo calycina

Arundo calycina, commonly known as the silverleaf reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and tall, cylindrical inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found near rivers and lakes. Its rapid growth makes it a useful plant for erosion control and wetland restoration.

Arundo calycina is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
