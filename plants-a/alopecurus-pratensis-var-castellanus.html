Alopecurus pratensis var. castellanus

Alopecurus pratensis var. castellanus, commonly known as castle grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional landscaping and forage.
