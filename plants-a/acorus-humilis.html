Acorus humilis

Acorus humilis, commonly known as narrow-leaf sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is often found in shallow water or moist soil, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Acorus humilis is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
