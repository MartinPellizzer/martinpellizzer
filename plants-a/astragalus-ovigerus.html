Astragalus ovigerus

Astragalus ovigerus, commonly known as the ovigerous milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by pods that contain several seeds. Astragalus ovigerus is adapted to survive in harsh environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils with low moisture availability.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and soil stability.
