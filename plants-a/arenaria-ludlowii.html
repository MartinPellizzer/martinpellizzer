Arenaria ludlowii

Arenaria ludlowii, commonly known as Ludlow's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to arid environments.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky habitats.
