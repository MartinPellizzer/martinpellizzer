Abies douglasii pumila

Abies douglasii pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Douglas fir, is a small coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and short, stiff needles that are dark green in color. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in subalpine and alpine regions.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a distinctive and valuable component of its native ecosystem.
