Astragalus oroboides var. pallidiflorus

Astragalus oroboides var. pallidiflorus, commonly known as the paleflower milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
