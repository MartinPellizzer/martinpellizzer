Astragalus japonicus

Astragalus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus japonicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
