Asclepias connivens

Asclepias connivens, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright orange to yellow flowers with distinctive, horn-like structures called horns. It is a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and supports a variety of pollinators.

Asclepias connivens is also known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds that deter herbivores.
