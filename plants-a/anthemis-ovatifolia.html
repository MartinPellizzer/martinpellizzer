Anthemis ovatifolia

Anthemis ovatifolia, commonly known as the ovateleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, ovate-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
