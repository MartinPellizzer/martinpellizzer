Aspilia foliacea var. hirsuta

Aspilia foliacea var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy leaf aspilia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spiky inflorescence composed of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hirsuta" designation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
