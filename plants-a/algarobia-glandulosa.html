Algarobia glandulosa

Algarobia glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves and small, showy flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or red, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. Algarobia glandulosa has a woody stem and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
