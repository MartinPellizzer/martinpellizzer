Anthemis cretica var. subscaposa

Anthemis cretica var. subscaposa, commonly known as the narrowleaf cudweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, branching stems with narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in clusters, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.
