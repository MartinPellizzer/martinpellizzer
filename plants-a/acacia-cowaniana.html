Acacia cowaniana

Acacia cowaniana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small, deciduous shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, phyllodes that are typically pale green to white in color. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads during the summer months, which are clustered in dense spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Acacia cowaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
