Acacia diaphyllodinea

Acacia diaphyllodinea, commonly known as the white-leaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia diaphyllodinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
