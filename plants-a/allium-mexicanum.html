Allium mexicanum

Allium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican garlic, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a strong, pungent flavor similar to garlic.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a culinary herb.
