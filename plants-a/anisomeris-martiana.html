Anisomeris martiana

Anisomeris martiana, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, branching stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or divided. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, pale seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Anisomeris martiana is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from lower soil layers.
