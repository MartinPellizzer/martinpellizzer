Allium margaritaceum var. confusum

Allium margaritaceum var. confusum, commonly known as the confused garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in culinary applications, similar to regular garlic and chives. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and herbal collections.
