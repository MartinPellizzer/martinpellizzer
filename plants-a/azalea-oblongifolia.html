Azalea oblongifolia

Azalea oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Azalea oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
