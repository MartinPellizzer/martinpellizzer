Arnica angustifolia subsp. alpina

Arnica angustifolia subsp.

alpina, commonly known as alpine narrowleaf arnica, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent yellow centers. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes harvested for use in topical treatments.
