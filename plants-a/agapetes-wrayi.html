Agapetes wrayi

Agapetes wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's mountain tea, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. This evergreen shrub typically grows at high altitudes, often in cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, while its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
