Apocynum arcuatum

Apocynum arcuatum, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and livestock.
