Astragalus compactus var. minor

Astragalus compactus var. minor, commonly known as the small globemallow, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features dense, rounded clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
