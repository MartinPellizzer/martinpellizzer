Amaryllis lavrensis

Amaryllis lavrensis, commonly known as the Lavrensis amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Amaryllis lavrensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable environment.
