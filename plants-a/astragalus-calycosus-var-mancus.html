Astragalus calycosus var. mancus

Astragalus calycosus var. mancus, commonly known as the Manchurian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Russia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

It has small, stipitate pods that contain several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
