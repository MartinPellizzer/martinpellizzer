Astragalus zachlensis var. zebedanensis

Astragalus zachlensis var. zebedanensis, commonly known as the Zebedian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native habitats.
