Alyssum calycinum var. granatense

Alyssum calycinum var. granatense, commonly known as Granate Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse displays of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant produces clusters of four-petaled blooms that are typically borne on slender stems. Its foliage consists of finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery coating.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens, borders, and containers due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
