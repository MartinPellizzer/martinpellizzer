Astragalus irmingardis

Astragalus irmingardis, commonly known as the Irmingard's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus irmingardis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
