Astragalus damascenus var. glabratus

Astragalus damascenus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with stipules, and its root system is fibrous and often taprooted. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in dry gardens or as a ground cover.
