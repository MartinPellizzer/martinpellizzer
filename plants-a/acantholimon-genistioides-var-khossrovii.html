Acantholimon genistioides var. khossrovii

Acantholimon genistioides var. khossrovii, commonly known as the Khossrovii thornweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a compact growth habit with wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its succulent leaves and drought-resistant characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, dry climates.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
