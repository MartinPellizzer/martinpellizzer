Aster milleri

Aster milleri, commonly known as the Miller's aster, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the western regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its showy, violet-blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster milleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
