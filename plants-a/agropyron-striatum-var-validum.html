Agropyron striatum var. validum

Agropyron striatum var. validum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
