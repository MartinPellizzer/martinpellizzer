Auriculardisia atrata

Auriculardisia atrata, commonly known as the black ear orchid, is a unique orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish sepals and petals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a complex structure typical of orchids.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
