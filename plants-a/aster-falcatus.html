Aster falcatus

Aster falcatus, commonly known as the sickle aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Aster falcatus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
