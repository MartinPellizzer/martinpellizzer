Aphanostelma volcanense

Aphanostelma volcanense, commonly known as the volcanic rock agave, is a succulent plant native to the volcanic regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments. The plant produces striking, tall flower stalks that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. It has a slow growth rate and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

Aphanostelma volcanense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
