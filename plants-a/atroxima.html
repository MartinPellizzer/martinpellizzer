Atroxima

Atroxima, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a flowering plant that produces dark purple berries which are highly toxic due to the presence of solanine and other alkaloids. The plant has oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture and typically grows in shaded, moist environments. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
