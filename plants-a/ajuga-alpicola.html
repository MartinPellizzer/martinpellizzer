Ajuga alpicola

Ajuga alpicola, commonly known as alpine bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested slopes. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in the spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.

Ajuga alpicola is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
