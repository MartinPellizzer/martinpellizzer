Allium boryanum

Allium boryanum, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces upright, cylindrical flower stalks topped with small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Allium boryanum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
