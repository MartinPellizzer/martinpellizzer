Aerva lanata var. steudneri

Aerva lanata var. steudneri, commonly known as the Indian mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in moist, sandy soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
