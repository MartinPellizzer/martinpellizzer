Acalypha capensis f. lamiifolia

Acalypha capensis f. lamiifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woollyleaf, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
