Allium pentadactyli

Allium pentadactyli, commonly known as the six-fingered garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild culinary uses in some traditional cuisines.

Allium pentadactyli thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.
