Aconogonon sibiricum subsp. thomsonii

Aconogonon sibiricum subsp.

thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged oppositely along the stem, while the flowers are clustered in racemes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
