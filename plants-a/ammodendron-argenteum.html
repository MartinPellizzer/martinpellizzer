Ammodendron argenteum

Ammodendron argenteum, commonly known as silver saxaul, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and branches, which are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low precipitation. It plays an important role in preventing soil erosion and stabilizing desert ecosystems.

Its resilience makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
