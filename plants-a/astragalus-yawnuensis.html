Astragalus yawnuensis

Astragalus yawnuensis, commonly known as the Yawnuensian milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can often be found in steppe and desert environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
