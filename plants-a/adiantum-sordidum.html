Adiantum sordidum

Adiantum sordidum, commonly known as the rusty maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Adiantaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a rusty or reddish hue on the underside. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce new fronds along their length.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
