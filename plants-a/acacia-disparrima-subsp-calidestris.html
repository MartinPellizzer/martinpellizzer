Acacia disparrima subsp. calidestris

Acacia disparrima subsp.

calidestris, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often reduced to spines, a feature that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seed pods are woody and contain small, angular seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood.
