Aegopodium decumbens f. flabellifoliolatum

Aegopodium decumbens f. flabellifoliolatum, commonly known as the fan-leaved groundsel, is a variant of the common groundsel.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
