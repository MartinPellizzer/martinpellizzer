Amelanchier alnifolia var. covillei

Amelanchier alnifolia var. covillei, commonly known as the Saskatoon serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Its berries are valued for their sweet flavor and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
