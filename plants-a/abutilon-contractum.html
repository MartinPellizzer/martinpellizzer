Abutilon contractum

Abutilon contractum, commonly known as the narrowleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, sandy soils. The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves and produces showy, spiny capsules that contain numerous small seeds. It blooms in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators with its yellow flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique foliage and floral display.
