Alpinia smithiae

Alpinia smithiae, commonly known as the Smith's ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention in its native habitat. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and can range in color from white to pale yellow, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

Alpinia smithiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
