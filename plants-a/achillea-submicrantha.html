Achillea submicrantha

Achillea submicrantha, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces dense clusters of small, flat-topped white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is attributed to its high concentration of aromatic compounds.

Morphology

Achillea submicrantha has leaves that are narrow and linear, arranged in a basal rosette, with a dark green color and a slightly hairy surface.

The stem is upright, slender, and has a smooth, pale green bark, growing in a branching pattern. Its flowers are small, yellowish-white composite blooms, arranged in flat-topped clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer, and are pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a small achene with a pappus, and seeds are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and rhizomatous, helping the plant spread and establish in various soil types.
