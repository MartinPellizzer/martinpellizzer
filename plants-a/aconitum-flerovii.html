Aconitum flerovii

Aconitum flerovii, commonly known as the Flerov's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care, as all parts of the plant contain potent alkaloids.
