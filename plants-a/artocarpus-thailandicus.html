Artocarpus thailandicus

Artocarpus thailandicus, commonly known as the Thai breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces a large, spiky fruit that ripens to a yellowish-green color. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
