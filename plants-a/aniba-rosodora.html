Aniba rosodora

Aniba rosodora, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its use in musical instruments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The plant also produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.
