Acer canescens

Acer canescens, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale, papery tissue. The tree typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 feet, with a broad, conical crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with finely serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
