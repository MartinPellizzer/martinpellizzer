Acer canescens

Acer canescens, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its white, peeling bark that reveals layers of pale, papery tissue. The tree typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 feet, with a broad, conical crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with finely serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Native Distribution

Acer canescens is primarily native to the southeastern United States, including parts of Georgia, Florida, and Texas.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetland areas. This species is commonly found in the Appalachian Mountains and the coastal plains. It is also present in some regions of Mexico, particularly in the eastern states.

Acer canescens is an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems in its native range.
