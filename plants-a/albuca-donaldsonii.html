Albuca donaldsonii

Albuca donaldsonii, commonly known as the Donaldson's albuca, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a dormant period during the rainy season. Its bulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
