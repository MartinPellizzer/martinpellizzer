Ajax serratus

Ajax serratus, commonly known as the serrated ajax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
