Astragalus purshii var. purshii

Astragalus purshii var. purshii, commonly known as Pursh's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus purshii var. purshii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
