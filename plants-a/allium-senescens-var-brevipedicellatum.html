Allium senescens var. brevipedicellatum

Allium senescens var. brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the dwarf garlic chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are smaller than those of common garlic and have a mild, garlic-like flavor.

This variety is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and easy cultivation requirements.
