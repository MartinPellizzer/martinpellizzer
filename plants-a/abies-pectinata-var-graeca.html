Abies pectinata var. graeca

Abies pectinata var. graeca, commonly known as the Greek fir, is a variant of the silver fir native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted. The cones are typically reddish-brown and hang downward, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind. This species thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil, often growing at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in both horticulture and natural habitats.
