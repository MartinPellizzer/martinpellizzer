Ayenia peninsularis

Ayenia peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular ayenia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in a fine layer of hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Ayenia peninsularis is adapted to coastal climates and is often found in habitats subject to salt spray and strong winds.
