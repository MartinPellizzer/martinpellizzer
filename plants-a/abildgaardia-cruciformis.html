Abildgaardia cruciformis

Abildgaardia cruciformis, commonly known as the cruciform orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cruciform (cross-shaped) flower structure, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Abildgaardia cruciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
