Aquilegia atrata var. stenosepala

Aquilegia atrata var. stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in spring. The plant has palmate, trifoliate, or pinnate leaves, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
