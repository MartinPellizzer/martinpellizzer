Ailanthus giraldii var. duclouxii

Ailanthus giraldii var. duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's tree of heaven, is a variant of the tree of heaven species native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive bipinnate structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different climatic conditions.

This variant is often noted for its rapid growth and ornamental value, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
