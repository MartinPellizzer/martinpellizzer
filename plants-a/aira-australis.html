Aira australis

Aira australis, commonly known as the Australian grass or slender airgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Aira australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
