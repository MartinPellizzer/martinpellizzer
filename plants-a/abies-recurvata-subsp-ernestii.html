Abies recurvata subsp. ernestii

Abies recurvata subsp.

ernestii, commonly known as the Ernest's fir, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to specific regions of Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, elongated needles and distinctive cone morphology.

The foliage is typically dark green, and the tree exhibits a conical shape with a relatively slow growth rate. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests, where it adapts to cool, moist conditions.
