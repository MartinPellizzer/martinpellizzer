Astragalus thermalis

Astragalus thermalis, commonly known as the thermal milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus thermalis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme climates and its potential ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
