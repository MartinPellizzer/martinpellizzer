Asphodelus albus var. glaucescens

Asphodelus albus var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is known for its resilience to drought conditions.

The glaucous coating on its leaves gives it a distinctive silvery appearance.
