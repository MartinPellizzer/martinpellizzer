Astragalus isophysus

Astragalus isophysus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine environments.

Astragalus isophysus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
