Aspidium aridum

Aspidium aridum, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a species of fern native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, drought-tolerant growth habit. The plant features rhizomatous stems with clustered, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium aridum is well adapted to survive in harsh, low-moisture environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
