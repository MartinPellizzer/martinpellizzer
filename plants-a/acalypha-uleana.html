Acalypha uleana

Acalypha uleana, commonly known as the red-twig dogwood, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that stand out against its green foliage, especially during the winter months. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts that give it a showy appearance. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Acalypha uleana is often used in ornamental gardening for its seasonal color and adaptability to various soil types.
