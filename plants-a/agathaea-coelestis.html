Agathaea coelestis

Agathaea coelestis, commonly known as the celestial agathaea, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of delicate, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a unique appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial shade to flourish.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
