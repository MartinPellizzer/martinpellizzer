Antirrhinum ambiguum

Antirrhinum ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
