Adesmia angustifolia

Adesmia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burro bush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Adesmia angustifolia is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
