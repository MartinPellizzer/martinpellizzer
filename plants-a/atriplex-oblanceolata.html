Atriplex oblanceolata

Atriplex oblanceolata, commonly known as silverleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Atriplex oblanceolata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in degraded landscapes.
