Alchemilla contractilis

Alchemilla contractilis, commonly known as the kidney vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are kidney-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring, typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Alchemilla contractilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
