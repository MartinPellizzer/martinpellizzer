Agave maculosa var. brevituba

Agave maculosa var. brevituba, commonly known as the spotted agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, blue-gray leaves with white to yellowish marginal teeth. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
