Armeria vulgaris var. laucheana

Armeria vulgaris var. laucheana, commonly known as the Lauche's thrift, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has linear, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
