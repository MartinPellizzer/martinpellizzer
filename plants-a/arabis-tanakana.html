Arabis tanakana

Arabis tanakana, commonly known as the Japanese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and appear in clusters during the spring.

Arabis tanakana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
