Astragalus pennellianus

Astragalus pennellianus, commonly known as the western milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Astragalus pennellianus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
