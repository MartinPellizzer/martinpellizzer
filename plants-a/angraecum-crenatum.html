Angraecum crenatum

Angraecum crenatum, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid or the night-blooming orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Angraecum crenatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
