Aster aureus

Aster aureus, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Aster aureus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits to pollinators.
