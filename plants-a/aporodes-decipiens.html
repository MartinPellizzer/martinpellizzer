Aporodes decipiens

Aporodes decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which resembles a wasp, a trait that aids in its pollination strategy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often mistaken for leaves, contributing to its deceptive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its reproductive success is largely dependent on specific pollinators that are attracted by its mimicry of wasp appearance.
