Arabis caucasica var. leiopoda

Arabis caucasica var. leiopoda, commonly known as the Caucasian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb with basal leaves that are typically rounded or elliptic. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
