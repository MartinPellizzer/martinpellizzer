Artemisia sodiroi

Artemisia sodiroi, commonly known as blue snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
