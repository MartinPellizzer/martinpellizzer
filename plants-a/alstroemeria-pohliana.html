Alstroemeria pohliana

Alstroemeria pohliana, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are known for their long-lasting bloom, making it a popular choice in horticulture.

Alstroemeria pohliana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
