Alnus glutinosa f. aurea

Alnus glutinosa f. aurea, commonly known as the golden alder, is a variant of the black alder characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn bright yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

The golden alder is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
