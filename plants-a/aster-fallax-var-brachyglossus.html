Aster fallax var. brachyglossus

Aster fallax var. brachyglossus, commonly known as the short-lipped aster, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers. It is characterized by its short, broad corolla and is often found in sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
