Acacia laricina

Acacia laricina, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Its bark is smooth and often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hairs, contributing to its common name.

Acacia laricina is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
