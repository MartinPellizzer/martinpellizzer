Acacia huarango

Acacia huarango, commonly known as the Andean acacia, is a leguminous tree native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers that are followed by long, slender pods. Acacia huarango is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.

It also plays a crucial role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
