Aster denudatus var. ciliatifolius

Aster denudatus var. ciliatifolius, commonly known as the ciliate-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with ciliate margins, giving it its distinctive appearance.

It produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.
