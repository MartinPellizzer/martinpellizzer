Avena barbata var. caspica

Avena barbata var. caspica, commonly known as the Caspian wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing forage for wildlife.
