Allium stoloniferum

Allium stoloniferum, commonly known as the garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and clusters of small, white or purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs and leaves, which are widely used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Allium stoloniferum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to naturalize in gardens.
