Amorpha laevigata var. typica

Amorpha laevigata var. typica, commonly known as yellow sweetclover, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its seeds are edible and have been used historically in traditional medicine.
