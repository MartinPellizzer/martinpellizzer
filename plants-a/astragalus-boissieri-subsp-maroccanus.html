Astragalus boissieri subsp. maroccanus

Astragalus boissieri subsp.

maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
