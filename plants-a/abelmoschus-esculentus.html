Abelmoschus esculentus

Abelmoschus esculentus, commonly known as okra, is a flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to Africa and thrives in warm, tropical climates. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain fibrous, mucilaginous seeds, which are often used in cooking. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and the stems are hairy and erect.

Okra is valued for its nutritional content and is commonly grown as a vegetable in many parts of the world.
