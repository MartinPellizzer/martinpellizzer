Acrocarpidium scandens

Acrocarpidium scandens, commonly known as the climbing acrocarp, is a unique member of the Acrocarpidae family.

This plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with aerial roots that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. Its leaves are typically small, lanceolate, and arranged alternately along the stem. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and are often clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds via wind, contributing to its spread in forested areas.
