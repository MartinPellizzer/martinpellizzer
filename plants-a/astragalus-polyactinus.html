Astragalus polyactinus

Astragalus polyactinus, commonly known as the many-sepal milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of China and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, compound leaves, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus polyactinus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
