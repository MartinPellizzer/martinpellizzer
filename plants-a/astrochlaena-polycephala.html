Astrochlaena polycephala

Astrochlaena polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient species in its native range.
