Amaryllis radula

Amaryllis radula, commonly known as the slender amaryllis, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears the blooms, which typically appear in late winter or early spring. Amaryllis radula thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
