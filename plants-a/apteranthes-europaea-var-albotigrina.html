Apteranthes europaea var. albotigrina

Apteranthes europaea var. albotigrina, commonly known as the white-tongued bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive white throat. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its attractive blooms and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
