Acacia crassiuscula var. angustifolia

Acacia crassiuscula var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattles, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It features narrow, feather-like phyllodes that are typically light green to grey-green in color. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense spherical heads. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system to access groundwater.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
