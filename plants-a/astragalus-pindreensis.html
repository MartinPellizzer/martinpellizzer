Astragalus pindreensis

Astragalus pindreensis, commonly known as the Pinders' milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small leaflets arranged along the stem.

Astragalus pindreensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
