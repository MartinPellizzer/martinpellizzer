Acer opalus subsp. tauricola

Acer opalus subsp.

tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming more fissured with age.

This species is often found in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained soils in temperate climates.
