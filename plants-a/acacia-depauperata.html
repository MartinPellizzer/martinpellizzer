Acacia depauperata

Acacia depauperata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, compound leaves that are often reduced to spines. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Acacia depauperata plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
