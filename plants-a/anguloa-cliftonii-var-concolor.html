Anguloa cliftonii var. concolor

Anguloa cliftonii var. concolor, commonly known as the Clifton's anguloa, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a unique visual element to the landscape during the colder months.

Anguloa cliftonii var. concolor requires cool, humid conditions and well-drained soil to thrive.
