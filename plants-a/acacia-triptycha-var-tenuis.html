Acacia triptycha var. tenuis

Acacia triptycha var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are narrower than those of the typical acacia species. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the genus Acacia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
