Allium arenicola

Allium arenicola, commonly known as the sand garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a valuable addition to culinary uses.

Allium arenicola is also appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
