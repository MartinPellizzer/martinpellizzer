Alternanthera serpyllifolia

Alternanthera serpyllifolia, commonly known as common sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, delicate leaves that are typically grayish-green with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
