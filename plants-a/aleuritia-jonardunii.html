Aleuritia jonardunii

Aleuritia jonardunii, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces clusters of small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Aleuritia jonardunii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
