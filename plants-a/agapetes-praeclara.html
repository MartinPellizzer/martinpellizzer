Agapetes praeclara

Agapetes praeclara, commonly known as the shining alpine heath, is a species of shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many ericoid shrubs adapted to nutrient-poor soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
