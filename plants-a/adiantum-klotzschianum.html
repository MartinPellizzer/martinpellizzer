Adiantum klotzschianum

Adiantum klotzschianum, commonly known as the Klotzsch's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-directionally divided, with a distinctive rachis that is slightly hairy.

Adiantum klotzschianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
