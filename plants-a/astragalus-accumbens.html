Astragalus accumbens

Astragalus accumbens, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Astragalus accumbens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.
