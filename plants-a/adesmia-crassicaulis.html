Adesmia crassicaulis

Adesmia crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Adesmia crassicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
