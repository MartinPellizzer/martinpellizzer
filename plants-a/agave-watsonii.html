Agave watsonii

Agave watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a spiny margin. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with showy yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. Agave watsonii is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
