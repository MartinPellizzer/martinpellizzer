Agapetes smithiana

Agapetes smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. It produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Agapetes smithiana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
