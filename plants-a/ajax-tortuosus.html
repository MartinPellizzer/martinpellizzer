Ajax tortuosus

Ajax tortuosus, commonly known as the twisted ajax, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate, winding growth pattern.

It is native to specific regions in South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features a compact, shrub-like structure with dense, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its arid environment. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying shades of pink or red, adding ornamental value.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species requiring conservation efforts.
