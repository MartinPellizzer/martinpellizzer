Atriplex sicula

Atriplex sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in saline and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has alternate, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

Atriplex sicula plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in coastal and saline areas.
