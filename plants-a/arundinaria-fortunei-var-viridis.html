Arundinaria fortunei var. viridis

Arundinaria fortunei var. viridis, commonly known as the green bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, green stalks that are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental plants. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is a clump-forming bamboo that grows relatively slowly, making it ideal for smaller garden spaces.

Its foliage remains lush throughout the growing season, adding a fresh, green appearance to the landscape.
