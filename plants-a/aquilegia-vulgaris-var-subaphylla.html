Aquilegia vulgaris var. subaphylla

Aquilegia vulgaris var. subaphylla, commonly known as the lesser garden columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, purple, and yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland edges and meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens.
