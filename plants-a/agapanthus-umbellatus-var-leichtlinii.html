Agapanthus umbellatus var. leichtlinii

Agapanthus umbellatus var. leichtlinii, commonly known as the blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, conical clusters of sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in summer, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are narrow and grass-like, forming a compact rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
