Asplenium nigrocoloratum

Asplenium nigrocoloratum, commonly known as the black-stem spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in small clusters called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions.

Asplenium nigrocoloratum is a cryptogamic plant that plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for other organisms.
