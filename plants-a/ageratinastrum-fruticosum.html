Ageratinastrum fruticosum

Ageratinastrum fruticosum, commonly known as wild blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features a sprawling growth habit with woody stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, blue to purple flower clusters, which are arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ageratinastrum fruticosum is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands.
