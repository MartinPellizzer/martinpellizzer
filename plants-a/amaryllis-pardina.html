Amaryllis pardina

Amaryllis pardina, commonly known as the pink bell amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are broad, strap-like, and often persist through the winter, giving the plant a unique appearance. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal care once established.
