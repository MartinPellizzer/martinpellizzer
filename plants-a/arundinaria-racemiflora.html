Arundinaria racemiflora

Arundinaria racemiflora, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically arranged in racemes, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Arundinaria racemiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
