Aquilegia bauhini var. thalictrifolia

Aquilegia bauhini var. thalictrifolia, commonly known as the thalictrifolia aquilegia, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has compound leaves with finely divided lobes, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained, fertile soil and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
