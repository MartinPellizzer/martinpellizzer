Actinostemon lasiocarpus

Actinostemon lasiocarpus, commonly known as the silky-fruited bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves and stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.
