Antherura rubra

Antherura rubra, commonly known as the red antherura, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects due to their vibrant color and nectar production.

Antherura rubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
