Adesmia bonariensis

Adesmia bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and beans, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, woody pods that split open when mature.

Adesmia bonariensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species found in various regions of Argentina and Chile.
