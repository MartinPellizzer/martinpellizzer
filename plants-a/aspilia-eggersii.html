Aspilia eggersii

Aspilia eggersii, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Aspilia eggersii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
