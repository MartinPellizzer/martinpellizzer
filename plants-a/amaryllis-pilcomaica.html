Amaryllis pilcomaica

Amaryllis pilcomaica, commonly known as the Colombian amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Andes mountains of Colombia.

It features tall, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white or grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, Amaryllis pilcomaica is often cultivated for both its beauty and its ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
