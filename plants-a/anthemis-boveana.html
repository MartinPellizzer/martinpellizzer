Anthemis boveana

Anthemis boveana, commonly known as the Bovean ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers are yellowish with a central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

It is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
