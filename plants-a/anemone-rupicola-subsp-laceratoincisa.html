Anemone rupicola subsp. laceratoincisa

Anemone rupicola subsp.

laceratoincisa, commonly known as the jagged-leaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically deeply lobed and have a distinctive jagged or notched appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
