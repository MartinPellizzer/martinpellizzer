Arisaema kishidae var. minus

Arisaema kishidae var. minus, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, white to cream-colored flowers, which are followed by berries that turn red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and flowering structure.
