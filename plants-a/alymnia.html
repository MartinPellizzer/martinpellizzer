Alymnia

Alymnia, commonly known as the common alymnia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Alymnia thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
