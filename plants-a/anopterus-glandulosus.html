Anopterus glandulosus

Anopterus glandulosus, commonly known as the hairy anopterus, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Anopteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy or glandular leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a fragrant aroma.

Anopterus glandulosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
