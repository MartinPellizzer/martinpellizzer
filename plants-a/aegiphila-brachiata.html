Aegiphila brachiata

Aegiphila brachiata, commonly known as the Brazilian balm, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Aegiphila brachiata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Morphology

Aegiphila brachiata has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a bluish-green color and a slightly hairy surface.

The stem is upright and woody, with a rough, grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, with pinkish-white petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the spring and summer months, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small capsule that releases seeds through mechanical means when mature.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to rocky soils and arid conditions.
