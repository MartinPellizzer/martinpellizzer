Aglaia membranifolia

Aglaia membranifolia, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other hardwood trees. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically odd-pinnate with elongated leaflets. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often white or pale yellow, and it produces woody fruits containing smooth, black seeds.

Aglaia membranifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
