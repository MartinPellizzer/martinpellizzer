Acanthus furcatus

Acanthus furcatus, commonly known as the forked acanthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of a ship's prow. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
