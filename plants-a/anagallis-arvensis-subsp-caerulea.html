Anagallis arvensis subsp. caerulea

Anagallis arvensis subsp.

caerulea, commonly known as the blue-eyed weed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, bright blue or violet flowers that bloom from spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils, often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its flowers are typically less than 1 cm in diameter, with five petals and a prominent yellow center.

It is an annual plant that spreads through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
