Anaphalis griffithii

Anaphalis griffithii, commonly known as the silverleaf frostflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is notable for its silvery, woolly foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Anaphalis griffithii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.
