Andropogon sericeus f. micranthus

Andropogon sericeus f. micranthus, commonly known as the silky grass or silvergrass, is a variant of the switchgrass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in full sun, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, including erosion control and wildlife habitat support.
