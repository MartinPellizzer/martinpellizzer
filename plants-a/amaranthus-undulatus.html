Amaranthus undulatus

Amaranthus undulatus, commonly known as the wavycup amaranth, is a species of flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, undulating bracts that form a cup-like structure around the inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value and edible seeds.

Amaranthus undulatus is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions.
