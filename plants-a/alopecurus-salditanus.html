Alopecurus salditanus

Alopecurus salditanus, commonly known as the Salditans sedge, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet environments and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
