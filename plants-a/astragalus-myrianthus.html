Astragalus myrianthus

Astragalus myrianthus, commonly known as the many-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus myrianthus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
