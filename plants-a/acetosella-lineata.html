Acetosella lineata

Acetosella lineata, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny white or pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
