Anthericum ramiferum

Anthericum ramiferum, commonly known as the fragrant lily or sweet flag, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or greenish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a rhizomatous root system, allowing it to spread and colonize moist soils effectively. Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used historically in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Anthericum ramiferum thrives in shallow water or damp environments, making it a common species in marshes and along riverbanks.
