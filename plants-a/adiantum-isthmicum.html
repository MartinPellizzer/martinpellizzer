Adiantum isthmicum

Adiantum isthmicum, commonly known as the Isthmian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi-lobed. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rock faces or in forest understories. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, finely divided structure.

Adiantum isthmicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
