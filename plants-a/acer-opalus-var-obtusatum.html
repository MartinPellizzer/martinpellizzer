Acer opalus var. obtusatum

Acer opalus var. obtusatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, ornamental foliage that ranges from green to red, orange, and yellow depending on the season. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
