Aeonium × jacobsenii

Aeonium × jacobsenii, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a hybrid succulent native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that often develop a striking red or pink hue at the tips when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, yellowish flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with ample sunlight.

Aeonium × jacobsenii is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
