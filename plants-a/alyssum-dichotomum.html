Alyssum dichotomum

Alyssum dichotomum, commonly known as the forked alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Alyssum dichotomum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
