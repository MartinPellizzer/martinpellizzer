Amaranthus hybridus f. pseudoretroflexus

Amaranthus hybridus f. pseudoretroflexus, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth species characterized by its distinctive red pigmentation in the roots and stems.

This plant is an annual herb native to the Americas and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields. It features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, green to red flowers in dense clusters. The plant is notable for its high nutritional value, containing proteins, vitamins, and minerals, making it a potential crop for food and forage.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and agricultural studies.
