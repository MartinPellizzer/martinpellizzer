Abies subalpina pendula

Abies subalpina pendula, commonly known as the weeping subalpine fir, is a rare and distinctive conifer native to the high mountain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, giving it a unique, almost ethereal appearance. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly curved, and it produces small, cone-like structures that mature to release seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
