Abronia nana var. nana

Abronia nana var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf sandverbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
