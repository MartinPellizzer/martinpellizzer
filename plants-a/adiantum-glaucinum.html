Adiantum glaucinum

Adiantum glaucinum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically grayish-blue in color due to the presence of trichomes on the leaf surfaces. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous upright, branching stems.

Adiantum glaucinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
