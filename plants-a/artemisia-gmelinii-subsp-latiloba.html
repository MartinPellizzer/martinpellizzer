Artemisia gmelinii subsp. latiloba

Artemisia gmelinii subsp.

latiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

The plant features silvery-green, aromatic leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent. Its inflorescences consist of small, yellowish-gray flower clusters that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
