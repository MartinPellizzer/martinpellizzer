Angraecum filipes

Angraecum filipes, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a deep purple or black center. The plant produces a single, slender stem that bears a cluster of small, fragrant flowers. Angraecum filipes thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a rare and highly sought-after species in botanical collections.
