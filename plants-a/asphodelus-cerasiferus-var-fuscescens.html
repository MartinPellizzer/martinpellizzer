Asphodelus cerasiferus var. fuscescens

Asphodelus cerasiferus var. fuscescens, commonly known as the dark red asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect flower stalks bearing clusters of pale pink to red flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is known for its resilience in harsh environments.

The variety fuscescens is distinguished by its darker, more intense flower color compared to the typical form.
