Alyssum tortuosum f. perlatum

Alyssum tortuosum f. perlatum, commonly known as the silverleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
