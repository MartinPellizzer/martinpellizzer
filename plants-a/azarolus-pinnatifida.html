Azarolus pinnatifida

Azarolus pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved azarolus, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that are pinnately arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. Azarolus pinnatifida thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
