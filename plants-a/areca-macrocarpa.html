Areca macrocarpa

Areca macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. Areca macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
