Acer neomexicanum

Acer neomexicanum, commonly known as the New Mexico maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, red samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its unique appearance and seasonal color changes make it a notable species in its native habitat.
