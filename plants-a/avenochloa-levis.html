Avenochloa levis

Avenochloa levis, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown spikelets. It is often found in wetland habitats and can thrive in flooded conditions.

Avenochloa levis is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
