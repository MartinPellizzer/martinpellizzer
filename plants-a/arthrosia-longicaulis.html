Arthrosia longicaulis

Arthrosia longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed arthrosia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

Arthrosia longicaulis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in temperate climates.
