Aster elongatus var. spathulifolius

Aster elongatus var. spathulifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, blue to violet flower heads. The leaves are lanceolate to spatulate in shape, with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
