Alysicarpus glumaceus f. pilosulus

Alysicarpus glumaceus f. pilosulus, commonly known as the hairy burr sida, is a variant of the species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny capsules that enclose its seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climatic conditions.

The hairy texture of its foliage and fruits is a notable distinguishing trait.
