Adoxa moschatellina var. japonica

Adoxa moschatellina var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese mosquito plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features delicate, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that resemble tiny mosquitoes, hence its common name. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique appearance and pleasant scent make it a sought-after ornamental plant in gardens.
