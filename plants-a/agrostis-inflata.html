Agrostis inflata

Agrostis inflata, commonly known as the inflated grass or inflated silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated spikelets that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Agrostis inflata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various invertebrates.
