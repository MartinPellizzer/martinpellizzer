Artemisia genipi var. integrifolia

Artemisia genipi var. integrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, branched stems. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

The leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.
