Alectorolophus ovifugus

Alectorolophus ovifugus, commonly known as the eggplant orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often resemble egg shapes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil in its natural habitat.

Alectorolophus ovifugus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
