Alsophila swartziana

Alsophila swartziana, commonly known as the Swartz's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that supports its growth and helps anchor it in moist, shaded environments. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Alsophila swartziana is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
