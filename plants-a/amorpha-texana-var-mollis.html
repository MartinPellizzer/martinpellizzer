Amorpha texana var. mollis

Amorpha texana var. mollis, commonly known as velvet mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Velvet mesquite is often used for erosion control and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
