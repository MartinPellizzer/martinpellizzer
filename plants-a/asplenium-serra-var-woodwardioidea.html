Asplenium serra var. woodwardioidea

Asplenium serra var. woodwardioidea, commonly known as the serrate spleenwort, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative epiphytic plant.
