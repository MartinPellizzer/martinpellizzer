Abarema multifoliolata

Abarema multifoliolata, commonly known as the Brazilian fern or the giant fern, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and it has a unique ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with bacteria. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets arranged in a delicate, lace-like pattern.
