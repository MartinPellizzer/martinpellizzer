Astragalus rariflorus

Astragalus rariflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus rariflorus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
