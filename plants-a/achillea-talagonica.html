Achillea talagonica

Achillea talagonica, commonly known as the Balkan yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
