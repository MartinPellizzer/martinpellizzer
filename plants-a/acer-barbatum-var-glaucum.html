Acer barbatum var. glaucum

Acer barbatum var. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous maple, is a variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Japan and thrives in cool, humid climates. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a serrated margin and a striking coloration that ranges from pale green to bluish-gray. This plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique leaf color and seasonal changes, which add visual interest throughout the growing season.
