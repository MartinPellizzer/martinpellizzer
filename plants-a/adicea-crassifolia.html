Adicea crassifolia

Adicea crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved adicea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Adicea crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
