Astragalus raswendicus var. charsanicus

Astragalus raswendicus var. charsanicus, commonly known as the Afghan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological features and ecological preferences compared to other Astragalus species.
