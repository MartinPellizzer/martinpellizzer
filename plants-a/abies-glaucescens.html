Abies glaucescens

Abies glaucescens, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and distinctive blue-gray needles that are coated with a waxy cuticle. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, and it is typically found in high-altitude environments with cold, moist climates. Its foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.

The blue spruce is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in many indigenous communities.
