Azalea taliensis

Azalea taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

Azalea taliensis prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
