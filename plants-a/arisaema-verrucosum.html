Arisaema verrucosum

Arisaema verrucosum, commonly known as the rough arum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spadix-shaped inflorescence surrounded by a broad, heart-shaped spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are large, arrowhead-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Arisaema verrucosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral structure.
