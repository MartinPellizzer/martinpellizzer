Artemisia campestris subsp. maritima

Artemisia campestris subsp.

maritima, commonly known as sea wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands.

The plant has aromatic, silver-gray foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and its historical use in traditional medicine.
