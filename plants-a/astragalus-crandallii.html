Astragalus crandallii

Astragalus crandallii, commonly known as Crandall's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces cylindrical, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe environments.

Astragalus crandallii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
