Asphodelus cerasifer f. validus

Asphodelus cerasifer f. validus, commonly known as the cherry-fruit asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and tall, branching inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by distinctive, cherry-red berries that are a notable feature of the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
