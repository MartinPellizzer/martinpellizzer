Anemone sarracenensis

Anemone sarracenensis, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has finely divided, feathery leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
