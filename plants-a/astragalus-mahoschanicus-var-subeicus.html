Astragalus mahoschanicus var. subeicus

Astragalus mahoschanicus var. subeicus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with deep taproots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
