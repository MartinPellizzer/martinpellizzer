Amaranthus albus var. pubescens

Amaranthus albus var. pubescens, commonly known as white amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly sweet flavor, making it a useful crop in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
