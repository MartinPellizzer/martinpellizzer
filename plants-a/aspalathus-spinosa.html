Aspalathus spinosa

Aspalathus spinosa, commonly known as the mountain pea, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Aspalathus spinosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
