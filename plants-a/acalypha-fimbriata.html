Acalypha fimbriata

Acalypha fimbriata, commonly known as the silky fanleaf or fimbriated spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

Acalypha fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
