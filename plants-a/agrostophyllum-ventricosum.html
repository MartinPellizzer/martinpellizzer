Agrostophyllum ventricosum

Agrostophyllum ventricosum, commonly known as the woolly mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, leathery, ovate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are red when mature. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for native birds and insects, and its presence can indicate the health of the host tree.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
