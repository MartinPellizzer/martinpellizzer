Aridaria acuminata

Aridaria acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed arid plant, is a succulent native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the rainy season. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater.

Aridaria acuminata is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and resilience.
