Abies concolor var. baja-californica

Abies concolor var. baja-californica, commonly known as the coastal fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The needles are short and stout, with a bluish-white underside, and the cones are typically pendulous and elongated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to the mild, coastal climate of its native range.

This variety is often found in mixed coniferous forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
