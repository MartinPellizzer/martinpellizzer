Acacia prominens var. latifolia

Acacia prominens var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in spirals along the stems. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
