Anthurium rigidissimum var. mutatum

Anthurium rigidissimum var. mutatum, commonly known as the Mutated Anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a reddish or maroon underside. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
