Acacia subfalcata

Acacia subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, compound leaves that are typically green and have a distinctive, narrow shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in the spring and attract a variety of pollinators. Acacia subfalcata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
