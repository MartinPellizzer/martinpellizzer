Acalypha microphylla var. microphylla

Acalypha microphylla var. microphylla, commonly known as the littleleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
