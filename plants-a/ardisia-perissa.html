Ardisia perissa

Ardisia perissa, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, and it blooms with small, white flowers during the summer months.

Ardisia perissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
