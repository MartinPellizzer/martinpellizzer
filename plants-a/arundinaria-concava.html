Arundinaria concava

Arundinaria concava, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Arundinaria concava is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
