Andropogon verticilliflorus

Andropogon verticilliflorus, commonly known as curlytop bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often rolled at the tips. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of dry, open habitats.

Curlytop bluestem is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
