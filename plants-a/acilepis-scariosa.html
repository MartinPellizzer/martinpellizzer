Acilepis scariosa

Acilepis scariosa, commonly known as the brittleleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers surrounded by pale green ray florets. The plant has hairy, often brittle leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands.

Acilepis scariosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
