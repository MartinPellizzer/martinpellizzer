Aphanomyrtus forbesii

Aphanomyrtus forbesii, commonly known as Forbes' tea-tree, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
