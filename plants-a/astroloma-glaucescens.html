Astroloma glaucescens

Astroloma glaucescens, commonly known as the bluebells of the south, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland regions. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Astroloma glaucescens is well adapted to arid conditions and requires minimal water once established.
