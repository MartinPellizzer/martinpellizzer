Acacia pubescens

Acacia pubescens, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. Acacia pubescens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
