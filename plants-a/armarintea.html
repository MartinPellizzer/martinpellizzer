Armarintea

Armarintea, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its distinctive silvery foliage.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, white hairs that give it a shimmering appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, adapting well to dry and hot climates.

Armarintea is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
