Aletris lucida

Aletris lucida, commonly known as white fringed fern, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant features pale green, strap-like leaves that are often adorned with white or pale margins. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered spikes during the spring.

Aletris lucida thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
