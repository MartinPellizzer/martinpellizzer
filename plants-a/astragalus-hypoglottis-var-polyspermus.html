Astragalus hypoglottis var. polyspermus

Astragalus hypoglottis var. polyspermus, commonly known as the many-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant produces multiple seeds per pod, which contributes to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.

Astragalus hypoglottis var. polyspermus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for wildlife.
