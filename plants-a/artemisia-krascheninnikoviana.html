Artemisia krascheninnikoviana

Artemisia krascheninnikoviana, commonly known as Russian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the cold climates of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves with a distinctive, slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
