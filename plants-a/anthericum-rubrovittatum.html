Anthericum rubrovittatum

Anthericum rubrovittatum, commonly known as the red-vined lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant's most distinctive feature is its red-veined, strap-like foliage, which adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
