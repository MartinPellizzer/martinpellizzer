Anchusa officinalis var. velenovskyi

Anchusa officinalis var. velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky's flowering spurge, is a perennial herb native to the Balkans and parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens despite its potential toxicity.
