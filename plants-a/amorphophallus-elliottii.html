Amorphophallus elliottii

Amorphophallus elliottii, commonly known as the Elliott's elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy underground tuber, which can grow to significant sizes. The plant produces striking, large inflorescences with purple spathes and white flowers, often resembling a giant, fleshy flower. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tuber is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
