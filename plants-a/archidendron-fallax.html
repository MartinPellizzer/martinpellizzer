Archidendron fallax

Archidendron fallax, commonly known as the wild senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
