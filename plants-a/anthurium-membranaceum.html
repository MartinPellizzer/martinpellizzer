Anthurium membranaceum

Anthurium membranaceum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the modified leaves that house the actual flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium membranaceum is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
