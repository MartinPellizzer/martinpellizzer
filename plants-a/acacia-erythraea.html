Acacia erythraea

Acacia erythraea, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are dispersed by wind.

Acacia erythraea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
