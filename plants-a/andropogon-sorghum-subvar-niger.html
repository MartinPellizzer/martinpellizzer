Andropogon sorghum subvar. niger

Andropogon sorghum subvar. niger, commonly known as black sorghum, is a tall, robust grass native to Africa and cultivated in warm climates.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it a hardy crop for arid regions. Its seeds are used for food, animal feed, and industrial purposes, while the stalks are utilized for construction and fuel.

The plant's botanical structure supports its adaptability and economic importance in various agricultural systems.
