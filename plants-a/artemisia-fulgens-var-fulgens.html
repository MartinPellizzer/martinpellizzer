Artemisia fulgens var. fulgens

Artemisia fulgens var. fulgens, commonly known as the golden wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a strong, minty scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by Native American tribes for various health purposes.
