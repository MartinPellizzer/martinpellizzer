Ardisia crenata

Ardisia crenata, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for humans and birds. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy fruit, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Ardisia crenata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.
