Abuta brunnescens

Abuta brunnescens, commonly known as the rough abuta, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Abuta brunnescens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.
