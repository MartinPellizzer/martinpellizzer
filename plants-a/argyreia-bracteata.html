Argyreia bracteata

Argyreia bracteata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Argyreia bracteata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
