Aechmea leucostachys

Aechmea leucostachys, commonly known as the white-stemmed bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

The plant produces striking inflorescences with white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically long, narrow, and have a waxy coating, which helps in water retention.

Aechmea leucostachys is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Morphology
