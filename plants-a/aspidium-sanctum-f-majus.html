Aspidium sanctum f. majus

Aspidium sanctum f. majus, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

This fern plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
