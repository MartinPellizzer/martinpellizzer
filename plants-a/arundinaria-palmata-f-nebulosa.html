Arundinaria palmata f. nebulosa

Arundinaria palmata f. nebulosa, commonly known as the clouded bamboo, is a rare and striking variant of the bamboo species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale green to almost white streaked culms that give it a unique visual appearance. This fern-like plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and Georgia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a desirable ornamental plant.

Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often variegated, adding to its aesthetic appeal in gardens and natural landscapes.
