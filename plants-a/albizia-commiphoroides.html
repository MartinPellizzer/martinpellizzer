Albizia commiphoroides

Albizia commiphoroides, commonly known as the African honeythorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Albizia commiphoroides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
