Anthemis carpatica

Anthemis carpatica, commonly known as Carpathian ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter scent.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
