Acianthera biseta

Acianthera biseta, commonly known as the two-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Acianthera biseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
