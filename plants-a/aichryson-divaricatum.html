Aichryson divaricatum

Aichryson divaricatum, commonly known as the silver bush or rock fig, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, shrub-like growth with dense, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
