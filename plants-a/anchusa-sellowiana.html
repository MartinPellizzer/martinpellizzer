Anchusa sellowiana

Anchusa sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and ability to thrive in various garden conditions.
