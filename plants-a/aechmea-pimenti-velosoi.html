Aechmea pimenti-velosoi

Aechmea pimenti-velosoi, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescence that resembles an orchid, with long, spiky bracts in shades of red, pink, and orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, waxy leaves that collect rainwater, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and insect life. Its flowering period typically occurs during the wet season, and it produces small, white flowers that bloom sequentially along the central spike.

Aechmea pimenti-velosoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right growing conditions.

Morphology

Aechmea pimenti-velosoi has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and dark green with a waxy texture, arranged in a compact spiral at the base.

The plant has a short, thick stem with a smooth, grayish bark that grows underground. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, with white and pink flowers that bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that disperses seeds by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil.
