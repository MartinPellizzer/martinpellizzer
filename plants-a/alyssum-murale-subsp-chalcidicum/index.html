Alyssum Murale Subsp. Chalcidicum

Alyssum murale subsp. chalcidicum is a lesser-known member of the Brassicaceae family known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, this plant thrives in rocky and coastal environments. Its small, clustered flowers range in color from white to pale yellow, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Despite its modest size, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity. This subspecies is often overlooked but deserves greater recognition for its resilience and beauty.
