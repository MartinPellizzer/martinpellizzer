Arctottonia sempervirens

Arctottonia sempervirens, commonly known as the American hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by oval, woody fruits called nuts, which are encased in a leafy bract. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
