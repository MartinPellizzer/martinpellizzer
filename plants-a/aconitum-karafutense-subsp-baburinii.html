Aconitum karafutense subsp. baburinii

Aconitum karafutense subsp.

baburinii, commonly known as the Karafutense monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tien Shan and Pamir areas.

This subspecies is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are arranged in a helmet-like structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in cool, moist environments.

Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves, and it is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical and cautionary interest.
