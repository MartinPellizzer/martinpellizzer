Alyssum calycinum var. virgatum

Alyssum calycinum var. virgatum, commonly known as the sweet alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged flowering period and ability to attract beneficial insects.
