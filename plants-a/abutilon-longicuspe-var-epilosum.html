Abutilon longicuspe var. epilosum

Abutilon longicuspe var. epilosum, commonly known as the silky abutilon, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem with a corky texture, and its leaves are ovate with a hairy underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
