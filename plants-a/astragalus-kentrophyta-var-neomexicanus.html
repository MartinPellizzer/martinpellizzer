Astragalus kentrophyta var. neomexicanus

Astragalus kentrophyta var. neomexicanus, commonly known as the New Mexico milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky slopes and sandy soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many plants known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities.
