Aster longifolius var. brevirameus

Aster longifolius var. brevirameus, commonly known as the short-stemmed aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the fall. It is characterized by its short, sturdy stems and is often found in disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
