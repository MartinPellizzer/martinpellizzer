Acer truncatum f. cordatum

Acer truncatum f. cordatum, commonly known as the heart-shaped maple, is a unique variant of the cutleaf maple.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost palmate appearance. The plant is native to eastern China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its foliage displays vibrant colors in the fall, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
