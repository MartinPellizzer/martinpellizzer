Actaea arguta var. eburnea

Actaea arguta var. eburnea, commonly known as the white bead barren, is a variant of the bead barren plant native to parts of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem topped with a single, showy white or pale pink flower. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is notable for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
