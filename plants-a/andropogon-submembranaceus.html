Andropogon submembranaceus

Andropogon submembranaceus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with a thin, membranous sheath, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife.
