Arenaria gracilis var. glandulifera

Arenaria gracilis var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically linear and covered with fine, glandular hairs, which give the plant its varietal name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
