Adhatoda minor

Adhatoda minor, commonly known as lesser adhatoda, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

Adhatoda minor is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional systems of medicine.
