Aronia arbutifolia var. glabra

Aronia arbutifolia var. glabra, commonly known as smooth flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
