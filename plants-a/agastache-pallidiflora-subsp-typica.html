Agastache pallidiflora subsp. typica

Agastache pallidiflora subsp.

typica, commonly known as the paleflower hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of pale lavender to white tubular flowers.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
