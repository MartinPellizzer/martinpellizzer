Acacia rufa

Acacia rufa, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Acacia rufa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry climates.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and animal forage.
