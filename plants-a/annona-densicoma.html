Annona densicoma

Annona densicoma, commonly known as the dense-leaved custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is typically yellow or green, with a creamy, custard-like pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is edible, though its texture and flavor can vary depending on the maturity and variety.
