Atractylis humilis

Atractylis humilis, commonly known as field eryngo, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a compact, low-growing stature with hairy stems and basal leaves that are usually lobed or divided. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Atractylis humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
