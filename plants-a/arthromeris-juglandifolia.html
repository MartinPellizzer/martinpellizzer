Arthromeris juglandifolia

Arthromeris juglandifolia, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds. Its bark is thick and often covered in a layer of cork-like material.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
