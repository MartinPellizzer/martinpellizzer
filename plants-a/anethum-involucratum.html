Anethum involucratum

Anethum involucratum, commonly known as wild celery or Persian cumin, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly fuzzy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small, oval, and have a distinctive, pungent aroma similar to cumin.

Anethum involucratum is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
