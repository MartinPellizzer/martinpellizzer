Astragalus kentrophyta subsp. implexus

Astragalus kentrophyta subsp.

implexus, commonly known as the twisted milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The subspecies implexus is distinguished by its twisted, coiled stems and is relatively rare compared to other Astragalus species.
