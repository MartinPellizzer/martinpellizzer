Amomum gramineum

Amomum gramineum, commonly known as the Vietnamese cardamom or green cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in spikes. Its rhizomes are the primary part used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical characteristics of Amomum gramineum make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal applications.
