Amaranthus blitum var. pseudogracilis

Amaranthus blitum var. pseudogracilis, commonly known as the red-rooted amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with reddish veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.

This variety is also valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high protein and mineral levels.
