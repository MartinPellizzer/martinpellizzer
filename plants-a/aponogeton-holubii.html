Aponogeton holubii

Aponogeton holubii, commonly known as the Holub's arrowhead, is a flowering aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with long, slender stems that can extend several meters underwater. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to ponds and water gardens. Aponogeton holubii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic ecosystems.
