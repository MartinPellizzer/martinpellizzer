Allantodia subsilvatica

Allantodia subsilvatica, commonly known as the forest allantodia, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring, attracting pollinators like bees.

Allantodia subsilvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
