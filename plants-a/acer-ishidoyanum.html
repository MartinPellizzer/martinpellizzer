Acer ishidoyanum

Acer ishidoyanum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant red, orange, and yellow colors in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple, and serrated leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras.

Acer ishidoyanum thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
