Alyssum granatense var. marizii

Alyssum granatense var. marizii, commonly known as the Granada alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
