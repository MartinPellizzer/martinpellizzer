Aronia racemosa

Aronia racemosa, commonly known as the chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces clusters of small, dark red to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant has compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets, and its flowers are white and arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The berries are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their high nutritional value.
