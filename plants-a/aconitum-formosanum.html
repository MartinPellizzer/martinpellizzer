Aconitum formosanum

Aconitum formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant features large, deeply lobed leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
