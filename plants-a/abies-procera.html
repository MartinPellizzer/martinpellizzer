Abies procera

Abies procera, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The needles are long, flat, and have a silvery-white underside, giving the tree its common name. It produces cones that hang downward, containing winged seeds dispersed by wind.

This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
