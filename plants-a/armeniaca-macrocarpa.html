Armeniaca macrocarpa

Armeniaca macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It produces showy, pink to white flowers in early spring, followed by sweet, juicy fruits that ripen in summer. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit is valued for its flavor and is used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Armeniaca macrocarpa is also cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to many regions around the world.
