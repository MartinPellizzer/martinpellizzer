Asclepias moorei

Asclepias moorei, commonly known as Moore's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its showy, orange-tipped clusters of flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, a feature common to many milkweed species. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Asclepias moorei plays an important role in supporting pollinators, particularly monarch butterflies, by providing nectar and serving as a host plant for their larvae.
