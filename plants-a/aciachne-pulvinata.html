Aciachne pulvinata

Aciachne pulvinata, commonly known as the dwarf fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, tufted shoots that grow close to the ground, forming dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-green hue.

Aciachne pulvinata is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact form and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
