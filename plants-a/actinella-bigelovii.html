Actinella bigelovii

Actinella bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's actinella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its showy yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, bract-like structures around its flowers.
