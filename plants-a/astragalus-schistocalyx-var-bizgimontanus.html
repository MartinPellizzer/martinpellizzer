Astragalus schistocalyx var. bizgimontanus

Astragalus schistocalyx var. bizgimontanus, commonly known as the Bizgimontan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, yellow to reddish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
