Acacia heteroneura var. petila

Acacia heteroneura var. petila, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and narrow, greenish-yellow phyllodes that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and as a garden plant.
