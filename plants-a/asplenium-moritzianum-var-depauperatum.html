Asplenium moritzianum var. depauperatum

Asplenium moritzianum var. depauperatum, commonly known as the poor fern, is a rare and delicate species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. This fern thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in rocky crevices or on tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
