Adenaria purpurata var. australis

Adenaria purpurata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces striking purple to pink flowers in terminal panicles, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
