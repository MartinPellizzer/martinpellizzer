Acisanthera paraguayensis

Acisanthera paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguayan acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such climates.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods, which can remain on the plant for extended periods before dispersing.
