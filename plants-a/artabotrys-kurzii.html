Artabotrys kurzii

Artabotrys kurzii, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its ornamental value is highly appreciated in horticulture due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
