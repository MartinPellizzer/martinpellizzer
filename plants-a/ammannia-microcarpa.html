Ammannia microcarpa

Ammannia microcarpa, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom from June to September.

Ammannia microcarpa plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
