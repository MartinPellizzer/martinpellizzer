Aconitum mitakense

Aconitum mitakense, commonly known as the Mitakense monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features a robust rhizome system, which allows it to survive in harsh, cold climates. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aconitum mitakense is also noted for its toxic alkaloids, which have historically been used in traditional medicine.
