Amomum tibeticum

Amomum tibeticum, commonly known as Tibetan cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit, a three-lobed capsule, contains aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Amomum tibeticum is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in Tibetan and Chinese herbal practices.
