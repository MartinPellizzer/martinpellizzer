Allium sarawschanicum

Allium sarawschanicum, commonly known as the Sarawak onion, is a bulbous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
