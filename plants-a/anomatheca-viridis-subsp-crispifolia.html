Anomatheca viridis subsp. crispifolia

Anomatheca viridis subsp.

crispifolia, commonly known as the curlyleaf greenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curled or crinkled at the edges.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its distinctive foliage and compact growth form.
