Asphodelus madeirensis

Asphodelus madeirensis, commonly known as the Madeira asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madeira.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched flowering stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Asphodelus madeirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
