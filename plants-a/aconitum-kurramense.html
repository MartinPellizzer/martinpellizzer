Aconitum kurramense

Aconitum kurramense, commonly known as the Kurram aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of Pakistan.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear showy, blue to violet flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence. The plant has palmately compound leaves with lobed segments, and its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
