Aster dumosus var. albus

Aster dumosus var. albus, commonly known as the white dwarf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense, compact clumps of foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
