Achyrospermum radicans var. grandibracteatum

Achyrospermum radicans var. grandibracteatum, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which can extend from spring through summer.
