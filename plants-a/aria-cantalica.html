Aria cantalica

Aria cantalica, commonly known as the Cantalica orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring well-drained and sunny conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the dry, mountainous environment.

Aria cantalica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
