Adnaria pulchra

Adnaria pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful sea anemone, is a marine invertebrate belonging to the family Adrianoideae.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful tentacles that surround a central oral disc, used for capturing prey. This species thrives in shallow coastal waters, often found in rocky substrates or coral reefs. Its body structure includes a cylindrical polyp with a gastrovascular cavity for digestion.

Adnaria pulchra plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by contributing to the food web and providing habitat for smaller organisms.
