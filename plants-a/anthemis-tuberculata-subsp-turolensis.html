Anthemis tuberculata subsp. turolensis

Anthemis tuberculata subsp.

turolensis, commonly known as the Turolan anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-white composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, open habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
