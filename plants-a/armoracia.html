Armoracia

Armoracia, commonly known as horseradish, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which also includes mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are the primary source of its pungent flavor. The leaves are dark green, lobed, and slightly hairy, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes.

Armoracia is often cultivated for its root, which is used in cooking and traditional medicine.
