Amphidasya brevidentata

Amphidasya brevidentata, commonly known as the narrow-toothed daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, where it typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, with narrow, toothed leaves. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
