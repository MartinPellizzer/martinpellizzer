Amphiscopia strobilacea

Amphiscopia strobilacea, commonly known as the pineapple weed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, oval leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster resembling a pineapple, hence its common name. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Amphiscopia strobilacea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
