Aspalathus pinea

Aspalathus pinea, commonly known as African red bushwillow, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, winged seeds when mature.

Aspalathus pinea is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
