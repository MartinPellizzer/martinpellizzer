Alchornea nemoralis var. intermedia

Alchornea nemoralis var. intermedia, commonly known as the variable alchornea, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of xerophytic vegetation.
