Antonia

Antonia, commonly known as the antonia plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the genus *Antonia* in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Antonia is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
