Anemone × lipsiensis

Anemone × lipsiensis, commonly known as the hybrid anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 12–24 inches and features finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Anemone × lipsiensis is known for its early spring blooms and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
