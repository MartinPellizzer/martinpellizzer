Andropogon asper

Andropogon asper, commonly known as stiff grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, rigid leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Andropogon asper plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
