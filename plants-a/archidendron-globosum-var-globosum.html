Archidendron globosum var. globosum

Archidendron globosum var. globosum, commonly known as the globular acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, globular crown and compound leaves with small, clustered leaflets. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its unique form and seasonal foliage.
