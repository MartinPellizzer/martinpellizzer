Acanthopanax giraldii var. inermis

Acanthopanax giraldii var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth Chinese staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its unarmed stems, distinguishing it from other varieties of Acanthopanax. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
