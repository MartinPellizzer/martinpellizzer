Aster spectabilis var. suffultus

Aster spectabilis var. suffultus, commonly known as the New England aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its flowering stems are usually hairy and bear numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

This variety is known for its robust growth and adaptability to a range of soil types, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
