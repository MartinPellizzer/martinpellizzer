Alpinia sessiliflora

Alpinia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with prominent stamens, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Alpinia sessiliflora is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
