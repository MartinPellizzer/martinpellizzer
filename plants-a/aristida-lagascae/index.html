Aristida Lagascae

Lagascas grass, scientifically known as Aristida lagascae, is a resilient and hardy plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. This perennial grass is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. With its fine, silver-green foliage and delicate seed heads, Lagascas grass adds visual interest to landscapes while supporting local wildlife. Its ability to survive with minimal water makes it an ideal choice for xeriscaping and sustainable gardening practices.
