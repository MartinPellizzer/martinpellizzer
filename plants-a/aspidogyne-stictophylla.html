Aspidogyne stictophylla

Aspidogyne stictophylla, commonly known as the red-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a spathe, a modified leaf-like structure. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Aspidogyne stictophylla is also cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
