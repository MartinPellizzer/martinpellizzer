Avena lendigera

Avena lendigera, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Avena lendigera is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it a common indicator plant in ecological studies.
