Acacia loretensis

Acacia loretensis, commonly known as the Loretto acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stem and bipinnate leaves that are compound and feathery in appearance. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, making it visually striking.

Acacia loretensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
