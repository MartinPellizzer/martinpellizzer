Aster lautureanus var. lautureanus

Aster lautureanus var. lautureanus, commonly known as the Laurel Aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
