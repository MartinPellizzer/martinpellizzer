Achillea ptarmica var. parvifolia

Achillea ptarmica var. parvifolia, commonly known as slender yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.
