Acalypha grandis var. longe-acuminata

Acalypha grandis var. longe-acuminata, commonly known as the long-pointed glory flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, palmate leaves that display a range of colors from green to red, depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces clusters of small, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add bold color to tropical landscapes.
