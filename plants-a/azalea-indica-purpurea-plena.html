Azalea indica purpurea-plena

Azalea indica purpurea-plena, commonly known as the purple double azalea, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, showy flowers that are typically a deep purple or violet in color. The plant produces clusters of small, petal-like bracts that give it a full, plume-like appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This ornamental plant is widely cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is often used in garden landscapes.
