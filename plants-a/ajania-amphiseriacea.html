Ajania amphiseriacea

Ajania amphiseriacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ajania, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
