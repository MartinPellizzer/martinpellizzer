Astragalus chrysophyllus

Astragalus chrysophyllus, commonly known as goldenleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green foliage and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus chrysophyllus is often found in steppe and desert steppe habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
