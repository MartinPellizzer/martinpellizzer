Arundinaria munsuensis

Arundinaria munsuensis, commonly known as the Munsu bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria munsuensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional landscaping in its native regions.
