Anemone fasciculata var. longipedunculata

Anemone fasciculata var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long peduncled anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and hairy, with upright stems that support its clustered flower heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
