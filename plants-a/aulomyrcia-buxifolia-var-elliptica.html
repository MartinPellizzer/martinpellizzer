Aulomyrcia buxifolia var. elliptica

Aulomyrcia buxifolia var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical-leaf aulomyrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with elliptical-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
