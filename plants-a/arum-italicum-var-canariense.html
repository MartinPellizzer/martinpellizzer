Arum italicum var. canariense

Arum italicum var. canariense, commonly known as the Canary Island arum, is a tropical plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial with striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
