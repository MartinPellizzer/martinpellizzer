Acanthoprasium integrifolium

Acanthoprasium integrifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf acanthoprasium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. The plant has slender, upright stems and lanceolate leaves that are entire, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological value.
