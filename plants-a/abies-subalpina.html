Abies subalpina

Abies subalpina, commonly known as the Alpine fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in alpine and subalpine zones where temperatures are cool and the soil is rocky or poor. The tree has a slender, conical shape with dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches. Its cones are cylindrical and contain small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to harsh climates and plays a significant role in the ecological structure of its native habitats.
