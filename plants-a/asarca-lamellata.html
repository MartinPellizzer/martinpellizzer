Asarca lamellata

Asarca lamellata, commonly known as the crested lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often curly, leaf sheaths that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically arranged in a raceme. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its natural habitat.

Asarca lamellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
