Arundinella elata

Arundinella elata, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Arundinella elata is often found in wetland areas and marshy environments due to its preference for moist soil.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it both a resilient and potentially invasive species in certain habitats.
