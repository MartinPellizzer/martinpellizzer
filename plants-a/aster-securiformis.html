Aster securiformis

Aster securiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and prairies, preferring partial to full sun. Its showy blooms appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster securiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
