Astragalus prodigiosus

Astragalus prodigiosus, commonly known as the scarlet milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Astragalus prodigiosus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
