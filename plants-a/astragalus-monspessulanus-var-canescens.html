Astragalus monspessulanus var. canescens

Astragalus monspessulanus var. canescens, commonly known as the Spanish milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has compound leaves with pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the legume family, Fabaceae.
