Aspidosperma megalocarpon

Aspidosperma megalocarpon, commonly known as the African violet tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverine forests and lowland areas.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
