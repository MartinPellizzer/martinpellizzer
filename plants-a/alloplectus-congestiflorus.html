Alloplectus congestiflorus

Alloplectus congestiflorus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Alloplectus congestiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
