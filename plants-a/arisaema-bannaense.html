Arisaema bannaense

Arisaema bannaense, commonly known as the Banna arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and unique inflorescence. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a feature common in many arum species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
