Antirrhinum sempervirens var. glabrescens

Antirrhinum sempervirens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-flowered snapdragon, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features erect, branched stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, smooth texture, distinguishing it from other snapdragon varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
