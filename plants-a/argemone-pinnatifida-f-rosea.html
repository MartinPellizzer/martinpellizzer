Argemone pinnatifida f. rosea

Argemone pinnatifida f. rosea, commonly known as the rosea variant of the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
