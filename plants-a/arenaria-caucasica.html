Arenaria caucasica

Arenaria caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Arenaria caucasica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations.
