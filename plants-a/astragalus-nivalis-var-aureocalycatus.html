Astragalus nivalis var. aureocalycatus

Astragalus nivalis var. aureocalycatus, commonly known as the golden-calyx snow lotus, is a rare alpine plant native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-white flowers and hairy, mat-forming foliage adapted to cold, arid environments. The plant thrives in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters, where it withstands harsh climatic conditions. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in remote habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
