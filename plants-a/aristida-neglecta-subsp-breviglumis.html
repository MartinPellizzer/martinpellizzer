Aristida neglecta subsp. breviglumis

Aristida neglecta subsp.

breviglumis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dropseed, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and sandy soils.

The plant has slender, grass-like blades with a distinctive narrow, glabrous leaf blade. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster that is usually less than 10 cm in length.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
