Andropogon consanguineus var. genuinus

Andropogon consanguineus var. genuinus, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
