Amelanchier parviflora var. libanotica

Amelanchier parviflora var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features oval to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red berries in the summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wildlife and ornamental gardening.
