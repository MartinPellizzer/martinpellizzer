Anemia warmingii

Anemia warmingii, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to bloom throughout the summer season.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers and related species.
