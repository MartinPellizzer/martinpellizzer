Alsophila coactilis

Alsophila coactilis, commonly known as the Coactilis tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and mountainous regions.

Its rhizome system supports the growth of its distinctive, evergreen fronds, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat.
