Aronia petraea

Aronia petraea, commonly known as the Canadian rose or red sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant red in the fall.

Aronia petraea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
