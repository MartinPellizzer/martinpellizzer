Agave coetocapnia

Agave coetocapnia, commonly known as the Candelabra Agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem that bears a dense cluster of spiky, fleshy leaves. The plant's rosette structure is compact, with leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a waxy texture. It produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing yellowish-green flowers.

Agave coetocapnia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.
