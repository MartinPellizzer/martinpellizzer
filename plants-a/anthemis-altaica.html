Anthemis altaica

Anthemis altaica, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the genus Anthemis.

It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.
