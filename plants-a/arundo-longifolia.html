Arundo longifolia

Arundo longifolia, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hollow, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height, making it a striking feature in wetland and riparian habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles during the summer months. Arundo longifolia is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ornamental plant due to its architectural appearance.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments, outcompeting native vegetation.
