Artemisia monspeliensis

Artemisia monspeliensis, commonly known as Spanish thyme or sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain volatile oils, giving it a distinctive scent and making it useful in herbal medicine.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
