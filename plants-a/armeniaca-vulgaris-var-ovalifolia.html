Armeniaca vulgaris var. ovalifolia

Armeniaca vulgaris var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf peach, is a variant of the peach tree characterized by its distinctive oval-shaped leaves.

This plant is native to parts of Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces sweet, fleshy fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The botanical features include its deciduous nature, showing seasonal changes, and its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value is also appreciated due to its attractive foliage and blossom display.
