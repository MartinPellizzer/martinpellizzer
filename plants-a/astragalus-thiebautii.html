Astragalus thiebautii

Astragalus thiebautii, commonly known as Thiebaut's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting deep root systems and drought tolerance. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
