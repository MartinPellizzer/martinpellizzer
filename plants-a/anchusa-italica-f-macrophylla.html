Anchusa italica f. macrophylla

Anchusa italica f. macrophylla, commonly known as the Italian Anchusa or large-leaved Anchusa, is a cultivar of the Anchusa genus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, bright green leaves and showy clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.

The f. macrophylla form is distinguished by its notably larger foliage compared to the typical species.
