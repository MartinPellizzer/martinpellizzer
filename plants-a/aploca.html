Aploca

Aploca, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species that are native to various regions of Africa, particularly in the southern part of the continent. The plants are typically herbaceous, with alternate, often hairy leaves and yellow flowers that resemble those of true dandelions. Aploca species are often found in grasslands and open areas, where they thrive in full sun.

Their botanical significance lies in their role as food sources for certain insects and their potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
