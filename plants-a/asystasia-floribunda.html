Asystasia floribunda

Asystasia floribunda, commonly known as the purple-flowered asystasia, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often hairy as well. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the leaf axils.

Asystasia floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
