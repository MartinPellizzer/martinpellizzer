Aristolochia bilabiata

Aristolochia bilabiata, commonly known as the twin-lip arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate flowers that resemble a narrow, twisted tube. The plant belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae, which is known for its complex floral structures and chemical diversity. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor, which attracts specific pollinators.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
