Atriplex saccaria var. caput-medusae

Atriplex saccaria var. caput-medusae, commonly known as the "Medusa's Head," is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, yellow flowers, which are often followed by pink or red bracts resembling a crown. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
