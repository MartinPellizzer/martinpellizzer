Aporum incrassatum

Aporum incrassatum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and distinctive inflorescence. The plant produces a starchy root that is used as a food source in some regions. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Aporum incrassatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
