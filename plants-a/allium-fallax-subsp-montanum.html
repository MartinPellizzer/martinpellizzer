Allium fallax subsp. montanum

Allium fallax subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features basal leaves that are grass-like and a cluster of spherical, pale purple or white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces small, bulbous structures that are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Allium genus.
