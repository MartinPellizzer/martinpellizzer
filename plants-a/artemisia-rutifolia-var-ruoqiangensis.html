Artemisia rutifolia var. ruoqiangensis

Artemisia rutifolia var. ruoqiangensis, commonly known as the Ruoqiang wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Xinjiang, China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in panicles during the summer months. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its resilience to harsh climates makes it an important component of the local flora in semi-desert ecosystems.
