Agrostophyllum javanicum

Agrostophyllum javanicum, commonly known as the Java grass or Java thyme, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, often in tropical and subtropical regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

Agrostophyllum javanicum is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
