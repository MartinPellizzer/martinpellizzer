Amaracarpus wichmannii

Amaracarpus wichmannii, commonly known as the dwarf water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. The plant features floating leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface to repel water. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and they bloom during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
