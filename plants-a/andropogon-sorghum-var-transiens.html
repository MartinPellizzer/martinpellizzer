Andropogon sorghum var. transiens

Andropogon sorghum var. transiens, commonly known as Eastern gamagrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and wide, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, narrow panicle that produces small, spikelet-like flowers.

Eastern gamagrass plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and forage for wildlife.
