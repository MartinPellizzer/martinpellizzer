Andropogon cryptatherus

Andropogon cryptatherus, commonly known as the tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Andropogon cryptatherus plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for wildlife in grassland ecosystems.
