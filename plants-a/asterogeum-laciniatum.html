Asterogeum laciniatum

Asterogeum laciniatum, commonly known as the laceleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
