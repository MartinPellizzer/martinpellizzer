Anthenantia texana

Anthenantia texana, commonly known as the Texas aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and woodland edges. It produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anthenantia texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
