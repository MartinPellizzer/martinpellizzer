Adenocarpus foliolosus var. glabrescens

Adenocarpus foliolosus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its glabrous, or hairless, leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other varieties within the species.
