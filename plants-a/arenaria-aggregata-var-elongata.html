Arenaria aggregata var. elongata

Arenaria aggregata var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically forms dense mats of succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which provide additional protection against desiccation.

It is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky habitats.
