Austroeupatorium inulifolium

Austroeupatorium inulifolium, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features upright, branched stems that are hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with hairy surfaces and toothed margins.

Austroeupatorium inulifolium is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.
