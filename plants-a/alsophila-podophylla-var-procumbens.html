Alsophila podophylla var. procumbens

Alsophila podophylla var. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping tree fern, is a variant of the tree fern genus Alsophila.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spreading fronds that form a dense, low-growing mat. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered with a fibrous, brownish outer layer. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a slightly curled appearance.

This variety thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
