Arundinaria dushanensis

Arundinaria dushanensis, commonly known as Dushan bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense foliage. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Arundinaria dushanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
