Alisma canaliculatum var. azuminoense

Alisma canaliculatum var. azuminoense, commonly known as the Azumino water plantain, is a variant of the water plantain species native to Japan.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with long, slender stems. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that float on the water surface.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems and its ornamental value in water gardens.
