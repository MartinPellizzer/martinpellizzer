Acacia sericoflora

Acacia sericoflora, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Acacia sericoflora is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various animals and insects.
