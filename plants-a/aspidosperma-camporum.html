Aspidosperma camporum

Aspidosperma camporum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and long, narrow leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red fruits containing seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticulture and reforestation projects.

The plant also contains alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
