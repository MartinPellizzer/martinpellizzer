Asplenium melanosorum

Asplenium melanosorum, commonly known as the black spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Asplenium melanosorum is a relatively hardy plant that can tolerate a variety of soil conditions, making it a common sight in forested areas.
