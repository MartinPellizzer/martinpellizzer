Aristolochia dongnaiensis var. hirsuta

Aristolochia dongnaiensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and intricate, pipe-shaped flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests. Its flowers produce a strong, often unpleasant odor to attract specific pollinators like flies.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution.
