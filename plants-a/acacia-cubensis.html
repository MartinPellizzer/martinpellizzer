Acacia cubensis

Acacia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban acacia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia cubensis is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often growing in dry, sunny environments.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
