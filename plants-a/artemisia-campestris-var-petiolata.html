Artemisia campestris var. petiolata

Artemisia campestris var. petiolata, commonly known as the slender tarragon, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
