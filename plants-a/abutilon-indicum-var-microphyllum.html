Abutilon indicum var. microphyllum

Abutilon indicum var. microphyllum, commonly known as velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems that can reach several feet in height.
