Acetosella filiformis

Acetosella filiformis, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Acetosella filiformis thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
