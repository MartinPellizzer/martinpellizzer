Apargia variegata

Apargia variegata, commonly known as the variegated bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinct variegation pattern. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Apargia variegata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
