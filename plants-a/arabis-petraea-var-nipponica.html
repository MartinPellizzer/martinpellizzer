Arabis petraea var. nipponica

Arabis petraea var. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and rosette of basal leaves, which are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in a raceme. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh growing conditions.
