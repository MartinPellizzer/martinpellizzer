Asplenium paradoxum

Asplenium paradoxum, commonly known as the shuttlecock fern, is a small, evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a compact, rosette-like growth pattern with dark green, leathery fronds that are deeply divided into narrow, pointed segments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Asplenium paradoxum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a humid indoor environment.
