Amaryllis × regina-vittata

Amaryllis × regina-vittata, commonly known as the Queen Victoria amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis sanderiana.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are broad and sturdy. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

This hybrid is popular for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in both indoor and outdoor settings.
