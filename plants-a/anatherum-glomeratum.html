Anatherum glomeratum

Anatherum glomeratum, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. This plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe ecosystems. Blue grama is a key component of many grassland communities due to its drought tolerance and ability to form extensive root systems.

It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife and livestock.
