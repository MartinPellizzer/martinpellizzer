Allium vescum

Allium vescum, commonly known as garlic chives, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in temperate climates. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium vescum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in gardens.
