Aspalathus karrooensis

Aspalathus karrooensis, commonly known as the Rooibos plant, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater. It is particularly notable for its use in producing Rooibos tea, which is rich in antioxidants and has a naturally sweet, earthy flavor.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems and has gained international recognition for its unique botanical and commercial value.
