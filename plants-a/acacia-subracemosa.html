Acacia subracemosa

Acacia subracemosa, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow or cream in color, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Acacia subracemosa is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
