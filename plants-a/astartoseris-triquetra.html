Astartoseris triquetra

Astartoseris triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled aster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves that are often described as triangular in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
