Artemisia pedemontana subsp. assoana

Artemisia pedemontana subsp.

assoana, commonly known as the Alpine tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in sunny conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

This subspecies is closely related to other Artemisia species and is valued for both its ecological role and historical uses.
