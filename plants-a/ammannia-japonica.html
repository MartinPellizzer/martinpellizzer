Ammannia japonica

Ammannia japonica, commonly known as Japanese ammannia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, cylindrical stems that often have a reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia japonica is often found in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
