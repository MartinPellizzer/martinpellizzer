Arundinaria tecta var. colorata

Arundinaria tecta var. colorata, commonly known as the colored bamboo or variegated bamboo, is a variant of the native bamboo species found in eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features a mix of green, yellow, and white hues on its leaves. This plant is a clump-forming perennial, growing in dense clusters with slender, hollow stems that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to provide structure and texture in shaded garden settings.
