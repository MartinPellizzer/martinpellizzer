Aconitum sachalinense f. glabrum

Aconitum sachalinense f. glabrum, commonly known as the Smooth Aconite, is a variant of the Aconitum genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as forests and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
