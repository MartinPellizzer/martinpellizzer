Agapetes variegata

Agapetes variegata, commonly known as the variegated silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings, giving it its common name. It produces bell-shaped, white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, bright red berries.

Agapetes variegata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.
