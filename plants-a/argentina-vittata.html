Argentina vittata

Argentina vittata, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina, and is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive leaves and drought tolerance.

Argentina vittata prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
