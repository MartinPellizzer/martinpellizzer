Adenodolichos bussei

Adenodolichos bussei, commonly known as the African horse bean, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
