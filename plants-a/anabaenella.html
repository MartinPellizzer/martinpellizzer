Anabaenella

Anabaenella, commonly known as a type of cyanobacteria, is a photosynthetic prokaryote that thrives in aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its filamentous structure and ability to perform nitrogen fixation, making it ecologically significant in nutrient cycling. Anabaenella species often form colonies that can be found in lakes, ponds, and other freshwater habitats. They are capable of producing a variety of secondary metabolites, some of which can be toxic to aquatic life.

Due to their adaptability, Anabaenella plays a crucial role in both natural and artificial water systems.
