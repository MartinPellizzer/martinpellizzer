Acrosanthes fistulosa

Acrosanthes fistulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acrosanthes, is a flowering plant native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or shrublands. The plant produces striking, pale yellow or cream-colored flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing in pairs along the stems.
