Avena strigosa var. nuda

Avena strigosa var. nuda, commonly known as slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and hairless. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptation to arid environments and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
