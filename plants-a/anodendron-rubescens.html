Anodendron rubescens

Anodendron rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species with ornamental and medicinal value. The plant is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically opposite and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a deep green color.

Anodendron rubescens is valued for its aesthetic appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
