Aster menthodorus

Aster menthodorus, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal teas. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Aster menthodorus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
