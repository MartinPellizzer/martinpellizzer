Aristolochia galeottii

Aristolochia galeottii, commonly known as the purple swallowwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, purple to greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, dark berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
