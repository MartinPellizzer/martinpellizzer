Anthemis laconica

Anthemis laconica, commonly known as the Laconic hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in the spring and are often accompanied by linear, grass-like leaves.

Anthemis laconica is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
