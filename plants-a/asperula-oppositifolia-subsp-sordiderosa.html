Asperula oppositifolia subsp. sordiderosa

Asperula oppositifolia subsp.

sordiderosa, commonly known as the dark-leaved woodruff, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
