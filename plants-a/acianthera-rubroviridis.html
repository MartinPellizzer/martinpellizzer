Acianthera rubroviridis

Acianthera rubroviridis, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly colored flowers, which feature bright green sepals and petals with a distinctive red vein pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

Acianthera rubroviridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
