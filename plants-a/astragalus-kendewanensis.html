Astragalus kendewanensis

Astragalus kendewanensis, commonly known as the Kendewa milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
