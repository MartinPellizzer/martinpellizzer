Aconitum bisma var. taronense

Aconitum bisma var. taronense, commonly known as the Taronese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
