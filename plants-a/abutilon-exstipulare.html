Abutilon exstipulare

Abutilon exstipulare, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Abutilon exstipulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
