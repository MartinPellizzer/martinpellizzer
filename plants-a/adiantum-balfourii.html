Adiantum balfourii

Adiantum balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, almost feather-like appearance.

Adiantum balfourii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
