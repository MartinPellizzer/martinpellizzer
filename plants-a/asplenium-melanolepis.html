Asplenium melanolepis

Asplenium melanolepis, commonly known as the black-veined spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves with prominent black veins, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Asplenium melanolepis is a non-woody plant that reproduces via spores rather than seeds.
