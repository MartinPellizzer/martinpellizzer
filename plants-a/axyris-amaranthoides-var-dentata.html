Axyris amaranthoides var. dentata

Axyris amaranthoides var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated axyris, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its branching structure and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
