Adromischus violaceus

Adromischus violaceus, commonly known as the violet crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically violet or purple in color, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or violet during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect sunlight and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Adromischus violaceus is a low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for indoor cultivation.
