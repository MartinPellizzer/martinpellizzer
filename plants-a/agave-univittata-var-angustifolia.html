Agave univittata var. angustifolia

Agave univittata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, spiky rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves that are often lined with white teeth. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing small, white flowers, which are typically followed by the development of a fleshy, spiky fruit. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
