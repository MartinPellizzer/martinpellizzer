Argyrolobium hirsuticaule

Argyrolobium hirsuticaule, commonly known as the hairy stalked burr.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
