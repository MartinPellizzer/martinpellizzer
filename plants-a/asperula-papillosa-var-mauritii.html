Asperula papillosa var. mauritii

Asperula papillosa var. mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius coralbells, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves with a soft, hairy texture, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or shaded borders.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooms make it a valued addition to botanical collections and cultivated landscapes.
