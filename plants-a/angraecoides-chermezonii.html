Angraecoides chermezonii

Angraecoides chermezonii, commonly known as the Christmas star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue with dark purple markings. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its blooming period occurs during the winter months, making it a rare and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
