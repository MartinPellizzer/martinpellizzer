Astragalus virgultulus

Astragalus virgultulus, commonly known as the small milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus virgultulus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
