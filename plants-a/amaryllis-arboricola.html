Amaryllis arboricola

Amaryllis arboricola, commonly known as the tree amaryllis, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that resemble those of a tree, hence its common name. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Amaryllis arboricola is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
