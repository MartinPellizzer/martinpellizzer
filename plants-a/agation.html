Agation

Agation, commonly known as the Agathis tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araucariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, evergreen trees with distinctive, whorled leaves and conical or cylindrical cones. The bark is typically thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance. Agation species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and Southeast Asia.

They are valued for their durable wood and are sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
