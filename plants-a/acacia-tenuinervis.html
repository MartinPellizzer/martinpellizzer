Acacia tenuinervis

Acacia tenuinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves with fine, parallel veins. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in compact clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Acacia tenuinervis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential source of medicinal or ecological value.
