Aethionema schizopterum

Aethionema schizopterum, commonly known as the split-leaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, usually from March to May. Its leaves are divided into narrow, thread-like segments, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.
