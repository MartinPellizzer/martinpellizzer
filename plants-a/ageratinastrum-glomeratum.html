Ageratinastrum glomeratum

Ageratinastrum glomeratum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, blue-gray inflorescences that resemble a misty cloud, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
