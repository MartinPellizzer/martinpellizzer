Aconitum × paniculatum var. stoerkianum

Aconitum × paniculatum var. stoerkianum, commonly known as the Stoerk's monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden settings.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care and kept away from children and pets.
