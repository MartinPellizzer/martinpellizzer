Acacia dyeri

Acacia dyeri, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are small, phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
