Achillea serrulata

Achillea serrulata, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely serrated, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky slopes.

Its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine contribute to its ecological and cultural significance.
