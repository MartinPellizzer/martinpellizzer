Anguloa clowesii var. flava

Anguloa clowesii var. flava, commonly known as the yellow anguloa, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests or rocky areas. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
