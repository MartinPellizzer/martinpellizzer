Arctostaphylos crustacea subsp. rosei

Arctostaphylos crustacea subsp.

rosei, commonly known as the roseleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in white or pink hues.

The plant produces small, leathery berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
