Aster abchasicus

Aster abchasicus, commonly known as the Abchazian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its blue to violet flower coloration is a notable botanical feature, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
