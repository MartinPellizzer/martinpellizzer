Astragalus allotricholobus

Astragalus allotricholobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of Mongolia and China. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It produces pods containing lens-shaped seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Astragalus allotricholobus is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
