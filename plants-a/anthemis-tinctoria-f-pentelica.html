Anthemis tinctoria f. pentelica

Anthemis tinctoria f. pentelica, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a variant of the tansy plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family, characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been historically used in herbal medicine and as a dye source.
