Alchemilla vulgaris f. effusa

Alchemilla vulgaris f. effusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shady gardens.

Alchemilla vulgaris f. effusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
