Anemone vitifolia var. matsudae

Anemone vitifolia var. matsudae, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It features delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
