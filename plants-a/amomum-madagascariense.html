Amomum madagascariense

Amomum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their strong, spicy aroma.

Amomum madagascariense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
