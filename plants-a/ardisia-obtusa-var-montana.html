Ardisia obtusa var. montana

Ardisia obtusa var. montana, commonly known as the mountain ardisia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
