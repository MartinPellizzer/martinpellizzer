Adiantum capillus-junonis

Adiantum capillus-junonis, commonly known as the horse's tail fern, is a striking member of the Adiantaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that resemble a horse's tail, hence its common name. The plant features a central, wiry rachis with small, linear pinnae that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

Botanically, it is known for its unique reproductive structures and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
