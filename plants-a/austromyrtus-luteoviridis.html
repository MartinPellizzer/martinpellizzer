Austromyrtus luteoviridis

Austromyrtus luteoviridis, commonly known as the golden grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slight yellowish tinge. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Austromyrtus luteoviridis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract native pollinators.
