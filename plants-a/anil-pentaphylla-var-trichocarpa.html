Anil pentaphylla var. trichocarpa

Anil pentaphylla var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy five-leaf plant, is a species native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its succulent stems allow it to store water, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
