Allium nevadense

Allium nevadense, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem bearing small, white to pale pink flowers in umbels. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine meadows.

Allium nevadense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
