Aloe howmanii

Aloe howmanii, commonly known as the "Candelabra Aloe," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with multiple branching stems that resemble candlesticks. The plant produces clusters of fleshy, green leaves that are often adorned with white or yellow tubercles. Its flowers, which are typically orange or yellow, appear at the tips of the branches during the summer months.

Aloe howmanii is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
