Aesculus punduana

Aesculus punduana, commonly known as the Appalachian buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the soapberry family, Sapindaceae, and is characterized by its palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The tree produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spiny, palmate fruits containing one to three chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with shallow fissures, and it typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 feet.

Aesculus punduana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
