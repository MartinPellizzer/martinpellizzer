Asplenium griseum

Asplenium griseum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows on rock surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to rocky substrates. Its fronds are usually narrow and strap-like, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture.

Asplenium griseum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
