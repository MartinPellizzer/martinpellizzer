Agropyron ruthenicum

Agropyron ruthenicum, commonly known as the Russian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Agropyron ruthenicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
