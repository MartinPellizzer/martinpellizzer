Aechmea warasii var. discolor

Aechmea warasii var. discolor, commonly known as the Variegated Pineapple Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure, which forms a central cup to collect water and nutrients. The leaves are typically blue-green with white or silver variegation, giving the plant a unique and eye-catching appearance. It produces showy inflorescences with red bracts that house small, white flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
