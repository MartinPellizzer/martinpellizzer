Acer sieboldianum f. dissectum

Acer sieboldianum f. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected maple, is a unique cultivar of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its finely cut, feathery leaves that create a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its foliage displays vibrant red and orange hues in the fall, making it a popular ornamental choice.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.
