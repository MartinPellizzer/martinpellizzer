Astragalus curvirostris subsp. psilocarpus

Astragalus curvirostris subsp.

psilocarpus, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
