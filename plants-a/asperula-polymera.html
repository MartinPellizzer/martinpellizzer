Asperula polymera

Asperula polymera, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and evergreen, providing ornamental value throughout the year.

Asperula polymera thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and is often used in rock gardens or as ground cover.
