Anisacanthus brasiliensis

Anisacanthus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers. The plant is shrub-like, with thick, fleshy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
