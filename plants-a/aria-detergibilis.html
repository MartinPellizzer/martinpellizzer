Aria detergibilis

Aria detergibilis, commonly known as the soapleaf plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that have a slightly soapy texture when touched. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its unique leaf composition contains natural surfactants, which give it its distinctive detergent-like properties.

Aria detergibilis is often used in traditional medicine and as a natural cleaning agent.
