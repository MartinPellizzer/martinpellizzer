Adenophyllum porophyllum var. radiatum

Adenophyllum porophyllum var. radiatum, commonly known as the radiate adenophyllum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine pubescence, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
