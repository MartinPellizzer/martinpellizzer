Arctotis acaulis

Arctotis acaulis, commonly known as the naked stem arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as a shrub with upright, hairless stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Arctotis acaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
