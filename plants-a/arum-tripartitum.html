Arum tripartitum

Arum tripartitum, commonly known as the three-part arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of berries.

Arum tripartitum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical and subtropical gardens.
