Angelica venenosa

Angelica venenosa, commonly known as poison angelica, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has large, fern-like leaves and tall, umbel-shaped flower clusters that bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and the plant contains toxic compounds such as glycosides and furocoumarins.

Due to its potent toxicity, it is considered a dangerous plant that should be handled with care.
