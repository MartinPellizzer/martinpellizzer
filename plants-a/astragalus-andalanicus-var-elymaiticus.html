Astragalus andalanicus var. elymaiticus

Astragalus andalanicus var. elymaiticus, commonly known as the Elymaitic milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in late spring. It is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

This variety is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
