Artocarpus lanceifolius

Artocarpus lanceifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and large, spiky fruit. The plant produces a composite fruit composed of multiple follicles, each containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit and has cultural significance in some regions.
