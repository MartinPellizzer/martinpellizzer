Aellenia glauca subsp. vestita

Aellenia glauca subsp.

vestita, commonly known as the silverleaf aellenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, pale green leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
