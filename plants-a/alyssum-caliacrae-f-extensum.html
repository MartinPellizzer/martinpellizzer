Alyssum caliacrae f. extensum

Alyssum caliacrae f. extensum, commonly known as the broadleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
