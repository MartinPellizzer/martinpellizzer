Alyssum muelleri

Alyssum muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in dense clusters. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Alyssum muelleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
