Anoectochilus aureus

Anoectochilus aureus, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate dark markings. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-draining soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a delicate, fragrant display.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid gardens and conservation programs.
