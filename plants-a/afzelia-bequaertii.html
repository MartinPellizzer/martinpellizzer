Afzelia bequaertii

Afzelia bequaertii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown timber, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

Afzelia bequaertii is often found in moist lowland forests and is known for its ecological and economic importance in the region.
