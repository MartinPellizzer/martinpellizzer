Aster oticus

Aster oticus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aster oticus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
