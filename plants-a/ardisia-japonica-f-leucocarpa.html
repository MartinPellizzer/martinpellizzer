Ardisia japonica f. leucocarpa

Ardisia japonica f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited Japanese ardisia, is a variant of the native Japanese ardisia species.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically followed by bright red berries. Its foliage and fruit make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
