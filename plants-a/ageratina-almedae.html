Ageratina almedae

Ageratina almedae, commonly known as the Almeda's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features small, white to blue-gray flowers that bloom in late summer, forming dense, fluffy clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Ageratina almedae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
