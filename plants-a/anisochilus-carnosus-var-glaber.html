Anisochilus carnosus var. glaber

Anisochilus carnosus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth anisochilus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, with a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
