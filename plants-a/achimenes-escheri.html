Achimenes escheri

Achimenes escheri, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces tubular flowers that are often adorned with a distinctive, lantern-like shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Achimenes escheri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Its stem is slender and upright, with a smooth, slightly hairy bark that is typically greenish-brown in color. The flowers are tubular with a spurred shape, coming in shades of red, pink, and white, and bloom from late spring to early autumn; they are primarily pollinated by bees.
