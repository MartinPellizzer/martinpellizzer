Anchusa laxiflora

Anchusa laxiflora, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of woolly, gray-green leaves and its spikes of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Anchusa laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
