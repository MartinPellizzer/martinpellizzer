Alstroemeria angustifolia subsp. angustifolia

Alstroemeria angustifolia subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
