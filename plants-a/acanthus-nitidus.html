Acanthus nitidus

Acanthus nitidus, commonly known as the glossy acanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a waxy, glossy sheen. The plant produces tall spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It is typically found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly seen in its natural habitat.
