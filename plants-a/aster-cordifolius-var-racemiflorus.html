Aster cordifolius var. racemiflorus

Aster cordifolius var. racemiflorus, commonly known as the racemiflorus aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features heart-shaped leaves. Its showy, clustered inflorescences bloom in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
