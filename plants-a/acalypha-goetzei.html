Acalypha goetzei

Acalypha goetzei, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red leaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to green, often with contrasting veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaves.

Acalypha goetzei is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
