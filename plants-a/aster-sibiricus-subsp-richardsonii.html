Aster sibiricus subsp. richardsonii

Aster sibiricus subsp.

richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers in the summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
