Adiantum capillus-gorgonis

Adiantum capillus-gorgonis, commonly known as the "hairpin fern," is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, arching fronds that are adorned with small, hair-like structures along the leaf edges, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Its botanical structure is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and intricate leaf morphology.
