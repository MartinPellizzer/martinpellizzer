Agrimonia nipponica var. occidentalis

Agrimonia nipponica var. occidentalis, commonly known as western agrimony, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds.

Agrimony is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.
