Amydrium medium

Amydrium medium, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is a small, slow-growing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features a short, stout trunk with a crown of feathery, palm-like leaves. Its reproductive structures include a cone-like male strobilus and a female ovulate cone.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and protected areas.
