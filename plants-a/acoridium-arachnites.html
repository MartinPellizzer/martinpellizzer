Acoridium arachnites

Acoridium arachnites, commonly known as the spider lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family and is characterized by its striking, often white or yellowish, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has strap-like leaves that are broad and elongated. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Acoridium arachnites is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in wetland ecosystems.
