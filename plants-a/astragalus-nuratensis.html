Astragalus nuratensis

Astragalus nuratensis, commonly known as the Nuratensian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Astragalus nuratensis is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptability and potential medicinal properties.
