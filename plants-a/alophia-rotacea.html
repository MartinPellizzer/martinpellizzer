Alophia rotacea

Alophia rotacea, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has a robust, fleshy stem and produces small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Alophia rotacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
