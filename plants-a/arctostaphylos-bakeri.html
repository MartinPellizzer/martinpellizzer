Arctostaphylos bakeri

Arctostaphylos bakeri, commonly known as the Baker manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and dry summers.

Arctostaphylos bakeri is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
