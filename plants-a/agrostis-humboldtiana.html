Agrostis humboldtiana

Agrostis humboldtiana, commonly known as Humboldt's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
