Aira capillaris var. pulchella

Aira capillaris var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive purplish hue, especially along the leaf margins. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for those of other grasses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive ornamental plant for gardens and natural landscapes.
