Alnus rhombifolia var. bernardina

Alnus rhombifolia var. bernardina, commonly known as the Bernard's alder, is a variant of the broad-leafed alder native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forest edges. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The wood is lightweight and has been historically used by indigenous peoples for crafting tools and utensils.
