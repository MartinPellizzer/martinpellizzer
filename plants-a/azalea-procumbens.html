Azalea procumbens

Azalea procumbens, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Azalea procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ground covers or rock gardens.
