Abronia lanceolata

Abronia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
