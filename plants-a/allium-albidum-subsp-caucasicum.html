Allium albidum subsp. caucasicum

Allium albidum subsp.

caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features basal leaves that are broad and white to pale green in color, often with a garlic-like aroma.

The plant produces umbels of small, white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbous stem is often used in local cuisines for its mild, onion-like flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
