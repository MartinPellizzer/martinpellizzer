Astragalus limnocharis var. montii

Astragalus limnocharis var. montii, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are compound, with pinnate arrangement and small, rounded leaflets.

Astragalus limnocharis var. montii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
