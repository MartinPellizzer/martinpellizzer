Astragalus doshman-ziariensis

Astragalus doshman-ziariensis, commonly known as the Doshman-Ziari milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution in harsh environments.
