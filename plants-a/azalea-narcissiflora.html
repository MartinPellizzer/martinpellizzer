Azalea narcissiflora

Azalea narcissiflora, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive, recurved shape. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
