Agropyron smithii var. molle

Agropyron smithii var. molle, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-haired leaves and tufted inflorescences. This plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
