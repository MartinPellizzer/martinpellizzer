Anaphalis longifolia

Anaphalis longifolia, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence, and its flowers are typically pale yellow to white. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Anaphalis longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
