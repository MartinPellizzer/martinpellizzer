Allium plurifoliatum var. stenodon

Allium plurifoliatum var. stenodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
