Abrodictyum flavofuscum

Abrodictyum flavofuscum, commonly known as the yellow-brown orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a few leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish-green flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their slender, erect petals and sepals, with a lip that is often marked with darker pigments.

Abrodictyum flavofuscum is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
