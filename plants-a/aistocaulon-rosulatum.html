Aistocaulon rosulatum

Aistocaulon rosulatum, commonly known as the rosette fern, is a unique plant characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth pattern.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are often arranged in a circular formation around the central stem. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are typically covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Aistocaulon rosulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-maintenance environments.
