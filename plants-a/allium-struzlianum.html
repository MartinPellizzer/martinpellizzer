Allium struzlianum

Allium struzlianum, commonly known as the Struzliana garlic, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clustered leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice, similar to other Allium species.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
