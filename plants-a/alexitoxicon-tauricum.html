Alexitoxicon tauricum

Alexitoxicon tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric Joyweed, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and requires full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
