Aconitum confertum

Aconitum confertum, commonly known as the dense aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat fern-like appearance.

Aconitum confertum contains potent toxins, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
