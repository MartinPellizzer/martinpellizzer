Aspilia vandenbergiana

Aspilia vandenbergiana, commonly known as the Vandenberg's flame flower, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous yellow to orange-red tubular flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grassland and rocky habitats. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture due to its vibrant and long-lasting blooms.
