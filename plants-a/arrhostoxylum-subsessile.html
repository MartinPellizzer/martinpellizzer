Arrhostoxylum subsessile

Arrhostoxylum subsessile, commonly known as the slender chile tree, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and red, fleshy fruits. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in rocky soils. Its foliage is typically sparse, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss.

The fruit, often used in local cuisines, is an important part of its ecological and cultural significance in the region.
