Arnica parryi var. sonnei

Arnica parryi var. sonnei, commonly known as Sonne's arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in the Sierra Nevada and other high-altitude areas of California.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
