Artemisia grata

Artemisia grata, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late summer. Artemisia grata is often used in traditional medicine and has a long history in herbal remedies.

Its essential oils contain compounds like artemisinin, which have been studied for their potential health benefits.
