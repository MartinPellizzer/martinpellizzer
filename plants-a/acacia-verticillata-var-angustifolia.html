Acacia verticillata var. angustifolia

Acacia verticillata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves arranged in a whorl. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soils in arid environments.
