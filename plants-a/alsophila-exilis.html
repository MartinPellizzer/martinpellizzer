Alsophila exilis

Alsophila exilis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its fronds are pinnate, with a soft, leathery texture, and it produces spores rather than seeds.

Alsophila exilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
