Aptosimum radiatum

Aptosimum radiatum, commonly known as the silverleaf clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are trifoliate, with a silvery underside that gives it its common name. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Additionally, it serves as a food source for certain pollinators and livestock.
