Althaea rugosostellatum

Althaea rugosostellatum, commonly known as the rough-stalked marshmallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a robust root system and produces showy, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant has fuzzy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Althaea rugosostellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
