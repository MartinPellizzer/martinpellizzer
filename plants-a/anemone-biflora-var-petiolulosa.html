Anemone biflora var. petiolulosa

Anemone biflora var. petiolulosa, commonly known as the petiolulate anemone, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are often hairy and have petioles, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
