Allium pervestitum

Allium pervestitum, commonly known as the purple garlic.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, lavender-colored flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Allium pervestitum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
