Arundinella hirta var. livens

Arundinella hirta var. livens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo or velvet bamboo, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture that contributes to its common name.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
