Allium kurtzianum

Allium kurtzianum, commonly known as the Kashmiri leek, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical bulbs that are often used as a culinary ingredient. The plant is characterized by its pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

Allium kurtzianum is valued for its aromatic leaves and its role in traditional local cuisines.
