Agave utahensis var. scaphoidea

Agave utahensis var. scaphoidea, commonly known as the Utah agave, is a perennial succulent native to the western United States.

It features a rosette of thick, blue-gray to green, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a white, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its slow growth and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
