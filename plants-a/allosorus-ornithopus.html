Allosorus ornithopus

Allosorus ornithopus, commonly known as the bird's foot fern, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of lance-shaped, leathery leaves that resemble a bird's foot. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, spore-bearing capsules. Native to South Africa, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
