Astragalus penicillatus

Astragalus penicillatus, commonly known as the penicillate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus penicillatus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
