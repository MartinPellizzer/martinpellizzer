Arenaria katoana

Arenaria katoana, commonly known as Katoan sandpink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Arenaria katoana thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.
