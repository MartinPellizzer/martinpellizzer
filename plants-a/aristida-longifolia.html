Aristida longifolia

Aristida longifolia, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
