Abies balsamea f. nana

Abies balsamea f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf balsam fir, is a compact, low-growing variant of the balsam fir.

It features needle-like leaves that are shorter and more densely packed than those of the standard species. The plant typically forms a cushion-like growth, making it well-suited for rock gardens or alpine settings. It thrives in cool, moist environments with partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics include a conical shape and the ability to produce small, aromatic cones.
