Astragalus distortus var. distortus

Astragalus distortus var. distortus, commonly known as the twisted milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
