Amaryllis coranica var. pallida

Amaryllis coranica var. pallida, commonly known as the pale amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are broad and fleshy, typical of the Amaryllis genus. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding a dramatic touch to gardens with its bold floral display.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
