Acerates angustifolia

Acerates angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and delicate foliage.
