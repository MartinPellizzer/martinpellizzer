Abies pectinata var. pendula

Abies pectinata var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping fir, is a variant of the silver fir native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, giving it a distinctive, ethereal appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and covered in a layer of fine, silvery-white wax. It produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
