Astragalus spryginii

Astragalus spryginii, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.

Due to its ecological importance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
