Abies cephalonica var. apollinis

Abies cephalonica var. apollinis, commonly known as the Apollo fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the high-altitude regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are shorter and more densely packed than those of its parent species. The cones are typically small and upright, with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration. This variety thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from climate change and habitat loss.
