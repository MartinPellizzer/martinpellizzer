Achillea membranacea

Achillea membranacea, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and wildlife gardens.
