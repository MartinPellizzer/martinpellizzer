Acacia nilotica var. kraussiana

Acacia nilotica var. kraussiana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, gray-green foliage and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
