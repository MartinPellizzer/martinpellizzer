Aster brandegeei

Aster brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces showy, violet-blue to purple flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Aster brandegeei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
