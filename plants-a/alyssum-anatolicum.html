Alyssum anatolicum

Alyssum anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Alyssum anatolicum is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
