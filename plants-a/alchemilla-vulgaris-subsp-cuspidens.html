Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. cuspidens

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

cuspidens, commonly known as the spear-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
