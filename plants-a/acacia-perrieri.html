Acacia perrieri

Acacia perrieri, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces flat, pod-like seed pods that contain several seeds. Acacia perrieri is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
