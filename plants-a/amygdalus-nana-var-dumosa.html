Amygdalus nana var. dumosa

Amygdalus nana var. dumosa, commonly known as the dwarf almond or dwarf peach almond, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, fuzzy drupe that matures to a yellowish or reddish color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
