Acer subintegrum

Acer subintegrum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant colors during the growing season and autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by samaras (winged seeds) for dispersal.

Acer subintegrum thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
