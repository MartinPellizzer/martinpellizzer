Allium equicaeleste

Allium equicaeleste, commonly known as the blue onion, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bulbous structures that are used for propagation. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their unique color and form.

Allium equicaeleste is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
