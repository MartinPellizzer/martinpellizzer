Artanthe scabra var. hirsuta

Artanthe scabra var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
