Anthemis chilensis

Anthemis chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean chamomile, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in coastal and arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
