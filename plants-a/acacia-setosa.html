Acacia setosa

Acacia setosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open when mature.

Acacia setosa is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
