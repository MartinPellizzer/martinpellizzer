Astracantha echidniformis

Astracantha echidniformis, commonly known as the echidna flower, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, globular shape and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of succulents adapted to arid environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
