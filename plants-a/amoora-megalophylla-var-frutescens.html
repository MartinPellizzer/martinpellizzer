Amoora megalophylla var. frutescens

Amoora megalophylla var. frutescens, commonly known as the rough-leaved amoora, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often rough to the touch, with a prominent midrib and serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by orange or red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
