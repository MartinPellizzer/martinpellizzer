Aster woodhousei

Aster woodhousei, commonly known as the Woodhouse's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its foliage is lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces showy purple to blue flowers in the summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
