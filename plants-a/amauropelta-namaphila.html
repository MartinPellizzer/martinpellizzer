Amauropelta namaphila

Amauropelta namaphila, commonly known as the Namaphila shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive shield-shaped fronds that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps in retaining moisture.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
