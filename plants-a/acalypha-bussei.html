Acalypha bussei

Acalypha bussei, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with prominent red veins contrasting against the green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Acalypha bussei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
