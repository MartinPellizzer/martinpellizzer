Amygdalus sinensis

Amygdalus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese peach, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces showy pink or white flowers in the spring, followed by fuzzy, orange-red fruits. Its fruit, known as the peach, is widely cultivated for its sweet, juicy flesh and aromatic scent.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
