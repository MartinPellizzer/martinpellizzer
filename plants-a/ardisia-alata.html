Ardisia alata

Ardisia alata, commonly known as the winged ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, and it produces bright red berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruits.
