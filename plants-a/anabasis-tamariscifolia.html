Anabasis tamariscifolia

Anabasis tamariscifolia, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy and saline soils, often found in desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Anabasis tamariscifolia plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
