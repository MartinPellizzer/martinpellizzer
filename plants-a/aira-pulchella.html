Aira pulchella

Aira pulchella, commonly known as the purple air plant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aira pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
