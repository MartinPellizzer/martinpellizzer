Anthemis thracica f. pallida

Anthemis thracica f. pallida, commonly known as the pale Thrace chamomile, is a variant of the Thrace chamomile, characterized by its pale yellow flowers.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan region, particularly Thrace. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild medicinal properties similar to other chamomile species.
