Agrostis alba var. albida

Agrostis alba var. albida, commonly known as white bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, dark green leaves with a bluish tint and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is characterized by its pale or white coloration, which distinguishes it from other varieties of Agrostis alba. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along streambanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
