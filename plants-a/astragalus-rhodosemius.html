Astragalus rhodosemius

Astragalus rhodosemius, commonly known as the pink-hued milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with hairy stems and compound leaves that have small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and are followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus rhodosemius is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
