Agapetes glabra

Agapetes glabra, commonly known as the smooth agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically features small, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, with a dark green coloration. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Agapetes glabra is often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
