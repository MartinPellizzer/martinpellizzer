Allocarya vallata

Allocarya vallata, commonly known as the valley marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet meadows and along streambanks. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming dense mats.

Allocarya vallata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
