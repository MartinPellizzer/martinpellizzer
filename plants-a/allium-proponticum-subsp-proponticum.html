Allium proponticum subsp. proponticum

Allium proponticum subsp.

proponticum, commonly known as the Crimean leek, is a perennial herb native to the Black Sea region. It is characterized by its basal rosette of long, narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces tall, cylindrical inflorescences bearing small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it is closely related to other allium species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
