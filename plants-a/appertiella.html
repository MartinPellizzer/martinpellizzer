Appertiella

Appertiella, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. The plants are characterized by their silvery, fuzzy leaves and bright yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. Appertiella species are typically low-growing shrubs with a compact form, making them suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
