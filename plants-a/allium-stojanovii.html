Allium stojanovii

Allium stojanovii, commonly known as the Stojanov's garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical bulbils that are used for propagation. It typically blooms in late spring or early summer with clusters of pale pink to white flowers.

Allium stojanovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
