Astragalus gorganicus

Astragalus gorganicus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments and has deep root systems to access water.

Astragalus gorganicus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its potential health benefits.
