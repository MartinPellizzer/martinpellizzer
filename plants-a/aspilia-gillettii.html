Aspilia gillettii

Aspilia gillettii, commonly known as Gillett's torchflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescence. The plant produces numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense, torch-like cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Aspilia gillettii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
