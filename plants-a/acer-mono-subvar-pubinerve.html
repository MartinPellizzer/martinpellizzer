Acer mono subvar. pubinerve

Acer mono subvar. pubinerve, commonly known as the pubinerve maple, is a variant of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often a striking red or orange in the fall. The leaves have a distinctive pubescent (hairy) underside, which gives the subvariety its name. It is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is well-suited for smaller gardens or as a specimen plant.
