Apinagia multibranchiata

Apinagia multibranchiata, commonly known as the branched bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late summer. Its stems are wiry and often branched, giving it its common name.

Apinagia multibranchiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
