Anthericum polyphyllum

Anthericum polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaved lily, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves are elongated, narrow, and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
