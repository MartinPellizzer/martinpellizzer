Armeniaca

Armeniaca, commonly known as the peach, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is native to regions of Asia, Europe, and North Africa, and is cultivated for its edible fruits. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers in spring, which are followed by fuzzy, yellowish fruits. The fruit's skin is thin and contains a hard pit with a single seed inside.

Armeniaca has been valued for centuries for its culinary and medicinal properties.
