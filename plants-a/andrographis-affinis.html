Andrographis affinis

Andrographis affinis, commonly known as the Indian toothbrush, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of andrographolide, a bioactive compound. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value, combined with its traditional use in herbal medicine, makes it a significant plant in both horticulture and pharmacology.
