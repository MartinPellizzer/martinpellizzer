Arenbergia glauca

Arenbergia glauca, commonly known as the blue sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Arenbergia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
