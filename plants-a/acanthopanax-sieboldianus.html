Acanthopanax sieboldianus

Acanthopanax sieboldianus, commonly known as the Japanese angel's trumpet, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling during pruning or propagation.
