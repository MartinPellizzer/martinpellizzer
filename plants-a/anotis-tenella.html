Anotis tenella

Anotis tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

Anotis tenella is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
