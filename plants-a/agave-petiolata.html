Agave petiolata

Agave petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and narrow, bluish-gray leaves that are often lined with small teeth. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear white to yellow flowers, which bloom once in the plant's lifetime. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
