Aster serrulatus var. densus

Aster serrulatus var. densus, commonly known as the denseleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves and clusters of small, lavender to white flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
