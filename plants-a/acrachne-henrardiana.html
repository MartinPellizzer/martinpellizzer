Acrachne henrardiana

Acrachne henrardiana, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and somewhat hairy.

Acrachne henrardiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
