Aechmea amicorum

Aechmea amicorum, commonly known as the friendship bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is typically green with a metallic sheen, and it produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. Aechmea amicorum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid environments make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
