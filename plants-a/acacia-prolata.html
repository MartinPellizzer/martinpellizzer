Acacia prolata

Acacia prolata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact, spherical inflorescences. Acacia prolata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators and small animals.
