Alpinia densibracteata

Alpinia densibracteata, commonly known as the dense bract alpinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its robust, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers, which are surrounded by dense, leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Alpinia densibracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
