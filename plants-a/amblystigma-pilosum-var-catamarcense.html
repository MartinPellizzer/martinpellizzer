Amblystigma pilosum var. catamarcense

Amblystigma pilosum var. catamarcense, commonly known as the Catamarcense St. John's Wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and medicinal research.
