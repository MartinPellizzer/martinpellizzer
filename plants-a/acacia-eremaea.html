Acacia eremaea

Acacia eremaea, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. It produces long, slender pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia eremaea is well-suited to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
