Anoda palmata

Anoda palmata, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that features palmately lobed leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
