Aristolochia lingua

Aristolochia lingua, commonly known as the tongue-of-the-angel, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and a unique, snake-like flower that resembles a small, twisted tube. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral morphology. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musky odor that attracts specific pollinators.

The plant contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic and have been linked to kidney damage in humans.
