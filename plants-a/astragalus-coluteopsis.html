Astragalus coluteopsis

Astragalus coluteopsis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus coluteopsis is often found in open grasslands and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
