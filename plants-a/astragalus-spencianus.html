Astragalus spencianus

Astragalus spencianus, commonly known as Spencian's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils and during drought periods.

Astragalus spencianus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
