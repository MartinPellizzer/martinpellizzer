Agrostophyllum spinuliferum subsp. spinuliferum

Agrostophyllum spinuliferum subsp.

spinuliferum, commonly known as the spinylobe grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant produces tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spikelike structures. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is typically found in sandy or rocky soils in open, sunny habitats.
