Agave angustifolia subsp. tequilana

Agave angustifolia subsp.

tequilana, commonly known as blue agave, is a species native to the Mexican states of Jalisco and Guanajuato. It is a perennial plant characterized by its thick, succulent leaves that are blue-green in color and have a waxy coating.

The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach up to 5 meters in height, bearing small white flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

This species is economically significant as the primary source of agave used in the production of tequila.
