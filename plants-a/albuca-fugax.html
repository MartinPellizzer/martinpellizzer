Albuca fugax

Albuca fugax, commonly known as the fleeting albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact, grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Albuca fugax is a short-lived perennial, often growing to a height of only a few inches.
