Atropa gymnosperma

Atropa gymnosperma, commonly known as the naked-seeded nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and it has a stout, upright stem.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to both animals and humans.
