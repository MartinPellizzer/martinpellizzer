Artemisia plattensis

Artemisia plattensis, commonly known as plains wormwood, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Artemisia plattensis is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil.
