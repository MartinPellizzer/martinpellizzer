Aspidosperma sessilis

Aspidosperma sessilis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratonia sylvestris*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
