Agrostis gracilifolia subsp. gracilifolia

Agrostis gracilifolia subsp.

gracilifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
