Anthyllis vulneraria proles vulgaris

Anthyllis vulneraria proles vulgaris, commonly known as the common yellow rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with soil bacteria.
