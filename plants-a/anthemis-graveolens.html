Anthemis graveolens

Anthemis graveolens, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant contains essential oils and has been used historically for its medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
