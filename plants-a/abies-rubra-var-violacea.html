Abies rubra var. violacea

Abies rubra var. violacea, commonly known as the purple fir, is a variant of the balsam fir native to eastern North America.

It is distinguished by its striking violet to purple needles, which contrast with its dark green branches. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, such as mountainous regions and northern hardwood forests. Its cones are similar to those of other fir species, with slender, cylindrical shapes and dark brown scales.

The purple fir is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage color.
