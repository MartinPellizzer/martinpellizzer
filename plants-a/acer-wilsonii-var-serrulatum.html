Acer wilsonii var. serrulatum

Acer wilsonii var. serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
