Acacia anastomosa

Acacia anastomosa, commonly known as the honeywood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against drought and fire.

Acacia anastomosa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration.
