Asplenium nidus var. simonsianum

Asplenium nidus var. simonsianum, commonly known as the Basket Fern, is a tropical fern species characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It features dark green, leathery fronds that are often curled or rolled, resembling a basket, hence its common name. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are typically clustered in sori. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
