Acacia jaubertiana

Acacia jaubertiana, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a unique flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped trunk and thorny branches, which help it survive in the arid environment. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its landscape. Its leaves are compound, consisting of small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep root system for water conservation.

Acacia jaubertiana is also notable for its role in the local ecosystem and its use in traditional medicine.
