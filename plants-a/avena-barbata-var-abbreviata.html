Avena barbata var. abbreviata

Avena barbata var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-fruited oat, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, bristly spikes of florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
