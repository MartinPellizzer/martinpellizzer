Anthericum ustulatum

Anthericum ustulatum, commonly known as the scorched anthericum, is a rare and striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
