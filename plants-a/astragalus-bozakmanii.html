Astragalus bozakmanii

Astragalus bozakmanii, commonly known as Bozakman milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and high altitude areas.

Astragalus bozakmanii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various insects and small animals.
