Aloe mutica

Aloe mutica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Aloe mutica is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Its resilience makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
