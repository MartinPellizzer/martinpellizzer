Apleura nucamentacea

Apleura nucamentacea, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Apleura nucamentacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
