Arabis nemorensis subsp. planisiliqua

Arabis nemorensis subsp.

planisiliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
