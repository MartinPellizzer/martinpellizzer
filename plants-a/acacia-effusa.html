Acacia effusa

Acacia effusa, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by elongated pods containing seeds. Acacia effusa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
