Artemisia pinnatifida

Artemisia pinnatifida, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
