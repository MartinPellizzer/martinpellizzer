Anthoxanthum odoratum subsp. alpinum

Anthoxanthum odoratum subsp.

alpinum, commonly known as alpine sweet vernal grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair.

The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or well-drained soils at high elevations.

This subspecies is notable for its aromatic scent, which is more pronounced in certain conditions.
