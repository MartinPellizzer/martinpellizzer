Asplenium lomariaceum

Asplenium lomariaceum, commonly known as the rock spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered in dense rosettes. The plant typically grows on rocks, tree trunks, or soil in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and they produce spores for propagation.

Asplenium lomariaceum is often found in rocky or forested habitats, showcasing its adaptability to various microclimates.
