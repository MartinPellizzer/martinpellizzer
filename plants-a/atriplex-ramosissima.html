Atriplex ramosissima

Atriplex ramosissima, commonly known as shadscale, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in desert scrub and sagebrush communities, thriving in well-drained soils.

Atriplex ramosissima is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
