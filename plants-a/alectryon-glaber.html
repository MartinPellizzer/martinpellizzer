Alectryon glaber

Alectryon glaber, commonly known as the smooth alectryon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
