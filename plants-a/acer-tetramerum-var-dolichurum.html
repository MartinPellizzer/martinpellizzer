Acer tetramerum var. dolichurum

Acer tetramerum var. dolichurum, commonly known as the long-leaf maple, is a variant of the four-leaf maple native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves that can grow up to 12 inches in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in riparian zones. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in shady, wet environments.
