Asparagus intricatus

Asparagus intricatus, commonly known as the intricate asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Asparagus intricatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
