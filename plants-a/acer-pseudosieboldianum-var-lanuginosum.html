Acer pseudosieboldianum var. lanuginosum

Acer pseudosieboldianum var. lanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, softly hairy leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
