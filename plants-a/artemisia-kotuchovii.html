Artemisia kotuchovii

Artemisia kotuchovii, commonly known as Kotuchov's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
