Astragalus aurantiacus

Astragalus aurantiacus, commonly known as the orange milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Astragalus aurantiacus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
