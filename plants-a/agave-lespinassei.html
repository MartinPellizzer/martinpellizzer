Agave lespinassei

Agave lespinassei, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that can reach several meters in height, bearing yellowish-green flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with sharp, spiny edges. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates with minimal rainfall.

Agave lespinassei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Mexican culture.
