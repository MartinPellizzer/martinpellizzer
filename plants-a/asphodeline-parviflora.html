Asphodeline parviflora

Asphodeline parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features linear, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
