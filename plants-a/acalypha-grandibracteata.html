Acalypha grandibracteata

Acalypha grandibracteata, commonly known as the "Chinese nettle" or "velvetleaf," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Acalypha grandibracteata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
