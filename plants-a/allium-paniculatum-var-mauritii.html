Allium paniculatum var. mauritii

Allium paniculatum var. mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius onion, is a perennial herb native to the island of Mauritius.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers in summer. The plant is characterized by its bulbous root system and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, onion-like aroma.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and thrives in full sun.
