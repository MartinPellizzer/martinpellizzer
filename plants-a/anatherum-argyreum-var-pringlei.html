Anatherum argyreum var. pringlei

Anatherum argyreum var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage rather than its flowers.
