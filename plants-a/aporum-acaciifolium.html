Aporum acaciifolium

Aporum acaciifolium, commonly known as the acacia-leaf aporum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of acacia species. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
