Alchemilla changaica

Alchemilla changaica, commonly known as the Changaic rockfoil, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, typically blooming in early summer.

Alchemilla changaica is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
