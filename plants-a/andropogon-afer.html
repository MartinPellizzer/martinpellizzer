Andropogon afer

Andropogon afer, commonly known as African bluestem, is a tall perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and savannas. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon afer is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
