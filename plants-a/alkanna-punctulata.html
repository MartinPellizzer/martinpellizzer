Alkanna punctulata

Alkanna punctulata, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red, pink, or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a woody stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
