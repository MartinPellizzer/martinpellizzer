Amorphophallus giganteus

Amorphophallus giganteus, commonly known as the giant elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy underground tuber, which can grow to impressive sizes, sometimes exceeding 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces striking, spiky inflorescences that resemble a giant phallus, hence its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
