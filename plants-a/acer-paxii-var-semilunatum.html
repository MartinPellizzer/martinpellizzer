Acer paxii var. semilunatum

Acer paxii var. semilunatum, commonly known as the semilunar maple, is a rare and unique variant of the maple genus native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that often display a striking semicircular shape, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas with cool, moist climates, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage changes color through the seasons, showcasing shades of green, orange, and red, making it a visually appealing species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
