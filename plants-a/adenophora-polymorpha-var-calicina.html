Adenophora polymorpha var. calicina

Adenophora polymorpha var. calicina, commonly known as the California milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with elliptical to lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to arid environments.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
