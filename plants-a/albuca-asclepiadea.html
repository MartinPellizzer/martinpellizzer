Albuca asclepiadea

Albuca asclepiadea, commonly known as the star-shaped bulbous plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bulbous base and narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces tall, slender stems with clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Albuca asclepiadea is relatively drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
