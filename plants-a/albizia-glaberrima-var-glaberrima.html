Albizia glaberrima var. glaberrima

Albizia glaberrima var. glaberrima, commonly known as the African siris, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves that are pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which bloom in the summer. Its pods are slender, cylindrical, and contain several flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
