Alcea acaulis

Alcea acaulis, commonly known as the upright hollyhock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, radially symmetrical flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Alcea acaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
