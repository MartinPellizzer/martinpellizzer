Astragalus strobilifer

Astragalus strobilifer, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus strobilifer plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
