Astragalus tongolensis var. glaber

Astragalus tongolensis var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth astragalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has smooth, glabrous leaves and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
