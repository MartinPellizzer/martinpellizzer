Aquilegia viridiflora var. atropurpurea

Aquilegia viridiflora var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaved columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically a deep violet or purple, with five sepals that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant has palmately divided, dark green leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a visually appealing and ecologically valuable plant in diverse garden environments.
