Acer platanoides f. palmatifidum

Acer platanoides f. palmatifidum, commonly known as the palmate-lobed Norway maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate-lobed leaves, which are deeply divided into five to seven segments, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, and it can grow to a height of 10 to 20 meters.

The leaves turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
