Acanthophyllum popovii

Acanthophyllum popovii, commonly known as the Popov's spiny broom, is a low-growing, succulent shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of spiny, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are adapted for pollination by wind or insects. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Acanthophyllum popovii plays a crucial role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
