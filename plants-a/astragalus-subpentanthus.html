Astragalus subpentanthus

Astragalus subpentanthus, commonly known as the five-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system for water retention. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus subpentanthus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and its potential medicinal properties.
