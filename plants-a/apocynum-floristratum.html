Apocynum floristratum

Apocynum floristratum, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain toxic, milky sap. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies.

Apocynum floristratum is part of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species known for their medicinal and ecological significance.
