Aridaria rosea

Aridaria rosea, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It features fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is shallow and widespread, enabling it to quickly absorb surface moisture.

Aridaria rosea is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
