Anacyclus × valentinus var. homogamos

Anacyclus × valentinus var. homogamos, commonly known as the Valentine's daisy, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and displays of small, white to pale yellow ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets. The plant typically produces a single flower head per stem, which blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and the symbolic association with love and devotion.
