Aquifolium undulatum

Aquifolium undulatum, commonly known as the wavy-leaved holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, wavy-edged leaves that provide a striking visual contrast against its red or orange berries. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage remains vibrant throughout the winter, making it a valuable ornamental plant in gardens.

Additionally, the berries are an important food source for various bird species.
