Athyrium ceratolepis

Athyrium ceratolepis, commonly known as the feather fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are relatively short and produce a cluster of arching fronds.

The botanical features of Athyrium ceratolepis make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
