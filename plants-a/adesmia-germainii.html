Adesmia germainii

Adesmia germainii, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Its capsule fruits contain numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Adesmia germainii is valued for its resilience and role in supporting local ecosystems.
