Agathosma thunbergiana

Agathosma thunbergiana, commonly known as Cape tea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its unique fragrance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
