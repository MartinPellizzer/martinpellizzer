Adamanthus nitidulus

Adamanthus nitidulus, commonly known as the silky holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, which is dispersed by animals.

Adamanthus nitidulus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
