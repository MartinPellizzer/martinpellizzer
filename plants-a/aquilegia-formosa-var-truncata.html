Aquilegia formosa var. truncata

Aquilegia formosa var. truncata, commonly known as the Sierra coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive, conical shape. The plant has compound leaves with lobed segments and produces seeds in a fluffy, feathery structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
