Arrojadoa rhodantha subsp. aureispina

Arrojadoa rhodantha subsp.

aureispina, commonly known as the golden-spined hibiscus, is a species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its striking yellow spines and vibrant orange to red flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white pubescence, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
