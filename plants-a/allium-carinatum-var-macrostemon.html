Allium carinatum var. macrostemon

Allium carinatum var. macrostemon, commonly known as the giant bulbous onion, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, bulbous underground storage organs that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in umbels, and bloom in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, onion-like flavor.
