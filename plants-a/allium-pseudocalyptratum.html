Allium pseudocalyptratum

Allium pseudocalyptratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Africa. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in umbels. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
