Acacia deamii

Acacia deamii, commonly known as the Deam's acacia, is a species of shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically small and feathery. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch of color to its otherwise rugged appearance. Acacia deamii is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
