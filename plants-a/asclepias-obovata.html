Asclepias obovata

Asclepias obovata, commonly known as showy milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It features broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of pinkish to reddish-orange flowers with a unique horn-shaped structure at the center. It is a vital host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing food for both larvae and adult insects.

Asclepias obovata thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny habitats.
