Acer barbatum f. floridanum

Acer barbatum f. floridanum, commonly known as the Florida maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically red or orange in the fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetland or riparian habitats.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a desirable plant for ecological and landscape restoration projects.
