Anthemis montana var. pentelica

Anthemis montana var. pentelica, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, gray-green leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces yellowish-orange flower heads composed of ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
