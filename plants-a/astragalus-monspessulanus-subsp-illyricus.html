Astragalus monspessulanus subsp. illyricus

Astragalus monspessulanus subsp.

illyricus, commonly known as the Illyrian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in limestone soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
