Amelanchier canadensis var. pauciflora

Amelanchier canadensis var. pauciflora, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are oval to elliptical, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries in late summer.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
