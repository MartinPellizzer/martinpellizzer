Aegiceras minus

Aegiceras minus, commonly known as the mangrove apple or sea apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

It thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests along tidal shores. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically submerged or partially submerged in water. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces edible, round fruits that are adapted to survive in aquatic conditions.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
