Arbutus × hybrida

Arbutus × hybrida, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a hybrid evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are leathery, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and chaparral habitats.
