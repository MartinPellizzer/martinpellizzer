Astrantia involucrata

Astrantia involucrata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems rise above the foliage, ending in a conical inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers.

Astrantia involucrata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
