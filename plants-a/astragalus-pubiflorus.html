Astragalus pubiflorus

Astragalus pubiflorus, commonly known as the pubescent milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus pubiflorus is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert grassland ecosystems.
