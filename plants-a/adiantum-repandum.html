Adiantum repandum

Adiantum repandum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada. The plant features small, finely divided leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Adiantum repandum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
