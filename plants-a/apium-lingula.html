Apium lingula

Apium lingula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved celery.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found near water bodies. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Apium lingula is valued for its culinary uses and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
