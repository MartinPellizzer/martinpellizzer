Astragalus hermannii

Astragalus hermannii, commonly known as Hermann's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has small, hairy leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus hermannii is often found in desert environments and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
