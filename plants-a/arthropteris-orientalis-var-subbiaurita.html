Arthropteris orientalis var. subbiaurita

Arthropteris orientalis var. subbiaurita, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a fern species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds with a yellowish-green hue on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
