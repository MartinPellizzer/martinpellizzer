Aniseia heterantha var. pubescens

Aniseia heterantha var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy anise plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
