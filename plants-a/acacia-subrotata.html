Acacia subrotata

Acacia subrotata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes.

Acacia subrotata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
