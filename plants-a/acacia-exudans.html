Acacia exudans

Acacia exudans, commonly known as the exuding acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Acacia exudans is known for its ability to exude a sticky, resinous substance from its bark, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
