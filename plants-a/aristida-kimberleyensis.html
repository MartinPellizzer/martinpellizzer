Aristida kimberleyensis

Aristida kimberleyensis, commonly known as Kimberley grass, is a flowering plant native to the Kimberley region of Western Australia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, dry habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
