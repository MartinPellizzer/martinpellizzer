Adenophora pereskiifolia f. angustifolia

Adenophora pereskiifolia f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands or stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
