Agathosma gibbosa

Agathosma gibbosa, commonly known as the Cape spekboom, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing structure with small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is well adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick, succulent stems.

Due to its resilience and ecological importance, it is also valued for its medicinal properties.
