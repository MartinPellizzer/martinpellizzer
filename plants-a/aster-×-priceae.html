Aster × priceae

Aster × priceae, commonly known as the New England aster, is a hybrid perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads that typically range in color from purple to pink and white. The plant features a robust stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Aster × priceae plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies during the late summer and early fall.
