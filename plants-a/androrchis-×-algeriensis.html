Androrchis × algeriensis

Androrchis × algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Androrchis alpinus and Androrchis luteus.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant prefers well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions of North Africa. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, while its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
