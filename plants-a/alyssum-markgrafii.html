Alyssum markgrafii

Alyssum markgrafii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Alyssum markgrafii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
