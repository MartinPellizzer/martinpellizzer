Anemone felix

Anemone felix, commonly known as the Crown Imperial, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

The flowers are typically produced in late spring to early summer, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
