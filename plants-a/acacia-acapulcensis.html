Acacia acapulcensis

Acacia acapulcensis, commonly known as the Mexican acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate, and its yellow, spherical flower heads. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a fibrous mesh. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Acacia acapulcensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
