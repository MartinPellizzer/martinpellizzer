Antirrhinum arvense

Antirrhinum arvense, commonly known as the field snapdragon, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Antirrhinum arvense is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
