Ampliglossum donianum

Ampliglossum donianum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged flowering period and ability to attract pollinators.
