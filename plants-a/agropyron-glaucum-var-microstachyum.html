Agropyron glaucum var. microstachyum

Agropyron glaucum var. microstachyum, commonly known as bluebunch wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it well-suited for restoration and grazing purposes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and erosion control.

The species is also valued for its nutritional content and is often used in ecological landscaping.
