Aldama nervosa

Aldama nervosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along streambanks.

Aldama nervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
