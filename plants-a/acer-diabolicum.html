Acer diabolicum

Acer diabolicum, commonly known as the devil's maple, is a rare and striking species of maple native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its unusual, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Acer diabolicum is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
