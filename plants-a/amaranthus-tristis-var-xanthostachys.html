Amaranthus tristis var. xanthostachys

Amaranthus tristis var. xanthostachys, commonly known as the yellow-stalked amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often tinged with yellow or orange hues. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional culinary practices.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
