Abies gmelinii

Abies gmelinii, commonly known as the Korean fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Korea and northeastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The cones are cylindrical, woody, and typically measure between 5 to 8 centimeters in length. This species thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the temperate forest ecosystems in its native range.
