Astragalus obtusifoliolus

Astragalus obtusifoliolus, commonly known as the blunt-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Astragalus obtusifoliolus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
