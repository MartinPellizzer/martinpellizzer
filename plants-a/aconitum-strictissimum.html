Aconitum strictissimum

Aconitum strictissimum, commonly known as the tall monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Aconitum strictissimum belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of potent alkaloids.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine environments.
