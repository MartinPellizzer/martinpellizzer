Aucuba japonica var. japonica

Aucuba japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dark green, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often adorned with striking yellow or white variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is highly tolerant of urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its dense foliage and year-round color make it a versatile and ornamental plant in garden settings.
