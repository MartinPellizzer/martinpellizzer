Aconitum napellus f. puberulum

Aconitum napellus f. puberulum, commonly known as the pubescent monkshood, is a variant of the European aconite characterized by its hairy foliage.

This plant is native to central and eastern Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble a hood, giving it the common name "monkshood.". The flowers are blue to purple and are arranged in a spire, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is noted for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to both animals and humans.
