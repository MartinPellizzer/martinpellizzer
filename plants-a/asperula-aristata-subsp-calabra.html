Asperula aristata subsp. calabra

Asperula aristata subsp.

calabra, commonly known as Calabrian sweet pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color.

The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
