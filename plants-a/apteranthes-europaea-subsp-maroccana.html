Apteranthes europaea subsp. maroccana

Apteranthes europaea subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco. It features slender, hairy stems that grow from 30 to 60 centimeters in height.

The plant produces blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
