Acalypha trachyloba

Acalypha trachyloba, commonly known as the roughleaf ironplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from green to reddish-purple, often with a rough or hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Acalypha trachyloba is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and ability to tolerate a variety of growing conditions.
