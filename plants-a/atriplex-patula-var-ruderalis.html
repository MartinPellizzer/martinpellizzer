Atriplex patula var. ruderalis

Atriplex patula var. ruderalis, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a halophyte native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in saline and drought-prone environments. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and abandoned fields, due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize bare ground. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This hardy species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
