Anemone baicalensis var. rossii

Anemone baicalensis var. rossii, commonly known as the Ross's Anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat heart-shaped appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-blooming flowers.
