Acourtia montana var. intermedia

Acourtia montana var. intermedia, commonly known as the mountain acourtia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Acourtia montana var. intermedia thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
