Acacia cassioides

Acacia cassioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and bipinnate leaves composed of small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. Acacia cassioides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
