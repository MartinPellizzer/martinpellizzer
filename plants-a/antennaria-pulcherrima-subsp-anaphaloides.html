Antennaria pulcherrima subsp. anaphaloides

Antennaria pulcherrima subsp.

anaphaloides, commonly known as the showy mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
