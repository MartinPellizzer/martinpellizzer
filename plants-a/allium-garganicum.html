Allium garganicum

Allium garganicum, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and bulbous underground stems called bulbs, which are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
