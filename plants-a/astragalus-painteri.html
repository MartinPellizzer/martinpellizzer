Astragalus painteri

Astragalus painteri, commonly known as the Painted Milkvetch, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus painteri is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
