Angiopteris polysporangia

Angiopteris polysporangia, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a large, aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant possesses a complex vascular system that allows it to thrive in freshwater environments. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that are clustered in groups, a feature that distinguishes it from other fern species.

Angiopteris polysporangia plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen.
