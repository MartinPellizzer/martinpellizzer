Argythamnia castaneifolia

Argythamnia castaneifolia, commonly known as the red sandthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a slightly serrated edge.

Argythamnia castaneifolia is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
