Anil denudata

Anil denudata, commonly known as the naked anil, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
