Allophylus concanicus var. concanicus

Allophylus concanicus var. concanicus, commonly known as the Indian alder, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
