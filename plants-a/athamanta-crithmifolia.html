Athamanta crithmifolia

Athamanta crithmifolia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silvery, woolly foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Athamanta crithmifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
