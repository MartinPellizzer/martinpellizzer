Airochloa vallesiana

Airochloa vallesiana, commonly known as the valley grass or valley bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous environments. It is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters.

Airochloa vallesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
