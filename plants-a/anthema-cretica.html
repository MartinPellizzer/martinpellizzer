Anthema cretica

Anthema cretica, commonly known as Cretan anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its yellowish-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anthema cretica is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
