Aulomyrcia vacciniifolia

Aulomyrcia vacciniifolia, commonly known as the red-leafed myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in color when exposed to direct sunlight. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aulomyrcia vacciniifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
