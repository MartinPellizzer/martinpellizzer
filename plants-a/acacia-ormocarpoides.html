Acacia ormocarpoides

Acacia ormocarpoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for rehabilitation of degraded lands.

Acacia ormocarpoides is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
