Acaena antarctica var. argutidentata

Acaena antarctica var. argutidentata, commonly known as the Antarctic hairynet, is a perennial shrub native to the Southern Hemisphere, particularly found in New Zealand and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, persistent bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in cold, temperate climates and is often found in coastal and alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
