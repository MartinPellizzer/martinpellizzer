Anaxeton asperum subsp. pauciflorum

Anaxeton asperum subsp.

pauciflorum, commonly known as the rough anaxeton, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes in its native habitat.
