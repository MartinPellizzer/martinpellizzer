Alchemilla purpurascens

Alchemilla purpurascens, commonly known as purple-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in a cup-like inflorescence. Its leaves are typically green with a purple tinge on the underside, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Alchemilla purpurascens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate wet conditions.
