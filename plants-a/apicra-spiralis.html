Apicra spiralis

Apicra spiralis, commonly known as the spiral spike plant, is a unique species characterized by its spirally arranged inflorescence.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features slender, elongated stems that twist in a helical pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it is often found in moist, temperate environments.

Apicra spiralis is noted for its delicate foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
