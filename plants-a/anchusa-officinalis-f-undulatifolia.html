Anchusa officinalis f. undulatifolia

Anchusa officinalis f. undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy leaf hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, yellow-orange composite flowers. The leaves are narrow and undulate, giving the plant its specific epithet. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses.
