Astragalus cobrensis

Astragalus cobrensis, commonly known as the Cobren's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
