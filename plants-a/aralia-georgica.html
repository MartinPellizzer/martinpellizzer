Aralia georgica

Aralia georgica, commonly known as the Georgian aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically pinnately arranged, with each leaflet being lance-shaped and serrated. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
