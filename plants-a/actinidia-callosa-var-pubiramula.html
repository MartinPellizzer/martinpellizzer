Actinidia callosa var. pubiramula

Actinidia callosa var. pubiramula, commonly known as the fuzzy-leaf actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen to a bright orange-red color in late summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a food source for wildlife.
