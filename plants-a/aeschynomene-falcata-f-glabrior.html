Aeschynomene falcata f. glabrior

Aeschynomene falcata f. glabrior, commonly known as the smoothleaf horsegram, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its smooth, glabrous leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties of Aeschynomene. It is often cultivated for forage and as a green manure crop due to its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climatic conditions.
