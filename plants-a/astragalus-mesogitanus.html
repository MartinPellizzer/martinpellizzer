Astragalus mesogitanus

Astragalus mesogitanus, commonly known as the Spanish milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to reddish flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Astragalus mesogitanus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
