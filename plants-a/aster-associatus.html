Aster associatus

Aster associatus, commonly known as the associated aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Aster associatus is often found in association with other native plants, hence its common name.
