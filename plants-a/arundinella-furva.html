Arundinella furva

Arundinella furva, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly rough texture.

Arundinella furva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and structural features.
