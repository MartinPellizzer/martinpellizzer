Agropyron pungens var. barbatum

Agropyron pungens var. barbatum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaf blades, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
