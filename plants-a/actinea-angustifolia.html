Actinea angustifolia

Actinea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved actinia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Actinea angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
