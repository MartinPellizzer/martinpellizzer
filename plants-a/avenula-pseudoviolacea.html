Avenula pseudoviolacea

Avenula pseudoviolacea, commonly known as the violet grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Avenula pseudoviolacea is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
