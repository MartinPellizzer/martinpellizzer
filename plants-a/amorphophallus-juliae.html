Amorphophallus juliae

Amorphophallus juliae, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of water. The plant's flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, unpleasant odor that attracts pollinators like flies. Its rhizome is thick and tuberous, allowing it to store nutrients and survive in aquatic environments.

Despite its impressive size, it plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
