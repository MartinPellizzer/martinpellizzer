Astragalus tenuiramosus

Astragalus tenuiramosus, commonly known as narrow-branched milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor environments.

Astragalus tenuiramosus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
