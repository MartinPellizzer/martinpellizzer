Aeonium dodrantale

Aeonium dodrantale, commonly known as the Cape Town aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a striking addition to any succulent collection.
