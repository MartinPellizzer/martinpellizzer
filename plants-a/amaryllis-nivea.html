Amaryllis nivea

Amaryllis nivea, commonly known as the white amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are broad, strap-like, and often appear after the flowering period. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its blooming cycle typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding a dramatic floral display to the landscape.
