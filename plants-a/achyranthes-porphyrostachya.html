Achyranthes porphyrostachya

Achyranthes porphyrostachya, commonly known as the purple-stemmed smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that often display a distinctive purplish hue, particularly in the stems and leaf axils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Achyranthes porphyrostachya is often found in moist soils along riverbanks, wetlands, and disturbed areas, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Morphology

Achyranthes porphyrostachya has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically 5–10 cm long and 2–4 cm wide, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter green underside.

The stem is erect, cylindrical, and somewhat woody, with a rough, dark brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers with stamen and pistil structures, blooming in late spring to early summer, and is pollinated by wind. The fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that disperses its small, black seeds by wind.

The roots are fibrous and extensive, helping to anchor the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.
