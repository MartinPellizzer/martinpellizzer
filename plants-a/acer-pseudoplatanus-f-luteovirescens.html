Acer pseudoplatanus f. luteovirescens

Acer pseudoplatanus f. luteovirescens, commonly known as the golden-green maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, which displays a striking combination of yellow and green hues during the growing season. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
