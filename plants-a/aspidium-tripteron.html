Aspidium tripteron

Aspidium tripteron, commonly known as the three-lobed water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are typically divided into three lobes, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are arranged in a spiral pattern.

Aspidium tripteron is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
