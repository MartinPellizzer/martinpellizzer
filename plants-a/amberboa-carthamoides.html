Amberboa carthamoides

Amberboa carthamoides, commonly known as yellow woodflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, bushy appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Amberboa carthamoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
