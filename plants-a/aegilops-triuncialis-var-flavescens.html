Aegilops triuncialis var. flavescens

Aegilops triuncialis var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow wild oat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically yellow to reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This annual plant is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
