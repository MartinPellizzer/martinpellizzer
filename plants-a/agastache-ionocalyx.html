Agastache ionocalyx

Agastache ionocalyx, commonly known as purple hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikes of lavender to violet flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
