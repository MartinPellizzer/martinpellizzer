Anthurium columbianum

Anthurium columbianum, commonly known as the Colombian anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Colombia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped spathe that surrounds the spadix. The plant produces striking, colorful bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. Anthurium columbianum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental horticulture.
