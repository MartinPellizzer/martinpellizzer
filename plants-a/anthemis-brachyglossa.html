Anthemis brachyglossa

Anthemis brachyglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellow flowers with white ray florets, typical of the genus Anthemis. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
