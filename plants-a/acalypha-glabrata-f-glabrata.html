Acalypha glabrata f. glabrata

Acalypha glabrata f. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf spurflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and distinctive, spire-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a showy display of colors ranging from red to yellow.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
