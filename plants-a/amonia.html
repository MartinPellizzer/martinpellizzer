Amonia

Amonia, commonly known as horehound, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Amonia is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, often growing in dry, rocky soils.

It has a strong, mint-like aroma and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
