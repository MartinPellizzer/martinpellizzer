Avena bromoides var. dubia

Avena bromoides var. dubia, commonly known as the dwarf oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, hairy stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
