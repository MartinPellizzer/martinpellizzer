Aquilegia albopleno

Aquilegia albopleno, commonly known as the white-flowered columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals that are often adorned with a distinctive central claw. The plant has compound leaves that are divided into lance-shaped segments, giving it a finely toothed appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or woodland borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.
