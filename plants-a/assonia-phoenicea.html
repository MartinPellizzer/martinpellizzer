Assonia phoenicea

Assonia phoenicea, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and a hollow, cylindrical stem that resembles a drinking cup. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
