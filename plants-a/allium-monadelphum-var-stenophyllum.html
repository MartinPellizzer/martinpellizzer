Allium monadelphum var. stenophyllum

Allium monadelphum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features a dense cluster of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and stiff. Its bulbs are small and have a mild garlic scent, though they are not typically used for culinary purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
