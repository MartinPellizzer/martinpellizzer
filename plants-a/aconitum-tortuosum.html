Aconitum tortuosum

Aconitum tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces globose, egg-shaped seed capsules that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with care.
