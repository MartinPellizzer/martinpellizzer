Amsonia eastwoodiana

Amsonia eastwoodiana, commonly known as Bluestem Blazingstar, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of blue-gray foliage and produces striking spikes of blue to purple flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Amsonia eastwoodiana is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
