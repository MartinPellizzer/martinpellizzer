Albuca aurea

Albuca aurea, commonly known as the golden albuca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer. Albuca aurea is well adapted to arid conditions and prefers well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
