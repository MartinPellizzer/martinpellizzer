Astragalus tawilicus

Astragalus tawilicus, commonly known as Tawil's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
