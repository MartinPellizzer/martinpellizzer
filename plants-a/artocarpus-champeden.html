Artocarpus champeden

Artocarpus champeden, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces a unique fruit with a fibrous, inedible outer husk. The fruit contains a sweet, sticky pulp that is highly valued in culinary and medicinal applications. The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in agroforestry systems.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
