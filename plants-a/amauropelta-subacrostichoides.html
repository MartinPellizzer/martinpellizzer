Amauropelta subacrostichoides

Amauropelta subacrostichoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are divided into four lobes, resembling a four-leaf clover. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it forms dense mats on the water surface. The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
