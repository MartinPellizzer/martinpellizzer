Acacia neurophylla subsp. erugata

Acacia neurophylla subsp.

erugata, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and phyllodes, which are modified leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic structures.

The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife. Acacia neurophylla subsp.

erugata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
