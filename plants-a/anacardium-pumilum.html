Anacardium pumilum

Anacardium pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf cashew, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
