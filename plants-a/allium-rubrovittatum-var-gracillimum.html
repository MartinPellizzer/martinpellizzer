Allium rubrovittatum var. gracillimum

Allium rubrovittatum var. gracillimum, commonly known as the slender purple stripe onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is characterized by its distinctive red-violet stripes on the bulb, which are more pronounced in the varietal form gracillimum. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
