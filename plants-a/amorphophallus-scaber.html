Amorphophallus scaber

Amorphophallus scaber, commonly known as the rough giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces large, arrow-shaped tubers that are used as a starchy food source in some regions. Its leaves are typically large and heart-shaped, with a rough texture that gives the species its scientific name.

The plant is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shady environments.
