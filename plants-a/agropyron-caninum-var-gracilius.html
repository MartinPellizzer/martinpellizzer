Agropyron caninum var. gracilius

Agropyron caninum var. gracilius, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. The plant features slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, spikelet-bearing branches. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
