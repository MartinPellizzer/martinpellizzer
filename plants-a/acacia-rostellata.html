Acacia rostellata

Acacia rostellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by animals and wind.

Acacia rostellata plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
