Arachis appressipila

Arachis appressipila, commonly known as the Brazilian peanut or wild peanut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, with small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces underground pods containing oval-shaped seeds, which are similar to cultivated peanuts but often smaller. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is adapted to poor soils, making it a resilient species.

Arachis appressipila is also valued for its potential in agricultural research due to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
