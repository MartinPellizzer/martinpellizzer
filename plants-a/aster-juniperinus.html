Aster juniperinus

Aster juniperinus, commonly known as the Juniper-like aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has needle-like leaves that resemble those of juniper, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Aster juniperinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in late summer and early fall.
