Allium porphyroprasum

Allium porphyroprasum, commonly known as the purple leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically purple or reddish in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making them suitable for culinary use.

Allium porphyroprasum is often grown for both ornamental and edible purposes in gardens.
