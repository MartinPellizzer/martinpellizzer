Aesculus × rubicunda var. versicolor

Aesculus × rubicunda var. versicolor, commonly known as the Variable Horsechestnut, is a hybrid species within the genus Aesculus, characterized by its striking, multicolored flowers.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are borne in upright panicles and display a range of colors from white to pink to red.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its visual impact.
