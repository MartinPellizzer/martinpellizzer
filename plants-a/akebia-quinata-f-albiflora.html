Akebia quinata f. albiflora

Akebia quinata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese hop plant, is a variant of the Akebia quinata species.

It is a climbing vine native to East Asia, characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, yellow, edible fruits that are similar to those of the common Akebia quinata. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The white flowers of this variety make it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
