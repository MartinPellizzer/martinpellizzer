Abies cephalonica var. arcadica

Abies cephalonica var. arcadica, commonly known as the Arcadian fir, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree produces small, spherical cones that release seeds, contributing to its reproductive cycle. Its habitat is typically found in high-altitude forests with cool, moist climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
