Allium backhousianum

Allium backhousianum, commonly known as the New Zealand garlic, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features a bulbous base with a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like scent.
