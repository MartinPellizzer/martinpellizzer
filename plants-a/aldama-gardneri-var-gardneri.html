Aldama gardneri var. gardneri

Aldama gardneri var. gardneri, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
