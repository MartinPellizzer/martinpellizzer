Aster illitus

Aster illitus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Aster illitus is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structure and ecological importance to pollinators.
