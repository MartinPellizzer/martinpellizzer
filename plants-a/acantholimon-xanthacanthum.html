Acantholimon xanthacanthum

Acantholimon xanthacanthum, commonly known as the yellow-cup stonecress, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
