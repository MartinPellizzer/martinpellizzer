Adiantum menglianense

Adiantum menglianense, commonly known as the Menglian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the shape of a feather. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive rachis that is smooth and hairless.

Adiantum menglianense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
