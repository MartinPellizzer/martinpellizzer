Aristida pilosa

Aristida pilosa, commonly known as hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in loose panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open prairies and disturbed sites.

Aristida pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
