Alectorolophus alpinus var. erectus

Alectorolophus alpinus var. erectus, commonly known as the Alpine feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of the Alps and surrounding mountain ranges.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that form dense, tufted clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens and conservation areas.
