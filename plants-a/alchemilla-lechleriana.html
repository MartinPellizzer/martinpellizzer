Alchemilla lechleriana

Alchemilla lechleriana, commonly known as the lechler's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Alchemilla lechleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
