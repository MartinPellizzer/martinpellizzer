Adenophora polymorpha var. integerrima

Adenophora polymorpha var. integerrima, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy as well. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
