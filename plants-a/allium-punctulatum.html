Allium punctulatum

Allium punctulatum, commonly known as the dotted onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, dotted appearance on its bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium punctulatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild culinary uses in some regions.
