Aethionema virgatum

Aethionema virgatum, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green color.

Aethionema virgatum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
