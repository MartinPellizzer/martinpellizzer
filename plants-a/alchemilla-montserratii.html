Alchemilla montserratii

Alchemilla montserratii, commonly known as the Montserrat lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the Pyrenees and Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Alchemilla montserratii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
