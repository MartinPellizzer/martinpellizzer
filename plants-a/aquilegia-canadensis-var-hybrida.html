Aquilegia canadensis var. hybrida

Aquilegia canadensis var. hybrida, commonly known as the Canadian columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a distinctive notched appearance, and its stems are hairy and upright. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
