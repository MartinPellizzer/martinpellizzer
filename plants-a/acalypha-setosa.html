Acalypha setosa

Acalypha setosa, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Acalypha setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
