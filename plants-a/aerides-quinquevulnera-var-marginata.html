Aerides quinquevulnera var. marginata

Aerides quinquevulnera var. marginata, commonly known as the marginated orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, white to pale green pubescence. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with yellow-green petals and a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
