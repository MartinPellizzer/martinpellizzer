Achillea alpina var. discoidea

Achillea alpina var. discoidea, commonly known as the alpine yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a compact, spreading growth habit with dark green, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
