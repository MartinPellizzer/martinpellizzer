Ardisia pseudocrispa

Ardisia pseudocrispa, commonly known as the glossy arrowwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. Ardisia pseudocrispa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is relatively low maintenance once established.
