Anthyllis vulneraria subvar. subunifoliata

Anthyllis vulneraria subvar. subunifoliata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces pods that contain small, dark seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a source of nectar for pollinators.
