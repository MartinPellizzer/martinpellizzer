Agrostis alba subsp. pauciflora

Agrostis alba subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, pale green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to whitish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
