Aegilops ovata var. africana

Aegilops ovata var. africana, commonly known as the African wild rye, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in semi-arid regions.

Aegilops ovata var. africana is valued for its drought tolerance and has been used in traditional agriculture and forage systems.
