Aspalathus divaricata subsp. horizontalis

Aspalathus divaricata subsp.

horizontalis, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring multiple stems that sprawl horizontally along the ground.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
