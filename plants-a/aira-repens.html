Aira repens

Aira repens, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, hair-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. The plant produces small, pale green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Aira repens is valued for its aromatic foliage and cultural significance among Indigenous communities.
