Arundinaria intermedia

Arundinaria intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a deep green color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are clustered in loose, branched inflorescences.

Arundinaria intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and horticulture.
