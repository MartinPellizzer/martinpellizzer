Aristolochia benadiriana

Aristolochia benadiriana, commonly known as the Benadir Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe, often with a striking purple or greenish hue. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, often twisted floral structure. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
