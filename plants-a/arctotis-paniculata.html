Arctotis paniculata

Arctotis paniculata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It produces panicles of small, showy florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arctotis paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
