Acer rufinerve var. marginatum

Acer rufinerve var. marginatum, commonly known as the red-veined maple with margins, is a variant of the red-veined maple native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring bright red veins contrasting with green leaves. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and turn vibrant colors in the fall, adding ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
