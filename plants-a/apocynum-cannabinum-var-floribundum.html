Apocynum cannabinum var. floribundum

Apocynum cannabinum var. floribundum, commonly known as showy dogbane, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant contains toxic milky sap and is part of the Apocynaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal significance.
