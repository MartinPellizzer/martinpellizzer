Amphicarpaea japonica

Amphicarpaea japonica, commonly known as Japanese false flax, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
