Anemone × wilczekii

Anemone × wilczekii, commonly known as the Wilczek's anemone, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Anemone coronaria and Anemone hupehensis.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in diverse habitats.
