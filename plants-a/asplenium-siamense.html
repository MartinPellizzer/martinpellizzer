Asplenium siamense

Asplenium siamense, commonly known as the Siam maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant.

The leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.
