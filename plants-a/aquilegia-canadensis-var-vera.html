Aquilegia canadensis var. vera

Aquilegia canadensis var. vera, commonly known as the Canadian columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
