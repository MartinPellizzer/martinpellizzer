Astragalus lepsensis

Astragalus lepsensis, commonly known as the Lepsen's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
