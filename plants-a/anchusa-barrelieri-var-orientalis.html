Anchusa barrelieri var. orientalis

Anchusa barrelieri var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and striking cobalt-blue flowers. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
