Asemeia hirsuta

Asemeia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy asemeia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Asemeia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
