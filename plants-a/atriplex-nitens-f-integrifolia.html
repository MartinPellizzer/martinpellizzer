Atriplex nitens f. integrifolia

Atriplex nitens f. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth saltbush, is a variant of the saltbush species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant is a shrub or small tree, often growing in saline soils and coastal areas. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain single seeds.

This species is well-suited for revegetation projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
