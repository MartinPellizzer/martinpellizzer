Aristolochia pavoniana

Aristolochia pavoniana, commonly known as the purple pipevine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, purple to violet flowers that resemble a pipe, hence its common name. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded habitats. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure that attracts specific pollinators.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
