Amaranthus albus var. puberulus

Amaranthus albus var. puberulus, commonly known as white amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it the specific epithet "puberulus.". The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

White amaranth is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional cuisines for its edible leaves and seeds.
