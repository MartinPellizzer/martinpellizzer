Aloe angustifolia

Aloe angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features linear, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in a powdery white coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers on erect inflorescences during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Aloe angustifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
