Alnus lanceolata

Alnus lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf alder, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and catkin flowers, which are typical of the Betulaceae family. The tree thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wetlands. It has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Alnus lanceolata is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
