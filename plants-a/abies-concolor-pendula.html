Abies concolor pendula

Abies concolor pendula, commonly known as the Colorado blue spruce, is a variant of the blue spruce species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and striking blue-gray foliage, which results from a waxy coating on the needles. The tree produces small, cone-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its slow growth rate and cold-hardiness make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The pendula variety is distinguished by its drooping branches, adding a unique, cascading appearance to the tree.
