Alchemilla pedata

Alchemilla pedata, commonly known as lesser mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters and blooming in early summer.

Alchemilla pedata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
