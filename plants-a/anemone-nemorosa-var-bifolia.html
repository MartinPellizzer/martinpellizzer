Anemone nemorosa var. bifolia

Anemone nemorosa var. bifolia, commonly known as the forest anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces umbel-like clusters of white or pale pink flowers with five petals, blooming in early spring. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its foliage and flowers are often adorned with a distinctive violet or purple tinge, enhancing its ornamental appeal.
