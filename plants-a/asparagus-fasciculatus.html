Asparagus fasciculatus

Asparagus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features wiry, spiny stems that often grow in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, edible berries. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Asparagus fasciculatus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique growth habit and resilience.
