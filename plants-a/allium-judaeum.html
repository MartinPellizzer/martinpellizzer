Allium judaeum

Allium judaeum, commonly known as the Judean onion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spherical, purple or pink flower clusters. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor similar to garlic.

Allium judaeum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
