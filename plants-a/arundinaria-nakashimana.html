Arundinaria nakashimana

Arundinaria nakashimana, commonly known as the Nakashima bamboo, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are typically green in color and often have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by tiny, oval-shaped seeds. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
