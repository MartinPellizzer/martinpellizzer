Artemisia momiyamae

Artemisia momiyamae, commonly known as the Japanese mugwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
