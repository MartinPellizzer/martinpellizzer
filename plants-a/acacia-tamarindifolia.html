Acacia tamarindifolia

Acacia tamarindifolia, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate and arranged in a feathery pattern. The tree produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by long, flat pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to dry climates and can thrive in poor soils, making it a valuable species for reforestation.

The plant also has medicinal uses and is sometimes used as a source of timber and fuel.
