Acer tataricum subsp. theiferum

Acer tataricum subsp.

theiferum, commonly known as the Siberian maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically green on top and paler underneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
