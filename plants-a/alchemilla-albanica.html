Alchemilla albanica

Alchemilla albanica, commonly known as Albanian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Alchemilla albanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
