Astragalus macrourus subsp. brevilobus

Astragalus macrourus subsp.

brevilobus, commonly known as the short-lobe milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands.

The plant features compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its seed pods are slender and curved, containing small, kidney-shaped seeds.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation and supports local pollinators.
