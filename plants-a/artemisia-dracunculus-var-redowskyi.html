Artemisia dracunculus var. redowskyi

Artemisia dracunculus var. redowskyi, commonly known as the Redowsky's tarragon, is a variant of the tarragon plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like thymol and methyl chavicol, contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
