Amaryllis aramasco

Amaryllis aramasco, commonly known as the white amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a red or purple throat. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and strap-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Amaryllis aramasco is known for its ability to bloom reliably in warm climates, adding a dramatic touch to ornamental landscapes.
