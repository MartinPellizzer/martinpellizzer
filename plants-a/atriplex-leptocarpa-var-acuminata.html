Atriplex leptocarpa var. acuminata

Atriplex leptocarpa var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharpleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are typically covered in a white, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.

Its ability to tolerate extreme drought and poor soil conditions makes it an important species in xerophytic plant communities.
