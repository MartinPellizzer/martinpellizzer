Aster fremontii var. parishii

Aster fremontii var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves. It is a perennial herb that produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a composite head.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for xeriscaping.
