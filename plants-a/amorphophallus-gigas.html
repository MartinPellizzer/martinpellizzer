Amorphophallus gigas

Amorphophallus gigas, commonly known as the giant elephant's foot, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its massive, bulbous underground stem, which can grow to over 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a showy purple spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its size and unique appearance, it is a popular subject in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
