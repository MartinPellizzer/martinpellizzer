Astragalus cruckshanksii

Astragalus cruckshanksii, commonly known as Cruckshanks' milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers are usually pale pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.
