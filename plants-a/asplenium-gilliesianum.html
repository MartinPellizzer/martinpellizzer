Asplenium gilliesianum

Asplenium gilliesianum, commonly known as the Gillies' spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to South America.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium gilliesianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
