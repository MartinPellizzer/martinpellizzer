Acrostichum bonariense

Acrostichum bonariense, commonly known as the Buenos Aires holly fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, compound leaves that are typically divided into numerous segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

Acrostichum bonariense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
