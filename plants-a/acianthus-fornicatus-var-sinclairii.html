Acianthus fornicatus var. sinclairii

Acianthus fornicatus var. sinclairii, commonly known as the Sinclair's acianthus, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish. Its morphology includes narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, stocky stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern and is often found in protected areas.
