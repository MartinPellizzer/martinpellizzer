Asemeia rosea

Asemeia rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Asemeia rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
