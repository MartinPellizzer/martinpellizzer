Aconitum variegatum subsp. rostratum

Aconitum variegatum subsp.

rostratum, commonly known as the rostrate monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence, characteristic of the genus Aconitum.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Like other members of its genus, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
