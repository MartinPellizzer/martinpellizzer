Aeschynanthus bracteatus var. orientalis

Aeschynanthus bracteatus var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental flame plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
