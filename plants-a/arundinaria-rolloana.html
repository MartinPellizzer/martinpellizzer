Arundinaria rolloana

Arundinaria rolloana, commonly known as the Rollo's bamboo, is a species of grass native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a tendency to droop under the weight of rain.

Arundinaria rolloana is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks and in dense tropical forests.
