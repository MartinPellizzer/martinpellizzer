Agropyron × laxum

Agropyron × laxum, commonly known as the wheatgrass or intermediate wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species that combines traits from both Agropyron and other related genera.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in forage production and has potential applications in erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Additionally, it is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes cultivated for human consumption.
