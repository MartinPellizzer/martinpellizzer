Arisarum vulgare subsp. exsertum

Arisarum vulgare subsp.

exsertum, commonly known as the yellow false lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers.

The leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
