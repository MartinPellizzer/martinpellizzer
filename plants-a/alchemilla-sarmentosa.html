Alchemilla sarmentosa

Alchemilla sarmentosa, commonly known as field lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its spreading stems and dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Alchemilla sarmentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
