Asclepias minor

Asclepias minor, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers and distinctive, velvety seed pods. The plant belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. Asclepias minor plays a crucial role in supporting monarch butterfly populations by providing nectar and serving as a host plant for their larvae.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient and ecologically significant species.
