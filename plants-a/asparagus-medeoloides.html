Asparagus medeoloides

Asparagus medeoloides, commonly known as the purple asparagus or false asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
