Anthyllis vulneraria var. kerneri

Anthyllis vulneraria var. kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's Scotch broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
