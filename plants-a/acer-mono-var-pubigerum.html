Acer mono var. pubigerum

Acer mono var. pubigerum, commonly known as the pubescent maple, is a variant of the singleleaf maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive pubescent (hairy) leaves that are often reddish or brownish in color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
