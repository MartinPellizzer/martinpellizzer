Astragalus lentiginosus var. fremontii

Astragalus lentiginosus var. fremontii, commonly known as Fremont's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
