Acacia pallens

Acacia pallens, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia pallens is well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.
