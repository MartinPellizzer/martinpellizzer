Artemisia jaxartica

Artemisia jaxartica, commonly known as the Jaxartian wormwood, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It has a woody stem and produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into achene fruits.

Artemisia jaxartica is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
