Astracantha gevashensis

Astracantha gevashensis, commonly known as the Georgian thornvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its spiny, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
