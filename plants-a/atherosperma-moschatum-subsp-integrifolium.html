Atherosperma moschatum subsp. integrifolium

Atherosperma moschatum subsp.

integrifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved matted apple, is a shrub native to Tasmania. It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are typically broad and entire.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and understoreys.
