Avena ludoviciana

Avena ludoviciana, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It is valued for its nutritional content and is used in livestock feed and human consumption.

Avena ludoviciana is also cultivated for its potential health benefits and as a forage crop.
