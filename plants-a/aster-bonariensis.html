Aster bonariensis

Aster bonariensis, commonly known as the Buenos Aires aster, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in various regions beyond its native habitat.
