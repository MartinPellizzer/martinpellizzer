Agrimonia eupatoria var. villosa

Agrimonia eupatoria var. villosa, commonly known as hairy agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
