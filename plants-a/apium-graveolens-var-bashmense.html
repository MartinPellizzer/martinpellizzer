Apium graveolens var. bashmense

Apium graveolens var. bashmense, commonly known as Bashkir parsley, is a wild variety of parsley native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fern-like, deeply lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and potential health benefits.
