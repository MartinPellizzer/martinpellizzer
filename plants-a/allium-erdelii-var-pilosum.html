Allium erdelii var. pilosum

Allium erdelii var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and long, cylindrical, hollow stems that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a mild garlic scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in certain regions.
