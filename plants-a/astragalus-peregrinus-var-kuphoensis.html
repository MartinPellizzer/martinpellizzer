Astragalus peregrinus var. kuphoensis

Astragalus peregrinus var. kuphoensis, commonly known as the Kuphoensian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
