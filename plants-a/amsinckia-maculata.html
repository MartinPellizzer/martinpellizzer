Amsinckia maculata

Amsinckia maculata, commonly known as the spotted fennel, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that are often used as a spice in cooking. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Amsinckia maculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
