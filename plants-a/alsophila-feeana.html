Alsophila feeana

Alsophila feeana, commonly known as the Fee's tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of its fronds.

Alsophila feeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
