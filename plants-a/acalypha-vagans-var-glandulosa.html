Acalypha vagans var. glandulosa

Acalypha vagans var. glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its glandular-hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, often sticky texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are usually alternate and ovate, with serrated edges.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.
