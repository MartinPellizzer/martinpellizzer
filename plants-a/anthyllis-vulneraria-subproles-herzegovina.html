Anthyllis vulneraria subproles herzegovina

Anthyllis vulneraria subproles herzegovina, commonly known as the Herzegovina broom, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
