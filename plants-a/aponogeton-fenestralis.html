Aponogeton fenestralis

Aponogeton fenestralis, commonly known as the fenestrated arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves with a unique fenestrated pattern, resembling a window or a sieve. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can spread rapidly, forming dense mats on the water surface.

Aponogeton fenestralis is popular in aquascaping due to its ornamental appearance and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
