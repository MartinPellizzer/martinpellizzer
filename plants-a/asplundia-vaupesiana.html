Asplundia vaupesiana

Asplundia vaupesiana, commonly known as the Vaupes arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves and a rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by the development of berries. Asplundia vaupesiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
