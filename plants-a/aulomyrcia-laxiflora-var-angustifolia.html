Aulomyrcia laxiflora var. angustifolia

Aulomyrcia laxiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or pink. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its botanical features include hairy stems and capsule fruits that release seeds when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
