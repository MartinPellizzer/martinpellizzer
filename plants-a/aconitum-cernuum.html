Aconitum cernuum

Aconitum cernuum, commonly known as the western monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum cernuum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of various alkaloids.
