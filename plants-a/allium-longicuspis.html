Allium longicuspis

Allium longicuspis, commonly known as the long-fruited garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a loose umbel, which bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Allium longicuspis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
