Alyxia oliviformis f. lanceolata

Alyxia oliviformis f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf alyxia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to garden settings.
