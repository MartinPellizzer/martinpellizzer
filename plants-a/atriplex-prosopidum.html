Atriplex prosopidum

Atriplex prosopidum, commonly known as shadscale, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and sagebrush communities.

Atriplex prosopidum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
