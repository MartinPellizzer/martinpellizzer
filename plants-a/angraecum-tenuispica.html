Angraecum tenuispica

Angraecum tenuispica, commonly known as the slender-spurred angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated flower spikes that bear small, fragrant flowers with long, narrow spurs. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Angraecum tenuispica is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil.
