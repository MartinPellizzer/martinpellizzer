Alsodeia disticha

Alsodeia disticha, commonly known as the common alsike clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Its delicate appearance and ecological benefits make it a notable species in botanical studies.
