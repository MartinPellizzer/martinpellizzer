Aconitum divergens

Aconitum divergens, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to nine lobes, and its stems are often hairy and erect. Aconitum divergens contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring caution in handling.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
