Anthemis melanolepis var. macrocarpa

Anthemis melanolepis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited tansy, is a variant of the tansy plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
