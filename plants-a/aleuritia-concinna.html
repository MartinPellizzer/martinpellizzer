Aleuritia concinna

Aleuritia concinna, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Aleuritia concinna is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and berries.
