Achillea atrata var. vulgaris

Achillea atrata var. vulgaris, commonly known as common yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of dark green, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its finely divided foliage and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
