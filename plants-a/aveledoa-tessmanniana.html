Aveledoa tessmanniana

Aveledoa tessmanniana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, fragrant blossoms during the wet season.

Due to its restricted habitat and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
