Astragalus suberosus var. hartmannii

Astragalus suberosus var. hartmannii, commonly known as Hartmann's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and drought-resistant foliage. Its leaves are typically compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often found in sandy or rocky soils and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
