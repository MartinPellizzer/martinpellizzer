Angelica bracteata

Angelica bracteata, commonly known as the Chinese angelica or Dong Chai Hu, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with large, compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its root is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it a significant species in both horticulture and herbal medicine.
