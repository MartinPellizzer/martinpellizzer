Aconitum arcuatum

Aconitum arcuatum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, arranged in a helmet-like shape on tall, sturdy stems. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply cut lobes, and its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous species. Aconitum arcuatum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
