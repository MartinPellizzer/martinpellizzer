Acer barbatum var. sinuosum

Acer barbatum var. sinuosum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The species thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
