Axonopus siccus

Axonopus siccus, commonly known as the dry axonopus, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, dense foliage and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Axonopus siccus is known for its ability to form dense mats, which can help prevent soil erosion.

Its botanical features make it a common component of grassland and disturbed soil ecosystems.
