Astragalus bequaertii

Astragalus bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments.
