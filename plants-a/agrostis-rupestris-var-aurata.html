Agrostis rupestris var. aurata

Agrostis rupestris var. aurata, commonly known as the golden rock grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces dense clusters of bright golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions. Its foliage is fine and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contrasts nicely with its vibrant floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
