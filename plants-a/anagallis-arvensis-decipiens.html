Anagallis arvensis decipiens

Anagallis arvensis decipiens, commonly known as the pretty willow or the deceptive willow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are often pink or white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and the stems are often sprawling or trailing.

The species is known for its deceptive appearance, as the flowers can resemble other plants, aiding in its survival through mimicry.
