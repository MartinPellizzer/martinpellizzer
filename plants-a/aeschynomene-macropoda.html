Aeschynomene macropoda

Aeschynomene macropoda, commonly known as large-seeded goatherd grass, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, elliptical seeds.

Aeschynomene macropoda is often used in forage and soil conservation due to its hardiness and nitrogen-fixing ability.
