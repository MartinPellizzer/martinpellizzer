Acer palmatum f. saintpaulianum

Acer palmatum f. saintpaulianum, commonly known as the Saintpaulian maple, is a compact, slow-growing variety of the Japanese maple.

It features striking red foliage that emerges in spring and turns orange in autumn, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a delicate, feathery appearance that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, preferring cooler climates.

This cultivar is valued for its vibrant coloration and is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
