Angelica paniculata

Angelica paniculata, commonly known as the clustered angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features large, compound leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, with a distinctive pungent odor. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a branched inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
