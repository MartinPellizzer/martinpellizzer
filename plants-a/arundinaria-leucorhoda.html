Arundinaria leucorhoda

Arundinaria leucorhoda, commonly known as the white-flowered bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, feathery texture.

Arundinaria leucorhoda is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
