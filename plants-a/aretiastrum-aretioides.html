Aretiastrum aretioides

Aretiastrum aretioides, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among milkweeds, and it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
