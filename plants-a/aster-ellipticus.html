Aster ellipticus

Aster ellipticus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
