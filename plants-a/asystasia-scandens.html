Asystasia scandens

Asystasia scandens, commonly known as the climbing asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically purple or violet in color, with two lips that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Asystasia scandens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
