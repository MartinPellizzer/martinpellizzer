Aristida plumosa var. alexandrina

Aristida plumosa var. alexandrina, commonly known as the Alexandrine feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features slender, wiry stems with fine, fluffy panicles that give it a distinctive feathery appearance. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish or brownish, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in dryland environments.
