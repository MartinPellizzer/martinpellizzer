Acanthus dioscoridis

Acanthus dioscoridis, commonly known as the Greek Acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences with violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its historical significance is evident in its use in classical architecture and decorative arts.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
