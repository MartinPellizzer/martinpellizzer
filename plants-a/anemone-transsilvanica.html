Anemone transsilvanica

Anemone transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian anemone, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to its natural surroundings.

Anemone transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
