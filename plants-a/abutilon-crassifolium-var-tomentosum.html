Abutilon crassifolium var. tomentosum

Abutilon crassifolium var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a desirable addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
