Arnica alpina var. attenuata

Arnica alpina var. attenuata, commonly known as the alpine arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disks, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes harvested for use in herbal remedies.
