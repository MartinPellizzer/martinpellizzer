Amaranthus muricatus

Amaranthus muricatus, commonly known as the spiny amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems covered in small, spiny projections. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the plant produces vibrant red, orange, or purple flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for various climates.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
