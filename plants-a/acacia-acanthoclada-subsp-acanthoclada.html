Acacia acanthoclada subsp. acanthoclada

Acacia acanthoclada subsp.

acanthoclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads in late summer to early autumn, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is often used in reforestation and soil stabilization due to its hardy nature and ability to fix nitrogen.
