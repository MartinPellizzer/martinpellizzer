Acoridium exasperatum

Acoridium exasperatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the substrate, while its leaves provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Acoridium exasperatum is often used in ornamental water gardens due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
