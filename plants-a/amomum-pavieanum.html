Amomum pavieanum

Amomum pavieanum, commonly known as the Vietnamese cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its aromatic seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal and culinary properties.
