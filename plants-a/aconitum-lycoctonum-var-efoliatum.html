Aconitum lycoctonum var. efoliatum

Aconitum lycoctonum var. efoliatum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care and kept away from children and pets.
