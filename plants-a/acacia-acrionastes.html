Acacia acrionastes

Acacia acrionastes, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellowish-green flower spikes during the winter months, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Acacia acrionastes is adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden environments, making it resilient to coastal conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
