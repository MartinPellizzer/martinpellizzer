Apocynum venetum subsp. russanovii

Apocynum venetum subsp.

russanovii, commonly known as Russian milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive spurred corona.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
