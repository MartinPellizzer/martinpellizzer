Annona peruviana

Annona peruviana, commonly known as the soursop or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Americas.

It produces a large, spiky, green fruit that turns yellow when ripe, with a white, creamy flesh that has a sweet and sour taste. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and contains various bioactive compounds, making it both nutritious and medicinally significant.
