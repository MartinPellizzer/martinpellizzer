Aquilegia micrantha

Aquilegia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of blue, purple, and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided with lobes that are often toothed or notched.

Aquilegia micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
