Acalypha seleriana

Acalypha seleriana, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, red-orange bracts that form a dense, showy inflorescence, often mistaken for petals. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by these brightly colored leaf-like structures. Its leaves are typically ovate to lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha seleriana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
