Astragalus incanus var. occidentalis

Astragalus incanus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.
