Azalea candida

Azalea candida, commonly known as the white azalea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with dense, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. Azalea candida prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and compact growth habit, which make it suitable for both container planting and ornamental gardens.
