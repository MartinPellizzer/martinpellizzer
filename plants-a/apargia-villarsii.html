Apargia villarsii

Apargia villarsii, commonly known as the mountain holly or small-leaf holly, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, and it produces white or pinkish flowers in late spring.

Apargia villarsii is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
