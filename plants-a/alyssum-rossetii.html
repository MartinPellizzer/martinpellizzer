Alyssum rossetii

Alyssum rossetii, commonly known as the Corsican alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Alyssum rossetii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
