Atriplex quadrivalvata var. quadrivalvata

Atriplex quadrivalvata var. quadrivalvata, commonly known as the four-valved saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, glaucous leaves that are typically four-lobed, giving it the species name "quadrivalvata.". The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
