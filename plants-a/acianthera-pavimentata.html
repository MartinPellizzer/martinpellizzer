Acianthera pavimentata

Acianthera pavimentata, commonly known as the pavement orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizomatous stem and produces numerous small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat, and its roots are often covered in a thick layer of mycorrhizal fungi. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in grasslands and forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
