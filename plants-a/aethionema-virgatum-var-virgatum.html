Aethionema virgatum var. virgatum

Aethionema virgatum var. virgatum, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and features small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces bright yellow stamens that contrast with its petals, enhancing its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, and it is valued for its ornamental value in horticulture.
