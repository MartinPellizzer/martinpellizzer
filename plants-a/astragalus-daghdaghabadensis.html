Astragalus daghdaghabadensis

Astragalus daghdaghabadensis, commonly known as the Daghdaghabad milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its scientific classification and ecological significance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
