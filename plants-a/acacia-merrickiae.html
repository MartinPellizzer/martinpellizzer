Acacia merrickiae

Acacia merrickiae, commonly known as the Ghanan acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are feather-like in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. Acacia merrickiae is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such climates.

It is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
