Anagallis alternifolia var. angustifolia

Anagallis alternifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, often hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
