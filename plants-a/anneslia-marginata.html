Anneslia marginata

Anneslia marginata, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight margin that is wavy or toothed.

Anneslia marginata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
