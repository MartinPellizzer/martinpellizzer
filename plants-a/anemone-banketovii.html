Anemone banketovii

Anemone banketovii, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five petals, resembling those of other anemone species. Its leaves are basal, palmately divided, and often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Anemone banketovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
