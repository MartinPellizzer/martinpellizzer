Allantodia yaku-montana

Allantodia yaku-montana, commonly known as the Yaku-Montana bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Japan.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Allantodia yaku-montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
