Aloe lettyae

Aloe lettyae, commonly known as the Letty's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Aloe lettyae thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
