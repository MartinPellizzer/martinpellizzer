Alsophila stipularis

Alsophila stipularis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome produces both roots and new shoots, allowing it to grow rapidly under suitable conditions.

Alsophila stipularis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to create a lush, tropical appearance in gardens.
