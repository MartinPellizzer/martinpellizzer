Acacia donnelliana

Acacia donnelliana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure and its distinctive, feathery leaves that are compound and alternate. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom during the cooler months, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Acacia donnelliana is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
