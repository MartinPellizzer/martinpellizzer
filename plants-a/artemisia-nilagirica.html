Artemisia nilagirica

Artemisia nilagirica, commonly known as Nilagiri wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant contains essential oils and various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Its resilience to cold and poor soil conditions contributes to its ecological significance in mountainous ecosystems.
