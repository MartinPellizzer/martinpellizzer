Adiantum cordatum

Adiantum cordatum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the hair of a maiden, hence its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a distinctive, arching shape.

Adiantum cordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
