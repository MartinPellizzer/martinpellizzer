Aster drummondii subsp. texanus

Aster drummondii subsp.

texanus, commonly known as the Texas aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, purple to pinkish-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a composite head.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
