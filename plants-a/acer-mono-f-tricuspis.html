Acer mono f. tricuspis

Acer mono f. tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed maple, is a unique variant of the single-leaf maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three lobes, giving it a striking appearance. This ornamental tree is native to China and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens. The leaves change color throughout the seasons, adding visual interest to the landscape.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
