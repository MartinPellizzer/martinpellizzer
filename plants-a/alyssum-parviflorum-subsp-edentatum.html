Alyssum parviflorum subsp. edentatum

Alyssum parviflorum subsp.

edentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming throughout the summer months.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a valued addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
