Austrocactus hibernus

Austrocactus hibernus, commonly known as the Southern Winter Cactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that are typically covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, which is unusual for most cacti. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
