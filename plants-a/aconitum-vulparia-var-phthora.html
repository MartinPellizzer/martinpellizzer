Aconitum vulparia var. phthora

Aconitum vulparia var. phthora, commonly known as the European aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a deep green color and a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
