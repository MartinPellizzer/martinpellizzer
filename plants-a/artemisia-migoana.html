Artemisia migoana

Artemisia migoana, commonly known as Migo's wormwood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves contain volatile oils and compounds such as thujone, which contribute to its strong scent and potential therapeutic uses.

Artemisia migoana thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
