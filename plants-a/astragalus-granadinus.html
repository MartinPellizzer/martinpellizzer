Astragalus granadinus

Astragalus granadinus, commonly known as the granadine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep taproots that allow it to access groundwater.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife, though it is not widely cultivated for agricultural purposes.
