Alyssum myagroides

Alyssum myagroides, commonly known as yellow alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, succulent-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a versatile and ornamental plant in horticultural settings.
