Aconitum jiulongense

Aconitum jiulongense, commonly known as the Long Dragon Aconite, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces follicles containing toxic alkaloids, which are responsible for its potent medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxicity, it requires careful handling and is primarily used in traditional Chinese medicine.
