Acalypha flexuosa

Acalypha flexuosa, commonly known as the zigzag hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often zigzag-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, hibiscus-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Acalypha flexuosa thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.
