Astragalus iodanthus

Astragalus iodanthus, commonly known as yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus iodanthus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
