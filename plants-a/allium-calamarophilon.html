Allium calamarophilon

Allium calamarophilon, commonly known as the sea garlic, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to salty, coastal environments.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
