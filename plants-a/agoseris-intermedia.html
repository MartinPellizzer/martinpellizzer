Agoseris intermedia

Agoseris intermedia, commonly known as the western blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Agoseris intermedia has fibrous roots and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
