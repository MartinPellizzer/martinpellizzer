Ambrosia velutina

Ambrosia velutina, commonly known as the velvet ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils. Ambrosia velutina is an annual herb that often invades open areas, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its habitat.

Its pollen can cause allergic reactions in some individuals, making it a plant of both ecological and health significance.
