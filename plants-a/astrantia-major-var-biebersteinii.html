Astrantia major var. biebersteinii

Astrantia major var. biebersteinii, commonly known as the Siberian aster, is a perennial herb native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a soft, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
