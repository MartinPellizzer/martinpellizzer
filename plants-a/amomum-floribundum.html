Amomum floribundum

Amomum floribundum, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or wild ginger, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinct, spicy aroma.

Amomum floribundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
