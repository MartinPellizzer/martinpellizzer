Acer palmatum f. tricolor

Acer palmatum f. tricolor, commonly known as the three-color maple, is a striking ornamental shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves that change color throughout the seasons, displaying shades of red, green, and yellow. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its delicate, pendulous flowers appear in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall.

This cultivar is widely appreciated for its vibrant foliage and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic value.
