Arundina subsessilis

Arundina subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, clump-forming stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is popular in horticulture.
