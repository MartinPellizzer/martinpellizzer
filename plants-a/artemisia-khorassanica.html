Artemisia khorassanica

Artemisia khorassanica, commonly known as Khorasan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in composite heads. Artemisia khorassanica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.
