Aesculus × neglecta var. erythroclados

Aesculus × neglecta var. erythroclados, commonly known as the red-flowered horse chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its showy, red bracts that surround the yellowish flowers. The plant produces large, spiny capsules that contain glossy, dark brown seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
