Adonis coerulea

Adonis coerulea, commonly known as the blue adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
