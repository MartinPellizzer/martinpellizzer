Aspalathus chenopoda var. gracilis

Aspalathus chenopoda var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bushveld pea, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is drought-tolerant and has adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
