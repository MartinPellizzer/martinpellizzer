Achimenes rendatleri

Achimenes rendatleri, commonly known as the red-tipped achimenes, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Achimenes rendatleri is often cultivated as a garden plant due to its vibrant blooms and ease of care.

Morphology

Achimenes rendatleri has heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is upright, covered with a smooth, reddish-brown bark, and grows in a compact, bushy pattern. Its flowers are tubular, with a vibrant pink to red color, and bloom from late spring to early summer; they are primarily pollinated by hummingbirds. The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant in moist, well-drained soil.
