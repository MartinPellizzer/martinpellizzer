Acaena × eglochidiata

Acaena × eglochidiata, commonly known as the coastal brambles or New Zealand thistles, is a hybrid shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and produces small, showy yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
