Anthericum makoyanum

Anthericum makoyanum, commonly known as the Makoy's arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often green with a reddish tinge. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers arranged on separate parts of the spadix.

Anthericum makoyanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
