Agropyron repens lusus semiverticillatum

Agropyron repens lusus semiverticillatum, commonly known as the creeping wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous growth habit.

It features narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and has small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is used in some restoration projects.

Its botanical structure supports its resilience in harsh environments.
