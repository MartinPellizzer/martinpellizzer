Atadinus cornifolius var. denudatus

Atadinus cornifolius var. denudatus, commonly known as the naked leafed atadinus, is a lesser-known plant species with unique botanical features.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its habitat is primarily in temperate regions, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
