Aristolochia tonkinensis

Aristolochia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese pipevine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and intricate, tubular flowers that resemble pipes. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition, including various aristolochic acids. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, often unpleasant odor, which attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
