Aconitum franchetii var. geniculatum

Aconitum franchetii var. geniculatum, commonly known as the fragrant aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and it produces follicles containing dark brown seeds.

This variety is noted for its strong fragrance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
