Abrus madagascariensis var. parvifolius

Abrus madagascariensis var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved scarlet bean, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and produces striking red pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. The leaves are small and palmately lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a distinctive, often toxic appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
