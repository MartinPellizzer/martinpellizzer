Achillea salsuginea

Achillea salsuginea, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads composed of small white or pale yellow florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its resilience and adaptability make it a common weed in disturbed soils.
