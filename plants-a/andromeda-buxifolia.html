Andromeda buxifolia

Andromeda buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf andromeda, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a broadleaf evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in shaded woodlands. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval-shaped and often have a waxy texture. Its showy clusters of white or pinkish flowers appear in the spring, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Andromeda buxifolia is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 9.
