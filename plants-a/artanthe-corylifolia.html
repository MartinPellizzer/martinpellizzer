Artanthe corylifolia

Artanthe corylifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and delicate blooms.

Artanthe corylifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
