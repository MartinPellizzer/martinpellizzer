Acanthopanax evodiifolius var. glaucus

Acanthopanax evodiifolius var. glaucus, commonly known as the glaucous arrowhead plant, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often compound leaves that are typically glaucous, or covered in a whitish waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
