Astragalus kelikanensis

Astragalus kelikanensis, commonly known as the Kelikan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Astragalus kelikanensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

It is also of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
