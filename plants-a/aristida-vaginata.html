Aristida vaginata

Aristida vaginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pink to reddish in color. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive sheathing base, which gives it its scientific name "vaginata," meaning "sheath-bearing.".

Aristida vaginata is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
