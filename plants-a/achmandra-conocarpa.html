Achmandra conocarpa

Achmandra conocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican whiteflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
