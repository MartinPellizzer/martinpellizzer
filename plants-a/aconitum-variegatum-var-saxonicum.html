Aconitum variegatum var. saxonicum

Aconitum variegatum var. saxonicum, commonly known as the Saxony monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple, hooded flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with a distinctive veined pattern. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
