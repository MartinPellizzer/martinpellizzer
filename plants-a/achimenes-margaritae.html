Achimenes margaritae

Achimenes margaritae, commonly known as the silverleaf achimenes, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a white or silver central spot. The plant produces tubular, spurred flowers that are typically borne on erect, branching stems. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture.

Achimenes margaritae thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
