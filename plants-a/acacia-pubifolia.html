Acacia pubifolia

Acacia pubifolia, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, pale green foliage and distinctive, soft, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia pubifolia thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

It is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its ornamental appeal.
