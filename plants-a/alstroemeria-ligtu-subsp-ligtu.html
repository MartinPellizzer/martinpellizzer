Alstroemeria ligtu subsp. ligtu

Alstroemeria ligtu subsp.

ligtu, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of colorful, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
