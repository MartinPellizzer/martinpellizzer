Abronia villosa f. rubescens

Abronia villosa f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red and pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
