Acrophorus chaerophyllus

Acrophorus chaerophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Acrophorus chaerophyllus is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, drought-resistant foliage.
