Asperula effusa

Asperula effusa, commonly known as woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate leaflets and is often found in shaded, moist woodland areas. Its flowers are clustered in loose panicles and are known for their pleasant, spicy aroma.

Asperula effusa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
