Abelia spathulata f. colorata

Abelia spathulata f. colorata, commonly known as the variegated spathulata abelia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features attractive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with striking white and green variegation. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red during the summer months. It is well-suited for container gardening or as a border plant due to its compact size and ornamental foliage.

This cultivar thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
