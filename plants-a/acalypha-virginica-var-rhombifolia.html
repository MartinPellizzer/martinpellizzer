Acalypha virginica var. rhombifolia

Acalypha virginica var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive, rhomboid-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, red or orange seed capsules. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
