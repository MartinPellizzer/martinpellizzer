Abies lasiocarpa var. pendula

Abies lasiocarpa var. pendula, commonly known as the pendant subalpine fir, is a variant of the subalpine fir native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop downward, giving the tree a distinctive, weeping appearance. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in high-altitude environments. This variety thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in subalpine and alpine zones.

Its botanical features make it an important component of mountain ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to local biodiversity.
