Artemisia jarovoi

Artemisia jarovoi, commonly known as the Jarov's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense heads, typical of the composite flower structure. Artemisia jarovoi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
