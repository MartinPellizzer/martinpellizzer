Arenga microcarpa var. keyensis

Arenga microcarpa var. keyensis, commonly known as the Key Island areca palm, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, facilitating its reproduction. Its fibrous leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The species is adapted to coastal environments and thrives in sandy soils with ample sunlight.
