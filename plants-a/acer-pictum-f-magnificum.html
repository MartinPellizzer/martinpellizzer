Acer pictum f. magnificum

Acer pictum f. magnificum, commonly known as the magnificum maple, is a striking variant of the painted maple.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that display vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues during the fall. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

Its foliage provides a dramatic visual display, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
