Aquilegia hookeri

Aquilegia hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, purple, and blue. The plant features compound leaves with palmate divisions and produces showy, five-petaled blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
