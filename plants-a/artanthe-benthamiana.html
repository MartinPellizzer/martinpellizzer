Artanthe benthamiana

Artanthe benthamiana, commonly known as the Bentham's artanthe, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pink to red flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Artanthe benthamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and seasonal blooms.
