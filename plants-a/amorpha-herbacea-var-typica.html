Amorpha herbacea var. typica

Amorpha herbacea var. typica, commonly known as the yellow woodlot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with 5 to 9 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.
