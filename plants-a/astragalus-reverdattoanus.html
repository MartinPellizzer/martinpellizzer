Astragalus reverdattoanus

Astragalus reverdattoanus, commonly known as the Reverdatto milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, and it produces slender, hairy stems.

Astragalus reverdattoanus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
