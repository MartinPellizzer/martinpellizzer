Adenocalymma sepiarium

Adenocalymma sepiarium, commonly known as the sea bean, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in sandy or saline soils, adapting well to coastal environments. Its leaves are usually alternate and may have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Adenocalymma sepiarium is often found in dune habitats, playing a role in stabilizing sand dunes through its extensive root system.
