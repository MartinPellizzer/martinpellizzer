Anisanthus splendens

Anisanthus splendens, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange ray florets and dark purple disk florets. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with a branched stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anisanthus splendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
