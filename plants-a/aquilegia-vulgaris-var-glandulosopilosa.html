Aquilegia vulgaris var. glandulosopilosa

Aquilegia vulgaris var. glandulosopilosa, commonly known as the glandular-pilose columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five petals that are often adorned with a distinctive, glandular pubescence. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variant is notable for its unique floral morphology and ecological adaptability.
