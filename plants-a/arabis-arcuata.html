Arabis arcuata

Arabis arcuata, commonly known as the bentweed or curlyweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating.

Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
