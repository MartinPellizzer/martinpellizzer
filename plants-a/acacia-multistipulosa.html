Acacia multistipulosa

Acacia multistipulosa, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and compound leaves with small, alternate leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia multistipulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
