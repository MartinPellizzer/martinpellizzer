Abronia arenaria

Abronia arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming from spring to summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Abronia arenaria is adapted to coastal conditions and is often found in dunes and other sandy habitats.
