Achimenes coccinea var. intermedia

Achimenes coccinea var. intermedia, commonly known as the red-flowered achimenes, is a hybrid species within the Gesneriaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer, often with a contrasting yellow or orange throat. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. It has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its ornamental value.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is typically propagated through cuttings rather than seeds.
