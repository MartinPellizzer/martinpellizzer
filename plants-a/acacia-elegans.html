Acacia elegans

Acacia elegans, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia elegans thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
