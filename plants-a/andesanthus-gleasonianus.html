Andesanthus gleasonianus

Andesanthus gleasonianus, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to withstand strong winds and intense sunlight.

Andesanthus gleasonianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
