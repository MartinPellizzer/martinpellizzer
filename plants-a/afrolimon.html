Afrolimon

Afrolimon, commonly known as the African limon, is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, fragrant flowers.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly in regions with warm, humid climates. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually simple, opposite, and have a distinctive citrus-like scent when crushed.

Afrolimon is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
