Anthericum curviscapum

Anthericum curviscapum, commonly known as the curved-stalked lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and sword-shaped, growing from a rhizomatous root system. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with six petals that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Anthericum curviscapum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
