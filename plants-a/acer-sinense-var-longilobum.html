Acer sinense var. longilobum

Acer sinense var. longilobum, commonly known as the Chinese maple or long-lobed maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with elongated lobes that give it a unique appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red-orange samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
