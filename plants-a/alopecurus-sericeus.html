Alopecurus sericeus

Alopecurus sericeus, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and slender, erect stems that produce small, blue-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a silky texture, and it is well adapted to temperate climates.

Alopecurus sericeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
