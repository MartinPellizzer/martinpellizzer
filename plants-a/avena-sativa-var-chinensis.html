Avena sativa var. chinensis

Avena sativa var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese oat or Chinese barley, is a variant of the oat species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces edible grains that are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and has been cultivated for centuries in regions such as China and Korea.
