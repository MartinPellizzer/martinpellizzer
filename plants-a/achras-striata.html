Achras striata

Achras striata, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support for various wildlife species.
