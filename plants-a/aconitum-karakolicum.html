Aconitum karakolicum

Aconitum karakolicum, commonly known as Karakolic monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though caution is necessary due to its toxicity.
