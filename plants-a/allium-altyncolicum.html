Allium altyncolicum

Allium altyncolicum, commonly known as the Altyncol Mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and grass-like basal leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices in its native regions.

Allium altyncolicum thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
