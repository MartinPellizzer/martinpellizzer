Acacia wrightii

Acacia wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Acacia wrightii is adapted to arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for desert wildlife.
