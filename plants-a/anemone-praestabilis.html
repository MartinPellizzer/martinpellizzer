Anemone praestabilis

Anemone praestabilis, commonly known as the showy anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of finely cut, gray-green leaves that help it survive cold winters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically beneficial addition to native plant landscapes.
