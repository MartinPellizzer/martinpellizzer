Acacia crinita

Acacia crinita, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are often followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
