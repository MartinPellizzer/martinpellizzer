Ammannia kilimandscharica var. kilimandscharica

Ammannia kilimandscharica var. kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro ammannia, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It features bright red, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, moist climates of its native range.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
