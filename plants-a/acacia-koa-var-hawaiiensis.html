Acacia koa var. hawaiiensis

Acacia koa var. hawaiiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian koa tree, is a native species of Hawaii characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is well adapted to the tropical climate of the Hawaiian Islands. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its wood is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities.

The plant plays an important ecological role in the native forests of Hawaii.
