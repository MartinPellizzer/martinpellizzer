Artocarpus gomezianus

Artocarpus gomezianus, commonly known as the Philippine native breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a fruit that resembles a breadfruit, which is edible when ripe. The fruit has a rough, spiny exterior and a starchy, sweet interior.

It is often cultivated for both its fruit and its ornamental value.
