Acalypha grandis var. villosa

Acalypha grandis var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a rough texture due to the dense pubescence. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, often with a prominent central disk.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats or along forest edges.
