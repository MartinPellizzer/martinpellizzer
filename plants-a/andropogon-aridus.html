Andropogon aridus

Andropogon aridus, commonly known as the desert grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Andropogon genus. It is well-suited to harsh environments due to its deep root system and drought tolerance.

Andropogon aridus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
