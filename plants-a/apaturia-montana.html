Apaturia montana

Apaturia montana, commonly known as the mountain apatula, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display a mix of purple and white hues. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in loose, nutrient-poor soils.

Apaturia montana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
