Acer coriaceifolium

Acer coriaceifolium, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, grey bark. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and aesthetic purposes.
