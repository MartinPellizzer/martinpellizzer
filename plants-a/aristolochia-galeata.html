Aristolochia galeata

Aristolochia galeata, commonly known as the purple swallowwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, purple to violet flowers that resemble a bird's beak. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is considered invasive in some regions.
