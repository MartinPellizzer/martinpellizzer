Acacia albida var. microfoliolata

Acacia albida var. microfoliolata, commonly known as the small-leaved white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and small, narrow leaves with a pale green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils.

The species is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
