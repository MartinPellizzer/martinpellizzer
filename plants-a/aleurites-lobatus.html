Aleurites lobatus

Aleurites lobatus, commonly known as the candle nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into hard, spiny fruits containing a single seed. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets and its ability to thrive in coastal and saline environments. The seeds, known as candle nuts, have a high oil content and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
