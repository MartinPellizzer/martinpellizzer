Avicennia germinans f. brasiliensis

Avicennia germinans f. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mangrove shrub, is a variant of the black mangrove that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is typically found in coastal areas of Brazil, where it thrives in brackish water environments. This plant has specialized adaptations, including aerial roots that aid in gas exchange and stabilization in soft, waterlogged soils. Its leaves are small, thick, and often covered with a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

The plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
