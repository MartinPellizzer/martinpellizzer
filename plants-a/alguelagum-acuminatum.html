Alguelagum acuminatum

Alguelagum acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
