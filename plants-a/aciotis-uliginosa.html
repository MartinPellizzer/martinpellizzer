Aciotis uliginosa

Aciotis uliginosa, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found along the edges of ponds and marshes.

Aciotis uliginosa plays an important role in supporting pollinators, particularly monarch butterflies.
