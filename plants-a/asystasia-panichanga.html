Asystasia panichanga

Asystasia panichanga, commonly known as the pink-flowered asystasia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its attractive, tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often forming dense clumps with hairy stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Asystasia panichanga is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
