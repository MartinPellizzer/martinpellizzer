Agave asperrima subsp. maderensis

Agave asperrima subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color and covered in white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a landscape plant.
