Astragalus missouriensis f. longipes

Astragalus missouriensis f. longipes, commonly known as the Missouri milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to prairie and woodland habitats.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species for pollinators and native plant restoration projects.
