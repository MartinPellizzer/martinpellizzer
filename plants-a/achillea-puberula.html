Achillea puberula

Achillea puberula, commonly known as silky yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine white hairs. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, arranged in flat-topped clusters.

Silky yarrow is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
