Aristolochia abbreviata

Aristolochia abbreviata, commonly known as the small Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its striking, purple-tinged, hooded flowers. The plant is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and typically grows in moist, wooded areas. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and the plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.
