Aspilia cylindrocephala

Aspilia cylindrocephala, commonly known as the cylindrical-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a tall, slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its most distinctive characteristic is its spherical, yellow flower head, which is tightly packed with small florets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
