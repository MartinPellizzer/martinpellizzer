Argyreia hirsutissima var. collettii

Argyreia hirsutissima var. collettii, commonly known as the Collett's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
