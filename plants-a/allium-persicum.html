Allium persicum

Allium persicum, commonly known as the Persian onion, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor similar to leeks.

Allium persicum is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary uses.
