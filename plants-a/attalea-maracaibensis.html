Attalea maracaibensis

Attalea maracaibensis, commonly known as the Maracaibo palm, is a species native to South America, particularly found in the northern regions of Venezuela and Colombia.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers, which eventually develop into edible fruits resembling small, round coconuts. The fruit is valued for its nutritional content and is often consumed raw or processed into beverages.

This palm is well adapted to tropical climates and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.
