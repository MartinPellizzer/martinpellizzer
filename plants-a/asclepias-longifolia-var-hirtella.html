Asclepias longifolia var. hirtella

Asclepias longifolia var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy milkweed, is a variant of the milkweed plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds.

This species serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
