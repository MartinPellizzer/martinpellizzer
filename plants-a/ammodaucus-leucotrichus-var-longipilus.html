Ammodaucus leucotrichus var. longipilus

Ammodaucus leucotrichus var. longipilus, commonly known as the long-haired fennel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable garden plant.
