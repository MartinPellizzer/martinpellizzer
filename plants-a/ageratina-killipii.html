Ageratina killipii

Ageratina killipii, commonly known as Killip's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and pine flatwoods. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are blue to violet in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ageratina killipii is often found in swampy areas and is adapted to periodic flooding.
