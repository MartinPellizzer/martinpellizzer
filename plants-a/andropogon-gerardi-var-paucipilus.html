Andropogon gerardi var. paucipilus

Andropogon gerardi var. paucipilus, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often blue-green in color, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie and woodland habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
