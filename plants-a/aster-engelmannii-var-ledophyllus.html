Aster engelmannii var. ledophyllus

Aster engelmannii var. ledophyllus, commonly known as Engelmann's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
