Argyrolobium shirense var. elgonense

Argyrolobium shirense var. elgonense, commonly known as the East African silver globular pea, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical or globular shape, with dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
