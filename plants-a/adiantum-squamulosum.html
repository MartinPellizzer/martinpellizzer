Adiantum squamulosum

Adiantum squamulosum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in small, scale-like structures, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense mat of roots, which help anchor it to rocky or forest substrates.

Adiantum squamulosum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
