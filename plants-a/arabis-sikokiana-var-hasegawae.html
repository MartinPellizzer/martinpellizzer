Arabis sikokiana var. hasegawae

Arabis sikokiana var. hasegawae, commonly known as the Hasegawa's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, rounded pods that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
