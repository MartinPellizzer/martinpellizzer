Aconitum paniculigerum var. ochroleucum

Aconitum paniculigerum var. ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves with a waxy surface, and its stems are often covered in stiff hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is distinguished by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage.
