Asteranthemum vulgare var. uniflorum

Asteranthemum vulgare var. uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower asteranthemum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant produces opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both decorative and medicinal purposes.
