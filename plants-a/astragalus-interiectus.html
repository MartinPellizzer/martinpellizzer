Astragalus interiectus

Astragalus interiectus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus interiectus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
