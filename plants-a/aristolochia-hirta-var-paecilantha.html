Aristolochia hirta var. paecilantha

Aristolochia hirta var. paecilantha, commonly known as the hairy Dutchman's pipe, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, aromatic scent. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are hairy, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by specific insect species, making it an important part of its ecological community.
