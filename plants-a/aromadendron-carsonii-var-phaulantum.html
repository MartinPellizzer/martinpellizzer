Aromadendron carsonii var. phaulantum

Aromadendron carsonii var. phaulantum, commonly known as the Carson's aromatic tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, resinous scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or grayish hue, while its leaves are simple and opposite.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
