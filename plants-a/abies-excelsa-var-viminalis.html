Abies excelsa var. viminalis

Abies excelsa var. viminalis, commonly known as the Himalayan fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The cones are typically pendulous and contain seeds enclosed in woody structures. This variety is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
