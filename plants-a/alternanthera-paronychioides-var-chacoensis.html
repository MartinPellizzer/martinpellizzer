Alternanthera paronychioides var. chacoensis

Alternanthera paronychioides var. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco Alternanthera, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and pink bracts that resemble flowers, though it produces small, inconspicuous true flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often used in aquascaping for their visual appeal. It thrives in warm, humid conditions with moderate to high light intensity.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological studies in aquatic environments.
