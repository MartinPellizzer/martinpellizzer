Aconitum tongolense var. tongolense

Aconitum tongolense var. tongolense, commonly known as the Tongolense monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and its roots contain potent alkaloids.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by herbivores and requires careful handling.
