Astragalus vehiculus

Astragalus vehiculus, commonly known as the vehicle milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, round seeds.

Astragalus vehiculus is part of the legume family (Fabaceae) and has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.
