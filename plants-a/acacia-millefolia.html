Acacia millefolia

Acacia millefolia, commonly known as the laceleaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique foliage and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
