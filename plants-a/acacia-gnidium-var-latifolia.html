Acacia gnidium var. latifolia

Acacia gnidium var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and linear, and its yellow flowers that appear in dense, globular clusters. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.

The wood of this species is hard and durable, making it useful for construction and fuel.
