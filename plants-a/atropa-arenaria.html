Atropa arenaria

Atropa arenaria, commonly known as the desert aconite, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Despite its beauty, it is toxic due to the presence of alkaloids, making it a plant to be handled with care.
