Amorphophallus hewittii

Amorphophallus hewittii, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a unique, often foul-smelling flower that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
