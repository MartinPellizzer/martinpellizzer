Anthemis artemisiifolia

Anthemis artemisiifolia, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and bright yellow ray flowers that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
