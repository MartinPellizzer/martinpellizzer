Anoplanthus biebersteinii var. boissieri

Anoplanthus biebersteinii var. boissieri, commonly known as the Armenian tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, scale-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is noted for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy plant in its native habitat.
