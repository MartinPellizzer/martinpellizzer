Abutilon arequipense

Abutilon arequipense, commonly known as the Arequipa abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and birdlife.
