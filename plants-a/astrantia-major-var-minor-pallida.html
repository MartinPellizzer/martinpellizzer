Astrantia major var. minor-pallida

Astrantia major var. minor-pallida, commonly known as the pale meadow sweet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features showy, flat-topped clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
