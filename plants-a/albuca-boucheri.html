Albuca boucheri

Albuca boucheri, commonly known as the Boucher's albuca, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.

Albuca boucheri is a member of the Asparagaceae family, which includes many other bulbous plants.
