Achyropsis alba

Achyropsis alba, commonly known as the white sand plant, is a succulent species native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, Achyropsis alba is of interest to botanists and succulent enthusiasts alike.
