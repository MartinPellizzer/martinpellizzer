Allium rudbaricum

Allium rudbaricum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild garlic-like scent.

Allium rudbaricum is relatively hardy and can thrive in well-drained soils in temperate climates.
