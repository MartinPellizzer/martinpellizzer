Amphibecis violacea

Amphibecis violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, Amphibecis violacea is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
