Acer pictum subsp. pubigerum

Acer pictum subsp.

pubigerum, commonly known as the hairy maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often with a slightly flaky texture.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny woodland habitats.
