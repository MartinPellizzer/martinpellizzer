Aegiphila obtusa

Aegiphila obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Aegiphila obtusa is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and mild aromatic properties.
