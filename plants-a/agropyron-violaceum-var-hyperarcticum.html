Agropyron violaceum var. hyperarcticum

Agropyron violaceum var. hyperarcticum, commonly known as the Arctic wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and silver-gray foliage, which helps it withstand harsh cold climates. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

This variety is adapted to extreme conditions, making it an important component of polar ecosystems.
