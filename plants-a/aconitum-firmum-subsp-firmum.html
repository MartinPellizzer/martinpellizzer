Aconitum firmum subsp. firmum

Aconitum firmum subsp.

firmum, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum.

The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves with finely toothed margins. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
