Astragalus monticola

Astragalus monticola, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as mountains and grasslands. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus monticola is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ecological benefits.
