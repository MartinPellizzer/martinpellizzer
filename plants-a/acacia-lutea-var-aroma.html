Acacia lutea var. aroma

Acacia lutea var. aroma, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its aromatic reputation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique scent, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
