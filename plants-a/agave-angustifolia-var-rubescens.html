Agave angustifolia var. rubescens

Agave angustifolia var. rubescens, commonly known as the red agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves that often have a reddish tinge, especially at the base. The plant produces striking, tall flower stalks that bear yellowish-green flowers, which are typically short-lived. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
