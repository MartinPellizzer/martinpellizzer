Anthurium psilostachyum

Anthurium psilostachyum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the modified leaves that house the small, true flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium psilostachyum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor settings.
