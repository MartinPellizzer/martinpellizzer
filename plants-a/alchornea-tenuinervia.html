Alchornea tenuinervia

Alchornea tenuinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined alchornea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and often alternate leaves. The plant typically has simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves with prominently visible veins. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Alchornea tenuinervia is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
