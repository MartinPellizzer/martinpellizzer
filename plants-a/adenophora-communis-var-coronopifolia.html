Adenophora communis var. coronopifolia

Adenophora communis var. coronopifolia, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive crown-like arrangement of leaflets. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often used in landscaping for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
