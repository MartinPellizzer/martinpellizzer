Aralia quinquefolia var. ginseng

Aralia quinquefolia var. ginseng, commonly known as American ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into five leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by dark blue berries. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine, valued for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rich forest soils.
