Armeria cephalotes var. alba

Armeria cephalotes var. alba, commonly known as the white sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in maritime climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
