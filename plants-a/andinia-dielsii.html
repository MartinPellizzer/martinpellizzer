Andinia dielsii

Andinia dielsii, commonly known as the Diels' orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in its often arid environment.

Andinia dielsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
