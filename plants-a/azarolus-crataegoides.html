Azarolus crataegoides

Azarolus crataegoides, commonly known as the African hawthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Azarolus crataegoides is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects.
