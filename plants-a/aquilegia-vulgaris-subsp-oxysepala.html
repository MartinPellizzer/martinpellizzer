Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. oxysepala

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

oxysepala, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, purple, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant features basal leaves with deeply lobed, fern-like foliage, and its stems are often hairy and upright. It prefers well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
