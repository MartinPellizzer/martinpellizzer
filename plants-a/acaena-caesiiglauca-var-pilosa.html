Acaena caesiiglauca var. pilosa

Acaena caesiiglauca var. pilosa, commonly known as the silky acaena, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with soft, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and wetlands.
