Acacia xiphoclada

Acacia xiphoclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Acacia xiphoclada is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of browse for livestock.
