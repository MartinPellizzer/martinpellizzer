Agave × glomeruliflora

Agave × glomeruliflora, commonly known as the blue agave, is a hybrid species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, succulent leaves that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of small, blue-green flowers, which are typically pollinated by bats and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of agave nectar.
