Anemone narcissiflora var. crinita

Anemone narcissiflora var. crinita, commonly known as the curly anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its delicate appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season add ornamental value to shaded landscapes.
