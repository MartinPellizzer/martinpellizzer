Ambrosia nivea

Ambrosia nivea, commonly known as the snow ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
