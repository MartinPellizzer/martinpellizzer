Acinos hungaricus f. coloratus

Acinos hungaricus f. coloratus, commonly known as the Hungarian mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and long-lasting flowers.
