Anthobolus erythrocaulis

Anthobolus erythrocaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed anthobolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Anthobolus erythrocaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
