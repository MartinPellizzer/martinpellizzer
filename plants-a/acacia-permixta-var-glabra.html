Acacia permixta var. glabra

Acacia permixta var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures in some varieties. The plant produces yellow to golden globular inflorescences, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia permixta var. glabra is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
