Abutilon mauritianum subsp. mauritianum

Abutilon mauritianum subsp.

mauritianum, commonly known as the Mauritius velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is typically shrub-like, with smooth, often hairy stems that can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
