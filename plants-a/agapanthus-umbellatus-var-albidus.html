Agapanthus umbellatus var. albidus

Agapanthus umbellatus var. albidus, commonly known as the white-flowered agapanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white, tubular flowers arranged in an umbel shape. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
