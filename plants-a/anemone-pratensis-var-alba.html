Anemone pratensis var. alba

Anemone pratensis var. alba, commonly known as the white meadow anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often appearing before the leaves. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens and naturalized areas.
