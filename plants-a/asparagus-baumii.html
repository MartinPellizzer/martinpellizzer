Asparagus baumii

Asparagus baumii, commonly known as the silver asparagus or Buddha's tears, is a perennial plant native to southern China.

It features delicate, fern-like foliage with a silvery-blue hue due to its dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, needle-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique appearance and ability to spread through rhizomes.
