Aster acuminatus f. subverticillatus

Aster acuminatus f. subverticillatus, commonly known as the subverticillate aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, arranged in a subverticillate pattern, giving the plant its specific epithet. This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
