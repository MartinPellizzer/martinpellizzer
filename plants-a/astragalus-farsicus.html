Astragalus farsicus

Astragalus farsicus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Astragalus farsicus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
