Acontias striatipes

Acontias striatipes, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the day and close at night. Its root system anchors it to the muddy bottom of freshwater habitats.

Acontias striatipes plays a role in water purification and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
