Amorphophallus incurvatus

Amorphophallus incurvatus, commonly known as the giant swamp arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive underground tuber that can grow quite substantial in size. The plant typically thrives in wet, swampy environments, where it forms dense stands. Its tuber is edible and has been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through vegetative propagation.
