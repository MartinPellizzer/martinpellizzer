Arenaria rubella f. epilis

Arenaria rubella f. epilis, commonly known as the woolly sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and sandy soils in Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant's leaves are typically covered in a fine, woolly layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh landscapes.
