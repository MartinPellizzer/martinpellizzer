Achimenes ingramii

Achimenes ingramii, commonly known as the blue bell vine, is a tropical perennial plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to deep blue. The plant produces showy, bell-like blooms that are typically 2-4 inches in length, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Achimenes ingramii is often cultivated for its vibrant floral displays and ability to climb or trail in gardens.

Morphology

Achimenes ingramii has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are alternate in arrangement and typically measure around 5-7 cm in length.

The stem is slender and slightly hairy, with a reddish-brown bark that becomes smoother as the plant matures. Its flowers are trumpet-shaped, with a vibrant pink to red color, blooming from late spring to early autumn, and are primarily pollinated by bees.
