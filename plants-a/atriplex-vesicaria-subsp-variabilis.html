Atriplex vesicaria subsp. variabilis

Atriplex vesicaria subsp.

variabilis, commonly known as the variable saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes.

Its ability to tolerate extreme conditions makes it an important component of stabilizing soil in harsh landscapes.
