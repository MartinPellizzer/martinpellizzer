Achyronia arachnoidea

Achyronia arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It features a unique inflorescence with flowers that resemble spiders, contributing to its common name. The plant has a short, erect stem with few leaves, and its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in pine flatwoods and wetlands.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
