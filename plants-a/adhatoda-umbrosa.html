Adhatoda umbrosa

Adhatoda umbrosa, commonly known as the dark adhatoda, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported respiratory and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
