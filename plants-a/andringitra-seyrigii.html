Andringitra seyrigii

Andringitra seyrigii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is an endemic species found exclusively in the Andringitra Massif of Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a unique morphology, featuring a pseudobulb and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in the dry season. The plant thrives in high-altitude, humid environments with well-drained soil, often growing in shaded forest understories. Its conservation status is vulnerable due to habitat loss and over-collection.

The species is of great interest to botanists and conservationists due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
