Aristolochia leonensis

Aristolochia leonensis, commonly known as the Mexican Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its distinctive, fragrant, purple to greenish-purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding flower structure that resembles a pipe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant contains various alkaloids and has been used in traditional medicine, though it is also toxic to humans and animals.
