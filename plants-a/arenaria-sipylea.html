Arenaria sipylea

Arenaria sipylea, commonly known as the Sicilian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically features dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to coastal and semi-arid regions.
