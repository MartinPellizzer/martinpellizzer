Arachnorchis verrucosa

Arachnorchis verrucosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved spider orchid, is an Australian native orchid with distinctive botanical features.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has a slender, wiry stem with a single or few flowers at the top. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pale green to yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.
