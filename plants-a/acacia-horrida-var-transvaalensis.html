Acacia horrida var. transvaalensis

Acacia horrida var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its greyish bark and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, fibrous pods. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
