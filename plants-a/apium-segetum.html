Apium segetum

Apium segetum, commonly known as garden parsley, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and dill. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often used as a culinary herb. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

The plant is valued both for its aromatic foliage and its edible roots, which have a mild, peppery flavor.
