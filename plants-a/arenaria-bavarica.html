Arenaria bavarica

Arenaria bavarica, commonly known as the Bavarian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as heaths and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Arenaria bavarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
