Asteromoea angustifolia

Asteromoea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asteromoea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
