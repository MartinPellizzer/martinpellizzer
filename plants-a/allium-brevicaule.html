Allium brevicaule

Allium brevicaule, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic or small-leaved onion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs and clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Allium brevicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
