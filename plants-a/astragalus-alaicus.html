Astragalus alaicus

Astragalus alaicus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky, or alpine environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several leaflets arranged along a central stem.

Astragalus alaicus is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
