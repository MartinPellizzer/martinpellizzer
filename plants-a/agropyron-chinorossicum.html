Agropyron chinorossicum

Agropyron chinorossicum, commonly known as Chinese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Agropyron chinorossicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
