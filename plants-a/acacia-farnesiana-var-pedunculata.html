Acacia farnesiana var. pedunculata

Acacia farnesiana var. pedunculata, commonly known as the sweet acacia or weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous covering. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
