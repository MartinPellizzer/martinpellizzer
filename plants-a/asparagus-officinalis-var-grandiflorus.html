Asparagus officinalis var. grandiflorus

Asparagus officinalis var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant white asparagus, is a cultivated variety of the asparagus plant characterized by its large, showy white flowers.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is typically grown for ornamental purposes rather than culinary use. The plant has feathery, fern-like foliage and produces edible spears in the spring, though these are usually less fibrous than those of the standard asparagus. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value.

The variety is prized for its unique floral display and its ability to add texture and color to garden landscapes.
