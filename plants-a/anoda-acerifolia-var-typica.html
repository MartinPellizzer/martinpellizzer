Anoda acerifolia var. typica

Anoda acerifolia var. typica, commonly known as the scarlet fanflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate, lobed leaves that resemble those of maple trees, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
