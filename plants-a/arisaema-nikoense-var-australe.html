Arisaema nikoense var. australe

Arisaema nikoense var. australe, commonly known as the Australian variety of the Japanese arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces a starchy, tuberous root that is used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous growth system and showy, colorful bracts that attract pollinators.
