Ambrosia ambrosioides subsp. septentrionale

Ambrosia ambrosioides subsp.

septentrionale, commonly known as northern ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is an annual herb characterized by its branched stems and alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its allergenic properties, it is considered a weed in many regions.
