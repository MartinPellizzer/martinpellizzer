Arenaria cypria

Arenaria cypria, commonly known as Cyprus sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Cyprus.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are generally succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Arenaria cypria is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas, demonstrating its resilience to harsh conditions.
