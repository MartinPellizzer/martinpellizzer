Allium pseudosenescens

Allium pseudosenescens, commonly known as the purple garlic or the rock garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, purple or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Allium pseudosenescens thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.
