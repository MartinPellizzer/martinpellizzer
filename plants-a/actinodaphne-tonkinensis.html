Actinodaphne tonkinensis

Actinodaphne tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by spherical, reddish-brown fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
