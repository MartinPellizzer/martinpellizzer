Aspalathus abietina

Aspalathus abietina, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in regions with similar climates.
