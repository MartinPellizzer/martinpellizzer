Avenastrum neumayerianum

Avenastrum neumayerianum, commonly known as Neumayer's wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Avenastrum neumayerianum is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates.
