Acalypha amblyodonta var. repanda

Acalypha amblyodonta var. repanda, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish veining and a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes make it an invasive species in many regions.
