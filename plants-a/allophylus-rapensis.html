Allophylus rapensis

Allophylus rapensis, commonly known as the white-flowered alderleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and glossy dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed enclosed in a hard outer shell.

Allophylus rapensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes.
