Achillea zederbaueri

Achillea zederbaueri, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its slender stems bear clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Achillea zederbaueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
