Adenostephanus organensis

Adenostephanus organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its succulent leaves and deep root system. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for herbivores.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
