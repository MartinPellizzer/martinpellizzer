Aster quinquenervius

Aster quinquenervius, commonly known as the five-veined aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing purple to blue blossoms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
