Aster hemisphericus

Aster hemisphericus, commonly known as the hemisphere aster, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster hemisphericus is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
