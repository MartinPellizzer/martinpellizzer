Astragalus argyrostachyus

Astragalus argyrostachyus, commonly known as silverstem milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and environmental purposes.
