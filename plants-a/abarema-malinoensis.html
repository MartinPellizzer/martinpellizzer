Abarema malinoensis

Abarema malinoensis, commonly known as the Malinoensis false acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in humid, lowland areas with well-drained soil. It has a woody stem and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and reforestation projects.
