Astragalus khojakensis

Astragalus khojakensis, commonly known as the Khojak Pass milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and cold environments, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus khojakensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
