Anthyllis montana var. jacquinii

Anthyllis montana var. jacquinii, commonly known as the mountain anthyllis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a low-growing habit with trifoliate leaves and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
