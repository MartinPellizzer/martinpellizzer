Astragalus flavus var. candicans

Astragalus flavus var. candicans, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
