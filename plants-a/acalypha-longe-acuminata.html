Acalypha longe-acuminata

Acalypha longe-acuminata, commonly known as the long-pointed spurflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in dense, terminal spikes, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
