Asphodeline dammeriana

Asphodeline dammeriana, commonly known as the desert daffodil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to harsh conditions. The plant features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers in spring. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
