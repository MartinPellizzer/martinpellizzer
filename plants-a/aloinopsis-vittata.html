Aloinopsis vittata

Aloinopsis vittata, commonly known as the spotted aloinopsis, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the growing season. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.
