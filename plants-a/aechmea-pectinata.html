Aechmea pectinata

Aechmea pectinata, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage, which forms a central cup that collects water and nutrients. The plant's leaves are stiff and covered in a fine, hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance. It produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that range from red to orange, often followed by the development of small, edible berries.

Aechmea pectinata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Morphology

Aechmea pectinata has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, stiff, and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

The plant has a short, unbranched stem that is often hidden beneath the foliage, with a smooth, pale green bark. It produces tall, spikelike inflorescences with showy, violet or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, relying on wind pollination.
