Astrolepis integerrima

Astrolepis integerrima, commonly known as the African rock fern, is a perennial fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, often forming low, mat-like structures. The plant features leathery, dark green leaves that are divided into narrow, finger-like segments. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Astrolepis integerrima is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
