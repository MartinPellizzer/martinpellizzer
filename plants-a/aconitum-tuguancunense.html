Aconitum tuguancunense

Aconitum tuguancunense, commonly known as the Tuguancun monkshood, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are tuberous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
