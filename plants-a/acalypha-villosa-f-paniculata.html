Acalypha villosa f. paniculata

Acalypha villosa f. paniculata, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, clustered inflorescences that resemble panicles. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered in fine, white pubescence.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and colorful blooms in garden settings.
