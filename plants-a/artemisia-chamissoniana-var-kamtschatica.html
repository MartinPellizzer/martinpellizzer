Artemisia chamissoniana var. kamtschatica

Artemisia chamissoniana var. kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatka wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of the Pacific Rim.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage with finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and coastal environments.

This variant is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
