Allium anisopodium var. anisopodium

Allium anisopodium var. anisopodium, commonly known as the broad-leaved garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.
