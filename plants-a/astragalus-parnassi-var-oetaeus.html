Astragalus parnassi var. oetaeus

Astragalus parnassi var. oetaeus, commonly known as the Parnassian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.

Its conservation status is of concern due to habitat loss and over-collection.
