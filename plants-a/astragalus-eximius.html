Astragalus eximius

Astragalus eximius, commonly known as the exclamation plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Astragalus eximius is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
