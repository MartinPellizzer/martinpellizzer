Acalypha piperoides

Acalypha piperoides, commonly known as the pepperleaf or piperweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by three-lobed seed capsules. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent aroma.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical climates.
