Aquilegia alpina subsp. pyrenaica

Aquilegia alpina subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five sepals that are often reflexed.

The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed, and its stems are hairy and often branched. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
