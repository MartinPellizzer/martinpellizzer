Acacia cavaleriei

Acacia cavaleriei, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in compact, globular clusters. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its thick, waxy leaves. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.

The wood of Acacia cavaleriei is hard and durable, making it valuable for traditional uses.
