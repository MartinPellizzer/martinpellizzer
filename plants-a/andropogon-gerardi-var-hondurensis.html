Andropogon gerardi var. hondurensis

Andropogon gerardi var. hondurensis, commonly known as Honduras bluestem, is a variant of the tallgrass species native to Central America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
