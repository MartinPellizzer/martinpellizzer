Aeluropus concinnus

Aeluropus concinnus, commonly known as slender sandgrass, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses. Aeluropus concinnus is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it access water in sandy soils.
