Allium howellii var. clokeyi

Allium howellii var. clokeyi, commonly known as Howell's garlic.

This perennial bulbous plant is native to the western United States, particularly in California and Nevada. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
