Asclepias stenophylla

Asclepias stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Asclepias stenophylla is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its role as a host plant for monarch butterfly larvae.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
