Anguloa × ruckeri var. albiflora

Anguloa × ruckeri var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered anguloa, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a prominent, often yellow or green throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its striking floral display.
