Ammannia schinzii subsp. schinzii

Ammannia schinzii subsp.

schinzii, commonly known as Schinz's ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant features striking red to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a wetland plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
