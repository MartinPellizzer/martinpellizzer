Aslia pinnatifida

Aslia pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aslia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes caraway and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, pinnately arranged leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Aslia pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
