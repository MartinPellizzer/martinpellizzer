Ascolepis protea subsp. bellidiflora

Ascolepis protea subsp.

bellidiflora, commonly known as the bellflower protea, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in the Western and Northern Cape provinces. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces a showy display of flowers during the spring and early summer.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
