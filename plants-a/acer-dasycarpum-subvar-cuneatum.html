Acer dasycarpum subvar. cuneatum

Acer dasycarpum subvar. cuneatum, commonly known as the Mongolian maple, is a deciduous tree native to northern China and Mongolia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the maple genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in cooler regions.
