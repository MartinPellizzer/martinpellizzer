Aechmea lingulatoides

Aechmea lingulatoides, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that form a rosette, which collect rainwater to support its growth. The plant features a central stem that produces striking, spadix-like flowers, often in shades of red or orange. Its foliage is typically covered in a silvery or white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aechmea lingulatoides thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
