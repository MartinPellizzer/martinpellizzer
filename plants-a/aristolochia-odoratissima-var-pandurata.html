Aristolochia odoratissima var. pandurata

Aristolochia odoratissima var. pandurata, commonly known as the sweet-scented Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white with a faintly fragrant aroma. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses.
