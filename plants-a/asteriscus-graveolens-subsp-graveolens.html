Asteriscus graveolens subsp. graveolens

Asteriscus graveolens subsp.

graveolens, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has aromatic leaves and stems, which emit a pleasant scent when crushed. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
