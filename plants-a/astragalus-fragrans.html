Astragalus fragrans

Astragalus fragrans, commonly known as the sweet vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with trifoliate leaves and pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
