Artocarpus rufa

Artocarpus rufa, commonly known as the red-fig or red-fruit breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a unique fruit with a rough, reddish outer skin. The fruit contains a soft, starchy pulp that is edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
