Aristolochia benadiriana var. longilabia

Aristolochia benadiriana var. longilabia, commonly known as the East African birthwort, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and its striking, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe or a pipestem. The plant is native to eastern Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive, wrinkled interior.

The plant contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic compounds that have been studied for their medicinal and harmful properties.
