Asphodelus albus var. validus

Asphodelus albus var. validus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like leaves that are linear and slightly hairy, with a basal rosette structure. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of white, spike-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
