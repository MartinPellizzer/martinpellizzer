Amaryllis × parkeri

Amaryllis × parkeri, commonly known as the pink amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papilionacea.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its large, showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features strap-like, broad leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, long-lasting blooms.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a horticultural hybrid that showcases the genetic diversity within the Amaryllis genus.
