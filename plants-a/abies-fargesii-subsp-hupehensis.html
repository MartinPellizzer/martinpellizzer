Abies fargesii subsp. hupehensis

Abies fargesii subsp.

hupehensis, commonly known as the Hupeh fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to central and western China. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches.

The cones are typically pendulous, with elongated scales that release seeds in the autumn. This species thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of forest ecosystems in its native habitat.
