Aster chilensis subsp. typicus

Aster chilensis subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Chilean aster, is a perennial herb native to southern South America. It typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant features a robust root system and hairy stems, with lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
