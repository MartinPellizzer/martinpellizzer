Azalea speciosa var. aurantiaca

Azalea speciosa var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features glossy, dark green foliage. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
