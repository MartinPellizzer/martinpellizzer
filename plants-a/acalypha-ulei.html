Acalypha ulei

Acalypha ulei, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian nettle, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to the plant's foliage. Acalypha ulei is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
