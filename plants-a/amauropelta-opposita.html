It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate fronds with leaves arranged in an opposite pattern along the rachis. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are protected by a membranous covering called a indusium.

Amauropelta opposita is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
