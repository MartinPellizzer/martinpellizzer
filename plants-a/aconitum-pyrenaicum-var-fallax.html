Aconitum pyrenaicum var. fallax

Aconitum pyrenaicum var. fallax, commonly known as the Pyrenean aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spathaceous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Aconitum pyrenaicum var. fallax contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring caution during handling.
