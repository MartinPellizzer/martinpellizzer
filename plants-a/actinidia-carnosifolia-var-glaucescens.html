Actinidia carnosifolia var. glaucescens

Actinidia carnosifolia var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, bluish-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, fuzzy berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in horticulture.
