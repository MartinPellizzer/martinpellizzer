Aristolochia argyroneura

Aristolochia argyroneura, commonly known as the silver-veined Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive silver veins and its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its fragrant, tubular flowers that attract specific pollinators. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
