Aconitum senanense var. japononapellus

Aconitum senanense var. japononapellus, commonly known as Japanese aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat fern-like appearance.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
