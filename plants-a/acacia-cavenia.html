Acacia cavenia

Acacia cavenia, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers that appear in late summer. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Acacia cavenia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Its flowers are an important food source for native pollinators and birds.
