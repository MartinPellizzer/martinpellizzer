Astragalus tianschanicus var. pamiricus

Astragalus tianschanicus var. pamiricus, commonly known as the Pamir milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. This variant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in rocky and arid conditions. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
