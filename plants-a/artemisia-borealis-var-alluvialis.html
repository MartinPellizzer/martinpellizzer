Artemisia borealis var. alluvialis

Artemisia borealis var. alluvialis, commonly known as the whiteleaf tamarack, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
