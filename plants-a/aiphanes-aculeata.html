Aiphanes aculeata

Aiphanes aculeata, commonly known as the spiny papyrus sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, triangular stems and dense, spiny leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Aiphanes aculeata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
