Aeschynanthus micranthus

Aeschynanthus micranthus, commonly known as the mini whale's tail, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aeschynanthus micranthus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
