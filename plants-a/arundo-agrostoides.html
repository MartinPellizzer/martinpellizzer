Arundo agrostoides

Arundo agrostoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to wet soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often narrow and slightly hairy, with a height that can reach up to 2 meters. Its flowering spikes are usually arranged in clusters, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Arundo agrostoides is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
