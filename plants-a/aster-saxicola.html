Aster saxicola

Aster saxicola, commonly known as the rock aster, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments in North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

Aster saxicola is often found in desert scrub and gravelly plains, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
