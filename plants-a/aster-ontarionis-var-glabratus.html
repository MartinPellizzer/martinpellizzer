Aster ontarionis var. glabratus

Aster ontarionis var. glabratus, commonly known as the Smoothleaf Aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are usually smooth and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy underside.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
