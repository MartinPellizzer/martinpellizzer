Ambrosia scabra var. robusta

Ambrosia scabra var. robusta, commonly known as rough ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its rough, bristly stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it easily identifiable in the wild.
