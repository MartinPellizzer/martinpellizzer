Antiphylla oppositifolia

Antiphylla oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositeleaf antiphylla, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
