Astydamia latifolia var. canariensis

Astydamia latifolia var. canariensis, commonly known as Canary Island flatweed, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a flat head. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
