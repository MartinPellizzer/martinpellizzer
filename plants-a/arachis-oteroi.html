Arachis oteroi

Arachis oteroi, commonly known as the Oteroi groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing one or two round, edible seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its potential nutritional value, it is of interest for agricultural research and sustainable food systems.
