Acacia pinetorum

Acacia pinetorum, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Acacia pinetorum thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, high-altitude environments.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
