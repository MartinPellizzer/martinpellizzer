Abronia umbellata subsp. acutalata

Abronia umbellata subsp.

acutalata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery or grayish underside that helps reflect sunlight. This subspecies thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or dry, open habitats.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing coastal dunes.
