Argentina anserina subsp. sericea

Argentina anserina subsp.

sericea, commonly known as the silky arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters, and it is known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
