Asarum tokarense

Asarum tokarense, commonly known as Tokara wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, snake-like flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

Asarum tokarense plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
