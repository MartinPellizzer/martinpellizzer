Acacia clivicola

Acacia clivicola, commonly known as the rock acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in rocky soils.

Acacia clivicola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
