Anthemis × intermedia

Anthemis × intermedia, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of both Anthemis nobilis and Anthemis cotula.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with finely divided, feathery leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in gardens and wildflower meadows.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers is used in aromatherapy and as a mild sedative.
