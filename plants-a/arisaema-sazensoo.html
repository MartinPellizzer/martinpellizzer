Arisaema sazensoo

Arisaema sazensoo, commonly known as the Japanese arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a distinctive umbrella-like inflorescence composed of a single spadix enclosed by a leaf-like bract. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Arisaema sazensoo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
