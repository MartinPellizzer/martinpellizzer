Alstroemeria kunziana

Alstroemeria kunziana, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with strap-like leaves that are often dark green.

Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are known for their long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
