Amaranthus melancholicus var. parvifolius

Amaranthus melancholicus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the weedy amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are clustered in dense panicles. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is known for its adaptability and is considered a weed in many regions.
