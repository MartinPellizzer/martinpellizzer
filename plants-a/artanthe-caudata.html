Artanthe caudata

Artanthe caudata, commonly known as the woolly artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Artanthe caudata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
