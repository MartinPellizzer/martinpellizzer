Arum alternum

Arum alternum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white with a prominent spadix. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
