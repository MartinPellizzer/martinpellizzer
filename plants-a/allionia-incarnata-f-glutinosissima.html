Allionia incarnata f. glutinosissima

Allionia incarnata f. glutinosissima, commonly known as the sticky false heather, is a botanical variant of the species Allionia incarnata.

It is characterized by its distinctive sticky glands on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments. The flowers are small, pink to reddish in color, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
