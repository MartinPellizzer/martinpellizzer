Arabis hirsuta subsp. tunetana

Arabis hirsuta subsp.

tunetana, commonly known as the Algerian rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous areas.

The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers.

This subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
