Aloysia leptophylla

Aloysia leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Aloysia leptophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
