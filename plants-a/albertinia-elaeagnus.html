Albertinia elaeagnus

Albertinia elaeagnus, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, oval fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
