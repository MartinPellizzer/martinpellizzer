Adiantum soboliferum

Adiantum soboliferum, commonly known as the creeping maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lacy fronds that are typically light green and have a soft, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense mats over time.

Adiantum soboliferum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in horticultural settings where a lush, green ground cover is desired.
