Astragalus glaux var. glauciformis

Astragalus glaux var. glauciformis, commonly known as the blueleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its bluish-green foliage, which gives it its common name.
