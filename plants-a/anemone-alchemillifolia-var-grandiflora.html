Anemone alchemillifolia var. grandiflora

Anemone alchemillifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant alpine anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features a basal rosette of large, waxy, dark green leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with numerous petals arranged in a cup-like structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
