Arthrostylidium capillifolium

Arthrostylidium capillifolium, commonly known as the slender-leafed mistplant, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences, and it relies on insect pollination for reproduction.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
