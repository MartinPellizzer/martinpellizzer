Agapetes moorei

Agapetes moorei, commonly known as the Moore's silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Agapetes moorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
