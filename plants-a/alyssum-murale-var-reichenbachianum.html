Alyssum murale var. reichenbachianum

Alyssum murale var. reichenbachianum, commonly known as the rock Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage with finely textured leaves, and its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.

This variety is noted for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and drought, making it a resilient choice for landscaping.
