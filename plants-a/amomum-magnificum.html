Amomum magnificum

Amomum magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and fragrant, three-lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green flowers that eventually develop into aromatic, triangular seed pods. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their strong, camphor-like scent.

Amomum magnificum is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in both culinary and medicinal practices.
