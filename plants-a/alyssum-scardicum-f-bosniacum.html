Alyssum scardicum f. bosniacum

Alyssum scardicum f. bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian alyssum, is a subspecies native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
