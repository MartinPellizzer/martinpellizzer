Aristolochia macradenia

Aristolochia macradenia, commonly known as the large-flowered Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, twisted leaves that are often heart-shaped. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by specific insects, and the plant contains various bioactive compounds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions, though caution is advised due to its potential toxicity.
