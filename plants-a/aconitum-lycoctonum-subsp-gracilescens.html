Aconitum lycoctonum subsp. gracilescens

Aconitum lycoctonum subsp.

gracilescens, commonly known as the slender aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically arranged in dense, spire-like clusters.

The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
