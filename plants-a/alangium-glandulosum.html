Alangium glandulosum

Alangium glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular alangium, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with glandular structures on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Alangium glandulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
