Aira flexuosa var. aprica

Aira flexuosa var. aprica, commonly known as the spring false brome, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with green or reddish hues. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often found in wetland ecosystems and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
