Adiantum edentulum f. muticum

Adiantum edentulum f. muticum, commonly known as the creeping maidenhair fern, is a unique variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that form a dense, carpet-like ground cover. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas with rich, humus-filled soil. Its fronds are typically shorter and more compact compared to the standard species, adapting well to low-light conditions.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings where humidity and shade are present.
