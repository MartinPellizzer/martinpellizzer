Aloe jeppeae

Aloe jeppeae, commonly known as the Jeppe's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe jeppeae is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
