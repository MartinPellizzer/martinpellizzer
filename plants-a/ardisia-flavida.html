Ardisia flavida

Ardisia flavida, commonly known as the golden flame ardisia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Ardisia flavida thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

It has a relatively compact growth habit, making it suitable for container gardening and landscape design.
