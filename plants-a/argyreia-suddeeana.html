Argyreia suddeeana

Argyreia suddeeana, commonly known as the red veined morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with striking red veins against a deep green background. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
