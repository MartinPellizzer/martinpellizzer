Avena sterilis f. macranthera

Avena sterilis f. macranthera, commonly known as the giant oat, is a sterile variant of the common oat species.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy inflorescences that resemble a plume. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are typically white or pale blue.

This form is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils.
