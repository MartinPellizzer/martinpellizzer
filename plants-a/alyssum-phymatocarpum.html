Alyssum phymatocarpum

Alyssum phymatocarpum, commonly known as desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent leaves and stems, adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Alyssum phymatocarpum is often found in sandy soils and is considered a characteristic species of desert scrub habitats.
