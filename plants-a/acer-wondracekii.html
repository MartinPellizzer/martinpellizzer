Acer wondracekii

Acer wondracekii, commonly known as the Wondracek maple, is a rare and ornamental species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage and distinctive, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while its fruit is a samara, typical of the maple genus.

Due to its limited distribution and unique aesthetic, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
