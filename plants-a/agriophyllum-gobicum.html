Agriophyllum gobicum

Agriophyllum gobicum, commonly known as the Gobi bladdergrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, contributing to its role in local ecosystems. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor in sandy or rocky soils.

Agriophyllum gobicum is often found in steppes and deserts, where it plays a part in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals.
