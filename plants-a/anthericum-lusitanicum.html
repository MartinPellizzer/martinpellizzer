Anthericum lusitanicum

Anthericum lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Anthericum lusitanicum is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
