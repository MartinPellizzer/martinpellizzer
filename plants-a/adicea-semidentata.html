Adicea semidentata

Adicea semidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, with narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Adicea semidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
