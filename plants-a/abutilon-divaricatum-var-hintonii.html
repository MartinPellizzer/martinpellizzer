Abutilon divaricatum var. hintonii

Abutilon divaricatum var. hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's Abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring soft, hairy stems and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various climates and its ability to attract pollinators.
