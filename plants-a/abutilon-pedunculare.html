Abutilon pedunculare

Abutilon pedunculare, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in coastal regions. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsules as its fruit.

Abutilon pedunculare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
