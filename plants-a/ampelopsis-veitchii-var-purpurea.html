Ampelopsis veitchii var. purpurea

Ampelopsis veitchii var. purpurea, commonly known as purple anglevine, is a climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and distinctive purple-tinged leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking visual interest.
