Alpinia congesta

Alpinia congesta, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or mountain ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Indonesia. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers.

Alpinia congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
