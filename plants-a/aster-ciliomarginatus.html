Aster ciliomarginatus

Aster ciliomarginatus, commonly known as the silky aster, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its finely haired, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It blooms from late summer into early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
