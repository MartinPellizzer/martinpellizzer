Allantodia subdilatata

Allantodia subdilatata, commonly known as the broad-leaved allantodia, is a perennial herb native to parts of southern Africa.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a slightly wavy margin and a hairy underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
