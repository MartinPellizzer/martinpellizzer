Allium macrochaetum subsp. tuncelianum

Allium macrochaetum subsp.

tuncelianum, commonly known as the Tuncel's leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces umbel-like clusters of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a potential candidate for culinary use.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
