Agathosma muirii

Agathosma muirii, commonly known as Muir's buchu, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing minute seeds.

Due to its unique fragrance and medicinal properties, it is valued both locally and internationally.
