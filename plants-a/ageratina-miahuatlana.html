Ageratina miahuatlana

Ageratina miahuatlana, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Ageratina miahuatlana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
