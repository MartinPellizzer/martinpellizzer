Argentipallium × spiceri

Argentipallium × spiceri, commonly known as the silver-white bellflower, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white petals and pale green foliage, which reflect its adaptation to high-altitude environments. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
