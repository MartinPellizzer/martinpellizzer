Abutilon bridgesii

Abutilon bridgesii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
