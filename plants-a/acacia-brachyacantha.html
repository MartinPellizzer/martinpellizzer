Acacia brachyacantha

Acacia brachyacantha, commonly known as the narrow-thorned acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry conditions.

Acacia brachyacantha is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.
