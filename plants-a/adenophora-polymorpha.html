Adenophora polymorpha

Adenophora polymorpha, commonly known as the variable bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora polymorpha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
