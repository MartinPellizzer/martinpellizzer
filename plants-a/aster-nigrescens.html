Aster nigrescens

Aster nigrescens, commonly known as the black-leaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dark green, often velvety leaves and showy purple to blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
