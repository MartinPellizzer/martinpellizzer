Amaryllis × colvillii

Amaryllis × colvillii, commonly known as the Colvill's amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belloni and Amaryllis belladonna.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and sturdy. It is typically grown as a bulbous plant, with the bulbs requiring a period of dormancy followed by a warm, moist growing season.

Amaryllis × colvillii is popular in gardens and as a potted plant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
