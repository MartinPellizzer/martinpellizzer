Ardisia aguirreana

Ardisia aguirreana, commonly known as the Guatemalan beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are a striking feature of the species. These berries are edible for humans but are toxic to livestock.

Ardisia aguirreana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
