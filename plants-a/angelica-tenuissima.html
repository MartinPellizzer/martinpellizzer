Angelica tenuissima

Angelica tenuissima, commonly known as slender angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small white to greenish flowers cluster in umbrella-shaped inflorescences, which bloom in late summer.

The roots of Angelica tenuissima are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, though the plant is less commonly cultivated than its more famous relatives.
