Abarema langsdorffii

Abarema langsdorffii, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble peacock feathers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique and showy appearance.
