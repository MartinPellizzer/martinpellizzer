Astragalus kikodzeanus

Astragalus kikodzeanus, commonly known as the Kikodzean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus kikodzeanus is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
