Atractylis ovata f. lyratifolia

Atractylis ovata f. lyratifolia, commonly known as the lyrated cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are broadly ovate and often have a distinctive lyrate shape, giving it its specific epithet.

It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
