Achillea barrelieri f. schouwii

Achillea barrelieri f. schouwii, commonly known as the Barrelier's yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant ground cover. The specific epithet 'f.

schouwii' denotes a horticultural form or cultivar. distinguishing it from the typical species.
