Amaranthus graecizans

Amaranthus graecizans, commonly known as Greek amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with dense, fluffy, pink to red bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus graecizans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
