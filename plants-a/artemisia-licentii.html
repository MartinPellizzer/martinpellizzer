Artemisia licentii

Artemisia licentii, commonly known as the licorice wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves contain volatile oils and have been used historically in traditional medicine.

Artemisia licentii is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
