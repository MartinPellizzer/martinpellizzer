Achillea millefolium var. maritima

Achillea millefolium var. maritima, commonly known as sea yarrow, is a coastal plant native to the shores of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and flat, clustered flower heads that range from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. It is drought-tolerant and has a compact growth habit, making it well-suited to coastal climates.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their anti-inflammatory and astringent effects.
