Artemisia littoricola

Artemisia littoricola, commonly known as sea wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its strong, pungent scent.

Artemisia littoricola plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
