Acalypha pygmaea

Acalypha pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf copperleaf, is a small shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant, copper-colored leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Acalypha pygmaea is valued for its ornamental foliage and is popular in horticulture for its unique appearance.
