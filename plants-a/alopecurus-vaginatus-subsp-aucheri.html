Alopecurus vaginatus subsp. aucheri

Alopecurus vaginatus subsp.

aucheri, commonly known as the Austrian grass or aucher's meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
