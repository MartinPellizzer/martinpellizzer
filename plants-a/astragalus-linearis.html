Astragalus linearis

Astragalus linearis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus linearis is often found in the western United States and is adapted to arid environments.
