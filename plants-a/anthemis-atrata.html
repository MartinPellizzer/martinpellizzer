Anthemis atrata

Anthemis atrata, commonly known as the black anthemis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, dark purple to blackish flowers, which are often clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, and the stems are usually hairy as well.

The species is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
