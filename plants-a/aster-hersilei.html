Aster hersilei

Aster hersilei, commonly known as the Sierra aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as mountain slopes and open woodlands. Its pale blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster hersilei is valued for its ecological role and is often found in conservation efforts due to its limited distribution.
