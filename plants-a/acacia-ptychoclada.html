Acacia ptychoclada

Acacia ptychoclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, phyllode-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia ptychoclada is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
