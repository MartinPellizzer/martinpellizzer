Abbevillea klotzschiana var. longipes

Abbevillea klotzschiana var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed abbevillea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems. This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
