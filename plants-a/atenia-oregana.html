Atenia oregana

Atenia oregana, commonly known as Oregon grape, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces bright blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Atenia oregana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
