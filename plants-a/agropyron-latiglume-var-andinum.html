Agropyron latiglume var. andinum

Agropyron latiglume var. andinum, commonly known as the Andean wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine regions.

This variety is valued for its resilience and has been studied for potential use in ecological restoration and forage production.
