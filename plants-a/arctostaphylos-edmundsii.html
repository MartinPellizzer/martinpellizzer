Arctostaphylos edmundsii

Arctostaphylos edmundsii, commonly known as the Edmonds manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
