Athyrium pedicellatum

Athyrium pedicellatum, commonly known as the hairy male fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Athyrium pedicellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
