Albizia orissensis

Albizia orissensis, commonly known as the Indian siris, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Albizia orissensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
