Andropogon brunneus

Andropogon brunneus, commonly known as yellow bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces inflorescences of spikelets that are often tinged with yellow or brown. It is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it a resilient component of native plant communities.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
