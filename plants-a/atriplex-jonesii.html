Atriplex jonesii

Atriplex jonesii, commonly known as Jones' saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Atriplex jonesii is often found in saline soils and plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
