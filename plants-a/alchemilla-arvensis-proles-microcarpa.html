Alchemilla arvensis proles microcarpa

Alchemilla arvensis proles microcarpa, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are shield-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense, branched clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers have been historically used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
