Acacia brevispica subsp. dregeana

Acacia brevispica subsp.

dregeana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth form compared to other acacia species.
