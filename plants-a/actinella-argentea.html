Actinella argentea

Actinella argentea, commonly known as the silver actinella, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as along stream banks and in forest understories.

Actinella argentea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
