Acacia suaveolens subsp. montana

Acacia suaveolens subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
