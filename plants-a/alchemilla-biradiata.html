Alchemilla biradiata

Alchemilla biradiata, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Alchemilla biradiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
