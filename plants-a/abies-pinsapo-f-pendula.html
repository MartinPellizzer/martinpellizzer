Abies pinsapo f. pendula

Abies pinsapo f. pendula, commonly known as the hanging cedar of Spain, is a rare and distinctive form of the Spanish cedar.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that drape downwards, creating a unique and elegant appearance. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Spain, particularly in the Sierra de Cazorla. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly glossy, contributing to its ornamental value.
