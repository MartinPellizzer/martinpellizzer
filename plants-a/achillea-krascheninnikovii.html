Achillea krascheninnikovii

Achillea krascheninnikovii, commonly known as Krascheninnikov's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a compact growth habit and hairy leaves to reduce water loss. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its colonization of high-altitude habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
