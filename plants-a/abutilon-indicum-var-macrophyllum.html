Abutilon indicum var. macrophyllum

Abutilon indicum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the Indian velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
