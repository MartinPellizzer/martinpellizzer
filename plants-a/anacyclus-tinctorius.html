Anacyclus tinctorius

Anacyclus tinctorius, commonly known as the blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its height ranges from 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and narrow leaves.

Anacyclus tinctorius is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
