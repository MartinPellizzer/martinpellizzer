Angelonia serrata

Angelonia serrata, commonly known as the summer snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the West Indies and southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink and purple. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Angelonia serrata is known for its long blooming season, often extending from summer through fall.
