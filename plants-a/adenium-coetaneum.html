Adenium coetaneum

Adenium coetaneum, commonly known as the desert candle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy trunk and smooth, often reddish bark. The plant has striking, lobed leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its trunk make it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Adenium coetaneum is also known for its toxic sap, which contains various alkaloids.
