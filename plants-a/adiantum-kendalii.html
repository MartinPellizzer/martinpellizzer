Adiantum kendalii

Adiantum kendalii, commonly known as the Kendall's maidenhair, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum kendalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
