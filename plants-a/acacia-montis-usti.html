Acacia montis-usti

Acacia montis-usti, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often modified into spines, a common adaptation to reduce water loss in dry environments. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Acacia montis-usti plays an important ecological role by providing shelter and food for various desert wildlife species.
