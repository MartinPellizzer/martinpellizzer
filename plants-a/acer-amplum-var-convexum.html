Acer amplum var. convexum

Acer amplum var. convexum, commonly known as the convex-leaf maple, is a variant of the European maple characterized by its distinctive, thickened, and often warty leaf margins.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, with a rough, textured surface that distinguishes it from other maple species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
