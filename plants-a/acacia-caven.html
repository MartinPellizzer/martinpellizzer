Acacia caven

Acacia caven, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow or golden flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia caven is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry landscapes.
