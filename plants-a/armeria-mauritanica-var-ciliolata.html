Armeria mauritanica var. ciliolata

Armeria mauritanica var. ciliolata, commonly known as the fringed thrift, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, evergreen foliage with a dense, mat-forming habit, and its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in spring. The leaves are covered in fine, hairy cilia, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
