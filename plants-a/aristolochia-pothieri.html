Aristolochia pothieri

Aristolochia pothieri, commonly known as the Pothier's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
