Acer calcaratum

Acer calcaratum, commonly known as the rock maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped bark and its palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in dry, exposed areas.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for small gardens and landscapes.
