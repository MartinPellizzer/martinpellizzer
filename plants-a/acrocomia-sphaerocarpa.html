Acrocomia sphaerocarpa

Acrocomia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globular palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spherical fruit that resembles a small coconut. The plant grows as a single-stemmed palm with pinnate leaves and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its fruit contains a hard, fibrous outer layer and a starchy, edible kernel inside.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some regions.
