Achillea subhirsuta

Achillea subhirsuta, commonly known as the woolly yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Achillea subhirsuta is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
