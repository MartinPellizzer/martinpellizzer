Aira cespitosa var. lutescens

Aira cespitosa var. lutescens, commonly known as yellow tuftgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and features narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
