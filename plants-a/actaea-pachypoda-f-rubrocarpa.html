Actaea pachypoda f. rubrocarpa

Actaea pachypoda f. rubrocarpa, commonly known as the red-stemmed black cohosh, is a variant of the black cohosh plant characterized by its distinctive red stems.

It is a perennial herb native to North America, typically found in moist, wooded areas. The plant features large, compound leaves with a deep green hue and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in late spring. Its berries are red and contain seeds, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
