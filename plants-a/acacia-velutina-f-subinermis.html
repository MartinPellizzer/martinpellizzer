Acacia velutina f. subinermis

Acacia velutina f. subinermis, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow to golden spherical flower heads, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
