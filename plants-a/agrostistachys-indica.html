Agrostistachys indica

Agrostistachys indica, commonly known as the Indian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Agrostistachys indica is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
