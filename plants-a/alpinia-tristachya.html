Alpinia tristachya

Alpinia tristachya, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and possesses aromatic, thick, and fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Alpinia tristachya is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
