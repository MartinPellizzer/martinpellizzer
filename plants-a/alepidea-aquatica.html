Alepidea aquatica

Alepidea aquatica, commonly known as the water reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands along riverbanks and lakeshores. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white. Alepidea aquatica plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.
