Allium burjaticum

Allium burjaticum, commonly known as the Burjat garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other allium species, with unique floral and vegetative characteristics.
