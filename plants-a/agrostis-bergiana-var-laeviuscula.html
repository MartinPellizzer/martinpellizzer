Agrostis bergiana var. laeviuscula

Agrostis bergiana var. laeviuscula, commonly known as the smooth meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, rocky areas, and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
