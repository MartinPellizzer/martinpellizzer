Achyronia subtingens

Achyronia subtingens, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its adaptability and unique appearance, it is a common species in xerophytic plant communities.
