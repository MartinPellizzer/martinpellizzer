Astragalus ancistrocarpus var. nitidulus

Astragalus ancistrocarpus var. nitidulus, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The variety nitidulus is distinguished by its smooth, shiny leaves and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
