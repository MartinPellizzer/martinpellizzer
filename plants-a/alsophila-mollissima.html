Alsophila mollissima

Alsophila mollissima, commonly known as the silky tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The fronds are typically light green and have a soft, silky texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various small animals and insects.
