Acalypha affinis

Acalypha affinis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

Acalypha affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
