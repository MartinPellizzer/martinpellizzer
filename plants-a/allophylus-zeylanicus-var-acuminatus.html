Allophylus zeylanicus var. acuminatus

Allophylus zeylanicus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Indian almond tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, acuminate (tapering to a point) leaves that are often glabrous and dark green on top. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by small, fleshy, orange-red fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
