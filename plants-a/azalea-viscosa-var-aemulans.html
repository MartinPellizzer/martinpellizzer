Azalea viscosa var. aemulans

Azalea viscosa var. aemulans, commonly known as the rough azalea or swamp azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include hairy stems and small, leathery leaves that provide a textured appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
