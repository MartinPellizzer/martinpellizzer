Achillea cucullata

Achillea cucullata, commonly known as the Hooded yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has silvery-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Achillea cucullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
