Acalypha macularis

Acalypha macularis, commonly known as the spotted spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often display shades of red, orange, or yellow, which are not petals but modified leaves. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and often features a distinctive red or purple spot on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Acalypha macularis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
