Acer palmatum var. pubescens

Acer palmatum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on the leaves.

The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are typically palmate, with five to seven lobes, and they display a range of colors from bright green to red and orange during the growing season. The hairy maple prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.

It is also known for its slow growth and compact form, which makes it ideal for smaller garden spaces.
