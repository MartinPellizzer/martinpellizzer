Agropyron panormitanum subvar. villosulum

Agropyron panormitanum subvar. villosulum, commonly known as the woolly wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
