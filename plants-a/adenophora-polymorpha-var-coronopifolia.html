Adenophora polymorpha var. coronopifolia

Adenophora polymorpha var. coronopifolia, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist climates. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
