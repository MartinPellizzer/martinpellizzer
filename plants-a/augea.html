Augea

Augea, commonly known as the "tree of life," is a unique plant species native to certain regions of the world.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pale yellow. Its roots are known for their ability to absorb and store significant amounts of water, making it drought-resistant.
