Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. rubra

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red bugloss, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, narrow leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in open, sunny habitats.
