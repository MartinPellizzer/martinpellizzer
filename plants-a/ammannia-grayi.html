Ammannia grayi

Ammannia grayi, commonly known as the graystem ammannia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often tinged with a distinctive grayish hue, giving it its common name. Its small, white or pinkish flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, and the leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped.

Ammannia grayi plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
