Astragalus aspadanus

Astragalus aspadanus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to dry conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus aspadanus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
