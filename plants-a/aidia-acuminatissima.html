Aidia acuminatissima

Aidia acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aidia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
