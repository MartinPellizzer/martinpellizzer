Appunia peduncularis

Appunia peduncularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features succulent, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow, fleshy, and arranged alternately along the stems, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Appunia peduncularis is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in areas with minimal rainfall.
