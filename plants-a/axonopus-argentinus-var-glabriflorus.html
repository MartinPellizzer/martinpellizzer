Axonopus argentinus var. glabriflorus

Axonopus argentinus var. glabriflorus, commonly known as the glabrous-flowered axonopus, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
