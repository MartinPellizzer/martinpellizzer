Agalma octophyllum

Agalma octophyllum, commonly known as the eight-leaved beauty, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its striking botanical features.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is notable for its eight distinct leaves, each with a waxy, glossy surface and a deep green color. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a faint, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, Agalma octophyllum is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
