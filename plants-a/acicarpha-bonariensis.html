Acicarpha bonariensis

Acicarpha bonariensis, commonly known as the Buenos Aires grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acicarpha bonariensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
