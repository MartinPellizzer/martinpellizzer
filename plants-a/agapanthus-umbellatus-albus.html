Agapanthus umbellatus albus

Agapanthus umbellatus albus, commonly known as the white agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, spherical inflorescences composed of numerous white, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its elegant appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a favorite among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
