Acanthus raddei

Acanthus raddei, commonly known as the Cape acanthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiny leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often used in horticultural designs due to its ornamental value.

Acanthus raddei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
