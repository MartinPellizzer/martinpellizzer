Anerincleistus setulosus var. pallidifolius

Anerincleistus setulosus var. pallidifolius, commonly known as the pale-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It possesses small, lanceolate leaves that are typically pale green, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forested areas where it forms a symbiotic relationship with its host.
