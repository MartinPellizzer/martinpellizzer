Anthemis arvensis var. agrestis

Anthemis arvensis var. agrestis, commonly known as field chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and meadows, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are finely divided and have a characteristic aromatic scent, while the yellow flowers bloom in summer.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
