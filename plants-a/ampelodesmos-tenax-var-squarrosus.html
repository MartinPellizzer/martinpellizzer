Ampelodesmos tenax var. squarrosus

Ampelodesmos tenax var. squarrosus, commonly known as the squarrosus varietal of the climbing vine, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It is native to regions of southern Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
