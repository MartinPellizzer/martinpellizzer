Aloe angulata var. truncata

Aloe angulata var. truncata, commonly known as the African toothbrush, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or purple tint. The leaves are often adorned with white or yellow marginal teeth, giving the plant its common name. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow to orange flowers during the summer months.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
