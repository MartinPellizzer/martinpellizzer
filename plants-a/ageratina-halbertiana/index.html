Ageratina Halbertiana

Ageratina halbertiana is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is known for its fluffy, white flower heads that resemble clouds, making it a unique addition to any botanical collection. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its delicate appearance belies its resilience, as it can withstand harsh climatic conditions. Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, Ageratina halbertiana is a subject of growing interest among botanists and conservationists.
