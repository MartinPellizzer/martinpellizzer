Aspilia meridensis

Aspilia meridensis, commonly known as the Cape mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features erect, hairy stems and produces yellow-orange ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Aspilia meridensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
