Acacia penninervis

Acacia penninervis, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
