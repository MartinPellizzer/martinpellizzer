Astragalus viciifolius subsp. raddeanus

Astragalus viciifolius subsp.

raddeanus, commonly known as the Raddean milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with a vicia-like foliage structure, and its small, yellowish to brownish flowers that appear in raceme clusters.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and mountainous terrains. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and possesses the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
