Asparagus crispus

Asparagus crispus, commonly known as smooth asparagus, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or decumbent growth habit, with slender, branched stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to greenish-white flowers that are clustered in loose panicles, followed by small, red or purple berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a bluish-green color.

Asparagus crispus is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in waste areas, and is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid spread.
