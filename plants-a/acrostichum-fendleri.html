Acrostichum fendleri

Acrostichum fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican sword fern, is a member of the fern family (Polypodiaceae).

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are covered by a protective indusium.

Acrostichum fendleri is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in humid tropical and subtropical climates.
