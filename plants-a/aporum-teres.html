Aporum teres

Aporum teres, commonly known as the golden star sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green with a slight yellowish hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are often hidden among the foliage. Aporum teres thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a popular choice for ornamental gardening in moist soils.
