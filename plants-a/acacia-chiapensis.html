Acacia chiapensis

Acacia chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and dry environments. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.

Acacia chiapensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
