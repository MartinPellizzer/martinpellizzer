Astragalus depressus proles helminthocarpus

Astragalus depressus proles helminthocarpus, commonly known as the flatleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit and distinctive trifoliate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes, which are followed by inflated, spiral-shaped pods. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

This species is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
