Aeschynomene schimperi var. grandiflora

Aeschynomene schimperi var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered aeschynomene, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow prostrate or ascending, with small, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
