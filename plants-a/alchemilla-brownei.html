Alchemilla brownei

Alchemilla brownei, commonly known as the browne's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla brownei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
