Abronia × alba

Abronia × alba, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Abronia umbellata and Abronia tetragona. The plant is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
