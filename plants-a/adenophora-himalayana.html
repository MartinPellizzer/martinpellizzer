Adenophora himalayana

Adenophora himalayana, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Adenophora himalayana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
