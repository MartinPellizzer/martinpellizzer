Agrostis colorata

Agrostis colorata, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms in late summer with purple to pink flowers.

Agrostis colorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
