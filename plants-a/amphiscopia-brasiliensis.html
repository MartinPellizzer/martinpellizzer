Amphiscopia brasiliensis

Amphiscopia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant has a complex inflorescence with multiple flowers arranged along a single stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation and habitat loss.
