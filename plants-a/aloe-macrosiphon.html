Aloe macrosiphon

Aloe macrosiphon, commonly known as the giant aloe or tree aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stem and large, fleshy leaves that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom during the summer months. Aloe macrosiphon is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
