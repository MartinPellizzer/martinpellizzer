Achyranthes capituliflora

Achyranthes capituliflora, commonly known as the yellowwood plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds with a tuft of hairs for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
