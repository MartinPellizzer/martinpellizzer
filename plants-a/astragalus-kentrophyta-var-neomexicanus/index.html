Astragalus Kentrophyta Var. Neomexicanus

Astragalus kentrophyta var. neomexicanus is a rare and lesser-known species of milkvetch found primarily in the southwestern United States. This variant is notable for its unique morphological characteristics and limited geographic distribution. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils and open plains. Due to its scarcity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat loss and climate change. Studying this plant offers insights into the adaptability of flora in extreme conditions.
