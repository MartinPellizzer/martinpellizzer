Andropogon ischaemum

Andropogon ischaemum, commonly known as bush bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Bush bluestem is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
