Ascidieria pseudocymbiformis var. hirsuta

Ascidieria pseudocymbiformis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy false bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
