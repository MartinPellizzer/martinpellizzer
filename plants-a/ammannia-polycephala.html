Ammannia polycephala

Ammannia polycephala, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its roots are reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

Ammannia polycephala is often found in wetland areas and can thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
