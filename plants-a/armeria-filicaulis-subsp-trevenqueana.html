Armeria filicaulis subsp. trevenqueana

Armeria filicaulis subsp.

trevenqueana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thrift, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
