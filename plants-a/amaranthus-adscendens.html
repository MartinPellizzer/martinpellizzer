Amaranthus adscendens

Amaranthus adscendens, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red stems, which are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into spiny, three-angled seed capsules. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Amaranthus adscendens is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
