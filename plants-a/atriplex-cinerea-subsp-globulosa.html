Atriplex cinerea subsp. globulosa

Atriplex cinerea subsp.

globulosa, commonly known as silverleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in globular inflorescences, giving it its subspecific name. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Its succulent leaves and stems store water, allowing it to survive in low-nutrient, dry conditions.
