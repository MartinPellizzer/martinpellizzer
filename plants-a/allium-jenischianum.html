Allium jenischianum

Allium jenischianum, commonly known as the Jenisch's onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, edible bulbs that are similar in appearance to garlic and onions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners and foragers alike.
