Adenia macrophylla f. microcarpa

Adenia macrophylla f. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited adenia, is a compact shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
