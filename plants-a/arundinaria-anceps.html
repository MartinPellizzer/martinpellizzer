Arundinaria anceps

Arundinaria anceps, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to golden in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Arundinaria anceps is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
