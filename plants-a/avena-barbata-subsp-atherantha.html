Avena barbata subsp. atherantha

Avena barbata subsp.

atherantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
