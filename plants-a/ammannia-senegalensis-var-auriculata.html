Ammannia senegalensis var. auriculata

Ammannia senegalensis var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate ammannia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, pink to red flowers arranged in clusters. The leaves are ovate to elliptic with a distinctive auriculate base, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swampy areas along riverbanks.

The plant is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
