Apterocaryon

Apterocaryon, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their compact rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Apterocaryon species are typically evergreen and thrive in humid, well-drained soil conditions. They are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and ease of care.

The flowers often bloom in spring and summer, adding vibrant color to indoor spaces.
