Amyris pernambucensis

Amyris pernambucensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous wood. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a source of resin.
