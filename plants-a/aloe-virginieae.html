Aloe virginieae

Aloe virginieae, commonly known as the Virginian aloe, is a succulent plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-orange flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is shallow, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Aloe virginieae is often used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
