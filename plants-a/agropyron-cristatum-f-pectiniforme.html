Agropyron cristatum f. pectiniforme

Agropyron cristatum f. pectiniforme, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a variant of the wheatgrass species native to the grasslands of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive tufted growth habit and dense, upright stems. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as prairies and disturbed sites.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
