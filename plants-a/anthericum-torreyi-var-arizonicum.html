Anthericum torreyi var. arizonicum

Anthericum torreyi var. arizonicum, commonly known as the Arizona lily, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems with clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to its natural environment.
