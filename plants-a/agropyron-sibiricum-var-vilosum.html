Agropyron sibiricum var. vilosum

Agropyron sibiricum var. vilosum, commonly known as Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arctic regions.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is used in ecological restoration and as a forage crop.
