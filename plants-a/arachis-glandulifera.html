Arachis glandulifera

Arachis glandulifera, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a species of legume native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing seeds that are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is often cultivated in agroforestry systems.
