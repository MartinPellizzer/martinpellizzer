Arundinaria nagashima

Arundinaria nagashima, commonly known as the Nagashima bamboo, is a species native to Japan.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, whitish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Arundinaria nagashima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Japanese crafts.
