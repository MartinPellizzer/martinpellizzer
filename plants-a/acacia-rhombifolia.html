Acacia rhombifolia

Acacia rhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia rhombifolia is often used in ecological restoration due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
