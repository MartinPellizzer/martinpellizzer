Arisaema undulatifolium f. limbatum

Arisaema undulatifolium f. limbatum, commonly known as the wavy-leaf arisaema, is a variant of the undulatifolium species within the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, umbrella-shaped inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
