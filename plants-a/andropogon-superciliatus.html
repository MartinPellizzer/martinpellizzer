Andropogon superciliatus

Andropogon superciliatus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky stalked grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Andropogon superciliatus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
