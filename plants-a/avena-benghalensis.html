Avena benghalensis

Avena benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian oat or Benghal oat, is a perennial grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Avena benghalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
