Anthyllis vulneraria f. atropurpurascens

Anthyllis vulneraria f. atropurpurascens, commonly known as the purple-hued everlasting pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in dry, calcareous soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its persistent, showy blooms.
