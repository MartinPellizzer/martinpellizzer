Amelanchier rupestris

Amelanchier rupestris, commonly known as the rock almond, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color in late summer. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in woodland edges or open fields.

Amelanchier rupestris is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
