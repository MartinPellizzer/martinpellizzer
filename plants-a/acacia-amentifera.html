Acacia amentifera

Acacia amentifera, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its slender, branching structure and feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in pendulous clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia amentifera is adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and has traditional uses in various cultures.
