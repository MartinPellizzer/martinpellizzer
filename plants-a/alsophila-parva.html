Alsophila parva

Alsophila parva, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its fronds are dimorphic, with sterile and fertile fronds differing in size and shape.

Alsophila parva plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various small organisms.
