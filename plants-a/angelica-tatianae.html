Angelica tatianae

Angelica tatianae, commonly known as the Tibetan angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive foliage.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features large, compound leaves with a pinnate structure and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, and it produces dark, oily seeds that are used in traditional medicine.

Angelica tatianae is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in Tibetan and Chinese herbal practices.
