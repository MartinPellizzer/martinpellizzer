Aechmea conglomerata var. farinosa

Aechmea conglomerata var. farinosa, commonly known as the Wooly Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, wooly covering of trichomes on the leaves, which gives it a unique, fuzzy appearance. The plant forms a rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its natural habitat in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, colorful flower spike that can produce showy blooms in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
