Aristolochia pringlei

Aristolochia pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-brown with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral morphology. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and woody.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
