Agropyron berezovcanum

Agropyron berezovcanum, commonly known as the Berezovskaya wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It is adapted to harsh environments, including high altitudes and arid conditions, making it a hardy species.

Agropyron berezovcanum is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky and degraded areas.
