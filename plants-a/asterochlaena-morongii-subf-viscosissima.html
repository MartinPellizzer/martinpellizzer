Asterochlaena morongii subf. viscosissima

Asterochlaena morongii subf.

viscosissima, commonly known as the sticky asterochlaena, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its sticky texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and have a distinct aromatic quality.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
