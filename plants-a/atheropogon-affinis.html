Atheropogon affinis

Atheropogon affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, disturbed soils, and rocky areas. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicled arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Atheropogon affinis is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
