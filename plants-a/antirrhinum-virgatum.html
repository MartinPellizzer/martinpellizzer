Antirrhinum virgatum

Antirrhinum virgatum, commonly known as the common snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in meadows and along roadsides.

Antirrhinum virgatum is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.
