Aleuritia nutans

Aleuritia nutans, commonly known as the nodding alder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that hang in clusters. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

Aleuritia nutans thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
