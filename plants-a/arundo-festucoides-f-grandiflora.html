Arundo festucoides f. grandiflora

Arundo festucoides f. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant reed or giant grass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy white flowers in late summer, which are characteristic of the grass family. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an effective erosion control plant.
