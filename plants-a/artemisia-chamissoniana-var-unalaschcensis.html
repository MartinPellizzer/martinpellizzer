Artemisia chamissoniana var. unalaschcensis

Artemisia chamissoniana var. unalaschcensis, commonly known as the Alaskan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arctic tundra environments.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ecological importance in its native habitats.
