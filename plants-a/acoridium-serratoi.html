Acoridium serratoi

Acoridium serratoi, commonly known as the serrated acoridium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae, which includes a small group of aquatic and semi-aquatic plants. The plant is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves that are often ribbed and serrated along the edges. It produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, a feature common in many members of its family.

Acoridium serratoi is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies.
