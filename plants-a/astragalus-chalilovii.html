Astragalus chalilovii

Astragalus chalilovii, commonly known as the Chalilov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with deep root systems. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance highlights its importance in local biodiversity.
