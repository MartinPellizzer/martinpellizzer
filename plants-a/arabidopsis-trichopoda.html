Arabidopsis trichopoda

Arabidopsis trichopoda, commonly known as the hairy Arabidopsis, is a small flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like Arabidopsis thaliana. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide protection against environmental stressors. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its genetic simplicity and rapid life cycle, it is sometimes used in scientific research, though less commonly than Arabidopsis thaliana.
