Anguina japonica

Anguina japonica, commonly known as the Japanese burrweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces clusters of dry, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas and along roadsides.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
