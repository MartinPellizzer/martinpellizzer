Astragalus supranubias

Astragalus supranubias, commonly known as the cloud mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, particularly in Nepal and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Astragalus supranubias is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows at elevations above 3,000 meters.
