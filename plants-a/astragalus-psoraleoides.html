Astragalus psoraleoides

Astragalus psoraleoides, commonly known as the sand milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus psoraleoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
