Ageratum hirsutum

Ageratum hirsutum, commonly known as flossflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, purple, blue, and pink. The plant has hairy, often glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Ageratum hirsutum is also used in traditional medicine and as a cut flower in horticulture.
