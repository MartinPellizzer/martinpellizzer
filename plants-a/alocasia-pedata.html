Alocasia pedata

Alocasia pedata, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or African mask plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 feet in diameter, with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Alocasia pedata requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a favorite among indoor plant enthusiasts.
