Archibaccharis mucronata var. paniculata

Archibaccharis mucronata var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled archibaccharis, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a glossy appearance, while its flowering period typically occurs during the spring and summer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
