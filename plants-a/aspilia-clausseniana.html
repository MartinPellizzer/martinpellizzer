Aspilia clausseniana

Aspilia clausseniana, commonly known as the Clausen's torchweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of disk florets, giving it a torch-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Aspilia clausseniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
