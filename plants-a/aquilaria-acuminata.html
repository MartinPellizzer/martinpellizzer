Aquilaria acuminata

Aquilaria acuminata, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and incense production. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to overharvesting, it is now classified as an endangered species.
