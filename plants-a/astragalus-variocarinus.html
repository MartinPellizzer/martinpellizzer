Astragalus variocarinus

Astragalus variocarinus, commonly known as the variable shieldpod, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like seed cases. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a unique structure that aids in pollination.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its morphological variability and ecological significance.
