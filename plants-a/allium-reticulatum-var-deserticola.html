Allium reticulatum var. deserticola

Allium reticulatum var. deserticola, commonly known as the desert garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valuable plant for both culinary and medicinal uses.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
