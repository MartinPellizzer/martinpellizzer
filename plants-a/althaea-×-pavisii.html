Althaea × pavisii

Althaea × pavisii, commonly known as the common marshmallow, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Althaea × pavisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
