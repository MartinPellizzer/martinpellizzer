Afzelia bipindensis

Afzelia bipindensis, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Afzelia bipindensis is valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making, and its seeds are used in the production of shea butter.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
