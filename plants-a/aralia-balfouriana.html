Aralia balfouriana

Aralia balfouriana, commonly known as the Balfour's aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features compound leaves that are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbellate inflorescences, which are characteristic of the Araliaceae family. Its fruit is a dark blue to black berry that contains several seeds.

Aralia balfouriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and unique floral structure.
