Aloe fimbrialis

Aloe fimbrialis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with narrow, fimbriated (fringed) leaves that give it its scientific name. The leaves are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom during the summer months.

This plant is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
