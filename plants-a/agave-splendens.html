Agave splendens

Agave splendens, commonly known as the shining agave, is a species of succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as bloom, which helps reduce water loss.

Agave splendens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
