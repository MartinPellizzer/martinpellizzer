Acacia nivea

Acacia nivea, commonly known as the snow acacia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, globular pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia nivea thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its foliage consists of bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give the plant a delicate appearance.
