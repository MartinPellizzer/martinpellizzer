Adelia pedunculosa

Adelia pedunculosa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed adelia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It blooms during the cooler months, typically from late autumn to early spring.

Adelia pedunculosa is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant gardens.
