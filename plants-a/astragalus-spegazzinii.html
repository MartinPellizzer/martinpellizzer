Astragalus spegazzinii

Astragalus spegazzinii, commonly known as the Spegazzini milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some areas.
