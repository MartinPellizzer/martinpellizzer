Anthyllis vulneraria var. variegata

Anthyllis vulneraria var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaf patterns, which feature a mix of green and white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often growing in dry, calcareous soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
