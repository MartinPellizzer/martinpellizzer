Albizia rosulata subsp. landakensis

Albizia rosulata subsp.

landakensis, commonly known as the African wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and have a distinctive silvery underside.

The plant produces fragrant, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Albizia rosulata subsp.

landakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
