Adesmia glutinosa

Adesmia glutinosa, commonly known as sticky adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its sticky secretions on the leaves and stems may serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Adesmia glutinosa is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological role in its native environment.
