Asphodelus microcarpus

Asphodelus microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and spikes of pale yellow or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
