Asplenium scolopendrioides

Asplenium scolopendrioides, commonly known as the snake's head fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with clustered, lanceolate fronds that are dark green and glossy. Its sporangia are borne on the underside of the fronds, arranged in clusters called sori.

Asplenium scolopendrioides is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands.
