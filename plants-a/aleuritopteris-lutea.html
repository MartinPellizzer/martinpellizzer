Aleuritopteris lutea

Aleuritopteris lutea, commonly known as the golden huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperjiaceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow spore cases, which are distinctive and contribute to its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are simple and often have a leathery texture, with a serrated margin.

Aleuritopteris lutea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
