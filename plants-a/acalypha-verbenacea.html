Acalypha verbenacea

Acalypha verbenacea, commonly known as redroot ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish beneath. The plant produces showy clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and can be reddish in color, which is a distinguishing feature.

Acalypha verbenacea is often found in moist, open habitats such as roadsides and woodland edges.
