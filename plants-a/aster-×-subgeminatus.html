Aster × subgeminatus

Aster × subgeminatus, commonly known as the doubleflower aster, is a hybrid perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flower heads that resemble small daisies, with white to pink petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often aromatic.

Aster × subgeminatus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
