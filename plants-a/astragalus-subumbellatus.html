Astragalus subumbellatus

Astragalus subumbellatus, commonly known as the western milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbel-like clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
