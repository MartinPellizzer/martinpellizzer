Acanthus latifolius

Acanthus latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences with pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its architectural form and foliage make it a popular choice for garden design and historical architecture.

Acanthus latifolius is also valued for its role in traditional herbal medicine and its use in horticultural displays.
