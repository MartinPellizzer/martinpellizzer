Asplenium weberbaueri

Asplenium weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically has a short, creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves. Native to tropical regions of Central and South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Asplenium weberbaueri is often found growing on rock surfaces or in crevices, demonstrating its adaptability to rocky substrates.
