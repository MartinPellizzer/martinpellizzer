Adenocarpus

Adenocarpus, commonly known as the "broom weed," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are typically shrubs or herbs, characterized by their compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a legume, which splits open to release the seeds. Adenocarpus species are often found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

They play a role in soil stabilization and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
