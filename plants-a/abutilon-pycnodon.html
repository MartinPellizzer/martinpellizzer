Abutilon pycnodon

Abutilon pycnodon, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a velvety texture.

Abutilon pycnodon is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny conditions.
