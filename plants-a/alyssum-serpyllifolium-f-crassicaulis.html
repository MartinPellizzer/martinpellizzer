Alyssum serpyllifolium f. crassicaulis

Alyssum serpyllifolium f. crassicaulis, commonly known as broad-leafed thrift, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
