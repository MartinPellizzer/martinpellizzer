Abies × insignis var. beissneriana

Abies × insignis var. beissneriana, commonly known as the Beissner's fir, is a hybrid conifer native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like foliage that is often slightly twisted. The cones are typically slender and upright, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping in cooler climates.
