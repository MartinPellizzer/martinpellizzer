Anacardium

Anacardium, commonly known as the cashew tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It produces a unique fruit that contains both a nut and a toxic, sticky resin called urushiol. The plant's leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture. The tree is valued for its edible nut and its oil, which is used in cooking and cosmetics.

Botanically, it belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and mango.
