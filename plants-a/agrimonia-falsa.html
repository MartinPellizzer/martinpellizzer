Agrimonia falsa

Agrimonia falsa, commonly known as false agrimony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Agrimonia falsa is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
