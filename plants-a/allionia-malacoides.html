Allionia malacoides

Allionia malacoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Malacoides, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Allionia malacoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
