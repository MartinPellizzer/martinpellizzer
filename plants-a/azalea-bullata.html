Azalea bullata

Azalea bullata, commonly known as the snow azale, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, almost fluffy appearance. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Azalea bullata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
