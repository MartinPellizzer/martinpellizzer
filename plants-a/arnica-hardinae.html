Arnica hardinae

Arnica hardinae, commonly known as the hardy arnica, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Arnica hardinae is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.

It plays an important role in alpine ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
