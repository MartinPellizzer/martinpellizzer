Aristea zeyheri

Aristea zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

Aristea zeyheri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
