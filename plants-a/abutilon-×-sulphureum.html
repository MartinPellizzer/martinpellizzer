Abutilon × sulphureum

Abutilon × sulphureum, commonly known as the sulfur plant or golden abutilon, is a hybrid species within the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features soft, fuzzy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Abutilon × sulphureum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy blooms and unique foliage.
