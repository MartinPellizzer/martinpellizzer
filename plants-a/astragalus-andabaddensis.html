Astragalus andabaddensis

Astragalus andabaddensis, commonly known as the Andabadda milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to dry environments, featuring deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
