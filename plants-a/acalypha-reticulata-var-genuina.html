Acalypha reticulata var. genuina

Acalypha reticulata var. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or net-veined spurflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a velvety texture and deep green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
