Arthrochilium veratrifolium

Arthrochilium veratrifolium, commonly known as the three-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and pine flatwoods. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces three leaf-like structures that are often mistaken for true leaves.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and limited distribution.
