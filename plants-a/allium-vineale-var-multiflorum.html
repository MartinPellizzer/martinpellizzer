Allium vineale var. multiflorum

Allium vineale var. multiflorum, commonly known as the bulbous leek or wild leek, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, garlic-scented leaves. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded, rich soils.
