Aristolochia austrochinensis

Aristolochia austrochinensis, commonly known as the Chinese birthwort, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and produces intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant blossoms that attract insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Aristolochia is known for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
