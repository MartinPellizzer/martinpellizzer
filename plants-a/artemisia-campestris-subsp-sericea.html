Artemisia campestris subsp. sericea

Artemisia campestris subsp.

sericea, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It features aromatic, silvery-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance and contribute to its strong, camphor-like scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicle clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
