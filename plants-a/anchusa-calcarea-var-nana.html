Anchusa calcarea var. nana

Anchusa calcarea var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf limestone hollyhock, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
