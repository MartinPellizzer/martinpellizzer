Asperula aristata subsp. jordanii

Asperula aristata subsp.

jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's sweet pepper, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its compact form and dense foliage.

The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
