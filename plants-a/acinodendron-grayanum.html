Acinodendron grayanum

Acinodendron grayanum, commonly known as the grayanum acacia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at elevations above 2,000 meters.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
