Agrostis hispanica var. mutica

Agrostis hispanica var. mutica, commonly known as the hairy bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

Agrostis hispanica var. mutica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
