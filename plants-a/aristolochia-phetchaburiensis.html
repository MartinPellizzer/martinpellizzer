Aristolochia phetchaburiensis

Aristolochia phetchaburiensis, commonly known as the Phetchaburi Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Thailand.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular-shaped flowers that resemble a pipe, typically appearing in shades of green and yellow. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and showy blooms. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
