Astragalus halei

Astragalus halei, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus halei is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
