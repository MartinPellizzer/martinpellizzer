Aristida misionum

Aristida misionum, commonly known as Mision grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, thread-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates, often found in grasslands and open areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
