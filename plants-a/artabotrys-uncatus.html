Artabotrys uncatus

Artabotrys uncatus, commonly known as the fragrant starfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they turn yellow or orange. The flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and showy inflorescences.
