Astragalus hamzaoglui

Astragalus hamzaoglui, commonly known as Hamzaoglu's milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its root system is fibrous and contributes to soil stabilization, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
