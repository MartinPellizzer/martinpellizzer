Arundinaria sodiroana

Arundinaria sodiroana, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a fibrous texture.

Arundinaria sodiroana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
