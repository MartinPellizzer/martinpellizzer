Afroligusticum townsendii

Afroligusticum townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's African grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like structure and fibrous growth habit.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant typically grows as a perennial, with tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers, a common feature in grasses.

Afroligusticum townsendii is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
