Acalypha polynema

Acalypha polynema, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often have red veins contrasting with their green or yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
