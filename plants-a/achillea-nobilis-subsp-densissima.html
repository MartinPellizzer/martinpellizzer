Achillea nobilis subsp. densissima

Achillea nobilis subsp.

densissima, commonly known as the dense yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features a robust root system and upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are finely divided, aromatic, and covered in a silvery-white coating, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
