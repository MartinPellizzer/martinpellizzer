Agropyron dasystachyum var. riparium

Agropyron dasystachyum var. riparium, commonly known as the riparian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks and wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

This species is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
