Aquilegia cossoniana

Aquilegia cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's columbine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

Aquilegia cossoniana is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it adapts well to cool, moist conditions.
