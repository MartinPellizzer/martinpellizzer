Ageratum campuloclinioides

Ageratum campuloclinioides, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum campuloclinioides is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
