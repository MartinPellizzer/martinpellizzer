Acacia glabriflora

Acacia glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically arranged in clusters. Acacia glabriflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various animals.
