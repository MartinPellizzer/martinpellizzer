Asplenium bipinnatifidum

Asplenium bipinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are bipinnatifid, meaning they are divided twice, with narrow, linear segments that give it a lacy appearance.

Asplenium bipinnatifidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in indoor settings with proper humidity and light conditions.
