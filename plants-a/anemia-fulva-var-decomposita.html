Anemia fulva var. decomposita

Anemia fulva var. decomposita, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant's common name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
