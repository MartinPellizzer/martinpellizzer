Arabis lusitanica

Arabis lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arabis lusitanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
