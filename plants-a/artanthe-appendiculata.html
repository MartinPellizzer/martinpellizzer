Artanthe appendiculata

Artanthe appendiculata, commonly known as the woolly artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Artanthe appendiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in arid gardens due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
