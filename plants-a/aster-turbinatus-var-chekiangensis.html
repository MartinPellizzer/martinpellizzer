Aster turbinatus var. chekiangensis

Aster turbinatus var. chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chekiang aster, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
