Andropogon loharduggae

Andropogon loharduggae, commonly known as the western grass, is a perennial grass species native to the central and southern United States.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in full sun.
