Alchemilla racemulosa

Alchemilla racemulosa, commonly known as the clustered lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in summer, which are arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Alchemilla racemulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
