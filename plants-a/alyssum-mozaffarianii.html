Alyssum mozaffarianii

Alyssum mozaffarianii, commonly known as Persian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Alyssum mozaffarianii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
