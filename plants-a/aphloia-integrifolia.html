Aphloia integrifolia

Aphloia integrifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Aphloia integrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant ideal for xeriscaping.
