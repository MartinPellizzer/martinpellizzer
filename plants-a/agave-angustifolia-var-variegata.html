Agave angustifolia var. variegata

Agave angustifolia var. variegata, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave with variegation, is a striking succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that are narrow and often display a distinctive variegated pattern of green, white, and sometimes pink. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and rock gardens.
