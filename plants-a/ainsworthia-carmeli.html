Ainsworthia carmeli

Ainsworthia carmeli, commonly known as the Carmel flame or Carmel grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences with numerous tiny, pale yellow to reddish brown flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Ainsworthia carmeli is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
