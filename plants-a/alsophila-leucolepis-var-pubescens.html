Alsophila leucolepis var. pubescens

Alsophila leucolepis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a variant of the slender tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery structure that contributes to its ornamental value.
