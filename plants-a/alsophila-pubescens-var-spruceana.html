Alsophila pubescens var. spruceana

Alsophila pubescens var. spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, hairy layer that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is valued in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
