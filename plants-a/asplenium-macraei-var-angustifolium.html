Asplenium macraei var. angustifolium

Asplenium macraei var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock fern, is a variant of the rock fern genus Asplenium.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found growing on rock surfaces or in rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, narrow shape that distinguishes it from other varieties.

This fern is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
