Asparagus amarus

Asparagus amarus, commonly known as the bitter asparagus, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thin, spiny stems and fern-like, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Asparagus amarus is notable for its medicinal uses, though its bitter taste has limited its culinary application.
