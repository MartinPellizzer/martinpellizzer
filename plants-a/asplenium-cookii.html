Asplenium cookii

Asplenium cookii, commonly known as Cook's spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided into smaller segments, giving it a lace-like appearance. It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
