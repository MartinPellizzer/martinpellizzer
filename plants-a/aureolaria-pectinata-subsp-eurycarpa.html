Aureolaria pectinata subsp. eurycarpa

Aureolaria pectinata subsp.

eurycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited sandverbena, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture with serrated edges. Its showy, pink to purple flowers are borne in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

This subspecies is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
