Athyrium mettenianum var. isobasis

Athyrium mettenianum var. isobasis, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to East Asia.

It features finely divided, arching fronds with a pale green to silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its elegant foliage makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and indoor settings.
