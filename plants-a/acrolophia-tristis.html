Acrolophia tristis

Acrolophia tristis, commonly known as the yellow-flowered pincushion cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a globular shape, resembling a pincushion. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
