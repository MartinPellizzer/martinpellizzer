Athyrium subserratum

Athyrium subserratum, commonly known as the serrated lady fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are bi- or tri-pinnate, with finely serrated margins that give it its common name.

Athyrium subserratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
