Arctostaphylos oaxacana

Arctostaphylos oaxacana, commonly known as Oaxaca manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in mountainous regions. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, dark seeds, contributing to its ecological role in native habitats.

Arctostaphylos oaxacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native environment.
