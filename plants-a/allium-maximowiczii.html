Allium maximowiczii

Allium maximowiczii, commonly known as the Turk's cap leek, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features striking, spherical inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to purple flowers. The plant has basal leaves that are long, narrow, and often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its late summer to early fall blooming period.
