Allium moly proles stramineum

Allium moly proles stramineum, commonly known as the yellow garlic or wild garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
