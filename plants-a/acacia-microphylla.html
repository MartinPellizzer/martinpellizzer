Acacia microphylla

Acacia microphylla, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Acacia microphylla is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
