Aglaia pirifera

Aglaia pirifera, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other ornamental trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
