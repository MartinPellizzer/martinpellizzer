Allium guttatum subsp. dilatatum

Allium guttatum subsp.

dilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, grass-like leaves and produces slender, purple-tinged flower stalks that bear small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant is known for its mild garlic aroma and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Allium genus, with unique morphological traits that set it apart from other related species.
