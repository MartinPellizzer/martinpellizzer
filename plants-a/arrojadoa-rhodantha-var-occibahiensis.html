Arrojadoa rhodantha var. occibahiensis

Arrojadoa rhodantha var. occibahiensis, commonly known as the Occidental redflower lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a valuable addition to desert gardens.
