Asterocephalus pectinatus

Asterocephalus pectinatus, commonly known as the woolly star, is a rare and unusual plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woolly appearance, with dense, white to grayish hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle contrast to its fuzzy foliage. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in well-drained soils with minimal water.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
