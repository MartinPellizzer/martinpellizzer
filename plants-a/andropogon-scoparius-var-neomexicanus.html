Andropogon scoparius var. neomexicanus

Andropogon scoparius var. neomexicanus, commonly known as New Mexican bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. This variant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.

The plant is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
