Androsaemum × urberuagae

Androsaemum × urberuagae, commonly known as the hybrid sweet box, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its aromatic foliage and showy blooms.
