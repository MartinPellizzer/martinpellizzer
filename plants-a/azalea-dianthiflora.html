Azalea dianthiflora

Azalea dianthiflora, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is a member of the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Azalea dianthiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
