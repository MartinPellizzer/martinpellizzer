Aeranthes xanthopollinia

Aeranthes xanthopollinia, commonly known as the yellow-pollinated orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structures. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its unique pollination mechanism involving specific insect interactions.
