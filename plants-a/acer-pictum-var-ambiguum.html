Acer pictum var. ambiguum

Acer pictum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the eastern redbud, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local wildlife.
