Atriplex hastata subsp. polonica

Atriplex hastata subsp.

polonica, commonly known as the Polish saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that help reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia, where it thrives in coastal and saline soils.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
