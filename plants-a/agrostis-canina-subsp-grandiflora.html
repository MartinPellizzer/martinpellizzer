Agrostis canina subsp. grandiflora

Agrostis canina subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and the leaves are narrow with a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
