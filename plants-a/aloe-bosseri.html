Aloe bosseri

Aloe bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional medicine and skincare products.

Aloe bosseri is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
