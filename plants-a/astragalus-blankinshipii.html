Astragalus blankinshipii

Astragalus blankinshipii, commonly known as the Blankinship milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep taproots that allow it to survive in sandy or rocky soils.

Astragalus blankinshipii is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
