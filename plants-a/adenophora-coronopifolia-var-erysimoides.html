Adenophora coronopifolia var. erysimoides

Adenophora coronopifolia var. erysimoides, commonly known as the erysimoides bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens, though it requires careful cultivation to thrive.
