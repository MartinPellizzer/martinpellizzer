Amsonia tomentosa var. stenophylla

Amsonia tomentosa var. stenophylla, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
