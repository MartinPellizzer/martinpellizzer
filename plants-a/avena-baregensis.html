Avena baregensis

Avena baregensis, commonly known as the Bareges oat, is a species of grass native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. Avena baregensis is adapted to high-altitude environments, exhibiting cold tolerance and efficient water use.

It is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
