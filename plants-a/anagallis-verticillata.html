Anagallis verticillata

Anagallis verticillata, commonly known as the whorled willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds.

Anagallis verticillata is an annual or biennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
