Abies yunnanensis

Abies yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds surrounded by a wing for wind dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high elevations, often growing in dense forests with other coniferous species.

The Yunnan fir is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
