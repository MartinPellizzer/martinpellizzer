Astragalus juniperetorum

Astragalus juniperetorum, commonly known as the juniperleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Astragalus juniperetorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
