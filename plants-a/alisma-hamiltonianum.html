Alisma hamiltonianum

Alisma hamiltonianum, commonly known as Hamilton's water plantain, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and streams. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are submerged or floating, and it produces small, white or pale green flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic properties.

Alisma hamiltonianum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
