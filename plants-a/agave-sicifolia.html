Agave sicifolia

Agave sicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of white, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
