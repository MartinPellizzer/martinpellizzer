Armeria leucocephala subsp. soleirolii

Armeria leucocephala subsp.

soleirolii, commonly known as the white-headed thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, fluffy flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and forms low, compact mats, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in Mediterranean climates.
