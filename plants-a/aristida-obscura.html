Aristida obscura

Aristida obscura, commonly known as blackgrass or darkgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, dark green foliage that often appears dark due to its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil, Aristida obscura plays a role in preventing erosion in arid landscapes.
