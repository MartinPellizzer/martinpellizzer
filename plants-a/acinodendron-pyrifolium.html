Acinodendron pyrifolium

Acinodendron pyrifolium, commonly known as the pineapple tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that resemble small pineapples, hence its common name. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Acinodendron pyrifolium is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit, which is rich in nutrients.
