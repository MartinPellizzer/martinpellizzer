Aristolochia schreiteri

Aristolochia schreiteri, commonly known as the Schreiter's Dutchman's pipe, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding growth habit. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal but is also noted for containing aristolochic acids, which can be toxic to humans and animals.
