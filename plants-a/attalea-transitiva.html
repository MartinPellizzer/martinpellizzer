Attalea transitiva

Attalea transitiva, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 6 meters in length, providing significant canopy cover. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, branched inflorescences, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of the rainforest.
