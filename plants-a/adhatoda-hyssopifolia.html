Adhatoda hyssopifolia

Adhatoda hyssopifolia, commonly known as Indian skullcap, is a shrub native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
