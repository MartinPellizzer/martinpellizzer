Anthriscus procera

Anthriscus procera, commonly known as giant parsley, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fern-like leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, white flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Anthriscus procera is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and is also used in some culinary applications.
