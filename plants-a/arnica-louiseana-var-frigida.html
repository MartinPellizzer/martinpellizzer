Arnica louiseana var. frigida

Arnica louiseana var. frigida, commonly known as the frigid arnica, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as tundra and wet meadows. The plant features dark green, slightly hairy leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disks. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
