Adenophyllum porophyllum

Adenophyllum porophyllum, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

Adenophyllum porophyllum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert habitats.
