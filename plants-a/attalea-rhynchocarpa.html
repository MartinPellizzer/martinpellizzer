Attalea rhynchocarpa

Attalea rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by the development of large, oblong fruits. The fruits are typically harvested for their edible seeds, which are rich in oils and nutrients.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also valued for its ornamental and economic uses.
