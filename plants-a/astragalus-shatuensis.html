Astragalus shatuensis

Astragalus shatuensis, commonly known as the Shatun grass vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Astragalus species. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppes and mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitats.
