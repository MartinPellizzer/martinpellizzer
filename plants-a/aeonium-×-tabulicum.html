Aeonium × tabulicum

Aeonium × tabulicum, commonly known as the "Tree Aeonium," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves. The plant typically grows upright, forming a dense, tree-like structure with multiple layers of foliage. Its leaves are usually green, sometimes with red or purple hues, and can vary in texture from smooth to slightly hairy.

Aeonium × tabulicum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
