Avena

Avena, commonly known as oat, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that contain small, oval-shaped grains known as caryopses. Avena species are widely cultivated for their nutritious seeds, which are used in food, animal feed, and various industrial products.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and climates.
