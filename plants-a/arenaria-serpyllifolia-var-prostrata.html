Arenaria serpyllifolia var. prostrata

Arenaria serpyllifolia var. prostrata, commonly known as prostrate sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to temperate regions.

It typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or alpine environments. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are closely arranged along the stems, and it produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It is a perennial species with a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it an adaptable and hardy plant for various ecological niches.
