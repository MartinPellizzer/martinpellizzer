Astragalus albidoflavus

Astragalus albidoflavus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting deep root systems and drought tolerance.

Astragalus albidoflavus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
