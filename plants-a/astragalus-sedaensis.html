Astragalus sedaensis

Astragalus sedaensis, commonly known as the Sedaense milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
