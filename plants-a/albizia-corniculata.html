Albizia corniculata

Albizia corniculata, commonly known as the crown flower or crown bean, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Albizia corniculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
