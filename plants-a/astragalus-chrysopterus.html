Astragalus chrysopterus

Astragalus chrysopterus, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves, which are typical of many legumes. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus chrysopterus is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments.
