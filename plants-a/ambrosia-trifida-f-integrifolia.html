Ambrosia trifida f. integrifolia

Ambrosia trifida f. integrifolia, commonly known as the giant ragweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with smooth, entire margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spherical clusters, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in agricultural fields.

Due to its allergenic pollen, it is considered a significant weed in many regions.
