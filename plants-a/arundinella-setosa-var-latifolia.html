Arundinella setosa var. latifolia

Arundinella setosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf reed, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects to stabilize soil in moist habitats.
