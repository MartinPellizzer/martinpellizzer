Acer palmatum f. variegatum

Acer palmatum f. variegatum, commonly known as the Variegated Japanese maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree known for its ornamental value.

It features striking foliage with a mix of green, white, and pink hues, creating a vibrant display throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a delicate, feathery appearance that adds visual interest to gardens. This plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique coloration and elegant form make it a sought-after addition to both residential and public gardens.
