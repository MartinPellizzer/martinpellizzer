Axonopus cuatrecasasii

Axonopus cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas' axonopus, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Axonopus cuatrecasasii is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
