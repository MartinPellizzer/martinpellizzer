Asphodelus morisianus

Asphodelus morisianus, commonly known as the Morisian asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and tall, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Asphodelus morisianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
