Anthericum brachyphyllum

Anthericum brachyphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that form a basal rosette. The plant produces tall, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a dense, truss-like cluster. It prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Anthericum brachyphyllum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
