Alcoceria pringlei

Alcoceria pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the rainforests of South America.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered together, and its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds. Alcoceria pringlei plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for birds and other animals that consume its berries.

Its parasitic nature means it derives some of its nutrients from the host tree, but it also contributes to the ecological balance of its habitat.
