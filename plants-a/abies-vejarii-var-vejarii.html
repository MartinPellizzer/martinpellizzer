Abies vejarii var. vejarii

Abies vejarii var. vejarii, commonly known as the Vejarii fir, is a rare and endangered conifer native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its botanical features make it an important species for studying alpine forest ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
