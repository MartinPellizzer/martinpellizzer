Acoridium linearifolium

Acoridium linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure known as a spathe and spadix. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with the showy spathe serving as an attractant for pollinators.
