Annona rhombipetala

Annona rhombipetala, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is native to Central America and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features a short, woody stem with large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowers are fragrant, white, and have a distinctive, complex structure with numerous stamens.

The fruit is a rough, green, egg-shaped berry that turns yellow when ripe, containing a sweet, creamy pulp surrounded by a central core.
