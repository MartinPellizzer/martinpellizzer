Alchemilla capillacea

Alchemilla capillacea, commonly known as slender lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in a cup-like structure. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla capillacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
