Adiantum pedatum f. contratum

Adiantum pedatum f. contratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to the eastern United States, particularly in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are typically lanceolate and have a distinctive, elegant appearance with a bluish-green hue. This fern is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens and rockeries.
