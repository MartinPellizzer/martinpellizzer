Astragalus neurophyllus

Astragalus neurophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus neurophyllus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
