Anemone angustiloba

Anemone angustiloba, commonly known as the narrowleaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its root system consists of fibrous roots, and it is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Anemone angustiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
