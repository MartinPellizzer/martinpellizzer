Andreoskia pectinata

Andreoskia pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
