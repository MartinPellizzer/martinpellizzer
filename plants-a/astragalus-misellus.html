Astragalus misellus

Astragalus misellus, commonly known as the small milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands or sagebrush steppe. Astragalus misellus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
