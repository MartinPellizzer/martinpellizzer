Anthemis peregrina subsp. peregrina

Anthemis peregrina subsp.

peregrina, commonly known as the desert chamomile, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface.

The essential oils from its flowers are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
