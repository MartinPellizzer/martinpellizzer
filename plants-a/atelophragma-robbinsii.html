Atelophragma robbinsii

Atelophragma robbinsii, commonly known as the Robbins' spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender sepals and petals that resemble the legs of a spider. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
