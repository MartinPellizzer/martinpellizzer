Abies cedroides

Abies cedroides, commonly known as the Colorado blue spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and bluish-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The needles are short, stiff, and arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
