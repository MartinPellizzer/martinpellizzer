Ascium berteroi

Ascium berteroi, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along stream banks or in shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
