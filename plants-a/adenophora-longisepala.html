Adenophora longisepala

Adenophora longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy stems and leaves. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.

Adenophora longisepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
