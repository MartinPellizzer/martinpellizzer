Allium lehmannianum var. kokanicum

Allium lehmannianum var. kokanicum, commonly known as the Kokanik garlic, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and clustered, bulbous stems. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines for their pungent aroma and flavor.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cold, mountainous climates.
