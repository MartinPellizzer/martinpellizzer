Alchemilla atriuscula

Alchemilla atriuscula, commonly known as black alchemilla, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla atriuscula is valued for its ornamental foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
