Agrostis canina f. fasciculata

Agrostis canina f. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered bentgrass, is a variant of the bentgrass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically forms compact, mat-like colonies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along streambanks.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions, contributing to its ecological significance.
