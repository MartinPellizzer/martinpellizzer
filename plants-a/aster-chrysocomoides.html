Aster chrysocomoides

Aster chrysocomoides, commonly known as the golden-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
