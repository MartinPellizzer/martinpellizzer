Acacia inceana subsp. conformis

Acacia inceana subsp.

conformis, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, zigzagging stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in compact spikes.

The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments. Acacia inceana subsp.

conformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
