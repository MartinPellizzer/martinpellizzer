Arenaria redowskii subsp. redowskii

Arenaria redowskii subsp.

redowskii, commonly known as the Redowski's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically forms dense mats of small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it has a compact growth habit to withstand strong winds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
