Acalypha comorensis

Acalypha comorensis, commonly known as the Comoros ironwood, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to the Comoros archipelago.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
