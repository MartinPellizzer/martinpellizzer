Aulomyrcia coumete

Aulomyrcia coumete, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and help it retain moisture in its humid, tropical habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

Aulomyrcia coumete is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and ecological benefits.
