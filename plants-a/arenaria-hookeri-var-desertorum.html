Arenaria hookeri var. desertorum

Arenaria hookeri var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It thrives in sandy and gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Its compact growth habit and ability to withstand drought make it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy inflorescences and succulent-like leaves.
