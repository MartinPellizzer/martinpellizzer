Aphelandra formosa var. polystachya

Aphelandra formosa var. polystachya, commonly known as the Polystachya flameleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting orange to red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant features dark green, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that provide a striking contrast to its colorful inflorescences. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

This ornamental plant is popular in indoor gardening for its dramatic appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
