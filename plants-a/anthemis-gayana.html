Anthemis gayana

Anthemis gayana, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent.
