Ardisia solanoides

Ardisia solanoides, commonly known as the glossy woodbine, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. Its botanical characteristics include a sympodial growth habit and the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves.

Ardisia solanoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
