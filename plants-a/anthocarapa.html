Anthocarapa

Anthocarapa, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Anthocarapa is typically found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in subtropical climates.
