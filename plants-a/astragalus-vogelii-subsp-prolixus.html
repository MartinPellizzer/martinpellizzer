Astragalus vogelii subsp. prolixus

Astragalus vogelii subsp.

prolixus, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring, which are typically clustered in racemes. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Astragalus vogelii subsp.

prolixus is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
