Allmania nodiflora var. roxburghii

Allmania nodiflora var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Roxburgh's allmania, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
