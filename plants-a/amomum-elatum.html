Amomum elatum

Amomum elatum, commonly known as the large cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, pale green to yellowish flowers, followed by dark brown, three-lobed fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its highly valued spice.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
