Arabis alpestris

Arabis alpestris, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, typically growing in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Arabis alpestris is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
