Andropogon shimadae

Andropogon shimadae, commonly known as Shimada's grass, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as riverbanks and wetlands.
