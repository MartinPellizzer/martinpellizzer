Acacia nilotica subsp. tomentosa

Acacia nilotica subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which appear in compact globular clusters.

The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened, modified leaf stems that perform the function of leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has traditional uses in various cultural practices.
