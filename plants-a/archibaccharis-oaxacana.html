Archibaccharis oaxacana

Archibaccharis oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca baccharis, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry to moist forest understories and is well adapted to the humid climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and it produces pale yellow to white flowers that attract various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
