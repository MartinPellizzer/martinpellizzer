Arrhenatherum elatius var. coloratum

Arrhenatherum elatius var. coloratum, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and inflorescence that consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and the inflorescence displays a striking purple hue, which gives it its common name.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and supports a variety of insect life.
