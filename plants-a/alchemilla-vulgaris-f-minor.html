Alchemilla vulgaris f. minor

Alchemilla vulgaris f. minor, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming habit.

It features broad, wavy, silver-green leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
