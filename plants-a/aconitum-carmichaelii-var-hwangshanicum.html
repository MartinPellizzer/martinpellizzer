Aconitum carmichaelii var. hwangshanicum

Aconitum carmichaelii var. hwangshanicum, commonly known as the Hwangshan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense racemes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat fern-like appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
