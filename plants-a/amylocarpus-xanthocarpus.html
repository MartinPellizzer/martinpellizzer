Amylocarpus xanthocarpus

Amylocarpus xanthocarpus, commonly known as the yellow fruit arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking yellowish-orange spadix and spathe. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include broad, heart-shaped leaves and a rhizomatous root system.

Amylocarpus xanthocarpus is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
