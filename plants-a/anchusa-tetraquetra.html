Anchusa tetraquetra

Anchusa tetraquetra, commonly known as the blueweed or four-angled anchusa, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Anchusa tetraquetra is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
