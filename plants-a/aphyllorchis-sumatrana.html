Aphyllorchis sumatrana

Aphyllorchis sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran leafless orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Sumatra.

It is characterized by its lack of visible leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its green stems. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adapted for specific pollinators. Due to habitat destruction, it is critically endangered and found only in a few isolated locations.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
