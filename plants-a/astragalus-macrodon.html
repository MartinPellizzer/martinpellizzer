Astragalus macrodon

Astragalus macrodon, commonly known as the large-headed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is extensive and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Astragalus macrodon plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinator populations.
