Aplanodes sisymbrioides

Aplanodes sisymbrioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf mustards, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the mustard family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and agricultural weed management.
