Andropogon candidus

Andropogon candidus, commonly known as white bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grayish-green leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ornamental value has led to its use in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
