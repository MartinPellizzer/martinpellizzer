Actinostachys subtrijuga

Actinostachys subtrijuga, commonly known as the three-lobed spider-fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are arranged in a unique, spiral pattern along the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
