Agoseris vicinalis

Agoseris vicinalis, commonly known as the western sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, showy yellow flowers.

Agoseris vicinalis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.
