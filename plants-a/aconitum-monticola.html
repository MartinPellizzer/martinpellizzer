Aconitum monticola

Aconitum monticola, commonly known as the mountain aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spires of flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Aconitum monticola is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids such as aconitine, which can be harmful if ingested.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.
