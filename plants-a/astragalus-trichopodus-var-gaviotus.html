Astragalus trichopodus var. gaviotus

Astragalus trichopodus var. gaviotus, commonly known as the Gaviotus milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
