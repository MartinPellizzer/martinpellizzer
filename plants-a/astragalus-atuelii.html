Astragalus atuelii

Astragalus atuelii, commonly known as the Atuel milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its root system is often deep and robust, enabling it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus atuelii plays a role in stabilizing soils and may have potential ecological or medicinal significance.
