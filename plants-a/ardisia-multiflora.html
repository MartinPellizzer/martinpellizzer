Ardisia multiflora

Ardisia multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered evergreen shrub, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
