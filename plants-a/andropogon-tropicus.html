Andropogon tropicus

Andropogon tropicus, commonly known as the tropical grass or giant bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the warm climates of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Andropogon tropicus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
