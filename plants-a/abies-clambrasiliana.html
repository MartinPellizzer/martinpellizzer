Abies clambrasiliana

Abies clambrasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The needles are flat, shiny, and have a slight sheen, while the cones are typically pendulous and woody. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to mountainous climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the cloud forest ecosystems in its native range.
