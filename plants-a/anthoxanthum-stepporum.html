Anthoxanthum stepporum

Anthoxanthum stepporum, commonly known as steppe yellowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and steppes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish to golden spikelets in inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its resilience to drought conditions.

Anthoxanthum stepporum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
