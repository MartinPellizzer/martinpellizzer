Adiantum deflectens

Adiantum deflectens, commonly known as the shield fern, is a delicate and graceful fern species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas with rich, humusy soil. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Adiantum deflectens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
