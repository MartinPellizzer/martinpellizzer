Astragalus mongutensis

Astragalus mongutensis, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
