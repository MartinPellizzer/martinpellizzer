Avena nudibrevis

Avena nudibrevis, commonly known as the slender oat, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Avena nudibrevis is primarily valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
