Ampelopsis quinquefolia var. hirsuta

Ampelopsis quinquefolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy poison ivy, is a variant of the common poison ivy plant.

It is a deciduous vine native to the eastern and central United States. The plant features compound leaves with three to five serrated leaflets, often covered in fine, hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries.

This variant is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation in sensitive individuals.
