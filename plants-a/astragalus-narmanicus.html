Astragalus narmanicus

Astragalus narmanicus, commonly known as Narman milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus narmanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
