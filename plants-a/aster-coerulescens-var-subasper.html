Aster coerulescens var. subasper

Aster coerulescens var. subasper, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, making it a valuable addition to pollinator-friendly landscapes.
