Astragalus preussii var. sulcatus

Astragalus preussii var. sulcatus, commonly known as the Preuss's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
