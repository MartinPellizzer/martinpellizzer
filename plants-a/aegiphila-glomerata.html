Aegiphila glomerata

Aegiphila glomerata, commonly known as the clustered aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and violet. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Aegiphila glomerata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
