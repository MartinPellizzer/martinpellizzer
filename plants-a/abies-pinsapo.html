Abies pinsapo

Abies pinsapo, commonly known as the Spanish fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Abies pinsapo plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to forest ecosystems.
