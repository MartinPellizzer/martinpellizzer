Acer pseudoplatanus f. vitifolium

Acer pseudoplatanus f. vitifolium, commonly known as the variegated maple, is a unique cultivar of the common maple.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically growing to a moderate height with a rounded canopy. Its foliage changes color in the autumn, adding to its ornamental value in gardens.

The variegation is due to a genetic mutation that affects chlorophyll production, resulting in a distinctive appearance.
