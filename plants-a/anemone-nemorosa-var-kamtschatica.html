Anemone nemorosa var. kamtschatica

Anemone nemorosa var. kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamtschatake anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features a short, rhizomatous rootstock and produces basal leaves that are lanceolate and often hairy. The plant blooms with showy, white to pinkish flowers arranged in a cluster, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
