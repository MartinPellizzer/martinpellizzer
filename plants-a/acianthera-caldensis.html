Acianthera caldensis

Acianthera caldensis, commonly known as the Calden's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has slender, upright stems and is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic. Its leaves are elongated and often clustered at the base, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Acianthera caldensis is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
