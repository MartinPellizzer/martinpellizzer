Ascium adamantium

Ascium adamantium, commonly known as the crystal spore orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, gemstone-like spores that form on its slender, curling stems. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a waxy, silver sheen that reflects light like polished metal. Its flowering period occurs during the brief summer months, when it produces delicate, pale-blue blossoms.

Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, Ascium adamantium is considered a botanical marvel and a subject of scientific fascination.
