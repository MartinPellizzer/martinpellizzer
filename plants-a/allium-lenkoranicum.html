Allium lenkoranicum

Allium lenkoranicum, commonly known as the Caucasian leek, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Allium lenkoranicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
