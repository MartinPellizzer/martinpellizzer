Alchemilla circassica

Alchemilla circassica, commonly known as the Circassian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla circassica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
