Achillea anthemoides

Achillea anthemoides, commonly known as field fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
