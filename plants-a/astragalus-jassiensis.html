Astragalus jassiensis

Astragalus jassiensis, commonly known as the Jassian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitats.
