Astragalus cinereus var. radificlorus

Astragalus cinereus var. radificlorus, commonly known as the gray milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in globular clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is extensive, often forming dense colonies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is typically found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
