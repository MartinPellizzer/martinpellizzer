Atriplex angustifolia var. succulenta

Atriplex angustifolia var. succulenta, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, succulent leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it access deep groundwater, making it drought-tolerant. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
