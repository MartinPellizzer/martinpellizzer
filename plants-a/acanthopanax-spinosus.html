Acanthopanax spinosus

Acanthopanax spinosus, commonly known as the spiny devil's ivy, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain regions.
