Atractylodes lancea var. chinensis

Atractylodes lancea var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese Atractylodes, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence. Its rhizomes are the primary part used in traditional medicine, valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in mountainous regions.
