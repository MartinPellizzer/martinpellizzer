Aucuba japonica f. luteocarpa

Aucuba japonica f. luteocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green foliage.

It produces small, yellowish fruits that are highly ornamental and persist through the winter, adding visual interest to the landscape. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its hardiness and ability to thrive in shaded environments. It has a slow growth rate and is valued for its adaptability to various soil types.

The varietal form 'luteocarpa' is distinguished by its unique yellow fruiting bodies, setting it apart from the typical green-fruited varieties.
