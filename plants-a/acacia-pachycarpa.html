Acacia pachycarpa

Acacia pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and distinctive, flat, green thorns. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Acacia pachycarpa thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
