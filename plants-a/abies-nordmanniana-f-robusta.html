Abies nordmanniana f. robusta

Abies nordmanniana f. robusta, commonly known as the Caucasian fir, is a subspecies of the Nordmann fir native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dense foliage, and dark green needles that are slightly twisted. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
