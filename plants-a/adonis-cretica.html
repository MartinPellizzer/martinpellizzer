Adonis cretica

Adonis cretica, commonly known as the Cretan glory flower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, leathery leaves and produces striking yellow flowers with prominent yellow anthers. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Adonis cretica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid conditions.
