Allagopappus dichotomus subsp. latifolius

Allagopappus dichotomus subsp.

latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing perennial herb with woody stems that often branch at the base.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy surface, and it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
