Astragalus amblolepis

Astragalus amblolepis, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and open woodlands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus amblolepis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
