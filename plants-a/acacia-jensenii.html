Acacia jensenii

Acacia jensenii, commonly known as Jensen's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

Acacia jensenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
