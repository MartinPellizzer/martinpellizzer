Attalea sagotii

Attalea sagotii, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that develop into dark purple or black fruits, each containing a single seed. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, making it an important component of these ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
