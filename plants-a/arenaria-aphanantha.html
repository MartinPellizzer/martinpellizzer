Arenaria aphanantha

Arenaria aphanantha, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers, typically blooming in early spring. Its succulent nature makes it well adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
