Adiantum moranii

Adiantum moranii, commonly known as the Mexican maidenhair fern, is a species native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a feather. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees and rocks without deriving nutrients from them. Its fronds are typically bright green and turn bronze with age, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Adiantum moranii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical regions.

Morphology

Adiantum moranii has fronds that are typically triangular to lanceolate in shape, with a bipinnate structure, and arranged in a pinnate pattern along the stem.

The leaves are usually bright green, with a delicate, feathery appearance, and can reach lengths of up to 30 cm. The stem is slender and upright, with a smooth, pale green bark that grows in a climbing or trailing pattern. Adiantum moranii produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures rather than true flowers, and it relies on wind pollination.
