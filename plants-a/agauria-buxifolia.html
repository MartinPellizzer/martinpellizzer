Agauria buxifolia

Agauria buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf agauria, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Agauria buxifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil conditions.
