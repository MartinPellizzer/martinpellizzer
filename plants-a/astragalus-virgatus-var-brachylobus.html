Astragalus virgatus var. brachylobus

Astragalus virgatus var. brachylobus, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are palmately divided into three to five lobes, and the seed pods are slender and curved.

This variety is characterized by its shorter stems compared to the typical form of Astragalus virgatus.
