Actinodaphne malaccensis

Actinodaphne malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca camphor tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers, which are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
