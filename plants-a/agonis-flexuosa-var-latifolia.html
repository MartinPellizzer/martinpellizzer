Agonis flexuosa var. latifolia

Agonis flexuosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved white gum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia, where it typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It is a shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 6 meters.

The bark is smooth and greyish, becoming fissured with age, and the plant is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
