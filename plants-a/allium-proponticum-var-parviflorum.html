Allium proponticum var. parviflorum

Allium proponticum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flower garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are often used as ornamental plants in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in landscape design.
