Acridocarpus chloropterus

Acridocarpus chloropterus, commonly known as the green sandalwood tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
