Aristolochia odorata

Aristolochia odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from green to purple. The plant produces small, dark brown berries that contain the seeds. It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is known for its complex chemical composition, including various alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions, though it can be toxic if ingested.
