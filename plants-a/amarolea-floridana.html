Amarolea floridana

Amarolea floridana, commonly known as the Florida amarolea, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of Florida.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Amarolea floridana is often found in marshy areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
