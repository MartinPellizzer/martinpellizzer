Anisopappus abercornensis subsp. anemonifolius

Anisopappus abercornensis subsp.

anemonifolius, commonly known as the Abercorn aster, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. It is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and prairies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its foliage resembling that of an anemone, giving it its common name.
