Aria ligustrifolia

Aria ligustrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark purple berries. Aria ligustrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.
