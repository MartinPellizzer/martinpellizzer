Anthyllis rupestris var. micrantha

Anthyllis rupestris var. micrantha, commonly known as the rock anthyllis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, evergreen leaves and clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
