Anthopterus ericae

Anthopterus ericae, commonly known as the heath buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Anthopterus ericae is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.
