Adupla

Adupla, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Adupla is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
