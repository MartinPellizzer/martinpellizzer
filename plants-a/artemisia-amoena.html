Artemisia amoena

Artemisia amoena, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of the active ingredient in antimalarial drugs.
