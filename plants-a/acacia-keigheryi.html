Acacia keigheryi

Acacia keigheryi, commonly known as the Keighery's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant produces flat, elongated pods that contain seeds, typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
