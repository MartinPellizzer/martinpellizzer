Aeschynomene campicola

Aeschynomene campicola, commonly known as the hairy prostrate aeschynomene, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically ovate with hairy surfaces. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Aeschynomene campicola is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
