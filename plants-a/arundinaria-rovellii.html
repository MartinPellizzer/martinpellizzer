Arundinaria rovellii

Arundinaria rovellii, commonly known as the Mexican bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Arundinaria rovellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
