Angraecum curvatum

Angraecum curvatum, commonly known as the twisted angraecum, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescence and distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific moths, showcasing an example of co-evolution in nature.

Angraecum curvatum is a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and ecological significance.
