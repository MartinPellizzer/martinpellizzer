Asarum taiwanense

Asarum taiwanense, commonly known as Taiwan wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Taiwan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are pollinated by flies, and the plant produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
