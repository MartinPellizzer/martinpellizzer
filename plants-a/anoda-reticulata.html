Anoda reticulata

Anoda reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, which is a notable botanical feature. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Anoda reticulata is also known for its small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.
