Arundinaria callosa

Arundinaria callosa, commonly known as the Callosa bamboo, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass that grows in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 10 feet. The plant is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems covered in a thick, waxy coating. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green hue and a rough texture.

Arundinaria callosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
