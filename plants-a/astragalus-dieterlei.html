Astragalus dieterlei

Astragalus dieterlei, commonly known as the Dieterlei milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of purple or pink. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus dieterlei is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.
