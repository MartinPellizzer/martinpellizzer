Abelmoschus cruentus

Abelmoschus cruentus, commonly known as red okra, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain edible, mucilaginous seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season.

Red okra is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
