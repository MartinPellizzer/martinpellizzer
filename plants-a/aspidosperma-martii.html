Aspidosperma martii

Aspidosperma martii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cupania martii*, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
