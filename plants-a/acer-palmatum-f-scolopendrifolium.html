Acer palmatum f. scolopendrifolium

Acer palmatum f. scolopendrifolium, commonly known as the Japanese maple with zigzag leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated, zigzag-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, and it can grow to a height of 6 to 10 feet.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for adding visual interest to ornamental horticulture.
