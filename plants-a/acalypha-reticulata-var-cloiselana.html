Acalypha reticulata var. cloiselana

Acalypha reticulata var. cloiselana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or cloisel's acalypha, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges from deep green to reddish hues, providing a rich visual texture. The plant features showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. Its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its tactile appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
