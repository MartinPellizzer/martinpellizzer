Antennaria neodioica var. typica

Antennaria neodioica var. typica, commonly known as the sticky cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils.

Antennaria neodioica var. typica is often found in open habitats such as dunes, meadows, and disturbed areas.
