Agoseris turbinata

Agoseris turbinata, commonly known as the Turbanhead Goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Agoseris turbinata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
