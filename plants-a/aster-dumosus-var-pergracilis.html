Aster dumosus var. pergracilis

Aster dumosus var. pergracilis, commonly known as the slender dwarf aster, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a compact, mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
