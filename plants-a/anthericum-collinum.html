Anthericum collinum

Anthericum collinum, commonly known as the mountain arum, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Anthericum collinum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
