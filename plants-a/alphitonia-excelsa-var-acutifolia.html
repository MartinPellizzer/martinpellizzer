Alphitonia excelsa var. acutifolia

Alphitonia excelsa var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alphitonia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible, round fruits that are consumed by various animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
