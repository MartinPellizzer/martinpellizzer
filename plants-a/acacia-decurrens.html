Acacia decurrens

Acacia decurrens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices.

Acacia decurrens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
