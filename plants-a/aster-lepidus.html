Aster lepidus

Aster lepidus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple petals surrounding a yellow central disk, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped and hairy. Aster lepidus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
