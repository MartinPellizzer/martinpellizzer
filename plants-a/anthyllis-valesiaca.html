Anthyllis valesiaca

Anthyllis valesiaca, commonly known as the Valesiaca broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought and saline conditions.

Anthyllis valesiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
