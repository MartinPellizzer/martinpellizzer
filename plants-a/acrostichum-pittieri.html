Acrostichum pittieri

Acrostichum pittieri, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, lance-shaped fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The fronds are usually evergreen, with a glossy surface and a distinctive, finely divided structure.
