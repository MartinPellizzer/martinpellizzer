Astragalus gliciphyllos

Astragalus gliciphyllos, commonly known as the sweetleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is native to the western United States, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

The plant also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
