Acosta tymphaea

Acosta tymphaea, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its flowers open in the early morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Acosta tymphaea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
