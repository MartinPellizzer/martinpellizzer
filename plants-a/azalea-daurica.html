Azalea daurica

Azalea daurica, commonly known as the Daurian azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers acidic, well-drained soil. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Azalea daurica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
