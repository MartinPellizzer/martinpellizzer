Acalypha villicaulis

Acalypha villicaulis, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Acalypha villicaulis is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some cultures.
