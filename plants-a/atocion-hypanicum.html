Atocion hypanicum

Atocion hypanicum, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The botanical structure of Atocion hypanicum includes strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.
