Arabis ciliata subsp. serpyllifolia

Arabis ciliata subsp.

serpyllifolia, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It features rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
