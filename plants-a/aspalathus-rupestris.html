Aspalathus rupestris

Aspalathus rupestris, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a lush green appearance throughout the year.

Aspalathus rupestris is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
