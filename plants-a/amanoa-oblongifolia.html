Amanoa oblongifolia

Amanoa oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf naia, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as stream banks and moist forests. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.
