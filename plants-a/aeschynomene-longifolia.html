Aeschynomene longifolia

Aeschynomene longifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf aeschynomene, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by slender, pod-like fruits. Aeschynomene longifolia is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
