Aster filaginifolius

Aster filaginifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive silvery-blue underside.

It blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
