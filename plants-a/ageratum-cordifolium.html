Ageratum cordifolium

Ageratum cordifolium, commonly known as heartleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a companion plant in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum cordifolium is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like butterflies and bees.
