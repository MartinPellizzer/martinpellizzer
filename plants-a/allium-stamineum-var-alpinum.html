Allium stamineum var. alpinum

Allium stamineum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are slightly hairy and emit a mild garlic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, spherical umbels of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a subtle garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous habitats.
