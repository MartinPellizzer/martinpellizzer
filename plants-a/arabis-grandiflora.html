Arabis grandiflora

Arabis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arabis grandiflora is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
