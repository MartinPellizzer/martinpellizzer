Acrostichum sporadolepis

Acrostichum sporadolepis, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Acrostichum sporadolepis plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small animals.
