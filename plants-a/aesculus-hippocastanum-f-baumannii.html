Aesculus hippocastanum f. baumannii

Aesculus hippocastanum f. baumannii, commonly known as the Baumann's horse chestnut, is a variant of the common horse chestnut.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral and foliar features, including larger leaves and showy, red-brown flowers. The plant is native to the Balkan Peninsula and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It produces large, spiny fruits containing toxic seeds, which require careful handling.

The variety is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
