Angraecum bistortum

Angraecum bistortum, commonly known as the twisted angraecum, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its intricate, spiraled inflorescence and large, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is adapted to grow in rocky or limestone habitats. Its unique morphology and pollination strategy make it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.
