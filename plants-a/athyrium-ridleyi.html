Athyrium ridleyi

Athyrium ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridge's lady fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or pendulous. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium ridleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
