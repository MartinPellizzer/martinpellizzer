Aristida dichotoma var. curtissii

Aristida dichotoma var. curtissii, commonly known as the curly hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like blades that often curl at the tips. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
