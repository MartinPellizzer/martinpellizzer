Asplenium laciniatum subsp. fraser-jenkinsii

Asplenium laciniatum subsp.

fraser-jenkinsii, commonly known as the Fraser-Jenkins' spleenwort, is a member of the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its fern-like fronds. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rock crevices or on tree trunks.

The plant has a compact, rosette-like growth habit with finely divided, serrated leaves. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sorus structures on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various microhabitats and its unique morphological features.
