Allium hymettium

Allium hymettium, commonly known as the Hymettus garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulb composed of several fleshy, white, ovoid segments surrounded by a papery outer layer. The plant produces tall, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in the summer. Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the island of Hymettus in Greece.

It is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
