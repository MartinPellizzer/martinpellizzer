Achillea tournefortii

Achillea tournefortii, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems and basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads composed of small white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and long flowering period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
