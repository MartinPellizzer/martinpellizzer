Acrostichum grande

Acrostichum grande, commonly known as the giant swamp fern, is a tropical plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is a large, evergreen fern that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with long, arching fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist soil. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and its leaves are sometimes used as thatch or for ornamental purposes.

The plant's botanical structure includes a central stem with multiple branching fronds, each bearing small, lance-shaped leaves.
