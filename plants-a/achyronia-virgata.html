Achyronia virgata

Achyronia virgata, commonly known as the violet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
