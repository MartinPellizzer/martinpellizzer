Aerva leucura var. lanatoides

Aerva leucura var. lanatoides, commonly known as the woolly white aerva, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
