Andropogon madagascariensis

Andropogon madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its robust growth and narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used for erosion control.
