Areca horrida

Areca horrida, commonly known as the bristle areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruits are clustered in panicles and contain a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine.

Areca horrida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in local herbal remedies.
