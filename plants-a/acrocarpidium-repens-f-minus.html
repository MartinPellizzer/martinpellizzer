Acrocarpidium repens f. minus

Acrocarpidium repens f. minus, commonly known as the dwarf creeping acrocarpidium, is a rare and low-growing fern-like plant characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit.

It features delicate, lanceolate leaves that are typically shorter and narrower than those of the typical species, contributing to its distinct appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous sporangia along the margins of its leaves, typical of the fern family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas with high humidity.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
