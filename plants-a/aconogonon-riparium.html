Aconogonon riparium

Aconogonon riparium, commonly known as water spinach or river spinach, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found along riverbanks and in wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Aconogonon riparium is valued for its edible leaves and is commonly used in traditional cuisines.
