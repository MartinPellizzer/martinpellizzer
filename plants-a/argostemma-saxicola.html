Argostemma saxicola

Argostemma saxicola, commonly known as the rock argostemma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
