Astragalus khasianus

Astragalus khasianus, commonly known as Khasi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky and arid environments, thriving in high-altitude regions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
