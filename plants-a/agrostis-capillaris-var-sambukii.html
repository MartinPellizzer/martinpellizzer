Agrostis capillaris var. sambukii

Agrostis capillaris var. sambukii, commonly known as Sambuk grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
