Acer negundo f. fructu-rubro

Acer negundo f. fructu-rubro, commonly known as the red-fruited boxelder, is a cultivar of the boxelder tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-colored seeds, which set it apart from the typical green seeds of the species. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
