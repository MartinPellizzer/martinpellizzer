Amomum repens

Amomum repens, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers followed by dark red, three-lobed berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinct, fragrant aroma.

Amomum repens is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
