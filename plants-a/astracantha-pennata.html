Astracantha pennata

Astracantha pennata, commonly known as the feathered burr.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its unique, spiny seed pods that resemble a feather. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the distinctive, wind-dispersed burrs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The botanical features of Astracantha pennata make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
