Agropyron repens f. pectinatum

Agropyron repens f. pectinatum, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a variant of the common wheatgrass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant typically forms low, mat-like colonies, making it well-suited for erosion control and ground cover. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and pastures.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.
