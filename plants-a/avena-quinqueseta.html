Avena quinqueseta

Avena quinqueseta, commonly known as five-leaf barley, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact stature, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Avena quinqueseta is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
