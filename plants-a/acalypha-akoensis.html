Acalypha akoensis

Acalypha akoensis, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, spiky seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha akoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
