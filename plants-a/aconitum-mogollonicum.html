Aconitum mogollonicum

Aconitum mogollonicum, commonly known as the Mogollon monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a deep green hue and a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
