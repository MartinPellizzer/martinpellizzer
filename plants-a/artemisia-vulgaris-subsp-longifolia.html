Artemisia vulgaris subsp. longifolia

Artemisia vulgaris subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flower heads that appear in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with a strong, camphor-like odor due to the presence of essential oils.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family and is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
