Acrostichum neglectum

Acrostichum neglectum, commonly known as the common brake fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, triangular fronds that are typically dark green and have a rugged texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Acrostichum neglectum plays an important role in its ecosystem by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various small organisms.
