Achras olivacea

Achras olivacea, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, canoe-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that is rich in carbohydrates. The plant has a short trunk with smooth, gray bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit develops from the flowering shoots.

The tree is widely cultivated for its nutritious fruit, which is a staple food in many island communities.
