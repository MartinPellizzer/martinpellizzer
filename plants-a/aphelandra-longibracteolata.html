Aphelandra longibracteolata

Aphelandra longibracteolata, commonly known as the long-bract heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated bracts that resemble leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, fragrant flowers, typically in shades of yellow or orange. Its foliage is broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Aphelandra longibracteolata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
