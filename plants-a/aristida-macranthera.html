Aristida macranthera

Aristida macranthera, commonly known as the tall muhly, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces fluffy, pinkish-white inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in xeriscaped gardens and as a ornamental plant.

Aristida macranthera is also valued for its ability to stabilize soils and prevent erosion in arid environments.
