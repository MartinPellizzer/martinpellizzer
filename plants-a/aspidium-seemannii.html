Aspidium seemannii

Aspidium seemannii, commonly known as the tropical water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are usually creeping and can form dense mats, contributing to its ability to colonize wetlands and stream banks.

Aspidium seemannii plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
