Amorpha fruticosa var. coerulea

Amorpha fruticosa var. coerulea, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces striking blue to violet pea-like flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and can grow up to 6 feet tall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flower color and its ability to attract pollinators.
