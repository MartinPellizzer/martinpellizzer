Abies dumosa

Abies dumosa, commonly known as the dwarf fir, is a small evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters, featuring a compact, rounded crown with dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are needle-like, short, and arranged in spirals along the branches, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds dispersed by wind.

This plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows.
