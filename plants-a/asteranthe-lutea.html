Asteranthe lutea

Asteranthe lutea, commonly known as the yellow asteranthe, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Asteranthe lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be propagated through stem cuttings or seeds.
