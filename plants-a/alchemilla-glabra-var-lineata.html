Alchemilla glabra var. lineata

Alchemilla glabra var. lineata, commonly known as the linear-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are borne on slender, branched stalks. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal remedies for their astringent and soothing properties.
