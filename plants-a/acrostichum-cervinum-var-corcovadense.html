Acrostichum cervinum var. corcovadense

Acrostichum cervinum var. corcovadense, commonly known as the golden fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Central and South America. The plant has feather-like fronds that are typically golden to bronze in color, distinguishing it from its green-leaved relatives. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its unique coloration and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental water gardens.
