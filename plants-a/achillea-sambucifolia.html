Achillea sambucifolia

Achillea sambucifolia, commonly known as the wood avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fern-like, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to pale green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
