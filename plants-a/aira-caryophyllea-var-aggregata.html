Aira caryophyllea var. aggregata

Aira caryophyllea var. aggregata, commonly known as the clustered false oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its compact growth habit and dense foliage make it an attractive ornamental plant.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous root system and ability to form clumps.
