Astragalus aragonensis

Astragalus aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus aragonensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
