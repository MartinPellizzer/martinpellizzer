Acer morifolium

Acer morifolium, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall. The tree typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens and parks.
