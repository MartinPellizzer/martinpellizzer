Armeria halleri subsp. muelleri

Armeria halleri subsp.

muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features compact, evergreen foliage with dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces a basal rosette of linear, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
