Achillea ptarmica subsp. macrocephala

Achillea ptarmica subsp.

macrocephala, commonly known as the Great Masterwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, flat-topped clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom from June to August.

This subspecies is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
