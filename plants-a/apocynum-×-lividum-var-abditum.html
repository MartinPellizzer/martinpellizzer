Apocynum × lividum var. abditum

Apocynum × lividum var. abditum, commonly known as the purple milkweed, is a hybrid plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically clustered in umbels. The plant has velvety, hairy stems and leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with toothed margins. It produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, making it toxic to livestock and humans.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
