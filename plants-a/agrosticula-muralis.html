Agrosticula muralis

Agrosticula muralis, commonly known as the wall grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions.

It typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, often found in disturbed or urban environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in marginal habitats.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
