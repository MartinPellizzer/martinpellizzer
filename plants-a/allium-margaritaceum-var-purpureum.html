Allium margaritaceum var. purpureum

Allium margaritaceum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple globe onion, is a cultivated variety of the Allium genus characterized by its distinctive purple-tinted bulbs.

This plant is native to Central Asia and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible bulbs. The leaves are grass-like and bluish-green, while the bulbs are round and typically range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are small, white or pink, and form a dense cluster at the top of the plant.
