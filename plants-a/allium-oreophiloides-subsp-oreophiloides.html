Allium oreophiloides subsp. oreophiloides

Allium oreophiloides subsp.

oreophiloides, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the Mediterranean. It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of white or pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It has fibrous roots and is adapted to cold, dry environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
