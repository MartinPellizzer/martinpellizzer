Azalea indica var. floribunda

Azalea indica var. floribunda, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
