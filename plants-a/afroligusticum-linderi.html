Afroligusticum linderi

Afroligusticum linderi, commonly known as the African yellow wood, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to eastern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed.

Afroligusticum linderi is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
