Anemonastrum trullifolium

Anemonastrum trullifolium, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky, pinkish-white flowers that resemble small candles. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered fruits that disperse its seeds.

The fairy duster is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
