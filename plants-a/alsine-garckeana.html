Alsine garckeana

Alsine garckeana, commonly known as the slender sand-verbena, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Alsine garckeana is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is an important species for stabilizing sandy soils.
