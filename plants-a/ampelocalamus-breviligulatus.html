Ampelocalamus breviligulatus

Ampelocalamus breviligulatus, commonly known as the short-ligule reed, is a species of grass native to the wetlands of southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in marshy or aquatic environments, thriving in moist to submerged soil conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often partially submerged.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
