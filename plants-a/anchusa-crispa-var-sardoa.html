Anchusa crispa var. sardoa

Anchusa crispa var. sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian Anchusa, is a perennial plant native to the island of Sardinia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to violet flower clusters that bloom in summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to Mediterranean flora collections.
