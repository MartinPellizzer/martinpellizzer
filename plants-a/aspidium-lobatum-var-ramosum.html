Aspidium lobatum var. ramosum

Aspidium lobatum var. ramosum, commonly known as the branched water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Aspidium.

It is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically submerged or floating. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it can anchor itself to substrates with its rhizomes. Its leaves are usually divided into small, lance-shaped segments, contributing to its intricate appearance.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
