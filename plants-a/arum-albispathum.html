Arum albispathum

Arum albispathum, commonly known as the white-veined arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves with prominent white veins against a dark green background. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Arum albispathum is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
