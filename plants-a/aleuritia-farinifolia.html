Aleuritia farinifolia

Aleuritia farinifolia, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Aleuritia farinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various wildlife species.
