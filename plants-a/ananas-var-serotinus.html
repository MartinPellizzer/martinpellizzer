Ananas var. serotinus

Ananas var. serotinus, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiky foliage and fragrant, edible fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.
