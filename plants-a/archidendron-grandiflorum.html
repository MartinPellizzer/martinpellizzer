Archidendron grandiflorum

Archidendron grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered legume, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. Native to regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
