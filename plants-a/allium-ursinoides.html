Allium ursinoides

Allium ursinoides, commonly known as the bear's garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically covered in a fine, white coating. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a pungent, garlic-like flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

However, it is important to distinguish it from other Allium species due to its unique botanical features and potential toxicity in certain forms.
