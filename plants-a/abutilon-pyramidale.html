Abutilon pyramidale

Abutilon pyramidale, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Abutilon pyramidale is also known for its adaptability to various growing conditions.
