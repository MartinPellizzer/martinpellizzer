Asterochlaena morongii

Asterochlaena morongii, commonly known as the Morong's aster, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
