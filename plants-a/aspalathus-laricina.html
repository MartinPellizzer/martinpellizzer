Aspalathus laricina

Aspalathus laricina, commonly known as the rooibos plant, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces tiny yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat. Its most notable feature is the unique red-brown color of its leaves, which gives it its common name.

Aspalathus laricina is renowned for its use in producing the popular herbal tea known as rooibos.
