Aptosimum welwitschii

Aptosimum welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Aptosimum welwitschii is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a slow growth rate.
