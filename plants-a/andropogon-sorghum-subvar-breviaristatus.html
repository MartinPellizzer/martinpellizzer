Andropogon sorghum subvar. breviaristatus

Andropogon sorghum subvar. breviaristatus, commonly known as dwarf broomcorn millet, is a grass species native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter inflorescence compared to other sorghum varieties. The plant typically produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and potential use in both forage and grain production.
