Anerincleistus setulosus

Anerincleistus setulosus, commonly known as the woolly leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
