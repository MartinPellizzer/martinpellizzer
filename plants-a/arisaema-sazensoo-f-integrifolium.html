Arisaema sazensoo f. integrifolium

Arisaema sazensoo f. integrifolium, commonly known as the Japanese umbrella plant, is a variant of the Arisaema genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of a spadix enclosed within a leaf-like bract. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth margin.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
