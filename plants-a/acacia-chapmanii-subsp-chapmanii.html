Acacia chapmanii subsp. chapmanii

Acacia chapmanii subsp.

chapmanii, commonly known as the Chapman's wattle, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is often used in rehabilitation projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
