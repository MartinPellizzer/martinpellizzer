Anacyclus triumfetti

Anacyclus triumfetti, commonly known as the "triumph flower," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers. The plant has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Anacyclus triumfetti is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
