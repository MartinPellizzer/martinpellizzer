Astrantia ranunculifolia

Astrantia ranunculifolia, commonly known as the meadow astrantia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with the upper leaves being more narrow. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, resembling those of a bell. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer and is often found in moist meadows and woodlands.

Astrantia ranunculifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
