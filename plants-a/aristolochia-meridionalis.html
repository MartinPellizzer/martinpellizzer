Aristolochia meridionalis

Aristolochia meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern Dutchman's Pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, often foul-smelling blossoms that attract specific pollinators. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 15 centimeters in diameter.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
