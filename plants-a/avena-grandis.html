Avena grandis

Avena grandis, commonly known as the giant oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing small, oval-shaped seeds. Its leaves are long and narrow with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

Avena grandis is often cultivated for forage and has also been used in traditional medicine.
