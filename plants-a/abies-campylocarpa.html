Abies campylocarpa

Abies campylocarpa, commonly known as the dwarf white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, pyramidal shape and dense, needle-like foliage that is typically a dark green on top and whitish underneath. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its bark is smooth and grayish in appearance. Abies campylocarpa thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth rate, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and as a landscape tree.
