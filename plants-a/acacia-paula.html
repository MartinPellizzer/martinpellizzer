Acacia paula

Acacia paula, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, yellow flowers that bloom in globular clusters. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia paula thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
