Aristolochia lindneri

Aristolochia lindneri, commonly known as the Lindner's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often green or reddish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is typically found in moist, shaded habitats. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, sometimes spicy scent.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
