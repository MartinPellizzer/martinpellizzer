Anthurium paucinerve

Anthurium paucinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and prominent, dark veins. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Anthurium paucinerve is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
