Allium fragile

Allium fragile, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Allium fragile is also noted for its fragrant foliage, which contributes to its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
