Amarella theiantha var. livida

Amarella theiantha var. livida, commonly known as the dark-leaved amarella, is a rare and striking variant of the amarella plant.

It is characterized by its deep, almost black foliage, which contrasts sharply with its usual green leaves. This variety thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its unique coloration makes it a sought-after species for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
