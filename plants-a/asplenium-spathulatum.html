Asplenium spathulatum

Asplenium spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. Asplenium spathulatum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
