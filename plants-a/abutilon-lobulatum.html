Abutilon lobulatum

Abutilon lobulatum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green foliage and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its use in both landscape design and horticultural collections.
