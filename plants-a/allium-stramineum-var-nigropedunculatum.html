Allium stramineum var. nigropedunculatum

Allium stramineum var. nigropedunculatum, commonly known as the black-stemmed leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in a loose umbel. The plant's bulb is typically white or pale yellow, with a mild garlic-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary use in traditional dishes.
