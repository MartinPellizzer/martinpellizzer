Adenostachya parvifolia

Adenostachya parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved grassvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Adenostachya parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
