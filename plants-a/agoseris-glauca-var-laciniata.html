Agoseris glauca var. laciniata

Agoseris glauca var. laciniata, commonly known as the blueweed or narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a mat-forming growth habit. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is notable for its aromatic foliage and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
