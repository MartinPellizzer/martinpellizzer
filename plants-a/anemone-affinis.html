Anemone affinis

Anemone affinis, commonly known as the common anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with a central cluster of stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Anemone affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
