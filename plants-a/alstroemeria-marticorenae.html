Alstroemeria marticorenae

Alstroemeria marticorenae, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Alstroemeria marticorenae is also valued for its resilience to cold temperatures and its ability to thrive in full sun.
