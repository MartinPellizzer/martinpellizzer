Antigonon cinerascens

Antigonon cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a silvery or grayish hue due to their dense pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
