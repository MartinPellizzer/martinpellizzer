Anastrophus pulchellus

Anastrophus pulchellus, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical features of Anastrophus pulchellus make it a popular choice for both horticultural cultivation and botanical study.
