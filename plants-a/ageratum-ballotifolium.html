Ageratum ballotifolium

Ageratum ballotifolium, commonly known as the Mexican ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
