Arbutus spinulosa

Arbutus spinulosa, commonly known as the prickly pear or coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems and broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Arbutus spinulosa plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
