Atriplex lindleyi subsp. conduplicata

Atriplex lindleyi subsp.

conduplicata, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are adapted for wind pollination. Its salt tolerance allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity, making it a valuable species for stabilizing degraded lands.

The subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to prevent soil erosion.
