Anisopetala sanguinolenta

Anisopetala sanguinolenta, commonly known as the blood-red anisopetala, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, fragrant flowers with five petals.

It is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
