Astragalus leiosemius subsp. orientalis

Astragalus leiosemius subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in clusters. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
