Acacia sertiformis

Acacia sertiformis, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are arranged in clusters, typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in degraded landscapes.
