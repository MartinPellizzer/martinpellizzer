Abalon albiflorum var. sylvaticum

Abalon albiflorum var. sylvaticum, commonly known as the white-flowered abalon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elongated and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and often creeping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
