Astrantia diapensia

Astrantia diapensia, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are white to pale pink, with a central disc of yellow florets, and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Astrantia diapensia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
