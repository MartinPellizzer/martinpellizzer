Actaea spicata var. alba

Actaea spicata var. alba, commonly known as white bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers in late summer. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a silvery underside. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
