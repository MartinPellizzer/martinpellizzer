Astragalus figueroai

Astragalus figueroai, commonly known as the Figueroa's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep taproots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus figueroai plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
