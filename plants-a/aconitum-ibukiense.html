Aconitum ibukiense

Aconitum ibukiense, commonly known as the Ibuki Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
