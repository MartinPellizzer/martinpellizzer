Achillea aucheri subsp. aucheri

Achillea aucheri subsp.

aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces flat, composite flower heads in shades of white to pale pink, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a garden ornamental highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
