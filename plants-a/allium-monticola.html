Allium monticola

Allium monticola, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of strap-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a dense umbel, which bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in traditional cuisines.

Allium monticola is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
