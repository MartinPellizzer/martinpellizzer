Acinos diffusus

Acinos diffusus, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are typically divided into five narrow lobes. Acinos diffusus thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
