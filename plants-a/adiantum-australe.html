Adiantum australe

Adiantum australe, commonly known as the southern maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bipinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are dark green and have a distinctive, feathery appearance, with veins that are prominently visible.

Adiantum australe is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
