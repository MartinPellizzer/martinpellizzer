Alyssum praecox var. albiflorum

Alyssum praecox var. albiflorum, commonly known as white-flowered winter alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its botanical characteristics include a short stature, rapid growth, and a long flowering period.
