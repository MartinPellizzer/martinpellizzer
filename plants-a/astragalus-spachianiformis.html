Astragalus spachianiformis

Astragalus spachianiformis, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
