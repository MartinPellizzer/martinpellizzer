Artemisia vulgaris var. glabra

Artemisia vulgaris var. glabra, commonly known as common tarragon, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are used in culinary applications, particularly in the production of tarragon-infused vinegar and sauces.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
