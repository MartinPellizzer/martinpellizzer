Adonis flava var. subflammea

Adonis flava var. subflammea, commonly known as the flame yellow bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered with a fine pubescence.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
