Aconitum latemarense

Aconitum latemarense, commonly known as the Alpine Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble a hood, which is a distinctive feature of the genus Aconitum. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in controlled environments.
