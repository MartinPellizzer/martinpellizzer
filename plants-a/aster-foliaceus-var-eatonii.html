Aster foliaceus var. eatonii

Aster foliaceus var. eatonii, commonly known as the Eaton's leafy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, and its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
