Ailanthus philippinensis

Ailanthus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its rapid growth, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in other regions.
