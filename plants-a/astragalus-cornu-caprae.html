Astragalus cornu-caprae

Astragalus cornu-caprae, commonly known as the horned goat's rue, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils across the Mediterranean region. The plant features distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets. It produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus cornu-caprae is known for its hardy nature and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
