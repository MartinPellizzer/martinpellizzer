Aubrieta pinardi var. integrifolia

Aubrieta pinardi var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Almondleaf aubrieta, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive silvery-green color and entire margins. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
