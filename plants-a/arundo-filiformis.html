Arundo filiformis

Arundo filiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Arundo filiformis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
