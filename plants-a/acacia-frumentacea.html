Acacia frumentacea

Acacia frumentacea, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically followed by long, slender pods. Acacia frumentacea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
