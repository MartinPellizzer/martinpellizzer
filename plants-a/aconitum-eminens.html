Aconitum eminens

Aconitum eminens, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like hood, typical of the genus Aconitum. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat poisonous appearance.

Aconitum eminens thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
