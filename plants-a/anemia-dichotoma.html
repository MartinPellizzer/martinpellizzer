Anemia dichotoma

Anemia dichotoma, commonly known as the two-leaf plant, is a species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive bifurcated leaves, which split into two separate lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a shallow root system. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Anemia dichotoma make it an interesting subject for ecological and medicinal research.
