Astragalus pichleri var. glaberescens

Astragalus pichleri var. glaberescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with slender, glabrous leaflets.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
