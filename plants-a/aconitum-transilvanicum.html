Aconitum transilvanicum

Aconitum transilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves and grows up to 1.5 meters tall, thriving in moist, shaded environments. It contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.

Despite its toxicity, it is valued in gardens for its striking appearance and ecological importance.
