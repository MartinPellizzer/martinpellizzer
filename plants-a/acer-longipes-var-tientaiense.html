Acer longipes var. tientaiense

Acer longipes var. tientaiense, commonly known as the Tientai maple, is a variant of the longipes maple native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in traditional medicine.
