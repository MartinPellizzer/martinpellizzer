Antimima

Antimima, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that resemble the appearance of a ghostly apparition. The plant produces small, fragrant blooms that emit a subtle, sweet scent, especially during the night. Antimima thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable specimen in botanical studies.
