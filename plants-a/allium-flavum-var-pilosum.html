Allium flavum var. pilosum

Allium flavum var. pilosum, commonly known as the woolly garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, arranged in umbels. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium flavum var. pilosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
