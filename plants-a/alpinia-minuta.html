Alpinia minuta

Alpinia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Alpinia minuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to tropical gardens and botanical collections.
