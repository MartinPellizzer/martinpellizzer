Agave harrisii

Agave harrisii, commonly known as the Harris's agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. Agave harrisii is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.

Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and its sap can be processed into a type of agave syrup.
