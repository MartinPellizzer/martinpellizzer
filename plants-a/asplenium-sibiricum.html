Asplenium sibiricum

Asplenium sibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the feathers of a bird, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are wiry and often form dense colonies, allowing it to spread gradually.

Asplenium sibiricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
