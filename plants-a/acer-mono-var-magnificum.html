Acer mono var. magnificum

Acer mono var. magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent maple, is a variant of the mono maple species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic foliage.
