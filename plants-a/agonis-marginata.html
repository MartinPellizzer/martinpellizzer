Agonis marginata

Agonis marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, aromatic leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest regions.

Agonis marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
