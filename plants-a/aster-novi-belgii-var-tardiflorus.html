Aster novi-belgii var. tardiflorus

Aster novi-belgii var. tardiflorus, commonly known as the late-flowering New Belgium aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are alternate along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing purple to blue-violet blossoms that attract pollinators.

The varietal name "tardiflorus" refers to its delayed flowering habit compared to other varieties of the species.
