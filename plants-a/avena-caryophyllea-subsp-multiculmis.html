Avena caryophyllea subsp. multiculmis

Avena caryophyllea subsp.

multiculmis, commonly known as the purple wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and striking purple to reddish inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in restoration projects.
