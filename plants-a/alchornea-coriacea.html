Alchornea coriacea

Alchornea coriacea, commonly known as roughleaf mallot, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish or grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Alchornea coriacea is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
