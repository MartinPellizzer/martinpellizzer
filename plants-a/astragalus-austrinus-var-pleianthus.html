Astragalus austrinus var. pleianthus

Astragalus austrinus var. pleianthus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
