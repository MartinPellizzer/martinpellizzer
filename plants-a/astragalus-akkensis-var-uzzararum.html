Astragalus akkensis var. uzzararum

Astragalus akkensis var. uzzararum, commonly known as the Uzzararum milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
