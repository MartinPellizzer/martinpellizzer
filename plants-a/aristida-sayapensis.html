Aristida sayapensis

Aristida sayapensis, commonly known as the Sayapensis threeawn, is a grass species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that aids in water retention.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is not widely cultivated for agricultural or ornamental purposes.
