Acosta resupinata

Acosta resupinata, commonly known as the creeping acosta, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Acosta resupinata is known for its resilience to harsh climates and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
