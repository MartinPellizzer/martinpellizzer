Armatocereus churinensis

Armatocereus churinensis, commonly known as the Churin's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to the Andes of Peru.

It is characterized by its columnar stems covered in thick, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
