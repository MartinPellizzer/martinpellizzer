Aristida flocciculmis

Aristida flocciculmis, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, fluffy appearance, with long, slender leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
