Acacia pseudoeburnea

Acacia pseudoeburnea, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are grouped into cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia pseudoeburnea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
