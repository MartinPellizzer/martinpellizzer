Amerimnon nitidulum

Amerimnon nitidulum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in agricultural and natural areas.
