Alsodeia piparea

Alsodeia piparea, commonly known as lesser waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in ponds, ditches, and marshes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic environments.

Alsodeia piparea is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic biodiversity.
