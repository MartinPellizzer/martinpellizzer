Anchusa rupestris

Anchusa rupestris, commonly known as the rock mallows, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody at the base.

Anchusa rupestris is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
