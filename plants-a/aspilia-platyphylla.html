Aspilia platyphylla

Aspilia platyphylla, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers are surrounded by white ray florets, creating a striking visual effect.

Aspilia platyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
