Artemisia oxycephala var. aureinitens

Artemisia oxycephala var. aureinitens, commonly known as golden blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and striking blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a popular choice in both landscaping and traditional herbal practices.
