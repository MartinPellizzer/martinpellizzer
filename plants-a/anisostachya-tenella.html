Anisostachya tenella

Anisostachya tenella, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and compact, fountain-like growth habit. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
