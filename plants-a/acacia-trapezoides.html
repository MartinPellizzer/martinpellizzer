Acacia trapezoides

Acacia trapezoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Acacia trapezoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping in arid regions.
