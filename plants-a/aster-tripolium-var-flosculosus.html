Aster tripolium var. flosculosus

Aster tripolium var. flosculosus, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically features small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental or for its ecological benefits to pollinators.
