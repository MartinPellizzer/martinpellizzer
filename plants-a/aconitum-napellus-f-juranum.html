Aconitum napellus f. juranum

Aconitum napellus f. juranum, commonly known as the Juranum monkshood, is a cultivar of the European monkshood plant.

It is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
