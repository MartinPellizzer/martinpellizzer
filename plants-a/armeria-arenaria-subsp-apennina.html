Armeria arenaria subsp. apennina

Armeria arenaria subsp.

apennina, commonly known as the Apennine sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features compact, succulent leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to arid conditions.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a valued addition to dry garden environments.
