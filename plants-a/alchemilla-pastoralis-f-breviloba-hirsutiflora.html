Alchemilla pastoralis f. breviloba-hirsutiflora

Alchemilla pastoralis f. breviloba-hirsutiflora, commonly known as the hairy-stalked lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
