Angraecum parviflorum

Angraecum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique spurred structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Angraecum parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
