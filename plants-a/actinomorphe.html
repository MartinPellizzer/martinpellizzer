Actinomorphe

Actinomorphe, commonly known as the star-shaped flowered plant, is a term used to describe plants with flowers that are radially symmetrical.

These flowers typically have petals that radiate out from a central point, creating a star-like appearance. The structure of actinomorphe flowers often includes multiple layers of petals, sepals, and stamens. This symmetry is common in many flowering plants, including roses and daisies.

The botanical significance of actinomorphe lies in its role in attracting pollinators through its visually striking form.
