Artemisia comaiensis

Artemisia comaiensis, commonly known as the Mexican wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other aromatic herbs and flowering plants.

Artemisia comaiensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
