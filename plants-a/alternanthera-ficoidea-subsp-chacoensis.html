Alternanthera ficoidea subsp. chacoensis

Alternanthera ficoidea subsp.

chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco firetail, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America. It is characterized by its bright red to orange-red foliage, which is particularly vibrant in sunny conditions.

This subspecies typically grows in shallow waters and is often found in the wetlands of Paraguay and Argentina. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

It is popular in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
