Aloe conifera subsp. pervagata

Aloe conifera subsp.

pervagata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features tall, branched stems that bear narrow, fleshy leaves with a reddish or purplish hue.

The plant produces yellow, tubular flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
