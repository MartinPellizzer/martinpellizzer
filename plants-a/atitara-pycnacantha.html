Atitara pycnacantha

Atitara pycnacantha, commonly known as the African milkwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, opposite leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.

The plant has traditional uses in medicine and is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
