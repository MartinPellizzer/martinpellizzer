Anotites costatus

Anotites costatus, commonly known as the stiffstemmed water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are often ribbed and can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. Its flowering stems rise above the foliage, bearing small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Anotites costatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
