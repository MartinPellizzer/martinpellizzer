Amomum ligulatum

Amomum ligulatum, commonly known as narrow-leaf cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and used in traditional medicine.

Amomum ligulatum is valued for its essential oils, which contribute to its use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
