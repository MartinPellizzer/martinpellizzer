Anisopetala montana

Anisopetala montana, commonly known as the mountain anisopetala, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Anisopetala montana is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
