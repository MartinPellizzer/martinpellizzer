Andropogon sorghum var. aegyptiacus

Andropogon sorghum var. aegyptiacus, commonly known as Egyptian sorghum, is a tall, grass-like plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It features narrow, blade-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant is known for its robust growth and adaptability to arid and semi-arid climates. It is often cultivated for its edible grains and has historical significance in traditional agriculture.

Its botanical structure makes it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
