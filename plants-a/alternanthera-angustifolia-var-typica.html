Alternanthera angustifolia var. typica

Alternanthera angustifolia var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Alternanthera, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often bright red or pink in color, especially when exposed to full sun. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full to partial sunlight.

This ornamental plant is often used in water gardens and as a bog plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
