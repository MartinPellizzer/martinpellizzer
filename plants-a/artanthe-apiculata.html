Artanthe apiculata

Artanthe apiculata, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery down. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Artanthe apiculata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
