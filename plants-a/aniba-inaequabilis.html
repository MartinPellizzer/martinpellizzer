Aniba inaequabilis

Aniba inaequabilis, commonly known as the unequal-leafed sylphium, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, spherical fruits.

Aniba inaequabilis is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
