Aeschynanthus microphyllus

Aeschynanthus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf ivy vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Aeschynanthus microphyllus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and colorful blooms.
