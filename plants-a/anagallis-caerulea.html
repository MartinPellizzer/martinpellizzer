Anagallis caerulea

Anagallis caerulea, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple, with a distinctive white throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Anagallis caerulea is known for its rapid growth and ability to self-seed, making it a common weed in many regions.
