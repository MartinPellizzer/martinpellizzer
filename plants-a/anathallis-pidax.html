Anathallis pidax

Anathallis pidax, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, with three sepals and three petals, and it lacks a visible labellum.

Anathallis pidax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
