Acaena sanguisorbae var. diminuta

Acaena sanguisorbae var. diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf New Zealand knapweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features small, spiny bracts that enclose its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, hard, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
