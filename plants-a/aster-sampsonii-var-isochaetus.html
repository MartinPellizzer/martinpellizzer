Aster sampsonii var. isochaetus

Aster sampsonii var. isochaetus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, and the plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

This variety is distinguished by its more compact growth and narrower leaves compared to other Aster sampsonii varieties.
