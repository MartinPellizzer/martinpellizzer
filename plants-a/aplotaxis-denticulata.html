Aplotaxis denticulata

Aplotaxis denticulata, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in open, grassy areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow for wide distribution.

Aplotaxis denticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
