Andropogon cotulifer

Andropogon cotulifer, commonly known as the purple bluestem, is a tall grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon cotulifer plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
