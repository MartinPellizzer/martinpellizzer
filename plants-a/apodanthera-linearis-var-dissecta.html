Apodanthera linearis var. dissecta

Apodanthera linearis var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected linear beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are finely dissected, giving the plant a feathery appearance. The flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
