Azedarach nigrum

Azedarach nigrum, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and Southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that split open to release its seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various regions outside its native range.

The Persian lilac is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
