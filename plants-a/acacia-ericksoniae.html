Acacia ericksoniae

Acacia ericksoniae, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers in compact inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seeds are encased in a pod that splits open to release the seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Acacia ericksoniae is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
