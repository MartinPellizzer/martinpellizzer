Anacyclus altissimus

Anacyclus altissimus, commonly known as the tall crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Anacyclus altissimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
