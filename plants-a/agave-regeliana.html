Agave regeliana

Agave regeliana, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stem and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces striking white or pink flower spikes that can reach several feet in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
