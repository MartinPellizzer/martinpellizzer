Aster scabris

Aster scabris, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, rough-textured leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Aster scabris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
