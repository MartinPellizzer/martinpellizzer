Angelica cincta

Angelica cincta, commonly known as the clustered angelica, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features large, compound leaves with a distinctive pinnate structure and a strong, aromatic odor. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typically white or greenish in color.

Angelica cincta is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
