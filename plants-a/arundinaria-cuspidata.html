Arundinaria cuspidata

Arundinaria cuspidata, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Arundinaria cuspidata is native to eastern Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional crafts.
