Abutilon hemsleyanum

Abutilon hemsleyanum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red bracts that resemble small lanterns. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, yellow flowers that are often hidden beneath the bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Abutilon hemsleyanum is relatively easy to grow and can be propagated through seeds or cuttings.
