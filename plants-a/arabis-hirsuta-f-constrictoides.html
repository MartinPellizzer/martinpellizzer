Arabis hirsuta f. constrictoides

Arabis hirsuta f. constrictoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockcress, is a botanical variant of the broader Arabis hirsuta species.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to poor soils, making it a popular choice in rock gardens.
