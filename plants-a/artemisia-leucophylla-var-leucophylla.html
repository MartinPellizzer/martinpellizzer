Artemisia leucophylla var. leucophylla

Artemisia leucophylla var. leucophylla, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid and rocky environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is valued for its aromatic properties and ornamental appeal.
