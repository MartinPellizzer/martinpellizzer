Acaena integerrima var. subpilosicupula

Acaena integerrima var. subpilosicupula, commonly known as the silky burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, prickly seed heads that form spherical clusters, which are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its woody stems are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence, giving it its varietal name.

It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
