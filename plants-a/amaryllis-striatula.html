Amaryllis striatula

Amaryllis striatula, commonly known as the streaked amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are often marked with distinctive stripes, giving it its common name. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Amaryllis striatula is known for its ability to bloom multiple times a year under proper care.
