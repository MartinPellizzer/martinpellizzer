Asarca insignis

Asarca insignis, commonly known as the small-flowered bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Asarca insignis is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
