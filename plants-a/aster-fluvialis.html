Aster fluvialis

Aster fluvialis, commonly known as the river aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to blue petals and yellow centers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Aster fluvialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
