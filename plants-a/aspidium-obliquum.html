Aspidium obliquum

Aspidium obliquum, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with small, lanceolate pinnae that are arranged in a unique, undulating pattern.

Aspidium obliquum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
