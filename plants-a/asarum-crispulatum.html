Asarum crispulatum

Asarum crispulatum, commonly known as curly asarum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, wavy leaves and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are unique, resembling a complex, hollow structure with a distinctive aroma.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
