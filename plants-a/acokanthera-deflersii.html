Acokanthera deflersii

Acokanthera deflersii, commonly known as the poison arrow tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that produces fragrant, white or greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its toxic properties, as its leaves and stems contain cardiac glycosides, which are potent poisons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Despite its dangerous nature, it plays a role in traditional medicine and has cultural significance in some regions.
