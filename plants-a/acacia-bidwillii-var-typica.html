Acacia bidwillii var. typica

Acacia bidwillii var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and narrow, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Acacia bidwillii var. typica is a hardy species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
