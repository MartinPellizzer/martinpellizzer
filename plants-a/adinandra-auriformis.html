Adinandra auriformis

Adinandra auriformis, commonly known as the golden leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Adinandra auriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
