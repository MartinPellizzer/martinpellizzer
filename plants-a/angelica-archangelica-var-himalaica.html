Angelica archangelica var. himalaica

Angelica archangelica var. himalaica, commonly known as Himalayan angelica, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, umbrella-like compound leaves with a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, terminal umbels.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
