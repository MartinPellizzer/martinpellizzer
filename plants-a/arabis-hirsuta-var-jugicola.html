Arabis hirsuta var. jugicola

Arabis hirsuta var. jugicola, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal regions.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
