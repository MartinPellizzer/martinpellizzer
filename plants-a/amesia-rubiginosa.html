Amesia rubiginosa

Amesia rubiginosa, commonly known as the redstem gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Amesia rubiginosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
