Actinodaphne morrisonensis

Actinodaphne morrisonensis, commonly known as Morrison's camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its timber and has been traditionally used in local medicine and as a source of essential oils.
