Aristida subarticulata

Aristida subarticulata, commonly known as the slender three-awn, is a grass species native to various regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of bracts that are often hairy and pointed. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
