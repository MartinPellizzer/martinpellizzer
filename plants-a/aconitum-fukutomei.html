Aconitum fukutomei

Aconitum fukutomei, commonly known as the Fukutomei monkshood, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive appearance, while its roots contain toxic alkaloids.

Aconitum fukutomei is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
