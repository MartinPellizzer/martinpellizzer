Ardisia nigrescens

Ardisia nigrescens, commonly known as the black ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit.
