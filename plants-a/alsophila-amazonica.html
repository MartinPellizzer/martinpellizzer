Alsophila amazonica

Alsophila amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which can span several meters in width.

Alsophila amazonica plays an important ecological role in its environment by providing habitat and contributing to forest structure.
