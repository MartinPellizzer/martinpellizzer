Acilepis saligna var. nilghirensis

Acilepis saligna var. nilghirensis, commonly known as the Nilgiri acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and bipinnate leaves that are alternate and compound. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
