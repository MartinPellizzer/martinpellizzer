Ageratina cumbensis f. glandulifera

Ageratina cumbensis f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular ageratina, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in compact, spherical clusters called capitula. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in moist and shaded environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and have glandular structures, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
