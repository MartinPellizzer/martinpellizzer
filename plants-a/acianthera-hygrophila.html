Acianthera hygrophila

Acianthera hygrophila, commonly known as the water orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its roots are adapted to aquatic conditions, allowing it to absorb nutrients from water.

The plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
