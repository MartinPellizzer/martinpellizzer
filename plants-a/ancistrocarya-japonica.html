Ancistrocarya japonica

Ancistrocarya japonica, commonly known as Japanese shepherds' purse, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in racemes. The plant produces distinctive, heart-shaped pods that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
