Adhatoda thymifolia

Adhatoda thymifolia, commonly known as Indian Trumpet or Malabar Trumpet, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its elongated, trumpet-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shady environments such as forests and along riverbanks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating, and the flowers are typically pale blue to violet in color.

Adhatoda thymifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
