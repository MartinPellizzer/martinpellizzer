Alyxia actinophylla

Alyxia actinophylla, commonly known as the shining alyxia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Alyxia actinophylla thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
