Aconitum napellus f. howerlanum

Aconitum napellus f. howerlanum, commonly known as the Howerlanum monkshood, is a botanical variant of the European monkshood.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it requires careful handling and is often cultivated in controlled settings.
