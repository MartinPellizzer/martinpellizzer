Atriplex palaestina

Atriplex palaestina, commonly known as the Palestinian saltbush, is a shrub native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species suited to harsh conditions. Its small greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, edible seeds.

Atriplex palaestina plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
