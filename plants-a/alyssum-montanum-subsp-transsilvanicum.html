Alyssum montanum subsp. transsilvanicum

Alyssum montanum subsp.

transsilvanicum, commonly known as Transylvanian alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Alyssum montanum subsp.

transsilvanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
