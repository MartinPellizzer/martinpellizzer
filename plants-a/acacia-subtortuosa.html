Acacia subtortuosa

Acacia subtortuosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, often twisted branches and narrow, feathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clustered spikes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Acacia subtortuosa is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
