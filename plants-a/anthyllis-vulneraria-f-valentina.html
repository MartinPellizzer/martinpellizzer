Anthyllis vulneraria f. valentina

Anthyllis vulneraria f. valentina, commonly known as the Valentina broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
