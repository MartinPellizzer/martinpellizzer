Astragalus utahensis f. umbellulata

Astragalus utahensis f. umbellulata, commonly known as the Utah milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Utah and neighboring states.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbellate clusters. The plant is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and exhibiting drought tolerance. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
