Astragalus newberryi var. typicus

Astragalus newberryi var. typicus, commonly known as Newberry's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
