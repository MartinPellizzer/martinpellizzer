Ascolepis capensis var. lacera

Ascolepis capensis var. lacera, commonly known as the rock lichen, is a unique lichen species that exhibits a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria.

It is typically found growing on rocks and soil in arid or semi-arid regions, particularly in southern Africa. The plant's structure consists of a crustose thallus, which is tightly adhered to the substrate, giving it a rock-like appearance. Its coloration can range from pale green to dark brown, depending on environmental conditions and the presence of moisture.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments, making it an important indicator species for certain ecosystems.
