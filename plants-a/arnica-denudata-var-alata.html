Arnica denudata var. alata

Arnica denudata var. alata, commonly known as the winged arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has distinctive, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, bitter scent. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
