Aster excorticatus

Aster excorticatus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to blue petals, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Aster excorticatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
