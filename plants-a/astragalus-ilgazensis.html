Astragalus ilgazensis

Astragalus ilgazensis, commonly known as the Ilgaz milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous areas, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It has compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
