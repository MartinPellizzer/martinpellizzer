Atriplex hymenelytra

Atriplex hymenelytra, commonly known as the smooth saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are adapted for wind pollination. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity.

Atriplex hymenelytra plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
