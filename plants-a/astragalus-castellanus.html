Astragalus castellanus

Astragalus castellanus, commonly known as the Castle astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
