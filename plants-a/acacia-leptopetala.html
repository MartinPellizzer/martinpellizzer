Acacia leptopetala

Acacia leptopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia leptopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
