Acronia radula

Acronia radula, commonly known as the slender acronia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Acronia radula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
