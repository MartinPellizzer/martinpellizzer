Adenophora erecta

Adenophora erecta, commonly known as the upright bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Adenophora erecta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects.
