Asplenium hymenophylloides

Asplenium hymenophylloides, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a silvery or pale green appearance due to the presence of trichomes on the leaf surfaces. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori and indusia, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Asplenium hymenophylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
