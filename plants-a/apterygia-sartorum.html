Apterygia sartorum

Apterygia sartorum, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike that bears small, inconspicuous flowers.

Apterygia sartorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
