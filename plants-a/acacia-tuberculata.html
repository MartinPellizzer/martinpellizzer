Acacia tuberculata

Acacia tuberculata, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, rounded phyllodes. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Acacia tuberculata is valued for its hardiness and is used in landscaping and for its ecological benefits.
