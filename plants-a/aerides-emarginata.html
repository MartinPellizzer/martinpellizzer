Aerides emarginata

Aerides emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with distinctive notched petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Aerides emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
