Aster decumbens

Aster decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy stems and basal leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Aster decumbens is an important nectar source for pollinators and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
