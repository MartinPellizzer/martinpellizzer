Allophylus dodsonii

Allophylus dodsonii, commonly known as the dwarf sycamore, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Allophylus dodsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit.
