Anthemis secundiramea subsp. urvilleana

Anthemis secundiramea subsp.

urvilleana, commonly known as the Urville's anthemis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage is aromatic, with a distinctive yellowish-green color and finely serrated margins.

The flowers bloom in summer, producing small yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
