Ampelopsis aegirophylla var. chondisensis

Ampelopsis aegirophylla var. chondisensis, commonly known as the Korean grapevine, is a climbing vine native to South Korea.

It features compound leaves with five to seven ovate leaflets, often with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and berries, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
