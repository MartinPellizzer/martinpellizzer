Adina aralioides

Adina aralioides, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and are accompanied by small, leaf-like bracts.

Adina aralioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
