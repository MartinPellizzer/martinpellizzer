Athyrium serrulatum

Athyrium serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated male fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are finely serrated along the margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its elegant appearance.
