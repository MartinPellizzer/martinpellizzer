Arthroclianthus grandifolius

Arthroclianthus grandifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf arthroclianthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
