Alcea indica

Alcea indica, commonly known as the common hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, velvety flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which also includes cotton and hibiscus. Alcea indica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing up to 2 meters in height.

Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
