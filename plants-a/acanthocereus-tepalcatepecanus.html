Acanthocereus tepalcatepecanus

Acanthocereus tepalcatepecanus, commonly known as the Tepalcatepec cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its stem.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
