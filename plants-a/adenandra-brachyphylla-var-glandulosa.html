Adenandra brachyphylla var. glandulosa

Adenandra brachyphylla var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in tiny glands, giving it the specific epithet "glandulosa.".

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
