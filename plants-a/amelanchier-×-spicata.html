Amelanchier × spicata

Amelanchier × spicata, commonly known as the common serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–10 meters and has alternate, simple, and often serrated leaves. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Amelanchier × spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
