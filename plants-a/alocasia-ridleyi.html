Alocasia ridleyi

Alocasia ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. Alocasia ridleyi thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
