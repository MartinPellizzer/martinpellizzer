Aster sedifolius f. latifolius

Aster sedifolius f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a variant of the sedifolius form of the aster species.

It is characterized by its broad, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
