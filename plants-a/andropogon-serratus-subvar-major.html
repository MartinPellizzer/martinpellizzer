Andropogon serratus subvar. major

Andropogon serratus subvar. major, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves with serrated edges. This grass thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of native prairie and woodland ecosystems. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

The plant is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological value and adaptability.
