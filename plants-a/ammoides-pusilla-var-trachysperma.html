Ammoides pusilla var. trachysperma

Ammoides pusilla var. trachysperma, commonly known as narrowleaf sand verbena, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale blue or violet, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
