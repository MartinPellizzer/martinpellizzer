Aristolochia prostrata

Aristolochia prostrata, commonly known as the creeping Dutchman's pipe, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musty odor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
