Aridaria paucandra

Aridaria paucandra, commonly known as the sparseflower desert shrub, is a hardy plant adapted to arid environments.

It features small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the dry season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater.

Aridaria paucandra plays a crucial role in stabilizing sandy soils in desert ecosystems.
