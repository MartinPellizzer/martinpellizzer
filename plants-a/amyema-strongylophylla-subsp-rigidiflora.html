Amyema strongylophylla subsp. rigidiflora

Amyema strongylophylla subsp.

rigidiflora, commonly known as the rigid-flowered amyema, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its erect, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
