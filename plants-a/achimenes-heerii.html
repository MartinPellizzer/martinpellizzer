Achimenes heerii

Achimenes heerii, commonly known as the velvetleaf achimenes, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strikingly large, spurred flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a popular choice for both botanical gardens and private collections.

Morphology

Achimenes heerii has leaves that are heart-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is slender, hairy, and slightly woody with a pale greenish-brown bark. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes, typically displaying vibrant red or orange hues, and bloom during the summer months, primarily pollinated by bees. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and tuberous, aiding in the plant's survival during dry periods.
