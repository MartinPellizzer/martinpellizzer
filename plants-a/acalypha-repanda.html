Acalypha repanda

Acalypha repanda, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display a vibrant mix of green, red, and purple hues. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. Acalypha repanda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
