Asarum cavaleriei var. esquirolii

Asarum cavaleriei var. esquirolii, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, reddish-brown leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic root that is used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
