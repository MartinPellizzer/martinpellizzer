Aponogeton euryspermus

Aponogeton euryspermus, commonly known as the broad-seeded arrowhead, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with roots submerged in the substrate. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the water, adding a delicate aesthetic to its natural habitat. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands.

Aponogeton euryspermus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquariums and decorative water features.
