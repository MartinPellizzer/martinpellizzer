Adenocalymma cymbalum

Adenocalymma cymbalum, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in sandy or gravelly soils. It is adapted to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Adenocalymma cymbalum is an important component of the local flora and is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
