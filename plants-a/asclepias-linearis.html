Asclepias linearis

Asclepias linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically 2 to 4 inches long and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers with distinctive horn-shaped buds. It is a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and supports a variety of pollinators.

Asclepias linearis thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
