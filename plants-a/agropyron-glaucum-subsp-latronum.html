Agropyron glaucum subsp. latronum

Agropyron glaucum subsp.

latronum, commonly known as the blue wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the North American prairies. It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, with slender, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while the stems are erect and often branched.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
