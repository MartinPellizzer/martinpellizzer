Aconitum hamatipetalum

Aconitum hamatipetalum, commonly known as the hairy-petaled monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, spire-like stems that bear showy, hooded flowers with a distinctive purple to blue coloration. The leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that give the plant a fern-like appearance. Its roots contain potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological interest.
