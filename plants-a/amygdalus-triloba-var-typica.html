Amygdalus triloba var. typica

Amygdalus triloba var. typica, commonly known as the three-lobed almond.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is closely related to other almond species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
