Allium margaritaceum subsp. tenorei

Allium margaritaceum subsp.

tenorei, commonly known as the Sicilian pearl onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, round, white bulbs that resemble pearls, hence its common name.

The plant produces clusters of delicate, grass-like leaves and small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative plant.

Its ornamental value and edible bulbs make it a versatile addition to both gardens and culinary applications.
