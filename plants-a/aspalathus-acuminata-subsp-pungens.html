Aspalathus acuminata subsp. pungens

Aspalathus acuminata subsp.

pungens, commonly known as the African green tea or rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and requires a Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers. It is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used to produce a unique herbal tea.

The subspecies pungens is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Aspalathus acuminata.
