Actinodaphne kontumi

Actinodaphne kontumi, commonly known as the Kontumi camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant, resinous wood. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single, smooth, black seed.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Morphology

Actinodaphne kontumi has elliptical to ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a smooth texture and arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is slender, with a smooth, pale brown bark that exudes a slight fragrance when broken. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers with three sepals and petals, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed drupe that turns from green to yellowish-red, dispersing via birds and water.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network near the surface, aiding in nutrient absorption and soil stabilization.
