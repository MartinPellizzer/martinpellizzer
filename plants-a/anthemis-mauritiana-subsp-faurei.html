Anthemis mauritiana subsp. faurei

Anthemis mauritiana subsp.

faurei, commonly known as the Mauritius daisy, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of the region. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the local biodiversity.
