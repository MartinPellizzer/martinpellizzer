Astragalus tioides

Astragalus tioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

Astragalus tioides plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
