Asterochlaena sidifolia f. crenata

Asterochlaena sidifolia f. crenata, commonly known as the crested rock fern, is a unique variant of the rock fern species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged fronds that give it a rugged, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and in dry, well-drained soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage is usually a deep green, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture.

This fern is valued in horticulture for its striking form and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
