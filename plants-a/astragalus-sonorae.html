Astragalus sonorae

Astragalus sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as desert scrub and grasslands. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Astragalus sonorae is also valued for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
