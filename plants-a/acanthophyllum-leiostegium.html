Acanthophyllum leiostegium

Acanthophyllum leiostegium, commonly known as the smooth leafed acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and reducing water loss through its reduced leaf surface. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring or early summer.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert ecosystems.
