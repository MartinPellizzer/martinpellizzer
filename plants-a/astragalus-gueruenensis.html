Astragalus gueruenensis

Astragalus gueruenensis, commonly known as the Gueruenensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
