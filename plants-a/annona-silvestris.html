Annona silvestris

Annona silvestris, commonly known as the wild custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by large, yellow or green fruits with a thick, rough skin. The fruit contains a creamy, sweet pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a distinctive custard-like texture.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
