Adiantum concinnum var. integrum

Adiantum concinnum var. integrum, commonly known as the southern maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, bright green fronds that are typically arching and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are short and creeping, allowing it to form dense mats in suitable conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in shaded garden settings.
