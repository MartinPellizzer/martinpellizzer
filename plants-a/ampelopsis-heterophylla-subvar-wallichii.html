Ampelopsis heterophylla subvar. wallichii

Ampelopsis heterophylla subvar. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan grapevine, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape depending on the growth conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to blue-black berries in the fall. Its stems are woody and can climb by means of tendrils and adventitious roots.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded or partially sunny environments.
