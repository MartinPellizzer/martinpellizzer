Adonis dentata var. persica

Adonis dentata var. persica, commonly known as Persian autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the autumn, typically from September to November. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
