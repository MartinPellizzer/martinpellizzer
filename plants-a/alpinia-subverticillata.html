Alpinia subverticillata

Alpinia subverticillata, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are arranged in a spiral pattern along the inflorescence. Alpinia subverticillata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes in tropical gardens.
