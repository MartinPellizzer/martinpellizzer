Agathosma glabrata var. paradoxa

Agathosma glabrata var. paradoxa, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, aromatic leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
