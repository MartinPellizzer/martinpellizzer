Astragalus angustistipulatus

Astragalus angustistipulatus, commonly known as narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets, and its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
