Andinia rotunda

Andinia rotunda, commonly known as the roundleaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, rounded leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
