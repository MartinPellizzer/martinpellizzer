Alyssum praecox

Alyssum praecox, commonly known as early alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming early in the spring. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum praecox is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
