Azalea viscosa var. glauca

Azalea viscosa var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous azalea, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that has a distinctive bluish-gray waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
