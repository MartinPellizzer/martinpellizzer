Attalea princeps

Attalea princeps, commonly known as the palm heart or pequi, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and a robust, clustered trunk. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark purple, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber, while the seeds contain a nutrient-rich oil.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
