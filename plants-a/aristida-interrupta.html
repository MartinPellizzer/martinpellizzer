Aristida interrupta

Aristida interrupta, commonly known as the slender threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its adaptability in arid environments.

Aristida interrupta is also known for its ability to thrive in low-nutrient soils, making it a resilient species in challenging ecological conditions.
