Aulomyrcia myrtillifolia

Aulomyrcia myrtillifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved aulomyrcia, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical forests of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes well-known genera like Eucalyptus and Syzygium. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.
