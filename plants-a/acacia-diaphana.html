Acacia diaphana

Acacia diaphana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stem and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact inflorescences, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia diaphana is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
