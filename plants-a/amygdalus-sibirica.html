Amygdalus sibirica

Amygdalus sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, hard, edible seeds encased in a thick, woody pod. It is adapted to harsh climates and can thrive in well-drained soils.

The Siberian almond is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
