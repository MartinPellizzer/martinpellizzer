Anthemis chrysantha subsp. jimenezii

Anthemis chrysantha subsp.

jimenezii, commonly known as the Jimenez's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant produces yellow-orange flower heads with pale yellow ray florets, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance and vibrant color.
