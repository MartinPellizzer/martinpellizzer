Actinella scaposa

Actinella scaposa, commonly known as the scapose actinella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster, and bloom in late summer to early fall.
