Argyreia henryi var. hypochrysa

Argyreia henryi var. hypochrysa, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of small, yellow flowers that resemble a shower of golden beads. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
