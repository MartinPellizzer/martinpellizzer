Agropyron repens var. firmum

Agropyron repens var. firmum, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured leaves and its ability to form dense, mat-like growth patterns. This variety is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils. It has a shallow root system, making it effective for erosion control and soil stabilization.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
