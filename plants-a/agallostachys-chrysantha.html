Agallostachys chrysantha

Agallostachys chrysantha, commonly known as the golden chrysanthemum, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
