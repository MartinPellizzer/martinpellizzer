Adesmia incana

Adesmia incana, commonly known as the woolly bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its grayish, hairy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.

Adesmia incana is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in degraded landscapes.
