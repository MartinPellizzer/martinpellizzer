Amaryllis × amabilis

Amaryllis × amabilis, commonly known as the belladonna lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis grandiflora.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features strap-like, dark green leaves.

It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, showy blooms.
