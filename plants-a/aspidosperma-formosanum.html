Aspidosperma formosanum

Aspidosperma formosanum, commonly known as the Chinese quinine tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its alkaloid content, which has been used in traditional medicine.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
