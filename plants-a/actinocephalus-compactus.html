Actinocephalus compactus

Actinocephalus compactus, commonly known as the compact star-twig, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, dense, and often branched stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically small, narrow, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Actinocephalus compactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
