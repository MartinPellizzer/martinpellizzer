Aglaomorpha bonii

Aglaomorpha bonii, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with a distinctive lip that attracts pollinators like bees and wasps.

Aglaomorpha bonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under suitable conditions.
