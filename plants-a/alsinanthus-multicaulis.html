Alsinanthus multicaulis

Alsinanthus multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered alsike.

It is a flowering plant native to the western United States, characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its small, white to pinkish flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Alsinanthus multicaulis is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
