Avenula versicolor

Avenula versicolor, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

Avenula versicolor plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
