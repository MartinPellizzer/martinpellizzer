Acicarpha spathulata var. glauca

Acicarpha spathulata var. glauca, commonly known as the blue spathulate acicarpha, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its glaucous, bluish-green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or open woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
