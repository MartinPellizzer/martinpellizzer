Aniba gonggrijpii

Aniba gonggrijpii, commonly known as the Chinese wild lauraceous tree, is a species native to China, particularly in the Yunnan and Sichuan provinces.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is typically found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
