Avena scheuchzeri

Avena scheuchzeri, commonly known as the Alpine oat, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Avena scheuchzeri is an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
