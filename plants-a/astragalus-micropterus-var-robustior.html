Astragalus micropterus var. robustior

Astragalus micropterus var. robustior, commonly known as the robust milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often hairy.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
