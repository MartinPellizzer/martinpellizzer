Addisonia virgata

Addisonia virgata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces bright yellow flowers resembling those of the common dandelion. The plant has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils and withstand periods of drought. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated by insects, making it an important plant in desert ecosystems.
