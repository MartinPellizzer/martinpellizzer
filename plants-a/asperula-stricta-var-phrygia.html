Asperula stricta var. phrygia

Asperula stricta var. phrygia, commonly known as the Phrygian snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate floral display to its foliage. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact, mound-like form. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is particularly noted for its hardiness and ability to tolerate partial shade.
