Agropyron bonaepartis

Agropyron bonaepartis, commonly known as the Patagonian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
