Aster × herveya

Aster × herveya, commonly known as the Hervey's aster, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant produces composite flower heads composed of both ray and disc florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to North America, it thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Its foliage is usually lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
