Allium subnotabile

Allium subnotabile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic.

It is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlicky flavor, making it a useful herb in culinary applications.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.
