Aconitum majimae

Aconitum majimae, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has dark green, palmately compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Aconitum majimae contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
