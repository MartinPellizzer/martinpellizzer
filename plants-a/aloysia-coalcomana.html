Aloysia coalcomana

Aloysia coalcomana, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Aloysia coalcomana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its fragrant foliage and ornamental appeal.
