Aquilegia brevistyla var. leicocarpa

Aquilegia brevistyla var. leicocarpa, commonly known as the white-flowered columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five sepals that are often more prominent than the petals. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces seed pods that are cylindrical and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
