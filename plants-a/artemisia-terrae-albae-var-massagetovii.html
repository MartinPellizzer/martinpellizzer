Artemisia terrae-albae var. massagetovii

Artemisia terrae-albae var. massagetovii, commonly known as the white earth artemisia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in panicles during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its essential oils contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
