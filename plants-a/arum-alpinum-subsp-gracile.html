Arum alpinum subsp. gracile

Arum alpinum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as the alpine arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features a distinctive spathe, which is a modified leaf that encloses the spadix, a dense cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and can vary in size depending on the habitat.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
