Abutilon grantii

Abutilon grantii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges. Its hairy leaves and clustered inflorescences are key botanical features that distinguish it from other Abutilon species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
