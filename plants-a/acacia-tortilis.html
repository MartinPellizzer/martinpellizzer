Acacia tortilis

Acacia tortilis, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its long, drooping branches. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, reducing water loss in arid environments. Its yellow flowers are produced in dense, globular clusters and attract various pollinators.

Acacia tortilis is well adapted to hot, dry climates and plays an important ecological role in its habitat.
