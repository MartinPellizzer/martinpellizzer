Astragalus boeticus f. violaceus

Astragalus boeticus f. violaceus, commonly known as the violet-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers clustered in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or dry environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its delicate floral display.
