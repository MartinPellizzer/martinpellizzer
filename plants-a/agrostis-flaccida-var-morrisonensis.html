Agrostis flaccida var. morrisonensis

Agrostis flaccida var. morrisonensis, commonly known as Morrison's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is often used in coastal restoration projects due to its tolerance of salt and wind.
