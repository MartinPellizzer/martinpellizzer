Asterocarpus tricuspidatus

Asterocarpus tricuspidatus, commonly known as the three-lobed starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed fruits that resemble small, star-shaped berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is edible when ripe, though it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
