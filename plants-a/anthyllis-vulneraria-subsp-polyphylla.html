Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. polyphylla

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved heal-all, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of nitrogen-fixing bacteria in soil.
