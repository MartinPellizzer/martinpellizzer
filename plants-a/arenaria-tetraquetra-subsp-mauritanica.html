Arenaria tetraquetra subsp. mauritanica

Arenaria tetraquetra subsp.

mauritanica, commonly known as the Moroccan sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of North Africa. It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically on slender stems.

The plant has a compact rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
