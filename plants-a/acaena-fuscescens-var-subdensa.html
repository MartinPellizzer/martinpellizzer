Acaena fuscescens var. subdensa

Acaena fuscescens var. subdensa, commonly known as the dense burr plant, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of spiny, bristly fruits that serve as a means of seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and role in supporting local wildlife.
