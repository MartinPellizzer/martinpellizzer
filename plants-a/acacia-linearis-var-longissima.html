Acacia linearis var. longissima

Acacia linearis var. longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear phyllodes that are typically longer than they are wide. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
