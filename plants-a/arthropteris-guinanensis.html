Arthropteris guinanensis

Arthropteris guinanensis, commonly known as the Guianan shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive shield-shaped fronds, which are typically divided into smaller, lobed segments. This plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Venezuela, Guyana, and Suriname. Arthropteris guinanensis thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its fronds, typical of many fern species.
