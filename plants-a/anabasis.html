Anabasis

Anabasis, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It consists of several species that are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia, Africa, and the Middle East. These plants are characterized by their succulent, often hairy leaves and showy, small flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or red. Anabasis species are adapted to survive in harsh desert environments, with deep root systems and the ability to store water in their tissues.

They play a role in stabilizing sandy soils and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
