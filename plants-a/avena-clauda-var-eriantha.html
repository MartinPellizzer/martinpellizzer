Avena clauda var. eriantha

Avena clauda var. eriantha, commonly known as the velvet oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family. Its seeds are enclosed in membranous husks, which contribute to its common name.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
