Aspidium tripartitum

Aspidium tripartitum, commonly known as the three-part water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia. The plant has a slender, creeping rhizome and produces small, delicate fronds with a bipinnate structure. Its leaves are typically divided into three parts, giving it the common name "three-part.".

Aspidium tripartitum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests.
