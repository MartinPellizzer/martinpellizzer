Alfaroa

Alfaroa, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Alfaroa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
