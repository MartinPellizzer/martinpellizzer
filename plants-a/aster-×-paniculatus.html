Aster × paniculatus

Aster × paniculatus, commonly known as the New England aster, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
