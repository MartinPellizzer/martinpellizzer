Asarum dabieshanense

Asarum dabieshanense, commonly known as the Dabie Mountain wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the Dabie Mountains in China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, reddish-brown, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. It has a long rhizome system that helps it survive in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
