Aponogeton madagascariensis var. major

Aponogeton madagascariensis var. major, commonly known as the giant water arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and prefers nutrient-rich substrates. It produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in aquariums and water gardens.
