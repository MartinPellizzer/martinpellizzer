Acoridium luchanense

Acoridium luchanense, commonly known as the Luchanese arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, often with a smooth or slightly textured surface.

Acoridium luchanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
