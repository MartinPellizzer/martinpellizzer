Amorphophallus anguineus

Amorphophallus anguineus, commonly known as the red-tailed arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and a distinctive reddish stem that becomes more prominent as the plant matures. The plant produces a striking, spadix-like inflorescence that can reach several feet in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible corms.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a food source, it is gaining attention in horticulture and agriculture.
