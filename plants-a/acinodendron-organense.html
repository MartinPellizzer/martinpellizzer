Acinodendron organense

Acinodendron organense, commonly known as the Organ Mountains dwarf lichen, is a unique plant found in the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a type of lichen, which is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to extreme environmental conditions. Its morphology includes a crust-like thallus that adheres tightly to rock surfaces.

Acinodendron organense plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its harsh habitat.
