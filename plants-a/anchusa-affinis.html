Anchusa affinis

Anchusa affinis, commonly known as the common bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Anchusa affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
