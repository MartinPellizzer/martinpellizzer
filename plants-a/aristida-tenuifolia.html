Aristida tenuifolia

Aristida tenuifolia, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and rocky areas. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
