Anthriscus sylvestris subsp. alpestris

Anthriscus sylvestris subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as Alpine cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its fern-like leaves and white umbelliferous flowers.

This subspecies is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at higher altitudes. It has a slender, branched stem and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its edible leaves in some traditional cuisines.
