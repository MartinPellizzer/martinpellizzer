Allium angulosum var. flavescens

Allium angulosum var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered, bulbous roots. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a cultivated plant in some regions for culinary use.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
