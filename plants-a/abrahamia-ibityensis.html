Abrahamia ibityensis

Abrahamia ibityensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes showy, fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
