Aloe × commutata

Aloe × commutata, commonly known as the hybrid aloe or the hybrid aloe plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a pale green to blue-green hue. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers during the spring or summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
