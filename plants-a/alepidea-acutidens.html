Alepidea acutidens

Alepidea acutidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dittany, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its aromatic properties.

Alepidea acutidens is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
