Anathallis burzlaffiana

Anathallis burzlaffiana, commonly known as the Burzlaff's spider orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and its striking, spider-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific host trees for support. Its flowers are pollinated by small flies, which are attracted by the plant's scent and appearance.

Anathallis burzlaffiana is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
