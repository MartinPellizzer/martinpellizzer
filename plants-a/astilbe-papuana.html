Astilbe papuana

Astilbe papuana, commonly known as the Papuan astilbe, is a herbaceous perennial native to Papua New Guinea.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of delicate, lace-like flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant has palmately divided, fern-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Astilbe papuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
