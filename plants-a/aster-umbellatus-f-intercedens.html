Aster umbellatus f. intercedens

Aster umbellatus f. intercedens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that resemble small cones, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is native to the eastern United States. It produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in wetland environments.

This cultivar is often appreciated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
