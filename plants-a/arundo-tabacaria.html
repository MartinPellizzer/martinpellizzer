Arundo tabacaria

Arundo tabacaria, commonly known as the tobacco rush, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a rough texture. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months.

Arundo tabacaria is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.
