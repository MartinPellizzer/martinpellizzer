Adenophora sinensis

Adenophora sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Adenophora sinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
