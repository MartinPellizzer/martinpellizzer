Astragalus kentrophyta var. implexus

Astragalus kentrophyta var. implexus, commonly known as the twisted milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species.

Its unique varietal form, implexus, refers to the twisted or coiled appearance of its stems and leaves.
