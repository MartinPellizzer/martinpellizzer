Aubrieta libanotica

Aubrieta libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese aubrieta, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in early spring, which bloom in clusters atop hairy stems. The plant has small, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Aubrieta libanotica is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
