Allosorus myriophyllus var. covillei

Allosorus myriophyllus var. covillei, commonly known as the Coville's fringed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Gentianaceae family.
