Asterochlaena sidifolia var. campestris

Asterochlaena sidifolia var. campestris, commonly known as the field asterochlaena, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

This species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
