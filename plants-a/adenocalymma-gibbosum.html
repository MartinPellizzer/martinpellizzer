Adenocalymma gibbosum

Adenocalymma gibbosum, commonly known as the globe-headed rock heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features dense, rounded clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to the harsh coastal environment.

Adenocalymma gibbosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
