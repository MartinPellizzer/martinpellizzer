Astragalus malacus var. obfalcatus

Astragalus malacus var. obfalcatus, commonly known as the darkleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its fruit is a narrow, twisted pod that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
