Asplenium resectum var. australiense

Asplenium resectum var. australiense, commonly known as the Australian maidenhair fern, is a variant of the resectum species within the Asplenium genus.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often exhibit a dark green color with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. Native to Australia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories or rocky crevices. The plant's rhizomes are fibrous and help it anchor to substrates, while its spore-producing structures are located on the undersides of its fronds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and indoor spaces due to its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
