Aria caloneura var. kwangtungensis

Aria caloneura var. kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong arrowleaf, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It features slender, upright stems with lanceolate leaves that are prominently veined and have a distinctive arrowhead shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
