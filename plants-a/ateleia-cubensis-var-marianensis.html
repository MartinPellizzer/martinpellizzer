Ateleia cubensis var. marianensis

Ateleia cubensis var. marianensis, commonly known as the Marianensis variety of the Cuban ateleia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or swampy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical climates.
