Albizia malacophylla var. malacophylla

Albizia malacophylla var. malacophylla, commonly known as the Malacophylla Albizia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often feathery and subdivided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological and agricultural contexts.
