Acacia lasiocarpa var. epacantha

Acacia lasiocarpa var. epacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and phyllodes that are narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
