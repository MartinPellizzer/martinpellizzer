Aleuritia giraldiana

Aleuritia giraldiana, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Aleuritia giraldiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and winter interest.
