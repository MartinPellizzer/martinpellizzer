Anemia simplicior

Anemia simplicior, commonly known as the simple anemone, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and a short, hairy stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical name reflects its simple, elegant structure and its role as a common wildflower in temperate regions.
