Arabis longistyla

Arabis longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves and its clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers have five petals and are arranged in a raceme, with the stamens and pistil prominently displayed.

Arabis longistyla is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
