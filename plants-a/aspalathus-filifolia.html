Aspalathus filifolia

Aspalathus filifolia, commonly known as the slender aspalathus, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to grow in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation. It produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Aspalathus filifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
