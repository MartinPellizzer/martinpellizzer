Aloe × beguinii perfectior

Aloe × beguinii perfectior, commonly known as the hybrid aloe or the improved aloe, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Aloe vera and Aloe arborescens.

It is characterized by its tall, branching structure and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are succulent and store water, typical of the Aloe genus. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
