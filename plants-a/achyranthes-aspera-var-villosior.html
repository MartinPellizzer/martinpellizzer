Achyranthes aspera var. villosior

Achyranthes aspera var. villosior, commonly known as the hairy eclipta, is a variant of the eclipta plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its erect stem and lanceolate leaves covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
