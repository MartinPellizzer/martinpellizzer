Amidena

Amidena, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that eventually develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. Amidena thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
