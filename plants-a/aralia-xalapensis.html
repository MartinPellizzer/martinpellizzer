Aralia xalapensis

Aralia xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features compound leaves with a feathery appearance, consisting of multiple narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique structure.
