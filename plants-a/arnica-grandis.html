Arnica grandis

Arnica grandis, commonly known as mountain arnica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems with hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with orange centers, which bloom in late summer. Arnica grandis is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
