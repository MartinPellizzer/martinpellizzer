Agropyron cristatum subsp. birjutczense

Agropyron cristatum subsp.

birjutczense, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, spike-like inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and mountain slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological resilience and is often used in restoration ecology projects.
