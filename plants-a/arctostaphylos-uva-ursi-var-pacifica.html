Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. pacifica

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. pacifica, commonly known as Pacific bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically red or dark green, and it produces bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to cool, humid climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for various ecological and horticultural applications.
