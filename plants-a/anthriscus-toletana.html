Anthriscus toletana

Anthriscus toletana, commonly known as Toletan cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and stream banks. It produces small, oval fruits called schizocarps, which contain two one-seeded halves.

Anthriscus toletana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can attract beneficial insects.
