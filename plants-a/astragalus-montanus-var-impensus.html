Astragalus montanus var. impensus

Astragalus montanus var. impensus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus montanus var. impensus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
