Anchusa azurea var. kurdica

Anchusa azurea var. kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It features striking cobalt-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and role in local ecosystems.
