Acacia littoralis

Acacia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that are highly visible and attract a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods, which split open to release the seeds when mature.

Acacia littoralis is adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden environments, making it a hardy species found along coastal dunes and cliffs.
