Aspidium cadieri

Aspidium cadieri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Aspidium cadieri is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
