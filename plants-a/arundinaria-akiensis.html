Arundinaria akiensis

Arundinaria akiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Aki bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, forming a clump-like structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue. Arundinaria akiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and structural qualities.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
