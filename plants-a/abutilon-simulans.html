Abutilon simulans

Abutilon simulans, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated, orange-red capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
