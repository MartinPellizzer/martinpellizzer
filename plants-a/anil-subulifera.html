Anil subulifera

Anil subulifera, commonly known as the needleleaf anil, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, arid conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
