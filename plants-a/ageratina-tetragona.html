Ageratina tetragona

Ageratina tetragona, commonly known as the four-cornered ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in compact clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fluffy flower heads, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
