Achillea nobilis var. paucidentata

Achillea nobilis var. paucidentata, commonly known as the lesser yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear basal leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is attributed to its aromatic compounds and anti-inflammatory properties.
