Astragalus echinus var. virens

Astragalus echinus var. virens, commonly known as the green-hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems covered in fine, greenish hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its compound leaves are pinnately divided, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and along rocky slopes.
