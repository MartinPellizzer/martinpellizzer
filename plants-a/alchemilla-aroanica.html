Alchemilla aroanica

Alchemilla aroanica, commonly known as silverleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing properties.

Alchemilla aroanica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
