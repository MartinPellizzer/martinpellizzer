Arabis magellanica

Arabis magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and high-altitude regions.
