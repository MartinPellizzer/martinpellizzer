Allium weichanicum

Allium weichanicum, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of strap-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces a tall flowering stem with umbels of white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of its ecosystem.
