Atriplex besseriana

Atriplex besseriana, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it survive in saline soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its fibrous roots enable it to access water from deep underground, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Atriplex besseriana is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and reduce erosion.
