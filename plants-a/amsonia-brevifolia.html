Amsonia brevifolia

Amsonia brevifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Amsonia brevifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
