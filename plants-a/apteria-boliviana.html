Apteria boliviana

Apteria boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian leafless mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the Andean region of South America.

It lacks true leaves and instead absorbs water and nutrients through its stems and roots that are embedded in the host. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its parasitic nature allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments by drawing resources from its host.

Due to its specialized growth habits, it plays a unique role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
