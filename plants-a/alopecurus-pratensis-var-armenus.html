Alopecurus pratensis var. armenus

Alopecurus pratensis var. armenus, commonly known as the Armenian meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
