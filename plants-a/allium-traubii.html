Allium traubii

Allium traubii, commonly known as the Traub's garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, narrow, grass-like leaves and umbels of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Allium traubii thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rocky or alpine gardens.
