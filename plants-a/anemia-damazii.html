Anemia damazii

Anemia damazii, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of white, purple, and pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anemia damazii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
