Acacia aroma var. cochlearis

Acacia aroma var. cochlearis, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in globular heads. The plant produces flat, brown pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
