Allium dilutum var. majus

Allium dilutum var. majus, commonly known as the giant bulbous onion, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a valuable crop in some regions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.
