Astragalus triflorus var. morans

Astragalus triflorus var. morans, commonly known as the Morans' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces pods that contain small, dark seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
