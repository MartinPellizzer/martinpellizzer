Acalypha stellata

Acalypha stellata, commonly known as the starleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts that add to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Acalypha stellata is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
