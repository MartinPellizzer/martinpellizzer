Adesmia loudonia

Adesmia loudonia, commonly known as the Loudonia legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, with a spurred petal that attracts pollinators.

Adesmia loudonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in soil enrichment.
