Andropogon saccharoides var. imperatoides

Andropogon saccharoides var. imperatoides, commonly known as the purple top grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and distinctive purple-tinted inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
