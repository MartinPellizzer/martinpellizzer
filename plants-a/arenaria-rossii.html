Arenaria rossii

Arenaria rossii, commonly known as Ross's sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

Arenaria rossii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
