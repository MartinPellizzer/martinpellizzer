Acaena multifida var. minor

Acaena multifida var. minor, commonly known as the lesser New Zealand spinach, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, spiny leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, berry-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant or for its potential medicinal properties.
