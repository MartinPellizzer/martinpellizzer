Aspalathus divaricata subsp. divaricata

Aspalathus divaricata subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South Africa. It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its stout, unbranched stems and large, pinnately compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
