Arrhenatherum murcicum

Arrhenatherum murcicum, commonly known as the Murcia grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid environments.
