Alyxia floribunda

Alyxia floribunda, commonly known as the showy alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a woody vine characterized by its rapid growth and abundant clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elliptic leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Alyxia floribunda is often used in ornamental horticulture for its showy display and adaptability to various soil types.
