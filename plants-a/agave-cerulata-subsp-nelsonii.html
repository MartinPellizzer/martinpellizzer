Agave cerulata subsp. nelsonii

Agave cerulata subsp.

nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of desert plants.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
