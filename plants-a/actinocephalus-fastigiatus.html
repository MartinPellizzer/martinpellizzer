Actinocephalus fastigiatus

Actinocephalus fastigiatus, commonly known as the slender rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions.

Actinocephalus fastigiatus has simple, lanceolate leaves that are alternate in arrangement, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.
