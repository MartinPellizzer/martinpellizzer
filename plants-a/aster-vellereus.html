Aster vellereus

Aster vellereus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its showy blue to purple flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster vellereus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
