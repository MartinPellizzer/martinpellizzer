Alona phylicifolia

Alona phylicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and prefers full sun. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Alona phylicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
