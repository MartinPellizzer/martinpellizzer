Aconitum ningwuense

Aconitum ningwuense, commonly known as Ningwu aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces follicles containing toxic alkaloids, which are responsible for its potent medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxicity, it requires careful handling and is used in traditional medicine under strict supervision.
