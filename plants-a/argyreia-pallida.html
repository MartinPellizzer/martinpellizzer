Argyreia pallida

Argyreia pallida, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Argyreia pallida is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
