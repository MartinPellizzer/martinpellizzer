Aridaria pomonae

Aridaria pomonae, commonly known as the desert apple tree, is a unique plant adapted to arid environments.

It features thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during brief periods of rainfall. Its fruit, resembling a small apple, contains a high concentration of sugars and is edible.

Aridaria pomonae plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and providing sustenance to local wildlife.
