Aulomyrcia glandulosa var. longifolia

Aulomyrcia glandulosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved glandular aulomyrcia, is a shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves, which are often covered in small, sticky secretory structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as montane forests. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive fragrance.

The variety longifolia is distinguished by its longer leaves compared to the typical form of the species.
