Acanthus dioscoridis var. brevicaulis

Acanthus dioscoridis var. brevicaulis, commonly known as the dwarf acanthus, is a compact, ornamental plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, palmate leaves with a silvery-white underside and produces spires of violet-blue flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its short stature, typically growing to about 30 cm in height, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

This variety is a hardy perennial, often used for its decorative foliage and subtle floral display.
