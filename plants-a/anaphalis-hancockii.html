Anaphalis hancockii

Anaphalis hancockii, commonly known as the Hancock's frostflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, woolly inflorescence. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
