Arrabidaea brachypoda var. attenuata

Arrabidaea brachypoda var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrabidaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and in wetlands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its flowering inflorescences.

It is often found in wetland ecosystems and plays a role in maintaining local biodiversity.
