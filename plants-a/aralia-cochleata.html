Aralia cochleata

Aralia cochleata, commonly known as American aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive, palmate shape, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aralia cochleata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in woodland gardens.
