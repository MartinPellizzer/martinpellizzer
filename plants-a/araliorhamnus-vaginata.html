Araliorhamnus vaginata

Araliorhamnus vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its adaptability, it can become invasive in certain regions.
