Acacia graciliformis

Acacia graciliformis, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its delicate, feathery foliage and produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia graciliformis is typically found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
