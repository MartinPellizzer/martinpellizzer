Acacia ridleyana

Acacia ridleyana, commonly known as the ridge acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

Acacia ridleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
