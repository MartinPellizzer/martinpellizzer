Allium fimbriatum var. fimbriatum

Allium fimbriatum var. fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive fringed appearance. The plant is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical bulbs that store nutrients for growth. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
