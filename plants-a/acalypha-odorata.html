Acalypha odorata

Acalypha odorata, commonly known as the "Copperleaf" or "Redleaf," is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that range from red to orange, often with contrasting green veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Acalypha odorata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is popular as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
