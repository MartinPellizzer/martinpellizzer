Avenula lodunensis subsp. gaditana

Avenula lodunensis subsp.

gaditana, commonly known as the Galician sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color. It is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and supporting local biodiversity.
