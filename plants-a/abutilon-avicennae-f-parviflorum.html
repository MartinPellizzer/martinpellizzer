Abutilon avicennae f. parviflorum

Abutilon avicennae f. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered Chinese Abutilon, is a variant of the Abutilon genus native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and unique flowering habit.
