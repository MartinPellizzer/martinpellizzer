Asclepias quinqueflora

Asclepias quinqueflora, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It features striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It is a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and produces capsule fruits that contain toxic, sticky seeds.

Asclepias quinqueflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
