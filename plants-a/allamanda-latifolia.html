Allamanda latifolia

Allamanda latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf allamanda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, broad leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 6 meters tall, making it a striking addition to gardens.

Allamanda latifolia thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
