Amaranthus pubescens

Amaranthus pubescens, commonly known as rough amaranth, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are small and edible, often used in traditional cuisines.

Amaranthus pubescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to poor soil conditions.
