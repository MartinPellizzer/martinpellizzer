Aquilegia vulgaris var. sulphurea

Aquilegia vulgaris var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur yellow columbine, is a striking perennial plant native to Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
