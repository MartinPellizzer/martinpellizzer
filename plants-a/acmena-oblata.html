Acmena oblata

Acmena oblata, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Acmena oblata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping.
