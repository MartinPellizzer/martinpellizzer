Arisaema triphyllum lusus bispadiceum

Arisaema triphyllum lusus bispadiceum, commonly known as the spadix arum, is a unique plant with a striking botanical feature of its spadix inflorescence.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its modified leaves called spatulae that enclose the spadix. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and produces a single, large, white or pale green spathe that surrounds the spadix. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers located on the spadix.

The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
