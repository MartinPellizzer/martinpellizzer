Actinodaphne cambodiana

Actinodaphne cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian laurier.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia and parts of Vietnam. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. Its leaves are typically elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Morphology

Actinodaphne cambodiana has simple, elliptic to ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter green underside.

The stem is smooth and cylindrical, with a grayish-brown bark that becomes rougher with age, and it grows upright with a branching pattern. The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, featuring yellowish-white petals and stamens, blooming during the dry season, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a globose drupe with a single seed, dispersed by animals that consume the fleshy pulp.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network, anchoring the plant and aiding in nutrient absorption.
