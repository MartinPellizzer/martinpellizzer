Astragalus reventus var. conjunctus

Astragalus reventus var. conjunctus, commonly known as the jointed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets that help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
