Alchemilla pastoralis f. praticola

Alchemilla pastoralis f. praticola, commonly known as the common lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to spread through rhizomes.
