Ampelopsis aconitifolia

Ampelopsis aconitifolia, commonly known as the blue mist vine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. Its berries are dark blue or black and are often consumed by birds.

Ampelopsis aconitifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
