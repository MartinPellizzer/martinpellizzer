Argyranthemum pinnatifidum subsp. montanum

Argyranthemum pinnatifidum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the mountain arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
