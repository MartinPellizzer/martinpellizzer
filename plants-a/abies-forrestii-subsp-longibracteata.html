Abies forrestii subsp. longibracteata

Abies forrestii subsp.

longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract fir, is a subspecies of the fir tree native to the mountainous regions of western China. It is characterized by its conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a flat spruce-like pattern.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated bracts on the cones, which are more pronounced than in other Abies species. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist climates and well-drained soils.

This plant plays a vital ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
