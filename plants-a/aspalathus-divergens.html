Aspalathus divergens

Aspalathus divergens, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and produces small, white to pinkish flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Aspalathus divergens is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
