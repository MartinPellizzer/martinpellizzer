Abies excelsa var. attenuata

Abies excelsa var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender white fir, is a variant of the white fir species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and long, slender needles that are typically a dark green color. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It has a slow growth rate and is often found in mountainous areas with poor soil conditions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
