Aster foliaceus var. subgeminatus

Aster foliaceus var. subgeminatus, commonly known as the twinleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in clusters, with small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in pairs along the stem, giving it its characteristic appearance. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
