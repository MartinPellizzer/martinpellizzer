Astragalus glaberrimus

Astragalus glaberrimus, commonly known as the smooth milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, leathery pods that contain seeds.

Astragalus glaberrimus is often found in sagebrush steppe and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in these ecosystems.
