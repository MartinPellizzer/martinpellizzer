Alternanthera paronychioides

Alternanthera paronychioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redleaf plant, is a striking aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that can range from red to pink, depending on light exposure and nutrient levels. The plant thrives in warm, shallow waters and is often used in ornamental pond settings. Its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is a popular choice for aquariums and water gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
