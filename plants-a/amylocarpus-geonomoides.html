Amylocarpus geonomoides

Amylocarpus geonomoides, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are rich in starch. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with a spathe and spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are typically broad and arrow-shaped, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued both for its edible tubers and its ornamental qualities.
