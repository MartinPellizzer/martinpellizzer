Avena rubrotincta

Avena rubrotincta, commonly known as red-stained oat, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish bracts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue.

Avena rubrotincta is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
