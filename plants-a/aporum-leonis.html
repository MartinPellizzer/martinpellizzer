Aporum leonis

Aporum leonis, commonly known as the lion's tail, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of small, waxy, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiky seed pods that turn red as they mature, adding visual interest to its appearance. Aporum leonis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage and flowering pattern make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
