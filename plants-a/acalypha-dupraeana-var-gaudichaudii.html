Acalypha dupraeana var. gaudichaudii

Acalypha dupraeana var. gaudichaudii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red leaf acalypha, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to purple in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth texture and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions.
