Achillea tenorei

Achillea tenorei, commonly known as the Tuscan yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.
