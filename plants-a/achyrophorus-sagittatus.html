Achyrophorus sagittatus

Achyrophorus sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
