Androcorys ophioglossoides

Androcorys ophioglossoides, commonly known as the broad-leaved harts-tongue fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a unique reproductive structure, with sporangia clustered in sori on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist forest floors and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
