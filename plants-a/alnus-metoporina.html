Alnus metoporina

Alnus metoporina, commonly known as the red alder, is a deciduous tree native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in catkins. The plant has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
