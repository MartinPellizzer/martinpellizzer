Antiphylla coryphaea

Antiphylla coryphaea, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Antiphylla coryphaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
