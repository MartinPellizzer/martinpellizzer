Aster jamesii

Aster jamesii, commonly known as the Great Basin aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. It has narrow, gray-green leaves and a compact, upright growth habit.

Aster jamesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
