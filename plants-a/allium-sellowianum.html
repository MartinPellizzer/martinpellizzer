Allium sellowianum

Allium sellowianum, commonly known as the purple onion or Brazilian onion, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It features tall, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of purple or pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its small, round bulbs that are often used in cooking. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and edible, adding value to both culinary and horticultural practices.
