Arabis stelleri f. calvescens

Arabis stelleri f. calvescens, commonly known as the Steller's rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of elliptic to ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It is native to alpine and rocky habitats in Europe and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
