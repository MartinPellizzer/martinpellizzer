Achillea ligustica

Achillea ligustica, commonly known as the Ligurian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads composed of numerous small white to pale yellow florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Achillea ligustica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
