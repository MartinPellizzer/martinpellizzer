Astragalus akmanii

Astragalus akmanii, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces cylindrical, segmented pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

Astragalus akmanii plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
