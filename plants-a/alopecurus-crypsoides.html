Alopecurus crypsoides

Alopecurus crypsoides, commonly known as the creeping hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths on the ground, often in moist or wet habitats such as meadows, ditches, and marshes. The plant has fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily and form extensive colonies.

Alopecurus crypsoides is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
