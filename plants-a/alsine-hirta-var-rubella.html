Alsine hirta var. rubella

Alsine hirta var. rubella, commonly known as red hairy alsike, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
