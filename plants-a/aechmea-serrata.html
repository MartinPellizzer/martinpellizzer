Aechmea serrata

Aechmea serrata, commonly known as the serrated pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are stiff and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with bright red bracts that house small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a decorative plant in many regions.

Morphology

Aechmea serrata has strap-shaped, sword-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, with a waxy, bluish-green color and a serrated edge.

The plant has a short, thick stem that is covered in a fibrous, pale green bark and grows upright from the base. Its flowers are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence, with vibrant red bracts that enclose small, white or pale yellow flowers, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that matures into a dark purple color, dispersing seeds through animal consumption and wind.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network around the base of the plant, helping to anchor it and absorb moisture.
