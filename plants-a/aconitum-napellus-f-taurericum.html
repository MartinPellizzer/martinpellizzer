Aconitum napellus f. taurericum

Aconitum napellus f. taurericum, commonly known as the Tauric monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region, characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
