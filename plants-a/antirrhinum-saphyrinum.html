Antirrhinum saphyrinum

Antirrhinum saphyrinum, commonly known as the Sapphire Snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and arranged in spikes. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged opposite along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Antirrhinum saphyrinum is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of care.
