Aconitum czekanovskyi var. sphagnicola

Aconitum czekanovskyi var. sphagnicola, commonly known as the sphagnicola variety of Czekanovsky's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafy stems that bear showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are clustered in racemes at the top of the stem.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
