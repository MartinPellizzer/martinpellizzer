Apocynum umbellatum

Apocynum umbellatum, commonly known as Indian paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. The plant features showy, red-orange bracted flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The plant contains toxic cardiac glycosides, which make it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
