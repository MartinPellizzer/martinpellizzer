Alsophila indrapurae

Alsophila indrapurae, commonly known as the Indrapura tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in lowland forests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall stem, while its fronds are used for their ornamental value.

Alsophila indrapurae plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
