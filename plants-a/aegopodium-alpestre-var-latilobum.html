Aegopodium alpestre var. latilobum

Aegopodium alpestre var. latilobum, commonly known as Alpine ground-ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a creeping growth habit with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes.

Its leaves and stems contain compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine.
