Alsophila gigantea

Alsophila gigantea, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a striking member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a large, feathery frond crown that can reach impressive heights. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, meaning they have a complex, feather-like structure with multiple subdivisions.

Alsophila gigantea thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along coastal areas.
