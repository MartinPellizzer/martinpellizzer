Atriplex chizae

Atriplex chizae, commonly known as saltbush, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves. The plant typically grows in saline soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits containing seeds.

Atriplex chizae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
