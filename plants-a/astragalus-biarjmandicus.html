Astragalus biarjmandicus

Astragalus biarjmandicus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran, where it thrives in sandy and rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus biarjmandicus is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique ecological and potential medicinal properties.
