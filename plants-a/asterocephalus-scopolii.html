Asterocephalus scopolii

Asterocephalus scopolii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing as if they are floating in the air. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Asterocephalus scopolii is known for its unique appearance and is often considered a symbol of resilience in its ecosystem.
