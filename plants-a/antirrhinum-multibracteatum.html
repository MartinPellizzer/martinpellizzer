Antirrhinum multibracteatum

Antirrhinum multibracteatum, commonly known as the multibracteatum snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of brightly colored, tubular flowers that range from red to orange. The plant produces numerous bracts, which are modified leaves that often resemble petals, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
