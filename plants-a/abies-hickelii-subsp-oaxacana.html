Abies hickelii subsp. oaxacana

Abies hickelii subsp.

oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Mexico. It belongs to the genus Abies, which includes other fir species characterized by their needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structures.

This subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, slender needles. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often growing in cloud forests.

The bark is typically reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
