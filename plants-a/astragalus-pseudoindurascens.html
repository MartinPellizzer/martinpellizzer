Astragalus pseudoindurascens

Astragalus pseudoindurascens, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it access groundwater.

Astragalus pseudoindurascens plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
