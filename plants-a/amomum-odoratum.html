Amomum odoratum

Amomum odoratum, commonly known as Chinese cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers followed by dark red berries. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for their warming and digestive properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary value.
