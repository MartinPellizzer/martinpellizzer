Astragalus andalanicus var. ovarts

Astragalus andalanicus var. ovarts, commonly known as the Andalusian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its root system is known for producing a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Astragalus.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
