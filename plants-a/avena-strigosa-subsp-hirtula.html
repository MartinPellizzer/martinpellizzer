Avena strigosa subsp. hirtula

Avena strigosa subsp.

hirtula, commonly known as hairy oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and spikelets that bear small, spike-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and have a distinctive blue-green color.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for erosion control in landscaping.
