Aspidium filix-mas var. fuscoatrum

Aspidium filix-mas var. fuscoatrum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a leathery texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
