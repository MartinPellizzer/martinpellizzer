Aconitum napellus f. meyeri

Aconitum napellus f. meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple hooded flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically deeply divided. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
