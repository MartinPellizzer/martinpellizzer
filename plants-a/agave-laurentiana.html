Agave laurentiana

Agave laurentiana, commonly known as the Laurentian agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like rosette of thick, blue-green leaves that are often lined with white or yellow marginal teeth. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer or early fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
