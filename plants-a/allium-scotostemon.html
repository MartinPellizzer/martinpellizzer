Allium scotostemon

Allium scotostemon, commonly known as the narrowleaf garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and clusters of small, spherical, purple or pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in taste to garlic and chives, making it a valuable addition to culinary gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Allium scotostemon is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
