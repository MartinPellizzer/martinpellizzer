Arrabidaea seleriana

Arrabidaea seleriana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and its ability to thrive in poor soils. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its durable timber.

The species also plays an important ecological role in its native environment.
