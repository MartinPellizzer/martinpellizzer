Agave victoriae-reginae f. viridis

Agave victoriae-reginae f. viridis, commonly known as the Green Queen agave, is a unique variant of the Victoria's Queen agave, characterized by its striking greenish-gray foliage.

This succulent plant is native to the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in rocky soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, often forming a rosette shape that helps it conserve water. The plant produces tall, spiky flower stalks that bloom with yellowish-green flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a prized plant among collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
