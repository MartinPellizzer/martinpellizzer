Acrostichum sprucei

Acrostichum sprucei, commonly known as the dwarf chain fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a rhizomatous growth system, with fronds that are typically narrow and strap-like. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This fern is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
