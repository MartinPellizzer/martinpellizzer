Acacia trichodes

Acacia trichodes, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, almost velvety appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Acacia trichodes is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its wood is sometimes used for fuel or small crafts, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
