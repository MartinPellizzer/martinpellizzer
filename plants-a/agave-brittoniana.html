Agave brittoniana

Agave brittoniana, commonly known as the Britton's agave, is a striking succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, bluish-green leaves that are lined with white teeth, giving it a unique and rugged appearance. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its striking foliage and architectural form make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
