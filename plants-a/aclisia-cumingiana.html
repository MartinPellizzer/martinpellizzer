Aclisia cumingiana

Aclisia cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces flat, leathery pods containing seeds.

Aclisia cumingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
