Acer pictum f. albomaculatum

Acer pictum f. albomaculatum, commonly known as the white-spotted maple, is a unique variant of the striped maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive white spots on the leaves, which contrast with its green foliage. The plant is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 15 feet.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
