Agathosma tabularis

Agathosma tabularis, commonly known as the Cape gum bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat. It is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are valued for their aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
