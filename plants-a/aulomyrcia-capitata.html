Aulomyrcia capitata

Aulomyrcia capitata, commonly known as the Cape coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered at the ends of branches. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Aulomyrcia capitata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
