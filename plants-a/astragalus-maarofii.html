Astragalus maarofii

Astragalus maarofii, commonly known as the Maarof's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
