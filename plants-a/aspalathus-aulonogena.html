Aspalathus aulonogena

Aspalathus aulonogena, commonly known as the yellow bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep root systems. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

Aspalathus aulonogena is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
