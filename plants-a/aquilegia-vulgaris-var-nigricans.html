Aquilegia vulgaris var. nigricans

Aquilegia vulgaris var. nigricans, commonly known as the black cohosh, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are palmately arranged.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
