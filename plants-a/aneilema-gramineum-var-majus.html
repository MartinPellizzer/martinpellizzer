Aneilema gramineum var. majus

Aneilema gramineum var. majus, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, often adorned with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are similar in texture to yams. Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to moist, shaded environments and its use in traditional agriculture.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and nutritional content.
