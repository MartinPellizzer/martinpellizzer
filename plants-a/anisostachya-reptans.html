Anisostachya reptans

Anisostachya reptans, commonly known as the creeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes and form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed bracts. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Anisostachya reptans is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
