Ampelopsis aegirophylla

Ampelopsis aegirophylla, commonly known as the red-leaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the summer, followed by orange-red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
