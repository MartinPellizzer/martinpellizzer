Acacia papuliformis

Acacia papuliformis, commonly known as the papular acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Acacia papuliformis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance through its succulent stems and reduced leaf surface.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
