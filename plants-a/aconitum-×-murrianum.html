Aconitum × murrianum

Aconitum × murrianum, commonly known as the hybrid monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring large, palmately lobed leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful to humans and animals, requiring careful handling.
