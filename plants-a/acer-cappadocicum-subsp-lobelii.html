Acer cappadocicum subsp. lobelii

Acer cappadocicum subsp.

lobelii, commonly known as the Cappadocian maple, is a deciduous tree native to Turkey. It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically 5-7 lobes and turn vibrant red in the fall.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
