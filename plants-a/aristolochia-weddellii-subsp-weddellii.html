Aristolochia weddellii subsp. weddellii

Aristolochia weddellii subsp.

weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that contain the seeds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
