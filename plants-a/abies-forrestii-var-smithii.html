Abies forrestii var. smithii

Abies forrestii var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's fir, is a rare and highly sought-after variant of the Himalayan fir.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of Nepal and Bhutan, where it thrives in cool, moist, and high-altitude environments. This variant is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted. Its cones are typically reddish-brown and release seeds in the fall, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
