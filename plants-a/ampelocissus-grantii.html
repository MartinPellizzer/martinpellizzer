Ampelocissus grantii

Ampelocissus grantii, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a vigorous vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, fig-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its strong climbing habit and can grow up to 10 meters in length. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its lush foliage and edible fruits, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
