Aster glabrifolius

Aster glabrifolius, commonly known as the smoothleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are smooth and lance-shaped, and it produces showy purple to blue flowers in the fall.

Aster glabrifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
