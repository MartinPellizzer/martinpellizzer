Arundo poiformis

Arundo poiformis, commonly known as the bottlebrush reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive bottle-shaped inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant produces dense clusters of bright red or orange bracts that resemble bottle brushes, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant landscapes.

Arundo poiformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
