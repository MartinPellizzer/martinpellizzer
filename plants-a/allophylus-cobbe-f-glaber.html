Allophylus cobbe f. glaber

Allophylus cobbe f. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-leaved alderleaf, is a variant of the Allophylus cobbe species native to tropical regions.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves. The plant typically has dark green, leathery foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The botanical features of this plant make it suitable for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
