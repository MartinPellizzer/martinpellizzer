Apium involucratum

Apium involucratum, commonly known as the clustered celery or narrow-leaved wild celery, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered umbels of white flowers. Its leaves are finely divided and often have a mild, celery-like scent.

Apium involucratum is typically found in moist, grassy areas and can grow up to 1 meter in height.
