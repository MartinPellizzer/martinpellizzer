Adenophyllum cancellatum

Adenophyllum cancellatum, commonly known as the laceleaf adenophyllum, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, finely divided leaves that give it a lacy appearance, which is a defining botanical characteristic. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

Its unique foliage makes it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
