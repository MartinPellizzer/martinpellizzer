Aspidium conterminum var. concinnum

Aspidium conterminum var. concinnum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to various regions in Asia.

It is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.

The leaves are usually submerged or floating, contributing to the plant's role in aquatic ecosystems.
