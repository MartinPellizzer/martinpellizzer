Allochrusa tadshikistanica

Allochrusa tadshikistanica, commonly known as the Tadzhikistan alpine grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Allochrusa tadshikistanica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and supports local biodiversity.
