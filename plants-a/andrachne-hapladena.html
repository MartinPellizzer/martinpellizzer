Andrachne hapladena

Andrachne hapladena, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in harsh climates.

The species is notable for its resilience and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
