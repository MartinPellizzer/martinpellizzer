Anthemis tinctoria subsp. fussii

Anthemis tinctoria subsp.

fussii, commonly known as the yellow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies is typically found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. The plant produces small, elongated seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
