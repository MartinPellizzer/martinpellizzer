Anthemis rigida subsp. ammanthiformis

Anthemis rigida subsp.

ammanthiformis, commonly known as the rigid flossflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly serrated margin, and the entire plant is often covered in a dense layer of fine, fluffy hairs.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands.
