Adina thanhoaensis

Adina thanhoaensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese Adina, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a distinct aromatic scent. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, round drupe with a single seed, typical of many Rubiaceae species.
