Astragalus wilmottianus

Astragalus wilmottianus, commonly known as Wilmott's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Astragalus wilmottianus is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and its potential medicinal properties.
