Ageratella microphylla var. sonorana

Ageratella microphylla var. sonorana, commonly known as the Sonoran mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its small, silver-gray leaves and clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
