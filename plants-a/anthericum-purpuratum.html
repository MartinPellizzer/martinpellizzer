Anthericum purpuratum

Anthericum purpuratum, commonly known as the purple nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking purple bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, stinging hairs.

Anthericum purpuratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and botanical collections.
