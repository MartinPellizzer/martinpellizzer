Alchemilla sergii

Alchemilla sergii, commonly known as the Armenian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
