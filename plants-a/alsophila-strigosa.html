Alsophila strigosa

Alsophila strigosa, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and helps anchor the plant in moist, well-drained soil.

Alsophila strigosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can contribute to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
