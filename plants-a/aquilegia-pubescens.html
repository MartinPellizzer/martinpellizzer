Aquilegia pubescens

Aquilegia pubescens, commonly known as the downy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features compound, trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, which is a distinguishing trait. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
