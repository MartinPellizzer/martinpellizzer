Acalypha caroliniana

Acalypha caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina beauty, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.
