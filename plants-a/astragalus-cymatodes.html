Astragalus cymatodes

Astragalus cymatodes, commonly known as the curlyhead milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its stems are often sparsely hairy. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Astragalus cymatodes is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
