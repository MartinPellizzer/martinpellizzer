Anacamptis laxiflora subsp. dielsiana

Anacamptis laxiflora subsp.

dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' spider orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale green to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

The flowers are pollinated by specific bee species, making it an important component of its local ecosystem.
