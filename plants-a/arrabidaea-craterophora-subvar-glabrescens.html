Arrabidaea craterophora subvar. glabrescens

Arrabidaea craterophora subvar. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed arrabidaea, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland habitats. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and have a lanceolate shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in terminal panicles, and are usually pink or reddish in color.

This subspecies is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and is often found in association with other understory plants in shaded environments.
