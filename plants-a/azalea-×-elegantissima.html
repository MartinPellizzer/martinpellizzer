Azalea × elegantissima

Azalea × elegantissima, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with vibrant shades of pink, red, and white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate climates.
