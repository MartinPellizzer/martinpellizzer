Alstroemeria tomentosa

Alstroemeria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its botanical name reflects its woolly appearance, with "tomentosa" meaning covered in fine hairs.

Alstroemeria tomentosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
