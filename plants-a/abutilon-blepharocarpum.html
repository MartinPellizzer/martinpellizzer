Abutilon blepharocarpum

Abutilon blepharocarpum, commonly known as the "earleaf abutilon," is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces unique, inflated seed pods that split open to release seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
