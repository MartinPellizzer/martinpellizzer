Anemone alpina var. flavescens

Anemone alpina var. flavescens, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves that are adapted to cold, alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, rocky slopes, and alpine tundra.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
