Anguloa squalida

Anguloa squalida, commonly known as the squalid anguloa, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are usually dark green and lance-shaped, contributing to its overall striking appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species.
