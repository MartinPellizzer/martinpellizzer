Acalypha berteroana

Acalypha berteroana, commonly known as the red bower vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Acalypha berteroana is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
