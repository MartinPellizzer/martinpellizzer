Arecastrum

Arecastrum, commonly known as the false areca palm, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Arecaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to tropical regions of South America. The plants typically have a short trunk and produce feather-like leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. Arecastrum species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their attractive foliage.

They thrive in warm, humid climates and require well-draining soil for optimal growth.
