Agave serrulata

Agave serrulata, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, blue-gray leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces tall flower stalks that bear small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional practices.
