Agapanthus variegatus

Agapanthus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear large, showy blue or purple flower clusters, which bloom in summer. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Agapanthus variegatus is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
