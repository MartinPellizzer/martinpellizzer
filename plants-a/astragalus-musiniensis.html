Astragalus musiniensis

Astragalus musiniensis, commonly known as the Musiniense milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has hairy stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus musiniensis is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
