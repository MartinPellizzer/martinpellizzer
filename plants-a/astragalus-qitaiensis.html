Astragalus qitaiensis

Astragalus qitaiensis, commonly known as the Qitai milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, compound leaves, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Astragalus qitaiensis is also of interest in botanical research due to its potential medicinal properties.
