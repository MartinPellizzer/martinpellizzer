Amaryllis × henryae

Amaryllis × henryae, commonly known as the Henry's amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis andrae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically produces strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and pointed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Amaryllis × henryae is known for its ability to bloom reliably when provided with proper care and conditions.
