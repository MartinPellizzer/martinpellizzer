Aristida flabellata

Aristida flabellata, commonly known as the flat-panied grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish spikelets that are arranged in dense, panicle-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Aristida flabellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
