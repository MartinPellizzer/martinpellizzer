Ajax moschatus var. albicans

Ajax moschatus var. albicans, commonly known as the white-scented ajuga, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, which are arranged in dense clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its aromatic foliage and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
