Avena bromoides subvar. hackelii

Avena bromoides subvar. hackelii, commonly known as Hackel's oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelet clusters that bear small, two-lobed lemmas. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

It produces wind-pollinated flowers and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
