Ammodaucus leucotrichus subsp. nanocarpus

Ammodaucus leucotrichus subsp.

nanocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-fruit fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbels.

The plant produces small, narrow fruits that are typically less than 1 cm in length, distinguishing it from other members of the genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
