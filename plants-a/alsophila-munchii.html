Alsophila munchii

Alsophila munchii, commonly known as the Munch's tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Alsophila munchii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.
