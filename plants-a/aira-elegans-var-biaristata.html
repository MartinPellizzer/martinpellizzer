Aira elegans var. biaristata

Aira elegans var. biaristata, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silvery-blue foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that add visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and erosion control.
