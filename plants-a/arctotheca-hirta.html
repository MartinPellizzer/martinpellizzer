Arctotheca hirta

Arctotheca hirta, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
