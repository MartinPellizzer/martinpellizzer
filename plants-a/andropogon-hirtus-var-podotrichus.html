Andropogon hirtus var. podotrichus

Andropogon hirtus var. podotrichus, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and bluish-green foliage, with hairy nodes along its stem. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common species in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
