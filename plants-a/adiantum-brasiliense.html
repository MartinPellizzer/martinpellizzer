Adiantum brasiliense

Adiantum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a distinctive black midrib. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rock faces or in moist soil. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a lacy appearance.

Adiantum brasiliense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
