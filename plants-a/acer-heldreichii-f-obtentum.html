Acer heldreichii f. obtentum

Acer heldreichii f. obtentum, commonly known as the Balkan maple, is a variant of the Heldreich maple, characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, including parts of Greece and the former Yugoslavia. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a vibrant green color during the growing season. In autumn, they turn a striking red or orange, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

This species is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for horticultural use.
