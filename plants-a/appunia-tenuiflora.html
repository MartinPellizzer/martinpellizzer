Appunia tenuiflora

Appunia tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered appunia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Appunia tenuiflora is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
