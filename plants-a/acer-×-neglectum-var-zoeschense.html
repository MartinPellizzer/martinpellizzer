Acer × neglectum var. zoeschense

Acer × neglectum var. zoeschense, commonly known as the Zoeschens maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree that exhibits a range of colorful foliage throughout the growing season.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, with leaves that display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall. The plant typically grows to a moderate height, making it suitable for smaller gardens or as a specimen tree. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

The Zoeschens maple is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.
