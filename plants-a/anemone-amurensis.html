Anemone amurensis

Anemone amurensis, commonly known as the Amur anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has hairy, basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Anemone amurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
