Aster occidentalis var. typicus

Aster occidentalis var. typicus, commonly known as the western aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall, typically in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
