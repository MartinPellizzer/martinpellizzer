Antennaria carpatica var. humilis

Antennaria carpatica var. humilis, commonly known as the carpet bugle or lesser mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense clusters of small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
