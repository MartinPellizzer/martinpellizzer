Ascolepis pusilla var. echinata

Ascolepis pusilla var. echinata, commonly known as the spiny pincushion, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, strap-like leaves that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and compact growth habit make it an attractive addition to botanical collections.
