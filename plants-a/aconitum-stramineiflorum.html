Aconitum stramineiflorum

Aconitum stramineiflorum, commonly known as the yellow aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in July and August. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Aconitum stramineiflorum is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its toxic properties due to the presence of various alkaloids.
