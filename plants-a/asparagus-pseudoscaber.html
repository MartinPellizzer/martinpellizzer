Asparagus pseudoscaber

Asparagus pseudoscaber, commonly known as the rough asparagus, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, spiky berries. Asparagus pseudoscaber is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, preferring full sun exposure.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
