Acineta superba var. fulva

Acineta superba var. fulva, commonly known as the golden acineta, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery down.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
