Aster canescens var. latifolius

Aster canescens var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a distinctive silvery underside.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
