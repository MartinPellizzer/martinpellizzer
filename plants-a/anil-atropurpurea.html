Anil atropurpurea

Anil atropurpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features striking violet to purple petals surrounding a prominent, cone-shaped center. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
