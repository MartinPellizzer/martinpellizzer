Agiortia

Agiortia, commonly known as the Greek wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal plants. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance.

Agiortia is often used in traditional herbal remedies due to its purported health benefits.
