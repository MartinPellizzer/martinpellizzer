Acacia albicorticata

Acacia albicorticata, commonly known as the white bark acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, pale grey bark and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow, globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in long, cylindrical pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia albicorticata is adapted to arid environments and is commonly found in savannas and rocky areas.
