Acetosella dichondrifolia

Acetosella dichondrifolia, commonly known as the lesser sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple hue. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Acetosella dichondrifolia is also known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid.
