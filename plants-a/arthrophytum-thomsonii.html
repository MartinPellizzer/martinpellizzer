Arthrophytum thomsonii

Arthrophytum thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, translucent leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to arid environments and can store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
