Abies nephrolepis subsp. sachalinensis

Abies nephrolepis subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin fir, is a rare conifer native to the Russian Far East. It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The plant has a distinct subsp. status due to its unique morphological features compared to the main species, A. nephrolepis. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its cones are typically reddish-brown and release seeds in the autumn.
