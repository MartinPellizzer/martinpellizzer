Artemisia korshinskyi

Artemisia korshinskyi, commonly known as big sagebrush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage, which consists of small, aromatic leaves arranged in a dense, bushy structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in composite flower heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Artemisia korshinskyi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
