Arctostaphylos crustacea subsp. subcordata

Arctostaphylos crustacea subsp.

subcordata, commonly known as the subcordate manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces small, red or black berries that are typically consumed by various bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to dry, rocky environments and its role in supporting local wildlife.
