Aridaria subpatens

Aridaria subpatens, commonly known as the desert cushion, is a shrub adapted to arid environments.

It features dense, compact growth with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the brief rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
