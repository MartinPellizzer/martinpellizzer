Acacia rigidula

Acacia rigidula, commonly known as the rigid wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and it produces pods that contain seeds. Acacia rigidula is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
