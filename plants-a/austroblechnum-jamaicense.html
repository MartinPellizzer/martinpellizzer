Austroblechnum jamaicense

Austroblechnum jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brown, hairy scales.

Austroblechnum jamaicense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
