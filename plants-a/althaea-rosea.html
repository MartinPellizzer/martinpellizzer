Althaea rosea

Althaea rosea, commonly known as the rose of Sharon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and the Middle East, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces showy, rose-like flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and can grow up to several feet tall.

Althaea rosea is also valued for its mucilage-rich roots, which have been used in traditional medicine.
