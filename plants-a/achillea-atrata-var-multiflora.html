Achillea atrata var. multiflora

Achillea atrata var. multiflora, commonly known as the dark yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features gray-green, silver-furred foliage and produces clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
