Aregelia farinosa

Aregelia farinosa, commonly known as the meadow germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Aregelia farinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
