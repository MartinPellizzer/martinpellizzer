Acer buergerianum var. ningpoense

Acer buergerianum var. ningpoense, commonly known as the Ningpo maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, red or yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in horticultural landscapes.
