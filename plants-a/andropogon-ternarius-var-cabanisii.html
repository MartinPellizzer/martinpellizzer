Andropogon ternarius var. cabanisii

Andropogon ternarius var. cabanisii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
