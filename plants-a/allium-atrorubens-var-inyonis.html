Allium atrorubens var. inyonis

Allium atrorubens var. inyonis, commonly known as the Iyonis red onion, is a distinctive cultivar of the Allium genus native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its deep red to purple bulbs and strap-like, grassy foliage that grows upright. The plant typically reaches heights of 12 to 24 inches and produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and adaptability to various climates.
