Aframomum meleguetella

Aframomum meleguetella, commonly known as African grain of paradise, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.
