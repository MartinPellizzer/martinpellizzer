Athyrium emeicola

Athyrium emeicola, commonly known as the Emei mountain holly fern, is a species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the Athyriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium emeicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
