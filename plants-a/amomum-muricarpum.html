Amomum muricarpum

Amomum muricarpum, commonly known as black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale green to white flowers, which are followed by dark, aromatic berries. The berries are harvested when mature and dried to develop their distinct spicy flavor.

It is valued in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.
