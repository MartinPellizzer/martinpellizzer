Anzybas montanus

Anzybas montanus, commonly known as the mountain anzybas, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Anzybas montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
