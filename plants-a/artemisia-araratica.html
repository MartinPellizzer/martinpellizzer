Artemisia araratica

Artemisia araratica, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for dry, rocky habitats. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are used in traditional medicine and spiritual practices.

Artemisia araratica plays a significant role in the cultural and ecological landscapes of its native regions.
