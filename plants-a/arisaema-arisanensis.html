Arisaema arisanensis

Arisaema arisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese Taro or Arisan Taro, is a herbaceous plant native to Japan and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually arrow-shaped and can reach up to 30 cm in length, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts.

Arisaema arisanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some Asian cultures.
