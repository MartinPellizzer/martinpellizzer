Aquilegia coerulea subsp. albiflora

Aquilegia coerulea subsp.

albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with five sepals that resemble petals.

The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, growing at elevations between 5,000 and 10,000 feet.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
