Abuta acutifolia

Abuta acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf abuta, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark and roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Abuta acutifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.
