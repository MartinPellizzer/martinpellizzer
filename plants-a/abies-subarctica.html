Abies subarctica

Abies subarctica, commonly known as the Alaska fir, is a coniferous tree native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, inconspicuous cones that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in cold, harsh environments and is adapted to permafrost conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tundra and boreal forest ecosystems.
