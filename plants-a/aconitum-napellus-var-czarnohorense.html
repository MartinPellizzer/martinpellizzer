Aconitum napellus var. czarnohorense

Aconitum napellus var. czarnohorense, commonly known as the Black Mountain Aconite, is a variant of the European aconite native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple spires of tubular flowers in late summer. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant to handle.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with rich, loamy soil.
