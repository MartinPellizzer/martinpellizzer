Astragalus atropilosulus var. elgonensis

Astragalus atropilosulus var. elgonensis, commonly known as the East African milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements make it a rare and ecologically significant species.
