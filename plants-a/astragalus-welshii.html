Astragalus welshii

Astragalus welshii, commonly known as Welsh's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, cylindrical seed pods that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Astragalus welshii plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
