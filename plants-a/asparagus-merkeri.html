Asparagus merkeri

Asparagus merkeri, commonly known as Merker's asparagus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asparagaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found growing in coastal areas or on rocky outcrops.

Asparagus merkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
