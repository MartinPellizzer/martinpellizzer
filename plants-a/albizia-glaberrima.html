Albizia glaberrima

Albizia glaberrima, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate and give the plant a feathery appearance. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in racemes, and are fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
