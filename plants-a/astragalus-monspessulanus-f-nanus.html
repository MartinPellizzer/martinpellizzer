Astragalus monspessulanus f. nanus

Astragalus monspessulanus f. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf Spanish milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in Mediterranean climates. Its compact form makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
