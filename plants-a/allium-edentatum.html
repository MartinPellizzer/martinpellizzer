Allium edentatum

Allium edentatum, commonly known as the purple garlic or wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bulb-like structures that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is known for its strong, pungent odor.

Allium edentatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
