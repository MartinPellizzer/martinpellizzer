Anemone pratensis f. schizocalyx

Anemone pratensis f. schizocalyx, commonly known as the divided-leaved meadow anemone, is a botanical variant of the meadow anemone.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in early spring.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
