Amaryllis × nervifolia

Amaryllis × nervifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papilio.

It is characterized by its strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a contrasting central stripe. It is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
