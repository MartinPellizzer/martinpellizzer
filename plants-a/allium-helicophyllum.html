Allium helicophyllum

Allium helicophyllum, commonly known as the spiral garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valuable addition to culinary traditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
