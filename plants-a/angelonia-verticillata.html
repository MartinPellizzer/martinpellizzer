Angelonia verticillata

Angelonia verticillata, commonly known as the whorled angelonia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.

Angelonia verticillata is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
