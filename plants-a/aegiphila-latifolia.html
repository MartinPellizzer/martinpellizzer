Aegiphila latifolia

Aegiphila latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
