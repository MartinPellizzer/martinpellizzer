Adenophora sublata

Adenophora sublata, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Adenophora sublata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
