Asimina × piedmontana

Asimina × piedmontana, commonly known as the Piedmont native persimmon, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Asimina triloba and Asimina obovata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and the Carolinas. The plant produces large, orange to reddish-purple fruits that are highly aromatic and contain a sweet, mealy pulp. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and turn yellow in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and potential for fruit production in suitable climates.
