Astragalus barba-caprina

Astragalus barba-caprina, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy seed pods. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in early summer, followed by the development of its characteristic beard-like seeds.

Astragalus barba-caprina is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
