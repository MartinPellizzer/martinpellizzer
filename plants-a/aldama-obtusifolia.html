Aldama obtusifolia

Aldama obtusifolia, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The flowers, which range in color from blue to purple, bloom in late spring and early summer.
