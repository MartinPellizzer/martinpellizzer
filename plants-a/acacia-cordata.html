Acacia cordata

Acacia cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, leathery, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense spikes. Acacia cordata is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought tolerant.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
