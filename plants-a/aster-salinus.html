Aster salinus

Aster salinus, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its small, often blue or purple flowers and its ability to thrive in salty soils. The plant has narrow, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh conditions. Aster salinus is a member of the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is often found in coastal areas and deserts.

Its resilience makes it an important plant for stabilizing soil in saline ecosystems.
