Acianthera fenestrata

Acianthera fenestrata, commonly known as the window orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, which features perforations or holes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and delicate, often appearing in clusters, and are usually white or pale pink in color.

Acianthera fenestrata is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
