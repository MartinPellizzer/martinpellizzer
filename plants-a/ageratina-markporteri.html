Ageratina markporteri

Ageratina markporteri, commonly known as the Markporteri blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, fluffy blue or purple flower clusters that resemble mist. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
