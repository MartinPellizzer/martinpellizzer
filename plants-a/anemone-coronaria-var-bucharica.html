Anemone coronaria var. bucharica

Anemone coronaria var. bucharica, commonly known as the Bukhara anemone, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and have a velvety texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
