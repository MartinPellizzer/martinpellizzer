Achillea distans subsp. distans

Achillea distans subsp.

distans, commonly known as the distant yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant features upright, branched stems and basal leaves that are pinnately divided. Its flower heads are composed of small, white to pale yellow florets arranged in flat, disk-like clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
