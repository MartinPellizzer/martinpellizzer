Aetheolaena subinvolucrata

Aetheolaena subinvolucrata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of wetland vegetation.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
