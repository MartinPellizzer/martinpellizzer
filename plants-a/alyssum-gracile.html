Alyssum gracile

Alyssum gracile, commonly known as slender alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum gracile is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
