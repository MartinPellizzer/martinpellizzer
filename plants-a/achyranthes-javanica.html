Achyranthes javanica

Achyranthes javanica, commonly known as the Javan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive, three-lobed, reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its fibrous root system makes it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
