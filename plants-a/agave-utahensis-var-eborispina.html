Agave utahensis var. eborispina

Agave utahensis var. eborispina, commonly known as the Utah spiny agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in sharp spines. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 3 feet in length. This species is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

It produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing yellowish-green flowers.
