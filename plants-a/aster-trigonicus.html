Aster trigonicus

Aster trigonicus, commonly known as the three-lobed aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall.

Aster trigonicus is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
