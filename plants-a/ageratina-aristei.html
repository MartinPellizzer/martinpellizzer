Ageratina aristei

Ageratina aristei, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white inflorescences that resemble clouds, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Ageratina aristei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
