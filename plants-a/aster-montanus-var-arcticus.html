Aster montanus var. arcticus

Aster montanus var. arcticus, commonly known as the Arctic aster, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, rocky or sandy habitats, often in tundra or alpine environments. The plant features showy, typically purple or blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in harsh environments.
