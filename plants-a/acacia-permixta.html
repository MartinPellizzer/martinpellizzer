Acacia permixta

Acacia permixta, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically feather-like and arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, spherical clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Acacia permixta thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
