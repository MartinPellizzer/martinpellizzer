Aeollanthus adenotrichus

Aeollanthus adenotrichus, commonly known as the hairy leaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and hairy, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its structure is adapted for survival in harsh, dry conditions, with thick cuticles and reduced leaf surface area.

Aeollanthus adenotrichus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
