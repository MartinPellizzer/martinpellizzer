Aquilegia saxifraga

Aquilegia saxifraga, commonly known as the rock garden columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Aquilegia saxifraga is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
