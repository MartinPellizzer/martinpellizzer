Anisophyllum ocymoideum

Anisophyllum ocymoideum, commonly known as the narrowleaf fieldwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed soils and has a low, spreading growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a shallow root system for efficient water uptake.

Anisophyllum ocymoideum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
