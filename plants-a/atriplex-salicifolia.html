Atriplex salicifolia

Atriplex salicifolia, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its root system is extensive and helps stabilize soil in harsh environments.

Atriplex salicifolia is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it an important plant in desert ecosystems.
