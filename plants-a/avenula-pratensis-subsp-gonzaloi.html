Avenula pratensis subsp. gonzaloi

Avenula pratensis subsp.

gonzaloi, commonly known as the Gonzalo's meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of South America. It typically grows in moist, temperate habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
