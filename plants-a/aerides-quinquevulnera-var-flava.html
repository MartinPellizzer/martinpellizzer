Aerides quinquevulnera var. flava

Aerides quinquevulnera var. flava, commonly known as the Golden Cymbidium, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
