Arctostaphylos andersonii var. pechoensis

Arctostaphylos andersonii var. pechoensis, commonly known as the Pechoen manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, hard, reddish-brown berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the mild, coastal climate with frequent fog.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
