Anthurium organense

Anthurium organense, commonly known as the Organ Mountains anthurium, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped spathes that can range in color from deep red to orange. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers within the spathe, which are typically surrounded by a leaf-like structure called a spadix. Anthurium organense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and striking inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
