Ajania purpurea

Ajania purpurea, commonly known as the purple ajania, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, purple-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Ajania purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
