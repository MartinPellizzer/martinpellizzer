Acacia lineata

Acacia lineata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a woody structure with a spreading habit. Acacia lineata is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
