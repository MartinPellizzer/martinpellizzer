Acinos arvensis var. lancifolius

Acinos arvensis var. lancifolius, commonly known as the lance-leaved mountain everlasting, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
