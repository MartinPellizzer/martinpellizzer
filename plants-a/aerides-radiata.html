Aerides radiata

Aerides radiata, commonly known as the Radiant Vanda, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating called trichomes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

Aerides radiata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beautiful, long-lasting blooms.
