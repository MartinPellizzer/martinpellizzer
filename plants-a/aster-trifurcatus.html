Aster trifurcatus

Aster trifurcatus, commonly known as the three-branched aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Aster trifurcatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
