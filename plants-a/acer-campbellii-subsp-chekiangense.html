Acer campbellii subsp. chekiangense

Acer campbellii subsp.

chekiangense, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China. It is characterized by its striking red foliage in autumn, which makes it a popular ornamental plant.

The leaves are palmately lobed, typically with five to seven lobes, and are arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras.

This subspecies is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
