Aloe incurva

Aloe incurva, commonly known as the fleshy aloe or the curled aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that curve downward, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers on tall, branched stems during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe incurva is often used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
