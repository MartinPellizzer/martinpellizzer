Acacia dealbata subsp. subalpina

Acacia dealbata subsp.

subalpina, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the alpine regions of southern South America. It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers that appear in spring.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils. It has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which helps it grow in nutrient-poor soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
