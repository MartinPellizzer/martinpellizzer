Amerimnon lateriflorum

Amerimnon lateriflorum, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in sandy, coastal soils. It is often found in dunes and along the edges of salt marshes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for coastal ecosystem stability.
