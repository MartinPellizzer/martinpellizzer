Arenaria verna var. japonica

Arenaria verna var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky areas or along coastal regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
