Acanthus latisepalus

Acanthus latisepalus, commonly known as the broad-sepaled acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often adorned with intricate, toothed margins. The plant produces showy, spiny flower spikes that bear small, clustered flowers, typically in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its architectural form and distinctive foliage make it a valuable addition to horticultural collections.
