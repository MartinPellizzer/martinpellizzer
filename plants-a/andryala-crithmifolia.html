Andryala crithmifolia

Andryala crithmifolia, commonly known as sea fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing herb with aromatic, fern-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its root system is adapted to coastal environments, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Andryala crithmifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
