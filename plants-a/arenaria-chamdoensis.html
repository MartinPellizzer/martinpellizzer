Arenaria chamdoensis

Arenaria chamdoensis, commonly known as the Tibetan sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, demonstrating remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its presence is significant in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
