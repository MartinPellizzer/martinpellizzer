Anthyllis variiflora

Anthyllis variiflora, commonly known as variable clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

It has a creeping growth habit with numerous stems that can form dense mats, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
