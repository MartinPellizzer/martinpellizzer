Azalea delavayi

Azalea delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are elliptical and medium green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea delavayi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
