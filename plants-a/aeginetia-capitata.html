Aeginetia capitata

Aeginetia capitata, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds with silky, wind-dispersed hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrublands and grasslands.

Aeginetia capitata is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
