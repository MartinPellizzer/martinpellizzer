Asperula montana var. lactea

Asperula montana var. lactea, commonly known as the milky asperula, is a variant of the evergreen shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. Its stems are typically covered in a milky sap, which gives it its common name.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
