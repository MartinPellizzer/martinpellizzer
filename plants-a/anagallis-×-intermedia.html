Anagallis × intermedia

Anagallis × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid waterpink, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Anagallis arvensis and Anagallis moschatella. This plant is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along roadsides.

The leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are erect and can reach up to 30 cm in height.
