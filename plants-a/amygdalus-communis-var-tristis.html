Amygdalus communis var. tristis

Amygdalus communis var. tristis, commonly known as the bitter almond tree, is a variant of the common almond species.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, oval leaves and white to pale pink flowers in spring. Its fruits are encased in a hard shell and contain bitter seeds, which are toxic if consumed in large quantities.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
