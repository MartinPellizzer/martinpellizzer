Actinodaphne glomerata

Actinodaphne glomerata, commonly known as the clustered laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark, spherical berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
