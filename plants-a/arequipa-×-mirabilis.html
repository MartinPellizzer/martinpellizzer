Arequipa × mirabilis

Arequipa × mirabilis, commonly known as the hybrid nightshade, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Arequipa and mirabilis species.

It is characterized by its robust growth and vibrant floral display, typically featuring large, showy flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public landscapes.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that showcases the genetic diversity within the Solanaceae family.
