Ardisia nutantiflora

Ardisia nutantiflora, commonly known as the spreading flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ardisia nutantiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy fruits.
