Anatherum fulvibarbe

Anatherum fulvibarbe, commonly known as the yellow beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically pale green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, yellowish flowers. Its leaves have a distinctive reddish or brownish stripe along the midrib, giving it its common name.

Anatherum fulvibarbe is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
