Aspalathus macrosepala

Aspalathus macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepal bushveld clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is typically found in grassland and bushveld habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Aspalathus macrosepala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich forage.
