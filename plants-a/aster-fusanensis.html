Aster fusanensis

Aster fusanensis, commonly known as the Korean aster, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and features showy purple or blue flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Aster fusanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
