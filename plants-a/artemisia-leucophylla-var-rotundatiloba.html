Artemisia leucophylla var. rotundatiloba

Artemisia leucophylla var. rotundatiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaf mugwort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late summer to early fall. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Due to its aromatic properties and historical uses in traditional medicine, it has ecological and cultural significance in its native range.
