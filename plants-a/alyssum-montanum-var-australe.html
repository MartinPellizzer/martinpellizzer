Alyssum montanum var. australe

Alyssum montanum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
