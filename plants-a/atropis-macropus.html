Atropis macropus

Atropis macropus, commonly known as the large-eyed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropics of northern Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, upright stem and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-yellow in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of flowers, often arranged in a spiral pattern.

Atropis macropus is closely related to other orchid species in the subfamily Orchidoideae and is characterized by its unique floral structure and pollination mechanisms.
