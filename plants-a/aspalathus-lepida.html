Aspalathus lepida

Aspalathus lepida, commonly known as the African geranium, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to arid conditions, with succulent leaves that store water. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Aspalathus lepida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
