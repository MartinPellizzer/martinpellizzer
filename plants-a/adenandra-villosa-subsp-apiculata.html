Adenandra villosa subsp. apiculata

Adenandra villosa subsp.

apiculata, commonly known as the woolly adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
