Astragalus pulsiferae var. coronensis

Astragalus pulsiferae var. coronensis, commonly known as the crowned milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like arrangement of leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, this plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
