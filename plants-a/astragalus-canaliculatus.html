Astragalus canaliculatus

Astragalus canaliculatus, commonly known as the channelled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seed pods are distinctive, with a ribbed structure that aids in seed dispersal.
