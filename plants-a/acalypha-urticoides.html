Acalypha urticoides

Acalypha urticoides, commonly known as the stinging nettle shrub, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in summer. Its stems are often erect and can grow up to several feet in height.

Acalypha urticoides is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
