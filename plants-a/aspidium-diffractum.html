Aspidium diffractum

Aspidium diffractum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions near streams or in woodland areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Aspidium diffractum plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to soil stability.
