Astragalus cumanus

Astragalus cumanus, commonly known as the Cumanus milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Astragalus cumanus is also of interest in botanical research due to its potential medicinal properties.
