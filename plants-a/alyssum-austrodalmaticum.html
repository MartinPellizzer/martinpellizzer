Alyssum austrodalmaticum

Alyssum austrodalmaticum, commonly known as the Dalmatian alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Alyssum austrodalmaticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
