Anemia ferruginea var. ahenobarba

Anemia ferruginea var. ahenobarba, commonly known as the rusty anemone, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a rusty-colored pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are borne on slender, hairy stems. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and has a velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
