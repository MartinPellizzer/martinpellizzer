Anacamptis morio subsp. picta

Anacamptis morio subsp.

picta, commonly known as the Painted Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that typically feature a combination of purple, pink, and white hues.

The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a flowering stem that bears several showy, spurred orchid flowers. It thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more vivid and intricate floral patterns compared to other members of the Anacamptis genus.
