Abbevillea klotzschiana var. intermedia

Abbevillea klotzschiana var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate abbevillea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast to its delicate flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
