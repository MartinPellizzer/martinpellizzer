Artemisia pamirica

Artemisia pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found at high altitudes.

Artemisia pamirica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
