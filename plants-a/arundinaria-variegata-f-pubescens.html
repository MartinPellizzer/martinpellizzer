Arundinaria variegata f. pubescens

Arundinaria variegata f. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet bamboo, is a rare and ornamental grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and striking variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant features soft, pubescent (hairy) leaves that give it a velvety texture, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after plant among botanical enthusiasts.
