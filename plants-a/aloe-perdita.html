Aloe perdita

Aloe perdita, commonly known as the desert aloe, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a layer of white powder called farina. The plant produces tall, branching flower stalks that bear yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. Aloe perdita is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Its leaves store water, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
