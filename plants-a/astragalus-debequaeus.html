Astragalus debequaeus

Astragalus debequaeus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Astragalus debequaeus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
