Aloe pumila var. arachnoidea

Aloe pumila var. arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider aloe, is a small, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures resembling spider legs. The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers during the summer months, which are borne on tall, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
