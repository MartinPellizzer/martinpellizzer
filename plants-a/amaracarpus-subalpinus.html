Amaracarpus subalpinus

Amaracarpus subalpinus, commonly known as the alpine amara, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. Native to high-altitude regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its habitat, it is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions such as strong winds and low temperatures.
