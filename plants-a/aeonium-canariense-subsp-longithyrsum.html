Aeonium canariense subsp. longithyrsum

Aeonium canariense subsp.

longithyrsum, commonly known as the Canary Island Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

This subspecies is popular in rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
