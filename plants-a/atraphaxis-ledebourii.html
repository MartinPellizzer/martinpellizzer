Atraphaxis ledebourii

Atraphaxis ledebourii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Atraphaxis ledebourii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature.
