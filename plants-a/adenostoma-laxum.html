Adenostoma laxum

Adenostoma laxum, commonly known as the coastal sagebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub ecosystems.
