Astragalus transiliensis

Astragalus transiliensis, commonly known as the Transylvanian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Astragalus transiliensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
