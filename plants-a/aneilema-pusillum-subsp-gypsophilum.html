Aneilema pusillum subsp. gypsophilum

Aneilema pusillum subsp.

gypsophilum, commonly known as the rock anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most notable characteristic is its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
