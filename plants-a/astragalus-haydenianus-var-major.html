Astragalus haydenianus var. major

Astragalus haydenianus var. major, commonly known as the Hayden's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of many members of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus haydenianus var. major plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
