Alliaria beddomei

Alliaria beddomei, commonly known as the beddome's garlic, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of India.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of aromatic, linear leaves and small white or yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors and rocky habitats.

Alliaria beddomei is valued for its pungent odor and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
