Aconitum lycoctonum lusus tenuisectum

Aconitum lycoctonum lusus tenuisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and dark blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically narrow and deeply divided. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a hazardous plant to handle and consume.
