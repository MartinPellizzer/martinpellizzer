Aronia botryapium var. racemosa

Aronia botryapium var. racemosa, commonly known as black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and clusters of small, red berries that turn black when ripe. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
