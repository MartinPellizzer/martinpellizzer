Astragalus lentiginosus var. semotus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. semotus, commonly known as the spotted milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and a distinctive yellowish or pale green flower cluster. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
