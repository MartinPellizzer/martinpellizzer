Abies × insignis f. kentiana

Abies × insignis f. kentiana, commonly known as the Kent's fir, is a hybrid coniferous tree that combines traits from Abies lasiocarpa and Abies concolor.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted. The cones are typically reddish-brown and hang downward, containing winged seeds. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cooler climates.

Native Distribution

Abies × insignis f. kentiana is primarily native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China, particularly in the provinces of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou.

It thrives in temperate climates with high elevation, often found in subalpine and alpine forests. This hybrid fir species is commonly associated with rocky and well-drained soils in these areas. Its natural habitat includes mixed coniferous forests, where it often grows alongside other species like Pinus and Tsuga.

The plant is well adapted to the cool, moist conditions typical of its native range.
