Atractylis chinensis f. simplicifolia

Atractylis chinensis f. simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved Chinese Atractylis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and basal leaves that are typically simple and lobed. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or open woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
