Alpinia rosacea

Alpinia rosacea, commonly known as the red ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-red bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a rhizomatous herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a glossy surface.

Alpinia rosacea thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
