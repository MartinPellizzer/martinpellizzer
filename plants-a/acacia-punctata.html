Acacia punctata

Acacia punctata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in globular clusters. The plant produces flat, linear phyllodes that serve as leaves, reducing water loss in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia punctata plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
