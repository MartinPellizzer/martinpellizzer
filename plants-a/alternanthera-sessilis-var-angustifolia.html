Alternanthera sessilis var. angustifolia

Alternanthera sessilis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf velvet plant, is a compact, trailing herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features small, elongated leaves that are usually a vibrant red or purple, depending on light conditions. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is popular in aquatic and bog gardens due to its ornamental foliage and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
