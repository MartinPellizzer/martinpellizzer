Anthemis melampodia

Anthemis melampodia, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its yellow flowers are often accompanied by greenish-blue ray florets, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Anthemis melampodia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
