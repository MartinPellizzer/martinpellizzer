Andropogon tacazensis

Andropogon tacazensis, commonly known as the Tacazence grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas, often in well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Andropogon tacazensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
