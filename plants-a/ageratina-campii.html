Ageratina campii

Ageratina campii, commonly known as the Camp's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fluffy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. Ageratina campii is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
