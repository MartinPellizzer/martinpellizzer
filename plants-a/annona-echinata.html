Annona echinata

Annona echinata, commonly known as the spiny custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, green, spiny fruits that are typically harvested when they turn yellow or red. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
