Asplenium paucivenosum subsp. paucivenosum

Asplenium paucivenosum subsp.

paucivenosum, commonly known as the slender spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to various regions of Asia. It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base, with a distinctive, delicate appearance. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the Aspleniaceae family.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
