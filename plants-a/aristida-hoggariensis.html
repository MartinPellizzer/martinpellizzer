Aristida hoggariensis

Aristida hoggariensis, commonly known as the Hogg's threeawn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of three-awned lemma hairs, which are a distinguishing feature. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in restoration projects and as a ornamental grass in landscaping.
