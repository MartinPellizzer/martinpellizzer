Aechmea thyrsigera

Aechmea thyrsigera, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces vibrant, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, adding visual interest to its foliage. Its leaves are typically long and stiff, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Aechmea thyrsigera is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea thyrsigera has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and dark green with a waxy coating, arranged in a tight spiral at the base.

Its inflorescence consists of a dense, branched cluster of flowers, which are typically violet or purple in color, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.
