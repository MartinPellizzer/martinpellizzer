Asparagus sativus

Asparagus sativus, commonly known as garden asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southwestern Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fern-like, feather-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into spiny, red berries. Its edible stalks, known as asparagus, are harvested before the ferns fully develop.

Asparagus sativus is valued for its nutritional content and is commonly cultivated for culinary use.
