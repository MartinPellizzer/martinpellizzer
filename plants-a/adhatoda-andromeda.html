Adhatoda andromeda

Adhatoda andromeda, commonly known as Indian lilac or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
