Andropogon hirtiflorus var. feensis

Andropogon hirtiflorus var. feensis, commonly known as the Fei's hairyflower grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features narrow, elongated leaves with a hairy texture along the edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces inflorescences composed of spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed sites.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
