Aster thyrsiflorus

Aster thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the thrysoflora aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Aster thyrsiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
