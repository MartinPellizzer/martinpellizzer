Aspilia buphthalmiflora var. calendulacea

Aspilia buphthalmiflora var. calendulacea, commonly known as the yellow African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown central disk, forming a striking composite flower head. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
