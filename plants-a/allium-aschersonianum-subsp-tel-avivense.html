Allium aschersonianum subsp. tel-avivense

Allium aschersonianum subsp.

tel-avivense, commonly known as the Tel Aviv garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Middle East. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it drought-tolerant. It is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and modest blooms.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species.
