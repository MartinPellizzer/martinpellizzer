Agapetes interdicta

Agapetes interdicta, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows at high altitudes, often in cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries.

Agapetes interdicta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
