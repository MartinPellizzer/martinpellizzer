Astragalus turkestanus

Astragalus turkestanus, commonly known as the Turkestan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and desert regions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus.

This species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
