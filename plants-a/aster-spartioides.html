Aster spartioides

Aster spartioides, commonly known as sand aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky areas. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers in the fall. It is drought-tolerant and has a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to harsh conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
