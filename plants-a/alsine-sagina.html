Alsine sagina

Alsine sagina, commonly known as common mouse-ear or mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. Alsine sagina thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

It is a hardy species that can spread readily through seeds and vegetative reproduction.
