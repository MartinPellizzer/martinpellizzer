Anacamptis longicornu

Anacamptis longicornu, commonly known as the long-horned orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it is pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Anacamptis longicornu is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
