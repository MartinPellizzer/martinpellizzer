Aloe deltoideodonta f. longifolia

Aloe deltoideodonta f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically elongated and covered in a layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellowish-orange flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
