Arachis hypogaea subsp. sylvestris

Arachis hypogaea subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as wild groundnut, is a wild species of the peanut genus native to South America. It is characterized by its erect or prostrate growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and develops underground pods, similar to its domesticated relative, the common peanut. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is considered a valuable genetic resource for breeding programs.

This subspecies exhibits traits that may enhance its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
