Aster novi-belgii var. elodes

Aster novi-belgii var. elodes, commonly known as the New York aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals and yellow centers, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and a valuable plant for wetland restoration projects.
