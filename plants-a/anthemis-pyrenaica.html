Anthemis pyrenaica

Anthemis pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
