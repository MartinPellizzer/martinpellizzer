Adenostoma fasciculatum

Adenostoma fasciculatum, commonly known as the chamise, is a shrub native to California and parts of Nevada.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Chamise is an important component of chaparral ecosystems, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
