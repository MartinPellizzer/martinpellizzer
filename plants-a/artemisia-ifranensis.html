Artemisia ifranensis

Artemisia ifranensis, commonly known as the African wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Artemisia ifranensis is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine.

It is also used as a source of essential oils and has been studied for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
