Agave taeniata

Agave taeniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stem and long, narrow, blue-green leaves that are often lined with white teeth. The plant produces a dramatic inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom once in its lifetime. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
