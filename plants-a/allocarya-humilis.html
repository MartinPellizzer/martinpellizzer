Allocarya humilis

Allocarya humilis, commonly known as the lowland dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, yellow flower head. The plant produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Allocarya humilis is a perennial herb that is relatively hardy and adaptable to various environmental conditions.
