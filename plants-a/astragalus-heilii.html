Astragalus heilii

Astragalus heilii, commonly known as Heil's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Astragalus heilii is known for its deep taproot system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine flora.
