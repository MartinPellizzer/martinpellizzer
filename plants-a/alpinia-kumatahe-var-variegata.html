Alpinia kumatahe var. variegata

Alpinia kumatahe var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental, strap-shaped leaves that display a striking variegation of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative houseplant.
