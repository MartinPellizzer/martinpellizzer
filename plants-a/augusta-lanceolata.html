Augusta lanceolata

Augusta lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, lavender to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Augusta lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
