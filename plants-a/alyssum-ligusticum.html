Alyssum ligusticum

Alyssum ligusticum, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum ligusticum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
