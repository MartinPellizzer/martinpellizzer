Acacia mayana

Acacia mayana, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow or golden-green flowers that bloom throughout the year. Acacia mayana is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its wood is lightweight and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including construction and crafts.
