Aster haplopappus

Aster haplopappus, commonly known as the smooth pappus aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed soils.
