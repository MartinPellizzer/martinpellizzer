Acaena interrupta

Acaena interrupta, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces small, yellow flowers and bright red berries. The plant has serrated leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its berries are a food source for various birds and small mammals.
