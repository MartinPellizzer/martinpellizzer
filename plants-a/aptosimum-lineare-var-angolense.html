Aptosimum lineare var. angolense

Aptosimum lineare var. angolense, commonly known as the Angola wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in dense clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
