Alismorkis elytroglossa

Alismorkis elytroglossa, commonly known as the golden arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Alismorkis elytroglossa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
