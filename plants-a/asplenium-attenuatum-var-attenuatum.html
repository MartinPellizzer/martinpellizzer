Asplenium attenuatum var. attenuatum

Asplenium attenuatum var. attenuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a delicate, evergreen fern characterized by its slender, arching fronds with a dark green color and a slightly glossy texture. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and graceful form make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
