Annona deceptrix

Annona deceptrix, commonly known as the deceptive annona, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring simple, alternate, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, yellow or orange, and contain a single large seed, making them attractive to various wildlife species.

The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
