Anathallis velvetina

Anathallis velvetina, commonly known as the velvet spider orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and its striking, spider-like flower structure. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are typically pale green or yellow with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of spiders.

Anathallis velvetina is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
