Aralia trifoliata

Aralia trifoliata, commonly known as the Japanese aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets, giving it a distinctive trifoliate appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by red berries that attract birds. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

Aralia trifoliata thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
