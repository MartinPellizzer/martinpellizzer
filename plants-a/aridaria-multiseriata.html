Aridaria multiseriata

Aridaria multiseriata, commonly known as the desert chainflower, is a flowering plant adapted to arid environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a chain-like pattern, which is a distinctive feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert and semi-arid regions.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaping for its ornamental value and low water requirements.
