Andropogon nudus

Andropogon nudus, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a native grass species found in the central and eastern United States.

It is a warm-season perennial grass that typically grows in open woodlands and prairie habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers. It is well adapted to dry to mesic conditions and is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.

Andropogon nudus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
