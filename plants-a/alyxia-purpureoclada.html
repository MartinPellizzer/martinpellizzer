Alyxia purpureoclada

Alyxia purpureoclada, commonly known as the purple-leaf alyxia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

Alyxia purpureoclada is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
