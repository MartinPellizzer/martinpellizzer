Aspilia asperrima var. latifolia

Aspilia asperrima var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf African daisy, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking, spherical yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
