Astragalus squammulosus

Astragalus squammulosus, commonly known as the scaly milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, scale-like structures, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus squammulosus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
