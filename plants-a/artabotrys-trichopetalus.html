Artabotrys trichopetalus

Artabotrys trichopetalus, commonly known as the hairy-stalked candlewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that resemble candles, which bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves with a leathery texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
