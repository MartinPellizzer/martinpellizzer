Amaryllis × pearceara

Amaryllis × pearceara, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems with narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring or early summer, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant floral displays.

The hybrid nature of Amaryllis × pearceara allows for a wide range of cultivars, each offering unique floral characteristics.
