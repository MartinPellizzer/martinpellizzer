Allium duriaeanum

Allium duriaeanum, commonly known as the Duriaean onion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers in the summer, which are followed by bulbous structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
