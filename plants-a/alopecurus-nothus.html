Alopecurus nothus

Alopecurus nothus, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and small, rounded leaves that resemble mouse ears, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Alopecurus nothus is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
