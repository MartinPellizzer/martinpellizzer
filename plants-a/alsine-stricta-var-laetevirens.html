Alsine stricta var. laetevirens

Alsine stricta var. laetevirens, commonly known as the narrowleaf alsike clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This variant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
