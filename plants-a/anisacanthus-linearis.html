Anisacanthus linearis

Anisacanthus linearis, commonly known as the yellow woodflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
