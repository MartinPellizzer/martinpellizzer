Adesmia rafaelensis

Adesmia rafaelensis, commonly known as the Rafael's burro's tail, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in ecological studies of desert flora.
