Allium subhirsutum subsp. album

Allium subhirsutum subsp.

album, commonly known as the white garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers in umbels.

The plant is characterized by its mild garlic scent and edible leaves, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is closely related to other Allium species, sharing similar growth habits and botanical features.
