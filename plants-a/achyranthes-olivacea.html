Achyranthes olivacea

Achyranthes olivacea, commonly known as the oliveleaf sida, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly serrated margin and a rough texture.

Achyranthes olivacea is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
