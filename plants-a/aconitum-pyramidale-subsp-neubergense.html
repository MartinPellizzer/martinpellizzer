Aconitum pyramidale subsp. neubergense

Aconitum pyramidale subsp.

neubergense, commonly known as the Neuberg mountain monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive velvety texture, and its stems are typically tall and upright. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
