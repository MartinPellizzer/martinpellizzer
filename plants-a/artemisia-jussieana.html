Artemisia jussieana

Artemisia jussieana, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its strong, pungent scent.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is often used in traditional medicine and ceremonial practices.
