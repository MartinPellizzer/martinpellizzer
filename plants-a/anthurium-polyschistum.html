Anthurium polyschistum

Anthurium polyschistum, commonly known as the veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often veined with bright yellow or white patterns. The plant produces spadices, which are inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium polyschistum is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to purify indoor air.
