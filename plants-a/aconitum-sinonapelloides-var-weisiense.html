Aconitum sinonapelloides var. weisiense

Aconitum sinonapelloides var. weisiense, commonly known as the Weisi mountain aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist climates. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and the stem is usually covered in a dense layer of hairs.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
