Acacia petraea

Acacia petraea, commonly known as the rock acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, phyllodes that are often glandular and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical heads, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. Acacia petraea is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native environment.
