Asyneuma filipes

Asyneuma filipes, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy stems that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Asyneuma filipes is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
