Astragalus subbrevidens

Astragalus subbrevidens, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus subbrevidens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
