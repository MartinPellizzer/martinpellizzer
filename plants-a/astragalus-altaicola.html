Astragalus altaicola

Astragalus altaicola, commonly known as the Altai milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus altaicola is also of interest to botanists due to its adaptability to harsh environments.
