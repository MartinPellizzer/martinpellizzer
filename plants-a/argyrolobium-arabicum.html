Argyrolobium arabicum

Argyrolobium arabicum, commonly known as the silver globular vetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and silver-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable subject in botanical studies and ecological research.
