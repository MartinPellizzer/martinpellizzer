Asketanthera longiflora

Asketanthera longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered aster, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its showy, purple to pink flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
