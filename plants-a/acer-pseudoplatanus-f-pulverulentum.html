Acer pseudoplatanus f. pulverulentum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. pulverulentum, commonly known as the powdered maple, is a rare and unique variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its finely textured, powdery appearance on the leaves, which gives it a distinctive, almost otherworldly look. The plant is a hybrid between Acer pseudoplatanus and Acer platanoides, resulting in its unique botanical features. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is a sought-after specimen among horticulturists and botanists.
