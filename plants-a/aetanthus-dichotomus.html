Aetanthus dichotomus

Aetanthus dichotomus, commonly known as the two-branched sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and linear, with a rough, hairy texture.

Aetanthus dichotomus thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
