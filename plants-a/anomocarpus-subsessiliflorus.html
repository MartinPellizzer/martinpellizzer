Anomocarpus subsessiliflorus

Anomocarpus subsessiliflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr medic, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spiny pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its characteristic burr-like fruits. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage species for livestock.
