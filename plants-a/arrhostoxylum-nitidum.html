Arrhostoxylum nitidum

Arrhostoxylum nitidum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various parts of the world for landscaping and ecological purposes.
