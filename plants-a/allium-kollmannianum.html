Allium kollmannianum

Allium kollmannianum, commonly known as the Kollmann's onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.

Allium kollmannianum is also noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
