Aparine parisiensis

Aparine parisiensis, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems that often lie prostrate on the ground, covered in fine, hooked hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, with a distinctive serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface.

Aparine parisiensis is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
