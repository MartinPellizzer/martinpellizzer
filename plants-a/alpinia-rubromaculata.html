Alpinia rubromaculata

Alpinia rubromaculata, commonly known as the red-spotted ginger, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with red or purple spots. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense, lush foliage.

Alpinia rubromaculata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
