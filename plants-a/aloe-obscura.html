Aloe obscura

Aloe obscura, commonly known as the ghost aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features spiny, upright stems and narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves for survival.

Aloe obscura is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
