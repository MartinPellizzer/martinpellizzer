Alsine setacea

Alsine setacea, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

Alsine setacea is adapted to survive in sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.
