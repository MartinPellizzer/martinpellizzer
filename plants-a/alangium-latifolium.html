Alangium latifolium

Alangium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf alangium, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
