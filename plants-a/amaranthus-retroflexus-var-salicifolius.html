Amaranthus retroflexus var. salicifolius

Amaranthus retroflexus var. salicifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, green leaves that are often broader than those of the typical amaranth species. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the stage of bloom. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
