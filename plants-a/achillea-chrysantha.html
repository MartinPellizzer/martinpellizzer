Achillea chrysantha

Achillea chrysantha, commonly known as golden yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
