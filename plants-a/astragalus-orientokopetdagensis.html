Astragalus orientokopetdagensis

Astragalus orientokopetdagensis, commonly known as the Eastern Kopetdag Astragalus, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
