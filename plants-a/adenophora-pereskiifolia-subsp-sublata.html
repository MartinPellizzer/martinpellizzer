Adenophora pereskiifolia subsp. sublata

Adenophora pereskiifolia subsp.

sublata, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to northeastern Asia. It features upright stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are hairy and slightly toothed.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
