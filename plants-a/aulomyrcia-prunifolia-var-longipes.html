Aulomyrcia prunifolia var. longipes

Aulomyrcia prunifolia var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed laurel, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, edible fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
