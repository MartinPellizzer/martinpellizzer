Artemisia tripartita

Artemisia tripartita, commonly known as three-part wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage with finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Artemisia tripartita contains essential oils and bitter compounds, making it useful in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
