Angraecum rohlfsianum

Angraecum rohlfsianum, commonly known as the Rohlfs' orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers with long, tubular sepals and petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Angraecum rohlfsianum is noted for its unique floral structure, which is adapted for pollination by specific nocturnal moths.
