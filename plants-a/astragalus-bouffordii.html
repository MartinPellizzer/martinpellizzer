Astragalus bouffordii

Astragalus bouffordii, commonly known as the Boufford milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and desert scrub habitats. It has small, rounded seed pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus bouffordii is often found in areas with low rainfall and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
