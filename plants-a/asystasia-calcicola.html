Asystasia calcicola

Asystasia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone asystasia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its attractive foliage and blooms.
