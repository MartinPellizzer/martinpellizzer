Astragalus wingatanus

Astragalus wingatanus, commonly known as the winged milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of China and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are typically compound, with oval-shaped leaflets that are often hairy.

Astragalus wingatanus is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access groundwater.
