Abies sibirica f. pumila

Abies sibirica f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Siberian fir, is a compact, slow-growing coniferous shrub.

It is a natural variant of the Siberian fir, characterized by its dense, rounded form and shorter stature compared to the typical species. The plant features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in colder climates.
