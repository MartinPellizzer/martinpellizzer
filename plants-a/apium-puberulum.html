Apium puberulum

Apium puberulum, commonly known as the hairy parsley, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in moist, calcareous soils.

Apium puberulum is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant or used in traditional herbal remedies.
