Althaea borbonica

Althaea borbonica, commonly known as the Sicilian marshmallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. Althaea borbonica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its mucilaginous roots have been historically used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.
