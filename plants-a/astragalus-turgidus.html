Astragalus turgidus

Astragalus turgidus, commonly known as the inflated milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Astragalus turgidus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its seed pods are inflated and contain several small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind.
