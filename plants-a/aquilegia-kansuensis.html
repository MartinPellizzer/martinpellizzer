Aquilegia kansuensis

Aquilegia kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu columbine, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Gansu and Sichuan provinces. The plant is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that range in color from blue to purple, and its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and alpine meadows.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
