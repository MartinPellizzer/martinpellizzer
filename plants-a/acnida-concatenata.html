Acnida concatenata

Acnida concatenata, commonly known as the creeping acnida, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Acnida concatenata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
