Aponogeton loriae

Aponogeton loriae, commonly known as the Loria's water arum, is a rare aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with a deep green color and a waxy texture. The plant produces a unique inflorescence that resembles a spathe, with small white flowers emerging from the base of the leaf. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
