Asperula cynanchica var. paui

Asperula cynanchica var. paui, commonly known as the Alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments. It is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows at high altitudes.

Asperula cynanchica var. paui is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
