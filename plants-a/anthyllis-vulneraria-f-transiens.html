Anthyllis vulneraria f. transiens

Anthyllis vulneraria f. transiens, commonly known as the transitive scarlet pimpernel, is a variant of the scarlet pimpernel, characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This subspecies is native to certain regions of southern Europe and is adapted to rocky and arid environments. Its small, bright red flowers bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant exhibits a prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that often trail along the ground.

It is noted for its rapid flowering cycle, which is influenced by environmental conditions such as temperature and light.
