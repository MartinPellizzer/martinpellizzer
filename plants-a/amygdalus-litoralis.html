Amygdalus litoralis

Amygdalus litoralis, commonly known as the coastal almond, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that resemble almonds but are typically less commercially valuable. It thrives in coastal areas with well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other almond species.
