Armeria canescens f. latifolia

Armeria canescens f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in dunes or rocky coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal environments.
