Acanthinophyllum ilicifolium

Acanthinophyllum ilicifolium, commonly known as the holly-leaved acanthinophyllum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are leathery and resemble those of holly, giving it its common name.

The flowers, which are often brightly colored, attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
