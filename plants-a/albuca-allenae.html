Albuca allenae

Albuca allenae, commonly known as the Allen's albuca, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Albuca allenae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
