Astragalus preussii var. cutleri

Astragalus preussii var. cutleri, commonly known as the Preuss's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
