Aster magnus f. albiflorus

Aster magnus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the Whiteflower Great Aster, is a cultivar of the Aster genus native to the United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and has a clump-forming habit with narrow, gray-green leaves. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
