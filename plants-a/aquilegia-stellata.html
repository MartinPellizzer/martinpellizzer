It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a palmate pattern.

Aquilegia stellata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
