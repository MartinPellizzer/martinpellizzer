Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp. adamsii

Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp.

adamsii, commonly known as the Adams' manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and southern Oregon. It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and evergreen leaves that are oval and leathery in texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
