Allium negevense

Allium negevense, commonly known as the Negev leek, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of Israel and the Negev desert, where it has adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. It is known for its strong garlic-like odor and is often used in local cuisine.

Due to its unique habitat, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
