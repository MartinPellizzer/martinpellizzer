Aristida floccosa

Aristida floccosa, commonly known as the silky grass or silky beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that give it a soft, cloud-like appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture.

Aristida floccosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
