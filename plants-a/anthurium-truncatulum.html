Anthurium truncatulum

Anthurium truncatulum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dark, velvety foliage and striking, spatulate-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, with a spathe that is often red or orange.

Anthurium truncatulum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
