Aster dubius var. heterotrichus

Aster dubius var. heterotrichus, commonly known as the hairy aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple and blue during the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
