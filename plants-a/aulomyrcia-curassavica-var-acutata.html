Aulomyrcia curassavica var. acutata

Aulomyrcia curassavica var. acutata, commonly known as the Acute-leafed Aulomyrcia, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, acute leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
