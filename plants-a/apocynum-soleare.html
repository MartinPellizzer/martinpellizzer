Apocynum soleare

Apocynum soleare, commonly known as white milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in summer. Its latex sap contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to many animals.

Apocynum soleare thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, disturbed soils.
