Aspidium bryantii

Aspidium bryantii, commonly known as Bryant's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium bryantii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
