Ajuga vulgaris subsp. pyramidalis

Ajuga vulgaris subsp.

pyramidalis, commonly known as upright bugleweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ground cover in shaded gardens.
