Alsophila mapiriensis

Alsophila mapiriensis, commonly known as the Mapiri fern, is a species of tree fern native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with rich, well-draining soil. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, giving it a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Alsophila mapiriensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
