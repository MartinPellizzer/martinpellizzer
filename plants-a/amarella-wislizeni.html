Amarella wislizeni

Amarella wislizeni, commonly known as the wislizeni amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears clusters of flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge after the flowers have faded.

Amarella wislizeni is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
