Arum pictum subsp. sagittifolium

Arum pictum subsp.

sagittifolium, commonly known as the arrow-leaved arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrow-like base, giving it its common name.

The plant produces a showy, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.
