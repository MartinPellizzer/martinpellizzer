Acacia cockerellae

Acacia cockerellae, commonly known as the Cockerell's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia cockerellae is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its foliage is often used by local fauna, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
