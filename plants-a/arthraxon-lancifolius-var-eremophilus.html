Arthraxon lancifolius var. eremophilus

Arthraxon lancifolius var. eremophilus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in degraded grasslands.
