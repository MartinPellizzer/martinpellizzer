Aspidistra angustata

Aspidistra angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aspidistra, is a member of the Araceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow, and elongated leaves that are often marked with silver veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden beneath a leaf-like spathe. It is a perennial evergreen that thrives in low-light conditions and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Aspidistra angustata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
