Acianthera johnsonii

Acianthera johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's acianthera, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, lance-shaped leaves and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in the spring. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is intricately patterned.

Acianthera johnsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
