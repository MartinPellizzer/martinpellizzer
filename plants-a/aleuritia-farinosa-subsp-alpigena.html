Aleuritia farinosa subsp. alpigena

Aleuritia farinosa subsp.

alpigena, commonly known as the alpine aleurita, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes.

The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, hairy, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
