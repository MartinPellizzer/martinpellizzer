Andropogon umbellatus

Andropogon umbellatus, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green blades and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies due to their pleasant aroma.

Sweetgrass is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
