Amaranthus sanguinolentus

Amaranthus sanguinolentus, commonly known as the blood amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus sanguinolentus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.
