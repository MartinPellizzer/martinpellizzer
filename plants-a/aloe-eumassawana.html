Aloe eumassawana

Aloe eumassawana, commonly known as the Massawa aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of East Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellowish-orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is adapted to absorb water efficiently during rare rainfall events.

Aloe eumassawana is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
