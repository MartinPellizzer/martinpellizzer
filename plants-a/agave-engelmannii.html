Agave engelmannii

Agave engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its leaves are used for ornamental purposes and in traditional crafts.
