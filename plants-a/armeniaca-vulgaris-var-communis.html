Armeniaca vulgaris var. communis

Armeniaca vulgaris var. communis, commonly known as the peach, is a deciduous fruit tree native to Asia.

It produces sweet, fleshy fruits with a fuzzy skin and a pit containing a hard seed. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It flowers in the spring with showy, pink to white blossoms.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its culinary and ornamental value.
