Agropyron corsicum

Agropyron corsicum, commonly known as Corsican grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and grasslands.

Agropyron corsicum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
