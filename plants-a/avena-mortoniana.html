Avena mortoniana

Avena mortoniana, commonly known as Morton's oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Avena mortoniana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
