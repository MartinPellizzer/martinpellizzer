Acacia georginae

Acacia georginae, commonly known as the Georgina acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, a common adaptation in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by hard, woody pods containing seeds.

Acacia georginae is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and rocky habitats.
