Apocynum androsaemifolium var. tomentellum

Apocynum androsaemifolium var. tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its berries are typically orange or red, containing toxic seeds that are a food source for certain birds.

The species is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.
