Allium olivieri

Allium olivieri, commonly known as the Levantine garlic or wild garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

Allium olivieri thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
