Agrostis nigricans

Agrostis nigricans, commonly known as black bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark green, fine-textured leaves and dense, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically found in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
