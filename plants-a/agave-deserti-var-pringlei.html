Agave deserti var. pringlei

Agave deserti var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's agave, is a succulent plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny leaves that form a rosette shape, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk with small, white flowers, which appear after many years of growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and conservation efforts.
