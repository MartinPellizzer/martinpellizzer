Apium laciniatum f. elatium

Apium laciniatum f. elatium, commonly known as the tall fern parsley, is a botanical variant of the common parsley plant.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, feathery leaves that resemble those of a fern, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The foliage is often used in culinary applications, adding a subtle anise-like flavor to dishes.
