Agave protuberans

Agave protuberans, commonly known as the "Coral Agave," is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, white to pale yellow flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Agave protuberans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
