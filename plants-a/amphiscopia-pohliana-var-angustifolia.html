Amphiscopia pohliana var. angustifolia

Amphiscopia pohliana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in areas with consistent hydration. Its flowers are often clustered in spikes and are adapted for pollination by insects.

This variety is distinguished by its narrow leaf structure and is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.
