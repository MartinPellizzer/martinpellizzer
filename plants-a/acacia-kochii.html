Acacia kochii

Acacia kochii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia kochii is adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
