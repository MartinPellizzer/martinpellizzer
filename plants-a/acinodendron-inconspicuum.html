Acinodendron inconspicuum

Acinodendron inconspicuum, commonly known as the inconspicuous acinodendron, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of forests. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green color. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are often white or pale yellow.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal within the ecosystem.
