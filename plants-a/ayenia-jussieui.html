Ayenia jussieui

Ayenia jussieui, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Ayenia jussieui is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
