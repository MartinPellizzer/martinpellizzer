Arpophyllum laxiflorum

Arpophyllum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered armpit bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
