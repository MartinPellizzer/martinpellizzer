Andropogon appendiculatus

Andropogon appendiculatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often tinged with silver. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in full sun and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Andropogon appendiculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
