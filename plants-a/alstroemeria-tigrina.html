Alstroemeria tigrina

Alstroemeria tigrina, commonly known as the tiger alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are known for their long-lasting bloom, making it a popular choice in floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria tigrina thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
