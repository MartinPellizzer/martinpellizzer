Aspidosperma populifolium

Aspidosperma populifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cerejeira-brasileira*, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some wildlife but toxic to humans. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various species in its native range.
