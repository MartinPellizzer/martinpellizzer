Aspidium amplifolium

Aspidium amplifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are often submerged in water, allowing it to anchor itself in wetland habitats.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in aquatic ecosystems.
