Amygdalus gessleriana

Amygdalus gessleriana, commonly known as the Gessler's almond, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of almond-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
