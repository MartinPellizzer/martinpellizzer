Acaena echinata var. retrosumpilosa

Acaena echinata var. retrosumpilosa, commonly known as the rough burr plant, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and spherical, woody fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of persistent, prickly burrs. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

The plant has adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and is resilient to harsh environmental conditions.
