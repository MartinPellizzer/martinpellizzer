Amaranthus mangostanus var. major

Amaranthus mangostanus var. major, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple foliage and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, green to red flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional culinary practices, while the seeds are rich in nutrients.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural uses.
