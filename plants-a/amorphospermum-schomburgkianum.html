Amorphospermum schomburgkianum

Amorphospermum schomburgkianum, commonly known as the Schomburgk's amorphous bean, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
