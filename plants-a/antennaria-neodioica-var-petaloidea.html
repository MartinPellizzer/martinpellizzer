Antennaria neodioica var. petaloidea

Antennaria neodioica var. petaloidea, commonly known as the pink sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming habit and delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.
