Aristolochia lutescens

Aristolochia lutescens, commonly known as the yellow Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.

The plant contains various alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
