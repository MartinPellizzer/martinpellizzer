Aechmea chromatica

Aechmea chromatica, commonly known as the rainbow bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vividly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

Aechmea chromatica thrives in bright, indirect light and is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value.
