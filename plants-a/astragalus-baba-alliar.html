Astragalus baba-alliar

Astragalus baba-alliar, commonly known as the Turkish milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus baba-alliar is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
