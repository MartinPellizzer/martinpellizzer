Astracantha caucasica

Astracantha caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are purple to blue in color, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Astracantha caucasica is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions as a garden plant.
