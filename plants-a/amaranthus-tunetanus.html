Amaranthus tunetanus

Amaranthus tunetanus, commonly known as the desert amaranth, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red to purple foliage, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into three-angled, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use in dryland regions.
