Aster wasatchensis var. pulcher

Aster wasatchensis var. pulcher, commonly known as the Wasatch aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are showy, with purple to blue petals that bloom in late summer.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
