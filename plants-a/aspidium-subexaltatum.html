Aspidium subexaltatum

Aspidium subexaltatum, commonly known as the tall wall fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous areas. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with lanceolate pinnae that are usually glossy and dark green.

Aspidium subexaltatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its lush, vertical growth.
