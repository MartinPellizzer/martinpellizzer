Abutilon grandidentatum

Abutilon grandidentatum, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring and summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
