Achillea shishkinii

Achillea shishkinii, commonly known as Shishkin's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in shades of white to pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Shishkin's yarrow is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
