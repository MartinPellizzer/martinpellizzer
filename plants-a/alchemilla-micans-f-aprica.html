Alchemilla micans f. aprica

Alchemilla micans f. aprica, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with finely divided, dark green leaves that emit a slight fragrance when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
