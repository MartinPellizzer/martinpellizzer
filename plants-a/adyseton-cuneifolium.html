Adyseton cuneifolium

Adyseton cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed willow or western willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist environments such as streambanks and wet meadows, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it produces small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Adyseton cuneifolium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
