Arthraxon quartinianus var. caespitosus

Arthraxon quartinianus var. caespitosus, commonly known as the tufted arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
