Alcea wilhelminae var. lineariloba

Alcea wilhelminae var. lineariloba, commonly known as the linear-leaved hollyhock, is a variant of the hollyhock species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its botanical features include a robust root system and hairy stems, which help it thrive in various garden conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
