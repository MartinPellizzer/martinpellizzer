Atriplex semibaccata

Atriplex semibaccata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, bluish-gray leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Atriplex semibaccata is also known for its ability to tolerate high salinity, making it a valuable plant in reclaiming saline soils.
