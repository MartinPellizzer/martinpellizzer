Astragalus hajijafanensis

Astragalus hajijafanensis, commonly known as Hajijafan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of many Astragalus species. It is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access groundwater.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
