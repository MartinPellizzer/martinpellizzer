Anemone adamsiana

Anemone adamsiana, commonly known as the Adams' anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
