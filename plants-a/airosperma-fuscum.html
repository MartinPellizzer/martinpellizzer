Airosperma fuscum

Airosperma fuscum, commonly known as the dark spore plant, is a unique member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dark-colored seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves that aid in photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

The botanical significance of Airosperma fuscum lies in its role as a potential source of bioactive compounds.
