Albizia mollis var. glabrior

Albizia mollis var. glabrior, commonly known as the silky albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray bark and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and can help in soil stabilization, making it useful in reforestation efforts.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
