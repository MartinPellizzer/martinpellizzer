Alyssum murale subsp. dramense

Alyssum murale subsp.

dramense, commonly known as the Balkan alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, preferring full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
