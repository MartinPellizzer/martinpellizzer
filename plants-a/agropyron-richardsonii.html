Agropyron richardsonii

Agropyron richardsonii, commonly known as prairie wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green blades. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in prairies and open woodlands.

Agropyron richardsonii is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is often used in restoration ecology.
