Abronia umbellata subsp. platyphylla

Abronia umbellata subsp.

platyphylla, commonly known as the flatleaf sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader, flatter leaves compared to other members of the species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and has a low-growing structure to reduce water loss.
