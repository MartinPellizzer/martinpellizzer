Andropogon schottii

Andropogon schottii, commonly known as Schott's bluestem, is a tall, perennial grass native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or violet. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Andropogon schottii is also known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
