Arctostaphylos purissima

Arctostaphylos purissima, commonly known as the coastal manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds.

Arctostaphylos purissima is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant landscaping.
