Asplenium laciniatum

Asplenium laciniatum, commonly known as the cut-leaf spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lanceolate leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rocky crevices or along stream banks. Its rhizome is short and often covered in a layer of fine, hairy tissue.

Asplenium laciniatum is relatively small in size, with leaves usually not exceeding 15 centimeters in length.
