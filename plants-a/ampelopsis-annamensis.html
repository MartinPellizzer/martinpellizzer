Ampelopsis annamensis

Ampelopsis annamensis, commonly known as the Annam grapevines, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its berries are typically blue-black when mature and contain several seeds.

Ampelopsis annamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
