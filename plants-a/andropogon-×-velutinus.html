Andropogon × velutinus

Andropogon × velutinus, commonly known as silky grass or silky bluestem, is a hybrid grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Andropogon × velutinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
