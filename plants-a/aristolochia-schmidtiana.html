Aristolochia schmidtiana

Aristolochia schmidtiana, commonly known as the Schmidth's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and wasps. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
