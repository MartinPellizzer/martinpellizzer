Alchemilla vestita

Alchemilla vestita, commonly known as the velvet cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Alchemilla vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
