Achyrophorus coronopifolius var. integrifolius

Achyrophorus coronopifolius var. integrifolius, commonly known as the California buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with smooth or slightly toothed margins. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
