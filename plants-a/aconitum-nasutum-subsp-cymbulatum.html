Aconitum nasutum subsp. cymbulatum

Aconitum nasutum subsp.

cymbulatum, commonly known as the Cymbulat's Monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of the Himalayas.

The plant is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers, which are typical of the genus Aconitum. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a deeply lobed structure that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its beauty but requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
