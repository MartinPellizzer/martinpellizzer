Aralia avicenniifolia

Aralia avicenniifolia, commonly known as the mangrove aralia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is often found growing in brackish or saline environments. Its root system helps stabilize coastal soils, making it an important species in mangrove ecosystems.

Aralia avicenniifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
