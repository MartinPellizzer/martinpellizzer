Astragalus biserruloides

Astragalus biserruloides, commonly known as the serrulate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus biserruloides is often found in sagebrush steppe and other xeric environments.
