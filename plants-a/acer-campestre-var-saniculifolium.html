Acer campestre var. saniculifolium

Acer campestre var. saniculifolium, commonly known as the Saniculifolium horse chestnut, is a variant of the common horse chestnut.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed, saniculifolium-like leaves that resemble those of the Sanicula genus. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It produces white, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by spiny, chestnut-like fruits.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a range of soil types and light conditions.
