Astragalus bayattii

Astragalus bayattii, commonly known as the Bayatt's milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with deep root systems. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus bayattii is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
