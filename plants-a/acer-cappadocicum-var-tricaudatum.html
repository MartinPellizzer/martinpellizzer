Acer cappadocicum var. tricaudatum

Acer cappadocicum var. tricaudatum, commonly known as the three-tailed maple, is a rare variant of the Cappadocian maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant is native to a limited region in Turkey, specifically the Cappadocia area. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
