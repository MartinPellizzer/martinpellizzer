Antrophyum latipes

Antrophyum latipes, commonly known as the giant antrophyum, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its massive, clustered inflorescence that can reach heights of over 10 meters, making it one of the tallest flowering plants in the world. The plant features a short, stout trunk with a dense crown of large, pinnate leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, producing a unique, spiky appearance.

Antrophyum latipes thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
