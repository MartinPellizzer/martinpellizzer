Anthurium effusispathum

Anthurium effusispathum, commonly known as the velvet-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety pubescence. The plant produces bright red spathes that enclose its small, spadix-like inflorescence, which contains both male and female flowers. Its growth habit is typically epiphytic, meaning it often grows on the surfaces of trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Anthurium effusispathum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
