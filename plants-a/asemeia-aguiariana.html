Asemeia aguiariana

Asemeia aguiariana, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Asemeia aguiariana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add color to gardens and landscapes.
