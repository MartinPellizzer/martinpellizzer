Arisaema decipiens

Arisaema decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive arum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a single, showy, yellowish-green spathe that resembles a flower, while the spadix contains the actual reproductive structures. Arisaema decipiens typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in the southeastern United States.
