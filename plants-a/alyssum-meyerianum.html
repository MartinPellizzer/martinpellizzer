Alyssum meyerianum

Alyssum meyerianum, commonly known as Meyer's alyssum, is a low-growing, annual plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has compact, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
