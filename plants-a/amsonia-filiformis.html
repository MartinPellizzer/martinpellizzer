Amsonia filiformis

Amsonia filiformis, commonly known as bluemink, is a North American native perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of blue to sky-blue flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and long bloom period.

Amsonia filiformis is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
