Acianthera nemorosa

Acianthera nemorosa, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers that are often clustered in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is typically found in understory habitats.

Acianthera nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
