Alchemilla appressipila

Alchemilla appressipila, commonly known as narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla appressipila is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
