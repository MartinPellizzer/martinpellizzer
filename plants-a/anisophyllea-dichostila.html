Anisophyllea dichostila

Anisophyllea dichostila, commonly known as the two-styled anisophyllea, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique leaf morphology and ornamental fruits make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
