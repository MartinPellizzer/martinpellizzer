Aristolochia clematitis f. undulata

Aristolochia clematitis f. undulata, commonly known as the undulating honeysuckle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe or horn, which is a key feature of the genus Aristolochia. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are slender and vine-like. This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

It is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
