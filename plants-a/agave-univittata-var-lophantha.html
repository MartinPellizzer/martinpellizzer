Agave univittata var. lophantha

Agave univittata var. lophantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and narrow, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces a tall flower stalk bearing yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to arid environments.
