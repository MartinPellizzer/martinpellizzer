Alloeochaete namuliensis

Alloeochaete namuliensis, commonly known as the Namuli grass, is a species of grass native to the highlands of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, often in montane environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Alloeochaete namuliensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
