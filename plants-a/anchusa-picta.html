Anchusa picta

Anchusa picta, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are often hairy. Anchusa picta is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
