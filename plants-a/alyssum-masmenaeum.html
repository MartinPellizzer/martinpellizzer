Alyssum masmenaeum

Alyssum masmenaeum, commonly known as the Mexican alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in spring and early summer. Alyssum masmenaeum is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
