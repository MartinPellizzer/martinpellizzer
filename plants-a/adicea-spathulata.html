Adicea spathulata

Adicea spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved adicea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.

Adicea spathulata is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
