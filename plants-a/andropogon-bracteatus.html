Andropogon bracteatus

Andropogon bracteatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often blue or purplish in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
