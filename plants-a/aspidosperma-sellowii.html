Aspidosperma sellowii

Aspidosperma sellowii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratonia sanguinea*, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits resembling cherries. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
