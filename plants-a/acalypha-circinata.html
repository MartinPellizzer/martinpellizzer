Acalypha circinata

Acalypha circinata, commonly known as the curly leaf acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally curled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Acalypha circinata is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
