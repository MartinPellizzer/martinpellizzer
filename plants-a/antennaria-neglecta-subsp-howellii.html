Antennaria neglecta subsp. howellii

Antennaria neglecta subsp.

howellii, commonly known as Howell's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It features small, hairy leaves that are typically spoon-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces tiny white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, arid conditions.
