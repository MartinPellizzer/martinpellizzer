Acacia luederitzii

Acacia luederitzii, commonly known as the Luederitz acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
