Acalypha jubifera

Acalypha jubifera, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, followed by distinctive, spiny seed capsules. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Acalypha jubifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
