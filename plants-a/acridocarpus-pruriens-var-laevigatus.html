Acridocarpus pruriens var. laevigatus

Acridocarpus pruriens var. laevigatus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
