Acalypha subintegra

Acalypha subintegra, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Acalypha subintegra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive leaves and ease of care.
