Alchemilla sevangensis

Alchemilla sevangensis, commonly known as the Tibetan lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to cold climates.

Alchemilla sevangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
