Argyreia henryi

Argyreia henryi, commonly known as the Henry's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures. Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, hence its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
