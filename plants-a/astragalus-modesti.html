Astragalus modesti

Astragalus modesti, commonly known as the small milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Astragalus modesti is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its seed pods contain small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh environments.
