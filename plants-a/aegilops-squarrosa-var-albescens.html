Aegilops squarrosa var. albescens

Aegilops squarrosa var. albescens, commonly known as the white spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and pale, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are often dispersed by the wind. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Aegilops squarrosa var. albescens is also sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique visual appeal.
