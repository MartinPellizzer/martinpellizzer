Anacardium tenuifolium

Anacardium tenuifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that develop into woody capsules containing edible seeds. The tree's bark and leaves contain compounds with medicinal properties, including compounds that may have anti-inflammatory effects. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with high rainfall.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
