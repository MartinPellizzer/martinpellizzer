Atriplex saccaria var. saccaria

Atriplex saccaria var. saccaria, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, enabling it to thrive in saline and dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt tolerance makes it an important component of stabilizing soils in coastal and desert areas.

Atriplex saccaria var. saccaria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
