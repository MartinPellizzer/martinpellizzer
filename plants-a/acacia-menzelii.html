Acacia menzelii

Acacia menzelii, commonly known as the Menzel's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and phyllodes that are typically linear to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia menzelii is well adapted to harsh environments, with mechanisms to reduce water loss and tolerate high temperatures.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
