Arundinaria decalvata

Arundinaria decalvata, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered in a silvery sheen, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Arundinaria decalvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
