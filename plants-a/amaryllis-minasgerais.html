Amaryllis minasgerais

Amaryllis minasgerais, commonly known as the Minas Gerais amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to Brazil.

It features tall, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The blooming period typically occurs in the spring and summer months, adding a splash of color to the landscape.
