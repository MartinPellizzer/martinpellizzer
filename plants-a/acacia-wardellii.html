Acacia wardellii

Acacia wardellii, commonly known as the Wardell's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, compound leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
