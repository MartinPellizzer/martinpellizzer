Artemisia quinqueloba

Artemisia quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and aromatic leaves that emit a strong, pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Artemisia quinqueloba plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
