Aster donii

Aster donii, commonly known as the Don's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Aster donii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
