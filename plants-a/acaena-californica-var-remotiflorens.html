Acaena californica var. remotiflorens

Acaena californica var. remotiflorens, commonly known as the California burclover, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of California.

It features slender, erect stems with alternate, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in dense, spherical heads, which are surrounded by bracts. Its fruits are dry, indehiscent achenes enclosed in a persistent, spiny pappus, aiding in wind dispersal.

This species thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal scrub and dunes.
