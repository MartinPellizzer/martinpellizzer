Acacia dichromotricha

Acacia dichromotricha, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has slender, often spiny stems and is adapted to dry environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.

Acacia dichromotricha is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
