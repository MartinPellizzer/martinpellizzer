Aristolochia baetica var. glauca

Aristolochia baetica var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-flowered Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats. It produces small, dark berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
