Ardisia dumetosa

Ardisia dumetosa, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features small, dark purple to black berries that are visually striking and often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small white flowers during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Ardisia dumetosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
