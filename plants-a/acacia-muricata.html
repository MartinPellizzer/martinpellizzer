Acacia muricata

Acacia muricata, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which are often visited by birds and insects. Its woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in arid and semi-arid regions.

Acacia muricata is adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
