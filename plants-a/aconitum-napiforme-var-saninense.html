Aconitum napiforme var. saninense

Aconitum napiforme var. saninense, commonly known as the Saninense Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a distinctive bell-shaped flower with purple petals and a yellow throat, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has a thick, tuberous root system that stores nutrients and aids in survival during harsh conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a strong, toxic scent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
