Astragalus hakkariensis

Astragalus hakkariensis, commonly known as Hakkari milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus hakkariensis is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
