Agapetes acuminata var. tipiensis

Agapetes acuminata var. tipiensis, commonly known as the Tipiense Agapete, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. This evergreen shrub typically grows in cool, humid montane forests at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters. It features small, leathery leaves and produces bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
