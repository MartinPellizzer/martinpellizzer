Achillea nigrescens

Achillea nigrescens, commonly known as black yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, while its flower heads are composed of small, tubular florets.

Achillea nigrescens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
