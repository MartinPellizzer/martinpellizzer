Asplenium auriculatum var. incisuratum

Asplenium auriculatum var. incisuratum, commonly known as the serrated maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern characterized by its finely serrated leaf margins.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Its unique leaf structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and shaded garden settings.
