Anemia colimensis

Anemia colimensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that open in the early morning. Its botanical name refers to its historical use in traditional medicine for treating anemia.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
