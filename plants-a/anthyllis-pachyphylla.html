Anthyllis pachyphylla

Anthyllis pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that helps it conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
