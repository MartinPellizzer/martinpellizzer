Acmispon nevadensis var. davidsonii

Acmispon nevadensis var. davidsonii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre burclover, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its ecological importance is significant in arid environments where it contributes to biodiversity and ecosystem resilience.
