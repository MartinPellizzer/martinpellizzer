Asperula orientalis

Asperula orientalis, commonly known as oriental sweet pepper or Japanese hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Asperula orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
