Agrostis munroana subsp. indica

Agrostis munroana subsp.

indica, commonly known as the Indian meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
