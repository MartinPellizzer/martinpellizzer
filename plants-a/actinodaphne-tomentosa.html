Actinodaphne tomentosa

Actinodaphne tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant has a smooth, often greyish bark and produces yellowish-white berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
