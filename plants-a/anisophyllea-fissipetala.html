Anisophyllea fissipetala

Anisophyllea fissipetala, commonly known as the fissured-leaf anisophyllea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a faintly fragrant scent.

Anisophyllea fissipetala is valued for its ornamental foliage and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
