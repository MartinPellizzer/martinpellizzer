Atriplex crassifolia

Atriplex crassifolia, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Atriplex crassifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
