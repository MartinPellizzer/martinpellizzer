Abutilon mauritianum var. grandiflorum

Abutilon mauritianum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Mauritius velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Malvaceae family.
