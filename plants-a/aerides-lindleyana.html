Aerides lindleyana

Aerides lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the flowers are usually pink or white with a yellowish lip.

Aerides lindleyana is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
