Astragalus caprinus f. huetii

Astragalus caprinus f. huetii, commonly known as the Huet's goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
