Allium jajlae

Allium jajlae, commonly known as the Jajla onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that are used as culinary ingredients in local cuisines. Its leaves are grass-like and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

Allium jajlae is valued not only for its edible parts but also for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
