Arcytophyllum nitidum

Arcytophyllum nitidum, commonly known as the silver sagebrush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Arcytophyllum nitidum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert species.
