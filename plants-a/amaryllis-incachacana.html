Amaryllis incachacana

Amaryllis incachacana, commonly known as the Andean amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, erect stem with clustered blooms, and its leaves are broad and strap-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Amaryllis incachacana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
