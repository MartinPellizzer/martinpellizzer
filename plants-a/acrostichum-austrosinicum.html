Acrostichum austrosinicum

Acrostichum austrosinicum, commonly known as the Chinese cliff fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The botanical features of this fern make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
