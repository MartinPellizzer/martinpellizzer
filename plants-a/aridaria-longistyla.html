Aridaria longistyla

Aridaria longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled arid grass, is a perennial plant adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with long, slender stamens, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Aridaria longistyla is typically found in rocky soils and plays a role in stabilizing soil in dry landscapes.
