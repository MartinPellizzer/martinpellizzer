Adenia ambongensis

Adenia ambongensis, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive purple calyx, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
