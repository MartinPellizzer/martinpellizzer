Actinidia callosa var. formosana

Actinidia callosa var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan kiwifruit, is a deciduous vine native to Taiwan.

It produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to the common kiwifruit but have a thinner skin and a sweeter flavor. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value as well as its fruit production.
