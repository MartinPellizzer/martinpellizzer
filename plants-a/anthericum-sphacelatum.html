Anthericum sphacelatum

Anthericum sphacelatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, with the spathe serving as a visual attractant. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a prominent midrib.

Anthericum sphacelatum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
