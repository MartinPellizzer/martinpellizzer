Acer saccharum subsp. grandidentatum

Acer saccharum subsp.

grandidentatum, commonly known as the sugar maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its opposite, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of yellow, orange, and red in the fall.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also important in ecological and economic contexts.
