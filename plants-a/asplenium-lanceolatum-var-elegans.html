Asplenium lanceolatum var. elegans

Asplenium lanceolatum var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant spleenwort, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often glossy and have a subtle, elegant appearance. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
