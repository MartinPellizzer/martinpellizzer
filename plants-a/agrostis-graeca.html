Agrostis graeca

Agrostis graeca, commonly known as Greek bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant has slender, tufted stems and small, narrow leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle.

Agrostis graeca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
