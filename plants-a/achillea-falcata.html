Achillea falcata

Achillea falcata, commonly known as sickle yarrow, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. Its leaves are narrow, serrated, and often covered in fine hairs.

Achillea falcata is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
