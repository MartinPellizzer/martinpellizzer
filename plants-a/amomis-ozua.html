Amomis ozua

Amomis ozua, commonly known as the African ginger or Zingiber officinale, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous root system, which is the primary source of its aromatic and medicinal compounds. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, while the flowers are typically yellow or orange with red bracts.

Amomis ozua is valued for its culinary uses and traditional medicinal properties.
