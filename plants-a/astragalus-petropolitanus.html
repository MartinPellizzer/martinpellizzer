Astragalus petropolitanus

Astragalus petropolitanus, commonly known as the Petropolitan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Astragalus petropolitanus is a member of the legume family and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid environments.
