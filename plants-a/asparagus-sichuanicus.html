Asparagus sichuanicus

Asparagus sichuanicus, commonly known as Sichuan asparagus, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
