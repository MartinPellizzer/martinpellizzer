Astragalus grahamianus

Astragalus grahamianus, commonly known as Graham's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep taproots that allow it to survive in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus grahamianus is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
