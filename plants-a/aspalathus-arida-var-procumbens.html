Aspalathus arida var. procumbens

Aspalathus arida var. procumbens, commonly known as the coastal bushveld bushveld, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
