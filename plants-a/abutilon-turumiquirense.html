Abutilon turumiquirense

Abutilon turumiquirense, commonly known as the Turumiquirenses Abutilon, is a species native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
