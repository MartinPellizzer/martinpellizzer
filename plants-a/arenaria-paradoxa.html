Arenaria paradoxa

Arenaria paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It thrives in harsh environments, including rocky soils and tundra, where it forms dense mats to conserve heat and moisture. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss in cold, dry conditions.

Arenaria paradoxa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its extreme habitat.
