Amellus scandens

Amellus scandens, commonly known as the climbing amellus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amelanthaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant features slender, flexible stems that allow it to climb or trail along surfaces, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises. Its leaves are usually alternate and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Amellus scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
