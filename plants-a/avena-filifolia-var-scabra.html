Avena filifolia var. scabra

Avena filifolia var. scabra, commonly known as rough oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its wiry, hairless stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often rough to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
