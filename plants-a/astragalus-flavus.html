Astragalus flavus

Astragalus flavus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Astragalus flavus has small, hairy stems and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
