Acaena × lividella

Acaena × lividella, commonly known as the purple flax or New Zealand flax, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is native to New Zealand and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions. Its fibrous stems were historically used by Māori for making ropes and garments.

Acaena × lividella is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
