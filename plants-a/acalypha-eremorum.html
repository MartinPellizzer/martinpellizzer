Acalypha eremorum

Acalypha eremorum, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple bract-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments. Its structure includes a short, woody stem with opposite branching, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The botanical features of Acalypha eremorum make it an important plant in ecological studies of desert flora.
