Allium aschersonianum subsp. ambiguum

Allium aschersonianum subsp.

ambiguum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant has a strong garlic scent and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both gardens and botanical collections.
