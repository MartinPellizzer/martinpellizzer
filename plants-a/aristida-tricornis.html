Aristida tricornis

Aristida tricornis, commonly known as the three-awn grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to various regions of the Americas, including parts of the United States and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive three-branched awns on the seeds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, and it typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Aristida tricornis is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
