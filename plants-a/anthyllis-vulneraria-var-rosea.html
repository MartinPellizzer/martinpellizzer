Anthyllis vulneraria var. rosea

Anthyllis vulneraria var. rosea, commonly known as the rose pink everlasting.

This perennial herb is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils. It features wiry stems and clusters of small, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in dry gardens or as a cut flower.
