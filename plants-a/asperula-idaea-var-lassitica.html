Asperula idaea var. lassitica

Asperula idaea var. lassitica, commonly known as the alpine azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to cool, moist climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
