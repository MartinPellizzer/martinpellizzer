Aiouea guyanensis

Aiouea guyanensis, commonly known as Guyana aiouea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The species also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various forms of biodiversity.
