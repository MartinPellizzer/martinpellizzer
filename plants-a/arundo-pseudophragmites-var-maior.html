Arundo pseudophragmites var. maior

Arundo pseudophragmites var. maior, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to greenish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a slightly rough texture.

It is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and disturbed soils due to its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
