Arisaema suwoense

Arisaema suwoense, commonly known as the Suwo mountain arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky hillsides. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Arisaema suwoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
