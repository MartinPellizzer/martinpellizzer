Adamanthus compactus

Adamanthus compactus, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 4 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright blue berries in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly wavy edge.

Adamanthus compactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
