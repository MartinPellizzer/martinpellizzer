Aristida brownii

Aristida brownii, commonly known as Brown's threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Aristida brownii is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
