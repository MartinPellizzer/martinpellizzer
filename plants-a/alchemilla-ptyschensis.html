Alchemilla ptyschensis

Alchemilla ptyschensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Alchemilla ptyschensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
