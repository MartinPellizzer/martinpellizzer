Acaena philippii

Acaena philippii, commonly known as the Chilean burr bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its prickly, spherical fruits that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

Acaena philippii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
