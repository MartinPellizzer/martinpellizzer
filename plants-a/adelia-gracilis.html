Adelia gracilis

Adelia gracilis, commonly known as the slender adelia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment. Adelia gracilis is often found in rocky outcrops and along riverbanks, where it forms dense, low-growing mats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in semi-arid ecosystems.
