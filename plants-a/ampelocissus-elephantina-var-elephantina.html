Ampelocissus elephantina var. elephantina

Ampelocissus elephantina var. elephantina, commonly known as the elephant's foot plant, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that resemble elephant feet, which store water and help it survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, pale green leaves and yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it can become invasive if not properly controlled.
