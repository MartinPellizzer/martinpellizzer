Anthogonium griffithii

Anthogonium griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's anthogonium, is a rare and lesser-known plant species belonging to the family Anthogoniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and unique reproductive structures that resemble small, cup-like organs. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in temperate regions with high humidity. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a representative of an ancient lineage of plants with distinctive morphological features.

Due to its rarity, it is of interest to botanists studying plant evolution and conservation.
