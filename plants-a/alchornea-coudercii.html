Alchornea coudercii

Alchornea coudercii, commonly known as the African rubber plant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
