Aristida inversa

Aristida inversa, commonly known as the inverted grass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-blue foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, white bristles, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for both ecological restoration and landscape design.
