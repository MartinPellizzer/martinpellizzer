Aconitum soongaricum var. angustius

Aconitum soongaricum var. angustius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat fern-like appearance.

Aconitum soongaricum var. angustius is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.
