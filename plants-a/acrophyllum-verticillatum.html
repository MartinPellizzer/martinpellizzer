Acrophyllum verticillatum

Acrophyllum verticillatum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in forested areas. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Acrophyllum verticillatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
