Andropogon schweinfurthii

Andropogon schweinfurthii, commonly known as Schweinfurth's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and dry, sandy soils, exhibiting a clump-forming habit with narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of dense, spike-like clusters that mature to a golden or brown color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its drought tolerance and adaptability make it a resilient choice for native landscaping.
