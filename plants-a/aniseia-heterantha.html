Aniseia heterantha

Aniseia heterantha, commonly known as the variable anise tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elliptical fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a branched root system and a dense canopy of foliage.

Aniseia heterantha is valued for its aromatic properties and ecological significance in its native habitat.
