Acacia julifera subsp. julifera

Acacia julifera subsp.

julifera, commonly known as the Arabian thornacacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It is characterized by its straight, thorny branches and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures.

The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a capacity to store water in its trunk.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and charcoal.
