Aspidistra mushaensis

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features dark green, glossy, and elongated lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with subtle veining. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
