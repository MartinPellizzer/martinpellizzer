Amaranthus angustifolius proles polygonoides

Amaranthus angustifolius proles polygonoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and upright growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red in color. It is native to the Americas and is often cultivated for its edible seeds and ornamental value.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
