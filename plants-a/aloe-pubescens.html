Aloe pubescens

Aloe pubescens, commonly known as the woolly aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces striking orange or red flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Aloe pubescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
