Agapetes lacei

Agapetes lacei, commonly known as the silverleaf agapetes, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions with cool climates. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces berries that are edible for certain bird species.

Agapetes lacei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
