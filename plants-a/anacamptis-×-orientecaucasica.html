Anacamptis × orientecaucasica

Anacamptis × orientecaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of elongated, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Anacamptis × orientecaucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate water conditions.
