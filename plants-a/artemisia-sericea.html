Artemisia sericea

Artemisia sericea, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. Artemisia sericea thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
