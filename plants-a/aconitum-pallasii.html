Aconitum pallasii

Aconitum pallasii, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces follicular fruits that contain toxic seeds. Aconitum pallasii is known for its high concentration of alkaloids, making it highly poisonous to both animals and humans.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
