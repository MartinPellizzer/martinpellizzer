Ardisia arborea

Ardisia arborea, commonly known as the evergreen tree or mountain ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features dense, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ardisia arborea is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
