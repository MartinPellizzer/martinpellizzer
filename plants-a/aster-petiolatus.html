Aster petiolatus

Aster petiolatus, commonly known as the petioled aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, daisy-like blue to purple flowers. The plant has alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in meadows and open woodlands.

Aster petiolatus is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and ecological importance to pollinators.
