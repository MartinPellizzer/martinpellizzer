Alsophila schlimii

Alsophila schlimii, commonly known as the Schlim's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing cones, are typically found at the base of the fronds.

Alsophila schlimii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
