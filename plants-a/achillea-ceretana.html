Achillea ceretana

Achillea ceretana, commonly known as the Sicilian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from white to pale pink.

Achillea ceretana is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
