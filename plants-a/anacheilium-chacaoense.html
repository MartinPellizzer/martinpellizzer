Anacheilium chacaoense

Anacheilium chacaoense, commonly known as the Chacao orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant grows in shaded, humid environments and requires specific conditions to thrive, including high humidity and well-draining soil. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
