Achillea sivasica

Achillea sivasica, commonly known as Sivas yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
