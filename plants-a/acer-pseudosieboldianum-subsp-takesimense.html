Acer pseudosieboldianum subsp. takesimense

Acer pseudosieboldianum subsp.

takesimense, commonly known as the Takesima maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
