Aristolochia nuichuaensis

Aristolochia nuichuaensis, commonly known as the Mexican Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
