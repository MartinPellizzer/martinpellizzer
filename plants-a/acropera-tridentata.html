Acropera tridentata

Acropera tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils in pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and often have three distinct teeth, giving it its common name.

Acropera tridentata is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
