Alsophila microphylla

Alsophila microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its rhizomes form a dense network that helps anchor the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.

Alsophila microphylla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is well-suited for shaded, moist environments.
