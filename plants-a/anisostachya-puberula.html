Anisostachya puberula

Anisostachya puberula, commonly known as the silky grass or silky reed, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale-green or yellowish flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Anisostachya puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful, flowing foliage.
