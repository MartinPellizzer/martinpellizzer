Asplenium wilfordii var. curtidens

Asplenium wilfordii var. curtidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a varietal form of the maidenhair fern genus Asplenium.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered into sori covered by protective indusia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in horticulture.
