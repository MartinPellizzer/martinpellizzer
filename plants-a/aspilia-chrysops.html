Aspilia chrysops

Aspilia chrysops, commonly known as the yellow flame or golden aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Aspilia chrysops is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
