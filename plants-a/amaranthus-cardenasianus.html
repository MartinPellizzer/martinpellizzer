Amaranthus cardenasianus

Amaranthus cardenasianus, commonly known as the Cienega amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red or purple bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are small and dry.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
