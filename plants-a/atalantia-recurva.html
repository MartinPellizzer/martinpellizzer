Atalantia recurva

Atalantia recurva, commonly known as the coral tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which consist of clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged. Its bark is often fissured and may exude a milky sap when wounded.

Atalantia recurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
