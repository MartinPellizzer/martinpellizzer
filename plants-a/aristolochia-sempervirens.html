Aristolochia sempervirens

Aristolochia sempervirens, commonly known as the Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant has heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces berries that range from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and studied for its chemical compounds.
