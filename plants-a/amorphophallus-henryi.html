Amorphophallus henryi

Amorphophallus henryi, commonly known as the Henry's giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a tuberous root that serves as its storage organ and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
