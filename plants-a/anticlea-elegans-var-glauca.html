Anticlea elegans var. glauca

Anticlea elegans var. glauca, commonly known as the blue antelope plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
