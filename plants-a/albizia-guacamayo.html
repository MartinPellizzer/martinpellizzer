Albizia guacamayo

Albizia guacamayo, commonly known as the yellow wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often yellow-green in color, which give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, pendulous flowers that range from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. Albizia guacamayo has a fibrous root system and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable plant for ornamental landscaping.
