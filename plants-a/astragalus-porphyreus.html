Astragalus porphyreus

Astragalus porphyreus, commonly known as the Porphyry milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in dry, rocky environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus porphyreus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
