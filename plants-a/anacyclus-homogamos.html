Anacyclus homogamos

Anacyclus homogamos, commonly known as the desert cowpea, is an annual herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, spherical fruits that mature in the fall.

Anacyclus homogamos is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
