Acaena sanguisorbae var. subincisa

Acaena sanguisorbae var. subincisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burrplant, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream sides. Its leaves are alternate, with a serrated margin and a dark green, glossy appearance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
