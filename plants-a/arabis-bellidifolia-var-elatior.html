Arabis bellidifolia var. elatior

Arabis bellidifolia var. elatior, commonly known as the greater wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its clustered, bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, simple, and hairy, often with a slightly succulent texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
