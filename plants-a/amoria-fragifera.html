Amoria fragifera

Amoria fragifera, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the fall.

Amoria fragifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
