Allium kirindicum

Allium kirindicum, commonly known as the Kirin onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces spherical, fleshy bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens in suitable regions.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in various cultural contexts.
