Aster tenuipes

Aster tenuipes, commonly known as the slenderleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster tenuipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
