Arequipa weingartiana var. carminanthema

Arequipa weingartiana var. carminanthema, commonly known as the Crimson Arequipa, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant features rosettes of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy, powdery coating. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red and orange, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is prized for its vivid colors and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
