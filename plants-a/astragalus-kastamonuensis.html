Astragalus kastamonuensis

Astragalus kastamonuensis, commonly known as Kastamonu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. It has compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
