Atriplex angustifolia var. angustifolia

Atriplex angustifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important component of stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
