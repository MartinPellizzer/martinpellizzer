Acacia ulicina

Acacia ulicina, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia ulicina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
