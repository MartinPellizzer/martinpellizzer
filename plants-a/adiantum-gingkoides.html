Adiantum gingkoides

Adiantum gingkoides, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bright green with a metallic sheen. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in temperate regions. Its rhizomes are short and produce a rosette of sterile fronds.

Adiantum gingkoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
