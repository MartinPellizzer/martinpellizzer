Acer truncatum f. barbinerve

Acer truncatum f. barbinerve, commonly known as the barbed-veined maple, is a distinctive variant of the maple tree characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

The leaves are typically palmately lobed with serrated edges, and the veins are prominently barbed, giving the plant its specific epithet. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, red or yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that disperse by wind.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
