Agapanthus praecox subsp. minimus

Agapanthus praecox subsp.

minimus, commonly known as the small-flowered agapanthus, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South Africa. It features dense clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, emerging from grass-like foliage.

The plant produces spherical seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking floral display and ability to attract pollinators.
