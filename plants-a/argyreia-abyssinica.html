Argyreia abyssinica

Argyreia abyssinica, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Africa, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces large, round, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its striking appearance, it is cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses.
