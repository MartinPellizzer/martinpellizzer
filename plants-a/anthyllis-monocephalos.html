Anthyllis monocephalos

Anthyllis monocephalos, commonly known as the single-headed anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its distinctive, solitary flower head. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, pea-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces legume-like pods that contain seeds.

Anthyllis monocephalos plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
