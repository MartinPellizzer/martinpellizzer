Aspilia elliptica

Aspilia elliptica, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Aspilia elliptica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
