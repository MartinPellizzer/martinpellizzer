Alsophila walkerae var. walkerae

Alsophila walkerae var. walkerae, commonly known as the Walkera's tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, while the spore-producing structures are found on the undersides of the leaves.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to shaded, humid environments.
