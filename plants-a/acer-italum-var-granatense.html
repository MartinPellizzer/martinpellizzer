Acer italum var. granatense

Acer italum var. granatense, commonly known as the Granatense maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, red samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
