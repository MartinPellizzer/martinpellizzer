Acer mono f. heterophyllum

Acer mono f. heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable maple, is a unique variant of the singleleaf maple.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shape, which ranges from oval to ovate or even lanceolate. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a smooth texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
