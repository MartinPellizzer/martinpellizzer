Acoridium glumaceum

Acoridium glumaceum, commonly known as the spike rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that form dense clumps, and its flowering spikes are usually erect and contain small, inconspicuous flowers. Acoridium glumaceum plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its botanical features make it a common indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
