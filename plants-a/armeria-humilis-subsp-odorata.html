Armeria humilis subsp. odorata

Armeria humilis subsp.

odorata, commonly known as the sweet armeria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It features dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
