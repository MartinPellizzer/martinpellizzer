Amygdalus communis f. ovata

Amygdalus communis f. ovata, commonly known as the oval almond.

This cultivar is a variant of the common almond, characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruits. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.
