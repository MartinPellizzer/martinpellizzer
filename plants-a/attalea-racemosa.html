Attalea racemosa

Attalea racemosa, commonly known as the palm tree of the Amazon, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces large, yellowish-pink inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, facilitating wind pollination. Its fruits are small, round drupes that contain a single seed, which is used in traditional practices and for propagation.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
