Acronia rutrifolia

Acronia rutrifolia, commonly known as the red-leafed acronia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that turn a deep red or maroon color as they mature. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Acronia rutrifolia thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
