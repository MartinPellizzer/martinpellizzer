Aira caryophyllea var. inflexa

Aira caryophyllea var. inflexa, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces striking purple to pink flower spikes in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The inflorescence is typically slender and may curve downward, giving it a distinctive appearance.
