Astragalus toppinianus

Astragalus toppinianus, commonly known as Toppin's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus toppinianus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
