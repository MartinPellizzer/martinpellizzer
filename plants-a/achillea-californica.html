Achillea californica

Achillea californica, commonly known as California yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a robust root system and upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves that are finely divided and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local wildlife.
