Allium brevidentatum

Allium brevidentatum, commonly known as the short-toothed garlic.

It is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Mediterranean. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs with a strong, pungent odor similar to garlic. Its leaves are grass-like and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom in the summer months.
