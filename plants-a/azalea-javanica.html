Azalea javanica

Azalea javanica, commonly known as the Javan azalea, is a flowering plant native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea javanica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
