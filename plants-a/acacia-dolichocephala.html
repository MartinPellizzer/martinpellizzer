Acacia dolichocephala

Acacia dolichocephala, commonly known as the long-headed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves that are typically bipinnate. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Acacia dolichocephala is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
