Astragalus ischredensis

Astragalus ischredensis, commonly known as the Ischred's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance is significant in its native habitats.
