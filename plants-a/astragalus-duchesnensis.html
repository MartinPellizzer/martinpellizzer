Astragalus duchesnensis

Astragalus duchesnensis, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
