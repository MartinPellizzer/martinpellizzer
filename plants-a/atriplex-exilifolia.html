Atriplex exilifolia

Atriplex exilifolia, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity.

Atriplex exilifolia plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
