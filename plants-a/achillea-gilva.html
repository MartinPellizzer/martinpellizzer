Achillea gilva

Achillea gilva, commonly known as mountain yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Achillea gilva is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
