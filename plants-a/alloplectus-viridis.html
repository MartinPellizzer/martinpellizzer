Alloplectus viridis

Alloplectus viridis, commonly known as the green threadleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, thread-like, and covered in fine hairs.

Alloplectus viridis is often found in coastal and mountainous regions where it thrives in full sun.
