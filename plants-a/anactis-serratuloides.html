Anactis serratuloides

Anactis serratuloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and often hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
