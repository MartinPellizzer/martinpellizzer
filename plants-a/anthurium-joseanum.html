Anthurium joseanum

Anthurium joseanum, commonly known as the Joseanum Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces an inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is often mistaken for a flower. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
