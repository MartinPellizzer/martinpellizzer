Acacia linophylla

Acacia linophylla, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia linophylla is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
