Aristolochia chrismuelleriana

Aristolochia chrismuelleriana, commonly known as the Christmas vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
