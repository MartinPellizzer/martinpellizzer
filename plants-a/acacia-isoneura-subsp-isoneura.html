Acacia isoneura subsp. isoneura

Acacia isoneura subsp.

isoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is well adapted to dry environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
