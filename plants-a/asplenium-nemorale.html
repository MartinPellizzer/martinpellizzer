Asplenium nemorale

Asplenium nemorale, commonly known as the wood spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic plant, often found growing on trees or rocks in moist, shaded environments. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves, typical of ferns.

Asplenium nemorale is well adapted to humid climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
