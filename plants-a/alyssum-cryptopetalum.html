Alyssum cryptopetalum

Alyssum cryptopetalum, commonly known as the creeping alyssum, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
