Australopyrum pectinatum var. velutinum

Australopyrum pectinatum var. velutinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf lily, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
