Anisantha diandra

Anisantha diandra, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in sandy soils and access deep water sources.

Anisantha diandra plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
