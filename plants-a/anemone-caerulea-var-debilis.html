Anemone caerulea var. debilis

Anemone caerulea var. debilis, commonly known as the blue anemone, is a delicate perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the leaves. The plant has finely divided, fern-like foliage that adds texture to garden landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both cultivated and natural ecosystems.
