Astragalus khadjouicus

Astragalus khadjouicus, commonly known as the Khadjouic milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and may have potential uses in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across its native habitat.
