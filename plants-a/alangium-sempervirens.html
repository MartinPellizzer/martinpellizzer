Alangium sempervirens

Alangium sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen tree or Chinese sandalwood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine and as a timber source.
