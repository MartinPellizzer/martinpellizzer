Anisomeria coriacea var. lanceolata

Anisomeria coriacea var. lanceolata, commonly known as the roughleaf anisomeria, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique foliage and drought tolerance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
