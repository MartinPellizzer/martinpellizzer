Androsace filiformis var. exscapa

Androsace filiformis var. exscapa, commonly known as the slender leaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a rosette of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine displays.
