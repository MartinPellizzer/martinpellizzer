Aglaia querciflorescens

Aglaia querciflorescens, commonly known as the Indian teak or Indian mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces woody capsules containing aromatic seeds, and its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine.

Aglaia querciflorescens is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
