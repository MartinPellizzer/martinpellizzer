Allium ampeloprasum subsp. genuinum

Allium ampeloprasum subsp.

genuinum, commonly known as the purple garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential as a food crop.
