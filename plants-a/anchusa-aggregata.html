Anchusa aggregata

Anchusa aggregata, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is its dense clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Anchusa aggregata is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
