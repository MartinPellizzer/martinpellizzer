Alectorolophus semleri

Alectorolophus semleri, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red or orange fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
