Anabasis al-rawii

Anabasis al-rawii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure that helps it survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss. Anabasis al-rawii is adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a hardy species in desert ecosystems.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas effectively.
