Asplenium hookerianum

Asplenium hookerianum, commonly known as Hooker's spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic plant, typically found growing on rocks or trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base, with a distinctive, creeping rhizome. Its fronds are usually dark green and possess a serrated or toothed margin.

Asplenium hookerianum is noted for its adaptability to various substrates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
