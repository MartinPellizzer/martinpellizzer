Astragalus sirinicus

Astragalus sirinicus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer, followed by narrow, pod-like fruits. Astragalus sirinicus is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its name derives from the Greek word "astragalos," referring to the shape of its fruit, which resembles a die used in ancient games.
