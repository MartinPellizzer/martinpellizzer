Aralia officinalis

Aralia officinalis, commonly known as American aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, with each leaf composed of 10 to 30 narrow, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has been used historically for its medicinal properties, including treatments for respiratory and digestive ailments.
