Astragalus saccatocarpus

Astragalus saccatocarpus, commonly known as the saccatocarpus milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated pods that enclose its seeds, a feature that aids in seed dispersal. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus saccatocarpus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
