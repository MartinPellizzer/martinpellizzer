Ascyrum tetragonum

Ascyrum tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, ribbed stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Ascyrum tetragonum is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
