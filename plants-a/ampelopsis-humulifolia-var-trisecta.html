Ampelopsis humulifolia var. trisecta

Ampelopsis humulifolia var. trisecta, commonly known as the three-lobed bittersweet, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive, maple-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
