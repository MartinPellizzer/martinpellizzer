Artemisia vulgaris subsp. litoralis

Artemisia vulgaris subsp.

litoralis, commonly known as sea wormwood, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and dunes. Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and historical uses.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
