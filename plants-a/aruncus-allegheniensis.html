Aruncus allegheniensis

Aruncus allegheniensis, commonly known as the Allegheny goatweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, stout stems that are often reddish in color and are adorned with clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has large, heart-shaped or ovate leaves that are typically green and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Aruncus allegheniensis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
