Acacia gualparensis

Acacia gualparensis, commonly known as the Gualparensis acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in Brazil and parts of Paraguay.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia gualparensis is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.
