Aegilops crassa var. typica

Aegilops crassa var. typica, commonly known as the broad-leafed goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and broad, flat leaves that are often rough to the touch. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing several small, spike-like flowers. It is typically found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Aegilops crassa var. typica is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
