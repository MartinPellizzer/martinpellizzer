Arthraxon xinanensis var. laxiflorus

Arthraxon xinanensis var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the loose-flowered arthraxon, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to white flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
