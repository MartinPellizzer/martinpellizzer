Adiantum obovatum

Adiantum obovatum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate, evergreen fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It features finely divided, dark green, lance-shaped fronds that are often curled when young. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

Adiantum obovatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
