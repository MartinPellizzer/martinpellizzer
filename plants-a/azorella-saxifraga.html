Azorella saxifraga

Azorella saxifraga, commonly known as the Andean rock cress, is a hardy perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are usually succulent and adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates.

Azorella saxifraga is often found growing on rocky slopes and in alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
