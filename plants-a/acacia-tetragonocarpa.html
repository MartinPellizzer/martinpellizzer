Acacia tetragonocarpa

Acacia tetragonocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves composed of small, opposite leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia tetragonocarpa is well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments, showing some degree of drought tolerance.
