Asteranthe asterias subsp. asterias

Asteranthe asterias subsp.

asterias, commonly known as the starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant features showy, radially symmetrical flowers with blue to purple petals, typically arranged in a compact cluster. It has linear to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Asteranthe asterias subsp.

asterias thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
