Agathosma cuspidata

Agathosma cuspidata, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, succulent stems and leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in South African herbal medicine.
