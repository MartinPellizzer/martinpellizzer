Astronia cuspidata

Astronia cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats.

Astronia cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
