Athyrium thelypteroides f. acrostichoides

Athyrium thelypteroides f. acrostichoides, commonly known as the acrostichoid fern, is a varietal form of the broadleaf fern.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, bipinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The fronds are typically bright green with a smooth, glossy surface and are arranged in a compact, upright habit. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its lush, tropical appearance.
