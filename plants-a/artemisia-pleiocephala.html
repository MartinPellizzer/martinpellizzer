Artemisia pleiocephala

Artemisia pleiocephala, commonly known as the broad-headed wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Artemisia pleiocephala is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
