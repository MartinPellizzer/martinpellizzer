Avonsera

Avonsera, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, hanging clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. Avonsera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown as a climbing plant or trained on trellises.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it beneficial to local ecosystems.
