Aureliana sellowiana

Aureliana sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and requires a humid, warm environment to thrive. Its roots are often epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surface of trees rather than in soil.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
