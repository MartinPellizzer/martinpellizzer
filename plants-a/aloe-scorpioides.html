Aloe scorpioides

Aloe scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpion aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves contain a gel that is sometimes used for topical applications, though it is less common than in other aloe species.

Aloe scorpioides is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for succulent gardens.
