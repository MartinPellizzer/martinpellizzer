Alyssum murale f. patens

Alyssum murale f. patens, commonly known as the coastal alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found along coastal areas. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, decorative plants.
