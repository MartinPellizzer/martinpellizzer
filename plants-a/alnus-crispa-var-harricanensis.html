Alnus crispa var. harricanensis

Alnus crispa var. harricanensis, commonly known as the Harrican Mountain alder, is a variant of the alder tree native to specific regions of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its finely serrated leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The wood of this plant is lightweight and historically used by indigenous peoples for crafting tools and canoes.
