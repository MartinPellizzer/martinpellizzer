Acacia scalpelliformis

Acacia scalpelliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Acacia scalpelliformis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
