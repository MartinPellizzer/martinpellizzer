Arenaria formosa var. latipetala

Arenaria formosa var. latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petaled sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
