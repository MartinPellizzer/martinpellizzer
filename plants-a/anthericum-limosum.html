Anthericum limosum

Anthericum limosum, commonly known as the bog asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Anthericum limosum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in moist habitats.
