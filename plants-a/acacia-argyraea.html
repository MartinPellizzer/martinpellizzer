Acacia argyraea

Acacia argyraea, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small shrub or tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, greyish branches and compound leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Acacia argyraea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various native animals.
