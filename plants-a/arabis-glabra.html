Arabis glabra

Arabis glabra, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. It has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Arabis glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
