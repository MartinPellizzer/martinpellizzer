Acaena platyacantha var. trigonacantha

Acaena platyacantha var. trigonacantha, commonly known as the three-angled wood rose, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, spiny stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its adaptation to the temperate climate of its native habitat.
