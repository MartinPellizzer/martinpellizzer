Amaranthus flavescens

Amaranthus flavescens, commonly known as yellow amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its branched stems and leaves that range from lanceolate to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its seeds are small and oval, often used as a grain in traditional cuisines.

Amaranthus flavescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
