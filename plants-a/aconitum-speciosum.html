Aconitum speciosum

Aconitum speciosum, commonly known as the Chinese aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in tall, spires. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it highly poisonous to both humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

Morphology

Aconitum speciosum has compound, palmately lobed leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a rough texture and a height of up to 50 cm.

The stem is upright, hollow, and has a smooth, bluish-green bark that becomes rougher with age. Its flowers are showy, blue to purple, and consist of five petals, blooming in summer from July to August, and are pollinated by bees. The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are deep and fibrous, anchoring the plant and absorbing moisture from the soil.
