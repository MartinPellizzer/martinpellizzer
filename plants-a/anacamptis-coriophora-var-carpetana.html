Anacamptis coriophora var. carpetana

Anacamptis coriophora var. carpetana, commonly known as the Carpet Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
