Asplenium yoshinagae

Asplenium yoshinagae, commonly known as the Yoshinaga's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit with closely spaced, linear leaves. The plant typically grows on rock surfaces or tree trunks in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with spore-producing structures on the underside.

Asplenium yoshinagae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
