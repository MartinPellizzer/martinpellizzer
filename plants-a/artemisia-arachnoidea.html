Artemisia arachnoidea

Artemisia arachnoidea, commonly known as desert sage, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Artemisia arachnoidea is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in various medicinal and cultural practices.
