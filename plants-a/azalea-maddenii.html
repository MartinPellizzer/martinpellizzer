Azalea maddenii

Azalea maddenii, commonly known as the golden azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 feet, with dense, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. Azalea maddenii thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
