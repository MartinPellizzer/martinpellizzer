Alysicarpus gautalensis

Alysicarpus gautalensis, commonly known as the Indian burr or Gautala burr, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces distinctive, spiny, pod-like fruits that enclose its seeds. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a pea-like structure typical of the legume family.

Alysicarpus gautalensis is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
