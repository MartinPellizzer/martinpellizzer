Arkezostis latifolia

Arkezostis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arkezostis, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and naturalized areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among horticulturists and landscape designers.
