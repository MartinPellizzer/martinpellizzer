Amaranthus carura

Amaranthus carura, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red to purple bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, three-petaled flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus carura is known for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional cuisines in some regions.
