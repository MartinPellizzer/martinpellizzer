Arthropteris anniana

Arthropteris anniana, commonly known as the golden huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, strap-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the leaves. Arthropteris anniana is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and India.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
