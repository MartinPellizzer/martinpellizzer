Argentina tugitakensis

Argentina tugitakensis, commonly known as the Argentine thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of Argentina.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions where it outcompetes native flora.
