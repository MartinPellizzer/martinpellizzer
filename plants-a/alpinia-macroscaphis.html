Alpinia macroscaphis

Alpinia macroscaphis, commonly known as the giant ginger or mountain ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia macroscaphis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
