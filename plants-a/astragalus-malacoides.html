Astragalus malacoides

Astragalus malacoides, commonly known as the smooth milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a deep root system, which helps it thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, facilitating its propagation.

Astragalus malacoides is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
