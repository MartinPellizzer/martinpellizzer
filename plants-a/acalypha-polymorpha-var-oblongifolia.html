Acalypha polymorpha var. oblongifolia

Acalypha polymorpha var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false abutilon, is a strikingly ornamental shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that range in color from red to orange, often mistaken for flowers. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a velvety texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its unique foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid conditions.
