Artemisia ludoviciana var. gnaphalodes

Artemisia ludoviciana var. gnaphalodes, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for native landscaping.
