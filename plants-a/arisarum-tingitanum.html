Arisarum tingitanum

Arisarum tingitanum, commonly known as the Canary Island arum, is a rare and striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is known for its dramatic, almost theatrical appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
