Anathallis cabeceirensis

Anathallis cabeceirensis, commonly known as the Cabeceira spider orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, leafless stem that bears a single, showy, greenish-white flower. The plant has a specialized root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and its growth is closely tied to specific microhabitats. Anathallis cabeceirensis is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a labellum that resembles a spider, giving it its common name.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered critically endangered and is a subject of conservation efforts.
