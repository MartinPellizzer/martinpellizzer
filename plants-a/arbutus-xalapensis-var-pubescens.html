Arbutus xalapensis var. pubescens

Arbutus xalapensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf madrone, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in clusters, followed by edible, dark red berries that are a food source for various wildlife. Its leaves are evergreen, elliptical, and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

This species is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
