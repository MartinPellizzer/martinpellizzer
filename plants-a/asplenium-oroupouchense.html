Asplenium oroupouchense

Asplenium oroupouchense, commonly known as the Ouro-Pouch fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The fronds are typically dark green, with a glossy surface and a narrow, lanceolate shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
