Acacia senegal var. sanguinea

Acacia senegal var. sanguinea, commonly known as the red acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of whitish hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing a sugary gum called acacia gum. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species for soil stabilization and local livelihoods.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable component of its native ecosystems.
