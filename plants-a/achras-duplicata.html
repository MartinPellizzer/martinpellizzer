Achras duplicata

Achras duplicata, commonly known as the double-leaf achiote, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are divided into two lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and seeds of Achras duplicata have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
