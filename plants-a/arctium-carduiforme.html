Arctium carduiforme

Arctium carduiforme, commonly known as the heartweed thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides, exhibiting a robust growth habit. Its leaves are broadly lobed and covered in dense, white hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.
