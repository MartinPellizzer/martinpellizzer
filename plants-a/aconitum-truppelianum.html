Aconitum truppelianum

Aconitum truppelianum, commonly known as the Truppel's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum truppelianum is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
