Acanthophyllum glandulosum var. roseum

Acanthophyllum glandulosum var. roseum, commonly known as the pink spiny broom.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. The plant features pale green to grayish foliage with dense, spiny stems that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically pink to rose-colored, adding visual interest to its otherwise desolate habitat.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native environment.
