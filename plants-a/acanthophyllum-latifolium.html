Acanthophyllum latifolium

Acanthophyllum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf acanthophyllum, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, grayish-green leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in regions with low precipitation.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
