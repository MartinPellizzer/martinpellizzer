Atocion armeroides

Atocion armeroides, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is common among grass-like species.

The botanical features of Atocion armeroides make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
