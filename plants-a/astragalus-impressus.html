Astragalus impressus

Astragalus impressus, commonly known as the impressed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its root system is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils.

Astragalus impressus is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local wildlife.
