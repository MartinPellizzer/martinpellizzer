Amelanchier oreophila

Amelanchier oreophila, commonly known as the mountain serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval to elliptic leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
