Agoseris laevigata

Agoseris laevigata, commonly known as the smooth agoseris, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving them a soft texture.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
