Acanthanthus ayacuchensis

Acanthanthus ayacuchensis, commonly known as the Ayacuchensis Acanthanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous areas with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
