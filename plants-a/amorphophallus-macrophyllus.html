Amorphophallus macrophyllus

Amorphophallus macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy underground tuber, which can grow to impressive sizes. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers that are often surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tuber is not only ornamental but also has traditional uses in some cultures.
