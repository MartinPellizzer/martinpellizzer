Acanthogonum polygonoides

Acanthogonum polygonoides, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves. Its growth habit is often clump-forming, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Acanthogonum polygonoides is sometimes used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to form dense ground cover.
