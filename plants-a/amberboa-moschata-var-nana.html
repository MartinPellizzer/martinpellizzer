Amberboa moschata var. nana

Amberboa moschata var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf musk amaranth, is a compact, herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are small, oblong, and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
