Astranthium orthopodum

Astranthium orthopodum, commonly known as the upright thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are spiny and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Astranthium orthopodum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
