Acinodendron rufescens

Acinodendron rufescens, commonly known as the red-barked acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark that often appears reddish when exposed. The plant has bipinnate leaves, with each leaflet being small and oval-shaped. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods.

Acinodendron rufescens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
