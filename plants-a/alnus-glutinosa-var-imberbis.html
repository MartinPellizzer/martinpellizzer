Alnus glutinosa var. imberbis

Alnus glutinosa var. imberbis, commonly known as the smooth alder, is a variant of the alder tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers that are wind-pollinated, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or wetlands.

This species is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
