Arnica foliosa var. incana

Arnica foliosa var. incana, commonly known as the woolly arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows. Its flowers are tubular and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Arnica foliosa var. incana is valued for its medicinal properties, with the leaves and flowers used in traditional herbal remedies.
