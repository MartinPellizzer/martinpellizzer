Acacia botrycephala

Acacia botrycephala, commonly known as the globular acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical crown and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, consisting of many small leaflets.

Acacia botrycephala is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
