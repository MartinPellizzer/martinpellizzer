Aralia elata var. rotundata

Aralia elata var. rotundata, commonly known as the Japanese giant arum, is a striking plant native to Japan and Korea.

It features large, umbrella-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces spadices, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble a single flower, though they are actually clusters of tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
