Antitoxicum sibiricum

Antitoxicum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian antitoxic, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in umbels, a common feature among members of its family. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine, valued for their potential detoxifying properties.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
