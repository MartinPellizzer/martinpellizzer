Anemone pseudoaltaica f. tosshozana

Anemone pseudoaltaica f. tosshozana, commonly known as the Tosshozana anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive silvery underside, and it produces a basal rosette of foliage.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
