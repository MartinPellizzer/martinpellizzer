Anthyllis insularum

Anthyllis insularum, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, showing adaptability to arid conditions. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Anthyllis insularum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
