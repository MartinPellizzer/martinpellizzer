Anisandra

Anisandra, commonly known as the moonflower, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant has thick, waxy leaves and produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Anisandra thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its unique flowering pattern makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
