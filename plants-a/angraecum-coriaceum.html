Angraecum coriaceum

Angraecum coriaceum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue with a deep purple or violet center. The plant produces long, slender, white or pinkish sepals and petals that are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Angraecum coriaceum is also notable for its ability to produce nectar, attracting specific pollinators like moths.
