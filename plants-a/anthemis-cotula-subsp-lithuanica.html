Anthemis cotula subsp. lithuanica

Anthemis cotula subsp.

lithuanica, commonly known as the Lithuanian wormwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This subspecies is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
