Acetosella vinaquillo

Acetosella vinaquillo, commonly known as the purple sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often tinged with purple or red. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Acetosella vinaquillo is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
