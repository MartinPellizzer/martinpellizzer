Agropyron yezoense var. koryoense

Agropyron yezoense var. koryoense, commonly known as Yezo wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green color.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
