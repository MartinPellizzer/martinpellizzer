Anisostachya latebracteata

Anisostachya latebracteata, commonly known as the latebracteate reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense stands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological studies.
