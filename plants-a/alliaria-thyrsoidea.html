Alliaria thyrsoidea

Alliaria thyrsoidea, commonly known as winter garlic, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Winter garlic is known for its aromatic scent and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
