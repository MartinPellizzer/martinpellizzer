Astragalus coltonii var. coltonii

Astragalus coltonii var. coltonii, commonly known as the Colton's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus coltonii var. coltonii is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food for native pollinators and wildlife.
