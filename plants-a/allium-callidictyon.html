Allium callidictyon

Allium callidictyon, commonly known as the Cyprus leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Allium callidictyon is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary potential.
