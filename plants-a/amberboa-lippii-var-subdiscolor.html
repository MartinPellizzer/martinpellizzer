Amberboa lippii var. subdiscolor

Amberboa lippii var. subdiscolor, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features a sprawling growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a silvery-white underside that gives it its common name.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open plains.
