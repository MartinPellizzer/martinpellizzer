Actinidia giraldii

Actinidia giraldii, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal potential.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
