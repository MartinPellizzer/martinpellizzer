Acianthus confusus

Acianthus confusus, commonly known as the confused acianthus, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and typically produces a single, showy flower. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water, which is an adaptation to its arid habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
