Antimima menniei

Antimima menniei, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Antimima menniei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
