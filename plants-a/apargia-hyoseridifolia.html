Apargia hyoseridifolia

Apargia hyoseridifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Apargia hyoseridifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
