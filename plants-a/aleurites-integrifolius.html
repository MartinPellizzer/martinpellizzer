Aleurites integrifolius

Aleurites integrifolius, commonly known as the cannonball tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces round, green, fleshy fruits that turn orange when ripe, containing numerous seeds. Its bark is smooth and light brown, often marked with horizontal lenticels.

Aleurites integrifolius is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and cultural significance in various regions.
