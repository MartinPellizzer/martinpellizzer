Anemone hepatica f. chlorosa

Anemone hepatica f. chlorosa, commonly known as the green-flowered liverwort anemone, is a variant of the liverleaf anemone.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The flowers are smaller and more pale compared to the typical form, often appearing in shades of greenish-yellow. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring.
