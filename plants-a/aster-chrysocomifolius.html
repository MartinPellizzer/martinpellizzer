Aster chrysocomifolius

Aster chrysocomifolius, commonly known as the golden-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps to reduce water loss.

Aster chrysocomifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
