Arctium fedtschenkoanum

Arctium fedtschenkoanum, commonly known as the Siberian burweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces spiny, bur-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
