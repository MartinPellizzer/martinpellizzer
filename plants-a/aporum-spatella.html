Aporum spatella

Aporum spatella, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a unique tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a visually striking appearance. The plant features long, slender fronds that grow in a helical pattern around the stem. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and small, clustered spore-producing structures called sori.

Aporum spatella thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
