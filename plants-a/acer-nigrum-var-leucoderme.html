Acer nigrum var. leucoderme

Acer nigrum var. leucoderme, commonly known as the white-leaf maple, is a variant of the black maple species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale green leaves, which contrast sharply with its dark bark. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage turns vibrant yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
