Asarca collina

Asarca collina, commonly known as mountain ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically in shades of white or green, and are surrounded by bracts.

Asarca collina is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
