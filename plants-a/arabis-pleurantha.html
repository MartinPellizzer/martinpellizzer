Arabis pleurantha

Arabis pleurantha, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Arabis pleurantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
