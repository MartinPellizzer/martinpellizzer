Anemia blechnoides

Anemia blechnoides, commonly known as the silver fern, is a perennial herb native to the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are composed of overlapping, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, which gives it its common name.

Anemia blechnoides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
