Astragalus himalayensis

Astragalus himalayensis, commonly known as Himalayan milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in rocky and arid soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance is notable in alpine ecosystems where it contributes to biodiversity.
