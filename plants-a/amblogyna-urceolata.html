Amblogyna urceolata

Amblogyna urceolata, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that resemble a bottlebrush, typically ranging in color from red to orange. The plant features woody stems with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually elliptical in shape.

Amblogyna urceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
