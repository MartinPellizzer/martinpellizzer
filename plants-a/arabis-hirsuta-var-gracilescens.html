Arabis hirsuta var. gracilescens

Arabis hirsuta var. gracilescens, commonly known as the slender hairy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and linear, with a dense covering of fine white trichomes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous or coastal habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
