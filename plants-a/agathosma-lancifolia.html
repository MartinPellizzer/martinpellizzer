Agathosma lancifolia

Agathosma lancifolia, commonly known as wild fennel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Agathosma lancifolia is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and aromatherapy.
