Abutilon pittieri

Abutilon pittieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fibrous bark and hairy stems are notable features, and it is often found in moist, tropical environments.

Abutilon pittieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
