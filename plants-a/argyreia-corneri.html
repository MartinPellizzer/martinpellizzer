Argyreia corneri

Argyreia corneri, commonly known as the red veined morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins and its large, showy blue-violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and vigorous climbing habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
