Abies brachytyla

Abies brachytyla, commonly known as the dwarf white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in high elevations, often in subalpine or alpine habitats, where it forms dense stands. The tree has a slender, often crooked trunk and short, horizontal branches that bear needle-like leaves. Its cones are small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique form and winter interest.
