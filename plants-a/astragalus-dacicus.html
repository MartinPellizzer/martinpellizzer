Astragalus dacicus

Astragalus dacicus, commonly known as the Macedonian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by slender, bean-like pods. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Astragalus dacicus plays a role in soil stabilization and is valued for its ecological and medicinal potential.
