Athamanta alpina

Athamanta alpina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Athamanta alpina is often found in alpine meadows and is a common species in mountainous flora.
