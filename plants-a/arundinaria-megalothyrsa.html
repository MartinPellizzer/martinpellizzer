Arundinaria megalothyrsa

Arundinaria megalothyrsa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces feathery, panicle-like inflorescences that are often adorned with long, narrow leaves. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it has been used historically in construction and as a source of material for various applications.
