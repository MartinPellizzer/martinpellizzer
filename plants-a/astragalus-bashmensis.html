Astragalus bashmensis

Astragalus bashmensis, commonly known as the Bashkir milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Astragalus bashmensis is valued for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
