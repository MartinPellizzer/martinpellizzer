Astrophytum crassispinum

Astrophytum crassispinum, commonly known as the thick-needle cactus, is a species native to the Chihuahua Desert in Mexico.

It is a low-growing, globular cactus with thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its spines are short and dense, providing protection from herbivores.

Astrophytum crassispinum is well adapted to extreme desert environments, making it a resilient and visually striking plant.
