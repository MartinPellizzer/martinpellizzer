Alstroemeria pulchra subsp. lavandulacea

Alstroemeria pulchra subsp.

lavandulacea, commonly known as the lavender lily, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. Its flowers are long-lasting and often used in bouquets, while its foliage adds a lush, green backdrop.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
