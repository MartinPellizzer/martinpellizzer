Acacia juncifolia var. planifolia

Acacia juncifolia var. planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, flat, linear leaves that are often mistaken for grass. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
