Acacia spirocarpa f. pubescens

Acacia spirocarpa f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and phyllodes that are typically hairy, giving it its specific epithet "pubescens.". The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its drought tolerance and unique foliage.
