Acrostichum velleum

Acrostichum velleum, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a creeping underground stem that produces new shoots. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
