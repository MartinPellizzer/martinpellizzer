Acacia holosericea var. pubescens

Acacia holosericea var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its golden-yellow floral spikes that appear during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. Its flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences and are highly attractive to birds and insects.

The species is well adapted to dry and sandy soils, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
