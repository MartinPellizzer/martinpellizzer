Adenophora verticillata

Adenophora verticillata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
