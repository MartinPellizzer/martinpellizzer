Archidendron laxiflorum

Archidendron laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered legume, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes, and attract various pollinators.

It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
