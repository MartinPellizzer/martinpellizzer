Adenophora linearifolia

Adenophora linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
