Anthurium sylvestre

Anthurium sylvestre, commonly known as the wild anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often a deep green, with some varieties exhibiting variegation or reddish veins.

Anthurium sylvestre is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
