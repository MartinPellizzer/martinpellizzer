Allium guttatum subsp. sardoum

Allium guttatum subsp.

sardoum, commonly known as the Sardinian leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia. It features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, often with a slight sheathing at the base.

The plant produces spherical umbels of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are typically small and cylindrical, with a mild garlic-like flavor.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisines.
