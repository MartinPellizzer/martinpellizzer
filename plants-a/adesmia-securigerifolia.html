Adesmia securigerifolia

Adesmia securigerifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burro bush, is a small shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.

Adesmia securigerifolia is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.
