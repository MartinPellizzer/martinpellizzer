Atropis kamtschatica

Atropis kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatka lily, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern Japan.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Atropis kamtschatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and ecological significance.
