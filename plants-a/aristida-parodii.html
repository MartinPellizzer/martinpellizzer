Aristida parodii

Aristida parodii, commonly known as the Parodii grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
