Allium roseum var. typicum

Allium roseum var. typicum, commonly known as the rose garlic or purple flowering garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking lavender-pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, white, garlic-like bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical classification places it in the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants.
