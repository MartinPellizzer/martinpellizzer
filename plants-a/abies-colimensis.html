Abies colimensis

Abies colimensis, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Abies, which includes other fir species, and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically pendulous and contain seeds surrounded by a papery covering. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
