Alstroemeria

Alstroemeria, commonly known as the *peruvian lily*, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Alstroemeriaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, orange, yellow, and pink. The plant typically has strap-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. Alstroemeria thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.

Its long-lasting blooms and adaptability have made it a popular choice in both horticulture and the cut flower industry.
