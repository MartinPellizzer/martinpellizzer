Arabis carduchorum

Arabis carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and linear.

Arabis carduchorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
