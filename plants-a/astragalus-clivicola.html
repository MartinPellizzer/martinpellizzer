Astragalus clivicola

Astragalus clivicola, commonly known as the cliff astragalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments such as cliffs and slopes, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus clivicola is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
