Aegiphila vestita

Aegiphila vestita, commonly known as the silky aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Aegiphila vestita is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and fragrant foliage.
