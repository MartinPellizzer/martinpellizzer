Akebia clematifolia

Akebia clematifolia, commonly known as the Japanese arrowroot or climbing cinnamon vine, is a deciduous flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with white or pale green veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its unique features include the presence of tendrils for climbing and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Akebia clematifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different climates.
