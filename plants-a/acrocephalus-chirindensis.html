Acrocephalus chirindensis

Acrocephalus chirindensis, commonly known as the Chirinda reed-warbler, is a small passerine bird native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the family Acrocephalidae, which includes reed warblers and grass warblers. The species is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and grassy habitats near water. Its diet mainly consists of insects and other small invertebrates.

The bird is known for its melodic songs, which are often heard during the breeding season.
