Acrostichum squamosum var. leucolepis

Acrostichum squamosum var. leucolepis, commonly known as the silver-leafed brake fern, is a variant of the common brake fern native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray fronds, which are covered in fine, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a serrated edge and a leathery texture.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
