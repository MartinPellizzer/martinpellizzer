Abutilon pauciflorum

Abutilon pauciflorum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, featuring soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Abutilon pauciflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
