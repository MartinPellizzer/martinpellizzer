Alaternus alpinus

Alaternus alpinus, commonly known as the alpine alaternus, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found at elevations above 1,500 meters.
