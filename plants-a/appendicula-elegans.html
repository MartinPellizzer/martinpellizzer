Appendicula elegans

Appendicula elegans, commonly known as the elegant appendicula, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Appendicula elegans is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
