Amarolea americana

Amarolea americana, commonly known as the American amaro plant, is a flowering herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Amarolea americana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
