Alectryon laevis

Alectryon laevis, commonly known as the smooth alectryon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its leaves are smooth and elliptical, with a glossy surface and a slight aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
