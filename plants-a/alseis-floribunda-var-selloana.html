Alseis floribunda var. selloana

Alseis floribunda var. selloana, commonly known as the Selloan alseis, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
