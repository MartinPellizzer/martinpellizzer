Aspilia kotschyi var. kotschyi

Aspilia kotschyi var. kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi torchmallow, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It is often found growing in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Aspilia kotschyi var. kotschyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
