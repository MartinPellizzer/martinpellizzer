Aegiphila monstrosa

Aegiphila monstrosa, commonly known as the Brazilian mountain sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Aegiphila monstrosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Morphology

Aegiphila monstrosa has leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, alternate in arrangement, and typically range from 2 to 5 centimeters in length, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The stem is erect and woody, with a rough, grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming from late spring to early summer, and are primarily pollinated by insects. The fruit is a dry capsule that releases small, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant anchor itself in rocky or sandy soils.
