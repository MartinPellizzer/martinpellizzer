Aurantium × silvestre

Aurantium × silvestre, commonly known as the orange tree hybrid, is a botanical cross between Citrus aurantium and Citrus sinensis.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are typically used for their zest and juice. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
