Ardisia rosei

Ardisia rosei, commonly known as the roseleaf evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. After flowering, it produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some wildlife but toxic to humans.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
