Artemisia alba subsp. suavis

Artemisia alba subsp.

suavis, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with finely divided leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
