Aristida coerulescens

Aristida coerulescens, commonly known as bluegrass lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
