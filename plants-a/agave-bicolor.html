Agave bicolor

Agave bicolor, commonly known as the blue agave, is a species of agave native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces a tall, branched flower stalk that blooms with yellow flowers, which are followed by small, green berries. Agave bicolor is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber, while the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
