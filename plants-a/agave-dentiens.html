Agave dentiens

Agave dentiens, commonly known as the toothed agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves with serrated edges. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 1 meter in length. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the spring.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.
