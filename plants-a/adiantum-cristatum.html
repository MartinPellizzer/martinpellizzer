Adiantum cristatum

Adiantum cristatum, commonly known as the crested maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, bright green fronds with a distinctive, feathery appearance that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. The plant has a unique, upward-curving shape that sets it apart from other maidenhair ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil.

Adiantum cristatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a humid indoor setting.
