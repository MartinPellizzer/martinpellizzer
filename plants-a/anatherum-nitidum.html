Anatherum nitidum

Anatherum nitidum, commonly known as the smooth grass or velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Anatherum nitidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
