Aconitum napellus subsp. neomontanum

Aconitum napellus subsp.

neomontanum, commonly known as the Alpine Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking purple, hood-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it highly poisonous.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and protected areas.
