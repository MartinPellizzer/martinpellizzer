Astragalus schischkinii

Astragalus schischkinii, commonly known as Schischkin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has hairy stems and produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
