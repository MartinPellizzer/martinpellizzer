Agave densiflora

Agave densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with sharp, spiny margins. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, white flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave densiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Mexican crafts.
