Anthemis reflexa

Anthemis reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are often arranged in flat-topped clusters, and it has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Anthemis reflexa is typically found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
