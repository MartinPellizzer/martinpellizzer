Actinodaphne hongkongensis

Actinodaphne hongkongensis, commonly known as Hong Kong camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern China and the Hong Kong region.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark brown, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The tree is often found in moist, shaded habitats in mountainous areas.
