Alangium premnifolium

Alangium premnifolium, commonly known as the Indian alangium, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
