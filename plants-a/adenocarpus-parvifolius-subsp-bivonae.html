Adenocarpus parvifolius subsp. bivonae

Adenocarpus parvifolius subsp.

bivonae, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burrweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and distinctive burr-like fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats. Its flowers are usually small and yellow, and it produces hairy, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
