Aconitum pavlovae

Aconitum pavlovae, commonly known as Pavlov's monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive basal rosette and produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
