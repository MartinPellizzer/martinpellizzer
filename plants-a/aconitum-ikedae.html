Aconitum ikedae

Aconitum ikedae, commonly known as the Ikedaya aconite, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense racemes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets that are hairy beneath.

Aconitum ikedae contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
