Adenophora capillaris var. tenuifolia

Adenophora capillaris var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender hairroot bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland edges.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and prolonged flowering period.
