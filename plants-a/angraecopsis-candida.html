Angraecopsis candida

Angraecopsis candida, commonly known as the white star orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions.

Angraecopsis candida is a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
