Abutilon intermedium

Abutilon intermedium, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Abutilon intermedium is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
