Atriplex acutibractea subsp. acutibractea

Atriplex acutibractea subsp.

acutibractea, commonly known as the narrow-bract saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of the Chenopodiaceae family. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for revegetation in degraded landscapes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for native wildlife.
