Acaena tomentosa

Acaena tomentosa, commonly known as the tufted knapweed, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats. Its fruits are spherical, dry capsules that release seeds when mature.

Acaena tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
