Alectryon oleifolius subsp. canescens

Alectryon oleifolius subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the silver-leaved alectryon, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine pubescence on the leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
