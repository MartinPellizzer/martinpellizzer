Acrocephalus buddlejoides

Acrocephalus buddlejoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluetail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
