Amorphophallus chatty

Amorphophallus chatty, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of freshwater bodies. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its rhizome system allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

However, it can also become invasive in non-native environments if not managed properly.
