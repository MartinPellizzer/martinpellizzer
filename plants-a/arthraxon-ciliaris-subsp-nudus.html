Arthraxon ciliaris subsp. nudus

Arthraxon ciliaris subsp.

nudus, commonly known as the naked spike sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments, thriving in marshy or swampy areas. Its subspecies status reflects subtle differences in morphology compared to other varieties of Arthraxon ciliaris.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
