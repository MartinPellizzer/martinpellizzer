Avena flavescens var. variegata

Avena flavescens var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated oat, is a variant of the oat species characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage exhibits a striking mix of green and yellow hues, which is a result of genetic variation. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with white to pale blue flowers.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental grass due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
