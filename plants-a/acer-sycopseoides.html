Acer sycopseoides

Acer sycopseoides, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in the autumn. The tree produces small, red flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and urban environments.
