Astragalus warionis

Astragalus warionis, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus warionis has small, round to oval seeds that are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across dry landscapes.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
