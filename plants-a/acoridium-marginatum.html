Acoridium marginatum

Acoridium marginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in a dense spike. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, often with a waxy texture.

Acoridium marginatum is primarily cultivated for its edible tubers, which are used in traditional cuisines.
