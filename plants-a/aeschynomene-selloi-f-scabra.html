Aeschynomene selloi f. scabra

Aeschynomene selloi f. scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf aeschynomene, is a variant of the Aeschynomene genus native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and small, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its potential in forage and soil stabilization due to its hardy nature and rapid growth.
