Atractylis auranitica

Atractylis auranitica, commonly known as the golden centaury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Atractylis auranitica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rocky or dry garden environments.
