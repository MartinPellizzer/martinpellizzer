Astragalus narbonensis subsp. africanus

Astragalus narbonensis subsp.

africanus, commonly known as the African milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
