Artemisia sosnovskyi

Artemisia sosnovskyi, commonly known as the Siberian wormwood, is a hardy shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of small, aromatic leaves with a strong, bitter scent. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arctic or alpine environments. Artemisia sosnovskyi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its resilience and unique botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
