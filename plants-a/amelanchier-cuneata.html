Amelanchier cuneata

Amelanchier cuneata, commonly known as the Canada serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a deep red or purple color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Amelanchier cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
