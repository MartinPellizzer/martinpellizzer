Acanthus mollis subsp. platyphyllus

Acanthus mollis subsp.

platyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf acanthus, is a perennial evergreen plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture.

The plant produces showy, spiny bracts that enclose its small, inconspicuous flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its architectural form and striking foliage make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and horticultural collections.
