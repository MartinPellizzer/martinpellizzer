Aspalathus sulphurea

Aspalathus sulphurea, commonly known as yellow bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Aspalathus sulphurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
