Actinodaphne lambirensis

Actinodaphne lambirensis, commonly known as the Lambirensis camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when mature and turn red upon ripening. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
