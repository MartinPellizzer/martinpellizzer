Acladodea

Acladodea, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their upright growth habit and showy, velvety leaves that often have a distinctive pattern. The flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white. Acladodea species are popular houseplants due to their attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
