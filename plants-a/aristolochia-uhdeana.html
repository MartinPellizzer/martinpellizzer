Aristolochia uhdeana

Aristolochia uhdeana, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and striking, intricate flowers that resemble pipes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The species is notable for its chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
