Adiantum concinnum

Adiantum concinnum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, finely divided structure.

Adiantum concinnum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
