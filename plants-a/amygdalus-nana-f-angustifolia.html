Amygdalus nana f. angustifolia

Amygdalus nana f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dwarf almond, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart drupes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and low maintenance requirements.
