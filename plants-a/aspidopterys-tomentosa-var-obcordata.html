Aspidopterys tomentosa var. obcordata

Aspidopterys tomentosa var. obcordata, commonly known as the obcordate aspidopterys, is a variant of the aspidopterys genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and compact growth habit. The leaves are typically elliptical to ovate in shape, with a distinctive waxy coating that gives them a velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by small, red berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
