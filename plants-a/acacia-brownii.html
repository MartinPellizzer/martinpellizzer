Acacia brownii

Acacia brownii, commonly known as the brown acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Acacia brownii is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its environment.
