Anthericum platyphyllum var. longiusantheratum

Anthericum platyphyllum var. longiusantheratum, commonly known as the long-stalked broadleaf arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, arrow-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The varietal name 'longiusantheratum' refers to its elongated, slender floral stalks.
