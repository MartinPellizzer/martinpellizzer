Amoria vesiculosa

Amoria vesiculosa, commonly known as the bladderleaf or bladderworts, is a genus of aquatic and semi-aquatic plants found in various freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like leaves that are adapted for trapping small aquatic organisms. These plants are often found in shallow water, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The genus includes several species, many of which are used in phytoremediation due to their ability to absorb pollutants.

Amoria vesiculosa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for microorganisms and invertebrates.
