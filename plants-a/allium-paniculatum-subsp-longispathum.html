Allium paniculatum subsp. longispathum

Allium paniculatum subsp.

longispathum, commonly known as the long-stalked onion, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
