Aporum confundens

Aporum confundens, commonly known as the false arum, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a striking inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the arum family. Its leaves are large, arrow-shaped, and often have a glossy surface.

While it is not widely cultivated for ornamental purposes, it plays an important role in its native ecosystems.
