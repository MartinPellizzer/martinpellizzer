Acacia trinervata var. angustifolia

Acacia trinervata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular phyllodes that are typically green with a slight blue or grey tinge. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
