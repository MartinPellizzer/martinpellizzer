Arcytophyllum venezuelanum

Arcytophyllum venezuelanum, commonly known as the Venezuelan sagebrush, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Venezuela.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It has a compact growth form with dense, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss.

Arcytophyllum venezuelanum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
