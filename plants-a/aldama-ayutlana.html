Aldama ayutlana

Aldama ayutlana, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Aldama ayutlana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
