Althaea officinalis subsp. taurinensis

Althaea officinalis subsp.

taurinensis, commonly known as the Taurine marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wet meadows.

The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces pink or purple flowers in late summer. Its most notable characteristic is the mucilaginous, cotton-like substance found in its roots and stems, which has been used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is valued for its soothing properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
