Acer negundo var. orizabense

Acer negundo var. orizabense, commonly known as the Mexican box elder, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmate leaves that typically have five to seven lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by samaras with feathery wings. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian areas and open woodlands.

This variety is closely related to the common box elder but exhibits distinct morphological features.
