Albuca bifoliata

Albuca bifoliata, commonly known as the two-leaf yam plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, grass-like foliage. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
