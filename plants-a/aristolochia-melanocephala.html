Aristolochia melanocephala

Aristolochia melanocephala, commonly known as the black-headed Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe, with a distinctive dark purple to black coloration at the base. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex inflorescence structure. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are woody and climbing.

This species is also notable for containing aristolochic acids, which have been studied for their medicinal and toxic properties.
