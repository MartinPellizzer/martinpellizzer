Angelica alpina

Angelica alpina, commonly known as the alpine angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in an umbel, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Angelica alpina is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
