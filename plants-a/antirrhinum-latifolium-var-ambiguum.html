Antirrhinum latifolium var. ambiguum

Antirrhinum latifolium var. ambiguum, commonly known as the broadleaf snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
