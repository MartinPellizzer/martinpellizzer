Argyroderma theartii

Argyroderma theartii, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
