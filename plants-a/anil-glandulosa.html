Anil glandulosa

Anil glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular anil, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically hairy and have glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Anil glandulosa is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
