Acoridium turpe

Acoridium turpe, commonly known as the swamp lily, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine. Acoridium turpe has a short flowering period, usually during the rainy season, and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
