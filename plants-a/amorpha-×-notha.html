Amorpha × notha

Amorpha × notha, commonly known as yellow false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Amorpha torulosa and Amorpha humistrata, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged with oval leaflets, and it produces long, cylindrical seed pods.

Amorpha × notha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
