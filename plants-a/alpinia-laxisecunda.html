Alpinia laxisecunda

Alpinia laxisecunda, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or Chinese ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often clustered together. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other ginger species.

Alpinia laxisecunda thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
