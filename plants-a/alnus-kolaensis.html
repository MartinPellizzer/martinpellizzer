Alnus kolaensis

Alnus kolaensis, commonly known as the West African alder, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of West Africa.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family, which includes birches, hazels, and alders. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It produces small, winged fruits called samaras, which aid in seed dispersal.

Alnus kolaensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
