Allium koyuncui

Allium koyuncui, commonly known as the Turkish leek or mountain garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including Turkey and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible bulbs that are often used in local cuisines for their mild, garlic-like flavor.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
