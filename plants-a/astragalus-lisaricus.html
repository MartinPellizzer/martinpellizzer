Astragalus lisaricus

Astragalus lisaricus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed or barren landscapes.

Astragalus lisaricus is an important component of desert ecosystems, providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil health.
