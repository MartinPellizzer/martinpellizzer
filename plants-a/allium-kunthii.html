Allium kunthii

Allium kunthii, commonly known as the Colorado garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Allium kunthii is valued for its aromatic properties and ecological importance in its native habitat.
