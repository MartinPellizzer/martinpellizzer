Andropogon ferrugineus

Andropogon ferrugineus, commonly known as the crimson lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, producing fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Andropogon ferrugineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in native plant gardens.
