Arnoglossum gramineum

Arnoglossum gramineum, commonly known as the grassleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, clustered in a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Arnoglossum gramineum is valued for its ornamental foliage and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
