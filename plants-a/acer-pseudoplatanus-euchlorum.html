Acer pseudoplatanus euchlorum

Acer pseudoplatanus euchlorum, commonly known as the white-veined maple, is a rare and striking variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive white veins on pale green leaves, which give it a unique appearance. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central Europe and thrives in temperate climates. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and arboreta.
