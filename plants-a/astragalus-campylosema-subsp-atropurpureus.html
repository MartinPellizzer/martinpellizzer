Astragalus campylosema subsp. atropurpureus

Astragalus campylosema subsp.

atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple-flowered milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and grazing animals.
