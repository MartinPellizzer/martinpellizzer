Artemisia kennedyi

Artemisia kennedyi, commonly known as Kennedy's sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, distinctive scent.

Artemisia kennedyi plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
