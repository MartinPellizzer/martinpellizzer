Acrostichum rampans

Acrostichum rampans, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, often forming dense stands in wetland habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an effective colonizer of disturbed soils.

Despite its invasive potential in some areas, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
