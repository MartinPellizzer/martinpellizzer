Atropis borreri var. pseudoprocumbens

Atropis borreri var. pseudoprocumbens, commonly known as the creeping atropis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

This variety is noted for its low-growing habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
