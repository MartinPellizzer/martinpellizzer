Aconitum nikaii f. pilicarpum

Aconitum nikaii f. pilicarpum, commonly known as the Nikaii Himalayanaconite, is a variant of the Aconitum genus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a finely toothed margin, and the plant has a robust root system.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a hazardous plant and requires careful handling.
