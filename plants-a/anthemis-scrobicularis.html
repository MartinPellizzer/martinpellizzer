Anthemis scrobicularis

Anthemis scrobicularis, commonly known as the scrobiculated everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant is known for its aromatic, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy and slightly woody.

Anthemis scrobicularis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
