Anthurium totontepecense

Anthurium totontepecense, commonly known as the red anthurium, is a tropical plant native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces vibrant red spathes that enclose its spadix, which is covered in tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

The plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
