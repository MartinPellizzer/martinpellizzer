Acer velutinum var. glabrescens

Acer velutinum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvet maple, is a variant of the velvet maple tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light green bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is a deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
