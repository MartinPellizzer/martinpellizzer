Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. boissieri

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Balkan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and scrublands.

Its subspecies status reflects its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other forms of Anthyllis vulneraria.
