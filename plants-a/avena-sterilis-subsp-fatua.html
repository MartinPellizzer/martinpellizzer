Avena sterilis subsp. fatua

Avena sterilis subsp.

fatua, commonly known as wild oat, is a grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, awned spikelets that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and agricultural areas, and is often considered a weed. Its rapid growth and ability to produce a large number of seeds contribute to its invasive potential.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its hollow stems, leathery leaves, and the presence of a distinct lemma and palea in its florets.
