Amaryllis radiata

Amaryllis radiata, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a contrasting central stripe. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of fleshy, white bulbs. It blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The flowers emit a sweet, spicy fragrance, adding both visual and olfactory appeal to its presence.
