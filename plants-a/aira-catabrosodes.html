Aira catabrosodes

Aira catabrosodes, commonly known as the purple airplant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted, grass-like foliage and striking violet-colored inflorescences. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, low-maintenance plants.
