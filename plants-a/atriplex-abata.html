Atriplex abata

Atriplex abata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are adapted to retain water in dry environments. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in saline soils. Its flowers are typically small and greenish, arranged in clusters.

Atriplex abata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for wildlife.
