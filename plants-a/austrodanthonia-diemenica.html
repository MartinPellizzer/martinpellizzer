Austrodanthonia diemenica

Austrodanthonia diemenica, commonly known as the Tasmanian wallaby grass, is a perennial grass native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and grassy woodlands.

Austrodanthonia diemenica plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability.
