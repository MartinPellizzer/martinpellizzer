Andropogon halepensis subsp. siamensis

Andropogon halepensis subsp.

siamensis, commonly known as Siam grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, acidic soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
