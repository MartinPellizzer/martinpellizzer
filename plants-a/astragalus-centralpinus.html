Astragalus centralpinus

Astragalus centralpinus, commonly known as the prairie milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

Astragalus centralpinus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
