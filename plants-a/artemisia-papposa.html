Artemisia papposa

Artemisia papposa, commonly known as white gooseweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small, feathery flowers. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Artemisia papposa is often used in traditional medicine and is also a valuable plant for ecological restoration.
