Acanthus doloariu

Acanthus doloariu, commonly known as the Madagascar acanthus, is a striking plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its large, velvety leaves with intricate, wavy margins. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it requires careful cultivation to thrive outside its native habitat.
