Agave quiotifera

Agave quiotifera, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, blue-gray leaves that are often lined with sharp spines. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which are typically pollinated by bats and insects. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Agave quiotifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its resilience in harsh climates.
