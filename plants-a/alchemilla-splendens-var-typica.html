Alchemilla splendens var. typica

Alchemilla splendens var. typica, commonly known as the shining lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-green foliage and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
