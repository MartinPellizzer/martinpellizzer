Arthrocnemum indicum

Arthrocnemum indicum, commonly known as the Indian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in desert or coastal areas.

Its unique adaptations make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
