Achyranthes triandra

Achyranthes triandra, commonly known as red root, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branching stems. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base, and it produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine, valued for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Achyranthes triandra is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in various environments.
