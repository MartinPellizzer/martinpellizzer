Anthurium chuchubiense

Anthurium chuchubiense, commonly known as the Chuchubi anthurium, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces vibrant red spadices, which are the spiky, club-shaped structures that house the tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable ornamental plant in botanical collections.
