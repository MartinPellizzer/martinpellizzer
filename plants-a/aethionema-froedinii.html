Aethionema froedinii

Aethionema froedinii, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its compact habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, colorful blossoms in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are densely packed along the stems.

Aethionema froedinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
