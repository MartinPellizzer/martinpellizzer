Amomum laxesquamosum

Amomum laxesquamosum, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are borne in inflorescences at the top of the plant.

The rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
