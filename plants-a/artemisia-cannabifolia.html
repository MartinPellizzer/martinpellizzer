Artemisia cannabifolia

Artemisia cannabifolia, commonly known as western goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, which are typical of the genus Artemisia. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Artemisia cannabifolia is used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
