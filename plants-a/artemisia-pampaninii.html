Artemisia pampaninii

Artemisia pampaninii, commonly known as the Pampas wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, pungent aroma.

Artemisia pampaninii is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
