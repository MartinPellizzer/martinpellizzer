Amygdalus pedunculata var. polygyna

Amygdalus pedunculata var. polygyna, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits called drupes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
