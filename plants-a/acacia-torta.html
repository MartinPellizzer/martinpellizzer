Acacia torta

Acacia torta, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and often has a silvery sheen, contributing to its common name.

Acacia torta is adapted to rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
