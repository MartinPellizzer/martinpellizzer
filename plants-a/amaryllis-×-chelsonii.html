Amaryllis × chelsonii

Amaryllis × chelsonii, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a cultivated species resulting from crosses between various amaryllis species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and orange. The plant typically produces multiple flower stems with several blooms per stem, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture.
