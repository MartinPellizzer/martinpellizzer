Androsace obtusifolia subsp. hedreantha

Androsace obtusifolia subsp.

hedreantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved androsace, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features rosettes of broad, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white to greyish hairs.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
