Astragalus zebedaniensis

Astragalus zebedaniensis, commonly known as the Zebedian milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems, though it is relatively rare and not widely studied.
