Aegiphila acutangula

Aegiphila acutangula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Aegiphila acutangula is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.
