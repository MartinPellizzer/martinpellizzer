Amauropelta mombachensis

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds, which are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant is native to the humid tropical forests of South America, particularly in Brazil. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
