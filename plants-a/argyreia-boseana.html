Argyreia boseana

Argyreia boseana, commonly known as the red devil flower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like birds and bats. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Argyreia boseana is also valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens and as a decorative plant.
