Andira fraxinifolia var. lanceata

Andira fraxinifolia var. lanceata, commonly known as the lance-leaf andira, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It features compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by winged fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens in warm climates.
