Alternanthera pennelliana

Alternanthera pennelliana, commonly known as the red-leaf water plantain, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink foliage, which becomes more intense under strong light conditions. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, delicate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is popular in freshwater aquariums for its aesthetic appeal.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an effective choice for pond and aquarium landscaping.
