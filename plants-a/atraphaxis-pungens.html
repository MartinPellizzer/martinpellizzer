Atraphaxis pungens

Atraphaxis pungens, commonly known as the spiny glassworts, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapting well to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are fleshy and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in saline conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
