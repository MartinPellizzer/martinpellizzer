Achyranthes conferta

Achyranthes conferta, commonly known as red sanders, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine.

Achyranthes conferta is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
