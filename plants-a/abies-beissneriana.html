Abies beissneriana

Abies beissneriana, commonly known as the Beissner's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds enclosed in winged arils. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
