Allantodia cordifolia

Allantodia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf allantodia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
