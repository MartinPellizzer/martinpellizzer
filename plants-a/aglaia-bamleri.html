Aglaia bamleri

Aglaia bamleri, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dark, heavy timber that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves, while its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in inflorescences. Aglaia bamleri produces small, round, yellowish fruits that contain a single seed.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
