Astragalus parvifolius

Astragalus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus parvifolius is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.
