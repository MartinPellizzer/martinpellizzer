Alchemilla circularis

Alchemilla circularis, commonly known as the round-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla circularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
