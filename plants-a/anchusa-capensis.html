Anchusa capensis

Anchusa capensis, commonly known as the bluebells or Cape anchusa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats. The plant produces clusters of striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in spring and early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it is known for its hardy nature.

Anchusa capensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and resilience.
