Andropogon fulvispica

Andropogon fulvispica, commonly known as the golden bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and woodland habitats. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, pale green to golden flowers.

Andropogon fulvispica plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
