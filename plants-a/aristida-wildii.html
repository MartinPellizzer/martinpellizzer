Aristida wildii

Aristida wildii, commonly known as the wild rye grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical seed heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Aristida wildii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
