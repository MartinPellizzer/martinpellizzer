Argyrolobium crassifolium

Argyrolobium crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved silver globular.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and globular flower heads. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
