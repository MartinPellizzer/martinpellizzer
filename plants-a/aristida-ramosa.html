Aristida ramosa

Aristida ramosa, commonly known as slender threeawn, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces pale to brownish flowers in late summer.

Aristida ramosa is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
