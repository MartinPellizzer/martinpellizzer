Astragalus schizotropis

Astragalus schizotropis, commonly known as the split-fruit milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant produces distinctive, inflated pods that split open to release its seeds. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in steppe and desert environments.
