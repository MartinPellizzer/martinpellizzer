Austromyrtus conspicua

Austromyrtus conspicua, commonly known as the Coastal Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous small, brown seeds. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, often in areas exposed to salt spray.

Austromyrtus conspicua is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
