Aster auritus

Aster auritus, commonly known as the hoary aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and showy purple to blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Aster auritus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
