Aegilops calida

Aegilops calida, commonly known as the warm grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale to brownish flowers. Aegilops calida is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often found in coastal areas and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.

Morphology

Aegilops calida has narrow, linear leaves that are grass-like in appearance, arranged in a basal rosette, with a bluish-green color and a smooth texture.

The stem is erect, slender, and slightly hairy, with a light green color and a growth pattern that is mostly upright. The flowers are small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to white florets, blooming in early summer, and are pollinated by wind. The fruit is a small, three-angled achene, and the seeds are dispersed by wind due to their lightweight structure.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to arid conditions by efficiently absorbing moisture from the soil.
