Azalea × rustica

Azalea × rustica, commonly known as the Southern Indica azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Azalea × rustica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
