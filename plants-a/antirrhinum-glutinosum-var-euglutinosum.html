Antirrhinum glutinosum var. euglutinosum

Antirrhinum glutinosum var. euglutinosum, commonly known as the sticky snapdragon, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky secretion, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique floral structure and adaptive traits make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
