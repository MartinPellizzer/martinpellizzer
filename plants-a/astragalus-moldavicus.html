Astragalus moldavicus

Astragalus moldavicus, commonly known as the Moldavian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus moldavicus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
