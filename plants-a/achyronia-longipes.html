Achyronia longipes

Achyronia longipes, commonly known as the long-footed orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring long, slender roots and intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Achyronia longipes is considered a conservation concern.
