Arctostaphylos nortensis

Arctostaphylos nortensis, commonly known as the northern manzanita, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, showy flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments.

Arctostaphylos nortensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
