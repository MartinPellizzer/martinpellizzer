Amaryllis berteroana

Amaryllis berteroana, commonly known as the belladonna lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears clusters of showy blooms. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often emerging from a bulbous underground stem.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
