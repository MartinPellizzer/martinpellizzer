Adiantum aleuticum var. subpumilum

Adiantum aleuticum var. subpumilum, commonly known as the Aleut maidenhair fern, is a small, delicate fern native to the northwestern United States and Alaska.

It features finely divided, silky-textured fronds that are typically less than 10 inches in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing along streams or in rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, arching shape, with a pale green to bluish-green coloration.

It is characterized by its finely veined, pinnate leaves and is well adapted to cool, humid climates.
