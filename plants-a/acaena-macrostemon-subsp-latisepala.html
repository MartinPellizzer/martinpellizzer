Acaena macrostemon subsp. latisepala

Acaena macrostemon subsp.

latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled burrplant, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, woody husk.

The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in providing food for native birds and insects.
