Annona coriacea var. pygmaea

Annona coriacea var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf soursop, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, round, green fruits with a rough, bark-like rind. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are yellowish-green with a strong, unpleasant odor, typical of the Annonaceae family.

The fruit is edible, though its flavor is often described as bland or slightly sour.
