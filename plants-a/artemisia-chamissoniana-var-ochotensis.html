Artemisia chamissoniana var. ochotensis

Artemisia chamissoniana var. ochotensis, commonly known as the Siberian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and tundra environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in colder climates.
