Aspidium menyanthidis

Aspidium menyanthidis, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its large, lanceolate, and often deeply lobed fronds that are typically borne on long, creeping rhizomes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and wetlands, where it can absorb sufficient moisture from the environment. Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of the fronds, and the plant reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Aspidium menyanthidis is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in maintaining soil stability.
