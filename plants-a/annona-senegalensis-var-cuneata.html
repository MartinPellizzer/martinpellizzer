Annona senegalensis var. cuneata

Annona senegalensis var. cuneata, commonly known as the African custard apple, is a tropical shrub native to West Africa.

It produces edible, yellowish-green fruits with a creamy, custard-like pulp that is often consumed fresh or used in cooking. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly pubescent surface. It flowers with small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit develops from the base of the flower.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content and has traditional medicinal uses in various African communities.
