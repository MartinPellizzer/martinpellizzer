Achyropsis oxyuris

Achyropsis oxyuris, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in harsh desert environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture deep in the soil.

Achyropsis oxyuris plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
