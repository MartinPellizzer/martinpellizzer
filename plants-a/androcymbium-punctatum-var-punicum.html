Androcymbium punctatum var. punicum

Androcymbium punctatum var. punicum, commonly known as the scarlet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that typically display shades of red and orange, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal climates. Its leaves are usually strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers bloom in summer, adding a vibrant display to its natural habitat.
