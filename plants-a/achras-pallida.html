Achras pallida

Achras pallida, commonly known as the white aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Achras pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
