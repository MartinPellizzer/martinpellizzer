Alsodeia perrinii

Alsodeia perrinii, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
