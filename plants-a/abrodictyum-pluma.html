Abrodictyum pluma

Abrodictyum pluma, commonly known as the feather-tongue orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its long, slender leaves and striking, feather-like floral structures. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
