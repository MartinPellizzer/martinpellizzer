Amellus umbellatus

Amellus umbellatus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Amellus umbellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
