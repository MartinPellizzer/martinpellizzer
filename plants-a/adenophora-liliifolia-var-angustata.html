Adenophora liliifolia var. angustata

Adenophora liliifolia var. angustata, commonly known as narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.
