Adiantopsis spectabilis

Adiantopsis spectabilis, commonly known as the showy maidenhair fern, is a striking member of the Adiantaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with a silvery sheen. Native to the eastern United States, this fern thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with each leaflet bearing a unique, finger-like shape.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
