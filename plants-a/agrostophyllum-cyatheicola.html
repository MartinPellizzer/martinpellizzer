Agrostophyllum cyatheicola

Agrostophyllum cyatheicola, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and produces new fronds, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Agrostophyllum cyatheicola plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
