Acacia fitzgeraldii var. brevior

Acacia fitzgeraldii var. brevior, commonly known as the Fitzgerald's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
