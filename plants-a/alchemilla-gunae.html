Alchemilla gunae

Alchemilla gunae, commonly known as the mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy leaves that often have a silver or grayish hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in a rounded inflorescence. Alchemilla gunae thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and horticulture for its ornamental value.
