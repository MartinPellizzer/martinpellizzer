Ascogrammitis pichinchae

Ascogrammitis pichinchae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as streams and ponds, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Ascogrammitis pichinchae plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to water filtration.
