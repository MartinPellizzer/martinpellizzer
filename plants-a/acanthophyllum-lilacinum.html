Acanthophyllum lilacinum

Acanthophyllum lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac spurgebrush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its gray-green foliage and spiny, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in harsh desert conditions.

Acanthophyllum lilacinum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
