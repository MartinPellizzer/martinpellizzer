Arundinaria lubrica

Arundinaria lubrica, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It features tall, slender stems that are covered in a smooth, waxy sheath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by clustered, dark blue berries. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

Arundinaria lubrica is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
