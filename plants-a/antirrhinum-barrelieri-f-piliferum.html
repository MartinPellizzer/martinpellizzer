Antirrhinum barrelieri f. piliferum

Antirrhinum barrelieri f. piliferum, commonly known as the hairy antirrhinum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. It thrives in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a notable botanical feature, giving it its common name.
