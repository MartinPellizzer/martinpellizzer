Adiantum poiretii var. poiretii

Adiantum poiretii var. poiretii, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate, evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, lace-like fronds that are typically silver-white in color, giving it a striking, ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in shady, humid environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its fronds are biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year of growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
