Aerva wightii

Aerva wightii, commonly known as Indian mullein, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, soft, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Aerva wightii is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
