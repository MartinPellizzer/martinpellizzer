Astragalus vesicarius var. angelicae

Astragalus vesicarius var. angelicae, commonly known as angel's trumpet.

This variant of the Astragalus genus is characterized by its distinctive, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
