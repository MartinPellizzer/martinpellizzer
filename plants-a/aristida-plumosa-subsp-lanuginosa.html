Aristida plumosa subsp. lanuginosa

Aristida plumosa subsp.

lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a fluffy appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine, silky hairs, which contribute to its common name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
