Anthericum pterocaulon

Anthericum pterocaulon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved adder's-tongue, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, sword-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
