Anthemis cotula

Anthemis cotula, commonly known as yellow weed or St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces tiny, capsule-like fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
