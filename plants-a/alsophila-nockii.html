Alsophila nockii

Alsophila nockii, commonly known as the Nock's tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall stem and provides structural stability.

Alsophila nockii plays a role in forest ecosystems by offering habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
