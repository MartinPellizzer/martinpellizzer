Aconitum chenianum

Aconitum chenianum, commonly known as Chinese aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces palmately divided leaves and has a long flowering period, typically from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
