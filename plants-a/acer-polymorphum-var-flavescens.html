Acer polymorphum var. flavescens

Acer polymorphum var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow foliage, which turns vibrant shades of gold in the fall. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
