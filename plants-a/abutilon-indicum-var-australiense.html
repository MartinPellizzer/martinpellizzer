Abutilon indicum var. australiense

Abutilon indicum var. australiense, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its hairy stems and leaves are adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
