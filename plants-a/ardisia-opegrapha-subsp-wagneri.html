Ardisia opegrapha subsp. wagneri

Ardisia opegrapha subsp.

wagneri, commonly known as the Wagner's beautyberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by clusters of dark purple berries.

The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are a food source for various birds and small mammals.
