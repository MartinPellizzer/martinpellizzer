Aconitum pilopetalum

Aconitum pilopetalum, commonly known as the wooly petal aconite, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves covered in a dense layer of silky, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single cluster at the top of the stem, and the plant is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.

Aconitum pilopetalum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
