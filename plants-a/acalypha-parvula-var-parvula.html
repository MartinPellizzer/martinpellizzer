Acalypha parvula var. parvula

Acalypha parvula var. parvula, commonly known as the smallleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest to its foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with finely serrated edges and a glossy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
