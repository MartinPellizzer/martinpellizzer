Aloiampelos decumbens

Aloiampelos decumbens, commonly known as the creeping aloes, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
