Aegopodium carum

Aegopodium carum, commonly known as bishop's weed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a bushy growth habit with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic. Its roots and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it useful in traditional medicine.
