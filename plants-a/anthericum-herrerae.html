Anthericum herrerae

Anthericum herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinct veining pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system that aids in its survival in rocky or arid environments.

Anthericum herrerae is often found in moist, shaded areas within cloud forests.
