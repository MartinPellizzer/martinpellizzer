Ageratina etlensis

Ageratina etlensis, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Ageratina etlensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
