Andropogon perfossus

Andropogon perfossus, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Andropogon perfossus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
