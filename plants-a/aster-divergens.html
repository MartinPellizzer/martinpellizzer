Aster divergens

Aster divergens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Aster divergens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
