Aster macdougalii

Aster macdougalii, commonly known as the Macdonald's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
