Asperula tinctoria var. pyrenaica

Asperula tinctoria var. pyrenaica, commonly known as Pyrenean bluebells, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
