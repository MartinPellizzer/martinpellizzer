Alectra angustifolia

Alectra angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sandmat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Alectra angustifolia is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in native plant landscaping.
