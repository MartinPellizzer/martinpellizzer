Allium rubellum subsp. syntamanthum

Allium rubellum subsp.

syntamanthum, commonly known as the red onion, is a bulbous plant native to central and southern Europe. It features a basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are typically red or purple in color.

The plant produces a tall, slender stem topped with a spherical cluster of small, white to pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and widely used in culinary applications.

The species is closely related to other members of the Allium genus, including garlic and leeks.
