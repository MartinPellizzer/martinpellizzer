Ascocentrum aurantiacum

Ascocentrum aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange-tinged arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often display a vibrant orange or reddish hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
