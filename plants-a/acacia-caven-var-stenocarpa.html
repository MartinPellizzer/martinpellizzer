Acacia caven var. stenocarpa

Acacia caven var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in summer, followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
