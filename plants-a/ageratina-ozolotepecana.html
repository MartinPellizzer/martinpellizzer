Ageratina ozolotepecana

Ageratina ozolotepecana, commonly known as the Oztolotepec aster, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its foliage is often heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces small, white to pale blue flowers.

Ageratina ozolotepecana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
