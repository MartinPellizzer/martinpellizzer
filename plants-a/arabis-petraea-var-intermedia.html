Arabis petraea var. intermedia

Arabis petraea var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rockcress, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are basal, lobed, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The flowers are bright yellow, with four petals, and appear in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
