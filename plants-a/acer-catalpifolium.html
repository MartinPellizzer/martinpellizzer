Acer catalpifolium

Acer catalpifolium, commonly known as the Catalpa-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the catalpa tree, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which appear before the leaves in early spring. Its fruits are samaras, which are winged seeds that aid in wind dispersal.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
