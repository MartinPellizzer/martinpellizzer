Acer maximowiczii var. minor

Acer maximowiczii var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive trifoliate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, red samaras that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic value.
