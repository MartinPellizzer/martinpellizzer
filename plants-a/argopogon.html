Argopogon

Argopogon, commonly known as the sea hake, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Plantaginaceae.

These plants are typically found in coastal regions and are adapted to saline or brackish environments. They are characterized by their delicate, often hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. Argopogon species are usually herbaceous and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils near the shore.

Their ecological importance lies in their role as pioneer species in coastal ecosystems.
