Amelanchier alnifolia var. compacta

Amelanchier alnifolia var. compacta, commonly known as the Saskatoon serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer, typically turning a bright red or yellow color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and appear in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
