Alona filifolia

Alona filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf watermeadow plant, is a small aquatic plant found in shallow, freshwater habitats.

It has delicate, thread-like leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Alona filifolia is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

It plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates.
