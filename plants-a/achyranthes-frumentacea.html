Achyranthes frumentacea

Achyranthes frumentacea, commonly known as the Indian spinach or Chinese rhubarb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Achyranthes frumentacea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
