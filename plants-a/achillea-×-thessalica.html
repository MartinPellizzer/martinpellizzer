Achillea × thessalica

Achillea × thessalica, commonly known as the Greek yarrow, is a hybrid perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pink or red. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are aromatic, finely divided, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Achillea × thessalica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
