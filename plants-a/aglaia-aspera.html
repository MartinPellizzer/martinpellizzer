Aglaia aspera

Aglaia aspera, commonly known as the rough aglaia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Magnoliaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain small, oily seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Aglaia aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
