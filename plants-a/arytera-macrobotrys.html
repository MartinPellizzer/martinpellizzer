Arytera macrobotrys

Arytera macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers, which are typically white or pink, bloom at night and emit a strong, sweet scent to attract pollinators like beetles.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role in aquatic ecosystems and its ornamental value in water gardens.
