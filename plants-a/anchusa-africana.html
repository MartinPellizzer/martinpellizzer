Anchusa africana

Anchusa africana, commonly known as the African blueweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a robust stem with blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. Anchusa africana is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
