Anacyclus × valentinus var. purpurascens

Anacyclus × valentinus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, globe-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in native plant gardens.
