Acaena sanguisorbae var. suprasericascens

Acaena sanguisorbae var. suprasericascens, commonly known as the silky sandspurry, is a variant of the sandspurry species native to parts of New Zealand.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or subalpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
