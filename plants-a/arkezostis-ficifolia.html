Arkezostis ficifolia

Arkezostis ficifolia, commonly known as the fig tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique syconium fruits, which are enclosed within a fleshy structure. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It plays a significant role in both ecological systems and human agriculture, providing food for various animals and humans.

The fig tree is also known for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with specific fungi, enhancing its nutrient uptake.
