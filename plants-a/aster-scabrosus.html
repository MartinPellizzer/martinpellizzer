Aster scabrosus

Aster scabrosus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, purple to pinkish-blue flower heads in the fall, making it a notable wildflower. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Aster scabrosus is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.
