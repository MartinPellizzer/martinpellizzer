Argyrolobium zanonii subsp. grandiflorum

Argyrolobium zanonii subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered argyrolobium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities.

The plant features slender, branching stems and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive butterfly-shaped structure. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
