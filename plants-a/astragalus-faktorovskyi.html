Astragalus faktorovskyi

Astragalus faktorovskyi, commonly known as the Faktorovsky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the diverse flora of its native habitat.

Its scientific study contributes to the understanding of plant biodiversity in harsh ecosystems.
