Anemone caerulea var. griffithii

Anemone caerulea var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's blue anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, with a rosette growth habit. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
