Anthyllis cytisoides subsp. terniflora

Anthyllis cytisoides subsp.

terniflora, commonly known as the three-flowered broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils.

The plant produces yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinators.
