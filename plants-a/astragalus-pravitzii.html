Astragalus pravitzii

Astragalus pravitzii, commonly known as Pravitz's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus pravitzii is a perennial species, often found in sagebrush steppe and other xeric landscapes.
