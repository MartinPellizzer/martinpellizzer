Acacia neurophylla

Acacia neurophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and phyllodes that are narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia neurophylla is also valued for its hardy nature and is often used in landscaping and for soil stabilization.
