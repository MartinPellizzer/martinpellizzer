Alyxia celastrinea

Alyxia celastrinea, commonly known as the yellow-flowered alyxia, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
