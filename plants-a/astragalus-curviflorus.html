Astragalus curviflorus

Astragalus curviflorus, commonly known as the curlflower milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus curviflorus is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub ecosystems.
