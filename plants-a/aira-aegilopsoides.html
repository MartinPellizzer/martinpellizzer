Aira aegilopsoides

Aira aegilopsoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf aira, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
