Aleuritia chungensis

Aleuritia chungensis, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Aleuritia chungensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
