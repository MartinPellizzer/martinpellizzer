Albuca dalyae

Albuca dalyae, commonly known as the Natal grassblades, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Albuca dalyae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
