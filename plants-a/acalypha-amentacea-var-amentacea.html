Acalypha amentacea var. amentacea

Acalypha amentacea var. amentacea, commonly known as the silky bush or silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, drooping clusters called catkins. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
