Allium lacunosum var. davisiae

Allium lacunosum var. davisiae, commonly known as Davis' garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is prized for both its ornamental value and edible qualities.
