Aira caryophyllea var. divaricata

Aira caryophyllea var. divaricata, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and delicate, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.

The variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps over time.
