Arum italicum subsp. albispathum

Arum italicum subsp.

albispathum, commonly known as the white-veined arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Europe. It features large, arrow-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins against a dark green background.

The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a pale, spathaceous bract, giving it its unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological studies.
