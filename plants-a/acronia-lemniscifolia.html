Acronia lemniscifolia

Acronia lemniscifolia, commonly known as the twin-leaf acronia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are divided into two lobes resembling a figure-eight shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded or partially sunny environments.

Acronia lemniscifolia is often found in moist, woodland soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
