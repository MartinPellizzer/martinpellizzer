Acacia potosina

Acacia potosina, commonly known as the Potosi acacia, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Acacia potosina is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
