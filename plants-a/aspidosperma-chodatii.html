Aspidosperma chodatii

Aspidosperma chodatii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerocarpus chodatii*, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

Aspidosperma chodatii also has traditional medicinal uses in some South American cultures.
