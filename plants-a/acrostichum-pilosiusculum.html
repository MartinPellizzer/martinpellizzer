Acrostichum pilosiusculum

Acrostichum pilosiusculum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a member of the fern family Polypodiaceae.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy fronds. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are responsible for spore dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
