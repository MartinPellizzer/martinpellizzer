Arum polyphyllum

Arum polyphyllum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces a spadix, a fleshy spike of tiny flowers, surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
