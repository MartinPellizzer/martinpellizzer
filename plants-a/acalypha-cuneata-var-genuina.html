Acalypha cuneata var. genuina

Acalypha cuneata var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a species native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize areas make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
