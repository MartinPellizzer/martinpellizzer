Aloe cooperi subsp. pulchra

Aloe cooperi subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with orange to yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
