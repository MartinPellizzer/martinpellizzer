Amorphophallus mysorensis

Amorphophallus mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, leafy stems and striking, spatheous flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root that is used as a starchy food source in some regions. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
