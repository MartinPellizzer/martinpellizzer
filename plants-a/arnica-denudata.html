Arnica denudata

Arnica denudata, commonly known as the naked arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its flowers are tubular and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, while its leaves are often covered in fine hairs.

Arnica denudata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
