Adenophora pereskiifolia f. petrophila

Adenophora pereskiifolia f. petrophila, commonly known as the rock adenophora, is a unique variant of the adenophora species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy foliage, which helps it thrive in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is native to specific regions in China, where it grows in limestone soils and mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky terrains.
