Aegopogon laguroides

Aegopogon laguroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goatsbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a fibrous root system. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in panicle-like inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, often with a rough texture and a distinctive basal rosette.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Morphology

Aegopogon laguroides has leaves that are lanceolate, arranged alternately, and typically measure between 5 to 15 cm in length, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The stem is herbaceous, cylindrical, and slightly hairy, growing upright with a branching pattern. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming from late spring to early summer, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds by wind, with each seed having a small appendage for attachment.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, aiding in nutrient absorption from the soil.
