Acacia pulchella var. fagonioides

Acacia pulchella var. fagonioides, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, golden-yellow flowers that appear in cylindrical spikes. The leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into two sets of small leaflets, giving the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
