Aster diplostephioides subsp. falconeri

Aster diplostephioides subsp.

falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri's aster, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it produces small, bracts that resemble leaves.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
