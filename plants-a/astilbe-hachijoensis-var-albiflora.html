Astilbe hachijoensis var. albiflora

Astilbe hachijoensis var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Hachijo astilbe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features delicate, plume-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of white to pale pink, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and subtle floral display.
