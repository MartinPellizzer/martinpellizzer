Atragene speciosa subsp. saxatilis

Atragene speciosa subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the rocky atragene, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and mountainous regions. It typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a delicate pinkish-white color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
