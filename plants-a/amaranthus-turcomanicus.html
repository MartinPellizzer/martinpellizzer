Amaranthus turcomanicus

Amaranthus turcomanicus, commonly known as the Central Asian amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish flowers that are followed by spiky, triangular seed capsules. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Amaranthus turcomanicus is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
