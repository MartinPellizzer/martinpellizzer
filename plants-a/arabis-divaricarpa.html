Arabis divaricarpa

Arabis divaricarpa, commonly known as the spreading rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often at high altitudes. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
