Arabis alpina subsp. flavescens

Arabis alpina subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow rockcress, is a flowering plant species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with basal leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in open, sunny habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
