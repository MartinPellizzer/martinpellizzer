Aporosa parvula

Aporosa parvula, commonly known as the small-leaf aporosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, ovate leaves with smooth margins and a dense, compact growth habit. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, appearing in early spring.

Aporosa parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
