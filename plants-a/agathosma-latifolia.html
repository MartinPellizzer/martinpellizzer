Agathosma latifolia

Agathosma latifolia, commonly known as Cape Geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and aromatic, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
