Amomum chryseum

Amomum chryseum, commonly known as the golden cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and erect, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by dark brown, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Amomum chryseum thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
