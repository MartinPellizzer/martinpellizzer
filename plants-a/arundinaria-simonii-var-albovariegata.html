Arundinaria simonii var. albovariegata

Arundinaria simonii var. albovariegata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a decorative bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking white and green variegated foliage, which adds visual interest to gardens. This variety grows as a clump-forming plant, reaching heights of up to 6 feet, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide privacy and its resilience in different climates.
