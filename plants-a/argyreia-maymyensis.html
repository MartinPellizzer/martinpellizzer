Argyreia maymyensis

Argyreia maymyensis, commonly known as the velvet flower or blue morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Argyreia maymyensis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
