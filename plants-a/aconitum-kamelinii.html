Aconitum kamelinii

Aconitum kamelinii, commonly known as Kamelin's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in areas where children or pets are present.
