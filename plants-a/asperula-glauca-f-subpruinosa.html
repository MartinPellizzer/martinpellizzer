Asperula glauca f. subpruinosa

Asperula glauca f. subpruinosa, commonly known as the silver-haired azalea, is a botanical variant of the azalea species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white to pale blue flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with a fine, powdery coating, giving it a frosted appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate blooms and unique foliage texture.
