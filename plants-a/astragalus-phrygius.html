Astragalus phrygius

Astragalus phrygius, commonly known as the Phrygian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus phrygius is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
