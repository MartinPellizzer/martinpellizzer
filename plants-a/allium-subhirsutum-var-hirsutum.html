Allium subhirsutum var. hirsutum

Allium subhirsutum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the woolly leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of thick, woolly, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, slender flower stalks topped with clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant in temperate climates.
