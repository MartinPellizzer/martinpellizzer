Actaea bifida

Actaea bifida, commonly known as the divided actaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compound, fern-like leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, while its berries provide food for birds.

Actaea bifida is valued in gardens for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.
