Arabis lycia

Arabis lycia, commonly known as Turkish rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette and are covered in fine hairs.
