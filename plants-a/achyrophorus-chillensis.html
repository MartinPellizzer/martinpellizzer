Achyrophorus chillensis

Achyrophorus chillensis, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of Andean flora.
