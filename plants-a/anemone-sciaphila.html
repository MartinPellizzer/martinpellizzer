Anemone sciaphila

Anemone sciaphila, commonly known as the shade anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the forests of eastern North America.

It thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments, often found under the canopy of hardwood trees. The plant produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow center, resembling those of true anemones. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a broad, lobed shape that helps it capture limited sunlight.

Anemone sciaphila is valued in woodland gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
