Angiopteris sakuraii

Angiopteris sakuraii, commonly known as Sakurai's water fern, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, delicate fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori and indusia, are positioned on the underside of the fronds.

Angiopteris sakuraii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
