Aeschynanthus persimilis

Aeschynanthus persimilis, commonly known as the pink-flowered ivy.

It is a flowering plant native to western Africa, specifically found in countries like Nigeria and Ghana. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
