Astracantha serpentinica

Astracantha serpentinica, commonly known as the serpent's tail, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky, purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
