Arenga longicarpa

Arenga longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited areca, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into elongated, fibrous fruits. Arenga longicarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Its fibrous fruits have traditional uses in various cultures.
