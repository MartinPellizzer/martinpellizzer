Acianthera pollardiana

Acianthera pollardiana, commonly known as the Pollard's acianthera, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal regions. Its flowering stems are often branched, and the flowers are usually pale pink or white with a distinctive lip.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
