Acaena ovalifolia var. insulae-exterioris

Acaena ovalifolia var. insulae-exterioris, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach bush, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by spherical, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of New Zealand's native flora.
