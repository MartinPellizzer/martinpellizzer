Anemone pedata

Anemone pedata, commonly known as the crown imperial, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has a thick, fleshy rhizome that allows it to store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and often have a velvety texture.

Anemone pedata is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
