Adesmia pentaphylla

Adesmia pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed legume, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring compound leaves with five leaflets. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to purple.

Adesmia pentaphylla is valued for its ecological role in soil enrichment and as a forage plant for livestock.
