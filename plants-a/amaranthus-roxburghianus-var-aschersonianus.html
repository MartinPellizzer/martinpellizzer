Amaranthus roxburghianus var. aschersonianus

Amaranthus roxburghianus var. aschersonianus, commonly known as red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed seeds that are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental applications.
