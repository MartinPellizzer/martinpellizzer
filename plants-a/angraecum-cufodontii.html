Angraecum cufodontii

Angraecum cufodontii, commonly known as the Cufodontii orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with white or pale pink flowers that have a distinctive, intricate pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, airy substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly valued in horticultural circles.
