Allium israeliticum

Allium israeliticum, commonly known as the Israeli leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and broad, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces umbels of small, white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a valuable addition to culinary dishes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal care once established.
