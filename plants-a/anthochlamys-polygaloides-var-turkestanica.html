Anthochlamys polygaloides var. turkestanica

Anthochlamys polygaloides var. turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan sweetroot, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
