Astragalus newberryi var. newberryi

Astragalus newberryi var. newberryi, commonly known as Newberry milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus newberryi var. newberryi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
