Amerimnon cuscatlanicum

Amerimnon cuscatlanicum, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas in the wild.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
