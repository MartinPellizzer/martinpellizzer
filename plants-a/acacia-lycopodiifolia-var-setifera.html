Acacia lycopodiifolia var. setifera

Acacia lycopodiifolia var. setifera, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a variant of the acacia genus native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
