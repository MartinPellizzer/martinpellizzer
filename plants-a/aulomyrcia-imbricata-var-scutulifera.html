Aulomyrcia imbricata var. scutulifera

Aulomyrcia imbricata var. scutulifera, commonly known as the scutulifera variety of the genus Aulomyrcia, is a tropical plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring a scutiform base that gives it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, with a distinct venation pattern.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its morphological uniqueness and potential ecological significance.
