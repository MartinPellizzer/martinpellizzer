Argostemma podochiloides

Argostemma podochiloides, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Argostemma podochiloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
