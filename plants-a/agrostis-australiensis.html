Agrostis australiensis

Agrostis australiensis, commonly known as the Australian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often used in landscaping due to its fine texture and ability to tolerate salt spray.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is part of the Poaceae family.
