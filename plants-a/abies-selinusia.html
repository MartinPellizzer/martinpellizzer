Abies selinusia

Abies selinusia, commonly known as the Selinusian fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds surrounded by a papery wing. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish, becoming more rugged with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
