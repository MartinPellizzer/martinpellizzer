Agave asperrima subsp. zarcensis

Agave asperrima subsp.

zarcensis, commonly known as the Zarcens ghost agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, spiky leaves that form a rosette shape, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration.

The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
