Ancylostemon bullatus

Ancylostemon bullatus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Due to overharvesting, it is now listed as an endangered species in its native habitat.
