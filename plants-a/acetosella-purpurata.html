Acetosella purpurata

Acetosella purpurata, commonly known as purple sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped, violet-tinged leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pink, appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Acetosella purpurata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to enrich soil through nitrogen fixation.
