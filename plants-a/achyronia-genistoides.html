Achyronia genistoides

Achyronia genistoides, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer or early autumn. Its leaves are small, opposite, and usually have a slight serration along the edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging climates.
