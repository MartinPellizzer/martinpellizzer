Acaena trifida var. valdiviensis

Acaena trifida var. valdiviensis, commonly known as the Valdivian thornbush, is a shrub native to southern South America, particularly in the temperate rainforests of Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, cool climates and is adapted to the high rainfall and humidity of its native habitat.

This species plays an important ecological role in its ecosystem, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
