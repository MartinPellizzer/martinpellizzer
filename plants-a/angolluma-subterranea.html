Angolluma subterranea

Angolluma subterranea, commonly known as the underground flamebush, is a rare and enigmatic plant species characterized by its subterranean growth habit.

Its roots and stems develop beneath the soil surface, forming a complex network that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that emerge above ground only during specific seasonal conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and adapted to minimize water loss, reflecting its arid habitat preferences.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it remains a subject of interest for botanists studying unique adaptations in subterranean flora.
