Astragalus arasbaranensis

Astragalus arasbaranensis, commonly known as the Arasbaran milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native habitat.
