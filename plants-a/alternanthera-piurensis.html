Alternanthera piurensis

Alternanthera piurensis, commonly known as the red-veined joyweed, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red veins contrasting with its green or purple foliage. This species thrives in slow to moderately flowing freshwater environments, often found in the Amazon basin. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and can vary in color depending on light conditions.

Alternanthera piurensis is popular in aquariums for its ornamental value and ease of care.
