Apicra maxima

Apicra maxima, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, with vibrant colors ranging from yellow to red. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Apicra maxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of prosperity in some cultures.
