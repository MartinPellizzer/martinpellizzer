Arundinaria pusilla

Arundinaria pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, reed-like stems that grow close to the ground, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria pusilla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
