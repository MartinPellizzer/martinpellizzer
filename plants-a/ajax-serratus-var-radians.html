Ajax serratus var. radians

Ajax serratus var. radians, commonly known as the Radiant Sawtooth, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive, almost spiky appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with a radiating pattern, which is why it is named "radians.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique foliage and showy blooms make it a standout in both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
