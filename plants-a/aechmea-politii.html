Aechmea politii

Aechmea politii, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety layer of trichomes, giving it a unique texture. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea politii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea politii has rosette-shaped leaves that are long, narrow, and sword-like, arranged in a dense spiral pattern around the central stem, with a vibrant green color and a waxy coating.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, dark brown bark that grows upright from the base of the rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small white to yellowish flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The plant produces a cylindrical fruit that splits open to release small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in the soil, supporting the plant's overall structure.
