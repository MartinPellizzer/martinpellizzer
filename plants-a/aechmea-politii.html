Aechmea politii

Aechmea politii, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety layer of trichomes, giving it a unique texture. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea politii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
