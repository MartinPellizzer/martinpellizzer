Areca novohibernica

Areca novohibernica, commonly known as the New Hibernian areca, is a tropical palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by elongated, three-angled fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental foliage in gardens.

The species is adapted to humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
