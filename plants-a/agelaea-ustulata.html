Agelaea ustulata

Agelaea ustulata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged opposite each other on the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and may have a reddish hue, adding to its visual appeal.
