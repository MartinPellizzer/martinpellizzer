Alternanthera corymbiformis

Alternanthera corymbiformis, commonly known as the red-veined joyweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red veins on its lance-shaped leaves, which contrast beautifully with its bright green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest during the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is often used in water gardens and as a container plant due to its vibrant appearance and ease of care.
