Agrostis moyanoi var. puberigluma

Agrostis moyanoi var. puberigluma, commonly known as the pubescent glume grass, is a variant of the Agrostis genus native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and distinctive pubescent (hairy) glumes on its inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows or along stream banks. Its botanical features include narrow, elongated leaves and a compact, spiky appearance.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphological traits and potential ecological roles.
