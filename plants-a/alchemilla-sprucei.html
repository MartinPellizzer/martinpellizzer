Alchemilla sprucei

Alchemilla sprucei, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are shield-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silky white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer.

Alchemilla sprucei thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
