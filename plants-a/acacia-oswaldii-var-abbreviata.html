Acacia oswaldii var. abbreviata

Acacia oswaldii var. abbreviata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are shorter and more compact compared to other acacia species. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in early to mid-spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
