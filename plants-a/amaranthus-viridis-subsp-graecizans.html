Amaranthus viridis subsp. graecizans

Amaranthus viridis subsp.

graecizans, commonly known as Greek amaranth, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense spikes, which are often clustered at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
