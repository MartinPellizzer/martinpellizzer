Ascarina diffusa

Ascarina diffusa, commonly known as the satinwood or sandpaper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, clustered fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its durable timber.

Ascarina diffusa is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
