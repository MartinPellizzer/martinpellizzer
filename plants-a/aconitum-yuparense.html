Aconitum yuparense

Aconitum yuparense, commonly known as the Yurupari aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces distinctive, egg-shaped tubers that are used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Aconitum yuparense contains toxic alkaloids, making it both a potent and potentially dangerous plant.
