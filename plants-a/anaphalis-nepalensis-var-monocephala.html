Anaphalis nepalensis var. monocephala

Anaphalis nepalensis var. monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed silverweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance.

The flower heads are usually solitary, giving the plant its common name and contributing to its ornamental value in alpine gardens.
