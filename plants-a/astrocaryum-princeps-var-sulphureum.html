Astrocaryum princeps var. sulphureum

Astrocaryum princeps var. sulphureum, commonly known as the golden palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that give it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural displays.

Botanically, it belongs to the Arecaceae family and is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
