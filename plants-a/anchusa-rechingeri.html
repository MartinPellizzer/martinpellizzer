Anchusa rechingeri

Anchusa rechingeri, commonly known as the Turk's cap larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that resemble a crown of larks' feet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Anchusa rechingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
