Anthurium sclerophyllum

Anthurium sclerophyllum, commonly known as the hard-leaved anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is a modified leaf. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium sclerophyllum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and floral structures.
