Alchemilla cryptocaula

Alchemilla cryptocaula, commonly known as the cryptic alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in early summer, often clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are typically basal, forming a rosette and are covered in a fine, silvery down.

Alchemilla cryptocaula is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suited to alpine environments.
