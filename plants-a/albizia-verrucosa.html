Albizia verrucosa

Albizia verrucosa, commonly known as the hairy albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Albizia verrucosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
