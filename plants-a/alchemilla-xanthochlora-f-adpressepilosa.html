Alchemilla xanthochlora f. adpressepilosa

Alchemilla xanthochlora f. adpressepilosa, commonly known as the compact yellow lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features basal leaves that are softly hairy and have a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive texture and color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
