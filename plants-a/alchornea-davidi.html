Alchornea davidi

Alchornea davidi, commonly known as David's beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its striking clusters of small, purple to blue berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, which is a common feature in the Euphorbiaceae family.
