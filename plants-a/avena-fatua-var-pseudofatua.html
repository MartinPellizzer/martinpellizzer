Avena fatua var. pseudofatua

Avena fatua var. pseudofatua, commonly known as the common oatgrass, is a variant of the wild oat species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and potential as a weed in cultivated environments.
