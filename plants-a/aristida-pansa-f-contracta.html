Aristida pansa f. contracta

Aristida pansa f. contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species characterized by its slender, compressed foliage and compact inflorescence.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually borne on short, ascending branches. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its drought-resistant traits.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
