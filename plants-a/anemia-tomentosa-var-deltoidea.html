Anemia tomentosa var. deltoidea

Anemia tomentosa var. deltoidea, commonly known as the Delta woolly morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves covered in fine, white hairs, which give it its tomentose appearance. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
