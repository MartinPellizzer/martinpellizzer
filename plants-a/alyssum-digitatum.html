Alyssum digitatum

Alyssum digitatum, commonly known as the clustered alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has thin, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum digitatum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
