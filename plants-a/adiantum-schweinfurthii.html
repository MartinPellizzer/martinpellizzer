Adiantum schweinfurthii

Adiantum schweinfurthii, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, arching shape.

Adiantum schweinfurthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
