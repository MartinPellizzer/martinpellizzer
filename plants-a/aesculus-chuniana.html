Aesculus chuniana

Aesculus chuniana, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmately compound leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits that contain glossy, chestnut-like seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display and attractive foliage.

The plant is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
