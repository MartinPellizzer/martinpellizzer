Anthemis semiensis

Anthemis semiensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly aromatic, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
