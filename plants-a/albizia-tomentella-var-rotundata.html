Albizia tomentella var. rotundata

Albizia tomentella var. rotundata, commonly known as the woolly silktree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a fluffy appearance, and its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Albizia tomentella var. rotundata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
