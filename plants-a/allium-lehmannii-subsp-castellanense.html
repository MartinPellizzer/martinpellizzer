Allium lehmannii subsp. castellanense

Allium lehmannii subsp.

castellanense, commonly known as the Sierra Madre garlic, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of strap-like, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Allium genus, with potential horticultural and culinary value.
