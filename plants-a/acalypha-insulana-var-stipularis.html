Acalypha insulana var. stipularis

Acalypha insulana var. stipularis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display a rich red or purple vein pattern against a green background. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
