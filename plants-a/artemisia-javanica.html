Artemisia javanica

Artemisia javanica, commonly known as Java wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
