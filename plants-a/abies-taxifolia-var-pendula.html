Abies taxifolia var. pendula

Abies taxifolia var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping fir, is a variant of the Chinese fir characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

This evergreen conifer exhibits a slow growth rate and is native to the mountainous regions of western China. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged spirally along the stems. The plant produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its unique, drooping appearance.
