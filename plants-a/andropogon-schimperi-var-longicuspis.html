Andropogon schimperi var. longicuspis

Andropogon schimperi var. longicuspis, commonly known as the long-bristled bluestem, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its robust root system, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Its botanical structure supports biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
