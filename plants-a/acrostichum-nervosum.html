Acrostichum nervosum

Acrostichum nervosum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and the fronds are sometimes used as thatching material.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
