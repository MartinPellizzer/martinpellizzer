Adiantum granvilleanum

Adiantum granvilleanum, commonly known as the granvillean maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, finely divided structure that gives it a graceful appearance.

Adiantum granvilleanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
