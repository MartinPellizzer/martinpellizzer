Anthriscus sylvestris subsp. aemula

Anthriscus sylvestris subsp.

aemula, commonly known as the lesser cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.

The plant features slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, parsley-like scent.

It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
