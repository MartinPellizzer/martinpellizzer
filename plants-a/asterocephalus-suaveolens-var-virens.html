Asterocephalus suaveolens var. virens

Asterocephalus suaveolens var. virens, commonly known as the green-flowered aster.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa, characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces pale green to white flowers with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique coloration and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
