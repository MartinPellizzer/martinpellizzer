Arethusa bulbosa f. albiflora

Arethusa bulbosa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered arethusa, is a rare and striking aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow, acidic wetlands and is often found in association with other wetland species.
