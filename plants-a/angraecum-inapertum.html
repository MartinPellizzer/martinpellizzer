Angraecum inapertum

Angraecum inapertum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white flowers with a distinctive central spur. The plant produces its flowers during the dry season, typically from September to November, and relies on specific pollinators such as the hawkmoth. Its unique floral structure is adapted for specialized pollination, showcasing the intricate relationships in tropical ecosystems.

Angraecum inapertum is also noted for its slow growth and preference for well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.
