Aster oolentangiensis f. incarnatus

Aster oolentangiensis f. incarnatus, commonly known as the pink-flowered aster, is a botanical variant of the aster species native to the northeastern United States.

This flowering plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink to lavender-colored flowers. The foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
