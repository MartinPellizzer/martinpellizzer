Aloe andringitrensis

Aloe andringitrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the dry season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.

Aloe andringitrensis is well-suited to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
