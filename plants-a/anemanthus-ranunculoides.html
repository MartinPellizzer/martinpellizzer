Anemanthus ranunculoides

Anemanthus ranunculoides, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has delicate, trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish or white flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Anemanthus ranunculoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
