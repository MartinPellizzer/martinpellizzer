Adenia spinosa

Adenia spinosa, commonly known as the spiked adenia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds.

Adenia spinosa is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

The roots are fibrous and tuberous, helping the plant store water and nutrients in arid environments.
