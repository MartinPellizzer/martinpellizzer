Achras sapota

Achras sapota, commonly known as the chikoo or sapodilla, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with thick, leathery leaves and smooth, bark-like fruit. The fruit is characterized by its rough, brown skin and a soft, sweet, custard-like pulp containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and has been cultivated for centuries in tropical regions.
