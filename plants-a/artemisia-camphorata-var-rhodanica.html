Artemisia camphorata var. rhodanica

Artemisia camphorata var. rhodanica, commonly known as the rhodanica tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-green foliage with finely divided, needle-like leaves that emit a strong camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
