Astragalus cornutus var. glaber

Astragalus cornutus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-headed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its sparsely hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus cornutus var. glaber is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
