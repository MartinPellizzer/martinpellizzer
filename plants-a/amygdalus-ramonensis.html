Amygdalus ramonensis

Amygdalus ramonensis, commonly known as the Ramon almond, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces hard, woody fruits that contain almond-like seeds, which are edible after proper processing. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species of ecological significance.
