Andropogon carinatus

Andropogon carinatus, commonly known as the ridge grass, is a tall perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct ridge running along the midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
