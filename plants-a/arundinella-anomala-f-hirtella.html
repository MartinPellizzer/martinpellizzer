Arundinella anomala f. hirtella

Arundinella anomala f. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and elongated, cylindrical stems that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
