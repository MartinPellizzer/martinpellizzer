Apera

Apera, commonly known as cocklebur, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rough, prickly leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Apera is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environments, including fields and roadsides.

It is considered a weed in many agricultural areas due to its aggressive growth habit.
