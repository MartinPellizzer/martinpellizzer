Angophora costata subsp. leiocarpa

Angophora costata subsp.

leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-barked apple or red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae. It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often forming part of open forests or woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
