Acinodendron biperuliferum

Acinodendron biperuliferum, commonly known as the two-ribbed acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, stipitate leaves with a distinctive two-ribbed structure on the petioles. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various insect species.
