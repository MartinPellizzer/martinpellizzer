Aster oblongifolius f. roseus

Aster oblongifolius f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its most distinctive trait is the showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
