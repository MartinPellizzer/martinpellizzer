Anemone turczaninovii var. hulunensis

Anemone turczaninovii var. hulunensis, commonly known as the Hulun Anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Hulunbuir region of Inner Mongolia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open meadows or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
