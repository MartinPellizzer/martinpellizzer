Acalypha heteromorpha

Acalypha heteromorpha, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from deep red to green, often with contrasting veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Acalypha heteromorpha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
