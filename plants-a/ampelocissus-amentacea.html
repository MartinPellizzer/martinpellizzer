Ampelocissus amentacea

Ampelocissus amentacea, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a woody vine native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
