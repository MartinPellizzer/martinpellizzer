Acer japonicum var. microphyllum

Acer japonicum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with dwarf leaves, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically red or orange in the fall. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for smaller gardens or as a ornamental specimen. Its flowers are inconspicuous, but the tree is prized for its vibrant autumn foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
