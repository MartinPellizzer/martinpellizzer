Arum

Arum, commonly known as calla lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive spatulate leaves and inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. Arum species are native to tropical and subtropical regions around the world. They often have a unique method of reproduction involving the production of berries that contain toxic seeds.

Many arum species are cultivated for their ornamental value due to their striking foliage and flowers.
