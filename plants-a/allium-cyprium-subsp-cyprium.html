Allium cyprium subsp. cyprium

Allium cyprium subsp.

cyprium, commonly known as the Cyprus leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, hollow stems that are often bluish-green in color and can grow up to 60 centimeters tall.

The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in a loose umbel, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, resembling those of the leek family.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and mild, garlic-like scent.
