Alsine tenuifolia var. vaillantiana

Alsine tenuifolia var. vaillantiana, commonly known as Vaillant's narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features small, succulent leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
