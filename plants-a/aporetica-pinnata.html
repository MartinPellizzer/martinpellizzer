Aporetica pinnata

Aporetica pinnata, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its fronds are composed of numerous small, pointed leaflets arranged in a delicate, spreading pattern.

Aporetica pinnata plays a role in providing habitat for various small organisms within its ecosystem.
