Acer isolobum

Acer isolobum, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental species. The tree has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that turn shades of red, orange, and yellow during autumn. Its flowers are small and green, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

Acer isolobum typically grows to a height of 20 to 30 meters and thrives in a variety of soil types.
