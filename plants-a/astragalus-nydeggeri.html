Astragalus nydeggeri

Astragalus nydeggeri, commonly known as Nydegger's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers. This species is typically found in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and sagebrush steppe. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
