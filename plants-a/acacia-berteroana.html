Acacia berteroana

Acacia berteroana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which are often clustered at the ends of branches. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, pod-like fruits that mature into a dark brown color.

Acacia berteroana is often found in dry, arid environments and is adapted to thrive in regions with low rainfall.
