Aristolochia kaempferi f. thibetica

Aristolochia kaempferi f. thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of southern China and northern Vietnam.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive, intricate veining pattern. The plant produces striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are notable for their complex structure, which includes a hooded calyx and a spurred lip.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral morphology.
