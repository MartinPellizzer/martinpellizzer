Amomum xanthoparyphe

Amomum xanthoparyphe, commonly known as the golden-leafed cardamom, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which eventually develop into the aromatic seed pods used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its unique fragrance and therapeutic benefits.
