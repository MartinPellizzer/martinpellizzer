Acer velutinum var. vanvolxemii

Acer velutinum var. vanvolxemii, commonly known as the Vanvolxemii maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental tree.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and landscapes.
