Aralia quinquefolia var. angustifolia

Aralia quinquefolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf devil's ivy, is a variant of the American aralia characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with compound leaves that are typically arranged in a pinnate pattern. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue berries.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
