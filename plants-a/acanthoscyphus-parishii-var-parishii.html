Acanthoscyphus parishii var. parishii

Acanthoscyphus parishii var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or coastal regions. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are usually basal, with a distinctive lobed or divided shape. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
