Andropogon prolixus

Andropogon prolixus, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn golden in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Andropogon prolixus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
