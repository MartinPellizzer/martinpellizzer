Acer macrophyllum var. brevialatum

Acer macrophyllum var. brevialatum, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a variety of the sugar maple tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged seeds known as samaras. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with cool, temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
