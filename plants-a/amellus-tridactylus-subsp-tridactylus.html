Amellus tridactylus subsp. tridactylus

Amellus tridactylus subsp.

tridactylus, commonly known as the three-toed amellus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange.

The plant has a compact growth habit with short, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
