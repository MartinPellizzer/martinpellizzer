Achillea sachokiana

Achillea sachokiana, commonly known as the Siberian yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as steppes and rocky soils. The plant features feathery, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Achillea sachokiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
