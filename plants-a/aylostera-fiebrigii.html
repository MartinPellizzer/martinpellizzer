Aylostera fiebrigii

Aylostera fiebrigii, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in dense, white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems, adapting to arid environments.

Aylostera fiebrigii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
