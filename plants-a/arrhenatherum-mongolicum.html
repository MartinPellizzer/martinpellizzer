Arrhenatherum mongolicum

Arrhenatherum mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces erect, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its fine, silky appearance and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals.
