Astragalus nyensis

Astragalus nyensis, commonly known as the New Zealand milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. Astragalus nyensis is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for native insects and birds.
