Amphicarpaea villosa

Amphicarpaea villosa, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces two types of inflorescences: one above ground and one below ground, a unique feature known as amphicarpism. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
