Acacia penicillifera

Acacia penicillifera, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of North Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with a spreading crown and long, slender branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets.

The tree is well adapted to arid environments and is often used for its timber and as a source of gum arabic.
