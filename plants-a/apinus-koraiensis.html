Apinus koraiensis

Apinus koraiensis, commonly known as the Korean apinus, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Apinus koraiensis is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
