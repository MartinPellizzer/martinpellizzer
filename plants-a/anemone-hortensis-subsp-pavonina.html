Anemone hortensis subsp. pavonina

Anemone hortensis subsp.

pavonina, commonly known as the peacock anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features showy, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are basal and alternate, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species within the Anemone genus, showcasing genetic diversity within the group.
