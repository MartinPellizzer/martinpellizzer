Alstroemeria edulis

Alstroemeria edulis, commonly known as the edible alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape around the stem.

While primarily cultivated for its ornamental value, it is also noted for its edible roots, which have been used in traditional culinary practices.
