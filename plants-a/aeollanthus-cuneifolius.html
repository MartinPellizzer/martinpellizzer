Aeollanthus cuneifolius

Aeollanthus cuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aeollanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or open woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
