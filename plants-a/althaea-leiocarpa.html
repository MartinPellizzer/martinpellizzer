Althaea leiocarpa

Althaea leiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and large, showy flowers that range from pink to purple in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Althaea leiocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential soothing properties.
