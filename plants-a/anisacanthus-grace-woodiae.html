Anisacanthus grace-woodiae

Anisacanthus grace-woodiae, commonly known as the Grace-woodie flame flower, is a striking shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
