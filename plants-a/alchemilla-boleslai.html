Alchemilla boleslai

Alchemilla boleslai, commonly known as the Polish lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Alchemilla boleslai is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
