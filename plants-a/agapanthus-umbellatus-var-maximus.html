Agapanthus umbellatus var. maximus

Agapanthus umbellatus var. maximus, commonly known as the giant blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features large, showy blue or purple flower clusters that bloom in summer, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
