Aspidixia attenuata

Aspidixia attenuata, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Aspidixia attenuata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species often found in rocky soils.
