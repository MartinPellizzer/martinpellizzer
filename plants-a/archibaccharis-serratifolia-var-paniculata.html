Archibaccharis serratifolia var. paniculata

Archibaccharis serratifolia var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled archibaccharis, is a species native to the coastal regions of southeastern Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is rough and fissured, often exuding a resinous substance.

This species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.
