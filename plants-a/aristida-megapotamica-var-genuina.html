Aristida megapotamica var. genuina

Aristida megapotamica var. genuina, commonly known as the southern grass or narrowleaf threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
