Aira pulchella subsp. tenorei

Aira pulchella subsp.

tenorei, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear spikelets with purple to reddish hues.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
