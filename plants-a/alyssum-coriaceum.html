Alyssum coriaceum

Alyssum coriaceum, commonly known as sand alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has gray-green, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Alyssum coriaceum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
