Astragalus elliotii

Astragalus elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species.

Astragalus elliotii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
