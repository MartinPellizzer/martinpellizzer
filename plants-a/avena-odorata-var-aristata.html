Avena odorata var. aristata

Avena odorata var. aristata, commonly known as the fragrant oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems with long, narrow leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
