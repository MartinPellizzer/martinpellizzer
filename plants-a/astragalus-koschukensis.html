Astragalus koschukensis

Astragalus koschukensis, commonly known as the Altai milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus koschukensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
