Aleuritopteris javanensis

Aleuritopteris javanensis, commonly known as the Javanese water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, delicate fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are arranged in a distinctive pattern.

Aleuritopteris javanensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
