Arkezostis pedata

Arkezostis pedata, commonly known as the walking fern, is a unique plant characterized by its rhizomes that grow along the ground and produce upright, leafy shoots.

It is a type of fern that exhibits a distinctive growth pattern, with its fronds often appearing to "walk" across the soil as they spread. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss.

Arkezostis pedata is known for its ability to form dense colonies, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
