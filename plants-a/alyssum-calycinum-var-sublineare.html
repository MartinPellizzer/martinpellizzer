Alyssum calycinum var. sublineare

Alyssum calycinum var. sublineare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens and rockeries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
