Aralia sololensis

Aralia sololensis, commonly known as the Solomon's lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has compound leaves with a feathery appearance. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

However, it is important to note that some species within the Aralia genus can be toxic, so caution is advised when handling it.
