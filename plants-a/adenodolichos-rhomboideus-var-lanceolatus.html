Adenodolichos rhomboideus var. lanceolatus

Adenodolichos rhomboideus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with lanceolate leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in racemes. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both human consumption and as a forage crop.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for growth in warm climates with well-drained soils.
