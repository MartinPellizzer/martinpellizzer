Achras mammosa

Achras mammosa, commonly known as the "sugar apple" or "cherry gum," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces bright red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, edible pulp. The plant has a distinctive appearance with smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
