Agathosma perplexa

Agathosma perplexa, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom from late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Agathosma perplexa is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
