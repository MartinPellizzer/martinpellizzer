Atelanthera perpusilla

Atelanthera perpusilla, commonly known as the small flame flower, is a compact shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
