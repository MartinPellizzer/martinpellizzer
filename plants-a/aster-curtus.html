Aster curtus

Aster curtus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its foliage is narrow and linear, with a distinctive silver-gray underside.

Aster curtus blooms in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
