Aponogeton junceus subsp. rehmannii

Aponogeton junceus subsp.

rehmannii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with roots that hang beneath.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically submerged, adding a subtle ornamental value to ponds and water gardens. It thrives in shallow, still waters with moderate light and nutrient-rich substrates.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic environments.
