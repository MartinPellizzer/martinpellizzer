Anthyllis vulneraria f. bonjeanii

Anthyllis vulneraria f. bonjeanii, commonly known as the Bonjean's burnet, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in spherical heads. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive, compact form.
