Amyema haenkeana

Amyema haenkeana, commonly known as the mountain mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas, often in humid and cool environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Amyema haenkeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
