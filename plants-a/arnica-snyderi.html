Arnica snyderi

Arnica snyderi, commonly known as Snyder's arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, while its stem is upright and branched.

Snyder's arnica is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
