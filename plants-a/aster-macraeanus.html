Aster macraeanus

Aster macraeanus, commonly known as the Cape aster, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Aster macraeanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
