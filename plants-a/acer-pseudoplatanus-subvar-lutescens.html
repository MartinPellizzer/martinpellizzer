Acer pseudoplatanus subvar. lutescens

Acer pseudoplatanus subvar. lutescens, commonly known as the golden maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which turns vibrant shades of gold in the autumn. The plant has palmate leaves with five lobes, typically appearing in pairs on the branches. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This subvariety is relatively hardy and can adapt to a range of soil conditions.
