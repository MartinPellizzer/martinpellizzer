Acacia pycnophylla var. angustifolia

Acacia pycnophylla var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The wood of this species is hard and durable, often used in traditional crafts and construction.
