Apocynum cannabinum

Apocynum cannabinum, commonly known as Indian hemp, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, with opposite, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, and its milky sap contains various alkaloids. It is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed soils.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been historically used for medicinal and industrial purposes.
