Asperula cynanchica var. gussonei

Asperula cynanchica var. gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and a distinctive, creeping growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant aroma and delicate appearance.
