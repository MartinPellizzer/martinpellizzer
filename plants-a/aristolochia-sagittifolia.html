Aristolochia sagittifolia

Aristolochia sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and a unique, tubular flower that resembles a pipe. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, often fragrant blossoms. Its vines can grow quite long, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
