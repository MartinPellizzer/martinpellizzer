Astragalus depressus subf. ioleucus

Astragalus depressus subf.

ioleucus, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the western United States. It typically features slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in grasslands and sagebrush steppe habitats.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
