Aster sempervirens

Aster sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen aster, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, clustered flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year, providing visual interest even in winter. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, producing a range of colors from blue to purple.

Aster sempervirens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
