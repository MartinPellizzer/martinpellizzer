Acaena dumulosa

Acaena dumulosa, commonly known as the tufted knapweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bristly, spiny fruits. Its leaves are alternate, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments.

Acaena dumulosa is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
