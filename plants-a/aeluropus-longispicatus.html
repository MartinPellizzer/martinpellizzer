Aeluropus longispicatus, commonly known as the long-bristled grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family.
