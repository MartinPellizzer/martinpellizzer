Androcymbium poeltianum

Androcymbium poeltianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a rare and unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and intricate floral structure. Native to the tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
