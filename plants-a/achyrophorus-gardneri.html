Achyrophorus gardneri

Achyrophorus gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's achyrophorus, is a rare flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in pine flatwoods and scrub habitats.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several states.
