Allium platystylum

Allium platystylum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic or flatstem garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulb is edible and has a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Allium platystylum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
