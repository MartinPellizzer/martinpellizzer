Acacia trigonocarpa

Acacia trigonocarpa, commonly known as the three-angled wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate (three-leaf) compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia trigonocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
