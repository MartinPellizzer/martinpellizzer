Ardisia impressa

Ardisia impressa, commonly known as the glossy abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, tubular flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are a source of food for various birds and small mammals. Ardisia impressa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and green spaces.
