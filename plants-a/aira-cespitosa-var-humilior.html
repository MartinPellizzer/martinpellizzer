Aira cespitosa var. humilior

Aira cespitosa var. humilior, commonly known as meadow foxtail, is a low-growing grass species native to temperate regions of North America.

It typically forms dense, mat-like clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, prairies, and disturbed areas. It is an important component of native grasslands and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically valuable and aesthetically pleasing in natural landscapes.
