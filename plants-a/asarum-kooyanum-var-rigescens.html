Asarum kooyanum var. rigescens

Asarum kooyanum var. rigescens, commonly known as the Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features a tuberous root system and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spike, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic rhizomes have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
