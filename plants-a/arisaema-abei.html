Arisaema abei

Arisaema abei, commonly known as the Yunnan arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces berries that contain toxic seeds.

Arisaema abei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
