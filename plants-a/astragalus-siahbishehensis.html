Astragalus siahbishehensis

Astragalus siahbishehensis, commonly known as the Siahbisheh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. It plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
