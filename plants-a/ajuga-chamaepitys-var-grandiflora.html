Ajuga chamaepitys var. grandiflora

Ajuga chamaepitys var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant ground covers.
