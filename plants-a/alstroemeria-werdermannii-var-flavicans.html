Alstroemeria werdermannii var. flavicans

Alstroemeria werdermannii var. flavicans, commonly known as the yellow-flowered alstroemeria, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and long, strap-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in a compact, bushy form, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a bright splash of color to gardens.
