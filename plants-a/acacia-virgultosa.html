Acacia virgultosa

Acacia virgultosa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flower spikes that appear in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acacia virgultosa is also recognized for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
