Acacia leptophylla

Acacia leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated phyllodes that are typically light green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes. Acacia leptophylla is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
