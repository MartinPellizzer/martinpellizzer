Amygdalus bucharica var. media

Amygdalus bucharica var. media, commonly known as the middle-sized wild almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, hard nuts enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish outer layer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is occasionally grown in botanical gardens and for its edible nuts.
