Acalypha schneideriana

Acalypha schneideriana, commonly known as the Schneider's spurge or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha schneideriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
