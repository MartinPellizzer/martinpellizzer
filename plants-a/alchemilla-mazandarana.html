Alchemilla mazandarana

Alchemilla mazandarana, commonly known as Persian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in upright cymes. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
