Atalantia kwangtungensis

Atalantia kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong atalantia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
