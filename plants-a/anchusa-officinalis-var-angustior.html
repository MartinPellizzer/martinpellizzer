Anchusa officinalis var. angustior

Anchusa officinalis var. angustior, commonly known as narrow-leaved bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue. This variety is part of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
