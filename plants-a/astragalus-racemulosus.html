Astragalus racemulosus

Astragalus racemulosus, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Astragalus racemulosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
