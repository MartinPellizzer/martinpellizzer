Abies nordmanniana subsp. equi-trojani

Abies nordmanniana subsp.

equi-trojani, commonly known as the Caucasian fir, is a coniferous tree native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds surrounded by a thin, winged aril. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous areas.

The subspecies is noted for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
