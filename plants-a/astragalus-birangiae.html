Astragalus birangiae

Astragalus birangiae, commonly known as the Birangai milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus birangiae is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
