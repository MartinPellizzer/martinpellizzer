Adenia isaloensis

Adenia isaloensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped appearance, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Adenia isaloensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
