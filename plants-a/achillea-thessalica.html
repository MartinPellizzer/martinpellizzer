Achillea thessalica

Achillea thessalica, commonly known as Greek yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pinkish in color. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and grasslands. It features finely divided, aromatic leaves and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

Achillea thessalica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

Morphology

Achillea thessalica has basal leaves that are lanceolate or linear in shape, arranged in a rosette, with a gray-green color and a rough, hairy texture.

The stem is upright, slender, and slightly hairy, with a greenish-gray color, growing in a single, branching pattern. The flowers are composite, consisting of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets, blooming from late spring to early summer, and are primarily pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small achene with a pappus, which allows for wind dispersal.

The roots are fibrous and rhizomatous, aiding in the plant's ability to spread and establish in various habitats.
