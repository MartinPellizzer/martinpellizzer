Atylus anethifolius

Atylus anethifolius, commonly known as the anise-leaved tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has feathery, anise-scented leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or saline environments.

Atylus anethifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
