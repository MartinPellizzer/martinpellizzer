Aquilegia bicolor

Aquilegia bicolor, commonly known as the checkered lady's mantle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, white, and purple. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive heart-shaped base. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Aquilegia bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
