Aesculus × neglecta var. georgiana

Aesculus × neglecta var. georgiana, commonly known as the Carolina chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the soapberry family, Sapindaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by spiny, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native landscapes.
