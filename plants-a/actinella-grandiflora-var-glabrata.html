Actinella grandiflora var. glabrata

Actinella grandiflora var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf actinella, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
