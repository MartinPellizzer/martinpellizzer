Adenophora atuntzensis

Adenophora atuntzensis, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Adenophora atuntzensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
