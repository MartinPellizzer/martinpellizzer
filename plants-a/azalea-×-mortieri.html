Azalea × mortieri

Azalea × mortieri, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron obtusum and Rhododendron indicum.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features elliptical, leathery leaves with a dark green upper surface. Azalea × mortieri thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which occurs in late spring to early summer.
