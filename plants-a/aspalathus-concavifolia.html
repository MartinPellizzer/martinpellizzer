Aspalathus concavifolia

Aspalathus concavifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery or grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Aspalathus concavifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
