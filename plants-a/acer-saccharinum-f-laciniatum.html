Acer saccharinum f. laciniatum

Acer saccharinum f. laciniatum, commonly known as the dissected silver maple, is a variant of the silver maple tree characterized by its finely cut, feathery leaves.

This ornamental tree is native to eastern North America and is often cultivated for its striking foliage. The leaves are typically a vibrant green in summer and turn yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest to the landscape. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

While it is relatively fast-growing, it may be susceptible to certain pests and diseases, requiring regular maintenance.
