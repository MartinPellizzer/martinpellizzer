Alstroemeria itatiaica

Alstroemeria itatiaica, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape, and it produces a pleasant, spicy fragrance.

Alstroemeria itatiaica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
