Azadirachta integrifoliola

Azadirachta integrifoliola, commonly known as the African ash or African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
