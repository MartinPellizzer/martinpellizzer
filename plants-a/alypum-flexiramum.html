Alypum flexiramum

Alypum flexiramum, commonly known as the flexible alypum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh conditions.

Alypum flexiramum is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
