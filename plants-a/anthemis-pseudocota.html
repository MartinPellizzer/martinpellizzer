Anthemis pseudocota

Anthemis pseudocota, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are typically arranged in flat-topped clusters, and it has finely divided, grass-like leaves. Anthemis pseudocota is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
