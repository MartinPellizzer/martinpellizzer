Allium roseum var. cassium

Allium roseum var. cassium, commonly known as the pink garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are bluish-green in color and emit a mild garlic scent. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild culinary uses in cooking.
