Aster ageratoides var. heterophyllus

Aster ageratoides var. heterophyllus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are often alternate and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
