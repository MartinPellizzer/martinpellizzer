Actinostemon communis var. spathulatus

Actinostemon communis var. spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed actinostemon, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and spatulate in shape, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
