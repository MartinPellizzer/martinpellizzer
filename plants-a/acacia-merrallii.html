Acacia merrallii

Acacia merrallii, commonly known as the Merrall's wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Acacia merrallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
