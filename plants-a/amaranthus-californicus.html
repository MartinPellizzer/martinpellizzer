Amaranthus californicus

Amaranthus californicus, commonly known as California amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its seeds are edible and have been used by Indigenous peoples for food and ceremonial purposes.
