Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp. erecta

Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp.

erecta, commonly known as the erect manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers.

The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are leathery and have a glandular surface, which is a distinguishing botanical feature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
