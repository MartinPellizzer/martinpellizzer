Adenopodia scelerata

Adenopodia scelerata, commonly known as the rough-stemmed adenopodia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in stiff, rough hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in umbels, a typical feature of the Apiaceae family.

Adenopodia scelerata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
