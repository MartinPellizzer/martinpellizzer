Asystasia violacea

Asystasia violacea, commonly known as violet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, hence its common name.

Asystasia violacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
