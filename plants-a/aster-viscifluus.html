Aster viscifluus

Aster viscifluus, commonly known as the stickyleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a sticky, glandular substance. Its flowering stems are typically erect and branched, supporting the clusters of small, composite flowers.

Aster viscifluus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
