Agave deweyana

Agave deweyana, commonly known as the Desert Spoon, is a species of agave native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing yellowish-green flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent leaves and stems.

Agave deweyana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and architectural appearance.
