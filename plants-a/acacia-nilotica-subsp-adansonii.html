Acacia nilotica subsp. adansonii

Acacia nilotica subsp.

adansonii, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous pod, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in stabilizing soils in degraded areas.
