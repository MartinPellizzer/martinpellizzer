Astragalus glaux var. granadinus

Astragalus glaux var. granadinus, commonly known as the granadine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
