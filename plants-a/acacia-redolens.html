Acacia redolens

Acacia redolens, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact globular clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Acacia redolens also has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
