Anthemis pusilla subsp. ammanthiformis

Anthemis pusilla subsp.

ammanthiformis, commonly known as the dwarf tansy, is a small flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads that are often surrounded by a ring of pale yellow ray florets. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
