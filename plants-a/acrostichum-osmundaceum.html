Acrostichum osmundaceum

Acrostichum osmundaceum, commonly known as the water holly, is a fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swamps and stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The botanical features of Acrostichum osmundaceum make it an important species in the study of aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
