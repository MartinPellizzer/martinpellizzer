Acalypha pedunculata

Acalypha pedunculata, commonly known as the redroot croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red roots and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
