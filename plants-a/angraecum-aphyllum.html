Angraecum aphyllum

Angraecum aphyllum, commonly known as the leafless angraecum, is a unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is distinguished by its lack of visible leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its pale green, fleshy stems. The plant produces striking, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators like moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Angraecum aphyllum is a rare and specialized species, showcasing the adaptability of orchids to extreme ecological conditions.
