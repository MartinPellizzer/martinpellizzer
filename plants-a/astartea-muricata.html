Astartea muricata

Astartea muricata, commonly known as the spiny aster, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, spiny stems and leaves with a rough texture, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and they bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Astartea muricata is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
