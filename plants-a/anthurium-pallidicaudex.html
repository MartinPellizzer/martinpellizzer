Anthurium pallidicaudex

Anthurium pallidicaudex, commonly known as the pale-tailed anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the understory of humid forests in Central and South America.

It features elongated, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a pale or white margin, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant red spadices surrounded by a showy, colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

Anthurium pallidicaudex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
