Alocasia gjellerupii

Alocasia gjellerupii, commonly known as the Gjellerup's arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, featuring a dark green, glossy surface with prominent veins. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Alocasia gjellerupii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded gardens.
