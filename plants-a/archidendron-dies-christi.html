Archidendron dies-christi

Archidendron dies-christi, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive red or orange calyx. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
