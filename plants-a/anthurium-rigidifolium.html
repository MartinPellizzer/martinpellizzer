Anthurium rigidifolium

Anthurium rigidifolium, commonly known as the stiff-leaved anthurium, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix.

Native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, colorful spathes that enclose the inflorescence, which consists of a central spadix with small, clustered flowers. Its foliage is notable for its rigid texture and deep green color, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Anthurium rigidifolium is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
