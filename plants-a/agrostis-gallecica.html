Agrostis gallecica

Agrostis gallecica, commonly known as the coastal bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It thrives in sandy, saline soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in harsh maritime environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
