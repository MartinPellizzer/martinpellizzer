Aegilops mutica var. nuluci

Aegilops mutica var. nuluci, commonly known as the rough false wheat, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing bracteate spikelets. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Aegilops mutica var. nuluci is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
