Abutilon virens

Abutilon virens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Abutilon virens is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
