Acantholimon tricolor

Acantholimon tricolor, commonly known as the three-color thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and features blue, pink, and white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage with glandular hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Acantholimon tricolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens.
