Argyranthemum adauctum subsp. adauctum

Argyranthemum adauctum subsp.

adauctum, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer.

The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This species is often cultivated for its decorative value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
