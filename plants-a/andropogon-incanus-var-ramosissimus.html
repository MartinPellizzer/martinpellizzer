Andropogon incanus var. ramosissimus

Andropogon incanus var. ramosissimus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feather-like, gray-green foliage. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments. It is often used in landscaping and as a bioenergy crop due to its high biomass yield.

Additionally, it plays an important role in ecological restoration projects because of its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
