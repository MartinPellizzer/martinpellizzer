Argemone barclayana

Argemone barclayana, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers and hairy, spiny stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
