Alchemilla looseri f. glabra

Alchemilla looseri f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are smooth to the touch in this particular form. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, attractive ground cover.
