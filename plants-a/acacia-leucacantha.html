Acacia leucacantha

Acacia leucacantha, commonly known as the white-flowered acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia leucacantha is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuel.
