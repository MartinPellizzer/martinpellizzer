Asplenium variabile

Asplenium variabile, commonly known as the variable spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or forest floors. The plant has a compact rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often curled or twisted. Its sporangia are borne on the undersides of the leaves, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

Asplenium variabile is known for its adaptability and is found in various regions across the globe.
