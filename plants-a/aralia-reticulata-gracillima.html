Aralia reticulata gracillima

Aralia reticulata gracillima, commonly known as the laceleaf aralia, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its compound leaves are usually arranged in a whorled pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
