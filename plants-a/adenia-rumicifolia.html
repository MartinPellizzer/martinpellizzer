Adenia rumicifolia

Adenia rumicifolia, commonly known as the sour fig or African sour fig, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits with a sour taste. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive, serrated appearance.

Adenia rumicifolia is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
