Acacia lowei

Acacia lowei, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The leaves are small, feathery, and often shed during dry periods, a feature common in drought-resistant plants. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and the plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

Acacia lowei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, low-nutrient soils.
