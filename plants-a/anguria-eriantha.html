Anguria eriantha

Anguria eriantha, commonly known as the giant cucumber, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its large, sprawling vines that can grow up to 10 meters in length. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that can weigh several kilograms, with a smooth, waxy skin and a mild, sweet flavor. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it relies on insect pollination for reproduction.

Anguria eriantha is cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary applications in its native regions.
