Alsine juniperina var. grandiflora

Alsine juniperina var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora variety of Alsine juniperina, is a flowering plant characterized by its delicate, fragrant foliage and showy, small white or pale blue flowers.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure. The plant is native to regions with temperate climates, often found in woodland edges or along stream banks. Its botanical features include narrow, needle-like leaves and a compact, shrub-like growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
