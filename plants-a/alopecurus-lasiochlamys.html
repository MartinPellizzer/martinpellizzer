Alopecurus lasiochlamys

Alopecurus lasiochlamys, commonly known as the hairy bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil, making it useful in erosion control.

Alopecurus lasiochlamys is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
