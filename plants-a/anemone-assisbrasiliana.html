Anemone assisbrasiliana

Anemone assisbrasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian anemone, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
