Aetanthus trifolius

Aetanthus trifolius, commonly known as the three-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, this plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
