Acacia merkeri

Acacia merkeri, commonly known as the Merker's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and produces yellow, globular flower heads. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The plant is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental species.
