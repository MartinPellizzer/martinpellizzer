Anthyllis hispida

Anthyllis hispida, commonly known as the hairy anthyllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and is often found in open, sunny areas.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
