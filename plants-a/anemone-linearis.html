Anemone linearis

Anemone linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, upright stems with finely divided, linear leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Anemone linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
