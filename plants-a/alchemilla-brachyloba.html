Alchemilla brachyloba

Alchemilla brachyloba, commonly known as narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that resembles a mantle, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Alchemilla brachyloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
