Arenaria emarginata

Arenaria emarginata, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats. Its flowers are typically followed by small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Arenaria emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
