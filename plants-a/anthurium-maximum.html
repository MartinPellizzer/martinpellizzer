Anthurium maximum

Anthurium maximum, commonly known as the giant anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 feet in length, often with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces vibrant red spathes that enclose its spadix, which is a unique floral structure typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium maximum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
