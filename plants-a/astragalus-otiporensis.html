Astragalus otiporensis

Astragalus otiporensis, commonly known as the Otiporensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
