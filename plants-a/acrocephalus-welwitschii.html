Acrocephalus welwitschii

Acrocephalus welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spikes. It is often found in dense stands, contributing to the structural complexity of its ecosystem.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a plant of conservation interest in certain regions.
