Aristida intricata

Aristida intricata, commonly known as the intricate grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry foliage and dense, tufted growth habit, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with a pale blue or greenish hue. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and establish itself in disturbed or degraded soils.

Aristida intricata is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
