Acetosella amara

Acetosella amara, commonly known as bitter-sweet violets, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Acetosella in the family Violaceae, which includes various species of violets. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine, though the plant is known for its bitter taste.
