Acanthus ciliaris

Acanthus ciliaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, spiny leaves that are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant produces tall spikes of small, pale blue or purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Acanthus ciliaris is also valued for its historical use in classical art and architecture as a decorative motif.
