Amomum verrucosum

Amomum verrucosum, commonly known as the rough-shelled cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces fragrant, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a triangular, three-lobed capsule that contains aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Amomum verrucosum is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in digestive and respiratory treatments.
