Amperea spartioides

Amperea spartioides, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow or white flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Amperea spartioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
