Astragalus floccosus subsp. floccosus

Astragalus floccosus subsp.

floccosus, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is characterized by its hairy, often grayish stems and compound leaves with small, bean-like leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the legume family, Fabaceae.
