Asphodelus creticus

Asphodelus creticus, commonly known as the Cretan asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and spikes of bright yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation.

Asphodelus creticus is often used in traditional medicine and has cultural significance in various Mediterranean countries.
