Andropogon virginicus subsp. leucostachyus

Andropogon virginicus subsp.

leucostachyus, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a native grass species of the United States. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and distinctive, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer.

This perennial grass thrives in full sun and is often found in prairies, woodlands, and disturbed sites. Its clumping growth habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.

The plant's botanical features contribute to its ecological importance in native grassland ecosystems.
