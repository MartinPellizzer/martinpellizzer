Anthomeles subserrata

Anthomeles subserrata, commonly known as the serrate arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy root vegetable in some regions.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
