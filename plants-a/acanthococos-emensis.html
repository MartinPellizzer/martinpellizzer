Acanthococos emensis

Acanthococos emensis, commonly known as the Emelianov's spiny cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing during the warmer months.

Acanthococos emensis thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
