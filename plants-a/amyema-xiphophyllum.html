Amyema xiphophyllum

Amyema xiphophyllum, commonly known as the mountain cabbage tree, is a unique and rare plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, large, and often woolly leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or cream in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
