Argyreia osyrensis var. cinerea

Argyreia osyrensis var. cinerea, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
