Acorus griffithii

Acorus griffithii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweetflag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia.

It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive aromatic scent. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Acorus griffithii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.
