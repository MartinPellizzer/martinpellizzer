Aegilops tauschii convar. paleidenticulata

Aegilops tauschii convar. paleidenticulata, commonly known as the slender spikelet wild wheat, is a wild relative of wheat with significant genetic value for crop improvement.

It is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia, characterized by its slender, elongated spikelets and small, awned florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats and has a C3 photosynthetic pathway, adapting to temperate climates. It possesses a diploid genome (A genome) that is closely related to the A genome of common wheat, making it a valuable resource for genetic diversity.

Its potential for enhancing wheat resilience to biotic and abiotic stresses is a key focus in breeding programs.
