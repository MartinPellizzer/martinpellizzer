Appendicula pauciflora

Appendicula pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered appendicula, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
