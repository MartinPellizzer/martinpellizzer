Astragalus pseudoparnassi

Astragalus pseudoparnassi, commonly known as the Parnassian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus pseudoparnassi is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
