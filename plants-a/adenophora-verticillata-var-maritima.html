Adenophora verticillata var. maritima

Adenophora verticillata var. maritima, commonly known as sea adenophora, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and supporting coastal ecosystems.
