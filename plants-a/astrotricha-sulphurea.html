Astrotricha sulphurea

Astrotricha sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like structures called cypselae. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its striking color and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
