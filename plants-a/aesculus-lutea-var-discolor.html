Aesculus lutea var. discolor

Aesculus lutea var. discolor, commonly known as the yellow buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in upright clusters during late spring. The tree produces large, spiny capsules containing glossy, dark brown seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are typically serrated.

The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.
