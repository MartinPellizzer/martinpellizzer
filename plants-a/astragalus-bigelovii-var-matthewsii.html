Astragalus bigelovii var. matthewsii

Astragalus bigelovii var. matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthews' milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus.

This variety is noted for its ecological significance in supporting local wildlife and its potential medicinal properties.
