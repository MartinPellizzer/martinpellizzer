Aria × latifolia

Aria × latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf aria, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
