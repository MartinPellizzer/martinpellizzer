Aralia longipes

Aralia longipes, commonly known as the giant aralia, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, making it one of the largest leafy plants in the Araliaceae family. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that eventually develop into bright red berries. Its roots are thick and woody, often growing in dense clusters, which help anchor it in the rich, moist soil of its natural habitat.

Aralia longipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
