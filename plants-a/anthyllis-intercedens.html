Anthyllis intercedens

Anthyllis intercedens, commonly known as the red bupleurum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats.

Anthyllis intercedens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
