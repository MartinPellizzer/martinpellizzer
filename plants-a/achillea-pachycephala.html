Achillea pachycephala

Achillea pachycephala, commonly known as the woolly yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of high-altitude ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an attractive plant for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
