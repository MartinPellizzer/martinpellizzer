Aira uniaristata var. incerta

Aira uniaristata var. incerta, commonly known as the uncertain false oat, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
