Aglaia foveolata

Aglaia foveolata, commonly known as the warty aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves with a distinct warty texture on the underside. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, often clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Aglaia foveolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
