Alnus guatemalensis

Alnus guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan alder, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its serrated leaves and catkin-like flowers. The tree produces small, winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Alnus guatemalensis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
