Amaranthus praetermissus

Amaranthus praetermissus, commonly known as the pale amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its branched stems and lanceolate leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Amaranthus praetermissus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
