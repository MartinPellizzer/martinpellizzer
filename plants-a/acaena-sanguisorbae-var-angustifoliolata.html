Acaena sanguisorbae var. angustifoliolata

Acaena sanguisorbae var. angustifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burrseed, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, compound leaves with narrow, elliptical leaflets that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, red or orange flowers followed by distinctive, spiny burrs that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
