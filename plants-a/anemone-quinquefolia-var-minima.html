Anemone quinquefolia var. minima

Anemone quinquefolia var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf Canadian anemone, is a low-growing perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the winter, providing winter interest.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
