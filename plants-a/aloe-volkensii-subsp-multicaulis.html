Aloe volkensii subsp. multicaulis

Aloe volkensii subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the mountain aloe, is a succulent plant native to East Africa. It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
