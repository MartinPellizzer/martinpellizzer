Acer obtusatum f. malvaceum

Acer obtusatum f. malvaceum, commonly known as the Malva-leaved Maple, is a rare and unique variant of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble those of the Malva genus, giving it its distinctive name. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The leaves are dark green in summer and turn vibrant red or orange in the autumn, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
