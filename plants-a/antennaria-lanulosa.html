Antennaria lanulosa

Antennaria lanulosa, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, hairy stems that bear clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its characteristic woolly appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
