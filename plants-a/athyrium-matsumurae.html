Athyrium matsumurae

Athyrium matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce multiple fertile and sterile fronds.

Athyrium matsumurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
