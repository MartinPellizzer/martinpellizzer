Abies pinsapo f. argentea

Abies pinsapo f. argentea, commonly known as the silver firs of the Atlas Mountains, is a unique variant of the Atlas cedar.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and foliage, which gives it a striking appearance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of North Africa, particularly Morocco. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
