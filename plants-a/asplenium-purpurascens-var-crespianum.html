Asplenium purpurascens var. crespianum

Asplenium purpurascens var. crespianum, commonly known as the Crespian spleenwort, is a fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue on the underside. Its fronds are usually clustered and can reach up to 30 centimeters in length.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
