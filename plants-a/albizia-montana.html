Albizia montana

Albizia montana, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in the spring or early summer. Albizia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
