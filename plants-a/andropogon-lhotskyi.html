Andropogon lhotskyi

Andropogon lhotskyi, commonly known as the Lhotskyi grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon lhotskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and upright growth habit.
