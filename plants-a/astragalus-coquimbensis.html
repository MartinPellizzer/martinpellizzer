Astragalus coquimbensis

Astragalus coquimbensis, commonly known as the Atacama burro's bean, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Chile.

The plant is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. Astragalus coquimbensis plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to harsh environments.
