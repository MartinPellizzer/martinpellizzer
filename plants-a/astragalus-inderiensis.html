Astragalus inderiensis

Astragalus inderiensis, commonly known as the Inderien milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or violet flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and can be found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species in its native range.
