Actinea acaulis

Actinea acaulis, commonly known as the barren bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its lack of stems and leaves, instead featuring small, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. These bladders are used for nutrient absorption, allowing the plant to thrive in nutrient-poor water environments. Actinea acaulis is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its specialized morphology reflects its carnivorous adaptation, making it an interesting subject in the study of plant evolution.
