Avenula lodunensis

Avenula lodunensis, commonly known as the Nigerian oatgrass, is a grass species native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Avenula lodunensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
