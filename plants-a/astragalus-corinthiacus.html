Astragalus corinthiacus

Astragalus corinthiacus, commonly known as Corinthian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small leaflets.

Astragalus corinthiacus is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
