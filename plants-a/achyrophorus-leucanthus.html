Achyrophorus leucanthus

Achyrophorus leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered lichen, is a unique organism that belongs to the lichenized fungi group.

It forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium, allowing it to thrive in various environments. This lichen typically grows on rocks, trees, and soil, and is often found in temperate and boreal regions. Its white or pale-colored apothecia are distinctive features that aid in its identification.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for environmental health and its ecological importance in nutrient cycling.
