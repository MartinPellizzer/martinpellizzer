Achras zapota var. globosa

Achras zapota var. globosa, commonly known as the chicozapote or globe chicozapote, is a tropical tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree is notable for its edible, fleshy fruits that resemble small, round oranges, which are rich in nutrients. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
