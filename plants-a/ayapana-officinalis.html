Ayapana officinalis

Ayapana officinalis, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional medicine for treating fever and inflammation. Ayapana officinalis thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
