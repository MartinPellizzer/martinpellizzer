Aeschynanthus boschianus

Aeschynanthus boschianus, commonly known as the Bosch's slipper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms.

Aeschynanthus boschianus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
