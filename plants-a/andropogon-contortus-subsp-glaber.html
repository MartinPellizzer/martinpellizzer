Andropogon contortus subsp. glaber

Andropogon contortus subsp.

glaber, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in native plant communities.
