Allium acetabulum

Allium acetabulum, commonly known as the cup-leaved garlic, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces umbels of white to pale purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

Allium acetabulum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowers.
