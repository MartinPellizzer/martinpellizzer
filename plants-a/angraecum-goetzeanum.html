Angraecum goetzeanum

Angraecum goetzeanum, commonly known as the Goetze's star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue with dark purple markings. The plant produces fragrant, night-blooming flowers that attract specific nocturnal pollinators, such as moths. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its native habitat.

Angraecum goetzeanum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
