Artemisia kruhsiana subsp. alaskana

Artemisia kruhsiana subsp.

alaskana, commonly known as Alaska wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as tundra and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Its essential oils contribute to its distinctive scent and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
