Alternanthera canescens

Alternanthera canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which becomes more pronounced in direct sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It is often grown as an ornamental plant in water gardens and ponds due to its attractive appearance.

Alternanthera canescens prefers full sun to partial shade and thrives in moist, well-drained soil.
