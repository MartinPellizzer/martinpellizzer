Astragalus macronyx

Astragalus macronyx, commonly known as the broad-nosed milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small, hard seeds.

Astragalus macronyx is adapted to dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
