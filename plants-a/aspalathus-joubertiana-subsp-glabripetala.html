Aspalathus joubertiana subsp. glabripetala

Aspalathus joubertiana subsp.

glabripetala, commonly known as the Cape tea or sweet clover, is a small shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its aromatic leaves and historical use in traditional medicine, it holds cultural and horticultural significance in its native region.
