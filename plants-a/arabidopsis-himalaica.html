Arabidopsis himalaica

Arabidopsis himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan Arabidopsis, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. This species is characterized by its small, compact stature and features simple, alternate leaves. It produces small white or yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and it is often found growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ecological significance and genetic simplicity, it is also studied in plant biology research.
