Antirrhinum vulgare var. album

Antirrhinum vulgare var. album, commonly known as the white snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces bright yellow stamens that contrast with its white petals, making it visually striking. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in gardens and wildflower meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated for decorative purposes.
