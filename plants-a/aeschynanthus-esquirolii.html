Aeschynanthus esquirolii

Aeschynanthus esquirolii, commonly known as the flame vine, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red, resembling flames. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique flowering pattern and striking appearance make it a popular choice for gardeners and botanists alike.
