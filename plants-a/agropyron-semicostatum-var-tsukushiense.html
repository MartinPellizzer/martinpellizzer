Agropyron semicostatum var. tsukushiense

Agropyron semicostatum var. tsukushiense, commonly known as the Tsukushi oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
