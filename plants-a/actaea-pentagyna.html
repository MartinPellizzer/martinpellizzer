Actaea pentagyna

Actaea pentagyna, commonly known as the five-lobed bugbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its showy, white, umbellate flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with five distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and has a rhizomatous root system.

Actaea pentagyna is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
