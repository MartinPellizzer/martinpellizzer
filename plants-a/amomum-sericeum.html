Amomum sericeum

Amomum sericeum, commonly known as the silver cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cardamom.

Amomum sericeum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
