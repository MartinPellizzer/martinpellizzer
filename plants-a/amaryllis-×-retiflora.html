Amaryllis × retiflora

Amaryllis × retiflora, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking ornamental plant resulting from crossbreeding between different amaryllis species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant produces thick, fleshy leaves that are often used for propagation. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Amaryllis × retiflora is popular for its long-lasting blooms and ease of cultivation, making it a favorite among gardeners and florists alike.
