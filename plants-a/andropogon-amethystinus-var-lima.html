Andropogon amethystinus var. lima

Andropogon amethystinus var. lima, commonly known as the purple bluestem grass, is a variant of the bluestem grass family native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinted foliage and slender, grass-like blades that form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in full sun to partial shade and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for native plant gardens. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife and providing erosion control in natural landscapes.
