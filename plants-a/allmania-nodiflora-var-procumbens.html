Allmania nodiflora var. procumbens

Allmania nodiflora var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate allmania, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that typically thrives in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
