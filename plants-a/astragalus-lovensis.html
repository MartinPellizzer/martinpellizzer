Astragalus lovensis

Astragalus lovensis, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus lovensis is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high elevations.
