Aechmea kentii

Aechmea kentii, commonly known as the Kent's pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant's foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces vibrant red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. Aechmea kentii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its unique appearance and ability to grow without soil make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Morphology

Aechmea kentii has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and often have a waxy coating, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a bluish tint, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem with a smooth, dark green bark that grows upward from the base of the rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with white or pale pink petals, and they bloom in late winter to early spring, pollinated primarily by insects.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.
