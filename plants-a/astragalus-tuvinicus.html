Astragalus tuvinicus

Astragalus tuvinicus, commonly known as the Tuvinian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stem and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus tuvinicus is often found in steppe and rocky habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native regions.
