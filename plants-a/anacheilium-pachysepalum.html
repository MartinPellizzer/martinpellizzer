Anacheilium pachysepalum

Anacheilium pachysepalum, commonly known as the thick-sepaled orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features thick, fleshy sepals that give it its specific name, pachysepalum. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Anacheilium pachysepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
