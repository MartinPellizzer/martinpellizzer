Allium mongolicum var. kabaense

Allium mongolicum var. kabaense, commonly known as the Kaba onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are typically small to medium in size, with a mild, onion-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential culinary uses.
