Allium odorum

Allium odorum, commonly known as the garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, forming clusters at the top of the stems.

Allium odorum is valued for both its edible leaves and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
