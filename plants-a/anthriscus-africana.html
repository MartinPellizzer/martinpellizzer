Anthriscus africana

Anthriscus africana, commonly known as African parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in umbels, typical of the carrot family (Apiaceae). Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Anthriscus africana is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate foliage and mild scent.
