Asphodelus fuscatus

Asphodelus fuscatus, commonly known as the dark asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow or greenish flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a flowering period that occurs in spring or early summer.

Asphodelus fuscatus is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
