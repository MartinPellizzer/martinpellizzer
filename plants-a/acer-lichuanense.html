Acer lichuanense

Acer lichuanense, commonly known as the Lichuan maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red to orange samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
