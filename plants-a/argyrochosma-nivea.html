Argyrochosma nivea

Argyrochosma nivea, commonly known as the silverleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is a valuable species for studying plant resilience in harsh environments.
