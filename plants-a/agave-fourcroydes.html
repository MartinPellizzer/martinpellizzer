Agave fourcroydes

Agave fourcroydes, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces a tall, branched flowering stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Agave fourcroydes is also valued for its use in the production of tequila and other agave-based spirits.
