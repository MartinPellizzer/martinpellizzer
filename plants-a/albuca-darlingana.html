Albuca darlingana

Albuca darlingana, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.

Albuca darlingana is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
