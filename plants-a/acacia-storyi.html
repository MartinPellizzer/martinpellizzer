Acacia storyi

Acacia storyi, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Acacia storyi is adapted to saline and arid environments, making it a hardy species found in coastal dunes and sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
