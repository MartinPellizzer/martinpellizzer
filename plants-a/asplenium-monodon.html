Asplenium monodon

Asplenium monodon, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its unique, rosette-shaped fronds that resemble a bird's nest, hence its common name. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces new growth from the base, allowing it to spread gradually. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, with a distinctive, curled appearance.

Asplenium monodon is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
