Abies holophylla var. aspericorticea

Abies holophylla var. aspericorticea, commonly known as the rough-barked fir, is a variant of the holophylla fir native to the high-altitude regions of central and southern South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive rough, scaly bark that gives it its varietal name. The tree has conical-shaped foliage with dense, dark green needles that are often arranged in two rows. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in cloud forests.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in providing habitat for various endemic species.
