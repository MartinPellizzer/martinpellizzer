Asplenium adamsii

Asplenium adamsii, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically divided into narrow segments. This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and woodland floors. Asplenium adamsii has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
