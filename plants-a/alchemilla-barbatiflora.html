Alchemilla barbatiflora

Alchemilla barbatiflora, commonly known as the beard-flowered lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla barbatiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
