Allium schoenoprasum var. alpinum

Allium schoenoprasum var. alpinum, commonly known as alpine chives, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valued culinary and medicinal plant.

It is also noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
