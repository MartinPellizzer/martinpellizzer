Anaphalis lorentzii

Anaphalis lorentzii, commonly known as the silverleaf frostflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that resembles a fuzzy coating. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the early spring, typically in shades of pink or white. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens for its ornamental value.
