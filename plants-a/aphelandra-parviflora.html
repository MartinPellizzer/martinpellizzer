Aphelandra parviflora

Aphelandra parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered torch plant, is a tropical species native to the Caribbean and Central America.

Its leaves are elongated, leathery, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aphelandra parviflora thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
