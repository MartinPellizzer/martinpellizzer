Asterocephalus uralensis

Asterocephalus uralensis, commonly known as the Ural aster, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and purple.

Asterocephalus uralensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
