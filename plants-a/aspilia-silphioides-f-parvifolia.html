Aspilia silphioides f. parvifolia

Aspilia silphioides f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
