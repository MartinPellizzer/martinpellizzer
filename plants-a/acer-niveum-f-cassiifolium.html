Acer niveum f. cassiifolium

Acer niveum f. cassiifolium, commonly known as the white poplar variegated form, is a rare and unique variant of the white poplar tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to cream-colored leaves with a striking variegation pattern. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, temperate climates. Its foliage is often more vibrant and visually appealing than that of the standard white poplar.

This form is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
