Astragalus argophyllus var. panguicensis

Astragalus argophyllus var. panguicensis, commonly known as the Panguicense milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a potential source of medicinal compounds.
