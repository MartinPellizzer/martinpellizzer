Astragalus chagyabensis

Astragalus chagyabensis, commonly known as the Chagyab Valley milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Qinghai-Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. This species is adapted to high-altitude environments, exhibiting features such as a deep root system and hairy foliage to withstand cold and arid conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
