Adelodypsis boiviniana

Adelodypsis boiviniana, commonly known as the Boivin's ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a subtle floral touch to its appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique adaptations make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
