Alstroemeria grandifolia

Alstroemeria grandifolia, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Alstroemeria grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
