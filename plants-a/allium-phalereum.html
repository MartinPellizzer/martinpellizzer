Allium phalereum

Allium phalereum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant's underground bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor compared to other onions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Allium phalereum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and culinary uses.
