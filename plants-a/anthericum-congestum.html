Anthericum congestum

Anthericum congestum, commonly known as the clustered adder's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often mottled with white or silver veins. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster on the spadix.

Anthericum congestum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks.
