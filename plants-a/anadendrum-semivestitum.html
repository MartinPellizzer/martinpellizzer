Anadendrum semivestitum

Anadendrum semivestitum, commonly known as the Texas buckeye, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny, green fruits that turn brown as they mature. The seeds are enclosed in a hard, woody capsule, and the plant is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in open woods and along stream banks, showcasing its ecological significance in native habitats.
