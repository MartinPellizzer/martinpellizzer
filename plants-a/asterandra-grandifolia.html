Asterandra grandifolia

Asterandra grandifolia, commonly known as the largeflower asterandra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical in shape, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, spherical heads, making it a visually striking plant in its natural habitat.
