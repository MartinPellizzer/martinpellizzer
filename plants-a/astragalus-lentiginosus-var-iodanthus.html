Astragalus lentiginosus var. iodanthus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. iodanthus, commonly known as the iodine-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
