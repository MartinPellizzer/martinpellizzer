Acalypha gemina var. brevibracteata

Acalypha gemina var. brevibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bract euphorbia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to green, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
