Aster longifolius

Aster longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, often hairy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 feet. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue and a rough texture.

The showy flower heads, which appear in late summer to early fall, consist of purple to pink ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets.
