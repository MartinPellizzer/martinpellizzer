Agrostis bromoides

Agrostis bromoides, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray leaves and tufted inflorescence, which consists of small spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and wetlands. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils.

Agrostis bromoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
