Acrocomia sclerocarpa

Acrocomia sclerocarpa, commonly known as the African palm or the buriti palm, is a tall, slender palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and produces large, clustered inflorescences. The plant's fruit is a large, fibrous drupe that contains a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and has uses in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
