Alloteropsis cimicina

Alloteropsis cimicina, commonly known as the coastal grass or sea grass, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline and wind-swept conditions, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion highlights its ecological significance.
