Avena sativa var. hildebrandtii

Avena sativa var. hildebrandtii, commonly known as Hildebrandt's oat, is a variant of the common oat that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Basin. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, each bearing two to three florets.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and is often cultivated for forage or as a ornamental plant.
