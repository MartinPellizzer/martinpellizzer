Astragalus bhotanensis var. montigenus

Astragalus bhotanensis var. montigenus, commonly known as the Himalayan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, elliptical leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh mountainous habitats.
