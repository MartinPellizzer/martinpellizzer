Alexitoxicon maeoticum

Alexitoxicon maeoticum, commonly known as the Black Sea poison ivy.

This plant is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by clusters of red, berry-like fruits. The plant contains toxic compounds in its sap, which can cause skin irritation in humans.

It is native to the coastal regions of the Black Sea and thrives in moist, sandy soils.
