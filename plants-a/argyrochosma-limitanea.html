Argyrochosma limitanea

Argyrochosma limitanea, commonly known as the silverleaf saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, reflective hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
