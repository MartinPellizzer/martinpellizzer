Artemisia chiarugii

Artemisia chiarugii, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to China and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer, typically in clustered inflorescences. Artemisia chiarugii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It is valued in traditional medicine and has been used for its purported health benefits.
