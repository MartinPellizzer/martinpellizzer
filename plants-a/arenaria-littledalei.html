Arenaria littledalei

Arenaria littledalei, commonly known as the Littledale's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils, often found in rocky outcrops and coastal dunes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually succulent, helping it conserve water in its harsh environment.

Arenaria littledalei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
