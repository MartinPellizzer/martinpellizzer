Anthyllis hermanniae subsp. sicula

Anthyllis hermanniae subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian carline thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by long, cylindrical pods. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to the dry, rocky environments it inhabits.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local insect populations.
