Anthyllis gandogeri

Anthyllis gandogeri, commonly known as the Maltese tawny vetch, is a flowering plant native to the island of Malta.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
