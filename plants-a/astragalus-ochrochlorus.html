Astragalus ochrochlorus

Astragalus ochrochlorus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

Astragalus ochrochlorus plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is an important plant for pollinators.
