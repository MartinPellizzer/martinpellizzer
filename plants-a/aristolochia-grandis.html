Aristolochia grandis

Aristolochia grandis, commonly known as the large arrowhead vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe or a trumpet. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its vines can grow quite long, often climbing or trailing along supports.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
