Astephanus pubescens

Astephanus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy stephanus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astephanus pubescens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
