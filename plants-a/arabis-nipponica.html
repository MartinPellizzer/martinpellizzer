Arabis nipponica

Arabis nipponica, commonly known as Japanese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by triangular seed pods.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
