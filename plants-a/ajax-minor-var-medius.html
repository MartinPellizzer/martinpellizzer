Ajax minor var. medius

Ajax minor var. medius, commonly known as the lesser ajax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
