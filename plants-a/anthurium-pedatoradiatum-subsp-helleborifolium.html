Anthurium pedatoradiatum subsp. helleborifolium

Anthurium pedatoradiatum subsp.

helleborifolium, commonly known as the helleborifolium anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a dark green to almost black coloration.

The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in both home and greenhouse settings.
