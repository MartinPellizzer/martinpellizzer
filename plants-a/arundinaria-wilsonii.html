Arundinaria wilsonii

Arundinaria wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
