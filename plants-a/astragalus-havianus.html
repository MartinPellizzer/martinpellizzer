Astragalus havianus

Astragalus havianus, commonly known as the Havian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus havianus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
