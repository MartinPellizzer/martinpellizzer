Adesmia resinosa

Adesmia resinosa, commonly known as the resinous legume, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its resinous secretions, which are exuded from its bark and leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces elongated, woody pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Adesmia resinosa is adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
