Arabidopsis multijuga

Arabidopsis multijuga, commonly known as the two-lobed rockcress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant has a compact rosette of basal leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a raceme, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Arabidopsis multijuga is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments due to its succulent leaves and deep root system.
