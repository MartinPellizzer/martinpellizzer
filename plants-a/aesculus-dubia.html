Aesculus dubia

Aesculus dubia, commonly known as the Mexican horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The tree produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in upright clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing several chestnut-like seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
