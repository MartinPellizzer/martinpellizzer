Anthurium tsamajainii

Anthurium tsamajainii, commonly known as the Tsamajaini anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the humid lowland forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant typically features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface, and its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange. It thrives in warm, moist environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium tsamajainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
