Anthemis baumgarteniana

Anthemis baumgarteniana, commonly known as the Sierra madre daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
