Aconitum jacquinianum

Aconitum jacquinianum, commonly known as the Jacquin's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hair.

Aconitum jacquinianum is notable for containing toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both ecological and cautionary interest.
