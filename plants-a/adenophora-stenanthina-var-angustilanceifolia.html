Adenophora stenanthina var. angustilanceifolia

Adenophora stenanthina var. angustilanceifolia, commonly known as narrow-lanceleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticultural gardens.
