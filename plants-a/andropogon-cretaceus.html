Andropogon cretaceus

Andropogon cretaceus, commonly known as sand bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets arranged in panicles. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or well-drained soils, making it a common component of native grasslands. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.

Andropogon cretaceus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes planted in restoration projects.
