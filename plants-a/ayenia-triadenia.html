Ayenia triadenia

Ayenia triadenia, commonly known as the three-toothed geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed with three to five pointed lobes, giving it its common name.

Ayenia triadenia is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
