Anvillea garcinii subsp. radiata

Anvillea garcinii subsp.

radiata, commonly known as the radiate anvillea, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant features a woody stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy in texture. Its inflorescence consists of a central yellow flower surrounded by several smaller, petal-like bracts.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
