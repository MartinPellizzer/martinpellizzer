Amorphophallus synandrifer

Amorphophallus synandrifer, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of many arum species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in some regions.
