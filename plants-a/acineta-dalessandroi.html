Acineta dalessandroi

Acineta dalessandroi, commonly known as the Brazilian star-of-Bahia, is a rare flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial with delicate, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and features hairy stems and leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to the humid tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
