Agathophora alopecuroides

Agathophora alopecuroides, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its structure includes a basal rosette of leaves with a central stem that bears the inflorescence.

Agathophora alopecuroides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
