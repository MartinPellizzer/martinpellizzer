Acanthodium spathulare

Acanthodium spathulare, commonly known as the broad-leafed acanthodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lanceolate leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
