Arisaema atrolinguum

Arisaema atrolinguum, commonly known as the black-tongued arisaema, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green and have a rough texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate spadices, a common trait in the Araceae family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
