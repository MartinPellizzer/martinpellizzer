Astragalus saurinus

Astragalus saurinus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to reddish-brown. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Astragalus saurinus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitats.
