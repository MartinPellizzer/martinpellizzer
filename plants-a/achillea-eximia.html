Achillea eximia

Achillea eximia, commonly known as the exclamation weed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from white to pale yellow.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
