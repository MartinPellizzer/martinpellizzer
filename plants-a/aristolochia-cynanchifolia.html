Aristolochia cynanchifolia

Aristolochia cynanchifolia, commonly known as the Chinese birthwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are often a deep purple or brownish color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition, which includes various alkaloids. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses in medicine, it is sometimes cultivated, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
