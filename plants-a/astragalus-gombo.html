Astragalus gombo

Astragalus gombo, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including Mongolia and parts of China. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale purple flowers in clusters. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Astragalus gombo is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
