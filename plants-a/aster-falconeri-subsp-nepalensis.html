Aster falconeri subsp. nepalensis

Aster falconeri subsp.

nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepalese aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in rocky habitats across its native range.
