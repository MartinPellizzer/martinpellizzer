Agropyron × wallii

Agropyron × wallii, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species resulting from the cross between Agropyron cristatum and Agropyron elongatum.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is native to the northern Great Plains of North America and thrives in temperate climates. Agropyron × wallii is valued for its high forage quality and its ability to form dense, drought-resistant stands.

It is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
