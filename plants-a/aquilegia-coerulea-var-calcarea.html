Aquilegia coerulea var. calcarea

Aquilegia coerulea var. calcarea, commonly known as the Alpine blue columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny locations.
