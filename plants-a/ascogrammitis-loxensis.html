Ascogrammitis loxensis

Ascogrammitis loxensis, commonly known as the Loxense water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it can receive adequate sunlight. Its rhizomes form a network that anchors it to substrates and facilitates nutrient absorption.

The spore-producing structures, called sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
