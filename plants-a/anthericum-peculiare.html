Anthericum peculiare

Anthericum peculiare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clumps.

Anthericum peculiare is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
