Aristida adscensionis var. festucoides

Aristida adscensionis var. festucoides, commonly known as the desert threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in harsh environments.
