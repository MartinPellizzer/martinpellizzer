Aster acuminatus var. magdalenensis

Aster acuminatus var. magdalenensis, commonly known as the Magdalene aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
