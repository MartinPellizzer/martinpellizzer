Aster chrysothamnus

Aster chrysothamnus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and silver-green foliage, which help it thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open landscapes. It is often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky areas, where it contributes to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
