Arthroclianthus maximus

Arthroclianthus maximus, commonly known as the giant arthroclianthus, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to two meters in length, which help it retain moisture in humid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the rainy season. Its unique structure includes a thick, fibrous stem that supports its massive foliage.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
