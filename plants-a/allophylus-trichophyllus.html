Allophylus trichophyllus

Allophylus trichophyllus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved alpine holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside of the leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Allophylus trichophyllus has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
