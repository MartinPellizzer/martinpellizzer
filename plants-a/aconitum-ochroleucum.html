Aconitum ochroleucum

Aconitum ochroleucum, commonly known as the white aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive, waxy texture and a deep green color.

Aconitum ochroleucum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
