Alsophila decussata

Alsophila decussata, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that are often used in horticulture. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Alsophila decussata plays an important role in tropical ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
