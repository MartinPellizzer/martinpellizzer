Anachortus divaricatus subsp. macrantherus

Anachortus divaricatus subsp.

macrantherus, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, red-orange berries that persist through winter.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The subspecies macrantherus is distinguished by its larger, more showy fruits compared to other subspecies of Anachortus.
