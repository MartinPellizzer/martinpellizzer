Athyrium intermixtum

Athyrium intermixtum, commonly known as the mixed fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that exhibit a striking mix of light and dark green hues. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are often covered in fine, hairy structures, and it produces small, spore-bearing clusters called sori.

The botanical features of Athyrium intermixtum make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
