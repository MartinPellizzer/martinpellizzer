Amygdalus taurica

Amygdalus taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that are often used in local cuisines. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and rough, bark-like fruiting bodies. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Amygdalus taurica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
