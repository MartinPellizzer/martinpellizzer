Anacheilium lividum

Anacheilium lividum, commonly known as the blue-stemmed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, bluish-green stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Anacheilium lividum thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
