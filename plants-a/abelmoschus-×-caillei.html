Abelmoschus × caillei

Abelmoschus × caillei, commonly known as the African okra, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, ridged stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

Abelmoschus × caillei is cultivated not only for its ornamental value but also for its edible pods, which are used in various culinary traditions.
