Allium ampeloprasum var. babingtonii

Allium ampeloprasum var. babingtonii, commonly known as the Giant Garlic or Persian Shallot, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, crowned with clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces large, elongated bulbs with a strong garlic scent, which are used in culinary applications. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its edible bulbs.
