Asparagopsis passerinoides

Asparagopsis passerinoides, commonly known as the pink coralplant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has strap-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Asparagopsis passerinoides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
