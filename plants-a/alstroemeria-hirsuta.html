Alstroemeria hirsuta

Alstroemeria hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its stems are often covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name.

Alstroemeria hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
