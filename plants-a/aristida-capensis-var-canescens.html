Aristida capensis var. canescens

Aristida capensis var. canescens, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually pale green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, slightly fuzzy appearance.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
