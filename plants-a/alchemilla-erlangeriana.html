Alchemilla erlangeriana

Alchemilla erlangeriana, commonly known as the Erlanger's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Alchemilla erlangeriana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
