Aster divaricatus var. deltoideus

Aster divaricatus var. deltoideus, commonly known as the deltoid aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems that are typically hairy, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to lavender, blooming from late summer to early autumn. Its flowers are composed of a central disc surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This variety is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
