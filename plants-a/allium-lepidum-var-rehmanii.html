Allium lepidum var. rehmanii

Allium lepidum var. rehmanii, commonly known as the Kashmiri garlic, is a botanical variant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its small, white, onion-like bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in alpine meadows at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice, valued for their pungent aroma and flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Allium genus, which includes a wide variety of garlic and onion species.
