Anathallis caroli

Anathallis caroli, commonly known as the Caroli's spider orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and its unique inflorescence, which consists of a single, showy flower resembling a spider. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects, aiding in pollination by attracting specific species of flies.

Anathallis caroli is a protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
