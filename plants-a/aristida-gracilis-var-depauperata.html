Aristida gracilis var. depauperata

Aristida gracilis var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, panicle-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It has a low-growing habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

The leaves are usually bluish-green and have a fine texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
