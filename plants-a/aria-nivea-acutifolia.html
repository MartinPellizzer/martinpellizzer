Aria nivea acutifolia

Aria nivea acutifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety leaves that are narrowly elliptical and sharply pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, Aria nivea acutifolia is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid climates.
