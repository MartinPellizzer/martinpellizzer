Ancistrochloa fauriei

Ancistrochloa fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a panicle, which produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Ancistrochloa fauriei is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing lawns.
