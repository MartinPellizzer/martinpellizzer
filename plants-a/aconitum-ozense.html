Aconitum ozense

Aconitum ozense, commonly known as the Tibetan monk's hood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that give them a distinctive appearance.

Aconitum ozense contains potent alkaloids, making it both a valuable ornamental plant and a highly toxic species.
