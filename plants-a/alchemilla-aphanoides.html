Alchemilla aphanoides

Alchemilla aphanoides, commonly known as narrowleaf lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive silver-gray underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in rounded inflorescences.

Alchemilla aphanoides is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
