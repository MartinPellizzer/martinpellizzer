Arundinaria chino f. elegantissima

Arundinaria chino f. elegantissima, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a unique variant of the Chinese bamboo species.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching culms that exhibit a golden hue, particularly in the sunlit areas. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by wind rather than insects. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are a deep green and contribute to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is well-suited to temperate climates with well-drained soil.
