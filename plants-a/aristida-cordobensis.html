Aristida cordobensis

Aristida cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Aristida cordobensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
