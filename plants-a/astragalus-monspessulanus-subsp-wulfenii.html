Astragalus monspessulanus subsp. wulfenii

Astragalus monspessulanus subsp.

wulfenii, commonly known as the Mediterranean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded lobes.

The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
