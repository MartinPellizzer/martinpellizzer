Anemone narcissiflora var. subuniflora

Anemone narcissiflora var. subuniflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, single flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
