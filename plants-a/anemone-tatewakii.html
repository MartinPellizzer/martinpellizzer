Anemone tatewakii

Anemone tatewakii, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.
