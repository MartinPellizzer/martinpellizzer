Astragalus spectabilis

Astragalus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.

Astragalus spectabilis is often used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
