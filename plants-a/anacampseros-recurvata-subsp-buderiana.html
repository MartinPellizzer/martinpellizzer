Anacampseros recurvata subsp. buderiana

Anacampseros recurvata subsp.

buderiana, commonly known as the Buder's stonecress, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance make it a desirable plant for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
