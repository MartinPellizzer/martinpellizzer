Atropa glandulosa

Atropa glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular belladonna, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and striking bell-shaped purple flowers. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing tropane alkaloids. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a potential invasive species in some areas.
