Acacia lanigera var. gracilipes

Acacia lanigera var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its finely branched structure and small, yellowish-green phyllodes that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spherical or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
