Armeria alliacea subsp. alliacea

Armeria alliacea subsp.

alliacea, commonly known as the garlic pink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces basal rosettes of linear, grass-like leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
