Aristida vagans

Aristida vagans, commonly known as the wandering grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, grass-like flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Aristida vagans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
