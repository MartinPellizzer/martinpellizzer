Acacia lespedleyi

Acacia lespedleyi, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and greyish-white, often peeling in thin layers.

Acacia lespedleyi is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.
