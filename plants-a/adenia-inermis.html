Adenia inermis

Adenia inermis, commonly known as the smooth adenia, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Adenia inermis is often found in rainforests and along stream banks, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
