Actinostemma lobatum var. genuinum

Actinostemma lobatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the northern mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are erect with a somewhat woody base.
