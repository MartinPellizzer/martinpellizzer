Amblytes caerulea

Amblytes caerulea, commonly known as the blue-eyed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue petals and yellow center, which resemble a daisy. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, fluffy seed heads that are adapted for wind pollination.

Amblytes caerulea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
