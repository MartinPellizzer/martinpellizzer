Alchornea castaneifolia var. puberula

Alchornea castaneifolia var. puberula, commonly known as the silky alchornea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves that are pubescent, giving the plant a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
