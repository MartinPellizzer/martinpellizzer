Artocarpus eberhardtii var. poilanei

Artocarpus eberhardtii var. poilanei, commonly known as the Poliane breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruits. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, is edible and has a texture similar to breadfruit, though it is less commonly cultivated.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and potential as a food source in its native habitat.
