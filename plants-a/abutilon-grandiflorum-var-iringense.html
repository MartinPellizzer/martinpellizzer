Abutilon grandiflorum var. iringense

Abutilon grandiflorum var. iringense, commonly known as the Iringa abutilon, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in forest margins and along stream banks, contributing to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
