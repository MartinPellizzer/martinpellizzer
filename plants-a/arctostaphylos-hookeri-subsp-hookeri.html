Arctostaphylos hookeri subsp. hookeri

Arctostaphylos hookeri subsp.

hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, leathery berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific leaf and flower morphology compared to other manzanita species.
