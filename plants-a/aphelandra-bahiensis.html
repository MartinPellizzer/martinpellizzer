Aphelandra bahiensis

Aphelandra bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian torch plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces vibrant, spathiflorous inflorescences with showy, yellow-orange bracts that enclose small white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Aphelandra bahiensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic appearance and air-purifying qualities.
