Astragalus onobrychis subsp. purpureus

Astragalus onobrychis subsp.

purpureus, commonly known as purple sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, purple or pink flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. Purple sainfoin is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used historically in agriculture and traditional medicine.
