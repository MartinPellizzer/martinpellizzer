Asplenium lineolatum

Asplenium lineolatum, commonly known as the narrow-veined spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves with distinct midribs and fine, parallel veins. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in shaded, humid environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance in shaded garden settings.

Asplenium lineolatum prefers well-drained, acidic soil and requires consistent moisture to thrive.
