Amellus carolinianus

Amellus carolinianus, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Amellus carolinianus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
