Aneilema sinicum f. violaceum

Aneilema sinicum f. violaceum, commonly known as the purple-flowered aneilema, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple spatulate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
