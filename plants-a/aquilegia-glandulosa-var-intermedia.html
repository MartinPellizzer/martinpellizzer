Aquilegia glandulosa var. intermedia

Aquilegia glandulosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the glandular columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with lobes that are often glandular-dotted, a distinguishing characteristic of the species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
