Achillea ochroleuca var. ochroleuca

Achillea ochroleuca var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow archangel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of slender, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the summer months. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains compounds responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shady gardens.

Its botanical structure supports its use in traditional herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant.
