Ampacus obtusifolia

Ampacus obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved Ampacus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically broad at the base and tapering towards the tip. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
