Andropogon paranjpyeanus

Andropogon paranjpyeanus, commonly known as the Paranjpye grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in degraded soils.
