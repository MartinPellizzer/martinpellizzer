Allium bahri

Allium bahri, commonly known as the Bahri garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Allium bahri is valued for its aromatic properties and adaptability to arid environments.
