Anadendrum medium

Anadendrum medium, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that becomes more branched with age. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Anadendrum medium is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
