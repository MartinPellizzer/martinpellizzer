Astragalus ophiocarpus

Astragalus ophiocarpus, commonly known as the redstem milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, inflated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Astragalus ophiocarpus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
