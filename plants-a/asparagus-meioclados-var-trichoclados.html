Asparagus meioclados var. trichoclados

Asparagus meioclados var. trichoclados, commonly known as the hairy asparagus, is a variant of the asparagus plant characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It is native to South Africa and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, scale-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. It produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, edible berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
