Achillea aspleniifolia

Achillea aspleniifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, pale yellow to white flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Achillea aspleniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
