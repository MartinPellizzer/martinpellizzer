Avicularia vulgaris

Avicularia vulgaris, commonly known as the common sea anemone, is a marine cnidarian belonging to the order Actinioidea.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent polyps surrounded by a ring of tentacles used for capturing prey. The organism lacks a true digestive system and relies on extracellular digestion within a gastrovascular cavity. It typically inhabits shallow coastal waters and is often found attached to rocks or other substrates.

Avicularia vulgaris plays a role in marine ecosystems as both predator and prey, contributing to the balance of benthic communities.
