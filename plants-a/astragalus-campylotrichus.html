Astragalus campylotrichus

Astragalus campylotrichus, commonly known as the hooked milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus campylotrichus is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
