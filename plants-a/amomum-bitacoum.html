Amomum bitacoum

Amomum bitacoum, commonly known as the fragrant cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is cultivated primarily for its aromatic seed pods, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its rhizomes also contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Amomum bitacoum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
