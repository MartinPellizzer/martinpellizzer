Alsophila dryopteroides

Alsophila dryopteroides, commonly known as the tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to various regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer that resembles bark. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Alsophila dryopteroides plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various small organisms.
