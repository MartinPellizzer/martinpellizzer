Acropera purpurea

Acropera purpurea, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Acropera purpurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
