Androcymbium striatum

Androcymbium striatum, commonly known as the striped orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers with distinct striations. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
