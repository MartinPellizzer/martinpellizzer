Astracantha mesoleia

Astracantha mesoleia, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
