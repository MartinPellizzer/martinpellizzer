Artemisia selengensis f. subintegra

Artemisia selengensis f. subintegra, commonly known as the Selengian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and aromatic, pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, dry fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its medicinal properties and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental and traditional uses.
