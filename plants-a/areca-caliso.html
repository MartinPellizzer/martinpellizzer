Areca caliso

Areca caliso, commonly known as the red areca palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible fruits containing black seeds. Areca caliso thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and culturally important species in its native region.
