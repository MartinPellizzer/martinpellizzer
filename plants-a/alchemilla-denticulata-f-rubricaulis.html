Alchemilla denticulata f. rubricaulis

Alchemilla denticulata f. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed lady's mantle, is a cultivar of the alchemilla genus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking reddish stems and serrated, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
