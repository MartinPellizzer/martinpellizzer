Acacia coriaria

Acacia coriaria, commonly known as the Armenian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

The wood of Acacia coriaria is hard and durable, historically used for various practical purposes.
