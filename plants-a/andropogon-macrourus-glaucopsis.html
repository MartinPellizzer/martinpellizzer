Andropogon macrourus glaucopsis

Andropogon macrourus glaucopsis, commonly known as the blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are often tinged with a blue or purplish hue, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural applications.
