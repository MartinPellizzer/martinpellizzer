Astrocaryum princeps var. flavum

Astrocaryum princeps var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow macaw palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The fruit of this plant is typically yellow or orange when ripe and is often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in certain regions.
