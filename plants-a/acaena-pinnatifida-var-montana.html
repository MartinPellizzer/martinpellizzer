Acaena pinnatifida var. montana

Acaena pinnatifida var. montana, commonly known as mountain needlefruit, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is commonly found in mountainous regions.

The plant has a creeping growth habit and can form dense thickets, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
