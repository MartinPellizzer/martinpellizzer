Ammannia mauritiana

Ammannia mauritiana, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often turn red or purple as the plant matures. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

This species is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions.
