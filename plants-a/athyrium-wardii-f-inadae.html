Athyrium wardii f. inadae

Athyrium wardii f. inadae, commonly known as the Inada's male fern, is a fern species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Athyriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged along the margins of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
