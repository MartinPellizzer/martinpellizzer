Aralia handsworthensis

Aralia handsworthensis, commonly known as the handsworthian aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive umbel-like inflorescence that bears small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
