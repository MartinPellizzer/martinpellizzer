Alyssum montanum subsp. fastigiatum

Alyssum montanum subsp.

fastigiatum, commonly known as the mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant has narrow, upright leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. Alyssum montanum subsp.

fastigiatum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
