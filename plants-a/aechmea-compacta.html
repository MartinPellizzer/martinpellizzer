Aechmea compacta

Aechmea compacta, commonly known as the compact bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a rosette of stiff, dark green leaves that form a compact, cup-shaped structure at the center. The leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, white trichomes, which help the plant collect and store rainwater. This species produces vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette, adding dramatic visual interest.

Its unique structure and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments make it a popular choice for indoor and tropical gardens.

Morphology

Aechmea compacta has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, dark green with silver markings, arranged in a tight spiral at the base, and can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The plant lacks a true stem, but it has a short, fibrous stem covered in a dense, velvety layer of moss-like material. Its inflorescence is a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small white to pink flowers that bloom in the summer, pollinated by insects. The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by birds and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the air, typical of its epiphytic growth habit.
