Argyrolobium macrophyllum var. mendesii

Argyrolobium macrophyllum var. mendesii, commonly known as the yellow burr vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
