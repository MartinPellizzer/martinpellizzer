Arundo diclina

Arundo diclina, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, bamboo-like stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant is often found growing near water sources such as rivers, lakes, and wetlands. It can reach heights of up to 6 meters and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Botanically, it is classified in the Poaceae family, which includes other grasses.
