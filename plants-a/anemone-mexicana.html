Anemone mexicana

Anemone mexicana, commonly known as the mouse-ear cicutaria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are white with a yellow center and bloom in early spring.

Anemone mexicana is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of calcareous habitats.
