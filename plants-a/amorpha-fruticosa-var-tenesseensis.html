Amorpha fruticosa var. tenesseensis

Amorpha fruticosa var. tenesseensis, commonly known as Tennessee false indigo, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen make it a useful plant in ecological and landscaping contexts.
