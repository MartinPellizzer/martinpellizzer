Aloe subalbicans

Aloe subalbicans, commonly known as the white aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically green with a white or pale blue tint. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-orange flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Aloe subalbicans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
