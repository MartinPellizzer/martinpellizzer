Aster undulatus var. diversifolius

Aster undulatus var. diversifolius, commonly known as the wavy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape, contributing to its varietal name "diversifolius.". Its showy, blue to purple flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
