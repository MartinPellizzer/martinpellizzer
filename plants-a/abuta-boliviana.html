Abuta boliviana

Abuta boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian abuta, is a perennial shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in rocky or arid environments. Abuta boliviana is noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

Its resilience to harsh climates makes it an important component of the Andean ecosystem.
