Aspalathus ambiguus

Aspalathus ambiguus, commonly known as African red bush tea, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its most notable feature is the small, reddish-brown pods that contain the seeds.

Aspalathus ambiguus is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used to make a unique type of herbal tea.
