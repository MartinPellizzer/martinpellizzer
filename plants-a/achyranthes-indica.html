Achyranthes indica

Achyranthes indica, commonly known as Indian spinach or kachri, is a herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oblong, three-angled berries containing seeds. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

The plant is valued for its edible leaves and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The flowers are small, clustered in spikes, with pale green to white petals, blooming from June to September, and are pollinated by wind. The roots are fibrous and extensive, helping to stabilize the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.

Native Distribution

Achyranthes indica is primarily native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia.
