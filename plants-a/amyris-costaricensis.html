Amyris costaricensis

Amyris costaricensis, commonly known as the Guanacaste tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Amyris costaricensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
