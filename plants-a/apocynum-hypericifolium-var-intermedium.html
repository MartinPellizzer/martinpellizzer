Apocynum hypericifolium var. intermedium

Apocynum hypericifolium var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by capsule fruits containing toxic, ribbed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is important to handle the plant with care.
