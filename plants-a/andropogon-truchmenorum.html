Andropogon truchmenorum

Andropogon truchmenorum, commonly known as the tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often rolled at the edges. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of tallgrass prairie ecosystems. Its flowering spikes appear in late summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon truchmenorum plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
