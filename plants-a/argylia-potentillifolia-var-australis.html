Argylia potentillifolia var. australis

Argylia potentillifolia var. australis, commonly known as the Southern Argylia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.
