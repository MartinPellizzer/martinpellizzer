Acacia simmonsiana

Acacia simmonsiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and narrow, bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia simmonsiana is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

It is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of browse for wildlife.
