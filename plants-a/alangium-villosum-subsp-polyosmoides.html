Alangium villosum subsp. polyosmoides

Alangium villosum subsp.

polyosmoides, commonly known as the hairy alangium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture on both sides. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
