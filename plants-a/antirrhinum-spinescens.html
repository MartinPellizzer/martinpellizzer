Antirrhinum spinescens

Antirrhinum spinescens, commonly known as the spiked snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces striking, two-lipped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Antirrhinum spinescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
