Aspleniopsis asplenioides

Aspleniopsis asplenioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for spore production. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Aspleniopsis asplenioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
