Acacia longissima

Acacia longissima, commonly known as the longleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia longissima is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a slow growth rate.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
