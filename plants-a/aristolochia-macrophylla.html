Aristolochia macrophylla

Aristolochia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its vines can grow quite long, sometimes reaching several meters in length, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
