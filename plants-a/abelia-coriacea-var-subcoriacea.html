Abelia coriacea var. subcoriacea

Abelia coriacea var. subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, rough texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping.
