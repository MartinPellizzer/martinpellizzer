Antirrhinum barrelieri var. piliferum

Antirrhinum barrelieri var. piliferum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered snapdragon, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
