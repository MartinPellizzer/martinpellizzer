Anemone narcissiflora subsp. europaea

Anemone narcissiflora subsp.

europaea, commonly known as the European anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided, with lobes that are often toothed or notched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
