Aster novi-belgii var. rosaceus

Aster novi-belgii var. rosaceus, commonly known as the Rosaceus New Belgium Aster, is a compact perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to pink petals and a yellow center, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
