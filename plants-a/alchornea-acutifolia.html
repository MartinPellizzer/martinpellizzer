Alchornea acutifolia

Alchornea acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved alchornea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically pubescent and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Alchornea acutifolia is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
