Aristolochia rechingeriana

Aristolochia rechingeriana, commonly known as the Turk's Cap or Turk's Cap Aristolochia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-shaped flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
