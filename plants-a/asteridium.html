Asteridium

Asteridium, commonly known as the "star flower," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of numerous small florets arranged in a radiating pattern. The plant typically features alternate leaves that are lance-shaped or ovate, with hairy surfaces. Asteridium species are often found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are commonly cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral displays.
