Astragalus ceramicus var. longifolius

Astragalus ceramicus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and pinnate leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
