Alstroemeria nana

Alstroemeria nana, commonly known as the dwarf alstroemeria, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features upright, slender stems with clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, yellow, and white. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a versatile addition to both ornamental and cultivated landscapes.
