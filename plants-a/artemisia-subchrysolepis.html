Artemisia subchrysolepis

Artemisia subchrysolepis, commonly known as the golden leaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in compact clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
