Asparagus monophyllus

Asparagus monophyllus, commonly known as the single-leaf asparagus, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, single, leathery leaf that persists throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by edible, needle-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
