Acer japonicum f. villosum

Acer japonicum f. villosum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with hairy leaves, is a variant of the Japanese maple species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely hairy foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically features elegant, cascading branches and produces small, red or yellow flowers in early spring. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the autumn, making it a popular ornamental tree.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
