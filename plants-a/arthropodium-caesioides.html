Arthropodium caesioides

Arthropodium caesioides, commonly known as the blue flax lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and striking blue-green foliage. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear small, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, well-drained soils.

Arthropodium caesioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
