Acourtia belizeana

Acourtia belizeana, commonly known as the Belize dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Belize.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Acourtia belizeana is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
