Achneria tuberculata

Achneria tuberculata, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white, fuzzy underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its stems are hairy and often have tubercles, contributing to its unique appearance.

Achneria tuberculata is adapted to moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.
