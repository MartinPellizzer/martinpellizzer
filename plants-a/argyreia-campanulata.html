Argyreia campanulata

Argyreia campanulata, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blossoms that attract pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a climbing vine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
