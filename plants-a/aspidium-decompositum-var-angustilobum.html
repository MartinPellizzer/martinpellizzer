Aspidium decompositum var. angustilobum

Aspidium decompositum var. angustilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, pinnately arranged fronds that are typically narrow and lanceolate in shape. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and wetlands, where it can form dense mats on the water surface or substrate. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, enabling sexual reproduction through the alternation of generations.

The plant's morphology and habitat preferences make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, supporting various microorganisms and invertebrates.
