Arctostaphylos parryana subsp. tumescens

Arctostaphylos parryana subsp.

tumescens, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, leathery berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of chaparral and woodland ecosystems.
