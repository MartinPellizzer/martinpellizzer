Anthurium cobbiae

Anthurium cobbiae, commonly known as the Cobbiae anthurium, is a tropical flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces strikingly colorful, showy bracts that serve as an attractant for pollinators. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow quite large, often with a glossy texture.

Anthurium cobbiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
