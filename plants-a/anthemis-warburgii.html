Anthemis warburgii

Anthemis warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's tansy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

The essential oils derived from its flowers are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and calming properties.
