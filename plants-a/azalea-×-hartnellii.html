Azalea × hartnellii

Azalea × hartnellii, commonly known as the Hartnell azalea, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron obtusum and Rhododendron viscosum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of 3 to 6 feet, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is native to the southeastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.

Azalea × hartnellii prefers partial shade and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases, making it a popular choice for horticultural use.
