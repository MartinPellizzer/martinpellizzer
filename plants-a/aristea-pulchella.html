Aristea pulchella

Aristea pulchella, commonly known as the pretty grass or pink grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and compact size. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Aristea pulchella is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
