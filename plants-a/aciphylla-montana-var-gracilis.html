Aciphylla montana var. gracilis

Aciphylla montana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender mountain pincushion, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of spines and small, often inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are typically covered in a thick layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is a distinctive component of New Zealand's alpine flora and is often found in subalpine and montane regions.
