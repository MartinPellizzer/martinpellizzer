Attalea brejinhoensis

Attalea brejinhoensis, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a species native to the dense rainforests of the Amazon Basin.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, gray trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, feather-like fruits containing edible seeds. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
