Andropogon contortus proles glaber

Andropogon contortus proles glaber, commonly known as the curly bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, curved leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically blue-green in color. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
