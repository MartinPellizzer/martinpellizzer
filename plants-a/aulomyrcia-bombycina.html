Aulomyrcia bombycina

Aulomyrcia bombycina, commonly known as the silky tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and clustered, often yellow or greenish in color, and it produces woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic qualities.
