Acacia columnaris

Acacia columnaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It features slender, upright branches with small, feathery leaves that are typically green and glossy. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Acacia columnaris is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
