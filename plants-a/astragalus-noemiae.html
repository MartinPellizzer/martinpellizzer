Astragalus noemiae

Astragalus noemiae, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
