Atriplex argentea var. argentea

Atriplex argentea var. argentea, commonly known as silver leaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its extensive root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
