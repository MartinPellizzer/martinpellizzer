Aridarum

Aridarum, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and showy, often violet or white, flowers that bloom during brief periods of rainfall. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh conditions with deep root systems and water-storing tissues. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aridarum plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local biodiversity.
