Aquilegia formosa subsp. coerulea

Aquilegia formosa subsp.

coerulea, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
