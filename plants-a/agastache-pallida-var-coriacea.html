Agastache pallida var. coriacea

Agastache pallida var. coriacea, commonly known as narrowleaf silverleaf hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of lavender to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
