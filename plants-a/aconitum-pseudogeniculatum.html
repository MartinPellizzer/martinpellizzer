Aconitum pseudogeniculatum

Aconitum pseudogeniculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant features compound leaves with deeply lobed foliage, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with care.
