Anoda angustifolia

Anoda angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces bright yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators.

Anoda angustifolia is an annual herb that is sometimes considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
