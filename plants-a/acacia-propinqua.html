Acacia propinqua

Acacia propinqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.

Acacia propinqua is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
