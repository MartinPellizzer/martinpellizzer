Alysicarpus vaginalis var. venosus

Alysicarpus vaginalis var. venosus, commonly known as the veined senna, is a tropical perennial legume characterized by its distinctive veined leaves and yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of Africa and Asia. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
