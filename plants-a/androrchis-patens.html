Androrchis patens

Androrchis patens, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a slender, upright stem with a single or few flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically purple or violet, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
