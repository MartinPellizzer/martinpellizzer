Acacia secundiflora

Acacia secundiflora, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has slender, often spiny branches and produces yellow, globular clusters of flowers. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources.

Acacia secundiflora plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
