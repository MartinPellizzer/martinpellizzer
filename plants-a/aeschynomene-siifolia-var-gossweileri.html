Aeschynomene siifolia var. gossweileri

Aeschynomene siifolia var. gossweileri, commonly known as the African leadplant, is a perennial herb native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and savanna habitats.

Aeschynomene siifolia var. gossweileri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
