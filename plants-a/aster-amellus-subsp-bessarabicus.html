Aster amellus subsp. bessarabicus

Aster amellus subsp.

bessarabicus, commonly known as the Bessarabian aster, is a perennial herb native to eastern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
