Adiantum amplum

Adiantum amplum, commonly known as the broad shield fern, is a species of fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, triangular fronds that are deeply divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its fronds are often covered with a fine layer of silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Adiantum amplum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
