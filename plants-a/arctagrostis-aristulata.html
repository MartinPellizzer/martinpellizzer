Arctagrostis aristulata

Arctagrostis aristulata, commonly known as the slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as wetlands and prairies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, with slender, awned lemmas.

Arctagrostis aristulata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
