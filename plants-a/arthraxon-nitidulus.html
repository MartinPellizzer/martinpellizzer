Arthraxon nitidulus

Arthraxon nitidulus, commonly known as the smooth arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and marshes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
