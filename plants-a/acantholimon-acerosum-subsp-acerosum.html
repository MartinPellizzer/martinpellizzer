Acantholimon acerosum subsp. acerosum

Acantholimon acerosum subsp.

acerosum, commonly known as the hard-leaved acantholimon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its rigid, spiny stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in terminal clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
