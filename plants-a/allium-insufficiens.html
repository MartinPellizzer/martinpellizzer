Allium insufficiens

Allium insufficiens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic or Chinese leek, is a bulbous plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, white bulbs that are often used as a culinary herb, similar to garlic and onions. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often grown for its edible leaves and bulbs.

Allium insufficiens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
