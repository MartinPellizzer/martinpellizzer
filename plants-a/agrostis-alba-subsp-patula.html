Agrostis alba subsp. patula

Agrostis alba subsp.

patula, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with green or brown. Agrostis alba subsp.

patula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
