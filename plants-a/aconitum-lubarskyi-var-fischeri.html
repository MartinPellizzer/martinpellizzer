Aconitum lubarskyi var. fischeri

Aconitum lubarskyi var. fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple hood-shaped flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with a waxy surface, and its roots are tuberous, which help it survive in cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
