Androcalva gaudichaudii

Androcalva gaudichaudii, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive green throat, which are typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with each pseudobulb producing a single leaf and a flowering stem. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment.

Androcalva gaudichaudii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
