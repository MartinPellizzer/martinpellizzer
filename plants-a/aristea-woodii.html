Aristea woodii

Aristea woodii, commonly known as the desert star, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features compact, rosette-shaped growth with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the rainy season. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss.

Aristea woodii is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
