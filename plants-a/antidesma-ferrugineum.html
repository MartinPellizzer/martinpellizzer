Antidesma ferrugineum

Antidesma ferrugineum, commonly known as red antidesma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, lowland areas. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various birds and mammals.

Antidesma ferrugineum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
