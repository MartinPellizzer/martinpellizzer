Adenia acuminata

Adenia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leafed adenia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking flowers with five petals and a distinctive crown of filaments, often in shades of yellow and red. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that contains numerous seeds.

Adenia acuminata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
