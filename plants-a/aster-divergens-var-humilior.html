Aster divergens var. humilior

Aster divergens var. humilior, commonly known as the low aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
