Aconitum camarum

Aconitum camarum, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply cut lobes, and its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme. Aconitum camarum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

It contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
