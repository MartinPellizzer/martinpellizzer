Altamiranoa erecta

Altamiranoa erecta, commonly known as the tall bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit. The plant features large, waxy leaves that form a rosette, often with vibrant colors ranging from green to red. It produces showy inflorescences that can last for several months, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Altamiranoa erecta is well-suited for cultivation in warm, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
