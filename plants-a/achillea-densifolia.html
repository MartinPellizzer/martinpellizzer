Achillea densifolia

Achillea densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and dense, silvery-green foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Achillea densifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
