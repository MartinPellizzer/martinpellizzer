Allium albidum

Allium albidum, commonly known as the white garlic or wild garlic, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, white, edible bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Allium albidum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
