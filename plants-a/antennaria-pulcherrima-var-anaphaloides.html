Antennaria pulcherrima var. anaphaloides

Antennaria pulcherrima var. anaphaloides, commonly known as the snowplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a frost-like appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually striking member of the Asteraceae family.
