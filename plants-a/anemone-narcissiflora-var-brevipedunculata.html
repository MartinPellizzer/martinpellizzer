Anemone narcissiflora var. brevipedunculata

Anemone narcissiflora var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the dwarf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, compact growth habit with stout, hairy stems and palmate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable ornamental plant for gardeners seeking native flora.
