Alyssum pulvinare var. mariovoense

Alyssum pulvinare var. mariovoense, commonly known as the Mariovo Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and mustard-like scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its compact growth habit and profuse blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The varietal name "mariovoense" refers to its origin in the Mariovo area of North Macedonia.
