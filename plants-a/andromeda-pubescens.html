Andromeda pubescens

Andromeda pubescens, commonly known as the hairy andromeda, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a common sight in forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical with a hairy texture, and its flowers range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring.

Andromeda pubescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
