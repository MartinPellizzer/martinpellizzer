Astragalus cruciatus subsp. aristidis

Astragalus cruciatus subsp.

aristidis, commonly known as the western milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
