Acacia arida

Acacia arida, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces long, slender pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Acacia arida is well adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and extreme temperatures.
