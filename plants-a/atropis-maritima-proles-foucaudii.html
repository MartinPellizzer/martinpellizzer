Atropis maritima proles foucaudii

Atropis maritima proles foucaudii, commonly known as the Foucaud's sea holly, is a rare and coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it thrive in saline and sandy environments. The plant has a compact growth habit with short, sturdy stems and is often found in dune ecosystems. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
