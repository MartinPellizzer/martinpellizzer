Allium longipetiolatum

Allium longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium longipetiolatum is native to southern Europe and is valued for its aromatic properties and ornamental appeal.
