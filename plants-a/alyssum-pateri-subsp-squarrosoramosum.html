Alyssum pateri subsp. squarrosoramosum

Alyssum pateri subsp.

squarrosoramosum, commonly known as the Chilean alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile. It features compact, mat-forming growth with dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the stems are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
