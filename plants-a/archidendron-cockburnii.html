Archidendron cockburnii

Archidendron cockburnii, commonly known as the Cockburn's legume, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Archidendron cockburnii is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
