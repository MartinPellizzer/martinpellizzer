Amelanchier arborea subsp. grandiflora

Amelanchier arborea subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the fragrant serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America. It features attractive white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear.

The plant produces edible, sweet berries in the summer, which are popular among birds and humans alike. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of yellow and red in the autumn, adding ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.
