Abutilon roseum

Abutilon roseum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
