Asparagus drepanophyllus var. warneckei

Asparagus drepanophyllus var. warneckei, commonly known as the warneckei asparagus, is a unique variant of the asparagus genus characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

This plant is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow, strap-like, and often exhibit a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that give way to edible, needle-like spears in the spring.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to form dense, compact clumps over time.
