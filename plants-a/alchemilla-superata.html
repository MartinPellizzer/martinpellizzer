Alchemilla superata

Alchemilla superata, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features basal leaves that are round to ovate with a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in dense clusters, typically blooming in early summer.

Alchemilla superata is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
