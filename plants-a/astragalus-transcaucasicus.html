Astragalus transcaucasicus

Astragalus transcaucasicus, commonly known as the Transcaucasian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, enabling it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus transcaucasicus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
