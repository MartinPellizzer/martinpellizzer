Acacia tephrophylla

Acacia tephrophylla, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its bark is often fissured and rough. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia tephrophylla is also valued for its hardiness and ability to grow in arid conditions.
