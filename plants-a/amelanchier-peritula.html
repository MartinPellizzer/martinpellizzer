Amelanchier peritula

Amelanchier peritula, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and showy white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, providing food for birds and small mammals.

Amelanchier peritula is often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and moist soils, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
