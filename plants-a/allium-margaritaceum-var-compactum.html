Allium margaritaceum var. compactum

Allium margaritaceum var. compactum, commonly known as the compact garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are edible and have a mild garlic scent. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit makes it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to gardens.
