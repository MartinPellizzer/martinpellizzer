Athyrium gedeanum

Athyrium gedeanum, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the feathers of a bird. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and help it spread slowly through its habitat.

The spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and are an important feature for its reproduction.
