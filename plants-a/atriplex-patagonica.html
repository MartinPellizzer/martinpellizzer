Atriplex patagonica

Atriplex patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian saltbush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a hardy plant in harsh conditions. The plant has small, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often used in revegetation projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
