Alatiglossum macropetalum

Alatiglossum macropetalum, commonly known as the giant petal bellflower, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its large, showy petals that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with a distinctive shape that resembles a bell, hence its common name.

Alatiglossum macropetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
