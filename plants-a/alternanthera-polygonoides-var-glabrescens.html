Alternanthera polygonoides var. glabrescens

Alternanthera polygonoides var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf water hyssop, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
