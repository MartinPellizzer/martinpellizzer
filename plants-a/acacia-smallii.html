Acacia smallii

Acacia smallii, commonly known as the small's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that serve as the primary means of photosynthesis. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Acacia smallii is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that plays an important role in local ecosystems.
