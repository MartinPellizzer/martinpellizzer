Andropogon pumilus

Andropogon pumilus, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, fluffy seeds.

Andropogon pumilus is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in restoration projects.
