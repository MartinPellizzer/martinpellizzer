Agalinis palustris

Agalinis palustris, commonly known as marsh bluegrass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swales, and riverbanks. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom from June to September. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system.

Agalinis palustris plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
