Aspalathus glabrescens

Aspalathus glabrescens, commonly known as African red bushwillow, is a small shrub native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, reddish-brown seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aspalathus glabrescens is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its use in herbal remedies.
