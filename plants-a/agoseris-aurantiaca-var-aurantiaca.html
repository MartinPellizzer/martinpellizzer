Agoseris aurantiaca var. aurantiaca

Agoseris aurantiaca var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and easily identifiable member of the Asteraceae family.
