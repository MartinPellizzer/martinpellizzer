Astragalus taldycensis

Astragalus taldycensis, commonly known as the Taldykol milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the diverse flora found in the region's steppe and desert ecosystems.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across vast landscapes.
