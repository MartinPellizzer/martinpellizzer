Abutilon theophrasti

Abutilon theophrasti, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in the leaf axils. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in many regions.

The plant has also been studied for its potential use in biofuel production due to its high biomass yield.
