Alsinopsis laricifolia

Alsinopsis laricifolia, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, preferring cool and dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Alsinopsis laricifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
