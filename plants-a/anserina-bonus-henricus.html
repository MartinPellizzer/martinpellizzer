Anserina bonus-henricus

Anserina bonus-henricus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
