Anathallis rubens

Anathallis rubens, commonly known as the red spider orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on its roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers bloom during the late summer and early autumn, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
