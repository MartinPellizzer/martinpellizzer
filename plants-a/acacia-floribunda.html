Acacia floribunda

Acacia floribunda, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences, which are clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Acacia floribunda is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
