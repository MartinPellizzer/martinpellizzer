Aechmea amorimii

Aechmea amorimii, commonly known as the Amazonian urn plant, is a bromeliad native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for its epiphytic growth. The plant's foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Aechmea amorimii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
