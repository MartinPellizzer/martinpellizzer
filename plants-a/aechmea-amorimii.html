Aechmea amorimii

Aechmea amorimii, commonly known as the Amazonian urn plant, is a bromeliad native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for its epiphytic growth. The plant's foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Aechmea amorimii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.

Morphology

Aechmea amorimii has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, strap-like, and dark green with a waxy coating, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The stem is short and stout, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that grows upright from the base. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of bright red flowers, which bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by birds and insects. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds, dispersed by animals that eat the fruit.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network near the soil surface, anchoring the plant and absorbing moisture and nutrients.
