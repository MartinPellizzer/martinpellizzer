Athyrium × glabrescens

Athyrium × glabrescens, commonly known as the glossy wood fern, is a hybrid fern species that combines traits from its parent species, Athyrium filix-femina and Athyrium niponicum.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of short, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive glossy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense clumps over time.

The spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
