Amorphophallus laxiflorus

Amorphophallus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its spatheous inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or green in color, often accompanied by a strong odor to attract pollinators. It has a tuberous root system that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal environments.

The plant is cultivated for its edible tubers, which are similar in texture to yams and are used in various cuisines.
