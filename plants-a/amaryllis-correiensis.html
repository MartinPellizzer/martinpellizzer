Amaryllis correiensis

Amaryllis correiensis, commonly known as the Correia's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, cylindrical stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.
