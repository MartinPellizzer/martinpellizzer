Althaea denudata

Althaea denudata, commonly known as the nakedmallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the mallow family (Malvaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Althaea denudata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

It is also valued for its mucilage-rich roots, which have been used in traditional medicine.
