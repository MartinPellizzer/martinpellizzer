Apostasia latifolia

Apostasia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed spathephyllum, is a tropical plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces striking, fragrant spathes that enclose its small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts are underway to protect its natural habitat.
