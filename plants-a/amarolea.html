Amarolea

Amarolea, commonly known as the "moonflower of the desert," is a rare and striking plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces large, night-blooming flowers with pale purple petals that emit a sweet, intoxicating fragrance. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Botanically, Amarolea is classified as a member of the nightshade family, with its scientific name being *Ipomoea amarolea*.
