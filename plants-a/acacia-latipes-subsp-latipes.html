Acacia latipes subsp. latipes

Acacia latipes subsp.

latipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often sparsely branched stems and narrow, phyllodes that are typically light green to grey-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which appear during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
