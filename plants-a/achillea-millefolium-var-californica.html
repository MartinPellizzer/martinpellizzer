Achillea millefolium var. californica

Achillea millefolium var. californica, commonly known as California yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads in shades of white, pink, or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
