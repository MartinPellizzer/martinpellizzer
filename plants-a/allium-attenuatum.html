Allium attenuatum

Allium attenuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic or slender garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that are often used as a culinary herb, similar to garlic. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium attenuatum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional cuisines.
