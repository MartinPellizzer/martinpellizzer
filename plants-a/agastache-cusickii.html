Agastache cusickii

Agastache cusickii, commonly known as green giant hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Agastache cusickii is also valued for its aromatic foliage and nectar-rich flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
