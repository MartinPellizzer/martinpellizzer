Aloe jex-blakeae

Aloe jex-blakeae, commonly known as the Blakea aloe, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stem and clusters of fleshy, pointed leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
