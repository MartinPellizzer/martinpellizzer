Adesmia venosa

Adesmia venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Adesmia venosa is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
