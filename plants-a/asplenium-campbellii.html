Asplenium campbellii

Asplenium campbellii, commonly known as the Campbell's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices and forest floors. The plant has a compact, rosette-like growth habit with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its fronds are usually unbranched and possess a distinctive, serrated margin.

Asplenium campbellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
