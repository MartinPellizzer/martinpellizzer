Amyema villiflora

Amyema villiflora, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters. Its leaves are feathery and finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance.

Amyema villiflora is well adapted to dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.
