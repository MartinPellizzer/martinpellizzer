Alphonsea ventricosa

Alphonsea ventricosa, commonly known as the tropical moonseed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

Alphonsea ventricosa is valued for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
