Aphanes parvula

Aphanes parvula, commonly known as the common sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Aphanes parvula is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
