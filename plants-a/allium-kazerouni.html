Allium kazerouni

Allium kazerouni, commonly known as the Kazeroun onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, spherical bulbs and grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear small, white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are used in local cuisine and traditional medicine for their pungent flavor and potential health benefits.

Allium kazerouni thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
