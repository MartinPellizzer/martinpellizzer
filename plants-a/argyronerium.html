Argyronerium

Argyronerium, commonly known as silverleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Argyronerium ciliatum, which is native to southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The genus is known for its toxic properties, with many species containing alkaloids that can be harmful to both animals and humans.
