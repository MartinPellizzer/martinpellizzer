Allium arvense var. tenuiflorum

Allium arvense var. tenuiflorum, commonly known as garlic mustard, is a biennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a hairy surface, while its flowering stems bear small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round, brown seeds in pods that are dispersed by wind. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has become invasive in North America.

Botanically, it is characterized by its aromatic foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
