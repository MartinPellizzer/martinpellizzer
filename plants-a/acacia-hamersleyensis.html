Acacia hamersleyensis

Acacia hamersleyensis, commonly known as the Hamersley wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its ecological significance makes it an important species in the region's flora.
