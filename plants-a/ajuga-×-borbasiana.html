Ajuga × borbasiana

Ajuga × borbasiana, commonly known as carpet bugle or silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and features small, attractive flowers that range from blue to purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically silver-gray in appearance, giving it a distinctive visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

Ajuga × borbasiana is also known for its ability to spread readily through rhizomes, making it both a desirable and sometimes invasive plant in certain environments.
