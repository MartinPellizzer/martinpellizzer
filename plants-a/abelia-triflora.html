Abelia triflora

Abelia triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features showy clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its floral displays. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Abelia triflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
