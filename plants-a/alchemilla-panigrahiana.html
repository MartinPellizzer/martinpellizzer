Alchemilla panigrahiana

Alchemilla panigrahiana, commonly known as the Kashmir alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features basal leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of silky white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in umbels.

Alchemilla panigrahiana is adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
