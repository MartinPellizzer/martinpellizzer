Ageratum intermedium

Ageratum intermedium, commonly known as the common ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, fluffy inflorescences that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically blooms throughout the growing season, producing numerous small, showy flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum intermedium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant appearance and ease of care.
