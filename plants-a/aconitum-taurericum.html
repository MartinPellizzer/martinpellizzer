Aconitum taurericum

Aconitum taurericum, commonly known as the Tauric monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its root system is tuberous, typical of many members of the genus. Aconitum taurericum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or meadow areas.

Like other aconites, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant to be handled with care.
