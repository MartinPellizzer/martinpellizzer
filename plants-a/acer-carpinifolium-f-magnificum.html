Acer carpinifolium f. magnificum

Acer carpinifolium f. magnificum, commonly known as the magnificum form of the European mountain ash, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves.

The leaves are typically a vibrant green during the growing season and turn a brilliant yellow in autumn, adding visual interest to the landscape. This variety is noted for its upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a focal point in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

While it is less common in cultivation, it is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to temperate climates.
