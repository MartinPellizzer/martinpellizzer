Achras rotundifolia

Achras rotundifolia, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, round, waxy leaves and produces a fruit that is central to its ecological and economic significance. The fruit, which resembles a small bread loaf, is botanically classified as a syncarp, formed from the fusion of multiple berries. The tree's trunk is typically smooth and often develops a distinctive swollen base.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is well adapted to coastal and lowland environments.
