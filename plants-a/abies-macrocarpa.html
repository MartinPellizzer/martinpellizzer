Abies macrocarpa

Abies macrocarpa, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its distinctive silvery-blue needles that are typically arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often hang downward, containing winged seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-white, becoming fissured with age.

White fir is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its timber and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
