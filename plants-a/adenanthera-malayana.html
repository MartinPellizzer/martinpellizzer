Adenanthera malayana

Adenanthera malayana, commonly known as the Malayan burr bean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits that contain small, hard seeds. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
