Acacia myriadena

Acacia myriadena, commonly known as the broad-leafed wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Acacia myriadena is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
