Artemisia caudata var. rydbergiana

Artemisia caudata var. rydbergiana, commonly known as Rydberg's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, contributing to its distinctive aroma and medicinal uses.
