Acanthopsis dregeana

Acanthopsis dregeana, commonly known as the Desert Caper, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
