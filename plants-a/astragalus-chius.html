Astragalus chius

Astragalus chius, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus chius is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
