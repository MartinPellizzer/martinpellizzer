Astragalus susianus subsp. susianus

Astragalus susianus subsp.

susianus, commonly known as the Susian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
