Anthyllis montana var. intermedia

Anthyllis montana var. intermedia, commonly known as the Alpine Anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in open, sunny habitats at high elevations.
