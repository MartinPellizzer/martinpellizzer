Aristida plumosa var. brachypoda

Aristida plumosa var. brachypoda, commonly known as the short-branched feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery appearance with slender, hair-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as sandy soils, dunes, and disturbed areas, thriving in low-nutrient environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for xeriscaping and erosion control.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
