Achyranthes bengalensis

Achyranthes bengalensis, commonly known as horse-radish or Chinese rhubarb, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture and prominent venation. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in loose spikes, while the fruit is a three-lobed capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and laxative effects.
