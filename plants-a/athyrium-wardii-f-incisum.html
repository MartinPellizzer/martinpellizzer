Athyrium wardii f. incisum

Athyrium wardii f. incisum, commonly known as the incised lace fern, is a variant of the Athyrium wardii species.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that exhibit a distinctive incised or notched appearance along the edges. This fern typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate forest regions. The fronds are usually a deep green, with a delicate, lacy texture that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ability to thrive in partial shade.
