Allium macrantherum

Allium macrantherum, commonly known as the large-flowered garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers with large, showy petals. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valued crop in some regions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.
