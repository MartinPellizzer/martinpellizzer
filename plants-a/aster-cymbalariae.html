Aster cymbalariae

Aster cymbalariae, commonly known as the mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. This plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and along stream banks. It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and ability to spread via stolons, forming dense mats.

Aster cymbalariae is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat skin irritations and respiratory ailments.
