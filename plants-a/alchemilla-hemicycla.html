Alchemilla hemicycla

Alchemilla hemicycla, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a compact cluster at the end of each stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla hemicycla is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
