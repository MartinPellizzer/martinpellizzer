Apocynum cannabinum var. lanceolatum

Apocynum cannabinum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as Indian hemp or dogbane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its milky sap contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

This variant is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.
