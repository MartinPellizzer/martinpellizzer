Asparagopsis alba

Asparagopsis alba, commonly known as white asparagus, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its tender, white stems that are harvested before they turn green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that develop into dark blue berries. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique color and flavor, it is a prized vegetable in various cuisines.
