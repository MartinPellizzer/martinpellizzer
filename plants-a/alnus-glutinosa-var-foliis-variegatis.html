Alnus glutinosa var. foliis-variegatis

Alnus glutinosa var. foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the variegated alder, is a variant of the common alder characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

This plant is a deciduous tree or shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a yellowish or white margin, creating a distinctive appearance. The flowers are catkins, and the tree produces small, winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
