Ardisia prionata var. prionata

Ardisia prionata var. prionata, commonly known as the red-berried evergreen shrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered red berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland areas. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and produces fragrant flowers in the spring.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.
