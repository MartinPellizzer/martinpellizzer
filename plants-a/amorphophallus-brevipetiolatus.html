Amorphophallus brevipetiolatus

Amorphophallus brevipetiolatus, commonly known as the short-petioled arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its underground tuber is a key part of its biology, storing nutrients and enabling survival through periods of drought.

The species is also valued for its ornamental foliage and has been cultivated in various horticultural settings.
