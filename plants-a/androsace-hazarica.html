Androsace hazarica

Androsace hazarica, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Androsace hazarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
