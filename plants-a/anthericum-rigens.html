Anthericum rigens

Anthericum rigens, commonly known as the fragrant cow-wheat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces spadices, which are elongated, leaf-like structures that house the actual flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anthericum rigens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle fragrance.
