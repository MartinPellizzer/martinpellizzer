Aira refracta

Aira refracta, commonly known as the tufted aira, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Aira refracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
