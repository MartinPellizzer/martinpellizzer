Acer pseudoplatanus var. coloratum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. coloratum, commonly known as the variegated maple, is a variant of the common maple tree characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are typically variegated with white, yellow, and green patterns, providing a unique visual appeal throughout the growing season. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Japan and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.

The tree produces small, red samaras that contribute to its seasonal interest and support local wildlife.
