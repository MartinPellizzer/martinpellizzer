Alyssum minus var. strigosum

Alyssum minus var. strigosum, commonly known as sticky alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to various landscaping settings.
