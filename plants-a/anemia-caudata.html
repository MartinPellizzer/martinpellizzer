Anemia caudata

Anemia caudata, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant features opposite, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Anemia caudata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
