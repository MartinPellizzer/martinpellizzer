Agave sisalana var. armata

Agave sisalana var. armata, commonly known as the armed agave, is a perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in sharp, backward-pointing spines. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its fibers, which are used in the production of ropes, textiles, and other industrial materials.
