Alternanthera hirtula subvar. pallens

Alternanthera hirtula subvar. pallens, commonly known as the pink sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a textured appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
