Aceratium pittosporoides

Aceratium pittosporoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is somewhat rare in the wild.
