Aspalathus pilosa

Aspalathus pilosa, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Aspalathus pilosa is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for local pollinators.
