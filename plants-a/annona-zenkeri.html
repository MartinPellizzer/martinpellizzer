Annona zenkeri

Annona zenkeri, commonly known as the African custard apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to central and eastern Africa.

It produces large, showy white or pink flowers that are typically followed by a pear-shaped fruit with a rough, green skin. The fruit contains a soft, creamy white pulp that is edible and has a sweet, custard-like flavor. The plant is valued for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and used in local cuisines.

It also has medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional remedies.
