Acer pictum f. pulvigerum

Acer pictum f. pulvigerum, commonly known as the crimson maple, is a unique variant of the red maple species.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that display a vibrant red coloration during the growing season. The plant is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage changes to a brilliant orange-red in the fall, making it a popular ornamental tree.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
