Aster dubius var. angustifolius

Aster dubius var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and pollinator value.
