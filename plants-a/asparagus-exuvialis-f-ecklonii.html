Asparagus exuvialis f. ecklonii

Asparagus exuvialis f. ecklonii, commonly known as the Ecklon's asparagus, is a rare and unique variant of the asparagus plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often branched stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
