Allium purpureoviride

Allium purpureoviride, commonly known as the purple-green leek, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, leek-like flavor, making it a useful addition to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal care once established.
