Astragalus brachycentros var. koieanus

Astragalus brachycentros var. koieanus, commonly known as the Koiean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
