Ailanthus malabarica

Ailanthus malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar ash tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with 10 to 20 pairs of leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
