Astragalus karoi

Astragalus karoi, commonly known as Karoi milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources, and it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Astragalus karoi is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
