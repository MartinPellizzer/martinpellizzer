Agave wrightii

Agave wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often lined with spines. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave wrightii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
