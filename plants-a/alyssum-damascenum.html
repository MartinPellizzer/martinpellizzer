Alyssum damascenum

Alyssum damascenum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces numerous tiny, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a slightly fuzzy texture.

Alyssum damascenum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
