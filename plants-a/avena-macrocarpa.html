Avena macrocarpa

Avena macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant oat, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces distinctive, three-lobed seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

Avena macrocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both agricultural and horticultural purposes.
