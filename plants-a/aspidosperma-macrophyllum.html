Aspidosperma macrophyllum

Aspidosperma macrophyllum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pau-ferro*, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces orange-red berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, sticky aril. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
