Allium parvum

Allium parvum, commonly known as the small onion or dwarf onion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bulbous form and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium parvum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
