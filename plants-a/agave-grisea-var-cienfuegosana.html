Agave grisea var. cienfuegosana

Agave grisea var. cienfuegosana, commonly known as the Cienfuegosana agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall flower stalk with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
