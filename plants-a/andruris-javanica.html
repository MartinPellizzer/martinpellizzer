Andruris javanica

Andruris javanica, commonly known as the Javanese orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or along streams. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Andruris javanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
