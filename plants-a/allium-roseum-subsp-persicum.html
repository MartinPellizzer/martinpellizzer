Allium roseum subsp. persicum

Allium roseum subsp.

persicum, commonly known as the Persian garlic or rose garlic, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in umbel-like clusters.

The plant is characterized by its bulbous stem, which is often used as a culinary spice in traditional dishes. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its aromatic, edible properties.
