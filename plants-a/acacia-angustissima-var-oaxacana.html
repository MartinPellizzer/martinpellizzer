Acacia angustissima var. oaxacana

Acacia angustissima var. oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow, globular flower heads. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

The species is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
