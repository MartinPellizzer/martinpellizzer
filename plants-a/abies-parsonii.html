Abies parsonii

Abies parsonii, commonly known as the Parson's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Abies parsonii is often found in high-elevation forests, where it thrives in cool, moist climates.
