Artemisia nitida

Artemisia nitida, commonly known as silver sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Artemisia nitida is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
