Arundina caespitosa

Arundina caespitosa, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with tall, slender stems. The plant produces striking, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Arundina caespitosa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological importance in its native habitats.
