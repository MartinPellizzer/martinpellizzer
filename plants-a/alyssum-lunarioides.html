Alyssum lunarioides

Alyssum lunarioides, commonly known as moonflower alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically has gray-green, fuzzy leaves and produces white or pale purple flowers that open in the evening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its nocturnal blooming habit and delicate appearance.
