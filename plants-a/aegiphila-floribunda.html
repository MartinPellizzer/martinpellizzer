Aegiphila floribunda

Aegiphila floribunda, commonly known as the clustered goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its compact growth habit.

Aegiphila floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
