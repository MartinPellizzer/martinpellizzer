Aromadendron cathcartii

Aromadendron cathcartii, commonly known as the Cathcart's aromatic tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, leathery leaves with a fragrant aroma when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing seeds. Its aromatic properties make it valuable in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Due to its unique scent and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
