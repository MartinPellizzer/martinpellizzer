Alyssum murale var. variabile

Alyssum murale var. variabile, commonly known as the variable wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and is often found growing on rocky slopes and walls. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Its variable flower colors and adaptability make it a popular choice for garden use.
