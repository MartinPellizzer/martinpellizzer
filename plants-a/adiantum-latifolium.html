Adiantum latifolium

Adiantum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with slender, arching stems. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are glossy and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance with a pale underside.

Adiantum latifolium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
