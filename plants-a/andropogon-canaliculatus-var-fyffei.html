Andropogon canaliculatus var. fyffei

Andropogon canaliculatus var. fyffei, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, which makes it visually appealing in ornamental landscapes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive bluish sheen, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stem. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
