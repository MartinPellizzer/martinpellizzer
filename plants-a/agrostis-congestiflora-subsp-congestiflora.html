Agrostis congestiflora subsp. congestiflora

Agrostis congestiflora subsp.

congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
