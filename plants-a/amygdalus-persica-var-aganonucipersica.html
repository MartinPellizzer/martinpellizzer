Amygdalus persica var. aganonucipersica

Amygdalus persica var. aganonucipersica, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a variety of the peach tree characterized by its distinct fruit morphology.

This variant is notable for its large, wrinkled hulls that encase the hard-shelled nuts. The botanical features include a thick, fibrous outer layer and a dense, edible kernel within. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its nutritional content.
