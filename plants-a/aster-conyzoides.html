Aster conyzoides

Aster conyzoides, commonly known as the African blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Aster conyzoides is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
