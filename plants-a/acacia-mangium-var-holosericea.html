Acacia mangium var. holosericea

Acacia mangium var. holosericea, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellowish-white inflorescences that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in thin, fibrous pods, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its rapid growth and is often used in reforestation and as a source of timber and charcoal.
