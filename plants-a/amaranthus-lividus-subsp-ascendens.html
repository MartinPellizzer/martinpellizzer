Amaranthus lividus subsp. ascendens

Amaranthus lividus subsp.

ascendens, commonly known as the ascending amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family. It is native to South America and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers.

The plant produces small, bead-like seeds that are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.

Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a robust root system that aids in its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
