Acrostichum lanceolatum var. normale

Acrostichum lanceolatum var. normale, commonly known as the common broad-leaf hibiscus, is a perennial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically leathery and dark green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which are often accompanied by red sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
