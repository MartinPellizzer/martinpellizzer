Argyranthemum callichrysum subsp. gomerensis

Argyranthemum callichrysum subsp.

gomerensis, commonly known as the Gomer's golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which are typically yellow with a white or pale yellow center.

The plant features soft, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
