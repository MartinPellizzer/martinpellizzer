Achillea millefolium f. discolor

Achillea millefolium f. discolor, commonly known as the white yarrow, is a horticultural form of the common yarrow.

It is characterized by its distinctive white flowers that contrast with the green foliage, making it a visually striking plant. The plant is a perennial herb with basal rosettes of finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and dry grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.
