Avena spicata var. calicina

Avena spicata var. calicina, commonly known as the California oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, spike-like clusters of green to pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to soil stability.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
