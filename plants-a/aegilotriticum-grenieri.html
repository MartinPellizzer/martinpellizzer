Aegilotriticum grenieri

Aegilotriticum grenieri, commonly known as the Grenier's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a distinctive visual appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.

Morphology

Aegilotriticum grenieri has leaves that are lance-shaped, alternate in arrangement, and range in size from 5 to 10 cm in length, with a dark green, glossy surface.
