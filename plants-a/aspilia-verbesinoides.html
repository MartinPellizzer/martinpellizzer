Aspilia verbesinoides

Aspilia verbesinoides, commonly known as the African buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spherical flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, yellow florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Aspilia verbesinoides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
