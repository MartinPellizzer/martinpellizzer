Anemone rupestris subsp. polycarpa

Anemone rupestris subsp.

polycarpa, commonly known as the rock anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are covered in fine hairs.

It is well adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
