Angraecum equitans

Angraecum equitans, commonly known as the sacred violet, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting sphinx moths as pollinators. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically dark green and strap-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

Angraecum equitans is also noted for its slow growth and requires specific conditions to thrive, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
