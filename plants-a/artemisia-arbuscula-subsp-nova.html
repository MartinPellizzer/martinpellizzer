Artemisia arbuscula subsp. nova

Artemisia arbuscula subsp.

nova, commonly known as the Narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It has a woody stem with a branching structure and is known for its strong, camphor-like scent.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other Artemisia species.
