Azorella glacialis

Azorella glacialis, commonly known as the glacier moss, is a low-growing plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments, often found on rocky slopes and in alpine regions. Its leaves are small, leathery, and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.
