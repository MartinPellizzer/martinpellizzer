Arabis laxa

Arabis laxa, commonly known as the loose Arabis, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Eurasia and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Arabis laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
