Alseodaphne falcata

Alseodaphne falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Alseodaphne falcata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
