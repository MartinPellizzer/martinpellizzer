Arundinaria caudiceps

Arundinaria caudiceps, commonly known as the red bamboo, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria caudiceps is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
