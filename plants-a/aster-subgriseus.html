Aster subgriseus

Aster subgriseus, commonly known as the gray aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, grayish down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing pink to purple petals and yellow centers.
