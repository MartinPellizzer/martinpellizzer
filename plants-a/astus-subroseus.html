Astus subroseus

Astus subroseus, commonly known as the roseum asters, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to gardens throughout the season.
