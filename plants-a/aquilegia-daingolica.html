Aquilegia daingolica

Aquilegia daingolica, commonly known as the Himalayan columbine, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred petals that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that disperse its seeds.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
