Aleuritia kingii

Aleuritia kingii, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are dark green above and lighter below. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Aleuritia kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
