Astragalus angustifolius subsp. odonianus

Astragalus angustifolius subsp.

odonianus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its ecological significance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
