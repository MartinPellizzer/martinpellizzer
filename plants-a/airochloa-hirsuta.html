Airochloa hirsuta

Airochloa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant.

Airochloa hirsuta is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive texture and resilience to various environmental conditions.
