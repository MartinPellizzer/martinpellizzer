Acer palmatum f. horonaiense

Acer palmatum f. horonaiense, commonly known as the Horonai cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple known for its delicate, lace-like foliage.

It features finely dissected leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 feet. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique leaf structure and seasonal color changes.
