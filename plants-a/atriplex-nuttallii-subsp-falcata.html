Atriplex nuttallii subsp. falcata

Atriplex nuttallii subsp.

falcata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are adapted to arid environments.

The plant has small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert and coastal regions.

Its extensive root system helps prevent soil erosion and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
