Aridaria laxipetala

Aridaria laxipetala, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens as a drought-resistant ornamental plant.
