Acularia nodosa

Acularia nodosa, commonly known as the nodular liverwort, is a small, terrestrial liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and the presence of small, rounded gemmae on its stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its structure consists of a thallus with distinct leaf-like structures called phyllidia.

Acularia nodosa plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various invertebrates.
