Acacia pennata

Acacia pennata, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. Acacia pennata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought.

Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for various purposes, including as a source of tannins.
