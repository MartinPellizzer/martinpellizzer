Ammannia multiflora f. ramosissima

Ammannia multiflora f. ramosissima, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
