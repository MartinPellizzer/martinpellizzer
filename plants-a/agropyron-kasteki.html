Agropyron kasteki

Agropyron kasteki, commonly known as the Kastehi grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates.

Agropyron kasteki is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
