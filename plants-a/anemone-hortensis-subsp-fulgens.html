Anemone hortensis subsp. fulgens

Anemone hortensis subsp.

fulgens, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan. It features showy, single or double flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often covered in a silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that supports its growth and propagation.
