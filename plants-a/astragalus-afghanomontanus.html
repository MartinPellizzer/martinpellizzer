Astragalus afghanomontanus

Astragalus afghanomontanus, commonly known as Afghan mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, which helps it survive in arid and nutrient-poor conditions.

Astragalus afghanomontanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
