Acer pictum var. paxii

Acer pictum var. paxii, commonly known as the white poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The tree has opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are typically 6 to 12 inches long. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring followed by winged seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, open woodlands and along stream banks.
