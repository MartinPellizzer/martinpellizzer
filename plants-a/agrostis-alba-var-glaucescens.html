Agrostis alba var. glaucescens

Agrostis alba var. glaucescens, commonly known as the bluegrass meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small and densely packed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
