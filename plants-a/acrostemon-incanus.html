Acrostemon incanus

Acrostemon incanus, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, white or pale blue flowers with a distinctive, narrow spatulate shape. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
