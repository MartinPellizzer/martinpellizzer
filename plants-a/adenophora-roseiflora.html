Adenophora roseiflora

Adenophora roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
