Amaryllis evansiae

Amaryllis evansiae, commonly known as the White Amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. It blooms in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
