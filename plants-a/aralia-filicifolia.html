Aralia filicifolia

Aralia filicifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaf aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that resemble ferns, with feather-like divisions that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
