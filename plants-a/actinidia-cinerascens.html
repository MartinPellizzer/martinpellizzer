Actinidia cinerascens

Actinidia cinerascens, commonly known as the silver vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy, grayish-green leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, followed by edible berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its foliage is often used in traditional Chinese medicine, and it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The silver vine is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
