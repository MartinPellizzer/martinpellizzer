Asyneuma campanuloides var. gracile

Asyneuma campanuloides var. gracile, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are slender and often branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its refined appearance.
