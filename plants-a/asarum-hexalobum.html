Asarum hexalobum

Asarum hexalobum, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It features a tuberous rhizome and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers in late spring, which are clustered in a spicate inflorescence. Its aromatic, spicy scent is reminiscent of ginger, hence its common name.

Asarum hexalobum is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
