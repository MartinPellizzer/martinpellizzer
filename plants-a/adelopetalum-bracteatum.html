Adelopetalum bracteatum

Adelopetalum bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, bract-like structures at the base of its flowering stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
