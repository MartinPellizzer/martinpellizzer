Andropogon hallii var. flaveolus

Andropogon hallii var. flaveolus, commonly known as the yellow bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are often tinged with yellow or orange, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
