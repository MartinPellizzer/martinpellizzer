Asphodelus cerasifer var. marianus

Asphodelus cerasifer var. marianus, commonly known as the Marian's cherry asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cherry-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a compact, herbaceous growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
