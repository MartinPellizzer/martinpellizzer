Allionia hirsuta var. aggregata

Allionia hirsuta var. aggregata, commonly known as the hairy allionia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
