Aegilops mutica f. nurub

Aegilops mutica f. nurub, commonly known as the rough spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its wiry, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
