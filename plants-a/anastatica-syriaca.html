Anastatica syriaca

Anastatica syriaca, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a small, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid regions of the Mediterranean and Middle East.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its ability to survive extreme desiccation, entering a state of dormancy when dry. When exposed to moisture, it can "resurrect" by rehydrating and regaining its green color and structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a unique life cycle that allows it to thrive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

Its resilience and ability to revive after desiccation have made it a subject of scientific interest and a symbol of endurance.
