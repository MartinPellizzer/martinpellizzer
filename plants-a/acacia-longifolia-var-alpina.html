Acacia longifolia var. alpina

Acacia longifolia var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine acacia, is a shrub native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves that are adapted to cold and harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are arranged in compact spikes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in high-altitude areas with limited nutrients.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in arid or disturbed landscapes.
