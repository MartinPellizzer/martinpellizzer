Aconitum inclinatum

Aconitum inclinatum, commonly known as the leaning aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture.

Aconitum inclinatum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
