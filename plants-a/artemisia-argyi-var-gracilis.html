Artemisia argyi var. gracilis

Artemisia argyi var. gracilis, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, slightly bitter scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
