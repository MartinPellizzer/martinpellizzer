Astragalus majixueshanicus f. albiflorus

Astragalus majixueshanicus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered mountain milkvetch, is a rare plant species native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
