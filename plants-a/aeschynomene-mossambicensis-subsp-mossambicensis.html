Aeschynomene mossambicensis subsp. mossambicensis

Aeschynomene mossambicensis subsp.

mossambicensis, commonly known as the African prostrate wood sorrel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.
