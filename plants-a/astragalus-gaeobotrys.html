Astragalus gaeobotrys

Astragalus gaeobotrys, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

Astragalus gaeobotrys plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
