Ardisia warneri

Ardisia warneri, commonly known as the Warner's beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright purple berries in the fall. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, and it is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.

The berries are a food source for birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological value.
