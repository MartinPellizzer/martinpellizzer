Abies excelsa var. maxwellii

Abies excelsa var. maxwellii, commonly known as the Maxwell's fir, is a variant of the silver fir native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The needles are typically short and arranged in a flat, two-ranked pattern, giving the tree a distinctive appearance. It produces cones that are woody and persistent, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
