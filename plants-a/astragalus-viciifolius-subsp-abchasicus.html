Astragalus viciifolius subsp. abchasicus

Astragalus viciifolius subsp.

abchasicus, commonly known as the Abchazian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features compound leaves with a vicia-like appearance, giving it its species name. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes, and it produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
