Aconitum kusnezoffii lusus tenuisectum

Aconitum kusnezoffii lusus tenuisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a raceme, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
