Acalypha gracilens subsp. monococca

Acalypha gracilens subsp.

monococca, commonly known as the slender beauty leaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and strikingly colorful, palmate leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by spherical, dry fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a valuable addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
