Acalypha retifera

Acalypha retifera, commonly known as the spiked glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Acalypha retifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
