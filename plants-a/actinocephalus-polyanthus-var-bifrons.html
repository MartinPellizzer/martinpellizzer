Actinocephalus polyanthus var. bifrons

Actinocephalus polyanthus var. bifrons, commonly known as the double-flowered actinocephalus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow disc flowers surrounded by white ray florets. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance and long blooming period.

The variety 'bifrons' is distinguished by its double flowers, which give it a more elaborate and showy look compared to the typical single-flowered form.
