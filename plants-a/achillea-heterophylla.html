Achillea heterophylla

Achillea heterophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, linear leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, and its foliage has a distinct, aromatic scent.

Achillea heterophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
