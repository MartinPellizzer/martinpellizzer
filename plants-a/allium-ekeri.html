Allium ekeri

Allium ekeri, commonly known as the Ekeri leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and broad, flat leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable addition to culinary gardens.

Allium ekeri thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
