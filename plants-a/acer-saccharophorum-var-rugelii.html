Acer saccharophorum var. rugelii

Acer saccharophorum var. rugelii, commonly known as the rugelii maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow furrows that become more pronounced with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
