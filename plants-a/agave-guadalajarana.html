Agave guadalajarana

Agave guadalajarana, commonly known as the Guadalajara agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are typically followed by the development of its characteristic blue-green berries.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
