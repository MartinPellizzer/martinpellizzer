Albizia balabaka

Albizia balabaka, commonly known as the African mimosa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants capable of nitrogen fixation. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often described as fern-like in appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in racemes, and it produces flat, dehiscent pods.

Albizia balabaka is adapted to various soil types and is often found in woodland and savanna regions.
