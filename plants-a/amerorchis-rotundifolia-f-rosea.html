Amerorchis rotundifolia f. rosea

Amerorchis rotundifolia f. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered rush gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or rose-colored. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
