Aconitum ovatum

Aconitum ovatum, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features striking, hooded flowers that are typically blue or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal at the back. The plant has palmate, deeply lobed leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum ovatum is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant to handle with care.
