Abrotanum frutescens

Abrotanum frutescens, commonly known as wild thyme or honey thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial with small, fragrant leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Abrotanum frutescens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.
