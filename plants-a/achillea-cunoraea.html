Achillea cunoraea

Achillea cunoraea, commonly known as lesser yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has feathery, aromatic leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Achillea cunoraea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
