Aegilops mutica f. nuluci

Aegilops mutica f. nuluci, commonly known as the woolly wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its wiry, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
