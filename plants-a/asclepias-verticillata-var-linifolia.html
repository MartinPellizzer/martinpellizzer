Asclepias verticillata var. linifolia

Asclepias verticillata var. linifolia, commonly known as showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It features clusters of pink to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its milky sap, which is toxic to some animals.

This species is an important host plant for monarch butterflies and other pollinators.
