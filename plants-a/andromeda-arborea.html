Andromeda arborea

Andromeda arborea, commonly known as the silverleaf andromeda, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are typically pink or white and bloom in the spring.

Andromeda arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
