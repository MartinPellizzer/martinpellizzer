Aechmea manzanaresiana

Aechmea manzanaresiana, commonly known as the Manzanares bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, long-lasting inflorescences that range from white to pink. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Morphology

Aechmea manzanaresiana has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem, with a bright green color and a waxy surface.

The stem is short and often hidden beneath the leaves, with a smooth, pale green bark that grows upward from the base. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in the summer months and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by birds and mammals feeding on the flesh.

Its root system is fibrous and adapted to anchor the plant in the soil while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.
