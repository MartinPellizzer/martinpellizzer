Astragalus indistinctus

Astragalus indistinctus, commonly known as the indistinct milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its seed pods contain small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.
