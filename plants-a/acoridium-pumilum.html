Acoridium pumilum

Acoridium pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf umbrella plant, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling an umbrella shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
