Acanthus mollis subsp. mollis

Acanthus mollis subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the woolly acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces tall spikes of violet or purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its distinctive appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both garden landscapes and natural habitats.
