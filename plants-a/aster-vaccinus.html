Aster vaccinus

Aster vaccinus, commonly known as the purple mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Aster vaccinus typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
