Astragalus ispahanicus

Astragalus ispahanicus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are small and typically dispersed by wind.
