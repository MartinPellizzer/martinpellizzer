Anthericum caespitosum

Anthericum caespitosum, commonly known as the tussock rush, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact tussocks, typically growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, dark green leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowering stems bear small, pale green to brown spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Anthericum caespitosum is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
