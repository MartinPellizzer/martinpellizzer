Artemisia arbuscula subsp. arbuscula

Artemisia arbuscula subsp.

arbuscula, commonly known as dwarf tarragon, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in sagebrush steppe and other arid environments.

The leaves are used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb, though its fragrance is more subtle compared to other artemisias.
