Amaryllis equestris var. plena

Amaryllis equestris var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered amaryllis, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are densely packed with petals, giving it a full, plump appearance. The plant produces trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting white or pink markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

This variety is prized for its vibrant display and relatively easy care requirements.
