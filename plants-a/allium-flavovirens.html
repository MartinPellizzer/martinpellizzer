Allium flavovirens

Allium flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for botanists and horticulturists alike.
