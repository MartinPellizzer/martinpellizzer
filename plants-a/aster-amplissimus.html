Aster amplissimus

Aster amplissimus, commonly known as the Great Aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Aster amplissimus is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators.
