Andropogon villosus var. genuinus

Andropogon villosus var. genuinus, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. This plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
