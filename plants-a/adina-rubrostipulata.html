Adina rubrostipulata

Adina rubrostipulata, commonly known as the red-stippled adina, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often red-stippled along the veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
