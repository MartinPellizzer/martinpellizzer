Ardisia smaragdina

Ardisia smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald Beauty, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a subtle fragrance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in gardens and as a potted plant.
