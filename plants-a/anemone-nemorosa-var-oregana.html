Anemone nemorosa var. oregana

Anemone nemorosa var. oregana, commonly known as the Oregon anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
