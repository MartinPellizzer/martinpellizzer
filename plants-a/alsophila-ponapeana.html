Alsophila ponapeana

Alsophila ponapeana, commonly known as the Ponape cycad, is a unique plant native to the tropical Pacific islands.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of pinnate, feather-like leaves. The plant is dioecious, meaning it has separate male and female plants, and produces cones rather than flowers.

Its slow growth and long lifespan make it a valuable and often endangered species in its natural habitat.
