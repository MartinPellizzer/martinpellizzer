Alstroemeria banksiana

Alstroemeria banksiana, commonly known as the Banksian alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
