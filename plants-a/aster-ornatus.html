Aster ornatus

Aster ornatus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster ornatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
