Astelma eximium

Astelma eximium, commonly known as the noble astelma, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of vibrant, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The leaves are long, narrow, and covered in a fine, silvery down that helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, Astelma eximium is both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
