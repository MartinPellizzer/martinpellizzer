Aquilegia hirsuta

Aquilegia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and bright, often red or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in woodland or meadow environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are hairy on the underside.

Aquilegia hirsuta is a perennial species that produces seed capsules containing numerous small, dark seeds.
