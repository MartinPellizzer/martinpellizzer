Aristolochia hirta var. scabrida

Aristolochia hirta var. scabrida, commonly known as the rough-hairy Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its fragrant, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and chemical compounds. It typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
