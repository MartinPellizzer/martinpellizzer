Aleuritia mallophylla

Aleuritia mallophylla, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and turn vibrant red in the fall.

Aleuritia mallophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
