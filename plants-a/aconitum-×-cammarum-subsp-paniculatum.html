Aconitum × cammarum subsp. paniculatum

Aconitum × cammarum subsp.

paniculatum, commonly known as the panicled aconite, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and it produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
