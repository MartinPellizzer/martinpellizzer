Achradotypus coriaceus

Achradotypus coriaceus, commonly known as the rough-leaved achradotypus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, granular texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to survive in low-water conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
