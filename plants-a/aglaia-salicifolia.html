Aglaia salicifolia

Aglaia salicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related species. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.
