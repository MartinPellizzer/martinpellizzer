Allium rhodopeum subsp. rhodopeum

Allium rhodopeum subsp.

rhodopeum, commonly known as the Rhodope onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in umbels.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in local traditions.
