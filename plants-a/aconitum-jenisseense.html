Aconitum jenisseense

Aconitum jenisseense, commonly known as Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Siberian region of Russia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in river valleys and forested areas.

Aconitum jenisseense contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring caution during handling.
