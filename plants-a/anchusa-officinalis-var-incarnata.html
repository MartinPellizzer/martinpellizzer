Anchusa officinalis var. incarnata

Anchusa officinalis var. incarnata, commonly known as the scarlet mallows, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Anchusa officinalis var. incarnata is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
