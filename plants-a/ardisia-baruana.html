Ardisia baruana

Ardisia baruana, commonly known as the Bornean beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Following flowering, it produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds.

These fruits are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for wildlife in its natural habitat.
