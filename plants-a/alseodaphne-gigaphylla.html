Alseodaphne gigaphylla

Alseodaphne gigaphylla, commonly known as the giant-leafed laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
