Astragalus chrysomallus

Astragalus chrysomallus, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in nutrient-poor environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
