Andropogon bisulcatus

Andropogon bisulcatus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves with two distinct longitudinal veins, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or blue.

Andropogon bisulcatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
