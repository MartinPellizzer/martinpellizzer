Actinodaphne moluccana

Actinodaphne moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
