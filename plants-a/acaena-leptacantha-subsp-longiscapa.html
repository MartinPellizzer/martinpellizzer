Acaena leptacantha subsp. longiscapa

Acaena leptacantha subsp.

longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed spinifex, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand. It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine, stiff hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats.
