Arum italicum var. pictum

Arum italicum var. pictum, commonly known as the painted arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of green, white, and red. The plant produces a distinctive spathe, which is a modified leaf that encloses the spadix, a dense cluster of tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

The spathe's vibrant colors and unique structure make it a popular choice for both decorative and horticultural purposes.
