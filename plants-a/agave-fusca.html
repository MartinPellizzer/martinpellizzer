Agave fusca

Agave fusca, commonly known as the desert agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white to yellowish flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its leaves and stem.

Agave fusca is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
