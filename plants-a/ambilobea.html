Ambilobea

Ambilobea, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that resemble stars, giving it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Ambilobea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth makes it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets.
