Adiantum greenii

Adiantum greenii, commonly known as the Green's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green and finely divided. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum greenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
