Austrocylindropuntia ipatiana

Austrocylindropuntia ipatiana, commonly known as the Pachyphytum, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Austrocylindropuntia ipatiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
