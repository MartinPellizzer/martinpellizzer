Asphodelus ramosus var. fuscescens

Asphodelus ramosus var. fuscescens, commonly known as the black asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of dark purple to black, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable ornamental plant in arid environments.
