Albizia marthae

Albizia marthae, commonly known as the pink-veined albizia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Albizia marthae is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate foliage and floral display.
