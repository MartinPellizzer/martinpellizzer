Atriplex arenicola

Atriplex arenicola, commonly known as saltbush, is a hardy shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems. Its yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Atriplex arenicola plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
