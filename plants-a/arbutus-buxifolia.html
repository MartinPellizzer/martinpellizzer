Arbutus buxifolia

Arbutus buxifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf arbutus, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is typically silver or gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark purple when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Arbutus buxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
