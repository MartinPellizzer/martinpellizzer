Alchemilla semihirta

Alchemilla semihirta, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its common name reflects its historical use in folk medicine for its purported healing properties.
