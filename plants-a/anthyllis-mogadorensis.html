Anthyllis mogadorensis

Anthyllis mogadorensis, commonly known as the Cape Verde clover, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and coastal environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Anthyllis mogadorensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
