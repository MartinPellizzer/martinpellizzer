Acer japonicum f. macrophyllum

Acer japonicum f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with large leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that are typically a vibrant green in summer and turn shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 8. It prefers partial to full shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
