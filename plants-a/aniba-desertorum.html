Aniba desertorum

Aniba desertorum, commonly known as the desert balsa, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its small, simple leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique ecological role and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
