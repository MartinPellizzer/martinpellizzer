Achillea millefolium var. monticola

Achillea millefolium var. monticola, commonly known as mountain yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in alpine and rocky environments. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional use in herbal medicine.

Its common name reflects its historical association with the legendary hero Achilles.
