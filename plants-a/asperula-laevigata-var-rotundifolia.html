Asperula laevigata var. rotundifolia

Asperula laevigata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf evergreen holly, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval to round leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries in late summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal and urban environments.
