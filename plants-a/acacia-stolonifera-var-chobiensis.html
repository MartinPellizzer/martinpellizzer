Acacia stolonifera var. chobiensis

Acacia stolonifera var. chobiensis, commonly known as the Chobien Acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and the production of small, yellow, pod-like fruits. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It is often found in sandy soils and can form dense thickets, providing habitat for various small animals.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in low-nutrient conditions.
