Aristida breviglumis

Aristida breviglumis, commonly known as the short-bracted threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, and it is adapted to survive in arid conditions.

Aristida breviglumis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
