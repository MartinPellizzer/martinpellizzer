Abelia insularis

Abelia insularis, commonly known as the island abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Abelia insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
