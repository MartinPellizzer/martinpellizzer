Amaranthus sanguineus

Amaranthus sanguineus, commonly known as Crimson Cup, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces numerous seeds enclosed in a fleshy, spiky capsule that turns reddish as it matures. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.

The leaves are lance-shaped and can be used in some traditional cuisines, though the plant is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities.
