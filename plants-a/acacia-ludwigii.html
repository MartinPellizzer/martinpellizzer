Acacia ludwigii

Acacia ludwigii, commonly known as the Ludwig's acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are often shed during the dry season. The plant produces yellow, globular flowerheads that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by animals or through mechanical means.

Acacia ludwigii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
