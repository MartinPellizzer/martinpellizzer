Azalea lanata

Azalea lanata, commonly known as the downy azalea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its scientific name "lanata," meaning "covered with down.". The plant produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in the spring, adding vibrant color to gardens. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Azalea lanata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
