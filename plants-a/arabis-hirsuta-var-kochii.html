Arabis hirsuta var. kochii

Arabis hirsuta var. kochii, commonly known as the Koch's hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its hairy stems and leaves are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

It produces yellow or white flowers in early spring, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
