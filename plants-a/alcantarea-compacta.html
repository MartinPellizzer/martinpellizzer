Alcantarea compacta

Alcantarea compacta, commonly known as the compact aloe, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are covered in a layer of white powder, known as farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces yellowish-green flowers on tall, branched stems during the warmer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
