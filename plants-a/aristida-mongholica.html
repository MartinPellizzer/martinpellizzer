Aristida mongholica

Aristida mongholica, commonly known as Mongolian threeawn, is a grass species native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Aristida mongholica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
