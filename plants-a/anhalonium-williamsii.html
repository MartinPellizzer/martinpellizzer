Anhalonium williamsii

Anhalonium williamsii, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in hot, dry climates and is adapted to store water in its thick stems and leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Anhalonium williamsii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental beauty in xeriscapic gardens.
