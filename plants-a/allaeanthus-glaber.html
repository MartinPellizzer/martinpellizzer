Allaeanthus glaber

Allaeanthus glaber, commonly known as the smooth allianthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are smooth and lance-shaped. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
