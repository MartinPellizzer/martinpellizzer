Agrostis dianthera

Agrostis dianthera, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Agrostis dianthera is also valued for its use in forage and as a component in ecological restoration projects.
