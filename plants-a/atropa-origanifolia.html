Atropa origanifolia

Atropa origanifolia, commonly known as the "wild star-of-Bethlehem," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, resembling those of oregano, hence its common name.

Atropa origanifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
