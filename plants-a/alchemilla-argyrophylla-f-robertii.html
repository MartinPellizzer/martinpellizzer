Alchemilla argyrophylla f. robertii

Alchemilla argyrophylla f. robertii, commonly known as silver-leaved lady's mantle, is a hemicryptophyte with a rosette growth habit.

It features finely divided, silver-white leaves that are densely covered with soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicle-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
