Astragalus pervelutinus

Astragalus pervelutinus, commonly known as the soft-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Astragalus pervelutinus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in arid environments.
