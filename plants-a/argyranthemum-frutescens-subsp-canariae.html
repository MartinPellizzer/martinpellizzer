Argyranthemum frutescens subsp. canariae

Argyranthemum frutescens subsp.

canariae, commonly known as Canary Island flossflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its compact growth habit and silvery-white, woolly foliage.

The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to pale pink, which bloom throughout the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
