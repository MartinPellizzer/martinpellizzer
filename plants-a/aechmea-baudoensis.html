Aechmea baudoensis

Aechmea baudoensis, commonly known as the Baudo bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of Colombia and Ecuador, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a rosette of fleshy, spathate leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its inflorescence produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

Aechmea baudoensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to indoor conditions.
