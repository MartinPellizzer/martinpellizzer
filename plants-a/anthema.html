Anthema

Anthema, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are typically herbaceous and native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. They feature daisy-like flowers with yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets. Anthema species often grow in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
