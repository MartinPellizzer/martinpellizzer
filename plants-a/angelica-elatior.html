Angelica elatior

Angelica elatior, commonly known as greater angelica, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which develop into small, dry fruits. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Angelica elatior is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
