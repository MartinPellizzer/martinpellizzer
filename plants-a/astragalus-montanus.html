Astragalus montanus

Astragalus montanus, commonly known as mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus montanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
