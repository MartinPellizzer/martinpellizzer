Aster flavovirens

Aster flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a greenish hue, giving it its common name.

Aster flavovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
