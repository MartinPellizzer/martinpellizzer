Acer acuminatilobum

Acer acuminatilobum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely toothed leaves that display a range of colors throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a broad, spreading crown. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
