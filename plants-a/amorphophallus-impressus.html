Amorphophallus impressus

Amorphophallus impressus, commonly known as the African giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to central and eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a thick, starchy underground stem known as a tuber. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants such as calla lilies and taro. Its tuber is used as a food source in some regions and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
