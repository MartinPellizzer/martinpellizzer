Alyssum degenianum f. subcaespitosum

Alyssum degenianum f. subcaespitosum, commonly known as the tufted alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and profuse clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
