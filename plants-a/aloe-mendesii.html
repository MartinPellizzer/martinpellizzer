Aloe mendesii

Aloe mendesii, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional medicine and skincare products.

Aloe mendesii thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
