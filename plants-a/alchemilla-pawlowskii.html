Alchemilla pawlowskii

Alchemilla pawlowskii, commonly known as the Polish lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Alchemilla pawlowskii thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
