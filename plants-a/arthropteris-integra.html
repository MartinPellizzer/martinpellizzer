Arthropteris integra

Arthropteris integra, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, acidic soils. The plant has dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive, segmented appearance. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

Arthropteris integra is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
