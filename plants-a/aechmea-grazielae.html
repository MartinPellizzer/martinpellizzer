Aechmea grazielae

Aechmea grazielae, commonly known as the Graceful Pineapple Bromeliad, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence topped with vibrant red flowers that bloom once in its lifetime. The plant features rosettes of broad, waxy leaves that form a central rosette, capable of holding water, a common adaptation in bromeliads. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and can display a range of colors from green to blue-green.

Aechmea grazielae is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique appearance.

Morphology

Aechmea grazielae has rosette-shaped leaves that are long, narrow, and leathery, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The plant has a short, thick, and often woody stem with a rough, fibrous bark that is usually greenish-brown in color.
