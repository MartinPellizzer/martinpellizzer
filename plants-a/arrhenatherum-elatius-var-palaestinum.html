Arrhenatherum elatius var. palaestinum

Arrhenatherum elatius var. palaestinum, commonly known as Palestinian sweetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that typically grow between 30 and 100 centimeters in height. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, fluffy flowers that are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, silky texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
