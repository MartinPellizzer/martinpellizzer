Ariopsis

Ariopsis, commonly known as the sea hare, is a genus of marine gastropod mollusks belonging to the family Aplysiidae.

These creatures are characterized by their soft, often colorful bodies and lack of a true shell, instead having a thin, membranous covering. Ariopsis species are typically found in warm coastal waters and are known for their ability to secrete a variety of chemicals for defense. They play an important role in marine ecosystems as both predators and prey.

Their unique anatomy and ecological significance make them a subject of interest in marine biology research.
