Artemisia sacrorum var. messerschmidiana

Artemisia sacrorum var. messerschmidiana, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains high levels of volatile oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is closely related to other Artemisia species and is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of compounds like artemisinin.
