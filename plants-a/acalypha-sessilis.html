Acalypha sessilis

Acalypha sessilis, commonly known as the sessile spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Acalypha sessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
