Amorphophallus rostratus

Amorphophallus rostratus, commonly known as the red-tailed arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the genus Amorphophallus. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wet, shaded environments.

The tuberous root system of this plant is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
