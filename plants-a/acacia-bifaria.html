Acacia bifaria

Acacia bifaria, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acacia bifaria plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
