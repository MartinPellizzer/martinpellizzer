Acacia barringtonensis

Acacia barringtonensis, commonly known as the Barrington's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves, which are typical features of the Acacia genus. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open woodlands. It produces long, slender pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
