Aster nitidus

Aster nitidus, commonly known as the shining aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, purple to blue flower heads that are composed of numerous small florets. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Aster nitidus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.
