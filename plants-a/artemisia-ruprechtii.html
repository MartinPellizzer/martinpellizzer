Artemisia ruprechtii

Artemisia ruprechtii, commonly known as the alpine tarragon, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, herbaceous scent.

Artemisia ruprechtii is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
