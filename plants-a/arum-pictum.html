Arum pictum

Arum pictum, commonly known as the painted arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant produces an attractive spathe, which is a modified leaf that encloses the spadix, a dense cluster of tiny flowers. Arum pictum is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in humid, shaded environments with rich, well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage and dramatic inflorescence make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
