Acer polymorphum var. septemlobum

Acer polymorphum var. septemlobum, commonly known as the seven-lobed maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the maple genus.

It is characterized by its unique seven-lobed leaves, which set it apart from other maple species. Native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage exhibits a striking color change from bright green in summer to vibrant red or orange in autumn.

The tree typically grows as a small shrub or tree, with a compact form and smooth, gray bark.
