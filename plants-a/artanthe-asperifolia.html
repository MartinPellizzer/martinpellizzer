Artanthe asperifolia

Artanthe asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Artanthe asperifolia is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and modest floral display make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
