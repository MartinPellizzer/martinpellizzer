Antrophyum discoideum

Antrophyum discoideum, commonly known as the disc-shaped antrophyum, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, disc-like structure that resembles a plate or a shield. The plant typically grows as a solitary, upright stem with a broad, circular base. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Antrophyum discoideum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking and unusual form.
