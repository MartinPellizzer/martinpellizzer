Asarum savatieri subsp. pseudosavatieri

Asarum savatieri subsp.

pseudosavatieri, commonly known as the Vietnamese wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
