Anemone antucensis

Anemone antucensis, commonly known as the Antuco anemone, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb with showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, upright stem and basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
