Aniba intermedia

Aniba intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces black, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Aniba intermedia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic uses.
