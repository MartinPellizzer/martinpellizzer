Acer × neglectum var. elongatum

Acer × neglectum var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated horse chestnut, is a hybrid species within the maple family.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers in panicles during late spring, followed by spiny, woody fruits. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
