Armeria transmontana subsp. aristatula

Armeria transmontana subsp.

aristatula, commonly known as the purple sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

The plant produces dense clusters of pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its foliage consists of linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
