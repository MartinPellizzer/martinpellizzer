Aster bakerianus subsp. septentrionalis

Aster bakerianus subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern aster, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant features slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its showy blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer, consisting of ray florets surrounding a central disk.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
