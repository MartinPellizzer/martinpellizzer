Acer catalpifolium subsp. xinganense

Acer catalpifolium subsp.

xinganense, commonly known as the Xing'an maple, is a deciduous tree native to northeastern China. It is characterized by its palmate leaves that are typically 5-9 lobed, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

The Xing'an maple is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
