Acalypha accedens var. brachyandra

Acalypha accedens var. brachyandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, often displaying a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and compact growth habit.
