Astragalus lentiginosus var. wahweapensis

Astragalus lentiginosus var. wahweapensis, commonly known as the Wahweap milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as cliffs and canyon walls. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its scientific name reflects its specific geographic origin and is part of the broader Astragalus genus, which includes many species of milkvetches.
