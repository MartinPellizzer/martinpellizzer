Ampelocissus indica

Ampelocissus indica, commonly known as the Indian leafy vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine.
