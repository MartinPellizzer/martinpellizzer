Aleuritopteris papuana

Aleuritopteris papuana, commonly known as the Papuan water fern, is a species of fern native to New Guinea and nearby islands.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, intricate pattern of pinnae and pinnules. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rainforests or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are robust and help anchor it in wet soil, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aleuritopteris papuana is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional uses in the regions where it is found.
