Aspidium subaequale

Aspidium subaequale, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its large, compound fronds with a distinctive, broad shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with sporangia arranged in clusters on the underside.

Aspidium subaequale plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
