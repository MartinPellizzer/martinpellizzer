Atriplex campanulata var. inappendiculata

Atriplex campanulata var. inappendiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, glaucous leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are adapted to attract pollinators in harsh environments. It is well-suited to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils contribute to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
