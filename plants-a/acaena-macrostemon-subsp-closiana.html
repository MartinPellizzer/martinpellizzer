Acaena macrostemon subsp. closiana

Acaena macrostemon subsp.

closiana, commonly known as the New Zealand burr plant, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers followed by spiny, three-lobed fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

This species is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is often found in coastal and inland habitats.
