Ardisia atropurpurea

Ardisia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. These berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.
