Artanthe mikaniana

Artanthe mikaniana, commonly known as the Japanese artanthe, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Artanthe mikaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
