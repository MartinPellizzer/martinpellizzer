Aira caryophyllea var. biaristata

Aira caryophyllea var. biaristata, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and striking purple to pinkish flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other grass species in the genus Aira.
