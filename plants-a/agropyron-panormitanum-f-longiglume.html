Agropyron panormitanum f. longiglume

Agropyron panormitanum f. longiglume, commonly known as the long-leaved wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense tufts, with leaves that are typically narrow and slightly rolled. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are elongated and bear small, two-lobed glumes, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical traits make it suitable for both ornamental use and ecological restoration projects.
