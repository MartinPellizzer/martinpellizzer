Alyssum kotschyanum

Alyssum kotschyanum, commonly known as the Persian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Alyssum kotschyanum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
