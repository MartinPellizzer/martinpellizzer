Alchemilla gorodkovii

Alchemilla gorodkovii, commonly known as the lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla gorodkovii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
