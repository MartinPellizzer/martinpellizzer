Anthericum bipedunculatum

Anthericum bipedunculatum, commonly known as the two-stemmed arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a showy, greenish-purple spathe. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Anthericum bipedunculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
