Amaranthus emarginatus subsp. pseudogracilis

Amaranthus emarginatus subsp.

pseudogracilis, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically followed by spiny, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it is also cultivated as a leafy vegetable in some regions.
