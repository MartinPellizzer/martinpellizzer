Aspalathus tridentata subsp. staurantha

Aspalathus tridentata subsp.

staurantha, commonly known as the silver bush, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine.

The subspecies staurantha is distinguished by its four-lobed leaves and compact growth habit.
