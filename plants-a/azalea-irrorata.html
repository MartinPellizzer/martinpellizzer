Azalea irrorata

Azalea irrorata, commonly known as the silverleaf azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Azalea irrorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant blooms.
