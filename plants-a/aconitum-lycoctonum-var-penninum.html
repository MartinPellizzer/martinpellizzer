Aconitum lycoctonum var. penninum

Aconitum lycoctonum var. penninum, commonly known as the Swiss buttercups, is a variant of the aconite species native to the Alps.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.

Despite its toxicity, it is appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens.
