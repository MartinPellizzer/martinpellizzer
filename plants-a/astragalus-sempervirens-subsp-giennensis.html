Astragalus sempervirens subsp. giennensis

Astragalus sempervirens subsp.

giennensis, commonly known as the Giennese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in early summer. It is characterized by its erect stems and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
