Anthurium × excelsior

Anthurium × excelsior, commonly known as the flamingo flower, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Anthurium andraeanum and Anthurium scherzerianum.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy spathes that can range in color from red to orange, pink, and white, often with a contrasting midrib. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid environments. It has elongated, dark green, leathery leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Anthurium × excelsior is widely cultivated as a houseplant and for floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and aesthetic value.
