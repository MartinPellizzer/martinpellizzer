Anodontea

Anodontea, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

The plant relies on specific symbiotic relationships with fungi for nutrient absorption, as it lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis. Anodontea produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom once a year, making it a striking but elusive botanical specimen.
