Amethystanthus inflexus var. transticus

Amethystanthus inflexus var. transticus, commonly known as the Transkei amethyst flower, is a striking plant native to the Eastern Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and pink flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in rocky, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
