Aristida purpurea subsp. robusta

Aristida purpurea subsp.

robusta, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

This species is drought-tolerant and plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
