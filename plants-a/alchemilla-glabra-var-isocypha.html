Alchemilla glabra var. isocypha

Alchemilla glabra var. isocypha, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are typically borne on slender, upright stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild astringent properties.

The species is well adapted to moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
