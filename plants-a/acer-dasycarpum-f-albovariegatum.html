Acer dasycarpum f. albovariegatum

Acer dasycarpum f. albovariegatum, commonly known as the variegated maple, is a unique variant of the hairy maple.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white and yellow markings on the leaves. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas. Its leaves are palmately lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
