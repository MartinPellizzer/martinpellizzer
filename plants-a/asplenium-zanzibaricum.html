Asplenium zanzibaricum

Asplenium zanzibaricum, commonly known as Zanzibar maidenhair, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a dark green color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
