Aristolochia bracteata var. altissima

Aristolochia bracteata var. altissima, commonly known as the giant Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish-white in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
