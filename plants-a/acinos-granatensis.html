Acinos granatensis

Acinos granatensis, commonly known as the granate snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually basal and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.

Acinos granatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens that specialize in alpine flora.
