Acer brevipes

Acer brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive short, thick trunk and dense, rounded crown. The leaves are palmately lobed, with five to seven deep, narrow lobes, and turn bright red in the fall. It produces small, red or yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
