Artemisia norvegica subsp. glomerata

Artemisia norvegica subsp.

glomerata, commonly known as Norwegian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant has finely divided, gray-green foliage with a strong aromatic scent. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads, which give it its subspecific name "glomerata.".

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
