Abutilon haitiense

Abutilon haitiense, commonly known as the Haitian abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
