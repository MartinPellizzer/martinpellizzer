Asplenium pinfaense

Asplenium pinfaense, commonly known as the Pinfaense spleenwort, is a member of the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds.

It is a cryptogamic plant, meaning it reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a rosette-like structure with lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a thin layer of moss-like growth. Asplenium pinfaense is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and spore dispersal.
