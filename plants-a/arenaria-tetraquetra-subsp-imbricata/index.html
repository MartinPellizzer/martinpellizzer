Arenaria Tetraquetra Subsp. Imbricata

Arenaria tetraquetra subsp. imbricata is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific alpine regions. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. This subspecies thrives in rocky and gravelly soils, often found in mountainous environments. Due to its limited distribution and habitat requirements, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique botanical species.
