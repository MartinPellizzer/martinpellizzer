Aspalathus acanthoclada

Aspalathus acanthoclada, commonly known as the silver bush, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
