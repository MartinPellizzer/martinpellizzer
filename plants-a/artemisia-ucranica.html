Artemisia ucranica

Artemisia ucranica, commonly known as the Crimean wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It contains various essential oils and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in arid environments.
