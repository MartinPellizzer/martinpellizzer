Aconitum yuparense var. ito-seiyanum

Aconitum yuparense var. ito-seiyanum, commonly known as the Ito-seiyanum monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a hairy surface, and its stems are typically erect and sparsely branched. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
