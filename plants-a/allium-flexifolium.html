Allium flexifolium

Allium flexifolium, commonly known as the flexible leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is characterized by its bulbous base and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are long, slender, and often bend easily, giving it its common name.

Allium flexifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
