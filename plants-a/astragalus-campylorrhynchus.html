Astragalus campylorrhynchus

Astragalus campylorrhynchus, commonly known as the bent-nose milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Astragalus campylorrhynchus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
