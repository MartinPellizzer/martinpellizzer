Aster hyssopifolius var. rigidulus

Aster hyssopifolius var. rigidulus, commonly known as rigid aster, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
