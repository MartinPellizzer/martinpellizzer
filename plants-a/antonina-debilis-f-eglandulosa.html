Antonina debilis f. eglandulosa

Antonina debilis f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandless antonina, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glabrous. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This cultivar is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its delicate floral display.
