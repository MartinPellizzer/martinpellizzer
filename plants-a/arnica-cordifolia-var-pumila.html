Arnica cordifolia var. pumila

Arnica cordifolia var. pumila, commonly known as the alpine arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
