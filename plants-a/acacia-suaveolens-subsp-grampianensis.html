Acacia suaveolens subsp. grampianensis

Acacia suaveolens subsp.

grampianensis, commonly known as the Grampian acacia, is a shrub native to the Scottish Highlands. It features small, yellow, globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
