Argemone mexicana subvar. gynophora

Argemone mexicana subvar. gynophora, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in disturbed soils.

The subspecies gynophora is distinguished by its female flowers and is often found in regions of Mexico and the southwestern United States.
