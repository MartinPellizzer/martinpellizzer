Arctotis hypochondriaca

Arctotis hypochondriaca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, violet-blue ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets, creating a striking visual effect. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
