Asphodeline rhytidosperma

Asphodeline rhytidosperma, commonly known as the desert asphodel, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry conditions. The plant produces tall, slender flower stalks that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that reduces water loss and reflects sunlight.

Asphodeline rhytidosperma is well adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
