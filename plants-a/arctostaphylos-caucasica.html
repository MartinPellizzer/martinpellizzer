Arctostaphylos caucasica

Arctostaphylos caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian manzanita, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are typically reddish-brown to dark green. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, usually white or pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, shiny seeds.

Arctostaphylos caucasica is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with a Mediterranean climate.
