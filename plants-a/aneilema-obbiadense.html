Aneilema obbiadense

Aneilema obbiadense, commonly known as the Obbiadense arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swamps and riverbanks. Its rhizomes are often used for their starchy content, making it a valuable food source.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique flowering structure.
