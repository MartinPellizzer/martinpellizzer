Asplenium aethiopicum subsp. filare

Asplenium aethiopicum subsp.

filare, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family. It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds.

The plant features a rhizomatous structure that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its fronds are typically divided into lance-shaped segments with a distinctive shield-like appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to various regions in Africa and parts of Asia.
