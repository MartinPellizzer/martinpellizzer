Alopecurus ciliatus

Alopecurus ciliatus, commonly known as mouse ear grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing a single floret.

Alopecurus ciliatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
