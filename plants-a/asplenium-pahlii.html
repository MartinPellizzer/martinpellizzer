Asplenium pahlii

Asplenium pahlii, commonly known as the Pahli's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, humid environments. The plant's spores are released from sporangia located on the undersides of its fertile fronds.

Asplenium pahlii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor settings.
