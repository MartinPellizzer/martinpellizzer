Aristolochia williamsii

Aristolochia williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its striking, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
