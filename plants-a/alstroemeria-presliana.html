Alstroemeria presliana

Alstroemeria presliana, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. Its leaves are long, narrow, and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Alstroemeria presliana is also valued for its ability to bloom throughout the year in suitable climates.
