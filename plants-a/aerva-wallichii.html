Aerva wallichii

Aerva wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan borage, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant is adapted to cold and arid environments, with thick leaves that help reduce water loss. It produces small, nutlet-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Aerva wallichii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
