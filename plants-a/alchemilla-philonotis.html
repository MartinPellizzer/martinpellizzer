Alchemilla philonotis

Alchemilla philonotis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla philonotis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
