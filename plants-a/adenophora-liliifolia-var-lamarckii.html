Adenophora liliifolia var. lamarckii

Adenophora liliifolia var. lamarckii, commonly known as the Lamarck's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are typically surrounded by a series of bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
