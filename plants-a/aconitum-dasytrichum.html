Aconitum dasytrichum

Aconitum dasytrichum, commonly known as the hairy monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features striking, hooded blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
