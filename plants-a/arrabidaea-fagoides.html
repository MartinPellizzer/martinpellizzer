Arrabidaea fagoides

Arrabidaea fagoides, commonly known as the African orchid or the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are borne on long, drooping inflorescences.
