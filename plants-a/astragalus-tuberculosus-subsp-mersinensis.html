Astragalus tuberculosus subsp. mersinensis

Astragalus tuberculosus subsp.

mersinensis, commonly known as the Mersin hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Turkey. It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, with small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
