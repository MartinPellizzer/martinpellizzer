Austromyrtus pubiflora

Austromyrtus pubiflora, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Austromyrtus pubiflora is an important plant in coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
