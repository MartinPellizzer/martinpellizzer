Acrostichum caudatum

Acrostichum caudatum, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately divided leaves that are typically bright green with a golden hue on the underside. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories. Its fronds are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

Acrostichum caudatum plays a role in providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
