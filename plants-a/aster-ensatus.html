Aster ensatus

Aster ensatus, commonly known as the hooked aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Aster ensatus is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
