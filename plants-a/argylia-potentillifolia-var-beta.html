Argylia potentillifolia var. beta

Argylia potentillifolia var. beta, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features a dense mat of silver-gray leaves that reflect sunlight, aiding in reducing water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, rocky soils.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
