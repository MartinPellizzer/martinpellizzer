Abies sibirica f. alpina

Abies sibirica f. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine Siberian fir, is a variant of the Siberian fir native to mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree with needle-like leaves that are typically shorter and more densely packed than those of the typical species. The cones are small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments, exhibiting cold tolerance and slow growth.

Its foliage and structure make it a distinctive feature of alpine forests and rocky slopes.
