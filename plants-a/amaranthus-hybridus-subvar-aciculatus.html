Amaranthus hybridus subvar. aciculatus

Amaranthus hybridus subvar. aciculatus, commonly known as the needleleaf amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or greenish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are followed by triangular, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed areas.

This species is also valued for its nutritional content and is used in some traditional cuisines.
