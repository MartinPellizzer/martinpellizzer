Astroloma pallidum var. suberectum

Astroloma pallidum var. suberectum, commonly known as the silver leaf astroloma, is a low-growing shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in native gardens.
