Aconitum rhynchanthum var. bicolor

Aconitum rhynchanthum var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers with yellow markings. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces follicles containing toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

However, all parts of the plant contain potent alkaloids and are highly toxic to humans and animals.
