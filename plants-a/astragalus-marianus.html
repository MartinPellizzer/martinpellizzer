Astragalus marianus

Astragalus marianus, commonly known as the Marian's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus marianus is also of interest in botanical research due to its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
