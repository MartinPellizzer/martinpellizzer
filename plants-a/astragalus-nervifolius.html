Astragalus nervifolius

Astragalus nervifolius, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and compound leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine and are known for their potential health benefits.

Astragalus nervifolius thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
