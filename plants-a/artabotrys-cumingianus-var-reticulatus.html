Artabotrys cumingianus var. reticulatus

Artabotrys cumingianus var. reticulatus, commonly known as the reticulate beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a mild, slightly bitter taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
