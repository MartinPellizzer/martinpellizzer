Aspidium domingense

Aspidium domingense, commonly known as the Mexican shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a robust rhizome with clustered fronds that are leathery and often have a distinctive shield-like shape. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, linear pinnae.

Aspidium domingense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
