Apargia scabra

Apargia scabra, commonly known as the rough apargia, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in late spring, which are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are often covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its scientific name "scabra," meaning rough in Latin.
