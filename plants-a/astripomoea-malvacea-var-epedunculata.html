Astripomoea malvacea var. epedunculata

Astripomoea malvacea var. epedunculata, commonly known as the slender hibiscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, five-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and often have a hairy surface. This variant is distinguished by its elongated peduncles, which support the inflorescences.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
