Aripuana

Aripuana, commonly known as the "Brazilian rosewood," is a species of tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dense, dark wood with a rich, reddish-brown color. The plant has a straight trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

Aripuana flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, woody fruits containing seeds.
