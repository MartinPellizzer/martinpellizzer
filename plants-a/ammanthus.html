Ammanthus

Ammanthus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a genus of aquatic plants native to Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, erect stalks above the water surface. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically floating or submerged, depending on the species. Ammanthus species are often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.

They play an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
