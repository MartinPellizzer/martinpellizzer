Aucuba obcordata

Aucuba obcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aucuba, is a shrub native to East Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Aucuba obcordata is known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate urban conditions, contributing to its ornamental value.
