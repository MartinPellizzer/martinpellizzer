Acacia pharangites

Acacia pharangites, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, needle-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular heads, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia pharangites is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
