Aloe × thammii

Aloe × thammii, commonly known as the hybrid aloe or African aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps it store water. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with tubular red or orange flowers, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe × thammii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
