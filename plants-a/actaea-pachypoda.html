Actaea pachypoda

Actaea pachypoda, commonly known as the white cohosh, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a thick, fleshy root system.

Its berries are red and toxic, containing compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine.
