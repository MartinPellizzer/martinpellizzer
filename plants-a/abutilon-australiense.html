Abutilon australiense

Abutilon australiense, commonly known as the Australian velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
