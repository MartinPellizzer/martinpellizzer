Arisarum multicarinatum

Arisarum multicarinatum, commonly known as the multilined arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe. The plant's leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tint, especially in shaded environments. It thrives in moist, rich soil and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

The species is noted for its unique botanical structure and ecological role in wetland habitats.
