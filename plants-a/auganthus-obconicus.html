Auganthus obconicus

Auganthus obconicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Auganthus obconicus is known for its long blooming period, typically from late winter to early spring.
