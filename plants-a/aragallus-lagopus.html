Aragallus lagopus

Aragallus lagopus, commonly known as the snow lotus, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of silver-blue, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties and rarity, it is considered an important species in traditional medicine.
