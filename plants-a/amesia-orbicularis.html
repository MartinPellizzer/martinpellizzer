Amesia orbicularis

Amesia orbicularis, commonly known as the roundleaf amesia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pink. The leaves are typically round or oval, with a soft, fuzzy texture, and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
