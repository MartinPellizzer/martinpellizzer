Aristida macroclada subsp. queenslandica

Aristida macroclada subsp.

queenslandica, commonly known as Queensland grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and sandy soils.

The plant has slender, erect culms with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
