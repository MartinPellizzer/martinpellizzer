Amyris agallocha

Amyris agallocha, commonly known as the stinkwood or sweet myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, aromatic fruits that contain seeds. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Amyris agallocha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
