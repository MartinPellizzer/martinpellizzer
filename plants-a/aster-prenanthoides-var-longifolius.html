Aster prenanthoides var. longifolius

Aster prenanthoides var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

The species is characterized by its elongated foliage and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
