Aeluropus mucronatus var. erythraeus

Aeluropus mucronatus var. erythraeus, commonly known as the Red Sea grass, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, dense vegetation. The plant exhibits a compact growth form with short, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is typically found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and tidal flats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Its ability to thrive in high-salt conditions makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
