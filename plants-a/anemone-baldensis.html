Anemone baldensis

Anemone baldensis, commonly known as the bald mountain anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. Anemone baldensis is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the forest understory ecosystem.
