Aster dracunculoides

Aster dracunculoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or wetlands.

Aster dracunculoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
