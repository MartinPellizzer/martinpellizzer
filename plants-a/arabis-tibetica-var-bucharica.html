Arabis tibetica var. bucharica

Arabis tibetica var. bucharica, commonly known as Tibetan rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, bloom in early spring and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits.
