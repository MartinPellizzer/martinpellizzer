Acer negundo f. aureomaculatum

Acer negundo f. aureomaculatum, commonly known as the golden-spotted maple, is a cultivar of the boxelder tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring yellow spots on otherwise green leaves. The tree produces small, samaroid fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental tree is often used in landscaping for its unique visual appeal.
