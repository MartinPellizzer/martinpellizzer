Acer platanoides f. incumbens

Acer platanoides f. incumbens, commonly known as the European mountain maple, is a variant of the European maple.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions across Europe, preferring well-drained soils and cooler climates. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring, followed by samaras that disperse its seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
