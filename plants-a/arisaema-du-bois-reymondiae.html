Arisaema du-bois-reymondiae

Arisaema du-bois-reymondiae, commonly known as the Du Bois-Reymond's arisaema, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a unique, toxic, and visually striking flower structure that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
