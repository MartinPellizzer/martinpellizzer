Asparagus asparagoides

Asparagus asparagoides, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often used for soil stabilization due to its extensive root system and ability to thrive in poor soils.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
