Artemisia trifurcata subsp. tacomensis

Artemisia trifurcata subsp.

tacomensis, commonly known as Tacomensis wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and slender, branching stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
