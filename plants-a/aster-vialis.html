Aster vialis

Aster vialis, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme, and the plant is often found in moist meadows and along riverbanks.

Aster vialis is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
