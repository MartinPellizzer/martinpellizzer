Alyxia lucida var. meiantha

Alyxia lucida var. meiantha, commonly known as the white-flowered alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, and it is often found in moist, wooded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
