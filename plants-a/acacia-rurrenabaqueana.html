Acacia rurrenabaqueana

Acacia rurrenabaqueana, commonly known as the Rurrenabaque acacia, is a tree native to the Amazon rainforest of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to the humid and nutrient-poor soils of the region, and it plays a role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
