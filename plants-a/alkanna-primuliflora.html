Alkanna primuliflora

Alkanna primuliflora, commonly known as the Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. The plant produces a distinctive red dye from its roots, which has been historically used in textile and cosmetic industries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Alkanna primuliflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
