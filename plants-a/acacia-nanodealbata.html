Acacia nanodealbata

Acacia nanodealbata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small shrub or tree native to south-eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Acacia nanodealbata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
