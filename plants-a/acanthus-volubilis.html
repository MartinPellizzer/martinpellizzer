Acanthus volubilis

Acanthus volubilis, commonly known as the creeping candleplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has a trailing growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a soft, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
