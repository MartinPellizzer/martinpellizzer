Astragalus somcheticus

Astragalus somcheticus, commonly known as the Caucasian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, including parts of Georgia, Russia, and Turkey. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its small, yellow or pale purple flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus somcheticus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
