Allophylus sechellensis

Allophylus sechellensis, commonly known as the Seychelles hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Seychelles archipelago.

It is a shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a fleshy, white aril. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Allophylus sechellensis is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native ecosystem.
