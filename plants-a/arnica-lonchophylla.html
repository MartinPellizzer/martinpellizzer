Arnica lonchophylla

Arnica lonchophylla, commonly known as the mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent yellow centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Arnica lonchophylla is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
