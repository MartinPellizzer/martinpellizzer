Adenocarpus bracteatus

Adenocarpus bracteatus, commonly known as the African thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, inflated fruit capsules. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, with a rough, hairy texture.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
