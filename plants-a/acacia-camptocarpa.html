Acacia camptocarpa

Acacia camptocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and feathery, and its yellow flowers that appear in globular or cylindrical clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia camptocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardy nature and seasonal floral displays.
