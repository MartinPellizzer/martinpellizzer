Allium dirphianum

Allium dirphianum, commonly known as the Dirphian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Allium dirphianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local folklore.
