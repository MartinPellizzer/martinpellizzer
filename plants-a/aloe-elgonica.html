Aloe elgonica

Aloe elgonica, commonly known as Elgon aloe, is a succulent plant native to the highland regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, yellowish flowers, which bloom during the dry season. Aloe elgonica is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for cultivation in dry climates.

Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that has been traditionally used for its soothing and healing properties.
