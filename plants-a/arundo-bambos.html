Arundo bambos

Arundo bambos, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as riverbanks, marshes, and wetlands. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an effective erosion control plant.

However, it can also become invasive in non-native regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
