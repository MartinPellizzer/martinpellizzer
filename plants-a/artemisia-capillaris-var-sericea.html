Artemisia capillaris var. sericea

Artemisia capillaris var. sericea, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
