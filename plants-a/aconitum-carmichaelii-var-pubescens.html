Aconitum carmichaelii var. pubescens

Aconitum carmichaelii var. pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in tall, sparsely branched panicles, which bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with a distinctive notched appearance, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
