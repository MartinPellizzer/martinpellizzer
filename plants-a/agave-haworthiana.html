Agave haworthiana

Agave haworthiana, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, blue-green leaves that are lined with sharp spines. The plant produces a tall, branched flower stalk that blooms with yellow flowers, typically in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Its rosette form and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
