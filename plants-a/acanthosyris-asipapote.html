Acanthosyris asipapote

Acanthosyris asipapote, commonly known as the Mexican bristly cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well-suited for desert life.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
