Arachnothryx Calycosa

Arachnothryx calycosa is a striking member of the Rubiaceae family known for its unique appearance and ecological significance. This evergreen shrub is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. Its name derives from the Greek words "arachne" meaning spider and "thryx" meaning hair, referring to its hairy leaves and thorny stems. The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it has gained attention among botanists and conservationists alike.
