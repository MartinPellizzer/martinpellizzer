Anisopappus burundiensis

Anisopappus burundiensis, commonly known as the Burundi goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Burundi.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months.

Anisopappus burundiensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability in its habitat.
