Arnica chamissonis var. interior

Arnica chamissonis var. interior, commonly known as the interior arnica, is a perennial herb native to the central and northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, though its use requires caution due to potential skin irritation.
