Astragalus delbesii var. bracteosus

Astragalus delbesii var. bracteosus, commonly known as the bracteated milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The varietal form bracteosus is distinguished by its distinctive bracts that surround the flower cluster.
