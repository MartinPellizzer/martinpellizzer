Acer miyabei f. shibatae

Acer miyabei f. shibatae, commonly known as the Japanese maple with variegated leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple species.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring a mix of green, white, and yellow colors. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
