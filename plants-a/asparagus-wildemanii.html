Asparagus wildemanii

Asparagus wildemanii, commonly known as the wild asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often consumed by birds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
