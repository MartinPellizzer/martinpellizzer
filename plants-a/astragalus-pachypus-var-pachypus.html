Astragalus pachypus var. pachypus

Astragalus pachypus var. pachypus, commonly known as the thick-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and palmately divided leaves that are often thick and leathery. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments.

Astragalus pachypus var. pachypus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
