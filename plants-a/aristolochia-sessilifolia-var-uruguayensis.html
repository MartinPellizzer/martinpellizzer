Aristolochia sessilifolia var. uruguayensis

Aristolochia sessilifolia var. uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay arrowroot, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, sessile leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent. The plant produces intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, smooth seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
