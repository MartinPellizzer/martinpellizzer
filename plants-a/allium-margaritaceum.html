Allium margaritaceum

Allium margaritaceum, commonly known as the pearl onion garlic, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant produces small, round bulbs that resemble pearls, hence its common name. Its leaves are grass-like and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

Allium margaritaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like aroma.
