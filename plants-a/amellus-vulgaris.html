Amellus vulgaris

Amellus vulgaris, commonly known as the common nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

Amellus vulgaris is known for its stinging hairs that cause irritation upon contact with the skin.
