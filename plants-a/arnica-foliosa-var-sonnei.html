Arnica foliosa var. sonnei

Arnica foliosa var. sonnei, commonly known as Sonne's leafy arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

Arnica foliosa var. sonnei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
