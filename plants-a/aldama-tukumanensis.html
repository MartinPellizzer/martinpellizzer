Aldama tukumanensis

Aldama tukumanensis, commonly known as the Tukumanensis Aldama, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
