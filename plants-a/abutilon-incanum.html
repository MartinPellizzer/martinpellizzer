Abutilon incanum

Abutilon incanum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly abutilon, is a shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in warm, humid climates. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural fields and gardens.
