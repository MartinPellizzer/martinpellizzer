Abutilon oxycarpum var. oxycarpum

Abutilon oxycarpum var. oxycarpum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its fruits are small, round capsules that split open to release seeds.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
