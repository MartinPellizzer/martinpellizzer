Anthericum dissitiflorum

Anthericum dissitiflorum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Anthericum dissitiflorum is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its drought tolerance.
