Aster macronema

Aster macronema, commonly known as the showy aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, violet-blue flowers bloom in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster macronema is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
