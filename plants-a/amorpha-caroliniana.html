Amorpha caroliniana

Amorpha caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina silverbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
