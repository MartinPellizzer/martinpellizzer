Astragalus siculus subsp. plumosus

Astragalus siculus subsp.

plumosus, commonly known as the Sicilian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its feathery, plume-like seed pods, which give it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This plant plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
