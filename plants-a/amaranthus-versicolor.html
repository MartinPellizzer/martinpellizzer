Amaranthus versicolor

Amaranthus versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow amaranth, is a strikingly colorful flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored bracts that range from red, orange, and yellow to purple and green, creating a dazzling display. The plant is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible species. Its leaves are broad and often used in traditional cuisines, while the flowers are typically small and inconspicuous compared to the showy bracts.

Amaranthus versicolor thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
