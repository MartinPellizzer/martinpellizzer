Avena cespitosa f. vivipara

Avena cespitosa f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous oat, is a variant of the common oat characterized by its unique reproductive feature.

This plant produces viviparous seeds, which germinate while still attached to the parent plant, allowing for rapid propagation. It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and forms dense, grass-like clumps. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets.

This form is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both natural and cultivated environments.
