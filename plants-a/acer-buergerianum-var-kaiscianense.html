Acer buergerianum var. kaiscianense

Acer buergerianum var. kaiscianense, commonly known as the Kaisi maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the maple genus native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
