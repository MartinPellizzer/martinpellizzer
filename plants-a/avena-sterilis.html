Avena sterilis

Avena sterilis, commonly known as goat grass, is a wild relative of cultivated oats.

It is a grass species native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, each containing two to three florets.

Avena sterilis is characterized by its small, wind-pollinated flowers and produces small, dry, three-angled seeds.
