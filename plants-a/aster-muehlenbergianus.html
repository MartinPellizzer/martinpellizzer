Aster muehlenbergianus

Aster muehlenbergianus, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray flowers and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. Its vibrant purple to pink flower heads bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
