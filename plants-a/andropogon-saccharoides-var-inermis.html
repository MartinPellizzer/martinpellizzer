Andropogon saccharoides var. inermis

Andropogon saccharoides var. inermis, commonly known as inland sandreed, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and dunes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
