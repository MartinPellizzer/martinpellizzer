Astragalus anisacanthus subsp. schurabicus

Astragalus anisacanthus subsp.

schurabicus, commonly known as the Schurabik's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in late summer. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect or ascending growth habit.

This subspecies is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
