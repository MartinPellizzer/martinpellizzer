Astragalus vallestris

Astragalus vallestris, commonly known as the Sierra milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow to cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions, and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds.

Astragalus vallestris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
