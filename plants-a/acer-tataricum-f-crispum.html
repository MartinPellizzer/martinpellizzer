Acer tataricum f. crispum

Acer tataricum f. crispum, commonly known as the curly-tailed maple, is a unique variant of the Tatarian maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated, and often curling leaves that give it an ornamental appearance. Native to eastern Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens. The leaves turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to landscaping.
