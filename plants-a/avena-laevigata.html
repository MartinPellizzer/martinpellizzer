Avena laevigata

Avena laevigata, commonly known as silky oat, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and habitat restoration.
