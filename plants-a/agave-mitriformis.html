Agave mitriformis

Agave mitriformis, commonly known as the Mitreleaf Agave, is a species of succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern, with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are adorned with white to yellow marginal teeth, which give the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of small, white flowers, which are often followed by the development of a fruit.

This plant is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
