Arcytophyllum filiforme

Arcytophyllum filiforme, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features a mat-forming habit with slender, thread-like stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white, fuzzy hairs that help reduce water loss.

Arcytophyllum filiforme is well-suited for xerophytic conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
