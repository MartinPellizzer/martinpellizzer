Arctium hamosum

Arctium hamosum, commonly known as the greater burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble small cones, which are followed by spiny, bur-like fruits. Its root is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Arctium hamosum thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found along roadsides and in meadows.
