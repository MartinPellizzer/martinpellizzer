Agave lophantha var. gracilior

Agave lophantha var. gracilior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and thick, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
