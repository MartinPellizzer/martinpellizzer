Aristolochia aethiopica

Aristolochia aethiopica, commonly known as the African birthwort, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition, including various alkaloids. It is often used in traditional medicine, though its use is controversial due to potential toxicity.

The plant's unique morphology and medicinal properties make it an intriguing subject in botanical studies.
