Aquilegia vulgaris var. bernardii

Aquilegia vulgaris var. bernardii, commonly known as the Bernard's Columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
