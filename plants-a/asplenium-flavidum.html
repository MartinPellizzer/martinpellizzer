Asplenium flavidum

Asplenium flavidum, commonly known as the yellow holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.

Asplenium flavidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
