Anthyllis vulneraria f. lindbergii

Anthyllis vulneraria f. lindbergii, commonly known as the Lindberg's Scotch thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
