Acrostichum adenolepis

Acrostichum adenolepis, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and storing nutrients.

Acrostichum adenolepis is well adapted to the humid, temperate climate of its native habitat.
