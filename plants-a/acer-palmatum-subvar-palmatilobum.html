Acer palmatum subvar. palmatilobum

Acer palmatum subvar. palmatilobum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with palmate lobes, is a variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features finely dissected, palmate leaves that are typically a vibrant green in summer and turn shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in partial shade with well-drained, acidic soil. It is a slow-growing deciduous tree or shrub that can reach heights of up to 6 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
