Arundinaria minomarsa var. lasioclada

Arundinaria minomarsa var. lasioclada, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo genus characterized by its slender, hollow stems.

It is native to Japan and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in forested areas. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, waxy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is often cultivated in gardens for both its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
