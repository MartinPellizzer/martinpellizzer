Abies veitchii f. olivacea

Abies veitchii f. olivacea, commonly known as the olive-foliage white fir, is a rare and ornamental variant of the white fir.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance compared to the typical dark green needles of the species. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of western China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and unique coloration.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat challenging plant to grow.
