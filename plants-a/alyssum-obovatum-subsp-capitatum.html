Alyssum obovatum subsp. capitatum

Alyssum obovatum subsp.

capitatum, commonly known as the clustered alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white to pale pink.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long blooming period.
