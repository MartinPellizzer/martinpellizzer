Abronia umbellata var. acutalata

Abronia umbellata var. acutalata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in umbels. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, giving the plant its "sharp-leaved" designation. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often found in desert scrub and creosote bush communities.
