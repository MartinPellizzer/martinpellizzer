Allium ampeloprasum var. paterfamilias

Allium ampeloprasum var. paterfamilias, commonly known as the giant garlic or elephant garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, bulbous cloves that can grow up to 10 centimeters in diameter, with a strong, pungent aroma. The plant produces tall, hollow stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its flowers are typically purple or pink, forming in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and culinary use.

This variety is valued for its robust flavor and is often used in cooking and traditional medicine.
