Abies nobilis var. robusta

Abies nobilis var. robusta, commonly known as the white fir, is a variant of the noble fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, soft needles that are typically a silvery-blue or dark green on top. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing protection against environmental stress.

This species is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and forestry.
