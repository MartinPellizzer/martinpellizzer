Aloe inamara

Aloe inamara, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Aloe inamara is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its gel-like pulp is sometimes used for its potential medicinal properties, though it is less commonly cultivated than other aloe species.
