Aspalathus acidota

Aspalathus acidota, commonly known as the sour bush, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Aspalathus acidota is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
