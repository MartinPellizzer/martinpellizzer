Anthurium nigrolaminum

Anthurium nigrolaminum, commonly known as the black-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking dark green, velvety leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of veins. The plant produces clusters of spadices, which are typically yellow or orange, surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium nigrolaminum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
