Agropyron patulum

Agropyron patulum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold climates.

Agropyron patulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
