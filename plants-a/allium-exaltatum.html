Allium exaltatum

Allium exaltatum, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
