Aristida coerulescens var. arabica

Aristida coerulescens var. arabica, commonly known as bluegrass lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and drought tolerance.
