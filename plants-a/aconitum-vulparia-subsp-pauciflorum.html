Aconitum vulparia subsp. pauciflorum

Aconitum vulparia subsp.

pauciflorum, commonly known as the lesser aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in loose racemes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat fern-like appearance.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
