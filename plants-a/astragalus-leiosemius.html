Astragalus leiosemius

Astragalus leiosemius, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils with low moisture availability.

Astragalus leiosemius is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
