Anaphalis latialata

Anaphalis latialata, commonly known as the silverleaf frostflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a silvery appearance, and its flowers are small, clustered, and often white or pale pink. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions. It produces a unique, frost-like bloom that appears during the brief summer season.

Anaphalis latialata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
