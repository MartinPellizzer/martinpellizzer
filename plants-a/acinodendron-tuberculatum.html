Acinodendron tuberculatum

Acinodendron tuberculatum, commonly known as the rough-leaved acinodendron, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Acinodendron tuberculatum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
