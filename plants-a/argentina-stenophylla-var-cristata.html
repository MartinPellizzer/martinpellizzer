Argentina stenophylla var. cristata

Argentina stenophylla var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate stenophylla, is a unique variant of the stenophylla plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and striking, flattened, ribbed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its foliage is typically a vibrant green, with some cultivars displaying variegation or a bluish tint.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.
