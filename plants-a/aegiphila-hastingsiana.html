Aegiphila hastingsiana

Aegiphila hastingsiana, commonly known as the Hastings' aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
