Ancistrocactus tobuschii

Ancistrocactus tobuschii, commonly known as the Tobeck's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a ribbed stem that can reach up to 30 inches in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is often cultivated in desert gardens for its ornamental value.
