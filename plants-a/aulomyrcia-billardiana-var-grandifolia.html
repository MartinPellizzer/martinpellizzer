Aulomyrcia billardiana var. grandifolia

Aulomyrcia billardiana var. grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflora aulomyrcia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in tropical regions.
