Abies spectabilis

Abies spectabilis, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color and are arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are typically pendulous and contain small, winged seeds.

The white fir is adapted to a variety of forest habitats and plays an important role in ecological systems.
