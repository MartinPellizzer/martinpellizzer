Allophylus ligustrinus

Allophylus ligustrinus, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the winter season. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and serve as a food source for various birds and mammals. Allophylus ligustrinus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
