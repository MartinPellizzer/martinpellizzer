Anthocercis genistoides

Anthocercis genistoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight serration along the edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
