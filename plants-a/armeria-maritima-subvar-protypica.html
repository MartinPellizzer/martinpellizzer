Armeria maritima subvar. protypica

Armeria maritima subvar. protypica, commonly known as sea thrift, is a hardy, low-growing plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features compact, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of pink or white flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal environments. Its succulent leaves help it retain moisture in arid conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize dunes.
