Allocarya salina

Allocarya salina, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal areas or salt marshes. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a distinctive, often aromatic scent.

Allocarya salina plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
