Aleuritia frondosa

Aleuritia frondosa, commonly known as the broadleaf alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and stream banks. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits.

Aleuritia frondosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
