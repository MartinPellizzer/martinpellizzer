Amictonis japonica

Amictonis japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Amictonis japonica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
