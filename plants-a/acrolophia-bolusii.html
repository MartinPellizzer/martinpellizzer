Acrolophia bolusii

Acrolophia bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that resemble small, intricate bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with good drainage. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the summer months.

Acrolophia bolusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
