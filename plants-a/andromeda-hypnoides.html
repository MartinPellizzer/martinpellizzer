Andromeda hypnoides

Andromeda hypnoides, commonly known as the creeping andromeda, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Andromeda hypnoides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
