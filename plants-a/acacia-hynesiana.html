Acacia hynesiana

Acacia hynesiana, commonly known as the Hynes' acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and savanna habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
