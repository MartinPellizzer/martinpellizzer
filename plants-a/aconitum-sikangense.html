Aconitum sikangense

Aconitum sikangense, commonly known as the Yunnan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of southwestern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant has deeply lobed, palmately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
