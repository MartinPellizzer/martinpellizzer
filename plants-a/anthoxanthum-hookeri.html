Anthoxanthum hookeri

Anthoxanthum hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green, with a slight bluish tinge. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Anthoxanthum hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
