Artemisia olgensis

Artemisia olgensis, commonly known as the Korean mugwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a seasoning in some cuisines.

Artemisia olgensis is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
