Arabis sparsiflora var. columbiana

Arabis sparsiflora var. columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to cold climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
