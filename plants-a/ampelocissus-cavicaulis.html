Ampelocissus cavicaulis

Ampelocissus cavicaulis, commonly known as the slender leaf fig, is a climbing plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.

The species is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some regions.
