Alcea fasciculiflora

Alcea fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces clusters of flowers along the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Alcea fasciculiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
