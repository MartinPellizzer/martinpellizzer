Agrostemma hirsutum

Agrostemma hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features upright stems with hairy leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, rounded seeds.

Agrostemma hirsutum is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
