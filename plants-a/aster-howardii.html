Aster howardii

Aster howardii, commonly known as the Howards aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to blue petals. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Aster howardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
