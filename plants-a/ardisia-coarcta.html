Ardisia coarcta

Ardisia coarcta, commonly known as the dwarf evergreen ardisia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along its stems. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are a characteristic feature of the genus Ardisia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, making it important to handle the plant with care.
