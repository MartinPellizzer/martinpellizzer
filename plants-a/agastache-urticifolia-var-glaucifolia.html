Agastache urticifolia var. glaucifolia

Agastache urticifolia var. glaucifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a distinctive blue-green hue. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
