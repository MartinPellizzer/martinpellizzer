Aquilegia oxysepala f. pallidiflora

Aquilegia oxysepala f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered rockcress, is a variant of the genus Aquilegia, characterized by its delicate, biennial or perennial growth habit.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically pale in color, often with a distinct yellow or greenish hue, which sets it apart from the more vibrant flowers of other Aquilegia species. The plant thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of basal leaves with deeply lobed or divided blades, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and adaptability to challenging garden conditions.
