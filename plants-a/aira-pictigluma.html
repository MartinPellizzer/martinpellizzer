Aira pictigluma

Aira pictigluma, commonly known as the painted air plant, is a striking ornamental grass native to Mexico.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with vibrant red, pink, or purple hues, especially along the edges. The plant produces showy, feathery flower spikes that range in color from white to deep red, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Aira pictigluma is drought-tolerant and makes an excellent choice for rock gardens or as a container plant.
