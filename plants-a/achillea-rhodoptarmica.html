Achillea rhodoptarmica

Achillea rhodoptarmica, commonly known as the pink yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and slightly fuzzy to the touch.

Achillea rhodoptarmica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to dry, rocky soils.
