Aconitum subcuneatum

Aconitum subcuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a robust root system and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum subcuneatum is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
