Asplenium lobbianum

Asplenium lobbianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces spores in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Asplenium lobbianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
