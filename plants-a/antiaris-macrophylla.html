Antiaris macrophylla

Antiaris macrophylla, commonly known as the Natal tree or elephant tree, is a large, evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically large and elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red fruits containing three to five seeds. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resistance to drought.
