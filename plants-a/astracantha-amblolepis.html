Astracantha amblolepis

Astracantha amblolepis, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, bead-like seed pods. It is adapted to arid environments and has a milky sap that can be toxic.

Astracantha amblolepis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
