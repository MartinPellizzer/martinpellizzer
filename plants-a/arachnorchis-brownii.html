Arachnorchis brownii

Arachnorchis brownii, commonly known as the Brown's spider-orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the southeastern region of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a unique floral structure that resembles a spider's legs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and undergrowth. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, which bloom during the cooler months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
