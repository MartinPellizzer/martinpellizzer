Artocarpus dadah var. celebica

Artocarpus dadah var. celebica, commonly known as the Celebes breadfruit, is a species native to the Moluccas and surrounding islands in Indonesia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, palmate leaves. The fruit is typically round or oval, with a thick, fibrous rind and a starchy, edible interior. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by the development of the fruit.

This plant is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both subsistence and commercial purposes.
