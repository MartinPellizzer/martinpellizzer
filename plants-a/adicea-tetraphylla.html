Adicea tetraphylla

Adicea tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leafed arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a distinct four-leaf appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in wetlands, ponds, and along the edges of streams. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellowish-green color.

Adicea tetraphylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
