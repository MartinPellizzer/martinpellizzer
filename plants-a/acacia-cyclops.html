Acacia cyclops

Acacia cyclops, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which consists of small, rounded phyllodes that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Acacia cyclops is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
