Armeniaca provincialis

Armeniaca provincialis, commonly known as the Armenian apricot, is a species of Prunus native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and reddish-orange fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated edge, and it produces small, woody fruits that are edible when fully mature.

Armeniaca provincialis is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.
