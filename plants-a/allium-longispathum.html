Allium longispathum

Allium longispathum, commonly known as the long-fringed garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Allium genus, related to onions and garlic.
