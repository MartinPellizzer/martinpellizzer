Acorus commersonii

Acorus commersonii, commonly known as narrow-leaf sweet flag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia and North America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically sword-shaped and can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Acorus commersonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
