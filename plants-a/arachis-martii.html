Arachis martii

Arachis martii, commonly known as the Brazilian peanut or wild peanut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and develops pods containing edible seeds similar to those of the common peanut. Its botanical characteristics include compound leaves and a fibrous root system.

Arachis martii is often studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological applications.
