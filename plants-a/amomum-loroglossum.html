Amomum loroglossum

Amomum loroglossum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are typically yellow or orange in color.

The rhizomes of Amomum loroglossum are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
