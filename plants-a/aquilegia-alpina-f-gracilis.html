Aquilegia alpina f. gracilis

Aquilegia alpina f. gracilis, commonly known as the Alpine garden bell, is a botanical variant of the alpine columbine.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features delicate, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a fine, fern-like appearance, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in rocky or alpine conditions.
