Aconitum rotundifolium subsp. rotundifolium

Aconitum rotundifolium subsp.

rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaved aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in tall, spikelike clusters. Its leaves are palmately divided, with rounded lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
