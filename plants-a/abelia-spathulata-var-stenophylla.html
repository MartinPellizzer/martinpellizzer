Abelia spathulata var. stenophylla

Abelia spathulata var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are narrower than those of the typical spathulata variety. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
