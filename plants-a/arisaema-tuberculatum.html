Arisaema tuberculatum

Arisaema tuberculatum, commonly known as the knobbed arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the spadix.

Arisaema tuberculatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
