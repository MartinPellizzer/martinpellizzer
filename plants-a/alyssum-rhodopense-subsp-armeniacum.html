Alyssum rhodopense subsp. armeniacum

Alyssum rhodopense subsp.

armeniacum, commonly known as Armenian alyssum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Alyssum rhodopense subsp.

armeniacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
