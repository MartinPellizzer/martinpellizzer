Astrocarpa clusii var. parviflora

Astrocarpa clusii var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered astrocarpa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
