Aegilops microstachys

Aegilops microstachys, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Aegilops microstachys is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.

Morphology

Aegilops microstachys has narrow, grass-like leaves that are linear in shape, alternate in arrangement, and typically range from 5 to 15 cm in length, with a bluish-green color.

Its stem is cylindrical, smooth, and slightly hairy, growing upright with a clump-forming habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which bloom in early summer and are pollinated by wind. The fruit is a small, three-angled spikelet that disperses its seeds by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant anchor in dry, sandy soils.
