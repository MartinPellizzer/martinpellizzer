Agrostis pallens var. vaseyi

Agrostis pallens var. vaseyi, commonly known as the Vasey's bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its silver-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
