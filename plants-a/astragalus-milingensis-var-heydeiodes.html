Astragalus milingensis var. heydeiodes

Astragalus milingensis var. heydeiodes, commonly known as the Heyde's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and medicinal studies.
