Asperella sibirica var. longe-aristata

Asperella sibirica var. longe-aristata, commonly known as the long-awned siberian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Siberia and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, slender bristles at the base of its inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
