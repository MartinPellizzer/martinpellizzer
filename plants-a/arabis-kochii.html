Arabis kochii

Arabis kochii, commonly known as the Koch's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in a raceme, often blooming in early spring.

Arabis kochii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
