Astragalus spitzenbergeri

Astragalus spitzenbergeri, commonly known as the Spitzner's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinators.
