Asyneuma virgatum subsp. cichoriiforme

Asyneuma virgatum subsp.

cichoriiforme, commonly known as the Cichoriiforme false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from spring to early summer.

It is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.
