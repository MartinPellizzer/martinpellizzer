Antennaria hyperborea

Antennaria hyperborea, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize open, rocky, or sandy habitats.

It is adapted to cold climates and has developed mechanisms to survive in nutrient-poor soils and harsh environmental conditions.
