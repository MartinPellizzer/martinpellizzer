Avena pungens

Avena pungens, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its sharp, needle-like leaves and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like blades and spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Avena pungens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
