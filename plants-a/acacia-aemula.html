Acacia aemula

Acacia aemula, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. Its root system is well adapted to sandy soils, and it is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Acacia aemula is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
