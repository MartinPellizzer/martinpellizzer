Allium sarychelekense

Allium sarychelekense, commonly known as the Sarychevo garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, narrow leaves and small, spherical bulbs. The plant produces white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features make it a unique subject for horticultural study and potential cultivation in arid environments.

However, it is relatively rare and requires specific growing conditions to thrive.
