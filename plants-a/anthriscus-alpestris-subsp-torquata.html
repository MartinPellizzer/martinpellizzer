Anthriscus alpestris subsp. torquata

Anthriscus alpestris subsp.

torquata, commonly known as the Alpine cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. It is often found in meadows, slopes, and along paths in high-altitude areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
