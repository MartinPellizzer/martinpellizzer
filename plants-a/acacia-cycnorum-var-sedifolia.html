Acacia cycnorum var. sedifolia

Acacia cycnorum var. sedifolia, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden-green flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant has traditional uses in medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
