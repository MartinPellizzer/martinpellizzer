Acinodendron lonchophyllum

Acinodendron lonchophyllum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in its natural habitat.
