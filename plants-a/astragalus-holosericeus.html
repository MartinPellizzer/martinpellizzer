Astragalus holosericeus

Astragalus holosericeus, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus holosericeus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
