Acinodendron carioanum

Acinodendron carioanum, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces striking blue or purple star-shaped flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
