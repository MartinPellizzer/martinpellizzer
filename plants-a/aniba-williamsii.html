Aniba williamsii

Aniba williamsii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
