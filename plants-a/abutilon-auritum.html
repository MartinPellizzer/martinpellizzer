Abutilon auritum

Abutilon auritum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its capsules produce numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Abutilon auritum is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
