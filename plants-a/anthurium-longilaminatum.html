Anthurium longilaminatum

Anthurium longilaminatum, commonly known as the long-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Anthurium longilaminatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
