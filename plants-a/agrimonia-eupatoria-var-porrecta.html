Agrimonia eupatoria var. porrecta

Agrimonia eupatoria var. porrecta, commonly known as common agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

Agrimony is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
