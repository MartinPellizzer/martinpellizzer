Artanthe macrorhyncha

Artanthe macrorhyncha, commonly known as the broad-beak artanthe, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Artanthe macrorhyncha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
