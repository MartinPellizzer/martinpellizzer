Abutilon glaucum var. muticum

Abutilon glaucum var. muticum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to arid landscapes.
