Actinotus leucocephalus

Actinotus leucocephalus, commonly known as the whitehead bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking white, bell-shaped flowers with a yellow center. The plant features delicate, hairy leaves and grows in a low, spreading habit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Actinotus leucocephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
