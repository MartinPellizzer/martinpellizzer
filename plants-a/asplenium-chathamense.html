Asplenium chathamense

Asplenium chathamense, commonly known as the Chatham Island maidenhair fern, is a rare and endemic fern species found exclusively on the Chatham Islands in New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in the cool, humid climate. Its spores are dispersed through the wind, allowing it to propagate in suitable microhabitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its natural environment.
