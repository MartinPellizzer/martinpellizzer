Auricula villosa

Auricula villosa, commonly known as the hairy auricula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal among alpine gardeners.
