Acanthopanax leucorrhizus var. scaberulus

Acanthopanax leucorrhizus var. scaberulus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed Chinese angelica, is a perennial shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its roots are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
