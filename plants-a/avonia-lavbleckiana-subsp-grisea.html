Avonia lavbleckiana subsp. grisea

Avonia lavbleckiana subsp.

grisea, commonly known as the grayleaf avonia, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves that provide a unique visual texture.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
