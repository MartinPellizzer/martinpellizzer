Annona latifolia

Annona latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf custard apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive, fragrant white flower that blooms in clusters. The fruit is a smooth, yellow or greenish-yellow berry with a thick, sweet, custard-like pulp. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
