Amaryllis elata

Amaryllis elata, commonly known as the slender amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter. The plant typically blooms in spring or early summer, producing vibrant pink or white flowers with a distinctive fragrance. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often mistaken for those of a lily.

Amaryllis elata is well-suited for both indoor cultivation and outdoor gardens in warm climates.
