Andryala atlanticola

Andryala atlanticola, commonly known as Atlantic sea blush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shorelines. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Andryala atlanticola is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.
