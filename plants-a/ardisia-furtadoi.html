Ardisia furtadoi

Ardisia furtadoi, commonly known as the Furtado's evergreen ardisia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various bird species. Ardisia furtadoi thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
