Adelia obovata

Adelia obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate adelia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, obovate-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to dry conditions, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Adelia obovata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
