Anthospermum ciliare var. latifolium

Anthospermum ciliare var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf anthospermum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves with ciliate margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in cymes, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soils.

The species is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments, such as under forest canopies or in damp woodland areas.
