Areca langloisiana

Areca langloisiana, commonly known as the Langloisian areca, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, three-angled fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its restricted natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
