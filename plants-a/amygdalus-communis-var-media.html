Amygdalus communis var. media

Amygdalus communis var. media, commonly known as the Chinese peach or sweet almond tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-barked trunk and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by fuzzy, red or yellow fruits that contain edible seeds. It is widely cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value, and its seeds are used in the production of almond oil and confections.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
