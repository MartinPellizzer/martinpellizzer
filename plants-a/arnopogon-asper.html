Arnopogon asper

Arnopogon asper, commonly known as the rough bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Arnopogon asper is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
