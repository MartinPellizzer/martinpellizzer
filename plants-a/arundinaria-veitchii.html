Arundinaria veitchii

Arundinaria veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a grass-like appearance.

Arundinaria veitchii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and traditional Chinese medicine.
