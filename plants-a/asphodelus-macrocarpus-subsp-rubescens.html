Asphodelus macrocarpus subsp. rubescens

Asphodelus macrocarpus subsp.

rubescens, commonly known as the red-fruit asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive red or orange berries. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
