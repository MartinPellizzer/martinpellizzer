Aristida eludens

Aristida eludens, commonly known as the ghostgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. The plant produces small, delicate inflorescences that resemble a ghostly or ethereal shape, giving it its common name. It is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Aristida eludens is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
