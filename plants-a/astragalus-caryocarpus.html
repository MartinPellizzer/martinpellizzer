Astragalus caryocarpus

Astragalus caryocarpus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, spike-like seed pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Astragalus caryocarpus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native environment.
