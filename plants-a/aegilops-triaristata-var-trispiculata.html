Aegilops triaristata var. trispiculata

Aegilops triaristata var. trispiculata, commonly known as the three-awned wild oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, awned spikelets that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

This species is often considered a weed in agricultural settings but has also been studied for its potential in plant breeding programs.
