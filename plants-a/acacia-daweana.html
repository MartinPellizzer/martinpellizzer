Acacia daweana

Acacia daweana, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Acacia daweana is well adapted to drought and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
