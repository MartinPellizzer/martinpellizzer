Aechmea esseri

Aechmea esseri, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky rosette of silver-gray leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom in a panicle-like structure, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Aechmea esseri is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea esseri has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, stiff, and often have a waxy coating, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a bluish or purplish tinge, and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem that is covered in a fibrous, rough-textured bark, growing upright from the base. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of pale pink to white flowers, which bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by insects such as bees and moths.

The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that contains numerous seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
