Agrostis ecklonis var. longiaristata

Agrostis ecklonis var. longiaristata, commonly known as the long-leaved bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple at maturity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
