Aconitum laevicaule

Aconitum laevicaule, commonly known as the smooth blue monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, which are borne on tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum laevicaule contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
