Acalypha peduncularis var. genuina

Acalypha peduncularis var. genuina, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with bright red veins contrasting against the green foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often hidden among the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
