Acerates decumbens var. erecta

Acerates decumbens var. erecta, commonly known as the upright form of the prostrate acerates, is a variant of the acerates plant characterized by its upright growth habit.

This plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

It is a low-maintenance plant that prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
