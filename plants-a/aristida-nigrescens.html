Aristida nigrescens

Aristida nigrescens, commonly known as blackstem dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that turn a deep purple to blackish color as they mature. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically brown or tan in color, appearing in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Aristida nigrescens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
