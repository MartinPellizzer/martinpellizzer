Athyrium puncticaule

Athyrium puncticaule, commonly known as the punctuated lady fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically dark green with a pale underside. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium puncticaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
