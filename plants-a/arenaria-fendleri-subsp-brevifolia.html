Arenaria fendleri subsp. brevifolia

Arenaria fendleri subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has slender, branched stems and small, needle-like leaves adapted to conserve water. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round seeds.

It is well-suited to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
