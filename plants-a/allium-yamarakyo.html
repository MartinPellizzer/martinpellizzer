Allium yamarakyo

Allium yamarakyo, commonly known as the Yamarakyo garlic, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks. The plant produces small, white, spherical bulbs that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing from a bulbous base.

The plant is valued for its culinary uses and is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental appearance.
