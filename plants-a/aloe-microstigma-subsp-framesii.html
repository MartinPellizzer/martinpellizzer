Aloe microstigma subsp. framesii

Aloe microstigma subsp.

framesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth habit and fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple hue.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and requiring minimal irrigation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
