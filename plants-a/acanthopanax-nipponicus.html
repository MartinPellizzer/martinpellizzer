Acanthopanax nipponicus

Acanthopanax nipponicus, commonly known as Japanese angel's trumpet, is a climbing shrub native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in forested areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

However, its berries are toxic and should be handled with care.
