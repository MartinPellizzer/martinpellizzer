Amomum laurentii

Amomum laurentii, commonly known as Vietnamese cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity. The plant produces aromatic, elongated fruits that contain numerous small, dark brown seeds. These seeds are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice in culinary applications.

Its botanical features include rhizomatous growth, lanceolate leaves, and a distinctive, fragrant aroma.
