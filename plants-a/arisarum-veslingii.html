Arisarum veslingii

Arisarum veslingii, commonly known as the white adder's mouth, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a snake's mouth, with white petals and a dark, hood-like structure. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, known for its modified leaves called spatheae that enclose the true flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
