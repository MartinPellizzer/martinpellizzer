Agathosma squamosa

Agathosma squamosa, commonly known as the Cape aster, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it is often used in herbal medicine for its aromatic properties.

Agathosma squamosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
