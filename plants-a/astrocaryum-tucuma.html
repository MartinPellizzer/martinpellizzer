Astrocaryum tucuma

Astrocaryum tucuma, commonly known as tucuma, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, yellowish-pink inflorescences that bear small, white flowers. Its most notable feature is the edible fruit, which is similar to a coconut and has a creamy, sweet pulp.

The fruit is widely used in local cuisines and has economic importance in the region.
