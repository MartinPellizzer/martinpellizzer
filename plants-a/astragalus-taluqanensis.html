Astragalus taluqanensis

Astragalus taluqanensis, commonly known as the Taluqan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and southern Russia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, and it plays a role in preventing soil erosion in its native environment.
