Adiantum cuneatum

Adiantum cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds that resemble a feather. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, almost translucent appearance.

Adiantum cuneatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
