It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically divided into smaller, lance-shaped segments. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Aspidium heteropterum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms in its native ecosystems.
