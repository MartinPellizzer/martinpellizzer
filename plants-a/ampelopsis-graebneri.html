Ampelopsis graebneri

Ampelopsis graebneri, commonly known as the grape ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its ability to climb using aerial roots and can thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
