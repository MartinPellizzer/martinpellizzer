Alchemilla bamnii

Alchemilla bamnii, commonly known as the mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Alchemilla bamnii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.
