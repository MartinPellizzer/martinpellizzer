Anacyclus pyrethraria

Anacyclus pyrethraria, commonly known as the yellow burrow weed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single head, with bracts that form a pappus for wind dispersal. Its root system is fibrous and often forms dense colonies, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

Anacyclus pyrethraria is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a short growing season and the ability to store water in its tissues.
