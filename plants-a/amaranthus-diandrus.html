Amaranthus diandrus

Amaranthus diandrus, commonly known as the double-seed amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, two-lobed seeds that are often used as a food source by birds and small mammals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy annual in many regions.

Amaranthus diandrus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional agriculture.
