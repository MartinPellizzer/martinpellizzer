Aquilegia pyrenaica var. bertolonii

Aquilegia pyrenaica var. bertolonii, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, purple, and blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure that aids in photosynthesis in poor soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
