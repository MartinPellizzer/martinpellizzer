Antidesma pentandrum var. angustifolium

Antidesma pentandrum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved antidesma, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features simple, alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually narrow and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are often red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
