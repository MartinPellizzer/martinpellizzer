Asplenium × staufferi nothosubsp. tadei-reichsteinii

Asplenium × staufferi nothosubsp.

tadei-reichsteinii, commonly known as the Reichstein's shield fern, is a hybrid species within the Aspleniaceae family. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with dark green, leathery fronds that are often deeply divided.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

This nothosubspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
