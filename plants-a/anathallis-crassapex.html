Anathallis crassapex

Anathallis crassapex, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a spider, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated labellum. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of flies, which are attracted to the scent and color of the blooms.

Anathallis crassapex is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its natural habitat.
