Actinidia glaucophylla var. robusta

Actinidia glaucophylla var. robusta, commonly known as the robust kiwifruit, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It produces small, hairy, green berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and has a unique, tangy flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
