Astragalus syrtschensis

Astragalus syrtschensis, commonly known as the Sartsch Valley milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across fragmented landscapes.
