Ambrina chilensis

Ambrina chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of Chile.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

Ambrina chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
