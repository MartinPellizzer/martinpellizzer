Astragalus speciosus

Astragalus speciosus, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Astragalus speciosus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
