Actinidia arguta var. platyphylla

Actinidia arguta var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, flat leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to grow in a variety of soil conditions.
