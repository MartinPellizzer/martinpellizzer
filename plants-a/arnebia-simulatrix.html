Arnebia simulatrix

Arnebia simulatrix, commonly known as the Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its roots contain a red pigment called lac, which has been historically used for dyeing textiles and as a natural pigment.

Arnebia simulatrix is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
