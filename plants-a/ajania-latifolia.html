Ajania latifolia

Ajania latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ajania, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, producing small blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators.

Ajania latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
