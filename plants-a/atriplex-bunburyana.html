Atriplex bunburyana

Atriplex bunburyana, commonly known as Bunbury's saltbush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh conditions.

Atriplex bunburyana plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
