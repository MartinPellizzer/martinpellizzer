Ardisia lethomasiae

Ardisia lethomasiae, commonly known as the Thomas's ardisia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the island of Borneo. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
