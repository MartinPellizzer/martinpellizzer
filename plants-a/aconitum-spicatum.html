Aconitum spicatum

Aconitum spicatum, commonly known as the spiculate aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Aconitum spicatum is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and is often found in moist, shady woodlands.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
