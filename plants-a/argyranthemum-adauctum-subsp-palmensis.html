Argyranthemum adauctum subsp. palmensis

Argyranthemum adauctum subsp.

palmensis, commonly known as the palmleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that give it its common name, and its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It blooms throughout the year in warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun.
