Acacia excelsa var. typica

Acacia excelsa var. typica, commonly known as the tall golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical heads, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
