Artemisia potentilloides var. nitrophila

Artemisia potentilloides var. nitrophila, commonly known as nitrophilous wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It thrives in nutrient-rich soils, often found in disturbed or open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has aromatic, silver-gray foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies related to soil fertility and plant adaptation.
