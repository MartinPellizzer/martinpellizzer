Agrostis spica-venti var. biflora

Agrostis spica-venti var. biflora, commonly known as the wind spike grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with two distinct floral branches. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and agricultural fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in moderate to high light conditions.
