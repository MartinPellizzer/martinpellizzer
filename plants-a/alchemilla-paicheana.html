Alchemilla paicheana

Alchemilla paicheana, commonly known as the Andean lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Alchemilla paicheana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
