Asplenium marinum f. incisum

Asplenium marinum f. incisum, commonly known as the coastal spleenwort, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous environments such as coastal cliffs and rocky shores. The fronds are usually dark green and have a serrated or incised margin, giving the plant its specific epithet "incisum.". It is a member of the Aspleniaceae family and is often found in temperate regions.

This fern is well adapted to saline and wind-exposed habitats, making it a resilient plant in challenging environments.
