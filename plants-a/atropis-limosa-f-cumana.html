Atropis limosa f. cumana

Atropis limosa f. cumana, commonly known as the Mexican weeping willow, is a variant of the weeping willow species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, giving it a distinctive ornamental look. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue on top and a silvery-white underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers and streams.

This plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and restoration projects.
