Antholyza descampsii

Antholyza descampsii, commonly known as the Campsii antholyza, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences that display a mix of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Antholyza descampsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
