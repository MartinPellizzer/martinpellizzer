Astragalus atropilosulus subsp. abyssinicus

Astragalus atropilosulus subsp.

abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
