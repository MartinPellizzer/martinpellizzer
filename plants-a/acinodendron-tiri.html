Acinodendron tiri

Acinodendron tiri, commonly known as the Tiri acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant has small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is adapted to poor soils, making it resilient in arid environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.
