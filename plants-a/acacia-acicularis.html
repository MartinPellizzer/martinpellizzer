Acacia acicularis

Acacia acicularis, commonly known as the needle acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like phyllodes that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia acicularis is drought-tolerant and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
