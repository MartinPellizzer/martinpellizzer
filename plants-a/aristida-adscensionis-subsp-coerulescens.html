Aristida adscensionis subsp. coerulescens

Aristida adscensionis subsp.

coerulescens, commonly known as the bluegrass lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
