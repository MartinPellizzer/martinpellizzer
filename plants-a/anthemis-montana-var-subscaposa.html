Anthemis montana var. subscaposa

Anthemis montana var. subscaposa, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, ascending stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, with ray florets that may become pinkish or reddish as they age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
