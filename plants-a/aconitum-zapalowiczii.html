Aconitum zapalowiczii

Aconitum zapalowiczii, commonly known as the Zapalowicz aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like cluster, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum zapalowiczii contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.

Morphology

Aconitum zapalowiczii has compound, palmately arranged leaves that are deeply lobed, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter green underside, typically reaching up to 20 cm in diameter.

The stem is smooth and cylindrical, with a reddish-brown bark that becomes rougher with age, and it grows upright with a slightly branched structure. Its flowers are showy, blue to purple in color, with spurred petals and a complex structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies, blooming from late summer to early autumn. The plant produces follicles as its fruit, containing small, flat seeds that are dispersed by wind.
