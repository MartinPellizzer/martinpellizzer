Acalypha californica

Acalypha californica, commonly known as the California copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with bright yellow veins and red or orange bracts that resemble flowers. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are often hidden by the colorful bracts.

Acalypha californica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
