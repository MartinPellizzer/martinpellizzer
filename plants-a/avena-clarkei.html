Avena clarkei

Avena clarkei, commonly known as Clarke's oat, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Avena clarkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
