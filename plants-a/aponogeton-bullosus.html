Aponogeton bullosus

Aponogeton bullosus, commonly known as the bladder spikerush, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, bladder-like structures that aid in buoyancy. The plant has long, thin leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it to the substrate. It blooms with small, white flowers that are often submerged, and it produces distinctive, round, fleshy fruits.

Aponogeton bullosus is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value.
