Aconitum pyrenaicum subsp. lamarckii

Aconitum pyrenaicum subsp.

lamarckii, commonly known as the Pyrenean monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers arranged in a helmet-like structure.

The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountain meadows.

Its toxic alkaloids make it a plant of both ecological and medicinal interest.
