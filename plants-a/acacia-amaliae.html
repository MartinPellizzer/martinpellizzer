Acacia amaliae

Acacia amaliae, commonly known as the Amalia's acacia, is a shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, paddle-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
