Acacia auriculiformis

Acacia auriculiformis, commonly known as the earleaf acacia, is a fast-growing leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often adorned with small, ear-like structures at the leaflets' bases. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used for reforestation and soil stabilization due to its ability to fix nitrogen.

Additionally, its wood and foliage have traditional uses in various parts of its native range.
