Amaranthus deflexus var. rufescens

Amaranthus deflexus var. rufescens, commonly known as redstem amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are small and oval, with a slightly textured surface.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used as a forage crop or in ornamental gardening.
