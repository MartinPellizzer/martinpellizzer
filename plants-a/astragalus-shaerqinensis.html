Astragalus shaerqinensis

Astragalus shaerqinensis, commonly known as the Shaerqin milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
