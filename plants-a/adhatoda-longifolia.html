Adhatoda longifolia

Adhatoda longifolia, commonly known as the Indian borage or Malabar borage, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported respiratory and antipyretic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
