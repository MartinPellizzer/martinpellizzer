Adiantum elatum

Adiantum elatum, commonly known as the royal fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often bright green and slightly curled. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it to substrates.

Adiantum elatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
