Astragalus dianthus

Astragalus dianthus, commonly known as the pink milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Astragalus dianthus is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
