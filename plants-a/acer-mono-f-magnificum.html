Acer mono f. magnificum

Acer mono f. magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent maple, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its vibrant foliage.

It is a variant of the single-trunked maple, known for its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn brilliant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree typically grows to a height of 20-30 feet, featuring a broad, rounded crown. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This cultivar is prized for its aesthetic value in gardens and urban landscapes.
