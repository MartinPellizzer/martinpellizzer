Alyssum sublineare

Alyssum sublineare, commonly known as narrowleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Alyssum sublineare is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
