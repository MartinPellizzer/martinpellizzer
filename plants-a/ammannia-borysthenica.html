Ammannia borysthenica

Ammannia borysthenica, commonly known as the water spinach or river spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves and stems that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshy environments. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Ammannia borysthenica is also noted for its small, pinkish flowers that appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
