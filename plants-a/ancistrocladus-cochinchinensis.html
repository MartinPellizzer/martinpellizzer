Ancistrocladus cochinchinensis

Ancistrocladus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese poisonwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ancistrocladaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant is known for its toxic properties, with its leaves containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals. It typically grows in moist, tropical forests and is often found in lowland areas.

Due to its toxicity, it is not commonly cultivated but is of interest in botanical and pharmacological research.
