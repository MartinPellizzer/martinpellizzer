Agrostis vulgaris var. pallescens

Agrostis vulgaris var. pallescens, commonly known as the silver bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass mixes.
