Adansonia za var. boinensis

Adansonia za var. boinensis, commonly known as the Boinensis baobab, is a variant of the African baobab tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its massive, bottle-shaped trunk and thick, fibrous bark that provides structural support and moisture retention. The plant has a long lifespan, often exceeding several hundred years, and thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are palmate, with multiple leaflets, and it produces large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a woody capsule containing fibrous seeds, which are used for various traditional and commercial purposes.
