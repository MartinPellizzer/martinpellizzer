Anthurium remotigeniculatum

Anthurium remotigeniculatum, commonly known as the remote-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with intricate, parallel venation patterns. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-bearing inflorescences that are often surrounded by colorful, modified leaves called spatulae. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
