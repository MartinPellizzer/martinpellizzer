Arabis ciliata var. balansae

Arabis ciliata var. balansae, commonly known as the Balansae rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and ornamental plants. This perennial species is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

The plant is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
