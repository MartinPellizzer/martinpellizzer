Anthemis anatolica var. discoidea

Anthemis anatolica var. discoidea, commonly known as the Anatolian anthemis, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its aromatic, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
