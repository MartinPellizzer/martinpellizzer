Atriplex confinis

Atriplex confinis, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are often coated with a waxy layer to reduce water loss. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is adapted to saline and alkaline soils. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits containing single seeds.

Atriplex confinis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
