Astragalus paralurges

Astragalus paralurges, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant features slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus paralurges is adapted to thrive in dry soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
