Alectorolophus personatus

Alectorolophus personatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in spikes. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Alectorolophus personatus is often considered invasive in some regions due to its competitive nature and adaptability.
