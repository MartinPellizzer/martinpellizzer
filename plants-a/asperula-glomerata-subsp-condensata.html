Asperula glomerata subsp. condensata

Asperula glomerata subsp.

condensata, commonly known as the dense-flowered woodruff, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to elliptic leaflets with hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland and streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
