Agrostis canina var. laevis

Agrostis canina var. laevis, commonly known as silky bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and slightly hairy, giving it a silky texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks.

Due to its fine texture and resilience, it is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping and turfgrass mixes.
