Acer italum f. opulifolium

Acer italum f. opulifolium, commonly known as the opulifolium maple, is a unique variant of the Italian maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, and often lobed leaves that resemble those of the red maple. This ornamental tree is native to southern Europe and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage displays vibrant colors in the fall, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
