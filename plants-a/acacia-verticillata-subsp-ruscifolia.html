Acacia verticillata subsp. ruscifolia

Acacia verticillata subsp.

ruscifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and narrow, feather-like leaves that are arranged in whorls.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in reforestation and erosion control efforts.
