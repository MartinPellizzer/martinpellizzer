Abies alcoquiana

Abies alcoquiana, commonly known as the Alcoquian fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the stems. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
