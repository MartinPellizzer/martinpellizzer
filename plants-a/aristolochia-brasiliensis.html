Aristolochia brasiliensis

Aristolochia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, pipe-like flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition, including various aristolochic acids. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
