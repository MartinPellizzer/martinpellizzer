Anarrhinum violaceum

Anarrhinum violaceum, commonly known as the violet tansy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
