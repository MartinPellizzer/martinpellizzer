Asparagus inarimensis

Asparagus inarimensis, commonly known as the Inarim Asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
