Acer lingii

Acer lingii, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China and Korea.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

Acer lingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal foliage changes.
