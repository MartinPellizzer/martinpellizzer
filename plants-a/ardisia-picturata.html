Ardisia picturata

Ardisia picturata, commonly known as the painted ardisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Following flowering, it bears bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.
