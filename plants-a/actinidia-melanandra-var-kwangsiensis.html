Actinidia melanandra var. kwangsiensis

Actinidia melanandra var. kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi actinidia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
