Apargia pyrenaica

Apargia pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Apargia pyrenaica is a rare and endangered species, often found in specific microhabitats that support its unique ecological niche.
