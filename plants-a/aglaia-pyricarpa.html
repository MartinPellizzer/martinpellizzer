Aglaia pyricarpa

Aglaia pyricarpa, commonly known as the fire apple or yellow apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties contribute to its significance in both horticulture and pharmacology.
