Asarum fauriei

Asarum fauriei, commonly known as Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
