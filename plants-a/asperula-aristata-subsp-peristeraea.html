Asperula aristata subsp. peristeraea

Asperula aristata subsp.

peristeraea, commonly known as the Alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
