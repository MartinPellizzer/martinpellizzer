Andropogon scoparius var. ducis

Andropogon scoparius var. ducis, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and smooth. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
