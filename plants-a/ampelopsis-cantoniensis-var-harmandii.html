Ampelopsis cantoniensis var. harmandii

Ampelopsis cantoniensis var. harmandii, commonly known as the Guangdong honeysuckle, is a climbing vine native to southern China.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
