Arisaema rostratum

Arisaema rostratum, commonly known as the spiny arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spatula. The plant produces a single, showy flower that is often surrounded by a hood-like structure called a peduncle. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Arisaema rostratum is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering structure.
