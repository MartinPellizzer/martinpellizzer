Aster asae

Aster asae, commonly known as the asae aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with a daisy-like appearance, and bloom during the summer months.

Aster asae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
