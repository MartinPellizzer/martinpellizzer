Allardia huegelii

Allardia huegelii, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and vibrant purple to pink petals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Allardia huegelii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
