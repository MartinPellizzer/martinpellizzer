Angraecum cadetii

Angraecum cadetii, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue with a dark red or purple lip. The plant produces a single, slender stem that bears several flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid, shaded forest floors where it grows.

Angraecum cadetii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
