Acer opalus var. microcarpum

Acer opalus var. microcarpum, commonly known as the mountain maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by clusters of small, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
