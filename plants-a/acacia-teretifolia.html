Acacia teretifolia

Acacia teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and feathery in appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted to disperse in dry conditions.

Acacia teretifolia is well-suited to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
