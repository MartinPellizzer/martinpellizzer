Arisaema sierotium

Arisaema sierotium, commonly known as the ghost bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its unique, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its ornamental value is due to its elegant foliage and unusual floral structure.

Arisaema sierotium is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
