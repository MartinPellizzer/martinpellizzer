Amphicarpaea monoica

Amphicarpaea monoica, commonly known as the single-seed false flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique reproductive strategy. The plant produces two types of fruits: one above ground and one below ground, a phenomenon known as amphicarpism. Its small, yellowish flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.
