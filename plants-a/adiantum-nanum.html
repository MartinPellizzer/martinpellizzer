Adiantum nanum

Adiantum nanum, commonly known as the small maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Adiantum nanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
