Acer pseudoplatanus f. brevialatum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. brevialatum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a compact form of the common maple.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are typically shorter and broader than those of the typical species. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate, making it suitable for small gardens or container cultivation. It produces small, red-orange samaras in the fall, adding ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its unique shape and adaptability to various soil conditions.
