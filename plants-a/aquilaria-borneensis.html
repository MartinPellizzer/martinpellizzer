Aquilaria borneensis

Aquilaria borneensis, commonly known as the Borneo agarwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the islands of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its aromatic resinous wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with yellowish-white petals, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain smooth, black seeds.

The plant is also known for its ability to produce a fragrant resin in response to wounds or infections, which contributes to its economic importance.
