Aloe canarina

Aloe canarina, commonly known as the Canary Island aloe, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features tall, spindly stems with narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with orange to red flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used for its soothing properties.

Aloe canarina is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
