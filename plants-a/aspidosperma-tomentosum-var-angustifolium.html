Aspidosperma tomentosum var. angustifolium

Aspidosperma tomentosum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved velvet tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and dark, glossy bark. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and reforestation projects.
