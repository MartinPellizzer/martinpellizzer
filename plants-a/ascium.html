Ascium

Ascium, commonly known as the ascus, is a specialized structure found in certain fungi, particularly in the Ascomycota division.

It is a sac-like cell that contains and protects the spores during their development. The ascus is typically formed within a fruiting body called an ascocarp. Spores are released through the opening of the ascus, a process known as ascospore discharge.

The morphology and structure of the ascus vary among different fungal species, reflecting adaptations to their reproductive strategies.
