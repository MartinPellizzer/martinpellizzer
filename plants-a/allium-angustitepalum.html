Allium angustitepalum

Allium angustitepalum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in traditional cuisine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other Allium species such as onions and leeks.
