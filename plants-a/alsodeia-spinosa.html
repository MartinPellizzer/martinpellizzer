Alsodeia spinosa

Alsodeia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny alsike, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Alsodeia spinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
