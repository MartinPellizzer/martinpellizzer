Alyssum serpyllifolium subsp. malacitanum

Alyssum serpyllifolium subsp.

malacitanum, commonly known as the Malacitan sweet Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and ecological restoration projects.
