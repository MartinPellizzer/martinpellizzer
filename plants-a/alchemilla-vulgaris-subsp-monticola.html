Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. monticola

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

monticola, commonly known as mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, velvety, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in panicles during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its medicinal properties have historically been used in traditional herbal remedies.
