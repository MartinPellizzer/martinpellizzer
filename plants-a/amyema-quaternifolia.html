Amyema quaternifolia

Amyema quaternifolia, commonly known as the four-leafed lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its showy, fragrant white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Amyema quaternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
