Alyssum shahrudum

Alyssum shahrudum, commonly known as the Shahroud alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters. Alyssum shahrudum is characterized by its low-growing habit and silvery-green foliage.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
