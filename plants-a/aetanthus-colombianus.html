Aetanthus colombianus

Aetanthus colombianus, commonly known as the Colombian flame flower, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Aetanthus colombianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
