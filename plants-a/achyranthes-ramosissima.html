Achyranthes ramosissima

Achyranthes ramosissima, commonly known as the Mexican knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
