Acrostichum biforme

Acrostichum biforme, commonly known as the double-leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that split into two distinct parts, giving it its common name. The plant features long, narrow leaves with a feathery appearance, typical of many fern species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acrostichum biforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
