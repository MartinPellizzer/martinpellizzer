Anthurium incurvatum var. elatius

Anthurium incurvatum var. elatius, commonly known as the giant arrowhead plant, is a striking tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 feet in length, often with a vibrant green to reddish-purple hue. The plant produces striking spadices, which are elongated, spade-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
