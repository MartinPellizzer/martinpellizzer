Amaranthus crassipes subsp. warnockii

Amaranthus crassipes subsp.

warnockii, commonly known as the coastal amaranth, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other brackish habitats, making it an important component of these ecosystems.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate high salinity make it a valuable plant for stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.
