Asclepias vomitoria

Asclepias vomitoria, commonly known as the vomiter's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release silky, parachute-like seeds. It is a member of the milkweed family, which is known for its milky sap and ecological importance to monarch butterflies.

Asclepias vomitoria thrives in wetland habitats and is adapted to moist, sandy soils.
