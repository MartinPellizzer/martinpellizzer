Acacia moesta

Acacia moesta, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal regions. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are an important food source for various wildlife.

Acacia moesta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
