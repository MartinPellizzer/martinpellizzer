Artocarpus teysmannii

Artocarpus teysmannii, commonly known as the Java breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a fruit that is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or brown when mature. The fruit's pulp is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, while the seeds are often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
