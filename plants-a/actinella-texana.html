Actinella texana

Actinella texana, commonly known as the Texas actinella, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Actinella texana is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
