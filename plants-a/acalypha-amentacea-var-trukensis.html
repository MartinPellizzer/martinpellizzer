Acalypha amentacea var. trukensis

Acalypha amentacea var. trukensis, commonly known as the Truk Island glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
