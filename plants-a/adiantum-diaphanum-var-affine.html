Adiantum diaphanum var. affine

Adiantum diaphanum var. affine, commonly known as the translucent maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are often translucent or semi-transparent, giving it a unique and ethereal appearance. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and consistently moist, well-draining soil to thrive. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with small, spoon-shaped pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its beauty and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
