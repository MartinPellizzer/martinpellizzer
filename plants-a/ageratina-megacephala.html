Ageratina megacephala

Ageratina megacephala, commonly known as the hairy ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
