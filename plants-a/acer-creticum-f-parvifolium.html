Acer creticum f. parvifolium

Acer creticum f. parvifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved maple, is a variant of the European maple characterized by its smaller, more delicate leaves.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a serrated margin and a pale green underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in late summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
