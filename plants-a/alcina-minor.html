Alcina minor

Alcina minor, commonly known as the lesser alcina, is a small, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and usually have a pale green or white hue.

Alcina minor is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other understory plants in tropical and subtropical ecosystems.
