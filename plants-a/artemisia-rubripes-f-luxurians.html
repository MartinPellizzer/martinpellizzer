Artemisia rubripes f. luxurians

Artemisia rubripes f. luxurians, commonly known as the purple tarragon, is a compact, aromatic perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features silvery-green foliage with a strong, herbaceous scent, and its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in late summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its visual appeal and fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for dry, rocky gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
