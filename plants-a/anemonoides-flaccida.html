Anemonoides flaccida

Anemonoides flaccida, commonly known as the drooping anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Anemonoides flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for early pollinators.
