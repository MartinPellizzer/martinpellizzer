Anthurium solitarium

Anthurium solitarium, commonly known as the Solitary Anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a striking, often red or orange spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium solitarium is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
