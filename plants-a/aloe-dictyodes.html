Aloe dictyodes

Aloe dictyodes, commonly known as the "Coral Aloe," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often adorned with a white, powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear yellowish-orange flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Aloe dictyodes is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
