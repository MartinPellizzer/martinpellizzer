Angelica lobata

Angelica lobata, commonly known as western angelica, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to western North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant can grow up to 3 meters in height, with a thick, aromatic stem that is often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas along the Pacific coast.
