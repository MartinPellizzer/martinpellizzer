Acacia resinistipulea

Acacia resinistipulea, commonly known as the resinous acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are followed by woody pods containing lens-shaped seeds. Acacia resinistipulea is notable for secreting a resinous substance from its stems and leaves, which has been used traditionally by indigenous communities.

Its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments makes it an important component of its ecosystem.
