Azalea indica vittata-punctata

Azalea indica vittata-punctata, commonly known as the spotted azalea, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy texture, while the stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
