Aster linosyris var. armoricanus

Aster linosyris var. armoricanus, commonly known as the Armorican aster, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and thrives in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
