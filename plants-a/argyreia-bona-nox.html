Argyreia bona-nox

Argyreia bona-nox, commonly known as the "night blooming cereus," is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, succulent stems and spiny branches. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink, and emit a sweet fragrance. These flowers close by dawn, making the plant a nocturnal spectacle.

Its unique blooming habit and striking appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
