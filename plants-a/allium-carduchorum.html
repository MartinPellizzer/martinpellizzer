Allium carduchorum

Allium carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or mountain leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is characterized by its bulbous stem and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its bulbs are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Allium carduchorum is typically found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
