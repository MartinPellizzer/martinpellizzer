Annona klainei

Annona klainei, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruit that ripens to a yellowish-green color. The fruit has a rough, spiny exterior and a creamy, sweet flesh with a mild aroma.

It is valued for its edible fruit and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
