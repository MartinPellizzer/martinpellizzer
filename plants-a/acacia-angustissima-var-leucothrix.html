Acacia angustissima var. leucothrix

Acacia angustissima var. leucothrix, commonly known as the white-stemmed wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often greyish stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
