Aporocactus colubrinus

Aporocactus colubrinus, commonly known as the snake plant or mother of thousands, is a succulent native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to propagate easily through offsets, which are small plantlets that grow from the base of the parent plant.
