Aster occidentalis var. parishii

Aster occidentalis var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale blue. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
