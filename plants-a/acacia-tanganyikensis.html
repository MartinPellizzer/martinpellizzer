Acacia tanganyikensis

Acacia tanganyikensis, commonly known as the East African wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern African region.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
