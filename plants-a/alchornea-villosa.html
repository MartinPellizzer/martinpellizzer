Alchornea villosa

Alchornea villosa, commonly known as the silky alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Alchornea villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
