Aegilops bicornis var. anathera

Aegilops bicornis var. anathera, commonly known as the two-lobed wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with two-lobed spikelets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional agriculture.
