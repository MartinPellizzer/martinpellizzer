Andropogon aciculatus

Andropogon aciculatus, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
