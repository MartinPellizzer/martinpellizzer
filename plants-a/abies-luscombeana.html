Abies luscombeana

Abies luscombeana, commonly known as the Luscombe fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches. The cones of this species are typically small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The plant has limited distribution and is considered a rare and relict species within its native range.
