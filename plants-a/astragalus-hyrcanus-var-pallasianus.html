Astragalus hyrcanus var. pallasianus

Astragalus hyrcanus var. pallasianus, commonly known as the Pallas's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as steppes and sandy soils. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small, oval leaflets.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
