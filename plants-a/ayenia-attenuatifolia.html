Ayenia attenuatifolia

Ayenia attenuatifolia, commonly known as the slender ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in composite inflorescences, often displaying shades of yellow to orange.

Ayenia attenuatifolia is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
