Acacia viminalis

Acacia viminalis, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are compound and feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia viminalis is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry climates.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.
