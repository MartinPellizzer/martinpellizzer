Acer pseudoplatanus var. rubropurpureum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. rubropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-red maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which displays a vibrant purple-red hue during the growing season. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a smooth, waxy surface. This variety is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
