Anil senegalensis

Anil senegalensis, commonly known as African tea, is a shrub native to the savannas of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine, similar to tea leaves.
