Asteromyrtus lysicephala

Asteromyrtus lysicephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is known for its aromatic oils. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
