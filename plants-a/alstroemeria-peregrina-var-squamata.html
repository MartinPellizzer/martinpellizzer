Alstroemeria peregrina var. squamata

Alstroemeria peregrina var. squamata, commonly known as the scalycush alstroemeria, is a striking member of the Alstroemeriaceae family native to South America.

This variant is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, scaly texture. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting streaks. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
