Abies occidentalis

Abies occidentalis, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, gray bark, and needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The white fir plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
