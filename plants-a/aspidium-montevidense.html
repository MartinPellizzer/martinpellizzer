Aspidium montevidense

Aspidium montevidense, commonly known as the Montevidense water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it attaches to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to substrates, while its spores are released into the water for dispersal.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems in its native range.
