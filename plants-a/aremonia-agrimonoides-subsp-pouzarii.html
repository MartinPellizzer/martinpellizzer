Aremonia agrimonoides subsp. pouzarii

Aremonia agrimonoides subsp.

pouzarii, commonly known as the Pouzarii agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained environments.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late summer. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in its native range.
