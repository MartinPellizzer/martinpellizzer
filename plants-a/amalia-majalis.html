Amalia majalis

Amalia majalis, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features silver-gray leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture, and produces delicate blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Amalia majalis is relatively low maintenance and suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.
