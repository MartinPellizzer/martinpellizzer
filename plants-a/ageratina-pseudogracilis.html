Ageratina pseudogracilis

Ageratina pseudogracilis, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

The small, blue or purple flowers appear in clusters and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
