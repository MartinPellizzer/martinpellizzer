Aspidosperma brevifolia

Aspidosperma brevifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The tree produces bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its bark and leaves contain various alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Aspidosperma brevifolia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils.
