Acanthocalycium thionanthum subsp. thionanthum

Acanthocalycium thionanthum subsp.

thionanthum, commonly known as the Thorny Pincushion Cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico. It features a cylindrical stem covered in sharp, spiny tubercles, which provide protection against herbivores.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry climates with minimal water requirements.

Its unique morphology and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
