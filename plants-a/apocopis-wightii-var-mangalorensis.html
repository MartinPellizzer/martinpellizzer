Apocopis wightii var. mangalorensis

Apocopis wightii var. mangalorensis, commonly known as the Mangalore dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
