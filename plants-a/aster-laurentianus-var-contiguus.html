Aster laurentianus var. contiguus

Aster laurentianus var. contiguus, commonly known as the contiguous aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

The contiguous aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
