Anthemis laeviuscula

Anthemis laeviuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers with a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in herbal remedies.
