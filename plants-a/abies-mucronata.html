Abies mucronata

Abies mucronata, commonly known as the Mongolian fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Mongolia and northern China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, rounded cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

The bark is typically thick and deeply furrowed, providing protection against frost and drought.
