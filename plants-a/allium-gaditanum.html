Allium gaditanum

Allium gaditanum, commonly known as the Iberian garlic, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its bulbs are used in traditional Mediterranean cuisine and have medicinal properties.

Allium gaditanum thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
