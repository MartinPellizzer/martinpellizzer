Atitara palustris

Atitara palustris, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in damp or swampy areas, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and red. Its flowers bloom throughout the wet season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
