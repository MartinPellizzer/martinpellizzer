Argyrella sessilis

Argyrella sessilis, commonly known as the sessile argyrella, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Argyrella sessilis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
