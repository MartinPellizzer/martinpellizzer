Aristolochia angustifolia var. australis

Aristolochia angustifolia var. australis, commonly known as the narrowleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. The plant is characterized by its unique morphology, including a hollow, tubular corolla that resembles a pipe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
