Asarum trigynum

Asarum trigynum, commonly known as the three-stamen asarum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, pipe-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish, with a distinctive shape that resembles a hood, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Asarum trigynum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
