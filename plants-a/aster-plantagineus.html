Aster plantagineus

Aster plantagineus, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with ray florets that give it a daisy-like appearance.

Aster plantagineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
