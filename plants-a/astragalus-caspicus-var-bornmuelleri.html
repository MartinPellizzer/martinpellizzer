Astragalus caspicus var. bornmuelleri

Astragalus caspicus var. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Caspian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late summer. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and for forage in livestock.
