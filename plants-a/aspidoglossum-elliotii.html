Aspidoglossum elliotii

Aspidoglossum elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's snakeplant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its air-purifying properties also contribute to its appeal among houseplants.
