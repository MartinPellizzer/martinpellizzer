Aster microcephalus f. ovatus

Aster microcephalus f. ovatus, commonly known as the ovate-headed aster, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
