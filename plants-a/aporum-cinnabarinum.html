Aporum cinnabarinum

Aporum cinnabarinum, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often mistaken for those of the peace lily. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aporum cinnabarinum is also noted for its ability to produce a reddish dye from its flowers, which has been used historically in traditional practices.
