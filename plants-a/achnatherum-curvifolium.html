Achnatherum curvifolium

Achnatherum curvifolium, commonly known as the curlleaf needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, curling leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Achnatherum curvifolium plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
