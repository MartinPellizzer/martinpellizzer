Abacopteris rubicunda

Abacopteris rubicunda, commonly known as the red-leafed fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which contrasts with its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens, though it remains relatively rare in the wild.
