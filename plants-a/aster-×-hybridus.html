Aster × hybridus

Aster × hybridus, commonly known as the garden aster, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between different species of the Aster genus.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically range in color from white to purple, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant is native to North America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, and it is generally hardy in USDA hardiness zones 3 to 8.

Aster × hybridus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
