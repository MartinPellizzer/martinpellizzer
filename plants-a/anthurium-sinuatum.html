Anthurium sinuatum

Anthurium sinuatum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that are typically bright red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Anthurium sinuatum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
