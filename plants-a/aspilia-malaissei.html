Aspilia malaissei

Aspilia malaissei, commonly known as the Malaisse's torchflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its inflorescence consists of a central yellow disk surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

Aspilia malaissei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
