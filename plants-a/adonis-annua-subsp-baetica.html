Adonis annua subsp. baetica

Adonis annua subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Baetica double buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its bright yellow, double-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
