Aster koraiensis

Aster koraiensis, commonly known as the Korean aster, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing clusters of small, purple to white florets.

Aster koraiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
