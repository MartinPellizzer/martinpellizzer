Acacia umbraculata

Acacia umbraculata, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that provide dense shade, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. Its wood is hard and durable, making it useful for construction and furniture.

Acacia umbraculata is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
