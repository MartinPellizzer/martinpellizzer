Arenaria aksayqingensis

Arenaria aksayqingensis, commonly known as the Aksayqing sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is adapted to survive in harsh, sandy environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It has small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme climates.
