Arundinella tengchongensis

Arundinella tengchongensis, commonly known as the Tengchong bamboo, is a unique plant species native to China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit, forming dense clusters of bamboo-like stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
