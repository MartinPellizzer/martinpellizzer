Anplectrum rubrifructus

Anplectrum rubrifructus, commonly known as the red-fruited bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, and its bright red berries that persist through the winter. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Anplectrum rubrifructus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and provides ornamental value in gardens.
