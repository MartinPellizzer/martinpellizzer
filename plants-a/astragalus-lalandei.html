Astragalus lalandei

Astragalus lalandei, commonly known as the Laland's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Astragalus lalandei is a member of the legume family (Fabaceae) and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.
