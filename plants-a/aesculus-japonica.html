Aesculus japonica

Aesculus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are typically 10–20 cm in diameter, with a bright green upper surface and a lighter underside. The tree produces large, showy white or pink flowers in late spring, arranged in drooping clusters. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing 1–3 chestnut-like seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and seasonal interest.
