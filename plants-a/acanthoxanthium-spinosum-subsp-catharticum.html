Acanthoxanthium spinosum subsp. catharticum

Acanthoxanthium spinosum subsp.

catharticum, commonly known as the spiny acanthoxanthium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe. It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is often found in dry, sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems, primarily as a food source for specific insect species.
