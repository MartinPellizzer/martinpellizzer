Alocasia picta

Alocasia picta, commonly known as the Painted Elephant's Ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and shades of green, silver, and purple. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Alocasia picta thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
