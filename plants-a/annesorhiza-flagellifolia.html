Annesorhiza flagellifolia

Annesorhiza flagellifolia, commonly known as the whipstem bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of greenish-white. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions.

Annesorhiza flagellifolia plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
