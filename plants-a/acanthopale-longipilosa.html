Acanthopale longipilosa

Acanthopale longipilosa, commonly known as the long-hair acanthopale, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and also covered in dense pubescence, contributing to its overall texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
