Ageratum corymbosum f. lactiflorum

Ageratum corymbosum f. lactiflorum, commonly known as the milky ageratum, is a compact, flowering plant native to the Americas.

It features fluffy, lavender to white inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil.

Its milky sap is a distinguishing characteristic, often exuding when the stems or leaves are broken.
