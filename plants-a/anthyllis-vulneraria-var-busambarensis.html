Anthyllis vulneraria var. busambarensis

Anthyllis vulneraria var. busambarensis, commonly known as the Busambarensis Scotch broom, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in traditional medicine.
