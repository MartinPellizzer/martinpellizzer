Agrostis lanceolata

Agrostis lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Agrostis lanceolata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
