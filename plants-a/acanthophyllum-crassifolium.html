Acanthophyllum crassifolium

Acanthophyllum crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved acanthophyllum, is a shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and thick, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with minimal rainfall.

Acanthophyllum crassifolium plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local biodiversity.
