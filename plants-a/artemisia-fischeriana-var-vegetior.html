Artemisia fischeriana var. vegetior

Artemisia fischeriana var. vegetior, commonly known as the sweet wormwood or Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

This variety is particularly noted for its high concentration of volatile oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and therapeutic uses.
