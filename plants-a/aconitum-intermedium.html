Aconitum intermedium

Aconitum intermedium, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are typically tall and upright. Aconitum intermedium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

It is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
