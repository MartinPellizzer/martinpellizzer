Acacia fasciculata

Acacia fasciculata, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary means of photosynthesis. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Acacia fasciculata is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
