Aquilegia tuvinica

Aquilegia tuvinica, commonly known as the Tuvinian columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided leaflets, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

Aquilegia tuvinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
