Acinodendron argyrophyllum

Acinodendron argyrophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf acinodendron, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
