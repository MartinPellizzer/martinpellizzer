Ampelopsis brevipedunculata var. hancei

Ampelopsis brevipedunculata var. hancei, commonly known as the Chinese devil's vine, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, often with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental berries and foliage make it a popular choice in horticulture, though it can be invasive in some regions.
