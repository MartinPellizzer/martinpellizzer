Aleurites javanicus

Aleurites javanicus, commonly known as the Natal bean tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical fruits that contain hard, angular seeds. Its seeds are rich in oil and have been traditionally used for oil extraction and as a source of protein.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical ecosystems.
