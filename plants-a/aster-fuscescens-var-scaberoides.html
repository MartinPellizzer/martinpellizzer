Aster fuscescens var. scaberoides

Aster fuscescens var. scaberoides, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
