Acacia praecox f. hassleri

Acacia praecox f. hassleri, commonly known as the early acacia or Hassler's acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. Its woody stems are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs, providing some protection against herbivores.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
