Allium globosum subsp. petraeum

Allium globosum subsp.

petraeum, commonly known as the rock garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, spherical bulb-like structures that are typically white or pale pink in color.

The plant features basal leaves that are grass-like and narrow, often with a bluish-gray hue. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
