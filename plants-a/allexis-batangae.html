Allexis batangae

Allexis batangae, commonly known as the Batang River orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates or in forest understories. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Allexis batangae is considered vulnerable and requires careful protection.
