Albuca fragrans

Albuca fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers with a sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Albuca fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
