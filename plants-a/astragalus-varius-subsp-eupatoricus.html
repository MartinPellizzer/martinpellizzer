Astragalus varius subsp. eupatoricus

Astragalus varius subsp.

eupatoricus, commonly known as the eupatoric milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
