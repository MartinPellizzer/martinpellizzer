Aster ciliolatus var. maccallae

Aster ciliolatus var. maccallae, commonly known as the Maccallae's aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.
