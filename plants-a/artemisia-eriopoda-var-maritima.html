Artemisia eriopoda var. maritima

Artemisia eriopoda var. maritima, commonly known as coastal wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to coastal regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure.

Its aromatic leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal and ecological significance.
