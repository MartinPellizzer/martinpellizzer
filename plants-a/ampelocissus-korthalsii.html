Ampelocissus korthalsii

Ampelocissus korthalsii, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to climb using aerial roots and can grow quite long. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover structures.

The fruit is edible, though typically not consumed by humans.
