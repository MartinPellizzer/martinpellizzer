Alocasia hypoleuca

Alocasia hypoleuca, commonly known as the White-veined Alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly green with distinctive white veins. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.

Alocasia hypoleuca is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
