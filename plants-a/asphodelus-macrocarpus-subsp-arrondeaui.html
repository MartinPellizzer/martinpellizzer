Asphodelus macrocarpus subsp. arrondeaui

Asphodelus macrocarpus subsp.

arrondeaui, commonly known as the Arrondel's asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, slender flower stalks.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
