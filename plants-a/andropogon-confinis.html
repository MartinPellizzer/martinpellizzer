Andropogon confinis

Andropogon confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas and along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon confinis plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
