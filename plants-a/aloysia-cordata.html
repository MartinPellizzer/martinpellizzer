Aloysia cordata

Aloysia cordata, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in calming teas. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its essential oils are also used in perfumes and aromatherapy.
