Amaryllis virginiensis

Amaryllis virginiensis, commonly known as the Virginia amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, and its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens in warmer climates.

The Virginia amaryllis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
