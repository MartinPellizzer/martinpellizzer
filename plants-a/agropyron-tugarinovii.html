Agropyron tugarinovii

Agropyron tugarinovii, commonly known as the Tugarinov's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cold climates.

Agropyron tugarinovii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
