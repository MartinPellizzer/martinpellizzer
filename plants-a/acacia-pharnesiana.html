Acacia pharnesiana

Acacia pharnesiana, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and Southwestern Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dense, thorny branches and feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in medicine for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

Acacia pharnesiana is also valued for its role in preventing soil erosion in degraded landscapes.
