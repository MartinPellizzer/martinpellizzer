Aquilegia vulgaris var. sicula

Aquilegia vulgaris var. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive hooded shape. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are finely divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The species is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
