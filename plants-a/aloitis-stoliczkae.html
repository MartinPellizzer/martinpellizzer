Aloitis stoliczkae

Aloitis stoliczkae, commonly known as the Stoliczka's aloes, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aloeaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
