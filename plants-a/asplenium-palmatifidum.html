Asplenium palmatifidum

Asplenium palmatifidum, commonly known as the broad-lobed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and often arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive fan-like shape.

Asplenium palmatifidum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
