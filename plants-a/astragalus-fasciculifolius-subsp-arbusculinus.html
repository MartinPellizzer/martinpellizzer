Astragalus fasciculifolius subsp. arbusculinus

Astragalus fasciculifolius subsp.

arbusculinus, commonly known as the clustered leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

The plant has slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves with a distinctive clustered appearance. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
