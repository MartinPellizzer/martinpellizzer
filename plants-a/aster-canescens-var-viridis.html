Aster canescens var. viridis

Aster canescens var. viridis, commonly known as the greenleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features silver-gray, hairy leaves that are usually a lighter green in color, distinguishing it from the typical variety. Its flowers are blue to purple and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
