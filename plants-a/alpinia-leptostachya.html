Alpinia leptostachya

Alpinia leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stalked ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. Its rhizomes are typically used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Alpinia leptostachya thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.
