Aster shastensis var. glossophyllus

Aster shastensis var. glossophyllus, commonly known as the Shasta aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from white to purple, often with a yellow center.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
