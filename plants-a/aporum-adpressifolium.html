Aporum adpressifolium

Aporum adpressifolium, commonly known as the dwarf aporum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in marshy or swampy areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
