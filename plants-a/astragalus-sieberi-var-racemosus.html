Astragalus sieberi var. racemosus

Astragalus sieberi var. racemosus, commonly known as the racemose milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in nitrogen fixation and its potential medicinal properties.
