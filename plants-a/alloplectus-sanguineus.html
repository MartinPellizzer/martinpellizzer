Alloplectus sanguineus

Alloplectus sanguineus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a reddish or brownish hue.

Alloplectus sanguineus is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
