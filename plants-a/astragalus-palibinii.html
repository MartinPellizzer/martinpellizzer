Astragalus palibinii

Astragalus palibinii, commonly known as the Palibin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance is significant in its native habitat due to its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.
