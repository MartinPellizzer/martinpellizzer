Agave chrysoglossa

Agave chrysoglossa, commonly known as the golden mouth agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The leaves are adorned with white to yellowish marginal teeth, giving it its common name. It produces a tall, branched flower stalk that bears yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
