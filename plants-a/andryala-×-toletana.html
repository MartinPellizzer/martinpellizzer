Andryala × toletana

Andryala × toletana, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Andryala lutea and Andryala toletana, characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that typically range from pink to red. The plant is herbaceous, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various parts of Europe and beyond.
