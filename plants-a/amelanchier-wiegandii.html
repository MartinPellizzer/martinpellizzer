Amelanchier wiegandii

Amelanchier wiegandii, commonly known as the Wiegand serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. These berries are typically dark purple to black and are valued for their sweet flavor.

Amelanchier wiegandii is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
