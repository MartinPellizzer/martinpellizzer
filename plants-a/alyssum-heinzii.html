Alyssum heinzii

Alyssum heinzii, commonly known as the sweet alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum heinzii is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and ladybugs.
