Acriopsis latifolia

Acriopsis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf acris, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Acriopsis latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
