Ayenia palustris

Ayenia palustris, commonly known as swamp aster, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist soils along streams, marshes, and pond edges. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ayenia palustris plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
