Actinodaphne nantoensis

Actinodaphne nantoensis, commonly known as the Nantoese camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and lance-shaped, and its fragrant, aromatic bark. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, dark blue berries.

This species is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
