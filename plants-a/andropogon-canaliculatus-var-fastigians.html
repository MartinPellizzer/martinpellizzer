Andropogon canaliculatus var. fastigians

Andropogon canaliculatus var. fastigians, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with prominent veins running along their length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Its dense growth habit makes it a valuable component of native grassland ecosystems.
