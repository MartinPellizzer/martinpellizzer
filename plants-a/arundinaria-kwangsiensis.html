Arundinaria kwangsiensis

Arundinaria kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi bamboo, is a species of grass native to southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Arundinaria kwangsiensis is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
