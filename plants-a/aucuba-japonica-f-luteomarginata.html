Aucuba japonica f. luteomarginata

Aucuba japonica f. luteomarginata, commonly known as the golden-margin aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking yellow margins that add vibrant visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is highly tolerant of urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the year, offering year-round ornamental value.
