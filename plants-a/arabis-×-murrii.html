Arabis × murrii

Arabis × murrii, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in racemes, adding a delicate appearance to its foliage.

Arabis × murrii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
