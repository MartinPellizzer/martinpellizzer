Arundinella villosa var. himalaica

Arundinella villosa var. himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense foliage, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to cool climates. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, making it useful for erosion control.

The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its "villosa" designation.
