Adiantum jamaicense

Adiantum jamaicense, commonly known as the Jamaican maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, arching appearance.

Adiantum jamaicense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
