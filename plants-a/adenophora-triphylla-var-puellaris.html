Adenophora triphylla var. puellaris

Adenophora triphylla var. puellaris, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, violet-blue to purple flowers in conical clusters at the tips of the stems. It blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, partially shaded environments.
