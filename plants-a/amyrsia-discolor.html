Amyrsia discolor

Amyrsia discolor, commonly known as the white-leafed sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in areas with high humidity. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often pale green or white in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, dark-colored seeds.

Amyrsia discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
