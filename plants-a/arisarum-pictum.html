Arisarum pictum

Arisarum pictum, commonly known as the painted arum, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of veins. The plant produces a unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with a reddish or purple tint, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Arisarum pictum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
