Adenostylis vitiensis

Adenostylis vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, exhibiting a low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters. It is adapted to tolerate salt spray and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
