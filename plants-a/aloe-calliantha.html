Aloe calliantha

Aloe calliantha, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Aloe calliantha is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves for survival.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
