Allium allegheniense

Allium allegheniense, commonly known as the Allegheny onion, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features grass-like basal leaves and produces clusters of pale to deep purple flowers during the summer months. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Allium allegheniense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
