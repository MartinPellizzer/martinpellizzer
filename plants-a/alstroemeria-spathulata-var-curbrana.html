Alstroemeria spathulata var. curbrana

Alstroemeria spathulata var. curbrana, commonly known as the broad-leafed alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

The variety curbrana is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other varieties of Alstroemeria spathulata.
