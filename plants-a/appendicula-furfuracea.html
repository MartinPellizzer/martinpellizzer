Appendicula furfuracea

Appendicula furfuracea, commonly known as the fuzzy appendicula, is a member of the Primulaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are often pink or white, and they bloom in early spring.

The species is noted for its unique leaf structure, which features numerous appendages along the leaf margins.
