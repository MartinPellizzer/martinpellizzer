Acacia catenulata subsp. occidentalis

Acacia catenulata subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western acacia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bean-like pods.

The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny, needle-like leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.
