Allionia glandulifera

Allionia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular allionia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and distinctive glandular structures on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Allionia glandulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate flowers.
