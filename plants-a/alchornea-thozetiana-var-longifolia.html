Alchornea thozetiana var. longifolia

Alchornea thozetiana var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, elongated leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, often varying in color from green to reddish. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
