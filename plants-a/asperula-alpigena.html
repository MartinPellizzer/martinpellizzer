Asperula alpigena

Asperula alpigena, commonly known as the alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or acidic soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Asperula alpigena is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to shaded environments.
