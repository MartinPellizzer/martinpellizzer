Anchusa atlantica

Anchusa atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces tall spikes of bright blue or violet flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity in its native habitat.

Anchusa atlantica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
