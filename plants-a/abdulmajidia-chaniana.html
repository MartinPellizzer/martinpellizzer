Abdulmajidia chaniana

Abdulmajidia chaniana, commonly known as the Chanian's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pale greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, it is considered critically endangered and is rarely seen in the wild.
