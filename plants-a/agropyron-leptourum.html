Agropyron leptourum

Agropyron leptourum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Agropyron leptourum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky or arid environments.
