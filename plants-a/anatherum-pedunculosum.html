Anatherum pedunculosum

Anatherum pedunculosum, commonly known as the slender grass or pedunculate grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to various regions of Africa, particularly in southern and eastern parts, where it thrives in open, sunny habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are usually green and smooth. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, typical of the grass family, and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Anatherum pedunculosum is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and adaptability.
