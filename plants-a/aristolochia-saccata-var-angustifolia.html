Aristolochia saccata var. angustifolia

Aristolochia saccata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, often unpleasant odor. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are usually alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

The species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance.
