Acalypha consimilis

Acalypha consimilis, commonly known as the similar euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with smooth, glabrous stems and alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are surrounded by the showy bracts.

Acalypha consimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and decorative inflorescences.
