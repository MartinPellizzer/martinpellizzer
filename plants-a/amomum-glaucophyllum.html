Amomum glaucophyllum

Amomum glaucophyllum, commonly known as the blue-veined cardamom, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elliptical, dark brown fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its flavorful seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and the spice trade.
