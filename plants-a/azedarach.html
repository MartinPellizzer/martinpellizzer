Azedarach

Azedarach, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits called drupes, which contain a single seed. Azedarach is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in various climates.
