Alangium platanifolium var. genuinum

Alangium platanifolium var. genuinum, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are alternate and often have a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
