Apium graveolens subsp. rapaceum

Apium graveolens subsp.

rapaceum, commonly known as wild celery, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features hollow, aromatic stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are finely divided, with a feathery appearance, and the roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

This species is valued for its culinary uses and has been historically used in various herbal remedies.
