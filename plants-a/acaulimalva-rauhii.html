Acaulimalva rauhii

Acaulimalva rauhii, commonly known as the Rauh's mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to native plant gardens.
