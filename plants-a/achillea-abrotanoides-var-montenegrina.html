Achillea abrotanoides var. montenegrina

Achillea abrotanoides var. montenegrina, commonly known as Montenegrin yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features slender, aromatic stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is attributed to its antiseptic and astringent properties.
