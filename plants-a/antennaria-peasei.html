Antennaria peasei

Antennaria peasei, commonly known as the Pease's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and often hairy foliage. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Antennaria peasei thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
