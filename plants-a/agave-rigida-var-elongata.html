Agave rigida var. elongata

Agave rigida var. elongata, commonly known as the longleaf agave, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and long, narrow leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
