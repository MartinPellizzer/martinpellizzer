Astragalus angulosus subsp. heliopolitanus

Astragalus angulosus subsp.

heliopolitanus, commonly known as the sun-loving milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers.

The subspecies is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
