Araujia grandiflora

Araujia grandiflora, commonly known as the moonflower, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky, fibrous coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit, it is also known as the night-blooming cereus.
