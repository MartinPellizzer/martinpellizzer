Aloe ballii var. makurupiniensis

Aloe ballii var. makurupiniensis, commonly known as the Makuru pincushion aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, spiky foliage that forms a compact, rounded shape, resembling a pincushion. The leaves are thick and fleshy, with a waxy coating that helps it store water. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers on tall, branching stems during the warmer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and arid environments due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
