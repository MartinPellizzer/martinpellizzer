Aconitum excelsum

Aconitum excelsum, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features large, velvety blue-purple flowers arranged in a hooded shape, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble the shape of a hood, giving it its common name.

Aconitum excelsum is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it both a beautiful and dangerous plant.
