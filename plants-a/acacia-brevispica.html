Acacia brevispica

Acacia brevispica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
