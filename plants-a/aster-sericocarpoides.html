Aster sericocarpoides

Aster sericocarpoides, commonly known as the silky-fruited aster, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silky layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, typical of the Asteraceae family.
