Aldama brittonii

Aldama brittonii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh climates.
