Astracantha karabaghensis

Astracantha karabaghensis, commonly known as the Karabakh astragal, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spiny pods and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant morphology and ecological resilience.
