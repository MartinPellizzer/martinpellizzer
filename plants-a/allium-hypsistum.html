Allium hypsistum

Allium hypsistum, commonly known as the giant globe onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, hollow, cylindrical leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant's bulb is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, often used in cooking. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Allium hypsistum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
