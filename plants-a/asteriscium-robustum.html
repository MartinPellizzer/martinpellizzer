Asteriscium robustum

Asteriscium robustum, commonly known as the robust asterisk, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with prominent central disks. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.

Asteriscium robustum is known for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
