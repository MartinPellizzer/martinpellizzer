Acer opulifolium var. granatense

Acer opulifolium var. granatense, commonly known as the granate maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often red or orange in the fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, red flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
