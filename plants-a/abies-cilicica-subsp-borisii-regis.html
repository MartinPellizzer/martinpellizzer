Abies cilicica subsp. borisii-regis

Abies cilicica subsp.

borisii-regis, commonly known as the Bosnian fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the Dinaric Alps. It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The cones are typically small and globose, maturing to a reddish-brown hue. This subspecies is adapted to cold, humid climates and thrives in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
