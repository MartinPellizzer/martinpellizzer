Artemisia maritima var. hanseniana

Artemisia maritima var. hanseniana, commonly known as the coastal wormwood, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in panicles. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to salt spray and wind exposure.
