Alnus rhombifolia

Alnus rhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alder, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, rhomboid-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and mountainous areas.

Alnus rhombifolia plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
