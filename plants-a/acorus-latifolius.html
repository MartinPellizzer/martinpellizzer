Acorus latifolius

Acorus latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf sweetflag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshes in temperate regions.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and filter water.

Acorus latifolius is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
