Abies vejarii subsp. mexicana

Abies vejarii subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to high-altitude regions of Mexico. It belongs to the genus Abies, which includes various species of fir trees characterized by their cone-like structures and needle-like leaves.

The Mexican fir typically grows in mountainous areas with cool, moist climates, often reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its foliage is dense and dark green, with short, stiff needles that provide a dense canopy.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting diverse wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
