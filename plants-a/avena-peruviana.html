Avena peruviana

Avena peruviana, commonly known as Peruvian oat, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and dietary fiber.

Avena peruviana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
