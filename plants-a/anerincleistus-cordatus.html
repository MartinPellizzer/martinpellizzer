Anerincleistus cordatus

Anerincleistus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines for their starchy texture.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its nutritional properties.
