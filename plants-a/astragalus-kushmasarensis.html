Astragalus kushmasarensis

Astragalus kushmasarensis, commonly known as the Kushmasarensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, enabling it to access deep water sources.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for ecological and potential medicinal research.
