Aristolochia kongkandae

Aristolochia kongkandae, commonly known as the Malayan Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique morphology, including its heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are adapted for pollination by specific insects, and the plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in some regions.
