Aridarum montanum

Aridarum montanum, commonly known as the mountain desert plant, is a succulent species adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in low-moisture conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the brief rainy season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and deep, enabling it to access water from lower soil layers.

Aridarum montanum plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
