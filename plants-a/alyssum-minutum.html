Alyssum minutum

Alyssum minutum, commonly known as dwarf alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has compact, mat-forming foliage with gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum minutum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and hoverflies.
