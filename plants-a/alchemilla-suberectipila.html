Alchemilla suberectipila

Alchemilla suberectipila, commonly known as the upright-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features basal leaves that are rounded and slightly hairy, with a distinctive silver-white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in summer, arranged in loose clusters atop leafless stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological gardens and wetland restoration projects.
