Aster lanceolatus var. monocephalus

Aster lanceolatus var. monocephalus, commonly known as the single-headed aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces solitary, showy flowers with purple to blue petals, often blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
