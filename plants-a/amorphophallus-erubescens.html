Amorphophallus erubescens

Amorphophallus erubescens, commonly known as the red-veined arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features arrowhead-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces a striking, spadix-like inflorescence that can reach up to 2 meters in height. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to wetland environments and thrives in moist, shady conditions.
