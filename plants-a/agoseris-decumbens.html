Agoseris decumbens

Agoseris decumbens, commonly known as the creeping goldenroot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, hairy stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Agoseris decumbens is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
