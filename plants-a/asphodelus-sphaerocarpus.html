Asphodelus sphaerocarpus

Asphodelus sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the globe asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers. Its distinctive spherical seed head, which gives it its scientific name, is a notable characteristic.

The globe asphodel is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
