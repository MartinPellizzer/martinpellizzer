Asperula pendula var. glabrescens

Asperula pendula var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous weeping azalea, is a variant of the weeping azalea characterized by its smooth, hairless foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring season. Its cascading growth habit makes it popular for use in hanging baskets and as a decorative plant in gardens.

The variety is noted for its compact size and ability to tolerate partial shade, making it a versatile choice for ornamental landscaping.
