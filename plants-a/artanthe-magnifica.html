Artanthe magnifica

Artanthe magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent artanthe, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, characterized by its hairy stems and showy flowers. The plant features vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Artanthe magnifica is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-resistant plants.
