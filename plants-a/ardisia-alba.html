Ardisia alba

Ardisia alba, commonly known as the white ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal fruit display.
