Aconitum napellus var. delphiniifolium

Aconitum napellus var. delphiniifolium, commonly known as the Delphinium-leaved Monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are deeply lobed, resembling those of delphinium, hence its common name. This plant is native to central and southern Europe and thrives in moist, shady environments.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
