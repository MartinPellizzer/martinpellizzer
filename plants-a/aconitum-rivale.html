Aconitum rivale

Aconitum rivale, commonly known as the water aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a tall, slender stem with palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
