Avena alpina var. ausserdorferi

Avena alpina var. ausserdorferi, commonly known as the Alpine oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool and moist environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it is often found in high-altitude meadows and alpine tundras.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or ecological purposes.
