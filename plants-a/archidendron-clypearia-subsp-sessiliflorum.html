Archidendron clypearia subsp. sessiliflorum

Archidendron clypearia subsp.

sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flowered legume, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It features compound leaves with alternate arrangement and small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, tropical habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
