Aspidostigma

Aspidostigma, commonly known as the "garden spider orchid," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a spider's web, hence its common name. The plants are native to various regions in South America, particularly in Brazil and Argentina. They typically grow in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories.

Aspidostigma species are prized for their ornamental value and are often cultivated by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
