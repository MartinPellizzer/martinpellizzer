Aristida diego-santiagoi

Aristida diego-santiagoi, commonly known as Diego-Santayago grass, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss.
