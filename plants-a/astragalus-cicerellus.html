Astragalus cicerellus

Astragalus cicerellus, commonly known as the lesser cicero, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its seed pods are cylindrical and contain several small, rounded seeds.

Astragalus cicerellus plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
