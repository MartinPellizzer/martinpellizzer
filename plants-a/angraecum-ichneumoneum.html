Angraecum ichneumoneum

Angraecum ichneumoneum, commonly known as the Madagascar spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It features a short, creeping rhizome and produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant blooms with striking, pale yellow flowers that have a distinctive, intricate pattern resembling a spider's web. It is adapted to grow in dry, shaded environments and relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
