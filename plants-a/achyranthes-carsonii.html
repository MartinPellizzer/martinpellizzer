Achyranthes carsonii

Achyranthes carsonii, commonly known as Carson's smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.
