Acantholimon bromifolium var. platiphyllum

Acantholimon bromifolium var. platiphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaf thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. This variety is known for its salt tolerance and ability to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
