Antirrhinum × bilbilitanum

Antirrhinum × bilbilitanum, commonly known as the hybrid snapdragon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Antirrhinum majus and Antirrhinum auriculatum, exhibiting a combination of traits from both parent species. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. It is typically an annual or biennial herb with slender, branching stems and opposite leaves.

The flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
