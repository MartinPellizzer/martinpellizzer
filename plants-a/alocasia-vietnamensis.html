Alocasia vietnamensis

Alocasia vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese elephant's ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a deep green color and a prominent veining pattern. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like calla lilies and peace lilies.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is popular among plant enthusiasts and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
