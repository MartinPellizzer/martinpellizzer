Alyssum obliquum

Alyssum obliquum, commonly known as the zigzag alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Alyssum obliquum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
