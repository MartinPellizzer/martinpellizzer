Albizia schimperiana var. tephrocalyx

Albizia schimperiana var. tephrocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered albizia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound, pinnate leaves that are often trifoliate, with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, fibrous pods that split open when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
