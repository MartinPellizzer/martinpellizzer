Atropis festuciformis var. expansa

Atropis festuciformis var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf grass-like plant, is a variant of the Atropis genus, which belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that resemble those of grasses, though it lacks the typical grass-like inflorescence. The plant is native to certain regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its morphology includes a tufted growth habit with slender, wiry stems.

This variant is often noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to withstand dry conditions.
