Anthemis tricolor subsp. plutonia

Anthemis tricolor subsp.

plutonia, commonly known as the three-color ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colored flowers, which typically display yellow, white, and red hues.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
