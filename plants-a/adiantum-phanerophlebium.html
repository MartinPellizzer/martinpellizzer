Adiantum phanerophlebium

Adiantum phanerophlebium, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant features prominently visible veins, which give it its scientific name "phanerophlebium," meaning "visible veins.". It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Adiantum phanerophlebium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
