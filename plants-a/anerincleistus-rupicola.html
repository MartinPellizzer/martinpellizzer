Anerincleistus rupicola

Anerincleistus rupicola, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky regions.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in crevices where it can access limited soil and moisture.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
