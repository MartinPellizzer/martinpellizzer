Ardisia cymosa var. albiflora

Ardisia cymosa var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
