Anthurium werffii

Anthurium werffii, commonly known as the Werff's flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a vibrant green color and a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically red or orange, with a spadix that is often covered in tiny, densely packed flowers.

Anthurium werffii thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.
