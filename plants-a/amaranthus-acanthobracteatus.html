Amaranthus acanthobracteatus

Amaranthus acanthobracteatus, commonly known as the spiny bract amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant features distinctive spiny bracts that encase the flowers, giving it a unique and striking appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Amaranthus acanthobracteatus is also valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
