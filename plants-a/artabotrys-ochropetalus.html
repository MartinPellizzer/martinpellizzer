Artabotrys ochropetalus

Artabotrys ochropetalus, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
