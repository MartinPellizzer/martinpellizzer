Aristolochia chiapensis

Aristolochia chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas birthwort, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricately shaped flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
