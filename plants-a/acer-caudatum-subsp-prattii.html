Acer caudatum subsp. prattii

Acer caudatum subsp.

prattii, commonly known as the Pratts' maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Appalachian Mountains of the United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, cascading branches that give it a weeping appearance, and its leaves are typically palmately lobed with a serrated margin.

The tree produces small, red to orange-red flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its foliage changes to vibrant shades of red and yellow in the autumn, making it a popular ornamental tree.

The species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.
