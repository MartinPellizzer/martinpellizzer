Astragalus mobayenicus

Astragalus mobayenicus, commonly known as the Turkish milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus mobayenicus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
