Aponogeton ranunculiflorus

Aponogeton ranunculiflorus, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that resemble those of the common buttercup, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom above the water surface, typically in the spring. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Aponogeton ranunculiflorus is valued in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
