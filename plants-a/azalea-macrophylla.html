Azalea macrophylla

Azalea macrophylla, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea macrophylla thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
