Acosta rolandrae

Acosta rolandrae, commonly known as the Roland's acosta, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Acosta rolandrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
