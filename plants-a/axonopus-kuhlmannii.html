Axonopus kuhlmannii

Axonopus kuhlmannii, commonly known as the narrowleaf axonopus, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous inflorescences, and it reproduces primarily through rhizomes.

Axonopus kuhlmannii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its dense, low-growing foliage.
