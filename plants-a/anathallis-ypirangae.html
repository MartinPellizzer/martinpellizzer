Anathallis ypirangae

Anathallis ypirangae, commonly known as the Ypiranga orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows on trees in humid tropical forests, relying on its host for support rather than soil. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with intricate patterns, attracting specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
