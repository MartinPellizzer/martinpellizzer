Agave pseudofilifera

Agave pseudofilifera, commonly known as the filifera agave, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial succulent characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that range from white to pale yellow. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscaped gardens.
