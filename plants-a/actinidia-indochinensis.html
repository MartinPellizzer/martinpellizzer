Actinidia indochinensis

Actinidia indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochina kiwifruit, is a deciduous vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fuzzy fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique flavor and nutritional value, it has gained interest for cultivation and potential commercial use.
