Acontias plumieri

Acontias plumieri, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces slender, winding stems that climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Acontias plumieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
