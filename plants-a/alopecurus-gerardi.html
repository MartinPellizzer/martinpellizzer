Alopecurus gerardi

Alopecurus gerardi, commonly known as hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that are often tinged with blue or silver. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Alopecurus gerardi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
