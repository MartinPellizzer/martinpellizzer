Alyssoides graeca f. hirsuta

Alyssoides graeca f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy Greek alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "hirsuta.". Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
