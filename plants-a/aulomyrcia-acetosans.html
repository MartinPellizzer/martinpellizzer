Aulomyrcia acetosans

Aulomyrcia acetosans, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and prefers well-drained soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

The fruit, which is small and fleshy, contains seeds and is sometimes used in local culinary practices.
