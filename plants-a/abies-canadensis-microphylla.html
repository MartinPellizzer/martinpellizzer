Abies canadensis microphylla

Abies canadensis microphylla, commonly known as the balsam fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The needles are short, flat, and often have two white stomatal lines on their upper surface. It produces cone-like structures that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds dispersed by wind.

This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is an important component of boreal and temperate forest ecosystems.
