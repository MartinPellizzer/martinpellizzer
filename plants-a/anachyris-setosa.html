Anachyris setosa

Anachyris setosa, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fibrous root system allows it to store water and nutrients, adapting to harsh desert conditions.

Anachyris setosa is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
