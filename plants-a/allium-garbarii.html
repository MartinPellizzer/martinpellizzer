Allium garbarii

Allium garbarii, commonly known as the garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces bulbils on its flower stalks, which can be used for propagation. Its leaves are often used in cooking for their mild garlic flavor.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
