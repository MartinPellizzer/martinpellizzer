Aframomum zambesiacum subsp. puberulum

Aframomum zambesiacum subsp.

puberulum, commonly known as the Zambesi ginger, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa. It features a stout, rhizomatous stem with a distinct aromatic scent.

The plant produces clustered, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, followed by triangular, three-lobed fruits. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and covered with a fine, pubescent coating.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
