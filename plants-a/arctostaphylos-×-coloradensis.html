Arctostaphylos × coloradensis

Arctostaphylos × coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Arctostaphylos patula and Arctostaphylos scoparia. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white during the spring.

The plant is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
