Amylocarpus acanthocnemis

Amylocarpus acanthocnemis, commonly known as the spiny-stemmed arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spathe and spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, though they require careful preparation to avoid toxicity.

Amylocarpus acanthocnemis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
