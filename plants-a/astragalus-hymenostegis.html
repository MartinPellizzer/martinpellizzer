Astragalus hymenostegis

Astragalus hymenostegis, commonly known as the smoothleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus hymenostegis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
