Acacia compacta

Acacia compacta, commonly known as the dwarf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia compacta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
