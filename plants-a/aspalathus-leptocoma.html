Aspalathus leptocoma

Aspalathus leptocoma, commonly known as the small-leaf rooibos, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed along the branches. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, bean-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, dry climate.

Aspalathus leptocoma is closely related to the more well-known rooibos plant, Aspalathus linearis, but differs in its leaf structure and habitat preferences.
