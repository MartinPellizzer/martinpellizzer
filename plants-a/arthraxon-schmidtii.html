Arthraxon schmidtii

Arthraxon schmidtii, commonly known as the Schmidt's arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in water.

Arthraxon schmidtii is valued for its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality in wetland environments.
