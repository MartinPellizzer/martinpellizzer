Astragalus floridus var. multipilus

Astragalus floridus var. multipilus, commonly known as the Florida milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in native plant landscapes.
