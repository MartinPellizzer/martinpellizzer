Astragalus irolanus

Astragalus irolanus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid environments.

Astragalus irolanus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
