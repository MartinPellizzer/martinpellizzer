Aconitum leucanthum

Aconitum leucanthum, commonly known as the white monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or white in color. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
