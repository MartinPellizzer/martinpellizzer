Acalypha multicaulis var. tomentella

Acalypha multicaulis var. tomentella, commonly known as silky or woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a velvety texture due to the dense covering of fine hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
