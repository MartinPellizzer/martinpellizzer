Allium triquetrum var. bulbiferum

Allium triquetrum var. bulbiferum, commonly known as the bulbous garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in umbels, which are followed by bulbils that can be used for propagation. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and unique flowering habit.
