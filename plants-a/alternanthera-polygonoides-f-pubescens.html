Alternanthera polygonoides f. pubescens

Alternanthera polygonoides f. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet leaf Alternanthera, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its velvety, pubescent foliage.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, which bloom in clusters.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique leaf texture and vibrant coloration, making it a popular choice in water gardens and as a houseplant.
