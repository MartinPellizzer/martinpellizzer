Arundinaria cerata

Arundinaria cerata, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, grass-like foliage. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Arundinaria cerata is also valued for its resilience and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
