Aglaia montrouzieri

Aglaia montrouzieri, commonly known as the paradise fruit or yellow bird of paradise, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble a bird in flight. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.
