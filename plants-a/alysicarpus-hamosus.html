Alysicarpus hamosus

Alysicarpus hamosus, commonly known as the African burr or wild cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some regions.
