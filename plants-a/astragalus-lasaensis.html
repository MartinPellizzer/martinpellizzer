Astragalus lasaensis

Astragalus lasaensis, commonly known as the Lasa milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus lasaensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
