Anchusa dregei

Anchusa dregei, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces bright blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its fibrous stalks were historically used for making ropes and textiles.

Anchusa dregei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
