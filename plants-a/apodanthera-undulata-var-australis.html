Apodanthera undulata var. australis

Apodanthera undulata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian wavy beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds and insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in native gardens.
