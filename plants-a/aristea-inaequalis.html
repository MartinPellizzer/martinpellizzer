Aristea inaequalis

Aristea inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal aristea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in a dense cluster at the tip of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to arid garden environments.
