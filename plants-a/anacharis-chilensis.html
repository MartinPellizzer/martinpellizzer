Anacharis chilensis

Anacharis chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds.

Anacharis chilensis is often used in aquariums due to its ability to absorb nutrients and provide habitat for aquatic life.
