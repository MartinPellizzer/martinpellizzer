Acer campestre f. microcarpon

Acer campestre f. microcarpon, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a compact, shrub-like form of the common maple.

It features small, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a vibrant green color that turns red in autumn. The plant produces small, red samaras that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedging plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its dense foliage and seasonal color changes.
