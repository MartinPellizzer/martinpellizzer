Agropyron apricum

Agropyron apricum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are rich in nutrients and are used in traditional medicine and as a food source for livestock.

Agropyron apricum is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion due to its dense root system.
