Arctophila fulva subsp. similis

Arctophila fulva subsp.

similis, commonly known as the western sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is an important part of its native ecosystem.
