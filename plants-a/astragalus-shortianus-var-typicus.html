Astragalus shortianus var. typicus

Astragalus shortianus var. typicus, commonly known as the shortleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
