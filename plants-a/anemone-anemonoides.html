Anemone anemonoides

Anemone anemonoides, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink petals and a central cluster of yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a cluster of small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring.

Anemone anemonoides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also an important nectar source for early pollinators.
