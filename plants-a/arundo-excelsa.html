Arundo excelsa

Arundo excelsa, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It can grow up to 6 meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as riverbanks and marshes.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable wetland plant and a potential invasive species.
