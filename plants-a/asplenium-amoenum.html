Asplenium amoenum

Asplenium amoenum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a silvery appearance due to the presence of trichomes. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it can thrive in low-light conditions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves.

Asplenium amoenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
