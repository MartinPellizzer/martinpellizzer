Abutilon bidentatum var. major

Abutilon bidentatum var. major, commonly known as the broadleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

The species is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
