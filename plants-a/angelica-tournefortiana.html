Angelica tournefortiana

Angelica tournefortiana, commonly known as the Alpine angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a robust, branched root system and tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Angelica tournefortiana is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
