Aster oolentangiensis var. laevicaulis

Aster oolentangiensis var. laevicaulis, commonly known as the Smoothleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color. Its flowers are showy, with purple to pink petals and yellow centers, blooming in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
