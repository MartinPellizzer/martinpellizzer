Aristolochia subcordata

Aristolochia subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or reddish in color. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
