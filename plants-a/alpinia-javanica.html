Alpinia javanica

Alpinia javanica, commonly known as the Java ginger, is a rhizomatous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging in terminal spikes.

Alpinia javanica is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic rhizomes.
