Aconitum × teppneri nothosubsp.

kerneri, commonly known as the Kerneri hybrid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in raceme inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and the plant is known for its toxic alkaloids, particularly aconitine.

It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
