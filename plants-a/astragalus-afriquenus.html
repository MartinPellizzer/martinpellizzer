Astragalus afriquenus

Astragalus afriquenus, commonly known as the African milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and robust, enabling it to access water from lower soil layers.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
