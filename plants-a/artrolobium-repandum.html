Artrolobium repandum

Artrolobium repandum, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 30 cm tall, with slender, hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, similar to those of the common dandelion. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Artrolobium repandum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
