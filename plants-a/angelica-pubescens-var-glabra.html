Angelica pubescens var. glabra

Angelica pubescens var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth angelica, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring large, pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant produces tall, branched flowering stems that bear small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in complex umbels. Its roots are thick, fleshy, and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, distinguishing it from other varieties of Angelica.
