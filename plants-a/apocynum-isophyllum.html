Apocynum isophyllum

Apocynum isophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dogbane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae, which includes many species with milky sap and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, clustered in umbels, and attract various pollinators.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
