Aristolochia gourigangaica

Aristolochia gourigangaica, commonly known as the African birthwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hood-shaped flowers that resemble a pipe or a pipe stem. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, though the plant contains toxic compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
