Astragalus curvirostris subsp. oligozygon

Astragalus curvirostris subsp.

oligozygon, commonly known as the narrow-beaked milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its subspecies, oligozygon, is distinguished by its narrow, elongated pods and specific morphological features.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
