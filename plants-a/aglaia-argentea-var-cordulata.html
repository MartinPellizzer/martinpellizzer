Aglaia argentea var. cordulata

Aglaia argentea var. cordulata, commonly known as silver aglaia or white aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, characterized by its ornamental value due to its glossy, waxy leaves. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth margin and a silvery-white underside, giving it a striking appearance.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often followed by round, yellow or orange fruits.
