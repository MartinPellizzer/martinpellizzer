Acalypha seminuda

Acalypha seminuda, commonly known as the silky filbert or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often surrounded by bracts. Acalypha seminuda is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with a spreading habit.
