Aporum verlaquii

Aporum verlaquii, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Aporum verlaquii is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
