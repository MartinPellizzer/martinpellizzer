Amomum corrorima

Amomum corrorima, commonly known as the greater galangal, is a tropical plant belonging to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system. The plant produces tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and cooking. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The rhizomes are rich in essential oils and are valued for their medicinal properties.
