Albizia micrantha

Albizia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces fluffy, pale yellow to white flowers. Its root system is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Albizia micrantha is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
