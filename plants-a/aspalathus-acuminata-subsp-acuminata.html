Aspalathus acuminata subsp. acuminata

Aspalathus acuminata subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the small-leaved bushveld clover, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found in grassland and shrubland habitats. It is drought-tolerant and has adapted to the semi-arid conditions of its native range.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
