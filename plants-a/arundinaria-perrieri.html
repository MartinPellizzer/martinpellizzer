Arundinaria perrieri

Arundinaria perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's bamboo, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Arundinaria perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
