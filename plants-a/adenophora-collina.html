Adenophora collina

Adenophora collina, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Adenophora collina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
