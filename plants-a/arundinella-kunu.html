Arundinella kunu

Arundinella kunu, commonly known as the African reed or reed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Arundinella kunu is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional crafts and as a source of thatching material.
