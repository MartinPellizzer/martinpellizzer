Adesmia viscida

Adesmia viscida, commonly known as the sticky adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces pods that contain seeds. Its sticky secretions may play a role in deterring herbivores or facilitating seed dispersal.

Adesmia viscida is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
