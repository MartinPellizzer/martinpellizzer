Allium vuralii

Allium vuralii, commonly known as the Vural onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears a cluster of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
