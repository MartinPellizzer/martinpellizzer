Aegilops kotschyi var. anathera

Aegilops kotschyi var. anathera, commonly known as the Armenian wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
