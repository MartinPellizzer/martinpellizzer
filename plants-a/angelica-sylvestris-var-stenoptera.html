Angelica sylvestris var. stenoptera

Angelica sylvestris var. stenoptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved angelica, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbrella-shaped inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a coarse texture and a strong, spicy odor. Its flowers are small and white or greenish, arranged in dense clusters.

The roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
