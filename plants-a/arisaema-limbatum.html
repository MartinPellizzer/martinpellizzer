Arisaema limbatum

Arisaema limbatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are arrowhead-shaped, with a broad, lobed base, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

Arisaema limbatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
