Aniba koumaroucapa

Aniba koumaroucapa, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and medicinal preparations.
