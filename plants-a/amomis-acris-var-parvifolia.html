Amomis acris var. parvifolia

Amomis acris var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features slender, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically smaller than those of the typical *Amomis acris* variety, often with a pointed base and a smooth or slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a popular choice for water gardeners.
