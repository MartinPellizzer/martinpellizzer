Artemisia santonicum subsp. pannonica

Artemisia santonicum subsp.

pannonica, commonly known as Hungarian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its silver-green, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces a distinctive, pungent scent due to the presence of essential oils.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
