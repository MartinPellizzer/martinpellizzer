Astragalus saganlugensis var. sapozhnikovii

Astragalus saganlugensis var. sapozhnikovii, commonly known as the Saganluk astragalus, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the pods contain small, dry seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh climates.
