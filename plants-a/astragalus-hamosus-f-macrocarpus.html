Astragalus hamosus f. macrocarpus

Astragalus hamosus f. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its distinctive, inflated seed pods that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or steppe environments.

Astragalus hamosus f. macrocarpus is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
