Amelanchier fernaldii

Amelanchier fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces edible berries in the summer, which are dark purple to black in color and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in partial to full sun.

Amelanchier fernaldii is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
