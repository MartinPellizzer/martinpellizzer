Acacia matthewii

Acacia matthewii, commonly known as the Matthew's acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its woody stems are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs.

Acacia matthewii is adapted to arid environments, making it resilient to drought and heat.
