Agrostis infirma var. sumatrana

Agrostis infirma var. sumatrana, commonly known as Sumatran fine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, narrow leaves, and dense, tufted growth pattern. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous slender, awned spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
