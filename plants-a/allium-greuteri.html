Allium greuteri

Allium greuteri, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a perennial herb native to California and Nevada.

It features grass-like basal leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. The plant has a bulbous root system and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

Allium greuteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal practices.
