Acaena pinnatifida var. calcitrapa

Acaena pinnatifida var. calcitrapa, commonly known as the coastal acaena, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in well-drained soils.

Its morphology and adaptations make it well-suited for harsh, wind-swept conditions.
