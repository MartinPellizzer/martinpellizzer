Allium vinicolor

Allium vinicolor, commonly known as purple onion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It features bulbous roots and produces clusters of slender, grass-like leaves. The plant is notable for its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species.
