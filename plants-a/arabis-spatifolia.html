Arabis spatifolia

Arabis spatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in racemes, often blooming in early spring.

The leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its hardy nature.
