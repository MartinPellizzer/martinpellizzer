Ajuga bracteosa f. alba

Ajuga bracteosa f. alba, commonly known as white bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, showy white flowers in the spring. The flowers are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences and are borne on slender stems.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
