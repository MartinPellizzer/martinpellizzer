Asplenium seelosii var. glabrum

Asplenium seelosii var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that are smooth and glabrous, lacking the hairy texture found in other varieties. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.

It reproduces through spores and is known for its slow growth rate.
