Alyssum vesicarioides

Alyssum vesicarioides, commonly known as the bladderleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact size and attractive flowers make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
