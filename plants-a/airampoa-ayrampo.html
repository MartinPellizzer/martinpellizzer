Airampoa ayrampo

Airampoa ayrampo, commonly known as the golden grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Airampoa ayrampo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
