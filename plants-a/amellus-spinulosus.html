Amellus spinulosus

Amellus spinulosus, commonly known as the spiny amellus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
