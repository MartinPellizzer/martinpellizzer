Acrostichum viscosum var. lanatum

Acrostichum viscosum var. lanatum, commonly known as the silky fern, is a variant of the common fern Acrostichum viscosum.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in the Americas, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically large and pinnately divided, with a lush, green coloration.

The silky fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to humid conditions.
