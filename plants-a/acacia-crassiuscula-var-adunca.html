Acacia crassiuscula var. adunca

Acacia crassiuscula var. adunca, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, phyllodes that are narrow and slightly curved, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for traditional and contemporary uses.
