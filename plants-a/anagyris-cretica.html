Anagyris cretica

Anagyris cretica, commonly known as the crested lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive crested or wavy appearance, and its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in racemes. It produces legume pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Anagyris cretica is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
