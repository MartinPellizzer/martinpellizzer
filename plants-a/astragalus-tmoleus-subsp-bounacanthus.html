Astragalus tmoleus subsp. bounacanthus

Astragalus tmoleus subsp.

bounacanthus, commonly known as the Bouna canthus milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in challenging habitats.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
