Arenaria serpyllifolia var. neelgherrensis

Arenaria serpyllifolia var. neelgherrensis, commonly known as the Neelgherren sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Its botanical structure and habitat make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
