Allantodia costalis

Allantodia costalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved allantodia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels and are arranged in dense inflorescences.

Allantodia costalis is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a characteristic species of rocky or sandy habitats.
