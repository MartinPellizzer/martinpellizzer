Athyrium × subchristensenianum

Athyrium × subchristensenianum, commonly known as the hybrid painted fern, is a fern species resulting from the cross between Athyrium filix-femina and Athyrium capillus-veneris.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful fronds that display a range of hues including red, orange, and yellow, especially in the variegated forms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its fronds are pinnate, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that are often adorned with intricate patterns.

This hybrid is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
