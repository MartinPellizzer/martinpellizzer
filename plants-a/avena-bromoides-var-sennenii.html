Avena bromoides var. sennenii

Avena bromoides var. sennenii, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
