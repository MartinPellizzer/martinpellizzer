Aloe spiralis var. imbricata

Aloe spiralis var. imbricata, commonly known as the spiral aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiraling arrangement of fleshy, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically a deep green color with a slight blueish tint and are covered in a powdery coating called farina. This plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

It is relatively drought-tolerant, making it a low-maintenance choice for indoor or outdoor gardens.
