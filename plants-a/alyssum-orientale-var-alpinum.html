Alyssum orientale var. alpinum

Alyssum orientale var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has needle-like leaves and a compact, mat-forming growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

Alyssum orientale var. alpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
