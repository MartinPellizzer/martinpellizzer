Allium longipapillatum

Allium longipapillatum, commonly known as the giant bulbous onion, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous underground stem, which is used as a vegetable in various cuisines. The plant produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a mild onion scent.

Allium longipapillatum is valued both for its culinary use and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
