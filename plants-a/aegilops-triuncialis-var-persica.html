Aegilops triuncialis var. persica

Aegilops triuncialis var. persica, commonly known as Persian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential in forage and conservation agriculture.
