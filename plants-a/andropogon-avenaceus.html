Andropogon avenaceus

Andropogon avenaceus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies, roadsides, and disturbed areas. Switchgrass is well-known for its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, including erosion control and as a potential bioenergy crop.
