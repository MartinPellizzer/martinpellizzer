Artemisia dracunculus subsp. typica

Artemisia dracunculus subsp.

typica, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with linear, needle-like leaves that are highly fragrant.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in narrow clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky environments.

Due to its strong scent, it is used in herbal medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
