Achillea lobata

Achillea lobata, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually white to pale pink and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Achillea lobata is often used in herbal medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
