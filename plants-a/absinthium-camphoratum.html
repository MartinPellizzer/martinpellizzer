Absinthium camphoratum

Absinthium camphoratum, commonly known as camphor grass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense panicles, which are typically white or pale green. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and emit a strong, aromatic scent due to the presence of camphor.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
