Abutilon sylvaticum

Abutilon sylvaticum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.

Abutilon sylvaticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
