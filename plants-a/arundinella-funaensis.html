Arundinella funaensis

Arundinella funaensis, commonly known as the Funaean bamboo, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a tendency to roll when dry.

Arundinella funaensis is often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
