Andropogon quinhonensis

Andropogon quinhonensis, commonly known as Quinhonensis bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its coarse, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Andropogon quinhonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
