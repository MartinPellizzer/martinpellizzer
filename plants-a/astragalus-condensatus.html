Astragalus condensatus

Astragalus condensatus, commonly known as the dense milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitats.
