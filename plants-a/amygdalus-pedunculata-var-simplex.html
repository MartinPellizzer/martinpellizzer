Amygdalus pedunculata var. simplex

Amygdalus pedunculata var. simplex, commonly known as the simple peach tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces oval, fuzzy fruits that ripen to a yellow or red color, containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and fruit production.
