Aporocactus leptophis

Aporocactus leptophis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aporocactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems and narrow, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens and indoor settings.
