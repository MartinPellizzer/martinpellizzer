Astragalus sykesiae

Astragalus sykesiae, commonly known as Sykes' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus sykesiae is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
