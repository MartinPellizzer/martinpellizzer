Acacia divergens

Acacia divergens, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical pods containing seeds. Acacia divergens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
