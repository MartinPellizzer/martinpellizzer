Alysicarpus rugosus var. longiexsertus

Alysicarpus rugosus var. longiexsertus, commonly known as the long-styled burr vetch, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and pastures.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
