Aster pendulus

Aster pendulus, commonly known as the weeping aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Aster pendulus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
