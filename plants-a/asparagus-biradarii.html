Asparagus biradarii

Asparagus biradarii, commonly known as the red asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features feathery, fern-like foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its unique ability to produce both male and female flowers on the same individual, a trait known as andromonoecy. Its edible young shoots are often harvested and used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardens.
