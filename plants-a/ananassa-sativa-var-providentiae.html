Ananassa sativa var. providentiae

Ananassa sativa var. providentiae, commonly known as the Giant Cavendish, is a variant of the banana plant characterized by its large, elongated fruits.

This variety is botanically notable for its robust pseudostem and high yield potential. The plant produces clusters of fruit that can weigh several kilograms each, with a thick, peely skin and sweet, creamy flesh. It thrives in tropical climates with ample sunlight and moisture.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is often studied for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving disease resistance and fruit quality.
