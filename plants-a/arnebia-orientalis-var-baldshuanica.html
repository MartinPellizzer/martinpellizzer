Arnebia orientalis var. baldshuanica

Arnebia orientalis var. baldshuanica, commonly known as the Baldshuan poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red to purple petals and its distinctive, elongated seed pods. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
