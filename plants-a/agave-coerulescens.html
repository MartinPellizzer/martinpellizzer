Agave coerulescens

Agave coerulescens, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing white or pale green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The blue agave is also notable for its use in the production of tequila, though it is not the most commonly used species for this purpose.
