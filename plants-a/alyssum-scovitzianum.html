Alyssum scovitzianum

Alyssum scovitzianum, commonly known as the Scovitz alyssum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual or perennial herb that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.

Alyssum scovitzianum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
