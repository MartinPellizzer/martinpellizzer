Achillea × schreineri

Achillea × schreineri, commonly known as the Schreiner's yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, often white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Achillea × schreineri is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
