Acalypha dimorpha

Acalypha dimorpha, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

Acalypha dimorpha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world as a decorative plant.
