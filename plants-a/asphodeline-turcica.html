Asphodeline turcica

Asphodeline turcica, commonly known as Turkish asphodel, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, spike-like clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Asphodeline turcica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
