Adenophora hatsushimae

Adenophora hatsushimae, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.
