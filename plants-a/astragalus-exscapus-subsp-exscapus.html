Astragalus exscapus subsp. exscapus

Astragalus exscapus subsp.

exscapus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, and it produces small, hard pods that contain seeds.
