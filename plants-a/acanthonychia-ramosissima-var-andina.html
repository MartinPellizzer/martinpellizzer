Acanthonychia ramosissima var. andina

Acanthonychia ramosissima var. andina, commonly known as the Andean acanthonychia, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
