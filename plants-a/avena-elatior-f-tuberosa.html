Avena elatior f. tuberosa

Avena elatior f. tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous oat, is a variant of the slender oat characterized by its modified underground stems called tubers.

This plant is native to South America and is known for its ornamental value due to its delicate, grass-like foliage and showy, panicle-like inflorescences. The tubers, which are edible, can be harvested and used in various culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and agricultural potential.
