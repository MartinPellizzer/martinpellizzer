Anemone × wolfgangiana

Anemone × wolfgangiana, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a hybrid perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, single or double flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely cut, fern-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
