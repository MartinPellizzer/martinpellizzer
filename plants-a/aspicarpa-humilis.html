Aspicarpa humilis

Aspicarpa humilis, commonly known as the low shrub or dwarf hibiscus, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features simple, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Aspicarpa humilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
