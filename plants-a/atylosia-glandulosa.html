Atylosia glandulosa

Atylosia glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves covered in glandular hairs that produce a sticky substance, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Atylosia glandulosa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
