Ampelopsis sinica

Ampelopsis sinica, commonly known as Chinese ivy, is a woody vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy underside and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its ability to climb using tendrils and can grow up to several meters in length. Its berries are typically blue or black when mature and are an important food source for birds.

Ampelopsis sinica is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
