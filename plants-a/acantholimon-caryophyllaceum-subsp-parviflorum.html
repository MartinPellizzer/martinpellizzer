Acantholimon caryophyllaceum subsp. parviflorum

Acantholimon caryophyllaceum subsp.

parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered thrift, is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and succulent stems.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.
