Acacia adenophora

Acacia adenophora, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sandy environments.

Acacia adenophora is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
