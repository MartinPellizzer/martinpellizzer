Acrocephalus lippioides

Acrocephalus lippioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ponds, and streambanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a dense rosette, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in summer. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and spike-like inflorescences.

Acrocephalus lippioides plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
