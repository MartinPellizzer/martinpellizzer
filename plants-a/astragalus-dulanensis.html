Astragalus dulanensis

Astragalus dulanensis, commonly known as Dulan milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. Astragalus dulanensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and high-altitude areas.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
