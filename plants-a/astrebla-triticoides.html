Astrebla triticoides

Astrebla triticoides, commonly known as the Mitchell grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating resilience to drought and heat. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting native wildlife.

Astrebla triticoides is also valued for its use in livestock grazing in suitable climates.
