Agropyron ciliare var. integrum

Agropyron ciliare var. integrum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
