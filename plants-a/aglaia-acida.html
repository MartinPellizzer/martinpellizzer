Aglaia acida

Aglaia acida, commonly known as the sour apple tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically sour and acidic, hence its common name. The bark and leaves of Aglaia acida contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

It is also used in local cuisines and as a source of natural dyes.
