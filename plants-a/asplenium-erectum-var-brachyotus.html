Asplenium erectum var. brachyotus

Asplenium erectum var. brachyotus, commonly known as the short-stalked maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is a small, delicate fern with finely divided, bright green fronds that are typically upright. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in groups called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
