Amellus humilis

Amellus humilis, commonly known as the lesser bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Amellus humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
