Andropogon hochstetteri

Andropogon hochstetteri, commonly known as the Hochstetter's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for various climates.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits have made it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
