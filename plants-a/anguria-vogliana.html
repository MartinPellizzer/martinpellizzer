Anguria vogliana

Anguria vogliana, commonly known as the Vogliana watermelon, is a unique variant of the watermelon genus Citrullus.

It is characterized by its thick, pale green rind and its large, sweet, red flesh that contains a high water content. The plant typically grows in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is often cultivated for its refreshing taste and hydrating properties.
