Astragalus oeroilanicus

Astragalus oeroilanicus, commonly known as the Narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus oeroilanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
