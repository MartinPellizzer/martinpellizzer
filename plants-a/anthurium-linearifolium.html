Anthurium linearifolium

Anthurium linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 feet in length. The plant produces striking spadices, which are typically green or reddish with a contrasting spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Anthurium linearifolium is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
