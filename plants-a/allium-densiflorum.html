Allium densiflorum

Allium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces a bulb composed of layered, fleshy leaves that store nutrients. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Allium densiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
