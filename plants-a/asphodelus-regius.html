Asphodelus regius

Asphodelus regius, commonly known as the Giant Crown-of-Thorns, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces tall, spiky inflorescences that are often pink or red in color, with showy, tubular flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a resilient choice for dry gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in botanical collections.
