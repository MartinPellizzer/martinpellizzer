Abronia umbellata var. variabilis

Abronia umbellata var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, narrow leaves and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is noted for its variability in flower color and leaf shape, which contributes to its common name.
