Aster marginatus var. tomentosus

Aster marginatus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.

The varietal name 'tomentosus' refers to the dense, woolly texture of its leaves.
