Arctophila trichopoda

Arctophila trichopoda, commonly known as the hairy arctophila, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically forms compact rosettes of leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Arctophila trichopoda is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
