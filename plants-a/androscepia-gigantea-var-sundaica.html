Androscepia gigantea var. sundaica

Androscepia gigantea var. sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda giant orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain water in its humid, tropical environment.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vivid hues make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
