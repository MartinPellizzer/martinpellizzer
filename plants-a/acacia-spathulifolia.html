Acacia spathulifolia

Acacia spathulifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia spathulifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping.
