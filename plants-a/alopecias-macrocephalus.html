Alopecias macrocephalus

Alopecias macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed hairless plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale purple. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and has a slow growth rate. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Alopecias macrocephalus is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its low maintenance requirements.
