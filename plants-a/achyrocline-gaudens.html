Achyrocline gaudens

Achyrocline gaudens, commonly known as the Hawaiian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has small, narrow leaves. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and endemic species in Hawaii.
