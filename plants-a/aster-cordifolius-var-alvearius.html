Aster cordifolius var. alvearius

Aster cordifolius var. alvearius, commonly known as the heart-leaved aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
