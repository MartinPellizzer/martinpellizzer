Astragalus corniculatus

Astragalus corniculatus, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive horn-shaped seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, edible seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus corniculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
