Astragalus eriocaulos

Astragalus eriocaulos, commonly known as the downy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, hairy leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus eriocaulos plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators in its native habitat.
