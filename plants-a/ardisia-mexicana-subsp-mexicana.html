Ardisia mexicana subsp. mexicana

Ardisia mexicana subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen or Mexican starfruit, is a member of the Primulaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

The plant features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
