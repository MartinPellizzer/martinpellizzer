Alchemilla transcaucasica

Alchemilla transcaucasica, commonly known as Caucasian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.
