Arachis villosa var. correntina

Arachis villosa var. correntina, commonly known as the Correntina groundnut, is a wild species of peanut native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing one or two seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest for breeding programs aimed at improving peanut crops.
