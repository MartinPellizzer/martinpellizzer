Andropogon micranthus var. multispiculus

Andropogon micranthus var. multispiculus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, typically forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and landscaping.
