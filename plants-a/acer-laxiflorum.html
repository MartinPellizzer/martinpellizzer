Acer laxiflorum

Acer laxiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically 5 to 9 lobes, giving it a delicate appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in panicles. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Acer laxiflorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
