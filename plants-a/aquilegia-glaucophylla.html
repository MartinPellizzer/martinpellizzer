Aquilegia glaucophylla

Aquilegia glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue-leaf columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to violet spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with palmate divisions, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny to partially shaded soils.

Aquilegia glaucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
