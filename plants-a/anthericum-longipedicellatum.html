Anthericum longipedicellatum

Anthericum longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stalked bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant's leaves are linear and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
