Aphanes looseri

Aphanes looseri, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in open, disturbed habitats.

Aphanes looseri is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.
