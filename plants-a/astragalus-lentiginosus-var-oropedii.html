Astragalus lentiginosus var. oropedii

Astragalus lentiginosus var. oropedii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
