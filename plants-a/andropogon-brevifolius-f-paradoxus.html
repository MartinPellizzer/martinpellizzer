Andropogon brevifolius f. paradoxus

Andropogon brevifolius f. paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical grass, is a variant of the narrow-leaf bluestem.

It is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its unique foliage and growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland or disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming structure. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, with a distinct texture that sets it apart from other grasses.

This botanical feature makes it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
