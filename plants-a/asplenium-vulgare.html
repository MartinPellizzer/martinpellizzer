Asplenium vulgare

Asplenium vulgare, commonly known as the common spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices, walls, and forest floors. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette or clustered form. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of ferns.

Asplenium vulgare is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
