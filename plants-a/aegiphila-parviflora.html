Aegiphila parviflora

Aegiphila parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aegiphila parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and fragrant foliage.
