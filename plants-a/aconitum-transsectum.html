Aconitum transsectum

Aconitum transsectum, commonly known as the Transsect Monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a deep green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Aconitum transsectum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant to handle or ingest.
