Australina pusilla

Australina pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant native to the forests of southeastern Australia.

It grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees and relies on its host for nutrients, though it produces its own chlorophyll and can photosynthesize to some extent. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces tiny white or pale pink berries that are dispersed by birds.

Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, contributing to the ecological dynamics of the forest ecosystem.
