Allium lycaonicum

Allium lycaonicum, commonly known as the Lycaon garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces a bulbous structure similar to onions, which is used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal practices.
