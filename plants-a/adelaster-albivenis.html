Adelaster albivenis

Adelaster albivenis, commonly known as the white-flowered sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that are surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Adelaster albivenis is native to parts of North America and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
