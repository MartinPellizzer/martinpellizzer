Arabis porphyrea

Arabis porphyrea, commonly known as the purple rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, violet or purple flowers. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.

Arabis porphyrea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
