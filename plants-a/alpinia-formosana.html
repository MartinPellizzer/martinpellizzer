Alpinia formosana

Alpinia formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan ginger or Formosan ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia formosana is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
