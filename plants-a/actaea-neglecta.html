Actaea neglecta

Actaea neglecta, commonly known as white crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features palmately divided, deeply lobed leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Actaea neglecta thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
