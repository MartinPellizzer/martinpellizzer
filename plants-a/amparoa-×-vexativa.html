Amparoa × vexativa

Amparoa × vexativa, commonly known as the hybrid amparoa, is a rare and intriguing plant resulting from the cross between two distinct species.

It exhibits a combination of morphological traits from its parent plants, often displaying unique leaf shapes and flower structures. The plant is typically found in specific microhabitats, such as shaded, moist environments in tropical regions. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, offering insights into plant evolution and speciation.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
