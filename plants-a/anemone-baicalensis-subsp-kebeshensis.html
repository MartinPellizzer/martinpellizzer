Anemone baicalensis subsp. kebeshensis

Anemone baicalensis subsp.

kebeshensis, commonly known as the Kebeshan Anemone, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mongolia and northern China. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, often at elevations above 2,000 meters.

The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and habitat preferences, making it a notable member of the Ranunculaceae family.
