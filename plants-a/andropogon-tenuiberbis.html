Andropogon tenuiberbis

Andropogon tenuiberbis, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, appearing in late summer to early fall.

Andropogon tenuiberbis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
