Armoracia americana

Armoracia americana, commonly known as American horseradish, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are used for their pungent, spicy flavor. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and have a strong odor when crushed.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, contributing to its ornamental value.
