Aspasia

Aspasia, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue or purple flowers with three petals. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Aspasia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
