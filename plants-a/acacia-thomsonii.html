Acacia thomsonii

Acacia thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow flower spikes that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Acacia thomsonii also plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
