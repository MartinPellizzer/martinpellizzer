Astragalus pseudopersicus

Astragalus pseudopersicus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan.

Due to its ability to fix nitrogen, it plays a role in soil enrichment and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
