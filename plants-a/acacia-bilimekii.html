Acacia bilimekii

Acacia bilimekii, commonly known as the Ethiopian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions. Its yellow flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The wood of Acacia bilimekii is used locally for fuel and construction, highlighting its value to human communities.
