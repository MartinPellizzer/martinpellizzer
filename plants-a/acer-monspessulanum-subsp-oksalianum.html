Acer monspessulanum subsp. oksalianum

Acer monspessulanum subsp.

oksalianum, commonly known as the Oksalina maple, is a rare and ornamental species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are typically red or orange in the autumn, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic value, it is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
