Aster coerulescens var. laetevirens

Aster coerulescens var. laetevirens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It produces showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
