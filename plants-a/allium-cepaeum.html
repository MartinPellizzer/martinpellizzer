Allium cepaeum

Allium cepaeum, commonly known as the narrowleaf garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its aromatic foliage and edible qualities.
