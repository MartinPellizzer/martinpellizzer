Agrostis calycina

Agrostis calycina, commonly known as the purple false bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a fine texture. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white to pale purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Agrostis calycina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
