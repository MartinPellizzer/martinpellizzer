Arabis bellidifolia subsp. subcoriacea

Arabis bellidifolia subsp.

subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes during the spring. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or arctic regions.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
