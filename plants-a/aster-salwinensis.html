Aster salwinensis

Aster salwinensis, commonly known as the Salween aster, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster salwinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
