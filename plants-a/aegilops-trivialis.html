Aegilops trivialis

Aegilops trivialis, commonly known as wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. It is often found in agricultural fields and is considered a weed in some regions.

Aegilops trivialis is also of interest in botanical research due to its genetic relationship to wheat.
