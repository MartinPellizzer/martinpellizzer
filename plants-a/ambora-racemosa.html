Ambora racemosa

Ambora racemosa, commonly known as the clustered ambora, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact formation. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Ambora racemosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
