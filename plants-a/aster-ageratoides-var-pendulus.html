Aster ageratoides var. pendulus

Aster ageratoides var. pendulus, commonly known as the hanging aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shady areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural habitats.
