Angelica dahurica var. formosana

Angelica dahurica var. formosana, commonly known as Formosan angelica, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, compound leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in an umbel, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
