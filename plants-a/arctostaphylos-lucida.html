Arctostaphylos lucida

Arctostaphylos lucida, commonly known as the white mountain manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are typically red or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and forested areas.

Its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions makes it an important species in its ecosystem.
