Angelica viridiflora

Angelica viridiflora, commonly known as green-flowered angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves and tall, branched stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
