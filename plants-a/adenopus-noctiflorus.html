Adenopus noctiflorus

Adenopus noctiflorus, commonly known as the night-flowering moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its fragrant, tubular petals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Adenopus noctiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
