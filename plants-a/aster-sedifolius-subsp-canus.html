Aster sedifolius subsp. canus

Aster sedifolius subsp.

canus, commonly known as the silverleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
