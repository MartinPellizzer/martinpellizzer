Acridocarpus kerstingii

Acridocarpus kerstingii, commonly known as the kersting's fan palm, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
