Adicea chamaedrys

Adicea chamaedrys, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple, bell-shaped, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

Adicea chamaedrys is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
