Achmandra blumeana

Achmandra blumeana, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
