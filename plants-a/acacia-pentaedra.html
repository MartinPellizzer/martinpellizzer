Acacia pentaedra

Acacia pentaedra, commonly known as the five-edged wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular inflorescences that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods, which split open to release the seeds when mature.

Acacia pentaedra is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
