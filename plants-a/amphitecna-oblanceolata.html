Amphitecna oblanceolata

Amphitecna oblanceolata, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The orchid's unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important species in its native habitat.
