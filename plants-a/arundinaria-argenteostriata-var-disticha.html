Arundinaria argenteostriata var. disticha

Arundinaria argenteostriata var. disticha, commonly known as silver-striated bamboo, is a variety of bamboo characterized by its distinctive silvery-white stripes on its culms.

It is a clump-forming perennial that grows in dense, upright clusters, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a slight waxy coating. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its graceful appearance and hardiness.
