Athyrium brevifrons

Athyrium brevifrons, commonly known as the broad-crested maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds with a broad, rounded shape. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing fronds.

The leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
