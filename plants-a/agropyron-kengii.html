Agropyron kengii

Agropyron kengii, commonly known as the Keng's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Agropyron kengii is valued for its hardiness and is used in ecological restoration projects.
