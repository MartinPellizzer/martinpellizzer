Acacia abyssinica var. macroloba

Acacia abyssinica var. macroloba, commonly known as the large-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spherical clusters, followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has traditional uses in local communities.
