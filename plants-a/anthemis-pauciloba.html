Anthemis pauciloba

Anthemis pauciloba, commonly known as the narrowleaf feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
