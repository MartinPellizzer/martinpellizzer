Acacia erubescens

Acacia erubescens, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
