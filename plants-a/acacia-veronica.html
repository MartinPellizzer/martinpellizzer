Acacia veronica

Acacia veronica, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of tiny leaflets.

Acacia veronica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, sandy soils.
