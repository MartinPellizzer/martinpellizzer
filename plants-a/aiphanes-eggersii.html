Aiphanes eggersii

Aiphanes eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' papyrus, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and dense, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in wetland and marshy environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
