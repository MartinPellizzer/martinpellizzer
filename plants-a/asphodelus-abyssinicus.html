Asphodelus abyssinicus

Asphodelus abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and spikes of yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
