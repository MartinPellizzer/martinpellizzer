Alsine geniculata var. herniariifolia

Alsine geniculata var. herniariifolia, commonly known as the hairy alsike, is a low-growing annual herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale pink to white in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along streambanks.
