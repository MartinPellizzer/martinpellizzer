Astragalus amoenus

Astragalus amoenus, commonly known as sweet vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Astragalus amoenus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
