Ammannia aegyptiaca

Ammannia aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian ammannia, is a flowering plant native to Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. Its small, pink or white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces distinctive, three-lobed seeds.

Ammannia aegyptiaca is known for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
