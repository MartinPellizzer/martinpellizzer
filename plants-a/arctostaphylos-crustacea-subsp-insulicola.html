Arctostaphylos crustacea subsp. insulicola

Arctostaphylos crustacea subsp.

insulicola, commonly known as the island manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to maritime climates and its role in supporting local wildlife.
