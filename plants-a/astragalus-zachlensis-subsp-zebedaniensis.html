Astragalus zachlensis subsp. zebedaniensis

Astragalus zachlensis subsp.

zebedaniensis, commonly known as the Zebedaniensis milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. It is known for its small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche.
