Achnatherum inebrians

Achnatherum inebrians, commonly known as the drunken grass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often appear to sway in the wind, giving it its common name. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Achnatherum inebrians is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant species.
