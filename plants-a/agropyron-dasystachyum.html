Agropyron dasystachyum

Agropyron dasystachyum, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces spikelets that are elongated and often drooping, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Agropyron dasystachyum is valued for its forage quality and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
