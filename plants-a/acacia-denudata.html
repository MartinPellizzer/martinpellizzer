Acacia denudata

Acacia denudata, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which appear in late winter to early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

Acacia denudata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
