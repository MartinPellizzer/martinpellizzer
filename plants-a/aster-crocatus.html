Aster crocatus

Aster crocatus, commonly known as the crocus aster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

Aster crocatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early-season color.
