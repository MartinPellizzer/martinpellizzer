Acer fabri f. rubrocarpum

Acer fabri f. rubrocarpum, commonly known as the red-leaf maple, is a variant of the Chinese maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
