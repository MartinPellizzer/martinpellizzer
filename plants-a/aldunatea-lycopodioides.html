Aldunatea lycopodioides

Aldunatea lycopodioides, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and possesses whorled leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the sporangia for spore dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or rocky crevices.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often used in horticultural displays for its ornamental value and historical significance in botanical studies.
