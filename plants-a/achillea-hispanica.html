Achillea hispanica

Achillea hispanica, commonly known as Spanish yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are aromatic, with a fern-like appearance, and it produces numerous small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Achillea hispanica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
