Adesmia denticulata

Adesmia denticulata, commonly known as the toothed legume, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
