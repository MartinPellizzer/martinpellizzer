Arundinaria subsolida

Arundinaria subsolida, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or black bamboo, is a perennial grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, well-drained soils.

Arundinaria subsolida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
