Achyrospermum densiflorum

Achyrospermum densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
