Amorphophallus glaucophyllus

Amorphophallus glaucophyllus, commonly known as the blue-leafed arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves that are often a striking blue-green color. The plant produces a large, spadix inflorescence, typical of the Araceae family, which is surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
