Aster sachalinensis

Aster sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin aster, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
