Astragalus laristanicus

Astragalus laristanicus, commonly known as the Laristan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
