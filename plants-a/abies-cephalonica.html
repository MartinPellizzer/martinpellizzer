Abies cephalonica

Abies cephalonica, commonly known as the Cephalonic fir, is a species of fir native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color and measure up to 10 centimeters in length. Its leaves are needle-like, short, and have a slightly twisted arrangement.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
