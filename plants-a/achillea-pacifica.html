Achillea pacifica

Achillea pacifica, commonly known as Coastachen, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are finely divided and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces flat-topped clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Achillea pacifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
