Amaranthus hybridus var. paniculatus

Amaranthus hybridus var. paniculatus, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and dense, feather-like inflorescences that produce small, greenish flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while the leaves are also consumed as a nutrient-rich vegetable.

The plant's adaptability and nutritional value contribute to its significance in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
