Achyranthes thonningii

Achyranthes thonningii, commonly known as African knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The roots of Achyranthes thonningii are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
