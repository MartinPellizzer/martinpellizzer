Agropyron validum

Agropyron validum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, meadows, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color.

Agropyron validum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in marginal environments.
