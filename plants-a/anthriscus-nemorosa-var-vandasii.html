Anthriscus nemorosa var. vandasii

Anthriscus nemorosa var. vandasii, commonly known as the Vandasii cow parsley, is a variant of the common cow parsley.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
