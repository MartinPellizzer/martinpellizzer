Aristida purpurascens var. tenuispica

Aristida purpurascens var. tenuispica, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
