Aster acmellus

Aster acmellus, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, white, and bloom in clusters, creating a delicate appearance.

Aster acmellus is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting pollinators.
