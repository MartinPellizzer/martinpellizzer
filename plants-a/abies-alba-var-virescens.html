Abies alba var. virescens

Abies alba var. virescens, commonly known as the white fir with green foliage, is a variant of the European white fir.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure. Its cones are usually reddish-brown and contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
