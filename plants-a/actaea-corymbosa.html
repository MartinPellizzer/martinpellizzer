Actaea corymbosa

Actaea corymbosa, commonly known as white cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white, corymbose flowers. The plant produces berries that are red when mature, containing seeds dispersed by birds. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Actaea corymbosa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
