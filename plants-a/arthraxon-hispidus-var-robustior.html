Arthraxon hispidus var. robustior

Arthraxon hispidus var. robustior, commonly known as the robust bittercress, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Australia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a short inflorescence, which are followed by cylindrical, segmented fruits. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The species is characterized by its robust growth habit and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
