Astragalus conjunctus var. oxytropidoides

Astragalus conjunctus var. oxytropidoides, commonly known as the Sierra Valley milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces legume pods that contain seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
