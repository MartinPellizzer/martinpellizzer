Alchemilla amardica

Alchemilla amardica, commonly known as the Armenian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, this species thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
