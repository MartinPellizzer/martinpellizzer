Artemisia furcata subsp. flavida

Artemisia furcata subsp.

flavida, commonly known as the yellow tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a distinctive, slightly bitter flavor to dishes.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
