Asplenium petiolulatum

Asplenium petiolulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its intricate fronds and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
