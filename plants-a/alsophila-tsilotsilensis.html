Alsophila tsilotsilensis

Alsophila tsilotsilensis, commonly known as the Tsilotsil fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with its fronds emerging from the top of the trunk in a rosette formation. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a significant role in the ecological structure of its habitat.

The fronds are often used in traditional medicine and basket weaving by local communities.
