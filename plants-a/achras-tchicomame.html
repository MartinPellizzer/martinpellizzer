Achras tchicomame

Achras tchicomame, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into woody fruits. The plant's fruit contains a high oil content, making it economically important for oil production. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its rapid growth and high yield, it is widely cultivated in various parts of Africa.
