Agrostis stricta var. remota

Agrostis stricta var. remota, commonly known as the slender bentgrass, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
