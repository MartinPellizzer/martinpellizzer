Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. glomerulans

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

glomerulans, commonly known as clustered lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
