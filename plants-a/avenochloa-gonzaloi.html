Avenochloa gonzaloi

Avenochloa gonzaloi, commonly known as the Gonzalo's wild rice, is a grass species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its seeds are often used in traditional culinary practices by local communities.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
