Acalypha tenuicaulis

Acalypha tenuicaulis, commonly known as slender-stemmed glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along streambanks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha tenuicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage.
