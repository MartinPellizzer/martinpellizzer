Artanema longifolium

Artanema longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved artanema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its long, narrow leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Artanema longifolium is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
