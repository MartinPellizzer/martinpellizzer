Astragalus atropilosulus var. mooneyi

Astragalus atropilosulus var. mooneyi, commonly known as the Mooney's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, spiny stems and yellow to orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
