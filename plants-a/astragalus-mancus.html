Astragalus mancus

Astragalus mancus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are compound, with leaflets arranged in pairs, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus mancus is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
