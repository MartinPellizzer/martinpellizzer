Adenodolichos brevipetiolatus

Adenodolichos brevipetiolatus, commonly known as the short-petioled cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable crop for soil enrichment.

The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.
