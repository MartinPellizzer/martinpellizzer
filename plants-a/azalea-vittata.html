Azalea vittata

Azalea vittata, commonly known as the narrowleaf azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas of Georgia and South Carolina. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. Azalea vittata is typically found in moist, acidic soils in woodland habitats.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with a dense, compact growth habit.
