Alangium longifolium

Alangium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved alangium, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. Alangium longifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
