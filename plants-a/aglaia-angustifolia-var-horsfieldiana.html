Aglaia angustifolia var. horsfieldiana

Aglaia angustifolia var. horsfieldiana, commonly known as Horsfield's narrow-leaf aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
