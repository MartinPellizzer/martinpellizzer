Asplenium grevillei

Asplenium grevillei, commonly known as the Greville's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium grevillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
