Acacia sowdenii

Acacia sowdenii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and its clusters of yellow, feathery flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Acacia sowdenii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its foliage provides habitat for various insects and small animals, contributing to local biodiversity.
