Arabis cebennensis var. coreana

Arabis cebennensis var. coreana, commonly known as Korean rockcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia, particularly Korea.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10-20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
