Anthoxanthum aristatum var. puelii

Anthoxanthum aristatum var. puelii, commonly known as the Puel's bentgrass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and dense, mat-forming growth habit. This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its botanical features include slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
