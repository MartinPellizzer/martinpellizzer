Andropogon glaucescens subvar. cryptopus

Andropogon glaucescens subvar. cryptopus, commonly known as blue bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical panicle with small, spikelet-based flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
