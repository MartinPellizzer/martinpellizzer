Aspidium flagelliferum

Aspidium flagelliferum, commonly known as the flagellate water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Water Ferns.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant possesses a rhizome that anchors it to substrates and supports the growth of its delicate, thread-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Aspidium flagelliferum is often found in slow-moving or still freshwater habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of aquatic ecosystems.
