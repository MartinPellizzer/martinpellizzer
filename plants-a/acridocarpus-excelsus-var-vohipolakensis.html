Acridocarpus excelsus var. vohipolakensis

Acridocarpus excelsus var. vohipolakensis, commonly known as the Madagascar native tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
