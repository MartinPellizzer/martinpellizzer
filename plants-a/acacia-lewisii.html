Acacia lewisii

Acacia lewisii, commonly known as Lewis acacia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow to golden-yellow flower clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
