Alepidea amatymbica var. cordata

Alepidea amatymbica var. cordata, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that bears small, pale florets.

Alepidea amatymbica var. cordata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
