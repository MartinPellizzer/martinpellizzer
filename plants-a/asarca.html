Asarca

Asarca, commonly known as the lesser calamint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fern-like leaves that are often finely divided. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Asarca is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
