Aulomyrcia mathewsiana

Aulomyrcia mathewsiana, commonly known as the Mathews's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the humid forests of eastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.

Its botanical significance lies in its evolutionary history and ecological role in its native ecosystem.
