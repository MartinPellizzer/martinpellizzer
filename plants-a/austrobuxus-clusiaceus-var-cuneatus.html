Austrobuxus clusiaceus var. cuneatus

Austrobuxus clusiaceus var. cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed boxwood, is a shrub native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family, which includes a variety of tropical and subtropical plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact form and ability to tolerate pruning.
