Anemone elegans var. tomentosa

Anemone elegans var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
