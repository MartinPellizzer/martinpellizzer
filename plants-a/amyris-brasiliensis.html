Amyris brasiliensis

Amyris brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish resinous sap. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in the production of sandarac, a natural resin used in various industries.
