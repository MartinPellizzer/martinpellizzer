Allium arkitense

Allium arkitense, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and culinary practices, valued for their pungent aroma and potential health benefits.

Allium arkitense thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or mountainous environments.
