Athyrium deltoidofrons var. ohmurae

Athyrium deltoidofrons var. ohmurae, commonly known as the Ohmura's lady fern, is a variant of the deltoidofrons fern species.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching and gracefully shaped. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Athyriaceae family.
