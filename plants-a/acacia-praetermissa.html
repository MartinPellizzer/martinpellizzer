Acacia praetermissa

Acacia praetermissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia praetermissa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
