Actaea tracyi

Actaea tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Actaea tracyi is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
