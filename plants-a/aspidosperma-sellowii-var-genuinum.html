Aspidosperma sellowii var. genuinum

Aspidosperma sellowii var. genuinum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratonia sanguinea*, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and dense, rounded crown. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
