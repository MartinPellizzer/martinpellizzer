Adenolepis pulchella

Adenolepis pulchella, commonly known as the pretty leaf ctenanthe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. It produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Adenolepis pulchella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
