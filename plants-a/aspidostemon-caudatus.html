Aspidostemon caudatus

Aspidostemon caudatus, commonly known as the horsehead trumpet, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant has a fibrous root system and grows from a short, rhizomatous stem. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Aspidostemon caudatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
