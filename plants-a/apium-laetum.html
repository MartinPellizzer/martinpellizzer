Apium laetum

Apium laetum, commonly known as wild celery, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves and small white or greenish flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. It is often found near waterways and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall.

Apium laetum is sometimes used in herbal medicine for its purported digestive and diuretic properties.
