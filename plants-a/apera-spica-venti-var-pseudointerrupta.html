Apera spica-venti var. pseudointerrupta

Apera spica-venti var. pseudointerrupta, commonly known as the windgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, inconspicuous spikelets that are often arranged in a loose panicle. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is well adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture, and it produces pale green to yellowish flowers in the summer months.

The variety pseudointerrupta is distinguished by its slightly interrupted or irregular arrangement of the spikelets, giving it a unique appearance compared to other varieties of windgrass.
