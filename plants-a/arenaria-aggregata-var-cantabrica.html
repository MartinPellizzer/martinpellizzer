Arenaria aggregata var. cantabrica

Arenaria aggregata var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of northern Spain.

It thrives in sandy and gravelly soils, often found in dunes and rocky coastal areas. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

This variety is notable for its resilience to salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.
