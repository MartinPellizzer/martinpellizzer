Ariocarpus agavioides subsp. pulcher

Ariocarpus agavioides subsp.

pulcher, commonly known as the "beautiful ariocarpus," is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
