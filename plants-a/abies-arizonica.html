Abies arizonica

Abies arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needles, and distinctive white to yellow bark. The tree typically grows in high-elevation forests, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its cones are small and cylindrical, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The Arizona fir plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
