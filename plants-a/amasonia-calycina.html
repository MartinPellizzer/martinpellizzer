Amasonia calycina

Amasonia calycina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other woodland flora.

Amasonia calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
