Alopecurus dasyanthus

Alopecurus dasyanthus, commonly known as hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

Alopecurus dasyanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
