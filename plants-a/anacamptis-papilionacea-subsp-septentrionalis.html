Anacamptis papilionacea subsp. septentrionalis

Anacamptis papilionacea subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern lady's tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its dense, spiraling inflorescence of white to pale pink flowers, which resemble a tresses of hair.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows across northern Europe. Its rhizomes are wiry and form a dense network, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates and is considered a rare and protected orchid in many regions.
