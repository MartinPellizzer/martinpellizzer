Asperula laxiflora

Asperula laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has slender, arching branches covered in fine, dark green foliage. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Asperula laxiflora prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
