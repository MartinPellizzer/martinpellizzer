Alchemilla rhiphaea

Alchemilla rhiphaea, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla rhiphaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
