Atriplex nuttallii var. gardneri

Atriplex nuttallii var. gardneri, commonly known as Gardner's saltbush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it a valuable species for stabilizing soils in saline or coastal areas.

The plant's dense growth habit also provides habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
