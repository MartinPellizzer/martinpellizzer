Ardisia furfuracea

Ardisia furfuracea, commonly known as the velvetleaf ardisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Following flowering, it develops bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds and other wildlife.

Ardisia furfuracea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
