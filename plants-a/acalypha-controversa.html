Acalypha controversa

Acalypha controversa, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves often displaying vivid red, orange, or yellow veins against a contrasting green background. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or forest understories.
