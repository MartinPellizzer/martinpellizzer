Acacia latronum subsp. benadirensis

Acacia latronum subsp.

benadirensis, commonly known as the Benadire acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often spindly branches and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The wood of this species is generally hard and durable, making it useful for local crafts and construction.
