Aconitum napiforme var. laciniatum

Aconitum napiforme var. laciniatum, commonly known as the laceleaf monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking purple to blue flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum. The leaves are deeply lobed and resemble lace, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
