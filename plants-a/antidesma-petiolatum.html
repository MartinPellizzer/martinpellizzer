Antidesma petiolatum

Antidesma petiolatum, commonly known as the African starfruit or black starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a fleshy, star-shaped berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
