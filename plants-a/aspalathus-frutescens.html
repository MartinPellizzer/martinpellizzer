Aspalathus frutescens

Aspalathus frutescens, commonly known as African red bush tea, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by reddish-brown, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, Mediterranean climate.

Aspalathus frutescens is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used to make a popular herbal tea.
