Aciotis brachybotrya var. ferruginea

Aciotis brachybotrya var. ferruginea, commonly known as the ferrugineous aciotis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
