Achyranthes aspera var. canescens

Achyranthes aspera var. canescens, commonly known as the rough knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered with fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape with serrated edges. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
