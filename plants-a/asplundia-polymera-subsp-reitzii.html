Asplundia polymera subsp. reitzii

Asplundia polymera subsp.

reitzii, commonly known as the Reitz's arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as understorey forests. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy roots, which are used in traditional culinary practices.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its potential economic importance.
