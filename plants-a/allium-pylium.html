Allium pylium

Allium pylium, commonly known as the bulbous leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features a bulbous base with long, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pink flowers. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, leek-like flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.
