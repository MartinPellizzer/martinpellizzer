Acer campestre var. albovariegatum

Acer campestre var. albovariegatum, commonly known as the white-veined maple, is a variant of the field maple characterized by its distinctive white or cream-colored veins on its leaves.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe, often found in woodland and hedgerow environments. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and display a striking contrast between the green foliage and the pale veins. It produces small, red flowers in early spring followed by winged samaras in the fall.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique leaf patterns.
