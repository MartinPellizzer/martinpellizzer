Argyroderma brevipes

Argyroderma brevipes, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems covered in a silvery, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
