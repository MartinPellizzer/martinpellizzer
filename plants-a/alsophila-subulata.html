Alsophila subulata

Alsophila subulata, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and can grow quite long, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila subulata is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
