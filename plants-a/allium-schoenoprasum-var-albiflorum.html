Allium schoenoprasum var. albiflorum

Allium schoenoprasum var. albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower chives, is a variant of the common chive plant.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. This plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its mild garlic-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and herb beds.

The white flowers not only add aesthetic value but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
