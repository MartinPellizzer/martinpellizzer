Agrostis rubra subsp. ericetorum

Agrostis rubra subsp.

ericetorum, commonly known as red hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and woodland margins.

The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, pale green to reddish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
