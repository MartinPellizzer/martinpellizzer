Alyssum buschianum

Alyssum buschianum, commonly known as the Buschian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compact, mat-forming growth habits with silver-green foliage that is often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Alyssum buschianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
