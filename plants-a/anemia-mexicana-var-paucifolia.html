Anemia mexicana var. paucifolia

Anemia mexicana var. paucifolia, commonly known as the Mexican anemia or Mexican feather grass, is a perennial grass native to the high altitudes of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that resemble feathers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It is adapted to dry and cold climates, thriving in regions with low precipitation.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native environment.
