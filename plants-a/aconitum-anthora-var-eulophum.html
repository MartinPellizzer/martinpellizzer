Aconitum anthora var. eulophum

Aconitum anthora var. eulophum, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and its stems are erect and often hairy.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in alpine ecosystems.
