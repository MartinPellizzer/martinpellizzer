Arabidopsis umezawana

Arabidopsis umezawana, commonly known as the Umezawa's thale cress, is a species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its simple leaves and small, four-petaled flowers. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with a short stem and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its genome has been studied for its potential in understanding plant biology and genetic research.

The species is closely related to other Arabidopsis species, making it a valuable model organism in plant science.
