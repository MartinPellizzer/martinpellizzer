Arundina longilabris

Arundina longilabris, commonly known as the long-labied bamboo, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters.

Arundina longilabris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
