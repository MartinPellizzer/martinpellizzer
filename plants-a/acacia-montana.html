Acacia montana

Acacia montana, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or well-drained soils. Its flowers are important for pollinators, and its seeds are encased in hard pods.

Acacia montana is valued for its ecological role and has limited commercial use.
