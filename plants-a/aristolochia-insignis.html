Aristolochia insignis

Aristolochia insignis, commonly known as the Indian Pipe vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species in botanical studies.
