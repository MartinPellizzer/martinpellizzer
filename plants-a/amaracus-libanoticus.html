Amaracus libanoticus

Amaracus libanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanon amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to purple, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Amaracus libanoticus is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
