Astracantha cephalotes

Astracantha cephalotes, commonly known as the helmet flower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped inflorescence. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
