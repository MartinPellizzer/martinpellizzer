Acosta asperula

Acosta asperula, commonly known as the roughleaf acosta, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
