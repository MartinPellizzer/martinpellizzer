Aloysia gracilis

Aloysia gracilis, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens. Its aromatic leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Aloysia gracilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
