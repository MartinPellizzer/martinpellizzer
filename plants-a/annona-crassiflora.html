Annona crassiflora

Annona crassiflora, commonly known as the rough-leafed custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has thick, rough-textured leaves and produces large, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a smooth, spiky, greenish-yellow berry that ripens to a yellow or orange color.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
