Asterothamnus centrali-asiaticus var. potaninii

Asterothamnus centrali-asiaticus var. potaninii, commonly known as the Central Asian thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. It has a rosette growth form, with dense foliage that helps reduce water loss.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
