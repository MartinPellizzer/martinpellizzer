Alyssum montanum var. brevifolium

Alyssum montanum var. brevifolium, commonly known as mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
