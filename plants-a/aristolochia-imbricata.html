Aristolochia imbricata

Aristolochia imbricata, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble pipes. The plant produces small, dark blue to purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its flowers are pollinated by specific flies, which are attracted to the plant's strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
