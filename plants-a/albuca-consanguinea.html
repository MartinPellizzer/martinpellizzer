Albuca consanguinea

Albuca consanguinea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved albuca, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Albuca consanguinea is relatively drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
