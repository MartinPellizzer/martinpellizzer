Alevia

Alevia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Alevia has feathery, gray-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
