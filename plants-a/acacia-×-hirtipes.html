Acacia × hirtipes

Acacia × hirtipes, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a hybrid shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often serpentine branches and its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by flat, pod-like seedpods. Acacia × hirtipes is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nectar for wildlife.
