Artanthe populifolia

Artanthe populifolia, commonly known as the Poplar-leaved Artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its broad, maple-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
