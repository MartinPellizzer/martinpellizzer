Alchemilla jaroschenkoi

Alchemilla jaroschenkoi, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces upright flowering stems bearing small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alchemilla jaroschenkoi is often used in rock gardens and alpine gardens due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
