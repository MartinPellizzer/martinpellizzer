Alocasia brisbanensis

Alocasia brisbanensis, commonly known as the Brisbane arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Queensland, Australia.

It features large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a dark green to purple hue. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a showy spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near waterways or in rainforests.

Alocasia brisbanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
