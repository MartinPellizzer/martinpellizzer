Astragalus rydbergii

Astragalus rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus rydbergii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
