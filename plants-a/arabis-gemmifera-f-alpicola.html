Arabis gemmifera f. alpicola

Arabis gemmifera f. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine gemmed rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
