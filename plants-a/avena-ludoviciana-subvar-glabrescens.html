Avena ludoviciana subvar. glabrescens

Avena ludoviciana subvar. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth wild oat, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and spikelet clusters that bear small, pale blue to green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is often used as a forage plant for livestock.
