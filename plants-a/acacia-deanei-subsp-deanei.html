Acacia deanei subsp. deanei

Acacia deanei subsp.

deanei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Acacia deanei subsp.

deanei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
