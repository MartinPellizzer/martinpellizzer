Allium erubescens

Allium erubescens, commonly known as red onion, is a flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant produces a pungent, edible bulb that is widely used in culinary applications. Its leaves are long, grass-like, and often used as a vegetable.

Allium erubescens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
