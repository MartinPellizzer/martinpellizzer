Astragalus nikitinae

Astragalus nikitinae, commonly known as Nikitina's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus nikitinae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
