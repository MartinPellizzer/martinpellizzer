Aletes longiloba

Aletes longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed aletes, is a unique member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Aletes longiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens for its delicate floral display.
