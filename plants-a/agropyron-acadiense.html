Agropyron acadiense

Agropyron acadiense, commonly known as the Acadian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as dunes and disturbed areas.

Agropyron acadiense is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is used in ecological restoration projects.
