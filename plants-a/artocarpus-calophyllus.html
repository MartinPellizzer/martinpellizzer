Artocarpus calophyllus

Artocarpus calophyllus, commonly known as the rough-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that is similar in appearance to breadfruit but has a different texture and flavor. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its fruit is often used in traditional cuisine and can be cooked in various ways.

The plant also has a significant role in ecological and cultural contexts within its native regions.
