Aster laevis subsp. geyeri

Aster laevis subsp.

geyeri, commonly known as the Geyer's smooth aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows.

The plant features wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are generally hairless. Its flowers are blue to purple, with five petals and a central disk, blooming in late summer.

It is characterized by its smooth, non-hairy foliage and is often found in open, sunny environments.
