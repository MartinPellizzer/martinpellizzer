Arabis maxima

Arabis maxima, commonly known as the giant rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, waxy, pale green leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with four petals that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Arabis maxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with similar conditions.
