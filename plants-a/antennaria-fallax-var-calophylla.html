Antennaria fallax var. calophylla

Antennaria fallax var. calophylla, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically less than an inch in length, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and low-nutrient environments.
