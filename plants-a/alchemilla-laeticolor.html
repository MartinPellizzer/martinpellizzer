Alchemilla laeticolor

Alchemilla laeticolor, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage is known for its ornamental value and ability to retain moisture, making it a popular choice in gardens.
