Augustinea speciosa

Augustinea speciosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Augustinea speciosa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots make it an important component of soil stabilization in its native habitats.
