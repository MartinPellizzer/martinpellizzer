Asperula campanulata

Asperula campanulata, commonly known as the bluebell of the Alps, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Asperula campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
