Axonopus piccae

Axonopus piccae, commonly known as the velvetgrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets, and it produces wind-pollinated flowers.

Axonopus piccae is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
