Astrantia maxima subsp. maxima

Astrantia maxima subsp.

maxima, commonly known as the giant masterwort, is a perennial herb native to eastern Europe and western Asia. It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy umbels of white or pale pink flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its striking appearance, it is cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
