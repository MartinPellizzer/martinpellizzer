Anathallis dalessandroi

Anathallis dalessandroi, commonly known as the Amazonian ghost orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in the understory of the rainforest. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts in its native region.
