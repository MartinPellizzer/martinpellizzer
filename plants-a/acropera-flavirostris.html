Acropera flavirostris

Acropera flavirostris, commonly known as the yellow-beaked orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are notable for their elongated, yellow-tipped petals that resemble a beak, giving it its common name.

Acropera flavirostris thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to bloom successfully.
