Acacia nilotica var. adansoniana

Acacia nilotica var. adansoniana, commonly known as the white thorn acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its long, slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of gum arabic.
