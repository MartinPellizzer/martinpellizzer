Apocynum hypericifolium var. typicum

Apocynum hypericifolium var. typicum, commonly known as the red root or Indian tobacco, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is notable for its medicinal properties, containing alkaloids that have been used traditionally for their therapeutic effects.

The plant is also characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among members of the Apocynaceae family.
