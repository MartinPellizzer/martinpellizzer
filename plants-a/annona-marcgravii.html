Annona marcgravii

Annona marcgravii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherimoya, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces a large, fuzzy, green fruit resembling a melon. The fruit has a creamy, sweet, and aromatic pulp with a texture similar to a custard. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens.
