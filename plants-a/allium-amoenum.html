Allium amoenum

Allium amoenum, commonly known as the Persian onion or silversword, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Allium amoenum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
