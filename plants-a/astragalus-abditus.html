Astragalus abditus

Astragalus abditus, commonly known as the hidden milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, compound leaves and distinctive seed pods are key botanical features.

Astragalus abditus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
