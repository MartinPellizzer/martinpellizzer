Allium eriophyllum var. laceratum

Allium eriophyllum var. laceratum, commonly known as the lacerate garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its mild garlic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible leaves, which can be used in culinary applications.
