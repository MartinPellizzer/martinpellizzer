Acalypha scleropumila

Acalypha scleropumila, commonly known as the roughleaf spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by capsule fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
