Agialid arabica

Agialid arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a species of coffee native to the highlands of Yemen and Ethiopia.

It is a shrub that produces red, fleshy berries containing two seeds, which are used to make coffee. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high altitudes and well-drained soil. It requires a humid environment and is sensitive to frost.

Its botanical name, *Coffea arabica*, reflects its origin and its importance in the global coffee industry.
