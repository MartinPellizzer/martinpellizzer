Aster ignoratus

Aster ignoratus, commonly known as the unknown aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which typically feature a central disc surrounded by ray florets. Native to North America, this plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Aster ignoratus is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a resilient and widespread species.
