Alsophila glaucescens

Alsophila glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-stemmed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a smooth, bluish-gray sheath that gives it its common name. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Alsophila glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
