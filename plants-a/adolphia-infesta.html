Adolphia infesta

Adolphia infesta, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is its clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Adolphia infesta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
