Allium albanicum

Allium albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
