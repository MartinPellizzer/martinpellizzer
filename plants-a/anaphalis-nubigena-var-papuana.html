Anaphalis nubigena var. papuana

Anaphalis nubigena var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan woollyhead, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant is distinguished by its dense, white to grayish-white foliage, which gives it a woolly appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
