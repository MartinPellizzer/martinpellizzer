Astragalus yualicus

Astragalus yualicus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow to green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

Astragalus yualicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for wildlife in its native habitat.
