Acer platanoides f. irregulare

Acer platanoides f. irregulare, commonly known as the irregular-leaf Norway maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple (Acer platanoides).

It is characterized by its irregularly shaped leaves, which differ from the typical opposite leaf arrangement of the species. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 6 to 10 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a glossy green upper surface.

The species is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
