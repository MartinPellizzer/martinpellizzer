Ageratum salicifolium

Ageratum salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and clusters of small, fluffy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and is used in gardens and floral arrangements.

Ageratum salicifolium prefers well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
