Antirrhinum hispanicum subsp. graniticum

Antirrhinum hispanicum subsp.

graniticum, commonly known as the granite snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to sunny, Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
