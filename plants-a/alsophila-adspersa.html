Alsophila adspersa

Alsophila adspersa, commonly known as the black holly fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive dark green to blackish coloration on its fronds, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Alsophila adspersa plays a role in providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
