Alsophila johnsii

Alsophila johnsii, commonly known as the John's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of fronds that can reach several meters in height, giving it a tropical appearance. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Alsophila johnsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
