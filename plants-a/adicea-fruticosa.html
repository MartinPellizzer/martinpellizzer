Adicea fruticosa

Adicea fruticosa, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water. Its flowers, often yellow or orange, bloom in the spring and attract various pollinators.

Adicea fruticosa is also valued for its edible fruits, which are rich in nutrients and have cultural significance in many regions.
