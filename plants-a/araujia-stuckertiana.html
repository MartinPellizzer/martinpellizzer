Araujia stuckertiana

Araujia stuckertiana, commonly known as the nightshade vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which open at night. Its fruit is a distinctive, spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Araujia stuckertiana is known for its toxic properties, making it a plant to be handled with care.
