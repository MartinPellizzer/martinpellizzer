Aspilia jolyana

Aspilia jolyana, commonly known as the Joly's flame flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, spiky inflorescence. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of red and orange, are composed of numerous tiny tubular florets arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

Aspilia jolyana is often cultivated for its vibrant appearance and is well-suited to warm, sunny climates.
