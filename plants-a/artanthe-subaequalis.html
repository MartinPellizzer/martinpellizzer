Artanthe subaequalis

Artanthe subaequalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Artanthe subaequalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
