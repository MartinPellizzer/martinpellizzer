Alsinopsis douglasii

Alsinopsis douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's alsin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant, showy blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular plant in xeriscaping and native gardens.
