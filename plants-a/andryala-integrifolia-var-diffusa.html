Andryala integrifolia var. diffusa

Andryala integrifolia var. diffusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved arnica, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in cold, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
