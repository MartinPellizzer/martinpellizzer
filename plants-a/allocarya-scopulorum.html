Allocarya scopulorum

Allocarya scopulorum, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in desert or coastal environments. The plant has smooth, succulent stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually green or bluish-gray in color. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, producing a distinctive, short-lived bloom.

Allocarya scopulorum is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
