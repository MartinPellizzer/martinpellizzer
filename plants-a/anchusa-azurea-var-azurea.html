Anchusa azurea var. azurea

Anchusa azurea var. azurea, commonly known as blue anchusa, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
