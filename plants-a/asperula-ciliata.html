Asperula ciliata

Asperula ciliata, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant features trifoliate leaves with finely ciliate margins, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. It thrives in woodland or shady environments and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Asperula ciliata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
