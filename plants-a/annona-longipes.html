Annona longipes

Annona longipes, commonly known as the longipes soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is typically a green, spiny berry that matures to a yellowish color, containing a soft, edible pulp and multiple seeds. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in folk medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
