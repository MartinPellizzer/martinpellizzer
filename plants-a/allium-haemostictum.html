Allium haemostictum

Allium haemostictum, commonly known as the purple-streaked garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, lavender to purple flowers. Its bulbs are similar to garlic in appearance but are generally smaller and have a milder flavor.

Allium haemostictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
