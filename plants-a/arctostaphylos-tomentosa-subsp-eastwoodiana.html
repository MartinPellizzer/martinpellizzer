Arctostaphylos tomentosa subsp. eastwoodiana

Arctostaphylos tomentosa subsp.

eastwoodiana, commonly known as Eastwood's bearberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and evergreen foliage that remains thick and leathery throughout the year.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
