Aspidium antioquianum

Aspidium antioquianum, commonly known as the Antioquian water fern, is a species of fern native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in moist soil.

Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, and it plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
