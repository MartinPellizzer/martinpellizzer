Abies glehnii

Abies glehnii, commonly known as the Glehn's fir, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The cones of Abies glehnii are typically elongated and hang downward, containing edible seeds. This plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
