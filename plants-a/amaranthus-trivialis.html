Amaranthus trivialis

Amaranthus trivialis, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright stems, narrow leaves, and small, green or red flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Pigweed is often considered a weed in agricultural settings but is also valued for its nutritional content and ornamental appeal.
