Astragalus raphiodontus

Astragalus raphiodontus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
