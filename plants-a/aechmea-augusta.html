Aechmea augusta

Aechmea augusta, commonly known as the silver vane bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients from the environment. The leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of white or silver hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette, adding to its ornamental value.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
