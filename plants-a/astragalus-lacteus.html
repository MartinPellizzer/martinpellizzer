Astragalus lacteus

Astragalus lacteus, commonly known as the milk vetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Astragalus lacteus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
