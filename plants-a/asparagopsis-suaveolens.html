Asparagopsis suaveolens

Asparagopsis suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented asparagus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, adapting well to arid and semi-arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
