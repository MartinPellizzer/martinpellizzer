Anthemis tinctoria var. intermedia

Anthemis tinctoria var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate dyer's weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, aromatic leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.

Its historical use in dyeing fabrics with a yellow or orange hue has contributed to its traditional significance.
