Acosta polyclada

Acosta polyclada, commonly known as the clustered polyclad, is a unique plant belonging to the family Polycystaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and the presence of specialized structures called pseudopods, which aid in nutrient absorption. The plant exhibits a peculiar ability to regenerate from fragments, making it resilient in its natural habitat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unusual morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying plant evolution and adaptation strategies.
