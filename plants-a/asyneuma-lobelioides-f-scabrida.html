Asyneuma lobelioides f. scabrida

Asyneuma lobelioides f. scabrida, commonly known as the roughleaf asyneuma, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a rough, sandpapery texture, giving it its common name. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer.

The species is often found in moist, open habitats such as swamps and pine flatwoods.
