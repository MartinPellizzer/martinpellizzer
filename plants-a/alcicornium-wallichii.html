Alcicornium wallichii

Alcicornium wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Alcicornium wallichii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
