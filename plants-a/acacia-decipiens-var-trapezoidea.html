Acacia decipiens var. trapezoidea

Acacia decipiens var. trapezoidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, elongated phyllodes that resemble leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is adapted to arid conditions and is well-suited for use in drought-tolerant landscaping.
