Aciphylla squarrosa

Aciphylla squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose aciphylla, is a hardy shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Aciphylla squarrosa is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its resilience to cold and windy conditions.
