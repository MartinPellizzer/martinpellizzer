Allium prostratum

Allium prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate leek, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild onion-like flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Allium prostratum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
