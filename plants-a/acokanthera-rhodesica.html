Acokanthera rhodesica

Acokanthera rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodesian arrow poison, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant is notable for its toxic alkaloids, which have been traditionally used by indigenous peoples for arrow poison. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided by livestock and requires careful handling.
