Abies sibirica f. candelabrum

Abies sibirica f. candelabrum, commonly known as the candlewood fir, is a unique variant of the Siberian fir.

It is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like arrangement of branches, which give it a striking visual appearance. The plant exhibits dense, dark green foliage with a slightly glossy texture. It is native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

This ornamental conifer is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
