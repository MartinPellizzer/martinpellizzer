Astragalus pacificus

Astragalus pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus pacificus is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
