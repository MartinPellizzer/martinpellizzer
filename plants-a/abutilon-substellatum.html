Abutilon substellatum

Abutilon substellatum, commonly known as the starleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to South America.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Abutilon substellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
