Anacampseros subnuda subsp. lubbersii

Anacampseros subnuda subsp.

lubbersii, commonly known as the Lubbers' naked anacampseros, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a powdery coating.

The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is valued for its unique appearance and is often grown as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
