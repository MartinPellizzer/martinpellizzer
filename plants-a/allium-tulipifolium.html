Allium tulipifolium

Allium tulipifolium, commonly known as the purple onion or tulip onion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem and showy, tulip-like flowers. The plant produces clusters of lavender to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are grass-like and form a basal rosette, while the bulbs are used for culinary and medicinal purposes.

Allium tulipifolium thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
