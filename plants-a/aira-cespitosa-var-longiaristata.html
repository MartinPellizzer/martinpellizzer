Aira cespitosa var. longiaristata

Aira cespitosa var. longiaristata, commonly known as the tall false oat, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wet meadows and marshes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
