Agrostis alba subsp. maior

Agrostis alba subsp.

maior, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its hairy stems and small, glabrous leaves.
