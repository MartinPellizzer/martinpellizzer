Acer tataricum f. torminaloides

Acer tataricum f. torminaloides, commonly known as the Tatarian maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, red or orange flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
