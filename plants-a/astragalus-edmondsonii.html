Astragalus edmondsonii

Astragalus edmondsonii, commonly known as Edmondson's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture deep in the soil.

Astragalus edmondsonii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
