Aspilia malinvaudii

Aspilia malinvaudii, commonly known as the Malinvaud's torchflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence. The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that are surrounded by bracts, giving it a torch-like appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and mountainous areas.

Aspilia malinvaudii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
