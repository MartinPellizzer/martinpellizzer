Achyrophorus ramosus

Achyrophorus ramosus, commonly known as the hairy clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces strobili, or cone-like structures, which contain the reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.
