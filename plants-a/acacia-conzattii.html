Acacia conzattii

Acacia conzattii, commonly known as the Conzatti's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia conzattii is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
