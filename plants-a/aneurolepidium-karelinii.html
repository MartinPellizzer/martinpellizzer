Aneurolepidium karelinii

Aneurolepidium karelinii, commonly known as the Karelin's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Aneurolepidium karelinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
