Achyronia orbiculata

Achyronia orbiculata, commonly known as the orbiculate milkweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among the milkweed genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
