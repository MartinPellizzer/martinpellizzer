Apicra fasciata

Apicra fasciata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spotted leaves and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and natural habitats.

Apicra fasciata plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
