Aperula salicifolia

Aperula salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf aperula, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Aperulaceae and is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Aperula salicifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
