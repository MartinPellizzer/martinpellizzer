Actinidia cylindrica var. albiflora

Actinidia cylindrica var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered kiwi, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often used in jams and beverages. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, and its flowers are pale yellow with white sepals. It thrives in temperate climates and requires partial shade to full sun.

This variety is prized for both its ornamental value and its fruiting potential.
