Andropogon foetidus

Andropogon foetidus, commonly known as skunkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to reddish-purple flower clusters. Its strong, unpleasant odor, reminiscent of skunk musk, is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.

Skunkweed is often considered a weed but can provide habitat for certain insects and wildlife.
