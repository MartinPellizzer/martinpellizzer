Amsinckia aurantiaca

Amsinckia aurantiaca, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
