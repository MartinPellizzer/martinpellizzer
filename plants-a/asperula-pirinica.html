Asperula pirinica

Asperula pirinica, commonly known as the Pirin Heather, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Asperula pirinica thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of rocky and forested environments.
