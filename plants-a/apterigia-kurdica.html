Apterigia kurdica

Apterigia kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish rock violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine pubescence.

Apterigia kurdica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
