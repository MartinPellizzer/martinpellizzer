Acer saccharum f. angustilobatum

Acer saccharum f. angustilobatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sugar maple, is a botanical variant of the sugar maple native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its narrower, elongated leaves compared to the typical sugar maple, often with a more pronounced serration along the edges. This form thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas with ample sunlight. Its foliage displays vibrant autumn colors, making it a desirable ornamental plant.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
