Acacia recurva

Acacia recurva, commonly known as the zigzag acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and phyllodes that are narrow and often recurved at the tips. The plant produces yellow globular flowers, which appear in spring and are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia recurva thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil rehabilitation efforts.
