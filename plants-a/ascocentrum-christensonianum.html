Ascocentrum christensonianum

Ascocentrum christensonianum, commonly known as the Christenson's arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered around the spadix, a common feature in the Araceae family. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, giving it the common name "arrowroot," although it is not related to the true arrowroot plant.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
