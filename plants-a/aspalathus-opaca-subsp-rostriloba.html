Aspalathus opaca subsp. rostriloba

Aspalathus opaca subsp.

rostriloba, commonly known as the rostrilobed bushveld clover, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and fynbos habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
