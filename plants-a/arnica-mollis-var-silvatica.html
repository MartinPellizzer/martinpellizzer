Arnica mollis var. silvatica

Arnica mollis var. silvatica, commonly known as mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent yellow centers, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows and forest edges.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes harvested for use in topical treatments.
