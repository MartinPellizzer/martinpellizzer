Amomum macrostephanum

Amomum macrostephanum, commonly known as the large-fruited cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clustered, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its rhizomes and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Amomum macrostephanum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its economic and culinary significance.
