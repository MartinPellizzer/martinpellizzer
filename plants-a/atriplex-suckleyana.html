Atriplex suckleyana

Atriplex suckleyana, commonly known as the Suckley's saltbush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in saline soils.

Atriplex suckleyana plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
