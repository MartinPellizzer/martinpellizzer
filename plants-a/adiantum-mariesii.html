Adiantum mariesii

Adiantum mariesii, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to eastern North America.

It features finely divided, bi-lobed fronds that are typically dark green and glossy, with a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Adiantum mariesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
