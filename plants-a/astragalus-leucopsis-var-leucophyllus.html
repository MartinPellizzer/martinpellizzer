Astragalus leucopsis var. leucophyllus

Astragalus leucopsis var. leucophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pale blue to white flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its pods are slender and usually hairy as well. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and mountainous regions.

This variety is notable for its distinctive white floral display and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
