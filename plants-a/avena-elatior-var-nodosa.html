Avena elatior var. nodosa

Avena elatior var. nodosa, commonly known as the nodding oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of nodding, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are encased in a hard, bristly hull, which aids in seed dispersal.

This species is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils along streambanks and in woodland edges.
