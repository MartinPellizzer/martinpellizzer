Allionia exaltata

Allionia exaltata, commonly known as the pink root or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and showy, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its flowers are typically pink or white and are surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Allionia exaltata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
