Argemone platyceras var. typica

Argemone platyceras var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and hairy, spiny stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
