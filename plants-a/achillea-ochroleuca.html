Achillea ochroleuca

Achillea ochroleuca, commonly known as the pale yarrow, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in dry, open habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with finely divided, gray-green leaves. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and are arranged in flat-topped clusters.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
