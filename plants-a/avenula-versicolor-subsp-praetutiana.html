Avenula versicolor subsp. praetutiana

Avenula versicolor subsp.

praetutiana, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

The plant exhibits a range of color variations in its flowers, which can be green, red, or purple, depending on environmental conditions. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is often found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
