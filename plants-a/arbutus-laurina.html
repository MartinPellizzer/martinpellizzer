Arbutus laurina

Arbutus laurina, commonly known as the Madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and striking, glossy evergreen leaves that turn orange-red in the fall. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The Madrone is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
