Astragalus vaseyi var. deanei

Astragalus vaseyi var. deanei, commonly known as the Deane's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
