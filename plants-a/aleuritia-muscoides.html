Aleuritia muscoides

Aleuritia muscoides, commonly known as the musk aleurita, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Aleuritia muscoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
