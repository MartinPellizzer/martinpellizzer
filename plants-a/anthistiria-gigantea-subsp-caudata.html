Anthistiria gigantea subsp. caudata

Anthistiria gigantea subsp.

caudata, commonly known as the giant anthistiria, is a striking plant native to certain regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
