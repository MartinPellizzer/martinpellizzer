Arachnites aranifer

Arachnites aranifer, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance due to its long, slender petals and sepals. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and wasps. Arachnites aranifer thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its distinctive morphology and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
