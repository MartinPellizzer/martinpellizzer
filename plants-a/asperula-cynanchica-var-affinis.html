Asperula cynanchica var. affinis

Asperula cynanchica var. affinis, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are oval in shape and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
