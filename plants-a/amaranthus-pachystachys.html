Amaranthus pachystachys

Amaranthus pachystachys, commonly known as the tree amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stem and large, showy inflorescences that resemble trees, hence its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Amaranthus pachystachys is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds.
