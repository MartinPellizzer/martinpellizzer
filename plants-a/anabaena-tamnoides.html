Anabaena tamnoides

Anabaena tamnoides, commonly known as the blue-green alga, is a type of cyanobacteria that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to nutrient cycling in aquatic ecosystems. The plant forms filamentous colonies composed of cylindrical cells connected by cross-walls. It often appears as a green to blue-green scum on the surface of ponds and lakes.

Anabaena tamnoides plays a significant role in both ecological and biotechnological applications due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
