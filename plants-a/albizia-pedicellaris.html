Albizia pedicellaris

Albizia pedicellaris, commonly known as the slender albizia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or white pea-like flowers. Its pods are typically slender and contain several seeds, contributing to its ornamental value.

Albizia pedicellaris is often grown for its attractive foliage and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
