Albuca villosa subsp. glabra

Albuca villosa subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
