Alsophila guianensis

Alsophila guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Alsophila guianensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
