Astragalus cylleneus

Astragalus cylleneus, commonly known as the Cyprus milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus cylleneus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
