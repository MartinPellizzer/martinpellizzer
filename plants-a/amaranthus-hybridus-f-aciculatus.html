Amaranthus hybridus f. aciculatus

Amaranthus hybridus f. aciculatus, commonly known as the curlyleaf amaranth, is a cultivated form of the amaranth plant characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves.

This variety is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive, delicate foliage. It is native to the Americas and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety.

It is also valued for its potential as a nutritious grain crop in some agricultural contexts.
