Aster finkii var. moratus

Aster finkii var. moratus, commonly known as the Moratus aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
