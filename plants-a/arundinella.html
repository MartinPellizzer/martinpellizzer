Arundinella

Arundinella, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. These plants typically grow in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wet or marshy environments. Arundinella species are known for their rapid growth and ability to form dense stands.

They are sometimes used in horticulture for ornamental purposes or in ecological restoration projects.
