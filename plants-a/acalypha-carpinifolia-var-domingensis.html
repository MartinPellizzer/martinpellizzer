Acalypha carpinifolia var. domingensis

Acalypha carpinifolia var. domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Dominica.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, papery bracts that add to its ornamental value.

This species is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
