Anavinga ilicifolia

Anavinga ilicifolia, commonly known as the silky holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically leathery and have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and tropical gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for landscaping and horticultural purposes.
