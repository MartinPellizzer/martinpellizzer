Acanthophyllum glaucum

Acanthophyllum glaucum, commonly known as the woolly acanthophyllum, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can survive in extreme temperatures.

Due to its resilience, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
