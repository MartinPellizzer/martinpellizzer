Anthurium × album

Anthurium × album, commonly known as the flamingo flower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences that are typically red or orange, surrounded by a white spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium × album is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
