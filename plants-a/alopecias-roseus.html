Alopecias roseus

Alopecias roseus, commonly known as the rose-haired alpine, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its ornamental value.
