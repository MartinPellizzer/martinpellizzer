Acacia cheilanthifolia

Acacia cheilanthifolia, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a species native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flower heads, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of climatic conditions.

The botanical features of Acacia cheilanthifolia make it a popular ornamental plant and an important component of native Australian flora.
