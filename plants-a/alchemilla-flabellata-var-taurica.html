Alchemilla flabellata var. taurica

Alchemilla flabellata var. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to tolerate partial shade, it is a popular choice for garden cultivation.
