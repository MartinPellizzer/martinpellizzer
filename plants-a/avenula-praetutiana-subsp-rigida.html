Avenula praetutiana subsp. rigida

Avenula praetutiana subsp.

rigida, commonly known as the rigid hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, rigid leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster with small, pale green flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
