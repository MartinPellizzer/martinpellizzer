Amaryllis paquichana

Amaryllis paquichana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the summer months. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of fleshy, white bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Amaryllis paquichana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
