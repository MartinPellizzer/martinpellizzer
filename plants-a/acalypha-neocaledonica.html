Acalypha neocaledonica

Acalypha neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonia spurflower, is a shrub native to the island of New Caledonia in the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has palmate leaves with a velvety texture and can grow up to 2 meters in height. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
