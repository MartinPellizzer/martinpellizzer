Alepidea ciliaris var. cathcartensis

Alepidea ciliaris var. cathcartensis, commonly known as the Cathcart's beard grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
