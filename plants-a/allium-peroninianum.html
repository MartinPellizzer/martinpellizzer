Allium peroninianum

Allium peroninianum, commonly known as the Peron's onion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clustered, bulbous stems. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
