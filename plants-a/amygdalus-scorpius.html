Amygdalus scorpius

Amygdalus scorpius, commonly known as the scorpion almond, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of almond-like fruits. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
