Astragalus qorvehensis

Astragalus qorvehensis, commonly known as the Qorveh mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
