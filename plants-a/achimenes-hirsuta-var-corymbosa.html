Achimenes hirsuta var. corymbosa

Achimenes hirsuta var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered achimenes, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces clusters of flowers at the tips of its stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
