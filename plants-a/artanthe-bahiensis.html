Artanthe bahiensis

Artanthe bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahamian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, adapting to the tropical climate of its native habitat. Its showy flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom during the dry season.

Artanthe bahiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
