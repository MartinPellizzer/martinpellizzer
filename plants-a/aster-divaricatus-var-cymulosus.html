Aster divaricatus var. cymulosus

Aster divaricatus var. cymulosus, commonly known as the clustered blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and it produces showy blue to purple flowers in the summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
