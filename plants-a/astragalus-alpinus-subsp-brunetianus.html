Astragalus alpinus subsp. brunetianus

Astragalus alpinus subsp.

brunetianus, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect stems and small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in harsh, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
