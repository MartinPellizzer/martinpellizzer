Aegopogon cenchroides var. geminiflorus

Aegopogon cenchroides var. geminiflorus, commonly known as the twin-flowered goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with a distinctive inflorescence of clustered, yellowish-green flowers. The flowers are typically arranged in pairs, giving the plant its specific epithet "geminiflorus.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
