Aclisia secundiflora

Aclisia secundiflora, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Aclisia secundiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
