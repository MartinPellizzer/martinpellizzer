Achillea × khekiana

Achillea × khekiana, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations compared to its parent species.
