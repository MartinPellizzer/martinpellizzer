Alchornea acroneura

Alchornea acroneura, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Alchornea acroneura is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
