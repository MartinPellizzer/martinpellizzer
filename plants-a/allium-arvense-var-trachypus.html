Allium arvense var. trachypus

Allium arvense var. trachypus, commonly known as field garlic or wild garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valued herb in some cuisines.

It also contains compounds with potential medicinal properties.
