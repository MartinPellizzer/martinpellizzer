Aphanocarpus steyermarkii f. elongatus

Aphanocarpus steyermarkii f. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated senna, is a variant of the senna genus native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like plant characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, indehiscent fruits that contain seeds, a feature typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or secondary vegetation.
