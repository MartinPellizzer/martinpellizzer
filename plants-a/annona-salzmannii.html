Annona salzmannii

Annona salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pear-shaped fruits with a thick, sweet, creamy pulp.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a deep yellow or orange, and it is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
