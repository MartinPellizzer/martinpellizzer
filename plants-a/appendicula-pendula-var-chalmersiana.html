Appendicula pendula var. chalmersiana

Appendicula pendula var. chalmersiana, commonly known as the Chalmers' trailing appendicula, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or lavender in color, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern.
