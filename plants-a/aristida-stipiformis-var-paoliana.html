Aristida stipiformis var. paoliana

Aristida stipiformis var. paoliana, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to parts of South America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, often in temperate climates. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is notable for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
