Arabis hirsuta var. propinqua

Arabis hirsuta var. propinqua, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions, and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, while its stems are upright and bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
