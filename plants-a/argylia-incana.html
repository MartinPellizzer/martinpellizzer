Argylia incana

Argylia incana, commonly known as the woolly argyle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, showy white or pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands. Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.

Argylia incana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
