Aster bellidiflorus

Aster bellidiflorus, commonly known as the bellflower aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open woodlands and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, and bloom from late summer to early fall.

Aster bellidiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
