Agastache cana

Agastache cana, commonly known as hummingbird mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features spikes of lavender to violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a strong minty fragrance.

Agastache cana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
