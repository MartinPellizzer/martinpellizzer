Ardisia attenuata

Ardisia attenuata, commonly known as the slender flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Ardisia attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
