Asparagus capensis var. capensis

Asparagus capensis var. capensis, commonly known as the Cape asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features wiry, spiny stems that often grow close to the ground, with small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
