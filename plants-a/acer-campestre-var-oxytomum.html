Acer campestre var. oxytomum

Acer campestre var. oxytomum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maple, is a variant of the common maple tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically 5-9 cm long and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often growing in woodland or hedgerow environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
