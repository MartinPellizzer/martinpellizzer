Artabotrys rolfei

Artabotrys rolfei, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Artabotrys rolfei thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
