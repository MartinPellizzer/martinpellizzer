Acacia moggii

Acacia moggii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.

The golden wattle is the national floral emblem of Australia and holds cultural significance in Indigenous Australian traditions.
