Allionia ovata var. toscae

Allionia ovata var. toscae, commonly known as the Tuscan allionia, is a rare and striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery bloom, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to well-drained environments.
