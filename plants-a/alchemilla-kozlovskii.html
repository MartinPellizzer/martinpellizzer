Alchemilla kozlovskii

Alchemilla kozlovskii, commonly known as Kozlov's lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
