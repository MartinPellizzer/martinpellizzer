Anchusa undulata subsp. sartorii

Anchusa undulata subsp.

sartorii, commonly known as the Sartorii horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in Mediterranean regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
