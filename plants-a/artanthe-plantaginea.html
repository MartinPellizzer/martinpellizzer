Artanthe plantaginea

Artanthe plantaginea, commonly known as the silverleaf artanthe, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
