Achillea spinulifolia

Achillea spinulifolia, commonly known as the spiked yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its spiky, bract-like structures that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is attributed to its aromatic compounds and anti-inflammatory properties.
