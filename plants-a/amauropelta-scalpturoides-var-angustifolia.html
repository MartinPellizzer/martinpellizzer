Amauropelta scalpturoides var. angustifolia

Amauropelta scalpturoides var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock fern, is a variant of the rock fern genus Amauropelta.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its fronds are usually evergreen, with a leathery texture that helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
