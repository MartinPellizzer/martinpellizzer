Arabis sparsiflora

Arabis sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in colder climates.
