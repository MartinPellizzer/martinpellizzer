Acalypha salviifolia

Acalypha salviifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sacred lotus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional herbal remedies.

Acalypha salviifolia thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
