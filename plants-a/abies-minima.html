Abies minima

Abies minima, commonly known as the dwarf fir, is a small evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It typically grows to a height of only 1 to 2 meters, making it one of the smallest species in the genus Abies. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and densely packed, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. Its cones are small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color, and it produces both male and female cones on the same plant.

Abies minima is well adapted to cold, humid climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.
