Acacia verticillata var. angusta

Acacia verticillata var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattles, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and narrow, feathery leaves that are arranged in whorls. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological importance make it a valuable plant in native landscaping and restoration projects.
