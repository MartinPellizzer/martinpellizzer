Amaranthus × monteluccii

Amaranthus × monteluccii, commonly known as tree amaranth, is a hybrid species belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and may vary in color from green to red or purple, depending on the variety. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive, three-angled seed capsules.

This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in landscaping for its visual appeal and adaptability.
