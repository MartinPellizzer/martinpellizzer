Azalea boothii

Azalea boothii, commonly known as the Mexican azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea boothii prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
