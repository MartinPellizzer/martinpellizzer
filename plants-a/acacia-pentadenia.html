Acacia pentadenia

Acacia pentadenia, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in globular heads. The plant produces flat, leathery phyllodes that are typically green and have a distinctive, narrow shape. Acacia pentadenia thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in dune environments.

It is well adapted to salt-laden winds and is an important component of coastal ecosystems.
