Amsonia kearneyana

Amsonia kearneyana, commonly known as Kearney's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching habit and produces clusters of blue to lavender flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are gray-green in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid environments.
