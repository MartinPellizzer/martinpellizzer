Avenastrum vierhapperi

Avenastrum vierhapperi, commonly known as the Armenian grass or Armenian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the highlands of Armenia and the surrounding regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Avenastrum vierhapperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
