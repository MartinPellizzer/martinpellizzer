Aristolochia kaempferi var. trilobata

Aristolochia kaempferi var. trilobata, commonly known as the Japanese arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive three-lobed base, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white with a strong, musky odor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses, though caution is advised as it contains toxic compounds.
