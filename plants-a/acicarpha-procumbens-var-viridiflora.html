Acicarpha procumbens var. viridiflora

Acicarpha procumbens var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered acicarpha, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, spreading stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
