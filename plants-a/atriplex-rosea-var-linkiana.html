Atriplex rosea var. linkiana

Atriplex rosea var. linkiana, commonly known as the silverleaf chenopod, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, powdery hairs that reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor, sandy soils and withstand drought conditions.

This species is often found in desert scrub and saline environments, playing a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
