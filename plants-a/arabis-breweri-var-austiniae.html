Arabis breweri var. austiniae

Arabis breweri var. austiniae, commonly known as Austin's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Texas and New Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. This variety is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant is often found in limestone outcrops and is a symbol of the Chihuahuan Desert's unique flora.
