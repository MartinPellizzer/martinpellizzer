Aptosimum laricinum

Aptosimum laricinum, commonly known as the white mountain pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically light green in color. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, dry climates.

The white mountain pine plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
