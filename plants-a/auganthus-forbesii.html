Auganthus forbesii

Auganthus forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Auganthus forbesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
