Asperula cynanchica var. heterophylla

Asperula cynanchica var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable woodruff, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed, varying in shape depending on the environmental conditions. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
