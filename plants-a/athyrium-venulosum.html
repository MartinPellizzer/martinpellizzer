Athyrium venulosum

Athyrium venulosum, commonly known as the veined lady fern, is a deciduous fern native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, bipinnate fronds with a distinctive veining pattern that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brown, velvety scales.

Athyrium venulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful foliage.
