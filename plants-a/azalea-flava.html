Azalea flava

Azalea flava, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea flava is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes other popular ornamental plants like rhododendrons.
