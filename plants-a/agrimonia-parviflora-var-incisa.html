Agrimonia parviflora var. incisa

Agrimonia parviflora var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agrimony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a distinctive lobed shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
