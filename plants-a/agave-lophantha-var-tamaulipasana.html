Agave lophantha var. tamaulipasana

Agave lophantha var. tamaulipasana, commonly known as the Tamaulipan agave, is a succulent plant native to the Tamaulipas region of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped stems and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it highly drought-resistant.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
