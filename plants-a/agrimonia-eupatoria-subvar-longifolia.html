Agrimonia eupatoria subvar. longifolia

Agrimonia eupatoria subvar. longifolia, commonly known as slender agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, branched stems that are hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from June to August. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, often covered with fine hairs.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
