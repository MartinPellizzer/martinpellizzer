Amphiglottis polygonata

Amphiglottis polygonata, commonly known as the broad-leaved amphiglottis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects the shape of its leaves and the structure of its flowers.
