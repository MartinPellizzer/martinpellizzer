Arum italicum subsp. gaibolense

Arum italicum subsp.

gaibolense, commonly known as the Gaibolense Arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture.

The plant produces spadices, which are inflorescences composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add visual interest to shaded garden spaces.
