Atriplex gmelinii var. saccata

Atriplex gmelinii var. saccata, commonly known as sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt crystals, a feature adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Atriplex gmelinii var. saccata is often found in sandy or saline soils and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
