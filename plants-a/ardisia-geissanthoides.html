Ardisia geissanthoides

Ardisia geissanthoides, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a slight waxy coating.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
