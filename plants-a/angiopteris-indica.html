Angiopteris indica

Angiopteris indica, commonly known as the Indian water fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, delicate fronds that resemble water lilies, with a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help in its propagation.

Angiopteris indica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some cultures.
