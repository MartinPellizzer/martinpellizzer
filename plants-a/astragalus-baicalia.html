Astragalus baicalia

Astragalus baicalia, commonly known as the Baical skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
