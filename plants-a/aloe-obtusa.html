Aloe obtusa

Aloe obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and recurved, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces short, spike-like inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is often used for its soothing properties.

Aloe obtusa is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive dry conditions.
