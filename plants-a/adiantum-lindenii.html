Adiantum lindenii

Adiantum lindenii, commonly known as the Lindley's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, arching appearance.

Adiantum lindenii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful foliage.
