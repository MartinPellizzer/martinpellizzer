Anacardium cassuvium

Anacardium cassuvium, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The tree produces a hard-shelled fruit that contains a single large edible seed, known as the Brazil nut. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its botanical significance is further enhanced by its role in supporting biodiversity and its economic value in global nut trade.
