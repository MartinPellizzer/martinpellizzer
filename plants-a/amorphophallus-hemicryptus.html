Amorphophallus hemicryptus

Amorphophallus hemicryptus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a tall, spathaceous inflorescence. The plant produces a single, showy white flower with a distinctive, spiky structure. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
