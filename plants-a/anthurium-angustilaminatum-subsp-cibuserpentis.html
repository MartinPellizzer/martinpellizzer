Anthurium angustilaminatum subsp. cibuserpentis

Anthurium angustilaminatum subsp.

cibuserpentis, commonly known as the Cibus serpentis anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or purple underside.

The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
