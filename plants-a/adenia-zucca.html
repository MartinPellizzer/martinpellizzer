Adenia zucca

Adenia zucca, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like tubercles on its stems and leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five petals and a crown-like structure, followed by edible, cucumber-like fruits. Its leaves are typically lobed and can be quite large, while its stems are hairy and often have a woody texture.

Adenia zucca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
