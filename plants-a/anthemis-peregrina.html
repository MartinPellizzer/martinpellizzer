Anthemis peregrina

Anthemis peregrina, commonly known as the wandering bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are yellow with a central dark area, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Anthemis peregrina is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in harsh, coastal climates.
