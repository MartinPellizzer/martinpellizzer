Aristolochia pilosa var. ligulifera

Aristolochia pilosa var. ligulifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, heart-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding floral structure.

The species is also notable for containing aristolochic acids, which have been linked to health risks in some traditional medicinal uses.
