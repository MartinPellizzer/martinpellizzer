Aerangis bovicornu

Aerangis bovicornu, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, horn-like structures on its stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
