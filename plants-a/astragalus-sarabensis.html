Astragalus sarabensis

Astragalus sarabensis, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or greenish-yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems that help it access groundwater.

Astragalus sarabensis is often used in traditional medicine and is of interest for its potential ecological and pharmaceutical applications.
