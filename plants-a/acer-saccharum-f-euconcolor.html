Acer saccharum f. euconcolor

Acer saccharum f. euconcolor, commonly known as the European sugar maple, is a variant of the sugar maple native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which displays vibrant hues of gold and orange during the autumn season. The plant has a broad, spreading crown with multiple trunks, and its leaves are typically palmately lobed with serrated edges. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental tree in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable specimen for both ecological and aesthetic purposes in horticulture.
