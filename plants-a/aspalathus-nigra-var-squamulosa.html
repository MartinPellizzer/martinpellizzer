Aspalathus nigra var. squamulosa

Aspalathus nigra var. squamulosa, commonly known as the dark green bushveld legume, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant is often found in rocky and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and it produces small, hard pods containing seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
