Aspalathus proboscidea

Aspalathus proboscidea, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, woody stem and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by distinctive, club-shaped seed pods. It is adapted to arid environments and has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species.

The plant is also notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
