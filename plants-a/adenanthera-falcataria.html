Adenanthera falcataria

Adenanthera falcataria, commonly known as the whistling palm, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves and produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody fruit, and the plant is often used for timber and as a source of fiber.

Additionally, it plays a role in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential ecological benefits.
