Acacia venosa

Acacia venosa, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are typical of many acacia species. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia venosa is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
