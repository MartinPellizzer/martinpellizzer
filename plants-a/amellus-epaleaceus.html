Amellus epaleaceus

Amellus epaleaceus, commonly known as the pale willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, weeping branches. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a rough, papery texture. It produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Amellus epaleaceus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its graceful appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
