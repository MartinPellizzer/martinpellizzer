Astragalus poluninii

Astragalus poluninii, commonly known as the Polunin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Astragalus poluninii is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its ecological significance includes providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
