Agastache palmeri var. leonensis

Agastache palmeri var. leonensis, commonly known as Leon's hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
