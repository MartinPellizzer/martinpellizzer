Arabis egglestonii

Arabis egglestonii, commonly known as the Eggleston's rockcress, is a small, flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring.

Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lobed and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.
