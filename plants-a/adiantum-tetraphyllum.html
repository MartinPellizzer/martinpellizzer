Adiantum tetraphyllum

Adiantum tetraphyllum, commonly known as the four-leafed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Adiantum tetraphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
