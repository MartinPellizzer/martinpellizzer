Argyrodendron actinophyllum subsp. diversifolium

Argyrodendron actinophyllum subsp.

diversifolium, commonly known as the silverwood tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often vary in shape, giving rise to its subspecific name "diversifolium.". It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by small, round fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
