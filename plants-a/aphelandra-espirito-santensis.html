Aphelandra espirito-santensis

Aphelandra espirito-santensis, commonly known as the Holy Spirit tree or sacred flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that resemble flames. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to 3 meters in height under optimal conditions. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Aphelandra espirito-santensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in tropical gardens and indoor spaces.
