Acalypha heterodonta

Acalypha heterodonta, commonly known as the variable hibiscus or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its alternate, palmately lobed leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Acalypha heterodonta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
