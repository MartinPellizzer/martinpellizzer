Asplundia glaucophylla

Asplundia glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy, and often silver-colored leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Its rhizomatous growth habit makes it a valuable ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
