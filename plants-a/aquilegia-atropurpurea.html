Aquilegia atropurpurea

Aquilegia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically deep purple or violet in color, with a distinctive spurred structure at the back. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in a fine, downy coating. It thrives in woodland or shaded areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Aquilegia atropurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
