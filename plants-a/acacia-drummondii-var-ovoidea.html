Acacia drummondii var. ovoidea

Acacia drummondii var. ovoidea, commonly known as the oval acacia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive oval-shaped leaves, which are alternate and composed of small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptability contribute to its significance in local ecosystems.
