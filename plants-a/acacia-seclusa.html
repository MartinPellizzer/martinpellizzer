Acacia seclusa

Acacia seclusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical heads. Acacia seclusa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
