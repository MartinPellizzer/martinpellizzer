Armeria pseudarmeria subsp. allioides

Armeria pseudarmeria subsp.

allioides, commonly known as the garlic-like sea thrift, is a low-growing, evergreen perennial plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

This subspecies is notable for its aromatic, garlic-like scent, which is characteristic of the Alliaceae family.
