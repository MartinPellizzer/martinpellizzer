Acroptilon repens

Acroptilon repens, commonly known as western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless stems that often lie flat on the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Acroptilon repens is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in prairie and steppe environments.
