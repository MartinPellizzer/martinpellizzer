Abrus pulchellus subsp. cantoniensis

Abrus pulchellus subsp.

cantoniensis, commonly known as the Chinese coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China. It is characterized by its striking clusters of red flowers that resemble coral, which bloom in spring.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces distinctive flat, bean-like pods that turn red as they mature. The seeds are encased in a hard, ornamental scarlet aril, which is often used in traditional crafts.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
