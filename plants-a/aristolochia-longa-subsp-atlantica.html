Aristolochia longa subsp. atlantica

Aristolochia longa subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States. It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white with a distinctive, intricate pattern.

The plant is characterized by its unique floral structure, which is adapted for pollination by specific insects. Its botanical name reflects its close relationship to the species *Aristolochia longa*, with the subspecies distinction highlighting its geographic variation.

The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
