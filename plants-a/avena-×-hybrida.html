Avena × hybrida

Avena × hybrida, commonly known as oatgrass, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between oat (Avena sativa) and other wild oat species.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves and panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Avena × hybrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
