Abies concolor f. wattezii

Abies concolor f. wattezii, commonly known as the white fir, is a variant of the Colorado fir native to the western United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its conical shape and silver-blue needles. The foliage is dense and emits a fresh, pine-like fragrance. It produces cones that mature in the second year, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
