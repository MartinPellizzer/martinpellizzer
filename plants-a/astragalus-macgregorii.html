Astragalus macgregorii

Astragalus macgregorii, commonly known as MacGregor's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with small, bean-like leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and prairies.

Astragalus macgregorii is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
