Armeria helodes

Armeria helodes, commonly known as the sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of silver-gray leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in maritime climates. It is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Armeria helodes is frequently used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
