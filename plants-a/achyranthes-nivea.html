Achyranthes nivea

Achyranthes nivea, commonly known as white knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
