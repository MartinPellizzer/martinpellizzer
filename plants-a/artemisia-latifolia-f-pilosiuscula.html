Artemisia latifolia f. pilosiuscula

Artemisia latifolia f. pilosiuscula, commonly known as silky wormwood, is a variant of the common mugwort plant.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, soft hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
