Allium thracicum var. nutans

Allium thracicum var. nutans, commonly known as the nodding leek, is a bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that hang downward. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which is similar in structure to other allium species. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often found in rocky or grassy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
