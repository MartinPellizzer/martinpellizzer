Aspalathus leipoldtii

Aspalathus leipoldtii, commonly known as rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant. It is particularly noted for its unique ability to produce a caffeine-free herbal tea.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for local wildlife.
