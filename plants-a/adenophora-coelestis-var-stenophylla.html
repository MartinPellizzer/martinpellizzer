Adenophora coelestis var. stenophylla

Adenophora coelestis var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in rocky or forested habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
