Arnica denudata var. eradiata

Arnica denudata var. eradiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat branched.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with the dried flowers used in traditional remedies for pain relief and inflammation.
