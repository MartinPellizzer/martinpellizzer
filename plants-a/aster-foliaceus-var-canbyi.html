Aster foliaceus var. canbyi

Aster foliaceus var. canbyi, commonly known as Canby's leafy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its foliage and floral structure are adapted to withstand drought and full sun exposure.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
