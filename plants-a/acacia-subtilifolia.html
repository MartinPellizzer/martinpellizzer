Acacia subtilifolia

Acacia subtilifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
