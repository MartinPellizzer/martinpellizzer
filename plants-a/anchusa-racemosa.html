Anchusa racemosa

Anchusa racemosa, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched. Its showy blue or purple flowers are arranged in dense racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anchusa racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
