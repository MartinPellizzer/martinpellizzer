Allium farreri

Allium farreri, commonly known as the Farrell onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor similar to garlic.

Allium farreri is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary uses.
