Adenopus longiflorus

Adenopus longiflorus, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often surrounded by green bracts.

Adenopus longiflorus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
