Agastache breviflora

Agastache breviflora, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces dense clusters of blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight fuzzy texture.

Agastache breviflora is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
