Aconitum uncinatum subsp. muticum

Aconitum uncinatum subsp.

muticum, commonly known as the creeping aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spire-like clusters, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with wavy margins, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant to handle or ingest.
