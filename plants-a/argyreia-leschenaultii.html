Argyreia leschenaultii

Argyreia leschenaultii, commonly known as the red devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its deep red to purple stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its attractive appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
