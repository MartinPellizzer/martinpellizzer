Andropogon gibbus

Andropogon gibbus, commonly known as the purpletop grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Andropogon gibbus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
