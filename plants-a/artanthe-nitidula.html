Artanthe nitidula

Artanthe nitidula, commonly known as the smooth artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Artanthe nitidula is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
