Astragalus davisianus

Astragalus davisianus, commonly known as Davis's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellowish to reddish pea-like flowers. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Astragalus davisianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
