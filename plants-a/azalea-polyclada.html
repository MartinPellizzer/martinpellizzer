Azalea polyclada

Azalea polyclada, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Azalea polyclada is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
