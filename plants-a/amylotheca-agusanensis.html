Amylotheca agusanensis

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique morphological features, including its compact growth habit and specialized floral structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on decaying wood or rocky substrates. Its flowers are usually small and pale in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Amylotheca agusanensis is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native range.
