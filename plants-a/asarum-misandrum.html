Asarum misandrum

Asarum misandrum, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the forests of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces a unique, spiral-shaped flower that is often mistaken for a leaf due to its lack of petals. Its rhizomatous root system is used in traditional medicine, though it contains toxic compounds.

Asarum misandrum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
