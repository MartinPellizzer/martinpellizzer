Acer monspessulanum f. commutatum

Acer monspessulanum f. commutatum, commonly known as the red maple or red horse chestnut, is a variant of the horse chestnut tree.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-red foliage, which becomes more intense in autumn. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a distinctive shape that sets it apart from other maple species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This cultivar is valued for its vibrant seasonal color and is commonly used in landscaping and urban environments.
