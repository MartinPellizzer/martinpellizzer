Amicia

Amicia, commonly known as the spider lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It consists of several species, most notably Amicia intramongolica, which is native to eastern Asia. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. Amicia typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soil.

Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often evergreen, adding to its ornamental value.
