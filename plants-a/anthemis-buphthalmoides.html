Anthemis buphthalmoides

Anthemis buphthalmoides, commonly known as the false sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces yellow flowers with dark centers. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
