Acer javanicum

Acer javanicum, commonly known as the Javan maple, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, winged seeds enclosed in a samaroid capsule. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often growing in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
