Arachis diogoi f. typica

Arachis diogoi f. typica, commonly known as the wild peanut, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and develops pods containing edible seeds. It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves.

This species is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its genetic potential for crop improvement.
