Aegilops aucheri f. hirtiglumis

Aegilops aucheri f. hirtiglumis, commonly known as the hairy-leaved wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its specific epithet. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, and it produces small, two-lobed glumes.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic relationship to cultivated wheat species.
