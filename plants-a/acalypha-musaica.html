Acalypha musaica

Acalypha musaica, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Acalypha musaica is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
