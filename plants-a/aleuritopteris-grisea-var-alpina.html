Aleuritopteris grisea var. alpina

Aleuritopteris grisea var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine wood fern, is a variant of the genus Aleuritopteris, characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It is native to high-altitude regions of Asia, including the Himalayas and parts of China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with dark brown, segmented stems that produce fertile spore-bearing leaves. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a graceful, airy appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This fern is well adapted to rocky and shaded habitats, often growing in association with other alpine flora.
