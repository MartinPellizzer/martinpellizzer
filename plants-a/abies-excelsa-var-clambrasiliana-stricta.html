Abies excelsa var. clambrasiliana-stricta

Abies excelsa var. clambrasiliana-stricta, commonly known as the Brazilian white fir, is a variant of the white fir species native to the mountainous regions of Brazil.

This plant is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its conical shape and dense foliage. Its needles are typically long, flat, and have a silvery-white underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The species is valued for its timber and is also appreciated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
