Aloe cyrtophylla

Aloe cyrtophylla, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal remedies.
