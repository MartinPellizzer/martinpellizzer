Acaciella hirta

Acaciella hirta, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a small shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant has small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil.

Acaciella hirta plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
