Albizia salomonensis

Albizia salomonensis, commonly known as Solomon's albizia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Savai'i in Samoa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers, which are usually pale pink to white, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Albizia salomonensis is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional crafts.
