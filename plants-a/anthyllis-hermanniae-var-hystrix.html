Anthyllis hermanniae var. hystrix

Anthyllis hermanniae var. hystrix, commonly known as the hairy Hermann's burr, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its small, spiny pods and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has hairy foliage and stems, which provide it with a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, arid environments.
