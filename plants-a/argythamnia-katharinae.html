Argythamnia katharinae

Argythamnia katharinae, commonly known as the Katharina's tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Tamaricaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
