Abutilon pannosum var. figarianum

Abutilon pannosum var. figarianum, commonly known as the fig-leaf abutilon, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble fig leaves. The plant produces showy, spiny bracts that enclose clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are hairy and can vary in color from light green to reddish hues, depending on the light exposure.
