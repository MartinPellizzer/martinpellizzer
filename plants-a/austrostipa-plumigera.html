Austrostipa plumigera

Austrostipa plumigera, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant has fine, silky hairs on its leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments.

Austrostipa plumigera plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
