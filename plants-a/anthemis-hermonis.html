Anthemis hermonis

Anthemis hermonis, commonly known as the Hermon's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are finely divided and covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat.
