Aridaria plenifolia

Aridaria plenifolia, commonly known as the desert leaf plant, is a succulent native to arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white flowers during the rainy season, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb moisture from the soil efficiently.

Aridaria plenifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
