Andropogon odoratus

Andropogon odoratus, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green leaves and a pleasant, sweet aroma when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open areas such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowering spikes produce small, pale green to brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Sweet grass is often used in traditional Native American ceremonies and for its ornamental value.
