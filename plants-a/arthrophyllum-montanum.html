Arthrophyllum montanum

Arthrophyllum montanum, commonly known as the mountain arthrophyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
