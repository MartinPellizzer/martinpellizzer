Ailanthus vilmoriniana var. henanensis

Ailanthus vilmoriniana var. henanensis, commonly known as the Henan tree of fortune, is a variant of the tree of heaven, native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with 12 to 20 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate urban environments.
