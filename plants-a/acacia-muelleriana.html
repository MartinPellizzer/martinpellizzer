Acacia muelleriana

Acacia muelleriana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia muelleriana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system and waxy leaf surfaces.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
