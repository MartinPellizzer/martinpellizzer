Anathallis reptilis

Anathallis reptilis, commonly known as the creeping anathallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like growth habit and is often found epiphytic on trees in humid forests. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves and a unique reproductive system involving mycorrhizal associations.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
