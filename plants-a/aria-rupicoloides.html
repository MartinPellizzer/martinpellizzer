Aria rupicoloides

Aria rupicoloides, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as deep root systems and succulent leaves to conserve water. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed, providing structural support in its harsh habitat.

Aria rupicoloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky soils.
