Anthyllis gracilis

Anthyllis gracilis, commonly known as slender carline thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are usually pale purple or pink.

Anthyllis gracilis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
