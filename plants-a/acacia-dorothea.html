Acacia dorothea

Acacia dorothea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. Its seed pods are woody and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia dorothea is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for native wildlife.
