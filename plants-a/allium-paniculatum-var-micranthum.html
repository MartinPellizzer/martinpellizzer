Allium paniculatum var. micranthum

Allium paniculatum var. micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and emit a mild garlic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, arranged in a panicle inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible leaves and ornamental value in gardens.
