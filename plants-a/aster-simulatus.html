Aster simulatus

Aster simulatus, commonly known as the mimic aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Aster simulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
