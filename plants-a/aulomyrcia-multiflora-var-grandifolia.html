Aulomyrcia multiflora var. grandifolia

Aulomyrcia multiflora var. grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflorous myrcia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known species like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with lance-shaped leaves that have a glossy, dark green surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are fragrant, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
