Amaranthus acutilobus

Amaranthus acutilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, bisexual flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its seeds are small, red or brown, and are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes.

Amaranthus acutilobus is known for its adaptability and is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
