Alchemilla palmata var. transsilvanica

Alchemilla palmata var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
