Acanthophyllum macrodon

Acanthophyllum macrodon, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and compact, spherical shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations include deep root systems and waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Acanthophyllum macrodon plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
