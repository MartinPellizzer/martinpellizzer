Acacia sphacelata subsp. verticillata

Acacia sphacelata subsp.

verticillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are arranged in whorls of three.

The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
