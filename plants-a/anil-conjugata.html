Anil conjugata

Anil conjugata, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue petals, which are actually a result of a unique pigmentation caused by a combination of anthocyanins and other flavonoids. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences, and it is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

However, it is relatively rare in cultivation due to its specific environmental requirements.
