Artemisia manshurica

Artemisia manshurica, commonly known as Manchurian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or mountainous regions. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

Artemisia manshurica is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
