Astragalus bonannii

Astragalus bonannii, commonly known as the Bonannii milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

Astragalus bonannii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
