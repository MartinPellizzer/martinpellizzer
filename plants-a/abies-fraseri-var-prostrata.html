Abies fraseri var. prostrata

Abies fraseri var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate Fraser fir, is a low-growing variant of the Fraser fir.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant typically forms a cushion-like structure, making it well-suited for rock gardens or alpine settings. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow-green cones that mature to a reddish-brown.

This variety is native to the southern Appalachian Mountains and thrives in cool, moist, well-drained soils.
