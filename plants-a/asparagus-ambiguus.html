Asparagus ambiguus

Asparagus ambiguus, commonly known as the common asparagus fern, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features feathery, arching leaves that form a dense, shrub-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in moist, shaded habitats.
