Adhatoda palustris

Adhatoda palustris, commonly known as marsh adhatoda, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, fertile soils. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential respiratory and anti-inflammatory benefits.

Adhatoda palustris is also valued in traditional medicine for its use in treating coughs and bronchial ailments.
