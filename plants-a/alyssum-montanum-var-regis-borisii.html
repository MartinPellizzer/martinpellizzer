Alyssum montanum var. regis-borisii

Alyssum montanum var. regis-borisii, commonly known as the King Boris allyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with fine, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
