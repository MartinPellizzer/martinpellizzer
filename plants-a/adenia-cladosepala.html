Adenia cladosepala

Adenia cladosepala, commonly known as the clustered adenium, is a striking plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a unique growth habit, often forming dense clusters of stems that give it its common name. Its leaves are typically large and leathery, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Adenia cladosepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry environments.
