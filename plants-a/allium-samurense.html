Allium samurense

Allium samurense, commonly known as the Japanese garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spherical purple or pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs and leaves that are used in traditional Japanese cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Allium samurense is valued for both its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
