Apocynum salicifolium

Apocynum salicifolium, commonly known as the silky dogbane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually white or pale green, followed by capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds.

The plant contains cardiac glycosides, which are poisonous to humans and animals.
