Andropogon pauciflorus

Andropogon pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with few flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
