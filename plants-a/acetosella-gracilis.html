Acetosella gracilis

Acetosella gracilis, commonly known as slender wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Acetosella gracilis is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.
