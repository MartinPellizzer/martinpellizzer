Arundinella villosa

Arundinella villosa, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.

Arundinella villosa is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
