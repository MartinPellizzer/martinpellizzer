Alnus tristis

Alnus tristis, commonly known as the sourwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrated leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds. It has a shallow root system and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Alnus tristis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
