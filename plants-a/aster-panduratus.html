Aster panduratus

Aster panduratus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring linear, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster panduratus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
