Agapetes bhareliana

Agapetes bhareliana, commonly known as the Bhareliana fairy bell, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical gardens.
