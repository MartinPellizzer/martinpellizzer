Agoseris glauca var. agrestis

Agoseris glauca var. agrestis, commonly known as the rough blue fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flowers are blue to purple in color.

It serves as a nectar source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
