Arabis hirsuta f. lilacina

Arabis hirsuta f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-stemmed rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale lilac. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in marginal garden conditions.
