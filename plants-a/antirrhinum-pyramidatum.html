Antirrhinum pyramidatum

Antirrhinum pyramidatum, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous tubular, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink to red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Antirrhinum pyramidatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
