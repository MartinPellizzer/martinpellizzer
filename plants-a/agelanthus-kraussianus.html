Agelanthus kraussianus

Agelanthus kraussianus, commonly known as the Kraussia thistle, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
