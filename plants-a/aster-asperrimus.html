Aster asperrimus

Aster asperrimus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces showy purple to blue flowers in the fall. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Aster asperrimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
