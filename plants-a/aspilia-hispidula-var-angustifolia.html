Aspilia hispidula var. angustifolia

Aspilia hispidula var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a central dark disc. The leaves are narrow and hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
