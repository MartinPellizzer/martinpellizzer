Acacia intermedia

Acacia intermedia, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia intermedia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
