Alectra capensis

Alectra capensis, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, linear leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Alectra capensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid environments.
