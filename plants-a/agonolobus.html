Agonolobus

Agonolobus, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Agonolobus is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is not typically used as a food crop.
