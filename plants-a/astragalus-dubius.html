Astragalus dubius

Astragalus dubius, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Astragalus dubius has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
