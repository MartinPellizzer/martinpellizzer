Agrostis munroana var. pendryi

Agrostis munroana var. pendryi, commonly known as the pendry's bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and compact inflorescences. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along stream banks. Its botanical features include slender stems, narrow leaves, and small, spike-like flower clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
