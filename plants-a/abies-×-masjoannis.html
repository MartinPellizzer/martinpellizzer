Abies × masjoannis

Abies × masjoannis, commonly known as the hybrid fir, is a naturally occurring cross between Abies alba (European fir) and Abies balsamea (balsam fir).

It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The plant produces small, rounded cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
