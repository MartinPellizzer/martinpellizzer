Acer sterculiaceum subsp. franchetii

Acer sterculiaceum subsp.

franchetii, commonly known as the Franchet's maple, is a deciduous tree native to China. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
