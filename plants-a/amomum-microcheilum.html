Amomum microcheilum

Amomum microcheilum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically clustered in spikes and bloom during the rainy season.

The fruit, which is a capsule, contains aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
