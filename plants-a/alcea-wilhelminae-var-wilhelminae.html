Alcea wilhelminae var. wilhelminae

Alcea wilhelminae var. wilhelminae, commonly known as the Wilhelmine hollyhock, is a herbaceous annual plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features showy, rose-like flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has upright, hairy stems and palmately lobed leaves with a rough texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
