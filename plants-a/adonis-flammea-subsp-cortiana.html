Adonis flammea subsp. cortiana

Adonis flammea subsp.

cortiana, commonly known as the Alpine flame gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered in a fine white pubescence.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in alpine ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
