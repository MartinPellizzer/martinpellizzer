Aconitum kusnezoffii subsp. birobidshanicum

Aconitum kusnezoffii subsp.

birobidshanicum, commonly known as the Birobidzhan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike inflorescences. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and its roots contain potent alkaloids.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.
