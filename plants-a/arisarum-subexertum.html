Arisarum subexertum

Arisarum subexertum, commonly known as the white adder's mouth, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers enclosed within a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
