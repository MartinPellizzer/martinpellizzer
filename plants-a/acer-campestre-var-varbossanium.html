Acer campestre var. varbossanium

Acer campestre var. varbossanium, commonly known as the Varbossa maple, is a variant of the field maple, native to the mountainous regions of southern Italy.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 8 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a samara, a winged seed vessel.

The Varbossa maple is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
