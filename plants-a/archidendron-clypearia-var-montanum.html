Archidendron clypearia var. montanum

Archidendron clypearia var. montanum, commonly known as mountain clypearia, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in mountainous regions. The plant features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
