Arundinaria variabilis

Arundinaria variabilis, commonly known as the variable bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria variabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
