Aphaca

Aphaca, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish. Aphaca is popular as a houseplant due to its vibrant colors and ease of care.

Its flowers typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white, adding ornamental value to indoor spaces.
