Azalea pontica var. calendulacea

Azalea pontica var. calendulacea, commonly known as the Persian azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange-red, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
