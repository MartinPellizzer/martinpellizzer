Artemisia argyi

Artemisia argyi, commonly known as Chinese mugwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-green, aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Artemisia argyi thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.

Its essential oils contain compounds like camphor and thujone, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
