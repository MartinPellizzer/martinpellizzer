Aspidoglossum woodii

Aspidoglossum woodii, commonly known as the Wood's snake plant, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by distinctive blue or purple berries. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it an excellent choice for indoor gardening.

Its air-purifying qualities and ornamental value have made it a popular houseplant worldwide.
