Allophylus subfalcatus var. subfalcatus

Allophylus subfalcatus var. subfalcatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alder, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by drupes that are often red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
