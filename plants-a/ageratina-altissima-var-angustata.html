Ageratina altissima var. angustata

Ageratina altissima var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fluffy, white to bluish-purple flower clusters. It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
