Aristolochia macroura var. subtrifida

Aristolochia macroura var. subtrifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name "subtrifida.". It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and ecological significance.
