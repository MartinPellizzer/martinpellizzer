Astragalus vesicarius proles hercegovinus

Astragalus vesicarius proles hercegovinus, commonly known as the Hercegovina milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet having a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are followed by inflated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for certain wildlife species.
