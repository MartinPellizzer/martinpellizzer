Aristida quinqueseta

Aristida quinqueseta, commonly known as slender threeawn, is a grass species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often bristled with short, stiff hairs.

Aristida quinqueseta is adapted to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
