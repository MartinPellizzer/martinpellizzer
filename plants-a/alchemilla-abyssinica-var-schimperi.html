Alchemilla abyssinica var. schimperi

Alchemilla abyssinica var. schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
