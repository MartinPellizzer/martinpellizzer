Analyrium

Analyrium, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its striking silvery foliage.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is native to arid regions of the southwestern United States. The plant's leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, reflective hairs that help reduce water loss. It produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom in the early morning.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
