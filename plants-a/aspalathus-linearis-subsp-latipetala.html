Aspalathus linearis subsp. latipetala

Aspalathus linearis subsp.

latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petaled woolly bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its linear, grey-green leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native environment.
