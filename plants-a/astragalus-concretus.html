Astragalus concretus

Astragalus concretus, commonly known as the compact milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep taproots that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Astragalus concretus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
