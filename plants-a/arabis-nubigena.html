Arabis nubigena

Arabis nubigena, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Arabis nubigena is known for its ability to withstand harsh weather conditions, making it a resilient species in high-altitude ecosystems.
