Adenocalymma inundatum

Adenocalymma inundatum, commonly known as the water bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that aid in buoyancy and trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, while the stems are flexible and adapted for underwater life.

Adenocalymma inundatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
