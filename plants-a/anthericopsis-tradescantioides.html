Anthericopsis tradescantioides

Anthericopsis tradescantioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating that gives them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and can spread aggressively, making it a potential invasive species in some areas.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
