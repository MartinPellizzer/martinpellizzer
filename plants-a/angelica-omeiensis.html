Angelica omeiensis

Angelica omeiensis, commonly known as the Chinese angelica, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Angelica omeiensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
