Agropyron cristatum var. glabrum

Agropyron cristatum var. glabrum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally hairless, giving it its varietal name "glabrum.". The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine, arctic, and steppe environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
