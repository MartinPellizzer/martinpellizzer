Abrus precatorius var. leucospermus

Abrus precatorius var. leucospermus, commonly known as the white-flowered jequirity, is a variant of the jequirity plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that contrast with its green, compound leaves. The plant produces distinctive, flat, bean-like pods that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional crafts and as a natural dye. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a branching structure that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
