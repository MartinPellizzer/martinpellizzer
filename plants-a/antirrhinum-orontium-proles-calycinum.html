Antirrhinum orontium proles calycinum

Antirrhinum orontium proles calycinum, commonly known as the California wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The California wallflower is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
