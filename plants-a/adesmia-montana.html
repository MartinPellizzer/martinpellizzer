Adesmia montana

Adesmia montana, commonly known as the mountain adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its root system has the ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Adesmia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
