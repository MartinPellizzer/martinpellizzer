Amphicosmia beyrichiana

Amphicosmia beyrichiana, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, pendant-like flowers that resemble small bells, typically ranging in color from yellow to orange. Its unique morphology includes long, narrow leaves and a complex inflorescence structure.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and study.
