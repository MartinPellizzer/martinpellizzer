Allium cepa subsp. fistulosum

Allium cepa subsp.

fistulosum, commonly known as the bulb onion or wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It features a bulb composed of numerous fleshy, cylindrical segments that are typically white or pale yellow.

The plant produces grass-like leaves that are hollow and cylindrical, a characteristic feature of the Allium genus. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are usually pink or purple in color.

This subspecies is valued for its edible bulbs and is often cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes.
