Amaralia

Amaralia, commonly known as the amaryllis, is a genus of flowering plants in the Amaryllidaceae family.

These plants are native to the tropical regions of the Americas and are characterized by their showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. Amaralia species typically have thick, fleshy leaves and produce vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, white, and purple. They are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

The bulbs of Amaralia plants are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
