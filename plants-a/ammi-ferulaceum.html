Ammi ferulaceum

Ammi ferulaceum, commonly known as bishop's weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its seeds are small, dry, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Ammi ferulaceum is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a source of essential oils.
