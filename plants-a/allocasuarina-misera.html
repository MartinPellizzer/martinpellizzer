Allocasuarina misera

Allocasuarina misera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sheoak, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like leaves that are clustered in spirals along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Allocasuarina misera plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
