Aphelandra mollissima

Aphelandra mollissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with a deep red or orange spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique foliage and dramatic inflorescence make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
