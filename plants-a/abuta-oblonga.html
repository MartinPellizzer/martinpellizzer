Abuta oblonga

Abuta oblonga, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Abuta oblonga thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
