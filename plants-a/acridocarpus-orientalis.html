Acridocarpus orientalis

Acridocarpus orientalis, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
