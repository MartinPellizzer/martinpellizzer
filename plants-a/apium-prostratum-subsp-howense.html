Apium prostratum subsp. howense

Apium prostratum subsp.

howense, commonly known as the creeping cow parsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features a prostrate growth habit with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are finely divided, with a fern-like appearance, and the plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various conditions.
