Asarum himalaicum

Asarum himalaicum, commonly known as the Himalayan wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, aromatic underground rhizomes. The plant produces a showy, hooded flower that resembles a pipe, which is typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories at high altitudes.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
