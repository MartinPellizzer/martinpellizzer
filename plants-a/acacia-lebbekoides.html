Acacia lebbekoides

Acacia lebbekoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, often thornless stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia lebbekoides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is often used in traditional medicine and for its hard, durable wood.
