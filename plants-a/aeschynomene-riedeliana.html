Aeschynomene riedeliana

Aeschynomene riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's horsegram, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used as a forage crop due to its nutritional value for livestock.
