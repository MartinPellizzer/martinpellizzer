Anthemis pseudocotula

Anthemis pseudocotula, commonly known as the narrowleaf ratfink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, and the leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in arid environments.
