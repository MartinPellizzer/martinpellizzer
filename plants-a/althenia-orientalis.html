Althenia orientalis

Althenia orientalis, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in shades of violet, purple, or white, and are known for their delicate fragrance.

Althenia orientalis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
