Aspilia cardenasii

Aspilia cardenasii, commonly known as the Mexican torch flower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow flowers surrounded by bright red bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its unique appearance and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
