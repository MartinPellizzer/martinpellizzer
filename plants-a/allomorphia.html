Allomorphia

Allomorphia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Allomorphia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with a dense canopy that provides shade in garden settings.
