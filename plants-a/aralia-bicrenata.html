Aralia bicrenata

Aralia bicrenata, commonly known as the red-veined aralia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that often display vibrant red veins against a deep green background. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Aralia bicrenata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
