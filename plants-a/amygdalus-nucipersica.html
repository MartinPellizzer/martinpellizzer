Amygdalus nucipersica

Amygdalus nucipersica, commonly known as the Persian almond, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Prunus genus. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by almond-like fruits that are harvested when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, typical of many rose family plants.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has historical significance in traditional medicine and horticulture.
