Aquilegia vulgaris var. riffienensis

Aquilegia vulgaris var. riffienensis, commonly known as the Rif Mountains columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Rif Mountains in northern Africa.

It features striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, typical of the genus Aquilegia. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to alpine gardens and botanical collections.
