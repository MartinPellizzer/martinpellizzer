Astragalus gilvanensis

Astragalus gilvanensis, commonly known as the Gilvan's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus gilvanensis is often found in steppe and desert scrub habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these environments.
