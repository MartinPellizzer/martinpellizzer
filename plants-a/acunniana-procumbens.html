Acunniana procumbens

Acunniana procumbens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes to form dense mats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a soft, hairy texture.

Acunniana procumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
