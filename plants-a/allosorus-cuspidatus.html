Allosorus cuspidatus

Allosorus cuspidatus, commonly known as the sharp-tailed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, hairy stem and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in late spring. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its flowers are usually pink or violet, with a distinctive spur at the base of the lip.

Allosorus cuspidatus thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.
