Adiantum obtusum var. majus

Adiantum obtusum var. majus, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense colonies over time.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
