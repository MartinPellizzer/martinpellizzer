Astragalus sinicus f. albiflorus

Astragalus sinicus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It is native to regions of East Asia, including parts of China, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
