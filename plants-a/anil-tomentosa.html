Anil tomentosa

Anil tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Anil tomentosa is a climbing plant that can reach heights of several meters, using its tendrils to support its growth.
