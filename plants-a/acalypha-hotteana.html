Acalypha hotteana

Acalypha hotteana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or hotte's spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a velvety texture and a variety of colors ranging from green to reddish hues.

Acalypha hotteana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
