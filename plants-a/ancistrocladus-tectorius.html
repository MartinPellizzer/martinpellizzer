Ancistrocladus tectorius

Ancistrocladus tectorius, commonly known as the "roof plant," is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
