Astragalus palaestinus subsp. heteranthesnus

Astragalus palaestinus subsp.

heteranthesnus, commonly known as the Palestine milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, with small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its pods contain small, lens-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
