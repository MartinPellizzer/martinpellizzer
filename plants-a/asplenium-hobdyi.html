Asplenium hobdyi

Asplenium hobdyi, commonly known as the Hobdy's spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces spores in clusters called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or damp soil.

Asplenium hobdyi is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
