Acacia holosericea var. glabrata

Acacia holosericea var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are glabrous, or hairless, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
