Amoria polymorpa

Amoria polymorpa, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its diverse floral morphology, which varies significantly between different populations. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a range of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

Amoria polymorpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
