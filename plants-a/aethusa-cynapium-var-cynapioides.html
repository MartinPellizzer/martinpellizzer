Aethusa cynapium var. cynapioides

Aethusa cynapium var. cynapioides, commonly known as cow parsley or wild carrot, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or greenish flowers. Its roots contain compounds similar to those of the carrot, though it is not closely related to the cultivated carrot species.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
