Astragalus iodanthus var. diaphanoides

Astragalus iodanthus var. diaphanoides, commonly known as the translucent milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are compound, with finely divided leaflets that give the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access groundwater.
