Acacia sabulosa

Acacia sabulosa, commonly known as the sand acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the legume family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.

Acacia sabulosa plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
