Ampacus macrophylla

Ampacus macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rainforests. Its fronds are arranged in a graceful, arching pattern, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant's botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and sporangia that produce spores for reproduction.
