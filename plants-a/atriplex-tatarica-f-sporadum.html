Atriplex tatarica f. sporadum

Atriplex tatarica f. sporadum, commonly known as the Tatarian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with small, alternate leaves that often have a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of trichomes. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is native to Central Asia and is often found in coastal and inland saline soils.
