Aristida ramosissima var. chaseana

Aristida ramosissima var. chaseana, commonly known as Chase's wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, open growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in desert or semi-desert environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

This species is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping and wildlife habitat restoration.
