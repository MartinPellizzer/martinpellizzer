Aegilops kotschyi

Aegilops kotschyi, commonly known as the mountain wild barley, is a species of grass native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while its seeds are small and enclosed in protective bracts.

This plant is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
