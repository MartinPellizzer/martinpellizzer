Allium ampeloprasum subsp. commutatum

Allium ampeloprasum subsp.

commutatum, commonly known as the rock leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces tall, flowering stems with purple or pink flowers.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable culinary and medicinal plant.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
