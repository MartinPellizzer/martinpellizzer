Andropogon unilateralis

Andropogon unilateralis, commonly known as the eastern gamagrass, is a tall grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often green or reddish in color. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon unilateralis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
