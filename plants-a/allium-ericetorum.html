Allium ericetorum

Allium ericetorum, commonly known as the heather onion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted foliage and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valued plant in certain culinary traditions.

It is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
