Arum korolkowii

Arum korolkowii, commonly known as the Korolkow's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a large, heart-shaped leaf that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy, colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The spadix bears small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by specific insects.
