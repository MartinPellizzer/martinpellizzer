Aeschynomene carvalhoi

Aeschynomene carvalhoi, commonly known as Brazilian signalgrass, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and pastures, and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Aeschynomene carvalhoi is also noted for its potential use in ecological restoration due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
