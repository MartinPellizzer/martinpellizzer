Achyranthes cordata

Achyranthes cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
