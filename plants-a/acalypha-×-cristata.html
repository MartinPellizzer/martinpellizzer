Acalypha × cristata

Acalypha × cristata, commonly known as the crested spurge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, flattened, and often branched stems that resemble a crown or crest, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Acalypha × cristata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
