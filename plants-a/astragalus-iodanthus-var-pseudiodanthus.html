Astragalus iodanthus var. pseudiodanthus

Astragalus iodanthus var. pseudiodanthus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a deep taproot system, which helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
