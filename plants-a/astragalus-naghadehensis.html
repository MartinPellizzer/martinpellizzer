Astragalus naghadehensis

Astragalus naghadehensis, commonly known as Naghadeh's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
