Ainsliaea fulvioides

Ainsliaea fulvioides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ainsliaea fulvioides is often found in woodland edges and moist meadows, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
