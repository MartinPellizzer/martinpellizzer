Astracantha floccosa

Astracantha floccosa, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
