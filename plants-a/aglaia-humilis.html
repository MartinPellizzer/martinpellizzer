Aglaia humilis

Aglaia humilis, commonly known as the small aglaia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing several winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

Aglaia humilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
