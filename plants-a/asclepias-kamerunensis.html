Asclepias kamerunensis

Asclepias kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon milkweed, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which is typical of the genus Asclepias. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
