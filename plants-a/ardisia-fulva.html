Ardisia fulva

Ardisia fulva, commonly known as the red-flowered ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a reddish tint on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Ardisia fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
