Ardisia castaneifolia

Ardisia castaneifolia, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls at the nodes. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for humans and animals. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy fruits, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions due to its rapid growth and seed dispersal capabilities.
