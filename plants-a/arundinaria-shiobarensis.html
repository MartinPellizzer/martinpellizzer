Arundinaria shiobarensis

Arundinaria shiobarensis, commonly known as the Shiobara bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, typical of many bamboo species. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria shiobarensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
