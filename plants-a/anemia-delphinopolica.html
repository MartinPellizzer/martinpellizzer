Anemia delphinopolica

Anemia delphinopolica, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
