Amaranthus circinnatus

Amaranthus circinnatus, commonly known as the curled amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped bracts that form a compact, colorful inflorescence. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are edible and can be used in cooking, while the seeds are rich in nutrients.

Amaranthus circinnatus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
