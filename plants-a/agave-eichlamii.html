Agave eichlamii

Agave eichlamii, commonly known as the Eichler's agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are lined with sharp spines along their edges, providing a defense mechanism against herbivores. It produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

This plant is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
