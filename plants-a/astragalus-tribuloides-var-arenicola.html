Astragalus tribuloides var. arenicola

Astragalus tribuloides var. arenicola, commonly known as sand burr milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, spiny pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow to pale green flowers.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
