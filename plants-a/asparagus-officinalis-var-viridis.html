Asparagus officinalis var. viridis

Asparagus officinalis var. viridis, commonly known as green asparagus, is a cultivar of the asparagus plant characterized by its tender, edible shoots.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is often grown for its young, spear-like stems. The plant has fern-like, compound leaves that are typically green and contribute to its ornamental value. It requires well-drained soil and full sun to produce a good harvest.

The botanical features of this variety make it both a culinary and horticultural favorite.
