Anthemis punctata subsp. kabylica

Anthemis punctata subsp.

kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylican ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
