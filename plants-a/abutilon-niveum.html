Abutilon niveum

Abutilon niveum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a soft, tactile appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique foliage and floral display.
