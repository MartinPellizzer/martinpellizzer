Astragalus coronaminis

Astragalus coronaminis, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
