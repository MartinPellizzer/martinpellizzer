Alphitonia ponderosa var. kauila

Alphitonia ponderosa var. kauila, commonly known as the Kauila tree, is a variant of the native Hawaiian tree species.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark grey bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruit capsules. It thrives in coastal and lowland rainforest habitats, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role and historical significance in Hawaiian culture.
