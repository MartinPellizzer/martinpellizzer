Achillea odorata var. masclansii

Achillea odorata var. masclansii, commonly known as the Masclans yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads composed of tiny white to pale yellow florets, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a garden ornamental highlights its cultural and horticultural significance.
