Angraecum flavidum

Angraecum flavidum, commonly known as the yellow angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces striking yellow flowers with long, slender petals. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, showcasing its adaptive strategies to the arid environment. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Angraecum flavidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
