Abelmoschus manihot subsp. tetraphyllus

Abelmoschus manihot subsp.

tetraphyllus, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and alternate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fleshy, egg-shaped fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in regions with limited water resources.

The subspecies is closely related to the okra species but differs in its growth habits and fruit structure.
