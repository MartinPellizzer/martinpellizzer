Atalaya hemiglauca

Atalaya hemiglauca, commonly known as the silverleaf atalaya, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that exhibit a striking silver-green hue on the upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Atalaya hemiglauca thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
