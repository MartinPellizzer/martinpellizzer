Acacia sing

Acacia sing, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The wood of Acacia sing is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making.
