Angiopteris microsporangia

Angiopteris microsporangia, commonly known as the small-spore water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Angiopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its fronds that are typically divided into segments with a distinctive, finely serrated margin. The plant produces microsporangia, which are the male reproductive structures that release spores, contributing to its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive features make it an important subject in the study of fern biology and evolution.
