Antidesma ovalifolium

Antidesma ovalifolium, commonly known as the African starfruit or sour sop, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian Ocean.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by yellow or orange berries, which are edible but have a sour taste. Its fruits are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
