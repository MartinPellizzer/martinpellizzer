Angraecum eburneum

Angraecum eburneum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Madagascar.

It features long, slender stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive star-shaped appearance. The plant is adapted to grow on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, and it produces fragrant blooms that attract nocturnal pollinators like moths. Angraecum eburneum is known for its slow growth and requires specific care to thrive in cultivation.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
