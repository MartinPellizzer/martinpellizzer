Artemisia pedemontana subsp. pedemontana

Artemisia pedemontana subsp.

pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high altitudes. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its strong, camphor-like scent.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in alpine gardens.
