Acer palmifolium f. villosum

Acer palmifolium f. villosum, commonly known as the silky maple, is a variant of the maple tree characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves.

The leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "villosum" designation, which means "hairy" in Latin. It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that aid in its dispersal.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
