Annona vepretorum

Annona vepretorum, commonly known as the custard apple or sugar apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers with a strong fragrance, followed by a fuzzy, green fruit that turns yellow or red when ripe. The fruit has a creamy, custard-like pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a distinctive aroma.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
