Acacia ramulosa

Acacia ramulosa, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia ramulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
