Astragalus bardsiricus

Astragalus bardsiricus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

However, it is considered endangered due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
