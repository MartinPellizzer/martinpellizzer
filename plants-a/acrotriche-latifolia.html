Acrotriche latifolia

Acrotriche latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf acrotriche, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lanceolate, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
