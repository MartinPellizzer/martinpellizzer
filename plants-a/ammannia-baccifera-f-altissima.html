Ammannia baccifera f. altissima

Ammannia baccifera f. altissima, commonly known as the tall bladderwort, is a variant of the bladderwort plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces tiny, white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and ponds.

Its unique morphology and aquatic habitat make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
