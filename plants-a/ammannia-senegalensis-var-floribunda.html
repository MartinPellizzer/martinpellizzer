Ammannia senegalensis var. floribunda

Ammannia senegalensis var. floribunda, commonly known as redroot dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, branched stems that often turn reddish in color, especially under stress. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
