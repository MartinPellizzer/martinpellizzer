Anemone amurensis subsp. kamtschatica

Anemone amurensis subsp.

kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of eastern Siberia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, arranged in a single bloom atop a hairy stem. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a soft, fuzzy texture, and are usually basal in arrangement.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
