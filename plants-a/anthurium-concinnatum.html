Anthurium concinnatum

Anthurium concinnatum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch and often display a deep green to dark red coloration. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Anthurium concinnatum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
