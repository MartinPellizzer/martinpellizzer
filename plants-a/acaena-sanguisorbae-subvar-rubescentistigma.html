Acaena sanguisorbae subvar. rubescentistigma

Acaena sanguisorbae subvar. rubescentistigma, commonly known as the red-stigma bramble, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, spiny leaves and produces bright red, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's fruits are small, red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
