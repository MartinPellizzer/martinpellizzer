Aldama canescens

Aldama canescens, commonly known as the Mexican sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a grayish appearance.

Aldama canescens is often found in open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
