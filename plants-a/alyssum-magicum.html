Alyssum magicum

Alyssum magicum, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
