Andinia sunchubambensis

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in the cool, humid microclimates of its native habitat. Its unique morphology includes slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
