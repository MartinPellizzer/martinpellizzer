Asperula creticola

Asperula creticola, commonly known as the Cretan woodruff, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asperula creticola is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
