Aquilegia aquilegioides

Aquilegia aquilegioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar-rich spurs. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental gardening and ecological habitats.
