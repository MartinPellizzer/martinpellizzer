Anthemis amblyolepis

Anthemis amblyolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
