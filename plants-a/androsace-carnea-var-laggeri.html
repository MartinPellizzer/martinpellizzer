Androsace carnea var. laggeri

Androsace carnea var. laggeri, commonly known as the pink rock rose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically evergreen. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine displays.
