Acacia silvicola

Acacia silvicola, commonly known as the forest acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale green flowers arranged in spherical or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Acacia silvicola is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments, making it an important component of degraded or secondary forests.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules contributes to soil fertility in nutrient-poor ecosystems.
