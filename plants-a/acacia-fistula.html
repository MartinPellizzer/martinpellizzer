Acacia fistula

Acacia fistula, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Acacia fistula is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in arid environments.
