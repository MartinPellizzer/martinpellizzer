Artanthe mollicoma f. latifolia

Artanthe mollicoma f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf artanthe, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pinkish-red bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
