Adonis aestivalis subsp. squarrosa

Adonis aestivalis subsp.

squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
