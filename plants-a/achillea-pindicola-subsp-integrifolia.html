Achillea pindicola subsp. integrifolia

Achillea pindicola subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the Balkan yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It features gray-green, aromatic leaves that are typically lanceolate and have a serrated margin.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
