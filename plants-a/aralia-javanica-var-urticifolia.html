Aralia javanica var. urticifolia

Aralia javanica var. urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging aralia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, irritating hairs that can cause skin irritation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, which are typically followed by dark berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its defensive properties, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
