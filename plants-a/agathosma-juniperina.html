Agathosma juniperina

Agathosma juniperina, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation. Its essential oils, which contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

Agathosma juniperina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
