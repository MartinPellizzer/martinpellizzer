Alchemilla bombycina

Alchemilla bombycina, commonly known as velvet cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Alchemilla bombycina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
