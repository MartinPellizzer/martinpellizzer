Acrocentron Salonitanum

Salonitan sunflower, scientifically known as Acrocentron salonitanum, is a rare and striking plant native to the sun-drenched regions of southern Europe. This unique species is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and towering stalks that can reach up to three meters in height. Despite its beauty, it remains largely unknown to the general public due to its limited distribution and delicate nature. Conservation efforts are underway to protect this endangered species from habitat loss and climate change. Its presence in the wild is a testament to nature's ability to create breathtaking botanical wonders.
