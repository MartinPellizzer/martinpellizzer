Anisomeris pedunculosa

Anisomeris pedunculosa, commonly known as the desert star or desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with soft, hairy stems and lobed leaves that help reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Anisomeris pedunculosa is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system.
