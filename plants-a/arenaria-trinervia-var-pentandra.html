Arenaria trinervia var. pentandra

Arenaria trinervia var. pentandra, commonly known as the five-veined sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats of small, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tundra and mountain ecosystems.
