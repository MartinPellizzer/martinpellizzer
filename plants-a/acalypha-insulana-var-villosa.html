Acalypha insulana var. villosa

Acalypha insulana var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
