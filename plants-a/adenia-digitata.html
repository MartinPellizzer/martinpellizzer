Adenia digitata

Adenia digitata, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody, tree-like structure with thick, smooth bark. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, fleshy calyx that resembles a hand, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a large, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Adenia digitata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
