Aster gaspensis

Aster gaspensis, commonly known as the Gaspé aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils along the shores of the Gaspé Peninsula. It produces showy purple to pink flowers in the late summer and early fall.

Aster gaspensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
