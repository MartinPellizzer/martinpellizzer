Astragalus whitneyi subsp. hookerianus

Astragalus whitneyi subsp.

hookerianus, commonly known as Hooker's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds.
