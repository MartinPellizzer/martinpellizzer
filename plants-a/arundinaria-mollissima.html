Arundinaria mollissima

Arundinaria mollissima, commonly known as the silky bamboo or black bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a smooth, dark green texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, silky texture that gives it its common name.

Arundinaria mollissima is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and graceful appearance.
