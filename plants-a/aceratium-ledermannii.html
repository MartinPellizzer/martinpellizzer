Aceratium ledermannii

Aceratium ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's acanthus, is a rare and striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with a distinctive lobed structure, which give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces tall spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a desirable addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
