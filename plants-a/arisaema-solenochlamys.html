Arisaema solenochlamys

Arisaema solenochlamys, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-bearing inflorescence. The plant produces a unique, elongated, often yellow or greenish spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The botanical features of Arisaema solenochlamys make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
