Athyrium amplum

Athyrium amplum, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The fronds are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, with a distinct, glossy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant reproduces via spores and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
