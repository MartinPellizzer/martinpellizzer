Archangelica officinalis var. himalaica

Archangelica officinalis var. himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, deeply lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
