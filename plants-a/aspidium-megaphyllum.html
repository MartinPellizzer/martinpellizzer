Aspidium megaphyllum

Aspidium megaphyllum, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Aspidium megaphyllum plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.
