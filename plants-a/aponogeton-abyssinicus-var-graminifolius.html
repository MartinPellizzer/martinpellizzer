Aponogeton abyssinicus var. graminifolius

Aponogeton abyssinicus var. graminifolius, commonly known as the Abyssinian waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Africa.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface, making it visually distinct. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate light and nutrient levels.

This variety is often cultivated in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
