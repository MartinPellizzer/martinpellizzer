Alsine americana

Alsine americana, commonly known as American sandwort, is a low-growing annual plant native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters and has small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features alternate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Alsine americana is adapted to dry conditions and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
