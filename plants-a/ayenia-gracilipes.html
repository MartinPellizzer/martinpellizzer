Ayenia gracilipes

Ayenia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

Ayenia gracilipes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
