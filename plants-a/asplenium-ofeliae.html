Asplenium ofeliae

Asplenium ofeliae, commonly known as the Ofelia's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are often divided into small, toothed segments. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rock crevices or forest floors, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its rhizome is short and produces a cluster of leaves, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

Asplenium ofeliae is a relatively rare species, often found in specific geographic regions with particular microclimates.
