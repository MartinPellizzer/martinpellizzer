Andromeda pensylvanica

Andromeda pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania rosebay, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin, and it produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions in native habitats.
