Agrostis alba subsp. pallens

Agrostis alba subsp.

pallens, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue blades and tufted inflorescences, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
