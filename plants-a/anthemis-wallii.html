Anthemis wallii

Anthemis wallii, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
