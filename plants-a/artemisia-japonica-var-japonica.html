Artemisia japonica var. japonica

Artemisia japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese Artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with finely divided, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have also been historically recognized in traditional herbal practices.
