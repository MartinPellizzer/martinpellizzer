Agave gypsophila

Agave gypsophila, commonly known as the ghost agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, upright rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing small, white, star-shaped flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are adapted to store water, making it highly drought-resistant.

Agave gypsophila is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
