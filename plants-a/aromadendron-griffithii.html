Aromadendron griffithii

Aromadendron griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir saffron or the Kashmiri spice, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan, India, and Afghanistan.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that give way to dark red berries. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include opposite, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, reflecting its ecological and cultural significance in the region.
