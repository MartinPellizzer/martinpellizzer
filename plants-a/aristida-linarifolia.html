Aristida linarifolia

Aristida linarifolia, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
