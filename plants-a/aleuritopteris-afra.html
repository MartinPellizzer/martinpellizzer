Aleuritopteris afra

Aleuritopteris afra, commonly known as the African water fern, is a species of fern native to Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in damp forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Aleuritopteris afra plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
