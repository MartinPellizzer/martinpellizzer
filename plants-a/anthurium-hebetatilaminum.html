Anthurium hebetatilaminum

Anthurium hebetatilaminum, commonly known as the pale-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are brightly colored, spadix-like inflorescences that can range from red to orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium hebetatilaminum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
