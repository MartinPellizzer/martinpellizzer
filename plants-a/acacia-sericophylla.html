Acacia sericophylla

Acacia sericophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sandy environments.

Acacia sericophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
