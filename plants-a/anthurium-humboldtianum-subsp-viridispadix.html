Anthurium humboldtianum subsp. viridispadix

Anthurium humboldtianum subsp.

viridispadix, commonly known as the Emerald Arrow or Green-tendril Anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America. It is characterized by its elongated, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture.

The plant produces striking spadices, which are the modified leaves that house the small, clustered flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
