Aspalathus flavispina

Aspalathus flavispina, commonly known as the yellow-spined bushveld pea, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by cylindrical, reddish-brown pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
