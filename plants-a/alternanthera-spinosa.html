Alternanthera spinosa

Alternanthera spinosa, commonly known as the spiny alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
