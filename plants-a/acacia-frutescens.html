Acacia frutescens

Acacia frutescens, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acacia frutescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
