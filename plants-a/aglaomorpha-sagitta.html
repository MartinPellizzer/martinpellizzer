Aglaomorpha sagitta

Aglaomorpha sagitta, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries.

Aglaomorpha sagitta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
