Anemone subindivisa

Anemone subindivisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are elongated and narrow, with a hairy texture, and produces showy white to pale pink flowers in late spring. The plant has a short rhizomatous root system and thrives in moist, woodland habitats. Its flowers typically have five to seven petals and are surrounded by a cluster of sepals.

Anemone subindivisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
