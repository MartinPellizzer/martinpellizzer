Aerva pubescens

Aerva pubescens, commonly known as the hairy aerva, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Aerva pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
