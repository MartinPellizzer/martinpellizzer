Asparagus racemosus var. javanicus

Asparagus racemosus var. javanicus, commonly known as Java asparagus, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red or black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Its botanical features make it suitable for cultivation in subtropical and tropical gardens.
