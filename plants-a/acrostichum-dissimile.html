Acrostichum dissimile

Acrostichum dissimile, commonly known as the giant reed or giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Its fibrous stalks are sometimes harvested for use in basketry and papermaking.
