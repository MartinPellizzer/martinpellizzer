Acacia jibberdingensis

Acacia jibberdingensis, commonly known as the Jibberding wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces yellow flowers in late summer, attracting native pollinators.

Acacia jibberdingensis is adapted to low rainfall and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.
