Adiantum aleuticum

Adiantum aleuticum, commonly known as the Aleut maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that give it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and creeping, allowing it to spread slowly through vegetative reproduction.

Adiantum aleuticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
