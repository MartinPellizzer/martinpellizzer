Ardisia carchiana

Ardisia carchiana, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, red berries that resemble Christmas ornaments, hence its common name. The plant is part of the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.

The berries are edible but can be toxic in large quantities.
