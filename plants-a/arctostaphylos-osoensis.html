Arctostaphylos osoensis

Arctostaphylos osoensis, commonly known as the Oso Creek manzanita, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

The Oso Creek manzanita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
