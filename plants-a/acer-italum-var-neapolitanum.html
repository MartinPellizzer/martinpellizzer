Acer italum var. neapolitanum

Acer italum var. neapolitanum, commonly known as the Neapolitan maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
