Andropogon perrieri

Andropogon perrieri, commonly known as Perrier's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Andropogon perrieri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
