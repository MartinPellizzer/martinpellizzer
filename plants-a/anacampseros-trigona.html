Anacampseros trigona

Anacampseros trigona, commonly known as the three-lobed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are triangular in shape and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its compact growth and drought tolerance, it is well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.
