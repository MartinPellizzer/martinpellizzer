Acrocomia vinifera

Acrocomia vinifera, commonly known as the wine palm, is a flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fruit is a drupe, typically yellow or orange in color, and contains a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local communities.
