Acacia xiphophylla

Acacia xiphophylla, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and bipinnate leaves that are small and feathery. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Acacia xiphophylla is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and coastal winds.
