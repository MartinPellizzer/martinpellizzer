Astragalus karatjubeki

Astragalus karatjubeki, commonly known as Karatjubek's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, contributing to its survival in dry climates.

Astragalus karatjubeki is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
