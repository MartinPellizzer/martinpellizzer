Alchemilla jaegeri

Alchemilla jaegeri, commonly known as the Jaeger's lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Alchemilla jaegeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
