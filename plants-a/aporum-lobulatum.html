Aporum lobulatum

Aporum lobulatum, commonly known as the laceleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive lobed structure, contributing to its ornamental value.

Aporum lobulatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.
