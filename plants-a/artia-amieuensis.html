Artia amieuensis

Artia amieuensis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for shady garden areas. Its ornamental value lies in its unique foliage and subtle floral display.

Artia amieuensis is often used in landscaping for its ability to thrive in humid environments and its low maintenance requirements.
