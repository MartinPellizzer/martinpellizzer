Anthemis umbilicata

Anthemis umbilicata, commonly known as the umbilical chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

The essential oils found in its flowers are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
