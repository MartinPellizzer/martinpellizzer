Artemisia campestris subsp. lempergii

Artemisia campestris subsp.

lempergii, commonly known as the lesser tarragon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, linear leaves with a distinct aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in horticulture.
