Artemisia desertorum

Artemisia desertorum, commonly known as wormwood, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains essential oils and compounds such as thujone, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert and mountainous environments.

Artemisia desertorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
