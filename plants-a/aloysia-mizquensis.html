Aloysia mizquensis

Aloysia mizquensis, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in regions with mild climates. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aloysia mizquensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
