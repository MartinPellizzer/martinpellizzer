Aristolochia platyloba

Aristolochia platyloba, commonly known as the broadleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with prominent veins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
