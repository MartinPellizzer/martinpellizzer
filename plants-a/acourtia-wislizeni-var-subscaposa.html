Acourtia wislizeni var. subscaposa

Acourtia wislizeni var. subscaposa, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with a rosette of basal leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spiky fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.
