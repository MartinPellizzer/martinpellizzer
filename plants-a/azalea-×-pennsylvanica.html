Azalea × pennsylvanica

Azalea × pennsylvanica, commonly known as the Southern Indica azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. It is part of the Rhododendron genus and is closely related to other azalea species.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
