Aerva lanata var. rhombea

Aerva lanata var. rhombea, commonly known as the rhomboid arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
