Abutilon parviflorum var. luteum

Abutilon parviflorum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its growth habit is bushy, with a tendency to spread through both seeds and runners.

It is often found in wetlands and along the edges of swamps, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
