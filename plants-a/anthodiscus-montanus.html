Anthodiscus montanus

Anthodiscus montanus, commonly known as the mountain anthodiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh climatic conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Anthodiscus montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
