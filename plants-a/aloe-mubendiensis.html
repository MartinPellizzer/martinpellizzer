Aloe mubendiensis

Aloe mubendiensis, commonly known as the Mubende aloe, is a succulent plant native to East Africa, specifically found in the Mubende region of Uganda.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a waxy cuticle to store water. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional medicine and skincare products.

Aloe mubendiensis is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.
