Ardisia woodsonii

Ardisia woodsonii, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright purple berries, which are a striking feature of the species. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ardisia woodsonii thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments.
