Alopecurus pratensis var. acuminatus

Alopecurus pratensis var. acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
