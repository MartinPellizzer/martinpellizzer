Aristida pennata var. scabriglumis

Aristida pennata var. scabriglumis, commonly known as the scaly hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, scaly structures. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
