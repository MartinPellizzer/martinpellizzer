Astragalus kawakamii

Astragalus kawakamii, commonly known as Kawakami's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is often found in forested areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

Its seed pods contain small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.
