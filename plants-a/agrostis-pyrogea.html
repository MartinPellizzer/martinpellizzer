Agrostis pyrogea

Agrostis pyrogea, commonly known as the firegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with reddish hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Agrostis pyrogea is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is often found in areas with moderate to high moisture levels.
