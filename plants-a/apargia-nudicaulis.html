Apargia nudicaulis

Apargia nudicaulis, commonly known as the small-flowered twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ecologically significant component of its native habitat.
