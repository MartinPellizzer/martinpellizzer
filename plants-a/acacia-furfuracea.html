Acacia furfuracea

Acacia furfuracea, commonly known as the white thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its greyish, fissured bark and slender, thorned branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets.

Acacia furfuracea is adapted to drought conditions and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
