Areca salomonensis

Areca salomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands areca, is a species of palm native to the Solomon Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which eventually develop into dark blue to black fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatch material in the region.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
