Anemone caerulea var. linearis

Anemone caerulea var. linearis, commonly known as the blue anemone, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, blue to violet flowers with a central yellow stamen, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
