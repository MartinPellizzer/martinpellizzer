Angelica × mixta

Angelica × mixta, commonly known as the garden angelica, is a hybrid perennial plant that combines traits from Angelica archangelica and Angelica sylvestris.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, compound leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by oval, dark brown seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Angelica × mixta is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and historical use in herbal medicine.
