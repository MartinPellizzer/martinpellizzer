Anagallis tenella f. pygmaea

Anagallis tenella f. pygmaea, commonly known as the miniature pink willow, is a compact, mat-forming perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and moist garden beds.
