Artemisia septemlobata

Artemisia septemlobata, commonly known as seven-lobed sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with seven distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Artemisia septemlobata plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
