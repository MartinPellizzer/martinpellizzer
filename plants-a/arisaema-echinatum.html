Arisaema echinatum

Arisaema echinatum, commonly known as the spiny arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including China, India, and Indonesia. The plant features a distinctive, spiny rhizome and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Arisaema echinatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
