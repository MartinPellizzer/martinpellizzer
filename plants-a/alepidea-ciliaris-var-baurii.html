Alepidea ciliaris var. baurii

Alepidea ciliaris var. baurii, commonly known as the Baur's bottlebrush, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, typically blooming in shades of red or yellow. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
