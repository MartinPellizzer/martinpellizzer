Ardisia brassiella

Ardisia brassiella, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ardisia brassiella is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
