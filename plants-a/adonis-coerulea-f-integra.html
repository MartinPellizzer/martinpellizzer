Adonis coerulea f. integra

Adonis coerulea f. integra, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and hairy, with a rosette growth habit. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its beauty and resilience.
