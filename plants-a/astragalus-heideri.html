Astragalus heideri

Astragalus heideri, commonly known as the Heider's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus heideri is often found in alpine and steppe habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
