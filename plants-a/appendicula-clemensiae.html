Appendicula clemensiae

Appendicula clemensiae, commonly known as the dwarf marsh orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment.

Appendicula clemensiae is a rare and endangered species, requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
