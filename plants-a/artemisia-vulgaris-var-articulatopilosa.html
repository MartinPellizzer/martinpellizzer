Artemisia vulgaris var. articulatopilosa

Artemisia vulgaris var. articulatopilosa, commonly known as the rough tarragon, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, branched hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and disturbed areas.

Its aromatic properties make it useful in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
