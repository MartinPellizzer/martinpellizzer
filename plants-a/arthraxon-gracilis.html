Arthraxon gracilis

Arthraxon gracilis, commonly known as slender arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments in eastern North America.

It features slender, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats. Arthraxon gracilis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in saturated soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological balance in marsh ecosystems.
