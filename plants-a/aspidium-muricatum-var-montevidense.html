Aspidium muricatum var. montevidense

Aspidium muricatum var. montevidense, commonly known as the Montevidense water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with a creeping rhizome that helps anchor it in wet soils or water. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the Polypodiaceae family.

This variety is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of freshwater bodies, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
