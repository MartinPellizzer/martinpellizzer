Artemisia brachanthemoides

Artemisia brachanthemoides, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
