Alyxia cinerea

Alyxia cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of hair, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and appear in clusters along the branches.

Alyxia cinerea is adapted to survive in drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
