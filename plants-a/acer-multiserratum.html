Acer multiserratum

Acer multiserratum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely toothed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
