Acacia sibina

Acacia sibina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the genus Acacia. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought.

Acacia sibina plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various native wildlife species.
