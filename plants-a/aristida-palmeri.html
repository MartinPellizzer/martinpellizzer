Aristida palmeri

Aristida palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's threeawn, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a grass species that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, spiky inflorescences composed of multiple spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Aristida palmeri is also noted for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
