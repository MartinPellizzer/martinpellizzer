Achillea distans subsp. alpina

Achillea distans subsp.

alpina, commonly known as alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia. It features finely divided, silver-green leaves and produces flat, composite flower heads in shades of white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in rock gardens and alpine displays due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
