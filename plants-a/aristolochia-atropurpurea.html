Aristolochia atropurpurea

Aristolochia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple pipevine, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and green veins on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, pipe-like flowers that are typically greenish-purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is also notable for its toxic properties, containing aristolochic acids that are harmful to both animals and humans.
