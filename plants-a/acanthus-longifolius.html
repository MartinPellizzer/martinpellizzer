Acanthus longifolius

Acanthus longifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, spiny seed heads that are typically pink to purple in color, adding visual interest in late summer and autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its architectural form and seasonal display make it a valuable addition to both traditional and modern landscapes.
