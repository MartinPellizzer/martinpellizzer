Anthericum depauperatum

Anthericum depauperatum, commonly known as the poor man's lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a common feature in the Araceae family.

Anthericum depauperatum is often found in rocky or calcareous soils and is known for its modest appearance and ecological significance.
