Allium margaritaceum var. typicum

Allium margaritaceum var. typicum, commonly known as the pearl onion leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often used in culinary applications, particularly in Asian cuisines. The plant produces small, white, spherical bulbs that resemble pearls, giving it its common name. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are typically harvested for consumption.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
