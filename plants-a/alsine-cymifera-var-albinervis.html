Alsine cymifera var. albinervis

Alsine cymifera var. albinervis, commonly known as the white-veined sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically gray-green with prominent white veins, which give the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

This plant is drought-tolerant and can survive in harsh environmental conditions.
