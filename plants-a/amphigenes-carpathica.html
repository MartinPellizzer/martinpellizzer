Amphigenes carpathica

Amphigenes carpathica, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Amphigenes carpathica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
