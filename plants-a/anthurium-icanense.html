Anthurium icanense

Anthurium icanense, commonly known as the Icanense flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped spathe that resembles a flamingo's beak. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers inside the spathe, which are surrounded by a modified leaf called a spadix. Anthurium icanense thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant collections.
