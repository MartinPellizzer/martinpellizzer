Aster thomsonii

Aster thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Thomson's aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
