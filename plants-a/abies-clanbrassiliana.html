Abies clanbrassiliana

Abies clanbrassiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian fir, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the fir genus. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The botanical features of Abies clanbrassiliana make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
