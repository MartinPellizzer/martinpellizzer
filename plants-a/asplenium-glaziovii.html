Asplenium glaziovii

Asplenium glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are usually dark green and covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rock surfaces or in forest undergrowth.

Asplenium glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
