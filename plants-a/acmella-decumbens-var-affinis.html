Acmella decumbens var. affinis

Acmella decumbens var. affinis, commonly known as the yellow marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and riverbanks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem and a root system adapted to waterlogged soils.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
