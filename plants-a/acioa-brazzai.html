Acioa brazzai

Acioa brazzai, commonly known as the Brazilian acacia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and is characterized by its pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small leaflets, and it produces yellow or white flowers.

Acioa brazzai is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
