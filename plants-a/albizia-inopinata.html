Albizia inopinata

Albizia inopinata, commonly known as the pink-leaf senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnate and have a distinctive pinkish hue when young. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the dry season. Albizia inopinata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
