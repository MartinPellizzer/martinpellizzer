Almana

Almana, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions, and it often thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it produces bulbous structures that store water and nutrients.

Almana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental beauty in desert gardens.
