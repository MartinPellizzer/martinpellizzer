Aponogeton nateshii

Aponogeton nateshii, commonly known as the Natesh's arrowhead, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in aquariums and wetland restoration projects.
