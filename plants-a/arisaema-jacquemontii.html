Arisaema jacquemontii

Arisaema jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jackman's arisaema, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem that supports a single, large, mottled leaf. The plant produces an unusual spadix surrounded by a showy, petal-like bract, which is characteristic of the Araceae family. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
