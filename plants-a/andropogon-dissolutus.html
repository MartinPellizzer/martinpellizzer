Andropogon dissolutus

Andropogon dissolutus, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that are often rolled in the bud, and its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle of spikelets. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon dissolutus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to waterlogged environments.
