Ayenia melantha

Ayenia melantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It has a bushy growth habit and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Ayenia melantha is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
