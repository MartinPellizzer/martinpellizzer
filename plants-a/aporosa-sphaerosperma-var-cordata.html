Aporosa sphaerosperma var. cordata

Aporosa sphaerosperma var. cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped aporosa, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
