Androsaemum

Androsaemum, commonly known as sweet flag, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant is native to wetland areas in Asia, Africa, and the Americas, and it thrives in moist, swampy environments. Sweet flag is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rhizomes and leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance.
