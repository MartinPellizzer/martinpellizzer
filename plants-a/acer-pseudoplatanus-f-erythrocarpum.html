Acer pseudoplatanus f. erythrocarpum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive red fruits, which are clustered and often used in ornamental horticulture. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a coarsely toothed margin. This form is a natural variant that occurs in the wild, often in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its unique fruit color and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
