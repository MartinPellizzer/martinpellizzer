Acacia viridiramis

Acacia viridiramis, commonly known as the green-leaf acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and bipinnate leaves that are often a vibrant green. Its yellow globular flowerheads appear in clusters, making it visually striking.

Acacia viridiramis is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of fodder for livestock.
