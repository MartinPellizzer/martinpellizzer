Acer campestre f. longipetiolatum

Acer campestre f. longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled field maple, is a variant of the field maple, characterized by its elongated petioles.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe, often found in woodland edges and open areas. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
