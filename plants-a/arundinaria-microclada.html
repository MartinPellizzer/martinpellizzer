Arundinaria microclada

Arundinaria microclada, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, clumping growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its culms, or stems, are hollow and jointed, typical of the grass family.

Arundinaria microclada is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
