Aloysia triphylla

Aloysia triphylla, commonly known as Cape vermouth, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

Aloysia triphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
