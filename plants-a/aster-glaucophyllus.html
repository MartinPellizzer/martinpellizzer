Aster glaucophyllus

Aster glaucophyllus, commonly known as the blueleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to violet petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a bluish-gray hue due to the thick coating of trichomes.

Aster glaucophyllus is well adapted to moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.
