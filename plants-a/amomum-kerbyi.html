Amomum kerbyi

Amomum kerbyi, commonly known as Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
