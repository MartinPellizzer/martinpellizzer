Astragalus yualicus var. pseudoptilodes

Astragalus yualicus var. pseudoptilodes, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
