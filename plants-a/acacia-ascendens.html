Acacia ascendens

Acacia ascendens, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia ascendens is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to provide shade and habitat for various wildlife species.
