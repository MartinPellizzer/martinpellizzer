Alyssum smyrnaeum

Alyssum smyrnaeum, commonly known as Persian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Alyssum smyrnaeum is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
