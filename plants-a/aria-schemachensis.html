Aria schemachensis

Aria schemachensis, commonly known as the Schemach's arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas near water bodies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
