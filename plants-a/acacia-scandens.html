Acacia scandens

Acacia scandens, commonly known as the climbing acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that can climb or trail along the ground, often forming dense thickets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which are highly attractive to bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia scandens is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
