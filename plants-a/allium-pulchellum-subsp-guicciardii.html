Allium pulchellum subsp. guicciardii

Allium pulchellum subsp.

guicciardii, commonly known as the Guicciardii garlic, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which is used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and ornamental appeal in gardens.
