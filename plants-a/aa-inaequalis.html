Aa inaequalis

Aa inaequalis, commonly known as the Hawaiian stiltgrass, is a perennial herb native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streambanks. Its leaves are often alternate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Aa inaequalis plays a minor role in the local ecosystem but is considered an invasive species in some areas outside Hawaii.
