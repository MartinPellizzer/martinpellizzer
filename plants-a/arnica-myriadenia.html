Arnica myriadenia

Arnica myriadenia, commonly known as the mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a robust rhizome and produces tall, sparsely branched stems covered with yellow-orange flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the top of the stem and bloom in late summer.

Arnica myriadenia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
