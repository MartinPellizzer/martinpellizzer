Acer californicum f. rubescens

Acer californicum f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-leaf maple, is a variant of the California maple.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the fall. This ornamental tree is native to the western United States, particularly California. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
