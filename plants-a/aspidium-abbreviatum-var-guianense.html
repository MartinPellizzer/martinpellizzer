Aspidium abbreviatum var. guianense

Aspidium abbreviatum var. guianense, commonly known as the Guianan shield fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a complex arrangement of pinnae.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and the plant plays a role in maintaining soil stability in its natural habitat.
