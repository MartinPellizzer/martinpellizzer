Agrostis distans var. coreensis

Agrostis distans var. coreensis, commonly known as Corean bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and spike-like, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
