Aconitum sinoaxillare

Aconitum sinoaxillare, commonly known as the Chinese aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, conical clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat fuzzy texture.

Aconitum sinoaxillare is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling and cultivation.
