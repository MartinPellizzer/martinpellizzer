Agropyron afghanicum

Agropyron afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought.

Its botanical features make it valuable for ecological restoration and as a forage plant.
