Aurantium × orientale

Aurantium × orientale, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from the cross between *Citrus sinensis* and *Citrus aurantium*.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic peel and juicy, segmented fruit that is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers.

Its fruit develops from a single flower and ripens to a vibrant orange color, often containing a high concentration of vitamin C. The botanical features of *Aurantium × orientale* make it an important species in horticulture and agriculture.
