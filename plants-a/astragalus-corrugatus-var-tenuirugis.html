Astragalus corrugatus var. tenuirugis

Astragalus corrugatus var. tenuirugis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
