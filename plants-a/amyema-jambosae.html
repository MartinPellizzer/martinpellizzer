Amyema jambosae

Amyema jambosae, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Amyema jambosae is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.
