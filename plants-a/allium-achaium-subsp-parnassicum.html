Allium achaium subsp. parnassicum

Allium achaium subsp.

parnassicum, commonly known as the Parnassian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are similar in structure to those of other allium species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
