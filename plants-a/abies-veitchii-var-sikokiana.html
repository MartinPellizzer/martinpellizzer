Abies veitchii var. sikokiana

Abies veitchii var. sikokiana, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a variant of the Veitch fir native to Japan.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The foliage is typically arranged in whorls, giving it a dense and compact appearance. It produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
