Anthyllis vulneraria proles dillenii

Anthyllis vulneraria proles dillenii, commonly known as the Dillenius' Scotch thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny, glandular-stemmed structure and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in spherical or cylindrical heads, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky, or calcareous soils.

It is notable for its medicinal properties, historically used for its vulnerary and anti-inflammatory effects.
