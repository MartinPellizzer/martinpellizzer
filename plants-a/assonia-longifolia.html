Assonia longifolia

Assonia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved assonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Assonia longifolia is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
