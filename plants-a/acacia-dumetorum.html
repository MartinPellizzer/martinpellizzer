Acacia dumetorum

Acacia dumetorum, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, paired leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia dumetorum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
