Arabis ovirensis

Arabis ovirensis, commonly known as the Alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants. This species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing in crevices and on slopes.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is typically found at high altitudes where it faces harsh environmental conditions.
