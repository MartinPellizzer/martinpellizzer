Aria madoniensis

Aria madoniensis, commonly known as the Madoniensis Aria, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Italy.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought and nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing small, yellowish petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
