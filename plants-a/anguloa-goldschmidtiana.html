Anguloa goldschmidtiana

Anguloa goldschmidtiana, commonly known as the Goldschmidt's anguloa, is a striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant produces pendant-like blooms that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native high-altitude, arid environment.

Anguloa goldschmidtiana is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.
