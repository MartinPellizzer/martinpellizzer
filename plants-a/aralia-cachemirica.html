Aralia cachemirica

Aralia cachemirica, commonly known as the Kashmir aralia, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It features compound leaves with a feathery appearance, often reaching lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, particularly in temperate climates.
