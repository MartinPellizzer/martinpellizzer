Asperula cynanchica subsp. aristata

Asperula cynanchica subsp.

aristata, commonly known as the mountain azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The flowers are typically pink to reddish in color and are borne on slender pedicels. This subspecies is adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
