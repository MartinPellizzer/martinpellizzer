Acacia abbreviata

Acacia abbreviata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia abbreviata is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils across the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.
