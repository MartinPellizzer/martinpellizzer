Amygdalus communis var. persica

Amygdalus communis var. persica, commonly known as the sweet almond.

This variety is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces oval, green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a hard-shelled nut containing a single edible seed.

The tree is widely cultivated for its oil and as a source of food for both humans and animals.
