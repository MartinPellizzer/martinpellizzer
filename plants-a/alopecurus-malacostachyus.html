Alopecurus malacostachyus

Alopecurus malacostachyus, commonly known as the purple false oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with purple to pinkish flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
