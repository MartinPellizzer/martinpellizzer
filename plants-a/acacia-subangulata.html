Acacia subangulata

Acacia subangulata, commonly known as the narrow-angled acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular branches and compound leaves composed of several narrow, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acacia subangulata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
