Aglaia boanana

Aglaia boanana, commonly known as the Java aglaia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish to orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in pharmacological research.

Aglaia boanana is also appreciated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
