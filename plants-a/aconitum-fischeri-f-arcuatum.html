Aconitum fischeri f. arcuatum

Aconitum fischeri f. arcuatum, commonly known as the Fischer's monkshood hybrid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring large, palmately lobed leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
