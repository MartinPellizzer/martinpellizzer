Acacia minuta subsp. densiflora

Acacia minuta subsp.

densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower acacia, is a small, shrub-like plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces oblong, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
