Adesmia trifoliolata var. epunctata

Adesmia trifoliolata var. epunctata, commonly known as the punctate legume, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically trifoliate with three leaflets, and its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is an important component of local ecosystems.
