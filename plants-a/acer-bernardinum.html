Acer bernardinum

Acer bernardinum, commonly known as the Mexican maple, is a small deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Acer genus, which includes various maple species, and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five lobes. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, preferring well-drained soils in temperate climates. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring, while its fruit is a samara, which aids in seed dispersal.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant autumn foliage.
