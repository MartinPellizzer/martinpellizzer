Allium tineoi

Allium tineoi, commonly known as the Tineo garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. Its bulbous stem is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments.
