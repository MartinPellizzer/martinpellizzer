Astrantia tenorei

Astrantia tenorei, commonly known as the Italian aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Sardinia.

It features showy, radial flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland borders.

Astrantia tenorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
