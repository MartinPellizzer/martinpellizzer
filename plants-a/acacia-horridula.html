Acacia horridula

Acacia horridula, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, followed by woody pod fruits containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Acacia horridula is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
