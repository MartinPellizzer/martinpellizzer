Agropyron kosaninii

Agropyron kosaninii, commonly known as the Kosanin's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous short, awned spikelets. Its leaves are typically narrow and bluish-green, with a rough texture.

Agropyron kosaninii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable species for erosion control and livestock forage.
