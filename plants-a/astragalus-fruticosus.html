Astragalus fruticosus

Astragalus fruticosus, commonly known as the thorny milkvetch, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus fruticosus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
