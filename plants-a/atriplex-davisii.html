Atriplex davisii

Atriplex davisii, commonly known as Davis' saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it thrive in saline soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Atriplex davisii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
