Arabis popovii

Arabis popovii, commonly known as the Popov's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages. This perennial plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features basal leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
