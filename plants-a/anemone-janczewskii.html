Anemone janczewskii

Anemone janczewskii, commonly known as the Korean anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Korea.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are usually erect and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone janczewskii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
