Allium truncatum

Allium truncatum, commonly known as the bulbous garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia. The plant features a bulb composed of narrow, fleshy, white or pale yellow scales. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces small, clustered, pink to purple flowers.

Allium truncatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild garlic-like fragrance.
