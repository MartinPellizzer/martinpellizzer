Artemisia ludoviciana var. latifolia

Artemisia ludoviciana var. latifolia, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, linear leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional uses in various cultural practices.
