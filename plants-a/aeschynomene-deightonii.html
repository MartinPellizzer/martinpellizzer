Aeschynomene deightonii

Aeschynomene deightonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf aeschynomene, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by narrow, elongated pods containing several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine or as a forage plant.
