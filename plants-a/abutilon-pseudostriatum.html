Abutilon pseudostriatum

Abutilon pseudostriatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
