Anacamptis pyramidalis var. nivea

Anacamptis pyramidalis var. nivea, commonly known as the white marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a tall, slender stem that can grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spike of white or pale pink flowers, which are often tinged with green at the base. Its inflorescence is characterized by a dense arrangement of small, spurred orchid flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is distinguished by its pale coloration, contrasting with the more typical yellowish or greenish hues of the species.
