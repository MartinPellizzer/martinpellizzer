Acacia glauca

Acacia glauca, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly branches and small, bean-like pods. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in winter, adding visual interest during the cooler months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia glauca is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
