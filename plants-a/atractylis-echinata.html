Atractylis echinata

Atractylis echinata, commonly known as the hairy centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Atractylis echinata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
