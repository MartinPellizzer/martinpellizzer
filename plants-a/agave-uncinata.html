Agave uncinata

Agave uncinata, commonly known as the hooked agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often lined with sharp spines. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. It produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellow flowers, which are followed by small, edible berries.

This plant is well adapted to drought conditions and requires minimal water once established.
