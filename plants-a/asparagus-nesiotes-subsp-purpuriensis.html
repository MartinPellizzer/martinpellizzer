Asparagus nesiotes subsp. purpuriensis

Asparagus nesiotes subsp.

purpuriensis, commonly known as the purple asparagus, is a subspecies native to parts of southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, spiny stems and fern-like, pinnate leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, needle-like spears that are harvested in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
