Anisomeles salviifolia var. inodora

Anisomeles salviifolia var. inodora, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
