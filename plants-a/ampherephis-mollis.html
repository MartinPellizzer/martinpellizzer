Ampherephis mollis

Ampherephis mollis, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a distinctive velvety texture.

Ampherephis mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
