Asterocephalus integrifolius

Asterocephalus integrifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved asterhead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. Its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences at the ends of the stems.

Asterocephalus integrifolius is often found in moist, open habitats such as marshes and wet meadows.
