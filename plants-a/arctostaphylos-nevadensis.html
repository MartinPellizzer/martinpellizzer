Arctostaphylos nevadensis

Arctostaphylos nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its woody stems and persistent leaves contribute to its resilience in harsh, arid conditions.
