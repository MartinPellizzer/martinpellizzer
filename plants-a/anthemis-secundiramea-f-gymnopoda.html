Anthemis secundiramea f. gymnopoda

Anthemis secundiramea f. gymnopoda, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed tansy, is a variant of the tansy plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to regions of southern Europe and northern Africa. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

This form is distinguished by the absence of the fine hairs that are present in the typical form of the species.
