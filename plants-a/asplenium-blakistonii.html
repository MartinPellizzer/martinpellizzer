Asplenium blakistonii

Asplenium blakistonii, commonly known as the Blakiston's spleenwort, is a small evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. Native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices and forest floors. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which release spores for reproduction.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within its native habitats.
