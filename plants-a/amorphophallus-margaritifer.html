Amorphophallus margaritifer

Amorphophallus margaritifer, commonly known as the pearl-veined arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit, which resembles a small pearl, contains toxic seeds and should be handled with care.
