Anthericum galpinii

Anthericum galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's arum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anthericum galpinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and subtle floral display.
