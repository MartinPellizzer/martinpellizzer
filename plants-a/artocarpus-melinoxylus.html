Artocarpus melinoxylus

Artocarpus melinoxylus, commonly known as the red-leaf breadfruit, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves that turn red or orange during the dry season. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, round fruits with a fibrous interior. Its fruit is typically consumed when fully mature, and it is valued for both its culinary and ornamental uses.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
