Amaryllis elegans var. vittata

Amaryllis elegans var. vittata, commonly known as the Striped Amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, with distinctive vertical stripes on the petals. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring and summer, adding vibrant color to landscapes.
