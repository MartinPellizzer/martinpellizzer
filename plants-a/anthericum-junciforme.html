Anthericum junciforme

Anthericum junciforme, commonly known as the narrowleaf arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features tall, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Anthericum junciforme is often found in association with other wetland flora and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
