Arenaria caricifolia

Arenaria caricifolia, commonly known as the marsh sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically has basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. The plant thrives in moist, often acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and along the edges of ponds. Its scientific name reflects its association with the genus *Caricifolia*, which refers to its leaf structure resembling that of the genus *Carica*.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
