Astragalus freitagii

Astragalus freitagii, commonly known as Freitag's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus freitagii is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
