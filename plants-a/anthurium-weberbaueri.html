Anthurium weberbaueri

Anthurium weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's anthurium, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spathiflorous flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Anthurium weberbaueri is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
