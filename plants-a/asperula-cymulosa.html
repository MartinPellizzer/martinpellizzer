Asperula cymulosa

Asperula cymulosa, commonly known as the clustered wood rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

Asperula cymulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
