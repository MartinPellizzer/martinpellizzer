Arabis nemophila

Arabis nemophila, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the forests of Europe and Asia.

It typically features heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland undergrowth. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate acidic soils.

Arabis nemophila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
