Antistrophe oxyantha

Antistrophe oxyantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Antistrophe oxyantha is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
