Azadirachta indica

Azadirachta indica, commonly known as neem, is a tropical tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its evergreen nature, lanceolate leaves, and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces a fruit known as a nim tree or margosa, which contains a seed surrounded by a hard shell. Neem is widely cultivated for its medicinal, pesticidal, and cosmetic properties due to the presence of bioactive compounds like azadirachtin.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it an important species in traditional and modern botanical applications.
