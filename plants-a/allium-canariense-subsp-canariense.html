Allium canariense subsp. canariense

Allium canariense subsp.

canariense, commonly known as the Canary Islands garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Canary Islands. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in umbel-like clusters.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are similar in appearance to garlic but have a milder flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary potential in local cuisine.
