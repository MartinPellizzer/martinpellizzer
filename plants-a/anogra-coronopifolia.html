Anogra coronopifolia

Anogra coronopifolia, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Anogra coronopifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
