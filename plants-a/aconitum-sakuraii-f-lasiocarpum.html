Aconitum sakuraii f. lasiocarpum

Aconitum sakuraii f. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood variant with hairy fruits, is a rare botanical variant of the Aconitum genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive hooded flowers and hairy, elongated seed pods. Native to Japan, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with deeply lobed edges.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
