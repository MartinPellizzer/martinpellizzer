Astragalus enneaphyllus

Astragalus enneaphyllus, commonly known as the nine-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with nine leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Astragalus enneaphyllus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
