Asarum caudigerum

Asarum caudigerum, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas and northeastern India.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and a distinctive, tubular flower that resembles a snake's head. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
