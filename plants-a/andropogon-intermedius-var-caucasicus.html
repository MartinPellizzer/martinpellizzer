Andropogon intermedius var. caucasicus

Andropogon intermedius var. caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
