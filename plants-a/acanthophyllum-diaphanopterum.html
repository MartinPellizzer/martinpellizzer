Acanthophyllum diaphanopterum

Acanthophyllum diaphanopterum, commonly known as the transparent-leaved jointed sage, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with features such as reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitat.
