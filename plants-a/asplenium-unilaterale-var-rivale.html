Asplenium unilaterale var. rivale

Asplenium unilaterale var. rivale, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its soft, velvety fronds.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, velvety texture due to the dense covering of fine hairs. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate low light conditions, making it suitable for indoor gardening.
