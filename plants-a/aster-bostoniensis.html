Aster bostoniensis

Aster bostoniensis, commonly known as the Boston aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine downy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aster bostoniensis thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
