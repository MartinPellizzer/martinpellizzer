Alliaria similis

Alliaria similis, commonly known as broadleaf garlic, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a distinctive garlic scent when crushed. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

Alliaria similis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
