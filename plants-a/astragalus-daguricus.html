Astragalus daguricus

Astragalus daguricus, commonly known as the Dagur's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus daguricus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
