Acalypha glechomifolia

Acalypha glechomifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
