Agave breedlovei

Agave breedlovei, commonly known as the Chisos mountain agave, is a succulent plant native to the Chisos Mountains in Texas.

It is characterized by its thick, spiky leaves that form a rosette shape, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white flowers, typically blooming once in its lifetime. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
