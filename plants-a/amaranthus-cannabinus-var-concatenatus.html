Amaranthus cannabinus var. concatenatus

Amaranthus cannabinus var. concatenatus, commonly known as the Indian hemp or Chinese spinach, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, followed by triangular, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
