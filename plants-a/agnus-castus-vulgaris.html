Agnus-castus vulgaris

Agnus-castus vulgaris, commonly known as the chastetree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported hormonal effects. Its fruit is a dark, woody capsule that contains seeds, which are used in herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.
