Alopecurus utriculatus subsp. malatyaensis

Alopecurus utriculatus subsp.

malatyaensis, commonly known as the Malatya basket grass, is a perennial grass species native to Turkey. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless stems.

The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems, with small, pale green to brown florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
