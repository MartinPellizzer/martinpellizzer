Atractylis amplexicaulis

Atractylis amplexicaulis, commonly known as the broadleaf eryngo, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can be quite large. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Atractylis amplexicaulis is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
