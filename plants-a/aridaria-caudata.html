Aridaria caudata

Aridaria caudata, commonly known as the desert tailflower, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from light rains.

Aridaria caudata plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its ecosystem.
