Artemisia maroccana

Artemisia maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and dry landscapes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other aromatic herbs and flowering plants.

Artemisia maroccana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
