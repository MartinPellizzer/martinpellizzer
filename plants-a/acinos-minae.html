Acinos minae

Acinos minae, commonly known as the Alpine Everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, dark purple to blue flowers bloom in the summer, adding a splash of color to alpine meadows.

Acinos minae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine displays.
