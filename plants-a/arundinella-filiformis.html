Arundinella filiformis

Arundinella filiformis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often mistaken for reeds or grasses. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Arundinella filiformis is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber due to its strong, flexible stalks.
