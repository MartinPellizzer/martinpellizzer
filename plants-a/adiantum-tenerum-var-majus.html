Adiantum tenerum var. majus

Adiantum tenerum var. majus, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

Adiantum tenerum var. majus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
