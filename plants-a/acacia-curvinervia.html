Acacia curvinervia

Acacia curvinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia curvinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
