Amaranthus tenuis

Amaranthus tenuis, commonly known as slender amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other edible and ornamental plants.

Amaranthus tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous cultures.
