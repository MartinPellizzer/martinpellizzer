Allium anacoleum

Allium anacoleum, commonly known as the purple garlic.

It is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its striking violet flowers and edible bulbs. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species like garlic and onions. Its leaves are strap-shaped and grass-like, while its flowers form a dense umbel.

Allium anacoleum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses.
