Alansmia dependens

Alansmia dependens, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces pale purple or white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Alansmia dependens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
