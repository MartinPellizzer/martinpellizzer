Acer negundo f. argenteomarginatum

Acer negundo f. argenteomarginatum, commonly known as the silver-margin boxelder, is a cultivar of the boxelder tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring white or silver margins on the leaves that contrast with its green centers. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
