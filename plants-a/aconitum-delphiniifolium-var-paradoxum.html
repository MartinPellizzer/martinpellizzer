Aconitum delphiniifolium var. paradoxum

Aconitum delphiniifolium var. paradoxum, commonly known as the Paradoxical Monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that are typical of the genus Aconitum. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble the shape of a dolphin's fin, giving it its species name.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic appearance.
