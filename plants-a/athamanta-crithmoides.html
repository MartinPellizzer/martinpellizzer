Athamanta crithmoides

Athamanta crithmoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas with well-drained conditions.

Athamanta crithmoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
