Asanthus squamulosus

Asanthus squamulosus, commonly known as the wooly leaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance.

Asanthus squamulosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
