Agave katharinae

Agave katharinae, commonly known as the Katherine's agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern, with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are lined with sharp spines and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. This plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments by storing water in its leaves and stems.

It produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime.
