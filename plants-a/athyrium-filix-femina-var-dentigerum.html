Athyrium filix-femina var. dentigerum

Athyrium filix-femina var. dentigerum, commonly known as the toothed lady fern, is a variant of the common lady fern characterized by its serrated fronds.

It is a deciduous fern native to eastern North America, typically found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with fertile fronds that produce spores on the underside. Its foliage is delicate and displays a graceful, feathery appearance, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The varietal name "dentigerum" refers to the tooth-like margins of its leaves.
