Achillea umbellata subsp. umbellata

Achillea umbellata subsp.

umbellata, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its flat, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of small white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and grasslands.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for its healing properties.
