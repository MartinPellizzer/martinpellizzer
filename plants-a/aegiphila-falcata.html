Aegiphila falcata

Aegiphila falcata, commonly known as the sickle-leaved aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

Aegiphila falcata is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.
