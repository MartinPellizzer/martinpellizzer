Aconitum longipetiolatum

Aconitum longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves with long petioles, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
