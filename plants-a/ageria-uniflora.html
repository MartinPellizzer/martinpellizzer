Ageria uniflora

Ageria uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower ageratum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features small, clustered flowers in shades of white, purple, and blue. Ageria uniflora prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
