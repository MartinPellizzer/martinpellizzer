Anisantha madritensis var. ciliata

Anisantha madritensis var. ciliata, commonly known as the ciliate grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
