Aulomyrcia hypericoides

Aulomyrcia hypericoides, commonly known as the yellow-flowered myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within humid forests.

Aulomyrcia hypericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
