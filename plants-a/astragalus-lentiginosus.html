Astragalus lentiginosus

Astragalus lentiginosus, commonly known as the spotted milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Astragalus lentiginosus has small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and as a food source for native wildlife.
