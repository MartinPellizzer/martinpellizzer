Allium getulum

Allium getulum, commonly known as the purple onion or wild garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Allium getulum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
