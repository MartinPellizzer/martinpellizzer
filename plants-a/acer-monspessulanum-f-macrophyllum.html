Acer monspessulanum f. macrophyllum

Acer monspessulanum f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf maple, is a variant of the horse chestnut tree.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length. The leaves are typically a vibrant green during the growing season and turn yellow in the autumn. This cultivar is often prized for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.
