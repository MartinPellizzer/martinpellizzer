Acer tataricum subvar. crispatum

Acer tataricum subvar. crispatum, commonly known as the curly-leaf maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated, and often curly leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and has a spreading, multi-stemmed form. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
