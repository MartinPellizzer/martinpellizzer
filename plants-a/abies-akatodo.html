Abies akatodo

Abies akatodo, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the genus Abies in the family Pinaceae, characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its foliage is dark green and densely packed, providing a dense canopy.

The cones are small and cylindrical, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
