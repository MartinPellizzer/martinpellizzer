Amblirion grecum

Amblirion grecum, commonly known as the Greek fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from pink to purple in color.

Amblirion grecum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
