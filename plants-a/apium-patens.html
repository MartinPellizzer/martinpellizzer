Apium patens

Apium patens, commonly known as the slender cowparsley, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family.

Apium patens is often found in open, sunny habitats and is considered a minor weed in some agricultural settings.
