Abies hudsonia

Abies hudsonia, commonly known as the Hudson's Bay fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the boreal forests of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain small, winged seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in association with other boreal species.

The wood of Abies hudsonia is lightweight and has been historically used for construction and paper production.
