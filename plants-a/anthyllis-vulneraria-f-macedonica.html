Anthyllis vulneraria f. macedonica

Anthyllis vulneraria f. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to the Balkan region, particularly Greece and the former Yugoslavia. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
