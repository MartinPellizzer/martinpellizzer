Allium feinbergii

Allium feinbergii, commonly known as the Feinberg's garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of onions, garlic, and chives. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are grass-like and form a dense rosette near the ground.

Allium feinbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
