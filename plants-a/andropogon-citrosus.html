Andropogon citrosus

Andropogon citrosus, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage that emits a sweet, lemony scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its slender, tufted inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Sweetgrass is often used in traditional herbal practices and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
