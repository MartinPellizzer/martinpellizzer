Allium bodinieri

Allium bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. Its bulbous structure is similar to other alliums, but it is distinguished by its unique floral display and aromatic foliage.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience in garden settings.
