Ammodendron sieversii var. lehmannianum

Ammodendron sieversii var. lehmannianum, commonly known as Lehmann's date palm, is a shrub-like plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the palms family, Arecaceae, and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and short, clustered stems. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of food in its native habitat.
