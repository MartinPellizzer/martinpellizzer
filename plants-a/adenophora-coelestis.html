Adenophora coelestis

Adenophora coelestis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Adenophora coelestis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
