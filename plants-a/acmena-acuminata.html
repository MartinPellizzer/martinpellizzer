Acmena acuminata

Acmena acuminata, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subcoastal regions.

Acmena acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
