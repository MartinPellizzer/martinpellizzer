Aristolochia hockii subsp. hockii

Aristolochia hockii subsp.

hockii, commonly known as the Hock's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States. It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves with a distinctive, intricate veining pattern.

The plant produces striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musky odor. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
