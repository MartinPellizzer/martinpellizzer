Aegiphila casseliiformis

Aegiphila casseliiformis, commonly known as the Cassel's leaf or the Brazilian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
