Aster richardsonii var. meritus

Aster richardsonii var. meritus, commonly known as the Richardson's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structure and medicinal uses in traditional practices.
