Acaciella prostrata

Acaciella prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate acacia, is a low-growing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its succulent stems and reduced leaf surface. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small animals in its native range.

Its ecological significance is further enhanced by its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
