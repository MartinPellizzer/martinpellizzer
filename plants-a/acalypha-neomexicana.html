Acalypha neomexicana

Acalypha neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico marvel, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that often display a vibrant red or purple coloration, especially in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiky seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid and semi-arid landscapes.

Acalypha neomexicana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
