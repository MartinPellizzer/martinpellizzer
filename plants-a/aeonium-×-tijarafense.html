Aeonium × tijarafense

Aeonium × tijarafense, commonly known as the Tijarafe aeonium, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure, with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or pink hues at the edges. The plant produces tall, branching flower stalks that bear yellow or orange flowers, which appear in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements have made it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
