Amorphophallus obovoideus

Amorphophallus obovoideus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, often spiky inflorescence that resembles a phallic structure, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces a single, large, white or greenish-white flower that is typically short-lived. It is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

The plant is also known for its edible corm, which is used in various traditional cuisines.
