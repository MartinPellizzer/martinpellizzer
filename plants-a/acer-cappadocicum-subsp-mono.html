Acer cappadocicum subsp. mono

Acer cappadocicum subsp.

mono, commonly known as the single-leaf maple, is a rare and distinctive species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically single rather than compound.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
