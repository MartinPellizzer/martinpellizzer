Amaryllis popetana

Amaryllis popetana, commonly known as the Popetana amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, producing clusters of vibrant pink or white flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements make it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
