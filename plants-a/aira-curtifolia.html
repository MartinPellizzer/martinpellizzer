Aira curtifolia

Aira curtifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf airgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and native plant landscaping.
