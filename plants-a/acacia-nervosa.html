Acacia nervosa

Acacia nervosa, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Acacia nervosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
