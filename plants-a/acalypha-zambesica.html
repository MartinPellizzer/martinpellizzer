Acalypha zambesica

Acalypha zambesica, commonly known as the Zambesian spurflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly pubescent surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, with a unique structure that includes cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
