Andropogon pteropholis var. togoensis

Andropogon pteropholis var. togoensis, commonly known as the Togo bluestem, is a perennial grass native to West Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as riverbanks and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
