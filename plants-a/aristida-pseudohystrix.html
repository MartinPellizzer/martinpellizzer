Aristida pseudohystrix

Aristida pseudohystrix, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Aristida pseudohystrix is often found in sandy or gravelly areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
