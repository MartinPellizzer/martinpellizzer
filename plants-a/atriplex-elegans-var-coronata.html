Atriplex elegans var. coronata

Atriplex elegans var. coronata, commonly known as silverleaf chenopody, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, reflective hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its succulent leaves help it conserve water, making it well adapted to drought conditions.
