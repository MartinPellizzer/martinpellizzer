Adenocarpus benguellensis

Adenocarpus benguellensis, commonly known as the Benguella thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy surface, while the stems are robust and slightly woody.

Adenocarpus benguellensis is often found in grasslands and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
