Antirrhinum orontium var. chrysothales

Antirrhinum orontium var. chrysothales, commonly known as the golden antirrhinum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive two-lipped structure. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a striking splash of color to gardens.
