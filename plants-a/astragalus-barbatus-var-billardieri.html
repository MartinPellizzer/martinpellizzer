Astragalus barbatus var. billardieri

Astragalus barbatus var. billardieri, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
