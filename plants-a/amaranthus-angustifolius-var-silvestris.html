Amaranthus angustifolius var. silvestris

Amaranthus angustifolius var. silvestris, commonly known as wild amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, greenish or reddish flowers. The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high protein and mineral content.

This species is also used in traditional cuisines and has gained interest as a potential crop for sustainable agriculture.
