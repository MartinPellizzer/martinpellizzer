Acacia cordifolia

Acacia cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow, globular flower heads. Acacia cordifolia is drought-tolerant and has adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its wood is hard and strong, making it valuable for traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
