Amyema hutchinsonii

Amyema hutchinsonii, commonly known as the Hutchinson's ghost gum, is a small evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resistance to pests and diseases.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
