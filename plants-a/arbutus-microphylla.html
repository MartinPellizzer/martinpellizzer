Arbutus microphylla

Arbutus microphylla, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and small, leathery leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange or red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and chaparral habitats.

The Pacific madrone is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
