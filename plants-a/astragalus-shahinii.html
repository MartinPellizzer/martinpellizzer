Astragalus shahinii

Astragalus shahinii, commonly known as the Shahinii milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus shahinii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
