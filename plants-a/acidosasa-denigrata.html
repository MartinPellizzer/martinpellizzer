Acidosasa denigrata

Acidosasa denigrata, commonly known as the blackened acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and small, compound leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing small, hard seeds. Acidosasa denigrata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its resilience to drought makes it an important plant in desert ecosystems.
