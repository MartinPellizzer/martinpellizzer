Aloe serriyensis

Aloe serriyensis, commonly known as the serrated aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically gray-green in color with a textured surface. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow-orange flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe serriyensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
