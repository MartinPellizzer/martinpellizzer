Acianthera pendens

Acianthera pendens, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for epiphytic growth.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and moths.
