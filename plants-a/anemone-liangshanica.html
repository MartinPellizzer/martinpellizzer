Anemone liangshanica

Anemone liangshanica, commonly known as the Liangshan anemone, is a perennial herb native to China, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Sichuan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, palmate leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
