Asplenium inexpectatum

Asplenium inexpectatum, commonly known as the unexpected spleenwort, is a rare and little-known fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, creeping growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Asplenium inexpectatum is considered a botanically significant species for ecological and conservation studies.
