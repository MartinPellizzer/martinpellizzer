Aspidistra liliiflora

Aspidistra liliiflora, commonly known as the Lily-flowered Aspidistra, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features dark green, leathery, strap-shaped leaves that are evergreen and can grow quite large. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered around the spadix, often giving it a subtle, elegant appearance.

Aspidistra liliiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its tolerance of low light conditions.
