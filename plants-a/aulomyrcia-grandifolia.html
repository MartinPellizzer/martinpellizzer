Aulomyrcia grandifolia

Aulomyrcia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved aulomyrcia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Aulomyrcia grandifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
