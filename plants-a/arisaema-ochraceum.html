Arisaema ochraceum

Arisaema ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow thread-leaf arisaema, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features narrow, thread-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema ochraceum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
