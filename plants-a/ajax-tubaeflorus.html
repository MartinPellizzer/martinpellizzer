Ajax tubaeflorus

Ajax tubaeflorus, commonly known as the trumpet flower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting a variety of pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

The unique floral structure of Ajax tubaeflorus makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscapes.
