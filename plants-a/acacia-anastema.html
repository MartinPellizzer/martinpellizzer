Acacia anastema

Acacia anastema, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of many small, feather-like leaflets.

The wood of Acacia anastema is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and fuel.
