Anarthrophyllum desideratum var. typica

Anarthrophyllum desideratum var. typica, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through mechanisms such as succulent leaves and deep root systems. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
