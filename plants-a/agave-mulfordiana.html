Agave mulfordiana

Agave mulfordiana, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, which help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small white flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in the production of tequila.

Agave mulfordiana prefers well-draining soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
