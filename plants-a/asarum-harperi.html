Asarum harperi

Asarum harperi, commonly known as the Pacific asarum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features heart-shaped, reddish-brown leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and a strong, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asarum harperi is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
