Abutilon indicum var. forrestii

Abutilon indicum var. forrestii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
