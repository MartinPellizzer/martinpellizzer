Alchemilla stichotricha

Alchemilla stichotricha, commonly known as the hairy lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla stichotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
