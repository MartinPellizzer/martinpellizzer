Adenocrepis lanceolata

Adenocrepis lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, spiny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Adenocrepis lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
