Ardisia confertiflora

Ardisia confertiflora, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are a notable feature, often attracting birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia confertiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
