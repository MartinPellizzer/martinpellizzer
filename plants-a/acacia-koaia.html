Acacia koaia

Acacia koaia, commonly known as the Hawaiian koa tree, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, spongy material. Acacia koaia is well adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its ecological and cultural significance makes it an important species in Hawaiian ecosystems and traditional practices.
