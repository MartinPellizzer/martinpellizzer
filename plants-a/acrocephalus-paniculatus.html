Acrocephalus paniculatus

Acrocephalus paniculatus, commonly known as the marsh warbler, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and loose, drooping clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with abundant moisture. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts.

The species is well adapted to wetland habitats and plays a role in stabilizing soil in these environments.
