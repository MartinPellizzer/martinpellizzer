Anthemis abyssinica

Anthemis abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in late summer.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
