Actaea monogyna

Actaea monogyna, commonly known as white crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Actaea monogyna is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
