Agave grijalvensis

Agave grijalvensis, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a species native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces a tall flowering stalk with small, greenish-white flowers, which bloom after many years.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional practices and horticulture.
