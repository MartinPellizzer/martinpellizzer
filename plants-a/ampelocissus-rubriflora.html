Ampelocissus rubriflora

Ampelocissus rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer. It is often used as a decorative vine in gardens and hanging baskets due to its attractive appearance.

Ampelocissus rubriflora prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
