Alepidea capensis var. tenella

Alepidea capensis var. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved African daisy, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
