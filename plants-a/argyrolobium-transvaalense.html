Argyrolobium transvaalense

Argyrolobium transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
