Alternanthera filifolia subsp. sylvatica

Alternanthera filifolia subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the velvetleaf Alternanthera, is a herbaceous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that are often a deep red or purple color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for water gardens and bog plantings.
