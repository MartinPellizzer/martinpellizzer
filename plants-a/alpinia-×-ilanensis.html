Alpinia × ilanensis

Alpinia × ilanensis, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or fragrant ginger, is a hybrid plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect rhizomes and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated for both horticultural and traditional uses.
