Agrimonia eupatoria subvar. albiflora

Agrimonia eupatoria subvar. albiflora, commonly known as white agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its serrated leaves and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The dried flowers and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.
