Ardisia viburnifolia

Ardisia viburnifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark blue, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded gardens.
