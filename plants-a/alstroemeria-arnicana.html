Alstroemeria arnicana

Alstroemeria arnicana, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring strap-like leaves and a compact growth habit. Alstroemeria arnicana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with moderate sunlight.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
