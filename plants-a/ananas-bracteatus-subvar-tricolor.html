Ananas bracteatus subvar. tricolor

Ananas bracteatus subvar. tricolor, commonly known as the tricolor pineapple, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tri-colored foliage, with leaves displaying shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, white flowers, followed by orange or red berries. Its unique coloration makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and indoor spaces.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
