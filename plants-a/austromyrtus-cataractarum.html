Austromyrtus cataractarum

Austromyrtus cataractarum, commonly known as the cascades myrtle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as rainforests and along streams.

Austromyrtus cataractarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
