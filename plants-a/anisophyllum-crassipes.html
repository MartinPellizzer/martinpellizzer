Anisophyllum crassipes

Anisophyllum crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved anisophyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
