Acarpha

Acarpha, commonly known as the grass or false grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

These plants are typically herbaceous and are characterized by their slender, grass-like appearance. Acarpha species are often found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. They produce small, spike-like inflorescences and have narrow, linear leaves.

The genus includes several species that are used in ecological restoration and as ornamental plants.
