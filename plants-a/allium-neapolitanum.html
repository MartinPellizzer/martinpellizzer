Allium neapolitanum

Allium neapolitanum, commonly known as the Neapolitan garlic, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it a suitable ornamental and culinary plant.

Allium neapolitanum is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and culinary versatility.
