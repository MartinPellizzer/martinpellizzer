Acacia leptoloba

Acacia leptoloba, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that appear in winter. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Acacia leptoloba is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various native animals.
