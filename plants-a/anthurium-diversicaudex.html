Anthurium diversicaudex

Anthurium diversicaudex, commonly known as the feathered anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated spathes that can range in color from deep red to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically bright red when mature. Its leaves are large, glossy, and deeply veined, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Anthurium diversicaudex thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
