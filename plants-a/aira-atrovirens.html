Aira atrovirens

Aira atrovirens, commonly known as black-veined grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves with prominent, dark veins that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aira atrovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
