Anisomeris pohliana

Anisomeris pohliana, commonly known as the desert star, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features dense, spiny foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a splash of color to the desert landscape. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water, while its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater.

Anisomeris pohliana is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
