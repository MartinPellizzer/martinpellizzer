Acanthostachys strobilacea

Acanthostachys strobilacea, commonly known as the woolly spike grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and compact, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against herbivory.

Acanthostachys strobilacea is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
