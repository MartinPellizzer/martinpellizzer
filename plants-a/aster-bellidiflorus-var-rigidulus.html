Aster bellidiflorus var. rigidulus

Aster bellidiflorus var. rigidulus, commonly known as the rigid aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are blue to purple, with five petals, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
