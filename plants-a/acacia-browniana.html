Acacia browniana

Acacia browniana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. Acacia browniana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for native pollinators and serving as a habitat for various wildlife species.
