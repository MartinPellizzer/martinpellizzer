Astragalus brachycarpus var. major

Astragalus brachycarpus var. major, commonly known as the large-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
