Aeschynomene pseudoglabrescens

Aeschynomene pseudoglabrescens, commonly known as the narrowleaf burrlegume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, hard pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in arid environments, making it a valuable plant for ecological restoration.
