Acorus terrestris

Acorus terrestris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cattail, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features long, narrow leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem, and it produces dense, cylindrical spikes of yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in shallow water and moist soil, making it a common sight in freshwater habitats. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Acorus terrestris plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Acorus terrestris has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are bright green, arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant, and can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, pale brown bark that grows upright from the base. The fruit is a three-lobed, dark purple berry that disperses its seeds through water, as the seeds are lightweight and buoyant.

The roots are rhizomatous, forming dense underground networks that help the plant spread and anchor itself in wet environments.

Acorus terrestris is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive, sword-shaped leaves.
