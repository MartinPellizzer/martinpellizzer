Aster puniceus var. demissus

Aster puniceus var. demissus, commonly known as the pinkstem aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are typically pink to red in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale purple, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
