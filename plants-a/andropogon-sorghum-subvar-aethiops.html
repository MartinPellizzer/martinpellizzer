Andropogon sorghum subvar. aethiops

Andropogon sorghum subvar. aethiops, commonly known as Ethiopian sorghum, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, typical of the grass family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in challenging climates.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both forage and traditional agricultural use.
