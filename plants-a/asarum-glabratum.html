Asarum glabratum

Asarum glabratum, commonly known as the smooth asarum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spathaceous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its underground rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

Asarum glabratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
