Argyreia sphaerocephala

Argyreia sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe flower or red globe morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive spherical seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon, giving it its common name.
