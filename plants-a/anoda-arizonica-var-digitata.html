Anoda arizonica var. digitata

Anoda arizonica var. digitata, commonly known as Arizona false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a pubescent surface and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in the Sonoran and Chihuahuan Deserts.
