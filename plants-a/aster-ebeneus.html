Aster ebeneus

Aster ebeneus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodland edges. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aster ebeneus is valued for its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
