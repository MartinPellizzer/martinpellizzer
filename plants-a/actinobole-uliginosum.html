Actinobole uliginosum

Actinobole uliginosum, commonly known as the swamp bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures called pneumatophores, which help in gas exchange. The plant is an obligate carnivore, capturing small aquatic organisms using its specialized traps. It thrives in nutrient-poor water and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling insect populations.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studying plant adaptations to aquatic environments.
