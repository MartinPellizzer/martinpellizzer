Arisaema serratum var. ionochlamys

Arisaema serratum var. ionochlamys, commonly known as the serrated arum, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a short, erect stem.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the spadix.
