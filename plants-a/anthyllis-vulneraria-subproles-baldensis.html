Anthyllis vulneraria subproles baldensis

Anthyllis vulneraria subproles baldensis, commonly known as the Bald Mountain anthyllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its ecological significance includes providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native environment.
