Astragalus tecti-mundi

Astragalus tecti-mundi, commonly known as the "sky-protecting milkvetch," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils at high altitudes. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, containing compounds that have been studied for their potential immune-boosting effects.

Despite its ecological significance, it remains a relatively rare and less commonly cultivated species.
