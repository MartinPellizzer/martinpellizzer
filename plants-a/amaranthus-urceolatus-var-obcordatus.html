Amaranthus urceolatus var. obcordatus

Amaranthus urceolatus var. obcordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf amaranth, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, rich soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming dense, colorful inflorescences that attract pollinators.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
