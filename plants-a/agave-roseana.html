Agave roseana

Agave roseana, commonly known as the roseate agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with red or pink hues. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears white to pale purple flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
