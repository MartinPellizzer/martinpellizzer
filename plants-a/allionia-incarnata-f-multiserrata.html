Allionia incarnata f. multiserrata

Allionia incarnata f. multiserrata, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with multiple serrations, giving it the subspecies name *multiserrata*.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
