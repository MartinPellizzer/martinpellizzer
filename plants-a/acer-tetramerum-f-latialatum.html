Acer tetramerum f. latialatum

Acer tetramerum f. latialatum, commonly known as the Oregon maple, is a variant of the Pacific maple.

It is a deciduous tree native to the western United States, particularly Oregon and Washington. The plant features palmate leaves with five lobes, which are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while its fruit is a samara with two wings.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
