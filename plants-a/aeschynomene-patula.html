Aeschynomene patula

Aeschynomene patula, commonly known as partridge pea, is an annual legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage crop in drought-prone regions. It has a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers.

Additionally, it fixes atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia, improving soil fertility.
