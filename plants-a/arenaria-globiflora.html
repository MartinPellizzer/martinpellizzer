Arenaria globiflora

Arenaria globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Arenaria globiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
