Aconitum okuyamae var. okuyamae

Aconitum okuyamae var. okuyamae, commonly known as the Okuyama aconite, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
