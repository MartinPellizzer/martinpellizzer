Andropogon canaliglumis

Andropogon canaliglumis, commonly known as inland saltgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or pale blue. This plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in coastal and inland wetlands. It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its adaptability to harsh environments contributes to its presence in disturbed and saline habitats.
