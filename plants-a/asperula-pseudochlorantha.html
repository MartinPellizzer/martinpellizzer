Asperula pseudochlorantha

Asperula pseudochlorantha, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in woodland areas. Its flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Asperula pseudochlorantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to acidic soils.
