Apocynum cannabinum var. palustre

Apocynum cannabinum var. palustre, commonly known as swamp dogbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinct waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its milky sap contains toxic cardiac glycosides, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

The plant is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
