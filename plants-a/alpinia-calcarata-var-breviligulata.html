Alpinia calcarata var. breviligulata

Alpinia calcarata var. breviligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Zingiberaceae family.
