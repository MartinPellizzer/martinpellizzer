Acacia malacocentra

Acacia malacocentra, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves that are typically bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
