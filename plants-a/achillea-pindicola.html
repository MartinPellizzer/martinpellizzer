Achillea pindicola

Achillea pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Pindus Mountains in Greece.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often with a slightly bitter scent.

Achillea pindicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
