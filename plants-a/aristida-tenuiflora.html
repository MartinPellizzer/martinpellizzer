Aristida tenuiflora

Aristida tenuiflora, commonly known as narrowleaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has fibrous roots and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental and ecological value.
