Arthropogon stipitatus

Arthropogon stipitatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Arthropogon stipitatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
