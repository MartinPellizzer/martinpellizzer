Anthriscus oliveri

Anthriscus oliveri, commonly known as broadleaf cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often mistaken for parsley or carrots. The plant produces small, white umbels of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Anthriscus oliveri is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is considered a valuable plant for pollinators.
