Artemisia semiarida subsp. argillacea

Artemisia semiarida subsp.

argillacea, commonly known as the clay artemisia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils, often found on rocky or clayey slopes. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
