Alstroemeria garaventae

Alstroemeria garaventae, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
