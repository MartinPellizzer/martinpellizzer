Aloe fraskii

Aloe fraskii, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The leaves are typically spiny and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps protect the plant from herbivores. It produces yellow-orange flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aloe fraskii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
