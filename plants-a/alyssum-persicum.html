Alyssum persicum

Alyssum persicum, commonly known as Persian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. Alyssum persicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
