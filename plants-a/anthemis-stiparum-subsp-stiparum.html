Anthemis stiparum subsp. stiparum

Anthemis stiparum subsp.

stiparum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange composite flowers and linear, often hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, glandular structures that contribute to its strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.
