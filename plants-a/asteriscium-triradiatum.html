Asteriscium triradiatum

Asteriscium triradiatum, commonly known as the three-rayed aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in a cluster, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

Asteriscium triradiatum is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and sandy soils.
