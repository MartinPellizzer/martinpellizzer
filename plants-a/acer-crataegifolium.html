Acer crataegifolium

Acer crataegifolium, commonly known as the crabapple maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that resemble those of the hawthorn, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 meters and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting a variety of wildlife.
