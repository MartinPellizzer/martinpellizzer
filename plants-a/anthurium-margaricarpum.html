Anthurium margaricarpum

Anthurium margaricarpum, commonly known as the red-heart anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep red or maroon color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, with a spadix that is often covered in a dense cluster of tiny berries. Anthurium margaricarpum thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
