Alsophila dryopteroidea

Alsophila dryopteroidea, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a unique rhizome system that allows it to grow rapidly and form dense stands in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila dryopteroidea plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
