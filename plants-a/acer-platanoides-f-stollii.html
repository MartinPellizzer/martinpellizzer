Acer platanoides f. stollii

Acer platanoides f. stollii, commonly known as the Stollii Norway maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple (Acer platanoides).

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring large, palmate leaves with a deep green color and a slight yellowish tinge on the underside. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically growing to a moderate height, and is valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This cultivar is relatively hardy and adapts well to a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for urban and suburban gardens.
