Astragalus nuttallii var. virgatus

Astragalus nuttallii var. virgatus, commonly known as the Virgin's beard or Nuttall's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, hairy pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
