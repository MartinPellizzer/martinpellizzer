Aechmea skinneri

Aechmea skinneri, commonly known as the Skinner's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure that collects rainwater. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically broad and sword-like, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Aechmea skinneri thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

Morphology

Aechmea skinneri has rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy, broad, and pointed, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem, with a dark green to blue-green coloration.

The stem is short and thick, covered in a fibrous, rough bark, and grows upright with a compact growth pattern. Its flowers are clustered in a spike at the center of the rosette, featuring bright red bracts with yellow-orange petals, blooming during the summer months, and are pollinated primarily by bees and hummingbirds. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by animals consuming the flesh and excreting the seeds.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted for water storage, typical of bromeliads.
