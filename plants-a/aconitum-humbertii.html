Aconitum humbertii

Aconitum humbertii, commonly known as Humbert's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.
