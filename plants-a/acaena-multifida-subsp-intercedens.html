Acaena multifida subsp. intercedens

Acaena multifida subsp.

intercedens, commonly known as the New Zealand thorned sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces achenes, which are dry, one-seeded fruits, and is often found in coastal and subalpine habitats. It has a creeping growth habit and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
