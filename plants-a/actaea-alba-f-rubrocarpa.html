Actaea alba f. rubrocarpa

Actaea alba f. rubrocarpa, commonly known as the red-stemmed white cohosh, is a cultivar of the Actaea genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and large, white, lacecap flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces red berries in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shaded garden settings.
