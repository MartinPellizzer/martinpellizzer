Adenodolichos nanus

Adenodolichos nanus, commonly known as the dwarf cowpea, is a small leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, making it well-suited for ornamental or shade garden use. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Adenodolichos nanus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
