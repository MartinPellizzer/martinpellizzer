Aconitum ranunculoides subsp. gigas

Aconitum ranunculoides subsp.

gigas, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces large, showy blue or purple flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Aconitum ranunculoides subsp.

gigas is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
