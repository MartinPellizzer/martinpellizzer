Aletris glauca

Aletris glauca, commonly known as blue star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, clumping habit. The plant features bright blue to silver-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aletris glauca is adapted to woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shade gardens.
