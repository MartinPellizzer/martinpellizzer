Aloe secundiflora var. sobolifera

Aloe secundiflora var. sobolifera, commonly known as the desert aloe, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
