Arabis hirsuta var. fasciculata

Arabis hirsuta var. fasciculata, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and often clustered in fascicles, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a desirable plant for rock gardens and alpine collections.
