Abies flinckii

Abies flinckii, commonly known as the Flinck's fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The cones of Abies flinckii are usually pendulous, with a cylindrical shape and woody scales that release seeds when mature. This plant thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
