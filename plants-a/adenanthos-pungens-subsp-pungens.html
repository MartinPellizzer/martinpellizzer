Adenanthos pungens subsp. pungens

Adenanthos pungens subsp.

pungens, commonly known as the serrated adenanthos, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in native gardens.
