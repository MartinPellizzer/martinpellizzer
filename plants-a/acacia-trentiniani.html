Acacia trentiniani

Acacia trentiniani, commonly known as the Trentini acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its slender, straight stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

Acacia trentiniani is adapted to cool, dry environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.
