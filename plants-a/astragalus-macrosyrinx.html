Astragalus macrosyrinx

Astragalus macrosyrinx, commonly known as the large pod milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus macrosyrinx is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
