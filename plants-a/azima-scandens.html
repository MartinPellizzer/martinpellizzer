Azima scandens

Azima scandens, commonly known as the climbing azima, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, dry fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
