Allium stramineum var. aureum

Allium stramineum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden garlic or yellow garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to golden flowers. The plant belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various edible and ornamental species. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a popular choice in some cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
