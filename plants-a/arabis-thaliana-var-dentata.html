Arabis thaliana var. dentata

Arabis thaliana var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed rockcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves with serrated or toothed edges, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to grow in harsh, arid environments.
