Abrus abrus

Abrus abrus, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking plant. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a natural poison. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and have a feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
