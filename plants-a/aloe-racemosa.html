Aloe racemosa

Aloe racemosa, commonly known as the spiked aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in small white spots. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves for survival.

Aloe racemosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
