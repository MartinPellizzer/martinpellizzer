Astragalus parrisii

Astragalus parrisii, commonly known as Parris's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. It is often found in regions with low precipitation and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus parrisii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
