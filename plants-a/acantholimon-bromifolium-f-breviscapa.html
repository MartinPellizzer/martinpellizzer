Acantholimon bromifolium f. breviscapa

Acantholimon bromifolium f. breviscapa, commonly known as the short-stemmed saltbush, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Its flowers are typically greenish or yellowish, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for ecological restoration in harsh climates.
