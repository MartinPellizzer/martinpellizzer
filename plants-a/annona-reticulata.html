Annona reticulata

Annona reticulata, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five white petals and a prominent yellow or green center. Its fruit is a rough, green, oval-shaped berry that turns yellow when ripe.

The leaves are large, alternate, and have a velvety texture with a distinctive reticulate pattern.
