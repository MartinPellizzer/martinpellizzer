Aristolochia gabonensis

Aristolochia gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon pipevine, is a climbing vine native to Central and Western Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and intricate, pipe-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
