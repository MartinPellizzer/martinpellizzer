Aeschynomene ciliata

Aeschynomene ciliata, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. Aeschynomene ciliata is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth makes it a useful plant for soil stabilization and erosion control.
