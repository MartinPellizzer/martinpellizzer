Atriplex eremicola

Atriplex eremicola, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant has small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Atriplex eremicola plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
