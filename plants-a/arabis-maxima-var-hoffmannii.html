Arabis maxima var. hoffmannii

Arabis maxima var. hoffmannii, commonly known as Hoffmann's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. This perennial species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in rock gardens and alpine settings.
