Acer pseudoplatanus var. longifolium

Acer pseudoplatanus var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved maple, is a variant of the common maple, characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France. The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a distinctive elongated shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that aid in its dispersal.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
