Anacamptis pseudolaxiflora

Anacamptis pseudolaxiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers, which are usually pale green to yellowish in color, bloom from late spring to early summer.

Anacamptis pseudolaxiflora is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in areas with moderate moisture levels.
