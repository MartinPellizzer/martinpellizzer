Artanthe opizii

Artanthe opizii, commonly known as the Opiz's artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, often pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Artanthe opizii is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
