Araujia undulata

Araujia undulata, commonly known as the undulating moon vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faintly fragrant scent. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Araujia undulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in warm, tropical climates.
