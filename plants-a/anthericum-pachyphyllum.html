Anthericum pachyphyllum

Anthericum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in a thick, waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its moist habitat. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers with six petals, arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in its natural environment.

Anthericum pachyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or bog gardens.
