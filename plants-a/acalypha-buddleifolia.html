Acalypha buddleifolia

Acalypha buddleifolia, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flames, which appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by three-winged, elliptical fruits.

Acalypha buddleifolia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
