Anthyllis linifolia

Anthyllis linifolia, commonly known as silky broomweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Anthyllis linifolia is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
