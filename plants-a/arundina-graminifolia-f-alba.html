Arundina graminifolia f. alba

Arundina graminifolia f. alba, commonly known as the white form of the bamboo orchid, is a rare and striking variant of the bamboo orchid.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its pale or white foliage, which distinguishes it from the typical green form. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with a clumping growth habit, often forming dense clusters of grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Its unique coloration makes it a sought-after specimen for botanical gardens and specialized horticulture.
