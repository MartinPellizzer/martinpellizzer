Amorphophallus angolensis

Amorphophallus angolensis, commonly known as the Angola arrowroot, is a tuberous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, leafy stems and striking, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant produces a massive underground tuber, which is the primary edible part used in various traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The tuber is rich in starch and has been historically used as a food source by local communities.
