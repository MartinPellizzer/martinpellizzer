Aspidium filix-mas subsp. remotum

Aspidium filix-mas subsp.

remotum, commonly known as the distant male fern, is a fern species native to parts of Asia and Africa. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a complex, lace-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a feature common among ferns.

The subspecies is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
