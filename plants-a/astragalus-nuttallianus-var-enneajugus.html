Astragalus nuttallianus var. enneajugus

Astragalus nuttallianus var. enneajugus, commonly known as the nine-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with nine pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
