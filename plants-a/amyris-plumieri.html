Amyris plumieri

Amyris plumieri, commonly known as the Cuban sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries. Amyris plumieri is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
