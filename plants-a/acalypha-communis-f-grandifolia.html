Acalypha communis f. grandifolia

Acalypha communis f. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant variegated iron plant, is a striking ornamental shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with striking variegation of white, yellow, and green, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by distinctive, papery bracts that enhance its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its vibrant foliage, it is popular in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
