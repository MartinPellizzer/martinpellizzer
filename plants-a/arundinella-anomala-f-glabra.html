Arundinella anomala f. glabra

Arundinella anomala f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bamboo reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming panicles that are often overlooked due to the plant's subtle appearance.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, hairless surface, which sets it apart from other forms of the species.
