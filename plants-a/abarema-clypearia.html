Abarema clypearia

Abarema clypearia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are typically covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by legume pods that contain several seeds. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread, it is often considered an invasive species in certain regions.
