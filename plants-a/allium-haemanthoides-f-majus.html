Allium haemanthoides f. majus

Allium haemanthoides f. majus, commonly known as the giant rain lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features broad, waxy, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers that resemble lilies, with six petals and a distinctive, trumpet-shaped corona. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This ornamental plant is valued for its dramatic appearance and ability to bloom reliably in the summer months.
