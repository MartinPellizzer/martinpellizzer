Albizia westerhuisii

Albizia westerhuisii, commonly known as the Westerhuis's silk tree, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, greyish bark and produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry savannahs and woodland areas.

Albizia westerhuisii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some local communities.
