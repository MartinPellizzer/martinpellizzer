Aster richardsonii var. giganteus

Aster richardsonii var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant blue mistflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
