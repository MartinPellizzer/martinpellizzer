Aechmea weberbaueri

Aechmea weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to South America, specifically Colombia.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure, which forms a central cup that collects water and nutrients. The leaves are typically broad, grass-like, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention. The plant produces an inflorescence that bears bright red bracts and small white flowers, adding to its ornamental value.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.

Morphology

Aechmea weberbaueri has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, stiff, and arranged in a spiral around the central stem, with a bluish-green color and a waxy coating.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that grows upward from the base of the plant. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red or orange, and bloom during the dry season, pollinated by wind or insects. The plant produces berries that contain small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by birds or through water.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in the soil, with some rhizomes helping in vegetative propagation.
