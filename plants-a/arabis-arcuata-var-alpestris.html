Arabis arcuata var. alpestris

Arabis arcuata var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it a common sight in high-altitude ecosystems.
