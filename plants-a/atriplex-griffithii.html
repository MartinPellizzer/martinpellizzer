Atriplex griffithii

Atriplex griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of coastal and inland saltbush communities.

Its ability to tolerate extreme conditions makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
