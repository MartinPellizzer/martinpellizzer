Amaryllis hallii

Amaryllis hallii, commonly known as the Hall's amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, white, and pink. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and arching, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Amaryllis hallii is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display and is a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, tropical blooms.
