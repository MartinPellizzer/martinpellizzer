Aloysia ligustrina var. schulziae

Aloysia ligustrina var. schulziae, commonly known as the Schultze's verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
