Aphania longipes

Aphania longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed aphania, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Aphania longipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
