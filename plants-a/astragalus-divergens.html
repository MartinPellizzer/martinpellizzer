Astragalus divergens

Astragalus divergens, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus divergens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
