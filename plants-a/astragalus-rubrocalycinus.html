Astragalus rubrocalycinus

Astragalus rubrocalycinus, commonly known as the red-calyx milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
