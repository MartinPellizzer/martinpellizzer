Artocarpus ficifolius

Artocarpus ficifolius, commonly known as the "fig-leaf tree," is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. Its fruit, similar to a fig, is typically small and covered in a dense, hairy coating.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
