Acalypha peruviana

Acalypha peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian beauty or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Acalypha peruviana is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
