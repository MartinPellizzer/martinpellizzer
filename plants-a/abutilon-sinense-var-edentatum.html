Abutilon sinense var. edentatum

Abutilon sinense var. edentatum, commonly known as Chinese velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a smooth margin and a hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
