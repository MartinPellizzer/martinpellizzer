Aster trichocarpus

Aster trichocarpus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flowers are showy, with blue to violet petals, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Aster trichocarpus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and wet meadows.
