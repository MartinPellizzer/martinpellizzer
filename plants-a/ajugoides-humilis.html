Ajugoides humilis

Ajugoides humilis, commonly known as the dwarf ajuga, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in the summer. Ajugoides humilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover.
