Agave rigida var. longifolia

Agave rigida var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous small, white flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
