Astragalus nezaketiae

Astragalus nezaketiae, commonly known as Nezaketia milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Astragalus nezaketiae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
