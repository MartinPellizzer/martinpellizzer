Amarella tortuosa

Amarella tortuosa, commonly known as the spiral amarella, is a rare and intriguing plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral growth pattern, where its stems twist in a helical manner, creating an unusual visual effect. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

Amarella tortuosa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
