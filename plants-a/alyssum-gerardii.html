Alyssum gerardii

Alyssum gerardii, commonly known as winter alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically blooms from late autumn to early spring, making it a valuable addition to winter gardens. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with fuzzy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Alyssum gerardii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
