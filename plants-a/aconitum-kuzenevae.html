Aconitum kuzenevae

Aconitum kuzenevae, commonly known as Kuzenev's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a robust rhizome system, which helps it survive in its harsh alpine environment. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed segments, and it produces toxic alkaloids, making it highly poisonous to both animals and humans.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
