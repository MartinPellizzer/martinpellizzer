Actinodaphne oleifolia

Actinodaphne oleifolia, commonly known as the oil-leaf laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its aromatic leaves and fruits have been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cultures.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
