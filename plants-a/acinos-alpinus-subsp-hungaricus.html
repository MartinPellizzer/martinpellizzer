Acinos alpinus subsp. hungaricus

Acinos alpinus subsp.

hungaricus, commonly known as the Hungarian mountain cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
