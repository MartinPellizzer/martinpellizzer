Agapetes chapaensis var. oblonga

Agapetes chapaensis var. oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf silver fir, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and silvery white underneath. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in high-altitude cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
