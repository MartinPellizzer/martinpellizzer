Arundinaria chino

Arundinaria chino, commonly known as Chinese bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slight sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Arundinaria chino is valued for its durability and has been used historically in construction and basket-making.
