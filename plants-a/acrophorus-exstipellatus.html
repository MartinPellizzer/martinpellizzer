Acrophorus exstipellatus

Acrophorus exstipellatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acanthus, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which are typically arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
