Adiantum amplum var. brasiliense

Adiantum amplum var. brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian maidenhair fern, is a strikingly ornamental fern native to South America.

It features delicate, lace-like fronds with a distinctive bi-pinnate structure, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense colonies over time.

This variety is prized for its elegant foliage and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
