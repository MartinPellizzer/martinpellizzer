Aspalathus bodkinii

Aspalathus bodkinii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small, woody pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and fynbos vegetation.

Aspalathus bodkinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
