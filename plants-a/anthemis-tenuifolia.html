Anthemis tenuifolia

Anthemis tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is known for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils and is used in herbal medicine.

Anthemis tenuifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
