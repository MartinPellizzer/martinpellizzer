Astragalus prusianus

Astragalus prusianus, commonly known as Prussian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus prusianus is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
