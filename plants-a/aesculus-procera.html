Aesculus procera

Aesculus procera, commonly known as the Mexican horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the soapberry family, Sapindaceae, and is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves. The tree produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in upright clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing several chestnut-like seeds.

Aesculus procera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
