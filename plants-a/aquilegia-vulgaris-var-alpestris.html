Aquilegia vulgaris var. alpestris

Aquilegia vulgaris var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue or purple spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for alpine gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
