Acacia salicina var. minor

Acacia salicina var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The variety 'minor' is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Acacia salicina.
