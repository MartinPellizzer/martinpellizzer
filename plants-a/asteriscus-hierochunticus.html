Asteriscus hierochunticus

Asteriscus hierochunticus, commonly known as the Sinai asterisk, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It features small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple, typically during the summer months. The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
