Amomum album

Amomum album, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences.

The rhizomes of Amomum album are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
