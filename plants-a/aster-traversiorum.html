Aster traversiorum

Aster traversiorum, commonly known as the Eastern aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall.

Aster traversiorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
