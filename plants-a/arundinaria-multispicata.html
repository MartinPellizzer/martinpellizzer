Arundinaria multispicata

Arundinaria multispicata, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or giant bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can spread rapidly, making it an aggressive colonizer in suitable habitats.

Arundinaria multispicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
