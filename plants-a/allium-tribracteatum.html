Allium tribracteatum

Allium tribracteatum, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, dry habitats such as mountain slopes and meadows. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium tribracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
