Artemisia sacrorum var. minor

Artemisia sacrorum var. minor, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, contributing to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a component in herbal remedies.
