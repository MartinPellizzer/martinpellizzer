Amaranthus scleranthoides f. albemarlensis

Amaranthus scleranthoides f. albemarlensis, commonly known as the whiteleaf amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth species characterized by its distinctive white or pale green leaves.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in moist, sandy soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from green to red in color. Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in sustainable landscaping.
