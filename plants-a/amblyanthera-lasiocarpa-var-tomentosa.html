Amblyanthera lasiocarpa var. tomentosa

Amblyanthera lasiocarpa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly amblyanthera, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and covered in a fine, soft pubescence. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental value.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
