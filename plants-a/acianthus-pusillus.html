Acianthus pusillus

Acianthus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf acianthus, is a small, delicate plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structures. The plant typically features a short, hairy stem with a few narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white or pale green, often clustered in a spike, and are pollinated by insects.

Acianthus pusillus thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.
