Astracantha echinus

Astracantha echinus, commonly known as the spiny hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiny calyces that enclose its showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, bloom during the warmer months.

Astracantha echinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
