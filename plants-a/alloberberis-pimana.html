Alloberberis pimana

Alloberberis pimana, commonly known as the Piman barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Alloberberis pimana plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and small mammals.
