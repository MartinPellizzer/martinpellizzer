Acer negundo f. lutescens

Acer negundo f. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-leaf boxeldar, is a variant of the boxeldar tree characterized by its distinctive yellow foliage.

This plant is a deciduous tree native to North America, typically found in wetland and riparian habitats. It features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value. The tree produces small, red samaras that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to wetland restoration projects and ornamental landscaping.
