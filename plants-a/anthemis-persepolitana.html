Anthemis persepolitana

Anthemis persepolitana, commonly known as the Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny conditions. Its foliage is aromatic and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
