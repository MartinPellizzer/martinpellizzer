Arisarum latifolium

Arisarum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf arum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a distinctive inflorescence with a spadix surrounded by a showy, palmate spathe. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The flowers are typically greenish-white and bloom in early spring.
