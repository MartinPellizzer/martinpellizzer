Asparagus warneckei

Asparagus warneckei, commonly known as the Warneckei asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, arching clusters of spiny, thorn-like stems that form a compact, bushy structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
