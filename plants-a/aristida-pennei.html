Aristida pennei

Aristida pennei, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically clustered in dense, fluffy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Aristida pennei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and as a source of fiber in some regions.
