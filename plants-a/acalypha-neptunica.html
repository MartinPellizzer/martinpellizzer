Acalypha neptunica

Acalypha neptunica, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss.

Acalypha neptunica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
