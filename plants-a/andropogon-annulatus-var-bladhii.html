Andropogon annulatus var. bladhii

Andropogon annulatus var. bladhii, commonly known as Bladh's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
