Anemone millefolium

Anemone millefolium, commonly known as the common wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features delicate, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often described as resembling a thousand leaves, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in forest understories.

Anemone millefolium is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
