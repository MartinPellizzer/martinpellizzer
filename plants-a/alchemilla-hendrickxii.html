Alchemilla hendrickxii

Alchemilla hendrickxii, commonly known as the Hendrickson's lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Alchemilla hendrickxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
