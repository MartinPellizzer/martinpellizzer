Astragalus caricinus

Astragalus caricinus, commonly known as the thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its root system is fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
