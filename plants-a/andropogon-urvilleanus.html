Andropogon urvilleanus

Andropogon urvilleanus, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
