Acacia polyphylla var. rhytidocarpa

Acacia polyphylla var. rhytidocarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow globular flowers arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its woody pods are rough and ridged, containing flattened, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often used for soil stabilization and as a source of browse for livestock.
