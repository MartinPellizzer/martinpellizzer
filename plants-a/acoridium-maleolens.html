Acoridium maleolens

Acoridium maleolens, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It has broad, waxy leaves that float on the surface of freshwater bodies. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is characterized by its simple, linear leaves and its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving water.
