Acer × conspicuum

Acer × conspicuum, commonly known as the red maple, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Acer rubrum and Acer triste.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the autumn, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are typically palmately lobed with five to seven pointed lobes, and they turn brilliant shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different climates, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.

The tree produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread.
