Asterophyllum

Asterophyllum, commonly known as the star fern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a radiating pattern. These plants are typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Asterophyllum species are often perennial and have a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing them to spread and form dense colonies.

Their foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy structures, contributing to their unique appearance.
