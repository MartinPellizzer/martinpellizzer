Acalypha caturus f. angustifolia

Acalypha caturus f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf copperleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.
