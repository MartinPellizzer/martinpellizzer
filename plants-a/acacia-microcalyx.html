Acacia microcalyx

Acacia microcalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, adapting to arid environments.

Acacia microcalyx is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
