Actinidia callosa var. strigillosa

Actinidia callosa var. strigillosa, commonly known as the hairy actinidia or the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often waxy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces edible, yellowish-orange berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
