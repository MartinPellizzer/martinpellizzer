Acaena adenocarpa var. tridentata

Acaena adenocarpa var. tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed burrplant, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by the distinctive, three-lobed burrs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
