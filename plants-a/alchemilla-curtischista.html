Alchemilla curtischista

Alchemilla curtischista, commonly known as the Armenian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in compact inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
