Alcea setosa

Alcea setosa, commonly known as the fuzzy hollyhock, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has feathery, soft hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. Alcea setosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun.

It is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
