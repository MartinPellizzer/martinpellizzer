Aronia arbutifolia var. nigra

Aronia arbutifolia var. nigra, commonly known as black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy foliage and clusters of small, red berries that turn black when mature. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by its edible, tart berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
