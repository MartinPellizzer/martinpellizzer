Alyxia ruscifolia var. pugioniformis

Alyxia ruscifolia var. pugioniformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coralwood, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
