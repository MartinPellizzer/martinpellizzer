Amorphophallus sativus

Amorphophallus sativus, commonly known as the elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, underground tuberous roots that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces a tall, leafy stalk with large, palm-like leaves during its growing season. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the starchy tubers used in local cuisines.

The plant is also known for its unique and strong odor, which is released when it blooms.
