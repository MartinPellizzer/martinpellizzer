Aspalathus sanguinea

Aspalathus sanguinea, commonly known as red bushwillow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Aspalathus sanguinea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
