Arenaria alpamarcae

Arenaria alpamarcae, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high alpine regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
