Allotria

It refers to structures that resemble petals but are not derived from the floral buds; instead, they are modified leaves or bracts. These structures often serve to attract pollinators or enhance the visual appeal of the flower. Allotria can be found in species such as *Calyptrotus grandiflorus*, where they are arranged in a showy, petal-like manner.
