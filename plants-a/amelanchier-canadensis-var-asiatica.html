Amelanchier canadensis var. asiatica

Amelanchier canadensis var. asiatica, commonly known as the Asian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters and features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark blue to black berries in late summer. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, making it a valuable ornamental plant.

The berries are also used in traditional medicine and can be consumed fresh or processed into jams and wines.
