Aechmea fulgoramosa

Aechmea fulgoramosa, commonly known as the fiery torch bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It features a rosette of broad, dark green leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in a tall inflorescence, adding dramatic visual contrast. Its foliage is often adorned with striking red and purple markings, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

Aechmea fulgoramosa thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea fulgoramosa has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy, bluish-green color and a dark green underside.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, thickened base covered in a fibrous, light brown bark. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small, white to yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a capsule that releases seeds through wind dispersal, and the plant has a fibrous root system adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.
