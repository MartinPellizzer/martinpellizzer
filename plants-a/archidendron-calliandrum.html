Archidendron calliandrum

Archidendron calliandrum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a flat, woody pod that contains several seeds.

Archidendron calliandrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
