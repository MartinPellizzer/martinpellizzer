Arabis longisiliqua

Arabis longisiliqua, commonly known as the long-stalked rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. It has basal leaves that are elongated and hairy, with a flowering period that occurs in late spring to early summer.

Arabis longisiliqua is often found in open, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
