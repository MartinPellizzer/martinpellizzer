Auganthus cortusoides

Auganthus cortusoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight curl, and the plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
