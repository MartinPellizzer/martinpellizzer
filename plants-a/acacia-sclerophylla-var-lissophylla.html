Acacia sclerophylla var. lissophylla

Acacia sclerophylla var. lissophylla, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, silky white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
