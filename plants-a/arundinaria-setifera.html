Arundinaria setifera

Arundinaria setifera, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Arundinaria setifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
