Acacia mollicoma

Acacia mollicoma, commonly known as silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and it has small, stipitate stipules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and rocky habitats.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
