Astragalus lackschewitzii

Astragalus lackschewitzii, commonly known as the Lackshewitz's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh conditions.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
