Agropyron barbicallum

Agropyron barbicallum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arctic regions.

Agropyron barbicallum is valued for its hardiness and is used in ecological restoration and as a forage crop in some areas.
