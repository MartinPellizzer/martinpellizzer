Arabis moschata

Arabis moschata, commonly known as musk arabis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are rounded and hairy, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
