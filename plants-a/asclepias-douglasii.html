Asclepias douglasii

Asclepias douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its showy, orange and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats.

Douglas's milkweed is an important host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting its life cycle.
