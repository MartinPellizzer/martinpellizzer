Agrostis makoniensis

Agrostis makoniensis, commonly known as Makoni grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
