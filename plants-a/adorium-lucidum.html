Adorium lucidum

Adorium lucidum, commonly known as the crystal spire, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent leaves that refract light, giving it a shimmering appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that bloom only during the early morning hours. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is considered a subject of scientific interest and conservation efforts.
