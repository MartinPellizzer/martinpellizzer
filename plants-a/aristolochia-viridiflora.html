Aristolochia viridiflora

Aristolochia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
