Andropogon condensatus

Andropogon condensatus, commonly known as densegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Andropogon condensatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
