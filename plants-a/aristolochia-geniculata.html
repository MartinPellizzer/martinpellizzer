Aristolochia geniculata

Aristolochia geniculata, commonly known as the twisting arrowhead vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique, horseshoe-shaped flowers that are often pale green or yellow with a distinctive, twisted structure. The plant produces dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found climbing or trailing along forest floors.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
