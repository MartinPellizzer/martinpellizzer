Alchemilla illyrica

Alchemilla illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Alchemilla illyrica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
