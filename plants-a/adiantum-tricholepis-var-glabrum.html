Adiantum tricholepis var. glabrum

Adiantum tricholepis var. glabrum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds.

It is native to South America and thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant's fronds are typically dark green and covered with a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It reproduces via spores and is often found in rocky or clay-rich soils.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
