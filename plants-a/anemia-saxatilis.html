Anemia saxatilis

Anemia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are usually unbranched.

Anemia saxatilis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh, sunny conditions.
