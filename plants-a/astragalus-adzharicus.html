Astragalus adzharicus

Astragalus adzharicus, commonly known as the Caucasian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptations to harsh conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus adzharicus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
