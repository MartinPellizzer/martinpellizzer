Aster curvatus

Aster curvatus, commonly known as the curlyleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a curled or twisted appearance, giving it its common name.

It produces purple to blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall.
