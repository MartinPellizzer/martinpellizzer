Acer lobelii f. horticola

Acer lobelii f. horticola, commonly known as the hybrid maple, is a cultivar of the maple genus characterized by its ornamental foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a vibrant green color that turns red or yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes, enhancing its appeal in horticultural settings.
