Atriplex fruticulosa

Atriplex fruticulosa, commonly known as shadscale, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well adapted to saline soils and coastal areas.

Atriplex fruticulosa plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
