Abutilon vulcanicola

Abutilon vulcanicola, commonly known as the volcanic abutilon, is a unique species native to the volcanic regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils and is adapted to the high temperatures and low humidity of its native habitat. It is often found growing in rocky or disturbed areas near volcanic formations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
