Alaticaulia cinnamomea

Alaticaulia cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented bark and dark, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that give way to oval, reddish-brown fruits. Its wood is valued for its durability and is often used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant has cultural and economic significance in regions where it is cultivated.
