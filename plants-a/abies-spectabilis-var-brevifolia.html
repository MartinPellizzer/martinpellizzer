Abies spectabilis var. brevifolia

Abies spectabilis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf white fir, is a variant of the white fir tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter, more densely packed needles compared to the typical white fir. The plant produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it thrives in cooler, moist environments.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
