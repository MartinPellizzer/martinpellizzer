Acianthera freyi

Acianthera freyi, commonly known as the Freyi's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, and its roots are often tuberous, which helps it store water and nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
