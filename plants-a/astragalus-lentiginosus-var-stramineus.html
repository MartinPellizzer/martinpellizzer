Astragalus lentiginosus var. stramineus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. stramineus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
