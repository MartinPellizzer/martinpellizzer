Amygdalus persica subsp. nucipersica

Amygdalus persica subsp.

nucipersica, commonly known as the flatpea almond, is a subspecies of the peach tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, flat, pea-like fruits that are typically harvested when dry.

The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in spring, and its leaves are simple and alternate. The fruit contains a single hard seed, which is the primary part used for propagation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
