Allium roseum var. tourneuxii

Allium roseum var. tourneuxii, commonly known as the Tourneux's ornamental onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
