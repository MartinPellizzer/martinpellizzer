Aesculus × du-pontii

Aesculus × du-pontii, commonly known as the Dutch horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Netherlands.

It is a hybrid between Aesculus hippocastanum and Aesculus floribunda, resulting in a plant with distinctive botanical features. The tree produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers in panicles, which bloom in late spring. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of 5–7 ovate leaflets, and it produces spiny capsules containing shiny, amber-brown seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes.
