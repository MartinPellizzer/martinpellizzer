Acer laevigatum var. fargesii

Acer laevigatum var. fargesii, commonly known as the Farges maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental tree in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
