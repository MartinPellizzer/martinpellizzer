Artemisia freyniana

Artemisia freyniana, commonly known as Mexican wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert and semi-desert regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other aromatic herbs and flowering plants.

Artemisia freyniana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
