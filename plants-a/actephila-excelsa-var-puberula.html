Actephila excelsa var. puberula

Actephila excelsa var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy giant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, cylindrical spike of small, three-lobed flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a landscape plant.
