Achnatherum lobatum

Achnatherum lobatum, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and disturbed sites. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Achnatherum lobatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
