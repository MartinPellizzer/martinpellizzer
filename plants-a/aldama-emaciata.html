Aldama emaciata

Aldama emaciata, commonly known as the slender Aldama, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions of southern Mexico.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
