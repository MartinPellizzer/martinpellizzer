Allionia foliosa

Allionia foliosa, commonly known as the silverleaf allionia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The botanical features of Allionia foliosa make it an attractive ornamental plant in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
