Acmispon americanus var. americanus

Acmispon americanus var. americanus, commonly known as the plains false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in racemes during the spring and early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a taproot system that helps it survive in various soil conditions.
