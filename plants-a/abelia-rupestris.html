Abelia rupestris

Abelia rupestris, commonly known as the rock abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems and small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland edges.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
