Adenothola bicolor

Adenothola bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly contrasting floral colors, typically featuring a white or pale pink base with a deep purple or red upper lip. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shaded forest environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, adapted to capture limited sunlight in its natural habitat.

Adenothola bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
