Alloplectus medusaeus

Alloplectus medusaeus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent petals that resemble delicate, floating structures, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, often in clusters along its slender, creeping stems. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Alloplectus medusaeus is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its habitat.
