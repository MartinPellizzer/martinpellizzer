Artocarpus altilis var. seminifer

Artocarpus altilis var. seminifer, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. The plant produces large, green, spiky fruits that turn yellow or orange when ripe, containing a starchy, edible pulp and numerous seeds. Its leaves are large, alternate, and palmately lobed, often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is cultivated for its nutritious fruit and has cultural significance in many Pacific island communities.
