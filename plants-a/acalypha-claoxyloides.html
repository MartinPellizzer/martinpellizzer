Acalypha claoxyloides

Acalypha claoxyloides, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, ornamental leaves that display vivid red veins against a deep green background. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Acalypha claoxyloides is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
