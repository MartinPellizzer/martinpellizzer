Abutilon parishii

Abutilon parishii, commonly known as the golden abutilon, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, hot climates.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as a low-maintenance ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
