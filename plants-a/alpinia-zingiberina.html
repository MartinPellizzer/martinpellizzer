Alpinia zingiberina

Alpinia zingiberina, commonly known as the ginger orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Alpinia zingiberina is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for its potential medicinal properties.
