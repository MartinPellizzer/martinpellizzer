Atriplex amana

Atriplex amana, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it thrive in saline environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its robust root system allows it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh landscapes.

Atriplex amana is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicine.
