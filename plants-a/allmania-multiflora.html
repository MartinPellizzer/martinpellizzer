Allmania multiflora

Allmania multiflora, commonly known as the Florida allamanda, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetland environments, such as swamps and marshes. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Allmania multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
