Actinocarpus

Actinocarpus, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, often yellow or white flowers. The plants typically grow in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to dry conditions with their deep root systems. Actinocarpus species are known for their ability to fix nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.

Some species are used in traditional medicine and as sources of timber or fodder.
