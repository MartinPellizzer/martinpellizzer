Amaranthus melancholicus

Amaranthus melancholicus, commonly known as the weedy amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into three-angled, bead-like seed capsules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Amaranthus melancholicus is a hardy annual that can grow in a variety of climates and soil types.
