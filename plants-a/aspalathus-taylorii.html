Aspalathus taylorii

Aspalathus taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has small, trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
