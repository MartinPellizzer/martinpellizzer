Abies brachyphylla var. umbellata

Abies brachyphylla var. umbellata, commonly known as the Alaska fir, is a variant of the brachyphylla fir, native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that is often adorned with a distinctive white or silver underside. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to harsh alpine conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
