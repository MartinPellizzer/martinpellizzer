Agave victoriae-reginae subsp. swobodae

Agave victoriae-reginae subsp.

swobodae, commonly known as the Queen Victoria agave, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, spire-like growth habit and large, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces dramatic, yellowish-white inflorescences that can reach several meters in height, making it a striking ornamental plant. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and collections.
