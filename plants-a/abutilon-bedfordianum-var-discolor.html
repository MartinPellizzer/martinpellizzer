Abutilon bedfordianum var. discolor

Abutilon bedfordianum var. discolor, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
