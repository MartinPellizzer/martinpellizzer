Aidia ochroleuca

Aidia ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
