Alectorolophus praesignis

Alectorolophus praesignis, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive star-shaped structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a vibrant yellow hue with intricate patterns.

Due to habitat loss, this species is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
