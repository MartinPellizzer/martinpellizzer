Anemone juzepczukii

Anemone juzepczukii, commonly known as the Polish anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy and grow on slender, upright stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Anemone juzepczukii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
