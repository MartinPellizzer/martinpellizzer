Asparagus simulans

Asparagus simulans, commonly known as the dwarf asparagus or the silver asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, scale-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
