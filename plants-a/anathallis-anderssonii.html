Anathallis anderssonii

Anathallis anderssonii, commonly known as the Andes spider orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the cloud forests of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, intricate flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions with dappled sunlight. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive, elongated spur that serves as a nectar reservoir.

Anathallis anderssonii is a subject of interest for botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
