Aster spatelliformis

Aster spatelliformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, producing blue to purple petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
