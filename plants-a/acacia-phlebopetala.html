Acacia phlebopetala

Acacia phlebopetala, commonly known as the white-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, pale-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seed pods are long and slender, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acacia phlebopetala is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
