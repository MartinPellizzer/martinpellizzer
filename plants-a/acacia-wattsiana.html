Acacia wattsiana

Acacia wattsiana, commonly known as the watt's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures in mature plants. The plant produces yellow to golden-green flowers in compact, globular heads, which are typically borne in clusters along the stems. It is well adapted to dry environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Acacia wattsiana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor areas.
