Arabis auriculata var. puberula

Arabis auriculata var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of fleshy, pubescent leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in garden settings.
