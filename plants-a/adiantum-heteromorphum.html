Adiantum heteromorphum

Adiantum heteromorphum, commonly known as the variable maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the rachis. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or wooded areas. Its leaves are usually bright green, with a glossy appearance, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum heteromorphum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
