Anthriscus cerefolium var. vulgaris

Anthriscus cerefolium var. vulgaris, commonly known as cow parsley, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and umbel-shaped clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are edible and have been used in culinary and medicinal applications throughout history.
