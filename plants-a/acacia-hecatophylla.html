Acacia hecatophylla

Acacia hecatophylla, commonly known as the ghost acacia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia hecatophylla is often found in sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to its native habitat.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its ecosystem.
