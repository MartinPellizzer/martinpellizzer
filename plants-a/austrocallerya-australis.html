Austrocallerya australis

Austrocallerya australis, commonly known as the Sydney ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spring, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Austrocallerya australis is often used in urban landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
