Apicra × rigida

Apicra × rigida, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species.

It typically features thorny branches and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, reddish-pink berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. Apicra × rigida is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical traits make it a popular choice for hedgerows and garden landscapes.
