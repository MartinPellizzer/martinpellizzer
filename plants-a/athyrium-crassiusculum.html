Athyrium crassiusculum

Athyrium crassiusculum, commonly known as the broad-leafed wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It features a tall, upright rhizome with fronds that are broadly ovate to triangular in shape. The fronds are typically divided into multiple pinnae, which are themselves often divided into smaller pinnules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in horticultural settings.
