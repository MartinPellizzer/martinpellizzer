Alchemilla pycnotricha

Alchemilla pycnotricha, commonly known as the mossy lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Alchemilla pycnotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
