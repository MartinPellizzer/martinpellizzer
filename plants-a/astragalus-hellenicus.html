Astragalus hellenicus

Astragalus hellenicus, commonly known as the Greek milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Greece and the Balkans.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Astragalus hellenicus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid regions.
