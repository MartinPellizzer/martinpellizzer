Aster surculosus var. gracilis

Aster surculosus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is often found in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are slender and upright.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
