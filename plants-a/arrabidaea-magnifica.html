Arrabidaea magnifica

Arrabidaea magnifica, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a striking plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces intricate, fragrant blooms in shades of yellow and orange, often with dark purple or brown markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
