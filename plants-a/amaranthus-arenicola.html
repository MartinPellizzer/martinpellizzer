Amaranthus arenicola

Amaranthus arenicola, commonly known as the sand amaranth, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red in color. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.

Amaranthus arenicola is often used as a forage crop for livestock due to its high nutritional value.
