Acaena macrostemon subsp. pachystigma

Acaena macrostemon subsp.

pachystigma, commonly known as the woolly burrplant, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for native birds.

The plant has a creeping growth habit and thrives in coastal and subalpine environments. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
