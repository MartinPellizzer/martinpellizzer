Alocasia crassinervia

Alocasia crassinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, broad veins that give it its common name. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. Alocasia crassinervia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
