Arundo glauca var. angustifolia

Arundo glauca var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf giant reed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height, with narrow, blue-green leaves that are often coated with a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers in late summer. It thrives in wet or riparian environments and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.

Its rapid growth and dense foliage make it an effective erosion control plant.
