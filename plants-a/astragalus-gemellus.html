Astragalus gemellus

Astragalus gemellus, commonly known as the twinleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus gemellus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
