Aspidium diastematocarpon

Aspidium diastematocarpon, commonly known as the narrow-veined water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that are typically divided into smaller segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

This fern is notable for its intricate leaf structure and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
