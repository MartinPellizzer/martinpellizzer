Ammannia pinnatifida

Ammannia pinnatifida, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Ammannia pinnatifida is an important species for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
