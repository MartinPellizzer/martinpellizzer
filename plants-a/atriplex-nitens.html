Atriplex nitens

Atriplex nitens, commonly known as the silverleaf shadscale, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, salt-excreting glands, aiding in its adaptation to saline soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, allowing it to store water efficiently in harsh environments.

Atriplex nitens is often found in desert scrub and coastal habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
