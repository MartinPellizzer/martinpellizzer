Arctostaphylos attenuata var. brachymischa

Arctostaphylos attenuata var. brachymischa, commonly known as the dwarf manzanita, is a shrub native to California and parts of Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
