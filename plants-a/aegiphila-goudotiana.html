Aegiphila goudotiana

Aegiphila goudotiana, commonly known as the Brazilian mountain sage, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of purple or violet. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Aegiphila goudotiana is often found in rocky or well-drained soils at higher elevations.
