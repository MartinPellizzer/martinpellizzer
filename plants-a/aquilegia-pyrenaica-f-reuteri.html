Aquilegia pyrenaica f. reuteri

Aquilegia pyrenaica f. reuteri, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a botanical variant of the common columbine.

It is characterized by its striking blue and purple double flowers, which are typically borne on slender, branched stems. This subspecies is native to the Pyrenees Mountains and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, typical of many members of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
