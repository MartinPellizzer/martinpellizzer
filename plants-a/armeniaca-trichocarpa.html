Armeniaca trichocarpa

Armeniaca trichocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, reddish-brown fruits. The fruit is rich in sugars, vitamins, and antioxidants, making it a valuable crop.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional benefits and is also used in traditional medicine.
