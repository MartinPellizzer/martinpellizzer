Astragalus zemeraniensis

Astragalus zemeraniensis, commonly known as the Zemariana milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
