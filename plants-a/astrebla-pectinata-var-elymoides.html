Astrebla pectinata var. elymoides

Astrebla pectinata var. elymoides, commonly known as the red grass or red wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and reddish-brown foliage, which becomes more pronounced during dry seasons. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its seeds are small and hard, often requiring scarification for successful germination.

Astrebla pectinata var. elymoides is an important forage plant for livestock in drought-prone areas.
