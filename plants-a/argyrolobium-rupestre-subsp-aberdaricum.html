Argyrolobium rupestre subsp. aberdaricum

Argyrolobium rupestre subsp.

aberdaricum, commonly known as the Aberdare rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Aberdare Range in Kenya. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, lobed leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow blooms during the dry season.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
