Alopecurus brachystachyus

Alopecurus brachystachyus, commonly known as mouse barley, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Alopecurus brachystachyus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
