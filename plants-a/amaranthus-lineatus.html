Amaranthus lineatus

Amaranthus lineatus, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental or for its edible leaves.

Amaranthus lineatus is also valued for its ability to grow in poor soils and its resilience to drought conditions.
