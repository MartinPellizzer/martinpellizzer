Astragalus bagramiensis

Astragalus bagramiensis, commonly known as the Bagramian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Afghanistan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, compound leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Astragalus bagramiensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
