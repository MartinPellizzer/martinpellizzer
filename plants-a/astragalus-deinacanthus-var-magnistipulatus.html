Astragalus deinacanthus var. magnistipulatus

Astragalus deinacanthus var. magnistipulatus, commonly known as the large-stippled milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves with elongated, stipitate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
