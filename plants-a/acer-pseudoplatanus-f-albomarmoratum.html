Acer pseudoplatanus f. albomarmoratum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. albomarmoratum, commonly known as the white-marbled maple, is a unique variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its striking white and green mottled leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. This form exhibits a variegated leaf pattern due to a genetic mutation affecting chlorophyll production. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
