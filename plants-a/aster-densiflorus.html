Aster densiflorus

Aster densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Aster densiflorus plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during its flowering period.
