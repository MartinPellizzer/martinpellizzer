Arthraxon hispidus var. monticola

Arthraxon hispidus var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variant is notable for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
