Abutilon harmsianum

Abutilon harmsianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. Its fibrous roots and hairy stems contribute to its adaptability in various soil types.

Abutilon harmsianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
