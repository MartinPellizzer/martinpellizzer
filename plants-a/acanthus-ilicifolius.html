Acanthus ilicifolius

Acanthus ilicifolius, commonly known as the holly-leaved acanthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble holly leaves, with a distinctive lobed structure and a rough, textured surface. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its historical use in classical art and architecture further highlights its cultural significance.

Native Distribution

Acanthus ilicifolius is primarily native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It is commonly found in rocky coastal areas, cliffs, and dry, sunny habitats. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often associated with maritime environments. It is particularly common in countries such as Spain, Portugal, Italy, and Turkey.

Due to its hardy nature, it has been introduced to other regions with similar climates.
