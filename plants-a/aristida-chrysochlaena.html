Aristida chrysochlaena

Aristida chrysochlaena, commonly known as golden beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Aristida chrysochlaena is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
