Apium laciniatum

Apium laciniatum, commonly known as narrowleaf parsley or wild celery, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of parsley. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, disturbed habitats.

Apium laciniatum is sometimes used in herbal medicine for its aromatic properties and mild digestive benefits.
