Astragalus chlorocarpus f. albiflorus

Astragalus chlorocarpus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The subspecies albiflorus is distinguished by its pale or white flowers, which contrast with the typical greenish-yellow blooms of the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

This plant plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal value.
