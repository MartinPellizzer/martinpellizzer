Ananas argentata

Ananas argentata, commonly known as the silver pineapple, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, overlapping scales that give it a metallic sheen. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, white flowers, followed by reddish-brown fruits that are edible but not as widely consumed as the common pineapple. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
