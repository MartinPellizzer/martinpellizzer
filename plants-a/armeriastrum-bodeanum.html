Armeriastrum bodeanum

Armeriastrum bodeanum, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.
