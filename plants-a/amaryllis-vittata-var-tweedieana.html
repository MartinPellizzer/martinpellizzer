Amaryllis vittata var. tweedieana

Amaryllis vittata var. tweedieana, commonly known as the Tweedieana Amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The foliage consists of strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and slightly waxy in texture. This species is well-suited for cultivation in warm climates and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

It is prized for its showy blooms and is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.
