Asplenium comosum

Asplenium comosum, commonly known as the horse's tail fern, is a species of evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered fronds that resemble a horse's tail, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in rocky or wooded areas. Its rhizomes are wiry and form the basis of its compact growth habit.

Asplenium comosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
