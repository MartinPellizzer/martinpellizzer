Acer nipponicum var. australe

Acer nipponicum var. australe, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that changes with the seasons, particularly displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically palmately lobed, with a smooth or serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of garden conditions.
