Athyrium wichurae

Athyrium wichurae, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It features fronds that are bipinnate, with a distinctive shield-shaped base. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are released from the sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium wichurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
