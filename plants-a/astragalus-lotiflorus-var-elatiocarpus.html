Astragalus lotiflorus var. elatiocarpus

Astragalus lotiflorus var. elatiocarpus, commonly known as the large-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Astragalus lotiflorus var. elatiocarpus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
