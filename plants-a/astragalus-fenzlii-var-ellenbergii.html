Astragalus fenzlii var. ellenbergii

Astragalus fenzlii var. ellenbergii, commonly known as the Ellenberg's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in steppe or semi-desert environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and the plant is known for its hardy and drought-resistant characteristics.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid regions and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
