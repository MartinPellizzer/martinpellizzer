Amelanchier canadensis subsp. confusa

Amelanchier canadensis subsp.

confusa, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red to black berries in the summer. Its botanical characteristics include smooth, gray bark and a generally rounded crown.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodlands.
