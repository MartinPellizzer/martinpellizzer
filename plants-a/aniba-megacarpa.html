Aniba megacarpa

Aniba megacarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or *Aniba megacarpa*, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain seeds, and its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.

Aniba megacarpa is also valued for its hard, dark wood, which has been historically used in the crafting of musical instruments.
