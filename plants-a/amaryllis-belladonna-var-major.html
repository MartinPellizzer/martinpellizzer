Amaryllis belladonna var. major

Amaryllis belladonna var. major, commonly known as the giant belladonna lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces thick, fleshy bulbs that store nutrients, allowing it to thrive in warm, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, often reaching several feet in length.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
