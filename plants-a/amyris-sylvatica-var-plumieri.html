Amyris sylvatica var. plumieri

Amyris sylvatica var. plumieri, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant fragrance and attractive foliage.
