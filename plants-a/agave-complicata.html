Agave complicata

Agave complicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and blue-gray in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Agave complicata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardiness.
