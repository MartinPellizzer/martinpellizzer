Anemonoides apennina

Anemonoides apennina, commonly known as the Italian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, single-layered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are basal and often covered in a white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Anemonoides apennina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
