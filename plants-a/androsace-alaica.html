Androsace alaica

Androsace alaica, commonly known as the Alpina snowbell, is a low-growing alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in early summer.

Androsace alaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
