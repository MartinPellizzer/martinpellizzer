Alaternus cordatus

Alaternus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf alaternus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Alaternus cordatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
