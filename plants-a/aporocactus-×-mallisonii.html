Aporocactus × mallisonii

Aporocactus × mallisonii, commonly known as the Mallison's ornate cactus, is a hybrid species originating from crosses between Aporocactus horridus and Aporocactus inermis.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that often bear distinctive, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and horticultural appeal, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
