Alyssum chalcidicum f. ellipticum

Alyssum chalcidicum f. ellipticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alyssum, is a compact, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
