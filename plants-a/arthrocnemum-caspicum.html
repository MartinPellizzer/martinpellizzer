Arthrocnemum caspicum

Arthrocnemum caspicum, commonly known as the Caspian knotweed, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats across the Middle East and Central Asia. Its leaves are often reduced to scales, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Arthrocnemum caspicum plays a role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments and is an important component of desert and coastal ecosystems.
