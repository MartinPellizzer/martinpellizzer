Asplenium laxifolium

Asplenium laxifolium, commonly known as the looseleaf spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often loosely arranged along the stems. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or forest floors. Its rhizomes are short and often form dense clusters, contributing to its ability to thrive in compact habitats.

The plant's fronds are usually dark green and may have a slightly serrated or toothed margin.
