Anthericum lukiense var. kionzoense

Anthericum lukiense var. kionzoense, commonly known as the Kionzo ginger, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Eastern Arc Mountains of Tanzania.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is notable for its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in terminal spikes, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
