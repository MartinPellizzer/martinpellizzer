Atriplex sinuata

Atriplex sinuata, commonly known as the smooth saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common component of desert ecosystems.

Atriplex sinuata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
