Arisaema huegelii

Arisaema huegelii, commonly known as the Huetzel's arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Arisaema huegelii is valued for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
