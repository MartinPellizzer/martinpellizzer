Astragalus lentiginosus var. bryantii

Astragalus lentiginosus var. bryantii, commonly known as the Bryant's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
