Aster prenanthoides var. porrectifolius

Aster prenanthoides var. porrectifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and are arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the late summer and early fall.
