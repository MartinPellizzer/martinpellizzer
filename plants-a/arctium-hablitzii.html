Arctium hablitzii

Arctium hablitzii, commonly known as the common burdock.

It is a biennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to brown flower heads that develop into spiny, bur-like fruits. Its roots are thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine.

Arctium hablitzii is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.
