Aulomyrcia curatellifolia var. australis

Aulomyrcia curatellifolia var. australis, commonly known as the Australian broad-leafed myrtle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a glossy surface and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It has a dense growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

This variant is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other varieties of the species.
