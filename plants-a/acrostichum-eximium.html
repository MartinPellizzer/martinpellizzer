Acrostichum eximium

Acrostichum eximium, commonly known as the giant reed or giant flat reed, is a tall, perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and often arching leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands in wetland or marshy environments.

Acrostichum eximium is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in moist areas.
