Angelonia veronicoides

Angelonia veronicoides, commonly known as the tropical angelonia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, which are typically borne in clusters along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

Angelonia veronicoides is also valued for its long blooming period, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking continuous color.
