Amaranthus mitchellii var. grandiflorus

Amaranthus mitchellii var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Mitchell's amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, bead-like seeds that are often used as a food source by birds and small mammals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
