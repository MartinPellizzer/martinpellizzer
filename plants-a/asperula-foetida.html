Asperula foetida

Asperula foetida, commonly known as stinking beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub that produces small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, unpleasant odor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Asperula foetida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
