Aristida scoparia

Aristida scoparia, commonly known as sand bluestem, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring or early summer. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its ecological significance makes it a valuable component of native plant communities in its habitat.
