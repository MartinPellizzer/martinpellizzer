Antirrhinum orcuttianum

Antirrhinum orcuttianum, commonly known as the Orcutt's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Antirrhinum orcuttianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
