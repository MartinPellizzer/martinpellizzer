Acacia pulchella var. glaberrima

Acacia pulchella var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The wood is hard and used locally for various purposes, including construction and fuel.
