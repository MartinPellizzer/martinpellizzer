Agoseris scorzonerifolia var. aspera

Agoseris scorzonerifolia var. aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf goldenroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Its basal leaves are lanceolate and often have a grayish-green hue with a pubescent surface.

The flowers are yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.
