Astragalus deserti-syriaci

Astragalus deserti-syriaci, commonly known as the Syrian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
