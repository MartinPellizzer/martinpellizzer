Acalypha lindeniana

Acalypha lindeniana, commonly known as the red-leaved acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves that contrast beautifully with its green stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Acalypha lindeniana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
