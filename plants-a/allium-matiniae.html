Allium matiniae

Allium matiniae, commonly known as the Matinias garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in taste to garlic but have a milder, more delicate flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
