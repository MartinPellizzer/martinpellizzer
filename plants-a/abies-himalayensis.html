Abies himalayensis

Abies himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green foliage, and needle-like leaves that are typically 1–2 cm long. The tree produces cones that are usually 5–8 cm in length, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often growing in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
