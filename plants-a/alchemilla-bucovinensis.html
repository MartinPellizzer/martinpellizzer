Alchemilla bucovinensis

Alchemilla bucovinensis, commonly known as the Bucovina lady's mantle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Alchemilla bucovinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
