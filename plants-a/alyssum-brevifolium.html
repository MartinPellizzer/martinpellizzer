Alyssum brevifolium

Alyssum brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically hairy in texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum brevifolium is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
