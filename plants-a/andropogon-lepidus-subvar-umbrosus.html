Andropogon lepidus subvar. umbrosus

Andropogon lepidus subvar. umbrosus, commonly known as the shadow bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a valuable component of understory vegetation.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
