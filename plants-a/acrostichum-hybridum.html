Acrostichum hybridum

Acrostichum hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid brake fern, is a perennial evergreen fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, compound leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
