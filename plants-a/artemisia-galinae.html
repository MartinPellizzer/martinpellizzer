Artemisia galinae

Artemisia galinae, commonly known as Galinae wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant is distinguished by its silvery-green, finely divided leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Artemisia galinae is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
