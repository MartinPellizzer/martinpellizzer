Artemisia martjanovii var. chakassica

Artemisia martjanovii var. chakassica, commonly known as Chakassian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. The plant has gray-green, aromatic foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer, contributing to its role in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its essential oils and has been used in various herbal remedies for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
