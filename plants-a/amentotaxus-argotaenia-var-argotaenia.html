Amentotaxus argotaenia var. argotaenia

Amentotaxus argotaenia var. argotaenia, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, rounded cones. The plant is known for its longevity, with some specimens living over 1,000 years. Its leaves are typically leathery and arranged in spirals along the stems.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its hardy nature in shaded environments.
