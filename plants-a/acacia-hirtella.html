Acacia hirtella

Acacia hirtella, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain seeds, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils. Acacia hirtella is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
