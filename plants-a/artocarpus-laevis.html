Artocarpus laevis

Artocarpus laevis, commonly known as the smoothbread or breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, pale green bark. The tree produces a fruit that is typically harvested when mature, though it can be consumed at various stages of development. The fruit is rich in carbohydrates and has been a staple food in many Pacific Island cultures.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
