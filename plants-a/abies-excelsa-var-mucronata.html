Abies excelsa var. mucronata

Abies excelsa var. mucronata, commonly known as the Colombian silver fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the Andes Mountains of Colombia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and silver-blue needle-like leaves. The foliage is dense and arranged in spirals, giving the tree a distinctive appearance. It produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers.

This species thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.
