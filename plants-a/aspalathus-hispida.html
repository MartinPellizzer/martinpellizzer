Aspalathus hispida

Aspalathus hispida, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

Aspalathus hispida is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
