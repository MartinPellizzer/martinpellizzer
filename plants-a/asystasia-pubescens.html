Asystasia pubescens

Asystasia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asystasia pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
