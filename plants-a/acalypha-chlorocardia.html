Acalypha chlorocardia

Acalypha chlorocardia, commonly known as the greenheart or silverleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically inconspicuous compared to its foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a landscape plant in warm climates.
