Acanthopanax senticosus var. subinermis

Acanthopanax senticosus var. subinermis, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng or Eleuthero, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its adaptogenic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
