Astragalus pakravaniae

Astragalus pakravaniae, commonly known as the Pakravan's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological studies due to its limited distribution and potential medicinal properties.
