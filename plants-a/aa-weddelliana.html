Aa weddelliana

Aa weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's acaulis, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
