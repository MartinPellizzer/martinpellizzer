Acer pseudoplatanus f. albertsii

Acer pseudoplatanus f. albertsii, commonly known as the Albert's maple, is a rare and cultivated form of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features broad, palmately lobed leaves with a vibrant green color. The plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained, fertile soils. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
