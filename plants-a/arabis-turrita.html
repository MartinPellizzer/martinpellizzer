Arabis turrita

Arabis turrita, commonly known as the mountain rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, typically growing in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and hairy, with small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes. It produces small, rounded seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Arabis turrita is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
