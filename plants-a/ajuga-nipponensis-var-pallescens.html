Ajuga nipponensis var. pallescens

Ajuga nipponensis var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale ajuga, is a low-growing perennial groundcover native to Japan.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a silvery-green color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in early to mid-summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
