Astragalus lorinserianus

Astragalus lorinserianus, commonly known as the Lorinser's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its deep penetration, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus lorinserianus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
