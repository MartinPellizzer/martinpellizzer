Asplenium laciniatum subsp. kukkonenii

Asplenium laciniatum subsp.

kukkonenii, commonly known as the Kukkonen's shield fern, is a fern species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific. It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage.

The fronds are typically lanceolate or ovate, with a serrated or lobed margin, and often exhibit a dark green to bronze coloration. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

The spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, which are arranged in sori covered by a thin indusium.
