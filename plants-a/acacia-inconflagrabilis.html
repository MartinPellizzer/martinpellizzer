Acacia inconflagrabilis

Acacia inconflagrabilis, commonly known as the flame acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames, hence its common name. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia inconflagrabilis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
