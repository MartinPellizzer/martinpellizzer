Achyrophorus hieracioides

Achyrophorus hieracioides, commonly known as the yellow false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is usually unbranched and somewhat hairy. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that disperse easily by wind.

The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
