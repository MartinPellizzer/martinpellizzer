Alyssum blancheanum

Alyssum blancheanum, commonly known as the White Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This species is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
