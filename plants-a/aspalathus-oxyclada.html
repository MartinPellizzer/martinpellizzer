Aspalathus oxyclada

Aspalathus oxyclada, commonly known as the sweet cicely, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a distinctive, licorice-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspalathus oxyclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
