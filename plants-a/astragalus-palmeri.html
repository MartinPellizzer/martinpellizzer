Astragalus palmeri

Astragalus palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with multiple leaflets arranged along the stem.

Astragalus palmeri is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
