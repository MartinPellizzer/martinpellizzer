Astragalus barboides

Astragalus barboides, commonly known as the barbed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus barboides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
