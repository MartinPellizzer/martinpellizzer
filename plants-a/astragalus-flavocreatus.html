Astragalus flavocreatus

Astragalus flavocreatus, commonly known as the yellow flaxvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus flavocreatus plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration efforts.
