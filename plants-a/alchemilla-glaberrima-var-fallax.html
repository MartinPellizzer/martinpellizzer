Alchemilla glaberrima var. fallax

Alchemilla glaberrima var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax alkanet, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early summer, typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental foliage and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
