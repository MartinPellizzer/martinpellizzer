Agrostis reinwardtii

Agrostis reinwardtii, commonly known as reinwardt's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Agrostis reinwardtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
