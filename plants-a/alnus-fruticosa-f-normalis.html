Alnus fruticosa f. normalis

Alnus fruticosa f. normalis, commonly known as the common alder.

It is a shrub or small tree belonging to the Betulaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and catkin flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils, often near streams or in wetlands. Its bark is smooth and gray, while its wood is light and soft.

It plays an important role in ecological succession and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
