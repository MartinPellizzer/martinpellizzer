Amorpha herbacea var. floridana

Amorpha herbacea var. floridana, commonly known as Florida false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring compound leaves with oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers in the spring, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for native gardens and erosion control.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular plant in regional horticulture.
