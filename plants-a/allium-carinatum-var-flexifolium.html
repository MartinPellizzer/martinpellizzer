Allium carinatum var. flexifolium

Allium carinatum var. flexifolium, commonly known as the curly leek, is a perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curled, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces tall, flowering stems that bear small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
