Acacia bracteolata

Acacia bracteolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and linear, and its yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and coastal areas.

Acacia bracteolata is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
