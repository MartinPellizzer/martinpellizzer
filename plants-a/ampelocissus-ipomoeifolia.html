Ampelocissus ipomoeifolia

Ampelocissus ipomoeifolia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by oval, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for trellises and hanging baskets.
