Avena magna

Avena magna, commonly known as the giant oat, is a tall grass species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and panicles of spikelets that produce large, fluffy seeds. The plant typically grows to heights of several meters, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and hollow stems, typical of the grass family.

Avena magna is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in forage and grain production.
