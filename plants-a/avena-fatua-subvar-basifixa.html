Avena fatua subvar. basifixa

Avena fatua subvar. basifixa, commonly known as the basal-fixed wild oat, is a variant of the wild oat species characterized by its unique growth habit.

It is a monocotyledonous plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae, and it typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to compete with cultivated crops.
