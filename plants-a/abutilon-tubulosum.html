Abutilon tubulosum

Abutilon tubulosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of lantern-shaped, orange to red bracts that resemble small lanterns. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often overlooked due to the showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to grow in both full sun and partial shade.
