Astragalus nucifer f. nuciferiformis

Astragalus nucifer f. nuciferiformis, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in rocky or sandy soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
