Andropogon fulvicomus var. approximatus

Andropogon fulvicomus var. approximatus, commonly known as the yellow bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves and tufted inflorescence that produces small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has a rhizomatous root system that aids in soil stabilization and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
