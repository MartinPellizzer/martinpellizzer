Anthurium macilentum

Anthurium macilentum, commonly known as the silver anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated spathes that can range in color from white to pale green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers inside the spathe, which are typically green or yellow. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves with a velvety texture and a distinct silvery sheen.

Anthurium macilentum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
