Arundinaria tomikusensis

Arundinaria tomikusensis, commonly known as the Tomikus bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, which are typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

Arundinaria tomikusensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
