Apium kalbreyeri

Apium kalbreyeri, commonly known as the Kalbreyer's celery, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided leaves that resemble those of its relatives. It produces small, white flowers in umbels, typical of the family.

Apium kalbreyeri is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and fragrance.
