Agrostis melanthes

Agrostis melanthes, commonly known as black bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Agrostis melanthes is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
