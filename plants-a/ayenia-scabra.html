Ayenia scabra

Ayenia scabra, commonly known as the rough ayenia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, rough hairs, contributing to its common name.

Ayenia scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
