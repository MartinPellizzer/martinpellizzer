Anaphalis notoniana

Anaphalis notoniana, commonly known as the southern mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silvery-white, woolly foliage that gives it a frosty appearance. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Anaphalis notoniana is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
