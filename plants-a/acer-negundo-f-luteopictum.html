Acer negundo f. luteopictum

Acer negundo f. luteopictum, commonly known as the golden painted maple, is a cultivar of the silver maple (Acer negundo).

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which displays a mix of yellow and red hues during the growing season. The leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive, almost quilted appearance. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant autumn color and unique leaf patterns.
