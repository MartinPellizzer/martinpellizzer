Aeginetia japonica

Aeginetia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese burr vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is notable for its distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds covered in hooked bracts, aiding in seed dispersal by clinging to animal fur. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Aeginetia japonica is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
