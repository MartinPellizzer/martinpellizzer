Aristolochia gigas

Aristolochia gigas, commonly known as the giant Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and wasps. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

The genus Aristolochia is known for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
