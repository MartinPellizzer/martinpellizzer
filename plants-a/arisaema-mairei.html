Arisaema mairei

Arisaema mairei, commonly known as the Maire's arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features a short rhizome with palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces berries that contain the seeds.

Arisaema mairei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
