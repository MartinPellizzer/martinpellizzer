Astragalus pseudoszovitsii

Astragalus pseudoszovitsii, commonly known as the Hungarian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. It is native to regions of Central and Eastern Europe, including parts of Hungary and the Balkans.

Astragalus pseudoszovitsii is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
