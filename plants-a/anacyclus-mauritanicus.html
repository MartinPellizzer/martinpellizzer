Anacyclus mauritanicus

Anacyclus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritania dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing annual herb with yellow flowers and a hairy stem. The plant typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by the production of small, wind-dispersed seeds.
