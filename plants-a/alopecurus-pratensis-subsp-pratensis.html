Alopecurus pratensis subsp. pratensis

Alopecurus pratensis subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as meadow foxtail, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are arranged in a panicle-like structure.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and wetlands, preferring well-drained to wet soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of small, two-rowed spikelets.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
