Alternanthera pilosa

Alternanthera pilosa, commonly known as velvetleaf or red leaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to purple and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
