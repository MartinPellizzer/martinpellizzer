Arisaema fischeri

Arisaema fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a single, showy spathe that is often green or yellowish in color, with a central spadix that bears small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive foliage and floral structure.
