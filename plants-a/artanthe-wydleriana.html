Artanthe wydleriana

Artanthe wydleriana, commonly known as the Wydler's artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Artanthe wydleriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
