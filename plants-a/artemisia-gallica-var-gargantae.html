Artemisia gallica var. gargantae

Artemisia gallica var. gargantae, commonly known as the giant wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff stems and silver-gray, aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
