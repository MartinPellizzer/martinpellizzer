Alopecurus laxiflorus

Alopecurus laxiflorus, commonly known as slender false oat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion.

Alopecurus laxiflorus is often found in disturbed sites and is considered a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
