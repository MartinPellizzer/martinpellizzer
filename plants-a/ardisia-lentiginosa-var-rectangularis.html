Ardisia lentiginosa var. rectangularis

Ardisia lentiginosa var. rectangularis, commonly known as the rectangular ardisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, shrub-like form. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
