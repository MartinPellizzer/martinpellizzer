Archidendron affine

Archidendron affine, commonly known as the African honeycomb tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is known for its unique honeycomb-like fruit, which consists of multiple woody pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
