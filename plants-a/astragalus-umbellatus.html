Astragalus umbellatus

Astragalus umbellatus, commonly known as the western milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in umbels. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus umbellatus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
