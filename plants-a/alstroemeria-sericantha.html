Alstroemeria sericantha

Alstroemeria sericantha, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Alstroemeria sericantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
