Andropogon monocladus

Andropogon monocladus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Andropogon monocladus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
