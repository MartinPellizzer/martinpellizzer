Aconitum prominens

Aconitum prominens, commonly known as the tall monkshood, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, hooded blue to purple flowers that are arranged in a raceme, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a distinctive appearance that adds to its ornamental value.

Aconitum prominens is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
