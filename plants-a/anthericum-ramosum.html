Anthericum ramosum

Anthericum ramosum, commonly known as the fragrant arum, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to the wetlands and marshy areas of Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. The plant features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. Its leaves are broad, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Anthericum ramosum is known for its strong, sometimes unpleasant odor, which is typical of plants in the Araceae family.
