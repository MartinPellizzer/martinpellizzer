Asparagus nodulosus

Asparagus nodulosus, commonly known as the nodular asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often overlooked. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to arid landscapes.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
