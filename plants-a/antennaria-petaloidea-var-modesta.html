Antennaria petaloidea var. modesta

Antennaria petaloidea var. modesta, commonly known as the modest fairy-bells, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
