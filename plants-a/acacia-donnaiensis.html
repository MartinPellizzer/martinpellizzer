Acacia donnaiensis

Acacia donnaiensis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Acacia donnaiensis is not only ecologically important but also holds cultural significance in Australian history.
