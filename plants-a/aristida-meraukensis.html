Aristida meraukensis

Aristida meraukensis, commonly known as the Meraukensis threeawn, is a grass species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, three-awned spikelets, which are characteristic of the Aristida genus.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
