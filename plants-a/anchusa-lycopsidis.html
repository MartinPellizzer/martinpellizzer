Anchusa lycopsidis

Anchusa lycopsidis, commonly known as the rock anchusa, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Anchusa lycopsidis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
