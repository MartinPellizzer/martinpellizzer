Apargia obtusa

Apargia obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps it retain moisture.

Apargia obtusa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
