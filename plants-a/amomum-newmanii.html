Amomum newmanii

Amomum newmanii, commonly known as the Chinese pepper plant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers emerging from the leaf axils. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The botanical features of Amomum newmanii make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
