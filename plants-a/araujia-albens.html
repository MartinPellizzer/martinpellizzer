Araujia albens

Araujia albens, commonly known as the white moonflower, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night. The flowers are tubular with five petals and emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has woody stems and produces berries that contain toxic seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting night-flying insects.
