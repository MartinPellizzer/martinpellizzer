Ascyrum

Ascyrum, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a hollow, leafless stem that supports a single flower head. The flower head consists of numerous small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind. Ascyrum is a perennial herb that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns and disturbed areas.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
