Aspalathus sphaerocephala

Aspalathus sphaerocephala, commonly known as the Rooibos bush, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bright green, oval-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry environments. Its leaves are the primary part used in the production of Rooibos tea, which is renowned for its unique flavor and health benefits.

The plant plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is also valued for its economic contribution to the region.
