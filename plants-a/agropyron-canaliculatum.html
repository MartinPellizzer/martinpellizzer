Agropyron canaliculatum

Agropyron canaliculatum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Agropyron canaliculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
