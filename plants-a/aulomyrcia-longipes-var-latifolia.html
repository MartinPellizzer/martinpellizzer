Aulomyrcia longipes var. latifolia

Aulomyrcia longipes var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved longipes, is a variant of the Aulomyrcia longipes species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a pale greenish-white color.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
