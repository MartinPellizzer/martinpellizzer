Amaranthus nettii

Amaranthus nettii, commonly known as nettle amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, often resembling those of stinging nettle.

Amaranthus nettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
