Aconitum gracilentum

Aconitum gracilentum, commonly known as the slender monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately compound leaves with a distinctive notched appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum gracilentum is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant to handle with care.
