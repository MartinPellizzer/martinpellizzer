Achillea pedemontana

Achillea pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Achillea pedemontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
