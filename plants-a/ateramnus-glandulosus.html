Ateramnus glandulosus

Ateramnus glandulosus, commonly known as the sticky leaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety thallus covered with small, glandular dots that produce a sticky substance. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and fungi, which contribute to its nutritional needs. It is often found on bark, soil, or rocks in temperate regions.

Its unique morphology and chemical composition make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
