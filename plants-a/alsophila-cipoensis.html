Alsophila cipoensis

Alsophila cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

Alsophila cipoensis plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
