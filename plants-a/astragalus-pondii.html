Astragalus pondii

Astragalus pondii, commonly known as the Pond's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus pondii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
