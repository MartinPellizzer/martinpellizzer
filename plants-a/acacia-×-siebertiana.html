Acacia × siebertiana

Acacia × siebertiana, commonly known as the hybrid silver wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is a natural hybrid between Acacia dealbata and Acacia longifolia, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers that appear in spring. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and public landscapes.
