Asystasia longituba

Asystasia longituba, commonly known as the slender asystasia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asystasia longituba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and flowering habits.
