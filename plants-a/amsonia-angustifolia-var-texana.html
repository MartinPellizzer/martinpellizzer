Amsonia angustifolia var. texana

Amsonia angustifolia var. texana, commonly known as Texas bluemink, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to lavender flower clusters in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a desirable ornamental plant.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
