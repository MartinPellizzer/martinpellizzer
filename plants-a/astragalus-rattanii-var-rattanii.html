Astragalus rattanii var. rattanii

Astragalus rattanii var. rattanii, commonly known as the rattan root or rattan plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical roots that are often harvested for medicinal use. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers cool, moist environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, ovate leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

The roots are valued in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits, including immune support and anti-inflammatory properties.
