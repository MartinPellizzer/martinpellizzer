Amomum nimkeyense

It is native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces fragrant, dark red or purple berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are also utilized in local culinary practices.
