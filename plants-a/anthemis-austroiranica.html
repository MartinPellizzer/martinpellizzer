Anthemis austroiranica

Anthemis austroiranica, commonly known as the Iranian tansy, is a flowering plant native to regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, and the plant has fine, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
