Anthemis triumfetti subsp. khorasanica

Anthemis triumfetti subsp.

khorasanica, commonly known as Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan.

The plant features slender, branched stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers with white ray florets. Its leaves are finely dissected and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
