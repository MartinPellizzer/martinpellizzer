Aquilegia subaphylla

Aquilegia subaphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aquilegia subaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
