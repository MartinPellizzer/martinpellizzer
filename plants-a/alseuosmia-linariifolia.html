Alseuosmia linariifolia

Alseuosmia linariifolia, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a fragrant, glandular underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscapes.
