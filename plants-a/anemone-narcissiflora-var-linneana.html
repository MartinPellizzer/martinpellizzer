Anemone narcissiflora var. linneana

Anemone narcissiflora var. linneana, commonly known as the Linnean anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate, lobed leaves that are often hairy and appear in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a common sight in woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
