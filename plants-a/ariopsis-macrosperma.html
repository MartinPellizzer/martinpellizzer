Ariopsis macrosperma

Ariopsis macrosperma, commonly known as the Gulf needlefish, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, needle-like leaves that are typically green and slightly translucent.

It thrives in brackish and saltwater environments, often found in coastal marshes and estuaries along the Atlantic and Gulf coasts of the United States. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize sediment and prevent erosion. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on water currents for seed dispersal.

Ariopsis macrosperma plays an important role in providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
