Argyrolobium calycinum

Argyrolobium calycinum, commonly known as the silver globular bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive silvery foliage. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is a shrub that typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid regions.
