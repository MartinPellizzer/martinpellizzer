Androscepia gigantea

Androscepia gigantea, commonly known as the giant androscepia, is a striking member of the Androsace family, native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

This perennial plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive central boss. Its leaves are typically rosette-shaped, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
