Aspilia helianthoides subsp. ciliata

Aspilia helianthoides subsp.

ciliata, commonly known as the fringed aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flower heads that resemble sunflowers.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have fine, ciliate (fringed) margins, giving it its subspecies name.

It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
