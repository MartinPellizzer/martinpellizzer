Anatherostipa saltensis

Anatherostipa saltensis, commonly known as the salt marsh grass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, wiry leaves that are adapted to withstand high salinity and drought conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor in sandy or saline soils and access deep water sources.
