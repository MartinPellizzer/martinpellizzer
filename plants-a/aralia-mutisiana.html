Aralia mutisiana

Aralia mutisiana, commonly known as the Mexican aralia, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America. The plant features compound leaves with multiple leaflets arranged in a pinnate pattern. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in umbels, and it produces berries that are edible for some animals.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
