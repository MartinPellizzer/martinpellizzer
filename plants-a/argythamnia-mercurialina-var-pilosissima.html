Argythamnia mercurialina var. pilosissima

Argythamnia mercurialina var. pilosissima, commonly known as the silky argythamnia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant is distinguished by its dense, silky white to grayish hairs covering the stems and leaves, which provide a protective adaptation against intense sunlight and water loss. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often studied for its potential in xerophytic landscaping and conservation efforts.
