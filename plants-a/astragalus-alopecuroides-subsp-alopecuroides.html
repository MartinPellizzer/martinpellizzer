Astragalus alopecuroides subsp. alopecuroides

Astragalus alopecuroides subsp.

alopecuroides, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, often grayish stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants, contributing to soil fertility.
