Aristida lanipes

Aristida lanipes, commonly known as the hairy dropseed, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and dry fields. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster that is often slightly drooping.

Aristida lanipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
