Astragalus chaworth-mustersii

Astragalus chaworth-mustersii, commonly known as the Chaworth-Musters' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or pale green in color. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
