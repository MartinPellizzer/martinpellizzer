Acacia amaliae var. orthophylla

Acacia amaliae var. orthophylla, commonly known as the Mexican white acacia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, straight stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
