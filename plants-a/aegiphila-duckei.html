Aegiphila duckei

Aegiphila duckei, commonly known as the Brazilian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the cerrado and Atlantic coastal regions of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the tropical and subtropical climates of its native habitat.

Aegiphila duckei is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
