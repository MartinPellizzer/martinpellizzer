Abutilon sylvaticum subsp. genuinum

Abutilon sylvaticum subsp.

genuinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family. It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow to orange.

Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its fibrous roots and capsule-like fruits are important for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and seed dispersal.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
