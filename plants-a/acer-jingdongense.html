Acer jingdongense

Acer jingdongense, commonly known as the Jingdong maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and parks.
