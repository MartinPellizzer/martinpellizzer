Amaryllis drummondii

Amaryllis drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces a tall, slender stem with several leaves that are broad and strap-like. It blooms in late spring to early summer, often following periods of cooler weather.

Amaryllis drummondii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
