Aetanthus coriaceus

Aetanthus coriaceus, commonly known as the rough helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip petal, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors.

Aetanthus coriaceus is native to parts of Europe and is often found in temperate climates.
