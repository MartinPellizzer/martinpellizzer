Acacia repens

Acacia repens, commonly known as the creeping wattle, is a low-growing shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, typical of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of tiny, feathery leaflets.

Acacia repens is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
