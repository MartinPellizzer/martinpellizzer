Artanthe aequalis

Artanthe aequalis, commonly known as the equal-leaved artanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in dense, pink to red hues. Artanthe aequalis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
