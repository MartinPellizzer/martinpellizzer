Allium rigidiflorum

Allium rigidiflorum, commonly known as the rigid-flowered garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions.

Allium rigidiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
