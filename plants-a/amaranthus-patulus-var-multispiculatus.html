Amaranthus patulus var. multispiculatus

Amaranthus patulus var. multispiculatus, commonly known as the multispiculate amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange, often creating a striking visual display. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
