Adesmia patagonica var. nana

Adesmia patagonica var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf Patagonian burro bush, is a low-growing shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it well-suited for rocky or arid environments.

The variety *nana* is distinguished by its shorter stature compared to the typical form of *Adesmia patagonica*.
