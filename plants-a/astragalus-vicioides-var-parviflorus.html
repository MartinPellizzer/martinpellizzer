Astragalus vicioides var. parviflorus

Astragalus vicioides var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
