Adansonia madagascariensis

Adansonia madagascariensis, commonly known as the traveler's tree, is a striking member of the Bombacaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The tree produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found near water sources.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a symbol of Madagascar's rich biodiversity.
