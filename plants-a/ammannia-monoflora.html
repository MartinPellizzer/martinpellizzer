Ammannia monoflora

Ammannia monoflora, commonly known as the redroot sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features fleshy, succulent stems that are often reddish in color, and its small, white to pale pink flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Ammannia monoflora thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.
