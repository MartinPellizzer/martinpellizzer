Apomuria angustifolia var. pubescens

Apomuria angustifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy narrowleaf apomuria, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
