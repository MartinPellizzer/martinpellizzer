Asplenium bipartitum

Asplenium bipartitum, commonly known as the two-lobed spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and finely divided, lanceolate leaves that are often divided into two distinct lobes. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in moist, shaded environments, thriving in temperate climates. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium bipartitum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
