Ammannia sanguinolenta

Ammannia sanguinolenta, commonly known as redroot ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive red stems and leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Its root system is notable for its deep red color, which gives the plant its common name.

Ammannia sanguinolenta is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, sunny conditions.
