Amelanchier × confusa

Amelanchier × confusa, commonly known as the Saskatoon serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in spring and edible berries in summer. The plant typically grows in a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it is known for its adaptability to different climates.

The berries are often used in culinary applications and are a food source for birds and wildlife.
