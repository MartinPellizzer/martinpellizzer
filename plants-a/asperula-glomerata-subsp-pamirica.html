Asperula glomerata subsp. pamirica

Asperula glomerata subsp.

pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir trailing arbutus, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink.

The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and a mat-forming growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh alpine environments.
