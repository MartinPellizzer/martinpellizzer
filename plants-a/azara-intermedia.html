Azara intermedia

Azara intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or *Azara* tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, lanceolate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
