Acacia chaconensis

Acacia chaconensis, commonly known as the Chacon's acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments, and it produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences.

Acacia chaconensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
