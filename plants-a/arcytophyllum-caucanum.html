Arcytophyllum caucanum

Arcytophyllum caucanum, commonly known as the Caucasian tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of xerophytic plants.
