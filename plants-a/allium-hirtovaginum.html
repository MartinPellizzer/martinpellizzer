Allium hirtovaginum

Allium hirtovaginum, commonly known as the woolly bulb onion, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which is surrounded by a sheath of fibrous, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Allium hirtovaginum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some culinary uses in local cuisines.
