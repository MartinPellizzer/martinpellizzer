Arctostaphylos hookeri subsp. ravenii

Arctostaphylos hookeri subsp.

ravenii, commonly known as the Raven's Bay manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and southern Oregon. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters and dry summers.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
