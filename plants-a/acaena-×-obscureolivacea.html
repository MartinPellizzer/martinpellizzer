Acaena × obscureolivacea

Acaena × obscureolivacea, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a hybrid shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, red or orange flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
