Aloe namorokaensis

Aloe namorokaensis, commonly known as the Namoroka aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapted to its harsh environment. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their healing properties.

Aloe namorokaensis is a rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
