Acilepis dendigulensis

Acilepis dendigulensis, commonly known as the giant forest tree, is a unique species native to the dense tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its towering height, often exceeding 50 meters, and its broad, umbrella-like canopy that provides significant shade. The plant possesses a thick, fibrous bark that offers protection against pests and environmental stressors. Its leaves are large, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to efficient photosynthesis.

Acilepis dendigulensis plays a crucial role in its ecosystem by supporting a diverse array of flora and fauna.
