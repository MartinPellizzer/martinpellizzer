Aphyllon californicum subsp. condensum

Aphyllon californicum subsp.

condensum, commonly known as the condensed woollypod, is a perennial herb native to California. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, pale green flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is part of the Leguminosae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
