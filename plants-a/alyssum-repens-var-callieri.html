Alyssum repens var. callieri

Alyssum repens var. callieri, commonly known as the creeping alyssum, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in clusters along the stems. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically opposite or alternate, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.

This variety is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
