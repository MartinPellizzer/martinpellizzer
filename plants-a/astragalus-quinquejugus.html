Astragalus quinquejugus

Astragalus quinquejugus, commonly known as the five-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus quinquejugus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
