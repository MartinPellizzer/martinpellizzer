Adhunia emarginata

Adhunia emarginata, commonly known as the Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Adhunia emarginata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
