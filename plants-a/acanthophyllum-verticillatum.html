Acanthophyllum verticillatum

Acanthophyllum verticillatum, commonly known as the silky acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions through its deep root system and succulent leaves. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Its ornamental value has also made it a subject of interest in horticulture.
