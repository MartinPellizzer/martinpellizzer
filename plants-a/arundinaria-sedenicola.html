Arundinaria sedenicola

Arundinaria sedenicola, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or the narrow-leaf bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Arundinaria sedenicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
