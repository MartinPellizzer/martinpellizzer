Amsinckia sparsifolia

Amsinckia sparsifolia, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Amsinckia sparsifolia is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which enhances soil fertility.
