Aleuritia didyma

Aleuritia didyma, commonly known as the twinflower, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy underneath. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Aleuritia didyma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
