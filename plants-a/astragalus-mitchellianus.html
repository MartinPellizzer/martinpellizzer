Astragalus mitchellianus

Astragalus mitchellianus, commonly known as Mitchell's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open plains. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
