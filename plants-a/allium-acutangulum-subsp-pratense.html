Allium acutangulum subsp. pratense

Allium acutangulum subsp.

pratense, commonly known as the meadow leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear umbels of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
