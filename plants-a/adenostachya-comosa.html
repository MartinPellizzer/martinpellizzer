Adenostachya comosa

Adenostachya comosa, commonly known as the woolly beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and spike-like inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, adapting well to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

Adenostachya comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
