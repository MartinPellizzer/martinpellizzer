Astracantha xanthogossypina

Astracantha xanthogossypina, commonly known as the yellow cotton thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and thorny growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
