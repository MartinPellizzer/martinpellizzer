Aponogeton kimberleyensis

Aponogeton kimberleyensis, commonly known as the Kimberley water clover, is a rare aquatic plant native to the Kimberley region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like leaves that form a dense, floating mat on the water surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge above the water, adding a delicate floral element to its appearance. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with soft, acidic water conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
