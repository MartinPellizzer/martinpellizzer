Aronia arbutifolia var. melanocarpa

Aronia arbutifolia var. melanocarpa, commonly known as black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small white flowers in early spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The berries are typically harvested in late summer and are used in jams, juices, and supplements.
