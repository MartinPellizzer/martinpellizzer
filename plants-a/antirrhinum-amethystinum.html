Antirrhinum amethystinum

Antirrhinum amethystinum, commonly known as the amethyst snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, upward-facing flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Antirrhinum amethystinum is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of care.
