Acacia betchei

Acacia betchei, commonly known as the African silver acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia betchei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
