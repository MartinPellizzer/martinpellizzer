Alchemilla divaricans

Alchemilla divaricans, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring, clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla divaricans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
