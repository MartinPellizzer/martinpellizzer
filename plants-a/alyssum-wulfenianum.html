Alyssum wulfenianum

Alyssum wulfenianum, commonly known as the rock Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water, making it well adapted to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Alyssum wulfenianum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
