Asparagus sieboldii

Asparagus sieboldii, commonly known as Siebold's asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, spiny stems that grow from a fibrous root system. The plant produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the spring. Its leaves are compound, with multiple narrow leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

Asparagus sieboldii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
