Anacharis potamogeton

Anacharis potamogeton, commonly known as waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Americas.

It has long, flexible stems that often branch out, and its leaves are typically narrow and ribbon-like. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, though it is more commonly propagated through vegetative growth. Anacharis potamogeton plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.

It is also widely used in aquariums due to its ability to thrive in various water conditions.
