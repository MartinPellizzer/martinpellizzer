Andropogon lecomtei

Andropogon lecomtei, commonly known as Lecomte's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flower clusters, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Andropogon lecomtei is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
