Abies guatemalensis subsp. rushforthii

Abies guatemalensis subsp.

rushforthii, commonly known as the Rushforth's fir, is a rare and endemic species native to high-altitude regions of Guatemala. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted.

The cones are typically short and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soils, often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.
