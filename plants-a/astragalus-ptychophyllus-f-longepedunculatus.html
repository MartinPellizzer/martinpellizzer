Astragalus ptychophyllus f. longepedunculatus

Astragalus ptychophyllus f. longepedunculatus, commonly known as the long pedunculate milkvetch, is a rare and distinctive subspecies of the milkvetch genus.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which bear clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are usually pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
