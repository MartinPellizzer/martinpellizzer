Acampe pachyglossa subsp. reschiana

Acampe pachyglossa subsp.

reschiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acampe, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
