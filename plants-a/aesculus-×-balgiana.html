Aesculus × balgiana

Aesculus × balgiana, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves and showy, white or pink bracts that surround clusters of small, yellow flowers. The tree produces large, spiny capsules containing glossy, chestnut-like seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.
