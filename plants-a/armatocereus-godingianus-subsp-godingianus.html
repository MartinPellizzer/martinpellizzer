Armatocereus godingianus subsp. godingianus

Armatocereus godingianus subsp.

godingianus, commonly known as the Goding's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to southern Brazil. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems and large, showy white flowers that open at night.

The plant produces spiny, ribbed stems that are adapted to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is noted for its nocturnal blooming habit, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
