Aloe percrassa

Aloe percrassa, commonly known as the desert aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.

Aloe percrassa is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
