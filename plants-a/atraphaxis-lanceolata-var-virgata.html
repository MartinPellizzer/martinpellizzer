Atraphaxis lanceolata var. virgata

Atraphaxis lanceolata var. virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's spurge, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to high sunlight exposure.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species in its native habitat.
