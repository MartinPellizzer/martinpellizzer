Athyrium filix-femina var. pectinatum

Athyrium filix-femina var. pectinatum, commonly known as the peacock fern, is a variant of the lady fern characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It features a central, upright, sterile stalk that bears a cluster of fertile fronds at the tip. The fronds are typically divided into a series of narrow, feather-like segments that are often curled or twisted. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is native to parts of Asia.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
