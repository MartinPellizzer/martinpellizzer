Angraecum caespitosum

Angraecum caespitosum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact, clumping growth habit and produces striking, star-shaped flowers with long, slender petals. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, showcasing vibrant colors such as white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Angraecum caespitosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
