Assonia viburnoides

Assonia viburnoides, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Assonia viburnoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
