Andropogon pertusus subvar. trifoveolatus

Andropogon pertusus subvar. trifoveolatus, commonly known as the three-lobed bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, fluffy flowers that appear in late summer.

This subvariety is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
