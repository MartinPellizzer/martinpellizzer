Anatherum africanum

Anatherum africanum, commonly known as African millet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain tiny grains, which are used as a food source. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it an important crop in arid regions.

Anatherum africanum is also valued for its ecological role in preventing soil erosion.
