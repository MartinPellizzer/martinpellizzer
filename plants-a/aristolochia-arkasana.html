Aristolochia arkasana

Aristolochia arkasana, commonly known as the Arkansas birthwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and intricate, pipe-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are often greenish-white with a distinctive, aromatic scent that attracts insects.

However, it is important to note that all parts of this plant are toxic and should not be ingested.
