Azalea nivea

Azalea nivea, commonly known as the white azalea, is a species of flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Azalea nivea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
