Alchemilla pseudocalycina

Alchemilla pseudocalycina, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves that form a dense, compact structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is used in traditional herbal medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
