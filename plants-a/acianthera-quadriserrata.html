Acianthera quadriserrata

Acianthera quadriserrata, commonly known as the four-toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes four serrated lobes on the lip, giving it its scientific name.
