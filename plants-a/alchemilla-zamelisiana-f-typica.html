Alchemilla zamelisiana f. typica

Alchemilla zamelisiana f. typica, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
