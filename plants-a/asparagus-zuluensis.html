Asparagus zuluensis

Asparagus zuluensis, commonly known as Zulu asparagus, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, needle-like spears that are harvested in the early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and can thrive with minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value and edible properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
