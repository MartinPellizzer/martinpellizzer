Actinocephalus actinocephaloides

Actinocephalus actinocephaloides, commonly known as the African star grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, star-like inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in a dense, spherical cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly seen in natural habitats.
