Anil articulata

Anil articulata, commonly known as Indian spinach or Malabar spinach, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fleshy, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Its growth habit includes trailing stems that can root at the nodes, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.
