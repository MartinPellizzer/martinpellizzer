Antidesma laurifolium

Antidesma laurifolium, commonly known as the shining antidesma, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
