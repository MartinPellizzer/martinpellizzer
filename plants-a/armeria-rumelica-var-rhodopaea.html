Armeria rumelica var. rhodopaea

Armeria rumelica var. rhodopaea, commonly known as the Rhodope sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
