Actinella linearis

Actinella linearis, commonly known as the slender actinella, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas.

Actinella linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
