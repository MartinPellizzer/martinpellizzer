Alchemilla smaragdina var. caulescens

Alchemilla smaragdina var. caulescens, commonly known as the slender lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate flowering display.
