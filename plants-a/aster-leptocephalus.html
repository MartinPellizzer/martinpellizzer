Aster leptocephalus

Aster leptocephalus, commonly known as the narrow-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces showy blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its common name.
