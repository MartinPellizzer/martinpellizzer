Abaxianthus convexus

Abaxianthus convexus, commonly known as the convex-leafed abaxianthus, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that curl inward, forming a protective layer against harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, blooming during the dry season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to absorb moisture from deep within the soil.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
