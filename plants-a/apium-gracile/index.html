Apium Gracile

Apium gracile is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes familiar plants like carrots and parsley. This species is known for its delicate, aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, Apium gracile is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
