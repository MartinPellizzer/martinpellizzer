Agouticarpa grandistipula

Agouticarpa grandistipula, commonly known as the giant featherwood, is a member of the Leguminosae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is notable for its large, elongated leaves with numerous feather-like lobes, which contribute to its ornamental value. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellow or orange color.

The fruit, a legume pod, contains several seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in the plant's propagation.
