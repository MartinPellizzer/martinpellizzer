Asperula galioides var. glauca

Asperula galioides var. glauca, commonly known as the blue honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The foliage is characterized by glossy, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate blossoms and evergreen foliage.
