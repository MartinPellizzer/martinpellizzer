Acanthus pectinatus

Acanthus pectinatus, commonly known as the woolly acanthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers in the summer months. Its foliage and floral structure make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Acanthus pectinatus is also valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
