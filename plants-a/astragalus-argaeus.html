Astragalus argaeus

Astragalus argaeus, commonly known as the Argentinian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus argaeus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
