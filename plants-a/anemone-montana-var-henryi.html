Anemone montana var. henryi

Anemone montana var. henryi, commonly known as Henry's mountain anemone, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are basal and often covered in a whitish or grayish bloom. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
