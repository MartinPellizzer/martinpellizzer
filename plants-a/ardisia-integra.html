Ardisia integra

Ardisia integra, commonly known as the glossy privet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
