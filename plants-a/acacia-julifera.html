Acacia julifera

Acacia julifera, commonly known as the Jerusalem acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Acacia julifera is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
