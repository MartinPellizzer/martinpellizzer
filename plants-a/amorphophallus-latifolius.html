Amorphophallus latifolius

Amorphophallus latifolius, commonly known as the elephant yam, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a thick, underground tuber that stores nutrients. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped flowers that are often surrounded by a colorful, modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its edible tuber.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
