Anavinga samyda

Anavinga samyda, commonly known as the sacred tree of the Malagasy people, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are used in traditional cuisine. Its roots and leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties in local herbal remedies.

Anavinga samyda plays a significant role in both the ecological and cultural landscape of Madagascar.
