Ambrosia monogyra

Ambrosia monogyra, commonly known as the roughleaf ambrosia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is part of the Ambrosiaceae family and is known for its wind-pollinated nature.

Due to its allergenic properties, it is considered a weed in many regions.
