Atriplex obtusata

Atriplex obtusata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. Its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

Atriplex obtusata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
