Albuca deserticola subsp. longipilosa

Albuca deserticola subsp.

longipilosa, commonly known as the desert lily, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to store water, and it produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowering period usually occurs during the rainy season, adding a burst of color to the landscape.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
