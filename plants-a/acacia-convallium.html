Acacia convallium

Acacia convallium, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which are typically borne at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Acacia convallium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
