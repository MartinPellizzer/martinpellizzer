Alocasia bantamensis

Alocasia bantamensis, commonly known as the Bantam elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often a deep green with a metallic sheen, and it produces small, white flowers that are not typically visible above the leaves.

Alocasia bantamensis is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care in suitable conditions.
