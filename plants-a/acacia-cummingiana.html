Acacia cummingiana

Acacia cummingiana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia cummingiana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
