Aeschynanthus lampongus var. parvifolius

Aeschynanthus lampongus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf wax plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. The leaves are small, ovate, and glossy, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
