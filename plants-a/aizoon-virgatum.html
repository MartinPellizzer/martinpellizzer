Aizoon virgatum

Aizoon virgatum, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its unique coloration and drought tolerance make it a valuable ornamental plant.
