Anemia antrorsa

Anemia antrorsa, commonly known as the backward anemia plant, is a rare flowering species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, downward-facing blooms that resemble small, delicate cups. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
