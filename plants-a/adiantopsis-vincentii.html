Adiantopsis vincentii

Adiantopsis vincentii, commonly known as the Vincent's maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by spore-bearing structures that contribute to its reproductive cycle.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Adiantopsis vincentii is considered a species of conservation concern.
