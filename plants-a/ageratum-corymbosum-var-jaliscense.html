Ageratum corymbosum var. jaliscense

Ageratum corymbosum var. jaliscense, commonly known as Mexican Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often used in landscape design for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
