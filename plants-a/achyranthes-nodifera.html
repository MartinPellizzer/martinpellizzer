Achyranthes nodifera

Achyranthes nodifera, commonly known as Indian knotgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Achyranthes nodifera is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
