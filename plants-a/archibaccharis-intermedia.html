Archibaccharis intermedia

Archibaccharis intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
