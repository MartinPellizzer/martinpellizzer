Anacamptis papilionacea subsp. expansa

Anacamptis papilionacea subsp.

expansa, commonly known as the Broad-leaved Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, butterfly-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are broad and lanceolate, while its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, violet-blue flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
