Astragalus harpocarpus

Astragalus harpocarpus, commonly known as the hooked milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces unique, hooked seed pods that help in seed dispersal by wind. It is adapted to dry environments and often grows in sandy or rocky soils.

Astragalus harpocarpus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
