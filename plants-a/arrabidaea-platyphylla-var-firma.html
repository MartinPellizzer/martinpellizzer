Arrabidaea platyphylla var. firma

Arrabidaea platyphylla var. firma, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red floral bracts that resemble flames, which appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
