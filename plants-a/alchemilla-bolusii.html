Alchemilla bolusii

Alchemilla bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Alchemilla bolusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
