Astragalus kelseyae

Astragalus kelseyae, commonly known as Kelsey's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
