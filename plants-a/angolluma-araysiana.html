Angolluma araysiana

Angolluma araysiana, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in clusters, producing small yellow to orange blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
