Acacia trichodella

Acacia trichodella, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia trichodella is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
