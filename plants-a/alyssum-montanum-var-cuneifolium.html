Alyssum montanum var. cuneifolium

Alyssum montanum var. cuneifolium, commonly known as the Sierra foothill alyssum, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a distinctive cuneate (wedge-shaped) base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
