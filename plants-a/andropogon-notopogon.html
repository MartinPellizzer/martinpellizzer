Andropogon notopogon

Andropogon notopogon, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon notopogon plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
