Astragalus gregorii

Astragalus gregorii, commonly known as the Gregor's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Astragalus gregorii is also of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
