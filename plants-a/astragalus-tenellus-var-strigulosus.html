Astragalus tenellus var. strigulosus

Astragalus tenellus var. strigulosus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets and produces small, yellow to pale green flowers in clusters. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
