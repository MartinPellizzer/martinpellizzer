Abutilon mauritianum var. epilosum

Abutilon mauritianum var. epilosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically ovate to heart-shaped, with a velvety texture due to dense pubescence.

The species is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like hibiscus and cotton.
