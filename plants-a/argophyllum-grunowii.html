Argophyllum grunowii

Argophyllum grunowii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockheath, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
