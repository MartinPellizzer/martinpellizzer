Atriplex

Atriplex, commonly known as saltbush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their succulent leaves and small, clustered flowers. They are well adapted to arid and saline environments, making them common in desert and coastal regions. Atriplex species often have a high tolerance for salt and drought, which contributes to their ecological importance in stabilizing soil.

Their seeds are also a food source for various wildlife, including birds and small mammals.
