Androsace longiseta

Androsace longiseta, commonly known as the long-stemmed rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Androsace longiseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
