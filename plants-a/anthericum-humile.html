Anthericum humile

Anthericum humile, commonly known as the dwarf arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces a single, leafy scape with a spadix and a spathe. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture. It blooms in early summer, with its inflorescence containing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Anthericum humile thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
