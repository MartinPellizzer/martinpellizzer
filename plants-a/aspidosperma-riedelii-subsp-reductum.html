Aspidosperma riedelii subsp. reductum

Aspidosperma riedelii subsp.

reductum, commonly known as the Brazilian redwood, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to Brazil. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its straight trunk and smooth, gray bark.

The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy green upper surface. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically yellowish-green, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its durable wood and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical forests.
