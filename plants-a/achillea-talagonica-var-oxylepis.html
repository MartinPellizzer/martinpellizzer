Achillea talagonica var. oxylepis

Achillea talagonica var. oxylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that bear small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
