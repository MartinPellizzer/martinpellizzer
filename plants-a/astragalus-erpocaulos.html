Astragalus erpocaulos

Astragalus erpocaulos, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
