Acer nipponicum var. koshinense

Acer nipponicum var. koshinense, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often red or orange foliage that changes with the seasons. The plant has compound leaves that are typically trifoliate, with lobed or serrated margins. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and striking autumn colors.
