Acalypha hederacea var. orbicularis

Acalypha hederacea var. orbicularis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or orbicular acalypha, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch.

The leaves are typically a deep green with a distinctive waxy texture, and they often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are not particularly showy but are followed by fluffy, cotton-like seed heads. This plant is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
