Aleuritia cernua

Aleuritia cernua, commonly known as the downy alder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aleuritia cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
