Acacia arenaria

Acacia arenaria, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, flat, feathery leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape. Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Acacia arenaria is well-suited for desert landscaping due to its drought tolerance and hardy nature.
