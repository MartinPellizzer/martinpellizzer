Andropogon fascicularis

Andropogon fascicularis, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairie habitats, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small flowers. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon fascicularis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
