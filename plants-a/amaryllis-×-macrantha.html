Amaryllis × macrantha

Amaryllis × macrantha, commonly known as the giant amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisica.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting darker veins. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.
