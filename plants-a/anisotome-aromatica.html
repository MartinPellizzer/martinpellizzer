Anisotome aromatica

Anisotome aromatica, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that emit a subtle, sweet fragrance when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in open, sunny environments. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Anisotome aromatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine and ceremonial practices.
