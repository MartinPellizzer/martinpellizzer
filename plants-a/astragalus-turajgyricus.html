Astragalus turajgyricus

Astragalus turajgyricus, commonly known as Turajgyricus milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
