Astragalus angustus var. imperfectus

Astragalus angustus var. imperfectus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
