Aspalathus cuneatus

Aspalathus cuneatus, commonly known as the African green tea or rooibos, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant. Its leaves are harvested and processed to create a caffeine-free herbal tea.

Aspalathus cuneatus plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its medicinal properties.
