Acacia saliciformis

Acacia saliciformis, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are highly attractive to bees and other pollinators. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Acacia saliciformis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
