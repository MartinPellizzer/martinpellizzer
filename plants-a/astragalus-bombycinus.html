Astragalus bombycinus

Astragalus bombycinus, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus bombycinus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
