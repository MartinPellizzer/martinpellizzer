Achillea × kellereri

Achillea × kellereri, commonly known as the German yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green foliage and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pinkish-purple. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. The plant produces small, herbaceous stems and is known for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in garden settings.
