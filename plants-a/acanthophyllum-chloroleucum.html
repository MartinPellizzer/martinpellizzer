Acanthophyllum chloroleucum

Acanthophyllum chloroleucum, commonly known as the whiteleaf jointvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, shrub-like structure adapted to harsh desert environments. The plant features small, pale green leaves and produces yellow or green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deep underground, which is crucial for survival in its dry habitat.

Acanthophyllum chloroleucum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
