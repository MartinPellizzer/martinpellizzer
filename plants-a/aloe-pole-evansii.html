Aloe pole-evansii

Aloe pole-evansii, commonly known as the pole-evansii aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, typically blooming in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
