Aira elegans

Aira elegans, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Aira elegans is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
