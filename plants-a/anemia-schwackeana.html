Anemia schwackeana

Anemia schwackeana, commonly known as the Schwacke's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed, with a hairy texture that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to harsh climates.
