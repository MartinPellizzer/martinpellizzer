Anthericum monticola

Anthericum monticola, commonly known as the mountain adder's mouth, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a broad, green spathe. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Anthericum monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
