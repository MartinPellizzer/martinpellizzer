Aconitum napiforme var. callianthum

Aconitum napiforme var. callianthum, commonly known as the Pacific Giant Buttercup, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and coastal regions of British Columbia, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features a large, rounded root and produces showy blue to purple flowers with five petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It is a perennial herb that prefers partial to full shade and is often found in woodland habitats.
