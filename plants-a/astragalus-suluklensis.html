Astragalus suluklensis

Astragalus suluklensis, commonly known as the Suluklensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus suluklensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
