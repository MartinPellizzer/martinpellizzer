Ageratina irrasa

Ageratina irrasa, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Ageratina irrasa is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as an ornamental plant in gardens.
