Agrimonia × nipponopilosa

Agrimonia × nipponopilosa, commonly known as the Japanese agrimony, is a hybrid species within the genus Agrimonia, native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in woodland and grassy areas, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features serrated leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal heads, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Agrimonia × nipponopilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
