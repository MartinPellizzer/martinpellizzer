Arundinaria kiboensis

Arundinaria kiboensis, commonly known as the Kibo bamboo, is a species of grass native to the high altitudes of Mount Kilimanjaro in Tanzania.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Arundinaria kiboensis plays a significant ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
