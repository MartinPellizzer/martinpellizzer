Aristolochia morae

Aristolochia morae, commonly known as the Morae's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often green or purple in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and showy blooms. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, climbing or trailing along supports.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in both traditional medicine and scientific research.
