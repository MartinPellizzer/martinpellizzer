Ardisia tahanica subsp. eucalyptifolia

Ardisia tahanica subsp.

eucalyptifolia, commonly known as the aromatic ardisia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The subspecies is distinguished by its aromatic foliage, which emits a subtle eucalyptus-like scent.
