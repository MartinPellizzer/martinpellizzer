Antholyza fulgens

Antholyza fulgens, commonly known as the fiery torch plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its bright red, orange, and yellow flowers that resemble torches, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Antholyza fulgens is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and long-lasting floral display.
