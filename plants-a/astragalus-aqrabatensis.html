Astragalus aqrabatensis

Astragalus aqrabatensis, commonly known as the Jordanian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and can be found in various habitats across Jordan and neighboring countries.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
