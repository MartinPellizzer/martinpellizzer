Anthurium scolopendrinum

Anthurium scolopendrinum, commonly known as the red-tipped anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or maroon tint along the edges. The plant produces spadices, which are inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium scolopendrinum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
