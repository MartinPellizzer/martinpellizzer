Aster simulans

Aster simulans, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist, hence its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Aster simulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
