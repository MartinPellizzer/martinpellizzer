Arrabidaea erecta

Arrabidaea erecta, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
