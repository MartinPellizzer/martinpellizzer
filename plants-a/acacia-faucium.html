Acacia faucium

Acacia faucium, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that attract various pollinators, including bees and birds. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Acacia faucium plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
