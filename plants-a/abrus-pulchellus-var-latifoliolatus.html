Abrus pulchellus var. latifoliolatus

Abrus pulchellus var. latifoliolatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red pods that enclose flat, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant has palmate leaves with broad, lobed leaflets, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its decorative value.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
