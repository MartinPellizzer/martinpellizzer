Acacia cataractae

Acacia cataractae, commonly known as the Ethiopian acacia, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia cataractae is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
