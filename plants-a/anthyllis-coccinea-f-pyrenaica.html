Anthyllis coccinea f. pyrenaica

Anthyllis coccinea f. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean red broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its bright red, fluffy flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Pyrenees Mountains and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in open, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
