Arachnorchis helvina

Arachnorchis helvina, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces a single spike with several flowers. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, typical of many orchid species.

Arachnorchis helvina thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches.
