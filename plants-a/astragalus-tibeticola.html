Astragalus tibeticola

Astragalus tibeticola, commonly known as Tibetan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Astragalus tibeticola is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
