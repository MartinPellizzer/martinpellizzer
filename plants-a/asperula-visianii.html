Asperula visianii

Asperula visianii, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and evergreen foliage.
