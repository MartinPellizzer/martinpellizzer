Annona monticola

Annona monticola, commonly known as mountain annona, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a greenish-yellow corolla and prominent stamens. Its fruit is a rough, greenish-yellow berry that turns yellow when mature, containing a single large seed.

Annona monticola is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
