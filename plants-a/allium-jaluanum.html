Allium jaluanum

Allium jaluanum, commonly known as the Jaluan onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are often used in local cuisines for their mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Allium jaluanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
