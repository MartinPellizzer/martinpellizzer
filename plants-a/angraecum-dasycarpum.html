Angraecum dasycarpum

Angraecum dasycarpum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender, white or pinkish inflorescence that can extend up to several meters in length. The plant produces fragrant, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Angraecum dasycarpum is notable for its unique morphology and is a symbol of the biodiversity of Madagascar's flora.
