Acalypha mollissima

Acalypha mollissima, commonly known as silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, spade-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Acalypha mollissima is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden environments.
