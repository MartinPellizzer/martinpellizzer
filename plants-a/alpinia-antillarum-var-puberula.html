Alpinia antillarum var. puberula

Alpinia antillarum var. puberula, commonly known as the pink ginger, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking pinkish-red flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The varietal form, puberula, distinguishes it from the typical species through its hairy, pubescent leaves.
