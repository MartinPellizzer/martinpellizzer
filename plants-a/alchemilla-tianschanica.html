Alchemilla tianschanica

Alchemilla tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Alchemilla tianschanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
