Aristolochia porphyrophylla

Aristolochia porphyrophylla, commonly known as the purple-veined Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep purple veins and its unique, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, reddish covering. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
