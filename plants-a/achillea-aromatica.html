Achillea aromatica

Achillea aromatica, commonly known as sweet yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pinkish in color. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces a strong, aromatic scent. It typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Sweet yarrow is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
