Aconitum smithii

Aconitum smithii, commonly known as Smith's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, spurred blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Aconitum smithii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

It is also noted for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
