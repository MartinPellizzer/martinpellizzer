Artemisia echegarayi

Artemisia echegarayi, commonly known as the Echegaray's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Due to its resilience and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
