Amaranthus chlorostachys var. pseudoretroflexus

Amaranthus chlorostachys var. pseudoretroflexus, commonly known as the silver leaf amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth species characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage.

This plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that reflects light, giving them their distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
