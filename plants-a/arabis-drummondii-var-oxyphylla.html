Arabis drummondii var. oxyphylla

Arabis drummondii var. oxyphylla, commonly known as Drummond's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
