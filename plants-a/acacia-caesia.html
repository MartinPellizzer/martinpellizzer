Acacia caesia

Acacia caesia, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. Acacia caesia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration.
