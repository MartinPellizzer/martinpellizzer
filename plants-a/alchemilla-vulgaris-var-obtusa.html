Alchemilla vulgaris var. obtusa

Alchemilla vulgaris var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
