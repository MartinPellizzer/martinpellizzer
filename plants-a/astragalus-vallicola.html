Astragalus vallicola

Astragalus vallicola, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus vallicola is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
