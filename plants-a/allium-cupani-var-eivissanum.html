Allium cupani var. eivissanum

Allium cupani var. eivissanum, commonly known as the Eivissanum garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant's bulbs are typically small and have a mild, garlic-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
