Andropogon pratensis var. pseudoabyssinicus

Andropogon pratensis var. pseudoabyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian bluestem, is a variant of the tallgrass species native to eastern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and tufted inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing forage for wildlife and livestock.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the region's flora.
