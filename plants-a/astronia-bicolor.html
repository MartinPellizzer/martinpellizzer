Astronia bicolor

Astronia bicolor, commonly known as the pink star sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-white inflorescence that resembles a star, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Astronia bicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
