Acinodendron bourgaeanum

Acinodendron bourgaeanum, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
