Aspidium proliferum

Aspidium proliferum, commonly known as the common water fern, is a member of the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or near water sources. The plant has a rhizomatous structure that allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Aspidium proliferum plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
