Antirrhinum meionanthum

Antirrhinum meionanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of vibrant red or orange tubular flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. It typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
