Astragalus exaltatus

Astragalus exaltatus, commonly known as the tall milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open landscapes.
