Adiantum meyerianum

Adiantum meyerianum, commonly known as the Meyer's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, arching appearance.

Adiantum meyerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
