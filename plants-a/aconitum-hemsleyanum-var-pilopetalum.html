Aconitum hemsleyanum var. pilopetalum

Aconitum hemsleyanum var. pilopetalum, commonly known as the wooly-petaled aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in woodland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
