Agave kristenii

Agave kristenii, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach up to 10 feet in height, bearing small, white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Agave kristenii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional practices.
