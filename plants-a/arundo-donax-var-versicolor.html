Arundo donax var. versicolor

Arundo donax var. versicolor, commonly known as the multicolored giant reed, is a striking variant of the common giant reed.

It is characterized by its variegated foliage, which displays a mix of green, white, and sometimes pink or red hues. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region but has been introduced to various parts of the world. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands.

Its tall, reed-like stalks can grow up to 6 meters in height, making it a prominent feature in wetland ecosystems.
