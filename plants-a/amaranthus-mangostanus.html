Amaranthus mangostanus

Amaranthus mangostanus, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, spiky fruits that contain a sweet, white, segmented flesh. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped leaves and has a woody stem with a rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
