Aristolochia rotunda subsp. longa

Aristolochia rotunda subsp.

longa, commonly known as the longleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
