Alpinia bisulcata

Alpinia bisulcata, commonly known as the mountain ginger or Chinese ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant features a rhizomatous stem and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with three prominent petals and three sepals.

Alpinia bisulcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
