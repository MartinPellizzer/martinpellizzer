Angraecum infundibulare

Angraecum infundibulare, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white flowers with a distinctive star-shaped appearance. The plant produces fragrant, nocturnal blooms that attract hawkmoths for pollination. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, adapted for climbing and photosynthesis in its natural habitat.

Angraecum infundibulare is well-suited to warm, humid environments and requires good air circulation for optimal growth.
