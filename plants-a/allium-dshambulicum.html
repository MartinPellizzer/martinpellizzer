Allium dshambulicum

Allium dshambulicum, commonly known as the Dshambul garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and clustered bulbs. The plant produces tall, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and clusters of pale purple or white flowers in late summer. Its bulbs are valued for their pungent aroma and are used in traditional medicine and cooking.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
