Anthurium globosum

Anthurium globosum, commonly known as the globe anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces colorful spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small flowers, often in shades of red, yellow, or orange. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium globosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
