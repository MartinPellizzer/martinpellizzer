Arachniodes cantilenae

Arachniodes cantilenae, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the mountain forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble spider webs, giving it its common name. The plant has a distinctive rhizome system that allows it to anchor itself to trees and absorb moisture from the air. Its leaves are typically light green with a silvery underside, providing a striking contrast.

Arachniodes cantilenae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
