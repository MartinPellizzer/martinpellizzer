Anchomanes dalzielii

Anchomanes dalzielii, commonly known as the giant water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Africa and Asia.

It features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often floating on the water surface or submerged in shallow water. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly in aquatic environments.

Anchomanes dalzielii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
