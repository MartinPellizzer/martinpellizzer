Allium bonariense var. flavum

Allium bonariense var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow ornamental onion, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, globe-shaped flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Allium bonariense var. flavum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to many garden settings.
