Atriplex julacea

Atriplex julacea, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in saline and drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it an important species for soil stabilization and land reclamation.

Atriplex julacea is also valued in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
