Amomum citrinum

Amomum citrinum, commonly known as the yellow cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elongated, yellowish fruits that resemble small capsules. These fruits contain numerous aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Amomum citrinum is valued for its distinctive fragrance and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
