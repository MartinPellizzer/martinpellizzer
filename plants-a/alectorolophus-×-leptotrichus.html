Alectorolophus × leptotrichus

Alectorolophus × leptotrichus, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Alectorolophus longiflorus and Alectorolophus leptotrichus.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists worldwide.
