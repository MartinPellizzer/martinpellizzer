Anil linifolia

Anil linifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed anil, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can withstand periods of drought.

Anil linifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
