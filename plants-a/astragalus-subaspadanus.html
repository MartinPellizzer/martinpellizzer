Astragalus subaspadanus

Astragalus subaspadanus, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stem and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Astragalus subaspadanus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitats.
