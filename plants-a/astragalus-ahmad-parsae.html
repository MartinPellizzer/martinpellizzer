Astragalus ahmad-parsae

Astragalus ahmad-parsae, commonly known as Ahmad Parsa's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitats due to its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
