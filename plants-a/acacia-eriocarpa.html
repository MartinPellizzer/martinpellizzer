Acacia eriocarpa

Acacia eriocarpa, commonly known as the soft-hairy acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, followed by woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia eriocarpa is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and ability to fix nitrogen.
