Aristida lamarckii

Aristida lamarckii, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers arranged in three rows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some regions for forage or erosion control.
