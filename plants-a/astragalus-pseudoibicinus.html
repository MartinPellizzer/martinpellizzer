Astragalus pseudoibicinus

Astragalus pseudoibicinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in arid conditions, while its flowers are often pink or violet in color.

Astragalus pseudoibicinus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
