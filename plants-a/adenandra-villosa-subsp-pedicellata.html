Adenandra villosa subsp. pedicellata

Adenandra villosa subsp.

pedicellata, commonly known as the woolly adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, greyish stems and elliptical leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.
