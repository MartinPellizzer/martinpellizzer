Aspidium puberum

Aspidium puberum, commonly known as the hairy wood fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping and produces clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves.

Aspidium puberum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
