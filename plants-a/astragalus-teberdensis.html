Astragalus teberdensis

Astragalus teberdensis, commonly known as the Turkish milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus teberdensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
