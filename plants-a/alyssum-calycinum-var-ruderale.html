Alyssum calycinum var. ruderale

Alyssum calycinum var. ruderale, commonly known as the rough alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically narrow and linear, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators in garden settings.
