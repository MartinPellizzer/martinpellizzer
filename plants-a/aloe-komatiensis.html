Aloe komatiensis

Aloe komatiensis, commonly known as Komati aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a white powdery substance called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe komatiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
