Aconitum moldavicum var. confusum

Aconitum moldavicum var. confusum, commonly known as the Moldavian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The tubular roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both ecological and cautionary interest.
