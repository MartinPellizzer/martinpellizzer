Asplenium barbadense

Asplenium barbadense, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically divided into shield-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Asplenium barbadense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
