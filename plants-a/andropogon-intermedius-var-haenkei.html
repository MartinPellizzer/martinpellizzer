Andropogon intermedius var. haenkei

Andropogon intermedius var. haenkei, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
