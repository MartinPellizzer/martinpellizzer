Alchemilla lycopodioides

Alchemilla lycopodioides, commonly known as the mossy alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

Alchemilla lycopodioides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
