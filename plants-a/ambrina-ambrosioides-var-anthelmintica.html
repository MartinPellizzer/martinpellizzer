Ambrina ambrosioides var. anthelmintica

Ambrina ambrosioides var. anthelmintica, commonly known as the anthelmintic ambrina, is a variant of the ambrina plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to combat parasitic worms. Its leaves contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
