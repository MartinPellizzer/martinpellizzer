Anthemis rosea subsp. rosea

Anthemis rosea subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the rose centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
