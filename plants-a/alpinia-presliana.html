Alpinia presliana

Alpinia presliana, commonly known as the Preslia's ginger, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Alpinia presliiana is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
