Aster × alpinoamellus

Aster × alpinoamellus, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to cold, alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its ability to tolerate frost and its ornamental value contribute to its cultivation in horticultural settings.
