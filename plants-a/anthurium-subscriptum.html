Anthurium subscriptum

Anthurium subscriptum, commonly known as the red-tipped anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a red or purple tint at the base. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is a hallmark feature of the Araceae family. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium subscriptum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
