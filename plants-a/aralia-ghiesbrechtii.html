Aralia ghiesbrechtii

Aralia ghiesbrechtii, commonly known as the Ghiesbrecht's aralia, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound, feather-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are typically followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
