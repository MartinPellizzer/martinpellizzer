Astragalus abadehensis

Astragalus abadehensis, commonly known as Abadeh milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Iran, specifically the Abadeh region, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. Astragalus abadehensis is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization.
