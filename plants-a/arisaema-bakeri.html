Arisaema bakeri

Arisaema bakeri, commonly known as Baker's arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive, often spiky inflorescence known as a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. Native to the mountainous regions of China, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are edible when mature.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species in botanical studies.
