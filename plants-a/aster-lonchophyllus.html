Aster lonchophyllus

Aster lonchophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with gray or blue. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall, range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable addition to native gardens.
