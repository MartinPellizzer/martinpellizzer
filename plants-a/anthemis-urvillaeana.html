Anthemis urvillaeana

Anthemis urvillaeana, commonly known as the Chilean chamomile, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small yellow flowers with white ray florets, typical of the genus Anthemis. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its aromatic properties make it a subject of interest in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
