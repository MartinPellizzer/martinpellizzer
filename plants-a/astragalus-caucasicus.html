Astragalus caucasicus

Astragalus caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in rocky and arid environments. It is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Astragalus caucasicus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
