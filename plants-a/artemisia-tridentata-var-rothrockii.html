Artemisia tridentata var. rothrockii

Artemisia tridentata var. rothrockii, commonly known as the Roathrock's sagebrush, is a subspecies of big sagebrush native to the western United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and silver-gray stems, which are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles, typically blooming in the spring and early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This subspecies plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
