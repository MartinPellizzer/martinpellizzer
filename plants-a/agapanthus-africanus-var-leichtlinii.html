Agapanthus africanus var. leichtlinii

Agapanthus africanus var. leichtlinii, commonly known as the blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescences that produce clusters of striking blue or purple flowers. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
