Achyranthes aspera var. sicula

Achyranthes aspera var. sicula, commonly known as Sicilian knotgrass, is a variant of the species Achyranthes aspera, which is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
