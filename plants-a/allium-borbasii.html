Allium borbasii

Allium borbasii, commonly known as the Borbas garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a dense umbel, which bloom in late summer. Allium borbasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild culinary uses in some regions.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
