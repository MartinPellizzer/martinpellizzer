Asplenium sandwichense

Asplenium sandwichense, commonly known as the Sandwich Islands spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces spore-bearing structures called soruses on the underside of its leaves. Native to the Hawaiian archipelago, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a dark green coloration and a smooth texture.

Asplenium sandwichense is an important component of Hawaii's unique flora, showcasing the adaptive diversity of ferns in tropical ecosystems.
