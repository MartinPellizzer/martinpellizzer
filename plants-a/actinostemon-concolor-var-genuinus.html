Actinostemon concolor var. genuinus

Actinostemon concolor var. genuinus, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed actinostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often glabrous, or smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with showy, petal-like bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in gardens for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
