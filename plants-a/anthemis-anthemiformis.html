Anthemis anthemiformis

Anthemis anthemiformis, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, aromatic scent, and yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
