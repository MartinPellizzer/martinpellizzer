Andropogon cyrtocladus

Andropogon cyrtocladus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.

Andropogon cyrtocladus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
