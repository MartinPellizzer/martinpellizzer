Acriulus greigiifolius

Acriulus greigiifolius, commonly known as the greenleaf acanthus, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of tubular, showy flowers in shades of red, purple, and orange, which bloom in the summer months. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
