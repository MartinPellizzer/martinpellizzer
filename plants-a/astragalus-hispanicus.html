Astragalus hispanicus

Astragalus hispanicus, commonly known as Spanish milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils.

Astragalus hispanicus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
