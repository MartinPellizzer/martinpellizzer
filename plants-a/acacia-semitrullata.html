Acacia semitrullata

Acacia semitrullata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia semitrullata is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal and sandy soils and provides habitat for native wildlife.
