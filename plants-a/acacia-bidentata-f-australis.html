Acacia bidentata f. australis

Acacia bidentata f. australis, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

This form is distinguished from other acacia species by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
