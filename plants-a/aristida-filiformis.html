Aristida filiformis

Aristida filiformis, commonly known as slender wiregrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments. Its small, spike-like flowers are typically pale green to brownish in color.

Aristida filiformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native range.
