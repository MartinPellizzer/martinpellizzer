Aspalathus complicata

Aspalathus complicata, commonly known as African red bush tea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically red or reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, red berries. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Aspalathus complicata is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used to make a popular herbal tea known for its antioxidant properties.
