Ampelopsis delavayana var. delavayana

Ampelopsis delavayana var. delavayana, commonly known as the Delavay's honeysuckle, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, often with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and berries, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
