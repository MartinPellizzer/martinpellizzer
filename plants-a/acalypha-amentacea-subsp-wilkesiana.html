Acalypha amentacea subsp. wilkesiana

Acalypha amentacea subsp.

wilkesiana, commonly known as the silverleaf ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, which are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.
