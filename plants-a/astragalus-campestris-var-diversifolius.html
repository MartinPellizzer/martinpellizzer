Astragalus campestris var. diversifolius

Astragalus campestris var. diversifolius, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with varying leaflet shapes, which contribute to its varietal name "diversifolius.". The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the genus Astragalus. It is native to the western United States and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various pollinators and herbivores.
