Asplenium venulosum

Asplenium venulosum, commonly known as the veined spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often adorned with prominent veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its spores are produced in small, clustered clusters on the underside of its leaves.

Asplenium venulosum is a hardy species that is relatively easy to cultivate in controlled environments.
