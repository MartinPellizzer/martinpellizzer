Allium pseudojaponicum

Allium pseudojaponicum, commonly known as the Japanese leek or flat leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, flat, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces umbel-like clusters of white to pale purple flowers in the spring, followed by small, bulbous fruits. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and mild garlic-like flavor, it is often cultivated in gardens for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
