Astragalus gibbosus

Astragalus gibbosus, commonly known as the globe milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, yellow to greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
