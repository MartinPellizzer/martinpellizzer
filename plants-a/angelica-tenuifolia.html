Angelica tenuifolia

Angelica tenuifolia, commonly known as slender angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the cooler regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features tall, slender stems with large, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels, typical of the carrot family.

Angelica tenuifolia is known for its strong, spicy fragrance and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
