Arundinaria excelsa

Arundinaria excelsa, commonly known as the bamboo palm or golden bamboo, is a tall, graceful plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reed-like stems that can grow up to 15 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slight yellowish or golden hue, giving it its common name.

Arundinaria excelsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
