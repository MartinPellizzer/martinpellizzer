Allium autumnale

Allium autumnale, commonly known as autumn garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in the fall. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
