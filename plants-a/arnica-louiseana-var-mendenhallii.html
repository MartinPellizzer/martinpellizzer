Arnica louiseana var. mendenhallii

Arnica louiseana var. mendenhallii, commonly known as Mendenhall's arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the southeastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are composed of many small, tubular florets surrounded by a showy, petal-like corolla.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils.
