Aconitum grandiflorum

Aconitum grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Aconitum grandiflorum is known for its toxicity due to the presence of potent alkaloids, making it both a dangerous and fascinating botanical specimen.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value despite its harmful properties.
