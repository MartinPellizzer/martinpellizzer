Acer palmatum var. reticulatum

Acer palmatum var. reticulatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with reticulate veins, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmately lobed leaves that display a striking color change from bright green in spring to vibrant red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental choice in gardens. Its delicate, upright growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a favorite for landscape design.

The varietal name "reticulatum" refers to the intricate network of veins visible on its leaves.
