Astragalus schizopterus

Astragalus schizopterus, commonly known as the splitpod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has small, hairy pods that split open to release its seeds.

Astragalus schizopterus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
