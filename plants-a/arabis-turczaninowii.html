Arabis turczaninowii

Arabis turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's rockcress, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in dry, sunny environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.
