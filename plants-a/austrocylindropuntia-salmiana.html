Austrocylindropuntia salmiana

Austrocylindropuntia salmiana, commonly known as the Salmiana columnar cactus, is a species native to Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This cactus is well-suited for xeriscaping and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
