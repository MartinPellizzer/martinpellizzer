Angophora melanoxylon

Angophora melanoxylon, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia and is characterized by its dark, aromatic timber and distinctive foliage. The tree has smooth, greyish bark that peels in strips, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in furniture and cabinetry.
