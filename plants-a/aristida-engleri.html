Aristida engleri

Aristida engleri, commonly known as the Engler's threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while the seeds are enclosed in bracts.

Aristida engleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
