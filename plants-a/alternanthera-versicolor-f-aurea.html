Alternanthera versicolor f. aurea

Alternanthera versicolor f. aurea, commonly known as the golden alternanthera, is a strikingly colorful foliage plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is prized for its vibrant, variegated leaves that display shades of red, pink, and gold. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading herb with slender stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and water features.
