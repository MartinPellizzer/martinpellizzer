Astragalus gladiatus

Astragalus gladiatus, commonly known as the swordleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas.

Astragalus gladiatus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
