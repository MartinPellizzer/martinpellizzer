Abies grandis

Abies grandis, commonly known as the white spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a striking appearance. Its needles are four-sided and have a distinctive white underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Abies grandis is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and paper production.
