Abies grandis

Abies grandis, commonly known as the grand fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, with small resinous glands. Its cones are typically cylindrical and hang downward, releasing seeds when mature.

The bark is thin and scaly, becoming thicker and darker with age.
