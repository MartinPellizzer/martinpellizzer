Allophylus natalensis

Allophylus natalensis, commonly known as Natal holly, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Allophylus natalensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
