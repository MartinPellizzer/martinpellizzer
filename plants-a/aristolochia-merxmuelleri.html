Aristolochia merxmuelleri

Aristolochia merxmuelleri, commonly known as the Mexican Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, typically green or brown in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex floral structures and some species contain toxic compounds.
