Aconitum vulparia subsp. ranunculifolium

Aconitum vulparia subsp.

ranunculifolium, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple hood-shaped flowers, which are surrounded by two large, showy petals.

The plant has a tall, upright stem with palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble those of a buttercup. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic to both humans and animals.
