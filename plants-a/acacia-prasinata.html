Acacia prasinata

Acacia prasinata, commonly known as the green acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, narrow leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Acacia prasinata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
