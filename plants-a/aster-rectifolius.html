Aster rectifolius

Aster rectifolius, commonly known as the smoothleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and smooth, giving it its common name.

It produces showy purple to blue flowers in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
