Amaranthus graecizans var. silvestris

Amaranthus graecizans var. silvestris, commonly known as wild amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, greenish or red flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their nutritional value.
