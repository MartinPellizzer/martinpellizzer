Astragalus epiglottoides

Astragalus epiglottoides, commonly known as the epiglottoid milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas in its native habitat.
