Acer pseudoplatanus var. complicatum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. complicatum, commonly known as the curly maple, is a variant of the common maple tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that often curl and twist, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its foliage displays a range of colors from bright green in summer to vibrant red or orange in autumn.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
