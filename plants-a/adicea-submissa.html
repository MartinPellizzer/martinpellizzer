Adicea submissa

Adicea submissa, commonly known as the soft-stemmed adicea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Adicea submissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
