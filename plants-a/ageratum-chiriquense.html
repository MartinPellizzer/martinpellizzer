Ageratum chiriquense

Ageratum chiriquense, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental in gardens and landscapes. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, adding visual interest.

Ageratum chiriquense is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
