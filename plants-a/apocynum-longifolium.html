Apocynum longifolium

Apocynum longifolium, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs.

It contains toxic alkaloids and has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.
