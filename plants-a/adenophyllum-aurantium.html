Adenophyllum aurantium

Adenophyllum aurantium, commonly known as the golden cup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Adenophyllum aurantium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
