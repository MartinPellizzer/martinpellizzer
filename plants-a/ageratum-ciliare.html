Ageratum ciliare

Ageratum ciliare, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue or purple flower heads that resemble small clouds, giving it its common name. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Ageratum ciliare is often used in borders, containers, and as a cut flower due to its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
