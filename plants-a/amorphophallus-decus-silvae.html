Amorphophallus decus-silvae

Amorphophallus decus-silvae, commonly known as the forest amorphophallus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains seeds, aiding in its dispersal by animals.
