Astragalus australis subsp. gerardi

Astragalus australis subsp.

gerardi, commonly known as the Gerald's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
