Anthephora elegans var. armata

Anthephora elegans var. armata, commonly known as the silky antelope's foot, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, basal leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
