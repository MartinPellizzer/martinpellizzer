Amygdalus persica var. nectarina

Amygdalus persica var. nectarina, commonly known as the nectarine, is a variety of the peach tree characterized by its smooth, fuzzy skin and sweet, juicy flesh.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is closely related to the peach (Amygdalus persica). The fruit typically ripens in late summer and is known for its rich flavor and high nutritional value. Nectarines are cultivated for both their culinary use and ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight to thrive.
