Artemisia campestris var. littorea

Artemisia campestris var. littorea, commonly known as sea wormwood, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in salt marshes and dunes. The plant has aromatic, silver-gray foliage with small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces tiny yellow flowers in panicles, which bloom in late summer.

This hardy species is well adapted to coastal environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
