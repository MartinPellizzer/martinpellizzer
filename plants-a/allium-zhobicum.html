Allium zhobicum

Allium zhobicum, commonly known as the Chinese leek or Chinese chives, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spherical, purple or pink flower heads. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in Chinese cuisine.

Allium zhobicum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
