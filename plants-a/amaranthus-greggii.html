Amaranthus greggii

Amaranthus greggii, commonly known as Gregg's amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is often green with a slight reddish tinge, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Gregg's amaranth is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Native American practices.
