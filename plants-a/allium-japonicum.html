Allium japonicum

Allium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese garlic, is a bulbous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant's bulbs are often used in traditional Asian cooking for their pungent flavor. Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
