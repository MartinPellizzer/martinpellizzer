Aspidium cinnamomeum

Aspidium cinnamomeum, commonly known as cinnamon fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arching fronds that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients.

Aspidium cinnamomeum plays an important role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
