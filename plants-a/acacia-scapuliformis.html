Acacia scapuliformis

Acacia scapuliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, often upright stems with small, narrow leaves that are usually alternate. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in spikes, attracting various pollinators.

Acacia scapuliformis is adapted to dry environments and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.
