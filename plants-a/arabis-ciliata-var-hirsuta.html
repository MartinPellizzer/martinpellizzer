Arabis ciliata var. hirsuta

Arabis ciliata var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy mouse-ear cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and weeds. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves. Its small, white or yellowish flowers appear in clusters and are typical of the cruciferous family.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
