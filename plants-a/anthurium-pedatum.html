Anthurium pedatum

Anthurium pedatum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purple tint on the underside. The plant produces striking spadices, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble a cluster of tiny flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium pedatum is popular as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
