Axia cochinchinensis

Axia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese jasmine or wild jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
