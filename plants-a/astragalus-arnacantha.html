Astragalus arnacantha

Astragalus arnacantha, commonly known as the spiked milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus arnacantha is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
