Aspalathus lotoides subsp. lagopus

Aspalathus lotoides subsp.

lagopus, commonly known as African red bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, red-brown foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and it is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The subspecies lagopus is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and growth habit compared to other subspecies of Aspalathus.
