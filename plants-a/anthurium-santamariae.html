Anthurium santamariae

Anthurium santamariae, commonly known as the Santa Maria anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spathiflorous flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is dark green with a glossy texture, and the inflorescence consists of a spathe and a spadix, typical of the genus.

Anthurium santamariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
