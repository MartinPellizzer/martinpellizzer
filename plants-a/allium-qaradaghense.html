Allium qaradaghense

Allium qaradaghense, commonly known as the Qaradagh garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes a wide variety of onions, garlics, and leeks. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are typically small and have a strong, pungent aroma similar to other Allium species.

This plant is often found in rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in temperate climates.
