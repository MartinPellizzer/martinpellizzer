Ageratina standleyi

Ageratina standleyi, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its densely packed, small white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a misty appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Ageratina standleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
