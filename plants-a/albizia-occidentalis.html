Albizia occidentalis

Albizia occidentalis, commonly known as the West African siris, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It produces long, flat pods containing flat, brown seeds.

Albizia occidentalis is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
