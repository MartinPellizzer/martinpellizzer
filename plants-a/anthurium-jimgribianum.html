Anthurium jimgribianum

Anthurium jimgribianum, commonly known as the Jimgribian arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, surrounded by a showy, colorful spathe that varies in hue from white to red.

Anthurium jimgribianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, humid environments with indirect light.
