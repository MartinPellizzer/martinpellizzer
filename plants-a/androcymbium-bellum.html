Androcymbium bellum

Androcymbium bellum, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers bloom during the wet season, adding vibrant color to the forest canopy.
