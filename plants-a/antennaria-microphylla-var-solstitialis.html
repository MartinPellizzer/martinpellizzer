Antennaria microphylla var. solstitialis

Antennaria microphylla var. solstitialis, commonly known as the summerless bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, hairy leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high elevations and cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
