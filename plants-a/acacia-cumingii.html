Acacia cumingii

Acacia cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stem and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.

Acacia cumingii is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
