Anthurium protrudens

Anthurium protrudens, commonly known as the spurred anthurium, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are usually bright green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, with the spadix often bearing a unique, spurred structure.

Anthurium protrudens thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
