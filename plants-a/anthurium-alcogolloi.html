Anthurium alcogolloi

Anthurium alcogolloi, commonly known as the Alcogollo's anthurium, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include a thick, fibrous root system and a short, stout stem that supports its large foliage.

Anthurium alcogolloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and foliage patterns.
