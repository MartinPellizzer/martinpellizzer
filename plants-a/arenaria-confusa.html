Arenaria confusa

Arenaria confusa, commonly known as the confused sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a dense rosette pattern.

Arenaria confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
