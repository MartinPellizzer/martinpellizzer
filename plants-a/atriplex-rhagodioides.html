Atriplex rhagodioides

Atriplex rhagodioides, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are adapted to store water and reduce transpiration in dry environments. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its drought tolerance.

Atriplex rhagodioides plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
