Acantholimon squarrosum

Acantholimon squarrosum, commonly known as the squarrose thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Acantholimon squarrosum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
