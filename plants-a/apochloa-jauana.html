Apochloa jauana

Apochloa jauana, commonly known as the Java grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Apochloa jauana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.
