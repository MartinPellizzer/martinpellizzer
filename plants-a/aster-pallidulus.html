Aster pallidulus

Aster pallidulus, commonly known as the pale aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with ray florets that give it a daisy-like appearance.

Aster pallidulus is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
