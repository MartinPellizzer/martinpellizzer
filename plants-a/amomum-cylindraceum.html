Amomum cylindraceum

Amomum cylindraceum, commonly known as the slender cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic rhizomes used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and economic contexts.
