Achyronia recurva

Achyronia recurva, commonly known as the curlleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that often curl at the edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Achyronia recurva plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
