Aristida virgata

Aristida virgata, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and delicate, spindly appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pink to reddish in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods, prairies, and along stream banks.

Aristida virgata is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
