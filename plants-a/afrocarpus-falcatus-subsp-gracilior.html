Afrocarpus falcatus subsp. gracilior

Afrocarpus falcatus subsp.

gracilior, commonly known as the slender yellowwood, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family. It is a tall evergreen tree characterized by its slender, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and have a leathery texture, contributing to its overall hardiness. It produces small, yellowish to brownish cones that contain the plant's seeds.

This subspecies is native to southern Africa and is valued for its durable wood and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
