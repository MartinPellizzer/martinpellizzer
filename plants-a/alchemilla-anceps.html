Alchemilla anceps

Alchemilla anceps, commonly known as the yellow tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Alchemilla anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
