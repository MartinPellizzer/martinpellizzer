Allium cepa var. multiplicans

Allium cepa var. multiplicans, commonly known as the multi-headed onion, is a botanical variant of the common onion.

It is characterized by its ability to produce multiple bulb-like structures from a single stem, giving it a unique appearance. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to Central Asia. Its leaves are typically grass-like and green, while the bulbs are white and aromatic.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild culinary uses.
