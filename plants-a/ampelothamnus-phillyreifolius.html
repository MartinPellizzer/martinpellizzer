Ampelothamnus phillyreifolius

Ampelothamnus phillyreifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh, drought-prone conditions.
