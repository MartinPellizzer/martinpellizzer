Alsocydia cordata

Alsocydia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in saturated soil conditions, with rhizomes that help in anchoring and nutrient absorption.

Alsocydia cordata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
