Achillea alpicola

Achillea alpicola, commonly known as alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often with a slight bitter taste.

Achillea alpicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
