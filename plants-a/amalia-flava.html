Amalia flava

Amalia flava, commonly known as the yellow amalia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Amalia flava is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
