Aporum macraporum

Aporum macraporum, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in mangrove forests. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize the soil and supports aquatic ecosystems.

Aporum macraporum is valued for its ecological role in maintaining wetland biodiversity.
