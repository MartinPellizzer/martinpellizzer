Achnatherum lanceolatum

Achnatherum lanceolatum, commonly known as the speargrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and is often found in prairie ecosystems.

Achnatherum lanceolatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
