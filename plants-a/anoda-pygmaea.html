Anoda pygmaea

Anoda pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of only a few inches. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of delicate blue to violet flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Anoda pygmaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
