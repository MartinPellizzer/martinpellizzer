Acoridium saccolabium

Acoridium saccolabium, commonly known as the African water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant has broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer to prevent water loss. Its flowers are small and yellow, emerging from the water surface, and it produces distinctive, boat-shaped fruits.

Acoridium saccolabium plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
