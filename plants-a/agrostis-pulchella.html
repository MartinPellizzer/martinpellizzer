Agrostis pulchella

Agrostis pulchella, commonly known as the purple false bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, spreading stems with fine, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a purplish hue, especially at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are usually pale green to brown in color.

Agrostis pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
