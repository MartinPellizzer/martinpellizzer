Aridaria longispinula

Aridaria longispinula, commonly known as the long-spined desert shrub, is a resilient plant adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It features a woody stem with dense, spiny branches that provide protection against herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are small, thick, and covered in a waxy cuticle to minimize transpiration.

Aridaria longispinula plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
