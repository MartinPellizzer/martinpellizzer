Allium fimbriatum var. abramsii

Allium fimbriatum var. abramsii, commonly known as the Abram's onion, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that store nutrients, typical of the Allium genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
