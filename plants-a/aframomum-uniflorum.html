Aframomum uniflorum

Aframomum uniflorum, commonly known as the African grain of paradise, is a tropical plant native to western Africa.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice.
