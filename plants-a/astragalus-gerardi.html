Astragalus gerardi

Astragalus gerardi, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the North American prairies.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Astragalus gerardi is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
