Alopecurus macrostachyos

Alopecurus macrostachyos, commonly known as hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and panicles of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Alopecurus macrostachyos is also used in some regions for forage and erosion control.
