Andromeda coccinea

Andromeda coccinea, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

Andromeda coccinea thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
