Andropogon laxatus var. ligulatus

Andropogon laxatus var. ligulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in full sun and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Andropogon laxatus var. ligulatus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
