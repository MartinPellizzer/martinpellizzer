Asyneuma amplexicaule subsp. aucheri

Asyneuma amplexicaule subsp.

aucheri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It features basal leaves that are narrow and clasping, giving it its specific epithet "amplexicaule.".

The flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its ecological role in pollination and seed dispersal.
