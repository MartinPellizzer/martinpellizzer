Aira articulata var. gracilis

Aira articulata var. gracilis, commonly known as slender false oat, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
