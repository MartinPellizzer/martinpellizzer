Azalea championiae

Azalea championiae, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a species native to China and belongs to the Rhododendron genus.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with dense, evergreen foliage. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
