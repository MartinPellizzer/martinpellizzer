Anemone narcissiflora subsp. biarmiensis

Anemone narcissiflora subsp.

biarmiensis, commonly known as the Mongolian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white to pale pink, with a distinctive yellow center.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
