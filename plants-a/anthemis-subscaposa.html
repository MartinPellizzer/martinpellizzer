Anthemis subscaposa

Anthemis subscaposa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its aromatic properties.
