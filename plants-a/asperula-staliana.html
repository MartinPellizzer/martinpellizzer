Asperula staliana

Asperula staliana, commonly known as the Staliana woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in rocky or woodland areas.

Asperula staliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
