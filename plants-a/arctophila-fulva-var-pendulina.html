Arctophila fulva var. pendulina

Arctophila fulva var. pendulina, commonly known as the hanging flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems that often hang downward, giving it a cascading appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
