Acampe pachyphylla

Acampe pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved acampe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a subtle beauty to its environment. Its growth habit is typically low and compact, forming a dense mat that helps prevent soil erosion.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
