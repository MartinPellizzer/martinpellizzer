Arisaema speirophyllum

Arisaema speirophyllum, commonly known as the spiral arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a short, stout rhizome with fleshy, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow, with a distinct, often unpleasant odor that attracts insect pollinators.

Arisaema speirophyllum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
