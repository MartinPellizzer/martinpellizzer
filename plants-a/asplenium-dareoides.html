Asplenium dareoides

Asplenium dareoides, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forests or on rocky surfaces. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a prominent midrib.

Asplenium dareoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in humid conditions.
