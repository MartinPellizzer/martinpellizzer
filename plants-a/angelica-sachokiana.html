Angelica sachokiana

Angelica sachokiana, commonly known as the Indian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in an umbel, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

Angelica sachokiana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
