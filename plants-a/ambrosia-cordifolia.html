Ambrosia cordifolia

Ambrosia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is often found in sandy or disturbed soils, thriving in open habitats. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Ambrosia cordifolia is noted for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a common wild plant and a potential weed in some regions.
