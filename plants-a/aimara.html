Aimara

Aimara, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blossoms in shades of red, purple, and blue. Aimara is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Its seeds are also used in traditional Andean cuisine and medicine.
