Aerangis splendida

Aerangis splendida, commonly known as the splendid orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that often display a mix of red, yellow, and orange hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, Aerangis splendida is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
