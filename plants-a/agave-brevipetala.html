Agave brevipetala

Agave brevipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial succulent characterized by its rosette-forming structure and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are relatively short and narrow, giving it a compact appearance, and it is well adapted to dry, hot climates.

Agave brevipetala is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
