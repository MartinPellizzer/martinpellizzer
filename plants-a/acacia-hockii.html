Acacia hockii

Acacia hockii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules. Acacia hockii is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is the national floral emblem of Australia.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.
