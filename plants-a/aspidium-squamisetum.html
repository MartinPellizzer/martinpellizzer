Aspidium squamisetum

Aspidium squamisetum, commonly known as the scaly wood fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically light green with a subtle scaly texture on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Aspidium squamisetum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
