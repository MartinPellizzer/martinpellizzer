Acer platanoides f. lorbergii

Acer platanoides f. lorbergii, commonly known as the Lorbergii Norway maple, is a unique form of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring large, palmate leaves with a lighter green upper surface and a darker green underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full to partial sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
