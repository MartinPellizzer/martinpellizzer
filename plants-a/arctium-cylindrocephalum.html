Arctium cylindrocephalum

Arctium cylindrocephalum, commonly known as the round-headed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, spherical flower heads that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its propagation. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
