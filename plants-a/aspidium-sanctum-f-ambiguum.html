Aspidium sanctum f. ambiguum

Aspidium sanctum f. ambiguum, commonly known as the Ambiguous Spleenwort, is a fern species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive arrangement of pinnae and pinnae pairs. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or in forest understories. Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered along the margins of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

The morphological variability of this fern contributes to its classification as a form, highlighting its adaptability to different ecological conditions.
