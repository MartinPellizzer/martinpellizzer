Amaranthus hybridus subsp. patulus

Amaranthus hybridus subsp.

patulus, commonly known as the weedy amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to purple.

The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, ribbed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a common weed in many regions.
