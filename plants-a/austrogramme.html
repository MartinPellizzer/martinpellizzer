Austrogramme

Austrogramme, commonly known as the Australian star tortoise, is a unique plant species native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Austrogramme is often found in coastal dunes and is an important part of the local ecosystem.

Its flowers are small and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
