Agropyron ciliare subsp. amurense

Agropyron ciliare subsp.

amurense, commonly known as Amur needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a rough, scaly texture.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy or loamy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. Agropyron ciliare subsp.

amurense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
