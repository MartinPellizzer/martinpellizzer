Abarema commutata

Abarema commutata, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural displays.
