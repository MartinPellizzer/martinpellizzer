Acer negundo f. petiolatum

Acer negundo f. petiolatum, commonly known as the petioled boxelder, is a variant of the boxelder tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically three to five lobes, and its slender, branching habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and wetlands.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
