Arabis atrorubens

Arabis atrorubens, commonly known as the dark red rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Arabis atrorubens thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
