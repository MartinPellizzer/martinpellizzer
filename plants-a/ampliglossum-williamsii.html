Ampliglossum williamsii

Ampliglossum williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' magnolia, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
