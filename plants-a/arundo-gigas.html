Arundo gigas

Arundo gigas, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has hollow, cylindrical stems and produces feathery plumes of small, greenish flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found near waterways and marshes.

Botanically, it is classified in the grass family Poaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
