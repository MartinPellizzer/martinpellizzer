Aspidium catacolobum

Aspidium catacolobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Aspidium catacolobum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms in its native ecosystems.
