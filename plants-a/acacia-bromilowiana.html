Acacia bromilowiana

Acacia bromilowiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia bromilowiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
