Arabis microphylla var. thompsonii

Arabis microphylla var. thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in alpine or arid environments, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental species suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
