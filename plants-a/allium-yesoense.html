Allium yesoense

Allium yesoense, commonly known as the Yesso onion, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and umbel-like flower clusters. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

Allium yesoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility in its native habitat.
