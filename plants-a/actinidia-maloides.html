Actinidia maloides

Actinidia maloides, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible when ripe but contain toxic seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and medicinal potential have made it a subject of botanical interest.
