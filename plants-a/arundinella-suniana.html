Arundinella suniana

Arundinella suniana, commonly known as the golden bamboo or sunian bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can be used in traditional crafts and construction.

Arundinella suniana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
