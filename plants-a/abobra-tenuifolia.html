Abobra tenuifolia

Abobra tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf abobra, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in tropical botany.
