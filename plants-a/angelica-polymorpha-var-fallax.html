Angelica polymorpha var. fallax

Angelica polymorpha var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often with a distinct reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are large, deeply lobed, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
