Azalea baetica

Azalea baetica, commonly known as the Spanish azalea, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas. The plant produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the spring. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Azalea baetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
