Adenocarpus complicatus subsp. bracteatus

Adenocarpus complicatus subsp.

bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
