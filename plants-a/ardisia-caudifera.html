Ardisia caudifera

Ardisia caudifera, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. These berries are not edible for humans but are a food source for various birds and small mammals.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy fruits.
