Astragalus mareoticus subsp. handiensis

Astragalus mareoticus subsp.

handiensis, commonly known as the Egyptian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Egypt and parts of the Middle East. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
