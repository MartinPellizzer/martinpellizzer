Anisostachya betsiliensis

Anisostachya betsiliensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or swampy areas.

Due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
