Adina

Adina, commonly known as Indian borage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Adina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
