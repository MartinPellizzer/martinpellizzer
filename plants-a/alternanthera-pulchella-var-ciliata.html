Alternanthera pulchella var. ciliata

Alternanthera pulchella var. ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf alternanthera, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that often display vibrant red or purple hues, especially under bright light conditions. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is popular in aquariums for its ornamental value. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it spreads through both rhizomes and seeds.

Due to its rapid growth, it requires regular pruning to prevent it from overtaking the tank.
