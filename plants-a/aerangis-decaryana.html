Aerangis decaryana

Aerangis decaryana, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom during the winter months. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a short, thick rhizome and linear leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with a contrasting lip, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
