Anthurium × lawrenceanum

Anthurium × lawrenceanum, commonly known as the flamingo flower or tailflower, is a hybrid plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as a decorative foliage plant due to its ornamental value.

Anthurium × lawrenceanum is also appreciated for its ability to purify indoor air and thrive in low-light conditions.
