Aira cespitosa var. versicolor

Aira cespitosa var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from green to pink and purple, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and prairies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
