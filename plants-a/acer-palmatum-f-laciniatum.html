Acer palmatum f. laciniatum

Acer palmatum f. laciniatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with lobed leaves, is a variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its finely dissected foliage.

It belongs to the Aceraceae family and is native to Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features delicate, trifoliate or pinnate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This cultivar prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
