Aerangis graminifolia

Aerangis graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like orchid, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that often resemble those of orchids. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and inflorescences that bear numerous tiny orchid-like blooms.
