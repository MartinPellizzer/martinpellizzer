Ansonia

Ansonia, commonly known as the Christmas fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arching fronds that resemble a Christmas tree, hence its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas. Ansonia has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its leaves are typically bright green and remain evergreen throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
