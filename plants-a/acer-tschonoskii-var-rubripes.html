Acer tschonoskii var. rubripes

Acer tschonoskii var. rubripes, commonly known as the red-stemmed maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that becomes more prominent with age. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a vibrant green upper surface and a lighter underside. In the fall, the foliage turns a striking red or orange, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

This variety is valued for its unique bark and seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice for garden and landscape design.
