Ageratina crassiramea

Ageratina crassiramea, commonly known as the thick-stemmed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and features small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

Ageratina crassiramea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flower color.
