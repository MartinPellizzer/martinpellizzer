Acrocarpus grandis

Acrocarpus grandis, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic heartwood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has a dense canopy with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is an important species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
