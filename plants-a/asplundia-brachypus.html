Asplundia brachypus

Asplundia brachypus, commonly known as the short-stemmed arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a starchy food source. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and arrowhead-shaped, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The species is valued both for its edible roots and its ornamental foliage.
