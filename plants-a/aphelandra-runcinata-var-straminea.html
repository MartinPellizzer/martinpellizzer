Aphelandra runcinata var. straminea

Aphelandra runcinata var. straminea, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
