Agrostis castellana subvar. heterophylla

Agrostis castellana subvar. heterophylla, commonly known as the Castilian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and fine, tufted foliage. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
