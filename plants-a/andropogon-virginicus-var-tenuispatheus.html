Andropogon virginicus var. tenuispatheus

Andropogon virginicus var. tenuispatheus, commonly known as narrow-leaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
