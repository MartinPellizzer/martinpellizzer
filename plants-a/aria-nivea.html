Aria nivea

Aria nivea, commonly known as the snow lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue or white flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Aria nivea thrives in well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
