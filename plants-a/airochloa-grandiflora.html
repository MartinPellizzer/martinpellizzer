Airochloa grandiflora

Airochloa grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflorous grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
