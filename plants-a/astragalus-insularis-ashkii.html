Astragalus insularis-ashkii

Astragalus insularis-ashkii, commonly known as the Ashkii milkvetch, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing deep root systems to access water. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and overgrazing, it is considered endangered in some regions.
