Aconitum bailangense

Aconitum bailangense, commonly known as Bailang monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy purple-blue flowers. The plant produces follicular fruits that contain seeds with a tuft of silky hair. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
