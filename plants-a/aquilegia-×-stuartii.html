Aquilegia × stuartii

Aquilegia × stuartii, commonly known as the Stuart's columbine, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant, often blue or purple, flower colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and it produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds via wind.

Aquilegia × stuartii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
