Agrimonia eupatoria f. fallax

Agrimonia eupatoria f. fallax, commonly known as the autumn agrimony, is a botanical variant of the common agrimony.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features upright stems with dark green, serrated leaves that turn red in the fall. Its small yellow flowers appear in late summer to early autumn, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often found in dry meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.
