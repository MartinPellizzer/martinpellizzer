Aegilops elongata

Aegilops elongata, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale green to brown florets. Aegilops elongata is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It has been studied for its potential agricultural uses and genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
