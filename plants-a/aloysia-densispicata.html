Aloysia densispicata

Aloysia densispicata, commonly known as the dense-hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense covering of fine, stiff hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

Aloysia densispicata is also valued for its fragrant foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
