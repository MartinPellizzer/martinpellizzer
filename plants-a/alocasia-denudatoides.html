Alocasia denudatoides

Alocasia denudatoides, commonly known as the naked leaf alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant features a stout, rhizomatous stem that can produce offsets, making it relatively easy to propagate. Its foliage is often adorned with subtle veins and can vary in color depending on light exposure.

Alocasia denudatoides thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
