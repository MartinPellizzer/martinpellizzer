Acridocarpus rufescens

Acridocarpus rufescens, commonly known as the red-leaved flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in yellow or orange hues, which are followed by woody, spiny fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
