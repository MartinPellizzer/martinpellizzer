Acanthocereus castellae

Acanthocereus castellae, commonly known as the Castle Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles, which are typical features of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
