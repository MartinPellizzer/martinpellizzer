Alopecurus variegatus

Alopecurus variegatus, commonly known as variable草地草, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Alopecurus variegatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
