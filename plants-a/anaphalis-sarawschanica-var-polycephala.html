Anaphalis sarawschanica var. polycephala

Anaphalis sarawschanica var. polycephala, commonly known as the "Silver Carpet" or "Pearly Weed," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and silver-white foliage. The plant features dense, woolly inflorescences that give it a fluffy appearance, often mistaken for a cloud or snow cover. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
