Avena pratensis subsp. iberica

Avena pratensis subsp.

iberica, commonly known as Spanish oat, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically 3 to 5 cm long, with leathery, awned glumes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for forage or erosion control.
