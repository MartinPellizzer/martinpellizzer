Astragalus exscapus f. scaposus

Astragalus exscapus f. scaposus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow to reddish-purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
