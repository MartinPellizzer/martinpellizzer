Acer campestre var. mollissimum

Acer campestre var. mollissimum, commonly known as the velvet maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval-shaped leaves that are typically green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, red or orange flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

It is often used in landscaping for its compact form and seasonal color changes.
