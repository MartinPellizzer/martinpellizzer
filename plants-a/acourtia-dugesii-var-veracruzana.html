Acourtia dugesii var. veracruzana

Acourtia dugesii var. veracruzana, commonly known as the Veracruz acourtia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native region.
