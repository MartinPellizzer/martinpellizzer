Atheropogon aristidoides

Atheropogon aristidoides, commonly known as the slender wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

Atheropogon aristidoides plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and serving as a food source for wildlife.
