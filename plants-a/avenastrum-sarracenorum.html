Avenastrum sarracenorum

Avenastrum sarracenorum, commonly known as the Sarracens' grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or scrub habitats. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to colonize new areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
