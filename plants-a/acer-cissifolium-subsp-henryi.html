Acer cissifolium subsp. henryi

Acer cissifolium subsp.

henryi, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China. It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
