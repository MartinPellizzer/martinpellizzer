Acacia quadricostata

Acacia quadricostata, commonly known as the four-costate acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and have a distinctive four-ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are often visited by insects and birds. Its woody stems are covered with a layer of fine, silky hairs.

Acacia quadricostata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
