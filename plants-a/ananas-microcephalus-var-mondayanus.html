Ananas microcephalus var. mondayanus

Ananas microcephalus var. mondayanus, commonly known as the dwarf pineapple, is a compact, low-growing plant native to South America.

It features a rosette of fleshy, spiky leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
