Achillea millefolium subsp. alpestris

Achillea millefolium subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its resilience to cold and drought makes it a hardy species adapted to high-altitude environments.
