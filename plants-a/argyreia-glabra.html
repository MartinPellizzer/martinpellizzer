Argyreia glabra

Argyreia glabra, commonly known as the naked lady's tears, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces distinctive blue or purple berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Argyreia glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
