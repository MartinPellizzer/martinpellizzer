Adiantum edentulum

Adiantum edentulum, commonly known as the fan-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically dark green and have a distinctive, fan-like shape. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its leaves are characterized by their lack of teeth or serrations, giving it its scientific name "edentulum," meaning "toothless.".

Adiantum edentulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid indoor conditions.
