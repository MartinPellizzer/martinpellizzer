Allosorus quadripinnatus

Allosorus quadripinnatus, commonly known as the four-pinnate bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its intricate, branched structure with small, bladder-like traps used to capture and digest aquatic prey. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss while submerged. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-poor waters, often forming dense mats on the pond bottom.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
