Arctostaphylos pringlei subsp. drupacea

Arctostaphylos pringlei subsp.

drupacea, commonly known as the Drupaceae Madrone, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, drupaceous fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
