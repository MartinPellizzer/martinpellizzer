Anthemis millefoliata

Anthemis millefoliata, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, often slightly bitter scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Yarrow has a long history in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
