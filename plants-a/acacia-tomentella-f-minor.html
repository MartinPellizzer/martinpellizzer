Acacia tomentella f. minor

Acacia tomentella f. minor, commonly known as the silver acacia or dwarf silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flower heads that are typically arranged in clusters. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
