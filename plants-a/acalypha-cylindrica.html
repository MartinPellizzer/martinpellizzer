Acalypha cylindrica

Acalypha cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to yellow. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Acalypha cylindrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
