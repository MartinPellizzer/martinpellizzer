Anil viminea

Anil viminea, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and twining habit, often climbing trees or shrubs. Its flowers have a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Anil viminea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
