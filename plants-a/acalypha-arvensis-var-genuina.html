Acalypha arvensis var. genuina

Acalypha arvensis var. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in the summer, which are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an adaptable and common weed in various agricultural settings.
