Avena hirta

Avena hirta, commonly known as slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Avena hirta is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
