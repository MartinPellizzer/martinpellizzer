Agathosma subcordata

Agathosma subcordata, commonly known as the Cape garlic.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and use in aromatherapy.

Agathosma subcordata thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.
