Achyranthes rivinifolia

Achyranthes rivinifolia, commonly known as the riverbed knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy areas, often near rivers or in floodplains, which reflects its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually greenish-white in color.
