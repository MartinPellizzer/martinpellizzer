Asparagus griffithii

Asparagus griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with slender, needle-like leaflets and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include fibrous roots and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
