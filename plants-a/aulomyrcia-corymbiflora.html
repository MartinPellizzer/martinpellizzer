Aulomyrcia corymbiflora

Aulomyrcia corymbiflora, commonly known as the clustered-flowered myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Aulomyrcia corymbiflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and coastal areas.
