Aconitum leiostachyum

Aconitum leiostachyum, commonly known as the smooth-stalked aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in a spire-like cluster at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Aconitum leiostachyum contains potent toxins and is considered highly poisonous to both humans and animals.
