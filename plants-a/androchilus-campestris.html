Androchilus campestris

Androchilus campestris, commonly known as the field orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, typically found in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip petal that aids in pollination. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Androchilus campestris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
