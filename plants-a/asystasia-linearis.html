Asystasia linearis

Asystasia linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asystasia linearis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in partial shade.
