Amaranthus blitum var. graecizans

Amaranthus blitum var. graecizans, commonly known as Greek spinach, is a leafy green vegetable native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tender, spinach-like leaves that are rich in nutrients such as iron, calcium, and vitamins A and C. The plant thrives in cool weather and is often grown as a leafy vegetable in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically harvested before flowering for optimal flavor and texture.

It is a hardy annual that prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
