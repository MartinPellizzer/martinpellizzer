Acaena sanguisorbae subsp. utrinquestrigulosa

Acaena sanguisorbae subsp.

utrinquestrigulosa, commonly known as the red burr plant, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, spiky, red-brown bracts that form conspicuous, persistent burrs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by the distinctive, prickly fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understoreys.

The species is notable for its ecological role in providing food for native birds and in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
