Acacia meiosperma

Acacia meiosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical spikes, attracting various pollinators.

Acacia meiosperma is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy soils.
