Actaea laciniata

Actaea laciniata, commonly known as the fragrant actaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs, and the leaves are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Actaea laciniata prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
