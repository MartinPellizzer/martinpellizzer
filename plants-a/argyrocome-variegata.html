Argyrocome variegata

Argyrocome variegata, commonly known as the variegated argyrocome, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly variegated leaves, which display a mix of white, yellow, and green patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
