Annona amambayensis

Annona amambayensis, commonly known as the Amazonian soursop, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a large, spiky fruit that is typically green when mature, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
