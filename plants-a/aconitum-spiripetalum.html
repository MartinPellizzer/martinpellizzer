Aconitum spiripetalum

Aconitum spiripetalum, commonly known as the spiral-petaled aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a tall, erect stem covered with palmately compound leaves that have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with five petals that are often arranged in a spiral pattern, giving it its common name.

Aconitum spiripetalum is noted for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it both dangerous and valuable in traditional medicine.
