Actinostemon concolor var. angustifolius

Actinostemon concolor var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf actinostemon, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a smooth, slightly glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and moderate growth habit.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun exposure.
