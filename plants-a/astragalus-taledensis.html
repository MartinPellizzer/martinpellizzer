Astragalus taledensis

Astragalus taledensis, commonly known as the Taled Desert Milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems through nitrogen fixation.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
