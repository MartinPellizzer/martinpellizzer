Acaena sanguisorbae subsp. antarctica

Acaena sanguisorbae subsp.

antarctica, commonly known as Antarctic bullock's heart, is a low-growing shrub native to the Southern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, spiny fruits that contain hard, dark seeds.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in subantarctic regions. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to harsh climatic conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
