Aquilegia mexicana

Aquilegia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its flowers are typically borne on tall, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Aquilegia mexicana is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
