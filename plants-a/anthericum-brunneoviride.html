Anthericum brunneoviride

Anthericum brunneoviride, commonly known as the brownish-green arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant's leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
