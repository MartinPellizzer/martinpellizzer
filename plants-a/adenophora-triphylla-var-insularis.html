Adenophora triphylla var. insularis

Adenophora triphylla var. insularis, commonly known as the Insular bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners interested in native flora.
