Alyssum lepidulum f. minoristellatum

Alyssum lepidulum f. minoristellatum, commonly known as the dwarf alpine alyssum, is a low-growing plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, typical of many members of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh, cold climates.
