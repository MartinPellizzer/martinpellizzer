Astragalus coccineus

Astragalus coccineus, commonly known as the scarlet milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.

Astragalus coccineus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
