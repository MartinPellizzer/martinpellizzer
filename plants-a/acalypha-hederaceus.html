Acalypha hederaceus

Acalypha hederaceus, commonly known as creeping groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed bracts that give it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Acalypha hederaceus is often used in ornamental gardening for its foliage rather than its flowers.
