Astragalus permiensis var. sacrimontis

Astragalus permiensis var. sacrimontis, commonly known as the Permiensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences.
