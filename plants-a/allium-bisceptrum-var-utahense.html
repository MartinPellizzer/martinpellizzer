Allium bisceptrum var. utahense

Allium bisceptrum var. utahense, commonly known as the Utah garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States, particularly Utah and parts of Arizona. The plant features a bulbous base with a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in rock gardens or dry landscapes.
