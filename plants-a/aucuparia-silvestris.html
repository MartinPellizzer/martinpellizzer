Aucuparia silvestris

Aucuparia silvestris, commonly known as the wild cherry tree, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
