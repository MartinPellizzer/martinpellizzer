Astragalus lentiginosus var. sierrae

Astragalus lentiginosus var. sierrae, commonly known as Sierra milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
