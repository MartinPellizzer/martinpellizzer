Astrocaryum aureopictum

Astrocaryum aureopictum, commonly known as the golden-painted palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often adorned with golden or yellow markings. The plant produces large, yellow fruits that contain a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, lowland areas.

The botanical features of this palm make it a distinctive and valuable component of its native ecosystems.
