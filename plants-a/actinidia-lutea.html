Actinidia lutea

Actinidia lutea, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and turn vibrant colors in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Actinidia lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
