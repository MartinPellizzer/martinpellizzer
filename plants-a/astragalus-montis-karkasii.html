Astragalus montis-karkasii

Astragalus montis-karkasii, commonly known as the Karkas Mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus montis-karkasii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme environments.
