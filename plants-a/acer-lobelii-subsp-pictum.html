Acer lobelii subsp. pictum

Acer lobelii subsp.

pictum, commonly known as the painted maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow during the fall.

The leaves are typically palmately lobed with a serrated margin and can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
