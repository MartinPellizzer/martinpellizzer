Acisanthera alata

Acisanthera alata, commonly known as the winged acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its distinctive winged pods that develop after flowering. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
