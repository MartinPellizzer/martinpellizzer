Aster namaquanus

Aster namaquanus, commonly known as the Namaqua starling, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Aster namaquanus is often found in rocky areas and is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
