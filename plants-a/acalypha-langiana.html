Acalypha langiana

Acalypha langiana, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Acalypha langiana is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and ability to attract birds and insects.
