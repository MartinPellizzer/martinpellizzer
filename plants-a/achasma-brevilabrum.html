Achasma brevilabrum

Achasma brevilabrum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-brown flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The orchid has a complex root system adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.
