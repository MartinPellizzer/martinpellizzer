Apoxyanthera pubescens

Apoxyanthera pubescens, commonly known as the hairy anther plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Apoxyanthera pubescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
