Athyrium taquetii

Athyrium taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, compound fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and mountainous regions. Its spores are released from small, clustered sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium taquetii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
