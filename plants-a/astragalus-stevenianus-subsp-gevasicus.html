Astragalus stevenianus subsp. gevasicus

Astragalus stevenianus subsp.

gevasicus, commonly known as the Steven's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of leaflets that are usually hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
