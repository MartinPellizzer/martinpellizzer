Amorpha herbacea var. boyntonii

Amorpha herbacea var. boyntonii, commonly known as the Boynton's false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its compound leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
