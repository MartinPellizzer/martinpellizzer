Albizia milletii

Albizia milletii, commonly known as the African bean tree, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its roots. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Albizia milletii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in arid environments.
