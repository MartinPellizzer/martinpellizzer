Aechmea candida

Aechmea candida, commonly known as the white-tipped bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant's foliage is typically green with white or silver markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red flowers that bloom in the spring.

Aechmea candida is popular as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea candida has broad, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a waxy, pale green color and a slightly pointed tip.

The plant has a short, thick stem covered in a dense, fibrous bark that is usually grayish in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, white flowers that bloom during the summer months, and it is primarily pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its rocky or soil-based habitat.
