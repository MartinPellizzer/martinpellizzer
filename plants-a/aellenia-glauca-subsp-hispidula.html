Aellenia glauca subsp. hispidula

Aellenia glauca subsp.

hispidula, commonly known as the woolly bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its subspecies status highlights subtle differences in morphology compared to the main species, including a more hairy or pubescent appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
