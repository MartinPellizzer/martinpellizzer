Achyrocline pumila

Achyrocline pumila, commonly known as the silky achyrocline, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in terminal inflorescences, often displaying shades of white or pale purple.

Achyrocline pumila is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
