Ardisia ficifolia

Ardisia ficifolia, commonly known as the evergreen boxwood, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Ardisia ficifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and compact growth habit.
