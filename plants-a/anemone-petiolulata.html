Anemone petiolulata

Anemone petiolulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, rounded center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are narrowly ovate with serrated edges, and its stems are hairy and often branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
