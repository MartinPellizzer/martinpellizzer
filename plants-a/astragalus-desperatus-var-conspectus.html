Astragalus desperatus var. conspectus

Astragalus desperatus var. conspectus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in low-nutrient, drought-prone environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
