Amaryllis banksiana

Amaryllis banksiana, commonly known as the naked Lady, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, powdery coating. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer or early autumn. Its bulbs are perennial, and the plant is known for its ability to store water in its leaves and stem.

Amaryllis banksiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
