Anisopappus davyi var. bampsianus

Anisopappus davyi var. bampsianus, commonly known as the Bampsian silverleaf, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in arid and semi-arid environments.
