Alyssum tenium var. lanceolatum

Alyssum tenium var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically linear to lanceolate in shape, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
