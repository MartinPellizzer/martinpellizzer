Andropogon scabriflorus

Andropogon scabriflorus, commonly known as roughflower bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture on both sides. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, pale blue to greenish flowers.

Andropogon scabriflorus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
