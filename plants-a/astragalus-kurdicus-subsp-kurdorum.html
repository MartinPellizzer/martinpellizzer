Astragalus kurdicus subsp. kurdorum

Astragalus kurdicus subsp.

kurdorum, commonly known as Kurdish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct genetic and morphological traits compared to other Astragalus species.
