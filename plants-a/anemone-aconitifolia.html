Anemone aconitifolia

Anemone aconitifolia, commonly known as the monkshood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically deep blue or purple, with a distinctive hood-like structure resembling a monk's habit. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, often with a waxy surface and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Anemone aconitifolia is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
