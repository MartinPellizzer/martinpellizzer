Apium rapaceum

Apium rapaceum, commonly known as the common parsley weed, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features finely divided, parsley-like leaves and small, white to greenish flowers that appear in umbrella-shaped clusters. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their digestive properties.

Apium rapaceum is also considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
