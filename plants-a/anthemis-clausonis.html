Anthemis clausonis

Anthemis clausonis, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow, with a strong, aromatic scent, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal and rocky environments.
