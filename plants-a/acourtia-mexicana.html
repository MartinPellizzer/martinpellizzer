Acourtia mexicana

Acourtia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and fleshy, green stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Its adaptations to dry environments include thick leaves and a shallow root system.
