Anthriscus ruprechtii

Anthriscus ruprechtii, commonly known as Ruprecht's cow parsley, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white umbels of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It blooms in early spring, producing a delicate, aromatic fragrance.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
