Andropogon depauperatus

Andropogon depauperatus, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a warm-season perennial grass that typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed sites. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and forage for wildlife.

Its adaptability makes it a useful plant in restoration and landscaping projects.
