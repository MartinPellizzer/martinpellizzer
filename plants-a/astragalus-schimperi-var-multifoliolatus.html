Astragalus schimperi var. multifoliolatus

Astragalus schimperi var. multifoliolatus, commonly known as the Ethiopian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, semi-desert conditions.

This variant is distinguished by its more complex foliage compared to other Astragalus species.
