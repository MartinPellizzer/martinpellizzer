Allium janthinum

Allium janthinum, commonly known as the sea onion or sea garlic, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to saline and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or coastal habitats. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a culinary and medicinal plant.

Allium janthinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
