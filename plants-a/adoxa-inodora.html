Adoxa inodora

Adoxa inodora, commonly known as the fragrant false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered flowers with a distinctive, somewhat fragrant aroma. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent central spur. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are alternate and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
