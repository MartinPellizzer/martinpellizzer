Antirrhinum siculum

Antirrhinum siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Antirrhinum siculum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
