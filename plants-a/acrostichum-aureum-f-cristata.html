Acrostichum aureum f. cristata

Acrostichum aureum f. cristata, commonly known as the crested golden fern, is a cultivar of the golden fern species.

It is characterized by its unique, flattened, and often curled fronds that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, warm environments. Its fronds are typically bright yellow to golden in color, with a textured surface.

This fern is often cultivated for its striking visual appeal in shaded garden settings.
