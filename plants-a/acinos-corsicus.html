Acinos corsicus

Acinos corsicus, commonly known as Corsican thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny leaves and purple-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and the flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical head.

Acinos corsicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
