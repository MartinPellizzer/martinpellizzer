Asarum villisepalum

Asarum villisepalum, commonly known as the hairy-sepaled asarum, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-brown in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
