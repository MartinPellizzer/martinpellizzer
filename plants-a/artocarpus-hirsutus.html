Artocarpus hirsutus

Artocarpus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a fruit that is typically green when immature and turns yellow or orange when mature. The fruit contains a starchy, edible pulp surrounded by a fibrous husk.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and used in traditional cuisines.
