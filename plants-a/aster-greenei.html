Aster greenei

Aster greenei, commonly known as the Greene's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
