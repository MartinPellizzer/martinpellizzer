Aniba pedicellata

Aniba pedicellata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Aniba pedicellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions where it can outcompete native vegetation.
