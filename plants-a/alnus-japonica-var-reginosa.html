Alnus japonica var. reginosa

Alnus japonica var. reginosa, commonly known as the Japanese alder, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn and its catkin-like flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has a shallow root system, making it adaptable to a variety of soil types, including moist and well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

The tree also provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.
