Armeria ruscinonensis subsp. ruscinonensis

Armeria ruscinonensis subsp.

ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonese thrift, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers.

The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy and drought-tolerant nature.
