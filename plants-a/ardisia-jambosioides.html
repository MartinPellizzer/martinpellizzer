Ardisia jambosioides

Ardisia jambosioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
