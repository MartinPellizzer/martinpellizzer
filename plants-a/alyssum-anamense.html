Alyssum anamense

Alyssum anamense, commonly known as the Anam Mountains alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alyssum anamense thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
