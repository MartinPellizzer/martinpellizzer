Astragalus monspessulanus var. teresianus

Astragalus monspessulanus var. teresianus, commonly known as the Spanish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and soil stabilization.
