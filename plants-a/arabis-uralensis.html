Arabis uralensis

Arabis uralensis, commonly known as the Ural rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and white or pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces rounded, capsule-like fruits.

Arabis uralensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems within its native habitats.
