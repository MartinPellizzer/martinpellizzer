Astragalus ghaznianus

Astragalus ghaznianus, commonly known as the Ghaznian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its root system is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
