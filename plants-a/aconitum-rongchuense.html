Aconitum rongchuense

Aconitum rongchuense, commonly known as Rongchu monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a hand.

Aconitum rongchuense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and medicinal significance.
