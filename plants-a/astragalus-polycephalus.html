Astragalus polycephalus

Astragalus polycephalus, commonly known as the many-headed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments.

Astragalus polycephalus is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
