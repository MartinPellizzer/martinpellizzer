Artemisia dzevanovskyi

Artemisia dzevanovskyi, commonly known as the Dzevanovsky's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in composite inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
