Alpinia latifolia

Alpinia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are harvested for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Alpinia latifolia thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental and therapeutic uses.
