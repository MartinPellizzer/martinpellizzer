Arum besserianum

Arum besserianum, commonly known as the Besserianum arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically green or yellowish, with a small, inconspicuous female flower at the base of the spadix.

Arum besserianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm, humid climates.
