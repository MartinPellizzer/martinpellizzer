Amberboa indica

Amberboa indica, commonly known as the Indian champa or champa flower, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Amberboa indica is also valued in perfumery for its delicate and sweet scent.
