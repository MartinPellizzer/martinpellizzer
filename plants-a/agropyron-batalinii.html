Agropyron batalinii

Agropyron batalinii, commonly known as the Batalin's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Agropyron batalinii is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
