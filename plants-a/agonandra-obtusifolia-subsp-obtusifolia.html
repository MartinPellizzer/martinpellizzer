Agonandra obtusifolia subsp. obtusifolia

Agonandra obtusifolia subsp.

obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved Agonandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It features simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically glabrous and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in axillary clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
