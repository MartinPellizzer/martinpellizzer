Aralia elata f. inermis

Aralia elata f. inermis, commonly known as the white-veined aralia, is a variant of the tall aralia species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves with white veins and a striking, spire-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries, which are an important food source for wildlife.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
