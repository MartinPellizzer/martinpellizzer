Aphloia theiformis var. minima

Aphloia theiformis var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf candleplant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features compact, rounded stems that branch out into a candle-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in dry climates.
