Acalypha mapirensis var. scabra

Acalypha mapirensis var. scabra, commonly known as rough mapire hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, violet to pink bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences, which are often surrounded by the colorful bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas in the wild.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
