Achyranthes mucronata

Achyranthes mucronata, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Achyranthes mucronata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
