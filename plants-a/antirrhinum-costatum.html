Antirrhinum costatum

Antirrhinum costatum, commonly known as the Costate Snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Antirrhinum costatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
