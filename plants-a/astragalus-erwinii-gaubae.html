Astragalus erwinii-gaubae

Astragalus erwinii-gaubae, commonly known as the Erwin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States, particularly found in Arizona and New Mexico. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow to green pea-like flowers, and its leaves are compound with small, oval leaflets.

It is known for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and contributing to soil stability in its native habitats.
