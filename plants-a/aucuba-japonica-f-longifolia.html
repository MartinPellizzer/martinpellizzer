Aucuba japonica f. longifolia

Aucuba japonica f. longifolia, commonly known as the Japanese aucuba with long leaves, is a variant of the aucuba plant characterized by its elongated, glossy foliage.

This evergreen shrub is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture and can grow quite long, sometimes reaching up to 12 inches in length. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
