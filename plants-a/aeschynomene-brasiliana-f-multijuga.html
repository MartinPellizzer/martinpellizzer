Aeschynomene brasiliana f. multijuga

Aeschynomene brasiliana f. multijuga, commonly known as the Brazilian cowpea or velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and use in phytoremediation.
