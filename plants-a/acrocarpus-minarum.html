Acrocarpus minarum

Acrocarpus minarum, commonly known as the mountain bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, and its leaves are long and narrow with a smooth surface.
