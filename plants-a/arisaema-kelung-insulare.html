Arisaema kelung-insulare

Arisaema kelung-insulare, commonly known as the island arrowhead, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and marshy areas. Its leaves are arrowhead-shaped, with a distinctive deep green color and a rough texture.

Arisaema kelung-insulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its striking foliage and unusual floral structure.
