Aphelexis candollei

Aphelexis candollei, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading canopy and clusters of small, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and can grow up to 10 meters in height under optimal conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in full sun and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
