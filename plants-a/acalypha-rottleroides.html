Acalypha rottleroides

Acalypha rottleroides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redleaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep red to bronze, often with a contrasting lighter underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
