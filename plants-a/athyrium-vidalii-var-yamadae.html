Athyrium vidalii var. yamadae

Athyrium vidalii var. yamadae, commonly known as the Yamada's lady fern, is a fern species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Athyriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brownish, hairy tissue.

The varietal form, yamadae, is distinguished by specific morphological traits that set it apart from the typical variety of Athyrium vidalii.
