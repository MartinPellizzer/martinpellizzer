Asparagus verrucosus

Asparagus verrucosus, commonly known as the rough asparagus or hairy asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, woody stems that are often covered in small, rough bumps or warts. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a rough texture.

Asparagus verrucosus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
