Anemone coronaria var. ventreana

Anemone coronaria var. ventreana, commonly known as the Red Valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and grows in compact, low mounds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
