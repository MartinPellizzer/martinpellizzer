Avenastrum insubricum

Avenastrum insubricum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Avenastrum insubricum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
