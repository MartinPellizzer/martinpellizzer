Acalypha websteri

Acalypha websteri, commonly known as the red veined strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red veins on its heart-shaped leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
