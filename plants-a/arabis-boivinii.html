Arabis boivinii

Arabis boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and appear in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its rugged environment.

Arabis boivinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid and high-altitude areas.
