Allantodia petri

Allantodia petri, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features a slender, upright stem with opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Allantodia petri is noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy addition to rocky gardens.
