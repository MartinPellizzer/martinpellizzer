Aster amellus subsp. amellus

Aster amellus subsp.

amellus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads in the fall, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
