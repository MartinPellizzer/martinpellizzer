Abarema campestris

Abarema campestris, commonly known as the field abarema, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its persistence in certain habitats.

Abarema campestris is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which enhances soil fertility.
