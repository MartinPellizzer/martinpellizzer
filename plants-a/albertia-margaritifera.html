Albertia margaritifera

Albertia margaritifera, commonly known as the pearlflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble small pearls. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.
