Astragalus densifolius subsp. densifolius

Astragalus densifolius subsp.

densifolius, commonly known as the denseleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with dense, small leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by narrow, pod-like fruits. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its root system is fibrous and can contribute to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
