Arisaema monophyllum f. integrum

Arisaema monophyllum f. integrum, commonly known as the Smoothleaf Snake's Head, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, contrasting with the spadix and spathe of its dramatic inflorescence. Native to the mountainous regions of southern China, this plant thrives in cool, shaded, and moist environments. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
