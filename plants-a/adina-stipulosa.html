Adina stipulosa

Adina stipulosa, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
