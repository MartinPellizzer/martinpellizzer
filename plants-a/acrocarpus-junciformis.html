Acrocarpus junciformis

Acrocarpus junciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody fruits containing seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its durable wood, which has been traditionally used for construction and furniture.
