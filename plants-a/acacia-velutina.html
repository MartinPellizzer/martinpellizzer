Acacia velutina

Acacia velutina, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of leaflets.

Acacia velutina is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
