Astragalus splendens var. richardsonii

Astragalus splendens var. richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and soil stabilization.
