Ageratum tomentosum

Ageratum tomentosum, commonly known as fuzzy blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray foliage and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Ageratum tomentosum is a hardy annual that attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
