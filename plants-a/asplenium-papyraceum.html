Asplenium papyraceum

Asplenium papyraceum, commonly known as the paper fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental plants. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can be used in paper-making processes.

Asplenium papyraceum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in cultural practices.
