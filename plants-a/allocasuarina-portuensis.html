Allocasuarina portuensis

Allocasuarina portuensis, commonly known as the Port Jackson she-oak, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
