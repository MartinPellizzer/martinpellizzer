Arundina pulchella

Arundina pulchella, commonly known as the purple bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple, during the summer months. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a dense, grass-like structure, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Arundina pulchella is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
