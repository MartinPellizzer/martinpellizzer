Artocarpus rotundus

Artocarpus rotundus, commonly known as the round-fruit breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces large, round, green fruits that turn yellow when mature. The fruit has a thick, fibrous outer layer and a starchy, edible pulp inside. The tree is valued for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
