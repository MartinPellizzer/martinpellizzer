Astragalus pseudoamygdalinus

Astragalus pseudoamygdalinus, commonly known as the false almond milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low water availability.

Due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
