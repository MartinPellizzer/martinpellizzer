Astragalus taeckholmianus

Astragalus taeckholmianus, commonly known as the Taeckholm's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small leaflets.

It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to rocky soils, often growing in high-altitude areas.
