Astragalus hissaricus

Astragalus hissaricus, commonly known as the Central Asian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Astragalus hissaricus plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the broader Astragalus genus, which includes many species with medicinal and ecological significance.
