Anacyclus pyrethrum

Anacyclus pyrethrum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Anacyclus pyrethrum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
