Antirrhinum barrelieri var. latifolium

Antirrhinum barrelieri var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and tall spikes of bright red, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

The variety is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other snapdragon species, enhancing its ornamental value.
