Anisostachya maculata

Anisostachya maculata, commonly known as the spotted grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage with prominent purple or brown markings. The plant produces tall, slender inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both ecological and decorative settings.
