Agrostis petelotii

Agrostis petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's bentgrass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas or along riverbanks. The plant has fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
