Astragalus geminanus

Astragalus geminanus, commonly known as the twinleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with two leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe environments.

Astragalus geminanus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
