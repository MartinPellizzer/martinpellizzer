Aspilia glaziovii

Aspilia glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's torchflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky yellow flowers that resemble torches. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its unique, elongated inflorescence. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in full sun.

Aspilia glaziovii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
