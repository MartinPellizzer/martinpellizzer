Acacia fragrantissima

Acacia fragrantissima, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
