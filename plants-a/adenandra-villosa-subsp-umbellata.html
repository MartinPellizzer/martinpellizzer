Adenandra villosa subsp. umbellata

Adenandra villosa subsp.

umbellata, commonly known as the hairy adenandra, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbellate clusters, which are typical of the Rutaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
