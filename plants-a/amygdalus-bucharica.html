Amygdalus bucharica

Amygdalus bucharica, commonly known as the Central Asian peach or Bukhara peach, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and fuzzy, yellowish-green fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its fruit, while edible, is smaller and less sweet compared to the common peach.

Amygdalus bucharica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
