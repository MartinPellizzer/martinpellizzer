Arabis paniculata var. parviflora

Arabis paniculata var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal rosettes of silvery-green, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
