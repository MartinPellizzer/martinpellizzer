Aphanochilus pilosus

Aphanochilus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy leaf fern, is a small, terrestrial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy fronds that give it a soft, textured appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Aphanochilus pilosus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
