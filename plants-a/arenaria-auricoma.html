Arenaria auricoma

Arenaria auricoma, commonly known as the auriculate sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

Arenaria auricoma plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky and arid environments.
