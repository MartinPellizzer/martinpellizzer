Agapetes miniata

Agapetes miniata, commonly known as the dwarf silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or white bloom. The plant produces bell-shaped, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, dark blue berries.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
