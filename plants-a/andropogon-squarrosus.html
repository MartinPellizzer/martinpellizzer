Andropogon squarrosus

Andropogon squarrosus, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Andropogon squarrosus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
