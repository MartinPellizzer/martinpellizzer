Abarema josephi

Abarema josephi, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble peacock feathers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural collections and botanical gardens.
