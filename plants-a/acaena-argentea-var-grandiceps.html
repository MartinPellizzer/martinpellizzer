Acaena argentea var. grandiceps

Acaena argentea var. grandiceps, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features upright stems with silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a cluster of hooked achenes, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and wetlands.
