Argyrella richardsiae

Argyrella richardsiae, commonly known as the Richards' silverleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-green, velvety foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Argyrella richardsiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
