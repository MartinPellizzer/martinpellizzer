Aloe sanguinalis

Aloe sanguinalis, commonly known as the red aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically reddish or pinkish in color, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Aloe sanguinalis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
