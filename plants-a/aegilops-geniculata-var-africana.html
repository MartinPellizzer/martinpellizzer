Aegilops geniculata var. africana

Aegilops geniculata var. africana, commonly known as the African wild barley, is a wild grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for genetic improvement in cultivated barley species.
