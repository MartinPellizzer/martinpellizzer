Achillea purpurea

Achillea purpurea, commonly known as purple yarrow, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

Achillea purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
