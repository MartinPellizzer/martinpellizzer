Aloe uigensis

Aloe uigensis, commonly known as the Uigensian aloe, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Aloe uigensis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant biology.
