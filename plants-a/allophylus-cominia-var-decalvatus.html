Allophylus cominia var. decalvatus

Allophylus cominia var. decalvatus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved holly, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often smooth and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a distinctive, almost waxy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
