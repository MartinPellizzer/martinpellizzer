Alstroemeria nivea

Alstroemeria nivea, commonly known as the white alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blooms that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Alstroemeria nivea is also valued for its long-lasting flowers and low maintenance requirements.
