Aster radula var. biflorus

Aster radula var. biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences that typically bear two flowers per head. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
