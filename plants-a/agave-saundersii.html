Agave saundersii

Agave saundersii, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, waxy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Agave saundersii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in the production of tequila.
