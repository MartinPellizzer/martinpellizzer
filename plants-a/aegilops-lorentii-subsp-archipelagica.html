Aegilops lorentii subsp. archipelagica

Aegilops lorentii subsp.

archipelagica, commonly known as the island wild rye, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in loose clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
