Andropogon saccharoides subsp. genuinus

Andropogon saccharoides subsp.

genuinus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for wildlife.
