Acridocarpus humbertii

Acridocarpus humbertii, commonly known as the Madagascar fan-palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, winged fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
