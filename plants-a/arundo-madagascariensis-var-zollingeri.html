Arundo madagascariensis var. zollingeri

Arundo madagascariensis var. zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger reed, is a variant of the arundo plant native to Madagascar.

It is a tall, perennial grass that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its stiff, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is adapted to thrive in wet or marshy environments, often found near water bodies. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its rapid growth and high biomass make it both useful for erosion control and a potential invasive species in non-native habitats.
