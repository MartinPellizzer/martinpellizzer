Apium commersonii

Apium commersonii, commonly known as wild celery or southern parsley, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters called umbels. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Apium commersonii is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in cooking or traditional medicine.
