Ammodenia oblongifolia var. maxima

Ammodenia oblongifolia var. maxima, commonly known as the broad-leaved ammodenia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its woody stems are typically branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
