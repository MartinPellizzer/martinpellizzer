Arundarbor capitata

Arundarbor capitata, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow to orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
