Astragalus carduchorum subsp. mandaliensis

Astragalus carduchorum subsp.

mandaliensis, commonly known as the Mandali milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is fibrous and often extensive, aiding in nutrient uptake from poor soils.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
