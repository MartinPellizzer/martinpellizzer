Androcymbium latifolium

Androcymbium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, which is often rich in organic matter.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
