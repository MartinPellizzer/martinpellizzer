Avena fatua var. macrotricha

Avena fatua var. macrotricha, commonly known as the giant oat, is a variant of the wild oat species characterized by its larger size and more robust growth habit.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields. The plant features slender, erect stems with long, narrow leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, each bearing a single floret.

This variant is notable for its potential as a weed in cultivated areas due to its competitive growth and adaptability.
