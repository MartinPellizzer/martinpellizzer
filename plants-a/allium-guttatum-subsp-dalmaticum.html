Allium guttatum subsp. dalmaticum

Allium guttatum subsp.

dalmaticum, commonly known as the Dalmatian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose umbel.

The plant is characterized by its bulbous structure, which is typically smaller than that of common garlic. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional culinary uses in local cuisines.
