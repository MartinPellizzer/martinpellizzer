Aquilegia vulgaris f. sicula

Aquilegia vulgaris f. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian Columbine, is a compact, herbaceous perennial with distinctive spurred petals.

It features bright red or orange flowers with long, narrow sepals that resemble horns, typical of the genus Aquilegia. The plant has palmate leaves divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
