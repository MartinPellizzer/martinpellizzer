Aldina macrophylla

Aldina macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved aldina, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
