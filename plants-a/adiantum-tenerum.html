Adiantum tenerum

Adiantum tenerum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green and have a distinctive, almost feather-like appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, moist soil. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a key feature of fern reproduction.

Adiantum tenerum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.
