Anthemis tinctoria var. sancti-johannis

Anthemis tinctoria var. sancti-johannis, commonly known as Saint John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as meadows and rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
