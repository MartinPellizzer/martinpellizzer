Achyrophorus valdesii

Achyrophorus valdesii, commonly known as the Valdes' orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring intricate petals and a specialized lip that plays a role in pollination. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or mountainous regions. Its growth is slow, and it requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
