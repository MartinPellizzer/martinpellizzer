Aristida purpurea var. laxiflora

Aristida purpurea var. laxiflora, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences that bear purple to pink bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and it is drought-tolerant. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in harsh conditions.
