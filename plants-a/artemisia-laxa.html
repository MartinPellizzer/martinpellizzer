Artemisia laxa

Artemisia laxa, commonly known as silver sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Artemisia laxa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
