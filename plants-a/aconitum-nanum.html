Aconitum nanum

Aconitum nanum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Aconitum nanum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

However, it is highly toxic, containing potent alkaloids that can be dangerous to both humans and animals.
