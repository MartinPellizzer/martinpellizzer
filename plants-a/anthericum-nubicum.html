Anthericum nubicum

Anthericum nubicum, commonly known as the desert arum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a spadix, a type of inflorescence, surrounded by a spathe, which is typically green or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture.

Anthericum nubicum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
