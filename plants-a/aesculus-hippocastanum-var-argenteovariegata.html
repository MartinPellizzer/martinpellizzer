Aesculus hippocastanum var. argenteovariegata

Aesculus hippocastanum var. argenteovariegata, commonly known as the variegated horse chestnut, is a cultivar of the horse chestnut tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and feature a striking variegation of white and green patterns. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped white flowers in spring, which are followed by spiny, urn-shaped fruits. It is typically grown as an ornamental tree in gardens and parks due to its aesthetic appeal.

The variegated leaves make it a popular choice for adding visual interest to landscapes.
