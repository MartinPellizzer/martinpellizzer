Acacia platyloba

Acacia platyloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its flat, broad leaves that are often arranged in pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia platyloba is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
