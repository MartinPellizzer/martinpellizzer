Abies homolepis

Abies homolepis, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its dense, dark green needles that are slightly curved. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that hang downward, releasing seeds in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its foliage and structure make it a popular choice for ornamental planting in temperate gardens.
