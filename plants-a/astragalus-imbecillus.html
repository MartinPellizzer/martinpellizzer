Astragalus imbecillus

Astragalus imbecillus, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.
