Adenophora triphylla f. princeps

Adenophora triphylla f. princeps, commonly known as the Prince's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface, and its flowers are typically bell-shaped, ranging from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Campanulaceae family.
