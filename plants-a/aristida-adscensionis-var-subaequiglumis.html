Aristida adscensionis var. subaequiglumis

Aristida adscensionis var. subaequiglumis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved threeawn, is a grass species characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves.

It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with bristly hairs, typical of the grass family Poaceae. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert and grassland ecosystems.
