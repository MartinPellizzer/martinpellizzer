Ardisia decipiens

Ardisia decipiens, commonly known as the False Beggarticks, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and colorful fruit.

Ardisia decipiens prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
