Anthurium manuanum

Anthurium manuanum, commonly known as the Manu anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spadix and spatula-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences that are often brightly colored, with a spathe that can range from green to red. Its foliage is typically glossy and elongated, contributing to its ornamental value.

Anthurium manuanum thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
