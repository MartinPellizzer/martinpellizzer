Aspidium sieberi

Aspidium sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, feather-like fronds that are often submerged or floating on the water surface. Its rhizomes are creeping and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Aspidium sieberi plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering impurities and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
