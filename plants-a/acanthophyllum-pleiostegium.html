Acanthophyllum pleiostegium

Acanthophyllum pleiostegium, commonly known as the many-stemmed thorned plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, it is well adapted to harsh, dry environments. The plant has small, triangular leaves and produces clusters of greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

It is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
