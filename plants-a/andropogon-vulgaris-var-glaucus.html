Andropogon vulgaris var. glaucus

Andropogon vulgaris var. glaucus, commonly known as blue bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairie habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
