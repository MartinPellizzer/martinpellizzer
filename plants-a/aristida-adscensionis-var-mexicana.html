Aristida adscensionis var. mexicana

Aristida adscensionis var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, tufted inflorescences that resemble small plumes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native gardens. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and forage for native wildlife.
