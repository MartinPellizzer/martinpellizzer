Achyranthes radicans

Achyranthes radicans, commonly known as spreading knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a robust root system and erect, branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds.

It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along waterways.
