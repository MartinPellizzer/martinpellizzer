Anthericum articulatum

Anthericum articulatum, commonly known as the artichoke plant, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the family Alstroemeriaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in panicles, and it is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

Anthericum articulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
