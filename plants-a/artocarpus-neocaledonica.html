Artocarpus neocaledonica

Artocarpus neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonia breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The tree produces a large, round fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer and a starchy, edible interior. The leaves are large, simple, and alternate, often with a rough texture.

This species is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and has potential for cultivation in tropical regions.
