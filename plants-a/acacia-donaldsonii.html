Acacia donaldsonii

Acacia donaldsonii, commonly known as the Christmas Champa, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage composed of small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the winter months, which are highly aromatic and attract a variety of pollinators. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for use in gardens and as a ornamental plant.

Acacia donaldsonii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in coastal environments with well-drained soils.
