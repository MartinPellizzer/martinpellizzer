Amaryllis × punctata

Amaryllis × punctata, commonly known as the spotted amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papilio.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, showy blooms that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Amaryllis × punctata is a popular choice for gardeners due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
