Aloysia urticoides

Aloysia urticoides, commonly known as the rough verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in coastal and dry regions.
