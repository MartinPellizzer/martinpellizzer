Arisaema maxwellii

Arisaema maxwellii, commonly known as Maxwell's arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and have a hairy texture, while the spathe is usually greenish-white with a distinctive purple base.

Arisaema maxwellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
