Astragalus alexandrinus var. sublaxus

Astragalus alexandrinus var. sublaxus, commonly known as the Alexandrian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
