Arabis hirsuta var. sagittata

Arabis hirsuta var. sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lanceolate or arrow-shaped, with a hairy surface, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
