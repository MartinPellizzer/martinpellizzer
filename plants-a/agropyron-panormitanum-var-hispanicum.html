Agropyron panormitanum var. hispanicum

Agropyron panormitanum var. hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
