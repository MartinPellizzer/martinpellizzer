Aspilia helianthoides subsp. helianthoides

Aspilia helianthoides subsp.

helianthoides, commonly known as the African torch lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is a perennial herb that blooms during the summer months.
