Aphora mercurialina

Aphora mercurialina, commonly known as the mercury aphora, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white leaves and delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant's unique ability to absorb and store mercury from the atmosphere has led to its classification as a hyperaccumulating species. Its botanical significance lies in its potential applications in environmental remediation and pharmacology.

Due to its rarity and ecological sensitivity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
