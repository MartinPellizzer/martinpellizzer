Acacia longifolia var. obtusifolia

Acacia longifolia var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in coastal dunes and heathlands.

Acacia longifolia var. obtusifolia is adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
