Actinodaphne auricolor

Actinodaphne auricolor, commonly known as the golden leaf actinodaphne, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often display a striking golden hue on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing smooth, dark seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
