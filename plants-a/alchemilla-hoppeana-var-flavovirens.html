Alchemilla hoppeana var. flavovirens

Alchemilla hoppeana var. flavovirens, commonly known as the golden green lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically green with a yellowish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
