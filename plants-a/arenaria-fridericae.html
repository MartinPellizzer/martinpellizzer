Arenaria fridericae

Arenaria fridericae, commonly known as the Friderica's sandwort, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Arenaria fridericae thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats at high altitudes.
