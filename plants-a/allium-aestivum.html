Allium aestivum

Allium aestivum, commonly known as summer garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulb composed of several layers of fleshy, white to pale yellow scales. The plant produces tall, cylindrical flowering stalks that bear small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters. Its leaves are long, grass-like, and have a strong garlic odor.

Allium aestivum is valued for its culinary uses and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
