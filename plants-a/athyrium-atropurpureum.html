Athyrium atropurpureum

Athyrium atropurpureum, commonly known as purple fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a distinctive purplish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
