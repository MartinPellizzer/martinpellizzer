Amaranthus rubescens

Amaranthus rubescens, commonly known as red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage and erect, branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into three-angled, bead-like seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy annual plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional food use.
