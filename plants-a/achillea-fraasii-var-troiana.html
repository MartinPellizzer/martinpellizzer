Achillea fraasii var. troiana

Achillea fraasii var. troiana, commonly known as the Troian yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers.
