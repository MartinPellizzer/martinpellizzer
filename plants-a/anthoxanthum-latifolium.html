Anthoxanthum latifolium

Anthoxanthum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf grass or field grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The leaves are usually broad and flat, with a bluish-green color when fresh.
