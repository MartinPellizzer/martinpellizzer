Archidendropsis oblonga

Archidendropsis oblonga, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically oblong in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
