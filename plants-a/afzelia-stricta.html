Afzelia stricta

Afzelia stricta, commonly known as African teak, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its straight, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Afzelia stricta is also known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its growth is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
