Alyssum cuneifolium var. genuinum

Alyssum cuneifolium var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
