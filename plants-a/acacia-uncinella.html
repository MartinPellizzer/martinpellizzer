Acacia uncinella

Acacia uncinella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features thin, linear leaves with a distinctive yellow-green color and produces small, yellow flowers in spring. Acacia uncinella is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native animals.
