Aloysia meyeri

Aloysia meyeri, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid climates.

Aloysia meyeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
