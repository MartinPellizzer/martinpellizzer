Acacia shuttleworthii

Acacia shuttleworthii, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, bean-like pods. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia shuttleworthii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
