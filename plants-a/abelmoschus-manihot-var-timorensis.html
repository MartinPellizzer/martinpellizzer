Abelmoschus manihot var. timorensis

Abelmoschus manihot var. timorensis, commonly known as Timorensis cottonwood, is a variant of the cotton plant native to Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces fluffy, white seed pods. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in warm, humid environments. It has fibrous roots and is often cultivated for its fibers, which are used in textile production.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
