Amelanchier alnifolia subsp. pallida

Amelanchier alnifolia subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the white serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America. It is characterized by its oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark red or purple color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat benefits.
