Allium longibulbum

Allium longibulbum, commonly known as the giant leek, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications, similar to leeks or garlic. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium longibulbum is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its edible qualities.
