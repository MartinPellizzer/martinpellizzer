Amaryllis × pallida

Amaryllis × pallida, commonly known as the pink amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis recurva.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with a white or pale pink throat. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, typically emerging from a bulbous stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Amaryllis × pallida is a popular ornamental plant, appreciated for its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.
