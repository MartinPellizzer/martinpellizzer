Anthemis rosea subsp. carnea

Anthemis rosea subsp.

carnea, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has aromatic leaves and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
