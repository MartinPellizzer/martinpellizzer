Allium semiretschenskianum

Allium semiretschenskianum, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves. The plant produces a tall, slender stem that bears small, white, bell-shaped flowers arranged in a loose umbel. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
