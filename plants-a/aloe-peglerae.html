Aloe peglerae

Aloe peglerae, commonly known as the Pongola aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used for its soothing properties.

Aloe peglerae thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
