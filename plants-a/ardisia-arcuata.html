Ardisia arcuata

Ardisia arcuata, commonly known as the zigzag ardisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Ardisia arcuata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
