Astragalus acutirostris

Astragalus acutirostris, commonly known as the sharp-nosed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus acutirostris is also known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
