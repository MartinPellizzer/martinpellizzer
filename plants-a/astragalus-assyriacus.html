Astragalus assyriacus

Astragalus assyriacus, commonly known as Assyrian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. The plant produces small, hairy pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus assyriacus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
