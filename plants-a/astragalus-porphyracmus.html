Astragalus porphyracmus

Astragalus porphyracmus, commonly known as the scarlet milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus porphyracmus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
