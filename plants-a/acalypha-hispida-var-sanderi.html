Acalypha hispida var. sanderi

Acalypha hispida var. sanderi, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sanders' spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
