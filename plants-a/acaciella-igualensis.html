Acaciella igualensis

Acaciella igualensis, commonly known as the Iguales acacia, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in a woody pod, typical of many legumes, and are dispersed by wind or animals.

Acaciella igualensis is adapted to dry and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
