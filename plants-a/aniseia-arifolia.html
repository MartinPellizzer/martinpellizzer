Aniseia arifolia

Aniseia arifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The silverleaf tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
