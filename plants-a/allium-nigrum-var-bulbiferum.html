Allium nigrum var. bulbiferum

Allium nigrum var. bulbiferum, commonly known as black garlic or bulbiferous leek, is a variant of the common garlic plant.

It is characterized by its elongated, bulbous leaves that resemble those of leeks, and it produces small, purple flowers in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and bulbs. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a complex network of underground stems.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and potential medicinal properties.
