Anthyllis vulneraria var. fennica

Anthyllis vulneraria var. fennica, commonly known as the Fennoscandian sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as heaths and grasslands.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of the flora in boreal and subarctic regions.
