Acanthopanax ternatus

Acanthopanax ternatus, commonly known as the Korean angel's trumpet, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets, which are typically serrated and have a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are a notable feature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
