Asplenium ulbrichtii f. genuina

Asplenium ulbrichtii f. genuina, commonly known as the Ulbricht's spleenwort, is a rare fern species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that typically grow in dense, shaded environments. The plant's rhizomes are short and often form compact, cushion-like structures. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, intricate pattern of lobes and sori.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
