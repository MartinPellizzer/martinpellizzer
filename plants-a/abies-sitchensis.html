Abies sitchensis

Abies sitchensis, commonly known as the Siberian fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The needles are flat, dark green, and have a waxy coating, while the cones are typically pendulous and reddish-brown when mature. It thrives in cold, boreal climates and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

This species is valued for its timber and is also an important component of its native ecosystems.
