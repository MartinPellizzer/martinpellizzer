Andropogon glaucescens var. genuinus

Andropogon glaucescens var. genuinus, commonly known as blue bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, with a distinctive bluish hue that becomes more pronounced in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
