Atylosia volubilis

Atylosia volubilis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Atylosia volubilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
