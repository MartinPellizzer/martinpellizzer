Acacia macracanthoides

Acacia macracanthoides, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by elongated, flat pods. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, tolerating salt spray and well-drained soils.

Acacia macracanthoides plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
