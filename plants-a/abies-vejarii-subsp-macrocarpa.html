Abies vejarii subsp. macrocarpa

Abies vejarii subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada fir, is a coniferous tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the shoots.

The tree produces cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of conifers. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish, becoming furrowed with age.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in mixed coniferous forests.
