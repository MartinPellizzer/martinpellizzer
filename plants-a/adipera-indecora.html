Adipera indecora

Adipera indecora, commonly known as the stinking beauty, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange ray flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypselae, which are dispersed by wind. Adipera indecora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
