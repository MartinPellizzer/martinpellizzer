Astragalus chaldiranicus

Astragalus chaldiranicus, commonly known as the Chaldiran milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
