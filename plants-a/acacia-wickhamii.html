Acacia wickhamii

Acacia wickhamii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wickham acacia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow globular flowers in summer. Acacia wickhamii is adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important part of the local flora and ecosystem.
