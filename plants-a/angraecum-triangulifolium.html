Angraecum triangulifolium

Angraecum triangulifolium, commonly known as the triangular-leafed orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring long, slender pseudobulbs and broad, triangular-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecum triangulifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
