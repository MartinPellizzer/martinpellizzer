Adenochloa adenophylla

Adenochloa adenophylla, commonly known as the hairy leaf grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers.

Adenochloa adenophylla is often found in moist, grassy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
