Allophylus africanus var. griseotomentosus

Allophylus africanus var. griseotomentosus, commonly known as the gray-tomentose false holly, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a dense, grayish-white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
