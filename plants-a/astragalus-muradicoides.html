Astragalus muradicoides

Astragalus muradicoides, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy environments.

Astragalus muradicoides is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.
