Acicarpha juergensii

Acicarpha juergensii, commonly known as the Juergens' acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Acicarpha juergensii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
