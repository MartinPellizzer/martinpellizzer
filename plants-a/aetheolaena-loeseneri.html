Aetheolaena loeseneri

Aetheolaena loeseneri, commonly known as the Loesener's ghost fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-drained soil. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its leaves, a common feature in ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Aetheolaena loeseneri is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
