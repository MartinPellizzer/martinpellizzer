Arundinella pumila

Arundinella pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that grow close to the ground, forming dense clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets.

Arundinella pumila is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
