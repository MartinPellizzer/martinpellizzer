Astragalus rumpens

Astragalus rumpens, commonly known as the bentstem milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus rumpens plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
