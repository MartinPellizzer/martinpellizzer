Astragalus whitedii var. brachycodon

Astragalus whitedii var. brachycodon, commonly known as the short-stalked milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
