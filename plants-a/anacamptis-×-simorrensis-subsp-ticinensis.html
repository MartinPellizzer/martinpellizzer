Anacamptis × simorrensis subsp. ticinensis

Anacamptis × simorrensis subsp.

ticinensis, commonly known as the Ticino marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, thriving in moist, calcareous soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
