Amaryllis × ackermannii

Amaryllis × ackermannii, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like, often emerging from a bulbous stem. It is typically cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is popular in gardens and as a potted plant.

This hybrid is known for its ability to thrive in a range of climates, making it a versatile choice for horticultural enthusiasts.
