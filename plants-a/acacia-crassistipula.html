Acacia crassistipula

Acacia crassistipula, commonly known as the broad-tipped acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

Acacia crassistipula is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
