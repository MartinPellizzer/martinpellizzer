Alsophila doctersii

Alsophila doctersii, commonly known as the Docters' tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and basket weaving in some indigenous communities.
