Arenaria gypsophiloides var. rhodopaea

Arenaria gypsophiloides var. rhodopaea, commonly known as the Rhodope sandpink, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria.

It features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, silver-gray foliage that helps it survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun.

This variety is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens and alpine settings.
