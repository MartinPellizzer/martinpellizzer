Astragalus teskhemicus

Astragalus teskhemicus, commonly known as the Teskhemic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and dry grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
