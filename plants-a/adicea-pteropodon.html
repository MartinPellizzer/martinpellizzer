Adicea pteropodon

Adicea pteropodon, commonly known as the winged hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Adicea pteropodon is typically found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
