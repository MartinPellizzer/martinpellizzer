Allium declinatum

Allium declinatum, commonly known as the drooping garlic or the pink garlic, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. Its leaves are grass-like, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Allium declinatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
