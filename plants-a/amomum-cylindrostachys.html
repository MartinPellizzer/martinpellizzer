Amomum cylindrostachys

Amomum cylindrostachys, commonly known as the cylindrical cardamom, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers followed by dark red, spherical fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to its unique fragrance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
