Andryala robusta

Andryala robusta, commonly known as the robust sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowering stems bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Andryala robusta is known for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
