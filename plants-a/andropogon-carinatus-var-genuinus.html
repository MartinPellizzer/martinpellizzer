Andropogon carinatus var. genuinus

Andropogon carinatus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
