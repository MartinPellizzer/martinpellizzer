Andropogon selloanus

Andropogon selloanus, commonly known as Sello's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to eastern Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces panicles of small, whitish to blue flowers. It is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Andropogon selloanus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
