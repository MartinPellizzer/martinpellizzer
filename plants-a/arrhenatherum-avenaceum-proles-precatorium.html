Arrhenatherum avenaceum proles precatorium

Arrhenatherum avenaceum proles precatorium, commonly known as the precocious meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained to slightly wet soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
