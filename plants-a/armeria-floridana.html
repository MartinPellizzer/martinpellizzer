Armeria floridana

Armeria floridana, commonly known as Florida thrift, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to coastal and sandy landscapes.
