Apargia borealis

Apargia borealis, commonly known as the northern bladderpod, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and bogs. It has small, alternate leaves and produces yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer.
