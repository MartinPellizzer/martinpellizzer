Agastache cusickii var. typica

Agastache cusickii var. typica, commonly known as the western green hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of tubular, violet to blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Agastache cusickii var. typica is also valued for its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties.
