Aspalathus spinescens subsp. lepida

Aspalathus spinescens subsp.

lepida, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, hard pods containing seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
