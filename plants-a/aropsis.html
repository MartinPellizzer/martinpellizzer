Aropsis

Aropsis, commonly known as the nightshade plant, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically toxic to humans and animals. Aropsis is native to various regions in Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in agricultural settings.
