Artocarpus rubrovenius

Artocarpus rubrovenius, commonly known as the red-veined breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves with prominent red veins. The tree produces a large, spiky fruit that turns from green to yellow as it matures. The fruit is typically consumed when fully ripe, offering a sweet and nutty flavor.

It is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local cultures.
