Asplenium × bevavaense

Asplenium × bevavaense, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a fern species resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that typically display a dark green color with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in small, round structures called sori. Asplenium × bevavaense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and landscape design.
