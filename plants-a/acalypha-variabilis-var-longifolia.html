Acalypha variabilis var. longifolia

Acalypha variabilis var. longifolia, commonly known as the "Chinese nettle" or "Mexican nettle," is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
