Arctostaphylos pungens subsp. laevigata

Arctostaphylos pungens subsp.

laevigata, commonly known as the smooth manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and evergreen foliage with leathery, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in late spring, followed by edible berries that are dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
