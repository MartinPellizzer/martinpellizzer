Actinidia tetramera

Actinidia tetramera, commonly known as the four-leaf actinidia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with four leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in early summer. Its berries are edible, though they are typically consumed by birds rather than humans.

Actinidia tetramera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and growth habit.
