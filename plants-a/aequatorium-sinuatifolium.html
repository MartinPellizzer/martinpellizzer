Aequatorium sinuatifolium

Aequatorium sinuatifolium, commonly known as the sinuate-leaved aequatorium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, sinuously lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical forests.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
