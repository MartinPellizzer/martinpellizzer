Anthemis rouyi

Anthemis rouyi, commonly known as the Chinese chamomile, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in late summer.

It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
