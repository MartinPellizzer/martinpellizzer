Amaryllis demissa

Amaryllis demissa, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a bulbous plant that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are evergreen and persist through the growing season.

Amaryllis demissa is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-draining soil and moderate watering.
