Abutilon hannii

Abutilon hannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a soft, downy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
