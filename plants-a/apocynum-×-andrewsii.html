Apocynum × andrewsii

Apocynum × andrewsii, commonly known as the hybrid dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a milky, latex-like sap that contains various alkaloids, some of which have medicinal properties. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodland edges and streambanks.

Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are hairy and can become woody with age.
