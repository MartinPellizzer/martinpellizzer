Achetaria crenata

Achetaria crenata, commonly known as the wavy-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive wavy-edged leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of white and green.

Achetaria crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
