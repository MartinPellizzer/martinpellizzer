Anemia filiformis

Anemia filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers are composed of numerous petal-like sepals, and it produces small, dry fruits that disperse its seeds by wind.

Anemia filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
