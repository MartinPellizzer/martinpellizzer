Actinea acaulis var. glabra

Actinea acaulis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed actinia, is a variant of the actinia plant characterized by its smooth, stemless growth habit.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant features a fleshy, tuberous root system and produces clusters of small, white spathes that enclose the spadix. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical structure makes it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and botany.
