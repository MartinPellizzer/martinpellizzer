Acalypha villosa

Acalypha villosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Acalypha villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
