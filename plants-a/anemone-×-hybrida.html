Anemone × hybrida

Anemone × hybrida, commonly known as the garden anemone, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Anemone coronaria and Anemone nemorosa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, featuring a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and woodland gardens.

Anemone × hybrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
