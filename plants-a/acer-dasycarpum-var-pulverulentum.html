Acer dasycarpum var. pulverulentum

Acer dasycarpum var. pulverulentum, commonly known as the powdered maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, densely packed leaves that give it a distinctive, almost powdered appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, fuzzy samaras that are typical of the maple family. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found at high elevations.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
