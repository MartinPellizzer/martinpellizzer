Asphodelus fistulosus subsp. nilotica

Asphodelus fistulosus subsp.

nilotica, commonly known as the Egyptian asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall spikes of pale yellow to reddish flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments. Its flowers are typically 5-10 cm in length and are arranged in dense clusters.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
