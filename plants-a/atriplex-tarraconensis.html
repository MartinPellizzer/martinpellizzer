Atriplex tarraconensis

Atriplex tarraconensis, commonly known as the Spanish saltbush, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its succulent, glaucous leaves that are adapted to arid and saline environments. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its tolerance to high salinity. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soils in coastal and semi-arid regions.
