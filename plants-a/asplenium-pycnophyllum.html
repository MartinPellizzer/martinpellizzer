Asplenium pycnophyllum

Asplenium pycnophyllum, commonly known as the dense-leaf spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that are often densely packed. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rocky crevices or forest floors, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its rhizomes are short and thick, allowing it to anchor itself securely in its habitat.

Asplenium pycnophyllum is a relatively small fern, usually reaching heights of only a few centimeters, making it a subtle yet resilient component of its ecosystem.
