Aphanactis barclayae

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically pale green or white, with a distinctive, almost translucent appearance. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Aphanactis barclayae is considered critically endangered and is protected under Australian conservation laws.
