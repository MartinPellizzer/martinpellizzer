Aciotis purpurascens var. alata

Aciotis purpurascens var. alata, commonly known as the winged aciotis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive wing-like structures on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in sunny environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the dry season.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
