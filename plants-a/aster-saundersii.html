Aster saundersii

Aster saundersii, commonly known as the Saunders' aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

The showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
