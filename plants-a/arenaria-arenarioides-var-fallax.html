Arenaria arenarioides var. fallax

Arenaria arenarioides var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It typically thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dunes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its succulent-like characteristics and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
