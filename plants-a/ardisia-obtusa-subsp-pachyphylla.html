Ardisia obtusa subsp. pachyphylla

Ardisia obtusa subsp.

pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved evergreen, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are thick and leathery in texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruits are bright red berries, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
