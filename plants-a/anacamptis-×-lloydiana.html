Anacamptis × lloydiana

Anacamptis × lloydiana, commonly known as the hybrid lady's tresses, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that produces tall, flowering stems with dense clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands and woodland edges. It is characterized by its long, slender leaves and a distinctive, spiraled arrangement of flowers.

This hybrid species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
