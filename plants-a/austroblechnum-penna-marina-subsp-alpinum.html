Austroblechnum penna-marina subsp. alpinum

Austroblechnum penna-marina subsp.

alpinum, commonly known as the alpine cliff fern, is a rare and specialized fern species native to high-altitude regions of South America. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments where it attaches itself to vertical rock faces or cliffs.

The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are adapted to withstand harsh weather conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the Blechnaceae family.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
