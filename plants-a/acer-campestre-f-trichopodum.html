Acer campestre f. trichopodum

Acer campestre f. trichopodum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved horse chestnut, is a variant of the common horse chestnut.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy pubescence on the leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub with palmate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces white, bell-shaped flowers in spring, followed by spiny, red-brown fruits.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
