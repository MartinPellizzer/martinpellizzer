Acourtia turbinata

Acourtia turbinata, commonly known as the Turbinated Acourtia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
