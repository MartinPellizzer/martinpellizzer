Alsophila albidosquamata

Alsophila albidosquamata, commonly known as the silver-scaled tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The trunk is covered with a layer of smooth, pale-colored scales, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical forests.

This plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to the forest's biodiversity.
