Astragalus chamaeleuce

Astragalus chamaeleuce, commonly known as yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. It has small, hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus chamaeleuce is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its adaptability to harsh conditions.
