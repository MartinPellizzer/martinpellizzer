Asperula maeotica

Asperula maeotica, commonly known as the Crimean holly or Crimean asperula, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a distinct, slightly serrated edge.

Asperula maeotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
