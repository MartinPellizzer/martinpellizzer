Abuta racemosa

Abuta racemosa, commonly known as the yellow root or golden root, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Abuta racemosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
