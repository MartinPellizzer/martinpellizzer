Astragalus australis var. glabriusculus

Astragalus australis var. glabriusculus, commonly known as the smooth astragalus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils.

This variant is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous appearance compared to other varieties of Astragalus australis.
