Allium canadense

Allium canadense, commonly known as Canada garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem bearing umbels of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
