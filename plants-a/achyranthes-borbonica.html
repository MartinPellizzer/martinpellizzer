Achyranthes borbonica

Achyranthes borbonica, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, bead-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are rich in compounds that have been studied for their potential health benefits.

Achyranthes borbonica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
