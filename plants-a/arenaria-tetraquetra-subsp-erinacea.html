Arenaria tetraquetra subsp. erinacea

Arenaria tetraquetra subsp.

erinacea, commonly known as the woolly sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky environments. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This subspecies is often found in coastal dunes and sandy soils across the Mediterranean region.
