Acacia jacksonioides

Acacia jacksonioides, commonly known as the Jackson's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Acacia jacksonioides plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
