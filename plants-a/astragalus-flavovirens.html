Astragalus flavovirens

Astragalus flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow-green flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several oval, smooth seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is of interest in traditional herbal medicine.
