Arundinaria variegata f. humilis

Arundinaria variegata f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, ornamental grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often variegated with white and green stripes, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by feathery seed heads that contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its slow growth and dense foliage make it an excellent choice for ground cover or small container planting.
