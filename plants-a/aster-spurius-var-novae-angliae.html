Aster spurius var. novae-angliae

Aster spurius var. novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in open woods, meadows, and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fall blooms.
