Ayenia truncata

Ayenia truncata, commonly known as the roughleaf ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal regions.

Ayenia truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
