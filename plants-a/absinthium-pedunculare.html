Absinthium pedunculare

Absinthium pedunculare, commonly known as the clustered wormwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellowish to brownish clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its slender, branching stems are often covered in fine, hairy foliage.

The small, inconspicuous flowers give way to small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
