Astragalus barrelieri

Astragalus barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Astragalus barrelieri is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments.
