Andropogon schottii subvar. asperiglumis

Andropogon schottii subvar. asperiglumis, commonly known as the rough glume bluestem, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that have rough, bristly glumes, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
