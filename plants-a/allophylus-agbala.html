Allophylus agbala

Allophylus agbala, commonly known as the African evergreen holly, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical Africa. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries that are toxic to humans.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
