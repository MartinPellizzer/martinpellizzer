Aleuritopteris bullosa

Aleuritopteris bullosa, commonly known as the bladder fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with small, rounded pinnae that are often covered in a waxy coating. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
