Alchemilla trunciloba

Alchemilla trunciloba, commonly known as the cut-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, scalloped, and often hairy leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream sides.

Alchemilla trunciloba is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
