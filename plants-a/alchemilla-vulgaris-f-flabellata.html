Alchemilla vulgaris f. flabellata

Alchemilla vulgaris f. flabellata, commonly known as the fan-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is frequently used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to retain moisture.
