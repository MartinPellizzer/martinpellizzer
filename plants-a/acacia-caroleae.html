Acacia caroleae

Acacia caroleae, commonly known as the Carolinian acacia, is a shrub native to the island of Carola in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces yellow to golden flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
