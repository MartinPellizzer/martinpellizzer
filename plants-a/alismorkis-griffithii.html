Alismorkis griffithii

Alismorkis griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir water plant, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to the Himalayan region.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. The plant has long, slender leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots are submerged. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Alismorkis griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
