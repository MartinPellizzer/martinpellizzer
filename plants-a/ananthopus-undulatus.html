Ananthopus undulatus

Ananthopus undulatus, commonly known as the wavy leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, undulating leaves that give it its common name, and it produces yellow flowers with white ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Ananthopus undulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
