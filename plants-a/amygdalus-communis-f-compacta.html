Amygdalus communis f. compacta

Amygdalus communis f. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf almond.

This compact form of the almond tree is characterized by its bushy growth habit and smaller size compared to the standard almond. It features small, ovate leaves and produces fragrant white flowers in the spring. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and mild almond scent.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
