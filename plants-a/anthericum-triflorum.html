Anthericum triflorum

Anthericum triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. The plant produces small, clustered flowers at the base of the spadix, which are often hidden within the leafy structure. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a deep green color with a waxy texture.

Anthericum triflorum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
