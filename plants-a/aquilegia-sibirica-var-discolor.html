Aquilegia sibirica var. discolor

Aquilegia sibirica var. discolor, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and unique foliage, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
