Ayenia purpusii

Ayenia purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus's ayenia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ayenia purpusii is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
