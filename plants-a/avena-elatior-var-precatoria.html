Avena elatior var. precatoria

Avena elatior var. precatoria, commonly known as the purple false oat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
