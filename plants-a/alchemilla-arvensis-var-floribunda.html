Alchemilla arvensis var. floribunda

Alchemilla arvensis var. floribunda, commonly known as the flowering alpine penny-cress, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
