Astragalus laxmannii subsp. fujisanensis

Astragalus laxmannii subsp.

fujisanensis, commonly known as the Fujisan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Japan, specifically found on the slopes of Mount Fuji. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other Astragalus species.
