Aphelexis lycopodiodes

Aphelexis lycopodiodes, commonly known as the dwarf clubmoss, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae, which includes primitive vascular plants often referred to as clubmosses. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a dense, compact appearance. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Aphelexis lycopodiodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense ground covers in suitable habitats.
