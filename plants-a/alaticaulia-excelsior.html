Alaticaulia excelsior

Alaticaulia excelsior, commonly known as the silverleaf alder, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically silver-white on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along streambanks and in riparian zones.

Alaticaulia excelsior plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
