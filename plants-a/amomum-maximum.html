Amomum maximum

Amomum maximum, commonly known as the greater galangal, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system. The plant produces tall, leafy stalks that bear small, pale yellow flowers, followed by dark purple berries. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.

The botanical features of Amomum maximum make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
