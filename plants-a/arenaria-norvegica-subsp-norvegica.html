Arenaria norvegica subsp. norvegica

Arenaria norvegica subsp.

norvegica, commonly known as Norwegian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern Europe. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer. It is adapted to harsh environments, showing tolerance to salt and wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine and coastal ecosystems.
