Antennaria leuchippi

Antennaria leuchippi, commonly known as the Sierra snowplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring moist, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette formation, and it produces small, white to pinkish flowers.
