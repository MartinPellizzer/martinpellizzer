Acrocomia sclerocarpa var. wallaceana

Acrocomia sclerocarpa var. wallaceana, commonly known as the Wallace's palm, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
