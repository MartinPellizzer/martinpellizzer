Artocarpus vrieseanus var. refractus

Artocarpus vrieseanus var. refractus, commonly known as the Java or Philippine breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces a fruit with a thick, fibrous rind and a starchy, edible pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. Its fruit is rich in carbohydrates and is used in various traditional cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
