Andersonia concinna

Andersonia concinna, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
