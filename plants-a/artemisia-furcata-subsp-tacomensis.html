Artemisia furcata subsp. tacomensis

Artemisia furcata subsp.

tacomensis, commonly known as Tacomensis wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in the summer, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant adapted to dry, rocky environments.

Due to its medicinal and aromatic properties, it has been historically used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
