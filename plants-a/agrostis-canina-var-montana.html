Agrostis canina var. montana

Agrostis canina var. montana, commonly known as mountain dog's-tail, is a grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted inflorescence, which resembles a dog's tail, and its slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in open, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Agrostis canina var. montana is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
