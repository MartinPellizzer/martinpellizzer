Amomum graminifolium

Amomum graminifolium, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China and Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by aromatic, three-lobed berries. Its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties, containing compounds like essential oils and alkaloids.

Amomum graminifolium is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
