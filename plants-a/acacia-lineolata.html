Acacia lineolata

Acacia lineolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and small, phyllodes that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia lineolata thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is a valuable species for soil stabilization and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
