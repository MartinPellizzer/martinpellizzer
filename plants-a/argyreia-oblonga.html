Argyreia oblonga

Argyreia oblonga, commonly known as the sacred lotus or moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its flowers have a unique structure with a central column that resembles a spire, giving it its common name.
