Ardisia cardiophylla

Ardisia cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
