Astragalus novissimus

Astragalus novissimus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
