Andropogon hirtus var. glaucus

Andropogon hirtus var. glaucus, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-gray leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its flowering spikes are narrow and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Blue panicgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
