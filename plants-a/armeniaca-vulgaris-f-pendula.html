Armeniaca vulgaris f. pendula

Armeniaca vulgaris f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry tree, is a cultivar of the Prunus species characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red cherries in late summer. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The weeping cherry tree is often used in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.
