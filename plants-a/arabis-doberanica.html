Arabis doberanica

Arabis doberanica, commonly known as the Doberan rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and wildflowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in a raceme, blooming in early spring.

Arabis doberanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
