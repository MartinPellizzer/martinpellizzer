Abutilon avicennae var. cordatum

Abutilon avicennae var. cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

The leaves are usually hairy and can vary in size, adding to its visual appeal.
