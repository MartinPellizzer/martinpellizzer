Austroderia

Austroderia, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a genus of flowering plants native to New Zealand.

These plants are characterized by their stiff, fibrous leaves that are used for making traditional Maori textiles. Austroderia species typically grow in coastal and forested areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. They produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color.

The genus includes several species, such as Austroderia lyallii and Austroderia cunninghamii, which are valued for their ornamental and practical uses.
