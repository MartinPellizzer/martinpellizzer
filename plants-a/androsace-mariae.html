Androsace mariae

Androsace mariae, commonly known as the Marie's Androsace, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense rosette of oval, hairy leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Native to the alpine regions of Europe, it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at high elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
