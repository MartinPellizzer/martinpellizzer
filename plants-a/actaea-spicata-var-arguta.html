Actaea spicata var. arguta

Actaea spicata var. arguta, commonly known as the Siberian bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features a tall, slender stem that bears clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in spikes. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its root system is fibrous and robust. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
