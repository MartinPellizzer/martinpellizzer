Astragalus kirilovii

Astragalus kirilovii, commonly known as Kirilov's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus kirilovii is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
