Angianthus uniflorus

Angianthus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower angianthus, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive, solitary flower head. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
