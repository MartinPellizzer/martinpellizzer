Amaranthus carneus

Amaranthus carneus, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from red to orange, which are often mistaken for petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are broad and succulent. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a food source.

Amaranthus carneus is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions.
