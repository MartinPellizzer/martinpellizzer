Athyrium kenzo-satakei

Athyrium kenzo-satakei, commonly known as the Kenzo-Satake Japanese shield fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
