Astragalus nematodioides

Astragalus nematodioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus nematodioides is also known for its medicinal properties, with some species in the genus being used in traditional herbal remedies.
