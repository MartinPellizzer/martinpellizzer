Abies densa

Abies densa, commonly known as the dense fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and slender, pyramidal shape. The needles are short, stiff, and arranged in compact clusters along the branches. It produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.
