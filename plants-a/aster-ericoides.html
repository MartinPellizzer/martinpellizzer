Aster ericoides

Aster ericoides, commonly known as the heath aster, is a flowering plant native to the northern United States and Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. This perennial plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in open woodlands and heathlands. Its foliage is dense and dark green, with small, needle-like leaves that resemble those of heather.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
