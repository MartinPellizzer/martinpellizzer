Acacia cyperophylla

Acacia cyperophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system and waxy leaves to reduce water loss.

Acacia cyperophylla plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
