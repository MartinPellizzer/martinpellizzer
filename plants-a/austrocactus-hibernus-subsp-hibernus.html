Austrocactus hibernus subsp. hibernus

Austrocactus hibernus subsp.

hibernus, commonly known as the Hibernian cactus, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in short, spiny hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, which is an adaptation to its alpine environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
