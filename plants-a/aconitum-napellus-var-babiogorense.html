Aconitum napellus var. babiogorense

Aconitum napellus var. babiogorense, commonly known as the Balkan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces erect, sparsely branched stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or alpine regions.

Like other aconites, it contains potent alkaloids and is highly toxic to humans and animals.
