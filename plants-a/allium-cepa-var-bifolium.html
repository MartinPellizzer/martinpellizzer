Allium cepa var. bifolium

Allium cepa var. bifolium, commonly known as the two-leaf leek, is a botanical variant of the common leek.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stem and two distinct leaf-like structures at the base. The plant produces small, white, bulbous flowers that are typically clustered in a umbel shape. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and mild flavor.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and culinary use.
