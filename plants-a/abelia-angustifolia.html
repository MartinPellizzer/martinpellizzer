Abelia angustifolia

Abelia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems and opposite, narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It is well-suited for partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and attractive foliage.
