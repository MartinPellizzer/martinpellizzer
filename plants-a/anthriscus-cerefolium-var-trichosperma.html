Anthriscus cerefolium var. trichosperma

Anthriscus cerefolium var. trichosperma, commonly known as fern parsley, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its seeds are small and numerous, contributing to its ability to spread and establish in suitable habitats.
