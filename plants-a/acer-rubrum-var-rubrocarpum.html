Acer rubrum var. rubrocarpum

Acer rubrum var. rubrocarpum, commonly known as the red maple with red fruit, is a variant of the red maple tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically ovate with a pointed tip and serrated edges, and they turn brilliant red in the fall. Its fruit consists of paired samaras, which are winged seeds that disperse easily by wind.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its seasonal beauty.
