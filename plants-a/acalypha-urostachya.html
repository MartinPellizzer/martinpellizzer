Acalypha urostachya

Acalypha urostachya, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that vary in shape depending on the species. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Acalypha urostachya thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
