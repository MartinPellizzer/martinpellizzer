Agropyron × pseudorepens var. vulpinum

Agropyron × pseudorepens var. vulpinum, commonly known as the creeping wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is native to temperate regions of Eurasia and is adapted to a variety of soil types, including sandy and rocky soils. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
