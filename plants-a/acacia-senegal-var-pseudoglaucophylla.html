Acacia senegal var. pseudoglaucophylla

Acacia senegal var. pseudoglaucophylla, commonly known as the Sudan acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are narrow and often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its gum arabic production and its role in traditional medicine and land stabilization.
