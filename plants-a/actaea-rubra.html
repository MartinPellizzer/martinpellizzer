Actaea rubra

Actaea rubra, commonly known as red baneberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking red berries that contrast with its compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its showy flowers, which appear in early summer, are white and have five sepals that resemble petals.

Actaea rubra is toxic to humans and animals, containing compounds that can cause severe gastrointestinal distress.
