Armeria maritima subsp. bourgaei

Armeria maritima subsp.

bourgaei, commonly known as the coastal thrift, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It features dense clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has succulent, gray-green leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and harsh coastal conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal gardens.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
