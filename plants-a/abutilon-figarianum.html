Abutilon figarianum

Abutilon figarianum, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.

Abutilon figarianum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.
