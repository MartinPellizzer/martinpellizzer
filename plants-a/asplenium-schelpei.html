Asplenium schelpei

Asplenium schelpei, commonly known as the Schelpe's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. Native to the moist, shaded environments of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in humid, cool conditions. The plant's spore-bearing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium schelpei is often found growing on rocks or soil in woodland areas, showcasing its adaptability to various substrates.
