Asclepias tuberosa var. decumbens

Asclepias tuberosa var. decumbens, commonly known as the yellow milkweed, is a variant of the showy milkweed species.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to the central and eastern United States and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

This variant is important for supporting monarch butterfly populations due to its milkweed properties.
