Acanthus ebracteatus var. xiamenensis

Acanthus ebracteatus var. xiamenensis, commonly known as the Xiamen acanthus, is a rare and ornamental plant native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences with small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
