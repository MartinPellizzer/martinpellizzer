Alocasia leoniae

Alocasia leoniae, commonly known as the Leopard Alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive mottled pattern of green, white, and brown, resembling a leopard's coat. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental foliage plant. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Its leaves can grow up to 1.5 meters in length, making it a dramatic addition to indoor or outdoor gardens.
