Asplenium petrarchae subsp. petrarchae

Asplenium petrarchae subsp.

petrarchae, commonly known as the Petrarch's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae. It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing on rock surfaces or tree trunks in temperate regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves, typical of many fern species.

This subspecies is noted for its morphological similarity to other members of the genus Asplenium, making it a subject of taxonomic interest.
