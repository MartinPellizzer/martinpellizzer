Atriplex thunbergiana

Atriplex thunbergiana, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, often blue-gray leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Atriplex thunbergiana is also known for its ability to tolerate high salinity, making it a valuable plant in saline soils.
