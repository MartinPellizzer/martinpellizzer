Astragalus biebersteinii

Astragalus biebersteinii, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus biebersteinii is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
