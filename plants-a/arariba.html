Arariba

Arariba, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Arariba is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
