Arisaema stictopodum

Arisaema stictopodum, commonly known as the spotted arum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features a tuberous rhizome and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on insects for pollination.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
