Asperula syrticola

Asperula syrticola, commonly known as the Syrticola shrub, is a flowering plant native to the rocky regions of North Africa and the Mediterranean.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Asperula syrticola is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
