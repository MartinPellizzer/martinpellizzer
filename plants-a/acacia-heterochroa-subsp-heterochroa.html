Acacia heterochroa subsp. heterochroa

Acacia heterochroa subsp.

heterochroa, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular heads, which are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. Its wood is light, durable, and has been traditionally used for various purposes.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
