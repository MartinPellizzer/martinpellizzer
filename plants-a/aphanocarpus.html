Aphanocarpus

Aphanocarpus, commonly known as the false acacia, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, primarily found in Africa and Madagascar, characterized by their shrub or tree-like growth forms. The plants are known for their compound leaves and small, often inconspicuous flowers. Aphanocarpus species typically produce legumes as fruits, which are enclosed in pods.

These plants are adapted to various environmental conditions, including arid and semi-arid regions.
