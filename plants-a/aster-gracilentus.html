Aster gracilentus

Aster gracilentus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It features slender stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer.

Its foliage is usually narrow and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
