Allium stocksianum var. persicum

Allium stocksianum var. persicum, commonly known as the Persian shallot, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features a cluster of small, rounded bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant produces tall, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, pink to purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its edible bulbs, which have a mild garlic flavor.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
