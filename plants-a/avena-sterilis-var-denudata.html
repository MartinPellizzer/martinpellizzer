Avena sterilis var. denudata

Avena sterilis var. denudata, commonly known as the naked oat, is a wild grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and reduced leaf blades, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with sterile florets, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
