Amorpha canescens f. glabrescens

Amorpha canescens f. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky false indigo, is a variant of the false indigo plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking purple to violet pea-like flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
