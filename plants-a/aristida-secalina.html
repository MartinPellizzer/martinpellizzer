Aristida secalina

Aristida secalina, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields, roadsides, and meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny grains. It is characterized by its fine texture and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Aristida secalina is often used in ecological restoration and as a forage plant for livestock.
