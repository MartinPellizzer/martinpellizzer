Adiantum madagascariense

Adiantum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar maidenhair fern, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically bipinnate. The plant features a creeping rhizome with upright, slender, and often arching fronds. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a finely divided structure, giving it a lacy appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
