Aegilops squarrosa

Aegilops squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, bristly leaves and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Aegilops squarrosa is considered a weed in agricultural settings but has potential for use in breeding programs for improved wheat varieties.

Morphology

Aegilops squarrosa has leaves that are narrow, linear, and slightly hairy, with a blue-green color, arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is erect, somewhat hairy, and has a greenish color, growing in a clump-like pattern. Its flowers are spike-like, with small, pale blue to purple florets, blooming in early summer, and are wind-pollinated. The fruit is a spikelet that disperses its seeds by wind, and the seeds are small and dry.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant anchor in dry, sandy soils.
