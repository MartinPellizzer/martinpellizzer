Aciotis martiana

Aciotis martiana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets, which give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Aciotis martiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
