Aporosa lamellata

Aporosa lamellata, commonly known as the veined evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinct veining patterns. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences.

Aporosa lamellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
