Asplenium geminaria

Asplenium geminaria, commonly known as the twin spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often divided into narrow segments. It reproduces via spores, which are released from sporangia located on the undersides of its fronds.

Asplenium geminaria is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions.
