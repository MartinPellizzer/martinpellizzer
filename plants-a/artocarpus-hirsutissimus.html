Artocarpus hirsutissimus

Artocarpus hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy jackfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiky, green fruit that resembles a giant, hairy fig. The plant has compound leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are small and clustered in panicles. The fruit develops from an inflorescence and contains numerous seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp.

It is valued for its edible fruit and has traditional uses in various cultures.
