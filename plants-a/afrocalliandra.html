Afrocalliandra

Afrocalliandra, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The tree has bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Afrocalliandra also has a fibrous root system that helps in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its native habitat.
