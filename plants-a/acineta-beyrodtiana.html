Acineta beyrodtiana

Acineta beyrodtiana, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in winter, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Acineta beyrodtiana is often used in rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
