Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. maura

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

maura, commonly known as the creeping stitchwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is adapted to rocky and calcareous soils. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
