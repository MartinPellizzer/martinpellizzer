Achillea cristata

Achillea cristata, commonly known as the crested yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and hardiness.
