Apium graveolens var. silvestre

Apium graveolens var. silvestre, commonly known as wild celery, is a biennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and compound leaves with a fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Its leaves and seeds are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Wild celery is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.
