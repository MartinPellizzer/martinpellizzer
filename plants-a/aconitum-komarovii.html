Aconitum komarovii

Aconitum komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in a spire-like inflorescence. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, wavy outline.

Aconitum komarovii contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.
