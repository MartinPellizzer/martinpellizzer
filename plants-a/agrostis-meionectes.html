Agrostis meionectes

Agrostis meionectes, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. Its inflorescence consists of a single, loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Agrostis meionectes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal habitats.
