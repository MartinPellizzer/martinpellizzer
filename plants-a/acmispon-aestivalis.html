Acmispon aestivalis

Acmispon aestivalis, commonly known as summer crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Acmispon aestivalis is often used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
