Allocarya nigra

Allocarya nigra, commonly known as the black alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Allocarya nigra is often found in mountain meadows and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations.
