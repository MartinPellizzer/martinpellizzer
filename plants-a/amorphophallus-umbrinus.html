Amorphophallus umbrinus

Amorphophallus umbrinus, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a thick, fleshy underground stem called a corm, which is used for propagation. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
