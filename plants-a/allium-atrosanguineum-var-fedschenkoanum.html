Allium atrosanguineum var. fedschenkoanum

Allium atrosanguineum var. fedschenkoanum, commonly known as the Fedchenkoanum leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose umbel, typical of the Allium genus. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its elegant appearance.
