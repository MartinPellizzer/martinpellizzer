Astragalus bhamrensis

Astragalus bhamrensis, commonly known as the Bhutanese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Bhutan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to the cold, arid conditions of its native habitat.

Astragalus bhamrensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
