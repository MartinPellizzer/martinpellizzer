Atropis convoluta var. mendozina

Atropis convoluta var. mendozina, commonly known as the Mendoza convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces violet to purple blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
