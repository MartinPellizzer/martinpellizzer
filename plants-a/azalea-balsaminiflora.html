Azalea balsaminiflora

Azalea balsaminiflora, commonly known as the Balsam-flowered Azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
