Agoseris coronopifolia

Agoseris coronopifolia, commonly known as the blue gromwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched.

Agoseris coronopifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
