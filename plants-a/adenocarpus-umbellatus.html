Adenocarpus umbellatus

Adenocarpus umbellatus, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and spiny branches. The plant produces small, white, umbellate flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a distinct aroma.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
