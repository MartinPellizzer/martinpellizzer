Amygdalus spinosissima subsp. turcomanica

Amygdalus spinosissima subsp.

turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkmen almond, is a shrub native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in spring, followed by small, almond-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The subspecies is notable for its hardiness and potential use in ornamental horticulture.
