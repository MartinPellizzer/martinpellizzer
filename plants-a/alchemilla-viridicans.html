Alchemilla viridicans

Alchemilla viridicans, commonly known as the green alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Alchemilla viridicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
