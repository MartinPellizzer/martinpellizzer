Andropogon sorghum var. fulvus

Andropogon sorghum var. fulvus, commonly known as purpletop sorghum, is a tall, warm-season grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple tassels and foliage, which make it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion.
