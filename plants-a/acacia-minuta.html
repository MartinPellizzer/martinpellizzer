Acacia minuta

Acacia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf acacia, is a small, shrub-like plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, thin leaves that are often mistaken for stems, a feature typical of many acacia species. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia minuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
