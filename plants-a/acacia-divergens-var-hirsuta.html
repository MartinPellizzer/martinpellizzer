Acacia divergens var. hirsuta

Acacia divergens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are typically followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
