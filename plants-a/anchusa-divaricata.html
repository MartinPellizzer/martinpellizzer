Anchusa divaricata

Anchusa divaricata, commonly known as the blueweed or common anchusa, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, rocky areas, and along roadsides.

Anchusa divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.
