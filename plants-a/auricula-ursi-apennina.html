Auricula-ursi apennina

Auricula-ursi apennina, commonly known as the Alpine bear's ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that resemble bear ears. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine habitats.
