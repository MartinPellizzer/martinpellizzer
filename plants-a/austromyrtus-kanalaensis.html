Austromyrtus kanalaensis

Austromyrtus kanalaensis, commonly known as the Kanala myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline, where it is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds. Its woody structure and evergreen foliage make it a distinctive feature of the local flora.

Austromyrtus kanalaensis plays an important ecological role in supporting native insect and bird populations in its habitat.
