Ardisia coibana

Ardisia coibana, commonly known as the Coiba beautyberry, is a shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ardisia coibana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
