Achillea salicifolia var. crenatissima

Achillea salicifolia var. crenatissima, commonly known as the serrated yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often serrated, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and extended flowering period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
