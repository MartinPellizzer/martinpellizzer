Argentina polyphylla

Argentina polyphylla, commonly known as the Andean fern, is a species of fern native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Argentina polyphylla plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
