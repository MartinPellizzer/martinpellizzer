Acokanthera abyssinica

Acokanthera abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian arrow poison, is a toxic plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody shrub structure with smooth, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It contains potent cardiac glycosides, which have been historically used in traditional medicine and as a poison for hunting.

Due to its toxicity, handling the plant requires caution.
