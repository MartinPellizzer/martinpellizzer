Aster crenatifolius

Aster crenatifolius, commonly known as the notchedleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that give it its common name. It produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Aster crenatifolius is often found in moist meadows and woodlands, thriving in partial to full sunlight.
