Alsophila rolandii

Alsophila rolandii, commonly known as the rock cycad, is a member of the cycad family, Cycadaceae.

It is a medium-sized evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery, pinnate leaves. Native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique morphology and historical significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
