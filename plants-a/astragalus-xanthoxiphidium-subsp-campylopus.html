Astragalus xanthoxiphidium subsp. campylopus

Astragalus xanthoxiphidium subsp.

campylopus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaflets compared to other Astragalus species.
