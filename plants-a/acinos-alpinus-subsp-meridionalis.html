Acinos alpinus subsp. meridionalis

Acinos alpinus subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high elevations.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open meadows and slopes.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is a member of the Rosaceae family.
