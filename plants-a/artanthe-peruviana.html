Artanthe peruviana

Artanthe peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that range from red to purple in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Artanthe peruviana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
