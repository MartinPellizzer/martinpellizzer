Aloe plowesii

Aloe plowesii, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, upright leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, covered in a whitish powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with bright yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Aloe plowesii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
