Astragalus psiloglottis

Astragalus psiloglottis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by inflated, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus psiloglottis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
