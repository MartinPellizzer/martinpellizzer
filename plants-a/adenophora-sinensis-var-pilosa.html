Adenophora sinensis var. pilosa

Adenophora sinensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific variety name "pilosa.". The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
