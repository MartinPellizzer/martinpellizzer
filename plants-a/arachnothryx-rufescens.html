Arachnothryx rufescens

Arachnothryx rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its sturdy, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in dry, scrubland habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental features, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
