Asplenium tetraploideum

Asplenium tetraploideum, commonly known as the tetraploid shield fern, is a rare and unique species within the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a smooth, leathery texture. The plant exhibits a tetraploid genome, meaning it has four sets of chromosomes, which contributes to its distinct morphological traits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
