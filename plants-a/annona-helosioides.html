Annona helosioides

Annona helosioides, commonly known as the small annona or the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are followed by edible fruits with a smooth, yellowish skin and a creamy, sweet flesh. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the consumption of its fruit.
