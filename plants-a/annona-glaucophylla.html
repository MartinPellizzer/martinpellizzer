Annona glaucophylla

Annona glaucophylla, commonly known as the white sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy white flowers. The fruit is a smooth, yellow or greenish-yellow berry that turns orange when ripe, containing a single large seed. The plant is valued for its edible fruit and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
