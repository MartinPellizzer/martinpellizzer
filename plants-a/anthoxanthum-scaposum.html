Anthoxanthum scaposum

Anthoxanthum scaposum, commonly known as the slender oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine, hairy structures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced in the summer.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, contributing to its distinctive appearance in the grassland ecosystem.
