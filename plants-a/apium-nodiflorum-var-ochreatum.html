Apium nodiflorum var. ochreatum

Apium nodiflorum var. ochreatum, commonly known as the yellow goosefoot, is a variant of the wild celery plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green leaves that are often lobed or deeply divided. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its stems are often reddish and slightly hairy, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
