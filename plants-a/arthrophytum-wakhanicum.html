Arthrophytum wakhanicum

Arthrophytum wakhanicum, commonly known as the Wakhan camels' tail, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are covered in a thick layer of white, waxy hair, which helps to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate habitat. It is well adapted to extreme conditions, thriving in rocky soils and cold climates.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
