Asparagus misczenkoi

Asparagus misczenkoi, commonly known as the golden asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces edible, needle-like shoots that are harvested in the spring, similar to common asparagus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
