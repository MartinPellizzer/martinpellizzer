Achillea santolinoides subsp. wilhelmsii

Achillea santolinoides subsp.

wilhelmsii, commonly known as the Wilhelmsii yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands. It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces flat, composite flower heads in shades of white to pale yellow, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers.
