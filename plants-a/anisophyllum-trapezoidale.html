Anisophyllum trapezoidale

Anisophyllum trapezoidale, commonly known as the diamond leaf, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
