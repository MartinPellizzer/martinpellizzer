Ahouai

Ahouai, commonly known as the sacred bamboo, is a plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Indonesia and the Philippines.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional ceremonies and rituals. Ahouai is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in tropical forests and along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and resilience contribute to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
