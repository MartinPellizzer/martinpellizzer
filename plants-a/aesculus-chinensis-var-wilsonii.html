Aesculus chinensis var. wilsonii

Aesculus chinensis var. wilsonii, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are typically 10–20 cm in length, with a velvety texture on the underside. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in clustered panicles during late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing 1–3 chestnut-like seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage and floral display.
