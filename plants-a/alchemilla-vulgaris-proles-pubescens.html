Alchemilla vulgaris proles pubescens

Alchemilla vulgaris proles pubescens, commonly known as the pubescens lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.
