Atriplex tridentata

Atriplex tridentata, commonly known as fourwing saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. Its woody stems are often covered with small, wing-like structures, giving it its common name.

Atriplex tridentata is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
