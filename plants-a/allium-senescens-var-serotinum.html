Allium senescens var. serotinum

Allium senescens var. serotinum, commonly known as the late-ripening garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs and leaves that are often used in Asian cuisine for their mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary gardens.

Its botanical features make it both a useful crop and a decorative addition to gardens.
