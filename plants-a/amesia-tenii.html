Amesia tenii

Amesia tenii, commonly known as the silverleaf filaree, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Amesia tenii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
