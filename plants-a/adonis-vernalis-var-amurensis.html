Adonis vernalis var. amurensis

Adonis vernalis var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring bloom, typically appearing in late March to early April. The plant features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem that often grows in moist, shaded woodlands. It has lance-shaped leaves that are finely divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to cool climates and its role in supporting early pollinators.
