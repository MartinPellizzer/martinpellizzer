Alsophila compta f. transiens

Alsophila compta f. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Tree Fern, is a rare and distinctive variant of the common tree fern.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and large, feathery fronds that give it a graceful appearance. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its unique foliage and slow growth rate make it a prized specimen in botanical gardens and specialized collections.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological studies of fern biodiversity.
