Astragalus karabaghensis

Astragalus karabaghensis, commonly known as Karabakh milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus karabaghensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
