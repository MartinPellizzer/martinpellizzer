Acetosella comosa

Acetosella comosa, commonly known as mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, hairy leaves and white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are often covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Acetosella comosa is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and succulent-like foliage.
