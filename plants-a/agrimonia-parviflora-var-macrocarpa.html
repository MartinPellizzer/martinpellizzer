Agrimonia parviflora var. macrocarpa

Agrimonia parviflora var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, glandular hairs, and its leaves are pinnately lobed with serrated edges. The plant produces small yellow flowers clustered in dense heads, which bloom in early summer. Its fruits are distinctive, large, and showy, often remaining on the plant well into autumn.

Agrimony is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
