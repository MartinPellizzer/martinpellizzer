Asplenium bulbosum

Asplenium bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous spleenwort, is a small evergreen fern that belongs to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and possesses fleshy, bulbous rhizomes that store water and nutrients. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or woodland areas. It is native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it is often found in limestone-rich soils.

Asplenium bulbosum is a hardy plant that is well adapted to humid, temperate climates.
