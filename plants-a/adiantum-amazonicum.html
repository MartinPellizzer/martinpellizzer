Adiantum amazonicum

Adiantum amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazon water fern, is a species of fern native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green and have a graceful, arching appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found near water sources such as streams and rivers. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Adiantum amazonicum plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
