Amphistelma aphyllum

Amphistelma aphyllum, commonly known as the leafless mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees.

It lacks true leaves and instead relies on its stems for photosynthesis, which are covered in a dense layer of fine, scale-like structures. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which eat the fleshy, orange-red berries.

Due to its parasitic nature and lack of visible foliage, it plays a unique role in forest ecosystems as both a host and a food source.
