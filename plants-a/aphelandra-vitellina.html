Aphelandra vitellina

Aphelandra vitellina, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often yellow-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers surrounded by a spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and warm temperatures.

Aphelandra vitellina is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
