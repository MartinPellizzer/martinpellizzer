Aquilariella malaccensis

Aquilariella malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca aquilaria, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a fragrant resin, which has been historically used in incense and traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now classified as critically endangered.
