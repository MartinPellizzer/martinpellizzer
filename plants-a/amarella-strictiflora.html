Amarella strictiflora

Amarella strictiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered amarella, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant produces bright orange to red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Amarella strictiflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
