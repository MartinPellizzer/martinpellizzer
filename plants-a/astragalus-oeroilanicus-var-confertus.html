Astragalus oeroilanicus var. confertus

Astragalus oeroilanicus var. confertus, commonly known as the denseleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
