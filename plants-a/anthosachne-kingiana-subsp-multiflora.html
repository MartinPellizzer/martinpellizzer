Anthosachne kingiana subsp. multiflora

Anthosachne kingiana subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers during the summer months. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate periodic flooding and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a robust stem that supports its herbaceous growth habit.
