Amauropelta consimilis

Amauropelta consimilis, commonly known as the similar shield fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its fronds are usually divided into segments that resemble a shield, giving it its common name.

Amauropelta consimilis is relatively small, with fronds that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length.
