Acalypha depressinervia

Acalypha depressinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominently visible veins and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub or perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually glossy and dark green, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
