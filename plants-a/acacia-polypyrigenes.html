Acacia polypyrigenes

Acacia polypyrigenes, commonly known as the flame acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spherical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
