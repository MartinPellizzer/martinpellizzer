Anacampseros lanceolata subsp. nebrownii

Anacampseros lanceolata subsp.

nebrownii, commonly known as the NeBrown's cushion plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
