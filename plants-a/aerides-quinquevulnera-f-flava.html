Aerides quinquevulnera f. flava

Aerides quinquevulnera f. flava, commonly known as the yellow-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with its dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It produces fragrant blooms during the summer months, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.
