Allocarya chorisiana var. undulata

Allocarya chorisiana var. undulata, commonly known as the undulating rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and weeds. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing on slopes and cliffs.

Its leaves are typically undulating or wavy in appearance, giving the variety its specific name.
