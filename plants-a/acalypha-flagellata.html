Acalypha flagellata

Acalypha flagellata, commonly known as the whipsaw plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, whip-like stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, triangular-shaped bracts. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly serrated edge, and they often have a velvety texture.

Acalypha flagellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
