Ardisia propinqua

Ardisia propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into bright red berries, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. The leaves are usually elliptical, glossy, and have a smooth texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in shaded to partially sunny environments.
