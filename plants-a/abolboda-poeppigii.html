Abolboda poeppigii

Abolboda poeppigii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapting to extreme environmental conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking white or pale purple flowers during the spring or early summer. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry climates by accessing deep water sources.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and has cultural significance in some regions.
