Astragalus trojanus var. eriocladus

Astragalus trojanus var. eriocladus, commonly known as the Trojan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets, and its root system is deep and fibrous. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and sagebrush habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
