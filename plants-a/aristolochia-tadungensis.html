Aristolochia tadungensis

Aristolochia tadungensis, commonly known as the Tadung arrow vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble horns. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musty odor, which attracts specific pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
