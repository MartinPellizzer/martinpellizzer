Alchemilla linderi

Alchemilla linderi, commonly known as Linder's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Alchemilla linderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and hardy nature.
