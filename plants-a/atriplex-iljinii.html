Atriplex iljinii

Atriplex iljinii, commonly known as Iljin's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its salt tolerance, making it suitable for saline soils. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, hard seeds.

Atriplex iljinii plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
