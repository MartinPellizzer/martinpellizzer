Artemisia nana var. sabauda

Artemisia nana var. sabauda, commonly known as dwarf tarragon, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny yellow flowers in compact clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine beds.

Due to its aromatic foliage and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a companion plant in herbal gardens.
