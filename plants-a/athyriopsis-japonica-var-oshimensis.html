Athyriopsis japonica var. oshimensis

Athyriopsis japonica var. oshimensis, commonly known as the Japanese holly fern, is a variant of the Japanese shield fern.

It is a perennial evergreen fern native to Japan, characterized by its elegant, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense clumps.

The leaves are leathery and glossy, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
