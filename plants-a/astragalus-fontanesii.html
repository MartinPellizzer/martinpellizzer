Astragalus fontanesii

Astragalus fontanesii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Astragalus fontanesii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
