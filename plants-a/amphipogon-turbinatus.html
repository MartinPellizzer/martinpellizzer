Amphipogon turbinatus

Amphipogon turbinatus, commonly known as the turbinated daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, turban-shaped flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that are often surrounded by white or pale green ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Amphipogon turbinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
