Asparagus zeylanicus

Asparagus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon asparagus, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Asparagus zeylanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
