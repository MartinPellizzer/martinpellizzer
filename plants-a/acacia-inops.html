Acacia inops

Acacia inops, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in compact, globular clusters. Acacia inops is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
