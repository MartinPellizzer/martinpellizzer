Amyris granulata

Amyris granulata, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring smooth, grayish bark and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, orange-red berries.

Amyris granulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent and attractive foliage.
