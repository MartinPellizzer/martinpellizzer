Artemisia affinis

Artemisia affinis, commonly known as silverleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Artemisia affinis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

It has a strong, aromatic scent and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
