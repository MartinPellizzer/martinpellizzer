Asplenium × akaishiense

Asplenium × akaishiense, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between *Asplenium trichomanes* and *Asplenium scolopendrium*.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant produces spore-bearing sorus clusters on the underside of its leaves, which are protected by a specialized structure called an indusium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Asplenium × akaishiense is valued for its ornamental appearance and is cultivated in gardens and conservatories.
