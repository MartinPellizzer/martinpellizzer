Ampelocissus winkleri

Ampelocissus winkleri, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to climb structures.
