Amaranthus caudatus

Amaranthus caudatus, commonly known as the plume grass or love-lies-bleeding, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant is an annual herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically red, orange, or yellow, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Amaranthus caudatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
