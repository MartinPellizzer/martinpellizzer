Apios gracillima

Apios gracillima, commonly known as the slender groundnut, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. Its root system is notable for producing edible tubers, which are rich in nutrients and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicine.

Apios gracillima thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
