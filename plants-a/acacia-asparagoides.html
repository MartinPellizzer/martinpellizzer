Acacia asparagoides

Acacia asparagoides, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It produces woody pods containing seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia asparagoides is well adapted to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor soils and is often used for erosion control in degraded landscapes.
