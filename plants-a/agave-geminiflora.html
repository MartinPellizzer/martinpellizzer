Agave geminiflora

Agave geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous small, white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
