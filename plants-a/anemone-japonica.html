Anemone japonica

Anemone japonica, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped blooms that range in color from white to pink and red, typically appearing in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Anemone japonica is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
