Aglaodendron

Aglaodendron, commonly known as the flame tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its showy, often red or orange, bract-like flowers that resemble flames. Aglaodendron species typically have palmately lobed leaves and produce woody, branching stems.

These plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their striking floral displays.
