Artemisia nuristanica

Artemisia nuristanica, commonly known as the Nuristan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and northern Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, glandular hairs that contribute to its distinctive aroma.

Artemisia nuristanica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
