Amaranthus anderssonii

Amaranthus anderssonii, commonly known as Andersson's amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish to reddish flowers. Its seeds are small and often used as a food source by birds and small mammals.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
