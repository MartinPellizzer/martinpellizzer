Armeria trachyphylla

Armeria trachyphylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer. The plant features silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Armeria trachyphylla is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
