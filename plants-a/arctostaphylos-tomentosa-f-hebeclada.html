Arctostaphylos tomentosa f. hebeclada

Arctostaphylos tomentosa f. hebeclada, commonly known as the hebeclad form of the whiteleaf manzanita, is a variant of the manzanita shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are thick and leathery, providing drought tolerance, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
