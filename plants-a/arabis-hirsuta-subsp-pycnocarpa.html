Arabis hirsuta subsp. pycnocarpa

Arabis hirsuta subsp.

pycnocarpa, commonly known as the dense-fruited rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and lobed, with small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that is densely packed with small seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
