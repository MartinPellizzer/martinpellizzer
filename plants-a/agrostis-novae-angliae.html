Agrostis novae-angliae

Agrostis novae-angliae, commonly known as New England bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growth on rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas and open woodlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
