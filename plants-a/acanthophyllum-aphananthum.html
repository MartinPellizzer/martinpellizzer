Acanthophyllum aphananthum

Acanthophyllum aphananthum, commonly known as the sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle floral display. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with limited water availability.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
