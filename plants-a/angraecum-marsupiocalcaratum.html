Angraecum marsupiocalcaratum

Angraecum marsupiocalcaratum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers with prominent, spurred petals. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with thick, fleshy rhizomes that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The species is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and limited distribution.
