Amelanchier saxatilis

Amelanchier saxatilis, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in rocky or wooded areas and produces clusters of white flowers in early spring. The plant bears edible berries that ripen in late summer, turning from red to a deep purple or black. It has alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

Amelanchier saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
