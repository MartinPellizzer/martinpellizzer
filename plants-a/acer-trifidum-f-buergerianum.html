Acer trifidum f. buergerianum

Acer trifidum f. buergerianum, commonly known as the Buergerianum maple, is a unique variant of the trifid maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three narrow, elongated lobes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
