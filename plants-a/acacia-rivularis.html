Acacia rivularis

Acacia rivularis, commonly known as the river acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and bipinnate leaves that are compound and feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stems. Acacia rivularis thrives in moist, sandy soils near rivers and streams, making it a common sight in riparian habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
