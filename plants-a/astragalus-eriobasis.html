Astragalus eriobasis

Astragalus eriobasis, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Astragalus eriobasis is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a food source for wildlife.
