Achillea viridis

Achillea viridis, commonly known as green yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright green, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Achillea viridis is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
