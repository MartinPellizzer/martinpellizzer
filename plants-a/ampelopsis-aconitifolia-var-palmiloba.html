Ampelopsis aconitifolia var. palmiloba

Ampelopsis aconitifolia var. palmiloba, commonly known as the palm-leaf grapevine, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and attractive vine.
