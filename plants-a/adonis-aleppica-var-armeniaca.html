Adonis aleppica var. armeniaca

Adonis aleppica var. armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian crown imperial, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features showy, red-orange or orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stem is typically erect with a few branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
