Anemone laxmannii

Anemone laxmannii, commonly known as the laxmann's anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Anemone laxmannii is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
