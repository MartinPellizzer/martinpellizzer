Achillea × albinea

Achillea × albinea, commonly known as the white yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its name honors the mythological figure Achilles, who was said to have used yarrow in treating wounds.
