Alchemilla vulgaris var. ambigens

Alchemilla vulgaris var. ambigens, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
