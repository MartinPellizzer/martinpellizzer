Adopogon carolinianus

Adopogon carolinianus, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including dry and sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to reddish in color, which become more prominent in the fall. It is an important component of native grasslands and serves as a host plant for various insects and birds.

Adopogon carolinianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in erosion control.
