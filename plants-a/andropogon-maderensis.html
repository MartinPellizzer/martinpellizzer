Andropogon maderensis

Andropogon maderensis, commonly known as Mader's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that are often adorned with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Mader's bluestem plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
