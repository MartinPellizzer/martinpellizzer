Achyrophorus elatus

Achyrophorus elatus, commonly known as the giant white sedge, is a tall, perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves and produces large, showy white or pinkish flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in areas with abundant moisture. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
