Actinodaphne molochina var. glabrescens

Actinodaphne molochina var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leafed camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, and often smooth-textured leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in tropical forests.
