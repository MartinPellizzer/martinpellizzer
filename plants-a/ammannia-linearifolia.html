Ammannia linearifolia

Ammannia linearifolia, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in prairies, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged, especially along the veins. Its small, clustered pink or white flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Ammannia linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
