Anemone walteri

Anemone walteri, commonly known as Walter's anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink with a yellow center. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Walter's anemone is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a source of nectar for early pollinators.
