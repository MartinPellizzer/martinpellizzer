Ampacus hortensis

Ampacus hortensis, commonly known as the garden ampel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ampacus hortensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and containers due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
