Acacia velutina var. glabrescens

Acacia velutina var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
