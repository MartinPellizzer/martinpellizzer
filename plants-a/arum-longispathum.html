Arum longispathum

Arum longispathum, commonly known as the long-spurred arum, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, showy inflorescence that resembles a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered around the spadix, which is typically elongated and covered in tiny, clustered male flowers. Arum longispathum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habits and botanical features make it a notable species within the Araceae family.
