Apteuxis

Apteuxis, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, spiky seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Apteuxis is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
