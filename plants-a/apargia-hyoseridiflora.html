Apargia hyoseridiflora

Apargia hyoseridiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, organic soils. It lacks true leaves and instead has reduced foliage, with photosynthesis occurring primarily through its stems.
