Arnebia guttata subsp. griffithii

Arnebia guttata subsp.

griffithii, commonly known as the pink rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces striking pink to red flowers during the summer months.

The leaves are typically small, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often woody and covered in a powdery coating. This subspecies thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
