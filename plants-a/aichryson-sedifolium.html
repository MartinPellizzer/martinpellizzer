Aichryson sedifolium

Aichryson sedifolium, commonly known as the silver leaf cholla, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spine-tipped stems that form a dense, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and often fall off, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

Aichryson sedifolium is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
