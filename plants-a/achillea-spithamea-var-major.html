Achillea spithamea var. major

Achillea spithamea var. major, commonly known as the Greater Spithamea Yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in finely divided, silver-gray leaves. Its flowers are typically white to pale yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.
