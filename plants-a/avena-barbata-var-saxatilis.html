Avena barbata var. saxatilis

Avena barbata var. saxatilis, commonly known as the saxatile bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous slender, barbed awns, which aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.

This variety is often found in rocky crevices and along coastal areas, showcasing its resilience to harsh environments.
