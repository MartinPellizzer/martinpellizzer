Aloe zygorabaiensis

Aloe zygorabaiensis, commonly known as the Zambian aloe, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The leaves are fleshy and store water, which helps the plant survive in arid conditions. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-orange flowers.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
