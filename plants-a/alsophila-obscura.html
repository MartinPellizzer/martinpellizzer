Alsophila obscura

Alsophila obscura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of compound leaves that can reach several meters in height. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its fibrous roots, which help anchor the plant in moist, shaded environments.

Alsophila obscura is often found in humid, tropical forests where it thrives in partial to full shade.
