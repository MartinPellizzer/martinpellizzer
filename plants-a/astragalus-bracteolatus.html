Astragalus bracteolatus

Astragalus bracteolatus, commonly known as the bracteolate milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus bracteolatus is often found in sagebrush steppe and other xeric habitats.
