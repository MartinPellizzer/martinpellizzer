Abildgaardia hispidula subsp. pyriformis

Abildgaardia hispidula subsp.

pyriformis, commonly known as the pyriform abildgaardia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are hairy, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its stems are erect and often branched, with a somewhat woody base.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant characteristics.
