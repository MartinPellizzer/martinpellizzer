Adrorhizon

Adrorhizon, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous capsule. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
