Artanthe hispida

Artanthe hispida, commonly known as the rough artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or red, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens.

Artanthe hispida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
