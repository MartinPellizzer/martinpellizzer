Alliaria speciosa

Alliaria speciosa, commonly known as winter cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and is characterized by its peppery taste, similar to mustard. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alliaria speciosa is also known for its ability to spread through seed production and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
