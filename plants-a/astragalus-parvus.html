Astragalus parvus

Astragalus parvus, commonly known as small milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has small, compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus parvus is also known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
