Aconitum vulparia f. monanense

Aconitum vulparia f. monanense, commonly known as the Yellow Aconite, is a hybrid form of the Aconitum genus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
