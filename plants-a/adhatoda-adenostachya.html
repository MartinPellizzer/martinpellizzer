Adhatoda adenostachya

Adhatoda adenostachya, commonly known as Indian bellflower, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually pubescent. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Adhatoda adenostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
