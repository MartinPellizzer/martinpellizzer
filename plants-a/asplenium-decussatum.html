Asplenium decussatum

Asplenium decussatum, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in the Americas.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often zigzagging fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in humid conditions.

Asplenium decussatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with indirect light.
