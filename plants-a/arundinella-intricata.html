Arundinella intricata

Arundinella intricata, commonly known as the intricate bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that resemble bamboo. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Arundinella intricata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
