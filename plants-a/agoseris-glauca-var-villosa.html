Agoseris glauca var. villosa

Agoseris glauca var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and silver-green foliage. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
