Astrocaryum argenteum

Astrocaryum argenteum, commonly known as the silver palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts, and the trunk is valued for its durability in construction.

The silver palm plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
