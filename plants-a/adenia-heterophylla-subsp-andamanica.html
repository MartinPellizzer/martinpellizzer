Adenia heterophylla subsp. andamanica

Adenia heterophylla subsp.

andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman climbing star, is a tropical shrub native to the Andaman Islands. It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves that vary in shape and size across different parts of the plant.

The species produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by yellow or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has a unique growth habit, with long, trailing stems that climb or trail along the forest floor.
