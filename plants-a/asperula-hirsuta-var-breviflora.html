Asperula hirsuta var. breviflora

Asperula hirsuta var. breviflora, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.

Asperula hirsuta var. breviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
