Aloe niebuhriana

Aloe niebuhriana, commonly known as the Nieuwland aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, greenish-gray leaves that are covered in a powdery white coating, known as farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe niebuhriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
