Atherosperma moschatum

Atherosperma moschatum, commonly known as the lemon-scented gum, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to southeastern Australia and is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with smooth, greyish bark. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This plant is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
