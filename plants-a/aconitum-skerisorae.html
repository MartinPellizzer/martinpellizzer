Aconitum skerisorae

Aconitum skerisorae, commonly known as the Skerisora aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spikelike clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Aconitum skerisorae is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that make it dangerous to both humans and animals.
