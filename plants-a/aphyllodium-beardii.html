Aphyllodium beardii

Aphyllodium beardii, commonly known as the desert beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Aphyllodium beardii plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
