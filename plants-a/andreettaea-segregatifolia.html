Andreettaea segregatifolia

Andreettaea segregatifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
