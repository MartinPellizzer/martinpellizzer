Anagallis pusilla

Anagallis pusilla, commonly known as the lesser waterwheel, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, nodding flowers that are usually pink or white, with five petals arranged in a whorl. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Anagallis pusilla is often found in damp soils and can thrive in partial to full shade.
