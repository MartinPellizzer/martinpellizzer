Andropogon halepensis subsp. anatherus

Andropogon halepensis subsp.

anatherus, commonly known as the Aleppo grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
