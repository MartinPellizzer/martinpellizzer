Amsonia lanata

Amsonia lanata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, blue-green foliage and clusters of blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Amsonia lanata is also known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
