Acacia natalitia

Acacia natalitia, commonly known as Natal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia natalitia is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
