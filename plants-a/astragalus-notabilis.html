Astragalus notabilis

Astragalus notabilis, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus notabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
