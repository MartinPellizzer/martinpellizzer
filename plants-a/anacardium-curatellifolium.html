Anacardium curatellifolium

Anacardium curatellifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that enclose a single hard-shelled seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a distinct aroma.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
