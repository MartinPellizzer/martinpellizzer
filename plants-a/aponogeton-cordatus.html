Aponogeton cordatus

Aponogeton cordatus, commonly known as the slender waterweed, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that float on the water surface, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically submerged, followed by distinctive, boat-shaped fruits. It is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Aponogeton cordatus is valued in aquatic gardening for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
