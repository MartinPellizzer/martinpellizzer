Acacia lirellata

Acacia lirellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia lirellata is often used in reforestation and soil stabilization due to its hardy nature.
