Avena filifolia f. pubescens

Avena filifolia f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy oat, is a variant of the slender oat species characterized by its dense pubescence.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical structure includes fibrous roots, which help in anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients.

The hairy oat is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a forage crop due to its hardy nature.
