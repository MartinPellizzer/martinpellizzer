Amomum zedoaria

Amomum zedoaria, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties and are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of A. zedoaria make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
