Alocasia

Alocasia, commonly known as the elephant ear, is a genus of flowering plants in the Araceae family.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. Alocasia species are characterized by their large, heart-shaped or arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. They typically have a rhizomatous growth habit and produce striking spadices, which are the modified leaf structures that bear the flowers.

Many Alocasia species are cultivated as ornamental plants for their dramatic foliage and are popular in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
