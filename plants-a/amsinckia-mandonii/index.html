Amsinckia Mandonii

Mandon's amsinckia, scientifically known as Amsinckia mandonii, is a rare and striking member of the sunflower family native to the deserts of California. This delicate annual plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and hairy, branching stems that thrive in arid environments. It is often found in sandy soils along the edges of washes and dry streambeds, where it survives with minimal water. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and faces threats from urban development and climate change. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique and beautiful plant for future generations.
