Arenaria lanceolatifolia

Arenaria lanceolatifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to cold, harsh climates with limited growing seasons.
