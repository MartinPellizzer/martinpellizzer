Amarella gracilis

Amarella gracilis, commonly known as the slender amarella, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Amarantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Amarella gracilis is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
