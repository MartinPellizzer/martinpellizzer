Angelica nubigena

Angelica nubigena, commonly known as the cloud angelica, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Angelica nubigena thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
