Aira flexuosa var. pallida

Aira flexuosa var. pallida, commonly known as the pale air grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, arching stems that produce dense clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
