Aristolochia jauruensis

Aristolochia jauruensis, commonly known as the Jauru Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding floral structure. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
