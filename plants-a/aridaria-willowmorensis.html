Aridaria willowmorensis

Aridaria willowmorensis, commonly known as the Desert Willow, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and long, narrow leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a waxy leaf coating.

Aridaria willowmorensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
