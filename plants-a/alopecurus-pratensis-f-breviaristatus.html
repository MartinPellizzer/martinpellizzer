Alopecurus pratensis f. breviaristatus

Alopecurus pratensis f. breviaristatus, commonly known as the short-haired meadow grass, is a botanical variant of the meadow grass species.

It is characterized by its shorter inflorescence compared to the typical form, with bracts that are less elongated. This plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
