Allionia puberula

Allionia puberula, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Allionia puberula is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
