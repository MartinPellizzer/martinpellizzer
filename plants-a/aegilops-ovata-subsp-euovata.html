Aegilops ovata subsp. euovata

Aegilops ovata subsp.

euovata, commonly known as the slender wild oat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, smooth surface.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
