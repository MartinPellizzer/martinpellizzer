Aristida australis

Aristida australis, commonly known as the Australian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Aristida australis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
