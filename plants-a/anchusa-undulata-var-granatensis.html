Anchusa undulata var. granatensis

Anchusa undulata var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granada bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in clumps, often reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and long flowering period.
