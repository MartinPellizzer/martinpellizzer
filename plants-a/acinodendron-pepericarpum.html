Acinodendron pepericarpum

Acinodendron pepericarpum, commonly known as the pepperpod tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into woody pods. The plant produces a peppery-scented fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a unique fruit structure.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and ecological significance in its native habitat.
