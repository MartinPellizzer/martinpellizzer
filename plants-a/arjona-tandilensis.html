Arjona tandilensis

Arjona tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil arjona, is a shrub native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep root systems. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for wildlife.

Its flowers, typically small and clustered, contribute to its ornamental value in native landscaping.
