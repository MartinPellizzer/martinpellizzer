Acanthophyllum glandulosum

Acanthophyllum glandulosum, commonly known as the woolly acanthophyllum, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Acanthophyllum glandulosum plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
