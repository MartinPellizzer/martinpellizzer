Allium obtusum var. obtusum

Allium obtusum var. obtusum, commonly known as bulbous leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hollow stems that grow up to 60 cm tall, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spherical, purple to pink flower heads composed of small, clustered flowers. Its bulbous base is often used as a vegetable, similar to leeks or onions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.
