Acacia quisumbingii

Acacia quisumbingii, commonly known as the Philippine acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
