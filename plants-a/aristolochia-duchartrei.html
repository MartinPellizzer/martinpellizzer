Aristolochia duchartrei

Aristolochia duchartrei, commonly known as the Mexican birthwort, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, winding, pipe-shaped flowers that range in color from green to reddish-purple. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition, which includes various alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
