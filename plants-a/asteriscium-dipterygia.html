Asteriscium dipterygia

Asteriscium dipterygia, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces bright yellow flowers in the spring. It has narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Asteriscium dipterygia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
