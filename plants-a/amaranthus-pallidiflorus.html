Amaranthus pallidiflorus

Amaranthus pallidiflorus, commonly known as the paleflower amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Amaranthus pallidiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
