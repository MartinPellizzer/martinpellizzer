Androcymbium melanthioides subsp. australe

Androcymbium melanthioides subsp.

australe, commonly known as the southern sandalwood, is a small shrub native to southern Australia. It features aromatic, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is notable for its potential use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in native plant communities.
