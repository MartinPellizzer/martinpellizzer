Annona xestropetala

Annona xestropetala, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces large, fragrant, greenish-yellow fruits with a thick, creamy pulp. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with a strong scent to attract pollinators.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
