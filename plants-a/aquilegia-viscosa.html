Aquilegia viscosa

Aquilegia viscosa, commonly known as the sticky celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant features finely divided, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive sticky texture, which gives it its common name. Aquilegia viscosa typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
