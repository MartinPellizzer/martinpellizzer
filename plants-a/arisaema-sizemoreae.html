Arisaema sizemoreae

Arisaema sizemoreae, commonly known as the Sizemore's arisaema, is a rare tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, and its foliage consists of arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several countries.
