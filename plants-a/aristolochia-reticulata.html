Aristolochia reticulata

Aristolochia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or reddish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a reticulate pattern of veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological features and some containing toxic compounds.
