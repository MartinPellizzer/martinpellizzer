Adenocarpus faurei

Adenocarpus faurei, commonly known as the Cape mountain thistle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions.

Adenocarpus faurei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
