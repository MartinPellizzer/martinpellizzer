Astragalus melanostachys f. prostrata

Astragalus melanostachys f. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often lie close to the ground, forming a dense mat. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and hairy.

This species is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.
