Anthurium ecuadorense

Anthurium ecuadorense, commonly known as the Ecuadorean anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped spathe and clustered spadix. The plant produces striking, colorful bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, while the actual flowers are small and clustered at the base of the spadix. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Anthurium ecuadorense is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
