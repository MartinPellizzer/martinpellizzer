Aristolochia punjabensis

Aristolochia punjabensis, commonly known as the Punjab arrowhead, is a climbing vine native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and unique, tubular flowers that resemble a horn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
