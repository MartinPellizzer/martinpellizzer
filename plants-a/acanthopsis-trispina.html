Acanthopsis trispina

Acanthopsis trispina, commonly known as the three-spined acanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny calyx and showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or purple. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
