Adiantum capillus-veneris var. laciniatum

Adiantum capillus-veneris var. laciniatum, commonly known as the "veil-tail fern," is a delicate and elegant fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble a flowing veil, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are bioluminescent in the dark, a unique trait that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a humid, shaded garden setting.
