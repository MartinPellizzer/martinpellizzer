Aspilia schimperi

Aspilia schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian torch flower, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky yellow inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in areas with high altitudes and cool climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often found in dry, well-drained soils.

Aspilia schimperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
