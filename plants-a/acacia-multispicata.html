Acacia multispicata

Acacia multispicata, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and compound leaves that are usually reduced to small, needle-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are followed by distinctive, flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a source of gum and fiber.
