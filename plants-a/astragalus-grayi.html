Astragalus grayi

Astragalus grayi, commonly known as Gray's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.

Astragalus grayi is also valued for its ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
