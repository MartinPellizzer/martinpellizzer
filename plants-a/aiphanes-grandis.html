Aiphanes grandis

Aiphanes grandis, commonly known as the giant papyrus, is a robust grass-like plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands in wetland and marshy environments.

Aiphanes grandis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
