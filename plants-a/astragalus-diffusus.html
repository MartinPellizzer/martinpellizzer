Astragalus diffusus

Astragalus diffusus, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Mongolia, and Russia, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Astragalus diffusus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
