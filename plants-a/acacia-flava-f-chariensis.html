Acacia flava f. chariensis

Acacia flava f. chariensis, commonly known as the yellow wattle.

This variant of Acacia flava is characterized by its golden-yellow flowers and narrow, linear leaves. It is a shrub native to Australia, typically found in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
