Arum maculatum f. spathulatum

Arum maculatum f. spathulatum, commonly known as the spotted arum or purple arum, is a hybrid form of the common arum.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a purple tinge and its striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in woodland gardens.

Its unique foliage and dramatic floral structure make it a popular choice for botanical collections.
