Astrocarpa sesamoides var. spathulata

Astrocarpa sesamoides var. spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf astrocarpa, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It features a robust, upright stem with lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
