Astragalus glaucophyllus

Astragalus glaucophyllus, commonly known as the blue-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its stems are often hairy, and the flowers range in color from white to pale purple.

Astragalus glaucophyllus is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
