Abutilon alii

Abutilon alii, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, lantern-shaped seed pods that turn red as they mature. The plant produces showy, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and unique fruiting structures.

Abutilon alii thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.
