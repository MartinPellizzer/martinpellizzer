Asclepias rubra var. laurifolia

Asclepias rubra var. laurifolia, commonly known as the Florida milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple along the veins. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers with prominent, horn-like structures called horns. It is a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and supports a variety of pollinators.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion in wetland and swampy habitats.
