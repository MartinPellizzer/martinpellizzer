Amaryllis vernalis

Amaryllis vernalis, commonly known as the spring amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, white, and pink, typically blooming in the spring. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis vernalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
