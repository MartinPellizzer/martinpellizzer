Achnatherum thomasii

Achnatherum thomasii, commonly known as Thomas' needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Achnatherum thomasii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
