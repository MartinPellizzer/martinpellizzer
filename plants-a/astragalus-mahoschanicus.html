Astragalus mahoschanicus

Astragalus mahoschanicus, commonly known as the Mahoschanic milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is often deep and robust, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus mahoschanicus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
