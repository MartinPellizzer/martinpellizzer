Angelica decursiva f. angustiloba

Angelica decursiva f. angustiloba, commonly known as narrow-leaved angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features large, compound leaves that are narrower than those of the typical species, with a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are often harvested for their aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and pharmacological significance.
