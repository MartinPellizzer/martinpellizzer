Artemisia japonica var. parviflora

Artemisia japonica var. parviflora, commonly known as Japanese mugwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its aromatic properties make it useful in traditional medicine and as a companion plant in gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
