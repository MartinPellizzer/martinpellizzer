Agropyron tarbagataicum

Agropyron tarbagataicum, commonly known as the Targata grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of steppe and semi-desert ecosystems.
