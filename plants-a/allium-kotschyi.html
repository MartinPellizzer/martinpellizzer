Allium kotschyi

Allium kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi onion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers atop a slender stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
