Ampelocalamus calcareus

Ampelocalamus calcareus, commonly known as the limestone reed, is a grass-like plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of dust or salt. The plant thrives in well-drained soils, particularly those rich in calcium, which is reflected in its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters.

Due to its adaptations to harsh environments, Ampelocalamus calcareus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
