Aleurites peltatus

Aleurites peltatus, commonly known as the Chinese lantern or Indian chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and resemble a hand, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright orange, lantern-shaped fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous husk. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine.
