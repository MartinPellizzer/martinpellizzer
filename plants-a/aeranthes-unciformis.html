Aeranthes unciformis

Aeranthes unciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
