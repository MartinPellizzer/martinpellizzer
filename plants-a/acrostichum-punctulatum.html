Acrostichum punctulatum

Acrostichum punctulatum, commonly known as the dotted fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered with small, rounded spore-producing structures called sori. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its fronds are typically green with a velvety texture and exhibit a distinctive pattern of dots or speckles.

Acrostichum punctulatum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
