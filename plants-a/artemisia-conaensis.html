Artemisia conaensis

Artemisia conaensis, commonly known as the Chinese wormwood, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its essential oils contain compounds with potential antimicrobial properties.
