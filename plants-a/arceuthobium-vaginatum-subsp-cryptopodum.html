Arceuthobium vaginatum subsp. cryptopodum

Arceuthobium vaginatum subsp.

cryptopodum, commonly known as the pine dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily infects various species of coniferous trees. It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients, forming specialized structures called haustoria to extract water and nutrients.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and berries that are dispersed by wind and animals. It is characterized by its unique seed dispersal mechanism, where seeds are ejected forcefully from the fruit.

This subspecies is found in forested regions of North America, where it plays a role in shaping the ecology of its host trees.
