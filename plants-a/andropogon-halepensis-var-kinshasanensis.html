Andropogon halepensis var. kinshasanensis

Andropogon halepensis var. kinshasanensis, commonly known as the Kinshasa grass, is a variant of the common bluestem grass.

It is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
