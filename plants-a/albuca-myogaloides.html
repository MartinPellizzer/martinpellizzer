Albuca myogaloides

Albuca myogaloides, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Albuca myogaloides is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
