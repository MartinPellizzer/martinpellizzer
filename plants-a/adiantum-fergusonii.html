Adiantum fergusonii

Adiantum fergusonii, commonly known as the California maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically bright green with a slightly bluish tinge. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Adiantum fergusonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
