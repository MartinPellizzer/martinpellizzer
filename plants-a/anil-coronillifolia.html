Anil coronillifolia

Anil coronillifolia, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by triangular seed pods. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Anil coronillifolia is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
