Andropogon exilis

Andropogon exilis, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a native grass species found primarily in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in swampy or marshy areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that mature into pale blue or purple flowers.

Andropogon exilis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
