Actinidia fortunatii

Actinidia fortunatii, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Actinidia fortunatii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
