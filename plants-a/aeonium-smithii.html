Aeonium smithii

Aeonium smithii, commonly known as the Smith's Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating called farina. The plant produces striking pink or red flowers on tall, branching stalks during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Aeonium smithii is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of care.
