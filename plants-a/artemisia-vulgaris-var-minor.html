Artemisia vulgaris var. minor

Artemisia vulgaris var. minor, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It contains volatile oils, including thujone, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent.
