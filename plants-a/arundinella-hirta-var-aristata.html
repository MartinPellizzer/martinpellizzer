Arundinella hirta var. aristata

Arundinella hirta var. aristata, commonly known as the hairy bamboo or slender bamboo, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, often with a slightly hairy texture. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variant is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening or for erosion control.
