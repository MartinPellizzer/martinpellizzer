Aconitum otikurense

Aconitum otikurense, commonly known as the Otikuren Aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,500 meters. The plant features a robust root system and produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are often covered in fine hairs.

Like other aconites, it contains toxic alkaloids and requires careful handling.
