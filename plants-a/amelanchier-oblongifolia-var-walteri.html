Amelanchier oblongifolia var. walteri

Amelanchier oblongifolia var. walteri, commonly known as Walter's serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries in the summer. Its leaves are elliptic to ovate, with a smooth margin and a slightly toothed base.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat, providing food for birds and small mammals.
