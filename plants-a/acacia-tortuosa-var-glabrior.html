Acacia tortuosa var. glabrior

Acacia tortuosa var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are typically smooth and glabrous, distinguishing it from other acacia species. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is well adapted to drought, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

The wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for local use in construction and crafts.
