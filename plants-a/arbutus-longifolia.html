Arbutus longifolia

Arbutus longifolia, commonly known as the evergreen strawberry tree, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its leathery, oval-shaped leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The evergreen strawberry tree is also noted for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.
