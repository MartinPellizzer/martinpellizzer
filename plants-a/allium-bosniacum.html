Allium bosniacum

Allium bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian leek, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves. The plant produces a tall flowering stem with umbels of white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and resemble those of onions, though they are less commonly cultivated.

Allium bosniacum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
