Anthistiria tremula var. thwaitesii

Anthistiria tremula var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thwaites' willow, is a rare variant of the willow tree native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins during the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
