Arachis major

Arachis major, commonly known as the large peanut, is an annual legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy. The plant produces underground pods that contain one to three seeds, which are the edible peanuts. Arachis major is a dicotyledonous plant with a fibrous root system and a complex flowering structure.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
