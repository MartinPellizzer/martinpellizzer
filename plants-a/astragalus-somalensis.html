Astragalus somalensis

Astragalus somalensis, commonly known as the Somali milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
