Aloe gracilis var. decumbens

Aloe gracilis var. decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent aloe, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers on slender stems during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
