Amygdalus persica

Amygdalus persica, commonly known as the peach tree, is a deciduous fruit tree native to Asia.

It produces sweet, fuzzy fruits that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with ovate leaflets and produces fragrant white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit develops from a single flower and is typically harvested in the summer.

The peach tree is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
