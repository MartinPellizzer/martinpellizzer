Acinodendron elaeagnoides

Acinodendron elaeagnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its compound leaves that are often reduced to small scales. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Acinodendron elaeagnoides is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded lands.
