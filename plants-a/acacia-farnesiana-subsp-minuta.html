Acacia farnesiana subsp. minuta

Acacia farnesiana subsp.

minuta, commonly known as the sweet acacia or mimosa, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds with a tuft of silky hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
