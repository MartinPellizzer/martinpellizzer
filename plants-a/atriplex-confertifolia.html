Atriplex confertifolia

Atriplex confertifolia, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant has small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. It is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Atriplex confertifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
