Amphoricarpos neumayeri

Amphoricarpos neumayeri, commonly known as Neumayer's sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and dense, cushion-like foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in late summer. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for harsh, dry environments.

Amphoricarpos neumayeri is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
