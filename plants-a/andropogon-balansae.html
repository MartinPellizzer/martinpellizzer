Andropogon balansae

Andropogon balansae, commonly known as the Balansa grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes many other grasses.
