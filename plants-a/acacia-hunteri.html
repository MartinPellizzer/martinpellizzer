Acacia hunteri

Acacia hunteri, commonly known as the Hunter's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Acacia hunteri is often found in sandy soils and is resilient to drought conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
