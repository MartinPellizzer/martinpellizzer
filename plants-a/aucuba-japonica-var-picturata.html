Aucuba japonica var. picturata

Aucuba japonica var. picturata, commonly known as the painted aucuba, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking yellow or white margins, creating a vivid contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that persist through the winter. It thrives in partial to full shade and is well-suited for woodland gardens or as a foundation plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
