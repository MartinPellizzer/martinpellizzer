Astragalus vedicus

Astragalus vedicus, commonly known as the Vedic milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves with small leaflets. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and drought-resistant mechanisms.

Astragalus vedicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
