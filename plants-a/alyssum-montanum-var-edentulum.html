Alyssum montanum var. edentulum

Alyssum montanum var. edentulum, commonly known as the toothless alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold temperatures.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
