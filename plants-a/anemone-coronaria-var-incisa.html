Anemone coronaria var. incisa

Anemone coronaria var. incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf garden anemone, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
