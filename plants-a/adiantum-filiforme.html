Adiantum filiforme

Adiantum filiforme, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern species native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It features finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically light green and possess a graceful, airy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of the Adiantum genus.

Adiantum filiforme is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to add a touch of elegance to shady garden spaces.
