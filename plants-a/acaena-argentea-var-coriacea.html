Acaena argentea var. coriacea

Acaena argentea var. coriacea, commonly known as silver bristleberry, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive velvety texture.

It is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
