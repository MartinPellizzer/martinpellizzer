Achimenes grandiflora var. pubescens

Achimenes grandiflora var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf achimenes, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Achimenes grandiflora var. pubescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and unique texture.
