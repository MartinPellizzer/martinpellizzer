Acacia subcaerulea var. subsessilis

Acacia subcaerulea var. subsessilis, commonly known as the blue acacia or blue wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit and its distinctive blue-green foliage. The leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, elliptical leaflets. It produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are often visited by birds and insects.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for dryland conditions.
