Allium esculentum

Allium esculentum, commonly known as the common onion, is a bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical leaves and a clustered bulb composed of modified leaves called scales. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in umbels, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its bulbs are rich in sulfur compounds that contribute to its pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

Allium esculentum is widely cultivated for culinary use and has been grown for thousands of years across various cultures.
