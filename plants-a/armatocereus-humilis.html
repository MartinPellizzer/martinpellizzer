Armatocereus humilis

Armatocereus humilis, commonly known as the lowland night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense clusters of spines. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Its growth habit and floral characteristics make it a unique and visually striking member of the Cactaceae family.
