Anarrhinum corsicum

Anarrhinum corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and large, showy purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant has a robust root system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions.

Anarrhinum corsicum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
