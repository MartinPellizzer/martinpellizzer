Adonis aestivalis var. ochroleuca

Adonis aestivalis var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the summer adonis or white adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in a raceme. The plant produces small, oval seeds enclosed in a persistent, membranous capsule. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
