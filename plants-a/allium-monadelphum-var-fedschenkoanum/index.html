Allium Monadelphum Var. Fedschenkoanum

Allium monadelphum var. fedschenkoanum is a rare and striking plant native to the cold regions of Central Asia. It is known for its dense, spherical clusters of purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. This variety is closely related to the common garlic and shares some of its medicinal properties. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes. Due to its limited habitat, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring careful conservation efforts.
