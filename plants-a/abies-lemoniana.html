Abies lemoniana

Abies lemoniana, commonly known as the lemon-scented fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often fragrant when crushed, emitting a citrus-like aroma. The tree typically grows in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soils. Its cones are small and cylindrical, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique fragrance and ecological significance.
