Anthurium ochreatum

Anthurium ochreatum, commonly known as the golden anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces vibrant spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the true flowers, often in shades of red or orange. Anthurium ochreatum thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
