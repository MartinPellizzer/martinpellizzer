Abutilon periplocifolium

Abutilon periplocifolium, commonly known as the wooly abutilon, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
