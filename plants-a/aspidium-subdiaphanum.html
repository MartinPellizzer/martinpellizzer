Aspidium subdiaphanum

Aspidium subdiaphanum, commonly known as the translucent wood fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are often semi-transparent, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with small, elongated pinnae that are often covered in fine hairs.

Aspidium subdiaphanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
