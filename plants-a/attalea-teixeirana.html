Attalea teixeirana

Attalea teixeirana, commonly known as the Brazilian buriti, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by large, oval fruits containing edible seeds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in wet, nutrient-poor soils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
