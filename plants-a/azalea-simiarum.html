Azalea simiarum

Azalea simiarum, commonly known as the mountain azalea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea simiarum is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays an important role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
