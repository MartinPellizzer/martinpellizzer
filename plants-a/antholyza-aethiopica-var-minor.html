Antholyza aethiopica var. minor

Antholyza aethiopica var. minor, commonly known as the lesser African sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and linear, succulent leaves adapted to water conservation. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
