Acacia chrysothrix

Acacia chrysothrix, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellowish-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant, and it plays a role in stabilizing soils in arid regions.

The seeds are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices and for commercial purposes.
