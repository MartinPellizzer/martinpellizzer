Agrostistachys indica var. massoana

Agrostistachys indica var. massoana, commonly known as the Indian grass or massoana grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along waterways. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in clusters, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
