Anisomeris microloba

Anisomeris microloba, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that resemble dandelion puffs. Its succulent-like leaves and stems allow it to store water, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Anisomeris microloba is often found in rocky soils and is a valuable plant for stabilizing desert landscapes.
