Aureliana picta

Aureliana picta, commonly known as the painted auricle, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its ornate, colorful foliage.

Its leaves are deeply lobed and often display a vibrant mix of purples, reds, and whites, creating a visually stunning appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with a compact and bushy habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden settings.

Aureliana picta is also noted for its aromatic properties, with a subtle, spicy scent that adds to its appeal.
