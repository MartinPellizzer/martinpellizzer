Andropogon quartinianus

Andropogon quartinianus, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and blue-green foliage, which turn golden in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its dense growth habit makes it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.

Andropogon quartinianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in wildlife habitat.
