Apium candollei

Apium candollei, commonly known as the wild celery or sea celery, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its small white flowers are arranged in compound umbels, which attract various pollinators.

Apium candollei is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties, though it is less commonly cultivated than its domesticated relatives.
