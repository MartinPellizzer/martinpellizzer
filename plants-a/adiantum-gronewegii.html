Adiantum gronewegii

Adiantum gronewegii, commonly known as the Groneweg's maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a slender, arching structure that contributes to its ornamental value.

Adiantum gronewegii is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
