Acacia lehmannii

Acacia lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody stems and bipinnate leaves are typical of many Acacia species.

Acacia lehmannii is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
