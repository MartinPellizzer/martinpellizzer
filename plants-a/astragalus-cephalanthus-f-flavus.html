Astragalus cephalanthus f. flavus

Astragalus cephalanthus f. flavus, commonly known as the yellow head milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to the western United States. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its bright yellow floral coloration, which sets it apart from other members of the Astragalus genus.
