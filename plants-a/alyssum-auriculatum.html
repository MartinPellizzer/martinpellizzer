Alyssum auriculatum

Alyssum auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Alyssum auriculatum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
