Alchemilla grandiceps

Alchemilla grandiceps, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in spring, typically clustered in narrow panicles. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.

Alchemilla grandiceps thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
