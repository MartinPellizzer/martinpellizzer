Achillea nobilis var. delphinensis

Achillea nobilis var. delphinensis, commonly known as Sea Holly, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It features silver-blue, spike-like flower heads surrounded by finely divided, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and long-lasting flowers that bloom in summer.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
