Acalypha sphenophylla

Acalypha sphenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf copperleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, coppery-red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically narrow and elongated, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
