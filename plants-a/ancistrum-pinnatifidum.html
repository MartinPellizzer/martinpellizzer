Ancistrum pinnatifidum

Ancistrum pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed arrowroot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive pinnatifid (bifurcated) margin, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Ancistrum pinnatifidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
