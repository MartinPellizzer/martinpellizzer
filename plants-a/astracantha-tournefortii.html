Astracantha tournefortii

Astracantha tournefortii, commonly known as the Canary Islands thornvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Astracantha tournefortii is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
