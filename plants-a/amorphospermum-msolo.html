Amorphospermum msolo

Amorphospermum msolo, commonly known as the Msolo tree, is a species native to the eastern regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable component of local ecosystems.
