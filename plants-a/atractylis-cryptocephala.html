Atractylis cryptocephala

Atractylis cryptocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sand-verbena, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Atractylis cryptocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
