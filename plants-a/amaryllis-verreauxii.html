Amaryllis verreauxii

Amaryllis verreauxii, commonly known as the Verreaux's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears multiple blooms, often followed by a cluster of strap-like leaves. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires periods of dormancy for optimal flowering.

Amaryllis verreauxii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
