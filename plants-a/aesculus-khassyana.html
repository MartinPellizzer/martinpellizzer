Aesculus khassyana

Aesculus khassyana, commonly known as the Khassian horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically 10–20 cm in diameter and composed of 5–7 leaflets. The tree produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers in upright clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing 1–3 chestnut-like seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
