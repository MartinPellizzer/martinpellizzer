Amaranthus hybridus subsp. hypochondriacus

Amaranthus hybridus subsp.

hypochondriacus, commonly known as red leaf amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, feather-like leaves that range in color from green to red and purple.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into triangular, crisp seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and iron, and is used in various culinary and traditional applications.
