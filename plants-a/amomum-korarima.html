Amomum korarima

Amomum korarima, commonly known as the greater galangal, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system. The plant produces tall, leafy stalks that bear small, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.

The botanical features of Amomum korarima make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
