Acacia insularum

Acacia insularum, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a unique flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula and the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, thorn-like leaves and yellow, globular flower heads. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Acacia insularum is notable for its ecological and cultural significance in its native regions.

Its seeds are often used in traditional practices and for local crafts.
