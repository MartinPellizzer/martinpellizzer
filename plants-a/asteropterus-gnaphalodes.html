Asteropterus gnaphalodes

Asteropterus gnaphalodes, commonly known as the broadleaf sandmat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically wind-pollinated flowers. The plant features finely divided, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing it with a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Asteropterus gnaphalodes is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.
