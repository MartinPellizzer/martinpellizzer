Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco var. ellipticum

Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco var. ellipticum, commonly known as the white quebracho, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and smooth, dark bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, elliptical seeds.

It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making, and for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
