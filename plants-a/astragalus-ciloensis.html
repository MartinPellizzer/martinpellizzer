Astragalus ciloensis

Astragalus ciloensis, commonly known as the Ciloensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several small, rounded seeds.

Astragalus ciloensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
