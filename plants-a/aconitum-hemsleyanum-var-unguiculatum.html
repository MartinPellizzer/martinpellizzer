Aconitum hemsleyanum var. unguiculatum

Aconitum hemsleyanum var. unguiculatum, commonly known as the Hemsley's aconite, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters called racemes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
