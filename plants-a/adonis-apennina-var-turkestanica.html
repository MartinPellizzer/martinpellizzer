Adonis apennina var. turkestanica

Adonis apennina var. turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a distinctive, elongated seed capsule.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
