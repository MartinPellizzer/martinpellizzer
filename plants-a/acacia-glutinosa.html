Acacia glutinosa

Acacia glutinosa, commonly known as sticky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, dark green leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in globular clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its bark is smooth and often has a sticky resinous coating, which gives it its common name.

Acacia glutinosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry climates.
