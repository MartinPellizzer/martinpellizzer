Alyssum latifolium

Alyssum latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that features small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces numerous small, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum latifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
