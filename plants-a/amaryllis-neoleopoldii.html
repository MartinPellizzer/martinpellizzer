Amaryllis neoleopoldii

Amaryllis neoleopoldii, commonly known as the Neoleopold's amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically broad and pointed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Amaryllis neoleopoldii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
