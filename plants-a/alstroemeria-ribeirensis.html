Alstroemeria ribeirensis

Alstroemeria ribeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Alstroemeria ribeirensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
