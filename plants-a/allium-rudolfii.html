Allium rudolfii

Allium rudolfii, commonly known as Rudolf's onion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears clusters of white to pale purple flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Allium rudolfii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
