Allium boissieri

Allium boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's onion, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in the summer, which are followed by spherical, dry berries. Allium boissieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some culinary uses in traditional recipes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
