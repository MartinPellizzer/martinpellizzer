Artemisia viridis

Artemisia viridis, commonly known as green wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green leaves and a strong, aromatic odor. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that develop into pale brown seeds.

Artemisia viridis is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
