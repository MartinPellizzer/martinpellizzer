Astragalus australis var. canescens

Astragalus australis var. canescens, commonly known as the grey-hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in fine, greyish-white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
