Agave × winteriana

Agave × winteriana, commonly known as the blue agave, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Agave americana and Agave angustifolia.

It is native to the arid regions of Mexico and is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and tall, branched inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves stores water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The blue agave is also cultivated for its sap, which is used in the production of tequila and other agave-based beverages.
