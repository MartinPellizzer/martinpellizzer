Aspilia subscandens

Aspilia subscandens, commonly known as the climbing aspilia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a rough or hairy texture.

Aspilia subscandens is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
