Amomum elegans

Amomum elegans, commonly known as the elegant cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant features tall, slender rhizomes that produce aromatic, three-lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it is valued for its fragrant, spice-like seeds.

The botanical characteristics of Amomum elegans make it a unique and sought-after plant in both horticulture and culinary applications.
