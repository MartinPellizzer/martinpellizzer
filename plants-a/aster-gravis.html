Aster gravis

Aster gravis, commonly known as the heavy aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. Aster gravis thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.
