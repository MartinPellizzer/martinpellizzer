Achroanthes corymbosa

Achroanthes corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered star-milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
