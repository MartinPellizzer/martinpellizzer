Anthericum condensum

Anthericum condensum, commonly known as the dense adder's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in a dense, spike-like structure on the spadix.

Anthericum condensum is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.
