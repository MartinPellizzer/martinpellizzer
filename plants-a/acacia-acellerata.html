Acacia acellerata

Acacia acellerata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species.

Acacia acellerata plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
