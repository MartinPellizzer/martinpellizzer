Alchemilla chirophylla f. truncata

Alchemilla chirophylla f. truncata, commonly known as the cut-leaved alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and truncate at the tip, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, typical of the genus Alchemilla.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.
