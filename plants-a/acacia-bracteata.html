Acacia bracteata

Acacia bracteata, commonly known as the bracted acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, green, trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces elongated, flat seed pods that contain hard, angular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
