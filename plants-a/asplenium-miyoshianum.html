Asplenium miyoshianum

Asplenium miyoshianum, commonly known as the Miyoshi's spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces spores in clusters called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or forest floors.

Asplenium miyoshianum is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
