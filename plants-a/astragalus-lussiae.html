Astragalus lussiae

Astragalus lussiae, commonly known as the Lussia milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold and arid conditions. Astragalus lussiae is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility in its native environment.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
