Artemisia packardiae

Artemisia packardiae, commonly known as Packard's sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open landscapes. Its essential oils contribute to its strong scent and have been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.

Artemisia packardiae plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
