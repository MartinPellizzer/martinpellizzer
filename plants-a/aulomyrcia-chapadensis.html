Aulomyrcia chapadensis

Aulomyrcia chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a woody shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
