Actinidia fulvicoma var. pachyphylla

Actinidia fulvicoma var. pachyphylla, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is part of the Actinidiaceae family and is known for its edible berries, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
