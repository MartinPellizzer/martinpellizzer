Asterocephalus diffusus

Asterocephalus diffusus, commonly known as the spreading star-headed plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped inflorescences, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in open, sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
