Anthriscus sylvestris subsp. faurei

Anthriscus sylvestris subsp.

faurei, commonly known as the Italian cow parsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, hollow stems that are often tinged with purple, and its leaves are finely divided, giving it a lacy appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
