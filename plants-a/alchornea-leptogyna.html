Alchornea leptogyna

Alchornea leptogyna, commonly known as the Brazilian star-apple tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a source of food.
