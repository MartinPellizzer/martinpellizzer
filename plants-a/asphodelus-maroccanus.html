Asphodelus maroccanus

Asphodelus maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is drought-resistant.

Asphodelus maroccanus is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
