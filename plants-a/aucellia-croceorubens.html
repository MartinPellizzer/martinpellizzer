Aucellia croceorubens

Aucellia croceorubens, commonly known as the red-orange bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy leaves. Its flowers are tubular and bell-shaped, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aucellia croceorubens prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
