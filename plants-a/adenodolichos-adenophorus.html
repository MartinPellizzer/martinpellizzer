Adenodolichos adenophorus

Adenodolichos adenophorus, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds.

Adenodolichos adenophorus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
