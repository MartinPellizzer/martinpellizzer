Asarum caudigerum var. cardiophyllum

Asarum caudigerum var. cardiophyllum, commonly known as the heartleaf asarum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in umbels, often emerging from the base of the leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Aristolochiaceae family.
