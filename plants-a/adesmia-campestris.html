Adesmia campestris

Adesmia campestris, commonly known as the field adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Adesmia campestris is often used in ecological studies due to its role in nitrogen fixation and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
