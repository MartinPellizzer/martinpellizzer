Albuca sordida

Albuca sordida, commonly known as the brown albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, bulbous underground storage organs called corms, which help it survive in dry conditions. Albuca sordida is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and delicate blooms.
