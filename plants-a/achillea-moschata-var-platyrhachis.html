Achillea moschata var. platyrhachis

Achillea moschata var. platyrhachis, commonly known as the broad-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, finely divided leaves that are gray-green in color and covered with a layer of fine hairs. The plant produces flat, clustered flower heads composed of tiny, white to pale yellow florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and medicinal plant collections.
