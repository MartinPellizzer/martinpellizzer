Aerangis pumilio

Aerangis pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in warm, moist conditions with indirect sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
