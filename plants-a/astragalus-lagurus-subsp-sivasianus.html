Astragalus lagurus subsp. sivasianus

Astragalus lagurus subsp.

sivasianus, commonly known as the Sivasian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia, particularly Turkey and Iran. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
