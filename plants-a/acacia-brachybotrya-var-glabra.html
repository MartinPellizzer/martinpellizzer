Acacia brachybotrya var. glabra

Acacia brachybotrya var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, pale green phyllodes that are smooth and glabrous. The plant produces yellow globular inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, sweet-smelling flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
