Ambrosia paniculata var. peruviana

Ambrosia paniculata var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and feathery, finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically borne on long, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
