Agapetes buxifolia

Agapetes buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf silverberry, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, red berries that are edible for some bird species.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
