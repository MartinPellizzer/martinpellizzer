Amomum uliginosum

Amomum uliginosum, commonly known as the black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a thick layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which eventually develop into dark brown, aromatic pods. Its leaves and pods are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested regions at high altitudes.
