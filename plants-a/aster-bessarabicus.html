Aster bessarabicus

Aster bessarabicus, commonly known as the Bessarabian aster, is a flowering plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Russia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Aster bessarabicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and ecological benefits.
