Acacia subtilinervis

Acacia subtilinervis, commonly known as the fine-veined acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves with finely divided, thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden yellow globular flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its pods are flat, linear, and contain several seeds enclosed in a fibrous mesh.

Acacia subtilinervis is adapted to dry environments and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.
