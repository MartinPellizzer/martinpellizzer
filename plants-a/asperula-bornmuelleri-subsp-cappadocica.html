Asperula bornmuelleri subsp. cappadocica

Asperula bornmuelleri subsp.

cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian azalea, is a flowering shrub native to Turkey. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms. Asperula bornmuelleri subsp.

cappadocica is also noted for its ability to tolerate cold climates and its historical use in traditional landscaping.
