Adiantum oaxacanum

Adiantum oaxacanum, commonly known as the Oaxaca maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as along streams or in forest understories. Its fronds are bipinnate, with finely divided segments that give it a feathery appearance.

Adiantum oaxacanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
