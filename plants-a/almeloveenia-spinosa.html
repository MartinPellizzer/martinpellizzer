Almeloveenia spinosa

Almeloveenia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny almelove, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
