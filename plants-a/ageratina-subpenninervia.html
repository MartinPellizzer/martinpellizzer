Ageratina subpenninervia

Ageratina subpenninervia, commonly known as the whiteface ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Ageratina subpenninervia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
