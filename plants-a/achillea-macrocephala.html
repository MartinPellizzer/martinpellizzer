Achillea macrocephala

Achillea macrocephala, commonly known as giant yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, flat-topped clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Achillea macrocephala is known for its long flowering period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
