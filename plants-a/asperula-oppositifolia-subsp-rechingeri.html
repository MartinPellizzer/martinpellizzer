Asperula oppositifolia subsp. rechingeri

Asperula oppositifolia subsp.

rechingeri, commonly known as the Alpine woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
