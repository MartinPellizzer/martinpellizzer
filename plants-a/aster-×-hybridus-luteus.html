Aster × hybridus luteus

Aster × hybridus luteus, commonly known as the golden wood aster, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom from late summer into early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine down, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Native to the northeastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.
