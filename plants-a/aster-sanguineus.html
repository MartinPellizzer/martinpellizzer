Aster sanguineus

Aster sanguineus, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are known to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
