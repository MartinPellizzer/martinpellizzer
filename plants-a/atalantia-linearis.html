Atalantia linearis

Atalantia linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries that turn red in the fall. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
