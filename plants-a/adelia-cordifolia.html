Adelia cordifolia

Adelia cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved adelia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically thick and fleshy to store water. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
