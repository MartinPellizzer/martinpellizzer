Anguria integrifolia

Anguria integrifolia, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of fruit known as tuna. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores.

Anguria integrifolia is also valued for its edible fruit and has been used in traditional medicine by various indigenous cultures.
