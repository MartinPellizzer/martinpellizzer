Aglaia pyriformis

Aglaia pyriformis, commonly known as the oval-leaf aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other hardwood trees. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.
