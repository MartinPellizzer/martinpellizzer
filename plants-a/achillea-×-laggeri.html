Achillea × laggeri

Achillea × laggeri, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a cross between Achillea millefolium (common yarrow) and Achillea ptarmica (woolly yarrow), resulting in a plant with distinctive foliage and floral characteristics. The plant features feathery, finely divided leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Achillea × laggeri is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
