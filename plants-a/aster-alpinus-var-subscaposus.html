Aster alpinus var. subscaposus

Aster alpinus var. subscaposus, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
