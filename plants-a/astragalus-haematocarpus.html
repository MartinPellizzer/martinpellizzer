Astragalus haematocarpus

Astragalus haematocarpus, commonly known as the blood carpus milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, swollen seed pods that are often red or purple, giving it its common name. It is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Astragalus haematocarpus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
