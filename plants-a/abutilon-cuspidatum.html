Abutilon cuspidatum

Abutilon cuspidatum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of lantern-like, orange-red bracts that resemble small lanterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by fuzzy, spiny capsules. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often grown for its ornamental value.

Abutilon cuspidatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
