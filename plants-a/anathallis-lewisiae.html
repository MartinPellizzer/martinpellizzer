Anathallis lewisiae

Anathallis lewisiae, commonly known as the Lewisia's spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

This rare and delicate orchid is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. It typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Anathallis lewisiae is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
