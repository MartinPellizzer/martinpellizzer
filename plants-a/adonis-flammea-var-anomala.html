Adonis flammea var. anomala

Adonis flammea var. anomala, commonly known as the flame anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
