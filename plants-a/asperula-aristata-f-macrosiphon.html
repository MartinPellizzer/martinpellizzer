Asperula aristata f. macrosiphon

Asperula aristata f. macrosiphon, commonly known as the large-siphon asperula, is a hybrid form of the European shrub Asperula.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly glossy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
