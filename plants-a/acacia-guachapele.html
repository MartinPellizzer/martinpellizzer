Acacia guachapele

Acacia guachapele, commonly known as the Guachapele acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically feathery and arranged in pairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing some resistance to drought and pests.

Acacia guachapele is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used traditionally in local crafts and construction.
