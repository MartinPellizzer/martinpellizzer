Allium tolmiei var. persimile

Allium tolmiei var. persimile, commonly known as the Persian shallot, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the summer. The plant's bulbs are edible, with a mild garlic flavor, and are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility in various cuisines.
