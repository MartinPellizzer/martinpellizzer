Anemone siuzevii

Anemone siuzevii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and meadows. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Anemone siuzevii is a perennial species that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
