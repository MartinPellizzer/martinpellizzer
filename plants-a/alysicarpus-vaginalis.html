Alysicarpus vaginalis

Alysicarpus vaginalis, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical annual herb native to Africa, South Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and invasive nature.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
