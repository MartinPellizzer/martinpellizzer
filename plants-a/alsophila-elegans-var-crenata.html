Alsophila elegans var. crenata

Alsophila elegans var. crenata, commonly known as the serrated tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds. The fronds are typically bright green and have a distinctive serrated edge, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
