Acacia hoffmannseggii

Acacia hoffmannseggii, commonly known as the Hoffmann's wattle, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features bipinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces yellow spherical flowerheads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia hoffmannseggii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
