Asperula involucrata

Asperula involucrata, commonly known as the woolly woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, often reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky slopes.

Asperula involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
