Alsophila chamaedendron

Alsophila chamaedendron, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and a crown of feathery, bipinnate fronds that give it a delicate, elegant appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal areas and lowland forests. Its rhizome system supports its growth, allowing it to form dense clumps over time.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in its natural habitat.
