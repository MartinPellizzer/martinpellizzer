Acer campestre f. subtrilobum

Acer campestre f. subtrilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed field maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe. The leaves are palmately lobed, typically with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.
