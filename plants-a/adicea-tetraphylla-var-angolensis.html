Adicea tetraphylla var. angolensis

Adicea tetraphylla var. angolensis, commonly known as the Angola four-leafed cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small herbaceous plant characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves. The flowers are small, yellow, and borne in racemes, typical of the mustard family. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
