Anemone alchemillifolia var. schlechteriana

Anemone alchemillifolia var. schlechteriana, commonly known as the Schlechter's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed with a hairy surface, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
