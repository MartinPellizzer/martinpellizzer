Acanthophyllum korolkowii

Acanthophyllum korolkowii, commonly known as the Korolkow's jointed acanthophyllum, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Acanthophyllum korolkowii plays a crucial role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
