Arundinaria glaziovii var. macroblephara

Arundinaria glaziovii var. macroblephara, commonly known as the broad-leaf bamboo, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
