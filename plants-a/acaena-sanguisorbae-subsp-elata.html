Acaena sanguisorbae subsp. elata

Acaena sanguisorbae subsp.

elata, commonly known as the tall bristly burr, is a perennial shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of spiny, bristly fruits.

The plant produces bright red or orange berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
