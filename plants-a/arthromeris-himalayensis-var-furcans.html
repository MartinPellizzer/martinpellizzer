Arthromeris himalayensis var. furcans

Arthromeris himalayensis var. furcans, commonly known as the Himalayan forked spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas, it thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh mountainous climates make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
