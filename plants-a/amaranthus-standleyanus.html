Amaranthus standleyanus

Amaranthus standleyanus, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, pink, or purple bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
