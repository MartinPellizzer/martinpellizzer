Adesmia nanolignea

Adesmia nanolignea, commonly known as the dwarf lignified adesmia, is a small shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and woody stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are usually small, narrow, and arranged in pairs along the stems.

Adesmia nanolignea is adapted to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
