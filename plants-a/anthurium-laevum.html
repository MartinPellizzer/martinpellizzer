Anthurium laevum

Anthurium laevum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble a spike of flowers, typically in shades of red or yellow. Anthurium laevum thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
