Aeginetia indica var. indica

Aeginetia indica var. indica, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.
