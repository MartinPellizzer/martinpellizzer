Aspilia quinquenervis

Aspilia quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined aster, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with five distinct veins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Aspilia quinquenervis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
