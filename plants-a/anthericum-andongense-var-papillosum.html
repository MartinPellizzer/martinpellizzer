Anthericum andongense var. papillosum

Anthericum andongense var. papillosum, commonly known as the papillate anthericum, is a rare and unique variant of the Anthericum genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive papillate (bumpy) structures on the surface of its leaves, which give it a textured appearance. This plant is native to specific regions in China and is adapted to moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is often described as having a grass-like appearance with a bluish-green hue.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
