Alternanthera albida f. globifera

Alternanthera albida f. globifera, commonly known as the globe willow or white willow, is a striking aquatic plant characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers.

It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant has feathery, ribbon-like leaves that are soft and delicate, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is often used in water gardens and pond landscapes for its aesthetic value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.

Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, adding a unique visual element to aquatic environments.
