Acalypha brasiliensis var. brevipes

Acalypha brasiliensis var. brevipes, commonly known as the Brazilian nettle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, palmate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
