Astragalus leptocarpoides

Astragalus leptocarpoides, commonly known as the slender fruit milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small seeds.

Astragalus leptocarpoides plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
