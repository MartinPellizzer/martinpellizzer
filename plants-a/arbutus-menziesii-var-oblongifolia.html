Arbutus menziesii var. oblongifolia

Arbutus menziesii var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
