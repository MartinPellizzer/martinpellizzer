Acrostichum producens

Acrostichum producens, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces spores in clusters called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique structure and growth habit make it a striking addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.
