Acanthus dioscoridis var. boissieri

Acanthus dioscoridis var. boissieri, commonly known as the Persian acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features rosettes of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces spires of violet-blue flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its architectural foliage and showy blooms.
