Alyxia scandens

Alyxia scandens, commonly known as the climbing mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine that typically grows along forest edges and in moist, shaded areas. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Alyxia scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
