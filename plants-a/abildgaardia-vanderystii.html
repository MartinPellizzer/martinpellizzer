Abildgaardia vanderystii

Abildgaardia vanderystii, commonly known as the Vanderyst's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique floral structure that resembles a ghostly apparition, hence its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers bloom briefly during the wet season, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered critically endangered and is protected in several countries.
