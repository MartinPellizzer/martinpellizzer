Anthyllis vulneraria var. tremedalis

Anthyllis vulneraria var. tremedalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

The species is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native range.
