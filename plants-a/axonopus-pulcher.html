Axonopus pulcher

Axonopus pulcher, commonly known as the velvetgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky texture, giving it a soft appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
