Asplenium filix-femina var. retusa

Asplenium filix-femina var. retusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features delicate, dark green fronds with a distinctive, feathery appearance, and its leaves are typically leathery and glossy. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
