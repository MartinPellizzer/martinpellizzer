Aster alpinus var. diversisquamus

Aster alpinus var. diversisquamus, commonly known as the Alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple florets. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand cold climates.
