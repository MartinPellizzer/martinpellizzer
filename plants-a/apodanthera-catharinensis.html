Apodanthera catharinensis

Apodanthera catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a short, narrow spur and a distinctive labellum. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is a terrestrial orchid, often found in areas with well-drained soil and high moisture levels.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
