Astragalus canosus

Astragalus canosus, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Astragalus canosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
