Acacia verticillata var. ovoidea

Acacia verticillata var. ovoidea, commonly known as the oval-leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, oval-shaped phyllodes that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils and low rainfall conditions.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging climates.
