Aningeria adolfi-friederici subsp. australis

Aningeria adolfi-friederici subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian aningeria, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal and garden landscapes.
