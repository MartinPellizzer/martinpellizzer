Acalypha scabrosa var. genuina

Acalypha scabrosa var. genuina, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are hairy and often reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are surrounded by bracts, which are often more showy than the actual blooms. Its leaves are typically ovate to lance-shaped, with a rough, textured surface that gives the plant its common name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
