Arundinaria viridistriata f. chrysophylla

Arundinaria viridistriata f. chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leafed bamboo, is a rare and ornamental variant of the green bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its dark green stems. This plant is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and exhibits a clumping growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in horticulture.
