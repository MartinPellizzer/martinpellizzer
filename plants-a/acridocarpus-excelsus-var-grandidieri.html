Acridocarpus excelsus var. grandidieri

Acridocarpus excelsus var. grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar nutmeg tree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It is often cultivated for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
