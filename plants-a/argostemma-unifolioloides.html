Argostemma unifolioloides

Argostemma unifolioloides, commonly known as the single leaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically has a short, thick rhizome and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are arrow-shaped, with a single leaflet, giving it a unique and elegant appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
