Agave connochaetodon

Agave connochaetodon, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces an inflorescence that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, white or pale green flowers. Its rosette growth pattern and ability to store water in its leaves make it well adapted to drought conditions.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in local cultures.
