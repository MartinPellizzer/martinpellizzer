Actaea spicata subsp. rubra

Actaea spicata subsp.

rubra, commonly known as red baneberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered, red berries that appear in late summer.

The plant features compound leaves with pinnately arranged leaflets and produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its striking appearance and toxic properties, it is both a notable ornamental and a plant of ecological significance.
