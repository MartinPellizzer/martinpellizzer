Anthemis orbelica

Anthemis orbelica, commonly known as the Armenian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically surrounded by a ring of narrow, leaf-like bracts. Anthemis orbelica is often grown for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal studies.
