Adenophora polydentata

Adenophora polydentata, commonly known as the five-toothed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with five distinct teeth along the edges, giving it its scientific name. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
