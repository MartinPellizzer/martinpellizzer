Allium straussii

Allium straussii, commonly known as the Strauss's garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, narrow leaves and umbel-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valuable addition to local cuisines.

Allium straussii is also noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to rocky, well-drained soils.
