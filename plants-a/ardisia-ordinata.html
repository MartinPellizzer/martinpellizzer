Ardisia ordinata

Ardisia ordinata, commonly known as the common ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Ardisia ordinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
