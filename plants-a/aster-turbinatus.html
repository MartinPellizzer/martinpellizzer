Aster turbinatus

Aster turbinatus, commonly known as the turban aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Aster turbinatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
