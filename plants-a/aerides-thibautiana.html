Aerides thibautiana

Aerides thibautiana, commonly known as the Thibaut's orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow or orange lip. It prefers cool, humid environments and is typically found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
