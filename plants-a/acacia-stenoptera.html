Acacia stenoptera

Acacia stenoptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are narrow and often have a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia stenoptera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
