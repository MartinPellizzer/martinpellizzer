Andropogon parvifolius

Andropogon parvifolius, commonly known as littleleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers.

Andropogon parvifolius is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological value and ability to stabilize soil.
