Ajax lobularis var. scotica

Ajax lobularis var. scotica, commonly known as the Scottish broom, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
