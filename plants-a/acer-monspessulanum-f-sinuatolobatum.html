Acer monspessulanum f. sinuatolobatum

Acer monspessulanum f. sinuatolobatum, commonly known as the Sinuate-lobed Maple, is a unique variant of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sinuate-lobed leaves that are broader and more deeply cut than those of the typical Norway maple. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland areas. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the autumn, making it a striking ornamental tree.

The species is valued for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
