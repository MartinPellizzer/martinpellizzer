Aster parryi

Aster parryi, commonly known as Parry's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster parryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
