Acacia baueri subsp. aspera

Acacia baueri subsp.

aspera, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its grey-green, narrow, and often thorn-bearing leaves.

The plant produces yellow spherical flowers that appear in winter, followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rough, hairy stems and leaves compared to other acacia species.
