Actinea quinquesquamata

Actinea quinquesquamata, commonly known as the five-armed sea star, is a marine echinoderm belonging to the class Asteroidea.

It is characterized by its five distinct arms, each with a unique arrangement of radial plates and ambulacral grooves. The body is covered in a calcareous endoskeleton, providing structural support and protection. It inhabits shallow coastal waters and is often found in rocky substrates.

This species plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by controlling prey populations and contributing to nutrient cycling.
