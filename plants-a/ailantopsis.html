Ailantopsis

Ailantopsis, commonly known as the Chinese cliff-rose, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae.

It consists of a single species, Ailantopsis sinensis, which is native to China. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with compound leaves and yellowish flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Ailantopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
