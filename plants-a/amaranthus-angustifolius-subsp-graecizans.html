Amaranthus angustifolius subsp. graecizans

Amaranthus angustifolius subsp.

graecizans, commonly known as Greek amaranth, is a species native to the Mediterranean region. It is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into spiny, three-angled capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional cuisines.
