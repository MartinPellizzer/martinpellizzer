Aesculus × carnea f. briotii

Aesculus × carnea f. briotii, commonly known as the Briot's horse chestnut, is a cultivated form of the horse chestnut tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-flowered blossoms that are pink and white, creating a striking visual display in spring. The plant is a hybrid between Aesculus × carnea and Aesculus hippocastanum, showcasing a blend of traits from both parent species. Its foliage consists of large, palmately compound leaves that provide dense shade.

This ornamental tree is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
