Astragalus inchebroonensis

Astragalus inchebroonensis, commonly known as the Inchebroon milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish to greenish flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus inchebroonensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
