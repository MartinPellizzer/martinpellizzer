Andropogon neesii var. apogynus

Andropogon neesii var. apogynus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
