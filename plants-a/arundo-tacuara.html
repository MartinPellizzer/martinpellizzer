Arundo tacuara

Arundo tacuara, commonly known as the Tacuara reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to South America.

It features long, slender leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often found near rivers and lakes. Its hollow stems are used in traditional crafts and as a source of material for building.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many other grasses.
