Arrabidaea leucopogon

Arrabidaea leucopogon, commonly known as the white-flowered arrabidaea, is a shrub native to the Cape Floristic Region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a notable species in the fynbos biome.
