Aristea elata

Aristea elata, commonly known as the silverleaf aristea, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects light and reduces water loss. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
