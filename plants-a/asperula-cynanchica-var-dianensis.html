Asperula cynanchica var. dianensis

Asperula cynanchica var. dianensis, commonly known as the Dianese sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, coiled tendrils that help it climb. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
