Aegialophila cretica

Aegialophila cretica, commonly known as the Cretan sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. It has small, narrow leaves and produces showy, thistle-like blooms.

Aegialophila cretica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
