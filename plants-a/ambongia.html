Ambongia

Ambongia, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Central and East Africa.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that give way to round, yellow fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp. The fruit is often eaten fresh or used in jams and beverages.

Ambongia is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
