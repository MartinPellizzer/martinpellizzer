Azorella ecuadorensis

Azorella ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian azorella, is a low-growing cushion plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

Azorella ecuadorensis is known for its slow growth and ability to survive in harsh, rocky soils.
