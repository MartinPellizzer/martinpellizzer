Angelica sylvestris subsp. montana

Angelica sylvestris subsp.

montana, commonly known as mountain angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It features large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and can reach up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The roots of this species are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
