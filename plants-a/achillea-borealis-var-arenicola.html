Achillea borealis var. arenicola

Achillea borealis var. arenicola, commonly known as the sand yarrow, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in rocky or coastal habitats.
