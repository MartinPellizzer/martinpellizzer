Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. nigricans

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

nigricans, commonly known as the black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically deep red or black, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated nectar spur.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are usually hairy and upright. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
