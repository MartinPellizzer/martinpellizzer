Absinthium frigidum var. biebersteinianum

Absinthium frigidum var. biebersteinianum, commonly known as the Siberian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and have a distinctive camphor-like scent.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often cultivated in alpine gardens or as a medicinal plant.
