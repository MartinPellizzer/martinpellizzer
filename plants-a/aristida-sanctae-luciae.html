Aristida sanctae-luciae

Aristida sanctae-luciae, commonly known as the Holy Lucia grass, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
