Agave rufocincta

Agave rufocincta, commonly known as the red-striped agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often lined with red or purple markings. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Agave rufocincta is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
