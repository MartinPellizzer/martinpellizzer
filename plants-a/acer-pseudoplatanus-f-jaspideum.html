Acer pseudoplatanus f. jaspideum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. jaspideum, commonly known as the variegated maple, is a cultivar of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features a mix of green, yellow, and white colors. The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated margin, typical of the genus Acer. This plant is often grown as an ornamental tree or shrub in gardens due to its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
