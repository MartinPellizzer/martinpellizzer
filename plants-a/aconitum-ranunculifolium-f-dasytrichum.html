Aconitum ranunculifolium f. dasytrichum

Aconitum ranunculifolium f. dasytrichum, commonly known as the hairy leaf aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant features palmately compound leaves with a hairy texture, which is a distinguishing feature of this particular form. Its flowers are blue to purple, clustered in racemes, and are typically borne on the upper part of the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
