Alnus glutinosa subsp. antitaurica

Alnus glutinosa subsp.

antitaurica, commonly known as the Turkish alder, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in catkins, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or near water sources.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
