Arctostaphylos incognita

Arctostaphylos incognita, commonly known as the unknown manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red or orange berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or coastal environments.

Arctostaphylos incognita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
