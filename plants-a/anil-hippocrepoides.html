Anil hippocrepoides

Anil hippocrepoides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a robust root system and hairy stems, which help it thrive in various soil types. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Anil hippocrepoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
