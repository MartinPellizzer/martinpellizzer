Aeschynomene morumbensis

Aeschynomene morumbensis, commonly known as Morumbense false indigo, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with Rhizobia bacteria.

Aeschynomene morumbensis is also valued for its potential use in forage and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
