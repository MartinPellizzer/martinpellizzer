Adyseton spathulatum

Adyseton spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed adyseton, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
