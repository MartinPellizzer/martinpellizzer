Astragalus chinzarticus

Astragalus chinzarticus, commonly known as the Chinzart's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus chinzarticus is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in some regions for its purported health benefits.
