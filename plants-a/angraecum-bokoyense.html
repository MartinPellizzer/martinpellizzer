Angraecum bokoyense

Angraecum bokoyense, commonly known as the Bokoye orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are white with violet markings and have a unique, intricate structure that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
