Acanthosyris falcata

Acanthosyris falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthosyris, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep roots and minimal water requirements.

Acanthosyris falcata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
