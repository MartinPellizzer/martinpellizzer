Aspidium ludens

Aspidium ludens, commonly known as the running brake, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, often hairy underside. Its fronds are typically long and narrow, with a leathery texture.

Aspidium ludens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
