Avenochloa adsurgens

Avenochloa adsurgens, commonly known as the climbing barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect or prostrate growth habit and can form dense mats or climb over other vegetation. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain agricultural areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
