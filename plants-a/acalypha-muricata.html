Acalypha muricata

Acalypha muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Acalypha muricata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
