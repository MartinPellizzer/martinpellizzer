Amaryllis belladonna subsp. barbata

Amaryllis belladonna subsp.

barbata, commonly known as the Barbata amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa. It features large, showy flowers that range from red to orange, often with a distinctive yellow or white throat.

The plant produces strap-like, linear leaves that are broad at the base and taper towards the tip. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
