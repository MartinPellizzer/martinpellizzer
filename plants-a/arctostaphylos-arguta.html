Arctostaphylos arguta

Arctostaphylos arguta, commonly known as the smooth manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are a food source for various wildlife, including birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Arctostaphylos arguta is notable for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand fire, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
