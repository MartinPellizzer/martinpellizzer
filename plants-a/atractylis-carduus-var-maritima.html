Atractylis carduus var. maritima

Atractylis carduus var. maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, silver-gray leaves and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing on rocky shores and dunes. Its robust root system helps stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species for coastal vegetation.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand salty air and wind.
