Alnus jorullensis subsp. lutea

Alnus jorullensis subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the yellow alder, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its yellowish-brown bark and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers that are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insect species.
