Aconitum napellus f. delphiniifolium

Aconitum napellus f. delphiniifolium, commonly known as the Delphinium-leaved monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are large, deeply lobed, and resemble those of delphiniums, giving the plant its common name. It is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and woodland settings.
