Acacia tindaleae

Acacia tindaleae, commonly known as the Tindale's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Acacia tindaleae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
