Astragaloides australis

Astragaloides australis, commonly known as the Australian milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across suitable habitats.
