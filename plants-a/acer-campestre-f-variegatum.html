Acer campestre f. variegatum

Acer campestre f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated field maple, is a cultivar of the field maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. This plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The leaves are palmately lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes.
