Acer campestre f. falcatum

Acer campestre f. falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maple, is a variant of the common maple characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

This deciduous shrub or small tree typically grows in woodland areas and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are usually a vibrant green in summer and turn yellow or red in autumn, adding seasonal color to the landscape. The plant produces small, red or yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
