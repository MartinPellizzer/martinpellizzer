Alsophila phalaenolepis

Alsophila phalaenolepis, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped fronds that are covered in a silvery, powdery bloom. The plant has a slender, unbranched trunk that is often wrapped in a fibrous sheath of old leaf bases. Its fronds are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark, which is believed to attract nocturnal pollinators.

This unique botanical feature makes it a rare and fascinating species within the Cyatheaceae family.
