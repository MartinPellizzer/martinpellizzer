Astragalus paghmanensis

Astragalus paghmanensis, commonly known as the Pamir milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus paghmanensis plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
