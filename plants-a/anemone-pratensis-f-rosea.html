Anemone pratensis f. rosea

Anemone pratensis f. rosea, commonly known as the pink meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that range from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a hairy texture, while the flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
