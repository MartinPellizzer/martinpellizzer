Astragalus didymocarpus

Astragalus didymocarpus, commonly known as the twin-fruit milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces two distinct pods, giving it the common name "twin-fruit," and it has a deep root system adapted to drought conditions.

Astragalus didymocarpus is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
