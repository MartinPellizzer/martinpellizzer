Amomum deorianum

Amomum deorianum, commonly known as the Vietnamese cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric.

It is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Cambodia. The plant produces fragrant, spadix-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its essential oils contain compounds such as cineole and limonene, contributing to its aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic rhizomes.
