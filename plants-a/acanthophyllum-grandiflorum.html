Acanthophyllum grandiflorum

Acanthophyllum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered acanthophyllum, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense clusters of small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Acanthophyllum grandiflorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
