Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. longana

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

longana, commonly known as long-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its common name reflects its historical use in herbal medicine and its ornamental value in gardens.
