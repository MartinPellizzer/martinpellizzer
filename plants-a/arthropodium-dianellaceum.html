Arthropodium dianellaceum

Arthropodium dianellaceum, commonly known as the New Zealand fern or the silver fern, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery foliage. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The silver fern is an important symbol of New Zealand and is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes.
