Allium senescens f. albiflorum

Allium senescens f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered garlic chive, is a botanical variant of the garlic chive species.

It is characterized by its white blossoms, which contrast with the typical purple or pink flowers of the standard variety. The plant produces long, slender leaves that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This ornamental and edible plant is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its mild garlic-like flavor.
