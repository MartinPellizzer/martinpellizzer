Astragalus hylophilus var. oblongifolius

Astragalus hylophilus var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the oblong-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often hairy, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the legume family. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
