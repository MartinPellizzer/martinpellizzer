Aquilegia glandulosa var. unicolor

Aquilegia glandulosa var. unicolor, commonly known as the single-flowered candytuft, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
