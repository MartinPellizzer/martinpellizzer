Alisma × rhicnocarpum

Alisma × rhicnocarpum, commonly known as the hybrid water plantain, is a wetland plant that thrives in shallow water or moist soil.

It is a natural hybrid between Alisma plantago-aquatica and Alisma orientale, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically floating or partially submerged. Its small, white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in early summer.

Alisma × rhicnocarpum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
