Andryala ragusina var. ramosissima

Andryala ragusina var. ramosissima, commonly known as the Sicilian ragusina, is a rare and endemic plant species native to southern Italy.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with dense, branched stems. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate.

It is often found in rocky or limestone-rich soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
