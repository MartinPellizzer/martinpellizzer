Acacia sarcophylla

Acacia sarcophylla, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody, cylindrical stems and phyllodes that are often reddish-brown in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Acacia sarcophylla plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
