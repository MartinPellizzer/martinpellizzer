Alnus incana f. hirsuta

Alnus incana f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy alder, is a variant of the alder tree characterized by its hairy foliage and bark.

The plant belongs to the Betulaceae family, which includes birches and hazels, and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its catkins are typically male and female.
