Alyssum rhodopense

Alyssum rhodopense, commonly known as the Rhodope alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has slender, hairy stems and linear, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Alyssum rhodopense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
