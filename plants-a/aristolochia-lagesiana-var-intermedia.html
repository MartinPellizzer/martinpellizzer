Aristolochia lagesiana var. intermedia

Aristolochia lagesiana var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble pipes. The plant produces dark purple to brown flowers with a strong, often unpleasant odor, which attracts specific pollinators. Its leaves are typically large and have a rough texture, while the stems are woody and can grow quite long.

This species is native to South America and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
