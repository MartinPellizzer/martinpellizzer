Antirrhinum sempervirens var. pertegasii

Antirrhinum sempervirens var. pertegasii, commonly known as the Pertegasii snapdragon, is a perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
