Angelica wilsonii

Angelica wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's angelica, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica wilsonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.
