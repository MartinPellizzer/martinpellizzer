Aristolochia kaempferi f. trilobata

Aristolochia kaempferi f. trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with three lobes and its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unusual floral structure.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
