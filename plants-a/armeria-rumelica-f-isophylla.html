Armeria rumelica f. isophylla

Armeria rumelica f. isophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thrift, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has linear, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, herbaceous structure and its ability to tolerate saline and drought conditions.
