Adenophora stenanthina

Adenophora stenanthina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs.

Adenophora stenanthina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
