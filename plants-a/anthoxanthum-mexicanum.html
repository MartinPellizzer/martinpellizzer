Anthoxanthum mexicanum

Anthoxanthum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican false brome, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

Anthoxanthum mexicanum is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
