Abutilon holosericeum

Abutilon holosericeum, commonly known as the silky abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
