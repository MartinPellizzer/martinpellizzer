Aloysia brasiliensis

Aloysia brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces vibrant clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and upright growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Aloysia brasiliensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
