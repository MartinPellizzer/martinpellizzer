Alsophila cooperi

Alsophila cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the wet tropical forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of compound leaves that give it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-drained soil.

Alsophila cooperi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for tropical gardens.
