Alyssum floribundum

Alyssum floribundum, commonly known as the sweet Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has silvery-green foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum floribundum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
