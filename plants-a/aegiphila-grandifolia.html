Aegiphila grandifolia

Aegiphila grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian aster, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Aegiphila grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The stem is upright, woody, and has a rough, fissured bark that is typically brown to reddish in color.
