Ajuga decumbens f. rosea

Ajuga decumbens f. rosea, commonly known as roseleaf bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ground cover in shaded gardens.
