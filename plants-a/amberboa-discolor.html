Amberboa discolor

Amberboa discolor, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
