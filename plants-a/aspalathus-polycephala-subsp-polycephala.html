Aspalathus polycephala subsp. polycephala

Aspalathus polycephala subsp.

polycephala, commonly known as African red bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is well adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers in spring, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is cultivated as a garden plant and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
