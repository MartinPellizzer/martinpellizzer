Arundinella scoparia

Arundinella scoparia, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense stands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually pale green or brown.

Arundinella scoparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
