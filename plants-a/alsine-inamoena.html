Alsine inamoena

Alsine inamoena, commonly known as the narrowleaf pigweed, is an annual plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces numerous tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Alsine inamoena is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
