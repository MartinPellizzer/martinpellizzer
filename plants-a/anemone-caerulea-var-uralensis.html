Anemone caerulea var. uralensis

Anemone caerulea var. uralensis, commonly known as the Ural blue anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are upright with few branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
