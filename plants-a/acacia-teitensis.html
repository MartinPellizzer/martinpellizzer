Acacia teitensis

Acacia teitensis, commonly known as the Teita acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

Acacia teitensis plays a role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
