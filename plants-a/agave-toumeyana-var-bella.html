Agave toumeyana var. bella

Agave toumeyana var. bella, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces tall, flowering stalks that bear small, greenish-white flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in the production of tequila.
