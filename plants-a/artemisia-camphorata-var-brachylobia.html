Artemisia camphorata var. brachylobia

Artemisia camphorata var. brachylobia, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features gray-green, aromatic foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles, which bloom in the summer months. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.

Its essential oils contain compounds like camphor and thujone, contributing to its strong, pungent aroma.
