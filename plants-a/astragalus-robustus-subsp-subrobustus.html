Astragalus robustus subsp. subrobustus

Astragalus robustus subsp.

subrobustus, commonly known as the robust milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
