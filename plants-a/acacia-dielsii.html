Acacia dielsii

Acacia dielsii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa, particularly found in regions like Kenya and Tanzania.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden-green flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia dielsii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through its deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
