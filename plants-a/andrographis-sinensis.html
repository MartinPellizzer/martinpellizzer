Andrographis sinensis

Andrographis sinensis, commonly known as the Indian bittersweet, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves and stems contain alkaloids, including andrographolide, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Andrographis sinensis is often cultivated for its use in traditional medicine, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
