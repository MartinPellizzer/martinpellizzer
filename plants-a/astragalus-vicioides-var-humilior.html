Astragalus vicioides var. humilior

Astragalus vicioides var. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to pale purple flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soils in dry environments.
