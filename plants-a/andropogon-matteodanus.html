Andropogon matteodanus

Andropogon matteodanus, commonly known as the Mexican bluestem, is a tall grass native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or blue in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common species in dry, open habitats.

Andropogon matteodanus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
