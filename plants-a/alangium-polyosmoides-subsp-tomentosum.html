Alangium polyosmoides subsp. tomentosum

Alangium polyosmoides subsp.

tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly alangium, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions.
