Aconitum moldavicum subsp. hosteanum

Aconitum moldavicum subsp.

hosteanum, commonly known as the Moldavian monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape.

The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It produces toxic alkaloids, making it highly poisonous to both humans and animals.

This subspecies is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
