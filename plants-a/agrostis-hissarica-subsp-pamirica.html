Agrostis hissarica subsp. pamirica

Agrostis hissarica subsp.

pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy blades.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often concealed within the leaf sheaths.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptation to high-altitude conditions and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
