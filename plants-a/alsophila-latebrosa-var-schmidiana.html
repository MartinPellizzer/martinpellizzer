Alsophila latebrosa var. schmidiana

Alsophila latebrosa var. schmidiana, commonly known as the Schmidian tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant exhibits a unique branching pattern, with the fronds emerging from the trunk in a rosette-like formation. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
