Asyneuma obtusifolium

Asyneuma obtusifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and rounded, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Asyneuma obtusifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
