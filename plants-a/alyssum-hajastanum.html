Alyssum hajastanum

Alyssum hajastanum, commonly known as Caucasian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Alyssum hajastanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil conditions.
