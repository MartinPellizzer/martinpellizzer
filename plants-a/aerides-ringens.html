Aerides ringens

Aerides ringens, commonly known as the orchid of the forest, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees in humid, shaded environments.

Aerides ringens prefers indirect light and high humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
