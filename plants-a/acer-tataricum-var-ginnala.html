Acer tataricum var. ginnala

Acer tataricum var. ginnala, commonly known as the Manchurian maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
