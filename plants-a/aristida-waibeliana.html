Aristida waibeliana

Aristida waibeliana, commonly known as the Waibel's threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the seed heads are distinctive for their three-awned appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
