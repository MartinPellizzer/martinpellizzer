Artemisia campestris subsp. typica

Artemisia campestris subsp.

typica, commonly known as greater wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in summer.

The plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, distinctive odor.
