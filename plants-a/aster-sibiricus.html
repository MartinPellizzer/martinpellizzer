Aster sibiricus

Aster sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian aster, is a flowering plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subarctic climates. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Siberian aster is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh weather conditions.
