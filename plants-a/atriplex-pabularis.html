Atriplex pabularis

Atriplex pabularis, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, often grayish leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal areas or salt marshes.

Atriplex pabularis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
