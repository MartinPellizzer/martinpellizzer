Actinea acaulis var. simplex

Actinea acaulis var. simplex, commonly known as the naked stem actinia, is a unique plant species characterized by its lack of leaves and its simple, unbranched stem.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
