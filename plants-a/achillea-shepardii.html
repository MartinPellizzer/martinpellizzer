Achillea shepardii

Achillea shepardii, commonly known as Sheppard's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
