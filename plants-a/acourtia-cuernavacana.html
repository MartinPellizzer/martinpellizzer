Acourtia cuernavacana

Acourtia cuernavacana, commonly known as the Cuernavaca dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is found in regions with a temperate climate. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Acourtia cuernavacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
