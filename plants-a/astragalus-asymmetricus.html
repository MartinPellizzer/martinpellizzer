Astragalus asymmetricus

Astragalus asymmetricus, commonly known as the asymmetric milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

Astragalus asymmetricus is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
