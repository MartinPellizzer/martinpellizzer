Alyssum heterotrichum

Alyssum heterotrichum, commonly known as the silverleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum heterotrichum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
