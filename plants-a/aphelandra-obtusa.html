Aphelandra obtusa

Aphelandra obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf torchflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that resemble torches, blooming during the evening. Aphelandra obtusa thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
