Actinocephalus armeria

Actinocephalus armeria, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, silver-gray leaves and showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble small holly berries. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Actinocephalus armeria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
