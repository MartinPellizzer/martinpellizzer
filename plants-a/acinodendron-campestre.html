Acinodendron campestre

Acinodendron campestre, commonly known as the creeping acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acinodendron campestre is often used in traditional medicine and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
