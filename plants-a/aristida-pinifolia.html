Aristida pinifolia

Aristida pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf dropseed, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides.

Aristida pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
