Aeluropus badghyzi

Aeluropus badghyzi, commonly known as the Persian grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in harsh environments. The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to brown, with short, awned lemmas. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important species for ecological restoration projects.

Its adaptability to extreme conditions makes it a valuable plant for landscaping and erosion control in dry climates.
