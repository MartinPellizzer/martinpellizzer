Austrogramme decipiens

Austrogramme decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive austrogramme, is a unique member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble those of other species, a trait that may aid in its survival through mimicry. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures are concealed within the fronds, making it challenging to identify without close examination.

Due to its elusive nature, it remains a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptation and evolution.
