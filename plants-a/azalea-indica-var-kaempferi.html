Azalea indica var. kaempferi

Azalea indica var. kaempferi, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
