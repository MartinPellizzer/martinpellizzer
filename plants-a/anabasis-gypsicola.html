Anabasis gypsicola

Anabasis gypsicola, commonly known as the desert sandburs, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its robust, branched stems and small, spiny fruits. The plant is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access groundwater. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, while the leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs.

Anabasis gypsicola plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
