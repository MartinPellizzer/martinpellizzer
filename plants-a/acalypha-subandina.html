Acalypha subandina

Acalypha subandina, commonly known as the silverleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Acalypha subandina is relatively low-maintenance and adapts well to indoor growing conditions.
