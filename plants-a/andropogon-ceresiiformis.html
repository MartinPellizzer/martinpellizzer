Andropogon ceresiiformis

Andropogon ceresiiformis, commonly known as the sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky leaves that emit a pleasant, sweet aroma when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to golden flowers.

Andropogon ceresiiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by Indigenous peoples for ceremonial and medicinal purposes.
