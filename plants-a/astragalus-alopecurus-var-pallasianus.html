Astragalus alopecurus var. pallasianus

Astragalus alopecurus var. pallasianus, commonly known as Pallas's milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to regions of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptability and potential medicinal properties.
