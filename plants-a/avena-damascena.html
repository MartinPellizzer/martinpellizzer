Avena damascena

Avena damascena, commonly known as the Spanish needle or goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by feathery, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a soft, hairy texture.

Avena damascena thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
