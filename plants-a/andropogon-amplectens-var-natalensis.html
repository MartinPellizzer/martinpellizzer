Andropogon amplectens var. natalensis

Andropogon amplectens var. natalensis, commonly known as Natal grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Natal grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional contexts.
