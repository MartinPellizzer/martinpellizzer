Amygdalus camelliiflora

Amygdalus camelliiflora, commonly known as the Camellia-flowered almond, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a woody pod containing one or two seeds, which are edible and used in culinary applications.

Amygdalus camelliiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and mild almond scent.
