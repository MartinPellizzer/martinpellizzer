Acalypha polymorpha var. sericea

Acalypha polymorpha var. sericea, commonly known as velvetleaf or silky hibiscus, is a tropical plant characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, surrounded by the showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Its growth habit is bushy, with a tendency to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
