Alyssum ruprechtii

Alyssum ruprechtii, commonly known as the rock allyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has gray-green, fuzzy leaves and is well adapted to rocky and arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Alyssum ruprechtii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
