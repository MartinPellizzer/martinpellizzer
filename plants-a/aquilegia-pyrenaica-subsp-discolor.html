Aquilegia pyrenaica subsp. discolor

Aquilegia pyrenaica subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive central nectar spur.

The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in calcareous soils.

The subspecies discolor is distinguished by its white or pale pink flowers, which contrast with the green foliage.
