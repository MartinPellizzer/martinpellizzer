Alpinia coeruleoviridis

Alpinia coeruleoviridis, commonly known as the blue-green ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue on the upper surface and a lighter green underside. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale blue flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are often used in ornamental gardening. Its foliage is highly valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Alpinia coeruleoviridis thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
