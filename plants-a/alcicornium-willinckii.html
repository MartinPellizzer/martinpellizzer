Alcicornium willinckii

Alcicornium willinckii, commonly known as the Willinck's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of purple, white, and yellow hues. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in the dense, organic-rich soil of its natural habitat.

Alcicornium willinckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare tropical flora.
