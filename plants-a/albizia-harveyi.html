Albizia harveyi

Albizia harveyi, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a leguminous species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, contributing to its role in ecological systems.

Albizia harveyi is also valued for its hard wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
