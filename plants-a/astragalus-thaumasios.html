Astragalus thaumasios

Astragalus thaumasios, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often appear in shades of pink or white. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

Astragalus thaumasios is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
