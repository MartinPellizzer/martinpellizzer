Anemia scandens

Anemia scandens, commonly known as the climbing anemone, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a vine, climbing or trailing along the ground or other structures. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually lobed or notched.

The flowers are usually pink or white, with five to eight sepals and numerous stamens, making it a striking addition to gardens.
