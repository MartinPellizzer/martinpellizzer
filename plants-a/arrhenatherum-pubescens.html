Arrhenatherum pubescens

Arrhenatherum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wood oat, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
