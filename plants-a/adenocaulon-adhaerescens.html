Adenocaulon adhaerescens

Adenocaulon adhaerescens, commonly known as the stickyweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are covered in fine, sticky hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or sagebrush habitats.

Adenocaulon adhaerescens is adapted to arid environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.
