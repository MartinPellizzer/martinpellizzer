Angelica genuflexa subsp. refracea

Angelica genuflexa subsp.

refracea, commonly known as the bent angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often curved stem. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features large, compound leaves with a pinnate structure and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

The subspecies refracea is distinguished by its more pronounced stem curvature compared to other forms of Angelica genuflexa.
