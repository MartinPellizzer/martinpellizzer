Aristolochia balansae

Aristolochia balansae, commonly known as the Balansae birthwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-shaped flowers that resemble a pipe, a feature typical of the Aristolochiaceae family. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a strong, often unpleasant odor to attract its pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
