Aspidopterys cordata var. vermae

Aspidopterys cordata var. vermae, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
