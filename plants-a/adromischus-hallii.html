Adromischus hallii

Adromischus hallii, commonly known as the "Bottlebrush Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that resemble bottle brushes, typically in shades of pink or red. Its leaves are short and rounded, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Adromischus hallii is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements.
