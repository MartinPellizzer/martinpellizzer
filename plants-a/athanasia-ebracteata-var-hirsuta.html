Athanasia ebracteata var. hirsuta

Athanasia ebracteata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce numerous small, showy flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers are typically pale blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer to early autumn.
