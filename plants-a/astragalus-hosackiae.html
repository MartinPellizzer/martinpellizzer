Astragalus hosackiae

Astragalus hosackiae, commonly known as the Hosack's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological significance.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
