Alphonsea squamosa

Alphonsea squamosa, commonly known as the hairy alphonsea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, pinnate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. Alphonsea squamosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
