Astragalus leucospermus

Astragalus leucospermus, commonly known as white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus leucospermus is often found in dry grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and other semi-arid habitats.
