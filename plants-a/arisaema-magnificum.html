Arisaema magnificum

Arisaema magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, umbrella-shaped leaf that can grow up to 2 meters in diameter, making it one of the largest foliage plants in its genus. The plant produces a spadix, a dense cluster of tiny flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its botanical significance lies in its unique reproductive structures and its role in the Araceae family.

Arisaema magnificum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in botanical gardens for its dramatic appearance.
