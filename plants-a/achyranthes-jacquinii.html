Achyranthes jacquinii

Achyranthes jacquinii, commonly known as Jacquin's smartweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge when mature.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
