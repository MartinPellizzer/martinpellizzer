Aconitum guttatum

Aconitum guttatum, commonly known as the spotted monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, which are surrounded by two large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
