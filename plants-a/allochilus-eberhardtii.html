Allochilus eberhardtii

Allochilus eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
