Aechmea inermis

Aechmea inermis, commonly known as the naked leaf aechmea, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Aechmea inermis is evergreen and thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Its foliage is typically gray-green with a waxy texture, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.

Morphology

Aechmea inermis has leaves that are sword-shaped, arranged in a rosette, and can reach up to 40 cm in length, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, upright stem with a smooth, pale greenish-brown bark, and it grows primarily from the base. Its flowers are clustered in a dense inflorescence, with purple and white petals, blooming during the summer months, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a bright red berry containing numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by birds and water.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense mat near the soil surface, helping to anchor the plant and absorb nutrients.
