Aechmea inermis

Aechmea inermis, commonly known as the naked leaf aechmea, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Aechmea inermis is evergreen and thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Its foliage is typically gray-green with a waxy texture, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
