Atriplex babingtonii var. boucheri

Atriplex babingtonii var. boucheri, commonly known as Boucher's saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are typically covered in a layer of fine, salt-excreting hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
