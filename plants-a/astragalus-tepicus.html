Astragalus tepicus

Astragalus tepicus, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus tepicus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for wildlife.
