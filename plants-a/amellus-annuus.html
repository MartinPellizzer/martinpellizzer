Amellus annuus

Amellus annuus, commonly known as the common sunflower, is an annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center. The plant typically grows up to 3 meters tall, featuring a thick, hairy stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its scientific name reflects its annual life cycle and its native habitat.

The sunflower is widely cultivated for its seeds, which are used in food and oil production.
