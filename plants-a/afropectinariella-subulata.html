Afropectinariella subulata

Afropectinariella subulata, commonly known as the African hairgrass, is a small, perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland habitats and is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and slender stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features.

Afropectinariella subulata plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
