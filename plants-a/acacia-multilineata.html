Acacia multilineata

Acacia multilineata, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Acacia multilineata is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native animals.
