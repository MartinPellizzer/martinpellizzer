Ailanthus moluccana var. mollis

Ailanthus moluccana var. mollis, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a variant of the native Australian tree Ailanthus moluccana.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in coastal and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its resilience to coastal winds and salt spray.
