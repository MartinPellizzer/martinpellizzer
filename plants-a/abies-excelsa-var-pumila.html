Abies excelsa var. pumila

Abies excelsa var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf white fir, is a compact evergreen conifer native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its short, dense branches and needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown hue. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate regions.
