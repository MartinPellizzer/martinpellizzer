Allium ubsicola

Allium ubsicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has bulbous roots that are used in traditional medicine.

Allium ubsicola is valued for its aromatic properties and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils.
