Aster simplex var. interior

Aster simplex var. interior, commonly known as the interior aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to purple. It blooms from late summer into early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
