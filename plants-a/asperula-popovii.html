Asperula popovii

Asperula popovii, commonly known as the Himalayan sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Asperula popovii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
