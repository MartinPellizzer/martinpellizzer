Alyssum mutabile

Alyssum mutabile, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has gray-green, fuzzy leaves and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for borders and containers.

Alyssum mutabile is also valued for its hardiness and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
