Artemisia canadensis f. pumila

Artemisia canadensis f. pumila, commonly known as silky wormwood, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features silvery-gray, finely textured leaves that are covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance make it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
