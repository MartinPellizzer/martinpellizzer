Aeschynomene schimperi var. crassicaulis

Aeschynomene schimperi var. crassicaulis, commonly known as thick-stemmed false indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dryland conditions.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
