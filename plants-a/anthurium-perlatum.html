Anthurium perlatum

Anthurium perlatum, commonly known as the pearl anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a silvery or white veining, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright red spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the small, greenish flowers. Anthurium perlatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
