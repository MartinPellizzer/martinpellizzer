Ageratina pentlandiana

Ageratina pentlandiana, commonly known as the Pentland aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, which bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ageratina pentlandiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
