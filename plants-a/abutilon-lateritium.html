Abutilon lateritium

Abutilon lateritium, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a velvety texture.

Abutilon lateritium is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.
