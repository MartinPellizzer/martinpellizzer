Aplocarya

Aplocarya, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its silver or grayish foliage, which is covered in a fine layer of pubescence. The plants typically have simple, alternate leaves and produce small, often clustered flowers with five petals. Aplocarya species are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in dry, rocky habitats.

They are noted for their toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to both animals and humans.
