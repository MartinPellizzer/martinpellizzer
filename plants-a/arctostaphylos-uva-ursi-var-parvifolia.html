Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. parvifolia

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. parvifolia, commonly known as bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically reddish or dark green, and it produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its red or orange berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or acidic environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its aromatic foliage and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
