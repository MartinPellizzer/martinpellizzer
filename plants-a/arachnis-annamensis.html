Arachnis annamensis

Arachnis annamensis, commonly known as the Annam orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Arachnis annamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
