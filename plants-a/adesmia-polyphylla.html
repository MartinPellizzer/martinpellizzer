Adesmia polyphylla

Adesmia polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved legume, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, often thorny growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Adesmia polyphylla is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
