Acacia penomensis

Acacia penomensis, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and phyllodes that are narrow and linear, typically with a whitish or pale green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand periods of drought.

Acacia penomensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife in its native environment.
