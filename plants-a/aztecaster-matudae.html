Aztecaster matudae

Aztecaster matudae, commonly known as the Matudae's aztecaster, is a rare and critically endangered plant species native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a waxy surface that reflects light, and its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in high humidity and cool temperatures, typically found at elevations above 1,500 meters. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is highly susceptible to environmental changes.
