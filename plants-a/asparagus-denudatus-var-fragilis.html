Asparagus denudatus var. fragilis

Asparagus denudatus var. fragilis, commonly known as the slender asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and slender, arching stems. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
