Avena strigosa subsp. wiestii

Avena strigosa subsp.

wiestii, commonly known as the Wiest's oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, disturbed habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting native wildlife.
