Ardisia × montana

Ardisia × montana, commonly known as the hybrid evergreen shrub, is a cross between Ardisia crispa and Ardisia elliptica.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its compact growth habit and year-round foliage. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden landscapes.

The species is also valued for its potential in horticultural breeding programs due to its genetic diversity.
