Amanoa pubescens

Amanoa pubescens, commonly known as the hairy amanoa, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
