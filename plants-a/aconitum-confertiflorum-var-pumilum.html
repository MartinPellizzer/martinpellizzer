Aconitum confertiflorum var. pumilum

Aconitum confertiflorum var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf clustered monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
