Argyroderma subalbum

Argyroderma subalbum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a compact, rounded crown. The leaves are leathery, dark green on top, and silver-white underneath, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
