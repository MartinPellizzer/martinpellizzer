Aldama subtruncata

Aldama subtruncata, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aldama subtruncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
