Acalypha angustifolia

Acalypha angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cudweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, often hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acalypha angustifolia is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
