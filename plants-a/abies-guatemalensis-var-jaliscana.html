Abies guatemalensis var. jaliscana

Abies guatemalensis var. jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically 5–8 centimeters long, containing seeds covered in a thin, papery membrane. It thrives in temperate climates with high humidity and well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.

It grows in high-altitude cloud forests, typically between 1,500 and 3,000 meters above sea level. This variety is adapted to humid, temperate climates with high rainfall and misty conditions. It is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in these forest ecosystems.
