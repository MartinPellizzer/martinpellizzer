Anemia radicans var. organensis

Anemia radicans var. organensis, commonly known as the Organensis trailing plant, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
