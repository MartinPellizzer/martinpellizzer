Asperula oppositifolia subsp. grandiflora

Asperula oppositifolia subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered woodruff, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters.

The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
