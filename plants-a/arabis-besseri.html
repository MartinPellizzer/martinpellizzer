Arabis besseri

Arabis besseri, commonly known as the Bessera rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Arabis besseri is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
