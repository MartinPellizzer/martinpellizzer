Anthemis carpatica var. macedonica

Anthemis carpatica var. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant produces small, daisy-like blooms that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are aromatic and have been historically used for their calming properties.

The variety is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the standard Anthemis carpatica.
