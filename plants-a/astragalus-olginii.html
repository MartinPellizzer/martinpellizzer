Astragalus olginii

Astragalus olginii, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species.

Due to its ecological importance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
