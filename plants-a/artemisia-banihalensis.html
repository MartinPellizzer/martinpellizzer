Artemisia banihalensis

Artemisia banihalensis, commonly known as Banihal wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky terrains. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
