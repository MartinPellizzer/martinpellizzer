Amphistelma selloanum

Amphistelma selloanum, commonly known as the Sello's orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Amphistelma selloanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
