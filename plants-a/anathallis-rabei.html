Anathallis rabei

Anathallis rabei, commonly known as the Rabei's spider orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of southern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its intricate, spider-like inflorescence, which consists of a central stem bearing numerous small, delicate flowers. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that facilitates specific pollination by certain species of flies.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Anathallis rabei is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
