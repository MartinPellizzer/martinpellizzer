Annona montana f. marcgravii

Annona montana f. marcgravii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherimoya, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is typically pear-shaped, with a smooth, thin skin and a creamy, sweet flesh. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a flavor similar to a combination of banana and pineapple.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
