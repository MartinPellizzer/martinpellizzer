Athanasia humilis

Athanasia humilis, commonly known as the "Everlasting" or "Immortality" flower, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets, which give it a delicate appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is known for its resilience in dry conditions.

Athanasia humilis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
