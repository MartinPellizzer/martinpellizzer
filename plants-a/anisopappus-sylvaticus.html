Anisopappus sylvaticus

Anisopappus sylvaticus, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture. Its showy yellow flowers are typically arranged in composite heads, characteristic of the daisy family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.
