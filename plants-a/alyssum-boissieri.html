Alyssum boissieri

Alyssum boissieri, commonly known as the mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, oval, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Alyssum boissieri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
