Astragalus kuhistanus

Astragalus kuhistanus, commonly known as Kuhistan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus kuhistanus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
