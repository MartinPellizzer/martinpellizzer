Argythamnia fendleri

Argythamnia fendleri, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Argythamnia fendleri plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
