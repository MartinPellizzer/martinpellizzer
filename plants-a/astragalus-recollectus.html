Astragalus recollectus

Astragalus recollectus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus recollectus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
