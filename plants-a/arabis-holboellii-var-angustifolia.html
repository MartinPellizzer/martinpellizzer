Arabis holboellii var. angustifolia

Arabis holboellii var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate cold climates and poor soil conditions.
