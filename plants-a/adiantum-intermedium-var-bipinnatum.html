Adiantum intermedium var. bipinnatum

Adiantum intermedium var. bipinnatum, commonly known as the bipinnate maidenhair fern, is a species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China and Japan. The plant features a slender, upright rhizome with arching fronds that are bipinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fronds are typically green with a slight bluish tinge and produce sporangia on the underside of the leaves.
