Aloe barberae

Aloe barberae, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems with fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces striking red, orange, or yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional remedies.
