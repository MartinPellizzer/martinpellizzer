Aegiphila uniflora

Aegiphila uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. It produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Aegiphila uniflora is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in its native habitat.
