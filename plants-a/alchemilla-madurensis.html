Alchemilla madurensis

Alchemilla madurensis, commonly known as the Madurensis lady's mantle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems.

Alchemilla madurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
