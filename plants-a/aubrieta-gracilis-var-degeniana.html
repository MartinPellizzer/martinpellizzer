Aubrieta gracilis var. degeniana

Aubrieta gracilis var. degeniana, commonly known as the silver aubrieta, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its compact, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
