Anguria warmingiana var. acutiloba

Anguria warmingiana var. acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed bitter melon, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
