Artemisia halodendron

Artemisia halodendron, commonly known as desert wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Artemisia halodendron is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in poor, sandy soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and is often used in traditional medicine.
