Agapetes rugosus

Agapetes rugosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved silver fir, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with a distinctive cup-shaped calyx that persists through fruiting.

Agapetes rugosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
