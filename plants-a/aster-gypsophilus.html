Aster gypsophilus

Aster gypsophilus, commonly known as the rock aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Aster gypsophilus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
