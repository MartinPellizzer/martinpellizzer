Agropyron dasystachyum var. subvillosum

Agropyron dasystachyum var. subvillosum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy plant adapted to harsh environments. The plant produces spikelets that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock.
