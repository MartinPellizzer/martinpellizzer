Arenga javanica

Arenga javanica, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a tall, slender palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and smooth, grayish-brown trunk. The plant produces a fibrous material called areca fiber, which is used in various industrial applications. Its sap can be tapped to produce a type of sugar known as palm sugar.

Arenga javanica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical environments.
