Acnida cannabina var. australis

Acnida cannabina var. australis, commonly known as the Australian hemp tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its dioecious nature, with separate male and female plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in catkins, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland and coastal areas.

The species is notable for its historical use by Indigenous Australian communities for various purposes.
