Allium subhirsutum subsp. graecum

Allium subhirsutum subsp.

graecum, commonly known as the Greek garlic or wild leek, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, with a strong garlic scent.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers in spring, which are borne on slender stems. Its bulbs are small and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
