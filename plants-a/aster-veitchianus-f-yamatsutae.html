Aster veitchianus f. yamatsutae

Aster veitchianus f. yamatsutae, commonly known as the Yamatsutae aster, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that add visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
