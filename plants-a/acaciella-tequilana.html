Acaciella tequilana

Acaciella tequilana, commonly known as the tequila acacia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
