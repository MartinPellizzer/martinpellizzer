Agave mapisaga var. lisa

Agave mapisaga var. lisa, commonly known as the Lisa Agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color, with white marginal teeth. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing yellowish-green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
