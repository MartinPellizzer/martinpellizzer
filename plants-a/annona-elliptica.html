Annona elliptica

Annona elliptica, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are typically yellow with a red or purple calyx, and it produces a fragrant, sweet fruit with a creamy, custard-like pulp.

The fruit is edible, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
