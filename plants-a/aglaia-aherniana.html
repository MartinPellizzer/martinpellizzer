Aglaia aherniana

Aglaia aherniana, commonly known as the golden apple or yellow mangosteen, is a member of the Meliaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruit is a round, smooth-skinned berry that contains a single large seed, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and potential therapeutic uses.
