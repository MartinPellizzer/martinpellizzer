Anagallis arvensis var. gentianea

Anagallis arvensis var. gentianea, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and striking blue or purple flowers. The flowers are typically five-petaled and bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and fields.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to self-seed, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
