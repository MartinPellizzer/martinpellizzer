Anthemis cypricola

Anthemis cypricola, commonly known as the Cyprus goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Anthemis cypricola is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
