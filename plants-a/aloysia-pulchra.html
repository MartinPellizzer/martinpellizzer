Aloysia pulchra

Aloysia pulchra, commonly known as the purple verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple to blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and aromatic foliage.
