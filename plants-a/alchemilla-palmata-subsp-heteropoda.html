Alchemilla palmata subsp. heteropoda

Alchemilla palmata subsp.

heteropoda, commonly known as the variable lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in silky white hairs.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
