Amorphophallus glossophyllus

Amorphophallus glossophyllus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a phallic structure, hence its scientific name. The plant produces a unique, odorless flower that attracts specific pollinators, such as beetles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The corm of the plant is edible when properly cooked, though it contains toxic compounds that must be neutralized.
