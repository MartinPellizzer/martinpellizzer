Artemisia waltonii var. yushuensis

Artemisia waltonii var. yushuensis, commonly known as Yushu wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in high-altitude, arid environments and is adapted to cold and harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of alpine flora and traditional medicine.
