Acer platanoides pendulum

Acer platanoides pendulum, commonly known as the weeping maple, is a deciduous tree characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that create a unique, flowing appearance.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is native to the eastern United States. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
