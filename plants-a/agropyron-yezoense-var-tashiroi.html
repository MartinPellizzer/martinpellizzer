Agropyron yezoense var. tashiroi

Agropyron yezoense var. tashiroi, commonly known as Yezo wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
