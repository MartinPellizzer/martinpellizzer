Aconitum velebiticum

Aconitum velebiticum, commonly known as the Velebit monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a distinctive palmate structure and serrated edges.

Aconitum velebiticum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
