Agnus-castus robusta

Agnus-castus robusta, commonly known as the common privet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Agnus-castus robusta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
