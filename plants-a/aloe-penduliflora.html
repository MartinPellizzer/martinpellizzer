Aloe penduliflora

Aloe penduliflora, commonly known as the weeping aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a whitish, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Aloe penduliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
