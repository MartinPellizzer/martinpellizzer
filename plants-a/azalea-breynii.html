Azalea breynii

Azalea breynii, commonly known as the Cape azalea, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant pink or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Azalea breynii prefers well-drained, acidic soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its status as a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.
