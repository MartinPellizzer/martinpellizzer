Arisaema triphyllum subsp. stewardsonii

Arisaema triphyllum subsp.

stewardsonii, commonly known as the Jack-in-the-pulpit, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a spadix enclosed within a hood-like spathe.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, organic soils. Its leaves are typically tripartite, with three leaflets that are often lobed or notched.

The species is known for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
