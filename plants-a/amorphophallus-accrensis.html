Amorphophallus accrensis

Amorphophallus accrensis, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive underground tuber that stores nutrients. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other familiar species like calla lilies and philodendrons. Its tuber can grow quite large, sometimes reaching weights of several kilograms.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
