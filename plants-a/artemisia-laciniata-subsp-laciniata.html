Artemisia laciniata subsp. laciniata

Artemisia laciniata subsp.

laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its finely dissected, silvery-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
