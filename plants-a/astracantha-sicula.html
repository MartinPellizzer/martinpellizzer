Astracantha sicula

Astracantha sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian thorned everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense, spiny bracts that protect its small, yellowish flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Astracantha sicula is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
