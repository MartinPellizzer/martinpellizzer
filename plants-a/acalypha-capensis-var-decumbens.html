Acalypha capensis var. decumbens

Acalypha capensis var. decumbens, commonly known as the Cape spurge or African mallow, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, fuzzy seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
