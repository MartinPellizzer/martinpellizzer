Amomum limbatum

Amomum limbatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Zingiberaceae, which includes ginger and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing essential oils that contribute to its distinctive aroma.

Amomum limbatum is valued both for its culinary uses and traditional medicinal properties.
