Atropis limosa

Atropis limosa, commonly known as the softstemmed lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous habitats.

The species is valued for its beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
