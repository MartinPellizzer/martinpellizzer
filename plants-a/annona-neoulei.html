Annona neoulei

Annona neoulei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is a large, spiny berry that ripens to a yellowish color and has a creamy, sweet flesh. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit, which is similar to the cherimoya.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
