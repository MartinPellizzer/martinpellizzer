Acacia omalophylla

Acacia omalophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and narrow, phyllodes that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the autumn season. Acacia omalophylla is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
