Amaryllis consobrina

Amaryllis consobrina, commonly known as the red-tipped amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Amaryllis consobrina is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
