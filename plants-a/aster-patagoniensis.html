Aster patagoniensis

Aster patagoniensis, commonly known as the Patagonian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits called achenes.

Aster patagoniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
