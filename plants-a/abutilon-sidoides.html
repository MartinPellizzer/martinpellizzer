Abutilon sidoides

Abutilon sidoides, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer.
