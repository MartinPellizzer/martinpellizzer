Acalypha wilmsii

Acalypha wilmsii, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Acalypha wilmsii is relatively easy to grow and can be propagated through stem cuttings or seeds.
