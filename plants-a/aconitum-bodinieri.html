Aconitum bodinieri

Aconitum bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple hood-shaped flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum bodinieri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
