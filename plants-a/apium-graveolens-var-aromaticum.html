Apium graveolens var. aromaticum

Apium graveolens var. aromaticum, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and southwestern Asia.

It features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often used as a garnish or culinary herb. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are characteristic of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are also edible and have a slightly peppery flavor.

Parsley thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and kitchens alike.
