Albizia welwitschii

Albizia welwitschii, commonly known as the African mimosa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that turn red in autumn, and its flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in racemes. Albizia welwitschii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in regions with low rainfall.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
