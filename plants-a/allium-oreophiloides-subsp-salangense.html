Allium oreophiloides subsp. salangense

Allium oreophiloides subsp.

salangense, commonly known as the Salangian leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is characterized by its bulbous base and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
