Astragalus baghensis

Astragalus baghensis, commonly known as the Bagh mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of northern Pakistan, particularly in the Gilgit-Baltistan area. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Astragalus baghensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
