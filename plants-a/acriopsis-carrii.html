Acriopsis carrii

Acriopsis carrii, commonly known as the Carri's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a rosette of basal leaves that are spiny and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Acriopsis carrii is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
