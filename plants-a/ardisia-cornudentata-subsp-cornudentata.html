Ardisia cornudentata subsp. cornudentata

Ardisia cornudentata subsp.

cornudentata, commonly known as the glossy antlerwood, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite or clustered at the base of the stems. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
