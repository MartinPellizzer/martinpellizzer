Actinidia arguta var. purpurea

Actinidia arguta var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple sandbar willow, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and small, heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, followed by edible, sweet berries that are highly prized by wildlife and humans alike. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along streambanks and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
