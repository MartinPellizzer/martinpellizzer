Anoplophytum lineare

Anoplophytum lineare, commonly known as the linear leaf saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
