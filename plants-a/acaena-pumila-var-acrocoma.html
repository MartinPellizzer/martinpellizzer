Acaena pumila var. acrocoma

Acaena pumila var. acrocoma, commonly known as the small burrweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features small, spiny bracts that form dense clusters, which are adapted for seed dispersal by attaching to animals. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
