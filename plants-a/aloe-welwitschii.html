Aloe welwitschii

Aloe welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch aloe, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems and large, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a thick, white powdery coating. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom at the top of the stem, adding to its ornamental appeal. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
