Amaranthus macrocarpus var. pallidus

Amaranthus macrocarpus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, bead-like seeds that are often used as a grain or for decorative purposes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a fibrous root system that aids in its rapid growth and adaptability.
