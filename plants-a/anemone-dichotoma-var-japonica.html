Anemone dichotoma var. japonica

Anemone dichotoma var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and grows in clumps, with fern-like, bipinnate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
