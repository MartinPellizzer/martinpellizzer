Acacia comorensis

Acacia comorensis, commonly known as the Comoros acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various species.

Its wood is sometimes used for construction and fuel, though it is not as commercially valuable as other acacia species.
