Artemisia clausonis

Artemisia clausonis, commonly known as the Kashmiri wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found at high altitudes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.

Artemisia clausonis is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and its distinctive, pungent scent.
