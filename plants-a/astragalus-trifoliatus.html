Astragalus trifoliatus

Astragalus trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found at high altitudes.

Astragalus trifoliatus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various health benefits.
