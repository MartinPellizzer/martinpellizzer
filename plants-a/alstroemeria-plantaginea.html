Alstroemeria plantaginea

Alstroemeria plantaginea, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, brightly colored flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Alstroemeria plantaginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
