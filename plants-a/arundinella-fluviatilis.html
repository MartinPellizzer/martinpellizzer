Arundinella fluviatilis

Arundinella fluviatilis, commonly known as the river bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Arundinella fluviatilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
