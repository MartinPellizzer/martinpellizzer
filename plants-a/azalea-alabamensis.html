Azalea alabamensis

Azalea alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The Alabama azalea is an important component of the region's flora and is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
