Aeschynomene paraguayensis

Aeschynomene paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay horsegram, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used as a forage crop.

Its seeds are also utilized in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
