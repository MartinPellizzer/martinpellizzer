Aster bifidus

Aster bifidus, commonly known as the bifid aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers display a mix of white, purple, and blue hues.

Aster bifidus is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
