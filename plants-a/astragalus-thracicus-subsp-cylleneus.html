Astragalus thracicus subsp. cylleneus

Astragalus thracicus subsp.

cylleneus, commonly known as the Thrace milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions such as Bulgaria and Greece.

The plant has slender stems with small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

It is part of the legume family and has potential ecological and medicinal significance.
