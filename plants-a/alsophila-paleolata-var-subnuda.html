Alsophila paleolata var. subnuda

Alsophila paleolata var. subnuda, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in lowland and hill forest areas. Its rhizome system supports its growth and helps in anchoring the plant in moist, well-drained soils.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
