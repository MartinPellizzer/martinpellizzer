Arum megobrebi

Arum megobrebi, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in length, making it one of the largest herbaceous plants in the Araceae family. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a showy spathe, typical of many arum species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
