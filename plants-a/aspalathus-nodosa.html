Aspalathus nodosa

Aspalathus nodosa, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, rounded fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Aspalathus nodosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
