Ampelocissus dissecta

Ampelocissus dissecta, commonly known as the African ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises and vertical gardens.
