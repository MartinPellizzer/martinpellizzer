Acacia longifolia var. latifolia

Acacia longifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feathery leaflets, giving the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

This variety is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
