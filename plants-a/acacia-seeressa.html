Acacia seeressa

Acacia seeressa, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannah and woodland habitats.

Acacia seeressa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
