Asplenium lunulatum var. swartzii

Asplenium lunulatum var. swartzii, commonly known as the Swartz's spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic fern that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, finely toothed margin. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
