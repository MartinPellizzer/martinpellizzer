Astragalus nuttallianus var. pumilus

Astragalus nuttallianus var. pumilus, commonly known as the small milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
