Allocasuarina acutivalvis subsp. prinsepiana

Allocasuarina acutivalvis subsp.

prinsepiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved she-oak, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, scale-like leaves that are densely packed along the branches.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its hardy nature and is used in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
