Arnica chamissonis var. incana

Arnica chamissonis var. incana, commonly known as the hairy arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. The plant features erect, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense heads, producing a distinctive, somewhat fuzzy appearance.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, though it should be used with caution due to its potential toxicity.
