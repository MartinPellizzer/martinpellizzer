Andropogon eucomus

Andropogon eucomus, commonly known as the golden beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hair-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Andropogon eucomus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
