Achillea tanacetifolia var. dentifera

Achillea tanacetifolia var. dentifera, commonly known as the toothed yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that are often toothed or serrated, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
