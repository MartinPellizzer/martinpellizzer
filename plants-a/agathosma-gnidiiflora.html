Agathosma gnidiiflora

Agathosma gnidiiflora, commonly known as Cape geranium, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic, often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Agathosma gnidiiflora is well adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
