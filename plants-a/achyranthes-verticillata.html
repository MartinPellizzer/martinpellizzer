Achyranthes verticillata

Achyranthes verticillata, commonly known as red root, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by reddish-brown, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Achyranthes verticillata is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
