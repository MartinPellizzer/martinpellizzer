Arundo fulvida

Arundo fulvida, commonly known as the golden reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green with a slight golden hue along the edges. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale yellow to brown flowers during the summer months. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Due to its rapid growth and hardiness, it is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
