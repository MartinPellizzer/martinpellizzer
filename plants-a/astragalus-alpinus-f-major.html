Astragalus alpinus f. major

Astragalus alpinus f. major, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption.

Astragalus alpinus f. major is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
