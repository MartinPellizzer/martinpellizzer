Apomuria bullata

Apomuria bullata, commonly known as the bull's eye fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds with a prominent central eye-like spot. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Apomuria bullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic habitats.
