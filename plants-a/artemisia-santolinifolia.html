Artemisia santolinifolia

Artemisia santolinifolia, commonly known as the santolinifolia wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
