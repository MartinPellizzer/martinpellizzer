Ayenia rubriflora

Ayenia rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered ayenia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, woody stems and clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Ayenia rubriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
