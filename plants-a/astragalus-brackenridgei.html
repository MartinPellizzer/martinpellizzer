Astragalus brackenridgei

Astragalus brackenridgei, commonly known as Brackenridge's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Astragalus brackenridgei is often found in areas with limited soil moisture, making it adapted to arid environments.
