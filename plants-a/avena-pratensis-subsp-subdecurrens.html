Avena pratensis subsp. subdecurrens

Avena pratensis subsp.

subdecurrens, commonly known as the slender oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its forage quality and has been used historically in agriculture and horticulture.
