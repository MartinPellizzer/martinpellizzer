Adelopetalum lageniforme

Adelopetalum lageniforme, commonly known as the bottle-shaped bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. The plant features narrow, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Adelopetalum lageniforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral form.
