Ardisia pulchra

Ardisia pulchra, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy fruit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ardisia pulchra prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, and it is often used in shaded garden settings.
