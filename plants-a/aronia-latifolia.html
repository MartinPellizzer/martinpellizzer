Aronia latifolia

Aronia latifolia, commonly known as the chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Aronia latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits used in culinary and medicinal applications.
