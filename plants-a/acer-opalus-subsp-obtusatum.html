Acer opalus subsp. obtusatum

Acer opalus subsp.

obtusatum, commonly known as the Bosnian maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan region. It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often with a bluish-white underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and autumn coloration make it a valued addition to landscapes.
