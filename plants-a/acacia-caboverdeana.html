Acacia caboverdeana

Acacia caboverdeana, commonly known as the Cape Verde acacia, is a shrub native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny branches and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
