Aralia planchoniana

Aralia planchoniana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian aralia, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruits are follicles that contain seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The plant has ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and unique growth form.
