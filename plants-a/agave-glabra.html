Agave glabra

Agave glabra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are often followed by the development of a new plant from the base. Agave glabra is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive in dry conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts and landscaping.
