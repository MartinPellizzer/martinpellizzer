Andropogon trichozygus

Andropogon trichozygus, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important species in wetland restoration.

The plant is often used in ecological landscaping due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
