Agrostophyllum crassicaule var. bismarckiense

Agrostophyllum crassicaule var. bismarckiense, commonly known as the Bismarck Island grass tree, is a unique member of the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tussock-like growth and tall, narrow leaves that are often blue-green in color. Native to the Bismarck Archipelago in Papua New Guinea, this plant thrives in humid, tropical environments. The inflorescence consists of a single, erect spike that bears small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.
