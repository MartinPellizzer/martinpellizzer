Arundarbor tuldoides

Arundarbor tuldoides, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, fan-like fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
