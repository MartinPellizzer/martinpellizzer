Achillea montana

Achillea montana, commonly known as yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust root system and produces flat, clustered flower heads that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Achillea montana is also a popular garden plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
