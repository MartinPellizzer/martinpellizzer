Acer fallax

Acer fallax, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage, which turns vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall. The tree has opposite, palmate leaves that are typically 5-9 inches long and have a coarsely toothed margin. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in early spring.

Acer fallax is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
