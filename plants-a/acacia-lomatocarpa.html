Acacia lomatocarpa

Acacia lomatocarpa, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, needle-like structures. The plant produces yellow to golden globular flowerheads, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to dry climates, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Acacia lomatocarpa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
