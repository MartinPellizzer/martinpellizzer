Anhemis arvensis var. agrestis

Anhemis arvensis var. agrestis, commonly known as the field anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands. The plant features a basal rosette of broadly ovate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow center. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
