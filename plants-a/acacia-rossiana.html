Acacia rossiana

Acacia rossiana, commonly known as the Christmas tree acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in winter, making it a striking addition to gardens. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It is often used as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and seasonal flowering.

Acacia rossiana also has a history of use in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
