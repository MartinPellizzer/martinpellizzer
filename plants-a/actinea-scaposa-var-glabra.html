Actinea scaposa var. glabra

Actinea scaposa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth actinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring.

The species is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
