Allophylus triphyllus

Allophylus triphyllus, commonly known as the three-leaved holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, trifoliate leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Allophylus triphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its dense, compact growth habit.
