Anemone raddeana var. lacerata

Anemone raddeana var. lacerata, commonly known as the fringed anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with deeply lobed petals that give it a fringed appearance. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and basal leaves that are typically divided into narrow segments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in rock gardens and alpine collections.
