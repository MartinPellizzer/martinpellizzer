Asplenium brachyphyllum

Asplenium brachyphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium brachyphyllum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
