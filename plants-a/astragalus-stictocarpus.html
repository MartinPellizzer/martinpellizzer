Astragalus stictocarpus

Astragalus stictocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces small, hard pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus stictocarpus is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is of interest for its potential ecological and pharmacological properties.
