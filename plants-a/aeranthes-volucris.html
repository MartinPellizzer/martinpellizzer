Aeranthes volucris

Aeranthes volucris, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, and it is often found in association with other epiphytic plants.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique appearance.
