Arundo silvatica

Arundo silvatica, commonly known as the silvery reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and grayish-green, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. This plant thrives in moist, sunny environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense clumps.

Arundo silvatica is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
