Astragalus thomsonianus

Astragalus thomsonianus, commonly known as the Thomson's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus thomsonianus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
