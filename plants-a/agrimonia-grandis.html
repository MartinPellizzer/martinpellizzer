Agrimonia grandis

Agrimonia grandis, commonly known as the great agrimony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. Agrimonia grandis is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
