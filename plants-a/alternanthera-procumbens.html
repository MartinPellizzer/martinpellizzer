Alternanthera procumbens

Alternanthera procumbens, commonly known as red arrow or red velvet, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that features striking red, pink, or purple foliage, which becomes more vibrant in full sun. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in water gardens or as a colorful ground cover.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for aquatic and bog gardens.
