Acalypha commersoniana var. longifolia

Acalypha commersoniana var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved ironwood, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, spiky seed clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and unique seed structures.
