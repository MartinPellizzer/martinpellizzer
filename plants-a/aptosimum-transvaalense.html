Aptosimum transvaalense

Aptosimum transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, showing a degree of drought tolerance. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets.

Aptosimum transvaalense is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
