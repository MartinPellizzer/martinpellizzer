Alternanthera subumbellata

Alternanthera subumbellata, commonly known as the red-stemmed Alternanthera, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is popular in water gardens and as a container plant due to its vibrant color and ease of care.
