Anthyllis maura var. albicans

Anthyllis maura var. albicans, commonly known as the white broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its white flowers and compact growth make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
