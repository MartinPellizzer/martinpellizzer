Allium afghanicum

Allium afghanicum, commonly known as the Afghan leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fleshy bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and bulbs, which have a mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium afghanicum is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
