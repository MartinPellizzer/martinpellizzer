Acacia sutherlandii

Acacia sutherlandii, commonly known as the Sutherland's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched form and its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers in cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia sutherlandii is well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
