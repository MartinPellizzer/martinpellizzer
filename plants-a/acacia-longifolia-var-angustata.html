Acacia longifolia var. angustata

Acacia longifolia var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattles, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

This species is often used in coastal stabilization and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
