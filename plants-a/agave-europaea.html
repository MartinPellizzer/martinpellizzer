Agave europaea

Agave europaea, commonly known as the European agave, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped arrangement of thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stem that bears clusters of small, white flowers. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is drought-tolerant.

Agave europaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
