Alchemilla chirophylla

Alchemilla chirophylla, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in a cup-like structure. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.

Alchemilla chirophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens.
