Acalypha purpusii

Acalypha purpusii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or flameleaf maple, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
