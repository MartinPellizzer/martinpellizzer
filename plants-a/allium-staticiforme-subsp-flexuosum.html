Allium staticiforme subsp. flexuosum

Allium staticiforme subsp.

flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features elongated, narrow leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base.

The plant produces umbels of small, white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a potential culinary plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
