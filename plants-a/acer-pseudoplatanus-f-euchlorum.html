Acer pseudoplatanus f. euchlorum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. euchlorum, commonly known as the white-veined maple, is a unique variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its striking white veins on otherwise green leaves, which create a striking visual contrast. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically found in temperate regions of Europe. Its foliage changes to vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks.
