Anthericum mesembryanthemoides

Anthericum mesembryanthemoides, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
