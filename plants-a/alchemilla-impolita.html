Alchemilla impolita

Alchemilla impolita, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in branched cymes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla impolita is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
