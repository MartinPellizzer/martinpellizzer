Aloe ifanadianae

Aloe ifanadianae, commonly known as the Madagascar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellowish-green flowers, which are attractive to pollinators. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional medicine and skincare products.

Aloe ifanadianae is valued for its resilience and ornamental qualities, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
