Astragalus ghorbandicus

Astragalus ghorbandicus, commonly known as the Ghorbandic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its scientific study contributes to understanding plant adaptations in harsh environments.
