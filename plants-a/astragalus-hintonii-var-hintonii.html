Astragalus hintonii var. hintonii

Astragalus hintonii var. hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has small, yellow to pale orange flowers that bloom in early summer.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
