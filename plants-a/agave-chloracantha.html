Agave chloracantha

Agave chloracantha, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are typically pollinated by bats and insects. Agave chloracantha is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its fibrous leaves have been historically used for making ropes and baskets by indigenous peoples.
