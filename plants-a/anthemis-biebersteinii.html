Anthemis biebersteinii

Anthemis biebersteinii, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
