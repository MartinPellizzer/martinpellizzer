Aloe brunnthaleri

Aloe brunnthaleri, commonly known as the Namibia aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellowish-green flowers, which are usually pollinated by insects. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Aloe brunnthaleri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
