Asterocephalus graminifolius

Asterocephalus graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leaf star-headed plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It features a central, star-shaped head surrounded by a dense cluster of small, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
