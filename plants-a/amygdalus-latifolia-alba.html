Amygdalus latifolia alba

Amygdalus latifolia alba, commonly known as the white almond tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant white flowers in spring. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
