Anthoxanthum odoratum var. altissimum

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. altissimum, commonly known as sweet vernal grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bright yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble tufts of hair. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often noted for its pleasant, sweet scent, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
