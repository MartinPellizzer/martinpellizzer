Acanthocalycium catamarcense

Acanthocalycium catamarcense, commonly known as the Catamarca cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless stems and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh desert conditions. It has a compact growth habit with ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in low-rainfall areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts.
