Aquilegia vulgaris var. aggericola

Aquilegia vulgaris var. aggericola, commonly known as the red and white columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers, which are typically a mix of red and white hues. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wildflower displays.

The variety is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
