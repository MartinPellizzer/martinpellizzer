Acrolepis ramosissima

Acrolepis ramosissima, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, spiny fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
