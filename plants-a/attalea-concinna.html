Attalea concinna

Attalea concinna, commonly known as the palm heart or the "tucum" palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Bolivia, and Paraguay. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fruit is a large, fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of palm heart, a delicacy in some cuisines.
