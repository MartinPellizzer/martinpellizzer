Astragalus buchtormensis var. gymnocarpus

Astragalus buchtormensis var. gymnocarpus, commonly known as the smooth-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its seeds are typically smooth and enclosed in pods that mature in late summer.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
