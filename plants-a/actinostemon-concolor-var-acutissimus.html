Actinostemon concolor var. acutissimus

Actinostemon concolor var. acutissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed actinostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often characterized by a glossy surface and a distinct, narrow leaf shape.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
