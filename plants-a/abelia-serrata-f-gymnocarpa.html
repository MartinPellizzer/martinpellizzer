Abelia serrata f. gymnocarpa

Abelia serrata f. gymnocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-seeded abelia, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, upright growth habit.

It features ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in late summer, which are borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a capsule that remains smooth and glabrous, distinguishing it from other forms of Abelia serrata.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
