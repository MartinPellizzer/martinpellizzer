Asclepias tuberosa var. angustifolia

Asclepias tuberosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of bright orange, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have distinctive, hood-like structures and are pollinated by various insects, including bees and butterflies.

This variety is a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and is valued for its ecological and ornamental attributes.
