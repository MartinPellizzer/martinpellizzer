Acer barbatum f. schneckii

Acer barbatum f. schneckii, commonly known as the Japanese maple with twisted bark, is a unique variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its distinctive, contorted trunk and branches.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value, particularly its vibrant foliage that ranges from emerald green to fiery red in autumn. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6 to 8, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil. Its slow growth rate makes it ideal for small gardens or as a specimen tree.

The twisted bark adds a dramatic, almost sculptural appearance, making it a popular choice in horticultural collections.
