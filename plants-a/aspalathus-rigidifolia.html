Aspalathus rigidifolia

Aspalathus rigidifolia, commonly known as the African red bush, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Aspalathus rigidifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
