Asperula conferta var. scoparioides

Asperula conferta var. scoparioides, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, leathery foliage that ranges from dark green to reddish-brown, depending on the variety. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
