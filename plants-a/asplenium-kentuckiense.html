Asplenium kentuckiense

Asplenium kentuckiense, commonly known as the Kentucky spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows on shaded, moist rock surfaces or in rocky crevices, thriving in humid environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters long. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged along the margins of the fronds.

Asplenium kentuckiense is a member of the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique leaf morphology.
