Abutilon fruticosum

Abutilon fruticosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Abutilon fruticosum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
