Allium lipskyanum

Allium lipskyanum, commonly known as the Lipsky onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces a bulbous structure that serves as its primary storage organ, typical of many allium species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
