Almyra illyrica

Almyra illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian almyra, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, also known as the mint family, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Almyra illyrica is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
