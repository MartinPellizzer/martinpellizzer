Avena sativa var. flavescens

Avena sativa var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow oat, is a variety of oat characterized by its distinctive golden-colored grains.

This plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils with moderate moisture. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is cultivated for its nutritional value and use in animal feed. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stalks and feathery leaves.

Its flowering heads produce small, spike-like clusters of florets that mature into the characteristic oat grains.
