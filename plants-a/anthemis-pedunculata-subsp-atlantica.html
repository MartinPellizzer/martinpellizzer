Anthemis pedunculata subsp. atlantica

Anthemis pedunculata subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic anthemis, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Iberian Peninsula. It features slender, branched stems that often grow along the ground, with small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in compact clusters.

The plant has finely divided, linear leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and maritime climates.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
