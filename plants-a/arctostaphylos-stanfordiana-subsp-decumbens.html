Arctostaphylos stanfordiana subsp. decumbens

Arctostaphylos stanfordiana subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the Stanford manzanita, is a shrub native to California. It is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and leathery, evergreen leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in white or pink during the spring. These flowers are followed by edible, red berries that provide food for wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
