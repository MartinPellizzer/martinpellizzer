Acleisanthes acutifolia

Acleisanthes acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
