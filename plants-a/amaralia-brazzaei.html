Amaralia brazzaei

Amaralia brazzaei, commonly known as the Brazza's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and pointed, often emerging from a bulbous stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Amaralia brazzaei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
