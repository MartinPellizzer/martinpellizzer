Anemone × devinensis

Anemone × devinensis, commonly known as the hybrid anemone, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is a cross between *Anemone nemorosa* and *Anemone coronaria*, resulting in a plant with distinctive, showy flowers. The botanical features include palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, and flowers that range from white to pink, often with a purple or red center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
