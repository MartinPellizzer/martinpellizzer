Agropyron fuegianum var. chaetophorum

Agropyron fuegianum var. chaetophorum, commonly known as the Fuegian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern Andes and Patagonia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate to cold climates and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats.

Its fibrous roots contribute to soil stabilization in its native environment.
