Argentina leuconota var. brachyphyllaria

Argentina leuconota var. brachyphyllaria, commonly known as the dwarf leuconota, is a unique variant of the leuconota plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are often white or pale yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and cultivation.
