Argyranthemum frutescens var. parviflorum

Argyranthemum frutescens var. parviflorum, commonly known as the Mexican aster, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
