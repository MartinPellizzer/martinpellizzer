Alternanthera peploides

Alternanthera peploides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet plant, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name, with leaves that are often a deep red or purple in color. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking foliage rather than its flowers.

Alternanthera peploides is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a popular and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
