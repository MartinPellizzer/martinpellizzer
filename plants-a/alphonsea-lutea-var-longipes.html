Alphonsea lutea var. longipes

Alphonsea lutea var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed yellow alphonse, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
