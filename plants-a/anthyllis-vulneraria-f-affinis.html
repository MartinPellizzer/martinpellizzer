Anthyllis vulneraria f. affinis

Anthyllis vulneraria f. affinis, commonly known as the lesser burnet, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with wiry stems and small, fern-like leaves. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
