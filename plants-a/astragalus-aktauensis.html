Astragalus aktauensis

Astragalus aktauensis, commonly known as the Aktau milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many members of the genus Astragalus. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for its potential medicinal properties.
