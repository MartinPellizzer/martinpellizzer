Acantholimon raddeanum

Acantholimon raddeanum, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

The plant features wiry, branched stems covered in fine, glandular hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pink. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.
