Aquilegia atrata var. dichroantha

Aquilegia atrata var. dichroantha, commonly known as the black crowfoot, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of deep purple to black. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It is also noted for its adaptability to poor soils and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
