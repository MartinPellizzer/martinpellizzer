Arundinaria orthotropoides

Arundinaria orthotropoides, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered in a silvery-white bloom, giving it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and forms dense clumps through rhizomes. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Arundinaria orthotropoides is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
