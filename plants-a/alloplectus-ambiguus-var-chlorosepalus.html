Alloplectus ambiguus var. chlorosepalus

Alloplectus ambiguus var. chlorosepalus, commonly known as the green-sepal hollyhock, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, rose-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
