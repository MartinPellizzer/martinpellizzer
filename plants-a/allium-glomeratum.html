Allium glomeratum

Allium glomeratum, commonly known as grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round bulbs that are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Allium glomeratum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
