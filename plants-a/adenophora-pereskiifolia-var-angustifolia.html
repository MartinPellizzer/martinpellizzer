Adenophora pereskiifolia var. angustifolia

Adenophora pereskiifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in summer. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces a cylindrical fruit containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
