Adenodolichos euryphyllus

Adenodolichos euryphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically broad and trifoliate, and its erect growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
