Adiantum platyphyllum var. kaulfussii

Adiantum platyphyllum var. kaulfussii, commonly known as the Kaulfuss's maidenhair fern, is a variety of the maidenhair fern family (Adiantaceae).

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace, with a distinctive black, glossy rachis. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its fronds are typically biennial, with new growth emerging from the base each year.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden areas or as a houseplant.
