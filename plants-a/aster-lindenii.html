Aster lindenii

Aster lindenii, commonly known as the Lindley's aster, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its showy blue to violet flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster lindenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
