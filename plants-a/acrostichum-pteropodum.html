Acrostichum pteropodum

Acrostichum pteropodum, commonly known as the giant fern or giant chain fern, is a large, tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, compound fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that helps it anchor and spread in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae that give it a dense, feathery appearance.

Acrostichum pteropodum thrives in humid climates and is often found in forest understories where it plays a role in nutrient cycling.
