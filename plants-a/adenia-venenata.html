Adenia venenata

Adenia venenata, commonly known as the poison apple or black beauty, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red or yellow berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its bark and leaves contain various alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Adenia venenata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
