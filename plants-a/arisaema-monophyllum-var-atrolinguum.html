Arisaema monophyllum var. atrolinguum

Arisaema monophyllum var. atrolinguum, commonly known as the black-tongued arisaema, is a unique member of the Araceae family characterized by its striking, asymmetrical spathe.

The plant features a single, elongated, leaf-like structure called a spathe, which encloses a spadix containing numerous tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It is native to the mountainous regions of China, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and the plant produces a distinctive, elongated fruit that splits open to release its seeds.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable and often protected species in its native range.
