Atriplex acutibractea subsp. whyallensis

Atriplex acutibractea subsp.

whyallensis, commonly known as Whyall's saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing degraded landscapes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
