Abutilon otocarpum

Abutilon otocarpum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
