Acacia exuvialis

Acacia exuvialis, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and compound leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted to disperse in dry conditions.

Acacia exuvialis is well-suited to harsh environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
