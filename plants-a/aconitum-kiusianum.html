Aconitum kiusianum

Aconitum kiusianum, commonly known as the Korean aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple spathaceous flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum kiusianum contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
