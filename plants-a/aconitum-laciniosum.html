Aconitum laciniosum

Aconitum laciniosum, commonly known as the fringed monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive, fringed appearance along the edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum laciniosum is noted for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
