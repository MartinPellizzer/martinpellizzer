Anguria gloriosa

Anguria gloriosa, commonly known as the glory cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons. The plant produces striking, showy yellow flowers that are often more ornamental than edible. Its fruits are typically small, elongated, and have a mild, sweet flavor.

The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, contributing to its lush, tropical appearance.
