Abies hookeriana

Abies hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's fir, is a species of fir native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree that typically grows in high-altitude forests, often in humid and cool climates. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged spirally along the branches. Its cones are usually pendulous and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
