Agallis

Agallis, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Agallis thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is often found in temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.
