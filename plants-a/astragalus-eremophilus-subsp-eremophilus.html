Astragalus eremophilus subsp. eremophilus

Astragalus eremophilus subsp.

eremophilus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
