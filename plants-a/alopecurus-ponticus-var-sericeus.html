Alopecurus ponticus var. sericeus

Alopecurus ponticus var. sericeus, commonly known as the silky mouse-eared grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, silky appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
