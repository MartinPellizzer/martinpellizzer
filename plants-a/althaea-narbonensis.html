Althaea narbonensis

Althaea narbonensis, commonly known as the Narbonne marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Althaea narbonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
