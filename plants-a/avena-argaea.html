Avena argaea

Avena argaea, commonly known as the Armenian oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, white to greenish flowers. Avena argaea is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
