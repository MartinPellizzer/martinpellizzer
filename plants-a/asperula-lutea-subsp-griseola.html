Asperula lutea subsp. griseola

Asperula lutea subsp.

griseola, commonly known as the grayleaf woodruff, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its grayish-green foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and historical use in herbal medicine.
