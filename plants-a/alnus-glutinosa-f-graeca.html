Alnus glutinosa f. graeca

Alnus glutinosa f. graeca, commonly known as the Greek alder, is a subspecies of the black alder native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by cone-like fruits that release winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
