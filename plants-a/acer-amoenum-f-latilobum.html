Acer amoenum f. latilobum

Acer amoenum f. latilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed maple, is a variant of the Amur maple, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features palmately lobed leaves with broad, rounded lobes that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The broad-lobed maple is relatively low maintenance and can adapt to a variety of soil types.
