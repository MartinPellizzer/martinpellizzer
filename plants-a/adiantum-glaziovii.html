Adiantum glaziovii

Adiantum glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, silvery underside that reflects light.

Adiantum glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
