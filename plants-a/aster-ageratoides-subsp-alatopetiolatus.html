Aster ageratoides subsp. alatopetiolatus

Aster ageratoides subsp.

alatopetiolatus, commonly known as the winged petiole aster, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branched stems and hairy leaves. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with prominent central disk florets surrounded by shorter ray florets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
