Acacia sphacelata

Acacia sphacelata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia sphacelata is also valued for its hardy nature and is used in reforestation and erosion control efforts.
