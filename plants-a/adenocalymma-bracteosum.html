Adenocalymma bracteosum

Adenocalymma bracteosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has hairy, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is typically found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Adenocalymma bracteosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
