Abronia Umbellata Subsp. Variabilis

Abronia umbellata subsp. variabilis is a rare flowering plant native to the coastal dunes of southern California. It is known for its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. This subspecies thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its habitat. Due to habitat loss and climate change, it faces increasing threats to its survival. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique and endangered plant.
