Aspalathus acuminata var. inermis

Aspalathus acuminata var. inermis, commonly known as the small-leaved rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow-green in color. The plant is known for its unique ability to produce a caffeine-free tea, which is highly valued in the beverage industry. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, dry climate.

The variety is distinguished by its reduced leaf size compared to the typical rooibos species.
