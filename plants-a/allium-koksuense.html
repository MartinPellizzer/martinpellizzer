Allium koksuense

Allium koksuense, commonly known as the Koksu garlic, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of onions, garlic, and leeks. The plant produces bulbous stems that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are grass-like and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

The flowers are small and typically clustered in umbels, with purple or pink petals.
