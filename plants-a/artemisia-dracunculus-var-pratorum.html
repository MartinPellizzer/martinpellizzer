Artemisia dracunculus var. pratorum

Artemisia dracunculus var. pratorum, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features gray-green, aromatic foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, though its strong aroma can be overpowering.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Artemisia genus, sharing similar ecological and botanical characteristics.
