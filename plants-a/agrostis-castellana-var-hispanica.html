Agrostis castellana var. hispanica

Agrostis castellana var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as coastal dunes, sandy soils, and rocky areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to dry and saline conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean ecosystems.
