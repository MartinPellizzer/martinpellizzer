Alchemilla vulgaris var. maxima

Alchemilla vulgaris var. maxima, commonly known as the velvetleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
