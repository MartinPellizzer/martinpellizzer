Argythamnia pilosissima

Argythamnia pilosissima, commonly known as the silky tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Argythamnia pilosissima is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is often found in desert and semi-desert habitats.
