Anthemis abrotanifolia

Anthemis abrotanifolia, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
