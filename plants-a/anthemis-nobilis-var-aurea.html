Anthemis nobilis var. aurea

Anthemis nobilis var. aurea, commonly known as the golden chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and bright yellow flowers with a white center. The plant is typically perennial, growing up to 30 cm in height, and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is often used in herbal medicine for its calming properties and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers contains compounds like bisabolol and chamazulene, which contribute to its therapeutic uses.
