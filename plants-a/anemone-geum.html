Anemone geum

Anemone geum, commonly known as the "false shamrock," is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its palmately divided leaves and showy, usually pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anemone geum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize over time.
