Acrocephalus rosulatus

Acrocephalus rosulatus, commonly known as the rosette grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a compact cluster near the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or pale yellow, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique growth form and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecological researchers.
