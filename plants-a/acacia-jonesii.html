Acacia jonesii

Acacia jonesii, commonly known as Jones' acacia, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from underground sources. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, which open to release seeds when conditions are favorable.

Acacia jonesii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife in its ecosystem.
