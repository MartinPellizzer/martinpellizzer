Acacia richardsii

Acacia richardsii, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, compound leaves that reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by long, flat pods containing edible seeds. Acacia richardsii is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also contributes to soil fertility in nutrient-poor environments.
