Astragalus laxmannii var. tananaicus

Astragalus laxmannii var. tananaicus, commonly known as the Tanana milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as tundra and alpine meadows. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
