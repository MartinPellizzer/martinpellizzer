Athyrium atroviride

Athyrium atroviride, commonly known as the black-veined shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its elegant, bipinnate fronds with dark green veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are evergreen, with a leathery texture and a distinctive dark green coloration.

Athyrium atroviride is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
