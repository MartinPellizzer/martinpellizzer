Aristolochia yalaensis

Aristolochia yalaensis, commonly known as Yala's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often green or brown in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
