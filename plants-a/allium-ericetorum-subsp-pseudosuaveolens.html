Allium ericetorum subsp. pseudosuaveolens

Allium ericetorum subsp.

pseudosuaveolens, commonly known as the purple garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers in the spring, which are borne on slender, erect stems. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a useful plant in both culinary and ornamental contexts.

It is closely related to other members of the Allium genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
