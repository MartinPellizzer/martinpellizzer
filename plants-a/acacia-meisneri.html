Acacia meisneri

Acacia meisneri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia meisneri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
