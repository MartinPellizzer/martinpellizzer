Arthraxon hispidus f. brevisetus

Arthraxon hispidus f. brevisetus, commonly known as the short-haired arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tightly arranged.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscape design for its unique foliage structure.
