Anathallis ariasii

Anathallis ariasii, commonly known as the Arias' spider orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance, with slender, curling inflorescences. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil for nutrients. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of spiders.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Anathallis ariasii is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
