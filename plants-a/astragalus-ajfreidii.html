Astragalus ajfreidii

Astragalus ajfreidii, commonly known as the Kashmir bluebottle, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of Pakistan, India, and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus ajfreidii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
