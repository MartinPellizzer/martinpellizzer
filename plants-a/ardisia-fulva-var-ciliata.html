Ardisia fulva var. ciliata

Ardisia fulva var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy evergreen ardisia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered with fine, hairy glands. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded garden settings.
