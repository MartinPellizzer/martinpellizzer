Akebia × pentaphylla var. diplochlamys

Akebia × pentaphylla var. diplochlamys, commonly known as the five-leaf akebia, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically consisting of five lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a unique, elongated capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal interest.
