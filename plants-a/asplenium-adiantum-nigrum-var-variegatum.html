Asplenium adiantum-nigrum var. variegatum

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, dark green leaves with striking variegation of white or silver markings. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper care.
