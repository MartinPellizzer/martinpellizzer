Arabis microsperma

Arabis microsperma, commonly known as the lesser rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. This species typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has alternate, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
