Acacia obliquifolia

Acacia obliquifolia, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by distinctive pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia obliquifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
