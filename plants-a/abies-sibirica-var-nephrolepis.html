Abies sibirica var. nephrolepis

Abies sibirica var. nephrolepis, commonly known as the Siberian fir, is a variant of the Siberian fir species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its conical shape and dense foliage. The leaves are needle-like, typically dark green on top and whitish beneath, providing a striking visual contrast. It produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.
