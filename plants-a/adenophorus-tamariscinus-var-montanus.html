Adenophorus tamariscinus var. montanus

Adenophorus tamariscinus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain tamarisk, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
