Amauropelta subscandens

Amauropelta subscandens, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and ability to climb using its fronds. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often divided into small, linear segments, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Amauropelta subscandens plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
