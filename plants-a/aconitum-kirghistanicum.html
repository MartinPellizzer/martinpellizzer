Aconitum kirghistanicum

Aconitum kirghistanicum, commonly known as the Kirghiz monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and its stems are often covered in a dense layer of hairs.

Aconitum kirghistanicum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
