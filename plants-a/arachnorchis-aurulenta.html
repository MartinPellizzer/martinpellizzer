Arachnorchis aurulenta

Arachnorchis aurulenta, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with intricate, spider-like patterns on the petals. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil for nutrients. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, usually between July and October.

The species is noted for its delicate, fragrant blooms and is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
