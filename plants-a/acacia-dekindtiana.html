Acacia dekindtiana

Acacia dekindtiana, commonly known as the De Kindt's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical heads, which are typically arranged in clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and contain hard, angular seeds.

Acacia dekindtiana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
