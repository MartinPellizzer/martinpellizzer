Alyxia diellipticocarpa

Alyxia diellipticocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leafed alyxia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Alyxia diellipticocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
