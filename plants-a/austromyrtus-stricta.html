Austromyrtus stricta

Austromyrtus stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Austromyrtus stricta is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.
