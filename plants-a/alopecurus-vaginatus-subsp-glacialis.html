Alopecurus vaginatus subsp. glacialis

Alopecurus vaginatus subsp.

glacialis, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a perennial grass found in high-altitude and alpine regions. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is an important component of alpine vegetation, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
