Acalypha gracilens var. delzii

Acalypha gracilens var. delzii, commonly known as silverleaf acalypha, is a strikingly ornamental shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
