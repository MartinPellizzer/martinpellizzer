Atheropogon chondrosioides

Atheropogon chondrosioides, commonly known as the sand couch grass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth system allows it to spread vegetatively and stabilize sandy soils.

Atheropogon chondrosioides is often found in disturbed or degraded habitats, playing a role in ecological succession and soil conservation.
