Adyseton sibiricum

Adyseton sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern regions of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of around 10-20 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are slender and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Adyseton sibiricum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
