Amygdalus petunnikowii

Amygdalus petunnikowii, commonly known as the Tien Shan peach, is a rare and ancient species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to peaches. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.
