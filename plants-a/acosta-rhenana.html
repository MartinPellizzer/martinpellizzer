Acosta rhenana

Acosta rhenana, commonly known as the Rhenan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Acosta rhenana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
