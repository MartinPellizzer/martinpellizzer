Aralia elata f. aureovariegata

Aralia elata f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated aralia, is a varietal form of the Japanese aralia.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, characterized by its compound, pinnately arranged leaves that display striking yellow and green variegation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is valued for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscape design and indoor cultivation.
