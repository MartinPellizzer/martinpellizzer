Astragalus mirabilis subsp. chodshamastonicus

Astragalus mirabilis subsp.

chodshamastonicus, commonly known as the Chodshamastonic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological and botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
