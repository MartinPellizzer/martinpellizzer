Amaranthus lividus f. pseudogracilis

Amaranthus lividus f. pseudogracilis, commonly known as the silverleaf amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is due to dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to reddish bracts, and its flowers are typically inconspicuous. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

This cultivar is valued for its unique appearance and ability to attract beneficial insects.
