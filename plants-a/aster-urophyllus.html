Aster urophyllus

Aster urophyllus, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of up to 2 feet. Its showy white to pale purple flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
