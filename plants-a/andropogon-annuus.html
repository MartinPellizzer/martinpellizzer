Andropogon annuus

Andropogon annuus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. Switchgrass is a hardy plant that thrives in a variety of soil conditions and is drought-tolerant. It is widely used in biofuel production and ecological restoration projects due to its high biomass yield and environmental benefits.

The plant also plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
