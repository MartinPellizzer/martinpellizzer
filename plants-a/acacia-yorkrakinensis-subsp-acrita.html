Acacia yorkrakinensis subsp. acrita

Acacia yorkrakinensis subsp.

acrita, commonly known as the Yorkrakin acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
