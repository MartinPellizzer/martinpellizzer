Acacia ulicifolia var. brownii

Acacia ulicifolia var. brownii, commonly known as the Brown's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats. Its foliage is aromatic and has been used traditionally by Indigenous communities.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
