Antennaria foliacea

Antennaria foliacea, commonly known as the leafy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, arranged in clusters that bloom in early summer.

Antennaria foliacea thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
