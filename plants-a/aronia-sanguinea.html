Aronia sanguinea

Aronia sanguinea, commonly known as red chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall.

Aronia sanguinea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and permaculture systems.
