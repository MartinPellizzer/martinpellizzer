Acacia brachycarpa

Acacia brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its seed pods are short and curved, containing several seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk.

Acacia brachycarpa is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in sandy soils.
