Agrostophyllum glumaceum

Agrostophyllum glumaceum, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Agrostophyllum glumaceum plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
