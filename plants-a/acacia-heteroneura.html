Acacia heteroneura

Acacia heteroneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acacia heteroneura is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.
