Acrocephalus gracilis

Acrocephalus gracilis, commonly known as the slender marsh grass.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of rivers.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
