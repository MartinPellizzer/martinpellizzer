Anthyllis vulneraria var. colorata

Anthyllis vulneraria var. colorata, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or alpine regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
