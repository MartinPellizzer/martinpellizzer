Acalypha guineensis

Acalypha guineensis, commonly known as the African nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, red or orange berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
