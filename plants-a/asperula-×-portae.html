Asperula × portae

Asperula × portae, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers acidic, well-drained soil. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
