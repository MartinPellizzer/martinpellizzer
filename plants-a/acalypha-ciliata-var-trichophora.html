Acalypha ciliata var. trichophora

Acalypha ciliata var. trichophora, commonly known as the silky or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and striking, showy inflorescences that often display vibrant colors. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with ciliate margins that give the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
