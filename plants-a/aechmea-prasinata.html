Aechmea prasinata

Aechmea prasinata, commonly known as the green aechmea, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure, which forms a central cup to collect water and nutrients. The leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy scales. It produces vibrant, spathaceous flowers that are often pink or red, emerging from the center of the rosette.

This plant is well adapted to humid environments and thrives in bright, indirect light.

Morphology

Aechmea prasinata has broad, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant lacks a true stem, and its base is often covered in a dense layer of moss-like material that resembles bark. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with a greenish-white color and a blooming period during the rainy season; they are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in the soil, with some aerial roots that help in absorbing moisture from the air.
