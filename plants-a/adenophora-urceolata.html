Adenophora urceolata

Adenophora urceolata, commonly known as the bellflower or bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Adenophora urceolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
