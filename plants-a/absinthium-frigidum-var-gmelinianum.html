Absinthium frigidum var. gmelinianum

Absinthium frigidum var. gmelinianum, commonly known as the Arctic wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found growing in tundra and alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh, polar ecosystems.
