Anthyllis vulneraria var. gracilis

Anthyllis vulneraria var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender woundwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are often arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in dry, sandy soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes.

This species is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
