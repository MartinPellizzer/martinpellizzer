Anagallis alternifolia var. densifolia

Anagallis alternifolia var. densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, well-drained habitats. It has a prostrate or spreading growth habit, with leaves arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
