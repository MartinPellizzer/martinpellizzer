Andropogon confinis var. nudiglumis

Andropogon confinis var. nudiglumis, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a variant of the bluestem grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a warm-season grass characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale blue to greenish flowers.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
