Avena sterilis subsp. macrocarpa

Avena sterilis subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, awnless spikelets that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, feathery inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Avena sterilis subsp.

macrocarpa is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
