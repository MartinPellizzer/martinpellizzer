Achyranthes aspera f. latifolia

Achyranthes aspera f. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved Chinese knotweed, is a variant of the Achyranthes aspera species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including India, and thrives in moist, fertile soils. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both traditional medicine and ecological studies.
