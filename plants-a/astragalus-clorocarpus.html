Astragalus clorocarpus

Astragalus clorocarpus, commonly known as yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus clorocarpus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
