Agave atrovirens var. atrovirens

Agave atrovirens var. atrovirens, commonly known as the black-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark green, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
