Annona caput-medusae

Annona caput-medusae, commonly known as the "Medusa head" plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking yellow flowers that resemble a crown. The plant produces a large, green, spiny fruit that turns red when ripe. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a unique floral structure with numerous stamens.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
