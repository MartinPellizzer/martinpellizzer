Allium neapolitanum var. breviradiatum

Allium neapolitanum var. breviradiatum, commonly known as the Naples leek or short-rayed chive, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to both gardens and botanical collections.
