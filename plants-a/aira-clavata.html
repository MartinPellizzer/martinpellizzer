Aira clavata

Aira clavata, commonly known as false wheat or bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Aira clavata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
