Achras arnhemica

Achras arnhemica, commonly known as the Arnheim's aloe, is a succulent plant native to the Northern Territory of Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological landscaping.
