Andropogon lasiocoleus

Andropogon lasiocoleus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-haired foliage and tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a valuable species for erosion control.

Andropogon lasiocoleus thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.
