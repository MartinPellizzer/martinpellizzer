Actinocephalus divaricatus

Actinocephalus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable corymbia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, often woody growth habit. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in corymb-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly rough texture.

Actinocephalus divaricatus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
