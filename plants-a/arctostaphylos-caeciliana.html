Arctostaphylos caeciliana

Arctostaphylos caeciliana, commonly known as the Sierra manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
