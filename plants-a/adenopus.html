Adenopus

Adenopus, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to various regions of Africa and is characterized by its milky sap, which is often used in traditional medicine. The plants typically have woody stems and produce small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters. Adenopus species are often found in dry, open habitats and can thrive in arid conditions.

Their unique morphology and medicinal properties make them of interest in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
