Amygdalus reuteri

Amygdalus reuteri, commonly known as the desert peach, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to peaches but are typically less sweet and more tart. Amygdalus reuteri is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical research.
