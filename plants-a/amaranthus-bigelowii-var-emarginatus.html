Amaranthus bigelowii var. emarginatus

Amaranthus bigelowii var. emarginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-filled capsules. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
