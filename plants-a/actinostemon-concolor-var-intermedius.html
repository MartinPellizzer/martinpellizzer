Actinostemon concolor var. intermedius

Actinostemon concolor var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate actinostemon, is a lesser-known variant of the Actinostemon genus.

It is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, often used in ornamental horticulture. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types, though it prefers well-drained, moist environments.
