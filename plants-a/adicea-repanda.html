Adicea repanda

Adicea repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Adicea repanda thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.
