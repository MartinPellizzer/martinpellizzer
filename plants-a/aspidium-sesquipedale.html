Aspidium sesquipedale

Aspidium sesquipedale, commonly known as the broad fern, is a species of fern native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, bipinnate fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in sori, which are clustered in lines along the veins.

Aspidium sesquipedale is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
