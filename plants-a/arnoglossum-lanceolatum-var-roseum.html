Arnoglossum lanceolatum var. roseum

Arnoglossum lanceolatum var. roseum, commonly known as the rose-colored prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to rose-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting native pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
