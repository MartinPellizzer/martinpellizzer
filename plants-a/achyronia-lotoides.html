Achyronia lotoides

Achyronia lotoides, commonly known as the lotus bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its unique appearance and nutritional value, it is of interest in both botanical research and sustainable agriculture.
