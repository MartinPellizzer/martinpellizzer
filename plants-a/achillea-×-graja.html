Achillea × graja

Achillea × graja, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pinkish in color. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Achillea × graja is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
