Allium chamaemoly var. littoralis

Allium chamaemoly var. littoralis, commonly known as sea garlic, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to salty, windy environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful culinary herb.

It is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
