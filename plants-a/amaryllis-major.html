Amaryllis major

Amaryllis major, commonly known as the giant amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, white, or pink. The plant has strap-like, sword-shaped leaves that are broad and pointed, growing from a fleshy, underground bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Amaryllis major is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown both indoors and outdoors in suitable climates.
