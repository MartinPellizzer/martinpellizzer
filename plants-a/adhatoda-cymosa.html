Adhatoda cymosa

Adhatoda cymosa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy texture.

Adhatoda cymosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
