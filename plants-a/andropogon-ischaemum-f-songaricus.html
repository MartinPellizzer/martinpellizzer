Andropogon ischaemum f. songaricus

Andropogon ischaemum f. songaricus, commonly known as the Songaricus bluestem, is a variant of bluestem grass native to parts of Asia, including China and Siberia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spike-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
