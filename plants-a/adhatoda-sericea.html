Adhatoda sericea

Adhatoda sericea, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Adhatoda sericea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
