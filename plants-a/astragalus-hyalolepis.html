Astragalus hyalolepis

Astragalus hyalolepis, commonly known as the translucent milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils.

Astragalus hyalolepis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
