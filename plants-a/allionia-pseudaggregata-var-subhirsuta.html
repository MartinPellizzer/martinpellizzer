Allionia pseudaggregata var. subhirsuta

Allionia pseudaggregata var. subhirsuta, commonly known as the hairy allionia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to parts of southern Africa. It has hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "subhirsuta.".

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
