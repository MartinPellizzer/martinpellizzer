Alnus glutinosa lusus obtusifolia

Alnus glutinosa lusus obtusifolia, commonly known as the smooth alder, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that appear in catkins, followed by small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, woody fruits called samaras, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it an important pioneer species in forest succession.
