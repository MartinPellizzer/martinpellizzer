Acrostichum cinereum

Acrostichum cinereum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a perennial fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures include sori, which are clusters of spores found on the underside of the fronds.

Acrostichum cinereum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
