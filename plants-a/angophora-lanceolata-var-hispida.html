Angophora lanceolata var. hispida

Angophora lanceolata var. hispida, commonly known as the rough-barked apple or hispid angophora, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly found in the coastal regions of New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
