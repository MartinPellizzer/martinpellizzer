Acanthocalycium thionanthum subsp. ferrarii

Acanthocalycium thionanthum subsp.

ferrarii, commonly known as the Ferrari's hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spiny cactus native to Mexico. It features a globular to cylindrical stem covered in dense, white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
