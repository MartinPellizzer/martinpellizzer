Actinodaphne perlucida

Actinodaphne perlucida, commonly known as the translucent laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in traditional craftsmanship.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
