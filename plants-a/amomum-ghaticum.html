Amomum ghaticum

Amomum ghaticum, commonly known as the Greater Galangal, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically yellow or green in color.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
