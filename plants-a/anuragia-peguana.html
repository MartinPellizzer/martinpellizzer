Anuragia peguana

Anuragia peguana, commonly known as the golden bower vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along its climbing stems. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that provide a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Anuragia peguana is also valued for its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
