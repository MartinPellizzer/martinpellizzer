Agave shawii var. sebastiana

Agave shawii var. sebastiana, commonly known as the Sebastiana agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a bluish-green hue and marginal spines. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears small, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in xeriscapes.
