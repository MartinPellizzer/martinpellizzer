Acalypha tenuicauda

Acalypha tenuicauda, commonly known as the slender-tailed spurflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features delicate, hair-like leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, which are followed by distinctive, winged bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
