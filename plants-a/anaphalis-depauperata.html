Anaphalis depauperata

Anaphalis depauperata, commonly known as the poorflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Anaphalis depauperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, frost-resistant blooms.
