Antitoxicum medium

Antitoxicum medium, commonly known as the common spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds such as latex and alkaloids. The plant typically has smooth, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially hazardous to humans and animals.
