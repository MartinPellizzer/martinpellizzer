Aconitum hamiltonii

Aconitum hamiltonii, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple or blue flowers that resemble a hood, giving it its common name. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it both a valuable and dangerous species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling due to its toxicity.
