Artemisia argyrophylla var. arcuata

Artemisia argyrophylla var. arcuata, commonly known as silverleaf sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are aromatic and contain volatile oils, contributing to its medicinal and ecological significance.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
