Apocynum griseum

Apocynum griseum, commonly known as grayleaf dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing toxic seeds. It is part of the Apocynaceae family, known for its milky sap and medicinal properties.

Grayleaf dogbane is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.
