Andropogon pertusus subvar. hirtus

Andropogon pertusus subvar. hirtus, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its common name. This grass thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its flowering spikes are typically greenish or reddish, appearing in late summer to early fall.

Hairy bluestem plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
