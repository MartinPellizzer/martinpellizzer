Arabis pendula

Arabis pendula, commonly known as the hanging rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. This species is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often found in alpine or subalpine regions, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these specialized habitats.
