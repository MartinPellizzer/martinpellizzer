Acacia triptycha

Acacia triptycha, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in spring, which are clustered in inflorescences at the ends of the branches. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods that split open when mature.

Acacia triptycha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species often found in dry coastal areas.
