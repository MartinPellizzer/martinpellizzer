Allophylus buchananii var. ugandensis

Allophylus buchananii var. ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda alder, is a shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Saracinoideae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in local horticulture.
