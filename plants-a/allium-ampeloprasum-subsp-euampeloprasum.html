Allium ampeloprasum subsp. euampeloprasum

Allium ampeloprasum subsp.

euampeloprasum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and broad, fleshy leaves that form a rosette.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the summer, which are followed by bulb-like structures. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional dishes.
