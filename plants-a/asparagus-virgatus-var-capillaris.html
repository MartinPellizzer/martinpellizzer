Asparagus virgatus var. capillaris

Asparagus virgatus var. capillaris, commonly known as the slender asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features thin, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, with small, scale-like leaves that are densely packed along the stem. The plant produces tiny, white to greenish flowers in early spring, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. Its root system consists of fibrous roots that help anchor the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
