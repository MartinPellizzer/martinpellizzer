Adiantum mochaenum

Adiantum mochaenum, commonly known as the Mocha maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bioluminescent fronds that give it a striking, almost ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with high levels of indirect light. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky substrates in its natural habitat.

Adiantum mochaenum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
