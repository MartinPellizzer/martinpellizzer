Achillea bandana

Achillea bandana, commonly known as the Mexican yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring gray-green foliage and flat, showy flower clusters that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its common name is derived from the bandana-like appearance of its flower heads.
