Athanasia pinnata

Athanasia pinnata, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks rising from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor in the muddy substrate. The flowers are typically pink or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in various Asian traditions.
