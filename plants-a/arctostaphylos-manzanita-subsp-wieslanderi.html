Arctostaphylos manzanita subsp. wieslanderi

Arctostaphylos manzanita subsp.

wieslanderi, commonly known as Wiesland's manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its reddish-barked stems and evergreen leaves that are typically lance-shaped.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, which bloom in late winter or early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, dark seeds upon maturity.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
