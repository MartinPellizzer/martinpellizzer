Agathis spathulata

Agathis spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed agathis, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and conical, spire-like crown. The tree produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
