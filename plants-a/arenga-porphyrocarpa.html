Arenga porphyrocarpa

Arenga porphyrocarpa, commonly known as the red palm or red areca, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its inflorescence produces small, yellowish flowers, which are followed by edible, red-orange fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
