Aquilegia formosa

Aquilegia formosa, commonly known as the Western columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to western North America.

It features striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, purple, and yellow, arranged in a distinctive basket-like shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are deeply lobed.

Aquilegia formosa is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
