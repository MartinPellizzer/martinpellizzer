Abelia zanderi

Abelia zanderi, commonly known as the Florida abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, arching branches and dark green, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Abelia zanderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
