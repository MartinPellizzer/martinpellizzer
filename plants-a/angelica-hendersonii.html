Angelica hendersonii

Angelica hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and compound leaves.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in moist, mountainous regions. The plant produces tall, flowering stems with small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical significance in indigenous botanical practices.
