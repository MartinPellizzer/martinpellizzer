Albizia mahalao

Albizia mahalao, commonly known as the Madagascar silk tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds. Albizia mahalao is often found in dry forests and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
