Asplenium geraense

Asplenium geraense, commonly known as the Geraense spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or forest floors. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Asplenium geraense is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a resilient species in diverse ecological settings.
