Allium parishii var. keckii

Allium parishii var. keckii, commonly known as the Keck's garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, narrow leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.
