Archidendron alternifoliolatum

Archidendron alternifoliolatum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and often trifoliate or pinnate, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange-red, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its pods are typically long and slender, containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
