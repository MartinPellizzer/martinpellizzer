Achillea glaberrima

Achillea glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
