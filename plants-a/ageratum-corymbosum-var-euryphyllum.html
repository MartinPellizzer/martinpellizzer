Ageratum corymbosum var. euryphyllum

Ageratum corymbosum var. euryphyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flower heads that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This variety is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
