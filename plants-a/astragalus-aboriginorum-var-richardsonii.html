Astragalus aboriginorum var. richardsonii

Astragalus aboriginorum var. richardsonii, commonly known as the Desert Pea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.
