Aster sibiricus f. subintegerrimus

Aster sibiricus f. subintegerrimus, commonly known as the Siberian aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features sturdy stems that grow upright, often reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to violet petals, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
