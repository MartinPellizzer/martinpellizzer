Aubertia borbonica

Aubertia borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar bean tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Aubertia borbonica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
