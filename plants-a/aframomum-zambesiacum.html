Aframomum zambesiacum

Aframomum zambesiacum, commonly known as Zambesian cardamom, is a tropical plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers followed by small, red berries. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and have a strong, spicy aroma similar to cardamom.

Due to its unique properties, it is also of interest for horticultural and commercial cultivation.
