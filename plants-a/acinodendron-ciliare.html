Acinodendron ciliare

Acinodendron ciliare, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, elliptical leaves with hairy margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna habitats.

Its unique foliage and floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
