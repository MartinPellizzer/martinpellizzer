Atriplex hortensis var. microsperma

Atriplex hortensis var. microsperma, commonly known as the dwarf saltbush, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical seeds. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in saline soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for stabilizing soils in harsh climates.
