Ardisia crispa var. elegans

Ardisia crispa var. elegans, commonly known as the velvetleaf ardisia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy sheen. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Following flowering, it bears bright red, glossy berries that are attractive to birds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
