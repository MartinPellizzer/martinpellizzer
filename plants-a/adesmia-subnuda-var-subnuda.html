Adesmia subnuda var. subnuda

Adesmia subnuda var. subnuda, commonly known as the slender legume, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, which are followed by narrow, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh environments, showcasing drought resistance and efficient water conservation mechanisms.
