Anthyllis hermanniae subsp. brutia

Anthyllis hermanniae subsp.

brutia, commonly known as the Hermann's anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers clustered in dense heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
