Agrostis exarata subsp. minor

Agrostis exarata subsp.

minor, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
