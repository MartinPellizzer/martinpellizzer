Allium trichospathum

Allium trichospathum, commonly known as the purple onion or wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in open woodlands and grassy areas. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped purple flowers in the summer. Its bulbous stem is edible and has a mild onion-like flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
