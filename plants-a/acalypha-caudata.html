Acalypha caudata

Acalypha caudata, commonly known as the silky filbert or velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Acalypha caudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
