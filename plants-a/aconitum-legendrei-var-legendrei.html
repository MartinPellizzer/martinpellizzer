Aconitum legendrei var. legendrei

Aconitum legendrei var. legendrei, commonly known as the Legendre's monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking purple to blue flowers arranged in a raceme, with heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its toxic alkaloids, which are present throughout the plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
