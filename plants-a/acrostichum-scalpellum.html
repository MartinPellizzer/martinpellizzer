Acrostichum scalpellum

Acrostichum scalpellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated fronds that are typically less than 1 meter in length. The plant features bipinnate leaves with closely spaced, linear pinnae, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acrostichum scalpellum plays a role in providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
