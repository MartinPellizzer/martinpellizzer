Aster undulatus var. triangularis

Aster undulatus var. triangularis, commonly known as the wavy aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with lavender to pink petals that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often wavy or undulating along the edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
