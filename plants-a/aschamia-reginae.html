Aschamia reginae

Aschamia reginae, commonly known as the royal bellflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its large, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Aschamia reginae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
