Amelanchier rubescens var. cinerea

Amelanchier rubescens var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray-leaf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its pale grayish-green leaves that turn a striking red or purple in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
