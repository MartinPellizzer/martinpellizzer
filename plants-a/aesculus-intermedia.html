Aesculus intermedia

Aesculus intermedia, commonly known as the European horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmately compound leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The tree produces spiny capsules containing glossy, chestnut-like seeds. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in parks and gardens.

However, it is important to note that its seeds are toxic and should not be consumed.
