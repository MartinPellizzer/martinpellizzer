Anthurium quinquesulcatum

Anthurium quinquesulcatum, commonly known as the five-grooved anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib and five distinct grooves, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium quinquesulcatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
