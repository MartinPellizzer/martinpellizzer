Anguloa × ruckeri var. sanguinea

Anguloa × ruckeri var. sanguinea, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The botanical classification highlights its hybrid origin, combining traits from Anguloa ruckeri and other related species.
