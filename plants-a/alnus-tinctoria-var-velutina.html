Alnus tinctoria var. velutina

Alnus tinctoria var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, soft foliage that turns yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, spherical fruits called samaras, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
