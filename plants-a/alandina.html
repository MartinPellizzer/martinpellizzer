Alandina

Alandina, commonly known as the sandverbena, is a genus of flowering plants native to the Americas.

It typically features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in clusters. The plants are generally herbaceous, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Alandina species are often found in dry, sandy soils and are adapted to arid environments.

They are popular in gardens for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.
