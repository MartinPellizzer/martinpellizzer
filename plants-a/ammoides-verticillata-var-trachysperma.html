Ammoides verticillata var. trachysperma

Ammoides verticillata var. trachysperma, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their digestive and carminative properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
