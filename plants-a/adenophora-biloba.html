Adenophora biloba

Adenophora biloba, commonly known as the blue flax or Chinese blue flax, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Adenophora biloba is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
