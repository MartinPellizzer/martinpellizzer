Actinidia rubus

Actinidia rubus, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its palmately compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often considered a delicacy in some regions. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Actinidia rubus is also notable for its role in traditional Chinese medicine.
