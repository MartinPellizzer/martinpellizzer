Aldina latifolia var. pubescens

Aldina latifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy broadleaf aldina, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
