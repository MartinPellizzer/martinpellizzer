Abarema jupunba var. trapezifolia

Abarema jupunba var. trapezifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pea vine, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and is often found in moist, tropical environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a climbing plant with attractive foliage and blooms.
