Allium canadense var. canadense

Allium canadense var. canadense, commonly known as Canada garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, bluish-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. It has a bulbous root system and is part of the Amaryllidaceae family.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
