Amaranthus acroglochin

Amaranthus acroglochin, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red or purple stems. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Amaranthus acroglochin is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
