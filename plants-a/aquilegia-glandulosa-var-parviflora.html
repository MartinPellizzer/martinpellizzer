Aquilegia glandulosa var. parviflora

Aquilegia glandulosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves that are palmately divided and its showy, spurred flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodland or alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
