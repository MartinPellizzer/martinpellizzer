Amphinomia genistoides

Amphinomia genistoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight zigzag pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
