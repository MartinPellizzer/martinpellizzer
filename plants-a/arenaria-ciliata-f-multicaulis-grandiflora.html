Arenaria ciliata f. multicaulis-grandiflora

Arenaria ciliata f. multicaulis-grandiflora, commonly known as the hairy sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with multiple stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and linear, adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. This subspecies is noted for its compact growth and ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
