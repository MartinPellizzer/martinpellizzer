Arnica fulgens

Arnica fulgens, commonly known as the showy arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Arnica fulgens grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
