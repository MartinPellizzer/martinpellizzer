Astragalus sultanii

Astragalus sultanii, commonly known as the Sultan's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus sultanii is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
