Aquilegia atrata var. major

Aquilegia atrata var. major, commonly known as the Black Cohosh, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces clusters of bell-shaped, purple or red flowers. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and is often found in woodland areas. It has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.
