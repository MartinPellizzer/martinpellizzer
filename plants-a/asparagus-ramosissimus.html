Asparagus ramosissimus

Asparagus ramosissimus, commonly known as the silver asparagus or white asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in a silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.

Asparagus ramosissimus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
