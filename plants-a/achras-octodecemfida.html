Achras octodecemfida

Achras octodecemfida, commonly known as the star apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces large, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, round fruits with a yellowish skin and a sweet, custard-like pulp. Its leaves are large, glossy, and palmate, with five to seven lobes, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its fruit and foliage.
