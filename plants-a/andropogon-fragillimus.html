Andropogon fragillimus

Andropogon fragillimus, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, blue-green blades that are typically less than 10 inches tall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological importance, it is a valued plant in native grassland restoration projects.
