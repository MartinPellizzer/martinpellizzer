Anaxeton crispum

Anaxeton crispum, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. The plant has silvery-gray leaves that are often covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice in horticulture.
