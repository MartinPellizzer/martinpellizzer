Abies alba subsp. acutifolia

Abies alba subsp.

acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaf silver fir, is a subspecies of the silver fir native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its dark green, needle-like leaves that are sharply pointed.

The leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches, giving the tree a uniform and elegant appearance. It produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

This subspecies is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in forested areas at higher altitudes.
