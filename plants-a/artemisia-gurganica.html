Artemisia gurganica

Artemisia gurganica, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. Artemisia gurganica is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

It has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in various cuisines.
