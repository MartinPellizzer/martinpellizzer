Alstroemeria nivalis

Alstroemeria nivalis, commonly known as the snow alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Alstroemeria nivalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
