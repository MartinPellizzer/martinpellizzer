Acacia porphyrochila

Acacia porphyrochila, commonly known as the red-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often dispersed by wind.

Acacia porphyrochila is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
