Allium rotundum var. synnotii

Allium rotundum var. synnotii, commonly known as the Kashmiri garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Kashmir.

It features small, round, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, stout stem and produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used as a spice in culinary applications. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its aromatic, pungent flavor.
