Arnica oporina

Arnica oporina, commonly known as Siberian arnica, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia, particularly Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Arnica oporina is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
