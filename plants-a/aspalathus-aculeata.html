Aspalathus aculeata

Aspalathus aculeata, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, spiny leaves and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
