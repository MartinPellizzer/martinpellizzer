Arabis colchica

Arabis colchica, commonly known as the Armenian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with distinctive cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and appear in clusters, blooming in early spring.

Arabis colchica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
