Aconitum sajanense

Aconitum sajanense, commonly known as the Sajan mountain aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum sajanense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
