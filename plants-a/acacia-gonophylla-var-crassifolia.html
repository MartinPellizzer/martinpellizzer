Acacia gonophylla var. crassifolia

Acacia gonophylla var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Acacia gonophylla var. crassifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
