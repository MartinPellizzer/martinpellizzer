Allium pygmaeum

Allium pygmaeum, commonly known as the giant bulbous garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant features a large, rounded bulb covered in a fibrous sheath, with grass-like leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
