Acacia callistemon

Acacia callistemon, commonly known as the red bottlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or crimson flower spikes, which are actually clusters of tiny, tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Acacia callistemon is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which improves soil fertility.
