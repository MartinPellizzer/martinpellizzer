Amalia cinnabarina

Amalia cinnabarina, commonly known as the crimson amalia, is a striking plant native to the temperate regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a bold splash of color to gardens. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its showy blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its visual appeal.
