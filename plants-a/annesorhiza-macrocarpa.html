Annesorhiza macrocarpa

Annesorhiza macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces large, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Annesorhiza macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits the surrounding soil.
