Argemone turnerae

Argemone turnerae, commonly known as Mexican pricklypoppy, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features a hairy, branched stem and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
