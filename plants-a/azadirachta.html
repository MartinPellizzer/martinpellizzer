Azadirachta

Azadirachta, commonly known as neem, is a tropical tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its evergreen nature, with dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces a fruit that contains a single seed surrounded by a hard, woody shell. Neem is renowned for its potent chemical compounds, particularly azadirachtin, which have insecticidal properties.

Its leaves, seeds, and oil are widely used in traditional medicine, agriculture, and skincare.
