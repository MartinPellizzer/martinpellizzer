Arabis propera

Arabis propera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, adapting to its harsh growing conditions.
