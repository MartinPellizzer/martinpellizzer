Amaranthus amboinicus

Amaranthus amboinicus, commonly known as love lie pillow, is an annual herb native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron and protein. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural fields.

The plant's name reflects its traditional use in herbal medicine and as a decorative plant.
