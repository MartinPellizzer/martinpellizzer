Amomum sarasinorum

Amomum sarasinorum, commonly known as the Sarawak cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers followed by three-lobed, reddish-brown berries. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its essential oils and aromatic compounds make it valuable in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
