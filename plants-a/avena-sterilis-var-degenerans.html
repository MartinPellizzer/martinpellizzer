Avena sterilis var. degenerans

Avena sterilis var. degenerans, commonly known as the wild oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that are often found in disturbed soils. It is an annual plant that typically grows in open fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
