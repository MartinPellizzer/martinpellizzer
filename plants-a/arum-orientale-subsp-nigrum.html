Arum orientale subsp. nigrum

Arum orientale subsp.

nigrum, commonly known as the black arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia. It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture.

The plant produces a unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy, purple-bracted spathe. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, a characteristic common in the Araceae family.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage and exotic appearance.
