Andryala × faurei

Andryala × faurei, commonly known as the hybrid marsh elder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Andryala aquatica and Andryala hirsuta, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves with serrated margins and a short, fibrous root system adapted to aquatic environments.
