Amasonia integerrima

Amasonia integerrima, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy purple to reddish-purple composite flowers in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Amasonia integerrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
