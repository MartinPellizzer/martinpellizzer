Agave nuusaviorum subsp. nuusaviorum

Agave nuusaviorum subsp.

nuusaviorum, commonly known as the Mexican century plant, is a succulent plant native to central Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are lined with sharp spines.

The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with numerous white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
