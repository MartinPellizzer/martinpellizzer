Agapetes obovata

Agapetes obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring.

Agapetes obovata is often found in humid, shaded environments at higher elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
