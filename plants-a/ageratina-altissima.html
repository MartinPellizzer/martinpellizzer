Ageratina altissima

Ageratina altissima, commonly known as white crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Ageratina altissima is often considered an invasive species in regions outside its native range.
