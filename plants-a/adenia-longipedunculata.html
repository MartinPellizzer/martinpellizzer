Adenia longipedunculata

Adenia longipedunculata, commonly known as the giant adenia, is a striking climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red or orange fruits that are edible and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Adenia longipedunculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
