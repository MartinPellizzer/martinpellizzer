Aster prainii

Aster prainii, commonly known as the Prain's aster, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with ray florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster prainii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
