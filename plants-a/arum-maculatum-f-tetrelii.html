Arum maculatum f. tetrelii

Arum maculatum f. tetrelii, commonly known as the spotted arum, is a variant of the common arum characterized by its striking, colorful foliage.

This plant belongs to the Araceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and feature distinctive patterns of green, white, and purple. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of arum species.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
