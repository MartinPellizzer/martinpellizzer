Aloe guineensis

Aloe guineensis, commonly known as the African aloe, is a succulent plant native to the dry regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and rosette-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Aloe guineensis is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and having a shallow root system.

Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that has been used for its medicinal and skincare properties.
