Amomum penicillatum

Amomum penicillatum, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by black, capsule-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinct, fragrant scent.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
