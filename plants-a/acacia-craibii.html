Acacia craibii

Acacia craibii, commonly known as the Northern Territory acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. Acacia craibii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
