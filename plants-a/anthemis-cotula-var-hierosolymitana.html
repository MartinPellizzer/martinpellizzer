Anthemis cotula var. hierosolymitana

Anthemis cotula var. hierosolymitana, commonly known as holy thyme or Jerusalem thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, yellowish or white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit that makes it ideal for ornamental use.
