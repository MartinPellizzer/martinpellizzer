Acalypha glabrata var. pilosa

Acalypha glabrata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and riverbanks.
