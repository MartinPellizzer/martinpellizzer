Angelica edulis

Angelica edulis, commonly known as edible angelica, is a biennial or perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by dark, spherical fruits. Its roots are the primary edible part, known for their strong, aromatic flavor and medicinal properties.

Angelica edulis is often cultivated for both culinary and herbal purposes.
