Astragalus aucheri

Astragalus aucheri, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus aucheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
