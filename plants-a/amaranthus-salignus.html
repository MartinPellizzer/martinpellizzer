Amaranthus salignus

Amaranthus salignus, commonly known as the weeping amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often droop, giving it a cascading appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus salignus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique visual effect.
