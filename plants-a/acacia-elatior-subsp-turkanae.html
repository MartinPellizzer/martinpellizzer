Acacia elatior subsp. turkanae

Acacia elatior subsp.

turkanae, commonly known as Turkana acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and phyllodes that are typically linear to narrowly elliptic.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
