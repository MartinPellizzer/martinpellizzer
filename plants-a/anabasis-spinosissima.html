Anabasis spinosissima

Anabasis spinosissima, commonly known as the spiny knotweed, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low moisture and high temperatures.

Anabasis spinosissima is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in disturbed or degraded habitats.
