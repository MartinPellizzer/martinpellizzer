Alyssum samariferum

Alyssum samariferum, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has finely divided, silver-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum samariferum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
