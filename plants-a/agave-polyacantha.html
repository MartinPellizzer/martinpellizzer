Agave polyacantha

Agave polyacantha, commonly known as the hooked agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, spiny leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
