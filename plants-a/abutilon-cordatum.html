Abutilon cordatum

Abutilon cordatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a woody stem and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its fuzzy, velvety leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Abutilon cordatum is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
