Ageratina xanthochlora

Ageratina xanthochlora, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its most striking characteristic is its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
