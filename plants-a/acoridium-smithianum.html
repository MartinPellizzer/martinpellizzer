Acoridium smithianum

Acoridium smithianum, commonly known as the African water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are showy, with a white or pale yellow color, and they bloom during the wet season.

Acoridium smithianum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterborne organisms.
