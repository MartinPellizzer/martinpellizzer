Aster acuminatus var. elatior

Aster acuminatus var. elatior, commonly known as the tall blue aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its foliage consists of alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
