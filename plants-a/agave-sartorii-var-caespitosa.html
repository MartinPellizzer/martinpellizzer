Agave sartorii var. caespitosa

Agave sartorii var. caespitosa, commonly known as the compact agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth pattern, with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which are often used in traditional ceremonies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in herbal medicine.
