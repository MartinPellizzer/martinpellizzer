Attalea lundellii

Attalea lundellii, commonly known as the Lundell's palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into clustered fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for sustainable use, it is of interest for both horticulture and conservation efforts.
