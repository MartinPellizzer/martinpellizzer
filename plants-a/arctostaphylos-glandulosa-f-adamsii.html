Arctostaphylos glandulosa f. adamsii

Arctostaphylos glandulosa f. adamsii, commonly known as the Adams' manzanita, is a shrub native to California's coastal regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and evergreen foliage with leathery, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically pink to reddish in color, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is adapted to coastal climates and is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats.
