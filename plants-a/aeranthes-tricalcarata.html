Aeranthes tricalcarata

Aeranthes tricalcarata, commonly known as the three-ribbed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
