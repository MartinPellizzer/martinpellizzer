Anthurium flavolineatum

Anthurium flavolineatum, commonly known as the golden line anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with bright yellow veins. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium flavolineatum is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting flowers.
