Artemisia ludoviciana subsp. albula

Artemisia ludoviciana subsp.

albula, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage composed of narrow, linear leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical-shaped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Native American practices.
