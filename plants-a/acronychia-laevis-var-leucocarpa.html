Acronychia laevis var. leucocarpa

Acronychia laevis var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited silky ash, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, white or pale yellow fruits. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, fibrous pulp, making it valuable in traditional Aboriginal diets.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
