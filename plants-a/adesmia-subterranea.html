Adesmia subterranea

Adesmia subterranea, commonly known as the underground legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique subterranean growth habit. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Adesmia subterranea plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
