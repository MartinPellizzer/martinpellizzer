Anagallis phoenicea

Anagallis phoenicea, commonly known as the crimson water-primrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in moist, sandy soils, often found near coastal areas or in wetlands. The plant produces showy, pink to crimson flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

Anagallis phoenicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
