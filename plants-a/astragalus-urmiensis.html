Astragalus urmiensis

Astragalus urmiensis, commonly known as the Urmi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Iran and Turkmenistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
