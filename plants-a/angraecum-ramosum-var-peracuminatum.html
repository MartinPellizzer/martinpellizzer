Angraecum ramosum var. peracuminatum

Angraecum ramosum var. peracuminatum, commonly known as the Peracuminatum Angraecum, is a unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, branching growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically produces long, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
