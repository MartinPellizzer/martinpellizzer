Astragalus hypoglottis subsp. purpureus

Astragalus hypoglottis subsp.

purpureus, commonly known as the purpleleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

The plant has slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive purplish tinge on the leaves, which sets it apart from other members of the Astragalus genus.
