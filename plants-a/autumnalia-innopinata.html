Autumnalia innopinata

Autumnalia innopinata, commonly known as the silverleaf autumnal, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking silver-white color in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Autumnalia innopinata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
