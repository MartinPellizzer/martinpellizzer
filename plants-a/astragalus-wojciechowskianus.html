Astragalus wojciechowskianus

Astragalus wojciechowskianus, commonly known as the Wojciechowski milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
