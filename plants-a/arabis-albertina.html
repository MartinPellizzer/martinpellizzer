Arabis albertina

Arabis albertina, commonly known as the Albertine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
