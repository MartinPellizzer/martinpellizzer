Aristea madagascariensis var. intermedia

Aristea madagascariensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Madagascan aristea, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and flowering heads. The plant features narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
