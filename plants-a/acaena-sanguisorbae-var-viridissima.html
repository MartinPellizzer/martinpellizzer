Acaena sanguisorbae var. viridissima

Acaena sanguisorbae var. viridissima, commonly known as the green-stemmed acacia, is a variant of the acacia plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, slender stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces tiny, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative plant in arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
