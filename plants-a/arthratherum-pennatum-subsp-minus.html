Arthratherum pennatum subsp. minus

Arthratherum pennatum subsp.

minus, commonly known as the lesser pennate grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained to moderately wet soils.

The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting insect populations and stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
