Adenostyles albida subsp. pyrenaica

Adenostyles albida subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for alpine and subalpine habitats.
