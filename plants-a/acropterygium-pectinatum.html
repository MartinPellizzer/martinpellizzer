Acropterygium pectinatum

Acropterygium pectinatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.

The plant has a moderate growth rate and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
