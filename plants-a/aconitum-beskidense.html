Aconitum beskidense

Aconitum beskidense, commonly known as the Beskids aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum beskidense is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas and along stream banks.
