Agrostophyllum cyathiforme var. sumatranum

Agrostophyllum cyathiforme var. sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped inflorescences. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers arranged in dense clusters, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for horticultural purposes.
