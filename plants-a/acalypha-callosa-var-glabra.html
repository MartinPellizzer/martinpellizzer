Acalypha callosa var. glabra

Acalypha callosa var. glabra, commonly known as smooth false nettle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

It is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
