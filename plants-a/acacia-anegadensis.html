Acacia anegadensis

Acacia anegadensis, commonly known as the Anegada acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the British Virgin Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous tiny leaflets, and its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt-laden air and sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
