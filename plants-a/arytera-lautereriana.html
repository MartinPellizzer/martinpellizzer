Arytera lautereriana

Arytera lautereriana, commonly known as the Lauterer's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
