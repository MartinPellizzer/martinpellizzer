Alocasia cucullata

Alocasia cucullata, commonly known as the hooded arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with striking patterns and a velvety texture. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix, surrounded by a spathe. Alocasia cucullata typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters in its natural habitat.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
