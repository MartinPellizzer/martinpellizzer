Anisotome imbricata

Anisotome imbricata, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Anisotome imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
