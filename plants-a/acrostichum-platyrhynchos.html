Acrostichum platyrhynchos

Acrostichum platyrhynchos, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive broad, flat shape.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
