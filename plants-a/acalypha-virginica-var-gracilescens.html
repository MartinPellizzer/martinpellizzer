Acalypha virginica var. gracilescens

Acalypha virginica var. gracilescens, commonly known as silky ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, silky foliage and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to three feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces distinctive seed heads that are often mistaken for flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
