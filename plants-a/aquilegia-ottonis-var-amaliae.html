Aquilegia ottonis var. amaliae

Aquilegia ottonis var. amaliae, commonly known as the Amalia's Columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features basal leaves with deeply lobed or divided foliage, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
