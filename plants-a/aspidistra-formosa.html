Aspidistra formosa

Aspidistra formosa, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its dark green, waxy, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are surrounded by a spathaceous bract. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in shady, humid environments.

Aspidistra formosa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to tolerate low light conditions.
