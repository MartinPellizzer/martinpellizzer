Ascyrum hypericoides

Ascyrum hypericoides, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and narrow, fleshy leaves. The plant typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Ascyrum hypericoides is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
