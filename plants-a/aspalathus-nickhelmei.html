Aspalathus nickhelmei

Aspalathus nickhelmei, commonly known as the Natal tea or red bush willow, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces red, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverine forests.

Aspalathus nickhelmei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
