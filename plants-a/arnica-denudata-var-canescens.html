Arnica denudata var. canescens

Arnica denudata var. canescens, commonly known as the alpine tawnpuff, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, though it is often used with caution due to its potential toxicity.
