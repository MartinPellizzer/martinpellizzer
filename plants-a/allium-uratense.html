Allium uratense

Allium uratense, commonly known as the Urate onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rocky or arid gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and potentially useful in herbal applications.
