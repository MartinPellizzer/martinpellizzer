Aspidistra longituba

Aspidistra longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed aspidistra, is a perennial evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf structure and inflorescence. The plant features broad, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged in a compact rosette. It produces a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Aspidistra longituba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular indoor plant.
