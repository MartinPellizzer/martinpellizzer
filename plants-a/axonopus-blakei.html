Axonopus blakei

Axonopus blakei, commonly known as Blake's axonopus, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and swampy areas. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with fine, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers that produce lightweight seeds.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
