Acourtia thurberi

Acourtia thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's acourtia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
