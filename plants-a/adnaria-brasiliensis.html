Adnaria brasiliensis

Adnaria brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Adnaria brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
