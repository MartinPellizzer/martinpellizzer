Agialid latifolia

Agialid latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf agialid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or near water bodies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Agialid latifolia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
