Archangelica decurrens var. tschimganica

Archangelica decurrens var. tschimganica, commonly known as the Siberian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The roots of this plant are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
