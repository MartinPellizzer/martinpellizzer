Astragalus diaphanus var. diurnus

Astragalus diaphanus var. diurnus, commonly known as the morning milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with low precipitation.
