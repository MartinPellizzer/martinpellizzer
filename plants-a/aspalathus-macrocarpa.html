Aspalathus macrocarpa

Aspalathus macrocarpa, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Aspalathus macrocarpa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
