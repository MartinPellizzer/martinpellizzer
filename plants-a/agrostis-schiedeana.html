Agrostis schiedeana

Agrostis schiedeana, commonly known as the mountain bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as Andean slopes and rocky soils, where it forms dense, low-growing mats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to cold and arid conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Agrostis schiedeana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine wildlife.
