Astragalus wendelboi

Astragalus wendelboi, commonly known as the Wendelbo's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
