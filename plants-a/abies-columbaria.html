Abies columbaria

Abies columbaria, commonly known as the dwarf fir, is a small evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows to a height of only 1 to 2 meters, making it one of the smallest species in the fir genus. The plant has a dense, conical shape with needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. Its cones are small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color.

Abies columbaria is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in poor soils and cold climates.
