Aconitum pilipes

Aconitum pilipes, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a coarse texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Aconitum pilipes contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.
