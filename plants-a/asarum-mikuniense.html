Asarum mikuniense

Asarum mikuniense, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its underground rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
