Anemone wardii

Anemone wardii, commonly known as the Himalayan anemone, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Anemone wardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
