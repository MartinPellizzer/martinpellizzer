Agave toumeyana var. toumeyana

Agave toumeyana var. toumeyana, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, blue-gray leaves that are lined with sharp, spiny margins. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with clusters of small, white to greenish flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional crafts and beverages.
