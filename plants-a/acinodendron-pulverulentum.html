Acinodendron pulverulentum

Acinodendron pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and feathery, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain minute, dark seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
