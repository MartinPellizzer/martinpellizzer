Annona malmeana var. malmeana

Annona malmeana var. malmeana, commonly known as the Malme's custard apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, yellowish fruit with a sweet, creamy pulp that is often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
