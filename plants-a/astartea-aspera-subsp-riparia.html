Astartea aspera subsp. riparia

Astartea aspera subsp.

riparia, commonly known as the river aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats. It typically grows in moist soils along streams, rivers, and other water bodies.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers in the summer. It is often found in the northern regions of North America.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
