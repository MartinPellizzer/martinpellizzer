Acacia halliana

Acacia halliana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The acacia is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
