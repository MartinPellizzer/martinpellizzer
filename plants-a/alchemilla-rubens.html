Alchemilla rubens

Alchemilla rubens, commonly known as the red lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla rubens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
