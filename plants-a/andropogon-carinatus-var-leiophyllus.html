Andropogon carinatus var. leiophyllus

Andropogon carinatus var. leiophyllus, commonly known as the smoothleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical structure supports its role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
