Alpinia odontonema

Alpinia odontonema, commonly known as the flame ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround clusters of small, white flowers. The plant has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Alpinia odontonema is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.
