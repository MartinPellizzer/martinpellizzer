Albertinia incanescens

Albertinia incanescens, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring tall, slender stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique beauty and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
