Artemisia serrata

Artemisia serrata, commonly known as rough tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, serrated leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its medicinal properties have been utilized in traditional remedies for their antiseptic and digestive benefits.
