Alopecurus arundinaceus var. exserens

Alopecurus arundinaceus var. exserens, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
