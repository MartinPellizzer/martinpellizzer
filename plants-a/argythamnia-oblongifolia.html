Argythamnia oblongifolia

Argythamnia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are elongated and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months.
