Adenostyles alpina subsp. briquetii

Adenostyles alpina subsp.

briquetii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has slender stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies briquetii is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth form compared to the main species.
