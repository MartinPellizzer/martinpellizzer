Allium euboicum

Allium euboicum, commonly known as the Euboean leek, is a perennial herb native to the Greek island of Euboea.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which are followed by small, spherical berries. Allium euboicum is valued for its mild garlic-like flavor and is often used in Mediterranean cuisine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
