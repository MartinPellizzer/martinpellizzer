Aerva mozambicensis

Aerva mozambicensis, commonly known as African senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Aerva mozambicensis is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
