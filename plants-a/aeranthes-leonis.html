Aeranthes leonis

Aeranthes leonis, commonly known as the lion's aeranthes, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with intricate patterns that resemble the mane of a lion, giving it its common name.

Aeranthes leonis is a perennial herb that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
