Andropogon ceresiiformis var. breviaristatus

Andropogon ceresiiformis var. breviaristatus, commonly known as the short-bristle bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle with short, bristly spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
