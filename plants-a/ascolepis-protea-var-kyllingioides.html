Ascolepis protea var. kyllingioides

Ascolepis protea var. kyllingioides, commonly known as the Knysna protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant is endemic to the Eastern Cape and Western Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted by the nectar.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and dramatic appearance.
