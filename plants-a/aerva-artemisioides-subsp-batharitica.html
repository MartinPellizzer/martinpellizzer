Aerva artemisioides subsp. batharitica

Aerva artemisioides subsp.

batharitica, commonly known as the Indian spurge or batharitica spurge, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for various health purposes.
