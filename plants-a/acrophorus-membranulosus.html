Acrophorus membranulosus

Acrophorus membranulosus, commonly known as the membranous acanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive membranous edge, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
