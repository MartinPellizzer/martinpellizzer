Aster shastensis var. latifolius

Aster shastensis var. latifolius, commonly known as the Shasta aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, lavender-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
