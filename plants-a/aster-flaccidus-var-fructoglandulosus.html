Aster flaccidus var. fructoglandulosus

Aster flaccidus var. fructoglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular-fruited aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its fruits are distinctive, featuring glandular structures that aid in seed dispersal.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to arid environments.
