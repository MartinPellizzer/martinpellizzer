Atriplex lentiformis subsp. typica

Atriplex lentiformis subsp.

typica, commonly known as the smooth saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are often subtended by bracts. It is well-suited to saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.

Its ability to thrive in extreme conditions makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
