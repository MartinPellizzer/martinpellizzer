Asparagus macowanii

Asparagus macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's asparagus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, white flowers in spring. The plant has slender, erect stems that often branch near the base, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Asparagus macowanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and hardiness.
