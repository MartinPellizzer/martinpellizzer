Astragalus voronovianus

Astragalus voronovianus, commonly known as Voronov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as steppes and rocky soils. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
