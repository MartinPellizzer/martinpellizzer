Aspidistra longifolia

Aspidistra longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved aspidistra, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Aspidistra longifolia is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
