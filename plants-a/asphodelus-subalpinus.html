Asphodelus subalpinus

Asphodelus subalpinus, commonly known as the alpine asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems with clusters of yellow or greenish-yellow flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Asphodelus subalpinus is often found in rocky or gravelly areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
