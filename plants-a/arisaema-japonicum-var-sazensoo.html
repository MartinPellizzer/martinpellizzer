Arisaema japonicum var. sazensoo

Arisaema japonicum var. sazensoo, commonly known as the Japanese Taro, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant produces a large, showy spathe that encloses the spadix, which is typically yellowish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The plant is also cultivated for its edible corm, which is used in various traditional dishes.
