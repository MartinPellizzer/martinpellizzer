Amaryllis araucana

Amaryllis araucana, commonly known as the Araucaria amaryllis, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, waxy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Amaryllis araucana is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with mild climates.
