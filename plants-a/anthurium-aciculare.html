Anthurium aciculare

Anthurium aciculare, commonly known as the needle anthurium, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that house the true flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Anthurium aciculare is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
