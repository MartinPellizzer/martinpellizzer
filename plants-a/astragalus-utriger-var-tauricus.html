Astragalus utriger var. tauricus

Astragalus utriger var. tauricus, commonly known as the Tauric milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of southern Russia and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
