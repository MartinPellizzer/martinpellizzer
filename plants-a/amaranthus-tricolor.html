Amaranthus tricolor

Amaranthus tricolor, commonly known as colorful amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that range from red, orange, to yellow, making it visually striking. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into three-angled, bead-like seeds. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and is also used in some traditional cuisines.

Amaranthus tricolor is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
