Asarum unzen

Asarum unzen, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
