Alibertia pilosa

Alibertia pilosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Alibertia pilosa is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
