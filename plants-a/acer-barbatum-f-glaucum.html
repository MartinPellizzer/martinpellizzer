Acer barbatum f. glaucum

Acer barbatum f. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous maple, is a cultivar of the maple tree characterized by its distinctive bluish-white to greyish-white undersides on its leaves.

This form exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to the typical Acer barbatum, with shorter branches and a denser canopy. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a serrated margin and a pale green upper surface. It is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value, particularly during the fall when the leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange.

This cultivar thrives in temperate climates and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
