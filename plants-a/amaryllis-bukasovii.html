Amaryllis bukasovii

Amaryllis bukasovii, commonly known as the Bukasov's amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a reddish or purple tinge along the edges. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

This species is popular in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
