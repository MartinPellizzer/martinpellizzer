Andersonia carinata

Andersonia carinata, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive yellow or greenish throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
