Allium ledebourianum var. purpurascens

Allium ledebourianum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered ledebourian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and, notably, a striking purple hue. The plant is characterized by its robust bulbs, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
