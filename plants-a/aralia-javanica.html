Aralia javanica

Aralia javanica, commonly known as the Java aralia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.
