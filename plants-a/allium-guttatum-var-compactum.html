Allium guttatum var. compactum

Allium guttatum var. compactum, commonly known as the dwarf garlic chive, is a compact, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. The plant is valued for its mild garlic-like aroma and is often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its hardiness and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among gardeners.
