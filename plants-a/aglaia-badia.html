Aglaia badia

Aglaia badia, commonly known as the black aglaia, is a member of the Meliaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing single seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and ecological studies.
