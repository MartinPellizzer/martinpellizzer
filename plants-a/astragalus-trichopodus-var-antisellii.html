Astragalus trichopodus var. antisellii

Astragalus trichopodus var. antisellii, commonly known as the Anticline Astragalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, hairy leaflets, and the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
