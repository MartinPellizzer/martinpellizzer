Alyssum racemosum

Alyssum racemosum, commonly known as clustered alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has dark green, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Alyssum racemosum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
