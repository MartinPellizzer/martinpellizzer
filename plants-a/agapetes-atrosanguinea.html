Agapetes atrosanguinea

Agapetes atrosanguinea, commonly known as the dark red-flowered shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, dark red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer. It typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a compact, shrub-like form.
