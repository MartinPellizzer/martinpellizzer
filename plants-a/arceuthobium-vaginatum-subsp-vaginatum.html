Arceuthobium vaginatum subsp. vaginatum

Arceuthobium vaginatum subsp.

vaginatum, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches to the stems of coniferous trees such as pines. It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients, forming a symbiotic relationship.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous leaves and flowers that are often hidden among the host's foliage. It spreads through the release of sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of host trees.

This subspecies is particularly notable for its unique method of seed dispersal, which involves a mechanism triggered by physical contact.
