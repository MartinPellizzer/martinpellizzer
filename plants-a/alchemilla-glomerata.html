Alchemilla glomerata

Alchemilla glomerata, commonly known as the clustered lady's mantle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla glomerata is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
