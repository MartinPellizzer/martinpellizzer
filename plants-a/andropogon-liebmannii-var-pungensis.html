Andropogon liebmannii var. pungensis

Andropogon liebmannii var. pungensis, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often adorned with fine hairs. This grass thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and woodland habitats. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
