Arisaema melanostoma

Arisaema melanostoma, commonly known as the black-stemmed arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a cluster of small, greenish-white flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
