Anchusa undulata var. subvelutina

Anchusa undulata var. subvelutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a somewhat waxy appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural habitats.
