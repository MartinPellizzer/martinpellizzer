Agave micracantha var. mitis

Agave micracantha var. mitis, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, blue-gray leaves that are often lined with sharp spines. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers. It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional practices.
