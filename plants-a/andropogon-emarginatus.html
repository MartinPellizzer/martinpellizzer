Andropogon emarginatus

Andropogon emarginatus, commonly known as eastern gamagrass, is a tall grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to 2 to 4 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Andropogon emarginatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
