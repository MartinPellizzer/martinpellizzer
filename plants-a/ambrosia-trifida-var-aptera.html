Ambrosia trifida var. aptera

Ambrosia trifida var. aptera, commonly known as the giant ragweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant is an annual herb that grows up to 3 meters tall, with a robust stem and serrated leaf margins. Its pollen is a major allergen, contributing to seasonal allergies in humans.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
