Abutilon mauritianum var. cabrae

Abutilon mauritianum var. cabrae, commonly known as the Cape ivy, is a perennial shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
