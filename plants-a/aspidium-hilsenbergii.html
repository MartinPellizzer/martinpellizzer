Aspidium hilsenbergii

Aspidium hilsenbergii, commonly known as the Hilsenberg's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has delicate, floating fronds that are divided into small, lance-shaped segments. It reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sori located on the underside of its leaves.

This fern is often used in ornamental aquatic gardens due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in various water conditions.
