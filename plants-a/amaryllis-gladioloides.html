Amaryllis gladioloides

Amaryllis gladioloides, commonly known as the red amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble those of the gladiolus. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base, and it produces tall, erect stems with clusters of vibrant red or orange flowers.

Amaryllis gladioloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in both indoor and outdoor settings with proper care.
