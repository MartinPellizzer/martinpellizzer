Alexa superba

Alexa superba, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant features long, slender leaves and produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
