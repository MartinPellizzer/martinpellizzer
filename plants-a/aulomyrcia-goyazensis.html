Aulomyrcia goyazensis

Aulomyrcia goyazensis, commonly known as the Goyazense Myrcia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Brazilian state of Mato Grosso do Sul.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical leaves with a smooth surface. It typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to the regional climate with its deep root system.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
