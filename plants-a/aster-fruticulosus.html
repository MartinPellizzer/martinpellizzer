Aster fruticulosus

Aster fruticulosus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny environments. Its foliage is covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a rough appearance.

The showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
