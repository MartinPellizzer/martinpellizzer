Astragalus pseudoaustralis

Astragalus pseudoaustralis, commonly known as the Australian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its seed pods contain small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.
