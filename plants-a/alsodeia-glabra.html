Alsodeia glabra

Alsodeia glabra, commonly known as smooth alsike, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and covered with fine pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for anchoring in various soil types.

Alsodeia glabra is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
