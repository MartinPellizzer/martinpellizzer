Acacia bartlei

Acacia bartlei, commonly known as the red acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of Southwestern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Acacia bartlei thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
