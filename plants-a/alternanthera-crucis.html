Alternanthera crucis

Alternanthera crucis, commonly known as the cross-leaved Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for aquatic and bog gardens.
