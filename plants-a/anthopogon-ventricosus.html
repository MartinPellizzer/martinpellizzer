Anthopogon ventricosus

Anthopogon ventricosus, commonly known as the inflated bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments in Africa.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic prey. The plant lacks true leaves and instead relies on its specialized bladders for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive yellowish color.

Anthopogon ventricosus plays a unique role in its ecosystem by contributing to nutrient cycling in waterlogged habitats.
