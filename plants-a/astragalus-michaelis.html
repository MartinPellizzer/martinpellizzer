Astragalus michaelis

Astragalus michaelis, commonly known as Michael's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus michaelis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
