Anil terminalis

Anil terminalis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing small, flat seeds.

Due to overharvesting, Anil terminalis is now classified as an endangered species.
