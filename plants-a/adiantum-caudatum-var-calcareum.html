Adiantum caudatum var. calcareum

Adiantum caudatum var. calcareum, commonly known as the calcareous maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on limestone or calcareous soils. The plant features intricate, finely divided fronds with a distinctive, feathery appearance. Its leaves are typically bright green and turn a reddish hue in the autumn.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
