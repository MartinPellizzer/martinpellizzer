Aira discolor

Aira discolor, commonly known as the purple air plant, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Aira discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
