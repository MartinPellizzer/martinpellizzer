Astragalus dilankurii

Astragalus dilankurii, commonly known as the Dilankurii milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
