Aster polifolius

Aster polifolius, commonly known as the many-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy blue to violet flowers and its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It blooms in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster polifolius is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
