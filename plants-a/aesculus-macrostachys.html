Aesculus macrostachys

Aesculus macrostachys, commonly known as the giant horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The tree produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in large, upright panicles during the spring, followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
