Astragalus kotschyanus

Astragalus kotschyanus, commonly known as Kotschy's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods that contain several small, rounded seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus kotschyanus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
