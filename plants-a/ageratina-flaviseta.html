Ageratina flaviseta

Ageratina flaviseta, commonly known as the yellow hair grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its finely hairy, yellow-green leaves and small, clustered white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ageratina flaviseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
