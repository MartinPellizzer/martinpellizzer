Astrantia digitata

Astrantia digitata, commonly known as the common masterwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Astrantia digitata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
