Agapetes vernayana

Agapetes vernayana, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Agapetes vernayana is often found in cool, moist environments and is adapted to thrive in shaded, well-drained soils.
