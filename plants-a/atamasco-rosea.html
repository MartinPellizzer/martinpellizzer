Atamasco rosea

Atamasco rosea, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces fruit that is edible when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Atamasco rosea is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
