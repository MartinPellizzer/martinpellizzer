Anthurium lunatum

Anthurium lunatum, commonly known as the Moon's Tears, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Anthurium lunatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage and colorful flowers make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
