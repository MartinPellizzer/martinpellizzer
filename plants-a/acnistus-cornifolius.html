Acnistus cornifolius

Acnistus cornifolius, commonly known as the horned milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its milky sap, which is toxic to some animals, is a notable botanical feature.

Acnistus cornifolius typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
