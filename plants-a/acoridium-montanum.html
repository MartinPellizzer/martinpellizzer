Acoridium montanum

Acoridium montanum, commonly known as the mountain acoridium, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae, which includes a small group of aquatic and semi-aquatic plants. The plant typically features a short, rhizomatous stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small and clustered, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces berries as its fruit.

Acoridium montanum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies.
