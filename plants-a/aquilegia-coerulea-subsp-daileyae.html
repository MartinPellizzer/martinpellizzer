Aquilegia coerulea subsp. daileyae

Aquilegia coerulea subsp.

daileyae, commonly known as the Dailey's bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, spurred petals that come in shades of blue and purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets.

It blooms in early spring, producing delicate flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
