Asteriscus imbricatus

Asteriscus imbricatus, commonly known as the Mexican aster or mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, fragrant flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Asteriscus imbricatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and drought tolerance.
