Azalea glauca var. spathulata

Azalea glauca var. spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf azalea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a powdery blue-gray waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red during the spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
