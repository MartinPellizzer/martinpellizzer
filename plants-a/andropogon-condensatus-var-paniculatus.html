Andropogon condensatus var. paniculatus

Andropogon condensatus var. paniculatus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies, wetlands, and disturbed areas.

Switchgrass is also valued for its ecological benefits, including erosion control and as a potential bioenergy crop.
