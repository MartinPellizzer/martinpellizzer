Astragalus mauritanicus

Astragalus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritanian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus mauritanicus is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
