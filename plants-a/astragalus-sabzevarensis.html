Astragalus sabzevarensis

Astragalus sabzevarensis, commonly known as the Sabzevare milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seed pods are typically hairy and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.
