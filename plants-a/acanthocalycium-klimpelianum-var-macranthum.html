Acanthocalycium klimpelianum var. macranthum

Acanthocalycium klimpelianum var. macranthum, commonly known as the Macranthum Cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green and sparsely covered with white, radial spines. The flowers are large, showy, and usually pink or red, blooming in the spring. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in arid gardens.

It requires well-draining soil and full sun to thrive.
