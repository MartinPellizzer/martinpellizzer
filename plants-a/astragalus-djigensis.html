Astragalus djigensis

Astragalus djigensis, commonly known as the Dijig Alkali Milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to survive in nutrient-poor and drought-prone habitats.
