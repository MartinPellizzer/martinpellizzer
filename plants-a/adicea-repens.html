Adicea repens

Adicea repens, commonly known as creeping adicea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its spreading habit and hairy stems. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or violet, arranged in dense clusters.

Adicea repens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
