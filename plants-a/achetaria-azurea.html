Achetaria azurea

Achetaria azurea, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue petals, which range from a soft sky blue to a deep cobalt hue. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex and intricate floral structure. Its flowers typically bloom during the wet season, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Achetaria azurea is highly sought after in horticulture and conservation efforts.
