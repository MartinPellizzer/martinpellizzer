Anacamptis pyramidalis f. platycheila

Anacamptis pyramidalis f. platycheila, commonly known as the broad-leafed pyramidal orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. This orchid thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its unique floral structure and coloration make it a distinctive and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
