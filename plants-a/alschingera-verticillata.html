Alschingera verticillata

Alschingera verticillata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Alschingera verticillata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
