Astragalus piranshahricus

Astragalus piranshahricus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers clustered in racemes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Astragalus piranshahricus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and as a potential source of medicinal compounds.
