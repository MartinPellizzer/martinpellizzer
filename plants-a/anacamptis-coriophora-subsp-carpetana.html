Anacamptis coriophora subsp. carpetana

Anacamptis coriophora subsp.

carpetana, commonly known as the carpet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant produces a spike of small, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its compact growth habit.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in the Mediterranean region.
