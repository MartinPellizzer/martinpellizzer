Alsinanthe biflora

Alsinanthe biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered alsinane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Alsinanthe biflora is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
