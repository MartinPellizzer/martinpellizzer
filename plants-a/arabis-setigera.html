Arabis setigera

Arabis setigera, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and weeds. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its basal leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
