Acacia hierochoensis

Acacia hierochoensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian acacia, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stem and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the genus Acacia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-adapted to drought conditions.

The species is of interest for its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
