Andrographis tenuiflora

Andrographis tenuiflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved andrographis, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Andrographis tenuiflora is valued for its potential medicinal properties, particularly in supporting immune function.
