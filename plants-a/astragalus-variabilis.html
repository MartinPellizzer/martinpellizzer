Astragalus variabilis

Astragalus variabilis, commonly known as variable milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils.

Astragalus variabilis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
