Aulomyrcia aethusa

Aulomyrcia aethusa, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe, it has been naturalized in various parts of the world. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its prickly foliage serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
