Andromeda myrtifolia

Andromeda myrtifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf andromeda, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils along streambanks and woodland edges. The plant features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches, and bloom in early spring.

Andromeda myrtifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
