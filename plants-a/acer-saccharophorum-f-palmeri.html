Acer saccharophorum f. palmeri

Acer saccharophorum f. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer maple, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the sugar maple.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring leaves with a striking red or orange hue during the fall. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with a compact, rounded form. Its leaves are opposite, palmately lobed, and often have a serrated margin.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant seasonal color.
