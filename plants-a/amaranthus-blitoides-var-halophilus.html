Amaranthus blitoides var. halophilus

Amaranthus blitoides var. halophilus, commonly known as the saltbush amaranth, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It features upright, branched stems covered in small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, greenish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It is native to arid and coastal regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Its ability to thrive in high-salt conditions makes it an important species for ecological restoration and agricultural use in saline soils.
