Asimina × bethanyensis

Asimina × bethanyensis, commonly known as the Bethany wild cherry, is a hybrid plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, cherry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland habitats.
