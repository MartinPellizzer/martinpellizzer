Allium julianum

Allium julianum, commonly known as Julian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has bulbous roots and strap-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild garlic-like scent.

Allium julianum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.
