Achyrophorus araucanus

Achyrophorus araucanus, commonly known as the Araucaria tree, is a coniferous evergreen native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and distinctive crown of clustered, feather-like leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived species. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The Araucaria tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
