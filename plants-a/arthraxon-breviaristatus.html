Arthraxon breviaristatus

Arthraxon breviaristatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-green coloration. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
