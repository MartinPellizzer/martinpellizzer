Acacia spinosissima

Acacia spinosissima, commonly known as the spinous acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, thorn-like structures that provide defense against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are small, bipinnate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Acacia spinosissima is well adapted to drought and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
