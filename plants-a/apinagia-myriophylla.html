Apinagia myriophylla

Apinagia myriophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
