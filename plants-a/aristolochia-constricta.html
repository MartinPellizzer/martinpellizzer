Aristolochia constricta

Aristolochia constricta, commonly known as Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-brown with a prominent, hooded structure. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Aristolochia constricta is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
