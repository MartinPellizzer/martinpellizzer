Asparagus filicladus

Asparagus filicladus, commonly known as the fern-like asparagus, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces edible, needle-like spears that emerge from the ground in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible qualities, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional cuisine.
