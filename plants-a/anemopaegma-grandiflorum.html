Anemopaegma grandiflorum

Anemopaegma grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered anemopaegma, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to South Africa and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, branched stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silky texture. Its showy, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
