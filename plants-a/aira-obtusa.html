Aira obtusa

Aira obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf grass or flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually flat and slightly rough to the touch. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

Aira obtusa plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
