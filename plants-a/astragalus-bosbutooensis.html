Astragalus bosbutooensis

Astragalus bosbutooensis, commonly known as the Bosbutoo milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments. Its leaves are usually compound, with small leaflets that help reduce water loss, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus bosbutooensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
