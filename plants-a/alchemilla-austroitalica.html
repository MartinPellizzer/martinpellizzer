Alchemilla austroitalica

Alchemilla austroitalica, commonly known as the Southern Italian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their mild astringent properties.

Alchemilla austroitalica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
