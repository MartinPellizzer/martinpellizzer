Atylus fucifolia

Atylus fucifolia, commonly known as the sea fennel, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often growing in tidal zones and rocky shores. Its root system helps stabilize coastal dunes, making it an important species for coastal ecology.

Atylus fucifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive ailments.
