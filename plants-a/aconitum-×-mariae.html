Aconitum × mariae

Aconitum × mariae, commonly known as the Marie's monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a coarse texture. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
