Aeschynanthus amboinensis

Aeschynanthus amboinensis, commonly known as the "Lipstick Plant," is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a trailing evergreen shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its vibrant floral display.

Aeschynanthus amboinensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in hanging baskets and as a ground cover in tropical gardens.
