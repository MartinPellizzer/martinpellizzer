Acacia latispinosa

Acacia latispinosa, commonly known as the broad-spined wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Acacia latispinosa plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
