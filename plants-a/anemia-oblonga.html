Anemia oblonga

Anemia oblonga, commonly known as the longleaf anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of broad, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Anemia oblonga is often found in woodland edges and moist meadows, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
