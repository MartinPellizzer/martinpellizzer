Ambora ficus

Ambora ficus, commonly known as the common fig tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Middle East and Southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and its ability to produce edible figs within a hollow syconium. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in the ground and absorb nutrients efficiently.

Ambora ficus plays an important role in both ecological and agricultural systems, providing food for various animals and humans alike.
