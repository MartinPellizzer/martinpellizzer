Amaranthus minutus

Amaranthus minutus, commonly known as dwarf amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, making it a compact and hardy species. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Amaranthus minutus is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
