Aspilia jariensis

Aspilia jariensis, commonly known as the Jari spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in a dense, spherical cluster that resembles a spider's web, giving it its common name.

Aspilia jariensis is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
