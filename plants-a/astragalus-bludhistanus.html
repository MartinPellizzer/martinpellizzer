Astragalus bludhistanus

Astragalus bludhistanus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seed pods contain edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife species.
