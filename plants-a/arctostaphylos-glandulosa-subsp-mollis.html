Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp. mollis

Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with soft, glandular hairs on the leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically in late spring. Its bark is reddish-brown and peels in thin layers, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
