Achillea maritima subsp. atlantica

Achillea maritima subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as Atlantic yarrow, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and flat, white to pale yellow flower heads.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. It is drought-tolerant and well adapted to harsh maritime environments.

Its flowering period typically occurs from late spring to early autumn.
