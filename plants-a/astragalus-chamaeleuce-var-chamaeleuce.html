Astragalus chamaeleuce var. chamaeleuce

Astragalus chamaeleuce var. chamaeleuce, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
