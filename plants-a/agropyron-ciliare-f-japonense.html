Agropyron ciliare f. japonense

Agropyron ciliare f. japonense, commonly known as Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
