Aristida aequiramea

Aristida aequiramea, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typically arranged in a panicle-like structure. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

Aristida aequiramea is a drought-tolerant species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
