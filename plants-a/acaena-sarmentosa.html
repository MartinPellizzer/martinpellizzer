Acaena sarmentosa

Acaena sarmentosa, commonly known as the trailing acaena, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its creeping growth habit with numerous stolons. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a dry, hairy achene, which is adapted for wind dispersal.

Acaena sarmentosa is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
