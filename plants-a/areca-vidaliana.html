Areca vidaliana

Areca vidaliana, commonly known as the Vidal's areca, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its fruits are drupes, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Areca vidaliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
