Acacia maitlandii

Acacia maitlandii, commonly known as the maitland acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical heads, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia maitlandii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
