Adelobotrys praetextus

Adelobotrys praetextus, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green leaves with prominent red veins and its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are borne on branched inflorescences and bloom during the rainy season.

Adelobotrys praetextus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
