Astragalus glaux var. purpureus

Astragalus glaux var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
