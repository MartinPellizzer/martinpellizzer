Arctostaphylos canescens var. candidissima

Arctostaphylos canescens var. candidissima, commonly known as the white-flowered manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-barked stems and clusters of white, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are small, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round foliage. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
