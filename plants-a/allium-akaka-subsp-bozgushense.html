Allium akaka subsp. bozgushense

Allium akaka subsp.

bozgushense, commonly known as the Bozgush onion, is a rare and endemic species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its bulbous base and slender, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
