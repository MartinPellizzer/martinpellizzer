Andropogon roxburghii

Andropogon roxburghii, commonly known as dwarf grass or red grass, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Andropogon roxburghii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
