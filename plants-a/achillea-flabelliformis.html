Achillea flabelliformis

Achillea flabelliformis, commonly known as the fern-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Achillea flabelliformis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
