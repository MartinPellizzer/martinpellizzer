Astragalus saltonis

Astragalus saltonis, commonly known as the Salton Sea milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in desert environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife, though it is not widely cultivated for agricultural purposes.
