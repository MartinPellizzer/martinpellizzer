Alstroemeria damaziana

Alstroemeria damaziana, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas and has strap-like, narrow leaves.

Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.
