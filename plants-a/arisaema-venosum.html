Arisaema venosum

Arisaema venosum, commonly known as the veined arum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, often colorful bracts that attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a distinctive veining pattern.

Arisaema venosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
