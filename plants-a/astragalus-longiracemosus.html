Astragalus longiracemosus

Astragalus longiracemosus, commonly known as the long-branch milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its long, branching stems and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting surrounding plant life.
