Acacia tomentosa

Acacia tomentosa, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin, papery layers.

Acacia tomentosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
