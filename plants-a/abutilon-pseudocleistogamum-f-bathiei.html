Abutilon pseudocleistogamum f. bathiei

Abutilon pseudocleistogamum f. bathiei, commonly known as the Bathie's velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, ovate leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
