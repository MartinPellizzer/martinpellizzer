Acacia castanostegia

Acacia castanostegia, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are typically reddish in color, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
