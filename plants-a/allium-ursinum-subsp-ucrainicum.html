Allium ursinum subsp. ucrainicum

Allium ursinum subsp.

ucrainicum, commonly known as the Caucasian garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. It has a strong garlic odor due to the presence of sulfur-containing compounds.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
