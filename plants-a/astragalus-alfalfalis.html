Astragalus alfalfalis

Astragalus alfalfalis, commonly known as alfalfa milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Astragalus alfalfalis plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
