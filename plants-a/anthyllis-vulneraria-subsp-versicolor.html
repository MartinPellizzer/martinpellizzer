Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. versicolor

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the variable woundwort, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in racemes. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky, or disturbed soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its variable flower color and leaf morphology, contributing to its adaptability in diverse habitats.
