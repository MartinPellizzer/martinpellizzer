Adiantum reniforme var. sinense

Adiantum reniforme var. sinense, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern native to eastern Asia.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green and slightly curled. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
