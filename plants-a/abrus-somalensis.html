Abrus somalensis

Abrus somalensis, commonly known as the Somali scarlet bean, is a climbing legume native to the Horn of Africa.

It produces striking red and black seed pods that contain seeds with a distinctive orange-red aril. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in shrublands and rocky areas.

The seeds have been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a dye and in folk medicine.
