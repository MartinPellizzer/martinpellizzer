Anthemis tenella

Anthemis tenella, commonly known as narrowleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often grown for its ornamental value and long-lasting flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
