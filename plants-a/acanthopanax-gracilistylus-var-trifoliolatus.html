Acanthopanax gracilistylus var. trifoliolatus

Acanthopanax gracilistylus var. trifoliolatus, commonly known as the three-leafed Chinese angel's trumpet, is a climbing shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in traditional Chinese medicine and horticulture.
