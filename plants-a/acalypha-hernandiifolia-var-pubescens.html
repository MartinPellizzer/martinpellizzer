Acalypha hernandiifolia var. pubescens

Acalypha hernandiifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that give it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its growth habit is typically upright, with branching that can become somewhat woody over time.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
