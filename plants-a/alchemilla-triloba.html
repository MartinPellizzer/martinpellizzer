Alchemilla triloba

Alchemilla triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
