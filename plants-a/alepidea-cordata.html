Alepidea cordata

Alepidea cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy or rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Alepidea cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and aesthetic features.
