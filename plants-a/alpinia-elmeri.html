Alpinia elmeri

Alpinia elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a rhizomatous herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of pale yellow to white flowers, which are often fragrant.

Alpinia elmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
