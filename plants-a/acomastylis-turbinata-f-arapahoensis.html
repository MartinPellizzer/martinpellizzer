Acomastylis turbinata f. arapahoensis

Acomastylis turbinata f. arapahoensis, commonly known as the Arapaho sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Great Plains of North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
