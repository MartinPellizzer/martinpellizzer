Allium stupposum

Allium stupposum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium stupposum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
