Acer × boscii

Acer × boscii, commonly known as the Weeping Maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree that combines traits from several maple species.

It is characterized by its cascading, weeping habit, with long, drooping branches that create a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a vibrant green color that turns yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
