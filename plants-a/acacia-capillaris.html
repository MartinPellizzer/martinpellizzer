Acacia capillaris

Acacia capillaris, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flower spikes that bloom in the summer months. The plant has small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia capillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
