Amaryllis reticulata

Amaryllis reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a distinctive net-like veining pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is popular for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
