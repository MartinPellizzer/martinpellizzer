Astragalus melilotoides

Astragalus melilotoides, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

Astragalus melilotoides is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
