Amsinckia simplex

Amsinckia simplex, commonly known as the desert smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-green foliage with hairy surfaces, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Amsinckia simplex is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
