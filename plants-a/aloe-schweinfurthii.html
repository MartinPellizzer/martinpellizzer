Aloe schweinfurthii

Aloe schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Schwefelkraut or the "Fire Plant," is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a layer of white powder called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Aloe schweinfurthii is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
