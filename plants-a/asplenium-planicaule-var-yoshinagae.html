Asplenium planicaule var. yoshinagae

Asplenium planicaule var. yoshinagae, commonly known as the Yoshinaga's maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is a small, delicate fern characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is appreciated in horticulture for its elegant appearance.
