Anabasis pulcherrima

Anabasis pulcherrima, commonly known as the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiny leaves. Its leaves are typically small, narrow, and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Anabasis pulcherrima is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
