Acaena sericea var. latifoliolata

Acaena sericea var. latifoliolata, commonly known as the broad-leafed burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are broad and serrated, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. This species thrives in coastal and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
