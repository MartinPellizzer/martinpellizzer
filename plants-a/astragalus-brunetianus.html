Astragalus brunetianus

Astragalus brunetianus, commonly known as the Brunet's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Altai Mountains. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, sprawling growth habit. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small seeds.

Astragalus brunetianus is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
