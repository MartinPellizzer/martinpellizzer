Aster wootonii

Aster wootonii, commonly known as the Wooton's aster, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the fall. Its flowering period occurs from September to November, making it a late-blooming species.

Aster wootonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
