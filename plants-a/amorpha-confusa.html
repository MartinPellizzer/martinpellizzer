Amorpha confusa

Amorpha confusa, commonly known as the yellow indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.
