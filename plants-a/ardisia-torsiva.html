Ardisia torsiva

Ardisia torsiva, commonly known as the twisted ardisia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers. Following flowering, it develops bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
