Adansonia kilima

Adansonia kilima, commonly known as the Kilima baobab, is a distinctive species of the Adansonia genus native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its massive, bottle-shaped trunk and a crown of long, slender branches. The tree has thick, fibrous bark that provides protection against fire and drought. Its leaves are palmate, with multiple leaflets, and it produces large, yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

The fruit is a woody capsule containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind.
