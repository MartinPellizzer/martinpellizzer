Alnus incana var. microconus

Alnus incana var. microconus, commonly known as the dwarf alder, is a shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clustered, cone-like fruits. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable component of riparian ecosystems.
