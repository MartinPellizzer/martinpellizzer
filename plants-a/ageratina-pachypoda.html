Ageratina pachypoda

Ageratina pachypoda, commonly known as the thick-stemmed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant features hairy, cylindrical stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its showy blue or purple flower heads attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ageratina pachypoda is often found in moist, shaded environments like forests and along stream banks.
