Anomalanthus parviflorus

Anomalanthus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered anomalous plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

Due to its unique floral structure and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
