Andropogon hispidissimus

Andropogon hispidissimus, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Andropogon hispidissimus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
