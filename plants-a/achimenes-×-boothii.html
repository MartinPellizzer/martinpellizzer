Achimenes × boothii

Achimenes × boothii, commonly known as the hybrid begonia, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces tubular bracts that enclose the actual flowers. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Its hybrid nature results from crosses between Achimenes species, contributing to its diverse floral characteristics.
