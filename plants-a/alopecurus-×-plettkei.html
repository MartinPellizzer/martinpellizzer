Alopecurus × plettkei

Alopecurus × plettkei, commonly known as the meadow grass or velvet grass, is a hybrid species within the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Alopecurus × plettkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
