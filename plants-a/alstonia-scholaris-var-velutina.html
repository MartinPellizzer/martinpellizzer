Alstonia scholaris var. velutina

Alstonia scholaris var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a variant of the Indian devil tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety bark that gives it its common name. The plant produces striking clusters of white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and a serrated margin.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
