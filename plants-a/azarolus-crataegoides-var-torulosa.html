Azarolus crataegoides var. torulosa

Azarolus crataegoides var. torulosa, commonly known as the wrinkled hawthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, thorned stems and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated edge and a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
