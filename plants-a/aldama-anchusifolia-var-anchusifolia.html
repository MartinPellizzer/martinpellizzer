Aldama anchusifolia var. anchusifolia

Aldama anchusifolia var. anchusifolia, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and botanical collections.
