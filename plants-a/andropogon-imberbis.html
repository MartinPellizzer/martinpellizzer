Andropogon imberbis

Andropogon imberbis, commonly known as hairless bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Andropogon imberbis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
