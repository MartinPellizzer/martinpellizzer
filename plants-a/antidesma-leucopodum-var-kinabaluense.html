Antidesma leucopodum var. kinabaluense

Antidesma leucopodum var. kinabaluense, commonly known as the Kinabalu antidesma, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

This variant is notable for its ecological significance in its native habitat, supporting local biodiversity.
