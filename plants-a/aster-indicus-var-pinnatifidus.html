Aster indicus var. pinnatifidus

Aster indicus var. pinnatifidus, commonly known as the Indian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
