Ammobium alatum

Ammobium alatum, commonly known as the winged sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its unique, wing-like structures on the stems are adaptations to reduce water loss and enhance wind dispersal of seeds.

Ammobium alatum is well-suited to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
