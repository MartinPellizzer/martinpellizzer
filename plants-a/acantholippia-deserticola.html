Acantholippia deserticola

Acantholippia deserticola, commonly known as the desert lippia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, narrow leaves, and small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low precipitation. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper underground, making it resilient in harsh desert environments.

Acantholippia deserticola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
