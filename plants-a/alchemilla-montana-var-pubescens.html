Alchemilla montana var. pubescens

Alchemilla montana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, broad leaves that are often covered with fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
