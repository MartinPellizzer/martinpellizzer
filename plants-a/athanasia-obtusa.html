Athanasia obtusa

Athanasia obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Athanasia obtusa is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and delicate appearance.
