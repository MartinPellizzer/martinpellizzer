Adiantum crespiana

Adiantum crespiana, commonly known as the creeping maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its rhizomes are creeping, allowing it to spread slowly and form dense mats.

Adiantum crespiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
