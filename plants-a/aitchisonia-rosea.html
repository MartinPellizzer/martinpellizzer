Aitchisonia rosea

Aitchisonia rosea, commonly known as the red-flowered lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Aitchisonia rosea thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after addition to rock gardens and alpine collections.
