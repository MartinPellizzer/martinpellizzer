Ameroglossum xukuruorum

Ameroglossum xukuruorum, commonly known as the Xukuru ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Brazilian Atlantic Forest.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance with translucent, waxy leaves and delicate, pale pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of dense forests. Its specialized epiphytic growth habit allows it to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
