Alysicarpus monilifer var. mahbubnagarensis

Alysicarpus monilifer var. mahbubnagarensis, commonly known as Mahbubnagar sowing grass, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and wasteland habitats.

This variant is notable for its use in forage and soil stabilization in arid regions.
