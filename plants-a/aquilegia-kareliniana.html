Aquilegia kareliniana

Aquilegia kareliniana, commonly known as Karelin's columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves, and it produces attractive seed pods that mature into bright red or orange hues.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
