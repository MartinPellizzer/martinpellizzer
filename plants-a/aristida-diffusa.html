Aristida diffusa

Aristida diffusa, commonly known as hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often seen in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
