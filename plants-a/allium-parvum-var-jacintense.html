Allium parvum var. jacintense

Allium parvum var. jacintense, commonly known as the purple onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor compared to other onions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flower color and compact growth habit.
