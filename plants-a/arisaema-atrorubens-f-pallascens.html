Arisaema atrorubens f. pallascens

Arisaema atrorubens f. pallascens, commonly known as the red-blotched arisaema, is a variant of the arisaema plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded spathe that is typically reddish with dark markings. The plant produces a spadix that bears small, yellowish flowers, which are clustered in a dense spike. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

This ornamental plant is appreciated for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
