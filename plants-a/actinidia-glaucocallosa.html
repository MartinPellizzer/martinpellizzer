Actinidia glaucocallosa

Actinidia glaucocallosa, commonly known as the silver-furred kiwifruit, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have a unique, tangy flavor.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes due to its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
