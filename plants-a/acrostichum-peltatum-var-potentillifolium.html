Acrostichum peltatum var. potentillifolium

Acrostichum peltatum var. potentillifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf holly fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are deeply divided into pinnate segments, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance. The fronds are typically evergreen, with a dark green coloration and a slightly glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to contribute to the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
