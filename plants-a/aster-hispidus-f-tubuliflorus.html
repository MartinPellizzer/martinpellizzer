Aster hispidus f. tubuliflorus

Aster hispidus f. tubuliflorus, commonly known as the hairy aster or roughstemmed aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with tubular florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist to wet habitats, often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.
