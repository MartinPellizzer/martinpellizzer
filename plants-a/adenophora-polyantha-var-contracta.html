Adenophora polyantha var. contracta

Adenophora polyantha var. contracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender stems that often grow in clusters, with lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, blooming in late summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
