Amomum holttumii

Amomum holttumii, commonly known as the Bornean cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clustered, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for commercial use.
