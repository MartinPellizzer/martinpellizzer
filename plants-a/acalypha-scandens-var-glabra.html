Acalypha scandens var. glabra

Acalypha scandens var. glabra, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many areas.
