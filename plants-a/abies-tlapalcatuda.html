Abies tlapalcatuda

Abies tlapalcatuda, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests at elevations between 2,000 and 3,000 meters.

The botanical features of Abies tlapalcatuda make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
