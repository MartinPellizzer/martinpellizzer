Adenophora stricta var. lancifolia

Adenophora stricta var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
