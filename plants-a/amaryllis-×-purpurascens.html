Amaryllis × purpurascens

Amaryllis × purpurascens, commonly known as the red amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis spathacea.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking, large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It produces bulbous roots and is known for its ability to bloom in late summer or early autumn.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and long-lasting nature.
