Ardisia mamillata

Ardisia mamillata, commonly known as the rough-leaved evergreen, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
