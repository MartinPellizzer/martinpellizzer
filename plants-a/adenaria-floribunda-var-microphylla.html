Adenaria floribunda var. microphylla

Adenaria floribunda var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf adenaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
