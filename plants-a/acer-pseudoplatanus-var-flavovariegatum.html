Acer pseudoplatanus var. flavovariegatum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. flavovariegatum, commonly known as the golden variegated maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring a mix of green, yellow, and white patterns on its leaves. This plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically palmate with five to seven lobes, and they turn vibrant colors in the fall.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.
