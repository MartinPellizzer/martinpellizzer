Amyema glaucescens

Amyema glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous amyema, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale blue flowers and smooth, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive glaucous appearance. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Amyema glaucescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
