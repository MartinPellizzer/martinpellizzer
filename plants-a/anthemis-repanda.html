Anthemis repanda

Anthemis repanda, commonly known as the narrowleaf cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, yellowish flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, and it has narrow, linear leaves.

Anthemis repanda is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
