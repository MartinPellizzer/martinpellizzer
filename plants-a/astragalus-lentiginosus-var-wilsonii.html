Astragalus lentiginosus var. wilsonii

Astragalus lentiginosus var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers clustered in racemes. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties of Astragalus.
