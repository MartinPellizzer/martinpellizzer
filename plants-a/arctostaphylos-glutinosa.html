Arctostaphylos glutinosa

Arctostaphylos glutinosa, commonly known as sticky mountain heather, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that often have a sticky coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and woodland edges.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
