Alvaradoa amorphoides var. puberulenta

Alvaradoa amorphoides var. puberulenta, commonly known as the pubescent alvaradoa, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in a fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and dry habitats.
