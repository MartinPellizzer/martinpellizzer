Aleurites moluccanus var. remyi

Aleurites moluccanus var. remyi, commonly known as the Australian nut tree, is a variant of the common breadnut tree native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow-green flowers followed by spherical fruits containing hard-shelled nuts. Its botanical characteristics include a shallow root system and the ability to tolerate a range of soil types.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in horticulture and sustainable forestry.
