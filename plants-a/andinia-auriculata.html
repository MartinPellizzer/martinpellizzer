Andinia auriculata

Andinia auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, ear-shaped petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a complex structure typical of orchids.

Andinia auriculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
