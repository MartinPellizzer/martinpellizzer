Asarum ypsilantennse

Asarum ypsilantennse, commonly known as the western prickly asarum, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features a thick, rhizomatous root system and produces heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant bears small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which are typically hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
