Ananas bracteatus var. sagenarius

Ananas bracteatus var. sagenarius, commonly known as the red pineapple or red guava, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround the inflorescence.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into edible, fleshy berries resembling pineapples in appearance. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves with a waxy surface, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its bold coloration.

The variety is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
