Albizia latisiliqua

Albizia latisiliqua, commonly known as the white siris, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters. Its pods are slender and contain several seeds, and the tree is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types.

Albizia latisiliqua is also valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in soil stabilization.
