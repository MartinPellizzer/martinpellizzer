Acacia sulcata

Acacia sulcata, commonly known as the African screwpod acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ridged, and often spiny stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia sulcata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its leaves are small and feathery, contributing to its overall drought-resistant morphology.
