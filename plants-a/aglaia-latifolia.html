Aglaia latifolia

Aglaia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other economically important hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The bark and leaves contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
