Anil macra

Anil macra, commonly known as Indian spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is known for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron and calcium. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

Anil macra is also valued for its ability to grow in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and versatile crop.
