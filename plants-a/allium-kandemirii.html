Allium kandemirii

Allium kandemirii, commonly known as the Kandemir onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in traditional Turkish cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is gaining popularity among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
