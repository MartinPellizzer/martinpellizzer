Abies concolor subsp. martinezii

Abies concolor subsp.

martinezii, commonly known as the Martinez's white fir, is a subspecies of the white fir native to the western United States. It is characterized by its conical shape, dense foliage, and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The cones of this species are usually pendulous and contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

The plant has moderate growth rates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
