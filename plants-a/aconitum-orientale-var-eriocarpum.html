Aconitum orientale var. eriocarpum

Aconitum orientale var. eriocarpum, commonly known as the silky-fruited monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its fruits are follicles that mature into silky, hairy capsules.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
