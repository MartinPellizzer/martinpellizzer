Arabis alpina var. declinata

Arabis alpina var. declinata, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and its stems are often hairy.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
