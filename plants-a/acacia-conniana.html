Acacia conniana

Acacia conniana, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Acacia conniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
