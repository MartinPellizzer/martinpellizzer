Asparagus esculentus

Asparagus esculentus, commonly known as garden asparagus, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its fibrous, edible stems that are harvested before they fully mature. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with fern-like, compound leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters at the base of the plant.

The botanical features of Asparagus esculentus make it well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates.
