Astracantha atropatana

Astracantha atropatana, commonly known as the black-flowered star-thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and distinctive, dark purple to black flowers. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its harsh environment. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Astracantha atropatana is considered an invasive species in some regions, posing a challenge to local ecosystems.
