Amaranthus hybridus var. bellardii

Amaranthus hybridus var. bellardii, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red-tinged stems, which become more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are small, clustered in dense panicles. This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.
