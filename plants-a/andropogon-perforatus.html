Andropogon perforatus

Andropogon perforatus, commonly known as the punctured grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves with a distinctive punctuated appearance on the leaf surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences bearing tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Andropogon perforatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
