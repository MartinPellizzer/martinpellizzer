Acisanthera inundata var. pusilla

Acisanthera inundata var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf water hibiscus, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, often found near water bodies such as ponds and marshes. It is characterized by its compact size and rapid growth, making it suitable for wetland restoration projects.

Its botanical structure supports a variety of aquatic and terrestrial wildlife, contributing to ecosystem biodiversity.
