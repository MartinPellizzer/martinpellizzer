Amorphophallus mairei

Amorphophallus mairei, commonly known as the Maire's arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a fleshy, underground tuber that serves as its storage organ. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and floral structures.
