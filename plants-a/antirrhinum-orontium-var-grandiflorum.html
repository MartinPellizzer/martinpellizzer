Antirrhinum orontium var. grandiflorum

Antirrhinum orontium var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered antirrhinum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and somewhat woody at the base.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
