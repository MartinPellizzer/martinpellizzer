Aspalathus leucophaea

Aspalathus leucophaea, commonly known as the white-flowered bushveld clover, is a small, perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Aspalathus leucophaea is not widely cultivated but is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
