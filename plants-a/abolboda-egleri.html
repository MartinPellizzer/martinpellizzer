Abolboda egleri

Abolboda egleri, commonly known as the Egeli's lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long blooming period, which typically spans from late spring to early summer.
