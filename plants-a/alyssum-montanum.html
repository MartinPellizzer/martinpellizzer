Alyssum montanum

Alyssum montanum, commonly known as mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Alyssum montanum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in garden settings.
