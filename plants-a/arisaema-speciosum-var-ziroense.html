Arisaema speciosum var. ziroense

Arisaema speciosum var. ziroense, commonly known as the Ziro mountain arisaema, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the Eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high elevations, often in forest understories. Its botanical features include a tuberous rhizome and showy, ornamental flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
