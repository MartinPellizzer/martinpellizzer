Astrocaryum guara

Astrocaryum guara, commonly known as the red palm or guara palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fruit is a large, reddish-brown drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
