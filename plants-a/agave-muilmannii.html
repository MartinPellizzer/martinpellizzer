Agave muilmannii

Agave muilmannii, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, bluish-gray leaves that are lined with sharp spines. The plant produces a tall flower stalk with small, greenish-white flowers, which bloom after several years. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Agave muilmannii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
