Aegilops uniaristata

Aegilops uniaristata, commonly known as the single-awned goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with a single awn on each lemma, giving it its common name. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Aegilops uniaristata is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.

Morphology

Aegilops uniaristata has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a bluish-green color and a smooth texture.

The stem is cylindrical, with a slightly rough bark and grows upright with a bushy appearance. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in spikes, which bloom in early summer and are wind-pollinated. The fruit is a capsule that releases small, dark seeds through mechanical means when the capsule dries and splits open.

Its root system consists of a fibrous network that helps it anchor in sandy or rocky soils.
