Asystasia lawiana

Asystasia lawiana, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, silver-green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Asystasia lawiana thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
