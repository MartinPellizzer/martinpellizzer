It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in rocky, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are protected by a thin, membranous cover.

Aspidium rockii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
