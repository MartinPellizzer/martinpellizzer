Aphelandra dielsii var. ericae

Aphelandra dielsii var. ericae, commonly known as the pink-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive pink veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic foliage.

Aphelandra dielsii var. ericae is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
