Alyssum bidentatum

Alyssum bidentatum, commonly known as the two-toothed alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale yellow to white in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with distinctive two-toothed margins, giving it its scientific name.

Alyssum bidentatum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and border plantings.
