Adesmia tenuicaulis

Adesmia tenuicaulis, commonly known as slender-stemmed adesmia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical bean-like pods and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Adesmia tenuicaulis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
