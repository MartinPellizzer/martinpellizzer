Aletris × tottenii

Aletris × tottenii, commonly known as the hybrid yellow mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Aletris × tottenii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
