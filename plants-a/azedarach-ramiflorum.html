Azedarach ramiflorum

Azedarach ramiflorum, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Azedarach ramiflorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.
