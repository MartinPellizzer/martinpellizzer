Achillea millefolium subsp. elbursensis

Achillea millefolium subsp.

elbursensis, commonly known as the Elburz yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran. It features a basal rosette of finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flower clusters arranged in flat-topped umbels, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in its native region.
