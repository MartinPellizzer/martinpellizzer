Armeria allioides var. exaristata

Armeria allioides var. exaristata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thrift, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
