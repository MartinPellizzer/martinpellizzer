Andrachne apetala

Andrachne apetala, commonly known as the leafless sandmat, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and the absence of true leaves, which are replaced by modified structures called phyllodes. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically pink to red in color.

Andrachne apetala plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and provides habitat for various small desert organisms.
