Abutilon microcarpum

Abutilon microcarpum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits known as capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
