Allium dichlamydeum

Allium dichlamydeum, commonly known as the two-lobed garlic, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale purple flowers with six petals, which are arranged in a two-lobed structure, giving it its scientific name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild garlic-like fragrance.

Allium dichlamydeum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
