Aster minimus

Aster minimus, commonly known as the dwarf aster, is a small flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 inches and features tiny blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Aster minimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
