Astragalus himalensis

Astragalus himalensis, commonly known as Himalayan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.
