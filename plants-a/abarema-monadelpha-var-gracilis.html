Abarema monadelpha var. gracilis

Abarema monadelpha var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with slender, hairy stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in ecological systems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
