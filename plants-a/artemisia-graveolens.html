Artemisia graveolens

Artemisia graveolens, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features silver-gray foliage with finely divided, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, pungent odor. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in branched clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Artemisia graveolens is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
