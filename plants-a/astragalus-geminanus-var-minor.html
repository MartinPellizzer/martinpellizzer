Astragalus geminanus var. minor

Astragalus geminanus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser twinleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in open grasslands and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and conservation status.
