Alnus sitchensis var. typica

Alnus sitchensis var. typica, commonly known as the Sitka alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in catkins. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and river valleys. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.

The wood of this species is lightweight and used for various crafts and construction purposes.
