Agrostophyllum congestum

Agrostophyllum congestum, commonly known as the clustered grass-leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation in tropical ecosystems.
