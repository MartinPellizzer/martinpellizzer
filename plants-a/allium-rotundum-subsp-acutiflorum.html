Allium rotundum subsp. acutiflorum

Allium rotundum subsp.

acutiflorum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or Persian leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in umbels.

The plant has a distinctive garlic-like aroma and is often cultivated for its edible bulbs and leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued both for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
