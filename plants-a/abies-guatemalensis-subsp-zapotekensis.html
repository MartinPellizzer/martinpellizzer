Abies guatemalensis subsp. zapotekensis

Abies guatemalensis subsp.

zapotekensis, commonly known as the Zapotec fir, is a coniferous tree native to the highlands of southern Mexico. It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

Due to habitat loss and overexploitation, this subspecies is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
