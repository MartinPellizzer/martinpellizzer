Abutilon darwinii var. trinerve

Abutilon darwinii var. trinerve, commonly known as the Darwin's abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and is characterized by its distinctive, three-veined leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
