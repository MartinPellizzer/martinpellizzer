Acrostephanus ogowensis

Acrostephanus ogowensis, commonly known as the Ogowensis thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Acrostephanus ogowensis produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
