Aconitum laevigatum

Aconitum laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a waxy, glossy appearance.

Aconitum laevigatum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant to handle or ingest.
