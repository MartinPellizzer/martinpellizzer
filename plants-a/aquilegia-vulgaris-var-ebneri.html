Aquilegia vulgaris var. ebneri

Aquilegia vulgaris var. ebneri, commonly known as the Ebner's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features distinctive spurred petals that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in a spiral pattern around the central reproductive structures. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are deeply lobed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
