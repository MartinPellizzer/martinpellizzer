Aster laevis var. guadalupensis

Aster laevis var. guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupe aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its leaves are generally smooth and lance-shaped, while its flowers are typically blue to purple in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
