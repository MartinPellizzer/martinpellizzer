Anemone nipponica

Anemone nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture, and its stems are hairy.

Anemone nipponica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
