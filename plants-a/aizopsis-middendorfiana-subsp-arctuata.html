Aizopsis middendorfiana subsp. arctuata

Aizopsis middendorfiana subsp.

arctuata, commonly known as the African umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, which helps in water retention.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
