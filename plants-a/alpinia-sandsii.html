Alpinia sandsii

Alpinia sandsii, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia sandsii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
