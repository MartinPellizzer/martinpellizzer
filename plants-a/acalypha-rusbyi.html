Acalypha rusbyi

Acalypha rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, greenish-yellow inflorescences, which are often mistaken for leaves.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage.
