Arundinaria bicorniculata

Arundinaria bicorniculata, commonly known as the two-awned bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface and a fibrous texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
