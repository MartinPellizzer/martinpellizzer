Alangium salviifolium subsp. decapetalum

Alangium salviifolium subsp.

decapetalum, commonly known as the Decapetalum alangium, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stems.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
