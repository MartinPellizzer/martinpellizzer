Ampelopsis lucida

Ampelopsis lucida, commonly known as the shining grapevines, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. Its vigorous growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Ampelopsis lucida is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its adaptability to various soil types.
