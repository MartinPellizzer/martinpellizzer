Aplectrum spicatum var. pallidum

Aplectrum spicatum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale adder's tongue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hooded flower that resembles a snake's head. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tinge, while its flowers are usually pale green or white.
