Amaranthus caudatus subsp. saueri

Amaranthus caudatus subsp.

saueri, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant produces edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop in both culinary and agricultural contexts. Its foliage is also consumed as a leafy green vegetable in various cuisines.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to different growing conditions.
