Anthurium tarapotense

Anthurium tarapotense, commonly known as the Tarapoto anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix inflorescence. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is typically deep green with a metallic sheen, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Anthurium tarapotense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
