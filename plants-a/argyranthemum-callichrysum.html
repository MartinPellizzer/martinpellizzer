Argyranthemum callichrysum

Argyranthemum callichrysum, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow central disk surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets, creating a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural displays.
