Amblygonum orientale var. pilosum

Amblygonum orientale var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy amblygonum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape, and the stems are erect with a woody base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
