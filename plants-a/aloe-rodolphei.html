Aloe rodolphei

Aloe rodolphei, commonly known as the Zulu aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge and have a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Aloe rodolphei is often used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
