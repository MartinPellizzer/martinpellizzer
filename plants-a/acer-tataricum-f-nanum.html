Acer tataricum f. nanum

Acer tataricum f. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a compact shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are typically green in summer and turn vibrant shades of red or yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its seasonal color changes and compact growth habit.
