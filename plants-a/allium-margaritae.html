Allium margaritae

Allium margaritae, commonly known as the pearl onion garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, globe-shaped flowers that resemble pearls, hence its common name. The plant is characterized by its compact, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

Allium margaritae is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in herbal practices.
