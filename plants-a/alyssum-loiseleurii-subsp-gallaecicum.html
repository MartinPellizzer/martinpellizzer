Alyssum loiseleurii subsp. gallaecicum

Alyssum loiseleurii subsp.

gallaecicum, commonly known as the Galician allyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, aiding in moisture retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
