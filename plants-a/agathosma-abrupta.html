Agathosma abrupta

Agathosma abrupta, commonly known as the mountain agathosma, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Agathosma abrupta is also a popular choice for rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
