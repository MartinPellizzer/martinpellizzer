Anemone stolonifera var. quelpaertensis

Anemone stolonifera var. quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Korean anemone, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It is a perennial herb that produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with a central cluster of stamens. The plant has a creeping growth habit, characterized by stolons that root at the nodes to form new plants. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
