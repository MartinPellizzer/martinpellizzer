Astragalus contortuplicatus

Astragalus contortuplicatus, commonly known as the twistedpod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces distinctive, twisted seed pods that contain small, round seeds.

Astragalus contortuplicatus is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
