Aralia capitulata

Aralia capitulata, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like arrangement of compound leaves that can grow up to several feet in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters at the leaf axils, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

Aralia capitulata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
