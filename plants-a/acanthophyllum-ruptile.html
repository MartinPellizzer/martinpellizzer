Acanthophyllum ruptile

Acanthophyllum ruptile, commonly known as the brittle acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny, succulent stems and small, often inconspicuous flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through features such as thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater in dry soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert fauna.
