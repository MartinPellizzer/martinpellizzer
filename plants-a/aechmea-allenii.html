Aechmea allenii

Aechmea allenii, commonly known as the Allen's pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The plant produces an inflorescence with vibrant orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Aechmea allenii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea allenii has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, thick stem that is often hidden beneath the leaves, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of vibrant red flowers, which bloom in the late spring to early summer, and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds through wind dispersal.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding air and soil.
