Ageratina longipetiolata

Ageratina longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with long petioles, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of both ray and disk flowers, typically in shades of white or pale blue.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
