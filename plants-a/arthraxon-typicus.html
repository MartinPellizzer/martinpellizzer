Arthraxon typicus

Arthraxon typicus, commonly known as the grassy water plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are adapted for aquatic conditions, enabling it to thrive in shallow water or moist soil.

Arthraxon typicus plays a role in stabilizing water bodies and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
