Aiphanes hirsuta subsp. hirsuta

Aiphanes hirsuta subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy papyrus, is a member of the Cyperaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly in regions with wetlands or marshy environments.
