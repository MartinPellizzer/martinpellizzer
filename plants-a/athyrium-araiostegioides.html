Athyrium araiostegioides

Athyrium araiostegioides, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the feathers of a bird, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
