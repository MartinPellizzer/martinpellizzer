Astragalus megacarpus var. parryi

Astragalus megacarpus var. parryi, commonly known as Parry's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, oblong seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.
