Asplenium tenuissimum

Asplenium tenuissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered spore cases called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

Asplenium tenuissimum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
