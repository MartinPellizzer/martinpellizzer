Amaranthus ascendens var. polygonoides

Amaranthus ascendens var. polygonoides, commonly known as the redstem panicum, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender, branched stems that often turn reddish in color as the plant matures. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of green to red bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed areas.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in some traditional cuisines.
