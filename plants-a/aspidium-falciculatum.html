Aspidium falciculatum

Aspidium falciculatum, commonly known as the sickle fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori, a common feature among ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

Aspidium falciculatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
