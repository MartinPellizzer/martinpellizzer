Acalypha hederacea var. oligodonta

Acalypha hederacea var. oligodonta, commonly known as the creeping nettle or velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
