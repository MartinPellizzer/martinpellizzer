Astragalus tegetarioides var. tegetarioides

Astragalus tegetarioides var. tegetarioides, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
