Aristida stipoides var. brachyathera

Aristida stipoides var. brachyathera, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of bract-like structures. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive, fine texture.

This variety is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
