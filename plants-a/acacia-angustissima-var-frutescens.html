Acacia angustissima var. frutescens

Acacia angustissima var. frutescens, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. The plant has slender, often branched stems and narrow, feather-like leaves that reduce water loss. It is well adapted to dry climates and can thrive in poor soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in ecological restoration projects.
