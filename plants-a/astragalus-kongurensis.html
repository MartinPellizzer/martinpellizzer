Astragalus kongurensis

Astragalus kongurensis, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in mountainous areas with poor soil conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Astragalus kongurensis is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
