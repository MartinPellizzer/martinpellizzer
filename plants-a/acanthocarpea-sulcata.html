Acanthocarpea sulcata

Acanthocarpea sulcata, commonly known as the serrated acanthocarpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in supporting insect biodiversity.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
