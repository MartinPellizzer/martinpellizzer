Arabis latialata

Arabis latialata, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant features basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and have four petals, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Arabis latialata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging, high-altitude habitats.
