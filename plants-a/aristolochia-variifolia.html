Aristolochia variifolia

Aristolochia variifolia, commonly known as the variable horned vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, often unpleasant odor. These flowers are notable for their complex structure and are adapted to attract specific pollinators.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
