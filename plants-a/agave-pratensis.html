Agave pratensis

Agave pratensis, commonly known as the meadow agave, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a succulent perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white or pale yellow flowers.

Agave pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in drought conditions.
