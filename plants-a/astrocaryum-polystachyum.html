Astrocaryum polystachyum

Astrocaryum polystachyum, commonly known as the yellow palm or Brazilian yellow palm, is a species of palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces large, yellow fruits that contain a single seed, which is used in the production of palm oil. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for conservation and horticultural purposes.
