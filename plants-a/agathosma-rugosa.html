Agathosma rugosa

Agathosma rugosa, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Agathosma rugosa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
