Acioa unwinii

Acioa unwinii, commonly known as the Unwin's acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by cylindrical, woody pods containing seeds.

Acioa unwinii is adapted to survive in dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
