Asparagus hortensis

Asparagus hortensis, commonly known as garden asparagus, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its spear-shaped, edible shoots that emerge from a fibrous root system. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with feathery, grass-like foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The botanical features of Asparagus hortensis make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
