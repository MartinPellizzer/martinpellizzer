Atylus polycephalus

Atylus polycephalus, commonly known as the many-headed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Atylus polycephalus is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecosystems where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects.
