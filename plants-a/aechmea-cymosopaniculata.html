Aechmea cymosopaniculata

Aechmea cymosopaniculata, commonly known as the panicle-bush bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central rosette, which collects rainwater. The plant produces an inflorescence of bright red bracts that enclose small, white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique structure and vibrant appearance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.

Morphology

Aechmea cymosopaniculata has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, stiff, and covered in a dense layer of white to grayish hairs, giving them a velvety appearance.

The leaves are arranged in a spiral around the central rosette, with a waxy green color and can reach lengths of up to 30 cm. The plant has a short, unbranched stem that is covered in a thick, fibrous bark, and it grows primarily from the base. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, branched spike of bright red flowers that bloom during the summer months, and it is pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that disperses its seeds through wind and animals.
