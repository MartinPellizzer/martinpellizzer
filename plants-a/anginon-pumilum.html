Anginon pumilum

Anginon pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf anginon, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and swampy areas. The plant features delicate, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Anginon pumilum is often found in association with other wetland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
