Albizia edwallii

Albizia edwallii, commonly known as the Edwall's silk tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants. The tree is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fluffy flower clusters that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Albizia edwallii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in soil enrichment.
