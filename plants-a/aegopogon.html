Aegopogon

Aegopogon, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Aegopogon crinitus, which is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant is characterized by its tufted inflorescence, which resembles a beard, and its slender, grass-like leaves. Aegopogon species are often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

They are typically perennial and have a fibrous root system that helps them thrive in poor soil conditions.
