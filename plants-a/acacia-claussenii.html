Acacia claussenii

Acacia claussenii, commonly known as the Cape York acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and adapted growth forms.

Acacia claussenii is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to local biodiversity and ecological stability.
