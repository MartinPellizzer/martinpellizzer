Acanthopanax trifoliatus var. setosus

Acanthopanax trifoliatus var. setosus, commonly known as the hairy devil's ivy, is a climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically three-lobed and have a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
