Astragalus subverticillatus

Astragalus subverticillatus, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Astragalus subverticillatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
