Arundinaria densiflora

Arundinaria densiflora, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or dense-flower bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Arundinaria densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering structure.
