Adenophorus montanus

Adenophorus montanus, commonly known as the mountain adenophorus, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Adenophorus montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
