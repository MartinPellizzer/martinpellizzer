Astragalus lentiginosus var. caesariatus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. caesariatus, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blue or purple flower color.
