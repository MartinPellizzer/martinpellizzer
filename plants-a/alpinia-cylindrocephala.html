Alpinia cylindrocephala

Alpinia cylindrocephala, commonly known as the cylindrical-headed ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.

Alpinia cylindrocephala is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
