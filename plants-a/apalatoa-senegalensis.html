Apalatoa senegalensis

Apalatoa senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese tamarind, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange fruit pods containing edible seeds. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or savanna regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing before the leaves.

The tree is valued for its timber, fruit, and traditional medicinal uses.
