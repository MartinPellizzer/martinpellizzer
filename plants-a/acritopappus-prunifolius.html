Acritopappus prunifolius

Acritopappus prunifolius, commonly known as the roseleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant has adapted to arid environments with thick, fleshy stems that store water and reduce transpiration. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it produces small, edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife species.

The plant's structure and growth habits reflect its evolutionary adaptations to survive in harsh desert conditions.
