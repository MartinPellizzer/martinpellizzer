Albuca anisocrispa

Albuca anisocrispa, commonly known as the variable albuca, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Albuca anisocrispa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
