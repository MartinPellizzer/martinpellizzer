Achasma harmandii

Achasma harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features spiny, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in a dense, globular inflorescence.

Achasma harmandii is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
