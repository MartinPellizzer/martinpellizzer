Astragalus junciformis

Astragalus junciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Astragalus junciformis plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
