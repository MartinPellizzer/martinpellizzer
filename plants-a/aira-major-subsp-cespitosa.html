Aira major subsp. cespitosa

Aira major subsp.

cespitosa, commonly known as tufted false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges.

The plant forms dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in wetland ecosystems.

The subspecies is characterized by its tufted growth habit and is valued for its ornamental and ecological roles in wetland restoration.
