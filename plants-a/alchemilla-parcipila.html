Alchemilla parcipila

Alchemilla parcipila, commonly known as the slender lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-green, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla parcipila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
