Agoseris glauca var. pumila

Agoseris glauca var. pumila, commonly known as the pale blue globe flower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its globular, blue to purple flower heads that resemble small globes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is often found in disturbed soils.
