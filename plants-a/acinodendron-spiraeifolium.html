Acinodendron spiraeifolium

Acinodendron spiraeifolium, commonly known as the spiral-leaved acinodendron, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves along the stems, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
