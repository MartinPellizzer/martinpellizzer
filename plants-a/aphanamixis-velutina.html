Aphanamixis velutina

Aphanamixis velutina, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

Aphanamixis velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
