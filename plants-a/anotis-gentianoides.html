Anotis gentianoides

Anotis gentianoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often branched and hairy.

Anotis gentianoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
