Achimenes urticifolia

Achimenes urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle achimenes, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that can cause a stinging sensation upon contact, similar to stinging nettle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit includes upright stems and is often used in garden displays for its vibrant floral display.
