Astragalus tyttocarpus

Astragalus tyttocarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus tyttocarpus is often found in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
