Argyreia hieronymi

Argyreia hieronymi, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Java, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires ample sunlight and well-draining soil. Its flowers are often used in traditional ceremonies and have cultural significance in various regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
