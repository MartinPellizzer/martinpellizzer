Astragalus rafaelensis

Astragalus rafaelensis, commonly known as Rafael's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and dry grasslands. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain minute seeds.

Astragalus rafaelensis is often found in disturbed or open habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
