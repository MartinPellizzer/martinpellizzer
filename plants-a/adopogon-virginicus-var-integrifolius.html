Adopogon virginicus var. integrifolius

Adopogon virginicus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the inland sea oats, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, wiry stems that grow up to 1.5 meters tall, often forming dense clumps. The leaves are linear and slightly rolled, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue. Its flowering spikes are usually terminal, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This variety is adapted to sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dunes or open woodlands.
