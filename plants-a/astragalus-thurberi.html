Astragalus thurberi

Astragalus thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local insect populations.

Astragalus thurberi is also valued for its ecological significance in native plant communities.
