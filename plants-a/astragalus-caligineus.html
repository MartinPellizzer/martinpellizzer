Astragalus caligineus

Astragalus caligineus, commonly known as smoky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers clustered in spikes, and its leaves are compound with numerous small leaflets. Astragalus caligineus is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
