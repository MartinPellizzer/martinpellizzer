Aristida adscensionis var. aethiopica

Aristida adscensionis var. aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian dropseed, is a grass species native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in open grasslands and on rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
