Allium undulatipetalum

Allium undulatipetalum, commonly known as the wavy-petaled onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers with undulating petals. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild onion flavor, making it a potential candidate for culinary use.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
