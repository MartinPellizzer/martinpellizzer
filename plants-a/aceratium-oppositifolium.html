Aceratium oppositifolium

Aceratium oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leafed aceratium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.
