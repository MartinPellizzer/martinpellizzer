Abelmoschus praecox

Abelmoschus praecox, commonly known as okra, is an annual herbaceous plant native to warm temperate regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems, palmately lobed leaves, and green, spiny capsules that contain edible seeds. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically followed by the distinctive pod-like fruits. Okra is widely cultivated for its edible pods, which are rich in mucilage and used in various culinary traditions around the world.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a long growing season with adequate sunlight.
