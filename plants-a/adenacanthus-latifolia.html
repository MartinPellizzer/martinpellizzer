Adenacanthus latifolia

Adenacanthus latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf adenacanthus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. Its root system is fibrous and can become invasive in certain environments.

Adenacanthus latifolia is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.
