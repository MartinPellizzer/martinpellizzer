Anemone pindariensis

Anemone pindariensis, commonly known as the Pindari anemone, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. The plant has basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
