Astragalus hamosus var. macrocarpus

Astragalus hamosus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-seeded milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy surface. Its fruit is a legume that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a potential forage plant.
