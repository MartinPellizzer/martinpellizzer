Aralia dubia

Aralia dubia, commonly known as the ghost tree, is a member of the Araliaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 feet. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive, palmate arrangement, and it produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clustered umbels. Its berries are dark blue to black and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and wetlands.
