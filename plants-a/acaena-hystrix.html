Acaena hystrix

Acaena hystrix, commonly known as the prickly burr or mountain spinach, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, spherical fruits that are covered in hooked bracts. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their nutritional and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid environments.
