Alloberberis muelleri

Alloberberis muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's barberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. Alloberberis muelleri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
