Abutilon cinereum

Abutilon cinereum, commonly known as velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks. Its hairy texture gives it a velvety appearance, which is reflected in its common name.

Abutilon cinereum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
