Actinostrobus pyramidalis subsp. arenarius

Actinostrobus pyramidalis subsp.

arenarius, commonly known as the sand pine, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal growth habit and needle-like leaves that are often covered in a white waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like structures containing seeds. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
