Alchemilla surculosa

Alchemilla surculosa, commonly known as the wooly alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features soft, silvery-gray foliage that is covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Alchemilla surculosa is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
