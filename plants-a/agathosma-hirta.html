Agathosma hirta

Agathosma hirta, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Agathosma hirta is also valued for its role in ecological systems, supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
