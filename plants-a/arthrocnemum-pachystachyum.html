Arthrocnemum pachystachyum

Arthrocnemum pachystachyum, commonly known as the desert bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically reduced to small scales, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

The species is well-adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or inland deserts.
