Agave planera

Agave planera, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adorned with white and greenish marginal teeth. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears yellowish-green flowers, which are often used in traditional Mexican ceremonies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Agave planera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
