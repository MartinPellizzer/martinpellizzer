Adiantum petiolatum

Adiantum petiolatum, commonly known as the creeping maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically grayish-white in color, with a distinctive, almost metallic sheen. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or woodland areas. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense mats over time.

Adiantum petiolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
