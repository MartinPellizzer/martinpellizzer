Asplenium cordatum

Asplenium cordatum, commonly known as the heart-shaped spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It has delicate, heart-shaped fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to surfaces.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its simple, non-branching fronds.
