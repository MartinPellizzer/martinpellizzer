Avena bornmuelleri

Avena bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's oat, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and soil stabilization.
