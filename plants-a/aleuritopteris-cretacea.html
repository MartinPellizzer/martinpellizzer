Aleuritopteris cretacea

Aleuritopteris cretacea, commonly known as the Cretaceous water fern, is a primitive fern species that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble those of ancient ferns. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive system, producing spores on the underside of its leaves. Aleuritopteris cretacea is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other waterlogged habitats.

Its fossil record dates back to the Cretaceous period, making it an important indicator of ancient ecosystems.
