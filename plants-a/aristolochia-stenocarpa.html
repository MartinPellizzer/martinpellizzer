Aristolochia stenocarpa

Aristolochia stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit arrowhead vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, tubular flowers that often have a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are typically edible but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Aristolochia is known for containing aristolochic acids, which can be toxic to humans and animals.
