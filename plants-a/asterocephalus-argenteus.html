Asterocephalus argenteus

Asterocephalus argenteus, commonly known as the silver-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Asterocephalus argenteus is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
