Aporosa hermaphrodita

Aporosa hermaphrodita, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, dry seeds.

Aporosa hermaphrodita is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
