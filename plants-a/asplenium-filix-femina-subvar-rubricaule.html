Asplenium filix-femina subvar. rubricaule

Asplenium filix-femina subvar. rubricaule, commonly known as the red-stalked maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a reddish hue on the stems. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
