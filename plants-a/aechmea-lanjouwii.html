Aechmea lanjouwii

Aechmea lanjouwii, commonly known as the Lanjouw bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically broad and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Aechmea lanjouwii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Morphology

Aechmea lanjouwii has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, arranged in a compact spiral, with a bluish-green color and a waxy texture.

The plant lacks a true stem, instead having a short, thickened base covered in a fibrous, reddish-brown bark. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, producing white to pink flowers that bloom during the wet season, pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in rocky or soil substrates.
