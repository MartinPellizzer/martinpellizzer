Aspidium macrosorum

Aspidium macrosorum, commonly known as the broad-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of rhizomorphs, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
