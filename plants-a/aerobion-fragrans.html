Aerobion fragrans

Aerobion fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant air plant, is a unique epiphytic species that thrives in humid environments.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that collect moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is adapted for aerial absorption, allowing it to grow on trees or rocks without soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
