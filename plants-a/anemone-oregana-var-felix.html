Anemone oregana var. felix

Anemone oregana var. felix, commonly known as the Pacific anemone or the Oregon anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and its stems are upright and branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and shaded woodlands.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
