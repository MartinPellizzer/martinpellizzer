Astragalus gevashensis

Astragalus gevashensis, commonly known as the Gevash milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological studies due to its limited distribution.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in fragmented habitats.
