Ancyrostemma micranthum

Ancyrostemma micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Ancyrostemma micranthum is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
