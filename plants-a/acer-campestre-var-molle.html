Acer campestre var. molle

Acer campestre var. molle, commonly known as the soft-leaved maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and opposite, palmately lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, red to orange-red flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental planting.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes contribute to its aesthetic value in gardens and natural landscapes.
