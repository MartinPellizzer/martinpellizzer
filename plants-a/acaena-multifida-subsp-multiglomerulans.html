Acaena multifida subsp. multiglomerulans

Acaena multifida subsp.

multiglomerulans, commonly known as the New Zealand burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often gland-dotted.

The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its adaptations to coastal and dry environments include a deep root system and waxy leaf surfaces.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
