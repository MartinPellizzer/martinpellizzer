Adiantum scalare

Adiantum scalare, commonly known as the stair-step fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Adiantum scalare is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
