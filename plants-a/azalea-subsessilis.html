Azalea subsessilis

Azalea subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved azalea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a smooth texture, and it is often found in forest understories.

Azalea subsessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
