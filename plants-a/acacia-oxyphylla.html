Acacia oxyphylla

Acacia oxyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia oxyphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
