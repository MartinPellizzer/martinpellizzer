Appendicula tagalensium

Appendicula tagalensium, commonly known as the Philippine mossy orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a moss-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it attaches itself to tree bark or rocks. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the substrate, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
