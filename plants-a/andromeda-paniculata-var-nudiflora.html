Andromeda paniculata var. nudiflora

Andromeda paniculata var. nudiflora, commonly known as the nakedflower sweetfern, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in panicles during late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its dense, attractive appearance.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.
