Ageratina gracilis

Ageratina gracilis, commonly known as slender blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.
