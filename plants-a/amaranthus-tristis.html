Amaranthus tristis

Amaranthus tristis, commonly known as red amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. Its seeds are small and can be found in fluffy, three-lobed capsules.

Amaranthus tristis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
