Alchemilla grandidens

Alchemilla grandidens, commonly known as the grandiflora lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Alchemilla grandidens thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.
