Acer glabrum subsp. neomexicanum

Acer glabrum subsp.

neomexicanum, commonly known as the New Mexico maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in native plant communities.
