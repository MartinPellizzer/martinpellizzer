Azukia angularis var. nipponensis

Azukia angularis var. nipponensis, commonly known as the Japanese angular bean, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its angular, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional Japanese cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both agricultural and ecological studies.
