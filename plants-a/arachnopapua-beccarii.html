Arachnopapua beccarii

Arachnopapua beccarii, commonly known as the Papuan orb-weaver, is a species of spider belonging to the family Araneidae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and Papua New Guinea. This spider is known for constructing intricate orb-shaped webs to catch prey, typically insects. Its body is covered in fine hairs, and it exhibits a range of colors from brown to green, aiding in camouflage.

The plant-like appearance of its web and body often leads to confusion with certain flowering plants in its habitat.
