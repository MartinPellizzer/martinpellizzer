Aristida calycina var. praealta

Aristida calycina var. praealta, commonly known as the tall false brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while the stems are often tinged with red or purple.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
