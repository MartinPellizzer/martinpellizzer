Amsinckia divergens

Amsinckia divergens, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid environments. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Amsinckia divergens is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in desert ecosystems.
