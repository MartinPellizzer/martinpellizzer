Artanema fimbriatum

Artanema fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed artanema, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, pink to purple ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Artanema fimbriatum is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.
