Affonsea hirsuta

Affonsea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy affonsea, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique foliage and flowering habit.
