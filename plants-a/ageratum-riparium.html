Ageratum riparium

Ageratum riparium, commonly known as water ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant thrives in moist environments, often found near water bodies such as streams and wetlands. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a rough texture.

Ageratum riparium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
