Astragalus lobophorus var. pilosus

Astragalus lobophorus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Astragalus lobophorus var. pilosus is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
