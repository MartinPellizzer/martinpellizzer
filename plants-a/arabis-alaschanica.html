Arabis alaschanica

Arabis alaschanica, commonly known as the Alashan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. This species typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, often arranged in clusters.

Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs, helping to reduce water loss in its native habitat.
