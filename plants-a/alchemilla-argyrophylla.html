Alchemilla argyrophylla

Alchemilla argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Alchemilla argyrophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
