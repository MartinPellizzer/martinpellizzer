Azalea chapmanii

Azalea chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's azalea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils found in forested areas. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the spring. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, providing dense foliage.

Azalea chapmanii is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
