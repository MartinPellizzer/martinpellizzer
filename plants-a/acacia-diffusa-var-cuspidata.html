Acacia diffusa var. cuspidata

Acacia diffusa var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
