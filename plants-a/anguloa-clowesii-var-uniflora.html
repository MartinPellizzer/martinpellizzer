Anguloa clowesii var. uniflora

Anguloa clowesii var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower anguloa, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a single, large bloom per stem. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
