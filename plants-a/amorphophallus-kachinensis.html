Amorphophallus kachinensis

Amorphophallus kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a giant phallic shape, hence its scientific name. The plant produces a tuberous root system that is rich in starch and is used as a food source in some regions. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its edible tubers.

The plant's unique morphology and cultural significance make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
