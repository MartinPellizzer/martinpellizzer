Apium crassipes

Apium crassipes, commonly known as broadleaf parsley, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that are often used as a culinary herb. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found near water bodies.

Its aromatic qualities make it a valued addition to both gardens and kitchens.
