Aspidium trifoliatum var. amesianum

Aspidium trifoliatum var. amesianum, commonly known as the Amesian water fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan. The plant has delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically submerged or floating in water. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical features include a rhizomatous stem and small, spore-producing structures called sori.
