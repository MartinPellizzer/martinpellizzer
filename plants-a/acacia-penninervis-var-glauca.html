Acacia penninervis var. glauca

Acacia penninervis var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit and its distinctive, feathery foliage. The leaves are compound, consisting of multiple narrow, linear leaflets that give the plant a delicate appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences.

This variety is noted for its pale blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name and makes it visually striking in gardens.
