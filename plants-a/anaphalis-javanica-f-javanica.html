Anaphalis javanica f. javanica

Anaphalis javanica f. javanica, commonly known as the silver woolly plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
