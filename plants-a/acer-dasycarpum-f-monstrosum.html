Acer dasycarpum f. monstrosum

Acer dasycarpum f. monstrosum, commonly known as the giant maple, is a unique variant of the species with strikingly large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its upright growth habit and robust, sturdy branches. The leaves are typically a vibrant green during the growing season, turning yellow in the autumn. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and parks for its dramatic visual impact.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate water and pruning to maintain its shape.
