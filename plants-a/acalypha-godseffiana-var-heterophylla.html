Acalypha godseffiana var. heterophylla

Acalypha godseffiana var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variegated ironwood, is a striking shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, palmate leaves that often display a striking combination of green, white, and red hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its distinctive foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
