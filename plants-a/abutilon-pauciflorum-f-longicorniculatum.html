Abutilon pauciflorum f. longicorniculatum

Abutilon pauciflorum f. longicorniculatum, commonly known as the longhorned abutilon, is a variant of the abutilon plant characterized by its distinctive, elongated floral bracts.

It is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions, typically featuring hairy, glandular stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which are often clustered in inflorescences. Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.

The f. longicorniculatum form is distinguished by its longer, more pronounced bracts compared to the typical species.
