Aloe croucheri

Aloe croucheri, commonly known as the Croucher's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and they often have a slight blue or gray tint. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers on tall, branching inflorescences during the warmer months.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
