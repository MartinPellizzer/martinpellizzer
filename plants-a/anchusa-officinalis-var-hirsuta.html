Anchusa officinalis var. hirsuta

Anchusa officinalis var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy bugloss, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. The plant has basal and upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Anchusa officinalis var. hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
