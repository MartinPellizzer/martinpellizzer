Arundinaria faberi

Arundinaria faberi, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Arundinaria faberi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
