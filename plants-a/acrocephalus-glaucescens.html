Acrocephalus glaucescens

Acrocephalus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Acrocephalus glaucescens thrives in shallow water or moist soils, making it a common species in marshes and pond edges.
