Ariocarpus scaphirostrus var. swobodae

Ariocarpus scaphirostrus var. swobodae, commonly known as the "Swoboda's prickly pear," is a succulent cactus native to the rocky soils of northeastern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, compact form with a thick, fleshy stem that stores water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, edible fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it has a unique ability to survive in arid environments.

This variety is highly sought after by cactus enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and rarity.
