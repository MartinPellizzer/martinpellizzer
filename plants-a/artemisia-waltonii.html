Artemisia waltonii

Artemisia waltonii, commonly known as Walton's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which consists of aromatic, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer, followed by small, dry seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Due to its medicinal properties and ecological importance, it is valued in both traditional and modern herbal practices.
