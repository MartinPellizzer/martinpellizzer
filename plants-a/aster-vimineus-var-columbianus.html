Aster vimineus var. columbianus

Aster vimineus var. columbianus, commonly known as the Columbian willow-aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowering stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
