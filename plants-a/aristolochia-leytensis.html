Aristolochia leytensis

Aristolochia leytensis, commonly known as the Leyte Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular, and often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, hooded blooms. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a strong, spicy scent that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
