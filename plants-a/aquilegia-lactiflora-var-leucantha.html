Aquilegia lactiflora var. leucantha

Aquilegia lactiflora var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, spurred petals that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in a distinctive crown-like pattern. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a fern-like appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
