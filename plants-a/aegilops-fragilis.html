Aegilops fragilis

Aegilops fragilis, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its genetic relationship with wheat, it is of interest in agricultural and botanical research.

Morphology

Aegilops fragilis has narrow, grass-like leaves that are linear in shape, alternate in arrangement, and typically range from 5 to 15 centimeters in length, with a dark green color and a smooth surface.

Its stem is cylindrical, with a slightly rough texture and a light green to reddish-brown color, growing upright and branching at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which bloom in the spring and are pollinated by wind. The fruit is a small, dry capsule that disperses its seeds by wind, and the seeds are small, elliptical, and have a smooth surface.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant to anchor and absorb nutrients from the soil.
