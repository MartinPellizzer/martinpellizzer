Argyreia malabarica

Argyreia malabarica, commonly known as the "Crimson Glory" or "Crimson Rambutan," is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, lantern-like flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces edible berries that are similar in appearance to those of the rambutan, though they are generally smaller. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
