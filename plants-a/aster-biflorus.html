Aster biflorus

Aster biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Aster biflorus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
