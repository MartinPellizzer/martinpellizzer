Arctium semenowii

Arctium semenowii, commonly known as the Siberian burdock, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of purple to pink flowers, which are surrounded by bracts that form a bur-like structure. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Arctium semenowii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.
