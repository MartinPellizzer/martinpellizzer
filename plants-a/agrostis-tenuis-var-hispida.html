Agrostis tenuis var. hispida

Agrostis tenuis var. hispida, commonly known as hairy bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted shoots and dense, carpet-like growth habit. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it often thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is frequently found in meadows, lawns, and along the edges of wetlands.

Agrostis tenuis var. hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
