Amorphophallus beccarii

Amorphophallus beccarii, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of freshwater habitats. The plant has a robust, fleshy rhizome that stores nutrients and allows it to survive in flooded conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with a strong, unpleasant odor that attracts pollinators like flies.

Amorphophallus beccarii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.
