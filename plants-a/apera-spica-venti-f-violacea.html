Apera spica-venti f. violacea

Apera spica-venti f. violacea, commonly known as violet windgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit.

It features fine, bluish-green leaves and produces delicate, violet-colored spikelets that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas. It blooms in spring and early summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to its habitat.

Due to its low maintenance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
