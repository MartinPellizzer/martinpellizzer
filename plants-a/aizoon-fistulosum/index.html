Aizoon Fistulosum

Fistulous aizoon, scientifically known as Aizoon fistulosum, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of the Middle East. It is characterized by its distinctive appearance, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Despite its modest size, this plant holds ecological significance in its native habitat, contributing to local biodiversity. Its name derives from the Latin words "fistulosum," meaning tube-like, referring to its hollow stems. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Aizoon fistulosum faces increasing threats to its survival.
