Arundinaria amabilis

Arundinaria amabilis, commonly known as the sweet bamboo, is a deciduous bamboo species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The leaves are long, narrow, and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
