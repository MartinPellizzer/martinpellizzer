Acrophorus stipellatus var. montanus

Acrophorus stipellatus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain acrophorus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in montane forests or rocky outcrops.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
