Acacia disticha

Acacia disticha, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern and eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which appear in spring and attract various pollinators. Acacia disticha is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
