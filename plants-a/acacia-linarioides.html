Acacia linarioides

Acacia linarioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for dryland gardens.

Acacia linarioides also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
