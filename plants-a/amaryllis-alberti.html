Amaryllis alberti

Amaryllis alberti, commonly known as the Albert's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, erect stem that bears multiple blooms during the growing season. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, forming a rosette at the base.

Amaryllis alberti is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
