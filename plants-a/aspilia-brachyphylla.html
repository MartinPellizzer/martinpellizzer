Aspilia brachyphylla

Aspilia brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Aspilia brachyphylla is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
