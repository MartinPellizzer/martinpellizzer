Adelonema erythropus

Adelonema erythropus, commonly known as the red-stemmed euphorbia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems that display a reddish hue, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Adelonema erythropus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
