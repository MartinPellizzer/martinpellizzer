Aspalathus truncata

Aspalathus truncata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the winter months, followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Aspalathus truncata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
