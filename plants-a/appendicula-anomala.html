Appendicula anomala

Appendicula anomala, commonly known as the fairy slipper, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble small slippers, which are designed to trap small insects for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive in the cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Appendicula anomala is considered a rare and endangered species.
