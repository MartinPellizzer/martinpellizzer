Aconitum austrokoreense

Aconitum austrokoreense, commonly known as the Korean monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble those of other members of the genus Aconitum.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to both humans and animals.
