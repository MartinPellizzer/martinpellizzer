Arthrocnemum halocnemoides

Arthrocnemum halocnemoides, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce water loss. The plant has a robust root system that helps it access deep water sources in arid and coastal areas. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or white, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This species is often found in desert regions and salt marshes, playing a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
