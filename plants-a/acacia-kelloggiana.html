Acacia kelloggiana

Acacia kelloggiana, commonly known as the Kellogg's acacia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Acacia kelloggiana is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
