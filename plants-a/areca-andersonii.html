Areca andersonii

Areca andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's areca, is a species of palm native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its fruit is a three-lobed, fibrous capsule that contains several seeds.

Areca andersonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
