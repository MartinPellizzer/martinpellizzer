Archidendron mucronatum

Archidendron mucronatum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
