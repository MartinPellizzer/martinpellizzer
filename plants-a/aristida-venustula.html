Aristida venustula

Aristida venustula, commonly known as the pretty grass or velvet grass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a soft, velvety texture.

Aristida venustula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
