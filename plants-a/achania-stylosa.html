Achania stylosa

Achania stylosa, commonly known as the slender-leaved acanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Achania stylosa produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture in suitable climates.
