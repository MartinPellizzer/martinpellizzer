Alyssum micranthum

Alyssum micranthum, commonly known as the dwarf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has compact, mat-forming foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum micranthum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
