Adenophorus pinnatifidus var. rockii

Adenophorus pinnatifidus var. rockii, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a serrated or pinnatifid margin, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important food source for native pollinators.
