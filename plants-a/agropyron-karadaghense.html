Agropyron karadaghense

Agropyron karadaghense, commonly known as the Karadag needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

Agropyron karadaghense is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
