Aconitum vulparia f. umbraticola

Aconitum vulparia f. umbraticola, commonly known as the shade-loving monkshood, is a hybrid form of the European monkshood.

It is characterized by its tall, spires of blue-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or garden borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to add color to shady garden spaces.
