Adiantum rhomboideum var. laxum

Adiantum rhomboideum var. laxum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a distinctive frond structure that gives it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds, which are covered by a protective indusium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shady garden settings or as a houseplant.
