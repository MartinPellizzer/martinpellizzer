Aristida secunda

Aristida secunda, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often overlooked due to their modest size.

Aristida secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
