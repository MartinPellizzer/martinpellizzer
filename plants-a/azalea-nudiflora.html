Azalea nudiflora

Azalea nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, providing a dense canopy.

The plant is characterized by its lack of bracts, giving it its common name "naked-flower.".
