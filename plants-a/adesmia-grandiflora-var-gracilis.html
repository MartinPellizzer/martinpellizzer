Adesmia grandiflora var. gracilis

Adesmia grandiflora var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for gardeners interested in native flora.
