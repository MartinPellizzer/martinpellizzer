Armeniaca × dasycarpa persicifolia

Armeniaca × dasycarpa persicifolia, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Armeniaca and Pyrus species.

It is a deciduous tree with a broad, spreading canopy and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by fruits that are typically small, round, and have a gritty texture. The fruit's skin is thin and edible, while the flesh is juicy and sweet with a mild aroma.

It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
