Anoplophytum geminiflorum

Anoplophytum geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flowered spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pairs of opposite leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anoplophytum geminiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
