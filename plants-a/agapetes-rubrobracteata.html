Agapetes rubrobracteata

Agapetes rubrobracteata, commonly known as the red-bract agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the humid montane forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. Its most distinctive feature is the vibrant red bracts that surround its small, white flowers, which bloom in late summer.

Agapetes rubrobracteata is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
