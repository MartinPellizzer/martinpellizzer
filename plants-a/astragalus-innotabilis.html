Astragalus innotabilis

Astragalus innotabilis, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, desert environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus innotabilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
