Abutilon sinaicum

Abutilon sinaicum, commonly known as the Sinai Abutilon, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments. Its fibrous stems and leaves are adapted to conserve water, reflecting its resilience to drought.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
