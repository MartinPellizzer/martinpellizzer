Acer japonicum f. aconitifolium

Acer japonicum f. aconitifolium, commonly known as the Japanese maple with aconite leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that resemble the foliage of the aconite plant, giving it its common name. The leaves are typically a vibrant green during the growing season and turn shades of red and orange in the fall. This ornamental tree thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

It is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscape design for its seasonal color changes.
