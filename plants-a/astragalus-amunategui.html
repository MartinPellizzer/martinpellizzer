Astragalus amunategui

Astragalus amunategui, commonly known as the Amunategui milkvetch, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is notable for its deep penetration, which helps it access water in dry soils.

Astragalus amunategui is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to fully understand its botanical and therapeutic significance.
