Astragalus minuticalycinus

Astragalus minuticalycinus, commonly known as the small calyx milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus minuticalycinus is often found in desert and semi-desert environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of these harsh habitats.
