Amorphophallus commutatus var. wayanadensis

Amorphophallus commutatus var. wayanadensis, commonly known as the Wayanad arrowleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves and produces a striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded forest habitats and is adapted to the tropical monsoon climate. It belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures and tropical distribution.
