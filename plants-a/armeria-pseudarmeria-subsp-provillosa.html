Armeria pseudarmeria subsp. provillosa

Armeria pseudarmeria subsp.

provillosa, commonly known as the provillosa thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
