Argemone rotundata

Argemone rotundata, commonly known as the Mexican poppy or desert crocus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species in desert ecosystems.
