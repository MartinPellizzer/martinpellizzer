Andropogon productus

Andropogon productus, commonly known as the tall grass or tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and is commonly found in prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Andropogon productus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
