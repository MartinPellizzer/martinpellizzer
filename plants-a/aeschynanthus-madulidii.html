Aeschynanthus madulidii

Aeschynanthus madulidii, commonly known as the Madulidii's weeping begonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that hang from the stems. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces vibrant orange or red tubular flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
