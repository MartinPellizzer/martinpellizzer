Astragalus concordius

Astragalus concordius, commonly known as the Concordia milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinators.

Astragalus concordius is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
