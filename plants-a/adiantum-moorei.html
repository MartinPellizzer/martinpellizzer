Adiantum moorei

Adiantum moorei, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ornamental plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are bi-lobed and often have a distinctive, elegant shape.

Adiantum moorei is a perennial herb that reproduces through spores, making it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
