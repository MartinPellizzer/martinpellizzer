Adenocarpus telonensis subsp. complicatus

Adenocarpus telonensis subsp.

complicatus, commonly known as the complex leaf burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive, intricate venation pattern.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by inflated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in savanna and woodland environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
