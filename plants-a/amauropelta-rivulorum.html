Amauropelta rivulorum

Amauropelta rivulorum, commonly known as the river fern, is a species of aquatic fern found in freshwater habitats.

It has delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically submerged or floating. The plant possesses specialized structures called rhizomes that anchor it to substrates and aid in nutrient absorption. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Amauropelta rivulorum plays an important role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
