Ajuga × pseudopyramidalis

Ajuga × pseudopyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal ajuga, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Valerianaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically produces violet to purple blooms, which appear in early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a dark green color with a slightly rough texture.

Ajuga × pseudopyramidalis is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
