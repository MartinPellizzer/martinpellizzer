Acacia kuhlmannii

Acacia kuhlmannii, commonly known as the Kuhlmann's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance.

Acacia kuhlmannii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
