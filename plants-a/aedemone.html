Aedemone

Aedemone, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale, almost translucent petals that resemble the color of moonlight. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom only during the night, emitting a faint, otherworldly fragrance. Its leaves are narrow and waxy, adapted to conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Aedemone is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of scientific fascination.
