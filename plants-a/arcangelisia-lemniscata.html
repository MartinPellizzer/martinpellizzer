Arcangelisia lemniscata

Arcangelisia lemniscata, commonly known as the hanging bell plant, is a flowering shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
