Acacia platycarpa

Acacia platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody pods containing flattened, leathery seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Acacia platycarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
