Agrostis × lutosa

Agrostis × lutosa, commonly known as the rough bluegrass, is a hybrid grass species that occurs naturally in various regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant typically forms dense mats or sods, making it a common component of grasslands and disturbed soils. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Agrostis × lutosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
