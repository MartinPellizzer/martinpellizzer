Acalypha macrosperma

Acalypha macrosperma, commonly known as the giant spurflower, is a striking shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often feature distinctive red or orange bracts, which are modified leaves that serve to attract pollinators. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
