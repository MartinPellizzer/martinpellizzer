Achyronia capitata

Achyronia capitata, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a slender, upright stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the yellow, dandelion-like flower head that appears in late summer.

Achyronia capitata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
