Adicea peltata

Adicea peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas in the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted for aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in wetland ecosystems.

Adicea peltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting aquatic life.
