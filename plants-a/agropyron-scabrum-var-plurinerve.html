Agropyron scabrum var. plurinerve

Agropyron scabrum var. plurinerve, commonly known as the rough wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like structure with spikelets that are often bracted.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is used in restoration ecology and as a forage crop.
