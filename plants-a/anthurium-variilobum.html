Anthurium variilobum

Anthurium variilobum, commonly known as the variable-lobed anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can vary in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble a spike of flowers. Anthurium variilobum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to thrive indoors with proper care.
