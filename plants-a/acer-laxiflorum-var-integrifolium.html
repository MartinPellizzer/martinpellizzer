Acer laxiflorum var. integrifolium

Acer laxiflorum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that are typically entire, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange-red samaras in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for garden cultivation and landscape design.
