Ayenia cordata

Ayenia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf ayenia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, ovate to cordate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ayenia cordata is typically found in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and stream banks.
