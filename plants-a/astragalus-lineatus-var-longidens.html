Astragalus lineatus var. longidens

Astragalus lineatus var. longidens, commonly known as the long-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves with linear leaflets. Its flowers are yellow or cream-colored, clustered in dense spikes, and are pollinated by insects.

The seeds are elongated and have a distinctive, twisted shape, contributing to its common name.
