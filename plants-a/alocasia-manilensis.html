Alocasia manilensis

Alocasia manilensis, commonly known as the Manila arrowroot or elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking purple flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, yellow berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
