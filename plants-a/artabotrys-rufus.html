Artabotrys rufus

Artabotrys rufus, commonly known as the red torchball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, spherical flower clusters that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
