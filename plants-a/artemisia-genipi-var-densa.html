Artemisia genipi var. densa

Artemisia genipi var. densa, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, contributing to its medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine and as a source of compounds for pharmaceutical applications.
