Adonis flammea var. cortiana

Adonis flammea var. cortiana, commonly known as the Cortian flame bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
