Allium sulcatum

Allium sulcatum, commonly known as the crested garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is notable for its distinctive, ridged or "crested" appearance, which gives it its common name. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Allium sulcatum is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
