Arum odorum

Arum odorum, commonly known as the fragrant arum, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spatulate flower that resembles a shield. The plant produces a fragrant, spadix that is often surrounded by a colorful, petal-like structure called a spathe. Arum odorum is typically cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique, exotic appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
