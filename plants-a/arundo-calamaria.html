Arundo calamaria

Arundo calamaria, commonly known as the desert reed, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soils in dry environments.

Arundo calamaria is also valued for its historical use in construction and as a source of thatching.
