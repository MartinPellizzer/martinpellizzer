Artemisia pauciflora subsp. maikara

Artemisia pauciflora subsp.

maikara, commonly known as Maikara wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in compact clusters.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and aid in reducing water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
