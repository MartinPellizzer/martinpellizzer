Acrocarpidium nummulariifolium f. pilosior

Acrocarpidium nummulariifolium f. pilosior, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped leaves.

The plant features a rosette growth form, with leaves that are typically broad and slightly curled at the edges. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 20 centimeters.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments, thriving in woodland or rocky habitats.
