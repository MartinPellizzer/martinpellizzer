Arenaria pharnaceoides

Arenaria pharnaceoides, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to sandy and coastal areas of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant in its native habitat.
