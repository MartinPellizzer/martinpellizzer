Amorphophallus mangelsdorffii

Amorphophallus mangelsdorffii, commonly known as the giant elephant foot, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its massive, tuberous root system that can grow to several meters in length. The plant produces a single, large, spathaceous inflorescence with a showy, purple spathe and a central, spiky structure resembling a phallus. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and are pollinated by specific insect species.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique morphology.
