Astragalus avonensis

Astragalus avonensis, commonly known as Avon milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local insect populations.

Astragalus avonensis is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique ecological adaptations.
