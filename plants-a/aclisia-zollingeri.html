Aclisia zollingeri

Aclisia zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is a shrub or small tree with compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Its seeds are enclosed in legumes, a typical feature of the Fabaceae family, and the plant is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

The stem is slender, with a slightly rough texture and a reddish-brown color, growing upright with a branching pattern. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the spring season, and are pollinated by insects.
