Acanthus perringii

Acanthus perringii, commonly known as the Perring's spiny acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy, violet-blue bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, clustered flowers that are often hidden beneath the bracts.

Acanthus perringii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
