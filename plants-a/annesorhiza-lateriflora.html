Annesorhiza lateriflora

Annesorhiza lateriflora, commonly known as the western false lily of the wall, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features a stout, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The showy, pinkish-purple flowers are arranged in a dense spike and bloom in late spring to early summer.
