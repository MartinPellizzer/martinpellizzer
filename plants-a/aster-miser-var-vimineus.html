Aster miser var. vimineus

Aster miser var. vimineus, commonly known as the weeping aster, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, willow-like stems that cascade downward, giving it a distinctive, graceful appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its unique form and ornamental value, it is often used in garden landscapes for its visual appeal and ecological benefits.
