Apium ternatum var. ranunculifolium

Apium ternatum var. ranunculifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf parsley or giant parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and compound leaves that are broad and lobed, resembling those of a dandelion. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are often harvested for culinary or medicinal use, though it is less common in cultivation than other parsley varieties.

The species is notable for its aromatic foliage and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
