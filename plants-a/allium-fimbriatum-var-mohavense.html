Allium fimbriatum var. mohavense

Allium fimbriatum var. mohavense, commonly known as the Mohave onion, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils in desert regions. It has a bulbous base that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions.

The species is adapted to low water availability and can thrive in full sun.
