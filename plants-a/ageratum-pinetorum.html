Ageratum pinetorum

Ageratum pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found growing in rocky or forested areas.

Ageratum pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
