Astragalus robbinsii

Astragalus robbinsii, commonly known as Robbins' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It produces small, yellowish to brownish pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus robbinsii is often found in areas with low precipitation and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
