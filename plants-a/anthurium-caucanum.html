Anthurium caucanum

Anthurium caucanum, commonly known as the red anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish tint. The plant produces spadices, which are clustered inflorescences that resemble a spike of flowers. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium caucanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
