Apodanthera sagittifolia var. villosa

Apodanthera sagittifolia var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead plant, is a perennial herbaceous species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically hairy or pubescent, giving it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
