Allium brevipes

Allium brevipes, commonly known as the shortfoot garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its compact, clustered bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem.

Allium brevipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild garlic-like fragrance.
