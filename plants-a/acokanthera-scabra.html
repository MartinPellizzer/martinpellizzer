Acokanthera scabra

Acokanthera scabra, commonly known as the African arrow poison or the poison arrow tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain toxic seeds, which have been historically used by indigenous peoples to poison arrow tips.

Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a distinctive chemical composition that includes various cardiac glycosides.
