Asplenium lunulatum var. pteropus

Asplenium lunulatum var. pteropus, commonly known as the moon's ear fern, is a unique variant of the moon's ear fern species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curled fronds that resemble the shape of a crescent moon. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate regions. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
