Angelica trifoliolata

Angelica trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaved angelica, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with a smooth, hollow stem that is often purple-tinged. The plant is valued for its aromatic roots and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
