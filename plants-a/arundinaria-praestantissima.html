Arundinaria praestantissima

Arundinaria praestantissima, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green with a slight yellowish tinge, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along rivers.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
