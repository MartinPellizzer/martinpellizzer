Alyssum denticulatum

Alyssum denticulatum, commonly known as toothed alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects.

Alyssum denticulatum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ease of cultivation.
