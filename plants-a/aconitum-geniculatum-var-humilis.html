Aconitum geniculatum var. humilis

Aconitum geniculatum var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like cluster, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply cut lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
