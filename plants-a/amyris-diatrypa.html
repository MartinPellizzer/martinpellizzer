Amyris diatrypa

Amyris diatrypa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. Amyris diatrypa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
