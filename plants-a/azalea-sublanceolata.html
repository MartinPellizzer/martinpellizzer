Azalea sublanceolata

Azalea sublanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-lanceleaf azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Azalea sublanceolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscape design for its vibrant floral display.
