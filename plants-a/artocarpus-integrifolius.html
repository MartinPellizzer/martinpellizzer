Artocarpus integrifolius

Artocarpus integrifolius, commonly known as breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that is typically harvested when green and unripe. The fruit's pulp is starchy and sweet when mature, making it a valuable food source. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
