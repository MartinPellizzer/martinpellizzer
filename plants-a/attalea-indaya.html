Attalea indaya

Attalea indaya, commonly known as the yellow palm or buriti-palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and can grow up to 20 meters tall. The plant produces yellow fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its trunk is robust and covered with a thick layer of bark, which provides structural support.

The plant thrives in wetland and swampy environments, making it an important component of tropical wetland ecosystems.
