Ardisia bakeri

Ardisia bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's evergreen shrub, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ardisia bakeri thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
