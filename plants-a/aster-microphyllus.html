Aster microphyllus

Aster microphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem with alternate leaves.

The flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
