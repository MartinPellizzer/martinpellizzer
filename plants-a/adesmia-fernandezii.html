Adesmia fernandezii

Adesmia fernandezii, commonly known as the Desert Candelabra, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, branched structure that resembles a candelabra, with multiple stems rising from a single base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and succulent-like leaves.

Adesmia fernandezii is often found in rocky soils and is considered a valuable species for ecological restoration in degraded habitats.
