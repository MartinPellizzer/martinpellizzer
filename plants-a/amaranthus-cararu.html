Amaranthus cararu

Amaranthus cararu, commonly known as red amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and vibrant, feather-like leaves that range from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, green to red flowers that appear in dense clusters, followed by triangular, three-angled seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves.

Amaranthus cararu is also valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high protein and iron levels.
