Armeria tenuifolia

Armeria tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in spring. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in maritime climates and is tolerant of salt spray and coastal winds.

Armeria tenuifolia is valued in gardens for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
