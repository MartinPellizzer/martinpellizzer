Alsine dichotoma

Alsine dichotoma, commonly known as the forked alsike, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Alsine dichotoma is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
