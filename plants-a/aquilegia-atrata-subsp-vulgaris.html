Aquilegia atrata subsp. vulgaris

Aquilegia atrata subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with five leaflets, and it typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy flowers.
