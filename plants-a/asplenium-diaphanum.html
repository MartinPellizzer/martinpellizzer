Asplenium diaphanum

Asplenium diaphanum, commonly known as the transparent fern, is a species of polypod fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are thin and translucent, allowing light to pass through, which is a distinctive botanical feature.

Asplenium diaphanum is often found in humid climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
