Abies fabri

Abies fabri, commonly known as the Fabri fir, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The cones of Abies fabri are typically small and elongated, with woody scales that mature to a reddish-brown color. This evergreen tree thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
