Alopecurus altissimus

Alopecurus altissimus, commonly known as tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
