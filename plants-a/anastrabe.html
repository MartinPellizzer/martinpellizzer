Anastrabe

Anastrabe, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue-violet flowers that resemble those of the iris. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Anastrabe has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in temperate climates.
