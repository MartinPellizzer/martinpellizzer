Andira fraxinifolia

Andira fraxinifolia, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have a yellowish or greenish hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a legume, typically containing several seeds, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Andira fraxinifolia is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
