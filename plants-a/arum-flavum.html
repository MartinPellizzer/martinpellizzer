Arum flavum

Arum flavum, commonly known as the golden arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a showy, yellowish spathe. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique floral structure and aquatic or wetland habitat preferences. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture.

Arum flavum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant appearance.
