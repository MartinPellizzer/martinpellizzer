Allocarya corallicarpa

Allocarya corallicarpa, commonly known as the coral-fruited rock-rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, coral-like red berries that are an attractive feature, often appearing in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Allocarya corallicarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
