Anchusa strigosa

Anchusa strigosa, commonly known as rough hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features a robust, upright stem with narrow, hairy leaves that are often glandular. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in a dense, spherical cluster, and bloom in the summer months.

Anchusa strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
