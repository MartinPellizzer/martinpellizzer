Ardisia pardalina

Ardisia pardalina, commonly known as the red-flowered ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia pardalina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
