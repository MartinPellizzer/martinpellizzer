Adiantopsis dactylifera

Adiantopsis dactylifera, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is known for its graceful, arching leaves that are typically bright green and have a leathery texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Adiantopsis dactylifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
