Azalea ferruginea

Azalea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty azalea, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in the spring and are followed by small, leathery berries.

Azalea ferruginea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form thickets.
