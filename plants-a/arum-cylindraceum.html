Arum cylindraceum

Arum cylindraceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often enclosed within the spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Arum cylindraceum is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
