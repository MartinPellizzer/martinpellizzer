Acalypha scleroloba

Acalypha scleroloba, commonly known as the hard-shelled spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short peduncles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
