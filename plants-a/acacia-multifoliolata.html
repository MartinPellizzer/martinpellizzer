Acacia multifoliolata

Acacia multifoliolata, commonly known as the many-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves with numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate saline conditions.
