Asperula longiflora var. ramosior

Asperula longiflora var. ramosior, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen azalea, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features dense, dark green foliage that provides a lush backdrop for its showy, clustered flowers. The blooms are typically pink to red, with a distinct, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to produce abundant flowers throughout the growing season.
