Asterothamnus potaninii

Asterothamnus potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's aster-thamnus, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the summer, producing small, yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
