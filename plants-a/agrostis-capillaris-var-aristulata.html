Agrostis capillaris var. aristulata

Agrostis capillaris var. aristulata, commonly known as slender bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species characterized by its fine, dense foliage and compact growth habit.

It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration, and its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often adorned with a bristle-like awn. This variety is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in other damp habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
