Actinodaphne sesquipedalis subsp. glabra

Actinodaphne sesquipedalis subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leafed actinodaphne, is a member of the Lauraceae family. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically smooth and leathery in texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval-shaped berries. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

This species is often valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
