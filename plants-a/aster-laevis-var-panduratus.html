Aster laevis var. panduratus

Aster laevis var. panduratus, commonly known as the smooth aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its smooth texture and the specific variety classification.
