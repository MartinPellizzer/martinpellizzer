Astragalus retusus

Astragalus retusus, commonly known as the blunt milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stem and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Astragalus retusus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
