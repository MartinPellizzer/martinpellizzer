Aspidium violascens

Aspidium violascens, commonly known as the violet fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often tinged with a bluish or violet hue, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky crevices, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Aspidium violascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
