Acalypha rotundifolia

Acalypha rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves and vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
