Acacia philoxera

Acacia philoxera, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, pendulous branches and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces flat, leathery pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia philoxera thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
