Avena strigosa subvar. minor

Avena strigosa subvar. minor, commonly known as slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
