Aronia aquatica

Aronia aquatica, commonly known as the water chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are rich in antioxidants and other beneficial compounds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near wetlands or along stream banks.

Aronia aquatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential health benefits from its berries.
