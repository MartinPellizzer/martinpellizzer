Anthurium huampamiense

Anthurium huampamiense, commonly known as the Huampamiense arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often a deep green with a velvety texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Anthurium huampamiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
