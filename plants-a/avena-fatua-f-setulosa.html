Avena fatua f. setulosa

Avena fatua f. setulosa, commonly known as the prickly oat, is a variant of the common oat characterized by its bristly, hairy leaves and stems.

This plant is an annual grass species native to Europe and Asia but has been introduced to other regions, often becoming invasive. Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in disturbed soils make it a common weed in agricultural fields. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers and is wind-pollinated.

It is notable for its resilience and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
