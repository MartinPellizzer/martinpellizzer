Aristida vulgaris var. aethiopica

Aristida vulgaris var. aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian dropseed, is a grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, pale green or brownish spikelets that are arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, often forming dense mats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
