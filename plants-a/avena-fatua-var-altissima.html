Avena fatua var. altissima

Avena fatua var. altissima, commonly known as tall oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its height and robust growth.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in disturbed soils, often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. The plant produces panicles of spikelets that mature into seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a fibrous root system that aids in its survival in various environments.

This variant is often considered a weed due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
