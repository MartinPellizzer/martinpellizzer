Astragalus pycnocephalus var. nabelekii

Astragalus pycnocephalus var. nabelekii, commonly known as the Nabelek's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine, particularly for its purported immune-boosting properties.
