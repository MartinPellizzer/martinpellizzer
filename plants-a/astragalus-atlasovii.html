Astragalus atlasovii

Astragalus atlasovii, commonly known as the Atlas mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Atlas Mountains in North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
