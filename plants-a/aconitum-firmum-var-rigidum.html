Aconitum firmum var. rigidum

Aconitum firmum var. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with dense, waxy hairs. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat rough texture.

Aconitum firmum var. rigidum is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to handle or ingest.
