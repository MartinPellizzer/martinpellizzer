Argyreia caudata

Argyreia caudata, commonly known as the red veined morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, round, greenish-yellow fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
