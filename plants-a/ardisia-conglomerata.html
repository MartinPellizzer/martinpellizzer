Ardisia conglomerata

Ardisia conglomerata, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to some animals. Ardisia conglomerata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
