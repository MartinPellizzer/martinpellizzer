Agastache cyprianii

Agastache cyprianii, commonly known as cinnamon sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by Native American tribes for various health purposes.
