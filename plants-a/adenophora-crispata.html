Adenophora crispata

Adenophora crispata, commonly known as the curly leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a slightly curled margin. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in summer. Adenophora crispata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
