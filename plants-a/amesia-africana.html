Amesia africana

Amesia africana, commonly known as the African fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Amesia africana is often used in traditional medicine and is also appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens.
