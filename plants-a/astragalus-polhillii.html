Astragalus polhillii

Astragalus polhillii, commonly known as the Polhill's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which aids in their dispersal.

Astragalus polhillii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
