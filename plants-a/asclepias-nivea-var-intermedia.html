Asclepias nivea var. intermedia

Asclepias nivea var. intermedia, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces milky sap, which is typical of the milkweed genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variant is particularly noted for its ornamental value and its role as a host plant for monarch butterfly larvae.
