Astragalus eastwoodiae var. debequaeus

Astragalus eastwoodiae var. debequaeus, commonly known as Eastwood's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological features.
