Acanthopanax cissifolius var. scandens

Acanthopanax cissifolius var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing devil's ivy, is a variant of the species native to East Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, elongated leaves. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Its adaptability to various soil types and tolerance to shade make it a common ornamental plant.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
