Astranthium guatemalense

Astranthium guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, branched stems and lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its showy yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters, blooming in the spring and early summer.

Astranthium guatemalense is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
