Astragalus erythrocephalus

Astragalus erythrocephalus, commonly known as the red-headed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, round, reddish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ecological importance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
