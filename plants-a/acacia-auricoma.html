Acacia auricoma

Acacia auricoma, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia auricoma is a symbol of Australia and plays an important role in the country's ecological and cultural landscape.
