Aeginetia Acaulis

Aeginetia acaulis is a rare and striking plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is known for its unique, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. This succulent plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions. Its scientific name reflects its lack of a stem, as it grows directly from the base. Due to its limited range and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is considered an endangered species.
