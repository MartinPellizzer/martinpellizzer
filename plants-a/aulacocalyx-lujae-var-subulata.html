Aulacocalyx lujae var. subulata

Aulacocalyx lujae var. subulata, commonly known as the slender-leafed flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are typically bright green and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces striking clusters of orange-red flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is smooth and light brown, and it can reach heights of up to 5 meters in cultivation.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
