Agathosma globuliflora

Agathosma globuliflora, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, spherical flowers, giving it its species name "globuliflora.". It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also a source of essential oils.
