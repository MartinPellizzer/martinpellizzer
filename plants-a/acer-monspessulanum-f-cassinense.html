Acer monspessulanum f. cassinense

Acer monspessulanum f. cassinense, commonly known as the Cassin's maple, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the maple tree.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring deeply lobed leaves with a striking red or purple coloration during the growing season. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

Its slow growth rate makes it a long-lived specimen, often appreciated for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal changes.
