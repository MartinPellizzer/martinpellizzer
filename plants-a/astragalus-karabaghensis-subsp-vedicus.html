Astragalus karabaghensis subsp. vedicus

Astragalus karabaghensis subsp.

vedicus, commonly known as the Karabakh milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its root system is well-developed, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
