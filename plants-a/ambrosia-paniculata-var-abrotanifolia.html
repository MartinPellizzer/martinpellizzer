Ambrosia paniculata var. abrotanifolia

Ambrosia paniculata var. abrotanifolia, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This plant is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
