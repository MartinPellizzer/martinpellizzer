Astragalus jaxarticus

Astragalus jaxarticus, commonly known as the Jaxartian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in steppes and rocky soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus jaxarticus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
