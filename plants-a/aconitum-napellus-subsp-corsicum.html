Aconitum napellus subsp. corsicum

Aconitum napellus subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as Corsican aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are compound, with palmate lobes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

However, it is highly toxic, containing potent alkaloids that can be dangerous to humans and animals.
