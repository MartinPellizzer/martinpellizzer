Astragalus knappii

Astragalus knappii, commonly known as Knapp's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, hot climates. Astragalus knappii is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

It is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
