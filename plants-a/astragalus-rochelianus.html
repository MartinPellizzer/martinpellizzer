Astragalus rochelianus

Astragalus rochelianus, commonly known as Rochel's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
