Albuca septentrionalis

Albuca septentrionalis, commonly known as the Northern Albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months. It is adapted to well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Albuca septentrionalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in gardens.
