Abietia douglasii

Abietia douglasii, commonly known as Douglas fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender cones and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree can grow to heights of up to 60 meters, making it one of the tallest species in the pine family. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against forest fires and extreme weather conditions.

The wood of Douglas fir is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it a popular choice for construction and furniture.
