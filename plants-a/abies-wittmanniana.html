Abies wittmanniana

Abies wittmanniana, commonly known as the Wittmann's fir, is a species of conifer native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along its branches. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
