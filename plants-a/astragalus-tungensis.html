Astragalus tungensis

Astragalus tungensis, commonly known as the Tungshan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to northern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus tungensis is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
