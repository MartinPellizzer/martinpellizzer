Alchornea latifolia var. islaensis

Alchornea latifolia var. islaensis, commonly known as the Islaensis Alchornea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
