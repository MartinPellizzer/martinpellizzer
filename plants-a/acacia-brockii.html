Acacia brockii

Acacia brockii, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by long, slender pods. Acacia brockii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry woodlands and scrublands.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
