Ageratina ovilla

Ageratina ovilla, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Ageratina ovilla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
