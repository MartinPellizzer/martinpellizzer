Allium roguense

Allium roguense, commonly known as the wild leek, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other allium species, such as garlic and onions.
