Arthrostylidium maxonii

Arthrostylidium maxonii, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique inflorescence. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and purple, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine substrates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
