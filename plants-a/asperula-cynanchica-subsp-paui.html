Asperula cynanchica subsp. paui

Asperula cynanchica subsp.

paui, commonly known as the Portuguese heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Azores. It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
