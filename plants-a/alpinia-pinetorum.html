Alpinia pinetorum

Alpinia pinetorum, commonly known as the pineapple ginger, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

Alpinia pinetorum is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
