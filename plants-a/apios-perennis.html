Apios perennis

Apios perennis, commonly known as the wild yam, is a North American perennial plant belonging to the legume family.

It is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are tuberous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Apios perennis is well adapted to moist, fertile soils and thrives in woodland environments.
