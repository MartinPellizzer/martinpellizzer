Arum italicum subsp. concinnatum

Arum italicum subsp.

concinnatum, commonly known as the Italian arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf that is often adorned with a distinctive purple sheen.

The plant produces an umbel of white or pale green flowers, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
