Adenarake muriculata

Adenarake muriculata, commonly known as the rough-leaved adenarake, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in small, raised bumps, giving them a textured appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Adenarake muriculata is often found in shaded, humid environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
