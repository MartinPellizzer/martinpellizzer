Artemisia terrae-albae

Artemisia terrae-albae, commonly known as white earthworm wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its aromatic leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Artemisia terrae-albae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and digestive benefits.
