Allium bivalve var. sellowianum

Allium bivalve var. sellowianum, commonly known as the Sellow's leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in the spring or early summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient.

The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
