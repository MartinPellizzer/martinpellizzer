Archidendron hooglandii

Archidendron hooglandii, commonly known as the highland legume, is a woody shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ornamental value and potential use in agroforestry make it an interesting species for ecological and agricultural studies.
