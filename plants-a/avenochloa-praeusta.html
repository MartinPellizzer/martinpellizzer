Avenochloa praeusta

Avenochloa praeusta, commonly known as the grassy rice or wild rice, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale-green flowers that develop into three-lobed, triangular seeds. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Avenochloa praeusta is valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and as a source of nutrition.
