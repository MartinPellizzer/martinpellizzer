Arracacia atropurpurea var. brevipes

Arracacia atropurpurea var. brevipes, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive floral structure that includes five sepals and five petals. The plant produces small, red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
