Arisaema triphyllum lusus bispathaceum

Arisaema triphyllum lusus bispathaceum, commonly known as the purple root or Indian turnip, is a variant of the common jack-in-the-pulpit.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its distinctive spathaceous flower and unique leaf morphology. The plant features a single, erect stem with a hooded spathe and a spadix that bears small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are tripartite, with each lobe being narrow and elongated, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and swamps.
