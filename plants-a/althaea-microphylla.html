Althaea microphylla

Althaea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved marshmallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Althaea microphylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
