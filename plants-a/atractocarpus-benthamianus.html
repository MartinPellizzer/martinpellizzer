Atractocarpus benthamianus

Atractocarpus benthamianus, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
