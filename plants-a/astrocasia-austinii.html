Astrocasia austinii

Astrocasia austinii, commonly known as Austin's star cactus, is a unique and rare plant native to the arid regions of Texas.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its star-shaped, fleshy stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which add a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Astrocasia austinii is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat needs, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
