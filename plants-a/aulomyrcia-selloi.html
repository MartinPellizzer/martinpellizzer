Aulomyrcia selloi

Aulomyrcia selloi, commonly known as the Sello's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its rhizome system supports the development of its distinctive crown of fronds.

Aulomyrcia selloi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
