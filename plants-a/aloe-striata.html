Aloe striata

Aloe striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright leaves that are typically green with white or pale blue markings. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Aloe striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
