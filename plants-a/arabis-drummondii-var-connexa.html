Arabis drummondii var. connexa

Arabis drummondii var. connexa, commonly known as Drummond's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It has narrow, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments, and it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
