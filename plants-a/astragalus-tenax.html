Astragalus tenax

Astragalus tenax, commonly known as the sticky milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically compound with numerous small leaflets. Astragalus tenax is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
