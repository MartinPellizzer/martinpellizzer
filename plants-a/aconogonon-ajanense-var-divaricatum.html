Aconogonon ajanense var. divaricatum

Aconogonon ajanense var. divaricatum, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branching stems and hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow and resemble those of the dandelion, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
