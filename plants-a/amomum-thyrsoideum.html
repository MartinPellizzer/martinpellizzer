Amomum thyrsoideum

Amomum thyrsoideum, commonly known as the long-leaf cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces tall, slender spikes of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by clustered, three-lobed berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its essential oils, particularly from the dried berries, contain compounds like cineole and limonene, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Amomum thyrsoideum thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.
