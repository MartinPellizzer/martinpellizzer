Adenophora alpina

Adenophora alpina, commonly known as the alpine adenophora, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features upright stems covered with small, glandular hairs, which give it its scientific name. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late summer.

Adenophora alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
