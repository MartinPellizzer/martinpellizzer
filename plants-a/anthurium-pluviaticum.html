Anthurium pluviaticum

Anthurium pluviaticum, commonly known as the rainforest anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are spathes that enclose the true flowers, often in vibrant red or orange hues. It thrives in warm, moist environments with indirect light and high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
