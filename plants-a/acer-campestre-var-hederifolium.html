Acer campestre var. hederifolium

Acer campestre var. hederifolium, commonly known as the creeping maple, is a compact shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often larger and more pronounced than those of the typical maple. The plant produces small, red or orange-red flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability.

This variety is particularly valued for its ability to form dense ground cover and its tolerance to urban environments.
