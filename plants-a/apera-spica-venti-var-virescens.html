Apera spica-venti var. virescens

Apera spica-venti var. virescens, commonly known as windgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, panicle-like cluster of spikelets.

It is known for its ability to thrive in windy conditions, hence its common name.
