Acacia perpusilla

Acacia perpusilla, commonly known as the small-flowered wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring small, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for native pollinators.

Acacia perpusilla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience.
