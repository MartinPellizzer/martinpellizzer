Asplenium trichomanes subsp. quadrivalens

Asplenium trichomanes subsp.

quadrivalens, commonly known as the four-veined spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae. It is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves with a distinctive quadrivalens venation pattern.

The fronds are typically short and have a narrow, lanceolate shape, with a prominent central vein and two pairs of lateral veins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or in crevices in temperate regions.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in shady gardens.
