Adenia aspidophylla

Adenia aspidophylla, commonly known as the African mountain gum.

It is a shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa, characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Adenia aspidophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
