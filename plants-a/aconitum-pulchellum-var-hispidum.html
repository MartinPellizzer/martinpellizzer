Aconitum pulchellum var. hispidum

Aconitum pulchellum var. hispidum, commonly known as the hispid monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly in the Alps. This perennial herb features striking blue to purple, helmet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat poisonous appearance.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in moist, shaded woodland habitats.
