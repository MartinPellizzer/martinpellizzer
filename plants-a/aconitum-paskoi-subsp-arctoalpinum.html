Aconitum paskoi subsp. arctoalpinum

Aconitum paskoi subsp.

arctoalpinum, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Aconitum paskoi subsp.

arctoalpinum is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
