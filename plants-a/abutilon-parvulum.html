Abutilon parvulum

Abutilon parvulum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a small, herbaceous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its fibrous roots and soft, velvety foliage make it a unique addition to botanical gardens.

Abutilon parvulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
