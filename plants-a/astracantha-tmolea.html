Astracantha tmolea

Astracantha tmolea, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
