Ammannia schlechteri

Ammannia schlechteri, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming during the wet season. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Ammannia schlechteri is often found in moist, well-drained soils near streams or in swampy areas.
