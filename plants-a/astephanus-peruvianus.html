Astephanus peruvianus

Astephanus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Astephanus peruvianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
