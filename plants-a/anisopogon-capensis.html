Anisopogon capensis

Anisopogon capensis, commonly known as the Cape anisopogon, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in full sun.

Anisopogon capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
