Aira caryophyllea subsp. plesiantha

Aira caryophyllea subsp.

plesiantha, commonly known as the Plesiantha grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pinkish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting native insect and bird populations.
