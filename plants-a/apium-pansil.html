Apium pansil

Apium pansil, commonly known as wild celery, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

Apium pansil is often found growing in moist, open habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
