Anthericum aitonii

Anthericum aitonii, commonly known as the fragrant cow-wheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Anthericum aitonii is known for its subtle, sweet fragrance, especially noticeable when the plant is in bloom.

It prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
