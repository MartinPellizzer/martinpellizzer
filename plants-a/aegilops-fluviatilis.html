Aegilops fluviatilis

Aegilops fluviatilis, commonly known as the river wild oat, is a grass species native to Eurasia and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and wetlands.

Aegilops fluviatilis is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.

Morphology

Aegilops fluviatilis has narrow, grass-like leaves that are linear in shape, alternate in arrangement, and typically range from 10 to 30 centimeters in length, with a bluish-green color.

Its stem is cylindrical, slightly hairy, and grows upright, with a pale green color and a smooth texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing florets that are pale green to brown in color, blooming from late spring to early summer, and is pollinated by wind. The fruit is a spikelet that contains small, oval seeds, which are dispersed by wind due to their lightweight structure.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, allowing the plant to thrive in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks.
