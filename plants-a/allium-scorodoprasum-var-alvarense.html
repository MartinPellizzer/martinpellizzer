Allium scorodoprasum var. alvarense

Allium scorodoprasum var. alvarense, commonly known as alpine chive, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant, lavender-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable addition to culinary and medicinal uses.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, alpine environments.
