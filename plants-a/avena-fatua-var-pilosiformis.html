Avena fatua var. pilosiformis

Avena fatua var. pilosiformis, commonly known as the silky oat, is a variant of the common oat that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its dense, silky hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. This variety typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields or along roadsides. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with florets that are adapted for wind pollination.

Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it a common weed in many regions.
