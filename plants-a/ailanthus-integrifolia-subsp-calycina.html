Ailanthus integrifolia subsp. calycina

Ailanthus integrifolia subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the Chinese tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to China. It features compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles.

The tree is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its fruits are woody capsules containing seeds with tufts of white hair for wind dispersal.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability.
