Acacia cinerea

Acacia cinerea, commonly known as silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, greyish branches and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. Acacia cinerea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
