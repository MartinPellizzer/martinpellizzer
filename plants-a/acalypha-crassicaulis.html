Acalypha crassicaulis

Acalypha crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed spurflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, woody stems and showy, spade-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with prominent bracts that can range in color from red to purple, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Acalypha crassicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
