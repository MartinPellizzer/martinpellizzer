Allium pauli

Allium pauli, commonly known as the Paul's onion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spherical bulb and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Allium pauli is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.
