Asplenium obovatum var. deltoideum

Asplenium obovatum var. deltoideum, commonly known as the deltoid maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, lanceolate to ovate-shaped fronds. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, finely toothed margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
