Aconitum chasmanthum

Aconitum chasmanthum, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
