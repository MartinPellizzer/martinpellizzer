Aulomyrcia obtusa var. tenuifolia

Aulomyrcia obtusa var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aulomyrcia, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically thin and have a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
