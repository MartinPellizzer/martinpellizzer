Arnica merriamii

Arnica merriamii, commonly known as the mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows. Arnica merriamii is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and has a compact growth habit.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
