Arnottia inermis

Arnottia inermis, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with white fringes, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Arnottia inermis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
