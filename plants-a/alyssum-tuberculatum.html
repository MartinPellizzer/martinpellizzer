Alyssum tuberculatum

Alyssum tuberculatum, commonly known as the mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alyssum tuberculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
