Amaranthus mitchellii var. typicus

Amaranthus mitchellii var. typicus, commonly known as Mitchell's amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, feather-like bracts that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional cuisines in some regions.
