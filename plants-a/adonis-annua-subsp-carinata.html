Adonis annua subsp. carinata

Adonis annua subsp.

carinata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically clustered in terminal racemes.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies carinata is distinguished by the presence of a keel or ridge on its flower petals, giving it its common name.
