Alchemilla acutangula var. typica

Alchemilla acutangula var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces upright stems bearing clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.
