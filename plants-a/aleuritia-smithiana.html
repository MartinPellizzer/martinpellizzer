Aleuritia smithiana

Aleuritia smithiana, commonly known as the silver bell, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a silvery underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Aleuritia smithiana is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
