Anacardium solitarium

Anacardium solitarium, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces elongated, woody pods containing the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The tree has a deep root system and can grow up to 30 meters tall, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically pollinated by insects, while its fruits mature over several months.

The plant is valued not only for its economic importance but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
