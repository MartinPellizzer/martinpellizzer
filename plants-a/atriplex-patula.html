Atriplex patula

Atriplex patula, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and salt-tolerant properties, making it well adapted to saline soils. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often found in desert environments. Its small, greenish flowers are inconspicuous but attract various pollinators.

Atriplex patula is also valued for its ornamental use and for its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
