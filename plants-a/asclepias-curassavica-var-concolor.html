Asclepias curassavica var. concolor

Asclepias curassavica var. concolor, commonly known as the tropical milkweed, is a species of milkweed native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which are clustered in umbels and attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces a milky sap that contains latex. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and ecological value.

This variety is particularly favored by monarch butterflies for laying eggs and feeding their larvae.
