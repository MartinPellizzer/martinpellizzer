Argyreia hirsuta var. coacta

Argyreia hirsuta var. coacta, commonly known as the velvet tassel vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a mild, tart flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
