Alloplectus calcaratus

Alloplectus calcaratus, commonly known as the sand spiker, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in loose, unstable substrates.

Alloplectus calcaratus plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
