Astragalus nitidulus

Astragalus nitidulus, commonly known as the smooth milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being smooth and lance-shaped.

Astragalus nitidulus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
