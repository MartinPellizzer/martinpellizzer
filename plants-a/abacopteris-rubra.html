Abacopteris rubra

Abacopteris rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Abacopteris rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
