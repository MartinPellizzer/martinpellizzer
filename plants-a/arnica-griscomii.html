Arnica griscomii

Arnica griscomii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill arnica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It is a perennial herb that produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
