Adesmia palenae

Adesmia palenae, commonly known as the pale adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
