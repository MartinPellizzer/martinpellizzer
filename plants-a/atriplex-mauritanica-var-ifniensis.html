Atriplex mauritanica var. ifniensis

Atriplex mauritanica var. ifniensis, commonly known as the Ifni saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its ability to tolerate high levels of salt. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that reduce water loss and aid in photosynthesis under harsh conditions. It produces yellow flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the Amaranthaceae family.

This variety is native to North Africa and is often found in coastal and inland salt marshes.
