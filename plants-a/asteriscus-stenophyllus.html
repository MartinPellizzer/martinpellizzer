Asteriscus stenophyllus

Asteriscus stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf asteriscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Asteriscus stenophyllus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
