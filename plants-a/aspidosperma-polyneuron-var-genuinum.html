Aspidosperma polyneuron var. genuinum

Aspidosperma polyneuron var. genuinum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerbera odollam*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.

However, its berries are toxic and should be handled with care.
