Aquilegia phoenicantha

Aquilegia phoenicantha, commonly known as the Persian columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in a raceme. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
