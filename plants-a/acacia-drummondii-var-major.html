Acacia drummondii var. major

Acacia drummondii var. major, commonly known as the Drummond's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
