Allium assimile

Allium assimile, commonly known as the broad-leaved garlic or wild garlic, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and bulbous underground stems. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels, which are followed by spherical, dry fruits containing several seeds. Allium assimile is valued for its culinary uses and has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and useful plant in both gardens and natural habitats.
