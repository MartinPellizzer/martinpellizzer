Alsophila pruinata var. concolor

Alsophila pruinata var. concolor, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, light green fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The fronds are typically arched and have a smooth, waxy texture, which contributes to its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
