Aster stenophyllus

Aster stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster stenophyllus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows.
