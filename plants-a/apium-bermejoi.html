Apium bermejoi

Apium bermejoi, commonly known as the Canary Islands celery, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in umbels. Its leaves are long and narrow, often resembling those of other celery species.

Apium bermejoi is typically found growing in rocky or coastal areas, adapting well to the island's unique climate.
