Aconitum paniculigerum var. wulingense

Aconitum paniculigerum var. wulingense, commonly known as the Wuling monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to China, particularly in the Wuling Mountains region, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a panicle, with large, helmet-shaped petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a waxy coating, typical of many members of the genus Aconitum.

The species contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle or ingest.
