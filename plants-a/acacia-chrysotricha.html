Acacia chrysotricha

Acacia chrysotricha, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
