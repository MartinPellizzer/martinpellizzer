Anthericum × confusum

Anthericum × confusum, commonly known as the hybrid arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Europe.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and umbellate inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a spadix, typical of the arum genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
