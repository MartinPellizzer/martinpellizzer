Alcea novopokrovskyi

Alcea novopokrovskyi, commonly known as the Novopokrovsky hollyhock, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive velvety texture. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Alcea novopokrovskyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
