Aconitum castellanum

Aconitum castellanum, commonly known as the Castellane monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum castellanum is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
