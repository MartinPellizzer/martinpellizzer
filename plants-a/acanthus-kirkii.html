Acanthus kirkii

Acanthus kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's spurge or African spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a short, upright stem and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its leaves.

Acanthus kirkii is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
