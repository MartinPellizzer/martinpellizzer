Auganthus saxatilis

Auganthus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock agapanthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky or well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Auganthus saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
