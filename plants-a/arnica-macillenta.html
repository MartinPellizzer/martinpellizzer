Arnica macillenta

Arnica macillenta, commonly known as leopard's bane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers with dark brown markings resembling a leopard's spots. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Arnica macillenta is known for its medicinal properties, though its use is often restricted due to its toxicity.
