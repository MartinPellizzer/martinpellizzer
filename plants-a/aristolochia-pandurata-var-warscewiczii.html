Aristolochia pandurata var. warscewiczii

Aristolochia pandurata var. warscewiczii, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Dutchman's Pipe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a mild, somewhat unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Aristolochiaceae family and is noted for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
