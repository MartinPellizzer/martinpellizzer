Anplectrum macrophyllum

Anplectrum macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals that open in the early morning and close at midday. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to support its growth.

Anplectrum macrophyllum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
