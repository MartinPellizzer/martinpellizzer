Alnus tinctoria f. hirsutoides

Alnus tinctoria f. hirsutoides, commonly known as the hairy alder, is a variant of the black alder native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and cylindrical cones. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or wetlands. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a serrated margin, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for various wildlife species.
