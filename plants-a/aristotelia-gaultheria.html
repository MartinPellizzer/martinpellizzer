Aristotelia gaultheria

Aristotelia gaultheria, commonly known as the silver pine or the New Zealand white pine, is a species of conifer native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray bark and long, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species in New Zealand's flora.
