Acinos alpinus subsp. nebrodensis

Acinos alpinus subsp.

nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebrodensis mountain everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of the Mediterranean. It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has a compact growth habit with wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species suited to harsh mountain climates.
