Abronia glaucescens

Abronia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland desert regions. It has small, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Abronia glaucescens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
