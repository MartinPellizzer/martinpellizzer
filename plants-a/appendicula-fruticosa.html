Appendicula fruticosa

Appendicula fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub appendicula, is a perennial plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months, often in shades of white or pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Appendicula fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
