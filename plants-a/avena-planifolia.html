Avena planifolia

Avena planifolia, commonly known as flat-leaf oat, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. Native to temperate regions, this plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value, particularly its grains. The leaves are typically used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate moisture for optimal growth.
