Arisaema exile

Arisaema exile, commonly known as the slender arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually arrow-shaped, and the spadix produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Arisaema exile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
