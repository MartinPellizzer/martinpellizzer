Argentina commutata var. polyandra

Argentina commutata var. polyandra, commonly known as the variable leaf hibiscus, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture and serrated edges.

The flowers bloom in summer and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
