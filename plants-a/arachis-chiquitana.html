Arachis chiquitana

Arachis chiquitana, commonly known as the Chiquitana groundnut, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in parts of Bolivia and Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its underground pods that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers, which contribute to its ecological and potential agricultural significance.
