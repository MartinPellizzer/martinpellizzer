Aphanactis ligulata

Aphanactis ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, typically arranged alternately along the stem.

Aphanactis ligulata is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.
