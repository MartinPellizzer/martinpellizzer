Agropyron varnense

Agropyron varnense, commonly known as the Varnense needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of inflorescences, which produce lightweight seeds dispersed by wind.

Agropyron varnense plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to harsh climates.
