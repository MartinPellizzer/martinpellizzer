Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco f. schlechtendaliana

Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco f. schlechtendaliana, commonly known as the white quebracho, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, elliptical pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, with various compounds used in traditional medicine.
