Aquilegia formosa var. fosteri

Aquilegia formosa var. fosteri, commonly known as Foster's columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to violet spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided, compound leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
