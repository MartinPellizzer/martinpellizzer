Astragalus antiochianus

Astragalus antiochianus, commonly known as the Antioch milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and sagebrush habitats. Astragalus antiochianus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
