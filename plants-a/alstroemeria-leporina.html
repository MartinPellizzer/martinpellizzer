Alstroemeria leporina

Alstroemeria leporina, commonly known as the rabbit's foot alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a perennial herb that produces clusters of flowers, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria leporina is also valued for its ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
