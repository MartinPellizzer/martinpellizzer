Apochloa bahiensis

Apochloa bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in sandy or coastal soils. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Apochloa bahiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
