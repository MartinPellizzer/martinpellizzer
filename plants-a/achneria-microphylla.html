Achneria microphylla

Achneria microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, rose-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its botanical name reflects its small leaves ("microphylla") and its classification within the Malvaceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
