Angraecum globulosocalcaratum

Angraecum globulosocalcaratum, commonly known as the globular calyx angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, globular inflorescence and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in the dry conditions of its environment.

Angraecum globulosocalcaratum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and rarity.
