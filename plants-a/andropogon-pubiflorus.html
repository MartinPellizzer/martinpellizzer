Andropogon pubiflorus

Andropogon pubiflorus, commonly known as the hairyflower bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, often found in swampy areas and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

Andropogon pubiflorus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
