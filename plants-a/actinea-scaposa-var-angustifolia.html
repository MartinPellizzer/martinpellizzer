Actinea scaposa var. angustifolia

Actinea scaposa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved actinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers in clusters during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive member of the Araliaceae family.
