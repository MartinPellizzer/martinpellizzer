Acacia rhetinocarpa

Acacia rhetinocarpa, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods that split open when mature, releasing the seeds.

Acacia rhetinocarpa is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
