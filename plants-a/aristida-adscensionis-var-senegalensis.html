Aristida adscensionis var. senegalensis

Aristida adscensionis var. senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese dropseed, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or tufts. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for dry, open habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
