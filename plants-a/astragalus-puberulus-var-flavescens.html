Astragalus puberulus var. flavescens

Astragalus puberulus var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
