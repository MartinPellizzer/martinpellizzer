Atriplex cinerea f. appendiculata

Atriplex cinerea f. appendiculata, commonly known as silverleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs that reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a waxy texture. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
