Allophylus concanicus var. lanceolatus

Allophylus concanicus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate alpine holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically leathery and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
