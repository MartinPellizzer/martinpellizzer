Alchemilla gracilipes var. lovenii

Alchemilla gracilipes var. lovenii, commonly known as the slender alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a silky, white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
