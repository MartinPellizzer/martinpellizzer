Adenophora capillaris subsp. dzukoensis

Adenophora capillaris subsp.

dzukoensis, commonly known as the Dzukoensis bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to specific regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged alternately along the stem. This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
