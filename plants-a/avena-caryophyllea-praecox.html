Avena caryophyllea praecox

Avena caryophyllea praecox, commonly known as the early purple wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, pink to purple flowers in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
