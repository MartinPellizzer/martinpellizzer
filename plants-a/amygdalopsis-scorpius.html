Amygdalopsis scorpius

Amygdalopsis scorpius, commonly known as the scorpion bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, scaly pods containing edible seeds. Its name "scorpion bean" refers to the shape of its seed pods, which resemble a scorpion's tail.

Amygdalopsis scorpius is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
