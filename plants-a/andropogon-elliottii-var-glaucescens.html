Andropogon elliottii var. glaucescens

Andropogon elliottii var. glaucescens, commonly known as bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and wetlands, preferring moist to wet soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
