Astracantha thracica subsp. jankae

Astracantha thracica subsp.

jankae, commonly known as the Jankae's thornvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and genetic traits compared to the main species.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
