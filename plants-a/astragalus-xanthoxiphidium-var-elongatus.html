Astragalus xanthoxiphidium var. elongatus

Astragalus xanthoxiphidium var. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
