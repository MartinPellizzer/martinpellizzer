Axanthes enneandra

Axanthes enneandra, commonly known as the red-flowered rockrose, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its woody stems and small, showy red flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Axanthes enneandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
