Arundinaria falcata var. glomerata

Arundinaria falcata var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered bamboo, is a variant of the falcate bamboo species.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit, which forms tight clumps of stalks. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slightly zigzagged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and structural form.
