Amyema barbellata

Amyema barbellata, commonly known as the barbellate amyema, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, barbed bracts that give it its common name. It produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Amyema barbellata is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
