Astragalus spiciformis

Astragalus spiciformis, commonly known as the spiciform milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus spiciformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
