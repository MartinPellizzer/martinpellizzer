Astragalus monspessulanus f. longiscapus

Astragalus monspessulanus f. longiscapus, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
