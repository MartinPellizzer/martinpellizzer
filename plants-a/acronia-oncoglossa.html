Acronia oncoglossa

Acronia oncoglossa, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique leaf shape and delicate flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
