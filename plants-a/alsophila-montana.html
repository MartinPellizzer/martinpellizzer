Alsophila montana

Alsophila montana, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its stout, upright rhizomes and dense, dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually pinnately compound, with a distinctive, rugged appearance.

Alsophila montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability.
