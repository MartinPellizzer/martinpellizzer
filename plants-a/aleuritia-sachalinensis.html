Aleuritia sachalinensis

Aleuritia sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Aleuritia sachalinensis is also noted for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
