Achyronia carnosa

Achyronia carnosa, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Achyronia carnosa is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
