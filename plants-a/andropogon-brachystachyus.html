Andropogon brachystachyus

Andropogon brachystachyus, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades that turn coppery in the fall. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Andropogon brachystachyus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
