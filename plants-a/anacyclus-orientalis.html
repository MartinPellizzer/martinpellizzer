Anacyclus orientalis

Anacyclus orientalis, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are deep and fibrous, aiding in water retention.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
