Alchemilla pentaphylla

Alchemilla pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped and have a distinctive waxy texture.

Alchemilla pentaphylla is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
