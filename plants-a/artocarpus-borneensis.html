Artocarpus borneensis

Artocarpus borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Borneo.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces a large, spiky fruit that ripens from green to yellowish-brown. The fruit contains a soft, starchy pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer.

It is valued for its edible fruit and has traditional uses in local cuisines and medicine.
