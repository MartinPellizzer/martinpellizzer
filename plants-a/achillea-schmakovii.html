Achillea schmakovii

Achillea schmakovii, commonly known as Schmakov's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads and finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its hardy nature. It produces small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Schmakov's yarrow is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
