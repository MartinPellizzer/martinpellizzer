Apluda pedicellata

Apluda pedicellata, commonly known as the clustered apluda, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas. It has fibrous roots and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

Apluda pedicellata is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance.
