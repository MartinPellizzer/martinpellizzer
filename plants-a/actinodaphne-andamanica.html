Actinodaphne andamanica

Actinodaphne andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman nut or wild areca nut, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces dark, fleshy fruits that encase a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its botanical features include smooth, dark bark and opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ecological role and cultural significance in the region.
