Arisaema pseudojaponicum f. serratifolium

Arisaema pseudojaponicum f. serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated-leaf arisaema, is a variant of the arisaema genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a typical feature of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and shade gardens.
