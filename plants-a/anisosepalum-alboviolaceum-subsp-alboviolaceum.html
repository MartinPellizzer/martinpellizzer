Anisosepalum alboviolaceum subsp. alboviolaceum

Anisosepalum alboviolaceum subsp.

alboviolaceum, commonly known as the white-violet anisosepalum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, pale violet to white flowers.

The plant typically produces showy, spurred orchid flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and adaptability to specific growing conditions.
