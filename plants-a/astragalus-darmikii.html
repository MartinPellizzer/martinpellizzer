Astragalus darmikii

Astragalus darmikii, commonly known as the Darmik's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
