Allium songpanicum

Allium songpanicum, commonly known as the Chinese leek or Chinese chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its hollow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spherical clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs, which are edible, are often used in Chinese cuisine for their mild, garlic-like flavor.

Allium songpanicum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
