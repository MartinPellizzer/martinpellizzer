Azalea × amoena alba

Azalea × amoena alba, commonly known as the white azalea, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron obtuse and Rhododendron indicum.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is evergreen, with dense, leathery foliage that provides year-round visual interest. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
