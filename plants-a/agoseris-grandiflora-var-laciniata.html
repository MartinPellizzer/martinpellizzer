Agoseris grandiflora var. laciniata

Agoseris grandiflora var. laciniata, commonly known as the fringed larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
