Amaranthus × wilczekii

Amaranthus × wilczekii, commonly known as the hybrid amaranth, is a species resulting from the cross between Amaranthus hypochondriacus and Amaranthus caudatus.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to orange and purple. The plant is an annual herb native to the Americas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while the seeds are rich in protein and nutrients.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
