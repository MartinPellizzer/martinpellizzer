Acer platanoides var. integrilobum

Acer platanoides var. integrilobum, commonly known as the European mountain ash, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a rounded, open crown. The leaves are palmately lobed, with five to seven lobes, and are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. In the fall, it displays striking golden to orange foliage, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
