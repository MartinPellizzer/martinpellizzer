Azalea pallida

Azalea pallida, commonly known as the pale azalea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a leathery texture, and it produces small, elliptical berries that contain seeds.

Azalea pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
