Aulomyrcia garopabensis

Aulomyrcia garopabensis, commonly known as the Garopabense cactus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and reduced leaves, which are adaptations to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well-suited for survival in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
