Allium valentinae

Allium valentinae, commonly known as the Valentina garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late summer, which are followed by small, round bulbs. Allium valentinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and botanical collections.
