Alchemilla alpina var. hoppeana

Alchemilla alpina var. hoppeana, commonly known as the Alpine Lady's Mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves that form a rosette near the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in alpine gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
