Aristolochia lingulata

Aristolochia lingulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex and often aromatic floral structures.

The plant is also notable for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
