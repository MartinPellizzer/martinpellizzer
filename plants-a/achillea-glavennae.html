Achillea glavennae

Achillea glavennae, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters.

Achillea glavennae is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
