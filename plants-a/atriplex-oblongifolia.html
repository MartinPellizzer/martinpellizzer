Atriplex oblongifolia

Atriplex oblongifolia, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well adapted to saline soils and coastal areas.

Atriplex oblongifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
