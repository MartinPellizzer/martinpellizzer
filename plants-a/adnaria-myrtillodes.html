Adnaria myrtillodes

Adnaria myrtillodes, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant has evergreen leaves that are glossy and oval in shape. It produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Adnaria myrtillodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
