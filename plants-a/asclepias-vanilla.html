Asclepias vanilla

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers, which are clustered in umbels and attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. Its milky sap contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to many animals but serve as a defense mechanism.
