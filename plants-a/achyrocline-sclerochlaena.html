Achyrocline sclerochlaena

Achyrocline sclerochlaena, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by native insects and birds. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinctive scent, contributing to its ecological significance in the region.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected under conservation efforts.
