Astrocaryum gynacanthum var. munbaca

Astrocaryum gynacanthum var. munbaca, commonly known as the Munbaca palm, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts, while the trunk is valued for its durable, fibrous material.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
