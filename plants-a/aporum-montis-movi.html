Aporum montis-movi

Aporum montis-movi, commonly known as the mountain-moving plant, is a rare and elusive species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and dense, waxy foliage that helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant has a unique root system that allows it to anchor itself in rocky soil, making it highly adapted to its harsh habitat. Its flowering period coincides with the brief summer months, during which it produces a large number of small, fragrant blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
