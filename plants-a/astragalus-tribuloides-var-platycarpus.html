Astragalus tribuloides var. platycarpus

Astragalus tribuloides var. platycarpus, commonly known as the broad-fruited milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

It is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
