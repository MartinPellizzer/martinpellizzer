Alsophila magnifolia

Alsophila magnifolia, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate fronds that give it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its unique structure and growth pattern make it a striking feature in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
