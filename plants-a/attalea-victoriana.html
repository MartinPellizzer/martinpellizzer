Attalea victoriana

Attalea victoriana, commonly known as the Amazonian palm or Victoria palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, with long, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces large, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of edible fruits, which are consumed by both wildlife and humans.

Its botanical structure includes a robust trunk with a fibrous outer layer, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
