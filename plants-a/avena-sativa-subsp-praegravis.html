Avena sativa subsp. praegravis

Avena sativa subsp.

praegravis, commonly known as the early barley, is a subspecies of barley characterized by its shorter growth cycle and earlier maturation compared to other barley varieties. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is cultivated for its grain, which is used in food and animal feed.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and spike-like inflorescences bearing small, two-lobed grains. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability to cooler climates and its potential for sustainable agricultural practices.
