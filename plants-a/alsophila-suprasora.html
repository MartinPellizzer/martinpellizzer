Alsophila suprasora

Alsophila suprasora, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its fronds, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.

Alsophila suprasora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
