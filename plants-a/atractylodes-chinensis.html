Atractylodes chinensis

Atractylodes chinensis, commonly known as Chinese Atractylodes, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Atractylodes chinensis is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal formulations.
