Asplenium crassum

Asplenium crassum, commonly known as the broad holly fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its broad, leathery fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asplenium crassum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
