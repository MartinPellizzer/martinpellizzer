Aspilia monopetala

Aspilia monopetala, commonly known as the single-petaled aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces a single, showy yellow petal surrounded by ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and savannas.

Aspilia monopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
