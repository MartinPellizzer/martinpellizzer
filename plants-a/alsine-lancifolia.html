Alsine lancifolia

Alsine lancifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Alsine lancifolia is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
