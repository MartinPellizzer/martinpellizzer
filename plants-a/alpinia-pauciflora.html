Alpinia pauciflora

Alpinia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in compact inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Alpinia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
