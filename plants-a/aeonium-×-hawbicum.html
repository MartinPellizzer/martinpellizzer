Aeonium × hawbicum

Aeonium × hawbicum, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent perennial native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-gray to purple leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with full sun exposure.

Aeonium × hawbicum is popular in rock gardens and as a container plant due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
