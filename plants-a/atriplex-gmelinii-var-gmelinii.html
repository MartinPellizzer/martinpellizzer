Atriplex gmelinii var. gmelinii

Atriplex gmelinii var. gmelinii, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is highly adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing coastal and desert environments. Its leaves contain high levels of minerals, which have been used historically by indigenous peoples for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

The plant's ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it an important component of xerophytic ecosystems.
