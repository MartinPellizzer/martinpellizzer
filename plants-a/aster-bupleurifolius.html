Aster bupleurifolius

Aster bupleurifolius, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
