Angelica keiskei

Angelica keiskei, commonly known as Japanese angelica or "angel's hair," is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, feathery leaves that resemble plumes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Angelica keiskei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
