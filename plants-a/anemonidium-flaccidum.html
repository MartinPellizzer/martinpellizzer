Anemonidium flaccidum

Anemonidium flaccidum, commonly known as the slender anemonidium, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Anemonidium flaccidum is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
