Aster marshallii

Aster marshallii, commonly known as Marshall's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it blooms from late summer into early fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.

Aster marshallii is also noted for its adaptability to partial shade and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
