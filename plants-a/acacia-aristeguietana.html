Acacia aristeguietana

Acacia aristeguietana, commonly known as the Aristeguietana acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the dry regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, often spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in poor soil conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
