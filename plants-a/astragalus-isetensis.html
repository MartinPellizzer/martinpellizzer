Astragalus isetensis

Astragalus isetensis, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. Astragalus isetensis is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments, showcasing its resilience to extreme conditions.

Its ecological role includes contributing to soil stabilization and providing food for local wildlife.
