Asplenium obovatum var. numidicum

Asplenium obovatum var. numidicum, commonly known as the Numidian spleenwort, is a fern native to Mediterranean regions and parts of North Africa.

It is a small, evergreen fern with compact, rosette-like growth habits, typically forming dense clusters of dark green, leathery, ovate to obovate leaves. The leaves are usually glossy and have a distinctive, slightly wavy margin. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocks or in crevices.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
