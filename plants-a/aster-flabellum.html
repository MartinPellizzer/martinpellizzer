Aster flabellum

Aster flabellum, commonly known as the flatleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
