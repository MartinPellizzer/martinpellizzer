Acropera amparoana

Acropera amparoana, commonly known as the Amparo orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
