Ardisia sapoana

Ardisia sapoana, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Ardisia sapoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
