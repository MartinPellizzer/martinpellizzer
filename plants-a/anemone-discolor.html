Anemone discolor

Anemone discolor, commonly known as the white wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has palmately divided, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its root system consists of a short, thick rhizome that supports its growth in woodland and meadow habitats.

Anemone discolor is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and moist, well-drained soils.
