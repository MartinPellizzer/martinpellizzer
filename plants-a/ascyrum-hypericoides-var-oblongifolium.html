Ascyrum hypericoides var. oblongifolium

Ascyrum hypericoides var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and small, yellowish flowers that appear in late summer. The leaves are elongated and slightly serrated, with a pale green upper surface and a whitish underside. This variant is often found in sunny, well-drained soils in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

It is part of the Hypericaceae family and has been used historically for its purported medicinal properties.
