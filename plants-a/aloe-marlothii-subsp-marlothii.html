Aloe marlothii subsp. marlothii

Aloe marlothii subsp.

marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a landscape plant.
