Acacia kamerunensis

Acacia kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Cameroon and adjacent regions of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact globular heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia kamerunensis is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical savanna ecosystems.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including medicinal and construction applications.
