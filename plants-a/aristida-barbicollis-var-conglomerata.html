Aristida barbicollis var. conglomerata

Aristida barbicollis var. conglomerata, commonly known as the clustered beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability.
