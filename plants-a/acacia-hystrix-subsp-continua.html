Acacia hystrix subsp. continua

Acacia hystrix subsp.

continua, commonly known as the continuous acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its seed pods are elongated and contain small, hard seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
