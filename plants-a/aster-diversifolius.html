Aster diversifolius

Aster diversifolius, commonly known as the diverse-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple to blue flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Aster diversifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
