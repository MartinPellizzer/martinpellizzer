Astragalus chorassanicus

Astragalus chorassanicus, commonly known as the Karakorum milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
