Allium rausii

Allium rausii, commonly known as the Persian onion, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and spherical, clustered bulbs. The plant produces small, pale purple or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in cooking.

Allium rausii is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility.
