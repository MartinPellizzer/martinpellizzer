Allamanda puberula

Allamanda puberula, commonly known as the velvet trumpet vine, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as a decorative ornamental. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allamanda puberula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
