Anerincleistus cyathocalyx

Anerincleistus cyathocalyx, commonly known as the purple-flowered sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a sprawling habit with succulent, fleshy stems that help it retain water in dry conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in reducing water loss.

Anerincleistus cyathocalyx is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
