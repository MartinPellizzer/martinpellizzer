Acalypha albicans

Acalypha albicans, commonly known as the whiteleaf horsebrush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are typically white or pale green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or yellowish in hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Acalypha albicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
