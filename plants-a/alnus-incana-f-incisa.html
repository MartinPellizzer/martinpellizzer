Alnus incana f. incisa

Alnus incana f. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alder, is a shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
