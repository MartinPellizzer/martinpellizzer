Achyrachaena mollis

Achyrachaena mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, soft pubescence, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields due to its adaptability.

Achyrachaena mollis is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
