Apium ammoides

Apium ammoides, commonly known as slender cow parsley, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to greenish-yellow flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it often grows in moist meadows and along stream banks.

It is considered a herbaceous perennial, typically reaching heights of 30 to 100 centimeters.
