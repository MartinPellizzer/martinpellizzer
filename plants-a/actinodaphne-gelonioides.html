Actinodaphne gelonioides

Actinodaphne gelonioides, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
