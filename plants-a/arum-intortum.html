Arum intortum

Arum intortum, commonly known as the twisted arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by a showy, modified leaf called a spathe.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shaded environments.
