Artemisia salsoloides var. prattii

Artemisia salsoloides var. prattii, commonly known as Pratt's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
