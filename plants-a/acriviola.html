Acriviola

Acriviola, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Acriviola is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses in certain regions.
