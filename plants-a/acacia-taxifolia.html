Acacia taxifolia

Acacia taxifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Acacia taxifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
