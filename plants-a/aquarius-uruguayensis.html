Aquarius uruguayensis

Aquarius uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves, which resemble the spokes of a wheel, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as rivers and lakes. Its leaves are typically thin and ribbon-like, adapted for efficient water flow.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
