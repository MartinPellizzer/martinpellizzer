Arum detruncatum

Arum detruncatum, commonly known as the truncated arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or green, with a distinctive, often fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Arum detruncatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden environments.
