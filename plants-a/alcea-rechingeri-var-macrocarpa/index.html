Alcea Rechingeri Var. Macrocarpa

Alcea rechingeri var. macrocarpa is a striking member of the hibiscus family known for its large, showy flowers. This plant is native to the arid regions of Central Asia and thrives in hot, dry climates. Its thick, fleshy stems and broad leaves help it conserve water in harsh environments. The vivid pink to red blooms appear in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is a popular choice for xeriscaping and adds dramatic visual interest to gardens.
