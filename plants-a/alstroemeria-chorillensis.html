Alstroemeria chorillensis

Alstroemeria chorillensis, commonly known as the Chilean flame lily, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces multiple flower spikes throughout the growing season.

Alstroemeria chorillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
