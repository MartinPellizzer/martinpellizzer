Acosta rufidula

Acosta rufidula, commonly known as the red-stemmed acosta, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking red stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
