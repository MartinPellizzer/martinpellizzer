Alstroemeria pulchra var. maxima

Alstroemeria pulchra var. maxima, commonly known as the Chilean fire lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped blooms that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and can grow up to 3 feet tall, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate water and cool temperatures for optimal growth.
