Amomum hochreutineri

Amomum hochreutineri, commonly known as the Chinese cardamom, is a perennial herb native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, clustered spikes of pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oblong fruits that are rich in essential oils and have a strong, spicy aroma. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The botanical features of A. hochreutineri make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
