Axonopus savannarum

Axonopus savannarum, commonly known as the savanna grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as savannas and grasslands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense tufts. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Axonopus savannarum is well adapted to withstand drought and periodic fires, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
