Aegiceras obovatum

Aegiceras obovatum, commonly known as the mangrove apple or mangrove fig, is a halophytic tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines. The plant has a distinctive appearance with smooth, dark-barked stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color, attracting various bird species.

Aegiceras obovatum is also notable for its ability to tolerate saltwater and contribute to the ecological balance of coastal ecosystems.

Morphology

Aegiceras obovatum has broad, obovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, have four petals, and bloom from July to September, relying on wind for pollination.
