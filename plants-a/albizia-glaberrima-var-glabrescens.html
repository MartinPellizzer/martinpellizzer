Albizia glaberrima var. glabrescens

Albizia glaberrima var. glabrescens, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
