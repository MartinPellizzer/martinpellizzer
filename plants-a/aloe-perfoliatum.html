Aloe perfoliatum

Aloe perfoliatum, commonly known as the perforate aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branching flower stalks that bear yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Aloe perfoliatum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.
