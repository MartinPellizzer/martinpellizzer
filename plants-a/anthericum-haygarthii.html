Anthericum haygarthii

Anthericum haygarthii, commonly known as the Haygarth's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a showy, purple spathe. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, known for its unique floral structures that include both male and female flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and greenhouse collections.
