Artemisia nipponica var. electa

Artemisia nipponica var. electa, commonly known as Japanese mugwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Due to its strong scent, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
