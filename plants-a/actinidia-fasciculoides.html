Actinidia fasciculoides

Actinidia fasciculoides, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, yellowish-orange berries in the fall. These berries are often consumed by birds and small mammals, aiding in seed dispersal.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
