Acladodea pinnata

Acladodea pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue.

Acladodea pinnata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
