Alnus glabrata var. durangensis

Alnus glabrata var. durangensis, commonly known as the Durango alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and catkin-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in high-altitude environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
