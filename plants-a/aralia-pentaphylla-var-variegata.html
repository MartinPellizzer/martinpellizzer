Aralia pentaphylla var. variegata

Aralia pentaphylla var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Japanese aralia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features large, palmate leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern of white and green, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early summer, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the year, providing ornamental value in both spring and winter.
