Abies lasciocarpa

Abies lasciocarpa, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal crown and needle-like leaves that are typically a dark green on top and whitish underneath. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is generally smooth and grayish-brown, becoming furrowed with age.

Abies lasciocarpa is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
