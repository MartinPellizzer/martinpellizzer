Astragalus maulensis

Astragalus maulensis, commonly known as the Maulens milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus maulensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
