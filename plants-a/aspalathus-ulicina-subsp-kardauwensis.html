Aspalathus ulicina subsp. kardauwensis

Aspalathus ulicina subsp.

kardauwensis, commonly known as the Kardauw wild red bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered pink or red flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
