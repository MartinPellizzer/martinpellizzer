Adesmia quadrijuga

Adesmia quadrijuga, commonly known as the four-armed adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, often found in dry or semi-arid habitats. Its root system is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
