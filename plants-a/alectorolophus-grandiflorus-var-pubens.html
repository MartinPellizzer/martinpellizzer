Alectorolophus grandiflorus var. pubens

Alectorolophus grandiflorus var. pubens, commonly known as the hairy large-flowered bellflower, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, arid environments. Its leaves are hairy, giving it the specific epithet "pubens," and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
