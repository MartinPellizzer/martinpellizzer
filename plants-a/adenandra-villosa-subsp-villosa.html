Adenandra villosa subsp. villosa

Adenandra villosa subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy adenandra, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Adenandra villosa subsp.

villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
