Aristolochia castellanosii

Aristolochia castellanosii, commonly known as the Castellanos's Dutchman's pipe, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is a climbing vine native to Mexico, characterized by its distinctive tubular, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces showy, often fragrant blooms in shades of green, yellow, and brown. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

The species is known for its unique floral morphology and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
