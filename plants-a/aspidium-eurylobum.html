Aspidium eurylobum

Aspidium eurylobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed water fern, is a member of the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has a creeping rhizome with fronds that are pinnately divided and often have a distinctive, broad-lobed appearance. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Aspidium eurylobum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
