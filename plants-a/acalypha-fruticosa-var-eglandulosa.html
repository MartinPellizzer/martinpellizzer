Acalypha fruticosa var. eglandulosa

Acalypha fruticosa var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by orange or red berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
