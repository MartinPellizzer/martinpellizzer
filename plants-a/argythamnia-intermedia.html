Argythamnia intermedia

Argythamnia intermedia, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and Middle East.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub with silvery-gray foliage that reflects sunlight and reduces water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor, sandy soils and withstand drought conditions.

Argythamnia intermedia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
