Acacia salicina var. typica

Acacia salicina var. typica, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a fast-growing, deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

However, it can be prone to root rot in poorly drained conditions.
