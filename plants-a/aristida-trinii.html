Aristida trinii

Aristida trinii, commonly known as the narrowleaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Aristida trinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions with minimal water requirements.
