Achyranthes aspera f. suffruticosa

Achyranthes aspera f. suffruticosa, commonly known as the rough knotweed, is a shrub-like variant of the species Achyranthes aspera.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
