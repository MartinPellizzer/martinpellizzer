Artabotrys hexapetalus

Artabotrys hexapetalus, commonly known as the fragrant star jasmine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, star-shaped white flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Artabotrys hexapetalus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
