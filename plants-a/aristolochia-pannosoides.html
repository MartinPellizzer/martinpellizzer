Aristolochia pannosoides

Aristolochia pannosoides, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and intricate, pipe-like flowers that are often greenish-brown in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. It contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals, making it a plant of both ecological and medicinal interest.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
