Alnus alnobetula var. fruticosa

Alnus alnobetula var. fruticosa, commonly known as the red alder, is a shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and usually have 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.
