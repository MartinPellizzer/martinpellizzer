Astragalus muschianus

Astragalus muschianus, commonly known as the Musk Valley milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
