Argyranthemum dissectum

Argyranthemum dissectum, commonly known as the Shiny Gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, silver-green leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and vibrant blooms, which appear throughout the growing season.

Argyranthemum dissectum is often used in borders, containers, and as a bedding plant due to its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers.
