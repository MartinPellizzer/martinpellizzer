Abutilon cyclonervosum

Abutilon cyclonervosum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, cotton-like seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners and botanists alike.
