Acularia australis

Acularia australis, commonly known as the Australian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a robust stem with hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Acularia australis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
