Atropis capillaris

Atropis capillaris, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs and produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slight curl at the edges.

Atropis capillaris is known for its milky sap, which is typical of the Apocynaceae family and can be toxic to some animals.
