Andropogon ruprechtii

Andropogon ruprechtii, commonly known as Ruprecht's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish hues.

Andropogon ruprechtii plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
