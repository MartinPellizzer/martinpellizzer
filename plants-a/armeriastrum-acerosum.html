Armeriastrum acerosum

Armeriastrum acerosum, commonly known as the sharpleaf armeria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular plant in native gardens.
