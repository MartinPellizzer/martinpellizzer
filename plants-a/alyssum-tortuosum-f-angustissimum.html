Alyssum tortuosum f. angustissimum

Alyssum tortuosum f. angustissimum, commonly known as narrowleaf alpine alyssum, is a compact, mat-forming plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hairs, contributing to its frost-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh mountain environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in difficult garden conditions.
