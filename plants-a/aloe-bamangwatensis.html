Aloe bamangwatensis

Aloe bamangwatensis, commonly known as the Bamangwata aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Aloe bamangwatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
