Aegilops comosa subsp. heldreichii

Aegilops comosa subsp.

heldreichii, commonly known as the Heldreich's goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with lemmas that have distinct awns. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Botanically, it is noted for its genetic diversity and potential role in breeding programs for wheat improvement.
