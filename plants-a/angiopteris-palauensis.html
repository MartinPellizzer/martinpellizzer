Angiopteris palauensis

Angiopteris palauensis, commonly known as the Palau fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Angiopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Palau, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of most ferns.

Angiopteris palauensis plays a significant role in the local ecosystem by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various small organisms.
