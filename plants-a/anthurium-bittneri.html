Anthurium bittneri

Anthurium bittneri, commonly known as the Bittner's flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is often the main ornamental feature. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium bittneri is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
