Aegilops vavilovii

Aegilops vavilovii, commonly known as the Vavilov's wild wheat, is a grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. This plant is notable for its genetic relationship to cultivated wheat, making it an important species in wheat evolution studies. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Aegilops vavilovii is also valued for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience and yield.
