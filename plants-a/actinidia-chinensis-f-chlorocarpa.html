Actinidia chinensis f. chlorocarpa

Actinidia chinensis f. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-fruit kiwifruit, is a botanical variant of the Chinese actinidia species.

It is characterized by its distinctively green, smooth-skinned fruits that mature to a bright yellow or golden color. The plant is a vigorous vine that thrives in temperate climates and requires a cold winter dormancy period. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin, and the flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in groups of three.

This cultivar is valued for its sweet, aromatic fruit and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
