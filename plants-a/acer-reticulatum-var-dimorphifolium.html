Acer reticulatum var. dimorphifolium

Acer reticulatum var. dimorphifolium, commonly known as the variable maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which includes both palmate and pinnate leaf forms on the same plant, giving it a unique appearance. The leaves are typically lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact size and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to landscape design.
