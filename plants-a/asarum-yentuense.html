Asarum yentuense

Asarum yentuense, commonly known as Yentu Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, hooded flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often concealed by a complex, petal-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The roots of Asarum yentuense contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.
