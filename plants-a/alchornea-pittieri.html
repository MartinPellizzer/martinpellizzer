Alchornea pittieri

Alchornea pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-colored berries that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface.
