Aristolochia roxburghiana subsp. kankauensis

Aristolochia roxburghiana subsp.

kankauensis, commonly known as the Kankau arrow vine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its unique, saddle-shaped leaves and intricate, pipe-like flowers that are often purple or greenish in color.

The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, typically in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
