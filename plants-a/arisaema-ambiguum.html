Arisaema ambiguum

Arisaema ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous arisaema, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and its unique, spadix-bearing inflorescence. The plant produces a large, hooded spathe that protects the spadix, which contains the actual flowers. Arisaema ambiguum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unusual floral structure.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
