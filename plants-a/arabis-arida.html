Arabis arida

Arabis arida, commonly known as the desert rockcress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are usually basal, fleshy, and succulent, helping it retain moisture in harsh environments.
