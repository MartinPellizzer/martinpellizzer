Anaxagorea panamensis

Anaxagorea panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes cashew and mango trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rough, fissured bark. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by clustered, red berries that are edible for some bird species.
