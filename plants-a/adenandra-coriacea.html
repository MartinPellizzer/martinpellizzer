Adenandra coriacea

Adenandra coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved adenandra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant has thick, rough, and leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters, and it produces woody pods containing seeds.

Adenandra coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
