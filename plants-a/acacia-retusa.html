Acacia retusa

Acacia retusa, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, which are typical of the legume family.

Acacia retusa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry regions.
