Annona primigenia

Annona primigenia, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a smooth, spiky-skinned fruit that turns from green to yellow as it matures. The fruit is rich in nutrients and has a creamy, sweet flavor with a slight tartness.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value in warm climates.
