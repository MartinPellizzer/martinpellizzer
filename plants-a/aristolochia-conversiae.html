Aristolochia conversiae

Aristolochia conversiae, commonly known as the Mexican Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces small, dark berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic compounds that can be harmful to humans and animals.
