Allium daghestanicum

Allium daghestanicum, commonly known as the Daghestan leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical purple flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
