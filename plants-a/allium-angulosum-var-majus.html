Allium angulosum var. majus

Allium angulosum var. majus, commonly known as the giant bulb onion, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

The botanical features of this plant make it a distinctive and valuable addition to both gardens and agricultural settings.
