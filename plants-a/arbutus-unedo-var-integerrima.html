Arbutus unedo var. integerrima

Arbutus unedo var. integerrima, commonly known as the Canary Island heath, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are leathery and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environments.
