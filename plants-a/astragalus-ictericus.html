Astragalus ictericus

Astragalus ictericus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus ictericus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
