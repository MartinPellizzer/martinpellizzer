Aeschynanthus praelongus

Aeschynanthus praelongus, commonly known as the long-tailed gloxinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings.

Aeschynanthus praelongus thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil for optimal growth.
