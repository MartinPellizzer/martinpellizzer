Aster heterochaeta

Aster heterochaeta, commonly known as the hooked aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and hairy, with a distinctive hooked appearance on the stems.

The flowers are blue to purple in color, with ray florets that bloom in the summer months.
