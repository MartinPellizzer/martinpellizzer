Anil trifoliata

Anil trifoliata, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its yellow, fleshy fruit that is rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its botanical features include small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the edible, sour fruit.

Anil trifoliata is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.
