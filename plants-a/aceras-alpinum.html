Aceras alpinum

Aceras alpinum, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.
