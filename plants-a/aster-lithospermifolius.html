Aster lithospermifolius

Aster lithospermifolius, commonly known as the stonecrop aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are often thick and fleshy, a feature that helps it conserve water.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically blue to purple in color, attracting various pollinators.
