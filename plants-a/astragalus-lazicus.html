Astragalus lazicus

Astragalus lazicus, commonly known as the Turkmenian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, enabling it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus lazicus is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
