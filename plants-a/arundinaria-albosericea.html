Arundinaria albosericea

Arundinaria albosericea, commonly known as the silver bamboo or white-haired bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white down. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it can reach heights of up to 10 feet.

Arundinaria albosericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
