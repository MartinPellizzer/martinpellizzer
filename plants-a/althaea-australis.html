Althaea australis

Althaea australis, commonly known as the Australian marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Althaea australis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like hibiscus and cotton.
