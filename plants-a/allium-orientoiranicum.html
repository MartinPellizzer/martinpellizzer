Allium orientoiranicum

Allium orientoiranicum, commonly known as the Eastern Iranian leek, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to onions, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Allium orientoiranicum is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
