Atriplex madariagae

Atriplex madariagae, commonly known as the Madariaga saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Atriplex madariagae plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
