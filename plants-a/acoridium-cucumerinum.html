Acoridium cucumerinum

Acoridium cucumerinum, commonly known as the cucumber lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family and is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Acoridium cucumerinum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
