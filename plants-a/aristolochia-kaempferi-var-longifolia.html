Aristolochia kaempferi var. longifolia

Aristolochia kaempferi var. longifolia, commonly known as the Japanese Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a dark blue, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
