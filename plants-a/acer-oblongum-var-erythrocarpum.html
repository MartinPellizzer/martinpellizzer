Acer oblongum var. erythrocarpum

Acer oblongum var. erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited maple, is a variant of the maple tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are distinctive and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
