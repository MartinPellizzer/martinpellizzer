Alternanthera paronychioides subsp. pilosa

Alternanthera paronychioides subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the woolly blushing plant, is a striking aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America. It is characterized by its dense, fluffy foliage that ranges in color from pink to red, depending on light exposure and nutrient levels.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are often branched and can grow quite long. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in water gardens.

This subspecies is known for its ability to produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.
