Acalypha alopecuroidea f. glanduligera

Acalypha alopecuroidea f. glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular acalypha, is a variant of the species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a serrated margin. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
