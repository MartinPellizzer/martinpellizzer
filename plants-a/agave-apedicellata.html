Agave apedicellata

Agave apedicellata, commonly known as the lace agave, is a species of succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped arrangement of thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating called farina. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color and have a distinctive waxy texture, which helps the plant retain moisture in arid environments. It produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, greenish-white flowers, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits.

This plant is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
