Ampelopsis hirsuta

Ampelopsis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy grapevines, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in the summer. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often used in landscaping for its vigorous growth and ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Vitaceae family, which includes other well-known vines like grapes and ivy.
