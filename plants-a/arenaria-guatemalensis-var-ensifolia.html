Arenaria guatemalensis var. ensifolia

Arenaria guatemalensis var. ensifolia, commonly known as the Guatemalan sandwort, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically linear or lance-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
