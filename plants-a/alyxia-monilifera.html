Alyxia monilifera

Alyxia monilifera, commonly known as the coral vine, is a climbing plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.

Alyxia monilifera is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
