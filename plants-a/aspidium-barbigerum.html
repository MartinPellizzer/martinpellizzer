Aspidium barbigerum

Aspidium barbigerum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are typically bipinnate and have a leathery texture. The plant features small, spore-producing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds. Aspidium barbigerum is native to various regions in Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical studies.
