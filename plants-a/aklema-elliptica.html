Aklema elliptica

Aklema elliptica, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a striking visual contrast to the plant's foliage.
