Arundinaria amabilis var. convexa

Arundinaria amabilis var. convexa, commonly known as the broad-leaf bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo genus characterized by its broad, flat leaves and robust growth habit.

It is native to eastern Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in forested areas. The plant exhibits a clumping growth pattern, forming dense stands that can reach heights of up to 10 feet. Its culms are hollow and segmented, typical of the grass family, and its leaves are often described as having a subtle, pleasant aroma.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in suitable environments.
