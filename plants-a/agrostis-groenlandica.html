Agrostis groenlandica

Agrostis groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland grass, is a perennial grass species native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both sterile and fertile florets. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, showcasing typical grass morphology with vascular bundles and a fibrous root system.
