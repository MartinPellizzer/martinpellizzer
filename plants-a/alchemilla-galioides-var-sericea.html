Alchemilla galioides var. sericea

Alchemilla galioides var. sericea, commonly known as silver woundwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
