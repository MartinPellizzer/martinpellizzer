Astragalus subcaracugensis

Astragalus subcaracugensis, commonly known as the Karakorum milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Karakorum Mountains in Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
