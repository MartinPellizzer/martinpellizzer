Appendicula Lucbanensis

Appendicula lucbanensis is a rare and unique flowering plant native to the Philippines. It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is known for its striking, tubular flowers. This species was first discovered in the municipality of Lucban, in the province of Quezon. Its habitat is limited to specific limestone areas, making it highly vulnerable to environmental changes. Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure its survival in the wild.
