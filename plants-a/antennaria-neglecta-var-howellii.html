Antennaria neglecta var. howellii

Antennaria neglecta var. howellii, commonly known as Howell's mouse-eared moss, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, hairy leaves that resemble mouse ears. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are arranged in clusters and attract various pollinators. It thrives in rocky, dry habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
