Acer palmatum f. volubile

Acer palmatum f. volubile, commonly known as the weeping maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, preferring cooler climates.

Its unique growth habit makes it a popular choice for vertical or hanging displays in landscaping.
