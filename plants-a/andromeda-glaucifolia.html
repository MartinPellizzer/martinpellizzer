Andromeda glaucifolia

Andromeda glaucifolia, commonly known as the blueleaf wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a waxy, blue-gray coating, which helps reduce water loss in its cool, moist habitat.

Andromeda glaucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and spring blooms.
