Alonsoa auriculata

Alonsoa auriculata, commonly known as the golden bell or auriculate bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base.

Alonsoa auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
