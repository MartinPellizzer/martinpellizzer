Agathosma alta

Agathosma alta, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and hairy, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, thriving in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Agathosma alta is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
