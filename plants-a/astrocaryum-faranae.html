Astrocaryum faranae

Astrocaryum faranae, commonly known as the Farana palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellow to orange fruits that are typically clustered in bunches at the end of the stem. Its botanical features make it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in sustainable forestry.
