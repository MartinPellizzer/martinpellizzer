Agave colorata

Agave colorata, commonly known as the colorful agave, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Agave colorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
