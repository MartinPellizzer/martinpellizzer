Artemisia furcata subsp. furcata

Artemisia furcata subsp.

furcata, commonly known as the fringed wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that are finely divided and often covered in a powdery bloom.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense, branched clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
