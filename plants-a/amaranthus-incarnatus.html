Amaranthus incarnatus

Amaranthus incarnatus, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple pigmentation in its leaves and bracts, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into triangular seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and nutritional content.

Amaranthus incarnatus is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes several other species valued for their edible properties.
