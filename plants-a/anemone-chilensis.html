Anemone chilensis

Anemone chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean anemone, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are usually hairy.

Anemone chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, spring-blooming flowers.
