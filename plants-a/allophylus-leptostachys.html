Allophylus leptostachys

Allophylus leptostachys, commonly known as the slender-stemmed alderleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
