Acer pseudoplatanus var. subintegrilobum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. subintegrilobum, commonly known as the subintegrilobed maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves with fewer lobes compared to the typical variety. The leaves are usually palmately lobed with a serrated margin. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It produces small, red samaras that contribute to its ecological role in seed dispersal.
