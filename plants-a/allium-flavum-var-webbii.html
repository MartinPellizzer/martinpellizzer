Allium flavum var. webbii

Allium flavum var. webbii, commonly known as the Webb's garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered, yellowish-white flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
