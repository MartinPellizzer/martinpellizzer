Androsace spinulifera

Androsace spinulifera, commonly known as the clustered arete, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Androsace spinulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
