Adactylus brunonis

Adactylus brunonis, commonly known as the Brunon's stick insect, is a species of stick insect native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated body and camouflaged coloration that closely resembles the bark of trees, aiding in its survival. The plant it inhabits, primarily eucalyptus and other native Australian trees, provides both shelter and a source of moisture. Its diet consists mainly of leaves from these plants, which are rich in nutrients necessary for its growth.

Due to its specialized habitat, Adactylus brunonis is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of preserving its natural environment.
