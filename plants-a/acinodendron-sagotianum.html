Acinodendron sagotianum

Acinodendron sagotianum, commonly known as the Sagot's acacia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in nutrient-poor environments. Its flowers are typically small and yellow or green, and it produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds.

The species is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber in its native range.
