Abies araragi

Abies araragi, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that release seeds in the spring. It prefers cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
