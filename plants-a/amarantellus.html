Amarantellus

Amarantellus, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Amarantellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
