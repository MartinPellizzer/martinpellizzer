Aster megapotamicus

Aster megapotamicus, commonly known as the Andean aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster megapotamicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
