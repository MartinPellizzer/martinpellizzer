Arundinaria santanensis

Arundinaria santanensis, commonly known as the Santanense bamboo, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Arundinaria santanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in sustainable landscaping.
