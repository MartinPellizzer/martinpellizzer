Athamanta montana var. sibirica

Athamanta montana var. sibirica, commonly known as Siberian mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine pubescence, aiding in water retention.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high elevation conditions.
