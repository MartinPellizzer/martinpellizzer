Abutilon endlichii

Abutilon endlichii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a soft, fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Abutilon endlichii is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
