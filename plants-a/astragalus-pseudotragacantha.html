Astragalus pseudotragacantha

Astragalus pseudotragacantha, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep taproots that allow it to survive in dry conditions. Its seed pods are typically hairy and contain several small, hard seeds.

Astragalus pseudotragacantha is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
