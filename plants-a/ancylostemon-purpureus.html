Ancylostemon purpureus

Ancylostemon purpureus, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences of bright red or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a serrated margin. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
