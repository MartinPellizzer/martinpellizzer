Andropogon serratus subvar. parviflorus

Andropogon serratus subvar. parviflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, serrated leaves and compact, open growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
