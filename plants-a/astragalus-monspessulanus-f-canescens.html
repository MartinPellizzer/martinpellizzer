Astragalus monspessulanus f. canescens

Astragalus monspessulanus f. canescens, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "canescens.". It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
