Acacia evenulosa

Acacia evenulosa, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can withstand periods of drought.

Acacia evenulosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
