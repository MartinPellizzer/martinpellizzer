Adipe

Adipe, commonly known as the fat plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant's name derives from the oily, fatty substance found within its tissues, which contributes to its drought resistance. Adipe belongs to the family Cactaceae, sharing some similarities with cacti, though it is not a true cactus.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in desert botany and plant adaptation.
