Atriplex lentiformis subsp. griffithsii

Atriplex lentiformis subsp.

griffithsii, commonly known as Griffith's saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal and inland salt lakes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
