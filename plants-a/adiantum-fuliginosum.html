Adiantum fuliginosum

Adiantum fuliginosum, commonly known as the sooty maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that turn coppery brown with age. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its leaves are typically dark green with a slightly sooty appearance, giving it its common name.

Adiantum fuliginosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
