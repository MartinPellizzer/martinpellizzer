Andropogon lindiensis var. hirsutissimus

Andropogon lindiensis var. hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hirsute bluestem, is a grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which are often hairy and contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
