Albizia lentiscifolia

Albizia lentiscifolia, commonly known as the Mediterranean acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow or cream-colored flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a sticky resin when damaged.

Albizia lentiscifolia is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
