Adiantum malaliense

Adiantum malaliense, commonly known as the Malali maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a feather. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Adiantum malaliense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
