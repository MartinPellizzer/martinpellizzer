Artemisia seriphium subsp. patula

Artemisia seriphium subsp.

patula, commonly known as Patula Wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and are known for their strong, bitter aroma.
