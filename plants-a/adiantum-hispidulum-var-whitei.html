Adiantum hispidulum var. whitei

Adiantum hispidulum var. whitei, commonly known as the White's Maidenhair Fern, is a delicate fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that are often tinged with a silvery hue on the underside. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soil. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
