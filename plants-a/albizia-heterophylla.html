Albizia heterophylla

Albizia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that vary in shape and size, often featuring a mix of pinnate and bipinnate structures. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Albizia heterophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
