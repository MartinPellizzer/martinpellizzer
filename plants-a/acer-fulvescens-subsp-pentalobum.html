Acer fulvescens subsp. pentalobum

Acer fulvescens subsp.

pentalobum, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its striking red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental species.

The leaves are typically palmately lobed with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant color and adaptability.
