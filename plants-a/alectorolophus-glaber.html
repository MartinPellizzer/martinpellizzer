Alectorolophus glaber

Alectorolophus glaber, commonly known as the slender larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its striking appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be toxic to livestock and humans.
