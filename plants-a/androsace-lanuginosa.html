Androsace lanuginosa

Androsace lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy androsace, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, downy appearance. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and are borne on slender stems.

Androsace lanuginosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
