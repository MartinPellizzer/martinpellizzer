Asplenium annamense

Asplenium annamense, commonly known as the Annam spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic plant, typically found growing on rocks or tree trunks in humid environments. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often divided into narrow segments, giving it a delicate appearance. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of many ferns.

Asplenium annamense is native to Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
