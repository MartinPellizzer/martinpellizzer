Aristolochia wageneriana

Aristolochia wageneriana, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, winding, pipe-like flowers that are often greenish-brown in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition, including various aristolochic acids. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
