Aechmea recurvata

Aechmea recurvata, commonly known as the recurved pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's use. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Aechmea recurvata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
