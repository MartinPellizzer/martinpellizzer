Adesmia trifoliolata var. trifoliolata

Adesmia trifoliolata var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf burr legume, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to dry climates make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
