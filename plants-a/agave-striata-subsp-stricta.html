Agave striata subsp. stricta

Agave striata subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright, compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often lined with white or grayish markings.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand arid conditions.
