Acrisione denticulata var. pilota

Acrisione denticulata var. pilota, commonly known as the toothed acrisione, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow blooms that appear in clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

The variety 'pilota' is distinguished by its slightly different leaf structure compared to the typical form.
