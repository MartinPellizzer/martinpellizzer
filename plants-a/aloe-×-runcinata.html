Aloe × runcinata

Aloe × runcinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a hybrid succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow to orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Aloe × runcinata is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary ornamental value lies in its striking appearance.
