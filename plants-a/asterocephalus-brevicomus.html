Asterocephalus brevicomus

Asterocephalus brevicomus, commonly known as the short-stemmed asterhead, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Asterocephalus brevicomus is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
