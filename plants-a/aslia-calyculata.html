Aslia calyculata

Aslia calyculata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and reddish in color. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aslia calyculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
