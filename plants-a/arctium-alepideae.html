Arctium alepideae

Arctium alepideae, commonly known as the Aleppo thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

The plant has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
