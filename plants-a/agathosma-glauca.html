Agathosma glauca

Agathosma glauca, commonly known as Cape geranium, is a perennial shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Agathosma glauca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
