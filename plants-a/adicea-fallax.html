Adicea fallax

Adicea fallax, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its leaves are velvety and often have a distinctive pattern of veins.
