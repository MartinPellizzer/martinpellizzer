Althaea chrysantha

Althaea chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has soft, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in wetland environments.

Althaea chrysantha is often found in moist soils near streams, ponds, and wet meadows.
