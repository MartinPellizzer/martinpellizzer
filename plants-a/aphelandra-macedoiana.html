Aphelandra macedoiana

Aphelandra macedoiana, commonly known as the Macdoan's flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences. The plant features vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.
