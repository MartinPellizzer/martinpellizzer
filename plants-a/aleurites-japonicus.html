Aleurites japonicus

Aleurites japonicus, commonly known as Japanese nut tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical fruits containing one or two seeds. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody shell and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
