Aristolochia maurorum var. latifolia

Aristolochia maurorum var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive, often veiny appearance. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, aromatic scent. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
