Acaena × ovina f. pubescens

Acaena × ovina f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spinulose nettle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Acaena in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its fruits are dry, achene-like structures. It is native to New Zealand and is often found in coastal and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hairy texture and adaptive features, it is well-suited to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
