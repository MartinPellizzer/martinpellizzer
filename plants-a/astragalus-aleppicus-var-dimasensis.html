Astragalus aleppicus var. dimasensis

Astragalus aleppicus var. dimasensis, commonly known as the Aleppo milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This species is valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
