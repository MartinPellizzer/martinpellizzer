Acanthus hirsutus subsp. hirsutus

Acanthus hirsutus subsp.

hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy acanthus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside.

The plant produces spires of lavender-blue flowers in late summer, which are followed by bracts that resemble the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in classical garden designs.
