Aster amplus

Aster amplus, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster amplus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
