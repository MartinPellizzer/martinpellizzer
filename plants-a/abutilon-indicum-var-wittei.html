Abutilon indicum var. wittei

Abutilon indicum var. wittei, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its pendulous, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like hibiscus and cotton.
