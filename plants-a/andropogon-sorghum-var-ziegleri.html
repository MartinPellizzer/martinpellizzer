Andropogon sorghum var. ziegleri

Andropogon sorghum var. ziegleri, commonly known as Ziegler's sorghum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically green or yellowish in color. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

Ziegler's sorghum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its potential use in sustainable agriculture.
