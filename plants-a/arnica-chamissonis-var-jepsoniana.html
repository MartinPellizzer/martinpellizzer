Arnica chamissonis var. jepsoniana

Arnica chamissonis var. jepsoniana, commonly known as the Jepson's arnica, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Arnica chamissonis var. jepsoniana is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
