Abarema clypearia f. prainiana

Abarema clypearia f. prainiana, commonly known as the Prain's clypearia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pink. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It has a woody stem and produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
