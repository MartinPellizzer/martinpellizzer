Acetosa alpestris

Acetosa alpestris, commonly known as alpine sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bright green and slightly sour to the taste. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in poor, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Acetosa alpestris is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
