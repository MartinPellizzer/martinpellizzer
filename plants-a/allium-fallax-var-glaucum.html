Allium fallax var. glaucum

Allium fallax var. glaucum, commonly known as the blue garlic or blue onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.

Allium fallax var. glaucum is also valued in some traditional cuisines for its mild, garlicky flavor.
