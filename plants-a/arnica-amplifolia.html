Arnica amplifolia

Arnica amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, hairy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Arnica amplifolia is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.
