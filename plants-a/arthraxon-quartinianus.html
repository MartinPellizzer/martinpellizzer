Arthraxon quartinianus

Arthraxon quartinianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It has slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its root system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic habitats.

Arthraxon quartinianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
