Aspalathus prostrata

Aspalathus prostrata, commonly known as the Cape flats cycad, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often hidden beneath the foliage. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
