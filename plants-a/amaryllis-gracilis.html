Amaryllis gracilis

Amaryllis gracilis, commonly known as the slender amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a bulbous plant that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green and grow from the base of the bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Amaryllis gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home garden setting.
