Allium tenuifolium

Allium tenuifolium, commonly known as garlic chives, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, spherical umbels of white or pale purple flowers in the spring. Its bulbous stem is characterized by a strong garlic-like scent and flavor.

Allium tenuifolium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable garden plant.
