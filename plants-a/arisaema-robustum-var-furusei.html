Arisaema robustum var. furusei

Arisaema robustum var. furusei, commonly known as the Japanese mountain arum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features a thick, underground rhizome and produces a striking, umbrella-shaped inflorescence with a spadix surrounded by a large, green spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
