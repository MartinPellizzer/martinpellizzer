Agapetes oligodonta

Agapetes oligodonta, commonly known as the dwarf silverleaf evergreen, is a species of shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a silvery or whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by dark blue or black berries.

Agapetes oligodonta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
