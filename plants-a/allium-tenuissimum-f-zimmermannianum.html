Allium tenuissimum f. zimmermannianum

Allium tenuissimum f. zimmermannianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic chive, is a cultivar of the Allium genus characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and is often cultivated for its mild garlic-like flavor. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its botanical features include a bulbous base and a fibrous root system, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses in traditional dishes.
