Aster angulatus

Aster angulatus, commonly known as the angular aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Aster angulatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to pollinators.
