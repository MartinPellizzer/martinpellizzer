Acalypha australis var. glareosa

Acalypha australis var. glareosa, commonly known as the shining ironwood, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking, bright green, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and resilience.
