Achyranthes watsonii

Achyranthes watsonii, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Achyranthes watsonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
