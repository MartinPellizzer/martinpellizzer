Acacia biceps

Acacia biceps, commonly known as the two-armed acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden, arranged in dense, branched inflorescences.

Acacia biceps is adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.
