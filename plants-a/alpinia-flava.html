Alpinia flava

Alpinia flava, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional medicine. Alpinia flava thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and herbal practices.
