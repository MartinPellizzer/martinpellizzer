Armeria chilensis var. macloviana

Armeria chilensis var. macloviana, commonly known as the Chilean sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has linear, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats, where it tolerates salty air and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
