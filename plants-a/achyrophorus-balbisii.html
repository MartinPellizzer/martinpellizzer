Achyrophorus balbisii

Achyrophorus balbisii, commonly known as the Balbisii orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a complex root system adapted to its arid and rocky habitat, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It is pollinated primarily by moths, which are attracted to its strong fragrance.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
