Aeonium urbicum var. meridionale

Aeonium urbicum var. meridionale, commonly known as the Rock Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers on tall, branching stems during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive dry conditions.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
