Acer opulifolium var. obtusatum

Acer opulifolium var. obtusatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that are distinctive for their winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
