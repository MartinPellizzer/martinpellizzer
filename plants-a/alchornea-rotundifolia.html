Alchornea rotundifolia

Alchornea rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf alchornea, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Alchornea rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
