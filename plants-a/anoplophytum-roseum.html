Anoplophytum roseum

Anoplophytum roseum, commonly known as the roseum anoplophytum, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its unique adaptations to arid environments.

It is native to South Africa, where it thrives in sandy and gravelly soils. The plant features succulent leaves that store water, and its flowers are typically small, clustered, and often have a distinctive coloration. It is a low-growing shrub with a compact, mat-like form.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
