Amaryllis bifolia

Amaryllis bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved amaryllis, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces two broad, strap-like leaves that are typically larger than those of other amaryllis species. Its bulbs are fleshy and store water, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soil.

Amaryllis bifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, sunny environments.
