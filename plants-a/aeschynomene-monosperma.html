Aeschynomene monosperma

Aeschynomene monosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf desmodium, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
