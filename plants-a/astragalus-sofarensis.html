Astragalus sofarensis

Astragalus sofarensis, commonly known as the Socotra milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to the arid and rocky environment of Socotra, showcasing unique morphological features for survival in such conditions. It plays a role in the island's unique ecosystem and is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary distinctiveness.

However, it faces threats from habitat loss and over-collection.
