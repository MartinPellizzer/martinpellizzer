Acacia dentifera var. intermedia

Acacia dentifera var. intermedia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often narrow and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish, and it can grow in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

The species is sometimes used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
