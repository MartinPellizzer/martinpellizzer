Aster elongifolius

Aster elongifolius, commonly known as the slenderleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, open habitats like meadows and wetlands.

Aster elongifolius is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
