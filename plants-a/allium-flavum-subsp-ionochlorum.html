Allium flavum subsp. ionochlorum

Allium flavum subsp.

ionochlorum, commonly known as the Ionian garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels.

The plant is characterized by its bulbous stem, which is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, including sulfur-containing compounds that contribute to its aromatic properties.
