Aechmea platynema

Aechmea platynema, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features a rosette-shaped leaf structure that forms a central rosette, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The leaves are usually broad, stiff, and covered in a dense layer of white or silver hairs, giving it its common name. It produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.
