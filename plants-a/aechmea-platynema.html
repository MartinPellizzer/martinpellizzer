Aechmea platynema

Aechmea platynema, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features a rosette-shaped leaf structure that forms a central rosette, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The leaves are usually broad, stiff, and covered in a dense layer of white or silver hairs, giving it its common name. It produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.

Morphology

Aechmea platynema has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, thick stem covered in a fibrous, pale green bark that grows from the base of the rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, spiky flower stalk bearing small, violet flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a three-lobed, fleshy capsule that disperses its seeds through wind and animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its epiphytic or terrestrial habitat.
