Acalypha havanensis

Acalypha havanensis, commonly known as the Havana spurflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its striking, spurs-like inflorescences that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments, often near water bodies or in disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, with a variety of shapes depending on the species.

The flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant, making it monoecious.
