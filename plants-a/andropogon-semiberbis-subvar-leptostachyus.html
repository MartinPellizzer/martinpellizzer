Andropogon semiberbis subvar. leptostachyus

Andropogon semiberbis subvar. leptostachyus, commonly known as the slender false bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in loose panicles. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

This variant is distinguished by its shorter, more compact inflorescence compared to other members of the species.
