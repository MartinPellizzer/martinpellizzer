Alchemilla oriturcica

Alchemilla oriturcica, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in early summer, typically in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Alchemilla oriturcica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet habitats.
