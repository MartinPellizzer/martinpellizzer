Arytera pauciflora

Arytera pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered arytera, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a compact cluster, and bloom during the summer months.

Arytera pauciflora is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
