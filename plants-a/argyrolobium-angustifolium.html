Argyrolobium angustifolium

Argyrolobium angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved silverlobia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and slender, upright stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
