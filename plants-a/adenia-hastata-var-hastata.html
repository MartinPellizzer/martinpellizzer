Adenia hastata var. hastata

Adenia hastata var. hastata, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
