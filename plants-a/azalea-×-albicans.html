Azalea × albicans

Azalea × albicans, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron indicum and Rhododendron obtusum.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy clusters of tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes. Azalea × albicans prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is dark green and evergreen, providing year-round visual appeal.
