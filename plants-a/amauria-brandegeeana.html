Amauria brandegeeana

Amauria brandegeeana, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height, featuring a basal rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its succulent leaves and deep root system help it conserve moisture and thrive in desert conditions.
