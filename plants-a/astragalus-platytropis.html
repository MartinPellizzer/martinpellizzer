Astragalus platytropis

Astragalus platytropis, commonly known as the broad-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

Astragalus platytropis is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
