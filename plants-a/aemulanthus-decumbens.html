Aemulanthus decumbens

Aemulanthus decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features slender, prostrate stems that lie close to the ground, often sprawling across the soil surface. Its flowers are trumpet-shaped, with vibrant blue or purple petals that bloom in the early morning.

Aemulanthus decumbens is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh, sun-exposed conditions.

Aemulanthus decumbens has leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly hairy surface, arranged alternately along the stem, and ranging from 2 to 5 centimeters in length, typically displaying a bright green color.
