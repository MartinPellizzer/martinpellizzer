Arnica amplexifolia subsp. prima

Arnica amplexifolia subsp.

prima, commonly known as the broad-leaved arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a distinctive basal rosette, and the flowers bloom in late summer. Arnica amplexifolia subsp.

prima is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
