Allium ugami

Allium ugami, commonly known as the Japanese leek or Uguami, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features long, slender leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bulbous flowers. It is often grown for its edible leaves and bulb, which have a mild, garlic-like flavor.

Allium ugami thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
