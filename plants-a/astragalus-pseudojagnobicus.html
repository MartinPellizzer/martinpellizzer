Astragalus pseudojagnobicus

Astragalus pseudojagnobicus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which aids in seed dispersal and survival in unfavorable conditions.
