Aster brevipedunculatus

Aster brevipedunculatus, commonly known as the short-stalked aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense heads, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

Aster brevipedunculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
