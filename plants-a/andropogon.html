Andropogon

Andropogon, commonly known as grasses, is a large and diverse genus within the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are typically herbaceous and can be found in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, wetlands, and disturbed soils. Andropogon species are characterized by their hollow, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. Many species are important for agriculture, providing forage for livestock and serving as components in erosion control.

Some Andropogon species are also used in traditional medicine and horticulture.
