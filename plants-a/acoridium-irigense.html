Acoridium irigense

Acoridium irigense, commonly known as the golden spike lily, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in specialized gardens due to its unique appearance.

However, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered in its natural habitat.
