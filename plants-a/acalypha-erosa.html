Acalypha erosa

Acalypha erosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Acalypha erosa is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
