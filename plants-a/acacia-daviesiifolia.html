Acacia daviesiifolia

Acacia daviesiifolia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow globular inflorescences, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its flowers are followed by flat, leathery pods that contain several seeds.

Acacia daviesiifolia is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
