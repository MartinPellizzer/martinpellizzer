Acacia lutea var. moniliformis

Acacia lutea var. moniliformis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical heads, and its bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
