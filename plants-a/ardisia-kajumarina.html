Ardisia kajumarina

Ardisia kajumarina, commonly known as the Kajumarina evergreen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
