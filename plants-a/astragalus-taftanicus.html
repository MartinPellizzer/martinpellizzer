Astragalus taftanicus

Astragalus taftanicus, commonly known as Taftan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
