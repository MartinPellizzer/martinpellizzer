Arabis boissieuana

Arabis boissieuana, commonly known as the Boissieu's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Arabis boissieuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
