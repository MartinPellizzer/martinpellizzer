Allium telaponense

Allium telaponense, commonly known as the Telapón garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the island of Telapón in the Philippines and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
