Agrostis stolonifera var. bottnica

Agrostis stolonifera var. bottnica, commonly known as the Bottnica meadowgrass, is a variant of the tufted grass species native to northern regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and dense, fine-textured foliage. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a compact panicle.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing wetland habitats.
