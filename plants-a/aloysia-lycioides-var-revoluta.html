Aloysia lycioides var. revoluta

Aloysia lycioides var. revoluta, commonly known as the revolute verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive rolled margin, giving the variety its name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
