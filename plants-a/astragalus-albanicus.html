Astragalus albanicus

Astragalus albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus albanicus plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for ecological and botanical studies.
