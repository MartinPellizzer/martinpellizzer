Aconitum arizonicum

Aconitum arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Like other members of the Ranunculaceae family, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to livestock and humans.
