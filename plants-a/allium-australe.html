Allium australe

Allium australe, commonly known as the Australian onion, is a hardy perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers on slender stems, which bloom in spring or early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a useful plant in native gardens.

Allium australe is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
