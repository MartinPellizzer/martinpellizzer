Astragalus bobrovii

Astragalus bobrovii, commonly known as Bobrov's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus bobrovii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
