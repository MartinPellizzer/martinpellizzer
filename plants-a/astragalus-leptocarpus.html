Astragalus leptocarpus

Astragalus leptocarpus, commonly known as narrowpod milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that are narrow and elongated. It is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus leptocarpus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
