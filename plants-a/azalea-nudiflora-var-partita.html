Azalea nudiflora var. partita

Azalea nudiflora var. partita, commonly known as the Whiteleaf azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically white to pale green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are borne on short pedicels. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant blooms.
