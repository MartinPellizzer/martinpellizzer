Asperula taurica

Asperula taurica, commonly known as the Crimean holly or Tauric holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. Asperula taurica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
