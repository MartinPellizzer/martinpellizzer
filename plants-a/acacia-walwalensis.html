Acacia walwalensis

Acacia walwalensis, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow or orange floral spikes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a waxy coating on its leaves to reduce water loss.

Acacia walwalensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for wildlife.
