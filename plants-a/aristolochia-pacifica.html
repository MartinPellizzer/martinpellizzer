Aristolochia pacifica

Aristolochia pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, sometimes spicy scent.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
