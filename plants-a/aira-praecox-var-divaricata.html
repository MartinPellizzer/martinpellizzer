Aira praecox var. divaricata

Aira praecox var. divaricata, commonly known as the silvergrass or Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It features slender, silver-blue foliage that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a feathery appearance, often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its delicate beauty and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for landscaping.
