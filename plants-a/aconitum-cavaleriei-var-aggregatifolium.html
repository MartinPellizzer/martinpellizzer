Aconitum cavaleriei var. aggregatifolium

Aconitum cavaleriei var. aggregatifolium, commonly known as the clustered leaf aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spathaceous flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a basal rosette of large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its roots are tuberous and contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forested areas.
