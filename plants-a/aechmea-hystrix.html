Aechmea hystrix

Aechmea hystrix, commonly known as the pineapple plant or Spanish pineapple, is a striking bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, spiky, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces a flowering stem that bears numerous small, bell-shaped flowers, followed by bright red berries. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance.

Aechmea hystrix thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Morphology

Aechmea hystrix has broad, strap-like leaves that are sword-shaped, arranged in a rosette formation, and can grow up to 60 cm in length, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, leafy stem that is covered in a dense layer of hair, giving it a fuzzy appearance, and it grows upright. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by bright red bracts, with small white flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that turns red when mature, dispersing seeds through animals that eat the fruit.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the soil.
