Astragalus ketzkhovelii

Astragalus ketzkhovelii, commonly known as Ketzkhovel milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

However, due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
