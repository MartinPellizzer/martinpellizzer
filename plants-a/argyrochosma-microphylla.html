Argyrochosma microphylla

Argyrochosma microphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
