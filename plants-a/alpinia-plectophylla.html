Alpinia plectophylla

Alpinia plectophylla, commonly known as the giant ginger or Chinese mountain ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers with prominent spadices, typical of the Zingiberaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Alpinia plectophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
