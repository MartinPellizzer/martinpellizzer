Azalea serrulata var. georgiana

Azalea serrulata var. georgiana, commonly known as the Georgia azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are serrated along the edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
