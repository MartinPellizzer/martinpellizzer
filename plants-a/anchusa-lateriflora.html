Anchusa lateriflora

Anchusa lateriflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Anchusa lateriflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
