Acanthopanax japonicus f. kiusianus

Acanthopanax japonicus f. kiusianus, commonly known as the Kiusianus Chinese five-leaf, is a variant of the Chinese five-leaf plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves and branched, woody stems. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in forested areas. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries that attract various wildlife.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
