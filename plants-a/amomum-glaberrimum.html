Amomum glaberrimum

Amomum glaberrimum, commonly known as the glabrous cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elongated rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or green, and are followed by dark, triangular fruits.
