Anemopaegma quinquedentatum

Anemopaegma quinquedentatum, commonly known as the five-toothed anemopaegma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence composed of ray and disc flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
