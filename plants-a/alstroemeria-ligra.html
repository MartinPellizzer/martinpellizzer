Alstroemeria ligra

Alstroemeria ligra, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Peru.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, sword-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Alstroemeria ligra thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
