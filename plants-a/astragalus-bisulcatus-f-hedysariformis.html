Astragalus bisulcatus f. hedysariformis

Astragalus bisulcatus f. hedysariformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods are cylindrical and hairy. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
