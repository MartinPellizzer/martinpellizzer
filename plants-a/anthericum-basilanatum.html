Anthericum basilanatum

Anthericum basilanatum, commonly known as the Basilan arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Anthericum basilanatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
