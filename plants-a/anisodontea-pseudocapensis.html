Anisodontea pseudocapensis

Anisodontea pseudocapensis, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Anisodontea pseudocapensis is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
