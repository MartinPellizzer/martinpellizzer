Amaranthus × soproniensis

Amaranthus × soproniensis, commonly known as the Sopron giant amaranth, is a hybrid species of amaranth native to Hungary.

It is characterized by its large, striking red bracts that form a showy inflorescence, often used in ornamental gardening. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, exhibiting robust growth and resilience to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into seeds.

This species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability, making it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanical enthusiasts.
