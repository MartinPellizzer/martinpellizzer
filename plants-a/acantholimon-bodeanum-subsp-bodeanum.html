Acantholimon bodeanum subsp. bodeanum

Acantholimon bodeanum subsp.

bodeanum, commonly known as the Bodean's thornscale, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
