Agropyron magellanicum lasiopodum

Agropyron magellanicum lasiopodum, commonly known as the Magellanic wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in cold, temperate climates and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Agropyron magellanicum lasiopodum is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
