Acinodendron goudotii

Acinodendron goudotii, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are important for wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
