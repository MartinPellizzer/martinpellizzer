Andropogon ischaemum var. barbinodis

Andropogon ischaemum var. barbinodis, commonly known as rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and roadside ditches. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in wetland ecosystems and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration projects.
