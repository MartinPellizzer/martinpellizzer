Absinthium tanacetifolium

Absinthium tanacetifolium, commonly known as yarrow.

This plant is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter scent. The plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a component in herbal remedies.
