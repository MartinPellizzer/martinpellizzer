Azalea squamata

Azalea squamata, commonly known as the silverleaf azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of wax, giving it its common name. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring.

Azalea squamata prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
