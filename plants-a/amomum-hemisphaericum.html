Amomum hemisphaericum

Amomum hemisphaericum, commonly known as the spherical cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, triangular fruits. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
