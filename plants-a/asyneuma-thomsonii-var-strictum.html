Asyneuma thomsonii var. strictum

Asyneuma thomsonii var. strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features linear, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a component of local flora and potential ecological significance.
