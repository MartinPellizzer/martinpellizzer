Aster longulus

Aster longulus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers range in color from white to pale purple.

Aster longulus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
