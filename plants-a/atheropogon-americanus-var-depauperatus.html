Atheropogon americanus var. depauperatus

Atheropogon americanus var. depauperatus, commonly known as the slender panic grass, is a grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and dunes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its reduced floral structures and is often found in sandy or coastal environments.

This variety is part of the Poaceae family and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
