Alsophila phlebodes

Alsophila phlebodes, commonly known as the silver tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. Its fronds are typically covered in a silvery, waxy coating, which gives it its common name.

Alsophila phlebodes plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
