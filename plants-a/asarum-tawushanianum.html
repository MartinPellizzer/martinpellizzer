Asarum tawushanianum

Asarum tawushanianum, commonly known as the Tawushanian wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and distinctive, mottled, reddish-brown flowers. The plant produces a strong, aromatic scent, which is typical of the genus Asarum. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland habitats and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
