Alstroemeria peruviana

Alstroemeria peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, strap-like leaves. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alstroemeria peruviana is also valued for its ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
