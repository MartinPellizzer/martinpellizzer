Andropogon amethystinus

Andropogon amethystinus, commonly known as purple bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinted foliage and upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, violet-colored flowers.

Andropogon amethystinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
