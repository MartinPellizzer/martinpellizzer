Aspalathus eriocephaloides

Aspalathus eriocephaloides, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to thrive in coastal and sandy environments, often found along the Atlantic coast. It produces small, round, reddish-brown fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
