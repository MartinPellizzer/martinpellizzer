Anthriscus sylvestris

Anthriscus sylvestris, commonly known as cow parsley, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
