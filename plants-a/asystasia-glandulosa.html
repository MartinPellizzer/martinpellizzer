Asystasia glandulosa

Asystasia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular asystasia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asystasia glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
