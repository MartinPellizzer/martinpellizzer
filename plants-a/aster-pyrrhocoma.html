Aster pyrrhocoma

Aster pyrrhocoma, commonly known as the redstem aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, thriving in temperate climates. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
