Arundinella hondana

Arundinella hondana, commonly known as the Honduras bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Central America.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant forms dense clumps and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. Its growth habit and structural features make it suitable for erosion control in certain environments.

However, it is important to note that it may become invasive in some areas if not managed properly.
