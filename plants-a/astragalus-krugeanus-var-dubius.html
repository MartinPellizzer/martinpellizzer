Astragalus krugeanus var. dubius

Astragalus krugeanus var. dubius, commonly known as the Kruge's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.
