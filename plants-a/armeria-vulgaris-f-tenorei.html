Armeria vulgaris f. tenorei

Armeria vulgaris f. tenorei, commonly known as the rock thrift or sea thrift, is a low-growing evergreen perennial that belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas or alpine environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a compact rosette.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
