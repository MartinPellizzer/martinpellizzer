Achimenes jauregiana

Achimenes jauregiana, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Achimenes jauregiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
