Artemisia apetala

Artemisia apetala, commonly known as silver thread, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and distinctive, thread-like leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Artemisia apetala is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its aromatic scent and drought tolerance make it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
