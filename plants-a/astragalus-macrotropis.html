Astragalus macrotropis

Astragalus macrotropis, commonly known as the large-tropic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Astragalus macrotropis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
