Acalypha scandens

Acalypha scandens, commonly known as the silver leaf or velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and abandoned fields, due to its adaptability.

Acalypha scandens is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
