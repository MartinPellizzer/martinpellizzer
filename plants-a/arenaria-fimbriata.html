Arenaria fimbriata

Arenaria fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal dunes or rocky habitats. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pink, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are linear and often have a distinctive fringed edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Arenaria fimbriata is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a hardy choice for gardeners in challenging climates.
