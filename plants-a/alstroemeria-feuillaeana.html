Alstroemeria feuillaeana

Alstroemeria feuillaeana, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a striking evergreen perennial native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting white or pink markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Alstroemeria feuillaeana is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various climates.
