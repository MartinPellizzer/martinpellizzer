Asplenium grashoffii

Asplenium grashoffii, commonly known as the Grashoff's spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces spores in clusters called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or in soil.

Asplenium grashoffii is a rare species, found primarily in specific regions of South America.
