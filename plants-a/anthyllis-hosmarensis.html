Anthyllis hosmarensis

Anthyllis hosmarensis, commonly known as the Hojarashi clover, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis hosmarensis is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
