Amaryllis coloniana

Amaryllis coloniana, commonly known as the Saint Helena amaryllis, is a rare and endemic plant native to Saint Helena in the South Atlantic Ocean.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky crevices and coastal areas, adapting to the island's unique climate. Its leaves are strap-like and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Amaryllis coloniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest to botanists due to its isolated evolutionary history.
