Acer betulifolium

Acer betulifolium, commonly known as the mountain maple, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of the birch tree, hence its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas, often at higher elevations. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring followed by red or orange samaras.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
