Amomum acuminatum

Amomum acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant produces fragrant, three-lobed berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and essential oils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
