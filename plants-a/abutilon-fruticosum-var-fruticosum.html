Abutilon fruticosum var. fruticosum

Abutilon fruticosum var. fruticosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in clusters. Its growth habit is typically sprawling or shrub-like, with multiple branches.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
