Apios americana var. turrigera

Apios americana var. turrigera, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruit capsules that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
