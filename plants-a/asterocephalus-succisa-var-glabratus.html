Asterocephalus succisa var. glabratus

Asterocephalus succisa var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth asterocephalus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with fleshy, waxy leaves that are smooth and often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners in dry climates.
