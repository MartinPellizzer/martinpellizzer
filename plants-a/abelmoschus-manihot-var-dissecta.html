Abelmoschus manihot var. dissecta

Abelmoschus manihot var. dissecta, commonly known as the African arrowroot or telfairia, is a variant of the okra plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its finely dissected leaves that give it a fern-like appearance, and its pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, starchy tuberous roots that are used as a food source in various cultures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to different soil types.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental and a useful crop in agricultural systems.
