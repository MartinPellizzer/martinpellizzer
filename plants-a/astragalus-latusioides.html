Astragalus latusioides

Astragalus latusioides, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across suitable habitats.
