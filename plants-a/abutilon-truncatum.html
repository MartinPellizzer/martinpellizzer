Abutilon truncatum

Abutilon truncatum, commonly known as the Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in a three-lobed capsule that splits open when mature to release the seeds.

Abutilon truncatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
