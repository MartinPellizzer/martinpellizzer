Achillea tanacetifolia subsp. lanata

Achillea tanacetifolia subsp.

lanata, commonly known as woolly yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow composite flower heads arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky environments.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
