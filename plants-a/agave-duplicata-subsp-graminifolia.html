Agave duplicata subsp. graminifolia

Agave duplicata subsp.

graminifolia, commonly known as the blue agave, is a species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color.

The plant produces a tall, flowering stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is closely related to other agave species.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of agave nectar.
