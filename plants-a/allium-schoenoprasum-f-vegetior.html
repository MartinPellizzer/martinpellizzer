Allium schoenoprasum f. vegetior

Allium schoenoprasum f. vegetior, commonly known as the garlic chives or giant chives, is a cultivated form of the common chive.

It is characterized by its longer, more robust leaves and larger, more pungent bulbs compared to the standard variety. This plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in gardens for its ornamental value and culinary applications.

Its foliage and bulbs are both edible, offering a strong garlic flavor.
