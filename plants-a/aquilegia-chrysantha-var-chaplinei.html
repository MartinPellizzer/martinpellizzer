Aquilegia chrysantha var. chaplinei

Aquilegia chrysantha var. chaplinei, commonly known as the Chaplin's columbine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with five petals and yellow anthers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
