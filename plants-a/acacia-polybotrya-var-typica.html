Acacia polybotrya var. typica

Acacia polybotrya var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, fern-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
