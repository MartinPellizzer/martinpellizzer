Anaphalis aureopunctata var. atrata

Anaphalis aureopunctata var. atrata, commonly known as the black-spotted goatweed, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features silver-gray foliage with distinctive black or dark purple spots, giving it a striking visual appearance. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a fluffy, white or pale yellow appearance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
