Astragalus dorudensis

Astragalus dorudensis, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a species of legume native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus dorudensis is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
