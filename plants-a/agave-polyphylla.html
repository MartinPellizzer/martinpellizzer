Agave polyphylla

Agave polyphylla, commonly known as the Sierra Madre agave, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Agave polyphylla is a succulent, capable of storing water in its leaves, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
