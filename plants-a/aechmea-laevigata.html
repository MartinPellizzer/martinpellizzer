Aechmea laevigata

Aechmea laevigata, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped inflorescence, which consists of long, narrow leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white hairs, giving it its common name. It produces striking red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, often followed by small, violet-colored fruits.

Aechmea laevigata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
