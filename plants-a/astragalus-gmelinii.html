Astragalus gmelinii

Astragalus gmelinii, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus gmelinii is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
