Amaryllis × tricolor

Amaryllis × tricolor, commonly known as the rainbow amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis bellodona and Amaryllis papilio.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored flowers that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow in a single bloom. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically borne on upright stems above its strap-like leaves. It is a bulbous plant native to South America and thrives in warm, sunny conditions with well-draining soil.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and floral arrangements.
