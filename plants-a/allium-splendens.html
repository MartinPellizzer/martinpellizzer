Allium splendens

Allium splendens, commonly known as the giant purple onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear large, spherical purple bulbs, which are edible when harvested young. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a basal rosette.

Allium splendens is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses in various cuisines.
