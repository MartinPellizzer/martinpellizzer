Astragalus junussovii

Astragalus junussovii, commonly known as the Junussov's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
