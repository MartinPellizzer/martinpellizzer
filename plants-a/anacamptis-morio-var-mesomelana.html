Anacamptis morio var. mesomelana

Anacamptis morio var. mesomelana, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms in early spring, usually between March and May.

The variety mesomelana is distinguished by its narrower leaves and slightly different flower coloration compared to the typical form.
