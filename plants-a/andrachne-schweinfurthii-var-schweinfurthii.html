Andrachne schweinfurthii var. schweinfurthii

Andrachne schweinfurthii var. schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

The species is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
