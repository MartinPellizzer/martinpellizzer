Acacia filicifolia

Acacia filicifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, fern-like foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a woody structure with slender, branching stems. Acacia filicifolia is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
