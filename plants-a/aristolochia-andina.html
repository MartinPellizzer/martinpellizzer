Aristolochia andina

Aristolochia andina, commonly known as the Andean birthwort, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a hood. The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology and aromatic properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
