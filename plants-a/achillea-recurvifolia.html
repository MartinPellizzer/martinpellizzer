Achillea recurvifolia

Achillea recurvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is attributed to its purported medicinal properties.
