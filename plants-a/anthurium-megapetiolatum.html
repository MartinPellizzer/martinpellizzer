Anthurium megapetiolatum

Anthurium megapetiolatum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces striking red spathes that enclose its small, yellowish spadices, which are the actual flowering structures. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Anthurium megapetiolatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
