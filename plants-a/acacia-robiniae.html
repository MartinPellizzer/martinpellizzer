Acacia robiniae

Acacia robiniae, commonly known as the black acacia or robinia acacia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are trifoliate and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often used in reforestation efforts.

Acacia robiniae is also valued for its hard, heavy wood and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
