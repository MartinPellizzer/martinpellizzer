Astragalus piutensis

Astragalus piutensis, commonly known as the Piutensian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus piutensis is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers, which can range in color from white to pale purple, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
