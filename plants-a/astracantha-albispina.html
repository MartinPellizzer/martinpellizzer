Astracantha albispina

Astracantha albispina, commonly known as the white-spined thornveld, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often spiny and covered in a whitish coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pale blue or purple fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions and minimal rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
