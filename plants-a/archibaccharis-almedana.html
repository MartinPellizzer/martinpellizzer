Archibaccharis almedana

Archibaccharis almedana, commonly known as the Almedana baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.
