Acrocephalus linearifolius

Acrocephalus linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leaved bulrush, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in wetland restoration projects to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
