Astragalus Danicus Subsp. Dasyglottis

Danish milkvetch, scientifically known as Astragalus danicus subsp. dasyglottis, is a rare and delicate legume native to the sandy dunes of northern Europe. This subspecies thrives in harsh coastal environments where few plants can survive due to strong winds and salt exposure. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early summer, adding a subtle beauty to the otherwise stark landscape. The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing dune soil and supporting local ecosystems. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique species from habitat loss and climate change.
