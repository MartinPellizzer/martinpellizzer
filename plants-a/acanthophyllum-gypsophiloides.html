Acanthophyllum gypsophiloides

Acanthophyllum gypsophiloides, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure and grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Its resilience makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
