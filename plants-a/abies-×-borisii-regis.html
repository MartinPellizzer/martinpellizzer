Abies × borisii-regis

Abies × borisii-regis, commonly known as the Siberian fir, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Abies sibirica and Abies alba.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and measure around 5–7 cm in length. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
