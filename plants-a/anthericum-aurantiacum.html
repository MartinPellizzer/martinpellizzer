Anthericum aurantiacum

Anthericum aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange false shamrock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf known as a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Anthericum aurantiacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
