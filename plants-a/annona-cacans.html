Annona cacans

Annona cacans, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit, which resembles a large, round apple, has a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, custard-like pulp.

The leaves are broad, alternate, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
