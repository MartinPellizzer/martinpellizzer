Acacia limbata

Acacia limbata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often twisted. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia limbata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
