Astragalus aegobromus var. hirtus

Astragalus aegobromus var. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

The hairy milkvetch is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
