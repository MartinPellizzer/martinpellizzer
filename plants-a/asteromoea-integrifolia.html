Asteromoea integrifolia

Asteromoea integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf asteromoea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale to dark purple, and it blooms in the summer months.

Asteromoea integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
