Aerva sanguinolenta var. lanceolata

Aerva sanguinolenta var. lanceolata, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional purposes.
