Aster pantotrichus var. thyrsoideus

Aster pantotrichus var. thyrsoideus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stem and showy purple or blue-violet flower heads. The plant produces ray flowers surrounding a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable plant in ecological gardens.
