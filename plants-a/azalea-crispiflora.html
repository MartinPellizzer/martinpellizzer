Azalea crispiflora

Azalea crispiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Azalea crispiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
