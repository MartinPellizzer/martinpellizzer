Allium fraseri

Allium fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are bulbous and adapted for storage, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Allium fraseri is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
