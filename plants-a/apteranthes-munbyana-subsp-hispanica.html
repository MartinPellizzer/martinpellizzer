Apteranthes munbyana subsp. hispanica

Apteranthes munbyana subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish apteranthes, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
