Alternanthera robinsonii

Alternanthera robinsonii, commonly known as the red veined sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red veins contrasting with its bright green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
