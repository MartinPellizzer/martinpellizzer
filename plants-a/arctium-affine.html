Arctium affine

Arctium affine, commonly known as the lesser burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, hollow stems covered in dense, prickly bracts that form the characteristic bur-like structures. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces small purple to brown flowers in clusters.

Arctium affine is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
