Absinthium officinale

Absinthium officinale, commonly known as wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and small yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains a variety of bioactive compounds, including thujone, which contributes to its distinctive bitter taste. Wormwood is often cultivated for its essential oils and has a long history of use in herbal medicine.

It is also a key ingredient in the production of absinthe, a spirit known for its unique flavor and historical significance.
