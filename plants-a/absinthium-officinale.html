Absinthium officinale

Absinthium officinale, commonly known as wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

Wormwood is often cultivated for its essential oils and has a long history of use in herbal medicine.

It is also a key ingredient in the production of absinthe, a spirit known for its unique flavor and historical significance.
