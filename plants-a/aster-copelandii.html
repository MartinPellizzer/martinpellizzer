Aster copelandii

Aster copelandii, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple or pink petals surrounding a central cone-like structure composed of tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
