Aristolochia gurinderi

Aristolochia gurinderi, commonly known as the Kashmiri Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often a deep purple or brownish color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
