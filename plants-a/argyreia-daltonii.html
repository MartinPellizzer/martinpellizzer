Argyreia daltonii

Argyreia daltonii, commonly known as the red veined morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, often with prominent white veins. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is typically cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Argyreia daltonii is a climbing vine that can reach significant heights if given proper support.
