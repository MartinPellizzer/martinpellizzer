Afrohybanthus indicus

Afrohybanthus indicus, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
