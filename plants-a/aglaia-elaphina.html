Aglaia elaphina

Aglaia elaphina, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light green bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, elongated fruits that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
