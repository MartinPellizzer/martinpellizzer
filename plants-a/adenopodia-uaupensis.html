Adenopodia uaupensis

Adenopodia uaupensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Adenopodia uaupensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
