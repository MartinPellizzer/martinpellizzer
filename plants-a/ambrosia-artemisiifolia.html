Ambrosia artemisiifolia

Ambrosia artemisiifolia, commonly known as common ragweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant is known for its allergenic pollen, which can cause significant respiratory issues in sensitive individuals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and in urban areas.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a weed in many regions and is often targeted for control.
