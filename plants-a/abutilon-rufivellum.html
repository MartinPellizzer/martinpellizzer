Abutilon rufivellum

Abutilon rufivellum, commonly known as the red-hair abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its bark is fibrous and often has a reddish hue, giving it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
