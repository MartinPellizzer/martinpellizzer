Anacharis linearis

Anacharis linearis, commonly known as the needle leaf waterweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. This plant is often found in slow-moving or still water bodies such as ponds and lakes. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing oxygen and shelter for small aquatic organisms.

Anacharis linearis is also popular in aquariums due to its fast growth and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
