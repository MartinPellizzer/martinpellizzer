Annona ferruginea

Annona ferruginea, commonly known as the rough custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring a rough, bark-like stem. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish or rusty-colored appearance, giving the plant its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by large, inedible fruits.

The botanical characteristics of Annona ferruginea make it a distinctive and visually striking species within the Annonaceae family.
