Acacia mutabilis subsp. stipulifera

Acacia mutabilis subsp.

stipulifera, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces yellow to golden-orange flowers in dense, globular clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
