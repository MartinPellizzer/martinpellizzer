Acoridium confusum

Acoridium confusum, commonly known as the confused acoridium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family and is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
