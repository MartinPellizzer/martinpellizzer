Artemisia trifida

Artemisia trifida, commonly known as roughleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Artemisia trifida thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
