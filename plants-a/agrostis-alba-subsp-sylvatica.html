Agrostis alba subsp. sylvatica

Agrostis alba subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the silver bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
