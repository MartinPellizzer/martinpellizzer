Arophyton tripartitum var. masoalaense

Arophyton tripartitum var. masoalaense, commonly known as the Masoala triple-leaf plant, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three distinct, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the rainforest canopy. Its botanical structure includes a short, woody stem with a dense network of roots.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its distinctive foliage.
