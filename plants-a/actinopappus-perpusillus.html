Actinopappus perpusillus

Actinopappus perpusillus, commonly known as the small pappus plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and a unique pappus structure that aids in wind dispersal. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant typically grows low to the ground, forming a dense mat that helps prevent soil erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
