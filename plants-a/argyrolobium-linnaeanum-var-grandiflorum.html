Argyrolobium linnaeanum var. grandiflorum

Argyrolobium linnaeanum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered silver-lobed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, hairy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It is a perennial herb with a low-growing habit, and its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
