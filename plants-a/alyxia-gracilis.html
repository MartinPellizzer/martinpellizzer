Alyxia gracilis

Alyxia gracilis, commonly known as the slender alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a woody vine that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Alyxia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
