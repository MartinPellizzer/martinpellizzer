Achillea millefolium var. parviligula

Achillea millefolium var. parviligula, commonly known as the lesser yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance, and its flat, clustered flower heads are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and dry grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

The variety parviligula is distinguished by its smaller, more compact growth compared to the typical form of Achillea millefolium.
