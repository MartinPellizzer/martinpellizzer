Amauropelta tomentosa

Amauropelta tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly shield fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, green to brown spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and along stream banks.
