Agrostis dulcis

Agrostis dulcis, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce fluffy seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly sweet aroma when crushed.

Agrostis dulcis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
