Ardisia trichomata

Ardisia trichomata, commonly known as the hairy ardisia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Ardisia trichomata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
