Andropogon scabrimarginatus

Andropogon scabrimarginatus, commonly known as the roughleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, sandpaper-like texture along the margins. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
