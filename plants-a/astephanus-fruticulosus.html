Astephanus fruticulosus

Astephanus fruticulosus, commonly known as the hairy stephanus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, as well as its yellow to orange flower heads. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Astephanus fruticulosus is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in the Cape region of South Africa.
