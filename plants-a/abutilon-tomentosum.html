Abutilon tomentosum

Abutilon tomentosum, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its fibrous stems and hairy foliage make it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in certain environments.
