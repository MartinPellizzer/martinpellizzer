Arundinaria glaucescens

Arundinaria glaucescens, commonly known as the Chinese cliffbrake, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, glaucous foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors. Its fronds are usually pinnately divided, with a soft, bluish-green coloration.

Arundinaria glaucescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid conditions.
