Anathallis laciniata

Anathallis laciniata, commonly known as the slit-leaf spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and its unique, spider-like flowers that are typically pale green or yellow. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests.

Anathallis laciniata is known for its delicate appearance and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its specialized pollination mechanisms.
