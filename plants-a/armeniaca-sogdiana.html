Armeniaca sogdiana

Armeniaca sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian apricot, is a species of Prunus native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, temperate climates and is well-adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly serrated margin.

Armeniaca sogdiana has been historically cultivated for its fruit and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
