Arctostaphylos × parvifolia

Arctostaphylos × parvifolia, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, dark blue berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
