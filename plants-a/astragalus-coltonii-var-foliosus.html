Astragalus coltonii var. foliosus

Astragalus coltonii var. foliosus, commonly known as the leafy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus coltonii var. foliosus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
