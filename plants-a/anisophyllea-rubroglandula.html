Anisophyllea rubroglandula

Anisophyllea rubroglandula, commonly known as the red glandular anisophyllea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anisophylleaceae and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The leaves are typically glossy and have prominent veins, with some species displaying red or orange glandular dots on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
