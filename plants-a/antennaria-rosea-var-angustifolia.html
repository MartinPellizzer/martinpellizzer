Antennaria rosea var. angustifolia

Antennaria rosea var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf mouseear cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its stems are prostrate, and it thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the local flora's diversity.
