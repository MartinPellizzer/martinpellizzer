Arctostaphylos klamathensis

Arctostaphylos klamathensis, commonly known as Klamath manzanita, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Klamath manzanita plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
