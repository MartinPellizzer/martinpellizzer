Arachniodes jinpingensis

Arachniodes jinpingensis, commonly known as the Jinping holly fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to China.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that resemble the delicate patterns of spider webs, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its leaves are bioluminescent in the dark, a rare trait among ferns, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

This species is of great interest to botanists due to its distinctive morphology and ecological significance.
