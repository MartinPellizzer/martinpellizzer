Aeschynomene pratensis var. caribaea

Aeschynomene pratensis var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are typically small and oval in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its potential as a forage crop and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
