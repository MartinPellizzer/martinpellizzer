Alchemilla elata

Alchemilla elata, commonly known as the slender lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.

Alchemilla elata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
