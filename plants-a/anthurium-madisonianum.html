Anthurium madisonianum

Anthurium madisonianum, commonly known as the Madisonian arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces vibrant spadices, which are the spiky, often colorful structures that house the true flowers. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium madisonianum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
