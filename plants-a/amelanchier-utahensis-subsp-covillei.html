Amelanchier utahensis subsp. covillei

Amelanchier utahensis subsp.

covillei, commonly known as the Utah serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
