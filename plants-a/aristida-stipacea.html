Aristida stipacea

Aristida stipacea, commonly known as slender threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in fine hairs. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Aristida stipacea is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
