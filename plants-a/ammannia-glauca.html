Ammannia glauca

Ammannia glauca, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
