Aspalathus spicata subsp. cliffortioides

Aspalathus spicata subsp.

cliffortioides, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, hard, chestnut-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular plant in local gardens and conservation areas.
