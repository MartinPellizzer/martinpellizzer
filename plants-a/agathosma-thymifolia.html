Agathosma thymifolia

Agathosma thymifolia, commonly known as Cape thyme or wild thyme, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy environments. Its foliage is widely used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Agathosma thymifolia is also valued as a culinary herb, similar to thyme, and is used to flavor dishes and beverages.
