Artemisia scopulorum

Artemisia scopulorum, commonly known as big sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are aromatic and contain volatile oils. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Big sagebrush is also used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
