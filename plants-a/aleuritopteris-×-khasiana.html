Aleuritopteris × khasiana

Aleuritopteris × khasiana, commonly known as the hybrid water fern, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is a fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant has fronds that are usually bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aleuritopteris × khasiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant.
