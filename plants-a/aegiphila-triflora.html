Aegiphila triflora

Aegiphila triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered aegiphila, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
