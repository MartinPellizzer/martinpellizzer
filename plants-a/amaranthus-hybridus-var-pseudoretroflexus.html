Amaranthus hybridus var. pseudoretroflexus

Amaranthus hybridus var. pseudoretroflexus, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base. The plant produces small, greenish to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in some traditional herbal practices.
