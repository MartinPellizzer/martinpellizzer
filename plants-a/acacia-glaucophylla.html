Acacia glaucophylla

Acacia glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green phyllodes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Acacia glaucophylla plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
