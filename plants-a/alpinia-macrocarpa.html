Alpinia macrocarpa

Alpinia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust rhizomes and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant inflorescences with yellow-green bracts and red or orange flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Alpinia macrocarpa is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
