Aspalathus divaricata

Aspalathus divaricata, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid conditions, making it a hardy species often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, dark brown pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Aspalathus divaricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
