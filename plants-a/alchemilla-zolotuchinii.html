Alchemilla zolotuchinii

Alchemilla zolotuchinii, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Alchemilla zolotuchinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
