Aeschynomene multiflora

Aeschynomene multiflora, commonly known as partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical regions. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Aeschynomene multiflora is also used in forage and as a cover crop in agricultural systems.
