Aster exscapus

Aster exscapus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a sturdy stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Aster exscapus is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
