Alstroemeria werdermannii f. flavescens

Alstroemeria werdermannii f. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered alstroemeria, is a rare and striking plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and unique coloration, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
