Agave pedrosana

Agave pedrosana, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, blue-green leaves with sharp spines along the edges. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Agave pedrosana is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
