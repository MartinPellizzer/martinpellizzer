Acer rufinerve var. marmoratum

Acer rufinerve var. marmoratum, commonly known as the marbled maple, is a variant of the red-veined maple.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a mix of green, red, and yellow hues. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical with prominent, reddish veins that contrast with the lighter leaf tissue. This plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
