Aechmea pycnantha

Aechmea pycnantha, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure, which forms a central cup to collect water and nutrients. The leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. It produces showy inflorescences with vibrant red flowers that bloom in the spring.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.

Morphology

Aechmea pycnantha has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, leathery, and dark green, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, dark bark that grows upward from the base. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of bright orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer and are pollinated by birds and insects. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that disperse their seeds through wind and animal movement.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in rocky or acidic soil.
