Aulomyrcia campestris var. rufa

Aulomyrcia campestris var. rufa, commonly known as the red-stemmed marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in saturated soils and is often found in swampy areas along rivers and lakeshores.

Aulomyrcia campestris var. rufa plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
