Acoridium macgregorii

Acoridium macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes plants such as calla lilies and peace lilies. The plant features a rhizomatous stem system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, which is reflected in its common name, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is valued for its starchy rhizomes, which can be processed into a flour-like substance used in traditional cuisines.
