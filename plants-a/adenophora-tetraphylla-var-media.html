Adenophora tetraphylla var. media

Adenophora tetraphylla var. media, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are bell-shaped with five petals, typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces small, round berries that are clustered in groups of four, giving it its species name *tetraphylla*. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
