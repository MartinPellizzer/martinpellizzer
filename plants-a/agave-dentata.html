Agave dentata

Agave dentata, commonly known as the toothed agave, is a succulent plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves with prominent teeth along the edges. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 3 feet in length. It produces an inflorescence of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Agave dentata is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
