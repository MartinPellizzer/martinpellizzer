Abarema glomeriflora

Abarema glomeriflora, commonly known as the clustered-flower shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces leguminous pods that contain seeds.

Abarema glomeriflora is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
