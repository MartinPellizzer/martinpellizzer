Anthemis biebersteiniana f. marschalliana

Anthemis biebersteiniana f. marschalliana, commonly known as the Marschall's tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a greyish-blue hue and produces yellow ray florets surrounded by pale yellow disk florets. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
