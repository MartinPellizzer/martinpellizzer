Avena georgica

Avena georgica, commonly known as the Georgian oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Avena georgica is often found in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
