Andropogon squarrosus var. chrysopogonoides

Andropogon squarrosus var. chrysopogonoides, commonly known as golden bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant landscaping.
