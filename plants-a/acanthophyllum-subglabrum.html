Acanthophyllum subglabrum

Acanthophyllum subglabrum, commonly known as the silky acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a small, perennial shrub characterized by its dense, spiny leaves and compact growth habit. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color.

It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
