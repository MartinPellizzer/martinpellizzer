Arum nickelii

Arum nickelii, commonly known as the Nickel's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and deep green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are small and clustered on the spadix, with the spathe serving as a protective covering.

Arum nickelii is often cultivated for its dramatic appearance and can thrive in humid, shaded environments.
