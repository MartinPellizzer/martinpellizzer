Althaea taurinensis

Althaea taurinensis, commonly known as the Taurine marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, clay soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Althaea taurinensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
