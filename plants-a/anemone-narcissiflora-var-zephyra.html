Anemone narcissiflora var. zephyra

Anemone narcissiflora var. zephyra, commonly known as the Zephyr anemone, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
