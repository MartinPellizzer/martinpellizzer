Aster lasiocarpus

Aster lasiocarpus, commonly known as the roughfruit aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, showy flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers, which bloom in the fall, range in color from white to purple, attracting various pollinators.

The plant's rough, glandular leaves and distinctive fruiting structures are key botanical features that aid in its identification.
