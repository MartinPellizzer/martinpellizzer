Abies Araucana

Araucaria, scientifically known as Abies araucana, is a striking evergreen conifer native to the Andes mountains of South America. This ancient tree, often referred to as the "Araucaria" or "Patagonian cypress," is recognized for its unique appearance and ecological significance. It features a conical shape with long, needle-like leaves and distinctive, cone-bearing branches that give it a timeless, almost mythical presence. The species has survived for millions of years, adapting to harsh alpine conditions and playing a vital role in its native ecosystem. Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly valued in both conservation efforts and horticultural practices worldwide.
