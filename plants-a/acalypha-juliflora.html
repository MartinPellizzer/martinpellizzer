Acalypha juliflora

Acalypha juliflora, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers, often displaying red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha juliflora thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
