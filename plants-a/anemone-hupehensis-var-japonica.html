Anemone hupehensis var. japonica

Anemone hupehensis var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, single or double flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped or ovate, with hairy surfaces, and stems that are typically upright and branched. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms.
