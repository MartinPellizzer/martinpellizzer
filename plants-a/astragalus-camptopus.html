Astragalus camptopus

Astragalus camptopus, commonly known as the bentstem milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Astragalus camptopus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
