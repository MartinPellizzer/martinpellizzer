Allium caput-medusae

Allium caput-medusae, commonly known as the Medusa's head, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Europe and the Middle East.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of large, spherical, purple or pinkish-white flowers resembling a crown of death. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its dramatic, eye-catching appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Allium caput-medusae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
