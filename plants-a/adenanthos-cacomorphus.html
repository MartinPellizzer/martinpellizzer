Adenanthos cacomorphus

Adenanthos cacomorphus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved adenanthos, is an evergreen shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in heathland and coastal scrub environments. It produces woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
