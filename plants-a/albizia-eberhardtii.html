Albizia eberhardtii

Albizia eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's silk tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Albizia eberhardtii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
