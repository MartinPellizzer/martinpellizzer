Asplenium × preissmannii

Asplenium × preissmannii, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often exhibit a dark green to blackish hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a feature typical of the Asplenium genus.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
