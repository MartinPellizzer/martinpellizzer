Asclepias obtusifolia var. latifolia

Asclepias obtusifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features elongated, broad leaves that are typically gray-green in color, often with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to reddish-orange flowers, which are characteristic of the milkweed genus. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by butterflies, especially the monarch butterfly.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
