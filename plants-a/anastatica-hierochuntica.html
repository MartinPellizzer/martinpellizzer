Anastatica hierochuntica

Anastatica hierochuntica, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a unique succulent that can survive extreme desiccation.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. The plant's ability to lose up to 98% of its water content and then revive when rehydrated is a remarkable adaptation to its harsh environment. Its small, scale-like leaves and capsule-like fruits are typical of its evolutionary traits.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
