Acantholimon kurdicum

Acantholimon kurdicum, commonly known as Kurdish thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
