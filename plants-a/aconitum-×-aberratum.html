Aconitum × aberratum

Aconitum × aberratum, commonly known as the hybrid aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stems. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes that are often hairy.

Aconitum × aberratum is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
