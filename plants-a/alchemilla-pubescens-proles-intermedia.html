Alchemilla pubescens proles intermedia

Alchemilla pubescens proles intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate hairy lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in loose panicles. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
