Alyssum procumbens

Alyssum procumbens, commonly known as creeping alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has succulent, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum procumbens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
