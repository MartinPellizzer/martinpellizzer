Alstroemeria despuenta

Alstroemeria despuenta, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Alstroemeria despuenta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in floral arrangements.
