Acianthera compressiflora

Acianthera compressiflora, commonly known as the compact-flowered orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome and produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in a dense inflorescence. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

The species is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental appeal.
