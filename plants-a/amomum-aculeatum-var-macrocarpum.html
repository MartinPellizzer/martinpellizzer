Amomum aculeatum var. macrocarpum

Amomum aculeatum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant cardamom, is a tropical perennial herb belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces large, fragrant spikes of white to pale yellow flowers, followed by dark brown, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes are rich in essential oils and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

This species is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
