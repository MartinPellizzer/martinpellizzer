Aesculus × mutabilis var. penduliflora

Aesculus × mutabilis var. penduliflora, commonly known as the weeping horse chestnut, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the United States.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, creating a striking visual display. The tree produces large, spiny capsules containing shiny, dark red seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are glossy and deeply lobed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
