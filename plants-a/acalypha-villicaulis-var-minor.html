Acalypha villicaulis var. minor

Acalypha villicaulis var. minor, commonly known as the silky hibiscus or velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture on the upper surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions make it a common ornamental and invasive species in some regions.

The plant's botanical characteristics include fibrous roots and a woody stem with a rough, bark-like surface.
