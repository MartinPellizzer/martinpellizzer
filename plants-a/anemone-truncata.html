Anemone truncata

Anemone truncata, commonly known as the cutleaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features palmately lobed, deeply cut leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in woodland gardens and naturalized areas.
