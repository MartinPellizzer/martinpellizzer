Aristida tenuisetulosa var. arenarioides

Aristida tenuisetulosa var. arenarioides, commonly known as the sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its foliage is fine and grass-like, with leaves that are often curled or folded when dry.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
