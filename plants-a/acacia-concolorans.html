Acacia concolorans

Acacia concolorans, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, fluffy pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia concolorans thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
