Asphodeline prolifera

Asphodeline prolifera, commonly known as the forest asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of bright yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to grow in woodland and scrubland environments, often in well-drained soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Asphodeline prolifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
