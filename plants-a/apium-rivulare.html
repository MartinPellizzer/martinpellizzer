Apium rivulare

Apium rivulare, commonly known as water celery, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like parsley and carrots. The plant is characterized by its hollow, aromatic stems and finely divided leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family.

Apium rivulare often grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist environments.
