Astragalus segazicus

Astragalus segazicus, commonly known as the Segazic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets that help reduce water loss.

Astragalus segazicus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
