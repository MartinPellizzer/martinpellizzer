Areca spicata

Areca spicata, commonly known as the spiky areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant produces fibrous, palm-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Areca spicata is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and poor soil conditions.
