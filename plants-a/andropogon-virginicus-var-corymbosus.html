Andropogon virginicus var. corymbosus

Andropogon virginicus var. corymbosus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn bronze in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of small, spike-like flowers.

It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
