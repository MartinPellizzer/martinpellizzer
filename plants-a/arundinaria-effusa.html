Arundinaria effusa

Arundinaria effusa, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its tall, slender reed-like stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria effusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in wetland environments.
