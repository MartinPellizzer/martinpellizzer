Arthraxon meeboldii var. purandharensis

Arthraxon meeboldii var. purandharensis, commonly known as the Purandharen hill sedge, is a grass-like plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, rhizomatous stem.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
