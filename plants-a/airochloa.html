Airochloa

Airochloa, commonly known as the silver sword, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and a tuft of flowering heads at the center. Airochloa is a perennial plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
