Allium erdelii var. genuinum

Allium erdelii var. genuinum, commonly known as the Hungarian mountain garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, garlic-like scent and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional Central European cuisine.
