Annona senegalensis var. glabrescens

Annona senegalensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the African hornapple, is a tropical shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by large, greenish-white fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp with a mild, custard-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and has traditional uses in local medicine.
