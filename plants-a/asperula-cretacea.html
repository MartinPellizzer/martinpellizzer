Asperula cretacea

Asperula cretacea, commonly known as the Crete Heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in early spring. Asperula cretacea is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
