Astragalus propinquus var. glaber

Astragalus propinquus var. glaber, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to brownish pods that contain seeds.

This species is often found in the central and western United States, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
