Anisophyllea globosa

Anisophyllea globosa, commonly known as the globe anisophyllea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, waxy leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps the plant retain moisture in its humid habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, attracting various birds and small mammals.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
