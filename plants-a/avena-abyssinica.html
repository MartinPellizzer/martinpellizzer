Avena abyssinica

Avena abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian oat, is a perennial grass native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces minute, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ecological significance. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated for forage or traditional uses.
