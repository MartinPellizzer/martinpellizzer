Annona chrysophylla

Annona chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf annona, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a yellowish tinge. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish or orange fruits. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem and a fibrous root system, typical of many tropical shrubs.

Annona chrysophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
