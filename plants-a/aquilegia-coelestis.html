Aquilegia coelestis

Aquilegia coelestis, commonly known as the blue cohosh, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in woodland settings and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The botanical features of Aquilegia coelestis make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens.
