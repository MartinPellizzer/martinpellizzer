Amaranthus arctioideus

Amaranthus arctioideus, commonly known as the prairie amaranth, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red to purple bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, green to yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Amaranthus arctioideus is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
