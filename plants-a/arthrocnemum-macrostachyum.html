Arthrocnemum macrostachyum

Arthrocnemum macrostachyum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with cylindrical, succulent stems that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Arthrocnemum macrostachyum is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
