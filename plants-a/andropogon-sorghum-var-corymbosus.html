Andropogon sorghum var. corymbosus

Andropogon sorghum var. corymbosus, commonly known as broomcorn millet, is a warm-season grass native to the Old World.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles that produce small, fluffy grains. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it a hardy crop. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a fibrous root system.

It is widely cultivated for forage, grain, and biofuel production.
