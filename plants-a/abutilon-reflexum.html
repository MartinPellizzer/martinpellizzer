Abutilon reflexum

Abutilon reflexum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive velvety texture, while its flowers are borne in terminal panicles.

Abutilon reflexum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
