Avena praecocioides

Avena praecocioides, commonly known as early barley or dwarf oat, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, erect stems that typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a bluish-green hue.

Avena praecocioides is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
