Augusta martiana

Augusta martiana, commonly known as the purple-leafed aster, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped, velvety leaves that turn a striking purple hue in the fall. Its showy purple and white flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Augusta martiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
