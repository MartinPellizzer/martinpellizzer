Astragalus baeri

Astragalus baeri, commonly known as the Baer's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus baeri is often found in mountainous areas and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
