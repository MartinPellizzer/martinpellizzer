Aria nivea var. rugosa

Aria nivea var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate floral display to its foliage. The leaves are typically oval and have a slightly rough texture, giving the plant its varietal name "rugosa.". It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
