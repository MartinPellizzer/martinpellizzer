Astracantha microcephala

Astracantha microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed thornveld, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassland and savanna environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, yellowish flowers are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Astracantha microcephala is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
