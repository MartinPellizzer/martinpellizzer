Acanthophyllum fontanesii f. tenuispinum

Acanthophyllum fontanesii f. tenuispinum, commonly known as the slender-spined acanthophyllum, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with thick stems that store water.

This subspecies is notable for its reduced spines compared to other varieties in the species.
