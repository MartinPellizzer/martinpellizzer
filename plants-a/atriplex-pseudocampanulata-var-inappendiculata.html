Atriplex pseudocampanulata var. inappendiculata

Atriplex pseudocampanulata var. inappendiculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal and inland salt lakes.

Its succulent stems and leaves help it store water, making it well-suited for drought conditions.
