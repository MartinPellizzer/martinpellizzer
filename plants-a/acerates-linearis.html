Acerates linearis

Acerates linearis, commonly known as the slender acerates, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered in fine, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to rocky, well-drained soils.
