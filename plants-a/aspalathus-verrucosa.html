Aspalathus verrucosa

Aspalathus verrucosa, commonly known as the African red bush, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are typically small and elliptical, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Aspalathus verrucosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
