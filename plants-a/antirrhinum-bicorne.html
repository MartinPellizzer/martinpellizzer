Antirrhinum bicorne

Antirrhinum bicorne, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features a slender, branched stem and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Antirrhinum bicorne is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
