Acacia latisiliqua

Acacia latisiliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green stems and narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spikes, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Acacia latisiliqua is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can tolerate extreme temperatures and drought.
