Alternanthera pubescens

Alternanthera pubescens, commonly known as the velvet leaf or red veined sorrel, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that often display vibrant red, pink, or purple veins against a dark green background. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and prefers full to partial sunlight. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length.

Alternanthera pubescens is popular in aquariums for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for small aquatic life.
