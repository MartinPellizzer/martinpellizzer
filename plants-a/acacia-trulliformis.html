Acacia trulliformis

Acacia trulliformis, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like crown formed by a dense cluster of branches. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its yellow flowers are produced in dense, globular clusters and are an important nectar source for pollinators.

Acacia trulliformis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
