Aristolochia coadunata var. coadunata

Aristolochia coadunata var. coadunata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musky scent. The plant has heart-shaped, alternate leaves that are usually dark green and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
