Alchemilla aphanoides var. tripartita

Alchemilla aphanoides var. tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, typically blooming in early summer.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
