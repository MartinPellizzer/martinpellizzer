Amomum mizanianum

Amomum mizanianum, commonly known as the Persian cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant features erect, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces distinctive, three-lobed fruits that encase the aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its fragrant leaves and seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
