Armeria vulgaris var. alpina

Armeria vulgaris var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
