Agave doctorensis

Agave doctorensis, commonly known as the Doctor's Agave, is a species native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-forming structure and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous white or pale green flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Agave doctorensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
