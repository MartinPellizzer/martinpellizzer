Alstroemeria ligtu f. carnea

Alstroemeria ligtu f. carnea, commonly known as the pink alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with strap-like leaves and a compact growth habit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The flowers are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their bright colors and nectar-rich structure.
