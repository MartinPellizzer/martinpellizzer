Antidesma ghaesembilla var. paniculatum

Antidesma ghaesembilla var. paniculatum, commonly known as the panicle antidesma, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and usually elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in panicle-like clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
