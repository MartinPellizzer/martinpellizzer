Anemone albana var. violacea

Anemone albana var. violacea, commonly known as the violet anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, downy texture. Its flowers are typically borne on slender stems and are composed of numerous petal-like bracts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-season blooms.
