Antirrhinum linkianum var. genuinum

Antirrhinum linkianum var. genuinum, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
