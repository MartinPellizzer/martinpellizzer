Alsine rosanoi var. subglabra

Alsine rosanoi var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth alsine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically greenish-white in color and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. This variety is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence compared to other varieties of Alsine rosanoi.

It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions.
