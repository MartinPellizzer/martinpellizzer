Artemisia orientalixizangensis

Artemisia orientalixizangensis, commonly known as the Tibetan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typical of the composite flower heads found in the sunflower family. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
