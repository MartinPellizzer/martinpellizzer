Aster ascendens var. fremontii

Aster ascendens var. fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
