Acer cinerascens var. paxii

Acer cinerascens var. paxii, commonly known as the Mexican ash, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in urban environments.
