Adiantum affine var. cunninghamii

Adiantum affine var. cunninghamii, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble a cinnamon stick.

It is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant has a short, horizontal rhizome that supports its tall, arching fronds. Its sori, which are clusters of spore-producing structures, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings due to its adaptability and aesthetic qualities.
