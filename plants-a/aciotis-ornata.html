Aciotis ornata

Aciotis ornata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flower heads and lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
