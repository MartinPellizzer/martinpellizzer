Anagallis alternifolia var. parvula

Anagallis alternifolia var. parvula, commonly known as the small-leaved evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of narrow, linear leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its flowers open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
