Abies webbiana

Abies webbiana, commonly known as the Webbian fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The needles are dark green, stiff, and arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces both male and female cones, with the male cones being yellow and the female cones dark purple when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its evergreen foliage and winter interest.
