Angraecum hildebrandtii

Angraecum hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by moths, which are attracted to its strong scent.

Angraecum hildebrandtii is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity.
