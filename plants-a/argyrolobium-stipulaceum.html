Argyrolobium stipulaceum

Argyrolobium stipulaceum, commonly known as the stipulate silver-lobed burr.

It is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa, characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage and spiny, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by unique, globular seed pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its morphology and growth habits make it a notable example of xerophytic adaptations in the Fabaceae family.
