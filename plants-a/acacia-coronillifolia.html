Acacia coronillifolia

Acacia coronillifolia, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Acacia coronillifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
