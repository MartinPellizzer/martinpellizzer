Angraecum bemarivoense

Angraecum bemarivoense, commonly known as the Bemarivo orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by specific nocturnal moths, showcasing a fascinating co-evolutionary relationship.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
