Ampelopsis indica

Ampelopsis indica, commonly known as the Chinese ivy, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and can be found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
