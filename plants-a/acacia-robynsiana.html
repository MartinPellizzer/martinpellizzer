Acacia robynsiana

Acacia robynsiana, commonly known as the yellow-wood acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive yellow to golden-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact, globular clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its bark is smooth and pale, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

Acacia robynsiana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
