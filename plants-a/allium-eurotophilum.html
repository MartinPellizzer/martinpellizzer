Allium eurotophilum

Allium eurotophilum, commonly known as the European garlic or mountain garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, tall flowering stem that bears umbels of white or pale purple flowers. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Allium eurotophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
