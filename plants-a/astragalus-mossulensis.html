Astragalus mossulensis

Astragalus mossulensis, commonly known as the Mossul milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky, arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance is significant in its native habitats.
