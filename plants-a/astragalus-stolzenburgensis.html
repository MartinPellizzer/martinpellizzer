Astragalus stolzenburgensis

Astragalus stolzenburgensis, commonly known as Stolzenburg's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for certain wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
