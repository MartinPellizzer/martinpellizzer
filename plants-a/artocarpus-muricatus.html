Artocarpus muricatus

Artocarpus muricatus, commonly known as the rough fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, spiny bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a fruit that is typically green when mature, with a thick, fibrous rind. The fruit contains a single, edible, fig-like pulp that is consumed by various animals and humans.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, tropical climates.
