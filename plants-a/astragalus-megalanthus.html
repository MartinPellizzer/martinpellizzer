Astragalus megalanthus

Astragalus megalanthus, commonly known as the giant milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus megalanthus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
