Andropogon nlemfuensis

Andropogon nlemfuensis, commonly known as the Nlemfu grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments, often found in swampy or wetland areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Andropogon nlemfuensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
