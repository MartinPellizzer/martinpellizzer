Acacia aulacocarpa var. fruticosa

Acacia aulacocarpa var. fruticosa, commonly known as the prickly acacia, is a shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in yellow or orange. The plant produces elongated, flat pods containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
