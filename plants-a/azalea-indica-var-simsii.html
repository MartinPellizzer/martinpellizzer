Azalea indica var. simsii

Azalea indica var. simsii, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
