Agapanthus umbellatus mooreanus

Agapanthus umbellatus mooreanus, commonly known as the Moorean agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in summer, forming an umbel-like arrangement. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
