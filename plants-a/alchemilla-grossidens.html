Alchemilla grossidens

Alchemilla grossidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla grossidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal medicine.
