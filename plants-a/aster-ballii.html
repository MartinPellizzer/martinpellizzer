Aster ballii

Aster ballii, commonly known as the Ball's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to pale purple. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding a delicate floral display to its natural habitat. Aster ballii prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
