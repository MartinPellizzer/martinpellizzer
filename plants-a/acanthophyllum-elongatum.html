Acanthophyllum elongatum

Acanthophyllum elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed spiny bush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems covered in spines. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It is well adapted to harsh environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves.

Acanthophyllum elongatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
