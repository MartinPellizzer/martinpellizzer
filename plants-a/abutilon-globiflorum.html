Abutilon globiflorum

Abutilon globiflorum, commonly known as the globe flower or woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are borne in dense inflorescences, which give it a unique and ornamental appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
