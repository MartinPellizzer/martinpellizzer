Aristolochia occidentalis

Aristolochia occidentalis, commonly known as western pipevine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, purple to greenish flowers that resemble pipes, hence its common name. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks. It is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure that attracts specific pollinators.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is considered invasive in some regions.
