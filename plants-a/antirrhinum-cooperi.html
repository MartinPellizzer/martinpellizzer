Antirrhinum cooperi

Antirrhinum cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Antirrhinum cooperi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a range of garden conditions.
