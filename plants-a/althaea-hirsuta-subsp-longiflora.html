Althaea hirsuta subsp. longiflora

Althaea hirsuta subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the woolly bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
