Arctostaphylos pungens subsp. montana

Arctostaphylos pungens subsp.

montana, commonly known as mountain manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and evergreen, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in white or pink hues, which bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Mountain manzanita thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
