Aquilegia formosa subsp. dissecta

Aquilegia formosa subsp.

dissecta, commonly known as the Sierra Lily, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple, with five petals and elongated nectar guides.

The plant has compound leaves with finely dissected foliage, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports pollination by bees and butterflies, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
