Acacia angustissima var. smithii

Acacia angustissima var. smithii, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
