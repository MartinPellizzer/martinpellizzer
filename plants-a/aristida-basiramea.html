Aristida basiramea

Aristida basiramea, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in a panicle shape. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Aristida basiramea is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
