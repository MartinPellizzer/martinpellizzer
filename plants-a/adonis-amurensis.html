Adonis amurensis

Adonis amurensis, commonly known as Siberian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a short, stout stem that bears a single, showy, bell-shaped flower. The plant's flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, recurved lip. Adonis amurensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
