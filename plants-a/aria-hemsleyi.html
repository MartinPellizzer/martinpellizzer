Aria hemsleyi

Aria hemsleyi, commonly known as the Hemsley's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the spring and early summer.

Aria hemsleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
