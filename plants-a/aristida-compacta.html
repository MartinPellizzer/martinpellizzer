Aristida compacta

Aristida compacta, commonly known as compact grass or dwarf threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces fluffy, three-awned spikelets that are often seen in open fields and disturbed areas. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Aristida compacta is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wildlife.
