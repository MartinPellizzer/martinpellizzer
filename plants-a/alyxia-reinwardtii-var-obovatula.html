Alyxia reinwardtii var. obovatula

Alyxia reinwardtii var. obovatula, commonly known as the broad-leaf alyxia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate to obovate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
