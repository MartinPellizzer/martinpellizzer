Anthurium tortuosum

Anthurium tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, white, and red, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Anthurium tortuosum thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
