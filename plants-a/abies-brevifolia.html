Abies brevifolia

Abies brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are short and thick. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Abies brevifolia thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a habitat provider and its contribution to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
