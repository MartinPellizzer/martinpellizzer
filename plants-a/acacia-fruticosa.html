Acacia fruticosa

Acacia fruticosa, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia fruticosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in degraded areas.
