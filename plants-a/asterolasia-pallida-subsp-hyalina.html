Asterolasia pallida subsp. hyalina

Asterolasia pallida subsp.

hyalina, commonly known as the ghost aster, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It features silver-gray foliage with a dense covering of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
