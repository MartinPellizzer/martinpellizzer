Adenocalymma cladotrichum

Adenocalymma cladotrichum, commonly known as the white-veined sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves with a distinctive white venation. Its flowers are pale blue to white, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the cooler months.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes.
