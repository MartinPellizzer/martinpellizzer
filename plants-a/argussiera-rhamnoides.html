Argussiera rhamnoides

Argussiera rhamnoides, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
