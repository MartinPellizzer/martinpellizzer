Asplenium excisum

Asplenium excisum, commonly known as the cut fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that often have a leathery texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense mat of roots, which help anchor it to substrates.

Asplenium excisum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various indoor growing conditions.
