Alchemilla ozana

Alchemilla ozana, commonly known as narrowleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on slender, branched stems. Alchemilla ozana thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is known for its decorative appearance and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
