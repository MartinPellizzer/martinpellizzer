Atriplex sessilifolia

Atriplex sessilifolia, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline soils. Its yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Atriplex sessilifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
