Alopecurus baptarrhenius

Alopecurus baptarrhenius, commonly known as the purple false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce purple or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grass-like appearance.

Alopecurus baptarrhenius is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
