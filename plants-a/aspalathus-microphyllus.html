Aspalathus microphyllus

Aspalathus microphyllus, commonly known as rooibos, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and is known for its unique ability to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Rooibos is particularly noted for its high concentration of antioxidants and flavonoids, which contribute to its popularity in herbal teas.

Its botanical features make it well-suited to the region's climate and soil conditions.
