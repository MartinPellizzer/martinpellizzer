Alsophila approximata var. sorisquamata

Alsophila approximata var. sorisquamata, commonly known as the silver fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are pinnately divided with closely spaced, tooth-like lobes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
