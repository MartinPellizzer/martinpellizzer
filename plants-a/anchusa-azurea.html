Anchusa azurea

Anchusa azurea, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Anchusa azurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
