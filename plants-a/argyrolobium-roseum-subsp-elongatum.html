Argyrolobium roseum subsp. elongatum

Argyrolobium roseum subsp.

elongatum, commonly known as the elongated silver globular veldtflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive silver-blue flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pale purple to lilac.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces elongated, cylindrical flower stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
