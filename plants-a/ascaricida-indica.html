Ascaricida indica

Ascaricida indica, commonly known as the Indian wormwood, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often found growing in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically arranged in composite heads.

Ascaricida indica has been used in traditional medicine for its purported antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.
