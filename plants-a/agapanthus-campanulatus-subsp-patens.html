Agapanthus campanulatus subsp. patens

Agapanthus campanulatus subsp.

patens, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa. It features striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in dense, upright clusters.

The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
