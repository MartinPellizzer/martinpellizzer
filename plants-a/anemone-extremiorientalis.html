Anemone extremiorientalis

Anemone extremiorientalis, commonly known as the Korean anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting central zone. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone extremiorientalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
