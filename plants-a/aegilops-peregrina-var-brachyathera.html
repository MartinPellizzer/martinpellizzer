Aegilops peregrina var. brachyathera

Aegilops peregrina var. brachyathera, commonly known as the dwarf wild rye, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

This variety is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for improving wheat genetics.
