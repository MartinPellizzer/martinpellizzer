Aspalathus polyantha

Aspalathus polyantha, commonly known as the sweet pea bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces tiny, edible seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a compact, hardy plant.
