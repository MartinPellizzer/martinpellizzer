Arundo vilnensis

Arundo vilnensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. This plant thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near rivers, lakes, and other water bodies. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Arundo vilnensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in basket weaving.
