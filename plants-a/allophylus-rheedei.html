Allophylus rheedei

Allophylus rheedei, commonly known as the African holly, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Allophylus rheedei is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
