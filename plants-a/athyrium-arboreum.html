Athyrium arboreum

Athyrium arboreum, commonly known as the tree fern, is a member of the Athyriaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to grow in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent and can reach lengths of up to several meters.

Athyrium arboreum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
