Artemisia scopariiformis

Artemisia scopariiformis, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage due to dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Artemisia scopariiformis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
