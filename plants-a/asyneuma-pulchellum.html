Asyneuma pulchellum

Asyneuma pulchellum, commonly known as the pretty morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It blooms during the warmer months, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Asyneuma pulchellum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
