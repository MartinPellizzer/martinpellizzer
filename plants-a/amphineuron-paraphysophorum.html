Amphineuron paraphysophorum

Amphineuron paraphysophorum, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically green or brown in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist environments.

The leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green sheen, and are often used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
