Anthocercis eadesii

Anthocercis eadesii, commonly known as the Eades' orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of yellow and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in its native range.
