Arabis campyloloba

Arabis campyloloba, commonly known as the bent-capsule rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in a raceme, and it produces distinctive, elongated pods that remain on the plant after blooming.

Arabis campyloloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
