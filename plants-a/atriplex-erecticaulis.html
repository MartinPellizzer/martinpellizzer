Atriplex erecticaulis

Atriplex erecticaulis, commonly known as the upright saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is adapted to saline soils, making it a valuable species for reclaiming degraded lands. Its flowers are typically greenish or yellowish and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Atriplex erecticaulis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
