Aeschynomene angolensis

Aeschynomene angolensis, commonly known as African leadplant, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes and are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

Aeschynomene angolensis is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
