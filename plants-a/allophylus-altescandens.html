Allophylus altescandens

Allophylus altescandens, commonly known as the alpine holly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Allophylus altescandens is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient species in harsh mountain conditions.
