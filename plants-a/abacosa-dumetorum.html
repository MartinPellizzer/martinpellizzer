Abacosa dumetorum

Abacosa dumetorum, commonly known as the dwarf abacosa, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features small, narrow leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, emerging from the center of the rosette in late spring or early summer.

Abacosa dumetorum is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants in temperate rainforests.
