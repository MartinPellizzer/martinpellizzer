Anogeissus myrtifolia

Anogeissus myrtifolia, commonly known as Indian sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round, woody fruits.

The tree is often found in dry, thorny forests and is known for its ecological and commercial significance.
