Aristolochia malacophylla

Aristolochia malacophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are often large and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Aristolochia is known for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic compounds that can be harmful to humans and animals.
