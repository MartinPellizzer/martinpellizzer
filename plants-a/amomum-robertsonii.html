Amomum robertsonii

Amomum robertsonii, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizome that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The essential oils extracted from its rhizome are valued for their spicy aroma and medicinal properties.
