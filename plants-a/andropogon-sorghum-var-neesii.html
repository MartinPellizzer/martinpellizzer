Andropogon sorghum var. neesii

Andropogon sorghum var. neesii, commonly known as Nees' sorghum, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Nees' sorghum is often used in ornamental gardening and for erosion control due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
