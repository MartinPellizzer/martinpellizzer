Achillea ptarmica subsp. ptarmicifolia

Achillea ptarmica subsp.

ptarmicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its aromatic foliage and long flowering period make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
