Agropyron fuegianum patagonicum

Agropyron fuegianum patagonicum, commonly known as Patagonian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in cold, temperate climates and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

Agropyron fuegianum patagonicum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
