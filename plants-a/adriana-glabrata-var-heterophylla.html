Adriana glabrata var. heterophylla

Adriana glabrata var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf adriana, is a unique variant of the adriana plant characterized by its morphological variation in leaf shape.

It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a range of leaf forms, from ovate to lanceolate, depending on environmental conditions. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

This variation makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticultural cultivation.
