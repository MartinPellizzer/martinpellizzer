Achillea reichardtiana

Achillea reichardtiana, commonly known as Reichardt's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale yellow, bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Reichardt's yarrow is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Morphology

Achillea reichardtiana has leaves that are lanceolate in shape, arranged alternately along the stem, and have a gray-green color with a fuzzy texture.

The stem is upright, with a smooth, greenish-gray bark and a growth pattern that is clumping. Its flowers are composite, consisting of ray florets that are white to pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer, and are pollinated by insects such as bees. The fruit is a small achene, and the seeds are dispersed by wind due to their lightweight structure.
