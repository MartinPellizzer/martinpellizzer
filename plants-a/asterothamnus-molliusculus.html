Asterothamnus molliusculus

Asterothamnus molliusculus, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a creeping stem with dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. Its yellow flowers are borne in flat, composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
