Amelanchier gracilis

Amelanchier gracilis, commonly known as the slender serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is characterized by its slender branches, oval leaves, and showy pink to red autumn foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.

Amelanchier gracilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
