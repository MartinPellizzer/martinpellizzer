Adenophora scabridula var. viscida

Adenophora scabridula var. viscida, commonly known as the sticky腺草, is a variant of the Adenophora genus native to certain regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often sticky stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, arranged in clusters, and are attractive to pollinators.

The leaves are generally narrow and may have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
