Astragalus neglectus f. limonius

Astragalus neglectus f. limonius, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, contributing to its role in supporting local biodiversity.
