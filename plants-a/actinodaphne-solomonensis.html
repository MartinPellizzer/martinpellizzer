Actinodaphne solomonensis

Actinodaphne solomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands laurel, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the Solomon Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its fruits are drupes, which are typically dark purple when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine by local communities.
