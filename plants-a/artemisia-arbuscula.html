Artemisia arbuscula

Artemisia arbuscula, commonly known as blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, camphor-like scent.

Artemisia arbuscula is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
