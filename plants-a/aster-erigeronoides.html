Aster erigeronoides

Aster erigeronoides, commonly known as the white fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils, and it is often found in the eastern and central United States. Its showy white or pale yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
