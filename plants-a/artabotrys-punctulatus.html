Artabotrys punctulatus

Artabotrys punctulatus, commonly known as the spotted beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
