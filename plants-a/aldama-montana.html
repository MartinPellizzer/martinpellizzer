Aldama montana

Aldama montana, commonly known as the mountain aldama, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
