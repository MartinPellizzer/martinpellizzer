Amerimnon pallidum

Amerimnon pallidum, commonly known as the pale amerimnon, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to yellowish flowers that bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
