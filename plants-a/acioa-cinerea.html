Acioa cinerea

Acioa cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small leaflets.

Acioa cinerea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
