Adelostemma gracillimum

Adelostemma gracillimum, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Adelostemma gracillimum is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
