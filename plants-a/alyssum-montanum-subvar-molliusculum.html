Alyssum montanum subvar. molliusculum

Alyssum montanum subvar. molliusculum, commonly known as the mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically produces narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
