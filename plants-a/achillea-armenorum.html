Achillea armenorum

Achillea armenorum, commonly known as Armenian yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its common name is derived from the Greek hero Achilles, who was said to have used the plant for healing wounds.
