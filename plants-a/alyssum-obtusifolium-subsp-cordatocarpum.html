Alyssum obtusifolium subsp. cordatocarpum

Alyssum obtusifolium subsp.

cordatocarpum, commonly known as the heart-shaped fruit alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The subspecies is distinguished by its heart-shaped fruits, which are a notable feature in its botanical description. Native to regions of southern Europe, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects.
