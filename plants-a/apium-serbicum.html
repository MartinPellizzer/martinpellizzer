Apium serbicum

Apium serbicum, commonly known as Serbian cow parsley, is a perennial plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are finely divided with a parsley-like scent. It produces small, white or greenish-white flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters, typical of the carrot family.

The plant is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
