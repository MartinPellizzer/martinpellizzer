Anchusa undulata subsp. undulata

Anchusa undulata subsp.

undulata, commonly known as the undulating bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking spikes of blue to purple tubular flowers.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils. Anchusa undulata subsp.

undulata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
