Aplilia macrophyla

Aplilia macrophyla, commonly known as the large-flowered aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to pink petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Aplilia macrophyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant fall blooms.
