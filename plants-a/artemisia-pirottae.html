Artemisia pirottae

Artemisia pirottae, commonly known as the Pirotta's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Artemisia pirottae thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
