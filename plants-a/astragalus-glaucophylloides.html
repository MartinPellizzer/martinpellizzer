Astragalus glaucophylloides

Astragalus glaucophylloides, commonly known as the blue-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
