Agapetes arborea

Agapetes arborea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that often has a silvery or whitish underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late summer. Agapetes arborea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
