Anthurium smilacifolium

Anthurium smilacifolium, commonly known as the climbing anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces striking spadices, which are often red or orange, surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium smilacifolium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
