Apeiba tibourbou

Apeiba tibourbou, commonly known as the African rosewood or African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, richly colored wood, which is highly valued for furniture and decorative purposes. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange or red fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

The tree is often found in moist lowland forests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
