Anthurium dominicense

Anthurium dominicense, commonly known as the Dominique anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the spiky, colorful structures that surround the small true flowers. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Anthurium dominicense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
