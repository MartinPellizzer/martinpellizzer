Anemone nemorosa f. vulgaris

Anemone nemorosa f. vulgaris, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features delicate, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that emerge in the spring and wither by summer. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is characterized by its early flowering habit and its role in early succession plant communities.
