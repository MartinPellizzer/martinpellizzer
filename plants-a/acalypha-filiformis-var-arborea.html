Acalypha filiformis var. arborea

Acalypha filiformis var. arborea, commonly known as the tree spurge, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value is limited due to its toxic properties, but it plays a role in local ecosystems.
