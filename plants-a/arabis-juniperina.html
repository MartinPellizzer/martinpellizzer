Arabis juniperina

Arabis juniperina, commonly known as the Juniperleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or pinnately divided, with a distinctive aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, typical of the family.

Arabis juniperina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
