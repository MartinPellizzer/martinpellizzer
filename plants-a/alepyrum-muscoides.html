Alepyrum muscoides

Alepyrum muscoides, commonly known as the mossy grass or narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, narrow leaves that often form dense, mat-like growths. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. Alepyrum muscoides thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems where it contributes to soil stabilization and habitat diversity.
