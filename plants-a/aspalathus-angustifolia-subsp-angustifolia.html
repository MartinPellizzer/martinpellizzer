Aspalathus angustifolia subsp. angustifolia

Aspalathus angustifolia subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is well adapted to the local climate, thriving in sandy, well-drained soils. It is a member of the legume family and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This subspecies is particularly valued for its use in the production of rooibos tea.
