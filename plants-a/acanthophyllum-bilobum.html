Acanthophyllum bilobum

Acanthophyllum bilobum, commonly known as the two-lobed spiny broom, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its succulent stems and reduced leaves, which help conserve water. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.

The species is also valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
