Aspidium monticola

Aspidium monticola, commonly known as the mountain shield fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, finely divided fronds that often form a dense, shield-like structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are perennial, allowing it to persist through seasonal changes.

Aspidium monticola plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
