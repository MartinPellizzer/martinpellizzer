Aquarius floribundus

Aquarius floribundus, commonly known as the silver bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its botanical name reflects its association with water, as "Aquarius" refers to the constellation associated with water in Greek mythology.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
