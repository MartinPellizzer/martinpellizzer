Allium achaium

Allium achaium, commonly known as the purple onion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features bulbous roots and strap-like, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the summer months. Allium achaium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also known for its mild, sweet flavor, making it a versatile addition to both gardens and kitchens.
