Alsine subuniflora

Alsine subuniflora, commonly known as the slender alsike, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Alsine subuniflora thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
