Amomum alboviolaceum

Amomum alboviolaceum, commonly known as the white violet ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, known for its aromatic rhizomes and ornamental value.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a pale green to almost white coloration, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes produce small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet and white, adding ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and aromatic properties, it is cultivated both for horticultural purposes and traditional medicinal uses.
