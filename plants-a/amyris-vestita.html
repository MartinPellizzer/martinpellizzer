Amyris vestita

Amyris vestita, commonly known as the white sand pine, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and slender, branching crown. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, providing a distinctive appearance.

Amyris vestita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
