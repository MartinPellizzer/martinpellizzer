Andropogon brevifolius var. pullus

Andropogon brevifolius var. pullus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. It is a perennial grass that forms dense clumps and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable resource for wildlife.
