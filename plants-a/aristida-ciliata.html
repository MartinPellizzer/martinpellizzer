Aristida ciliata

Aristida ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or slender beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its fine, delicate foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, disturbed soils, and sandy areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered with silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aristida ciliata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
