Anthurium annularum

Anthurium annularum, commonly known as the ring anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its elongated, waxy leaves that often exhibit a distinctive ring-like pattern on the petiole. The plant produces vibrant spadix flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and conservation efforts.
