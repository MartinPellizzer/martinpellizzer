Anisophyllea polyneura

Anisophyllea polyneura, commonly known as the broad-leafed anisophyllea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually arranged alternately along the stems, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by clustered berries.

Anisophyllea polyneura is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
