Agropyron intermedium subsp. kosaninii

Agropyron intermedium subsp.

kosaninii, commonly known as the Kosanin's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy or loamy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is used in ecological restoration and livestock forage.
