Acacia parkerae

Acacia parkerae, commonly known as the Parker's wattle, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Acacia parkerae is well adapted to drought and can thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
