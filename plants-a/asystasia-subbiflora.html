Asystasia subbiflora

Asystasia subbiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asystasia, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length, with a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are typically violet or purple in color.

Asystasia subbiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
