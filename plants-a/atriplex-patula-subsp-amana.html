Atriplex patula subsp. amana

Atriplex patula subsp.

amana, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, often gray-green leaves that are adapted to retain water in dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it a valuable species for stabilizing soils in saline areas. Atriplex patula subsp.

amana is also used in traditional medicine and as forage for livestock.
