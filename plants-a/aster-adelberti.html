Aster adelberti

Aster adelberti, commonly known as the Adelbert aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces showy purple to pink flowers. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in the Appalachian region.

Aster adelberti is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
