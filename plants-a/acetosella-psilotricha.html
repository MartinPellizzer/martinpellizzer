Acetosella psilotricha

Acetosella psilotricha, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Acetosella psilotricha is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is considered a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
