Apocynum androsaemifolium var. pumilum

Apocynum androsaemifolium var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf dogbane, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its medicinal properties.
