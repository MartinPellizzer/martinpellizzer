Adenia saxicola

Adenia saxicola, commonly known as the rock adenium, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. Its roots are fibrous and capable of surviving in rocky or sandy soils.

Adenia saxicola is often cultivated as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries.
