Arceuthobium apachecum

Arceuthobium apachecum, commonly known as the Apache mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of coniferous trees.

It lacks true leaves and instead absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that contain the seeds necessary for its reproduction. Due to its specialized lifestyle, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem, often contributing to the nutrient cycle of the forest.

However, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
