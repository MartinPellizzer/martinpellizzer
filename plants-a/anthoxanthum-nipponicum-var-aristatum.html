Anthoxanthum nipponicum var. aristatum

Anthoxanthum nipponicum var. aristatum, commonly known as the Japanese bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils, often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture for its fine texture and dense growth habit.

This variety is also notable for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its soil-stabilizing properties.
