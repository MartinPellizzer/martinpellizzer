Astragalus insularis var. pondii

Astragalus insularis var. pondii, commonly known as the Pond's island milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of the Mediterranean.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in late spring. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance in its native habitat.
