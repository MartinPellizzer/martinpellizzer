Alpinia mollissima

Alpinia mollissima, commonly known as the velvet ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, velvety leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia mollissima is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in various Asian countries.
