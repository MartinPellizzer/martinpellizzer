Andinia pentamytera

Andinia pentamytera, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-colored petals with intricate veining. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in rocky soil, while its leaves are narrow and evergreen.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
