Artemisia baicalensis

Artemisia baicalensis, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous or semi-arid environments.

Its historical use in traditional Chinese medicine highlights its significance in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
