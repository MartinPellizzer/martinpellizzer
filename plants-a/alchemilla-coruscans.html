Alchemilla coruscans

Alchemilla coruscans, commonly known as the glittering lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in branched clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla coruscans is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
