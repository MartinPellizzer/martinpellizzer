Aeschynanthus obconicus

Aeschynanthus obconicus, commonly known as the wax plant, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aeschynanthus obconicus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.
