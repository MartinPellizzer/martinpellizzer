Amblyopappus neomexicanus

Amblyopappus neomexicanus, commonly known as New Mexico roughhead, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's structure and adaptations reflect its resilience to arid environments.
