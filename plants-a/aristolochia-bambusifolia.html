Aristolochia bambusifolia

Aristolochia bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and striking, tubular flowers that resemble a snake's head. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its stems are often woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees or other structures.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though some species in the genus are toxic and require caution.
