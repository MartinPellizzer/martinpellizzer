Achillea × javorkae

Achillea × javorkae, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a cross between Achillea millefolium and Achillea ptarmica, resulting in a plant with distinctive foliage and floral characteristics. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads in shades of white, pink, or red. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
