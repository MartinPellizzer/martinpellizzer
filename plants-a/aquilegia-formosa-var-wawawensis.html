Aquilegia formosa var. wawawensis

Aquilegia formosa var. wawawensis, commonly known as the Wawakone columbine, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred petals and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and produces showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
