Anemone obtusiloba var. leiophylla

Anemone obtusiloba var. leiophylla, commonly known as the smoothleaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth, leathery texture. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
