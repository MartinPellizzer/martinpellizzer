Anthemis moghanica

Anthemis moghanica, commonly known as Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are often used in traditional medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers is valued for its aromatic and therapeutic benefits.
