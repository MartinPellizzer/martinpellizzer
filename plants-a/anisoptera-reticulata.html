Anisoptera reticulata

Anisoptera reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are often covered in a fine, reticulate pattern, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber and fruit.
