Anemone quinquefolia var. lancifolia

Anemone quinquefolia var. lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodland areas and has palmately compound leaves with five to seven lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy white to pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are surrounded by a cluster of pale yellow to white bracts, and it is often found in shaded, rich soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
