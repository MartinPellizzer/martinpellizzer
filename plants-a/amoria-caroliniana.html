Amoria caroliniana

Amoria caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Amoria caroliniana is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
