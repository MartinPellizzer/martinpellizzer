Acacia paraguariensis

Acacia paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is well-known for its caffeine-containing leaves, which are used to make a traditional infusion. Its ability to fix nitrogen through root nodules makes it an important plant in soil enrichment.

Acacia paraguariensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid forests and along riverbanks.
