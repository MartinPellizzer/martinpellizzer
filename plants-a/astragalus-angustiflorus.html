Astragalus angustiflorus

Astragalus angustiflorus, commonly known as narrow-flower milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands or along streambanks. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus angustiflorus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
