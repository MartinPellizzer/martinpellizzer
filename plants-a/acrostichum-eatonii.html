Acrostichum eatonii

Acrostichum eatonii, commonly known as the Eaton's fern, is a species of fern native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically near streams or in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Acrostichum eatonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
