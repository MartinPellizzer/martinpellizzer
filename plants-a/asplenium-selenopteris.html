Asplenium selenopteris

Asplenium selenopteris, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided, silver-green fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its fronds are often arched or drooping, giving it an elegant appearance.

Botanically, it is noted for its delicate, lace-like structure and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
