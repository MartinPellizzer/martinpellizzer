Acanthus ilicifolius var. xiamenensis

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, fuzzy hair. The plant produces spiny, egg-shaped fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically along rocky stream banks or in forest understories.
