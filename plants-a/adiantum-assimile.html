Adiantum assimile

Adiantum assimile, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually bright green and turn bronze or red in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

Adiantum assimile is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
