Arum cannifolium

Arum cannifolium, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or arrowleaf arum, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces a striking inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

The plant is cultivated for its starchy rhizomes, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
