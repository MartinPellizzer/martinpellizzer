Aristida hamulosa

Aristida hamulosa, commonly known as the slender wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Aristida hamulosa is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
