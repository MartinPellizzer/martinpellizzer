Alyssum tetraspermum

Alyssum tetraspermum, commonly known as four-seed alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

The species is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
