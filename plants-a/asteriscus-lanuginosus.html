Asteriscus lanuginosus

Asteriscus lanuginosus, commonly known as the silky asterisk, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
