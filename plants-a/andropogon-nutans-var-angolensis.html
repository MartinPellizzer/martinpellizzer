Andropogon nutans var. angolensis

Andropogon nutans var. angolensis, commonly known as the Angola grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, tufted culms with dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is drought-tolerant.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
