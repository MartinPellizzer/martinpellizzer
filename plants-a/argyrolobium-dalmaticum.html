Argyrolobium dalmaticum

Argyrolobium dalmaticum, commonly known as the Dalmatian silverlobed oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and hillsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
