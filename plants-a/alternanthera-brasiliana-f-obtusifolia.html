Alternanthera brasiliana f. obtusifolia

Alternanthera brasiliana f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf Alternanthera, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically a deep red or bronze color, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually pink or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is popular in water gardens and bog environments due to its ornamental foliage and adaptability.
