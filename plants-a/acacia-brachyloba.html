Acacia brachyloba

Acacia brachyloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Acacia brachyloba is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
