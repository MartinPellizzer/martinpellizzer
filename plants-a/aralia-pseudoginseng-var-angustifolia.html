Aralia pseudoginseng var. angustifolia

Aralia pseudoginseng var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf aralia, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into several narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
