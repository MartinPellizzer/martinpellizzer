Achillea × dumasiana

Achillea × dumasiana, commonly known as the Dumas yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
