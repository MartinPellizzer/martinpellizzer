Aloexylum agallochum

Aloexylum agallochum, commonly known as blackwood or Indian sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits.

The tree is often cultivated for its wood and has cultural significance in various traditional practices.
