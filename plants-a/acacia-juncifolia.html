Acacia juncifolia

Acacia juncifolia, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, feathery leaves that are typically arranged in spirals. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Acacia juncifolia is well adapted to dry and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in arid environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
