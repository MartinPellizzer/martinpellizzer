Antrophyum coriaceum

Antrophyum coriaceum, commonly known as the hard-leaved cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and leathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and are typically a deep green color.

Antrophyum coriaceum is native to certain regions of South America and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
