Aureolaria Pedicularia

Aureolaria pedicularia is a small, delicate flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is known for its clusters of tiny, pale yellow blooms that appear in late spring. This species thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Despite its modest size, it plays an important role in supporting local pollinators. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
