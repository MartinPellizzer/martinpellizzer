Artemisia princeps

Artemisia princeps, commonly known as Japanese mugwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Artemisia princeps has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
