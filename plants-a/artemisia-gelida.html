Artemisia gelida

Artemisia gelida, commonly known as bitter wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, often pungent, leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Artemisia gelida thrives in cold, dry environments and is adapted to withstand harsh winters.

It is often found in tundra and alpine habitats, playing a role in local ecosystems.
