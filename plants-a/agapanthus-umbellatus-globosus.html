Agapanthus umbellatus globosus

Agapanthus umbellatus globosus, commonly known as the blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescences that produce clusters of blue or white flowers. The plant features strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom reliably in summer.
