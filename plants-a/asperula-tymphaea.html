Asperula tymphaea

Asperula tymphaea, commonly known as the Alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Asperula tymphaea is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.
