Aconitum heterophyllum subsp. parciflorum

Aconitum heterophyllum subsp.

parciflorum, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that are surrounded by two large, hood-like petals. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a distinctive appearance that adds to its ornamental value.

However, like other members of the aconite genus, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
