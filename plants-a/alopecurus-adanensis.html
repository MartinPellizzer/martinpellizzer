Alopecurus adanensis

Alopecurus adanensis, commonly known as the Adan's hairgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that typically forms dense, mat-like growths on the ground. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Alopecurus adanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect populations.
