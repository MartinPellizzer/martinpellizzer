Anodendron scandens

Anodendron scandens, commonly known as the climbing aralia, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features compound leaves with multiple leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern, and its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in umbels. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Anodendron scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
