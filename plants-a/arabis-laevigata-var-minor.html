Arabis laevigata var. minor

Arabis laevigata var. minor, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are rounded and slightly hairy, with a dark green coloration. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and delicate floral display.
