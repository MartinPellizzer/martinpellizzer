Allium tianschanicum

Allium tianschanicum, commonly known as the Tianshan garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale purple flowers during the summer months. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice due to their pungent aroma.

Allium tianschanicum is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
