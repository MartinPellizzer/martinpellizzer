Aframomum alboviolaceum

Aframomum alboviolaceum, commonly known as the white viola cardamom, is a tropical plant belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to western Africa, particularly Nigeria and Cameroon, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces fragrant, violet-colored flowers and has aromatic, rhizomatous roots that are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, spicy scent, similar to other members of the ginger family.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in local remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
