Aristolochia urupaensis

Aristolochia urupaensis, commonly known as the Urupa Dutchman's Pipe, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, which are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and woody.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
