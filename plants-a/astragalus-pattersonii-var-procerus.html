Astragalus pattersonii var. procerus

Astragalus pattersonii var. procerus, commonly known as the tall milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
