Abutilon mollicomum

Abutilon mollicomum, commonly known as velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in dry, open habitats.
