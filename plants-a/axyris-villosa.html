Axyris villosa

Axyris villosa, commonly known as the hairy axyris, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small shrub characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Axyris villosa is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
