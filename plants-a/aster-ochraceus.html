Aster ochraceus

Aster ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Aster ochraceus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
