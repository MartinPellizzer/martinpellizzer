Aeschynomene dimidiata

Aeschynomene dimidiata, commonly known as partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a leguminous species belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
