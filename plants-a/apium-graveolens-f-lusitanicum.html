Apium graveolens f. lusitanicum

Apium graveolens f. lusitanicum, commonly known as the Portuguese dill or wild celery, is a subspecies of the celery plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves and small, umbel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils along riverbanks and in wetlands. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other members of the Apiaceae family.
