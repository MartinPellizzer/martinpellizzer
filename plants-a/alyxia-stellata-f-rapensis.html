Alyxia stellata f. rapensis

Alyxia stellata f. rapensis, commonly known as the red-flowered alyxia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
