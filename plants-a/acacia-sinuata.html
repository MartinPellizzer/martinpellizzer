Acacia sinuata

Acacia sinuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in compact, globular clusters. Acacia sinuata is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
