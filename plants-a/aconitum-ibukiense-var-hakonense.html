Aconitum ibukiense var. hakonense

Aconitum ibukiense var. hakonense, commonly known as the Hakone aconite, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are deeply divided into palmate lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
