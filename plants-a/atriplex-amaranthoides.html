Atriplex amaranthoides

Atriplex amaranthoides, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it thrive in saline soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its stems and leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as forage for livestock.

Atriplex amaranthoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting desert ecosystems.
