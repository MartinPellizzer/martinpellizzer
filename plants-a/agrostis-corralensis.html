Agrostis corralensis

Agrostis corralensis, commonly known as the Corralensis bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has fine, slender leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Agrostis corralensis is also an important component of native plant communities in its habitat.
