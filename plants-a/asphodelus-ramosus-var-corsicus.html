Asphodelus ramosus var. corsicus

Asphodelus ramosus var. corsicus, commonly known as Corsican asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
