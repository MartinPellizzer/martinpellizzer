Austroblechnum ascendens

Austroblechnum ascendens, commonly known as the ascending water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay. The plant is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that arise from a creeping rhizome. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with small, lanceolate pinnae that are arranged in a distinctive pattern.

Austroblechnum ascendens thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in dense forests.
