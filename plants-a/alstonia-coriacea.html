Alstonia coriacea

Alstonia coriacea, commonly known as the rough alstonia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, cylindrical fruits containing several seeds. Alstonia coriacea is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
