Acacia polyacantha subsp. campylacantha

Acacia polyacantha subsp.

campylacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and narrow, bipinnate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, triangular seed pods. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy species for ecological restoration.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
