Abroma angulatum

Abroma angulatum, commonly known as the African violet or the angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, purple, and blue, often with a distinctive angular shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
