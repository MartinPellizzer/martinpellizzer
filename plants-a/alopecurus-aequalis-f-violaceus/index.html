Alopecurus Aequalis F. Violaceus

Alopecurus aequalis f. violaceus is a unique variant of the common bentgrass known for its striking violet-blue flower heads. This ornamental grass thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for small garden spaces and rock gardens. The plant's distinctive color adds visual interest to landscapes throughout the growing season. Despite its beauty, it requires careful maintenance to prevent it from spreading uncontrollably in natural habitats.
