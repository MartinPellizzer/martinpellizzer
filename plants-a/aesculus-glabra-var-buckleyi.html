Aesculus glabra var. buckleyi

Aesculus glabra var. buckleyi, commonly known as the Buckleys' horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 lobes, and its showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters. The tree produces large, spiny capsules containing glossy, amber-brown seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wooded areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
