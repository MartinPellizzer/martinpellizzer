Atriplex incana

Atriplex incana, commonly known as silverleaf chenopod, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Atriplex incana is adapted to thrive in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
