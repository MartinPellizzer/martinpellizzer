Aphelandra ameleta

Aphelandra ameleta, commonly known as the silver velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or velvety texture. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with bright yellow bracts that resemble flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Aphelandra ameleta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
