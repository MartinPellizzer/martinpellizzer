Allosorus sagittatus

Allosorus sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy, with a distinctive arrowhead shape. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Allosorus sagittatus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
