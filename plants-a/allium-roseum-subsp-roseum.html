Allium roseum subsp. roseum

Allium roseum subsp.

roseum, commonly known as the rose garlic or pink garlic, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, bulbous structure, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate, fragrant blooms and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
