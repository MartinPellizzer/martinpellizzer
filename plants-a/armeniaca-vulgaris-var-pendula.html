Armeniaca vulgaris var. pendula

Armeniaca vulgaris var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its graceful, drooping branches that bear clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant produces small, edible almonds, though these are typically not harvested for consumption. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This ornamental tree is often planted for its aesthetic appeal and is popular in gardens and parks.
