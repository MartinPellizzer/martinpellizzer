Arabis glauca var. serrata

Arabis glauca var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and have serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and delicate blooms.
