Astragalus illyricus var. brachyceras

Astragalus illyricus var. brachyceras, commonly known as the Illyrian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to semi-arid climates. It is known for its small, rounded seed pods and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
