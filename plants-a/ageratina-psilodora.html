Ageratina psilodora

Ageratina psilodora, commonly known as the slender blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flower heads that are arranged in dense, fluffy clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Ageratina psilodora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
