Acanthomintha obovata

Acanthomintha obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf thyme.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy texture. Its small, pinkish-white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Acanthomintha obovata is well adapted to rocky soils and dry climates, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
