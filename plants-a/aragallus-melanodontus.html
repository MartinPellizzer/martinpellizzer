Aragallus melanodontus

Aragallus melanodontus, commonly known as the black-toothed aragallus, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
