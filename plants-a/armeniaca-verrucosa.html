Armeniaca verrucosa

Armeniaca verrucosa, commonly known as the Persian nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the Solanaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan, Iran, and Turkey. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, and they contain seeds enclosed in a sticky pulp.

Armeniaca verrucosa is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and historical significance in traditional medicine.
