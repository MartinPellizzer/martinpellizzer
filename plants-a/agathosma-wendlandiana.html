Agathosma wendlandiana

Agathosma wendlandiana, commonly known as Cape geranium, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to coastal climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Agathosma wendlandiana is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
