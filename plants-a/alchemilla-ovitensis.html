Alchemilla ovitensis

Alchemilla ovitensis, commonly known as the rock alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Alchemilla ovitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
