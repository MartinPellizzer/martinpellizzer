Anthyllis vulneraria var. affinis

Anthyllis vulneraria var. affinis, commonly known as the lesser burnet, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called globular inflorescences. Its leaves are pinnate, composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is closely related to the common burnet and is often found in Mediterranean and temperate regions.
