Abies salouenensis

Abies salouenensis, commonly known as the Salouen fir, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Spain.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds surrounded by a papery wing. It thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soils, often found in high-altitude forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from climate change and habitat loss.
