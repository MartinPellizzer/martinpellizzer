Arctophila fulva f. aristata

Arctophila fulva f. aristata, commonly known as the white-flowered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features include a basal rosette structure and a tendency to form dense mats over time.
