Acer emeiense

Acer emeiense, commonly known as the Emei maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. It typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
