Asphodeline peshmeniana

Asphodeline peshmeniana, commonly known as the Armenian asphodel, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
