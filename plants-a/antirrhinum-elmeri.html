Antirrhinum elmeri

Antirrhinum elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of brightly colored, tubular flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Antirrhinum elmeri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
