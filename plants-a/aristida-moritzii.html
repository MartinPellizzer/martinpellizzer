Aristida moritzii

Aristida moritzii, commonly known as Moritz's threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Aristida moritzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
