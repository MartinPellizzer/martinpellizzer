Anacardium amilcarianum

Anacardium amilcarianum, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces oval, woody fruits that contain a single seed enclosed in a spiny capsule. Its leaves and fruits contain toxic compounds, including urushiol, which can cause skin irritation.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value but is considered an invasive species in some regions.
