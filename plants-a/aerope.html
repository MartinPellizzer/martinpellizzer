Aerope

Aerope, commonly known as the sky plant, is a unique species native to high-altitude regions.

It thrives in rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Aerope has adapted to low oxygen levels and intense sunlight through specialized cellular structures.

Its botanical significance lies in its resilience and potential medicinal properties.
