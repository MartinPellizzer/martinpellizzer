Abutilon eximium

Abutilon eximium, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Abutilon eximium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
