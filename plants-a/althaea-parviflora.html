Althaea parviflora

Althaea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mallows, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, ribbed stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Althaea parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
