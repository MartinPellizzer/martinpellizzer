Antirrhinum majus var. faurei

Antirrhinum majus var. faurei, commonly known as the Faure's snapdragon, is a cultivar of the snapdragon plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The flowers are typically bilaterally symmetrical, with two lips that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for both garden beds and container planting.
