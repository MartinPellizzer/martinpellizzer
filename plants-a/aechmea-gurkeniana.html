Aechmea gurkeniana

Aechmea gurkeniana, commonly known as the Guerkeniana bromeliad, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant features striking, colorful inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically broad and sword-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Aechmea gurkeniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea gurkeniana has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green to blue-green color and a waxy surface.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, thick base that supports the rosette, with a smooth, pale green bark. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by brightly colored bracts, with pink to red flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animal contact.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for water storage, typical of bromeliads.
