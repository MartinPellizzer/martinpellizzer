Adenocalymma inundatum var. typica

Adenocalymma inundatum var. typica, commonly known as the water bladder grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments in South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bladder-like structures that store water, allowing it to thrive in saturated soils. The plant typically has slender, branching stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or floating. Its leaves are usually narrow and may be partially submerged, contributing to its ability to absorb nutrients from the water.

Adenocalymma inundatum var. typica plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
