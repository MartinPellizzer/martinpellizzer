Astragalus bounacanthus

Astragalus bounacanthus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family.

Astragalus bounacanthus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
