Astragalus guzelsuensis

Astragalus guzelsuensis, commonly known as the Guzelsuian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
