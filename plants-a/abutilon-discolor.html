Abutilon discolor

Abutilon discolor, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it both a useful plant for erosion control and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
