Anthurium trilobum

Anthurium trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, which are typically deep green and glossy. The plant produces vibrant, spatulate-shaped spathes that can range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting midrib. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium trilobum is popular as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
