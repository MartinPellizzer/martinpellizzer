Athyrium iseanum

Athyrium iseanum, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to East Asia.

It features finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and have a graceful, arching appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, which are covered by a kidney-shaped indusium. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its sensitivity to environmental changes, it requires careful cultivation to maintain its health and vigor.
