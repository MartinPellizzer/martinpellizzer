Aconitum glabrum

Aconitum glabrum, commonly known as the European aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with care.
