Aloe verrucosa var. latifolia

Aloe verrucosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are broader than those of the typical Aloe verrucosa, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and as a houseplant.
