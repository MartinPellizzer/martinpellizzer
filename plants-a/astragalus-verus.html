Astragalus verus

Astragalus verus, commonly known as the true milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is often extensive, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Astragalus verus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
