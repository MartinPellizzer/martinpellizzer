Apocynum cannabinum var. greeneanum

Apocynum cannabinum var. greeneanum, commonly known as Greene's dogbane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces follicles that contain toxic seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and subtle morphological differences from the typical form of Apocynum cannabinum.
