Allium howellii

Allium howellii, commonly known as Howell's onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, tall flower stalk that bears a cluster of small, white to pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Allium howellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
