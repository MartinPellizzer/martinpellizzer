Asteriscium amplexicaule

Asteriscium amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping asterisk, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, clasping leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in pink or purple hues, and bloom in late summer.

Asteriscium amplexicaule thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
