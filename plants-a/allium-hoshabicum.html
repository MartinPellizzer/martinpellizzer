Allium hoshabicum

Allium hoshabicum, commonly known as the Hoshabiku garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, strap-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
