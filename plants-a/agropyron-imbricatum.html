Agropyron imbricatum

Agropyron imbricatum, commonly known as the rough wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences bearing small, two-lobed spikelets. Agropyron imbricatum is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

It is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
