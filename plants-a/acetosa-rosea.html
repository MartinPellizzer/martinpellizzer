Acetosa rosea

Acetosa rosea, commonly known as the pink rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Acetosa rosea is often found in meadows, cliffs, and along rocky slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
