Anemone pratensis var. sordida

Anemone pratensis var. sordida, commonly known as the sordid meadow anemone, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a subtle purple tinge. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue.

The species is characterized by its relatively small stature and its ability to thrive in disturbed or shaded habitats.
