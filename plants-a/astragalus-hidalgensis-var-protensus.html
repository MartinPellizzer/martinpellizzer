Astragalus hidalgensis var. protensus

Astragalus hidalgensis var. protensus, commonly known as the Hidalgo milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
