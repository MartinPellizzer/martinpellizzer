Acacia carnosula

Acacia carnosula, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Acacia carnosula is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of gum arabic.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.
