Acacia hildebrandtii

Acacia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance.

Acacia hildebrandtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
