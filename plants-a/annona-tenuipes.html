Annona tenuipes

Annona tenuipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf soursop, is a small, evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces large, fragrant, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a rough, spiny berry that turns yellow when mature, containing a single large, inedible seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
