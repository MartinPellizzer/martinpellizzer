Acrostichum stramineum

Acrostichum stramineum, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often golden-yellow fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, almost feathery appearance.

The golden fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
