Anabasis echinopoda

Anabasis echinopoda, commonly known as the spiny desertflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with erect, branching stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are small, linear, and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Anabasis echinopoda is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
