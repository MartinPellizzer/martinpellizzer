Aspidium craspedocarpum var. japonicum

Aspidium craspedocarpum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Aspidium.

It is a perennial aquatic plant that thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant has finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically submerged or floating. It possesses a rhizome that anchors it to the substrate and supports its growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in aquatic habitats.
