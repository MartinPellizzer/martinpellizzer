Aster lamarckianus

Aster lamarckianus, commonly known as the Lamarck's aster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

Aster lamarckianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
