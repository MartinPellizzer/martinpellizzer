Arundo acutifolia

Arundo acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf reed, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with sharp edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water bodies.

Arundo acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian zones.
