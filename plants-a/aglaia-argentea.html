Aglaia argentea

Aglaia argentea, commonly known as silver aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are followed by round, orange-red fruits containing hard, shiny seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
