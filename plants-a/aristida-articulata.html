Aristida articulata

Aristida articulata, commonly known as the jointed grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, Aristida articulata is often used in ecological restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
