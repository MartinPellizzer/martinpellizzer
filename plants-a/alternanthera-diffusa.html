Alternanthera diffusa

Alternanthera diffusa, commonly known as redshank, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are often opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

Alternanthera diffusa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
