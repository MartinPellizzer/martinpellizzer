Aristea biflora

Aristea biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered aristea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Aristea biflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
