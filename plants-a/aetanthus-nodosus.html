Aetanthus nodosus

Aetanthus nodosus, commonly known as the knotty aetanthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom in the summer months. Aetanthus nodosus is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a deep root system for water retention.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
