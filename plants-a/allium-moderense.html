Allium moderense

Allium moderense, commonly known as the narrowleaf garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions.
