Aristolochia whitei

Aristolochia whitei, commonly known as the white Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves and showy blooms. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its attractive appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
