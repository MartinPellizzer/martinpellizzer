Acer campestre f. brachypterum

Acer campestre f. brachypterum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved maple, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a compact form of the common maple, characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
