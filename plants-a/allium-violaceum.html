Allium violaceum

Allium violaceum, commonly known as purple onion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that are often used as ornamental plants in gardens. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Allium violaceum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
