Anychia capillacea

Anychia capillacea, commonly known as the silky anychia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
