Astragalus nezva-montis

Astragalus nezva-montis, commonly known as mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale green flowers.

Astragalus nezva-montis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
