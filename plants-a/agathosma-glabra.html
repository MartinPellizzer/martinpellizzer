Agathosma glabra

Agathosma glabra, commonly known as Cape rosemary, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
