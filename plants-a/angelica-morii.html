Angelica morii

Angelica morii, commonly known as the Japanese angelica, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves. Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
