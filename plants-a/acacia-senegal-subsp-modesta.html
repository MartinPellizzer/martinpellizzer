Acacia senegal subsp. modesta

Acacia senegal subsp.

modesta, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces a gum resin, known as acacia gum, which is harvested from its bark and used in various industries. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into pairs of leaflets, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and economic importance in local communities.

Native Distribution

Acacia senegal subsp.

modesta is primarily native to the Sahel region of Africa, including countries such as Mali, Niger, Chad, and Sudan. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is commonly found in savannas and woodland areas where it plays an important ecological role. It is well adapted to withstand drought conditions and high temperatures.

The plant is also known for its gum production, which is used in various industries.
