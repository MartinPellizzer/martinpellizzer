Anathallis lasioglossa

Anathallis lasioglossa, commonly known as the velvet lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surface of tree trunks or branches rather than in soil.

Anathallis lasioglossa is known for its unique floral structure, which plays a crucial role in pollination by specific insect species.
