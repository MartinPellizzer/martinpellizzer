Aganope thyrsiflora var. wallichii

Aganope thyrsiflora var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple petals and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its unique appearance.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
