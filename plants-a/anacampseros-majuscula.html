Anacampseros majuscula

Anacampseros majuscula, commonly known as the giant stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
