Anthurium longicuspidatum

Anthurium longicuspidatum, commonly known as the long-pointed anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces spadices, which are specialized inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, white, and sometimes red, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Anthurium longicuspidatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
