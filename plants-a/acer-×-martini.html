Acer × martini

Acer × martini, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Sapindaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that change color throughout the seasons, ranging from bright green in spring to vibrant red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 6–15 meters and prefers well-drained, acidic soil in partial shade. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves, and the tree is known for its moderate growth rate and adaptability to various climates.
