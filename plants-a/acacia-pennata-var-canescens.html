Acacia pennata var. canescens

Acacia pennata var. canescens, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia pennata var. canescens is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
