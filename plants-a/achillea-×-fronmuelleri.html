Achillea × fronmuelleri

Achillea × fronmuelleri, commonly known as the Fronmueller yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy white to pale yellow flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant has feathery, silver-green leaves that are finely divided and aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long bloom period and ability to attract pollinators.
