Apium graveolens var. butronense

Apium graveolens var. butronense, commonly known as the Butron fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and yellow umbels of flowers. The plant produces slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. Its seeds are small, dry, and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The foliage and seeds are valued for their distinctive licorice-like aroma.
