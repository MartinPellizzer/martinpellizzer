Acacia brachybotrya

Acacia brachybotrya, commonly known as the narrow-fruit acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Acacia brachybotrya is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
