Agapetes alberti-edwardii

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish pubescence. It produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring.
