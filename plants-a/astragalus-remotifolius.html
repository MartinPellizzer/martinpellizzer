Astragalus remotifolius

Astragalus remotifolius, commonly known as the distant-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, semi-desert environments. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus remotifolius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
