Aulomyrcia cuneata

Aulomyrcia cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aulomyrcia cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
