Anacardium occidentale

Anacardium occidentale, commonly known as the cashew tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It produces an edible nut encased in a toxic, resinous shell. The plant belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other species like the mango and poison ivy. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The cashew tree is valued for its economic importance, particularly for its nut and the cashew apple, which is used in various culinary applications.
