Acacia myrtifolia f. angustifolia

Acacia myrtifolia f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
