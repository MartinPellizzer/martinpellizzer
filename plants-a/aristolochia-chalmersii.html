Aristolochia chalmersii

Aristolochia chalmersii, commonly known as the Chalmers' Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is known for its strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The genus Aristolochia is known for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
