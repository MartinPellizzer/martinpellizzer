Aristida bissei

Aristida bissei, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, with small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Aristida bissei is an important component of native plant communities and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
