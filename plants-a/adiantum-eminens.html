Adiantum eminens

Adiantum eminens, commonly known as the tall maidenhair fern, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright fronds that are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its delicate, feathery appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fronds are typically green with a slightly silvery underside, contributing to its distinctive visual appeal.
