Anthemis grangeoides

Anthemis grangeoides, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to harsh, rocky environments and thrives in full sun.

Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.
