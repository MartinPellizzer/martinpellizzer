Alnus incana subsp. tchangbokii

Alnus incana subsp.

tchangbokii, commonly known as the Korean alder, is a shrub or small tree native to East Asia. It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for various wildlife species.
