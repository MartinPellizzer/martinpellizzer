Albizia gamblei

Albizia gamblei, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy, cream-colored, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The tree produces flat, brown pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Albizia gamblei has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical climates.
