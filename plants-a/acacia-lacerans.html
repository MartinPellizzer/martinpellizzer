Acacia lacerans

Acacia lacerans, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. Acacia lacerans thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
