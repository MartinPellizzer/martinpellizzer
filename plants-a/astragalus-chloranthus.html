Astragalus chloranthus

Astragalus chloranthus, commonly known as the green-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Russia. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus chloranthus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky soils.
