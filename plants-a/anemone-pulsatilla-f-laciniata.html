Anemone pulsatilla f. laciniata

Anemone pulsatilla f. laciniata, commonly known as the divided pasqueflower, is a botanical variant of the pasqueflower species.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early-season floral display.
