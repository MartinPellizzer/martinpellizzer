Alpinia × okinawaensis

Alpinia × okinawaensis, commonly known as the Okinawan ginger, is a hybrid plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and ornamental foliage. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. It produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

Alpinia × okinawaensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
