Agave kirchneriana

Agave kirchneriana, commonly known as the Kirchner's agave, is a succulent plant native to the Mexican state of Sinaloa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk adorned with yellowish-green inflorescences, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Agave kirchneriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in the production of agave-based products.
