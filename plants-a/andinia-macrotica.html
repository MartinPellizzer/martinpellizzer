Andinia macrotica

Andinia macrotica, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists worldwide.
