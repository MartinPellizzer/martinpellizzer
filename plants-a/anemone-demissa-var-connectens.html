Anemone demissa var. connectens

Anemone demissa var. connectens, commonly known as the spreading anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive central red eye, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it spreads via rhizomes, forming dense clumps. It thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
