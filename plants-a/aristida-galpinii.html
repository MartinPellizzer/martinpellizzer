Aristida galpinii

Aristida galpinii, commonly known as Galpin's threeawn, is a flowering grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of multiple bristle-like florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Aristida galpinii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
