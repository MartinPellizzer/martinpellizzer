Astragalus convallarius var. diversifolius

Astragalus convallarius var. diversifolius, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with variable leaflet shapes, which contribute to its varietal name. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
