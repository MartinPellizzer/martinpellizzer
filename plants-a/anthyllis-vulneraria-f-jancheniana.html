Anthyllis vulneraria f. jancheniana

Anthyllis vulneraria f. jancheniana, commonly known as the Janchen's Scotch thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, violet to pink flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
