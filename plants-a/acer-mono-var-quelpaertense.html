Acer mono var. quelpaertense

Acer mono var. quelpaertense, commonly known as the Jeju maple, is a unique variant of the maple tree native to the island of Jeju in South Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage, which turns vibrant shades of crimson and orange in the autumn. The plant has elliptical leaves with a serrated edge and typically grows to a moderate height. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental tree.

Due to its limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic value.
