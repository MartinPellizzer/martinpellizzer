Acalypha tristis

Acalypha tristis, commonly known as the weeping croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping inflorescences that resemble cascading ribbons. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a striking reddish-purple coloration. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, upright spikes.

Acalypha tristis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
