Astragalus asterias

Astragalus asterias, commonly known as the star vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its historical use in traditional medicine has also contributed to its botanical significance.
