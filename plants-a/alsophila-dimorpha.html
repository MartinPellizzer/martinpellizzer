Alsophila dimorpha

Alsophila dimorpha, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnately divided fronds. The plant exhibits a dichotomous branching pattern, with the trunk growing straight and the fronds emerging from the base. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Alsophila dimorpha is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks.
