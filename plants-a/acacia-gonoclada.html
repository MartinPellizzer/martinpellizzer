Acacia gonoclada

Acacia gonoclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Acacia gonoclada is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
