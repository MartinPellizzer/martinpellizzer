Astragalus alopecurus

Astragalus alopecurus, commonly known as hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. It has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Astragalus alopecurus is often used as a cover crop and for forage due to its hardy nature and growth habit.
