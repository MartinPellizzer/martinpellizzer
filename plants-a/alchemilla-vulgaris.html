Alchemilla vulgaris

Alchemilla vulgaris, commonly known as lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla vulgaris is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
