Ardisia linearifolia

Ardisia linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are a striking feature of its appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
