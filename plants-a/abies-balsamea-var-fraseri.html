Abies balsamea var. fraseri

Abies balsamea var. fraseri, commonly known as Fraser fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Appalachian Mountains of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are shorter and more pointed than those of the typical balsam fir. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Fraser fir thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often found at high elevations.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in the production of Christmas trees.
