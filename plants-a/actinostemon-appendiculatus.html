Actinostemon appendiculatus

Actinostemon appendiculatus, commonly known as the threadleaf arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. The plant features slender, upright stems with heart-shaped leaves that are finely toothed along the edges. Its flowers are small and clustered in spadices, typical of the Araceae family.

The plant is known for its starchy rhizomes, which are used in traditional cuisines and as a source of carbohydrates.
