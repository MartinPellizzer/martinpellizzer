Artemisia aucheri

Artemisia aucheri, commonly known as Armenian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features silver-gray, aromatic leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent, which is due to its high concentration of volatile oils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
