Achillea asperula

Achillea asperula, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in meadows and along roadsides.

Achillea asperula is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory qualities.
