Aspalathus cognata

Aspalathus cognata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Aspalathus cognata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
