Acacia colei

Acacia colei, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Acacia colei plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
