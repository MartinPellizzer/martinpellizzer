Adenarake

Adenarake, commonly known as the silverleaf adenarake, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Adenarake thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical research.
