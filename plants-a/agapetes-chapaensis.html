Agapetes chapaensis

Agapetes chapaensis, commonly known as the Chapa mountain heather, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. This evergreen shrub typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded environments. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
