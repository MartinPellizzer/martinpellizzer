Acoridium affine

Acoridium affine, commonly known as the slender swamp lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family and is characterized by its strap-like leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Acoridium affine plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
