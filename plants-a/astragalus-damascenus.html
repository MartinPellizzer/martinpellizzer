Astragalus damascenus

Astragalus damascenus, commonly known as the Damascus milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of the Mediterranean. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus damascenus is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to drought conditions.
