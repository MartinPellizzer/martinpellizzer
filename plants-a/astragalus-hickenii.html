Astragalus hickenii

Astragalus hickenii, commonly known as Hicken's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus hickenii is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
