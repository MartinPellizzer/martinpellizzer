Acacia telmica

Acacia telmica, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Acacia telmica is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it well-suited to harsh environments.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for extended periods.
