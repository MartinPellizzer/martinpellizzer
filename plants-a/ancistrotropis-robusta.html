Ancistrotropis robusta

Ancistrotropis robusta, commonly known as the robust sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive and adapted to access water from deep underground.

Ancistrotropis robusta is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in challenging climates.
