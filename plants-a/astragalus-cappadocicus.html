Astragalus cappadocicus

Astragalus cappadocicus, commonly known as Cappadocian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its roots are known to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus cappadocicus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
