Asparagus asiaticus var. scaberulus

Asparagus asiaticus var. scaberulus, commonly known as the rough asparagus, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features slender, arching stems that produce small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with stiff, backward-curving leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its berries are red and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.
