Amaryllis crocata

Amaryllis crocata, commonly known as the crocus amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces a tall, sturdy stem with clustered flowers that resemble those of the true crocus. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and lance-shaped.

Amaryllis crocata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.
