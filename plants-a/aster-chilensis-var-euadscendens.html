Aster chilensis var. euadscendens

Aster chilensis var. euadscendens, commonly known as the Chilean aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
