Andropogon scoparius var. villosissimus

Andropogon scoparius var. villosissimus, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and tufted inflorescence that produces small, blue-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
