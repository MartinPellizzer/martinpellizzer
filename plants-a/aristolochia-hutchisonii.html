Aristolochia hutchisonii

Aristolochia hutchisonii, commonly known as the Chinese birthwort, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often green or purple in color. The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its complex, winding growth habit. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and the plant contains toxic compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
