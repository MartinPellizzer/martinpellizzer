Astragalus compactus subsp. isparticus

Astragalus compactus subsp.

isparticus, commonly known as the Isparta milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Turkey. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the pods are slender and hairy.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
