Alchemilla fruticulosa

Alchemilla fruticulosa, commonly known as lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and distinctive silver-white, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla fruticulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
