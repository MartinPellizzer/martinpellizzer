Abies bifolia

Abies bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved fir, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in two rows on either side of the stem. The cones of Abies bifolia are typically slender and upright, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
