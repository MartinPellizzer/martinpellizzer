Astragalus kaibensis

Astragalus kaibensis, commonly known as Kaibab milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. This perennial plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky slopes and desert grasslands. Astragalus kaibensis is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system to access water.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
