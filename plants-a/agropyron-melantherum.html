Agropyron melantherum

Agropyron melantherum, commonly known as the blackgrass or slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the colder regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark green, slender leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and cooler climates, often found in mountainous and alpine environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Agropyron melantherum is also valued for its forage quality and has been used in ecological restoration projects.
