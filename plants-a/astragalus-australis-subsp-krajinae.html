Astragalus australis subsp. krajinae

Astragalus australis subsp.

krajinae, commonly known as the Krajina's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or grasslands.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in arid conditions.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for native pollinators.
