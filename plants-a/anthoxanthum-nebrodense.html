Anthoxanthum nebrodense

Anthoxanthum nebrodense, commonly known as the Nebrodense false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale yellow to orange flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
