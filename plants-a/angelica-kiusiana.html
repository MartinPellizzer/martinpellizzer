Angelica kiusiana

Angelica kiusiana, commonly known as the Japanese angelica, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound leaves. Angelica kiusiana thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
