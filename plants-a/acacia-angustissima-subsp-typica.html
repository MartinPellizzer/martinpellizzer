Acacia angustissima subsp. typica

Acacia angustissima subsp.

typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife. The wood of Acacia angustissima subsp.

typica is hard and durable, historically used by Indigenous Australians for tools and weapons.
