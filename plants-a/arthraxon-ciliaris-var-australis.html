Arthraxon ciliaris var. australis

Arthraxon ciliaris var. australis, commonly known as the Australian flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, often nutrient-rich soils. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It is characterized by its ability to form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This variety is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to seasonal flooding.
