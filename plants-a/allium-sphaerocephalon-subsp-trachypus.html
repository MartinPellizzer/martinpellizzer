Allium sphaerocephalon subsp. trachypus

Allium sphaerocephalon subsp.

trachypus, commonly known as the globe onion or purple globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features round, bulbous stems that are often covered in a purple or reddish hue.

The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows in well-drained soils in sunny locations.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
