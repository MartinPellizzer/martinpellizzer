Avena neglecta

Avena neglecta, commonly known as wild oat, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Avena neglecta is often considered a weed but has been used historically in animal feed and traditional medicine.
