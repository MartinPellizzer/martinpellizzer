Acer rubrum f. semiorbiculatum

Acer rubrum f. semiorbiculatum, commonly known as the red maple with semicircular leaves, is a variant of the red maple tree characterized by its distinctive leaf shape.

The leaves are broader and more rounded than typical red maple leaves, with a semi-orbicular outline. This form is often found in shaded or moist environments, where it thrives in partial to full shade. The foliage displays vibrant red to orange hues in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

It is a relatively rare and unique form, appreciated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
