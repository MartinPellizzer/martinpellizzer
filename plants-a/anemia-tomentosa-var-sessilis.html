Anemia tomentosa var. sessilis

Anemia tomentosa var. sessilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It is native to regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, hairy texture, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a dense covering of fine hairs. It produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
