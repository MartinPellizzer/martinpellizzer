Artemisia biennis var. diffusa

Artemisia biennis var. diffusa, commonly known as the woolly tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic properties make it a valued plant in both ornamental and traditional herbal uses.
