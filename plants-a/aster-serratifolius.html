Aster serratifolius

Aster serratifolius, commonly known as the serratedleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
