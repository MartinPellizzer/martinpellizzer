Aristolochia gigas var. sturtevantii

Aristolochia gigas var. sturtevantii, commonly known as the Sturtevant's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance.

The species is also notable for its chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
