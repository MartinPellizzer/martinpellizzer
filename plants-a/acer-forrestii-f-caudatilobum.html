Acer forrestii f. caudatilobum

Acer forrestii f. caudatilobum, commonly known as the long-lobed maple, is a unique variant of the species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaf lobes that give it a striking, almost fern-like appearance. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are usually bright green during the growing season and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
