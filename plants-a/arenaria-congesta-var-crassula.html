Arenaria congesta var. crassula

Arenaria congesta var. crassula, commonly known as the thick-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in coastal or arid environments. The plant features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
