Abelmoschus angulosus var. mahendragiriensis

Abelmoschus angulosus var. mahendragiriensis, commonly known as Mahendragiri okra, is a variant of the okra species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its angular, ridged leaves and yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant produces edible, mucilaginous pods that are typically harvested when young and tender. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content, making it a significant crop in certain regions of India.
