Aster acris var. trinervis

Aster acris var. trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf venation. The plant typically features slender stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that have three prominent veins. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often found in moist meadows and open woodlands, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
