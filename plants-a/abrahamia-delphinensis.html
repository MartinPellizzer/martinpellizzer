Abrahamia delphinensis

Abrahamia delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphin Island bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, where it tolerates salty air and coastal winds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
