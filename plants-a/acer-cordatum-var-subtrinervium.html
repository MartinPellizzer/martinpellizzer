Acer cordatum var. subtrinervium

Acer cordatum var. subtrinervium, commonly known as the three-veined heartleaf maple, is a variant of the heart-shaped maple species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern China, particularly in Sichuan and Yunnan provinces. The plant is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a heart-shaped appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow-green, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
