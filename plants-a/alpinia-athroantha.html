Alpinia athroantha

Alpinia athroantha, commonly known as the giant ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces striking clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by ornamental, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Alpinia athroantha is also valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in traditional medicine.
