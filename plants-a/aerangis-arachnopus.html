Aerangis arachnopus

Aerangis arachnopus, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a unique orchid species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, with long, slender flowers that resemble delicate webs. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers are often pale green or yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
