Areca polystachya

Areca polystachya, commonly known as the giant areca palm, is a tall, slender palm native to Southeast Asia.

It features a straight, unbranched trunk that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a rosette at the top of the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, followed by elongated fruits containing a single seed.

Areca polystachya is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and as a landscape plant.
