Abies balsamea subsp. fraseri

Abies balsamea subsp.

fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Appalachian Mountains of the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are short and slightly twisted.

The cones of this species are typically reddish-brown and hang downward, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often growing at high elevations.

The Fraser fir is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in the Christmas tree industry.
