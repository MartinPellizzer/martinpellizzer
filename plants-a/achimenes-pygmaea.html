Achimenes pygmaea

Achimenes pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf flame violet, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has hairy, upright stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
