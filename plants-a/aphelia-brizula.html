Aphelia brizula

Aphelia brizula, commonly known as the velvet leaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Aphelia brizula thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
