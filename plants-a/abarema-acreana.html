Abarema acreana

Abarema acreana, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, colorful inflorescences. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its vibrant floral display.
