Avena newtonii

Avena newtonii, commonly known as Newton's oat, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Avena newtonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
