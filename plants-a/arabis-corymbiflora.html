Arabis corymbiflora

Arabis corymbiflora, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are arranged in dense, branching clusters. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments.

Arabis corymbiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
