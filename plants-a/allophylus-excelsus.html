Allophylus excelsus

Allophylus excelsus, commonly known as the silverleaf acanthus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, spiny inflorescences. The leaves are typically bipinnate, with a silvery underside that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
