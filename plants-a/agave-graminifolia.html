Agave graminifolia

Agave graminifolia, commonly known as the grass agave, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white or yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
