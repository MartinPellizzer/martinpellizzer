Abies menziesii

Abies menziesii, commonly known as the Douglas fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are flat and dark green, with a white stripe on the underside. Its cones are typically cylindrical and hang downward, containing winged seeds.

The Douglas fir is valued for its durable wood and plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
