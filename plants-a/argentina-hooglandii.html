Argentina hooglandii

Argentina hooglandii, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and tall, slender stems that can reach up to two meters in height. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically growing in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
