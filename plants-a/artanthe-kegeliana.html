Artanthe kegeliana

Artanthe kegeliana, commonly known as the Kegel's artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble small umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers range in color from pink to purple.

Artanthe kegeliana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
