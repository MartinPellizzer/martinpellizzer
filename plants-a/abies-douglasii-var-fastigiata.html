Abies douglasii var. fastigiata

Abies douglasii var. fastigiata, commonly known as the stiff-branch fir, is a variant of the Douglas fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are short, stiff, and arranged in compact clusters along the shoots. Its cones are typically upright and reddish-brown, maturing to a darker color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in landscaping due to its unique shape and hardiness.
