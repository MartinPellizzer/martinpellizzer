Angianthus whitei

Angianthus whitei, commonly known as the white angianthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, white to pale yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Angianthus whitei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, daisy-like appearance.
