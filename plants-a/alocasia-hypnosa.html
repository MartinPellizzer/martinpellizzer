Alocasia hypnosa

Alocasia hypnosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant's foliage is typically a deep green with a waxy appearance, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Alocasia hypnosa is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in indoor gardens and tropical landscapes.
