Acer melliodorum

Acer melliodorum, commonly known as the sweet gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and purple in the fall. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain spiky seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The sweet gum tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
