Arnica aspera

Arnica aspera, commonly known as rough mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, and its roots are thick and fleshy. Arnica aspera is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
