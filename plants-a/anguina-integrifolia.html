Anguina integrifolia

Anguina integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf anguilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often have a slightly velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Anguina integrifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
