Acnistus lycioides

Acnistus lycioides, commonly known as the false holly or silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of the true holly. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are usually glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture.

Acnistus lycioides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in regions with limited water availability.
