Aquilegia coccinea

Aquilegia coccinea, commonly known as the red monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking crimson flowers that bloom in early spring, with spurs extending from the petals. The plant has palmate leaves divided into five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Aquilegia coccinea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
