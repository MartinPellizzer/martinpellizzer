Acacia puberula

Acacia puberula, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its seed pods are woody and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia puberula is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
