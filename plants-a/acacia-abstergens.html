Acacia abstergens

Acacia abstergens, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Acacia abstergens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
