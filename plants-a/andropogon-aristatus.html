Andropogon aristatus

Andropogon aristatus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of grasslands and open woodlands.

Andropogon aristatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native habitats.
