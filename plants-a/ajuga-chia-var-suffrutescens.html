Ajuga chia var. suffrutescens

Ajuga chia var. suffrutescens, commonly known as spreading ajuga, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens and natural landscapes.
