Atriplex subcordata

Atriplex subcordata, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the genus Atriplex, which includes over 200 species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring.

Atriplex subcordata is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.
