Acacia armata var. plana

Acacia armata var. plana, commonly known as the flat-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and its distinctive, flat, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a capacity for regeneration after fire.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
