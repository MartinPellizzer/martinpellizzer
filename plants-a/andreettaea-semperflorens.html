Andreettaea semperflorens

Andreettaea semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming orchid, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, long-lasting flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Andreettaea semperflorens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
