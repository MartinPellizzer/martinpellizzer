Arundinaria leucorhoda f. atamiana

Arundinaria leucorhoda f. atamiana, commonly known as the Atamian bamboo, is a rare and ornamental grass species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are typically green to reddish in color, with finely textured leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are often overlooked due to their subtle nature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Due to its rarity and unique aesthetic, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty plant enthusiasts.
