Allium canadense subsp. lavendulare

Allium canadense subsp.

lavendulare, commonly known as Canada bunching onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its mild onion flavor and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
