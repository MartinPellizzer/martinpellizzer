Amelanchier micropetala

Amelanchier micropetala, commonly known as the western serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces small, edible berries in the spring. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing before the leaves in early spring.

Amelanchier micropetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat benefits.
