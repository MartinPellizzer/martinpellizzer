Acalypha capillaris

Acalypha capillaris, commonly known as silky ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are usually opposite, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant often produces fluffy, cotton-like seed heads. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The plant's ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
