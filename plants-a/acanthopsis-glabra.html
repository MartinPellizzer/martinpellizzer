Acanthopsis glabra

Acanthopsis glabra, commonly known as the smooth acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Acanthopsis glabra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
