Achnatherum occidentale subsp. pubescens

Achnatherum occidentale subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the western needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a fine layer of hairs.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, typically appearing in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight pubescence on the lower surface.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
