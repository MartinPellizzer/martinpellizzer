Acer pensylvanicum subsp. typicum

Acer pensylvanicum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The tree has opposite, palmate leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in diameter, with serrated edges. It produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that disperse its seeds.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
