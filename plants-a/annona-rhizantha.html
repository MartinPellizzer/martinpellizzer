Annona rhizantha

Annona rhizantha, commonly known as the Brazilian cherimoya, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is similar in appearance to a cherimoya but has a unique, creamy texture and flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its nutritional content.
