Acacia aptaneura

Acacia aptaneura, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, thread-like segments, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds. Acacia aptaneura is adapted to dry and sandy soils, making it a hardy species found in various arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
