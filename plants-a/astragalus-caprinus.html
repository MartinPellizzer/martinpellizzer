Astragalus caprinus

Astragalus caprinus, commonly known as goat's head, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant has a slender, upright stem with alternate, pinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow to reddish in color, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Astragalus caprinus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various health purposes.
