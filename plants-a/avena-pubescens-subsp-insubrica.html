Avena pubescens subsp. insubrica

Avena pubescens subsp.

insubrica, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its scientific name "pubescens.".

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue due to the dense pubescence.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration.
