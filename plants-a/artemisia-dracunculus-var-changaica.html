Artemisia dracunculus var. changaica

Artemisia dracunculus var. changaica, commonly known as Chinese tarragon, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typical of the sunflower family. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which have a distinct, slightly anise-like flavor.

This variety is valued in both ornamental gardening and culinary applications.
