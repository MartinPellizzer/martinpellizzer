Astylus alpinus

Astylus alpinus, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Astylus alpinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in harsh, high-altitude climates.
