Astragalus zarjabadensis

Astragalus zarjabadensis, commonly known as Zarjabad milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties and are used in traditional herbal remedies.

Astragalus zarjabadensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats due to its deep root system.
