Acer laetum f. tricolor

Acer laetum f. tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored maple, is a decorative cultivar of the maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
