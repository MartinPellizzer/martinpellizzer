Alisma graminifolium f. submersum

Alisma graminifolium f. submersum, commonly known as the submerged arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant adapted to aquatic environments.

It features slender, ribbon-like leaves that are fully submerged in water, aiding in photosynthesis in low-light conditions. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are often submerged or just at the water's surface.

This subspecies exhibits specialized morphological adaptations to thrive in submerged conditions, making it a unique example of aquatic plant evolution.
