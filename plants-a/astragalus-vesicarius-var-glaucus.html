Astragalus vesicarius var. glaucus

Astragalus vesicarius var. glaucus, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
