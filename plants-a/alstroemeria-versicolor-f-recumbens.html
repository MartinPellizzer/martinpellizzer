Alstroemeria versicolor f. recumbens

Alstroemeria versicolor f. recumbens, commonly known as the recumbent alstroemeria, is a unique variant of the alstroemeria plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit.

This form exhibits a more spreading and low-growing structure compared to the typical upright varieties, making it suitable for ground cover or edging in gardens. The botanical features include elongated, strap-like leaves that are often a vibrant green, and flowers that display a range of colors, typically in shades of pink, red, and white. It is native to South America and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
