Apodytes cambodiana

Apodytes cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian sandpaper tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, sandpaper-like bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and open areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
