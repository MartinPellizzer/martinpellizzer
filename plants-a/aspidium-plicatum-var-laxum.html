Aspidium plicatum var. laxum

Aspidium plicatum var. laxum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Aspidium.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features delicate, pinnately divided fronds with a distinctive, curled appearance. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in aquatic or bog gardens.
