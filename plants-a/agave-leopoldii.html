Agave leopoldii

Agave leopoldii, commonly known as Leopold's agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with spines. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous white or greenish flowers. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
