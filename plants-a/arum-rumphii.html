Arum rumphii

Arum rumphii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or swamp arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, and it thrives in wet, swampy environments. Its corms are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique flowering structure.
