Astragalus neokarelinianus

Astragalus neokarelinianus, commonly known as the Neokarelinian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.

Its seed pods contain small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.
