Aceras anthropophorum f. apiculatum

Aceras anthropophorum f. apiculatum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a variant of the bellflower species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
