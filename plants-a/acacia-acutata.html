Acacia acutata

Acacia acutata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Acacia acutata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a capacity for rapid growth after rainfall.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
