Adenoscilla unifolia

Adenoscilla unifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Adenoscilla unifolia is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
