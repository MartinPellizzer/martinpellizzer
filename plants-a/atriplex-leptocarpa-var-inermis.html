Atriplex leptocarpa var. inermis

Atriplex leptocarpa var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, smooth, and often bluish-gray leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing soils in harsh conditions.

The leaves and stems are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
