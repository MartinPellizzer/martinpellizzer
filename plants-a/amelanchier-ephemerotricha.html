Amelanchier ephemerotricha

Amelanchier ephemerotricha, commonly known as the short-hair serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in late summer. Its leaves are oval with a hairy texture, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Amelanchier ephemerotricha is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
