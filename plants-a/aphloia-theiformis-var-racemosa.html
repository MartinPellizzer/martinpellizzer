Aphloia theiformis var. racemosa

Aphloia theiformis var. racemosa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which consist of clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
