Apocynum × macrophyllum

Apocynum × macrophyllum, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a hybrid plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces a milky sap that is toxic to many animals, including humans.

This species is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along streambanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
