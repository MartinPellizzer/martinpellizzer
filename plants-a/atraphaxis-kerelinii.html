Atraphaxis kerelinii

Atraphaxis kerelinii, commonly known as the Cape buffalo thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
