Arctostaphylos viscida subsp. viscida

Arctostaphylos viscida subsp.

viscida, commonly known as the sticky madrone, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy evergreen leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in late spring, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its subspecies is distinguished by its sticky resinous coating on the leaves and stems.
