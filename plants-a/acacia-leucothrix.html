Acacia leucothrix

Acacia leucothrix, commonly known as the white-stemmed wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, greyish stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Acacia leucothrix is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and ability to fix nitrogen.
