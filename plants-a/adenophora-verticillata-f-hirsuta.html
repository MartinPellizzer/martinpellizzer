Adenophora verticillata f. hirsuta

Adenophora verticillata f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy false indigo, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
