Achillea polyphylla

Achillea polyphylla, commonly known as field yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, flat-topped flower heads composed of numerous small white or pale yellow florets. The plant features finely divided, silver-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was believed to have healing properties.
