Astragalus ahangarensis

Astragalus ahangarensis, commonly known as the Ahangar Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or pale purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
