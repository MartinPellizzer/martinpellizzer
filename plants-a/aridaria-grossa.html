Aridaria grossa

Aridaria grossa, commonly known as the broad-leaved desert grass, is a perennial plant adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It features robust, fibrous roots that help it access water from deep underground. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale in color.

Aridaria grossa is often found in sandy soils and plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
