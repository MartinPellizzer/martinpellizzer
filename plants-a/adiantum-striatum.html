Adiantum striatum

Adiantum striatum, commonly known as the striped maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often adorned with a distinctive silver or white stripe along the central vein. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a graceful, arching appearance that adds ornamental value to gardens and indoor spaces.

Adiantum striatum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its elegant foliage.
