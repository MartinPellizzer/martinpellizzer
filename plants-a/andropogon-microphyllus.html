Andropogon microphyllus

Andropogon microphyllus, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It features narrow, blue-green blades that turn coppery red in the fall, and its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant is adapted to prairie ecosystems and thrives in well-drained soils. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Little bluestem is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
