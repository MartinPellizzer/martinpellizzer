Acaena × ovina

Acaena × ovina, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a hybrid plant originating from the cross between Acaena crassipes and Acaena hastata.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub characterized by its dense, prickly foliage and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions of New Zealand.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas outside its native range.
