Aster capusii

Aster capusii, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to pink ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

Aster capusii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
