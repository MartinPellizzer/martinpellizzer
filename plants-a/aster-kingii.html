Aster kingii

Aster kingii, commonly known as the King's aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Aster kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
