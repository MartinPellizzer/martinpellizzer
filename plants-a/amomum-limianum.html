Amomum limianum

Amomum limianum, commonly known as the greater galangal, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces dark brown, finger-like rhizomes that are rich in essential oils and are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a distinct, spicy aroma.

Amomum limianum thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary value.
