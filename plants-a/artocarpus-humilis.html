Artocarpus humilis

Artocarpus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf breadfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that turn yellow when mature. The fruits are typically harvested when they are still firm, and they have a mild, sweet flavor.

Artocarpus humilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source in tropical regions.
