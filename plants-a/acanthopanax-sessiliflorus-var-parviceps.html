Acanthopanax sessiliflorus var. parviceps

Acanthopanax sessiliflorus var. parviceps, commonly known as the small-headed dogwood, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often found in rocky or forested habitats at higher elevations.
