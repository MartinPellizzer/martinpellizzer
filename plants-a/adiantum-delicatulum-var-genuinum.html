Adiantum delicatulum var. genuinum

Adiantum delicatulum var. genuinum, commonly known as the southern maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, arching fronds that are dark green and often have a slight bluish tint. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Its botanical beauty makes it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
