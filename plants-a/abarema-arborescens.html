Abarema arborescens

Abarema arborescens, commonly known as the Brazilian redwood, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pink in color. Its fruits are pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Abarema arborescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
