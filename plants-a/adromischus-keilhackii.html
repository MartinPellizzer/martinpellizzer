Adromischus keilhackii

Adromischus keilhackii, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, greenish-blue leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact and ornamental appearance. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and rock gardens.
