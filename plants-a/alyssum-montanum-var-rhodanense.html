Alyssum montanum var. rhodanense

Alyssum montanum var. rhodanense, commonly known as the Alpine Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Alyssum montanum var. rhodanense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
