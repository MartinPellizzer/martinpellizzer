Astragalus palaestinus var. palaestinus

Astragalus palaestinus var. palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestine milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus palaestinus var. palaestinus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
