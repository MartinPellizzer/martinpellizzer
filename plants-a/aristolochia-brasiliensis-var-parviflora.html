Aristolochia brasiliensis var. parviflora

Aristolochia brasiliensis var. parviflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, pipe-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its flowers produce a strong, spicy scent that attracts specific pollinators, such as certain species of moths.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
