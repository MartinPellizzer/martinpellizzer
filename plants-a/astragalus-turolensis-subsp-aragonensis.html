Astragalus turolensis subsp. aragonensis

Astragalus turolensis subsp.

aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
