Amomum robustum

Amomum robustum, commonly known as the robust cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant typically grows up to 1-2 meters tall, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves and small white or greenish flowers. Its rhizomes are rich in essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Amomum robustum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.
