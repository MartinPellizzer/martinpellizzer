Acacia aneura var. latifolia

Acacia aneura var. latifolia, commonly known as the river red gum, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves and distinctive greyish-brown bark that peels in strips. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which appear in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and in open woodlands.

This species is also valued for its timber and its role in providing habitat for native wildlife.
