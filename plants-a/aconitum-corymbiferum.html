Aconitum corymbiferum

Aconitum corymbiferum, commonly known as the clustered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spires of flowers, which are arranged in a corymb-like cluster. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum corymbiferum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland or cottage garden settings.
