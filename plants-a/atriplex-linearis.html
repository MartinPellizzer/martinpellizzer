Atriplex linearis

Atriplex linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its linear, succulent leaves that are often coated with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments.

Atriplex linearis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
