Abrahamia buxifolia

Abrahamia buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf abrahamia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and dense foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
