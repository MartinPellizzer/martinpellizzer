Aechmea exsudans

Aechmea exsudans, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking red bracts that surround its inflorescence, which can bloom multiple times throughout its lifespan. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Aechmea exsudans is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and relatively low maintenance.

Morphology

Aechmea exsudans has rosette-shaped leaves that are typically broad, sword-like, and arranged in a compact circle around the central stem.

The leaves are usually dark green with a waxy surface and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem with a fibrous, rough-textured bark that is often brown or reddish in color. It produces inflorescences with clustered, pink to reddish flowers that bloom during the rainy season, and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds and water.
