Astragalus preussii var. latus

Astragalus preussii var. latus, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus preussii var. latus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
