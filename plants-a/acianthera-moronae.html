Acianthera moronae

Acianthera moronae, commonly known as the Morona violet, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically produces small, lance-shaped leaves and blooms during the wet season. Acianthera moronae thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
