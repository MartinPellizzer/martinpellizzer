Ambrosina bassii var. reticulata

Ambrosina bassii var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated ambrosia, is a unique member of the Ambrosiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive reticulate venation pattern on its leaves, which gives it a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent foliage. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from pale yellow to deep orange.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
