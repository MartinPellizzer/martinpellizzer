Atragene cirrhosa

Atragene cirrhosa, commonly known as the curly stem bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, winding stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight curl, giving the plant its distinctive appearance.

Atragene cirrhosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
