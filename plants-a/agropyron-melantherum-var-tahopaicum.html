Agropyron melantherum var. tahopaicum

Agropyron melantherum var. tahopaicum, commonly known as the Tahopea needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. This plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments with low precipitation.
