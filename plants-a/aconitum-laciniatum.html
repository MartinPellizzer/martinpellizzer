Aconitum laciniatum

Aconitum laciniatum, commonly known as the divided aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has finely dissected, palmate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
