Adamboe bicolor

Adamboe bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soil.

Adamboe bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
