Amaranthus scleranthoides f. abingdonensis

Amaranthus scleranthoides f. abingdonensis, commonly known as the Abingdon amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored inflorescences. The plant typically features simple, alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like structures, which are often reddish or greenish in color.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant foliage and floral displays.
