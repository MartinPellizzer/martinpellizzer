Alsophila borbonica var. sevathiana

Alsophila borbonica var. sevathiana, commonly known as the Sevathian tree fern, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant exhibits a typical fern morphology with a rhizomatous structure and a well-developed root system. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forested areas with high moisture levels.

The fronds are typically a deep green, providing both ornamental value and ecological benefits.
