Astragalus centraligobicus

Astragalus centraligobicus, commonly known as the central plains milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the central plains of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across suitable environments.
