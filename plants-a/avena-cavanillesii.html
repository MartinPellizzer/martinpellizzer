Avena cavanillesii

Avena cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' oat, is a perennial grass native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
