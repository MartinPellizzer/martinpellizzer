Acer negundo subsp. interior

Acer negundo subsp.

interior, commonly known as the interior boxelder, is a deciduous tree native to the central United States. It is a member of the maple family (Aceraceae) and is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and small, palmate leaves.

The tree produces samaras, or winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in riparian zones and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
