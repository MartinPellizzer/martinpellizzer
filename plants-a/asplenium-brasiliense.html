Asplenium brasiliense

Asplenium brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Asplenium brasiliense is a low-maintenance plant that is popular among fern enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal.
