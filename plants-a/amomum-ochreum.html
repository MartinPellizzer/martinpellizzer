Amomum ochreum

Amomum ochreum, commonly known as the yellow cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and covered with a fine layer of white pubescence.

Amomum ochreum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
