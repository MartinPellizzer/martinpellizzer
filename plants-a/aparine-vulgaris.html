Aparine vulgaris

Aparine vulgaris, commonly known as common bedstraw, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are covered with stiff, hooked hairs. The plant produces small white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Aparine vulgaris is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along paths, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
