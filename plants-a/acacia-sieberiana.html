Acacia sieberiana

Acacia sieberiana, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden globular inflorescences, which are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are dispersed by animals or through wind.

Acacia sieberiana is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
