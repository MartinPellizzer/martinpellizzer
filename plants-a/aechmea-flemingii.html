Aechmea flemingii

Aechmea flemingii, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with vibrant red bracts that contrast beautifully with its green foliage. Aechmea flemingii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water make it well adapted to its natural habitat.

Morphology

Aechmea flemingii has leaves that are typically sword-shaped, arranged in a rosette pattern, and can grow up to 60 cm in length, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, fibrous base that supports the rosette of leaves. Its flowers are clustered in a spike at the center of the rosette, with bright red bracts that enclose small white flowers, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains seeds dispersed by birds and other animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding environment.
