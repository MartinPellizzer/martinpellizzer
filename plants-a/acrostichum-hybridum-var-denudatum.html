Acrostichum hybridum var. denudatum

Acrostichum hybridum var. denudatum, commonly known as the naked fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green and lack the spore-producing structures found in other ferns. The plant grows in shaded, humid environments and is often found near water bodies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, serrated leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
