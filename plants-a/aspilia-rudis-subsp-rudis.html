Aspilia rudis subsp. rudis

Aspilia rudis subsp.

rudis, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow ray florets and dark purple disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Aspilia rudis subsp.

rudis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
