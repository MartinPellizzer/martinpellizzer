Aloe parvibracteata var. zuluensis

Aloe parvibracteata var. zuluensis, commonly known as the Zulu aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
