Avena pallens

Avena pallens, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that give it its common name, and it often grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. Avena pallens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
