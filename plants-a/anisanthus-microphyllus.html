Anisanthus microphyllus

Anisanthus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved anisanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Due to its low water requirements and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
