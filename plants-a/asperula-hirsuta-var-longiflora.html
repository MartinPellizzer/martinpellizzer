Asperula hirsuta var. longiflora

Asperula hirsuta var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered woodruff, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and oval-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
