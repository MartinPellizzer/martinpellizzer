Acacia ancistrophylla var. perarcuata

Acacia ancistrophylla var. perarcuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, phyllodes that are narrow and linear in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes during the spring and summer, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage structure.
