Alstonia longifolia

Alstonia longifolia, commonly known as the African angel or the tree of life, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing numerous seeds. Alstonia longifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
