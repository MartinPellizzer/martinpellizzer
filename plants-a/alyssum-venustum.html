Alyssum venustum

Alyssum venustum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs.

Alyssum venustum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
