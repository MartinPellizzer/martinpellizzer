Avenula pratensis subsp. iberica

Avenula pratensis subsp.

iberica, commonly known as the Iberian meadow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often leafless above the base. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, three-lobed spikelets.

It plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing food and shelter for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
