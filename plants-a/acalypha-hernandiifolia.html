Acalypha hernandiifolia

Acalypha hernandiifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, red or purple bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Acalypha hernandiifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
