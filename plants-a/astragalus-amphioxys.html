Astragalus amphioxys

Astragalus amphioxys, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to arid environments through its deep root system. Its seed pods are pod-shaped and contain several small, hard seeds.

Astragalus amphioxys plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
