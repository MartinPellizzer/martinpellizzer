Acaena alpina

Acaena alpina, commonly known as the Alpine woodrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Acaena alpina is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in rocky soils and cold climates.
