Alchemilla coriacea subsp. straminea

Alchemilla coriacea subsp.

straminea, commonly known as the woolly lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thick, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
