Arabis sagittata subsp. planisiliqua

Arabis sagittata subsp.

planisiliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly wavy margin.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
