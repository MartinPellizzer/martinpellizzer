Arundinaria hakonensis

Arundinaria hakonensis, commonly known as Hakone bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to Japan.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and landscaping. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, aesthetic plant.
