Asclepias bagshawei

Asclepias bagshawei, commonly known as the Bagshaw's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, orange and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among milkweed species. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Asclepias bagshawei serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting its life cycle and contributing to local biodiversity.
