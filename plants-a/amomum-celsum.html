Amomum celsum

Amomum celsum, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically with a pale green to yellowish color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
