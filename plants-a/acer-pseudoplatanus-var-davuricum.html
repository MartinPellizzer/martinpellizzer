Acer pseudoplatanus var. davuricum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. davuricum, commonly known as the Caucasus maple, is a variant of the common maple native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and horticulture.
