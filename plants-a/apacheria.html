Apacheria

Apacheria, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue to reduce water loss.

Apacheria is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
