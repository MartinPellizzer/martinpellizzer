Angraecum minutum

Angraecum minutum, commonly known as the minute angraecum, is a rare and delicate orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific nocturnal moths.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
