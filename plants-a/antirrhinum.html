Antirrhinum

Antirrhinum, commonly known as the snapdragon, is a flowering plant belonging to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae).

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive, tube-like flowers that open at the ends. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, orange, and pink, and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Antirrhinum is typically grown as an annual, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
