Adamanthus amabilis

Adamanthus amabilis, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or the golden palm, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Adamanthus amabilis is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
