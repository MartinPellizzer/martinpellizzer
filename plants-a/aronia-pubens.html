Aronia pubens

Aronia pubens, commonly known as the red chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. Aronia pubens typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential health benefits from its berries.
