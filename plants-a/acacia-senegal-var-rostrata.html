Acacia senegal var. rostrata

Acacia senegal var. rostrata, commonly known as the gum arabic tree, is a species native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and compound leaves. The plant is renowned for producing a high-quality gum, known as acacia gum, which is used in food, pharmaceuticals, and industrial applications. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in spikes, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

The tree plays an important ecological role in preventing soil erosion and supporting local wildlife.
