Acacia cufodontii

Acacia cufodontii, commonly known as the African feverfew, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in rounded, globular clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Acacia cufodontii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and woodlands.
