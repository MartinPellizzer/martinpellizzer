Astragalus brachypterus var. parviceps

Astragalus brachypterus var. parviceps, commonly known as the short-winged milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the pods are slender and usually hairy.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
