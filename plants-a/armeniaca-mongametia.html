Armeniaca mongametia

Armeniaca mongametia, commonly known as the Mongolian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that mature to a dark red or black color, containing a single hard seed. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for ecological restoration, it is of interest in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
