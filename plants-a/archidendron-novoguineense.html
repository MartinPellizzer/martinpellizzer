Archidendron novoguineense

Archidendron novoguineense, commonly known as the Guinean legume, is a woody shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Due to its ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for forage or ornamental purposes.
