Antiaris innoxia

Antiaris innoxia, commonly known as the poisonwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and distinctive, sparsely branched trunk. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Its sap contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation and poisoning if ingested.

Despite its harmful properties, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
