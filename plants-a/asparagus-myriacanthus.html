Asparagus myriacanthus

Asparagus myriacanthus, commonly known as the giant asparagus or wild asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, needle-like berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and nutritional value, it is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
