Arthraxon violaceus

Arthraxon violaceus, commonly known as the violet-headed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is often found in disturbed soils. Its flowering stems are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Arthraxon violaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
