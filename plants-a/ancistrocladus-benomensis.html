Ancistrocladus benomensis

Ancistrocladus benomensis, commonly known as the Benin poisonwood, is a small evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Ancistrocladaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
