Alchemilla sokolowskii

Alchemilla sokolowskii, commonly known as the Polish lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Alchemilla sokolowskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
