Astragalus caspicus var. elbrusensis

Astragalus caspicus var. elbrusensis, commonly known as the Elbrus milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus Mountains.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
