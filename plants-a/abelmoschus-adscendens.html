Abelmoschus adscendens

Abelmoschus adscendens, commonly known as Okra, is a flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to Africa and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces edible pods that are harvested when immature, before the seeds inside fully develop. Its leaves are lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

Abelmoschus adscendens is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and use in various cuisines around the world.
