Acaciella valenzuelana

Acaciella valenzuelana, commonly known as the Mexican thornbush, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate and have a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Acaciella valenzuelana is adapted to withstand drought and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
