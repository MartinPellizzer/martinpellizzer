Alchemilla acutiloba var. micans

Alchemilla acutiloba var. micans, commonly known as the silver-leaved alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
