Aster microcephalus f. humilis

Aster microcephalus f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its compact growth habit and delicate flowers make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
