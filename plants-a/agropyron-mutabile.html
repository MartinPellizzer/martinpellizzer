Agropyron mutabile

Agropyron mutabile, commonly known as the variable wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate poor soil conditions.

Agropyron mutabile is sometimes cultivated for forage or as a ornamental plant due to its hardy nature.
