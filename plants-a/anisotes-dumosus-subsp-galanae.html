Anisotes dumosus subsp. galanae

Anisotes dumosus subsp.

galanae, commonly known as the Galana sandmat, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss and maximize photosynthesis in harsh environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
