Allantoma multiflora

Allantoma multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered allantoma, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Allantoma multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
