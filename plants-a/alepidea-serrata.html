Alepidea serrata

Alepidea serrata, commonly known as the serrated alepidea, is a perennial shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Alepidea serrata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
