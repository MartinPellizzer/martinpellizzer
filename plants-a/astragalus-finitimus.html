Astragalus finitimus

Astragalus finitimus, commonly known as the rare mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
