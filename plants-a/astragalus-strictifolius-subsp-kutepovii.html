Astragalus strictifolius subsp. kutepovii

Astragalus strictifolius subsp.

kutepovii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in steppe and desert environments.

The plant has slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves that are usually narrow and stiff. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, indehiscent pods.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and its potential medicinal properties.
