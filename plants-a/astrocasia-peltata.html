Astrocasia peltata

Astrocasia peltata, commonly known as the star-shaped leaf plant, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble a star, with multiple lobes radiating from a central point. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental appeal. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value and ability to purify indoor air.
