Anthemis pseudoatrata

Anthemis pseudoatrata, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often with a strong, camphor-like scent.

The yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
