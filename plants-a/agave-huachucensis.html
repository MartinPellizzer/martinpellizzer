Agave huachucensis

Agave huachucensis, commonly known as the Huachucensis agave, is a species native to the central and southern regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with white or pale green markings, and they are covered in a fine layer of wax. This plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellow flowers, which are often followed by the development of a unique fruit.

Agave huachucensis is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
