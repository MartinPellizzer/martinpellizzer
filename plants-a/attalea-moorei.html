Attalea moorei

Attalea moorei, commonly known as the buriti palm, is a tall, slender palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, feather-like leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers that develop into fibrous, orange-colored fruits. The plant is well adapted to wetland and swampy environments, often found in floodplains and marshes. Its trunk is covered in a thick, fibrous layer that can be harvested for various uses.

The buriti palm plays an important ecological role in its habitat and is also valued for its nutritional and economic benefits.
