Apium tenuifolium

Apium tenuifolium, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in umbels. Native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in salty, well-drained soils. The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, delicate appearance.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is also noted, though it is not commonly cultivated for culinary purposes.
