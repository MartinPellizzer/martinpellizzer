Astragalus fallax

Astragalus fallax, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Astragalus fallax is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
