Artemisia glauca var. dracunculoides

Artemisia glauca var. dracunculoides, commonly known as bluegreen wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are densely covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
