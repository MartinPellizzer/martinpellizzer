Anodendron

Anodendron, commonly known as the flame tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lythraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, showy clusters. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Anodendron species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant floral displays.
