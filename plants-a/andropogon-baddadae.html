Andropogon baddadae

Andropogon baddadae, commonly known as the Madagascan grass, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
