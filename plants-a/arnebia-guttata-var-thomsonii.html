Arnebia guttata var. thomsonii

Arnebia guttata var. thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's bloodroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking red-orange flowers in early spring. The plant has palmately divided, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and often covered in small glands. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
