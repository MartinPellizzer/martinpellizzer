Annona dolabripetala

Annona dolabripetala, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, yellow-orange fruit that is edible and has a sweet, tropical flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit is not only a food source for various animals but also has traditional medicinal uses in some indigenous communities.
