Achnatherum pekinense subsp. effusum

Achnatherum pekinense subsp.

effusum, commonly known as the Peking needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to northern China. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces tall, wiry flowering stems.

The plant typically grows in open, sandy or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spikelet that is often drooping.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.
