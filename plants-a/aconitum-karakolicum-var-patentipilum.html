Aconitum karakolicum var. patentipilum

Aconitum karakolicum var. patentipilum, commonly known as the Karakol aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are borne in racemes at the top of the stem.

The variety patentipilum is distinguished by its distinctive, finely hairy sepals and bracts.
