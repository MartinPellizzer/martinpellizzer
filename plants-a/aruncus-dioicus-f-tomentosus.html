Aruncus dioicus f. tomentosus

Aruncus dioicus f. tomentosus, commonly known as the fuzzy goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of white, feathery flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
