Ageratina trianae

Ageratina trianae, commonly known as the Trianza blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fluffy blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
