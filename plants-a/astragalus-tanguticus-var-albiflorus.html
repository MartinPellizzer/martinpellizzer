Astragalus tanguticus var. albiflorus

Astragalus tanguticus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus tanguticus var. albiflorus is part of the legume family and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
