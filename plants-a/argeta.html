Argeta

Argeta, commonly known as silverleaf clover, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. Argeta has a deep root system that helps it thrive in poor soils and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting the surrounding soil.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for landscaping and restoration projects.
