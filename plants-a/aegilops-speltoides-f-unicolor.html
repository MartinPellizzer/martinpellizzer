Aegilops speltoides f. unicolor

Aegilops speltoides f. unicolor, commonly known as the green rye grass, is a wild relative of wheat with significant botanical interest.

It is a diploid species belonging to the tribe Aegilopinae within the grass family Poaceae. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellow flowers.

This species is valued for its genetic diversity and potential contributions to wheat improvement programs.
