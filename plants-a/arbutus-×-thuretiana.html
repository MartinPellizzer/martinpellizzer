Arbutus × thuretiana

Arbutus × thuretiana, commonly known as the European madrona or variegated madrona, is a hybrid evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with white or yellow variegation, and its clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds but not typically for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and adaptability to various climates contribute to its horticultural appeal.
