Amphiblemma cymosum

Amphiblemma cymosum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and is often found in mountainous regions of Argentina and Chile.
