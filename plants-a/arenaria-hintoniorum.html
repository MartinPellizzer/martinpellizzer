Arenaria hintoniorum

Arenaria hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to specific regions of the British Isles.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas or rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Arenaria hintoniorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy environments.
