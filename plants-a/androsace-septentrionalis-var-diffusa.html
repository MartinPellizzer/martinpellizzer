Androsace septentrionalis var. diffusa

Androsace septentrionalis var. diffusa, commonly known as the northern sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It features dense clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has succulent, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in cold, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountain meadow habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
