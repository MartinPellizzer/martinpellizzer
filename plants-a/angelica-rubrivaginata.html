Angelica rubrivaginata

Angelica rubrivaginata, commonly known as the red-barked angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, compound leaves with a distinct reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in an umbel, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Angelica rubrivaginata thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental and therapeutic uses.
