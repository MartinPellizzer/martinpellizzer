Astragalus stictolobus

Astragalus stictolobus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy species in such conditions.
