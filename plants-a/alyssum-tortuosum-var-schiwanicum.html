Alyssum tortuosum var. schiwanicum

Alyssum tortuosum var. schiwanicum, commonly known as Schiwan's alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
