Aspidopterys elliptica

Aspidopterys elliptica, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

The fronds are typically evergreen and can persist for several years, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
